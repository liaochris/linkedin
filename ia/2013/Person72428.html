Karl Reichert's Experience

Director of the Capri Theater

Plymouth Christian Youth Center

February 2007 – Present (6 years 9 months) 2210 Oliver Ave. No, Minneapolis, MN 55411

Serves as the overall artistic director for the Capri Theater. Programs created with artistic associates include the popular Legends jazz series; a classical music series with the Saint Paul Chamber Orchestra; monthly Freedom of Xpression open mic; Capri After School Theater (CAST) and the Capri Youth Apprentice program in collaboration with Children’s Theatre Company; Camp Capri summer arts program for urban middle school youth in collaboration with Camden Music School, Hollywood Studio of Dance and Homewood Studios; and the Capri lobby gallery that features exhibits by Northside artists.

Leads the redevelopment of the Capri Theater into a first-rate performing arts venue, creating a sustainable impact on economic and neighborhood redevelopment in North Minneapolis. Guided $800,000 in renovations in 2009 as part of the first phase of the “Capri Theater Renaissance Campaign.” A second $6 million phase is in the works, and plans include a “glass box” performance/event space along West Broadway, an expanded lobby, dressing rooms, rehearsal rooms, restrooms, as well as a second floor of classrooms and multi-purpose space for youth arts programming.

Promotes utilization of the 250-seat venue by professional, emerging and amateur artists and performing arts organizations and raises significant revenue to contribute toward the venue’s operating costs. Utilization by rental clients continues to increase year to year, and the planned expansion will improve the Capri’s viability as a performing arts venue.

Manages the Capri’s day-to-day operations, including marketing, public relations and ticket sales for PCYC produced series and events, the Capri’s website (thecapritheater.org) and social media, rental sales and contracts, and represents PCYC and the Capri in arts networks including the Northside Arts Leadership Group, Minnesota Theater Alliance and Minnesota Citizens for the Arts.

  • Cantus Vocal Ensemble

    Vice Chair
    • September 2011 to Present

    Acclaimed as “the premier men’s vocal ensemble in the United States” (Fanfare), Cantus is committed to inspiring audiences with music performed at the highest level. Rehearsing and performing without a conductor or music director, the nine members of Cantus are renowned for adventurous programming spanning many periods and genres, including work commissioned specifically for the group. The Washington Post hails the ensemble’s sound as having both “exalting finesse” and “expressive power,” and refers to their music-making as “spontaneous grace.” Cantus shares its impeccable singing, engaging stage personality and heartfelt interpretations with audiences across the country as they tour and at home in Minneapolis and St. Paul. At home and on the road, the artists of Cantus share their own love of singing with thousands of student singers annually to ensure the future of choral arts. Cantus is pleased to be able share its music through performances, recordings and digital media.
