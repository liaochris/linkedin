Marco Wagemakers' Summary

Tired of trying to keep up with the rapidly evolving geo spatial technology? Frustrated by the amount of possibilities to make Geographic Inforation System (GIS) available, for instance, in mobile and web solutions? Sick of trying to align this technology to the business?

My name is Marco Wagemakers and have more than eight years’ experience in the Esri ArcGIS system. I always combine the passion for technology with a desire to help customers discover the right geospatial solution.

I have been helping the customers to design a perfect solution or architecture for many years now. These mean to really reap the benefits of GIS. This is not just technology, but it is about using technology to solve real business problems together with customers.

Marco Wagemakers' Experience

Product Consultant / Business Developer

ESRI Nederland

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Computer Software industry

January 2011 – Present (3 years)

As a Product Consultant I focus on functional and technical developments related to the Esri software in its entirety and in particular, ArcGIS apps, ArcGIS Runtime and ArcGIS Online. I ensure that my personal knowledge and skills of the products are linked to market demand. Because of that knowledge, I’m able to consult/advise internal colleagues and external clients in projects. The questions vary between functional requirements, technical support issues and architectural design in relation to products. I am able to demonstrate the value of using GIS software, not just feature functionality. In the role of Business Developer, I consult with and advise the Dutch Cadaster to reap the benefits of GIS.

Operationeel Informatie Management namens Esri Nederland

Kadaster

Government Agency; 1001-5000 employees; Government Administration industry

August 2010 – July 2011 (1 year)

I worked in the function of operational information management at the Dutch Cadaster/Office for land registry. It was focusing on the topographical domain of creating the topographical map scale 1:10,000. This whole process is done with ArcGIS for Desktop and ArcGIS Workflow Manager. On the top of ArcGIS for Desktop, there is an custom develop\topographic extension, created by the Dutch Cadaster. This extension is managed by the development team of the IT department of the Cadaster. My work focused on gathering and working out new requirements for this extension. Another task focused on the migration of all software components from ArcGIS 9.3 to ArcGIS 10.

Technisch Specialist

AQUAGIS BV

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Information Technology and Services industry

July 2005 – December 2010 (5 years 6 months)

I started as a support engineer in the support department at AQUAGIS. AQUAGIS was part of the Esri Nederlands Group. My work was based on answering Water Board questions related to the Esri technology and supporting the specific product for Water Management, the Integral Waterboard Information System (INTWIS). INTWIS was based on ArcGIS Desktop and ArcSDE. Then I started with software development in a range of mobile products in ArcPad. In that period Esri introduced ArcGIS Server and ArcGIS Mobile. As part of the development team we started to develop different Ledger web applications and also a Mobile Waterway Inspection solution. The last year I spent more time in consulting the clients in the opportunities for server and mobile solutions.

Young Leadership Program

Young Leadership Program of the CDA/CDJA

September 2008 – October 2009 (1 year 2 months)

1 Year training program for strict selected high potentials, guided by political leaders and businessmen to develop leadership competences.

Trainee

Nieuwland Advies

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Information Technology and Services industry

January 2004 – July 2006 (2 years 7 months)

As a work student, I supported mr R.S. de Waard in his promotion at the Technical University of Eindhoven. The goal of my work was based on developing GIS tools to support Simlandscape. Simlandscape is a tool for local planning to make feasible plans, in an interactive way and pro-active towards realization. It is a design and research support system for local planning, based upon the scenario method and Parcel-Based GIS. It consists of analogue and digital methods and techniques. It uses the scenario method in
combination of a multi-actor transformation model.

Marco Wagemakers' Education

Middlesex University/DNV Academy

MSc., IT Architecture

2008 – 2011

In 2008 beside my work I started the MSc. IT Architecture. I saw that the use of GIS within organizations was widening. This means that GIS has to fit in the organizational IT architecture. The study helped me to understand and gain experience for the knowledge and skills to create the processes and products that accompany IT Architecture. Especially in the context of the three dimension of Scope: Enterprise/Project, Control: Product/Process, Alignment: Business/Technology. My final assignment was about rapidly evolving technology, and focused on mobile technology. I improved the method of Technology Road mapping for this rapidly evolving technology and the relation that it had on the organizations.

Wageningen Universiteit en Researchcentrum

MSc., Geo-Information and Remote Sensing

2004 – 2006

In 2004 I started a master degree study in Geo-information and Remote Sensing at Wageningen University. During one year of lectures I gained knowledge on Geo-information in general and especially in the subject of Spatial Data Infrastructures, Geo-information Tools, Data Management and Spatial Modeling. Further I followed lectures in scientific skills e.g. research methods. I focused in a final assignment on a Water Improvement Measure tool based on ArcGIS for Desktop and the European policy plan of the Water Frame Work Directive.

Hogeschool van Hall Larenstein Velp

BSc., Land en Watermanagement

2000 – 2004

In 2000 I started a bachelor's degree study in Land and Water Management. Water is at the heart of many problems worldwide - and climate change means the list is growing. The search for solutions is becoming more challenging, and more greater creativity is required. In temperate lowlands, water needs room to move, so rising sea-levels can not always be counteracted by building higher embankments. The Bsc. Land and Water Management discussed how we can balance these problems with the nature and human activities. I focused on a final assignment of supporting the planning method Simlandscape with GIS tools.
