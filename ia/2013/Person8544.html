Ann Michael Henry's Summary

As founder of Mise En Place, effectiveness and productivity improvement consulting firm, Ann Michael Henry is a master at helping business professionals focus on their business, not just in it.

Busy professionals have constant demands put on them each day making for a potentially stressful work environment. By taking control of how information is managed and delivered via e-mail, paper, phone messages, and even drop-in visits, professionals can breathe easier and prioritize better. Experts at Mise En Place train, coach, and mentor on the four cornerstones of an effective work environment—workload management, paper management, e-mail management, and electronic information management.

Some of the most common benefits clients have reaped include (but are not limited to):
* Identify, clarify, communicate, and effectively work priorities
* Manage information better to support priorities
* Retrieve information on-demand
* Reduce duplication of efforts
* Improve decision-making speed, processes, and skills
* Increased productivity
* Reduced stress

Mise En Place provides professionals and their teams—anywhere within a business—the ability to utilize and master their time, energy, resources, and creativity more effectively. This, path to productivity ensures that time spent at work is quality, productive time; decreases stress and anxiety; improves ability to follow up and follow through on assignments and increased employee contribution to the bottom-line results of your organization.

Specialties: Co-author of three books on Workplace Productivity:
* Focus. Organization. Productivity.
* Exploring Productivity
* Insights on Productivity

Ann Michael Henry's Publications

  • Insights on Productivity: Indeas from Industry Professionals on Getting More Done in the Workplace

    • Network for Productivity Excellence
    • 2007
    Authors: Ann Michael Henry

    Highly focused, organized and productive people have 86,000 seconds to work with each day. Unfocused, disorganized and unproductive people have the exact same amount of time. How strange? Something other than time must be making a difference i the lives of these very different groups of people.

    If you are interested in what is making a difference…allocate some of your 86,400 seconds to reading this book! Stop trying to get the most out of life and learn to get the best out of life!

  • Exploring Productivity: Ideas from Industry Professionals on Getting More Done in the Workplace

    • Network for Productivity Excellence
    • 2007
    Authors: Ann Michael Henry

    The 24 authors of this book have one thing in common…they all teach others how to become more focused, organized and productive. After teaching a topic for several years, you typically find certain ideas qualify as just plain common sense. Common sense ideas, in turn, also have something in common. They are simple to understand, practical, easy to implement and they work!

    Exploring Productivity is a collection of ideas written by members of the Network for Productivity Excellence. Enjoy your journey through this collection of common sense ideas shared by seasoned experts.

  • Focus Organization Productivity: Ideas for Improving Success in the Workplace

    • Network for Productivity Excellence
    • 2006
    Authors: Ann Michael Henry

    The F Scale is used by meteorologists to rate tornado intensity. An F5 tornado is as bad as it gets… very devastating! There is no similar rating system for workplace intensity. However, many people are sucked into a vortex of chaos the moment they show up for work every day. They are, in effect, experiencing F5 workdays. Interestingly, there are still people among us who are not blown around by the winds of chaos.

    Regardless of the external workplace conditions, these people remain remarkable focused, organized and highly productive. Good News; the ideas in this book will help you learn how they do it… just in case you want to do it too.
