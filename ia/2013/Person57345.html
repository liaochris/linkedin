James Crotty's Summary

James Marshall Crotty (a.k.a. “Crotty” or “Jim Monk”) is best known as the cofounder, editor, and peripatetic publisher of Monk: the Mobile Magazine, which from 1986-1998 he ran from the dashboard of his 26-foot Fleetwood Bounder “Monkmobile.” He is the author of How To Talk American (Houghton Mifflin), The USA Phrasebook (Lonely Planet), and, with fellow “Monk” Michael Lane, of Mad Monks On the Road (Simon and Schuster), The Mad Monks’ Guide to New York City (Macmillan), The Mad Monks’ Guide to California (Macmillan), and works in TV, multimedia, and film, including the forthcoming urban debate documentary, Crotty’s Kids, which Crotty directed, produced, and in which he stars. As a media auteur, Crotty has been profiled in major media, including the BBC, Newsweek, the New York Times, ABC’S “Good Morning America, NPR’s “Morning Edition,” MTV, Fox News, Rolling Stone, CNN, USA Today, Wired, NHK, Der Stern, and hundreds more. Crotty is widely regarded as a pioneer of “the mobile office” (Portable Computing Magazine) and the inventor of “dashboard publishing” (Fact Sheet Five). Starting in 1994 with the Transom, continuing in 1997 with Hollywood Broadcasting and concluding in 2000 with Playboy, Crotty brought high-level multimedia content to the web long before the user-generated era took hold. In 2004, he helped launch the blog/video blog revolution with the independent politics and culture site, The Crotty Farm Report. He is currently the Business of Education columnist for Forbes and a Political Columnist for the Huffington Post. In addition to his writing and film work, Crotty is a founding Principal in L.A.-based Monk, which includes the media production firm Monk Media, Monk Space production rental, Monk Host, and Monk Post. In his spare time, Crotty volunteers as a debate coach and mentor, and leads Great Books discussion groups in cities around the world. For a portfolio of his work, please visit http://www.jamescrotty.com.

Cofounder/Director of Marketing

Monk Space

January 2007 – Present (6 years 10 months) 4414 West 2nd Street, Los Angeles, CA 90004

The latest creation of Michael Lane & James Crotty ("the Monks" of Monk Magazine fame), Monk Space is a raw production warehouse/studio for shooting independent and student films, music videos, webisodes, and photography. It is also available for private events and art shows.

The complex includes a 1000-square-foot raw production warehouse, adjoining 532-square-foot white studio, 654 square feet of outdoor space, and a 600-square-foot upstairs makeup/green room known as the Monk Annex. Located in production-friendly Koreatown, this 80-year-old brick building includes electric, Wi-Fi, track lighting, and two bathrooms. Warehouse and studio have tall ceilings and concrete floors. The warehouse has distressed brick walls, a skylight, and two sliding wooden door entrances that adjoin an outdoor patio and evocative brick alley.

There are two entrances to the street, plus parking for production trucks, vans, and cars. The combined spaces are perfect for shoots that need room for production, craft services, and holding.

Monk Space is centrally located near the 101 and I-10 freeways, a ten-minute drive from downtown L.A. and Hollywood respectively, and a short ten-minute walk from the Wilshire and Western subway stop. To learn more and to book a reservation at Monk Space, please go to: www.monkspace.com.
