John-Manuel Andriote's Summary

You might say I began my career in eighth grade, long before I headed west to earn a master's degree in journalism at Northwestern University. For a school history project, I photographed, researched and wrote a report about the history of the colonial homes in my 354-year-old hometown in Connecticut.

While I was at Northwestern, in the mid-eighties, I began to apply my developing skills as a journalist to the growing HIV/AIDS epidemic. Nearly three decades later, I still write about it and other health/medical subjects for The Atlantic, HuffPost, and others. My proudest achievement was authoring VICTORY DEFERRED, an award-winning history of AIDS in America. The interviews and other materials I used to develop the book are part of a collection curated by the Smithsonian.

I've also authored books about disco/dance music, fine cigars, even a history of Washington, D.C.'s oldest private club. In 2013, I wrote a book proposal for a renowned scientist and a top federal official. I will soon publish my first children's book. And I am working with my literary agent to final-polish a proposal for another nonfiction book.

I enjoy speaking with college students, and believe that talking about something "light" like dance music (subject of my book HOT STUFF) is the perfect "sugar that helps the medicine go down", making serious conversations possible.

I've developed annual reports, brochures, websites and winning grant proposals (largest was a $35 million USAID grant). And I've trained field staff in Africa and the Caribbean on documenting their work.

I've gone a bit "back to the future" using my multimedia skills to develop podcast walking tours and other digital historic tourism products--beginning with the same colonial homes and sites I first documented all those years ago.

Whether writing or speaking, or assisting clients with their communication needs, I enjoy using my skills to tell interesting stories about people who are helping to change the world.

John-Manuel Andriote's Publications

  • VICTORY DEFERRED: How AIDS Changed Gay Life in America

    • University of Chicago Press
    • October 1, 2011
    Authors: John-Manuel Andriote

    Victory Deferred tells the story of how AIDS transformed the lives of millions of people, changed the way medical research is conducted, and built the national LGBT civil rights movement in America.

  • HOT STUFF: A Brief History of Disco/Dance Music

    • December 1, 2012
    Authors: John-Manuel Andriote

    In this updated e-book digital-only edition of my 2001 HarperCollins trade paperback original, I look at the history of dance music--from the dance halls of the 1920s to the discos of the 1970s and to the mega-clubs of today where electronic dance music (EDM) DJs draw thousands to their live-mix performances. I explore the role of dance music in popular culture, how the rhythm of dance music and dancing get faster when times get tough as people seek a physical escape from their stressful lives. I explore the music, the clubs, the tastemakers and celebrators, the divas and the dancers who have defined entire eras.
