Performance Evangelist

Performance Management

July 2004 – Present (9 years 3 months) Denmark

Much has been made about the ROI of business coaching with most studies returning a 5-to-8 times return on investment, as well as numerous intangible benefits. Additionally, the stocks of companies that spend aggressively on employee development and training outperform those that do not.

Here are some tips for selecting your performance coach that can bring about some of the positive outcomes we discussed at the outset:

What are their credentials? – If your would-be performance coach does not have advanced training, education or experience, keep looking.

Do you click? – A good relationship is a better predictor of coaching outcomes than education or coaching philosophy. Nothing is more important than hitting it off when you meet a potential coach. If you can’t imagine spending time with them socially, look elsewhere.

Is there a process? – Skilled performance coaches follow a process for assessing and developing talent.

How will they exit? - Unethical coaches make a habit out of fostering dependency, ensuring that their services are needed indefinitely. Good coaches work with you to meet specific needs and then pack their bags.

What are the results? Look at their credentials!
