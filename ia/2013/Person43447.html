Ken Evans' Projects

  • Web-based Enterprise Managemnet - WBEM protocol

    • January 1996 to August 1996
    Team Members: Ken Evans, Scott Farrand, Michael Emanuel, Donna Ross

    Directed the multivendor team that created and launched this system management standard. Vendors included BMC, Cisco, Compaq, Intel and Microsoft.

    This new standard for monitoring and managing connected devices was meant to pick-up where larger systems standards such as RMON & SNMP left-off. By making almost any device a web-addressable entity, WBEM would allow new more flexible management of devices and applications services.

  • Microsoft Tiger - Interactive Video Fle Server

    • July 1994 to September 1995
    Team Members: Ken Evans, Mike Travis, Dave Mauldin

    Tiger was a distributed, fault-tolerant real-time fileserver. It provided data streams at a constant, guaranteed rate to a large number of clients, in addition to supporting more traditional filesystem operations. It was intended to be the basis for multimedia (video on demand) fileservers for Telco, cable and other VOD system providers prior to Microsoft's creation of hire Internet initiatives (IIS & IE).

  • Secure Digital Music Initiative - SDMI

    • October 1998 to May 2001
    Team Members: Ken Evans, Stuart Rosove

    Secure Digital Music Initiative (SDMI) was a forum formed in late 1998, composed of more than 200 IT, consumer electronics, security technology, ISP and recording industry companies, ostensibly with the purpose of developing technology specifications that protected the playing, storing and distributing of digital music.

    Specifically, the goals of the SDMI were to provide consumers with convenient access to music online and in new digital distribution systems, to apply digital rights management restrictions to the work of artists, and to promote the development of new music-related business and technologies. SDMI was a direct response to the widespread success of the MP3 file format.

    I was an industry representative to the SDMI during the time I worked at AudioTrak. I also was the Seattle organizer of the Pho digital entertainment dinner discussions. (http://www.onehouse.com/pho.htm)

  • Global Service Provider Network

    • April 1995 to September 1995
    Team Members: Ken Evans

    Develop a strategy for delivering professional services and maintenance contracts to Compaq's global accounts. Project resulted in a network of 3rd party service providers to provide break -fix and spare parts depot services around the world. Partner contracts included relationships with DEC, Xerox and other global service providers.
