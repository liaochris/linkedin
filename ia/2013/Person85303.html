Marc Philippon's Summary

World-renowned arthroscopic hip surgeon and orthopedic sports medicine specialist, Dr. Marc Philippon, is a hip expert and partner at The Steadman Clinic in Vail, CO — a premiere orthopedic clinic for sports-related and traumatic injuries.

Dr. Philippon is consistently recognized as one of the best orthopedic hip surgeons in America and is a leading consultant to the NHL, NFL, NBA and MLB. Treating high profile athletes from around the globe, he specializes in hip injuries in athletes who consistently use powerful hip rotations; pro-golfers, skiers, baseball and hockey players are examples of such athletes.

He is internationally known for performing joint preservation procedures utilizing arthroscopic techniques and for specializing in procedures to repair labral tears, cartilage damage, bone spurs and impingement. He is a leading expert in degenerative diseases of the hip such as arthritis and osteoarthritis.

Dr. Philippon earned his medical degree from McMaster University Medical School in Hamilton, Ontario, Canada in 1990 and completed his orthopedic surgery residency and sports medicine training at the University of Miami, Jackson Memorial Hospital in 1995. Dr. Philippon joined the Steadman Clinic in 2005 after a successful period at the University of Pittsburgh Medical Center where he served as Director of sports related hip disorders and was also Director of the UPMC Golf Sports Medicine Program. Previously, he was Chief of Orthopedic Surgery at Holy Cross Hospital in Fort Lauderdale.

Dr. Philippon publishes hip-related articles for scientific journals and medical publications on a regular basis and is a frequent lecturer and educator on surgeries and rehabilitation techniques.

Dr. Philippon is a Fellow with the American Academy of Orthopedic Surgeons and a Master Instructor with the Arthroscopy Association of North America. He is a Member of the American Orthopaedic Society for Sports Medicine and the Arthroscopy Association of North America.
