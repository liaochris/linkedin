Dave Stroup's Summary

I'm a digital strategist and political professional living in Washington, D.C.

Presently, I work in digital communications at the Democratic Senatorial Campaign Committee. Prior to that, I served as Digital Director for Aneesh Chopra's campaign for Virginia Lieutenant Governor.

From 2008 to early 2013, I worked in digital strategies at the Sierra Club's National Legislative Office. In that role, I oversaw the organization's 2012 election effort, and worked on the ground in both New Mexico and Virginia. In addition to election work at the Sierra Club, I provided online support for advocacy campaigns including protecting the Everglades, stopping the Keystone XL pipeline, and securing stronger pollution standards for coal plants. Originally from the Chicago area, I moved to D.C. in 2002 to attend Georgetown University. I'm also deeply involved in local politics, and have consulted for several progressive candidates for D.C. Council.

The Washington City Paper once described me as "relentlessly idealistic." I firmly believe we can improve our communities and our world through conversations and action. I've worked as both a writer and editor for various D.C.-based news sites and am skilled at design and photography. I also have several years of field operations experience, as well as a variety of skills honed from days working at a rock club and at a hardware store.
