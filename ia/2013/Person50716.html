George. Spafford's Summary

George is a Research Director at Gartner and an experienced practitioner in business and IT operations. He is a prolific author and speaker, and has consulted and conducted training on regulatory compliance, IT Governance, and process improvement in the U.S., Australia, New Zealand and China. Publications include co-authorship of "The Phoenix Project", “The Visible Ops Handbook", “Visible Ops Security” and "Information Security Governance".

George holds an MBA from Notre Dame, a BA in Materials and Logistics Management from Michigan State University and an honorary degree from Konan Daigaku in Japan. He is a ITIL Service Manager, TOCICO Jonah and a Certified Information Systems Auditor (CISA). George is a current member of the IIA, ISACA, ITPI, ITSMF, and the TOCICO.

Specialties: Consulting on the strategic use of IT, professional author, strategy, IT governance, IT process improvement, ITIL v2 and v3, CISA, Theory of Constraints (TOC) Jonah, ISO 27001:2005, ISO 27002:2005, COBIT, regulatory compliance, compliance frameworks, and training.

George. Spafford's Publications

  • The Phoenix Project: A Novel About IT, DevOps, and Helping Your Business Win

    • IT Revolution Press
    • January 10, 2013
    Authors: George. Spafford, Gene Kim, Kevin Behr

    Bill is an IT manager at Parts Unlimited. It's Tuesday morning and on his drive into the office, Bill gets a call from the CEO. The company's new IT initiative, code named Phoenix Project, is critical to the future of Parts Unlimited, but the project is massively over budget and very late. The CEO wants Bill to report directly to him and fix the mess in ninety days or else Bill's entire department will be outsourced. With the help of a prospective board member and his mysterious philosophy of The Three Ways, Bill starts to see that IT work has more in common with manufac- turing plant work than he ever imagined. With the clock ticking, Bill must organize work flow streamline interdepartmental communications, and effectively serve the other business functions at Parts Unlimited. In a fast-paced and entertaining style, three luminaries of the DevOps movement deliver a story that anyone who works in IT will recognize. Readers will not only learn how to improve their own IT organizations, they'll never view IT the same way again.
