Alana Joy's Summary

Alana Joy is a digital strategist with a passion for bold brands and innovative startups. She focuses on social media, community building, creative campaign creation, content strategy, blogger outreach initiatives, copywriting, brand positioning, app strategy... whatever it takes to help her clients exceed their objectives. She provides web/graphic design and creative branding services with a flair for WordPress customization and optimization.

Alana is currently on the advisory board for the Oregon State University New Media Certification program, helping to create relevant and valuable curriculum and will be teaching the course "Writing for the Online Environment" this coming Winter.

She was the mastermind and founder behind the Facebook Protest, which gained national media press attention raising awareness about the importance of privacy and transparency online. She was featured in the CNBC documentary "The Facebook Obsession". [clip from segment: http://bit.ly/FBcnbc] She was also interviewed by ABC news, which syndicated the segment nationally.

Alana was a video correspondent for BlackBerry PlayBook's The Hot Spot, and was co-Host of the weekly webisode This Week In Twitter.

She is a former co-Managing Director of Girls In Tech‘s Los Angeles chapter and was on the advisory board for Social Media Week LA.

She's done the occasional tech post for LA Weekly, and won an LA Web Award for being “LA’s Best Most Shameless Self-Promoter”: and she finds that just as amusing as you do.
