Joe Larson's Summary

My experience began with wildland firefighting, which led me to Forestry and Natural Resources (B.S. Cal Poly SLO, 2006). I discovered Geographic Information Systems (GIS, official minor) during my undergraduate and decided to make it my specialization. Also being a student of fire, I enjoy wildland fire behavior modeling.

With an appreciation for all types of applications of GIS, I have been exposed to various offerings from Free and Open Source Software for Geospatial (FOSS4G), and currently enjoy learning these toolkits - as they apply to mapping, disaster/emergency response and analysis.

Recent highlights of my professional experience include my assignment in Florida to assist with the Deepwater Horizon oilspill and my successful completion of S-495 Geospatial Fire Analysis, Interpretation and Application at the National Advanced Fire and Resource Institute.

My goals are the become more proficient with web, databases, programming - related to natural resources and/or sustainability.

Specialties

Free and Open Source Software for Geospatial (FOSS4G), GIS, Infrared Interpretation (Wildfires), Fire Incident Mapping Tools (FIMT - Wildfires), fire modeling, etc.
