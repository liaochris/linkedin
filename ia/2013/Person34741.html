Desiree Holmes Scherini's Summary

Desiree Holmes ScherinI holds a Bachelors Degree in Psychology, from the University of California. She is a practicing Certified Clinical Hypnotherapist as well as a Professional Artist. Following graduation she worked as a group facilitator for the Navy Alcohold and Drug Safety Action Program, administered by the University of Arizona. During her experience there she earned the higest level of Facilitator 4 in the program through University of Arizona Psychology Department training. After making a decision to be home and raise children she moved back into another passion; Artwork, which provided her flexibility to be home while still growing, expressing and sharing. In 2012 she chose to incorporate the helping therapies back into her life and was certified as a clinical hypnotherapist trough the National Association of Transpersonal Hypnotherapists, and has continued her studies in that area with workshops and personal study.
Desiree has studied painting, drawing and sculpture with well known artists she admires. Among them are Cedric and Joanette Egeli, John Ebersberger, Lee Boynton and Bonnie Roth Anderson who all studied under Henry Hensche in the Cape School of Art. They embrace the impressionist use of color. She has studied figure and head sculpture with Rick Casali, who has studied extensively in the area of portrait and figure. Her love for pastel painting and plein air work grew through her studies with Stan Sperlak.
Desiree's artwork is shown in galleries in and around the Mid-Atlantifc region. She also teaches Art through the Anne Arundel Community College and Maryland Hall for the Creative Arts, as well as independently.
