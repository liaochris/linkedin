Matt Wallaert's Summary

I tend to be working at the intersection of behavior change and business. Originally an academic, I started translating what we know about the psychology of human behavior into creating products and programs that change those behaviors to produce long-term satisfaction and solve social problems.

Things I'm proud of:

- I co-founded GetRaised.com, a side project that we continue to run as a free service to help close the gender wage gap. It has helped thousands of women earn millions of dollars over the past two years; 70% of women who submit a raise request get a raise, and the average raise is around $7K.

- I actively advise and invest in a number of startups and hold open office hours wherever I am to encourage entrepreneurship and behavioral design.

- I co-founded a product consulting company and grew it from 2 to 20+ people and revenues in the millions. Mostly wore my "product" hat: finding the right solution, then running the day-to-day of getting it produced by our team. Clients included TED, the AARP, American Express, Playboy, Thrillist, and others.

- I was the Head of Product at Thrive through its acquisition by LendingTree. People who used Thrive built about $6K in assets over a 9 month period ($3K in reduced debt, $2K in a savings account, $1K in retirement account) and raised their credit score about 20 points.

- I am frequently quoted in the press on how to encourage entrepreneurship, consumer psychology, decision making, and a variety of other topics at the intersection of technology, behavior change, and product creation.
