Fred Logue's Patents

  • Laser diode

    • United States Patent 6,940,883
    • Issued August 5, 2005
    Inventors: Fred Logue, James Christopher O'Gorman

    A laser diode for outputting light of a single mode comprises a substrate layer, a cladding layer and a compound light propagating layer comprising first, second, third and fourth layers. A wave guiding region of refractive index lower than its adjacent regions is defined by the third layer by two quantum wells positioned at the anti-node of light of a single mode which is propagating in the third layer for propagating and amplifying the single mode light. A central ridge locates the wave guiding region transversely of the direction of light propagation. The wave guiding region can also be defined by shaping the top cladding layer by forming an elongated central channel through the central ridge.

  • Method of making an inkjet printhead

    • United States Patent 7,497,961
    • Issued March 3, 2009
    Inventors: Fred Logue, Philip Keenan, Maurice Byrne, Odhran Hendley, Michelle Ryan Meleady

    A method of making an inkjet printhead comprises forming at least one ink ejection element on a surface of a substrate and forming a slot in the substrate to provide fluid communication between an ink supply and the ink ejection element. A protective coating is applied to the surface of the substrate prior to forming the slot. The protective coating comprises a non-polymeric material which is capable of forming a covalent bond with the substrate and is removed from the substrate without damaging the ink ejection element following slot formation

  • Optical waveguide and a method for providing an optical waveguide

    • United States Patent 6,978,057
    • Issued December 20, 2005
    Inventors: Fred Logue, James Christopher O'Gorman, Paul Mcevoy, David Mcdonald, Pascal Landais

    A laser diode having an optical path denned in an active layer which is sandwiched between a substrate layer and a top layer and denned by a ridge formed in the top layer outputs laser light of a single predetermined wavelength. Refractive index altering grooves extending transversely in the top layer are provided at spaced apart locations for altering the refractive index of the active layer along the optical path at partial reflecting locations for causing partial longitudinal reflections of the laser light generated in the optical path so that standing waves or harmonics thereof of the single predetermined wavelength are set up between the respective partial reflecting locations and a first mirror facet in the optical path. In order that the standing waves set up between the partial reflecting locations and the first mirror facet are harmonics of the predetermined single wavelength, the refractive index altering grooves are located along the ridge for forming the reflecting locations at distances from the first mirror facet which correspond to the effective length of the optical path resulting from the affect of the inclusion of the reflecting locations rather than at locations corresponding to the actual length of the light path.

Fred Logue's Volunteer Experience & Causes

  • Volunteer Experience

    • Adviser

      TheStory.ie
      • Civil Rights and Social Action
      January 2012 – present (1 year 9 months)

      New Morning IP provides pro bono services in the field of access to information.

      Access to information is an important component of a democratic society since it provides members of the public with the knowledge that is needed to hold various arms of the state accountable. It also enables persons to evaluate whether their rights have been vindicated by the state and accordingly is a key element in providing access to justice.

      Access to information is widely used by journalists reporting on the activities of public bodies. Furthermore access to information rights are an important tool for lawyers since it is often a quick and low cost way of gaining access to documents without resorting to litigation and discovery.

      Most modern democracies have access to information laws.

      We work with journalist Gavin Sheridan, editor of TheStory.ie, to whom we provide legal consultancy helping draft appeals to public bodies and the Information Commissioner.

      Our biggest success to date has been to persuade the Commissioner for Environmental Information that NAMA and Anglo Irish Bank (now IBRC) are public bodies and that the public has a right to access information on the environment that is held by them or on their behalf.
