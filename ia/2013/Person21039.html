Cathy Colmenares Suarez's Summary

Cathy Colmenares Suarez has worked to empower the Hispanic community in various capacities over the past 20 years. In addition to her current work as a dual language teacher, she has worked with with several nonprofit, community development, child advocacy, and volunteer programs. Her non-profit work has been featured in the Miami Herald, Miami Today, on NBC and on Univision. She has also been featured as an outstanding community leader in Miami Monthly Magazine.

Her career began in international marketing and eventually led to Spanish- language advertising/media. In the late 90's Cathy entered the dot.com industry and has been addicted to teachnology ever since. She has held senior positions in client services and marketing with Todobebé, a producer of Spanish-language parenting content; El Diario/La Prensa, New York’s oldest Spanish-language daily newspaper; StarMedia, Latin America’s first Internet portal; and One Economy, an international nonprofit dedicated to empowering low-income families through technology.

In 2009 Cathy relocated to San Antonio. Here she worked in a consulting capacity with various organizations to implement online, social media, marketing, and public relations strategies. In 2010, she led a successful fundraising campaign for the Leukemia & Lymphoma Society's 2010 Man of the Year candidate, Fernando Suarez. Through the use of online marketing, social media, PR and events, the winning campaign exceeded its goal and secured over $81,000 in a 10 week period. The campaign was featured on national and local media outlets including Univision, KSAT, KENS5, USA Today, Fortune.com and CBS.com and several others.

In 2010 Cathy changed careers in order to pursue her passion, to impact the lives of children. As a dual language teacher Cathy works works to eliminate the barriers of language and poverty. Her goal is to develop tech-savvy, culturally sensitive, and globally aware 21st century citizens.

Visit www.cathycsuarez.com for more.
