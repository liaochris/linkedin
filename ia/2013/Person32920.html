Dawn Lanier's Summary

After years of exploring how small and very small businesses are using the Internet to build a following and improve their bottom line, I crafted my own dream job as Entrepreneur in Chief of Yes You Can. My passion is helping women who run a business of their own on their own create a solid path to online success.

As Mark Twain once said, "Life is too short to live small".

I am particularly interested in meeting and talking with:

~ Women entrepreneurs (especially 'Party of One' business owners, consultants & independents)
~ Social media trailblazers
~ People who create/keep track of cool online tools
~ Thought leaders
~ Good people everywhere

Specialties

Listening, visioning, small business coaching, creating success strategies, and getting people to ACT on their dreams.

Dawn Lanier's Experience

Small Business Success Coach for Self Employed Women

Yes You Can

2007 – Present (6 years)

Yes You Can. It's both the name of my company and what I believe about YOU when it comes to effectively using the technology of the Internet to create your own success. That is, if you're ready to arm yourself with strategies that give your business greater exposure, and ACT on them with consistency and purpose.

If you look at the growing number of people whose words are being shared online, you'll see they're taking advantage of every opportunity to connect with their niche audience. They are 'bringing it', regularly, and so should you.

Why? You need that audience to help your business SHINE. To get it to the point that it thrives at the level you had in mind when you first started. To reinforce what you've always known – you can DO this.

It’s a tall order though, especially if you're a solo professional coach, consultant, designer, or any other flavor of the 'Party of One' self-employed business owner. The learning curve is steep, you don't know a soul who can explain how it all works, and finding the time is, well, who has time?

I do. I'm a net savvy small business coach on a mission to help other self employed women build their success by treating the Internet as a value partner, and making it their new BFF.

Have a question? Need a little help getting your online mojo together? Then let's connect!
