Joshua Theissen's Summary

Over a decade of design in a variety of disciplines including; graphic, web, industrial, illustration, and software (both in user interface and actual development). I have an expert level mastery of the Adobe Creative Suite including acting as an instructor in Illustrator, Photoshop, and InDesign. I am also fully capable in video, motion graphics, and 3d modeling/animation (Blender, Maya).

For the last decade(+), I've worked in a self-contained visual information center where I've managed thousands of projects from cradle-to-grave; that is, I've done everything from the initial sketches to the digital design, to delivery as well as maintaining the physical equipment needed along the way (manual graphics equipment, computers and software, printers, presses, bindery equipment, web servers [Apache, primarily], etc.) - which has given me an intimate knowledge of how to design for a platform by emphasizing its strengths and mitigating its weaknesses.

I also have some experience in software design and development, especially for the web. Programming languages I am moderately competent in include (in order from most to least competent); Javascript, PHP, Python, C++, Java, and Objective-C.

Additionally, I've been on Active Duty with the United States Army since 2001. Outside of graphic design, I've designed everything from functional furniture to electronics. I've worked in the fine arts as well, painting, sculpting, drawing, etc.. I like to think of myself as simply a designer and try to venture into as many different media and disciplines as possible - the real goal is simply to solve problems in creative and, hopefully, aesthetically pleasing ways.

I have lead teams varying in size from just myself and one other to an entire communications platoon numbering more than thirty individuals. I incorporate a lot of traditional business project management techniques into my creative team leading that seamlessly translates artistic accomplishment into successful products.

Joshua Theissen's Experience

Digital Illustrator

White House Communications Agency

Government Agency; 501-1000 employees; Military industry

October 2008 – Present (5 years) Washington D.C. Metro Area

As an illustrator in the Digital Multimedia Center for the White House Communications Agency, I was responsible for the overall 'look and feel' of the visual information products that we produced.

My team and I produced/reproduced visual information products at all levels of classification in support of Presidential, Vice Presidential, Higher Headquarters', and Agency missions. My emphasis was on 'special projects' - that is, projects that step outside the scope of the routine for a graphic designer. I utilized equipment and materials on hand to produce unique products to accomplish our mission in innovative and cost effective ways.

Some of my unique projects included; a Presidential Water Table (a piece of furniture that travels with the President to hold a glass of water and a wireless microphone during speeches while also looking pretty), a Presidential Trip Site Sand Table (large 4 ft. x 6 ft. mockup of a generic town highlighting all facets of a Presidential Trip from arrival to departure), Animated three-dimensional video splash used at the beginning of many agency produced videos and commercials, Multiple database systems and user interfaces for different types of record management, dozens of logos, hundreds of poster, and thousands of books, programs, and brochures.

Additional responsibilities included (but were not limited too); Equal Opportunity representative for the command (approx. 100 personnel), Website Administrator (Intranet/SharePoint), and IT Service Desk.

Multimedia Illustrators are primarily responsible for supervising, planning or operating multimedia imaging equipment in order to produce various kinds of visual displays and documents.
