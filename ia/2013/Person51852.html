Harry A. Layman's Summary

A Certified Amazon Web Services Cloud Solution Architect, I am a hands-on technologist, manager and strategic thinker, building teams, creating solutions and solving problems over 30 years. Strong interest and experience in mobile software, enterprise and web-scale solutions leveraging modern building-blocks for rapid solution creation and deployment. Adaptive learning & assessment, machine learning (and other aspects of Artificial Intelligence) and cloud-based architectures and solutions are of particular interest.

I have executive experience as CTO for a NASDAQ company, have served as SVP Engineering and CTO at three venture-funded startups, and was a founder / CEO of a software engineering firm I built for ten years and sold to The College Board in 1996. I hold an MBA and BS/Computer Science from Columbia University; I also earned a Master of Science degree from American University and have studied instructional design, cognition and statistics (for Educational measurement) at Teachers College. Received certifications in information systems auditing (CISA), IT security (CISSP) and project management (PMP), in addition to various vendor certifications.

Deep experience with offshore development and managing a range of service providers, IT divisions and services including app dev, infrastructure and operations. Three years living, working and managing in China including a management position at a Shanghai-listed software company, plus extensive work with global providers in India, China, Mexico and USA.

Experience designing and implementing device-centric apps including assessment systems and learning games for iOS; multiple titles for iPhone and iPad in App Store in 8 languages. I have created Android versions of some of these Apps, in addition to multi-platform 2D graphics learning games. See Hangzhouharry.github.io

Executive Director, Digital Assessments

The College Board

Nonprofit; 1001-5000 employees; Education Management industry

August 2010 – April 2013 (2 years 9 months)

This newly created position was formed in the corporate strategy function, and under the direction of the COO and the VP of Digital Testing, focused on the challenges arising from moving paper and pencil assessment products to digital forms, and of delivering digital assessments at scale. These challenges include issues specific to ways in which digital delivery will differ from analog forms (e.g. timing differences using a pencil to fill in bubbles verses typing a calculated value, scrolling required for long passages, etc.) and approaches to comparability that will support linking the measurement instruments across this divide.

Additional challenges related to large scale item production (which could arise from "test windows" and / or adaptive test designs) using auto-item generation, item templates & families, DOMC and other new item types, well as more authentic forms of assessment (along with automated and human-assistive scoring technologies), have been investigated as well. Hands on experience with open source Machine Learning-based solutions.

During this same three year plus window I have built a prototype assessment creation and delivery system for use with desktop computers and tablets, leveraging a state of the art back-end service provider, cross-platform tools to build a test authoring solution and using standard iOS development tools (and a backend service provider) for iPad test delivery.

Investigating machine learning for text (via the open source LightSIDE product) for fully automated scoring, or for a semi-automated "assistive scoring workbench" are additional areas of inquiry, as are various Security aspects and approaches for digital test delivery -- from keyboard analytics to AI-assisted online proctoring and "roving proctor" solutions.
