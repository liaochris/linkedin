Darrell Berry's Summary

Darrell Berry is a London-based consultant, researcher, writer and photographer.

In Tokyo in 1994, with Dr John Ricketts, he developed the idea of ‘social media’ -- a vision of the internet that contrasted the then new, but disappointingly document-centric WWW, acknowledged by Forbes magazine as possibly the earliest usage of the concept (see http://onforb.es/q4oQfJ)
 
Building on this idea, John and he built Matisse – the first massively multiuser social media system for the Mac -- first publicly demoed in Tokyo in 1995 via an international link with UK electronic act The Black Dog.
 
Moving to London, Darrell was appointed Partner and CIO at HHCL, Campaign’s Agency of the 1990s. HHCL launched the world's first ‘social’ corporate website, howellhenryland, in 1999.

Founded in 2002, his consultancy Ku24 specialised in the design of social network analysis and text analytics-based insight platforms, with end-clients including OpenAmplify, Kantar Video, MediaCom, Millward Brown and Bell-Pottinger Communications.
 
Darrell is co-founder (with Dr John Ricketts) of Significance Systems. SigSys blends social network and qualitative narrative analysis to provide consultancy and tools which deliver actionable, data-led insight to senior decision-makers in the UK, APAC and the US.

As a photographer, Darrell's images of the London fashion, nightlife and performance worlds have been published internationally, including by Vogue Italia and A Magazine.

Darrell is also founder and co-editor of emergent culture webzine BigShinyThing.

Specialties: Media analytics systems architecture, social network analysis, quantitative narrative analysis, rapid prototyping, nightlife/perfomance photography.
