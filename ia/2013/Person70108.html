Justin Fleming's Summary

Professional Experience:

I currently support the academic and research faculty and staff within the Duke Medicine organization. This work includes the configuration and troubleshooting of Windows, OS X, mobile devices, network configuration, printing, and more.

Most recently, I served as the project manager for OS X and Mac software release at MIT, responsible for testing, support, documentation, and deployment. I coordinated support for the Mac between operational and infrastructure requirements and the needs and best interests of Mac users across MIT. I also served as the primary liaison between MIT and Apple.

Working as an IT Consultant at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology over the last five years, I first performed deployment, preventative maintenance, and troubleshooting for information technology at several major departments across MIT. Additionally, I developed and maintained Mac imaging and deployment solutions for the IS&T departmental IT support team (or DITR). I most recently supported mobile devices from across the spectrum of mobile platforms, including iOS, Android, BlackBerry, and Windows Phone. This team’s support spanned email, phone, remote, in-person, and on-site functions, and also entailed device and carrier testing, documentation-writing, and managing the BlackBerry Enterprise Server (BES). I have also served on software release teams for new releases of Mac OS X to the MIT community, including 10.6 and 10.7.

In my three years working as a Campus Representative for Apple, Inc., my professional work included networking with customers, developing and executing marketing and sales strategies, and working on a one-on-one basis with clients to answer questions and solve technical problems.
