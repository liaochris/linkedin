AR Fiano's Summary

Author of Gabriel's World, Crime Fiction/LGBT Heroes.

Gabriel's World is a Mystery/Thriller series featuring NYC gay private investigator Gabriel Ross and bisexual artist Joel McFadden. The first book The Hanged Man and second book Two-Faced Woman are available on Amazon. I'm interested in meeting friends and colleagues around the world, so if you like the topics or the stories, please feel free to say hi.

You can read more about the books and characters at GabrielsWorld.com, which also features short stories and extras for readers. The series has gay, lesbian, bisexual, transgender, and straight cisgender characters. The stories are not about the characters being LGBT, but about having strong characters who happen to be LGBT.

Gabriel's World is also on Facebook, YouTube, Twitter, Pinterest, Goodreads. Live readings of stories are available in person or Skype, including in the "voices" of the characters (using voice-modification software). You can subscribe to the blog or newsletter on the website.

I am a writer and artist (bisexual and genderqueer) with a background in college education, communications, social justice, philosophy and law. My LinkedIn profile is strictly for myself as an author and not related to any other job I may have. I do not list here where else I work.

I am also strong LGBT advocate, particularly for youth--all youth, and with added focus on transgender youth. I welcome contacts in this area, and other friendly faces.

AR Fiano's Experience

Author

Gabriel's World

May 2012 – Present (1 year 6 months)

Gabriel is a brilliant and intuitive NYC private investigator who often becomes too close to his cases. Joel McFadden, Gabriel's former boyfriend, is an extraordinary artist with a difficult past. The two of them work on Gabriel's cases, finding themselves in extreme danger, and trying to deal with their feelings for each other.

In the first book The Hanged Man attorney Raymond Booth wants to hire Gabriel to probe a disturbing incident at Raymond’s charitable foundation. But then Raymond disappears. Raymond’s sister Toni hires Gabriel to find the missing man. Gabriel turns up evidence of abduction—and then Raymond turns up dead. Gabriel’s obsession with Raymond's death pulls him into the mystery Raymond wanted him to solve. Gabriel has help from journalist Alex Barclay, sparking a romance, and then Joel returns to NYC, leading to complications in Gabriel's personal life. Gabriel begins unraveling a sinister secret connected to the foundation–a cabal tracing back to the origins of Nazism. Gabriel endeavors to uncover the conspiracy of Raymond’s murder without losing his license, freedom, or life.

In the second book Two-Faced Woman, the story is set in duality: Gabriel has two female clients, and is up against two evil men. One client is Sophie Faulkner, a woman with a second personality who’s been falsely accused of murder. Gabriel must find out who the real killer is with the help of Sophie’s other self, Edward. Gabriel is also handling a difficult situation for Geneva Lennon, a transgender woman who’s discovered that her birth certificate isn’t actually hers. But while he’s putting his skills to good use, Gabriel is falling apart inside. His relationships with his boyfriend Alex and former boyfriend Joel are suddenly exploding. While still suffering from the trauma of previous events, Gabriel is desperate to reconnect with his spirituality. And now Gabriel must face human monsters who strike with a level of brutality that threatens to destroy him.
