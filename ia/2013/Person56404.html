Jacen Greene's Summary

Jacen Greene provides strategy, finance, and impact analysis consulting to both for-profit and nonprofit social enterprises. He has worked with social entrepreneurs in the U.S., India, Cambodia, Honduras and Nicaragua conducting training sessions, delivering technical assistance and coordinating capacity building programs.

Jacen's papers have been published in Case Studies in Social Entrepreneurship and Sustainability and the Oikos Case Quarterly, and he has written guest posts for Sustainable Business Oregon, Triple Pundit, and Real Leaders. In addition to helping plan the Elevating Impact Summit, he has presented or led workshops for the Fulbright Program, AmeriCorps VISTA, and at the Net Impact Conference (2011), International Conference on Business & Sustainability (2011), GoGreen PDX (2012), and Hacking Social Impact (2012).

Jacen graduated Beta Gamma Sigma with an MBA in sustainability from Portland State University and magna cum laude with a B.A. in China Studies from Willamette University.

Specialties: Social enterprise business model design, financial analysis, and impact analysis using SROI / IRIS / GRI / GHG Protocol. Social entrepreneurship education including curriculum and workshop design, instruction, and program management.
