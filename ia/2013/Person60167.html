James Budrow's Summary

DIRECTOR OF NORTHERN CALIFORNIA - James Budrow

James Budrow has been nominated and graciously accepted the position of CalNACHI's Director of Northern California in recognition of his efforts to promote the organization.

James attributes his personal success as a property inspector to the 20+ years hands-on experience in the construction industry, including many years as a general contractor specializing in residential remodeling. As an inspector, James spent four years training with a large franchise in the Sacramento Valley before starting the American Pride Home Inspection Service in 1999. James is a Member in good standing with the InterNational Association of Certified Home Inspectors and has performed more than 5000 home inspections in the Sacramento Valley. In addition, James is the President for the Sacramento Valley Chapter of the InterNational Association of Certified Home Inspectors (InterNACHI). He is dedicated to helping others in the home inspection industry, and believes that inspection excellence can best be achieved through continuing education and compliance with the standards of practice of the California InterNational Association of Certified Home Inspectors http://www.CalNACHI.org

Marketing Of Home Inspection Services, Residential Exterior Remodeling, Residential Defect Recognition, Home Maintenance Consultant, Relationship Marketing Strategies

At HomeNetDirect we believe that when a home is put on the market it should be certified and in “move-in-ready” condition. What is “move-in-ready” you ask? This means that the home has been inspected prior to being placed on the market. The homeowner has repaired any major health and safety issues and it is “move-in-ready”.

There are a few things that are inevitable during the process of selling your home.

•You're eventually going to get one or two perfect buyers for your home.
•Your home eventually will be inspected.
•There eventually will be a negotiation point when one or both of those buyers are going to want to negotiate the price of your home.

Will you be prepared? Will you have your home pre-inspected? Having your home pre-inspected allows you, the seller, to be prepared for negotiations.

Eventually there will be a sign placed in your front yard right above the real estate sign. The sign you want to see placed above that real estate sign is “Sold”. The sign you won’t want to see above that real estate sign is “Reduced Price”.

Get your home pre-inspected by a quality HomeNetDirect inspector before your home is placed on the market.
