Martin Musgrove's Experience

Managing Director

Southwest Yachting

January 1990 – Present (24 years) Solent

Responsible for the concept and set up of Southwest Yachting in 1990. I initially managed the company in my spare time, and recruited a team to run the business on a day to day basis allowing me to pursue my publishing career in London. The business grew to a level in 1995 which allowed me to take control full time. Based in the Solent, with yachts in Hamble, Beaulieu and Lymington, I developed a successful business offering RYA training, corporate hospitality, offshore racing and team building events with major blue chip companies. I was solely responsible for the sales, marketing, training and strategic planning of the business. I expanded Southwest Yachting into Yacht Brokerage in 2004, launching and establishing a new company which was successfully sold on to one of the UK’s largest groups of International Yacht brokers in 2007. Over the past 23 years I have created one of the most established Yacht Charter Companies and RYA training centres operating in the Solent today.

Publishing Director

Headway Home and Law

November 1992 – January 1995 (2 years 3 months)

Financially accountable for a turnover in excess of £6 million and a staff of 72. Reporting directly to the main board of directors, my responsibilities were for the publishing development/advertising/editorial/marketing/circulation of the consumer titles Health and Fitness, Taste, Vegetarian Living, Practical Householder, Poster Power, Head Bangers, Panstadia International and the Gardener. I was successful in introducing new business on controlled circulation titles, launching Telecommunications Development and International Trade Development.

Publisher, Group Advertisement Manager

Argus Media

Privately Held; 501-1000 employees; Publishing industry

December 1986 – November 1992 (6 years)

Commenced in the company as an Advertisement Manager where I was highly successful working across eleven titles within the consumer and business magazine division. Responsible for training, recruitment and budgeting whilst also still remaining in active sales with key clients. In my time I launched two very successful trade exhibitions in Harrogate and London off the back of two existing trade publications.

In 1989 I was promoted to Publisher and then became accountable to the Board for profit and loss, controlling a turnover of £2 million and a department of 32.

Advertisement Manager

Shay Publications
