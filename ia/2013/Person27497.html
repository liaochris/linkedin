Craig Faubel, PCC, MA's Summary

I am an executive coach assisting senior pastors and business leaders in maximizing their God-given gifts. My client's focus centers on personal leadership development, improving time and energy management and enhancing leadership development of the team. My business experience in leadership development and time management and my ICF coach training have differentiated me from Christian missional coaches. Presently, I am working with 23 pastors from the Lutheran Church Missouri Synod and other Christian denominations and with numerous business leaders. My specialty is leadership development.

I have been a successful business entrepreneur for the last 25 years selling to medium to large sized corporate companies in the print solutions industry. I handed my business over to my three assistants in order to pursue my dream of coaching and developing leaders.

My coach training consisted of completing the Executive and Professional Coaching Graduate Program at the University of Texas at Dallas and earning the designation as an Professional Certified Coach from the International Coach Federation.The Blackaby Ministries International has certified me as a Spiritual Leadership Coach. The Winslow Assessment Program has authorized me to be a Winslow Business Consultant. I have authored my own book titled: " Wisdom from Fred the LawnMower Salesman." My other writings consist of a monthly Motivational Newsletter designed to help leaders improve in all areas of their lives, personally and professionally which has been in print since June 2007.

Specialties: Executive Coaching for Senior and Associate Pastors;
Executive Coaching for CEO's and Presidents;
Administer and analyze personality assessments- the Winslow Dynamics System and the DISC Profile.

The purpose of an executive coach is to provide a trusting and collaborative relationship which focuses, clarifies and assists to produce client-desired results on specific business and personal goals. Concentration is focused on where the client is now and what he is willing to do to get where he wants to be in the future.
An executive coach is a confidential and intimate support partner who asks powerful questions, listens attentively, encourages, brainstorms, and challenges; holds the client accountable for the client's goals and action plans and reviews progress.
There is not a hierarchy and the agenda is the client's .
