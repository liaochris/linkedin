Kaleigh Somers' Summary

Good things? They start small. That's the theory I'm working with.

People want to focus on what they do well — not marketing their products and services. My passion is helping those people, organizations and companies tell their story so they can keep doing what they're doing without sacrificing client care and excellent service.

I believe in honest, accurate communication and have used my writing skills to craft email newsletters, webpage copy, social media messages, direct mail and advertising copy, white papers, brochures, invitations and protocols.

My biggest goals, in terms of comprehensive marketing, are educating and interacting with the client base. I've managed Facebook, Twitter, Pinterest, YouTube and LinkedIn accounts for professional organizations and have marketed via various email platforms.

At the end of the day, though, you've got to find the media. My media relations skills have been tested with non-profits, startups, service-based businesses and freelance opportunities.

Founder & Editor

Rewriting Life

June 2010 – Present (3 years 4 months)

Rewriting Life began, in 2010, as a means to put my name online. I wanted to be a published author and had a book fresh out of querying. After switching to Wordpress and ditching the petty posts, I started delving into topics people didn't take kindly to, like long distance relationships in college and the art of friendship as it grows and strengthens or weakens. I learned to have the voice I so often kept tucked under my bed for fear of upsetting other people. And then, quite crazily, the emails started: a girl from Australia who was fifteen and not eating, another from my hometown who had trampled those same paths years before.

Some of those emails came to me, and most didn't. Most came to my mother, who passed the sentiments along and made me think maybe blogging was more than blogging.

Now, I'm dedicated to empowering individuals through my blog and the art of personal storytelling. I've been featured on Freshly Pressed, where more than 5,000 people, in two days, read a single thank-you letter to my parents. I've used social media to nurture my readership connections and grown to a steady following of people from around the country and overseas.
