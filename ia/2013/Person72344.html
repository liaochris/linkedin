Karissa Morton's Summary

I earned a BA in English from Drake University, where I did significant coursework in literature and literary theory, culminating in a thesis on depictions of work & labor in utopian fiction with a special emphasis on Early Modern texts.

I am currently working under Larissa Szporluk as an MFA candidate in poetry at Bowling Green State University. My thesis, The Threshing Place, is a collection that revolves around the (dis)connection between the self and the body, particularly in terms of sexual identity, experience, & trauma.

My additional academic interests include utopian studies, Early Modern travel narratives, (post)-Marxism, cultural studies, and composition pedagogy.

Besides my writing and research, I currently teach courses in the craft of poetry in the Creative Writing department, as well as academic & argumentative writing in the General Studies Writing (GSW) department.
