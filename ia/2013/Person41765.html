Ephraim Julius Freed's Summary

I am an Intranet Nerd.

There is no better way to describe the passion I bring to my work with intranets, especially intranets that incorporate social software. Inspiring this passion is the potential of intranets to engage and empower employees, facilitate good collaboration and communication, strengthen positive company culture and improve efficiency.

While I work in the "Enterprise 2.0" and "social business" arena, I see this simply as people-centered business and technology. Intranets on their own do not change companies, but combined with smart organizational development and effective leadership, thoughtful intranets can become pillars of change, culture and growth.

I work for two companies: 1) the Intranet Benchmarking Forum (IBF) and 2) OpenRoad Communications, makers of ThoughtFarmer - social intranet software.

As a Consultant for OpenRoad I write, engage in discussions, and tweet about social intranets (online marketing) and provide instructions and training to new and existing clients.

As a Community Manager for IBF I work to build the community of intranet practitioners engaged with IBF, I manage member communications and I facilitate meetings and online events for intranet professionals.

Contact me about anything related to intranet planning, social intranets, collaboration, internal communications, "social business" or "enterprise 2.0." I have a passion for this field and a joy of discussing its intricacies.
