Greg Wilder's Summary

A broadly skilled musician, scientist and technologist with deep expertise in MIR (Music Information Retrieval) and related music search and discovery technologies. Demonstrated ability to take cutting-edge R&D to market in the fast-paced and highly competitive music industry. Strong background in music theory and cognition and author of multiple music technology patents. 8+ years of strategic negotiation, project and product management, and system engineering strategies for customers at the top-level of the music industry. Internationally credited composer, performer, sound designer and recording engineer.

Specialties:
Music Information Retrieval (MIR)
Computational creativity (Metacreation)
Author of numerous International Patents (several pending)
Pianist/Composer/Sound Designer with international broadcast/commercial/stage credits
Music awards include those from the American Composer's Forum, the William Penn Foundation, the Angel Orensanz Foundation, the Society of Composers International, and ASCAP

Greg Wilder's Experience

Chief Scientist and Artistic Director

Isomer Project

April 2012 – Present (1 year 6 months) Greater Philadelphia Area

The Isomer Project is a suite of intelligent software tools designed to combine musical models of human creativity with modern machine learning techniques to more fully understand and explore computational creativity.

Board Member

Clio Music (formerly Orpheus Media Research, Inc.)

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Music industry

October 2007 – April 2013 (5 years 7 months) San Francisco Bay Area

Founder and Chief Science Officer

Clio Music (formerly Orpheus Media Research, Inc.)

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Music industry

October 2007 – April 2012 (4 years 7 months) Greater New York City Area

Transformed 6+ years of privately funded R&D into successfully marketed line of music analysis software products.

Designed, developed, and implemented "Clio", a proprietary autonomous music analysis and motivic data mining technology capable of generating comprehensive models from music in any style or genre. Lead project management across multiple international technical firms. “Clio” software has analyzed tens of millions of music tracks for top-level music companies.

Authored multiple International and US Patents (e.g. WIPO Pub: WO/2009/085054, U.S. Patent No. 8,084,677).

Presented key technical seminars/lectures at Drexel University (2011), NYU (2009), the 2008 International Computer Music Conference (Queen's University, Belfast), and the Institute for Software Integrated Systems (Vanderbilt University, 2008).

Served as primary technology representative at conferences and client meetings world-wide. Lead white paper author responsible for management of publishing, presentation, and research community development. Conducted regular press interviews across a wide range of media.

Co-Founder

Philadelphia Camerata

Educational Institution; 1-10 employees; Fine Art industry

February 2007 – December 2010 (3 years 11 months)

The Philadelphia Camerata was established in February 2007 by Greg Wilder and Jeremy Gill as a platform to encourage discourse relating to matters surrounding the composition, performance, and promotion of contemporary music.

In December 2008, the Camerata re-launched as a community-driven portal to become the premier resource for area composers, performers, presenters, and advocates of new music.
