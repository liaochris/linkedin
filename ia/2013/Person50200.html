Greg Laugero's Summary

I help companies in the early stages of a digital product or channel gain focus, productivity, and get "unstuck". I can help specifically in the areas of product strategy, market analysis, customer insight, and integration of product management, UX design, and Agile. (See more at www.greglaugero.com)

My background is in market research, persona development, customer insight, product and UX strategy. The breadth and depth of my experience includes – B2B and B2C eCommerce systems, mobile applications for healthcare, retail kiosks, Internet-based interactive TV, and social media and community-based systems.

I publish on the integration of UX, product strategy and application development issues for a variety of publications, including CIO Magazine and CIO.com. My post on “Making Sense of Minimum Viable Products” has been globally distributed and referenced throughout the startup universe.

I have worked with companies of all sizes -- large companies (Microsoft, GlaxoSmithKline, Sterling Commerce, Qwest Communications, J&J, Pfizer, AstraZeneca, Kaiser Permanente), medium-sized companies (United Business Media, Westwood College), and start ups (Intelpeer, myStrength). I am comfortable operating in all of these situations (see my recommendations).

Specialties: market research, customer insight, personas, user experience design, user experience strategy, digital product strategy, product design, use cases, user stories, requirements, product roadmaps, customer research, focus groups, search UX, gamification, minimum viable products, startups, lean ux, social media strategy,

    • Johnny Holland Magazine

    Authors: Greg Laugero

    Minimum viable products are all the rage. The idea is to test the waters and make some sense out of the market before heavily investing in product development. That’s definitely oversimplified, but what I’m going to explore in this post is the “sense making” aspect of MVPs.

    Authors: Greg Laugero

    In this article, I compare user experience design to the qualifications and skills necessary to be a good customer experience officer. UX falls short because customer experience demands a level of business discipline that is usually lacking in UX practitioners.
