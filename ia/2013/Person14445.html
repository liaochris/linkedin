Bill Sempf's Summary

Hi! Thanks for checking out my profile. If you would like to share contact information, please introduce yourself. If you just send me a default invite, I will ignore it.

So on to the fun stuff: I help people build more secure Internet applications.

In doing this for years and years, I have gathered a lot of skills. I started with HTML in 1991, when you needed to compile the web server code from CERN. I started with .NET in 2000, when Microsoft was DHLing builds of the compiler to author's houses. I started with web services in 2002, when SOAP was largely something you washed with.

Eventually, I got into larger and larger connected systems, and now I have twenty years of experience in a variety of widely connected, loosely coupled transactional systems. As such, I have spent an inordinate amount of time in the last several years writing about and working with Web Services in SOAP and REST.

I am an author of Windows 8 Application Development with HTML5 fir Dummies, C# 5 for Dummies, and Visual Basic .NET for Dummies (among others), and a frequent contributor to MSDN, The Intel Developer Forum, and Builder.com.

I have recently been an invited speaker for BlackHat, CodeMash, INETA, the International XML Web Services Expo, DevEssentials, OWASP and the Association of Information Technology Professionals.

A graduate of The Ohio State University with a Bachelor’s of Science in Business Administration, Microsoft Certified Professional, Certified Internet Business Strategist and Certified Internet Webmaster, I have enabled secure implementations of hundreds of web applications for startups and Fortune 50 companies alike, and can do the same for you.

Specialties: Secure internet application design, identity, secure by design Windows software, penetration testing, Internet strategy, technical writing.
