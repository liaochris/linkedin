Jan Schultink's Summary

Jan is an internationally recognized designer of high-stakes presentations (investors, conferences, sales). His background is actually not in graphics design; he worked for almost a decade with McKinsey & Company advising Fortune 500 CEOs about strategy. This makes him one of the few people in the world with the rare combination of skills: visualization talent and business understanding.

Specialties

Presentation design, investor presentations, PowerPoint, public speaking

Idea Transplant designs high-stake presentation and pitches. We start with the content, similar to the first phase of a strategy consulting project, transform that content into a compelling story, and finally translate that story into effective presentation slides.

Associate Principal

McKinsey & Company

Partnership; 10,001+ employees; Management Consulting industry

November 1992 – June 2002 (9 years 8 months)

After joining McKinsey's as a Research Assistant in 1992, I served clients in a number of different skill levels: Fellow (analyst with INSEAD sponsorship), Associate (consultant), Engagement Manager (project manager) and Associate Principal (responsible for multiple projects). I worked mainly in strategy and corporate finance, from McKinsey's Amsterdam and London offices but on projects throughout Europe, in the Technology, Financial Services and Consumer Goods/Retailing sectors.
