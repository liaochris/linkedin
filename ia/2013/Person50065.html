Greg Bybee's Summary

Greg Bybee is an avid disruptive technologist focused on the education and enterprise IT spaces. He recently graduated from two masters programs at Stanford University, where he was a Siebel Scholar and an Arjay Miller Scholar. He currently advises a number of edtech startups and is a Fellow with NewSchools Venture Fund.

In the summer of 2012, he became the first product manager at Coursera and advised the Chinese social network Renren on their online education strategy. In addition to teaching in China and developing an ESL curriculum, Greg has volunteered on a number of education projects, including designing online courses (MOOCs) and building a secondary school in Ethiopia. Previously, Greg led vCloud product marketing at VMware and advised executives with McKinsey & Company.

Greg graduated with honors from Harvard's School of Engineering and Applied Sciences with a degree in Applied Mathematics. More recently, Greg received an MA in Education from Stanford's Graduate School of Education (GSE) and an MBA from Stanford's Graduate School of Business (GSB). There, he served on the advisory board for the Center for Global Business and the Economy, was the co-Chair of the Student Association Technology & Communications Committee, and was an elected member of the Academic Committee.

Living in San Francisco, Greg enjoys travel, running, photography, dancing (kinda goofily), learning to DJ, geeky gadgets, and (of course) edtech and social innovation.
