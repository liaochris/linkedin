David Tintner's Publications

  • How small news organizations can produce more data visualizations

    • University of Florida Journal of Undergraduate Research
    • September 2012
    Authors: David Tintner

    The purpose of this study is to examine how small news organizations use data visualizations on their news sites and understand their workflow process and challenges. The ultimate goal is to find ways for small news organizations to produce more data visualizations. Eight in-depth interviews were conducted with journalists from U.S. news organizations with an average daily circulation between 50,000 and 75,000. The findings showed that lack of time, staff reductions and lack of technical knowledge have forced many of these small news organizations to forgo data visualization projects. The results also showed an overwhelming dissatisfaction with the tools available for creating data visualizations. The findings lay the groundwork for developing an open-source platform for data visualization projects that small news organizations can use to meet their needs and combat their reported challenges.
