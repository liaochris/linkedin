Matt West's Summary

I have been developing for the web for 10+ years. My focus is the LAMP stock, primarily with CMS and framework design. I build web and mobile products from concept to launch.

In 2012 I accepted a developer position with Socha Dev. We have built apps for the FCC, Discovery Education, US Chamber of Commerce and have several more cool projects in the works.

In 2009 I left the academic world and joined Our Hometown, a small web development company that develops and hosts websites for newspapers from all over the U.S. I developed a set of advertisement and internal production modules for Drupal 6. The modules are currently running on over 150 Drupal sites. I also lead a rebranding initiative for the company, reworking our logo and public site.

While in school I was one of 900 students, and the only student in western NY, to participate in the Google Summer of Code program two years in a row. I developed a installation UI for Geeklog CMS in 2007. In 2008 I extended the project and added support for 3rd party plugin installation and migrating from a backup. I participated in the program again in 2008 and 2009 as a project mentor.

I have a B.S. in IT and a M.S. in International Business with a concentration on information security from D'Youville College. I wrote a project that advised IT managers about the current Botnet problem and included policy suggestions to mitigate Botnet threats.

I am currently not looking for full time employment but am available for interesting freelance web or mobile (iOS) projects.

Specialties: PHP (Drupal, Kohana, Symfony2/Doctrine2, CakePHP) development, jQuery, *nix/LAMP server (PHP, MySQL, Apache, Nginx), iOS, Subversion, Git
