Daniel Leavitt's Projects

  • MTV Fantasy Election 2012

    • April 2012 to November 2012
    Team Members: Daniel Leavitt, Scott Mallone, Celine Willard, , Edric Lapniramai, mike curts

    Large-scale fantasy game that attempted to recapture the youth energy of the '08 election by allowing players to draft and field political candidates, then play these teams of candidates against each other on the basis of good governance, transparency and social engagement.

    My role was as architect and lead developer, which consisted strategy, ideation and UX as well as team management and of course writing a whole ton of Ruby code.

    The app was funded by the Annenberg Foundation and MTV's Public Affairs Divison. .It integrated on the backend with data provided by political watchdog groups such as Politifact, OpenSecrets.org, RealClearPolitics, VoteSmart and Rock the Vote, as well as social media mainstays like Facebook and Twitter.

    Tech included data viz and evented concurrency for realtime on the frontend. Integration and processing of data from dozens of partners on the backend. Lots of lessons learned as well as white papers published and libraries extracted and open-sourced.

  • hyfn.com

    • December 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Daniel Leavitt

    Frontend craft therapy after an intense six months of full-stack dev on Fantasy Election. Fun with PushState and CSS3 using graceful degradation. Powered by Coffeescript and Middleman and uses some neat SASS tricks.

  • My College Dollars

    • November 2011 to January 2012
    Team Members: Daniel Leavitt

    Facebook-integrated app using The College Board's data to provide grant and scholarship advice to prospective college students who might not have access to it through traditional means.

    Role was as tech lead and primary Rails dev.

  • Volvo 3 Million Reasons to Believe

    • March 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Daniel Leavitt, Scott Wilson, Natacha Gaymer-Jones

    Project lead and Ruby dev for a socially-integrated microsite / app for Volvo highlighting the journeys of Irv (who's about to hit the three million mile mark on his Volvo.)

    Nicely designed site for a brand partner, utilizing Rails, MongoDB, Backbone, etc. Some interesting approaches for managing content as well - through judicious integration with Google Docs and Dropbox, we were able to bypass the asset management and floating copy decks that are typically a feature of agency partnerships.
