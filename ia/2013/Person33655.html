Debra Kurtz's Summary

Kurtz Consulting focuses on clients with sales and marketing solutions. We have deep vertical knowledge of healthcare, consumer products (direct-to-consumer) and small-to-medium businesses. Some recent engagements include:

• Marketing and clinical strategy for a medical device company leading to acquisition by Smith & Nephew for for $111 million
• Business strategy and marketing plan for a company entering a new market, leading to acquisition by its distributor
• Innovative new product launch for pharmaceutical, nutriceutical and medical diagnostics companies including gamechanging IP
• Marketing research to increase post-graduate enrollment for a leading university
• Assessment and performance management re-organization of a field sales force
• Loyalty program and word-of-mouth marketing
• Marketing and PR plan for a consumer packaged goods manufacturer
• Sales force audit and productivity optimization
• Blue Ocean Strategy for a service organization
* Visioning for a professional services firm
* Commercialization of a Direct To Consumer Medical Device

Prior to Kurtz Consulting, Kurtz was part of an executive team which grew a medical device company from under $20 million in annual sales into the $100 million market leader with over 80% market share and 15% EBITDA. Kurtz has experience in leading Sales, Marketing, Customer Service and Product Development functions.

  • Customer Relationship Management: Relating to Medtech Customers

    • MX: Business Strategies for Medical Technology Executives
    • March 2006
    Authors: Debra Kurtz

    Customer Relationship Management (CRM) was the much-heralded topic of the last decade, especially for companies seeking greater customer focus in their organizations. Amid reports that the post-implementation CRM scorecard is at best mixed, medical technology executives are left wondering what to expect from CRM and whether such initiatives can benefit their organizations.

    This article explores why organizations pursue CRM initiatives and what can be expected from such efforts. Various technology approaches and software options are reviewed and tips are provided from Med Tech executives who have implemented CRM initiatives.
