The best part of teaching is seeing students think about issues in new ways and develop into well-rounded professionals. Recruitment and Retention Director

Protective Life Corporation

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; PL; Insurance industry

1998 – 2004 (6 years)

I started my career at Protective Life straight out of graduate school. I was in charge of the annual Employee Feedback Survey. I also became an internal consultant for statistical measurement and quality improvement.

What I loved the most about the early years of my job at Protective, though, was when I functioned as an executive coach for our top 200 people. If you can imagine a senior leader allowing a twenty-something HR newbie help them be a better leader, go ahead. All I can say, though, is that it worked. I found a calling to work with people struggling through real, day-to-day issues in their jobs. I learned that you can never assume that just because someone is a senior executive that they don't want to learn and grow.

Later, I grew into a leadership role and managed the employee communications, organizational development, and recruitment process areas. I left Protective as a trailing spouse when my family relocated to Chattanooga, Tennessee.
