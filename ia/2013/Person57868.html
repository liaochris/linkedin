James Warren's Summary

James Warren is a Washington, DC - based international tax and business lawyer. In addition to having an extensive law practice involving banking and other commercial transactions, he is involved in numerous philanthropic and humanitarian ventures. A graduate of Cambridge University in England and Harvard Law School, he has been Tax Counsel at a major Wall Street law firm since 1999 and practicing law since 1990. His primary areas of practice are international business transactions, federal income tax controversies and tax-exempt entities. His clients have included several of the largest investment banks and brokerages in New York as well as the Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corporation.

Mr. Warren is also an English Solicitor and a former banker. He has worked in London, Dusseldorf, Germany, New York and Winston-Salem, North Carolina. He travels frequently to the Middle East and Europe and is conversant in Arabic and French.

Mr. Warren has been involved in a wide range of pro bono and other humanitarian projects. He served briefly on the prosecution team at the International Criminal Tribunal for Rwanda in Arusha, Tanzania, and as a neutral trial observer for Lawyers Without Borders in a political trial in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia.
