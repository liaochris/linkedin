Anna-Lysa Gayle's Summary

Anna-Lysa Gayle joined the WHSV-TV 3 staff in May 2013 as a reporter/photojournalist. She obtained a broadcast journalism degree from Howard University, while interning with professional news organizations in Washington, D.C. such as WTOP 103.5 FM, Fox 5 WTTG and CNN. In addition, she's participated in competitive professional development programs with the T. Howard Foundation, the National Association of Black Journalists (NABJ) short courses, the 2012 Online News Association (ONA) student newsroom, the 2013 Meredith-Cronkite Fellowship with CBS 5 KPHO and the CBC-UNC Diversity Fellowship with WRAL-TV.

Spotlight Network Television is a student run network on the campus of Howard University, that features campus news and entertainment programming. It was founded in 1983 by Jim Brown and Sonya Ray-Coleman. It started as a feature magazine program on WHUT-TV32, a PBS affiliate here in Washington, D.C. Today Spotlight Network Television is a training program for students on the campus of Howard University. WHUT-TV32 is the parent station of Spotlight Network Television.


As Senior Producer, I am responsible for leading the newly added Web Department, weekly story assignments, aiding reporters with story development and managing media relations for all campus events.
