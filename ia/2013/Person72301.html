Karin Ciano's Summary

I'm a former federal career clerk looking to meet good people, work on interesting cases, learn new things, and get back into federal court whenever I can. I practice civil rights and employment law. I am an experienced writer and teacher and enjoy taking on diverse freelance projects involving legal writing, legal research, and motion practice.

I help:

• Individuals who have experienced workplace discrimination or violations of civil rights
• Individuals who want to appeal their federal case
• Attorneys seeking quality legal research and writing at attractive rates
• Attorneys needing help managing litigation, responding to motions or discovery, or preparing for mediation, oral argument, or trial
• Attorneys needing subpoenas or federal discovery petitions
• Attorneys new to federal court who seek advice and guidance on federal practice
• Attorneys looking for co-counsel or local counsel in federal court
• Attorneys looking to improve their legal writing

Things I do:

• Legal writing (briefs, motions, mediation submissions, jury instructions, position pleadings, petitions, fee applications)
• Legal research (proficient in Fastcase, Google Scholar, and WestlawNext)
• Freelance litigation attorney
• Federal Sherpa™ consulting and assistance
• Civil rights law
• Employment law
• Mediation
• Discovery
• Appeals
• Local counsel
• Motions (TRO or preliminary injunction, dismissal, summary judgment, class certification, claim construction, Daubert, limine, suppression, post-trial, post-judgment, sanctions, contempt, premature appeal)
• Writing coach and CLE instructor
• Articles
• Presentations

I am passionate about mentoring law students and new lawyers, and love to help interested lawyers improve their legal writing.

I am committed to supporting low-cost, accessible opportunities to resolve disputes through mediation.

I volunteer as a master gardener with University of Minnesota Extension and love talking with people about their adventures in gardening.

Law Clerk

Federal Judiciary

Government Agency; 10,001+ employees; Judiciary industry

Adjunct Professor

William Mitchell College of Law

Educational Institution; 51-200 employees; Higher Education industry

August 2004 – May 2011 (6 years 10 months) Greater Minneapolis-St. Paul Area

For six years, I taught a section of twelve first-year evening students in the highly regarded Writing, Research, Advocacy & Persuasion (WRAP) program. I handled the writing side of the course which involved review and grading of student research and writing assignments including an office memo, client letter, demand letter, mediation submission, and memorandum of law on a motion in trial court. I assisted students in reviewing their own work through conferences and small group critique.

Career Law Clerk

Federal Judiciary

Government Agency; 10,001+ employees; Judiciary industry

August 2003 – August 2010 (7 years 1 month) Greater Minneapolis-St. Paul Area

For seven years, I assisted the Hon. James M. Rosenbaum (Ret.) of the United States District Court of the District Minnesota, with legal research and writing in connection with motions, trials, pleas and sentencings. I read hundreds of motion papers and position pleadings, and assisted the Court with drafting orders as needed. When Judge Rosenbaum sat by designation with the Eighth Circuit, I read appellate briefs and assisted with drafting opinions. I clerked over 20 trials, helping prepare instructions in jury trials and findings of fact in bench trials. I trained and mentored incoming clerks and interns. I also served on a committee of the U.S. District Court that reviewed civil judgment best practices.

Instructor, Lawyering Program

New York University School of Law

Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry

June 2000 – June 2003 (3 years 1 month) Greater New York City Area

For three years, I taught a 24-student section of an innovative first-year class whose curriculum blended legal doctrine, theory and skills. The class covered legal research, writing, citation, client interviewing, client counseling, negotiation, mediation, and oral argument. This full-time, non-tenure-track position required extensive research, review, and development of program curriculum. Together with other faculty I developed program-wide materials and exercises in negotiation, mediation and legal citation.

Litigation Associate

Debevoise & Plimpton

Partnership; 1001-5000 employees; Law Practice industry

November 1995 – February 2000 (4 years 4 months) Greater New York City Area

For over four years, I was a litigation associate at Debevoise & Plimpton. My practice focused on white collar crime, mass tort and insurance regulatory practice. I helped other attorneys at the firm write legal memoranda and briefs. I defended depositions, negotiated settlements and discovery agreements, and supervised staff attorney teams reviewing documents and analyzing claims. I advised the firm's transactional attorneys on drafting non-compete clauses in employment agreements. In keeping with the firm's longstanding tradition of pro bono service, I represented refugees in political asylum claims, and also briefed, argued and won a pro bono ineffective-assistance criminal appeal.

Karin Ciano's Publications

  • A Briefreader's Guide to Briefwriting

    • 59 The Federal Lawyer 42
    • January 2012
    Authors: Karin Ciano

    Federal law clerks have a remarkable opportunity to read arguments on a wide range of subjects that are drafted by lawyers who have varying levels of skill and experience. Although law clerks do not share the judge’s burden of decision-making, they are close enough to the process to observe what writers can do to make briefs helpful and memorable. From the perspective of an experienced reader, I offer some thoughts about writing briefs.
