Alistair Warwick's Projects

  • Early English Church Music

    • May 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Alistair Warwick, Sally Harper, Magnus Williamson, Matthew Chung-Salisbury, John Harper

    A new edition of settings of the Lady Mass, as celebrated in the Lady Chapel, for the initial volume in the chant sub-series of Stainer & Bell's 'Early English Church Music'.

    This edition, based on a number of sources, includes neum shapes from Wolfgang Hopyl's 'Graduale ad consuetudinem Sarum', 1507/8 newly drawn by Tim Symons.

  • Hymns for Prayer & Praise

    • September 1998 to December 2012
    Team Members: Alistair Warwick, John Harper, Philip Gaisford OSB, Peter Allen CR, Mark Hartley OCSO

    An invaluable compendium of hymns for celebration of the Divine Office (Liturgy of the Hours). Primarily for monastic communities, but also useful for cathedrals and greater churches.

Alistair Warwick's Publications

  • Mind the Gap

    • Choir & Organ
    • January 2007
    Authors: Alistair Warwick, Maggie Hamilton

    Interviewed by Maggie Hamilton, deputy editor of Choir and Organ, about gap-year study for organists and choral scholars. The Sacred Music Studies course from the University of Wales, Bangor and the Royal School of Church Music fills the gap nicely.

    "…There have been some exciting and unexpected results already, with students looking at what they're doing in a new light and developing their practice. I certainly wish that when I was a cathedral director of music that this course had been available; if it had, I'd have approached it more confidently – and achieved much more."

  • Musick fyne: Scottish Church Music up to 1603

    • Church Music Quarterly
    • June 2005
    Authors: Alistair Warwick

    We are very familiar with the names of William Byrd and Thomas Tallis, perhaps also with those of Robert Parsons, Christopher Tye and John Shepherd. But what about those from England's neighbours? While the names of many English composers trip off the tongue, until recently it has been harder to name many composers writing music for the Scottish Church.

  • Gregorian Chant sung in Latin

    • Church Music Quarterly
    • September 2003
    Authors: Alistair Warwick

    – reprinted in Church Times, June 2004
    Why sing in Latin, when our services are in our own language?

  • Just Treble-makers?

    • Church Music Quarterly
    • July 1999
    Authors: Alistair Warwick

    An introduction to the Voice for Life choir-training programme.

  • Choir-training notes

    • Bulletin of Panel of Monastic Musicians
    • 1999
    Authors: Alistair Warwick

    – reprinted in Music & Liturgy (2000)
    Following a workshop at the Panel of Monastic Musicians 1998 residential course, this article explores rehearsal and warm-up techniques, particularly directed at monks and nuns who sing the Mass and the Office daily.

  • Herald Triumph!

    • The Wire
    • 1995
    Authors: Alistair Warwick

    – online at Herald AV Publications
    – reprinted in Newsletter of The Association of Latin Liturgy (September 2003)
    An account of the recording of Dupré's 'Les Vêpres de la Vierge' Op. 18 in Notre-Dame Cathedral, Paris by the Schola Gregoriana of Cambridge, directed by Mary Berry with David Hill (choir organ) and Phillipe Lefebvre (grand orgues).

  • The Psalms

    • The Universe
    • 1986
    Authors: Alistair Warwick

    An article for 'Faith Alive' on the historic and liturgical use of the psalms in the liturgy.
