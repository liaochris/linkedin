Social Media Consultant

Waldorf Astoria Chicago / Elysian Hotel

Privately Held; 1001-5000 employees; Hospitality industry

February 2011 – January 2013 (2 years) Greater Chicago Area

Established a "hand-made" social-media presence from scratch for Elysian Hotel, and carried that forward as the hotel transitioned to become Waldorf Astoria Chicago in February 2012. I managed the hotel's social-media platforms through the transition until late 2012. I also created Twitter accounts for Elysian Spa & Health Club, and the hotel's two nationally recognized restaurants, Balsan and the two-Michelin-star RIA. I currently manage the daily conversation and content on its three Twitter accounts (hotel, restaurant/bar, spa) as well as Pinterest, and along with its marketing team contribute Facebook content.

I played an active role in developing guest loyalty and "getting out the vote" for major "readers' choice" campaigns. The hotel has been ranked #1 in the U.S. in various categories by Conde Nast Traveler, Travel + Leisure, US News & World Report, and TripAdvisor, as well as numerous accolades elsewhere. In instances of Reader's Choice Awards (such as Conde Nast Traveler and Travel + Leisure).

Ongoing strategies included:
— embodying the Waldorf Astoria Chicago experience with comfortable, careful, engaged, and enjoyable service, tone, and content;

—Interaction with every guest who establishes communication through Facebook and Twitter;

—Communication with parent company to sync the hotel's social-media strategies with Waldorf Astoria global brand;

— pro-actively establishing relationships with partnering companies, potential guests, and tastemakers through search and research strategies;

— providing daily content that captures/evokes/embodies the Waldorf Astoria Chicago experience, and retains the attention of guests who already “are sold” on the experience as well as potential future guests.

Results have included increased revenue, word-of-mouth, and customer service. Klout score reached 66, highest in comp set.

For content samples, see 2011/2012 Twitter/FB posts by @Waldorf_Chicago, @ElysianChiSpa, and @balsanbernards.
