Christopher Tully's Summary

THE EDUCATOR
Since 2002, I have served as the Multimedia Technology Instructor at Middle Bucks Institute of Technology. The program was awarded "Exemplary Program 2007" and "Outstanding Career and Technical Education Program 2008," by the PA Department of Education, “Distinguished Program 2009-2010”, "2010-2011", "2011-2012", "2012-2013" by Apple, Inc. I was acknowledged as an "Apple Distinguished Educator (ADE) 2007" by Apple, Inc. and served on the ADE Advisory Board from 2009 - 2012. Other acknowledgements include "Outstanding Teacher 2010" by the Association of Career and Technical Education, received the "Tree of Life Award 2010," by the Central Bucks Chamber of Commerce and the recipient of the "C. Thomas Olivo Outstanding Service Award" by the NOCTI.

In addition, I am an adjunct professor for Temple University and Moore College of Art teaching graduate and undergraduate education courses. I have also had the opportunity to render my services to Apple, Inc, Cisco, Inc., the National Occupational Competency Testing Institute (NOCTI) and the Pennsylvania Department of Education. Through this experience, I have written the Apple Final Cut Pro exam, the Cisco Web Design exam, the Pennsylvania Web Design Exam as well as the NOCTI Television Production, Web Design and Audio/Visual Communications exams.

THE INDUSTRY PROFESSIONAL

From websites to motion graphics, to digital signage, video editing for film and events, audio productions for on-hold phone systems and full narratives, instructional videos, Podcasts, web-based training and other types of tutorials, we do it all.

Christopher Tully's Publications

  • Educating 21st-Century Students

    • Techniques Magazine page 20
    • October 2012
    Authors: Christopher Tully, Tom Viviano, PhD.

    This article was written in collaboration with John Fala, Kathryn Strouse and Thomas Viviano. The article provides an up-close look at a successful career and technical education center.

  • Distinguished in PA

    • CTEduOnline
    • February 2013
    Authors: Christopher Tully

    The general purpose of a teacher evaluation system is to ensure that teachers are meeting the requirements of being effective educators by providing feedback on their instructional practices. This eBook will provide you with insights into the need for evaluation systems, studies on their effectiveness and strategies to reach the distinguished level on the new Pennsylvania teacher evaluation tool.

  • Social Media In and Out of the Classroom

    • CTEduOnline
    • February 2013
    Authors: Christopher Tully

    Social media has become a term that is synonymous with technology and the digital age. Generally speaking, it refers to a set of tools that allows individuals to communicate, collaborate and build relationships using technology. Social media is often used to share information, ideas, personal messages and other content, such as photos or videos. This book provides you with insight as to how you can easily and effectively incorporate Facebook, Google+, Twitter and YouTube into your classroom, school or district.

  • Making Better Videos

    • CTEduOnline
    • February 2013
    Authors: Christopher Tully

    There are several factors that can improve the quality of your videos. The most important factors are lighting, shot composition and audio quality. However, each decision you make regarding the location and the type of equipment will also impact your project. This guide will assist you in making your videos better.

  • In Classroom Mentor Teachers: An Addition to Mentor Teachers

    • ERIC
    • February 2013
    Authors: Christopher Tully, Tom Viviano, PhD.

    There have been numerous studies done on the impact that mentor teachers and new teacher induction plans have on the new teachers' success in the first couple of years. A lot of these studies were done in an attempt not only to determine how to attract good teachers, but to retain them. It is our hope in this article to also determine if by having a classroom coach and teacher mentors in place, we can mold these new teachers into a strong cultural fit. This article looks at what impact an inside-the-classroom coach would have in addition to the benefits previously obtained from having mentor teachers that teach their own classes.
