Keir Graff's Summary

In 2005, I sketched Booklist Online's home page on a cocktail napkin, and when we launched a year later, I formatted our content, edited the site, and wrote our only blog. Today I am at the center of a diverse array of online offerings that includes a website, a database of over 135,000 reviews, 5 blogs, 5 newsletters, social media, and a busy calendar of webinars.

When I'm not at work, I write and publish novels, pursue interesting freelance writing projects, and coach my sons' soccer teams. I'm always interested in new opportunities.

Specialties: Writing, editing, websites, blogs, newsletters, innovating and launching new products, SEO, project management, public speaking

Keir Graff's Experience

Editor, Booklist Online

American Library Association

Nonprofit; 201-500 employees; Nonprofit Organization Management industry

February 2005 – Present (8 years 8 months)

I maintain a lively dialogue between our print magazine, Booklist, and Booklist Online, a subscription-only review database with extensive public content. The first person in Booklist's history to be promoted from editorial assistant to senior editor, I made major contributions to the concept and design of Booklist Online before its launch in March 2006. My duties and accomplishments include:

• Design and project-manage ALA’s first profitable web product, a robust website and database with over 135,000 book reviews and 4,000 articles

• Lead development of content-management system for publishing print and online products; define content taxonomy and relationships to aid user discovery

• Build web traffic, through SEO and sharing, to nearly 2 million page views per month

• Develop content strategy for Booklist Publications, the premiere book review journal for librarians and its associated web products

• Write, commission, and edit web-only content to daily, weekly, and monthly deadlines

• Design, edit, and publish targeted newsletters with up to 100,000 subscribers; create production schedules; grow and refine mailing lists

• Drive traffic and boost user engagement through comprehensive social-media program

• Create or acquire new blogs while training and supervising staff and freelance bloggers

• Represent Booklist and strengthen publisher relations at 3–4 conferences annually

• Hire and manage editorial assistant; select and supervise 4–6 interns each year

• Read and review 50–60 books per year

Freelance Writer

Self - Employed

1996 – Present (17 years)

I am a published novelist and short-story author, a produced playwright, and an accomplished freelance writer. My most recent book, a middle-grade novel called The Other Felix (2011), was named "Best of the Best" by the Chicago Public Library. As a freelance writer, I have written feature articles and reviews on a wide variety of subjects--from food and drink to current events--for a diverse array of publications. I am represented in my fiction writing by Josh Getzler of Hannigan Salky Getzler Agency.

American Library Association

Nonprofit; 201-500 employees; Nonprofit Organization Management industry

September 2001 – January 2005 (3 years 5 months)

Although each day began with large boxes of books to open, I tried to make the most of this humble position and found many ways to expand my skill set:

Keir Graff's Publications

  • The Other Felix

    • Roaring Brook Press
    • October 2011
    Authors: Keir Graff

    Named one of Chicago Public Library's "Best of the Best" books in 2012, this middle-grade novel is about a boy who faces his fears of monsters and bullies with the help of a doppelganger he meets in dreamland. School Library Journal wrote: "The story has a beautifully crafted innocence reminiscent of The Little Prince."

  • The Price of Liberty

    • Severn House
    • July 2010
    Authors: Keir Graff

    A political thriller about a bulldozer operator who discovers that his defense-contractor boss is cooking the books, The Price of Liberty was a finalist for the Society of Midland Authors fiction prize. Said Publishers Weekly: "Graff’s cynical take on government waste and corporate greed plays well. McEnroe is as rugged as the desolate country he lives in, and his gritty do-what-ya-gotta-do actions keep the pages turning easily."
