Creative Director

AccuWeather, Inc.

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Media Production industry

July 1989 – October 2009 (20 years 4 months) State College, PA

My twenty year career at AccuWeather allowed me to participate in all levels of graphic design work, from a newspaper production artist, to shift manager, newspaper developer, development manager and finally creative director. I managed and directed a team of graphic designers who grew from two to seven staff members. I pioneered the use of QuarkXPress for the automated creation of newspaper weather pages in the early 90s, greatly increasing AccuWeather's efficiency, reducing error and allowing the department to substantially grow the number of clients we served. In the mid-90s when the web was in its infancy I developed the first web page for AccuWeather, which eventually grew into a website that has generated millions of dollars of advertising based and subscription based revenue. I've worked with a who's who in media, including the AP, Disney, AOL, Apple, Yahoo!, Google, the Wall Street Journal, and many others, as well as clients from diverse sectors such as Agriculture, Business, Government, Energy, Transportation, both domestically and internationally. I'm perhaps most proud of the number of ideas I've conceived for innovative new products and services, many which went on to become core services AccuWeather has offered to clients via print and online channels. I championed the blogging effort at AccuWeather by creating a "redesign blog" during one of AccuWeather.com's redesign projects - gathering feedback from website visitors and making the audience a part of the redesign experience, giving them a voice and ownership over their weather website.
