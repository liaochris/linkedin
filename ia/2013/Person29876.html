Microsoft

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; MSFT; Computer Software industry

October 2003 – November 2009 (6 years 2 months)

Created the New World of Work positioning for Microsoft that became the thought leadership platform for Microsoft’s Business Division from 2005 to 2007. This started as a speech delivered by Bill Gates at the CEO summit, which I wrote, along with executive mail and accompanying whitepaper. The New World of Work was highly influential in Europe, where it drove the transformation of many of the Microsoft offices as they adapted their work practices to include remote work and open plan offices that focused on collaboration and transparency. Het Nieuwe Werken, as it became known in the Netherlands, made the Microsoft office at Schiphol Airport one of the best places to work in Europe. I was a frequent coach and presenter to customers across Europe as they decided to emulate the success of the Microsoft Dutch office.

Adapted the New World of Work to become the thought leadership platform for the People-Ready Business (PRB) campaign as the New World of Business, which was the cornerstone of the 2007-2009 Microsoft Business Division marketing effort. Authored over a dozen whitepapers on the future of work, created and acted as general editor for a book series with Wiley that published a dozen books, including my own book, Listening to the Future, which encompassed Microsoft’s long range planning story and its New World of Work Business story. Also drove content through social media channels such as blogs and twitter. The New World of Work/Business became a highly rated Microsoft Executive Briefing Center topic, garnering me awards as Distinguished Speaker and the One Microsoft Award, as well as quality awards, for each of the years between its launch in 2006 and 2009.

Lead the development of the Microsoft Center for Information Work (CIW), an immersive experience that demonstrated the potential of technology to positively change people’s work experiences.
