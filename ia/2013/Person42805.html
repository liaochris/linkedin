  • Stability Verification for Large Scale Systems

    • August 2008 to Present
    Team Members: Erin Summers

    I study stability of large-scale dynamical systems such as the Internet, airplanes, and biological reaction networks. A system is unstable if the behavior deviates in such a manner that control of the system is lost. The consequences of a unstable system drastically different depending on the application: loss of control of the airplane or automotive that results in a crash; packet dropping from servers on the Internet; cancerous cell mutations, cell death, or deadly imbalances of chemicals in the body.

    Standard tools to assess the stability cannot be applied to large problems because the memory required and the computation time needed scales exponentially. I break up the graphs and studying smaller, tractable components of the larger system. Next, I use information about the interconnection of these subsystems to learn about the stability of the entire system.
