Max Duggan's Summary

Hello, I’m a designer and artist from Oklahoma where I’m finishing up a degree in Visual Communication. My passion is custom typography, user experience and identity. When I’m not designing, I’ve been known to make prints of armadillos and continue to work on a photo-documentary about truck drivers. Much of my art is made with my annoyingly talented friends, which inspired me to co-found and run Okie Collective, a company where I make collaborative art and promote other Okie artists through my design.

Max Duggan's Projects

  • Gentleman: Male care products

    Team Members: Max Duggan

    Gentleman is a fictional company for the guy that doesn’t just rush through his morning, but takes pride in his grooming skill. Art deco was chosen as a style, which led to an emphasis on geometric elements, such as the mark , where I played with alternating thickness to imply perspective. When it came to packaging, I integrated the mark across the tops of the boxes in white and let the background produce a reverse shape.

  • Classics: Interactive menu

    Team Members: Max Duggan

    Classics is a locally owned drive-in. The goal was to replace the traditional audio drive-in system and eliminate mis-heard orders. I proposed to give the end user all the power via an interactive touch screen. The system is based off a wire-frame, taking the user through main categories and more specialized options. All options that are selected are reflected on the receipt constantly displayed on the right.

  • Homeland: Neighborhood Market

    Team Members: Max Duggan

    Homeland is a regional grocery chain. The mark is based off the paper grocery bag and is filled with a cursive H to contrast to the sans serif of the logotype. The main mark is referenced in the secondary symbols which are set in the main mark’s shape. The packaging uses horizontal overlapping bars to create a store wide style that changes the colors based on the products.

  • La Baguette: Cafe & Bakery

    Team Members: Max Duggan

    La Baguette is a family owned French bakery and cafe in Norman, OK. Green was used to represent the cafe and blue for the bakery sides of the company. The logo, I made by hand to add a more casual feel. I felt the use of a baguette or cake mark would have unfairly bias the one side of the business. Instead, I developed a set of symbols for both the bakery & cafe and developed patterns of the symbols to use on the packaging.

  • Shell Creek: Wine labels

    Team Members: Max Duggan

    When designing a fictitious vineyard and wine labels, I decided to pull the name from the ranch I grew up on for part of my childhood “Shell Creek Ranch”. With the idea of a turtle shell, I eventually came across a mark based off of overlapping transparent shapes of color. I used specialty papers for each wine type and changed the colors in the logo to matches the grapes of the specific wine.
