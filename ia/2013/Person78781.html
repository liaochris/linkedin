Laura Nix Gerson's Summary

Laura is a Digital Media Producer developing online content, implementing social media campaigns and blogger relations, and producing video series for brands and local businesses via her sites MomAngeles and TheMomFair.

MomAngeles is an online resource for LA moms that puts the Best of LA in the palm of your hand via posts and directory listings and with the MomAngeles Mommy Card which offers Moms "perks" with businesses throughout LA.

Laura is an advocate for working Moms, small business and entrepreneurs and has been a trusted partner and resource for many companies for more than 20 years providing business development and marketing counsel to help grow businesses. Her clients have ranged from from Top 5 Law firms to local start-ups.

As an advocate for working Moms, Laura and her team at MomAngeles launched TheMomFair to create an online resource and events that help Moms #GetBacktoWork. For information about MomAngeles or TheMomFair contact her at laura@momangeles.com.

You can follow Laura @LauraNixGerson, @MomAngeles, @TheMomFair, @TheMommyAwards.
She is a regular contributor to @ModernMom, @MomsLA, @CBSLA, @MoonFrye, @HerReview and @HollywoodHotMoms.

      PHASE ONE Foundation

      August 2012 – present (1 year 4 months)

      PHASE ONE is a non-profit organization dedicated to supporting Phase I clinical research and treatment programs for patients with cancer. Once a new idea or novel drug is identified in the laboratory, the FDA requires three levels of clinical testing prior to the approval of that drug for widespread use. These studies are usually referred to as trials in Phase I, II, or III. The focus of a Phase I trial is to determine the appropriate dose and schedule of a given drug and its side effects. Patients enrolled usually have advanced cancers, which have progressed through standard therapy or for which standard therapy does not exist. With your support PHASE ONE hopes that its efforts to fund Phase I trials and research will help in the fight against cancer, by finding treatments that will transform what might be a terminal illness into one where patients can live a full and productive life.
