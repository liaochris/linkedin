Kent Fuka's Experience

Founder and CEO

Querium Corporation

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; E-Learning industry

February 2013 – Present (9 months) Austin, TX

Querium helps teen and adult learners master life's high-stakes tests so they can achieve their career ambitions. Designed for digital native students living connected lives, our mobile learning platform delivers personalized short-form lessons and practice tests in STEM subjects like Math and Science. Our first release is a smartphone-based product that helps incoming college freshmen prep for the new Texas Success Initiative (TSI) placement test which is required in public colleges starting August 26, 2013.

Member of the Board of Directors

Thinkwell

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; E-Learning industry

July 2013 – Present (4 months) Austin, Texas Area

Remain a board member at Thinkwell after Carl Tyson promoted to Chairman & CEO.

President

KNCL Management, Inc.

February 2001 – Present (12 years 9 months) Austin, Texas Area

KNCL Management provides a range of strategic consulting services to high-tech startups. KNCL Management also acts as General Partner of KNCL Associates Ltd, a limited partnership that makes angel investments and LP investments in venture capital firms.

Board Member

Santa Fe Business Incubator

Nonprofit; 1-10 employees; Nonprofit Organization Management industry

June 2012 – Present (1 year 5 months) Santa Fe, NM

Since 1997, SFBI has supported economic development in Santa Fe by helping entrepreneurs launch successful companies In a wide range of industries by providing office, light manufacturing and lab space in a 30,000 sq ft facility. SFBI serves an average of 17 companies at any one time.

Chairman

Thinkwell

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; E-Learning industry

February 1999 – June 2013 (14 years 5 months) Austin, Texas Area

Thinkwell is a specialist in online learning. Thinkwell's multimedia products in math, science, and social science integrate technology, art, and video with great teaching to produce a new path to success for students. Thinkwell's products and courses are being used by thousands of students at schools and universities across the globe. Kent has been Chairman of the Board since February, 1999. Thinkwell's venture investors include Rho Ventures and the LBJ Family.

Board Member

Medical Present Value

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Hospital & Health Care industry

1999 – 2002 (3 years) San Antonio, Texas Area

MPV is a software-as-a-service for revenue optimization at healthcare providers. The company's expert system technology models insurance contracts and helps clients recover 100% of what they are owed on healthcare claims. Kent lead the original Series A venture capital investment in MPV. MPV was acquired by 'big-three' credit reporting company Experian in 2011.

General Partner

CenterPoint Ventures

Partnership; 1-10 employees; Venture Capital & Private Equity industry

October 1996 – February 2001 (4 years 5 months) Austin, Texas Area

As a founding partner of this Texas-based VC firm, Kent helped raise, invest and manage approximately $450M in venture capital funds aimed at I.T. companies in Texas. CenterPoint's successful exits included Silicon Labs IPO, Active Power IPO, and MPV's acquisition by Experian.

Board Member

Applied Science Fiction

1996 – 2000 (4 years) Austin, Texas Area

ASF licensed revolutionary technologies in the imaging space. The company's Digital ICE technology was an industry standard for image correction for scanners. ASF was acquired by Kodak in 2003.

Board Member

Encentris

1996 – 2000 (4 years) Dallas/Fort Worth Area

Encentris created an enterprise project management system for use in coordinating world-wide I.T. projects.

Board Member

Object Reservoir

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Oil & Energy industry

1996 – 2000 (4 years) Austin, Texas Area

Object Reservoir is a cutting-edge software technology company that provides a unique approach and revolutionary solutions to challenging problems in the global Exploration and Production industry.

Board Member

NetBotz

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Computer & Network Security industry

1996 – 2000 (4 years) Austin, Texas Area

NetBotz developed easy-to-manage rack-mount appliances for monitoring the server room environment. The products incorporated video-cameras and environmental sensors. The company was acquired by American Power Conversion (APC) in 2005.

VP Customer Operations

Tivoli Systems

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; IBM; Computer Software industry

October 1991 – March 1996 (4 years 6 months) Austin, Texas Area

Kent joined the senior management team at Tivoli to run customer support and program management in the months leading up to the shipment of Tivoli's first product. Tivoli's VC investors included Austin Ventures, Kleiner-Perkins, Matrix and Norwest. Later as the company's international sales expanded, Kent took over international service responsibliity. Tivoli had a successful IPO in 1995 and was acquired by IBM in 1996.

Director of Product Planning

The Convex Computer Corp

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Computer Hardware industry

October 1982 – October 1991 (9 years 1 month) Dallas/Fort Worth Area

As a founding employee of this venture-backed startup in the Dallas area, Kent oversaw software development for layered products, and later for the ConvexOS operating system. Convex's VC investors included Sevin-Rosen, Sequoia, Interwest, and Rho. Kent ran the software support call center, was a product manager in the marketing organization, worked on the launch of Convex's second generation product, and finally served as Director of Product Planning, where he lead a team that conceived the company's Scalar Parallel Processor system. Convex had a successful IPO in 1987, and was acquired by Hewlett-Packard in 1995.

Regional Systems Engineer/Evangelist

Apollo Computer

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; HPQ; Information Technology and Services industry

1980 – 1982 (2 years) Greater Boston Area, Dallas Texas Area

Apollo Computer was an early venture-backed company headquarted near Boston. Apollo's VC investors included Greylock, Matrix and Venrock. The company was one of the first vendors of engineering workstations. Kent was involved in the sale and installation of many early Apollo customers. Apollo merged with Hewlett-Packard in 1989.

Software Project Manager

Data General

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; DGN; Computer Hardware industry

1975 – 1980 (5 years)

Kent began his time at Data General as a co-op student in DG's Dallas Texas sales office. On graduation to SMU, Kent moved to DG's headquarters outside Boston, where he became the youngest software project manager at DG, and oversaw the company's flagship Fortran compiler. He later started development work on DG's for C programming language compiler.
