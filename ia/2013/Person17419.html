Brian Hetzman's Summary

My expertise in video, marketing and the Internet has been forged over years, beginning with my work in broadcast television when I served as electronics art director at WPBF-TV, the ABC affiliate in West Palm Beach, Florida. I created the station's on-air look and produced graphics and animation design, including the station's virtual news sets. My efforts led to ADDY and national TELLY awards for commercial production at the station.

Exciting new opportunities in the world of multi-media followed.

I founded a development company that teamed with Motorola to create full-motion touchscreen video kiosks at Motorola's "Innoventions" exhibit at Disney's EPCOT Center. The company's major corporate accounts included Blockbuster Video, American Media, the parent company of the National Enquirer and other publications, Kiplinger Personal Finance, the National Medical Association, and Siemens.

My partnership with Siemens Information and Communication Networks (ICN) led to a opportunity to serve as senior webmaster for its E-Business Development division based in Lake Mary, Florida. As a member of the global e-business team, I was involved in the design and managed the corporate Internet site. I also served as architect and creator of the intranet site for Siemens ICN Carrier Division in the U.S. My creative Internet approaches supported the company's information technology and marketing departments.

I also worked with the global team by providing front-end development for the global e-commmerce solution based out of Munich, Germany.

My Internet and television expertise allowed me to forge a partnership with one of the largest automotive advertising agencies in the southeast. I partnered with Chumney & Associates to create a company, Florida Video Post, to serve the video and new media needs of the automotive industry.

It all adds up to a successful track record involving Fortune 100 companies, broadcast TV, new media and the Internet.
