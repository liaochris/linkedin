Brett Harris' Summary

I am a user experience professional. I figured out how computers work when I was in elementary school. I developed my first piece of software when I was in middle school. I earned a degree in Information Sciences and Technology at Penn State.

Since I started my career, I've focused on making software better. Not just through great interactions, simple navigation, or compelling interfaces, but by making software something with which users form positive relationships.

Brett Harris' Experience

Lead User Experience Architect

Association of American Medical College

Nonprofit; 501-1000 employees; Higher Education industry

January 2011 – Present (2 years 9 months) Washington D.C. Metro Area

Since the beginning of 2011, I have worked to mature various user experiences disciplines within IT. Our development staff is made up of 60 or so developers, augmented by up to 40 more off-shore. We are constantly working on new, re-engineering, and maintenance projects using Java, PHP, and Cold Fusion.

To unify our interfaces for consistency and to reduce development efforts, I established a core set of UI standards, interaction patterns, and a library of templates and other tools to empower our developers.

Additionally, I consult on projects to ensure a focus on user needs and to address challenging interactions.

Lead Software Engineer

Association of American Medical Colleges

Nonprofit; 501-1000 employees; Higher Education industry

December 2006 – December 2010 (4 years 1 month) Washington D.C. Metro Area

My first project with the AAMC was to lead a team of developers in creating a new application for medical students which allows them to apply for electives at schools other than their own. I was tasked with designing the user interface for a complex transactional application while gathering and evolving business requirements. Designing for the application's distinct user groups, medical students and medical school admissions staff, presented unique challenges. Our efforts were recognized by a 83% satisfaction rate and a 700% increase in participation after the first year.

My second undertaking has been implementation of CoreMedia, a content management system (CMS), for two large web properties. The first property, aamc.org, was converted from static HTML. I worked with consultants and our developers to learn the platform and implement the website in the CMS. My roles were additionally focused on assisting communication staff with content migration and information architecture for the new site. My previous experience in content management helped me lead the team to a successful completion. For the second property, aspiringdocs.org, I redesigned the interface to align with aamc.org while maintaining the existing presence of the site. I lead a small team in all aspects of the site migration and deployment.

In addition to these projects, I lead groups of colleagues focused on exploring design principles and user experience. Our "user group" of developers meets monthly to discuss current UI trends, problems we are facing, and to learn from each others' experience. This group spawned a second group of user experience advocates. Our UX group took on the challenge of exploring ways to improve user experience within our applications and made recommendations to IT and business leadership for achieving those goals. Both groups continue to meet to educate and support our development staff.

Senior Project Manager

Conduit Internet Technologies

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Information Technology and Services industry

August 2004 – October 2006 (2 years 3 months) State College, PA

As an account manager, it was my responsibility to take over at the end of the sales cycle. I would meet on site with clients for a week to train staff on the content management system (CMS) and to begin requirements gathering. These two objectives worked together by allowing me to provide visualization of concepts like content type definition and page template layouts. Helping the client understand the part of content analysis was key to transitioning them to using a CMS from hand coded website management. Additionally, because all of our clients' sites were about product catalogs and tailoring to specific markets, we spent a great deal of time on creating information architectures to both streamline internal management of data and segment products by market and application.

As a software engineer, I oversaw the development of product catalog management software for 2 ½ years. I was responsible for using/customizing our CMS to implement client websites and product catalogs. Because Conduit was a startup, the CMS was always changing and gaining new features as each new client had different needs. This rapid growth led to inconsistent code bases from client to client. So, I standardized our software to accommodate varying client needs while still being centrally maintainable and profitable. This was accomplished by creating XHTML/CSS widgets for content types and making them configurable within the software to work with custom content types. By standardizing our code, we were able to spend less time on customizations and deliver sites more efficiently.

Systems Developer

Bustin Industrial Products

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Industrial Automation industry

2000 – 2004 (4 years) East Stroudsburg, Pennsylvania

As a member of the IT department at a manufacturing company, I faced interesting user experience problems. Understanding internal and external customers' needs and behaviors was critical to the success of the applications I developed.

For internal customers, I developed systems to support sales, accounting, and engineering departments. These systems integrated legacy, mainframe systems. Many of the legacy systems were cumbersome and hated. Providing modern, easy to use interfaces reduced the interaction with them, which, in turn, increased productivity and helped people learn to like computers. Even the shop workers saw benefits and stopped grumbling about "stupid computers".

For external customers, I managed the website by creating HTML, CSS, and product content and images. I also enhanced the website with a customer portal which provided streamline quote requesting, shipment tracking, and other perks for regular customers. The portal also provided the sales team with leads and focused marketing demographics.
