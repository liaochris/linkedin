Joshua Turner's Summary

Eager for employment opportunities.

Joshua Turner's background includes service in the United States Marine Corps, two Senate internships, a third internship in Japan, leadership in an international cultural exchange conference, and admitted into the Phi Beta Kappa chapter at the University of Hawai’i (UH).

Joshua's work as a Geospatial Information Analysis Assistant at UH was published multiple times. His work included ArcGIS products for the Hawaii Legislature, a local area farm land partition negotiated between three government agencies, and several citations for research. He took a specialized training course not required to complete his major at UH to improve his knowledge and skill.

He was choosen to represent UH at the Ehime Prefectural International Center were he gave over 20 public presentations in Japanese during a three month long internship.

Joshua also represented UH at the 59th Japan-America Student Conference (JASC) in 2007 as a delegate, and an executive of the 60th JASC in 2008. As a leader of the 60th JASC he helped work to ensure that the 71 other participating students had transportation, lodging, meals, and exposure to business, government, and academic leaders in Portland, Los Angeles, Missoula, and Boston.

At the Fr. Ray Foundation in Thailand, he led a 4-month long project to produce blueprints for all 63 buildings owned by the foundation. He also taught 3 English classes which included responsibility for preparing all lesson plans, homework, and tests.

As an Intelligence Specialist, he was an expert in all-source fusion analysis. His work was done in nine different countries in Asia and a six-month tour in Iraq. He regularly briefed situational updates to staff and officers. He also excelled at Microsoft Office applications, photo editing, and maintenance of the unit website and databases.

Eligible for TOP SECRET based on a Single Scope Background Investigation (SSBI) by the Office of Personnel Management (OPM) on 20120103.
