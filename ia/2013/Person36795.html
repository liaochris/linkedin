  • A Picture's Worth a Thousand Words: Visual Media Tips for Small Businesses

    • Forbes
    • March 14, 2013
    Authors: Donna Moritz

    I was featured in this article by Forbes about the rise and rise of visual social media and how we can leverage the power of visuals in our markteing:

    "From blogs to short 140-character tweets to pictures, social media content is transforming from text to images. In 2012, visual pinboard site Pinterest saw the largest year-over-year increase of any social network, says the Nielsen 2012 Social Media Report.

    Here are some tips for how small businesses can make their social media more visual:

    Show, Don’t Tell

    Use visuals – pictures, images, infographics, videos, graphics, slideshows, and animations – to create emotional connections with your company, says Donna Moritz, a visual marketing specialist and founder of the blog Socially Sorted. Try to showcase not just your product, but also the culture and message behind your brand, she adds.

    Don’t forget to show off your staff as well, author and social media expert David Lee King told Inc. Create ongoing relationships with customers by showing the friendly faces behind your business, he advises.

    Be an Original

    Providing original content for others to share – especially when it links back to your business – will put your small business a step ahead, says Moritz. Over 80% of pins are actually repinned content on Pinterest, she points out, which means only 20% of the site’s posted content is original.

    Original content doesn’t need to be fancy – just unique. To create your own content, invest in photo- and video-editing training, use smartphone photo apps like Instagram and Phonto, or try a photo editing program like PicMonkey.com, Moritz advises.
