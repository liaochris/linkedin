MerriBeth Farnham's Summary

MerriBeth Farnham brings over two decades of public outreach and marketing experience to every new venture. She founded Farnham & Associates in 1998, evolving to the company name “HD PR Group” in 2009.

With a background in journalism, City government and management consulting, Ms. Farnham is well versed in tailoring programs to include the key audiences and information tools needed to address issue-oriented public outreach needs. Her expertise lends itself to a wide array of issues, from assisting with immediate response to the media and public during a crisis to long range planning that allows for meaningful stakeholder input from project conception through completion. Her team is adept in creating a comprehensive host of information tools from initial program branding to website design, media relations, speaker’s bureau development, video production and more.

Prior to founding her own public relations, media and marketing firm, Ms. Farnham worked as a consultant with the environmental engineering firm, Camp Dresser & McKee Inc., traveling across the nation to develop public outreach and marketing programs for municipal clients. In 1992, Ms. Farnham served as the Public Information Officer for the City of Cape Coral. Previously, she worked as a reporter for the local television station, WINK-TV and she maintains close contacts with reporter and editorial staffs from print, radio and television media throughout Southwest Florida.

HD PR Group has worked on vital projects for more than a dozen municipalities and agencies across the nation. Our firm would be pleased to provide references regarding our work.

March 1994 – February 1998 (4 years)

Well-timed, accurate and meaningful public interaction is critical to the success of watershed resources management planning. CDM has learned that by beginning active education and stakeholder involvement early in the planning process, citizens are much more likely to support later implementation efforts.

As the Senior Public Information Director for this global firm, MerriBeth used a wide array of tools and techniques to translate highly technical engineering concepts to effectively communicate with the various target audiences involved. This often involved consensus building with key stakeholder groups.

Examples of projects that required a public involvement component included :

o Wastewater and Potable Water Expansion Programs
o Introduction of Reclaimed Water/Facility Expansion
o Stormwater Education/Watershed Management
o Siting Issues related
o Financial issues, low interest loans and grant applications
o Drinking water source concerns
o Hazardous waste concerns
o Construction management customer relations

Ms. Farnham is based in Florida and has worked on environmental projects across the country. Today, her firm continues to actively provide services to CDM as well as many municipal clients.
