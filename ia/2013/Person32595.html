David Snyder's Summary

Representation: The Agency for Speakers and Entertainers, The Jeff Herman Literary Agency, Nashville Songwriters' Association, ASCAP.

At Snyder, Inc., and its affiliate, Data Genius Resources, David leads a team of some of the nation's most acclaimed thought leaders and practitioners in performance management, business intelligence, cognitive assessment technology and human capital alignment. He is a partner with Work Intelligence (WIQ) of Boston, an organization comprised of Harvard trained professionals in behavioral research and assessment.

He is also an author, playwright, entrepreneur, composer, published songwriter, multi-instrumentalist and producer who works with his wife Gayle and others in the production of new theatrical works. Additionally, he is Regional Coordinator for The Nashville Songwriter's Association,

Among other inventions, David has developed a character-based approach to selecting high performers through Behavioral Event Interviewing. The process has received wide industry acclaim and is discussed in David's book How to Hire a Champion. The book was named by Bloomberg television in Germany as one of the best books available on building high performing teams.

He also has developed a broad range of popular and best-selling on-line professional development programs at NC State University.

David is the author of the internationally acclaimed books How To Mind Read Your Customers and How to Hire a Champion, both of which are widely translated. He holds a graduate degree in psychology from Harvard and an undergraduate degree in English and Comparative Literature from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. His clients include financial institutions, health care organizations, pharmaceutical companies, educational institutions, manufacturers, trade shows and biotechnology leaders. See www.mindread.net.

Additionally, David serves as an advisor in hiring and organizational development to the American Red Cross.

Specialties

Business Intelligence and Customer Response Strategies. Also, two decades of experience in building high performing teams, creating competency models for high performing individuals and high performing organizations, process improvement, change management, establishing competency-based behavioral performance metrics, behavioral research, management development.

University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill

BA, English, Comparative Literature

I will never forget the experience of being able to study under the legendary Dr. Eugene Hannes Falk, a professor of French and Comparative Literature at UNC-CH from 1967 until he retired in 1986. Falk was awarded the French Order of Palmes Academiques in 1979, an award given to French citizens and a handful of U.S. teachers honoring excellence in education. His book "Types of Thematic Structure" (1967) is one of those books that changed my life and revolutionized my thinking. He let me into his graduate classes when I was only an undergraduate. His superb, highly personal teaching, encouragement and mentoring took my critical thinking and writing skills to a different stratosphere. I will be forever grateful.

David Snyder's Publications

  • How to Mind Read Your Customers

    • American Management Association (AMACOM)
    • February 15, 2001
    Authors: David Snyder

    How to Mind Read Your Customers has been cited as a classic in its genre and one of the best books written on the art of consultative selling, focusing intensively on the art and science of the sales process, plus providing an in-depth analysis of the psychology involved in building better business relationships. Drawing upon research at Harvard and the study of hundreds of leaders and successful companies, the book provides a road map for success in every area of one's life that requires negotiation and relationship management, including one's personal and family life. It was listed first in best books of the year upon its release by Sales and Marketing Management Magazine and has been translated into multiple languages across the world.

  • How to Hire a Champion

    • Career Press
    • January 1, 2008
    Authors: David Snyder

    How to Hire a Champion methodically describes the character traits and attributes of high performing individuals across the spectrum of professional disciplines. Based upon extensive research the book shows why character traits such as integrity, perseverance and a desire to prove one's value are the hallmark traits of those who are able to sustain success in their career and in life over the long term. The book also describes in detail how employers can find, develop and retain high performing individuals in order to build stronger teams, accelerate growth, improve earnings and reach organizational goals faster.

  • How to Be a Workplace Champion

    • In Progress
    Authors: David Snyder

    Using a novel experiment in journalism drawn from social networking, excerpts from How to Be a Workplace Champion are being serialized on the web as the book is being written, with commentary and feedback being supplied by high performing individuals. Including insights from job seekers, star performers, CEOs, career coaching experts and more, How to Be A Workplace Champion is describing the skills that job seekers and employees need to display and develop in the careers in order to get the best jobs, find the most fulfillment, and leave a lasting positive impact in every area of their professional lives, and their lives as local and global citizens.

  • Contributing Editor

    • Online Weekly series One Rung At A Time
    • October 10, 2010
    Authors: David Snyder

    David's new book in progress How to Be a Workplace Champion is being serialized at Headway Workforce Solutions as an online weekly series. One Rung At A Time helps job seekers with essential advice as they strive to climb the proverbial "corporate ladder."
