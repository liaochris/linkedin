Amanda Clifford's Summary

The work I find myself enjoying most comes with applying my skills for Non-Profit Organizations to demonstrate their operations, promote their goals, and assist in raising funds for future projects.

I want to be able to create my own opportunities to achieve goals of traveling internationally and documenting controversial, ecological, and social events and happenings.

I greatly enjoy telling stories with personality, heart, and character. I revel in the chance to work collaboratively with talented directors to achieve a vision of a flushed out script with a careful eye and a practiced hand.

Specialties

non-profit media creation, socially conscious documentary, narrative film, interviews, travel photography, still photography, digital photography, HD cameras, film cameras, 35mm, independent film

Amanda Clifford's Experience

Documentary Filmmaker

Learn to Live Global

July 2012 – Present (1 year 5 months) North Sulawesi Province, Indonesia ; Vientiane, Laos ; New Orleans, USA

As part of a two person operation, I am not only a cinematographer, but also part time editor and writer. While in Indonesia, I filmed Learn to Live's operations in four villages in North Sulawesi.

These health clinics, the doctors and nurses that ran them, and the patients that benefited from their care are the subjects of the promotional materials that my partner and I are compiling into short documentary pieces for web distribution.

Our goal is to gain awareness for the organization through the stories we captured in our documentaries so as to promote financial support and volunteer action that might benefit Learn to Live's future expansion.

Working with Learn to Live is a human experience I hope to share through the footage that we captured in the remote village clinics. I was inspired daily by this crew of medical professionals doing everything they could to improve the health and well being of each patient they saw, taking time not only to bandage their wounds, but to ask about their living conditions, their families, their lives.
