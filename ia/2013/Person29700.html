Danielle Holke's Summary

Danielle is a marketing professional with experience in digital, content development and community management, and five years of experience managing and curating the knithacker.com community she founded. She's currently community manager for best-selling author Naomi Wolf, media ecology professor Mark Crispin Miller and award-winning film Picture Day.

Danielle has worked with a wide range of organizations within the entertainment and cultural industries including SONY BMG, CBS and the Toronto International Film Festival. While managing the digital department at SONY BMG, she oversaw both web and mobile marketing efforts for large franchise projects such as American Idol, and for artists such as P!nk, Outkast and Justin Timberlake. Her expertise includes digital strategy, product marketing, content creation, music supervision, contract negotiation, business development and partnership marketing.

SELECTED PROJECTS

PICTURE DAY – challenging conventions, I started building the film's social profile pre-production. As a result, we had enthusiasts well-established & were able to contribute to all 3 screenings at the Toronto Film Festival selling out. The film stars Tatiana Maslany & continues to gain public attention & win awards through word-of-mouth marketing initiatives.

NAOMI WOLF – columnist & author of best-selling non-fiction, I manage Naomi's large, active & often controversial community. Early on, I implemented community guidelines to guide the debates & I also streamlined her content creation process. In 2012, I filmed, edited & produced her web series, “Democracy 101”.

MARK CRISPIN MILLER – best-selling author & NYU media ecology professor, I manage Mark's popular curated news service News From Underground & all of its digital touch-points. The community is supported entirely through grassroots fundraising, subscription donations, advertising & affiliate programs, all of which I manage.

NYUFASP – Concerned with NYU's planned expansion plans in Greenwich Village, NYUFASP is a group of NYU faculty who are working to raise awareness of the planned development. I manage the organization's web & social media initiatives which include two web sites, two newsletter programs & several social media accounts. In May 2013, NYUFASP won a Grassroots Preservation Award from the Historic Districts Council & in June 2013 it received a Village Award from the prestigious Greenwich Village Society for Historic Preservation.

KNITHACKER.COM – I founded this popular site which features unusual fiber art. Stats: 40-60k visits/mo, 17k+ Twitter, 5k+ Facebook, and active fans on Ravelry, Flickr and Pinterest. Featured by CRAFT, MAKE, Etsy, Knit Today, Knit Now, Vogue Knitting, Loopy Mango.
