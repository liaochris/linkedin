W. Chad Cowart, RLA's Summary

Mr. Cowart provides leadership and efficiency to project teams through his cooperative approach to design and planning. His goal is to improve the delivery of designed work by balancing the relationship between efficiency, sustainability, and client satisfaction. His open-minded attitude and ability to listen well enables both his clients and colleagues to participate in the design process which results in higher-quality work.

Mr. Cowart has a wide spectrum of skills in master planning, urban regeneration, real estate development, and physical design. He adds value to projects by balancing idealistic philosophies of design and planning with buildable solutions that are financially viable, address market acceptability, and are constituent oriented.

His engagement with both public and private development clients, community members at-large, municipal staff, multi-disciplinary teams, and comprehensive project management across all of these groups has sharpened his skill as a navigator of the development process.

W. Chad Cowart, RLA's Projects

  • Dr Phillips Center for the Performing Arts Plaza

    • February 2013 to Present
    Team Members: W. Chad Cowart, RLA, Thomas D. Harrington, ASLA, Chuck Bell

    As the project landscape architect, I led the design development of the Arts Plaza from concept to tender documents. The plaza is a one acre urban space situated between the entry of downtown Orlando's new performing arts center and City Hall. The project included working with City leadership and Arts Center leaders to confirm a design vision, accommodate sophisticated special event programming, and balance the often competing interests of City leaders and Arts Center operators. Flexibility, uniqueness, practicality, and architectural cohesion were critical features of this design assignment.

  • ONE Prize 2012 Competition

    • June 2012 to June 2012
    Team Members: W. Chad Cowart, RLA, Chris Merritt

    The ONE Prize 2012 competition is powered by the idea that social, ecological, and economic struggles can simultaneously be addressed through collaborative action and innovative design. Situated in the context of a struggling U.S. economy and the tension of stagnant unemployment, ONE Prize 2012 is a call to put design in the service of the community, to reinvigorate deindustrialized and depressed urban areas, and to repurpose spaces for economic growth and job creation. It aims to explore the socially, economically, and ecologically regenerative possibilities of urban transformation and design.

  • Yas Island Zone K Residential Community

    • July 2010 to January 2011
    Team Members: W. Chad Cowart, RLA, John Pritchard, Greg Bryla, Giuseppe Colosimo, Thierry Paret, AIA NCARB MRIAI LEED AP BD+C, Thomas Cunniffe, Tim Brouw

    A 220 hectare horizontally mixed-use community designed to accommodate nearly 1,400 dwelling units for expatriates and Emiratis.

  • Boca Raton Urban Form Study

    • June 2009 to June 2009
    Team Members: W. Chad Cowart, RLA, Gregg Logan, Leah Chambers, Brandon Cappellari, RLA, GRP, LEED AP BD+C, John Paul (JP) Weesner, PLA

    The focus of this project for the City of Boca Raton was the evolution of the office "park" to the office "place" - as former suburban workplaces transition to 18-hour destinations that support broader experiences for today's increasing Gen Y and Z workforce.

  • Cocoa Waterfront Master Plan

    • March 2008 to November 2008
    Team Members: W. Chad Cowart, RLA, Leah Chambers, John Paul (JP) Weesner, PLA, Brandon Cappellari, RLA, GRP, LEED AP BD+C, Hunter McKibben

    An urban regeneration master plan for the City of Cocoa, Florida with a specific focus on the core area adjacent to the Indian River known as Cocoa Village. The plan was a community-inspired master plan which involved a considerable amount of citizen input and included a set of achievable goals and near-term capital projects aimed at re-invigorating access to the waterfront and to the urban core for both transient residents along the Intracoastal Waterway and full-time Cocoa residents.

  • Jekyll Island Revitalization / Village Center

    • January 2009 to April 2009
    Team Members: W. Chad Cowart, RLA, Greg Bryla, Michael Chatham, AIA, LEED AP, Charles Ezelle

    A great collaborative effort to plan, design and build an authentic small town character on one of the South's finest legacies: Jekyll Island. The first phase is this convention center designed by HHCP. As landscape architects, we integrated the beach experience into the public park, streetscape and convention center exterior spaces. Jekyll Island Authority is a great client pushing the limits of creative change while maintaining respect for Jekyll's natural and architectural legacies.

  • Port Orange Riverwalk Park Master Plan

    • May 2008 to February 2009
    Team Members: W. Chad Cowart, RLA, Greg Bryla, Leah Chambers, Brandon Cappellari, RLA, GRP, LEED AP BD+C

    The Port Orange Riverwalk Conceptual Design Package was commissioned by the City of Port Orange, Florida as a means to bridge the public interests of the City with the private motivations of a master developer seeking to plan a mixed-use town center on 35 waterfront acres along the Intracoastal Waterway. Glatting Jackson led a design team supported by Halcrow and Zev Cohen to establish a design character framework plan for the public portion of the City's riverfront park.
    During the process, the team facilitated a design dialogue between the master developer and the City. This dialogue led to improvements in the developer's master plan as well as strengthened connections between the community and the riverfront park. The design team successfully created a vision for the design character of the park and initiated an urban design backbone that would lead the overall development of the project.

  • Abu Dhabi Metro Station Planning

    • June 2010 to May 2011
    Team Members: W. Chad Cowart, RLA, Blake Drury

    AECOM and Parsons Brickerhoff (part of the ADAPT joint venture team) were commissioned by the Abu Dhabi Department of Transport to study the feasibility of a metro system for the City of Abu Dhabi in the United Arab Emirates in 2010 and 2011. The AECOM Planning, Design + Development team prepared a station area planning analysis for multiple metro routes and specific station location design standards for each station along the Initial Operating Segment (Phase One). The products included an analysis of the existing and potential future conditions, orientation and location of station entrances, public realm standards, and pedestrian accessibility for each station along the IOS.
    AECOM followed the station analysis and guidelines with a Transit Oriented Development white paper for the DoT. This white paper included inputs from PB, AECOM, and Jones Lang LaSalle real estate advisors. Mr. Cowart prepared the overall outline and detailed portion of the Placemaking component of the paper. He also converted the white paper to a work proposal for submittal to the DoT which was incorporated into the Stage 2 works of the joint venture contract.

  • Maitland Westside Master Redevelopment Plan

    • January 2010 to July 2010
    Team Members: W. Chad Cowart, RLA, Leah Chambers, Kody Smith, Blake Drury

    The City of Maitland commissioned Glatting Jackson/AECOM to prepare a redevelopment plan for the west side of the city generally known as Maitland Center - a regionally significant office cluster in the Orlando metropolitan area. AECOM facilitated a visioning and implementation strategy with the city, business owners, and residents of the district.

  • Kissimmee Redevelopment Studies

    • September 2011 to October 2011
    Team Members: W. Chad Cowart, RLA, Chris Merritt

    Real Estate Research Consultants (RERC) hired AECOM to study several city-owned or targeted purchases for redevelopment potential. Chad Cowart prepared multiple development iterations for each site to demonstrate program yield and design character. The studies were used by RERC to calculate potential tax revenues for the redeveloped property.
