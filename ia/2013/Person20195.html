Carl Vaudrin's Summary

Carl Vaudrin was born in Trois-Rivieres (Quebec,Canada) to a family of musicians who introduced him to music at a very young age. At nine years old, he enrolled in Les Petits Chanteurs de Trois-Rivieres. There he received theoretical and instrumental training in piano and singing, under the supervision of Professor Monsignor Claude Thompson.

Carl continued his piano training at the post-secondary level, in college and university. He perfected his skills in jazz piano with Francois Bourassa and Jeffrey Johnston while continuing to study classical piano, improvisation and arrangement. Upon completion of his bachelor in 2001, Carl was asked to join The Showmen orchestra (a Montreal-based group). Carl also performed in several public concerts, including the Jazz Festival of Montreal in 2004, with the group "Cohiba Project" and "Projet Bleu Ciel". Apart from his role as a keyboardist, Carl also worked as a producer, arranger, sound engineer and, increasingly, as a composer. For example, he collaborated on projects such as the album “Prophetie” by Empreinte Galactique (2004) or the album “Immortels” by Longue Distance (2007). As a musical director, Carl performed in Spain and Portugal with the famous equestrian show Cavalia (2008). He recently recorded piano on Fred Pellerin's latest album "C'est un monde" (Folk album of the year @ ADISQ 2012).

Carl attended and completed UCLA's Film Scoring Program (2007-2008). He recently worked as music assistant for award-winning composers Lennie Moore (Watchmen Comic Motion Picture episodes 3-5) and Jesper Kyd (Assasin's Creed II, The Resistance series). He's currently working as composer on Hollywood Wasteland, a web-series that has already received remarkable reviews and awards such as "Outstanding Score in a Comedy Serie" @ the 2011 LA Web Series Festival.

Specialties: Music for multimedia - Electronic and cinematic oriented music - Musical sound design - Jazz piano
