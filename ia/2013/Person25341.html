Clarence Bos' Summary

Creativity, structure, perseverance and a regular schedule can help solve many problems. The largest problem we face today is a sustainable earth. Smaller parts of that have to do with how much energy, food, water and land we use to support ourselves. The flow of information through the internet can help enhance our sustainability or detract from it. WordPress, a key open source platform, allows for a variety of solutions through its use of freely available MySQL, PHP, Javascript and jQuery. Thinking of how we can use these means to be wise stewards of the earth, to inform, equip, educate and encourage each other can help provide unique means to the end.

To that end, a variety of solutions have been developed, some of which are presented below. One means to reducing the time spent with a problem is to simplify the code to its bare essentials, to direct efforts to a specific audience, to refrain from global broadcasting, and to adapt to a specific environment. To that end a flat file blog cms was developed (http://cms.cbos.ca) which uses three main files plus css to construct a bare bones, lightweight and "snappy" cms that renders content quickly even with a shared server.

In addition is a site (http://tech.cbos.ca/wp) which demonstrates some of the capabilities of WordPress when it is combined with Javascript, jQuery and Google Maps. Various countries can be selected on the Footprint tab and the ecological footprint viewed. A Solar tab demonstrates how the horsepower of PHP, a database and the interactivity of a javacript powered map can allow for the calculation of energy produced by solar power in various parts of the continental U.S. and southern Canada.

As we all need a place to live and many desire space and room of their own, The Sustainable Hectare (http://thesustainablehectare.cbos.ca) explores how a single hectare of arable land could be used by a family to live sustainably.
