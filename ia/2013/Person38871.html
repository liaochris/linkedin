David Van Steenkiste's Projects

  • Eastman Kodak Back-Printer Development & OEM Partnership

    • 1997 to 2000
    Team Members: David Van Steenkiste, Dave Kidder, Hans Meidel, Roy Heflin, Carl Rosati, Paul McGovern, Various Resources at Kodak

    Kodak was in the process of developing a new high speed digital photo processing machine for the high volume wholesale photo labs. They required some type of printer be mounted inside this machine that could print product information onto the the backside of the photos. The requirements were to be very low maintenance and very low operator intervention required due to the light sensitivity of the photo paper. Additionally, consumable costs needed to be reduced as well as printer hardware costs.
    Previously Kodak had used Continuous Ink Jet (CIJ) technology for similar applications, but were dissatisfied with the amount of service and downtime and cost that CIJ technology offered at that time.
    We sort of stumbled onto this application due to the failure of a competitor to execute with a new piezo printhead technology. Fas-Co had not yet tested this new printhead technology (the Micro-Coder, by Trident), when one of the Trident sales people brought us this potential opportunity. Being a small company we were able to react quickly. I quickly organized a project team and within two weeks our electronics and software team was able to customize some prototype electronics (lots of cost and jumpers), and I was able to design the ink delivery system, and adapt the mechanical parts to be able to execute a successful demonstration for Kodak. Successful enough to move to the next step (good thing they did not look under the hood at that time).
    Consequently, we worked with Kodak in the coming months to refine our product and to fully integrate with theirs. The result was the sale of several hundred printers and good spare parts an consumable business for a few years. At least until that part of their business became obsolete with the prevalence of digital cameras and affordable home photo printing.
    I led this project from beginning to phase out as the OEM Account Manager and technical liason.
