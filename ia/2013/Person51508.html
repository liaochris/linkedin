Hannah Elisha's Summary

Hannah Elisha’s practice areas include general civil litigation, environmental law, toxic tort, government and municipality defense, public record access, business litigation, and insurance defense.

Before joining Michel & Associates, P.C., Ms. Elisha cultivated experience in both the public and private sectors as a legal intern for the California Coastal Commission and law clerk for SmithTrager, LLP.

Ms. Elisha earned her Bachelor of Arts in Politics from Whitman College and graduated cum laude from Chapman University School of Law. During her tenure at Chapman, Ms. Elisha served as Editor-in-Chief of the Chapman Law Review and Vice President of the Business and Investment Law Society and was an awarded member of the Appellate Moot Court Board.

With half a decade of experience in the business sphere, Ms. Elisha also holds a Master of Business Administration from Chapman University Argyos School of Business and Economics and possesses well-developed business acumen which she applies in her practice.

Hannah Elisha's Projects

  • Law Review Article: Addressing the E-Waste Crisis: The Need for Comprehensive Federal E-Waste Regulation within the United States

    • September 2010 to October 2010
    Team Members: Hannah Elisha

    This comment links the social disparity of marginalized populations around the world in places such as China, India, and Africa, to the unethical and unregulated export of electronic waste (e-waste) from the United States and other developed countries. Outlining the health and environmental dangers posed by the e-waste stream, this article highlights the failure of the United States to implement effective e-waste policy and advocates for the implementation of a comprehensive federal regulatory framework in order to prevent the continued export of hazardous waste to developing nations.
