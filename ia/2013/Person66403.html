John L. Goodrum, Jr.'s Experience

Project Management Office Supervisor and Public Works Production Officer

United States Naval Facilities Engineering Command Southeast

August 2006 – September 2009 (3 years 2 months)

Directed 27 projects totaling $394M, including 822,299-square foot demolition (asbestos and chemical removal/abatement), 126,241-square foot renovation, and 741,562-square foot new construction project. Supervised and collaborated with Assistant Project Manager Level 2, Assistant Project Manager Level 3, and 100+ contractors from 5 companies involved in projects. Organized activities of engineering and planning staff, shops, resident office in charge of construction, and Facilities Management and Engineering Departments to ensure fulfillment of requirements. Conveyed updates regularly to Public Works Officer, Deputy Public Works Officer, Installation Commanding Officer, Installation Executive Officer, and other Public Works personnel.

Gathered, collated, and presented information for Public Works Office and for presentation to upper management by using MS Office Suite, Primavera SureTrak, Project Manager, AutoCAD, and Adobe Acrobat applications; resolved problems regarding phasing of demolitions, new construction, and client movement. Drove support contracts, oversaw project management office employees, partnered with regional comptroller to ensure funding for repairs, and monitored and planned expenditures to achieve budget goals for fiscal year objectives. Orchestrated and scheduled installation of data systems and telephones with construction contractor to promote timeliness as well as minimize time between government occupancy of new buildings and availability of building support systems for clients. Liaised with ROICC and Public Works Department via meetings and correspondences; managed weekly status reports. Anticipated and avoided or resolved issues concerning various project phases by maintaining SureTrak scheduling software and spreadsheets, along with creating flowchart demonstrating interrelationships with swing space moves, new building move-ins, affects on other projects/customers, NMCI moves, telephone moves, and other logistics issues.

Nuclear Specialist, Engineering Support Department—Nuclear Safety and Compliance

Southern Nuclear Operating Company

July 1992 – June 2004 (12 years)

Held security clearance while involved in safety implementation, procedural creation/revision, and software development and management. Maintained working knowledge of plant administrative, nuclear management, Operations Department, and reactor engineering procedures as well as Nuclear Regulatory Commission requirements for plant licensing and Codes of Federal Regulations (CFRs) applicable to commercial nuclear power plants, including 10CFR Appendix B, ANSI, EPA, and OSHA standards.

Developed and maintained Legacy Condition Reporting System; planned, developed, and provided guidance regarding Electronic Condition Reporting System. Utilized various applications, including MS Office Suite, Crystal Reports, Lotus 123, SQL, SyncPowr, and Nuclear Plant Management Information System/NUCLEIS. Tracked and monitored trends of events, occurrences, and issues reported via Legacy Condition Reporting System and from other programs, such as plant performance indicators, operating experience, and safety and health reports; analyzed, interpreted, and prepared VEGP quarterly trend reports and distributed reports to plant management, interested parties, and VEGP home page.

Honed expertise in root cause determination and functioned as instructor for root cause and corrective action qualification. Acted as Event Review Team Advisor; authored lessons, developed measurement standard, and instructed courses for VEGP Training Department to qualify plant personnel to perform root cause determinations and design corrective actions. Also served as Corrective Action Program Tracking and Trending Coordinator, Advisor to Daily Corrective Action Program Coordinator, Human Performance Coordinator,
Functioned as Reactor Engineer for approximately 5 reactor refueling outages; tracked reactor fuel bundle shuffles, computed 1/m plots needed to predict initial criticality of reactor during reactor start-up, and operated and documented readings of in-core instrumentation.
