Game Designer / Creative Director

Freelance

June 2009 – Present (4 years 7 months) New York, NY

My own freelance practice has involved collaboration with numerous clients on platforms spanning web, mobile, social networks, board games, and innovative new devices. Services I've provided include concept development of new games, game design documentation and leadership, creative direction of the development process including visual design and strategic direction, review and recommendations for games in development (including "game doctoring" to fix problems), design of game economies, vendor selection and management, development and writing of game content and characters, workshops on game design, and coaching of new or inexperienced game designers. Clients and properties I've worked with include Conde Nast, AdCouncil, Lifetime Television, AMC Television, Ogmento, Large Animal Games, Blue Fang Games, Powerhead Games, Arkadium, Nike, Hide & Seek, Atari, Curious Brain, Major League Baseball, Dartmouth College and Rutgers University among others.

Naomi Clark's Patents

  • Method and system for manipulating a digital representation of a three-dimensional object

    • United States Patent 7,755,620
    • Issued July 13, 2010
    Inventors: Naomi Clark, Ronny Scherer, Olga Timcenko, Peter Acker

    A method of manipulating a three-dimensional virtual building block model by means of two-dimensional cursor movements, the virtual building block model including a plurality of virtual building blocks each including a number of connection elements for connecting the virtual building block with another virtual building block according to a set of connection rules, the method comprising positioning by means of cursor movements in a computer display area representing a two-dimensional projection of said model, a two-dimensional projection of a first virtual building block to be connected to the structure, resulting in a two-dimensional position; determining, from the two-dimensional position, a number of three-dimensional candidate positions of the first virtual building block in the three-dimensional coordinate system; selecting one of said candidate positions based on the connection rules and a predetermined set of position rating rules; and connecting the first building block to said structure at the selected candidate position.
