Julie Blais Comeau's Experience

Business Etiquette & International Protocol consultant

Julie Blais Comeau

December 1999 – Present (13 years 9 months) Canada

Julie Blais Comeau is the real deal. A self proclaimed 'gauche girl turned gaffe-proof teacher', she is living proof of the power of etiquette. With etiquette knowledge, she built the career of her dreams.

With more than 5000 now confident participants, 500 engaging presentations, 50 satisfied clients and weekly media contributions, Julie Blais Comeau is Canada’s Leading Etiquette Expert.

She is a professional speaker, a training and development consultant, as well as an author. She has completed her first book Etiquette: Confidence and Credibility * You at your best and is now on a quest for a publisher.

With over a decade of HR and operations management positions, she knows what it takes to exceed expectations. Ms. Blais Comeau is certified by the Protocol School of Washington and as a National Tourism Trainer. She has contributed to the employability of Internationally Trained Individuals, Youth and Aboriginal community members.

Her professional accolades include the RÉFAP’s 2011 Self-Employed Business Woman of the Year.

A dynamic media contributor, Julie has regular segments on radio and television. She has been an expert guest on Entertainment Tonight Canada, CTV Morning Live, CBC, Radio-Canada and TVA. She is Huffington Post Canada’s Sticky Situations blogger and has been quoted in numerous publications such as The Globe and Mail and Reader’s Digest.

In private sessions or during national conferences, Julie facilitates the interpersonal skills for your professional success. Whether you are a graduate in quest of a promotion, an astronaut attending an awards banquet or a CEO travelling across the globe, Julie will empower you to achieve confidence, credibility and coherence.

Julie teaches 'Savoir-faire without pinkies or noses up in the air'; with a fresh contemporary approach. One thing is for sure, you will get the job or the promotion but more importantly you will not embarrass yourself or your employer.

Her mission is simple: You, at your best.
