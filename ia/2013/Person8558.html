Ann Quasman's Summary

My mission: To start a conversation each day that moves even one woman from talking to doing.

In realizing this effort, I've spent the past years developing venues centered around encouraging and facilitating a more conscious conversation about issues that matter to women and, ultimately, giving women a bigger and ever-expanding voice in the world - all in service of making lasting change.

As creator and host of WomanTalk Live radio and of the Conscious Conversations Café, I'm focused on getting to the heart of a topic and, even more, to the heart of women. I'm thrilled to say that this work has opened the door to many opportunities to emcee, give keynote presentations, contribute to panel discussions, write, serve on the board of amazing organizations and become a valued strategic partner to many businesses and organizations.

From September 2007 through 2013, I also presented the Girls Gone Great Scholarship Essay Contest, a program I founded to showcase young women who are juniors and seniors in high school in the state of Maryland, who have already demonstrated their passion and purpose by putting a unique vision to work locally, nationally or internationally. Girls Gone Great will be on hiatus in 2014 with plans to come back in 2015 with an extended focus.

I believe that what women have to say is not only important, but quite literally world-changing. With each conscious conversation, we shift more than we might imagine.

Ann Quasman's Experience

Host

WomanTalk Live Radio

Privately Held; Myself Only; Broadcast Media industry

February 2007 – Present (6 years 11 months)

I host a weekly radio show, each Saturday evening from 6-7 pm ET on Talkradio 680 WCBM Baltimore or online at http://tinyurl.com/listenlivewcbm

Since the show’s inception in 2007, I've interviewed hundreds of local, national and international celebrated authors, experts, social leaders, business leaders, entrepreneurs and more. Always, the conversation dives deep into issues and topics that matter to women and the men who love them.

WomanTalk Live stays active in the community by sponsoring women-focused events, sharing insights in the media and, also, by creating top-notch original programs just for women.

The Girls Gone Great Scholarship Essay Contest and Conscious Conversations Café radio are just two ways WomanTalk Live supports, showcases and engages women of all ages.

No matter what the program or event, WomanTalk Live has a single focus: To give women everywhere a bigger voice and a platform for growing, celebrating and living more fully.
