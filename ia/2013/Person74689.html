Kesav Mohan's Summary

My governing principles: Improve human welfare. Live adventurously. Work smarter, remain open-minded, and seek constant improvement.

I’m pursuing a course of work that involves operations, business development, and in-house legal work. My focus is on venture-backed start-ups in San Francisco or the Bay Area. I want to make a difference through education, e-commerce, or select consumer technologies.

Pursuing life with an adventure-seeking style has blessed me with opportunities. Over the years, I’ve held a variety of roles. I'm often brought into organizations as a “utility player” – someone who is given problems to solve, where information is incomplete or unavailable. I enjoy tackling these challenges.

Ultimately, my jobs involve improving efficiency and productivity. Even when I was a M & A and PE & VC attorney, I wanted to help companies effectively utilize their resources.

After studying global justice at Duke University and international relations at Dublin City University, I spent a year as a social entrepreneurship consultant for NGOs around the world.

My passion for start-ups originated in 2006. That year, while living in Dubai, I started my own company: Howtobewebsmart.com L.L.C. The company has had successes and failures. I use the entity to test out new ideas and make small investments.

Even when my career led me to work for more established companies, I found ways to engage with the start-up world. The energy and passion that is encapsulated in a start-up environment is contagious and hard to let go.

I’m jumping back into the start-up world. San Francisco, here I am.

I like helping people with these hidden gems: e-mail productivity, speed-reading, how to use virtual assistants, and traveling the world.

Contact me at: kesavm@gmail.com.

Kesav Mohan's Experience

CEO

Howtobewebsmart.com L.L.C.

June 2006 – Present (7 years 8 months)

In 2006, while living in Dubai, I founded Howtobewebsmart.com L.L.C. (“Howtobewebsmart"). During my tenure in the Middle East, hiring coders abroad became key to growing the business. Through outsourcing, Howtobewebsmart developed Firefox and Chrome extensions and stand-alone websites to help consumers save money when shopping online.

Over time, Howtobewebsmart has been a vehicle to try different business ideas. The company is currently a small investment entity and a consulting company.

Consulting activities involve:

1. Business Development – helping customers pitch prospects and close sales.

2. Operations – helping clients structure internal processes (creating efficient sales processes, e-mail productivity, efficient contractual development, etc.)

3. Outside Counsel – helping companies find outside counsel for their particular legal work. I also help negotiate billing arrangements with prospective outside counsel.

Lately, I have been reviewing a substantial number of existing billing arrangements and invoices to make sure companies are being fairly charged by outside counsel.

4. Tax Credits and Incentives – helping companies find tax credits and incentives (primarily in California).

5. Virtual Assistants – helping companies get up and running with virtual assistants.

EVP and Legal Adviser

BizLab

Public Company; 51-200 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry

November 2011 – July 2013 (1 year 9 months) Menomonee Falls, WI

BizLab, Inc. (“BizLab”) is an operating company that had four portfolio companies at the time I was hired: Rocket Clicks, Sterling Satellite, Be Your Own IT, and Godsell & Hughes. As Executive Vice President and Legal Adviser, my responsibilities were varied.

Initially, BizLab had $3 million and needed to diversify. I visited Israel to look at start-ups for investment. I saw about 100 companies total during my time in Tel Aviv.

However, my job at BizLab changed quickly. I spent ten months managing two sell-side Mergers and Acquisitions (“M & A”) transactions (one large deal and one small deal). In addition to the M & A work, I also did the following for BizLab and its four operating companies: managed the legal work of all the companies (200+ employees); handled the real estate work, tightened sales processes, improved operational effectiveness, managed the public affairs of the companies, as well as negotiated and managed a Department of Labor audit for our call center of about 120 team members.

Two of our portfolio companies, Rocket Clicks and Sterling Satellite, were heavily involved in Internet marketing. Through these companies, I built a network in the pay-per-click (“PPC”) and conversion rate optimization (“CRO”) arenas.

At BizLab, I worked with a stellar team, including: Jeff Hughes, Robert Pakulski, Jodi Brenner, Rob Sieracki, Steve Kroll, Brandon Bailey, Sarah Greco, Angela Dent, Benji Rabhan and many others.

Foley & Lardner LLP

Privately Held; 1001-5000 employees; Legal Services industry

August 2010 – November 2011 (1 year 4 months) Milwaukee, WI

Foley & Lardner LLP (“Foley”) hired me for its T & S and PE & VC departments. I worked on multiple M & A and VC transactions doing first and second year associate work.

At Foley, I developed three niches:

First, I helped start-ups get the legal advice they needed when they needed it (i.e., at the beginning). In the legal world, vaccines are better than cures. As someone who had just come from the start-up world, I could empathize with the fears start-ups held re: lawyers and legal bills.

Second, I helped start-ups raise money. This included helping review executive summaries and business plans, and connecting start-ups with investors. I helped Foley create an internal database called MatchMaker to put start-ups in touch with firm clients that were looking to deploy capital.

Finally, I taught companies how to manage their legal bills.

Kesav Mohan's Volunteer Experience & Causes

  • Volunteer Experience

    • Volunteer

      First Aid Training Programs in Prisons/Resource Development (Capetown, South Africa)
      • Social Services

      I helped create a first-aid training program in Pollsmoor Prison, located outside Capetown, South Africa. This was one of the most impactful experiences of my life. South African prisons have some of the worst violence in the world. Our core thesis was that if you taught prisoners how to perform first aid, they would value life more.

      The prisoners had next to nothing. It was in South Africa that I honed by resource development skills. I made close to 50 phone calls a day to various organizations, and helped raise a substantial amount of food, clothing, books, and other supplies for the prisoners.

      I ended up single handedly doubling the phone bill of the non-profit I worked at (the Amy Biehl Foundation, at the time about 20 employees). The resources we raised were worth the additional cost.

    • Volunteer

      Orphan Magic Project

      As a sophomore in college, I volunteered at an orphanage in Santa Cruz, Bolivia. Due to lack of financial resources, most orphans were told to leave the orphanage when they turned seventeen. Odds were, the departed orphans would get addicted to glue and die by the time they were 19.

      This work was the most emotionally taxing experience in my life. It was the first time without my social safety net. It opened my eyes to the challenges facing orphanages with limited resources.

      Upon my return to college, I created the Orphan Magic Project, a very small e-book full of “magic tricks” that orphanages could perform and teach to orphans with available resources (i.e. spoons, forks, knives). This e-book was distributed to 3,000+ orphanages worldwide.
