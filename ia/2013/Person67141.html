John Carlisle's Projects

  • Professional Door Dealer State-of-the-Industry Report

    • October 2011 to January 2012
    Team Members: John Carlisle, Amy Watson

    This state-of-the-industry report examines garage door and access-control industry trends from the supply and demand sides. Supply-side factors include cost of raw materials, supplier consolidations, labor market, safety regulations, transportation costs, and taxes and government regulation. On the demand side, factors include housing and economic recovery, energy efficiency, and technological advances.

    The report shares costs of production and providing service, including steel, fuel and labor prices. It discusses economic and housing-market recovery projections with regional input. Finally, it highlights changes in technology, safety, and government regulation as they affect demand.

  • From Coal to Coalition: Catalyzing partnerships to revitalize the Pilsen Industrial Corridor and the neighborhood’s livability - UPP 506 Studio Project, MUPP program, University of Illinois at Chicago

    • January 2013 to May 2013
    Team Members: John Carlisle, Jenny Kane, Lynn Reidl, Jake Rueter, Ben Gembler, cecile carroll, Yan Yin

    This process was a 16-week endeavor by 25 master's candidates in the urban planning and policy program at the University of Illinois at Chicago. This planning workshop course encourages candidates to research, debate, critically think, plan, design and present. ﻿​

    The project theme was "big ideas." As a group, we decided to focus on the spatial context of Pilsen's Industrial Corridor and more specifically to the closed Fisk Coal Plant facility. We understood that it will take years of coalition building and continued community engagement to create the change needed to have a healthy and vibrant area.

    The story of the Fisk Generating Station is not unique to Pilsen. A short distance away in Little Village, the Crawford Generating Station is being deactivated. Across the region, former industrial and commercial facilities have left expansive vacancies in their wake. Successful revitalization of this site could serve as a model, even a catalyst for similar efforts throughout the city and beyond.

    Team members took on roles as researchers, project manager, writers, designers, policy creators and evaluators, and presenters. I performed research, project management, writing, policy creation, and presentation.​
