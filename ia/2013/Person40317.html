E'Layne Koenigsberg's Experience

Artist - Business Owner

Art by E'Layne - 3 Hip Chics

2000 – Present (13 years) Tallahassee, Florida Area

I began my business art business, Gypsy In Me, over two decades ago on Maui, Hawaii, selling hand-painted clothing and designing jewelry. In addition to traveling the art show circuit for over twenty years, in 1996, I co-founded an art gallery called Wild Women Gallery. Currently I am passionately creating mixed media collage incorporating my photography and am loving writing articles for the amazing magazines published by Stamington. My next goal is to sign with a licensing company so I can concentrate my full attention to making art...not producing art...and teaching as well as coaching artist on their journey. 3 Hip Chics, the production wing of Art by E'Layne, works with over 130 gift store, catalogs and galleries.

Director of Operations

Railroad Square Art Park

Commercial Real Estate industry

1999 – Present (14 years) Tallahassee, Florida Area

Manages one of the most vibrant artist parks in the U.S. Position includes leasing 80 business/art spaces; bookkeeping and collection of rent; resolving tenant disputes; creating plans for future development in the park; conducting business meetings and organizing community events.

Crisis Counseling Consultant

State of Florida, DCF

1994 – 2010 (16 years) Tallahassee, Florida

Crisis Counseling Consultant - State of Florida, Department of Children and Families (DCF) Office of Mental Health - (1994 - 2010). Contracted to write a Mental Health and Substance Abuse Disaster Plan for the State; contracted to write Immediate and Regular Services Grant’s for over ten disasters; trained and dispatched a cadre of first responders; assisted in hiring and training management team at DCF central office; conducted conference calls with program supervisors and provider agencies to provide technical assistance; set up a statewide 800# for assisting survivors; conducted weekly conference calls with Program Managers and Community Liaisons to provide guidance and insure program compliance. Presently contracts with states when disaster strikes to train cadre of crisis counselors to provide services to disaster survivors.
