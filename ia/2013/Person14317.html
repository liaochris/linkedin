Bill Harding's Summary

With over Ten years experience, we are Fully trained and capable of servicing your pool and spa, including opening, closing, service and maintenance. We do expert weekly, bi-monthly, and monthly maintenance. We specialize in equipment repairs, replacements and upgrades, leak detection and repair, Loop-Loc Safety Cover sales and installation, BioGuard chemical sales and testing for your pool and spa (We have a computerized water testing station complete with a lazer printer right on our service vehicle), HelioCol Solar pool and spa heaters as well as gas heater and heat pump installation. We are a coveted Platinum Level Hayward Warranty Repair Center, and are warranty repair centers for Hayward, Raypak, Tigershark, Endless Pools, Master Spa Authorized Warranty repair site, and more...
We are members of The Association of Pool and Spa Professionals and The National Plasterers council and we are Certified Pool Start up Technicians. We perform pool inspections for real estate buyers and sellers and provide expert legal testimony. We complete underwater repairs including vinyl liner repairs, plaster and rebar repairs and leak detection as well as repair the problem when found. We can cut concrete and provide slab-jacking services when your concrete deck slides.

Specialties

Our specialties include mobile computerized water testing for your pool and spa, leak detection and repair on vinyl, plaster and fiberglass pools, and also equipment pad replacements and updates. We can perform underwater grinding to smooth the roughness on your pool finish, Acid wash the pool if neccesary, we are water chemistry experts with state of the art computerized water testing labs in our retail location as well as our service vehicle. We complete tile and coping repairs.

Bill Harding's Experience

President

The Pool Team of Carroll County, Maryland Inc.

April 2002 – Present (11 years 9 months)

After returning from the Republic of Panama in Central America in 2001 where I supervised as many as 55 men during the rebuilding of the Panama Canal Railroad (see Field Coordinator below), I started The Pool Team of Carroll County, Maryland, Inc.

With just one service vehicle, the company has grown to four full-time employees, a retail store and a website. We have grown our warehouse to 1,675 square feet that remains fully stocked throughout the year. We also maintain a full staff all year long as well. Our expertise includes servicing and repairing about 1,000 pools per year.

Assistant Project Administrator

FatStone Development, Panama

June 2001 – January 2002 (8 months)

Coordinator for 100+ field workers to construct an underground fiber optic duct complete with manholes across the isthmus of Panama and alongside the Panama Canal Railroad. I was instrumental in providing enforcement of safety regulations and training of safe railroading practices to contractors, and prevention of incidents for Railroad safety. I was the team leader to locate, excavate and protect over 300 underground utilities during the fiber optic project and the quality control of the various components of the communications infrastructure.

Utilities/Track Foreman

Neosho Central America, Panama

January 2000 – June 2001 (1 year 6 months)

I was hired as a Track Foreman to rebuild the Panama Canal Railway. After the first 3 months of the construction I was asked to be the Utilities Foreman primarily because the contractor was beginning to build the track bed and the existing utilities were being damaged. I remained as Utilities Foreman until the track construction was completed.

Field Coordinator

BridgeFarmer engineering, Panama

June 1998 – January 2000 (1 year 8 months)

I was responsible for managing 7 highly experienced and capable railroad workers. We maintained 45 miles of mostly jungle railroad. Our rolling stock consisted of 2 GP-30 Locomotives, over 20 boxcar, flatcar, coaches, and a 44,000# x 56' diameter weedwacker including a bonafide roundhouse, more than 20 additional outbuildings and a railroading history that many would envy! The experience of a lifetime.

Phone Coordinator

National Head Injury Foundation

July 1994 – July 1995 (1 year 1 month)

My responsibilities included providing information and referral to professionals, families, and persons with Traumatic Brain Injury(TBI) information and resources to address financial and personal issues involved with brain injury. The Foundation's action agenda emphasizes prevention, public awareness, public policy, public attitudes, social-political action, professional growth, information/referral, guidance on research needs, and a forum for diverse views.

Aviation Submarine Warfare Technician

United States Navy

Government Agency; 10,001+ employees; Military industry

March 1981 – August 1988 (7 years 6 months)

I was honored to be a member of The United States Navy and I am proud to call myself a Veteran. While in the Navy, I served in VF-142, F-14 Tomcats aboard the U.S.S. Eisenhower, CVN-69 and at NAS Oceana, Viginia Beach, Virginia. I worked on IKE's flight deck as a plane captain and then became an Aviation Electronics Technician, 3rd. class and transferred to AIMD (Aircraft Intermediate Maintenance Department). I cross rated to an Aviation Anti-Submarine Warfare Technician, reenlisted and transferred to AIMD-Nas Jacksonville, Fla. Where I was discharged and started college at UMBC for studies in Psychology and Social Work.
