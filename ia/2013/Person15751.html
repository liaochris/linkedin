Drew Dallet's Summary

Boom Creative is a design & marketing firm located just outside of Cleveland, Ohio. We have been actively involved in the graphic design community for 25 years, with an extensive range of experience. Our design spans the country, as well as having built relationships with local design firms, ad agencies, and area businesses, with a concentration on brand identity, print marketing, and web site design.

Staying active in the design community is a driving factor in the success of any design firm. Boom Creative volunteers any way we can. For four years our President, Drew Dallet, was the treasurer of AIGA Cleveland (American Institute of Graphic Arts), an organization whose purpose is to further excellence in communication design as a broadly defined discipline, strategic tool for business and cultural force.

Boom Creative's identity, print marketing, and web site designs have been awarded recognition from the Canton, Akron, and Cleveland Addy Awards, in addition to being seen in Graphis and Rockport Publishers books. Neenah Papers has also awarded us for excellence in identity and stationary design. In May 2005 AIGA Cleveland recognized Boom Creative for excellence in the Cleveland design community at the AIGA:52 Awards Exhibition.

Specialties: Our design spans the country, as well as having built relationships with local design firms, ad agencies, and area businesses, with a concentration on brand identity, print marketing, and web site design.

Drew Dallet's Experience

President, Creative Director

2002 – Present (11 years) Twinsburg, Ohio

Boom Creative is a unique marketing and graphic design firm located just outside of Cleveland, Ohio. We are professional graphic designers who focus on building brand identity, developing marketing materials, and web site design. Our goal is to capture your company or organization's mission, vision and identity, and express them in ways that resonate with your clientele.

Senior Designer

Karen Skunta & Company

January 2000 – February 2002 (2 years 2 months) Cleveland, Ohio

I began as a senior designer with Karen Skunta & Company in early 2000 -- one of the few design firms in Cleveland that focuses not only on its clients, but its people. In business for over 25 years, Karen Skunta & Company focuses on corporate identity, corporate collateral, package design, multimedia & web design. The one thing that separates KSCO form all the rest is their commitment to their people. It's here that after 12 years in the business I've really began to experience the true team atmosphere.

Art Director

Interlink New Media

August 1996 – December 1999 (3 years 5 months) Akron, Ohio

I was an Art Director with Interlink, at the time a startup web design firm, and became a complete design and marketing solutions firm. With clients reaching from Cypress, CA to Niagara Falls, NY, we have positioned ourselves as the total solution for our clients. We handle our clients web, TV, radio, print and multimedia. Within our first three years, while countless other "web firms" were folding, we continue to grow. Whereas we were four people in 1996, now we surround ourselves with over 25 employees that make Interlink an even stronger team. Our clients range from Toyo Tire Corp. BFGoodrich Aerospace, Davey Tree, The Pro Football Hall of Fame and Norton Abrasives. As Art Director, I've accepted to position of not only concepting and executing projects for my clients, but overseeing a design staff including junior and multimedia designers, making sure that the quality of work is up to Interlink's standards.

Graphic Designer

Becker Design

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Graphic Design industry

April 1995 – August 1996 (1 year 5 months) Milwaukee, Wisconsin

I was brought in by Becker Design as their first full-time designer. After less than four years in business, Becker has positioned themselves as a driving force in Milwaukee's design community. Our clientele included national and international companies such as Gatorade, Kohler, Healthy Choice and Rite-Hite Corporation as well as local businesses, including the Wisconsin Power Company (a merger of Wisconsin Electric and Gas). Within the year and a half I was with Becker Design, our work had been recognized by Graphis (Design 95), Rockport Publishers (Best of Business Cards 2, Letterhead & Logo Design 4 and Designer Posters), Print Magazine, French Paper and Neenah Paper. My time with Becker Design has not only strengthened my visual and creative skills, but it has strengthened my ability to work directly with clients and vendors.

Graphic Designer

Structure - A Division of The Limited

April 1994 – April 1995 (1 year 1 month) Columbus, Ohio

When Structure, a division of The Limited, Inc. and leading retail men's clothing chain, began building its first "complete" in-house design/marketing team, I was selected to oversee all prepress production, as well as lend my creative ability to Structure's in-store marketing. My design responsibilities included designing credit marketing collateral, equity signage and in-house marketing materials, as well as handling Structure's entire point-of-sale sign system. In addition, our entire marketing team was recognized as charter members of Structure's first mail order catalog design team.

Graphic Designer

Burrows Bonds Communications

August 1993 – April 1994 (9 months) Aspen, Colorado

Upon my arrival in Aspen, Colorado, I joined the design team of two longtime partners, Art Burrows and Michael Bonds. Hired for my design sense, as well as extensive knowledge of Macintosh operations, I was able to immediately put my design skills to work. Skiing, Aspen's most well known pastime, provided the market for most of our clientele. Jobs ranged from designing ski graphics and mountaintop signage to full color clothing catalogs and microbrewery labels and packaging. BBC's client list not only spanned the local market, but reached as far as Canada and Northern Europe.
