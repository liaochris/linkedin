Bree Normandin's Summary

I am a freelance writer based in Bat Cave, North Carolina. I have written for a variety of online publications, community newspapers, and businesses. My topic range includes articles and blogs about business, finances, current affairs, politics, social affairs, and profiles on businesses and people.

I enjoy writing for digital publications. I've worked with several digital marketing agencies on a range of projects including online copywriting assignments and social media campaigns. I've also helped businesses with writing handbooks, training manuals, and accessing grants.

Public Housing Case Manager

Homeward Bound of Asheville, NC

July 2010 – August 2011 (1 year 2 months) Asheville, North Carolina Area

I enjoyed working with Homeward Bound of Asheville. My clients were homeless men, women, and children. Many of these people faced issues with mental health, domestic violence, and substance abuse. It was my job to assist my clients in obtaining and maintaining housing. The task wasn't easy. I cheered with my clients during their successes and worked hard to help them get back up when they fell down. I connected my clients with community based services and helped them access services such as SSI, Food Stamps, Medicaid, Counseling, Medications, and more. I loved working here and wish I could have transported the organization with me when I moved. The staff is wonderful and the clients taught me so much!

Case Manager for Women's Division

Hendersonville Rescue Mission

October 2007 – September 2009 (2 years) Hendersonville, NC

Working at the Hendersonville Rescue Mission was a learning experience for me. I was first introduced to the crisis of homelessness at this nonprofit agency. I enjoyed working as a case manager for the women and children who stayed here. I assisted women who were affected by homelessness, domestic violence, substance abuse issues, and mental illness. I worked diligently to connect clients with needed services and advocated for them at every turn. Homelessness is a multidimensional problem with no real, one size fits all solution. I learned so much from the women I worked with. I have a special place in my heart for this organization and the staff and volunteers who work there.
