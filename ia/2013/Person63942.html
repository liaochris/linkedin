Jim Johnson's Summary

As an organizational consultant and strategist, I provide executives with the tools to bring their strategic visions to life and help their companies thrive.

What I Do
As most senior executives know, implementing an organization-wide strategy can be challenging. Everything must line up perfectly; the strategy must be clear, the leadership team must be unified behind it, and the company must have the resources to implement it. The most effective business leaders know how to stay ahead of the competition by harnessing all these aspects of strategy-execution.

I guide executives through the following steps:

1. Formulate, clarify and agree upon a business strategy.
2. Assess the organization’s readiness to execute.
3. Target necessary organizational changes.
4. Implement the strategy so everyone understands its what, why, and how as well as the roles, goals, and implications for each individual and department.

Before starting my consulting firm, EnterCap Partners, in 1997, I served as Director of Leadership and Organization Effectiveness for the Rohm and Haas Company. In this position I worked with the CEO and his team to align the leaders of the company and to create a comprehensive performance-management process, a companywide employee survey program, and a process for leadership succession and development.

In my view, the leader of a company is a steward to whom the organization’s assets have been entrusted, and it is his or her job to leave the organization in better shape than how it was found.

A leader has three main resources for achieving the company’s mission: (1) his or her own inner resources, and (2) a strategy grounded in the marketplace and (3)your organization. Your strategic plan requires a well implemented organizational plan. That is where I can add value.
For a deeper understanding of my philosophy, download my “5 Essentials of Strategy Execution” at www.entercappartners.com/strategy_essentials.
