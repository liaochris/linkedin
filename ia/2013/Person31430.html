David Price's Summary

David is a co-founder of the cloud-based web service DebateGraph, which helps groups collaborate in thinking through complex issues by building and sharing interactive maps of domains of knowledge from multiple perspectives.

DebateGraph's collaborative partners have included: the UK Prime Minister’s Office, the White House Office of Science and Technology Policy, the Royal Society of Arts, CNN, The Independent newspaper, and the European Commission.

Prior to co-founding DebateGraph—with the former Australian cabinet minister Peter Baldwin—David worked in public policy consultancy, TV documentary production, academic research, and mediation, with various public and private sector organisations including: the BBC, H. M. Treasury, British Telecom, Ofcom, and Virgin TV.

David has a Ph.D. from the University of Cambridge in organisational learning and environmental policy, and a B.Sc. in Business Administration from the University of Bath.

He lives with his wife and three children in Somerset, and is a Fellow of the Royal Society of Arts.
