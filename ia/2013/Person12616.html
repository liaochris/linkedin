  • New Concierge Service Offers Celebrity-Inspired Childbirth Luxuries

    • Travel Pulse
    • July 21, 2013
    Authors: Stephanie D. Johnson, James Ruggia


    As Prince William and Kate Middleton expect the delivery of their new royal baby at St. Mary's hospital not far from Kensington Palace, media attention is turning again to luxury-infused childbirth. The attention given to the royals and other celebrity births such as Beyonce’s and Jay Z’s is mirrored in Dallas-based Bed Rest Concierge—a full service agency that supplies mothers-to-be and new moms with all the services of a luxury concierge—in and out of maternity wards and birthing centers.

    Luxury maternity suites are a new niche within the medical tourism trend. A Deluxe Maternity Suite, for post partum recovery at Los Angeles’ Cedars-Sinai hospital, for example, features a private labor/delivery rooms with “comfortable birthing beds, soft colors and recessed lighting.” The post-partum suites provide overnight accommodations for a family member or friend with a personal nurse to attend to the mother and the baby’s needs.

    A Two Room/One Bath Suite is priced from $2,847 per day. A three-room, two-bath Deluxe Maternity Suite is priced at $4,000. Extra amenities can include stocked refrigerator, flat-screen TV and WiFi, deluxe food service for two (with both vegan and veggie meals), complimentary hotel-style robe, hair stylist, manicures and pedicures, parking and a personal doula to assist during the childbirth process.

    Bed Rest Concierge is just one service taking advantage of these new amenity-laden birthing experiences but if the press surrounding it is any indicator, it will soon be followed by many more.

    “A lot of hospitals are trying to go that way now,” says Stephanie Johnson, founder and CEO of Bed Rest Concierge, a Dallas-based company that can turn your everyday hospital room into a birthing center fit for a queen. “We all want a little piece of what Beyoncé and Jessica Simpson are having. Patients aren’t just looking for quality rating now, they’re looking for amenities.”
    Don’t happen to live near an elite birthing facility? No problem. For the right price, Bed Rest Concierge will bring the luxury to you. According to owner Stephanie Johnson, her clients – who include TV stars, athlete’s wives, socialites and even the daughter of a former president – have asked her to trick out their maternity suites with all kinds of goodies including 50-inch flat screen TVs, Egyptian cotton bedding and custom pillows and enough distilled water to fill a birthing tub.
    Other swanky extras new moms have requested: in-room chef services, aestheticians to perform facials, mani-pedis and other spa treatments, and ice cream and cupcake delivery at 2 a.m.


    “I wanted to make sure I had everything I needed,” she says. “I had my hair and makeup done and got a wax before delivery. I wanted it to be seamless and wonderful. I wanted all the comforts that I have at home.”

    Helms says having a bit of luxury in her hospital room put her at ease and made the birthing experience much less stressful.

    “It’s the only way to deliver,” she says. “I wish this was standard in all hospitals. Not just for the women who can afford it, but for all women.”
