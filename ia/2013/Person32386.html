David Partyka's Summary

PhD level chemist/entrepreneur with postdoctoral experience and a strong background in biology and mathematics. Expertise with synthetic inorganic and organometallic chemistry and ligand synthesis. Managing founder of Creative Chemistry LLC (a chemical synthesis startup), PhDave LLC (consulting, tutoring, and entrepreneurship), JADI Enterprises LLC (social networking and website design), and Test Boost Incorporated (an online test-boosting platform). My main goal is to maximize my helpfulness to others by leveraging my skills via clever and entrepreneurial endeavors. All of these ventures are connected through my nonprofit corporation, The PhDave Foundation, as a keystone organization.

    • September 23, 2008

  • Relativistic Functional Groups: Aryl Carbon-Gold Bond Formation by Selective Transmetallation of Boronic Acids

    • Angewandte Chemie, International Edition
    • November 17, 2006
    Authors: David Partyka, Matthias Zeller, A. D. Hunter, Thomas Gray

    A series of arylgold(I) complexes were synthesized in high yield by treating various phosphine-ligated gold(I) bromides with arylboronic acids in the presence of base. Functional groups (e.g., nitro) not tolerated in the presence of Grignard and organolithium transmetalation reagents were tolerated under these transmetalation conditions.

  • Synergistic binding of both Lewis acids and bases to phosphinidenes

    The copper-catalyzed synthesis of organogold triazolates was found to proceed in moderate to excellent yield using benzyl azide and a series of internal alkynes (as alkynylgold complexes).

  • Gold(I) Pyrenyls: Excited State Consequences of Carbon-Gold Bond Formation

    • Organometallics
    • June 8, 2007
    Authors: David Partyka, Arthur J. Esswein, Matt Zeller, Allen D. Hunter, Thomas Gray

    Two pyrenylgold(I) complexes (one ligated by IPr and the other by tricyclohexylphosphine) were synthesized and characterized crystallographically and spectroscopically. Time resolved spectroscopy of photoexcited complexes suggested possible excimer formation prior to radiative decay.

  • Carbon-Gold Bond Formation through [3+2] Cycloaddition Reactions of Gold(I) Azides and Terminal Alkynes

    New methodologies for the synthesis of organogold triazolates starting from azides and alkynes in the absence of catalyst were developed.

  • Dialkylbiarylphosphine Complexes of Gold(I) Halides. Gold−Aryl π-Interactions in the Solid State

    • Organometallics
    • December 20, 2007

    A crystallographic study of a series of dialkylbiarylphosphine-ligated gold(I) halides suggested the possibility of an aryl-gold pi interaction for all of these complexes in the solid state, adding further credence to the possible stabilizing nature of this interaction.

  • Probing the Steric Limits of Carbon−Gold Bond Formation: (Dialkylbiarylphosphine)gold(I) Aryls

    In a further exploration of transmetalation methodology developed by the Gray lab, a series of increasing hindered phosphine-ligated gold(I) bromides were transmetalated with increasingly bulky arylboronic acids in an attempt to probe the steric limits of this transmetalation reaction.

    • Inorganic Chemistry

    Phosphine- and IPr-ligated silver(I) acetate complexes were easily prepared, and found to be useful synthons in reactions with silylated nucleophiles.

  • Oxygen/Sulfur Substitution Reactions of Tetraoxometalates Effected by Electrophilic Carbon and Silicon Reagents

    • Inorganic Chemistry
    • December 3, 2004
    Authors: David Partyka, Richard H. Holm

    Tetraoxometalates (M = Mo, W, Re) were useful starting materials with respect to metal-sulfur bond formation using a variety of silylated thiolates. In general, M=O bond cleavage and M-S bond formation proceeded without redox for the isolated major products.

  • Constrained Digold(I) Diaryls: Syntheses, Crystal Structures, and Photophysics

    • Chemistry: A European Journal
    • January 11, 2012
    Authors: David Partyka, Thomas S. Teets, Matt Zeller, James B. Updegraff, Allen D. Hunter, Thomas Gray

    Constrained dinuclear arylgold(I) complexes were designed using diphosphines of the Xantphos series. Standard Gray group transmetalation methodology was exploited to synthesize the complexes, which were characterized spectroscopically, crystallographically, and by DFT. Calculations and spectroscopic data indicate the constraints imposed by the ligand system dramatically alter the photophysics of the dinuclear complexes relative to their mononuclear analogues.

  • Nucleophilic Reactivity and Oxo/Sulfido Substitution Reactions of MVIO3 Groups (M = Mo, W)

    • November 5, 2003
    Authors: David Partyka, Richard J. Staples, Richard H. Holm

    As a surrogate for biological systems, the reactivity of the MO3 (M = Mo, W) motif was explored using triazacyclohexane and triazacyclononane as ancillary, bystander ligation on M.
