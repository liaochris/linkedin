Mark Dillof, Ph.D.'s Summary

Dr. Mark Dillof offers executive coaching and life counseling, from a holistic perspective. He integrates the wisdom of business thinkers, like Peter Drucker, with insights from philosophy, psychology, world literature, and Eastern wisdom.

Dr. Dillof also offers entertaining seminars, integrating philosophy, psychology, comedy, and magic.

Specialties

Executive coaching and life counseling. Life transitions, existential crises, counseling for people perplexed by deep questions, existential anxiety and cosmic blues. Offering a Western approach to Eastern wisdom. In person and telephone counseling for people who have been through and are through with psychotherapy, and who are looking for something deeper.

Mark Dillof, Ph.D.'s Publications

  • The Paranoid Vision: Deciphering the Mystery of Malevolence

    • Mystical Kentuckian Press
    • March 29, 2013
    Authors: Mark Dillof, Ph.D.

    Everyday, we hear news reports about acts of madness and murder, and wonder what's behind it all. Mark Dillof contends that there exists a certain way of seeing the world called "the paranoid vision." Unlike clinical paranoia, it can infect an organization, even a society. It often hatches pernicious conspiracy theories and apocalyptic fantasies. Those under its sway include cult leaders, fanatics, terrorists, school shooters, and genocidal murderers. No worldview is more malevolent, nor more deadly. Dr. Dillof explores the paranoid vision from multiple perspectives, even contrasting it to the comic vision! Like a modern-day Virgil, he takes the reader on a Dantesque journey, from the inferno - created by the paranoid vision - to joyful wisdom.

  • Mysteries in Broad Daylight: A Journey into the Deeper Meaning of Everyday Life

    • Mystical Kentuckian Press
    • August 1, 2012
    Authors: Mark Dillof, Ph.D.

    Are you perplexed by life? Trying to figure out what it’s all about? There’s no need to climb the Himalayas, in search of a swami. Valuable clues can be found right in your own backyard.

    Everything about you — from your golf game to your dread of public speaking, from your relationship conflicts to your dreams at night, from your anxieties about your career to your eating disorder — can offer clues to life’s profoundest enigmas. Mark Dillof’s astonishing new book, Mysteries in Broad Daylight, will help you learn to decipher these everyday clues.

    What you read will surprise you, make you laugh and enlighten you! And it will transform how you see the world! Dr. Dillof’s book will introduce you to a Western route to Eastern wisdom. There’s no need to meditate or stand on your head as do yoga practicioners; you need only learn how to think deeply about your interests, desires, conflicts & anxieties.

    Mysteries in Broad Daylight consists of a trilogy of lively dialogues, thirty-five essays and instructions on deciphering the mysteries of everyday life. The dialogues derive from Dr. Dillof’s pioneering work as a philosophical counselor and spiritual life coach. You’ll find these conversations intriguing, thought-provoking and valuable as they shed light on your present struggles.

    After each dialogue and essay, there are questions for you to think about. The questions were created by Dr. Dillof to help you relate the insights from the book to your everyday life. Mysteries in Broad Daylight is interesting and entertaining, but more importantly it’s a practical manual for those who lust for a real life, one lived in the light of truth.

  • Awakening with the Enemy: The Origin and End of Male/Female Conflict

    • Philosophy Clinic Press
    • June 1, 2000
    Authors: Mark Dillof, Ph.D.

    If the many books that have been written on the subject of relationships were really helpful, the incidence of broken hearts and broken homes would be decreasing. Why, then, is connubial bliss at an all time low? Something is missing from our understanding of the roots of male/female dissatisfaction, and Awakening with the Enemy, a new book by philosopher Mark Dillof, reveals what it is.
    Dillof rejects the problem-solving approach to life's difficulties endemic to self-help books and radio psychologists. He explains why the "men are from Mars, women from Venus approach to relationships" ultimately fails, and why no new arrangement of male and female can be significantly better.

    The alternative? Rather than seeking to integrate the erotic polarities, Dillof explains how a couple can "awaken" from them. When - due to insight - their former "dance" together evaporates, they have "nothing between them anymore." This sounds negative, but it is really positive. "Nothing between," means that nothing is separating them from each other. Consequently, they are one! Awakening with the Enemy is a guide to using male/female conflict as a route to this new level of unity, one that few people even know exists. Here, then, is a uniquely Western route to Eastern wisdom.
