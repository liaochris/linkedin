    • Board Member

      Holly Community Theatre
      • Arts and Culture
      January 1999 – January 2003 (4 years 1 month)

      Around 1915 Dahlonega began being used for Hollywood Motion Pictures by Fox Film Company. They used an abandoned gold mine in their production "The Plunder" based off a novel by Roy North. Other silent movies that were shot in Dahlonega included "Big Jim Garrity" by Pathe Feres Company, "The Great Divide" filmed at Preacher Cut on Crown Mountain, "Driven" in the 1920s by Brabin Productions, and "The Daughter of Devil Dan" by Buffalo Pictures.

      Silent movies were shown in a courtroom in the courthouse and in the Price Memorial Auditorium. In the mid 1920's Robert D. Howser Sr. converted the lower floor of the Price Building into a theater and began showing films.

      In 1939 Mr. Randall Holly Brannon leased the Price Building. He added a ticket booth, an awning, double doors, and large THEATRE sign. The opening film was showing April 17, 1939 titled "Stagecoach" staring Marion Morrison or later known as John Wayne.

      In January 9, 1943 Dahlonega Theater temporarily closed due to a fire. At the end of WWII Brannon decided Price was not an adequate space and applied to build a new theater. On July 12, 1948 the Holly Theater celebrated its grand opening! The featured film MGM's "The Bride Goes Wild". It was as it is now. A solid brick building with a Georgia Marble facade. The interior covered with fabric and matching curtain - electrically operated. The theater held 500.

      The theater did well through the 1950s but in the 1960s people began to turn to television. Attendance dropped and when Brannon died in 1965 it was hard for his wife to keep the theater alive. People rented the building for various events then in 1976 it was reopened as a theater for a short time - in 1979 the theater was again closed.

      In 1980 it was used to house worship. After all this the building began to deteriorate. The building has now been restored and shows movies and plays. It is now a well known and loved place for the community of Dahlonega.
