Christopher Matthews, MEP's Summary

I have recently completed the FEMA EMI Master Exercise Practitioner Program (Series #23 - The Hurricane Series), and have over 18 years experience in Emergency Medical Services, coming from the entry level EMT-First Responder in 1994, through the military to corpsman in 1995, to certified EMT-Basic in 1996, then on to EMT-Intermediate and EMS Instructor in 1999, then completed Paramedic school in 2001. I held an Affiliate Faculty position with the NAEMT for Emergency Pediatric Care (EPC), and have been a course coordinator for PHTLS and AMLS. I am also an experienced AHA instructor for CPR (All levels)/AED/First Aid, ACLS, and PALS. I have enjoyed working in diverse environments (rural, suburban, metropolitan, military) in places such as Orlando/Cocoa Beach, FL; Inglewood/South Central LA/Long Beach, CA, Portola/Susanville, CA; MCB Camp Pendleton, CA; Reno, NV; and Fort Worth/DFW, TX (where I averaged ~2,000 calls/year and served as a training officer). I presently teach part-time at Truckee Meadows Community College EMS Programs in Reno, NV for all the EMT levels from First Responder through Paramedic, and have been the online component instructor for the hybrid Paramedic program.

I am also presently part-time employed with New Horizons Computer Learning Center in Reno, NV where I have put my skills in computers to good use teaching many Microsoft Office and Operating Systems programs, and the Adobe Creative Suite.

I am presently employed full-time with the Nevada State Health Division as their Public Health Preparedness Training Officer. My primary job is to prepare the Health Division staff, and its health partners throughout Nevada for bioterrorism & CBRNE events, as directed by the CDC's Public Health Emergency Preparedness (PHEP) grant. Additionally, I am learning to administrate ESi's WebEOC version 7.3+ and am becoming familiar with ESRI's ArcGIS to allow us to map, forecast, and respond to the public health aspects of disasters.

Specialties

Paramedicine, EMS Instruction, Computer Applications Instruction, New Employee Training and Continuing Education, Bioterrorism/CBRNE Preparedness Education, Redundant Communications.
