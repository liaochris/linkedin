Clement Leemans' Summary

Move! is a small international consulting practice, focussing on 'how people learn' in organizations. We help organizations build processes that effectively help people (individual, teams and the organization as a whole) to "change the way they do things" and by doing that improving their contribution to the mission and performance of their organization. We analyse 'why people do things' in an organization and try to identify the 'levers' that would help them change their behavior, not only looking at individual competenties and training but also taking into account organizational processes, reward systems, leadership and management climate, organizational structure, etc.. That holistic approach is set up with maximum involvement of all stakeholders in the organization and is meant to 'develop' in the organization the competencies to change and to learn and to develop a 'learning organization'

Specialties: Learning, behavioral change, work / learning relationship, organization development, process consulting, team development, experiential learning & instructional design

Clement Leemans' Projects

  • Towards a Learning Organization

    • January 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Clement Leemans, Sebastiaan Duts

    We are doing process consulting and organizational development consulting for the Belgian/Dutch unit of a large international Environmental Consultant. It is a redesign effort to create an organizational structure, working processes, roles and collaborative culture in order to create a truly learning organization that is developing the expertise necessary to create sustainable performance and development in a rapidly changing business context. As a knowledge business the future depends on developing the expertise necessary to answer customer needs within 3 or 5 years, knowing that those issues will fundamentally different from the once they are confronted with today.

  • How to create sustainable results with TPM

    • August 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Clement Leemans, Sebastiaan Duts

    We are working with an organization that is struggling getting sustainable performance improvement as the result of a TPM process. Issues here are how to create ownership on the floor and making sure well intended initiatives from TPM do not fall flat a few months later. How to create a culture where there is an awareness with all people that they contribute to the final results and that we can learn from each other and from our experiences.... so joint problem analysis, cross team collaboration, internal customer supplier relationship, first line management coaching and feedback....

  • Project Management Process Support

    • July 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Clement Leemans, Sebastiaan Duts

    To accompany the implementation of a Project Management process, I brought in several different kind of activities, all stakeholders together to focus not on 'who we do it', but on how do we create the best possible contribution to the business. This resulted in a changed vision on how you decide on doing a project or not, how to involve stakeholders, how to create a compelling business case, how to support the project team and leader, etc... It resulted also in a few structuring tools that became common language in the organization.

  • Quality, Safety & Environment : Community of Practice

    • October 2010 to Present
    Team Members: Clement Leemans, Yves Bergeron

    As a consequence of an organizational restructuring, the QSE responsibility was created at HQ level, with a central team having the responsibility to drive the QSE policies into the organization (12 sugar and alcohol plants). In order to do that Move! created a collaborative process that resulted in a 'community of practice' where not only learning and sharing is going on, but where people work on projects together, define the strategy and QSE objectives participatively, where follow up is done, etc... From a very silo like organization we are heading in a participative, learning organization.

  • Management Development

    • August 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Clement Leemans

    We are doing process consultation in a Management Development Project in Lafarge Algeria, where we combine individual Development Centers with large group workshops developing a learning and coaching culture.
