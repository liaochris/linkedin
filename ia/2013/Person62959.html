Jesse Noller's Summary

I’m a full stack developer and software engineer with extensive Python, web programming, and distributed systems experience. I am a core developer of the Python language. I am a member, director, and officer of the Python Software Foundation.

I am an experienced and respected community organizer. I am most deeply involved in the Python community. I was elected to serve on the governing board of the Python Software Foundation in 2010, and for the last two years I have been the chair of PyCon, the PSF’s flagship conference. I am internationally recognized as a writer, developer, and community advocate.

Professionally, I have worked in businesses of every size, from multinational corporations to startups, primarily small, focused teams. I know how to identify good ideas, develop them into products, see them through to market, advocate and sell, and respond quickly to changing demands.

I am well-versed in public communication, mediation, developer outreach and marketing. I pride myself on staying on top of new trends and technology and identifying where they can benefit the companies, communities and organizations I am involved with.

Specialties:
- Extensive Python experience in many domains ranging from distributed systems to web frameworks.
- Experience and familiarity with the best of breed tools within the Python ecosystem.
- Full-stack web development, including Javascript, CSS, and UI/UX design.
- Extensive Operating Systems skills with a focus on Linux and other UNIX-like operating systems.
- Direct experience with the storage, compute, and queuing cloud services on most major providers, including backend implementation and client-side developer interfaces.
- Broad experience writing technical articles explaining technology to developers and non- developers alike.
