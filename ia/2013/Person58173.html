Jane E Porter's Summary

janeeporter.co.uk

Jane E and her brother Guyan were brought up by wolves in a remote part of the north east of Scotland. As adolescents they were slowly introduced to society, teaching each other to read and write and eventually going to school in Aberdeen.

On leaving school, Janee travelled extensively around the world. She lived and worked in Europe, Eastern Asia and the Middle East, studied psychology, hypnotherapy and sociology before attending Anniesland College in 1999. There she was awarded Best Student in HNC Art & Design with Portfolio Presentation. After college she attended Grays School of Art and obtained a BA (Hons) in Fine Art. She has works held in the UK, US, Australia and The Middle East.

Her current practice considers the complexities of the creative mind and ponders it's fragility. The completed paintings are a physical objectification of the arcane, paying homage to it's memory and lamenting it's loss.

“I question nearly every day, why I am an artist and what I have to offer. It is hard to articulate without sounding like a cliché but it is a calling, an obsessive compulsion. I’m inspired and perplexed by modern culture; the media, celebrity, obsession, addiction, politics, war, abuse and the injustices in society and I make art about it. If my work offers you something new to look at and stimulates you in some way, it gives positive meaning to my obsession.
