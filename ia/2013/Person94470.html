Mike Adamson's Summary

I’m an experienced senior copywriter and brand strategist who writes for results. I get them by studying the target audience, pinpointing their emotional drivers and differentiating the brand in a way that’s both relevant to the viewer and unexpected enough to make them stop and pay attention. Hopefully they laugh, or show a friend, or burst into an embarrassing and wallet-producing display of emotion. Some call it copywriting. I call it communications alchemy – turning words into gold in the client’s pocket.

Specialties: advertising copywriter, brand strategy, marketing strategy, marketing copywriter, positioning, differentiation, copy for web, print and broadcast, storytelling

Mike Adamson's Projects

  • Urban Mattress Print Ads

    • 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Mike Adamson

    Mattress ads tend to all look the same. Pictures of beds surrounded by starbursts with craaaaaazy discounts. But then, most mattress stores do business the same way.

    Urban Mattress takes a consultative approach that's unique in the industry, donates 2% to local charities and offers 100% natural lines. They're educational and refreshingly honest, and wanted to run ads that reflected this. These print pieces that ran in Denver, Boulder and Austin newspapers are as unexpected as a salesman recommending a less expensive bed.

  • Bounce Dodgeball Direct Mail

    • 2010 to Present
    Team Members: Mike Adamson

    Bounce Logistics needed a direct mail piece that would delight current customers. They decided on creating a version of dodgeball that could be played in the office without sacrificing the animalistic competitiveness that makes the classic game great.

    I was charged with creating the rules. Inventing games is somewhat of a hobby of mine (typically with success, though dodge darts was a resounding failure), so this was right up my alley.

    The copy was fun and slightly irreverent, in line with Bounce's corporate personality, and incorporated industry terms that would resonate with recipients. The resulting piece was mailed with an inflated mini dodgeball and score sheet.

    It was a huge success with several customers requesting new balls to replace the ones that had gone out windows or landed on scissors.
