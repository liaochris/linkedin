Caleb Pirtle III's Experience

Founder, Editorial Director

Venture Galleries

March 2011 – Present (2 years 10 months)

Caleb and Linda Pirtle are launching Venture Galleries, a marketer of fine art showcasing the beauty and splendor of the American scene, both paintings and photography, and the publisher of travel-oriented eBooks, written by some of the nation's top new authors.

Venture Galleries will produce travel books, travel cookbooks, books for the business traveler, travel novels, and books for the road, showcasing both fiction and non-fiction: mysteries, thrillers, suspense, romance, romance intrigue, and historical narratives.

Owner/Director

CP3 Productions

September 2007 – Present (6 years 4 months)

Caleb Pirtle has authored more than fifty published books. His latest books are "Other Voices, Other Towns," and "Gamble in the Devil's Chalk," both published by Gallivant Press. Pirtle's works primarily include travel and history books, as well as several works of fiction and non-fiction. He and his partner, Frank Q. Dobbs, have also written the screenplay for one motion picture, "Hot Wire," teleplays for three television movies, "Gambler V: Playing for Keeps" and "Wildcat: Sarah Delaney and the Doodlebug Man"for CBS and "The Texas Rangers" for TNT. He has optioned his and Dobbs' screenplay, "Bum's Luck" to a Canadian production company.

Owner

CP3 Productions

September 2007 – March 2011 (3 years 7 months)

CP3 Productions handles the creative, development, and production end of custom and trade books. Pirtle wrote and produced the historical trilogy of the East Texas oilfield, "Echoes from Forgotten Streets," "Visions of Forgotten Streets," and "Life on Kilgore's Unforgettable Streets." Pirtle wrote, as well as supervised the design and publishing, of a major book entitled, "No Experience Required: Jackie Sherrill and Texas A&M's 12th Man Kickoff Team" and a grilling cookbook for Dick's Sporting Goods. For London Square Media, he wrote and produced the oversized coffee-table book, "Great Moments in Alabama Football History," showcasing the paintings of Rick Rush, known as America's Sports Artist.

Vice President of Editorial/Editorial Director

Dockery House Publishing

March 1985 – September 2007 (22 years 7 months)

For more than two decades, I was responsible for writing, as well as supervising design and production, for more than a hundred custom books for the nation's top corporations, retailers, and associations. At Dockery House, we also published custom magazines for retail giants like Ahold's eight supermarket chains, Supervalu Cub's supermarket chain, Sam's Club, and Sally Beauty Supply. Magazines were also published for a number of national associations. Many of the projects developed by Dockery House received national awards for editorial and design.

Travel Editor

Southern Living Magazine

October 1969 – November 1979 (10 years 2 months)

For almost a decade, during its first and formative years, I served as travel editor for Southern Living, acclaimed nationally during the 1970s, as the fastest growing and most successful magazine in the country. For that period of time, Southern Living won three Discover America Awards for its travel coverage of the region, and I was fortunate enough to be named Man of the Year by the Travel South Association.

Feature Writer, Reporter

Fort Worth Star-Telegram

Public Company; 501-1000 employees; MNI; Newspapers industry

1965 – 1967 (2 years)
