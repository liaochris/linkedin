Gauthier Giacomoni's Summary

As a Desk Associate for CBS Radio News Network, I monitor and pursue coverage of breaking and ongoing news. This includes coordinating with over 500 affiliated stations in the country and reporters globally. I also produce a weekly podcast feed for our affiliates and mobile apps.

During my internships in both commercial and public radio I have had the opportunity to pitch ideas, produce audio, write copy, and shadow reporters in the field.

As a recipient of a master's degree in broadcast journalism from Boston University I know how to cover news for TV, radio, and the internet.

While comfortable in all of these media, I prefer radio, and aspire to the title of European correspondent or producer for Radiolab.

I am also a French citizen, and being trilingual - I speak English, French, and Italian - I have a unique capability to understand European policy and politics.

In addition, I am also immersed in social media and realize its growing importance in the world of communications at large, and more specifically in journalism.

Specialties: Adobe Audition, AP ENPS, Audacity, Final Cut, Jutel RadioMan Suite, Newsboss, Pro Tools, and Soundslides.

Gauthier Giacomoni's Projects

  • Master's Thesis: Treatment Options for PTSD

    • September 2011 to December 2011
    Team Members: Gauthier Giacomoni

    Post Traumatic Stress Disorder, once known as shell shock or combat fatigue, it has usually been associated with the military. According to the department of Veterans Affairs, eleven to twenty percent - or up to four hundred thousand - of the soldiers deployed in Iraq and Afghanistan have Post Traumatic Stress Disorder. However, eight percent of American civilians also suffer from Post Traumatic Stress at some point in their lives. That's 25 million people who are affected. Gauthier Giacomoni looks into what treatment options are available to those who suffer from Post Traumatic Stress Disorder.
