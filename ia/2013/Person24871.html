Richard Chwalek's Summary

This is a case of self-aggrandizement in action! (& Adventure comes later.) Ashamedly, I will progress in this endeavor to not disappoint my audience or lack thereof. I begin by absorbing the bittersweet homage of your fleeting loyalty, fidelity, liberty and mutual agreement of my status as an effective yet never humble self-aggrandizer. My objectives here are to communicate something more important than my dental communication goals and even life itself, my life itself.

Yes, this planet and its people are suffering in multiple ways and for many reasons. However, my being insufferable as well as dispensing into a virtual subjugation of the English languish is preferable to our constant international and interpersonal retaliation, and this one (yes, one, it's a small world) planet's suffocation. We must speak up. Speak out. Speak about me, when possible.

Even I, a communicator of some notes about dentistry, ramblings of purely natural incoherence and annoying slants in my rants, must admit that politics has gotten to a point where I must take a stand for more than just... Yes, just. This is tough... Just myself. I have said it!

Now everyone, who is anyone, follow me to the ramparts! (Whatever ramparts are.) Let's make communication happen, like we learned in kindergarten.

Skip that. It wasn't a good time for me. Asking any teacher of mine would not be a good plan, whichever grade, because my school mates on LinkedIn would call me on it.

Therefore, we should now, as dental consultants and the regular people, discuss things as if we were Alan Page, or some other Notre Dame great, Jon Meacham, Thomas Jefferson or... or Plato.

Since my confidence is waning and self-deprecation has moved in with a vengeance, the rest of you talk, and I'll play with the clay as I eat paste. Now get on with it, civil like. (Adult-like if that helps any, which it doesn't seem to.)
