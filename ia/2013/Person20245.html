Game Designer & Project Manager

Gold Mountain Guest, UBC

September 2010 – December 2010 (4 months)

I was the game designer of the immersive browser-based mystery-adventure game, Gold Mountain Guest. Gold Mountain Guest caters to the teenagers of Canada who will soon be learning about the Chinese-Canadian history that has been hidden from the school curriculum regarding the settlement era circa 1910. As an effort of social justice for Chinese Canadians, content of this dark past will begin to surface and Gold Mountain Guest will guide users to seek out the content and learn from the Chinese Canadian historical archives. Working in a diverse team of both expertise and culture, collaboration and communication was of utmost importance in creating this socially influential online game.

May 2010 – September 2010 (5 months) Vancouver, Canada Area

Designing educational games to lead children towards a future with cleaner air. I acted as digital media consultant, interaction designer, and game designer for a non-profit organization that wishes to push social change via web portal and social games. I was responsible for helping the organization conceptualize, design, and plan the project to create an online game portal and its games.

User Experience Designer

The University of British Columbia

Educational Institution; 10,001+ employees; Higher Education industry

January 2010 – April 2010 (4 months) Vancouver, Canada Area

Working with a doctor of rheumatoid arthritis to conceptualize and design an online e-learning program that is more convenient and user-friendly for patients recently diagnosed with arthritis. I was responsible for ensuring that the interactions are comfortable and the media content is appropriately designed for arthritis patients who can experience discomfort in any of their affected joints. This program is designed to teach patients to manage their symptoms, maintain their health, and stay employed.
