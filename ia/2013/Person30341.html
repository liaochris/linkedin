Dan Porter's Summary

I have run five companies. The secret on how I have gone from music lover to classroom teacher to internet entrepreneur to the corporate life to CEO has to do with my unique skill set: the ability to spin good yarns, a constant curiosity, poor grapho-motor skills, a love of Kombucha and a desire to wear sneakers. Thus is the nature of my peripatetic and desultory career trajectory.

I have made three #1 apps. I think the silver bullet is that I'm not very good at games. Some years though I'm decent at fantasy football.

The biggest app of course was Draw Something. In 2012 I was lucky to work with an incredibly talented group of folks and after four years of banging our heads against the wall we made Draw Something. The game has well over 100 million downloads and is one of the most downloaded games ever in the history of the iPhone. We sold OMGPOP to Zynga for $180 million. It was 13 years between the two sales of my companies.

Before that, let me demonstrate the power of focus (not). I worked on three tech start-ups. I also ran two educational non profits. I was the President of Teach For America way back in the beginning. Add to that two big company corporate experiences, including the chance to work for Richard Branson. I have reported to the board and been on the board. I have managed and led 100 person team and have managed and led no one as well. I believe that may make me a jack of two trades.

How did I do it all you ask? I could insert numerous accolades I have won here, but instead I will let you in on my secret: Mostly luck, combined with vegetarian living, the ability to catch on to TV shows three seasons after everyone else does, the ability to cheer for losing sports teams (go Eagles), and a complete and utter inability to ever assemble a toy given to my kids. One last thing: everything about business I learned from The Wire.
