Matt Venables' Summary

I am passionate about building Ruby on Rails and iPhone apps.

I have spent most of my professional career developing highly-scalable web applications using Ruby on Rails, HTML5, Javascript (well, Coffeescript now), Grails, PHP, and JSP (yawn) as well as user friendly mobile apps using Objective-C. I also have several years of experience developing desktop and security software using C, C++, and Java.

I am usually working on a side project. In my free time, I built a workout music recommendation engine and community called jog.fm.

Since launching in November 2010, jog.fm experienced a tremendous growth in traffic resulting in several hundred thousand unique visitors per month, as well as thousands of iPhone app downloads per month. The site has been featured in Shape Magazine, Real Simple magazine, Runner's World, Lifehacker, UrbanDaddy, Thrillist, etc. (ICYW jog.fm is running on Heroku, Ruby 1.9.3, Rails 3.2.8, and Postgres).

Random things about me:
* I'd choose native over HTML5 for mobile apps nine times out of ten. But if the shoe fits, I'll wear it.
* I still love a good mobile-web enabled website.
* I never thought I'd find a language I like more than C until I met Ruby. Objective-C is up there, too.
* My name is in the Ruby 1.9.3-p194 commit log.
* Coffeescript is awesome. So is SCSS.
* I still don't like having my views rendered in client-side Javascript.
* Show me one business manager who writes user acceptance stories in Gherkin and I'll use Cucumber again.
* I organized (and occasionally speak at) the Boston Ruby on Rails Meetup Group and the MetroWest Ruby Users Group.
* I'm glad I don't use PHP or Java much anymore.
* I very much enjoy using vim
* Man, I love the New England Patriots.

I'd love to be in touch about potential projects, technological discussions, or anything else.

Note to recruiters: It's fine to contact me, but please don't contact me about anything involving .NET or Java. Also, please include the company name in your message.

Specialties
