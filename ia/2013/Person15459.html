Bob Mossing, MBA's Summary

Innovative, solutions-oriented leader with a MBA and more than 12 years’ management experience performing financial analysis, project management, inventory control, accounting, operations, and personnel control of large and small service vehicle fleets. Emphasis on budgeting, analysis, reporting, process improvement, negotiations, and P&L. Technical skills include: all Microsoft Office products with advanced skills in Excel and Access, Visual Basic, Crystal Reports, Oracle and JD Edwards financial systems. Excellent communicator at all levels.

Fifteen years of management experience with government and corporate organizations. Promoted rapidly, based upon consistent contributions to improved operating results, process improvements, and positive organizational change. Served as a member of an 8-person, Senior Executive Team and reported directly to the Chief Operating Officer.

  • How Washtenaw County's Outsourced Maintenance Turned its Light Fleet Around

    • Government Fleet
    • March 2012
    Authors: Bob Mossing, MBA, Thi Dao, Greg Basich

    Keeping maintenance in-house would require rebuilding the garage, putting in new equipment for the facility, and adding four additional staff members, a costly alternative. The second option, sending out work directly to shops, was projected to cost $1.5 million for three years, a cost that included the hiring of administrative staff. Partnering with the City didn't turn out to be the best option. The fleet department eventually chose LeasePlan USA, which submitted the winning proposal, expected to cost $1.26 million for three years, about equal to the fleet's operating cost. Mossing, however, expected it would come with better customer service, which it did.
