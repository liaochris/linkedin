Carolyn Winter's Summary

Since 1997 I have been fascinated with Resonance Repatterning as an extraordinary tool for raising consciousness and the positive benefits of doing so. Mostly I love discovering the patterns and connections in a field of energy (versus our psyche) and the precise modality to shift a field to a place of coherence.

Life provides us with pain and challenges but if we feel stuck or whirling around in a repeating pattern... it gives me joy to help others discover the underlying long forgotten pattern, bring it to awareness, shift it and experience profound changes.

A number of my colleagues and I say "you can repattern anything" and it's true. I have repatterned individuals with success for houses to sell or buy, for negotiations to go smoother than expected, for business results, for better meetings or client relations, for new habits to stick, for relationships to happen and more.

While there are no magic bullets really, with a pre-requistie to take responsiblity, have a little faith, be willing to 'see' the symbols and patterns... it can be quite amazing where the system will take you. Expect delightful and unexpected surprises.

Specialties

I love working with growth oriented individuals or small teams that love applying creative processes to their problem solving or project and who can appreciate the value of symbols, and patterns as part of their strategy for success. Give me your toughest problem and let's shift the energy field together!

Director, Glengarry Day Care Center

City of Edmonton

Government Agency; 10,001+ employees; Government Administration industry

August 1979 – August 1984 (5 years 1 month)

Executive Director for the Glengarry Day Care Center - an initiative of the local government to help establish day care in the province, provide training, and a model of care for community groups across the province. The 80 child space center was a partner in a number of special projects. A research project with Dr. Sheila Campbell of the University of Alberta, conducted at the day care in the early 80's helped to establish the need for an optimum space requirement for children in care. The center also hosted one of the first initiatives to integrate special needs children into regular daycare environment settings with a high degree of success.

Due to changing public policy, the center operation was transferred to a local non profit community group in 1984.

In this position I had the privilege to work with and learn from child care visionaries such as Andy Dorash, the late Janice McBeath and Jean Thompson.
