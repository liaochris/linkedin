Dave Bennett's Summary

I have just finished my first year at Carnegie Mellon's Entertainment Technology Center, where I am pursuing a Masters of Entertainment Technology.

In Fall 2010, I served as a programmer and designer for Building Virtual Worlds, where my fellow students and I were placed into small teams (4-5 people) and tasked with creating fully functioning worlds within two weeks.

In Spring 2011, I was a part of a seven-person team, charged with implementing the hardware used in Building Virtual Worlds into the Unity3D game engine. While we implemented many different pieces, my main focus was the Microsoft Kinect through the OpenNI implementation.

In the Summer of 2011, I was the Advanced Programming Teaching Assistant at the National Highschool Game Academy located at Carnegie Mellon, where I taught students gameplay programming tricks using Unity3D.

Currently, I am working with a team of 2 others to develop a fully-functional, enjoyable iPad game using Objective-C and the cocos2d game engine. Our goal is to release this on the Apple App Store shortly after the end of December.

My career goals are to obtain either a gameplay programming or game design role in the video game industry.

Specialties

Gameplay Programming, Game Design, Software Engineering, iOS (iPad), cocos2d, Unity3D, Kinect Development, Technical Writing
