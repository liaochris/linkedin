Jonathan Anderson's Summary

I’ve been fortunate to participate in work that’s on the leading edge of user-centered strategy and design, customer experience, and software development. Everything is converging around an increased attention to the quality of user experiences, around web-enabled or web-like software, and around technologies that can create unified experiences across multiple platforms, devices, and applications. I’ve built on my experience at UX Magazine, EffectiveUI, and in writing my book to undertake a major project to find ways to make dramatic improvements to the user-centered field and to increase the perception of user-centered design, research, and technology as being core strategic values.

My work can be very hard to explain because what I do day-to-day is extremely varied since my role is usually to be a jack-of-all-trades. If I’m performing any one job function this week or month, it’s always in the broader context of fulfilling the needs of that business (whatever they might be) and in the even broader context of the private equity holding and management activities of First Day.

My primary value has been to be an adaptable, fearless, fast-learning manager of and versatile resource to a large number of small businesses, where I hold the line in diverse functions while the companies are too small to hire specialized professionals for any given part of their business. This means I’ve had my hands in almost every aspect of starting, growing, and managing a small business, including finance, accounting, legal, management, HR, marketing/brand, PR, IT, resource management, facilities, general operations, corporate governance, project management, product development, change management, and many others.

Jonathan Anderson's Experience

Editor-in-Chief

UX Magazine

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Computer Software industry

June 2009 – Present (4 years 3 months)

As of December, 2012, I’ve stepped back from the day-to-day editorial operations of UX Magazine and changed title to Editor-in-Chief. This allows me to focus on finding ways of leveraging the magazine’s reach, influence, connections, brand, and community to do interesting new things to improve the UX field and sustain the magazine. I’m currently deeply engaged in hands-on research into the UX employment market and challenges faced by job seekers and hiring managers. I’m also working on bridging academia and research with the practitioner community, supporting better undergrad and grad programs in user-centered disciplines, and adding vitality to UX community activities.

I started as the Managing Editor in 2009 through an acquisition, and was responsible for overall management of the business and maintenance of the magazine's high editorial standards. UX Magazine is a central, one-stop resource for everything related to user experience. We provide a steady stream of current, informative, and credible information about UX and related fields to enhance the professional and creative lives of UX practitioners and those exploring the field. Our content is driven and created by an impressive roster of professionals who work in all areas of UX and cover the field from diverse perspectives.

I got involved with UX Magazine because of the strong unmet need for knowledge resources about how UX fits into technology initiatives, customer experience strategies, design thinking, products, and project and organizational dynamics. UX Magazine fosters discussion and shared learning within the community, and also creates an environment where people from all disciples within and outside of the UX field can cross paths and learn from each other.

My work at UX Magazine dovetailed nicely with my experience at EffectiveUI and in writing the book (see below). Through the magazine, I've enjoyed the privilege of working with some of the strongest minds and talents in the UX field.

Senior Advisor

Didus

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Management Consulting industry

December 2012 – Present (9 months)

Didus is an exclusive partner with UX Magazine, and I’ve recently joined them as a senior advisor to help fuel their potential with my experience in the UX field and in small business. Through Didus, I’m able to get intensively hands-on in the user-centered employment market to further my broader work, as well as help companies structure and build successful user-centered practices.

Didus is a staffing and career development service focused on user-centered roles in research, design, technology, and strategy. Through their exceptionally sophisticated knowledge of the UX field and the partnership with UX Magazine, Didus is working to improve the market for professionals in UX and user-centered roles through staffing, education, career development, and consulting. Didus helps professionals clearly articulate their value to companies who they've helped to develop a clear understanding of UX.

The user-centered field is experiencing tremendous growth and demand, but everyone is having trouble communicating what they need and the value of what they do in a context where key concepts are poorly defined and everyone understands them differently, and titles like "Experience Director" can mean totally different things depending on the size, location, and focus of a company. Companies struggle to define and articulate what they are looking for and don’t know how to create the right environment for UX to be successful, while UX professionals find it difficult to explain the breadth and focus of their experience.

As well, companies often don't feel they're getting the most out of their user-centered staff, so they contrive dysfunctional project management, HR, and management practices; meanwhile, employees feel they're prevented from doing their best work because the company misunderstands and mismanages them. Everyone just wants to do good work, but miscommunication and misunderstanding get in the way. I'm excited to partner with Didus to begin to fix this problem.

Jonathan Anderson's Publications

  • Effective UI

    • O'Reilly Media
    • February 10, 2010
    Authors: Jonathan Anderson, John McRee, Robb Wilson

    People expect effortless, engaging interaction with desktop and web applications, but producing software that generates enjoyable user experiences is much harder than many companies anticipate. With Effective UI, you'll learn proven user-experience strategies that will satisfy your clients and customers, drive business value, and increase brand strength.

    This book shows you how to capture the collaborative and cooperative spirit among designers, engineers, and management required for building engaging software. You'll also learn valuable methods for maintaining focus throughout the process -- whether you're a product manager who needs a clear roadmap, a developer or designer looking for guidance and advocacy, or a businessperson who wants to understand and manage user-experience software initiatives.

    Learn how to build software that will:

    - Generate engaging and interactive experiences between consumers and businesses, or between businesspeople and their information systems

    - Account for how people work with, think about, and consume information

    - Establish a richer means of collaboration and communication

    - Reduce frustration by streamlining complex tasks and creating processes that are more intuitive

    - Distinguish products, services, and brands to create a competitive advantage

    - Create scalable systems that adapt to changing user needs and behaviors
