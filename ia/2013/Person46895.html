Rich Garella's Summary

Communications professional and University of Pennsylvania graduate with an unusually diverse background in media, political development and project management.

Most recently, producer of "Who Killed Chea Vichea?," a Peabody Award–winning investigative documentary, funded through and broadcast by US public television.

Previously, coordinator of a national campaign for electoral integrity for MoveOn.org, member of communications technology team at The New York Times, consultant for USAID-funded democratic development agency, press officer for Cambodian political party, editor of daily newspapers in Cambodia, in addition to a wide variety of consulting roles.

Adept political analyst, expert writer and editor, visionary systems builder, effective team facilitator and methodical planner and project manager. Broad knowledge of communications technology and understanding of technical trends. Experienced in training organizational staff, journalists and political organizers. Experience in Cambodia, El Salvador, Nicaragua, Lebanon and the Thai-Burma border. Established record of building individual and organizational capacity in contexts of social and political change. US and Italian citizenship; can relocate.

  • Philly's Ballot Box

    • May 2007 to Present
    Team Members: Rich Garella, Jethro Heiko, Nick Jehlen, Karim Olaechea

    After Pennsylvania's courts struck a referendum on casino placement from the official ballot, ﻿Casino-Free Philadelphia planned and executed a successful "citizens’ ballot," held on the same day. As de facto election commissioner, I oversaw the development of an integrated multiplatform voting system, devised a secure chain of ballot custody and transparent balloting and counting procedures, and led training of more than 200 volunteers in polling, closing and tabulation procedures. Developed in three weeks, our voting system collected and counted more than 13,000 verifiable votes. The project restored the ability of Philadelphia's voters to express their opinions on casino location and earned favorable media coverage, including an editorial endorsement from the Philadelphia Inquirer (see Project URL).

  • Polygraph

    • November 2003 to March 2004
    Team Members: Rich Garella, Adam Lucas Feinstein

    Polygraph was one of the most talked-about ads of the 2004 US presidential election. Made for the MoveOn.org Voter Fund's Bush in 30 Seconds contest, it was aired by MoveOn in a $1.5 million campaign in 21 swing-state television markets and on CNN. Co-written by Rich Garella (producer) and Adam Feinstein (director).
