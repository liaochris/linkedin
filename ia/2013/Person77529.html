Kurt Lighthouse's Summary

With a sharp mind for concept and a keen eye for design, Kurt has an extensive background in building brands and creating compelling communication across media. His award-winning work has run the gamut from online and broadcast to print and merchandise design including the platinum selling “Instant Karma: The Campaign to Save Darfur” CD for Amnesty International.

It may be a cliché, but Kurt began his career in the mailroom – part time – at Cohn and Wells in San Francisco. A few months later, he joined Gardner, Geary, Coll & Young. After a misguided attempt at account management, Kurt lobbied to be placed in the creative department. Shortly thereafter, he was promoted to Senior Art Director – a natural progression for someone with a Political Science degree from Colgate University.

In 1998, Kurt left to help start a new agency, Swirl, and joined Collaborate as an Associate Creative Director in 1999. In 2006 he co-founded Citizen, a new kind of advertising agency dedicated to serving clients with socially positive agendas, where he served as VP and Creative Director.

Kurt's work has received dozens of awards and honors including the One Show, the Addys, Archive Magazine, Creative Arts, the Effies and the Webbys.
