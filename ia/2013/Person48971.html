Glenda Bonin's Summary

I have been a storyteller, story teacher, festival performer and workshop presenter for more than twenty-five years, and I love what I do!
My background includes fourteen years as a public relations, marketing and communications specialist for non-profit groups, and three years in the television industry. I have served on the boards of several community organizations, and coordinated four successful fund-raising events. I have written and produced an educational film for a museum, and on three occasions I received awards and recognition for my work in PR and communications.
I am an experienced innovator who appreciates the value of transferring skills and learning from one area to another. Life provides many opportunities to grow and contribute to the world around us, and this is what I constantly strive to do. Before I entered the regular workforce, I was a free-lance professional clown, magician and puppeteer. Each time I approach a new project, I call on many of the valuable skills I learned during that period of my life. In my current work as a touring performer, I am pleased to be able to share a lifetime of experience and innovation with others through the art of storytelling.

Specialties

As a touring artist and storyteller, I specialize in school assemblies and residency work for grades K through 8. Other areas of expertise include library programs, conference workshops and community events.

Interests:

I have produced five CDs of stories ("Tails You Might Have Herd Before - or Knot," "Winston, the Wide-Mouth Frog," "The Dead Don't Die in Tuttle," "Dusty Vail Tales." and "The Family Gazette." "Winston the Wide-Mouth Frog" is a 2009 Parenting Magazine merit award winning CD. A project I continue to develop is called "The Manicurist, The Millionaire and The Secret," a multi-media Chautauqua is about a rags-to-riches - riches-to-rags American woman's celebration of life from 1900 to 1958. My current efforts are to bring a non-profit organization and a for-profit business into a partnership to help produce an educational show for targeted communities across the United States in 2012.
