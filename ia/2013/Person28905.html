Damian Herrick's Summary

I love to find a needles in a haystack; hunting down or generating a huge dataset (or several), doing the dirty work of creating clean data, but most importantly, analyzing data to reach pragmatic, sensible guidance for business. I'm a scientist and coder at heart with a mind for business; I love to help companies make data-driven decisions for their business.

My business is built on this core value - your critical business needs and decisions should be guided by data of all forms. Your financial, transactional, marketing, and sales data live inside your business, and are augmented by social media, demographic, and other outside data sources. Together it all provides a true view of your business. I founded Lake Hill Analytics with the express goal of helping companies better understand and make better business decisions with their data.

Specialties: Data analysis, data collection, predictive analytics, software development, marketing analytics and research, product management, new product development, innovation management.

Damian Herrick's Publications

  • Predicting seismic velocity and other rock properties from clay content only

    • Geophysics, VOL. 68, NO. 6 (NOVEMBER-DECEMBER 2003); P. 1847–1856
    • November 30, 2003
    Authors: Damian Herrick, Lincoln Pratson, Anastasia Stroujkova, Fred Boadu, Peter Malin

    It has been shown that accurate predictions of com- pressional and shear-wave velocities can be derived from measurements of clay content, porosity, and bulk density. In this paper, we test the efficacy of using a sequence of established petrophysical formulations to go a step further and predict porosity and bulk density as well as velocity from clay content alone. Other variables, like grain and pore-fluid properties, are assumed to be con- stant, while empirical relationships are used to model such behaviors as grain packing and compaction.
