Who needs a ChemCert Accreditation?

Anyone who uses pesticides including herbicides, insecticides, fungicides and other chemicals or anyone seeking work as a parkland or green keeper, lawn mower, market gardener, flower grower, ground rig operator, landscape gardener, nursery operator, marina operator, wood preservation operator, farmer, or anyone who intends to use pesticides as a volunteer or on behalf of a company, local council or government agency needs to have a current chemical accreditation.

Legal Requirements: Under the NSW Pesticides Act 1999 anyone who uses pesticides in their business or as part of their job, or as a volunteer, must be trained and accredited in how to safely and legally use those pesticides. The ChemCert Course is recognised by the Office of Environment and Heritage, Department of Premier and Cabinet (formerly the EPA) as meeting the training requirements of the NSW Pesticides Act 1999.

Activities and Societies: Accreditation with National Industry body http://www.ChemCert.com.au/ providing training to all people involved in the safe handling use and transport of pesticides.
