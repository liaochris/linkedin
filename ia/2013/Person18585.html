Bryan Rhoads' Summary

Strategist focused on social and emerging media. Long-time early-stage builder of digital experiences. I founded the Intel Media Lab in May of 2012.

I currently lead content marketing for Intel's Integrated Media Team leading Intel's global content strategy across an emerging and evolved media landscape. I'm also the designer and "Editor-in-Chief" for iQ by Intel - iQ.intel.com launched in May 2012.

At Intel, I pioneered social media techniques that laid the foundations for Intel to leverage Web 2.0. In 2006, I designed and built Intel’s external blog platform at http://blogs.intel.com, establishing communications, legal, security and publishing guidelines that integrated social media into Intel’s marketing mix.

Starting in 2001, I led a 4-year research project with MIT that researched online trust and digital marketing strategies. My Intel and MIT Sloan researchers designed innovative approaches to increase user confidence and trust in online media experiences and communications. I am also the designer of the Intel Download Center and many high profile sections of Intel’s web systems.

I'm a regular speaker on digital innovation and social media with appearances at the Yale Center for Customer Insights, MIT Center for Digital Business, Innotech, SxSW, the Marketing Sciences Institute, etc.

On the side, I'm adjunct Marketing faculty at Portland State University where I teach Digital Marketing. I sit on the Executive Board of the Center for Consumer Research at St. Joseph’s University, the Marketing Advisory Track at Northeastern University and a member of the City Club of Portland’s New Leaders Council.

I moonlight as a grassroots bio-fuels advocate as well as an urban farmer in my backyard in Portland, Oregon w/ my lovely wife Shannon and two cute kids.
