Chris Hoogewerff's Summary

I’m an entrepreneurial adventurer at heart, and I love to learn and explore. I found my love for adventure and the outdoors on my 60-day solo cross-country road trip where I ended up national park hopping across the continent. Since then, I’ve taken every opportunity to get out there and explore. I often write about about my adventures along the way.

Professionally, I’m interested in the intersection of product, community management and excellent customer service. I kicked off my career by co-founding – and ultimately shutting down – a startup called Hello Parking, where we tried to monetize parking vacancies in the same way travel agencies book low unadvertised fares for hotels and air travel.

From there, I joined QuickPay, another startup in the parking space, where I’m currently the product manager. With QuickPay, we're adding a mobile commerce layer on top of the parking industry.

I love to learn, and am always on the hunt to acquire new skills that will make me more productive. Here are some I've picked up along the way:

• High-fidelity prototyping (InvisionApp, Balsaimq, Photoshop)
• Designing and prioritizing product requirements, managing and prioritizing bugs and feature requests (Jira, Confluence, FogBugz)
• Writing clear user stories for engineering teams
• Highly effective/targeted outbound and inbound marketing (MailChimp, Hubspot, Content Creation)
• Customer support and outreach
• User behavior and metrics analysis (MixPanel, Klaviyo, MySQL)
• Effective, courteous and enthusiastic communication
• Blogging and writing with an engaging “voice”
