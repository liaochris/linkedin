Copy editor, RedEye

Tribune Company

Privately Held; 10,001+ employees; Newspapers industry

January 2010 – Present (3 years 9 months)

My job is never lacking for depth. In addition to performing all the duties of a copy editor (ensuring stories are clean, writing captions and headlines, posting stories online, etc.), staff members at RedEye are required to generate ideas and lend any insight that would be beneficial to our readers. We are also encouraged to pursue projects we enjoy, and for me that includes writing sports columns.

My skills have expanded considerably in this job. Generating story ideas, working with reporters, organizing the section, providing input on design, posting stories to the Web, editing stories and writing headlines are all important components of my position. I also write a weekly column.

Assistant city editor, The Naperville Sun

Sun Publications

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Newspapers industry

March 2005 – November 2006 (1 year 9 months)

This job included all the duties of the news editor position I held previously with The Naperville Sun before the former position was phased out. But as assistant city editor I also pulled wire stories for our national and world coverage, tracked corrections in the newspaper and helped make corrections directly on pages. It is one of the most pivotal roles in the newsroom, as this person is in charge for the last half of the evening.

News editor, The Naperville Sun
