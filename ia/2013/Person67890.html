Jonathan Gosier's Summary

Jon Gosier is a globally recognized software developer, designer and philanthropist. He is the CEO of D8A a design agency and innovation firm that helps corporations and governments deploy data visualization and civic technology projects around the globe.

He is also the founder of Africa's tech consulting firm Appfrica and big-data startup MetaLayer. He is also the co-founder of the non-profit organizations AfriLabs, HiveColab and Abayima. He has held a Executive leadership roles at other tech for development organization's Ushahidi and QuestionBox.

In 2008 he founded Appfrica, one of Africa’s leading consultancies to help technology and media companies enter African consumer markets. Appfrica devotes a portion of its profits to angel investments into Africa’s next generation of brilliant minds through its Apps4Africa Accelerator.

In 2009 Jon spoke at TED in Oxford, UK about is work connecting rural African villages to the internet through a call center and light infrastructure. He is a two time Knight News Challenge Award recipient and alum of the THNK School for Creative Leadership.

Data is used an increasingly powerful force of control in our lives. Jon’s mission is to democratize access to tools used for interprting data, to ensure that everyone retains a voice in an increasingly complex world.

  • The New Challenge of Data Inflation

    • InQTel Quarterly
    • November 10, 2011
    Authors: Jonathan Gosier

    The online chatter of individuals, networks of friends or professionals, and the data being created by networked devices are growing exponentially, although our time to consume it all remains depressingly finite.     • May 5, 2010
    Authors: Jonathan Gosier

With all these channels of communication comes a deluge of overwhelming retweets, cross-chatter, spam, and inaccuracies. How do you distinguish signal from noise without getting overwhelmed? Can we somewhat automate the process of filtering content into more manageable portions without sacrificing accuracy and relevance?

Jonathan Gosier's Projects

  • Apps4Africa

    • August 2009 to Present
    Team Members: Jonathan Gosier, Mariéme Jamme, Bahiyah Yasmeen Robinson

    Apps4Africa is a unique acceleration program designed to discover, fund, and help scale Africa's brightest startup companies and social enterprises. We work with both for-profit companies as well as non-profits and offer $10,000 in funding under favorable terms. Our program is Pan-African, servicing innovators from across the African continent. With our Challenges program we

    There are two ways to be accepted into the Apps4Africa Acceleration program, through our Challenges or as an Entrepreneur-In-Residence program. Challenges are coordinated with fiscally sponsoring partners who work with us to shape a competitive funding opportunities for companies that meet specific criteria.

    Apps4Africa Entrepreneurs-in-Residence are impressive individuals working on an impressive product or service that has high potential for growth. We work with individuals and companies in any sector and at any stage of their journey to help them find greater opportunity than they might on their own. These entrepreneurs also participate in the Apps4Africa Accelerator. As a condition of our investment we're a bit more involved in these ventures.

    Established in 2009 by Appfrica, we're one of Africa's oldest acceleration programs for entrepreneurs.

  • Abayima

    • February 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Jonathan Gosier, Matthew Griffiths, Ahmed Mohamed Maawy, Barbara Birungi, Bahiyah Yasmeen Robinson

    The Abayima initiative was launched to help protect vulnerable citizens when mobile communication networks are compromised by malicious parties or oppressive governments. It works by turning the world’s existing 4 billion simple feature phones into e-readers.

  • GlobalHealthHub.org

    • May 2010 to Present
    Team Members: Jonathan Gosier

    The Global Health Hub is a 100% volunteer driven effort to provide an online gateway to news, commentary and resources related to global health and development from around the web. The Hub aggregates content from traditional and non-mainstream sources to help people interested and involved in global health & development to quickly find information.

    The site’s programming team is led by Michael Lipnick (@mlipnick) and TED Senior Fellow Jon Gosier (@jongos).
