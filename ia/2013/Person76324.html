Klokie Grossfeld's Summary

Daniel “Klokie” Grossfeld is a senior Web architect and entrepreneur with 16 years of experience engineering and designing complex data-driven websites, e-commerce, and media distribution systems for premier international clients. A founding partner of four enterprises in Europe and the US, Klokie is an ambitious, hard-working and agile leader with experience working in start-ups in Stockholm, New York, Paris, Barcelona, London, and Boston, and has a track record of delivering high-performing and well-designed solutions that build upon Web standards and open source technology. Currently based in Stockholm, Klokie is active as the Technical Director and co-founder of ODALISQUE Magazine, a critically-acclaimed online platform for exposing new talent in in fashion, arts, and culture, and as technology consultant for international clients within the design, fashion, film and music industries as well as several pan-European research projects.

Klokie Grossfeld's Publications

  • Specification of Media Related User Profiles

    • SARACEN (EU FP7)
    • May 1, 2012
    Authors: Klokie Grossfeld, Darren Mothersele, Neil Harris, Daniel Harris, Charalampos Z. Patrikakis, Lemonia Argyriou, N. Papaoulakis, Christina Androulaki, Stuart Porter, Dora Christofi, Elena Garrido

    This document outlines a proposal for the use of protocols and methods for ingestion and exchange of personal information between distributed social networking services, and for the standardisation of user profiles with support for including references to external audio and video metadata and user-specified preferences. The intent is to extend the current state of the art with respect to profile management within user-centric social networking.

    A survey of current best practices for encoding and distributing user and content metadata is provided in addition to a detailed analysis of user profiles related to me-dia and content preferences for market leading social network services (SNS). Extracting from these services the relevant metadata for creating profiles suitable for making content and user recommendations, this specification defines profile structures and methodology for exchange of user and content metadata across infrastructural and domain boundaries.

  • Social Networks Use of Personal Information Interfaces and Protection Of Privacy

    • SARACEN (EU FP7)
    • May 1, 2012
    Authors: Klokie Grossfeld, Darren Mothersele, Neil Harris, Daniel Harris, Lemonia Argyriou, Charalampos Z. Patrikakis, N. Papaoulakis, Christina Androulaki, Naeem Ramzan, Stuart Porter, Elena Garrido

    This document outlines a proposal for the use of protocols and methods for protecting users’ privacy and identities, as part of the exchange of personal information and preferences between distributed social networking services. Focusing on the dominant aspect in networking applications design – socialisation – it addresses those aspects of the user profile that are most affected by privacy concerns, and how they can be protected within a growing social network. Using the examples of the market leaders within this domain as well as ad hoc standards and best practices for social networking services, a prototype web service validation and testing framework for data privacy protection methods is described.

  • Enhanced User Experience Through Personalisation

    • SARACEN (EU FP7)
    • 2011
    Authors: Klokie Grossfeld, Dora Christofi, Georgios Dimosthenous, Charalampos Patrikakis, Chrysanthi Stefanoudaki, Christina Androulaki, Nikolaos Papaoulakis, Stuart Porter, Elena Garrido, Jeldrik Schmuch

    This deliverable focuses on the study of existing methods and tools which enhance the personalisation of the user’s media streaming experience through the SARACEN platform. The deliverable determines which techniques and mechanisms will be used by the SARACEN platform in order to provide personalised content to the user.
