Ali Mattu, Ph.D.'s Summary

I received by Ph.D. in clinical psychology from The Catholic University of America in Washington, D.C. I was born and raised in Silicon Valley and studied psychology at UCLA.

I am currently a post-doctoral fellow in clinical psychology at the NYU Langone Medical Center Department of Child and Adolescent Psychiatry/Child Study Center. Clinically, I treat anxiety, depression, and body-focused repetitive behaviors (trichotillomania/hair-pulling disorder, skin-picking, nail-biting).

Most recently I’ve focused my research on understanding how trichotillomania changes with age.

I am an award-winning teacher and regularly teach psychology and cognitive-behavior therapy.

I serve as the Past-Chair of the American Psychological Association of Graduate Students Committee.

Specialties

Cognitive Behavioral Treatment of Anxiety and Depression, Collaborative Teaching Methods, Trichotillomania Research
