Brooke Francesi's Summary

Brooke Francesi is a graphic designer and illustrator based in San Francisco, California. She currently serves as Associate Creative Director at B'stro, an interactive marketing and design agency based in San Francisco and Vancouver, Canada; she works on accounts like JOHNSON'S® Baby, University of California San Francisco, BC Centre for Disease Control, Valeant Pharmaceuticals, Roku, and SPLENDA®. Previously, she was Team Lead and Project Manager for the the Web Marketing division at Aptera Software in Fort Wayne, Indiana, and taught for 3 semesters as a Guest Lecturer in the Department of Visual Communication and Design at IPFW.

In addition to her daytime mission of making the world a more understandable and pleasurable place to live through design, Brooke is interested in typography as the basis of design; she wants to help others appreciate letterforms as physical structures. She regularly embarks on a variety of side projects that encourage social and physical interaction.

    Lecture Overview:

    What am I doing? We tend to ask ourselves this question too often — or not nearly enough. It’s an uncomplicated question with a complicated answer.     Gallery 21Ten
    • July 2013

    Artist Statement:
    More often than not, artists become collectors and curators in their own right — hoarding materials that may someday be amassed into a piece of work. The act is driven by fear. Any hoarded object could, at some point, be the missing piece to the perfect work of art. In an attempt to expose their tendencies to accumulate, artists Abigail VanCannon, Victoria Montgomery, and Brooke Francesi highlight their individual hoarding weaknesses and explore art that could be created from them.
