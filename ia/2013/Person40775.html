Elizabeth Santos' Summary

My approach is strategic rather than merely transactional. I specialize in creating business solutions that integrate an organization's strategy and vision. Due to my comprehensive knowledge of lease transactions, as well as an understanding of the Miami Market, I have established myself as a market leader and recognized expert in my market place.

I focus both on representing tenants and landlords. For landlords, I create strong, creative leasing strategies that provide solutions. Over the years, I have completed over 650 lease transactions so my experience is extensive.

For tenants, I work with companies seeking to renew or relocate their office lease. I also help companies dispose of unneeded office space by either subleasing the office or negotiating an early termination.

Contact me for a free consultation of your current situation and market analysis. Also, take a look at my blog: www.miamiofficenotes.com for my most recent articles and ramblings on office space.

Specialties: Office Brokerage for both Landlords and Tenants

Elizabeth Santos' Experience

Vice President

Sime Realty Corp.

August 2011 – Present (2 years 3 months) Miami, FL

I am focused on two things -- clients and office space in South Florida. For office users, I want to help my clients make informed decisions about their office space. Companies need to be sure they have all the facts and information possible before making a decision that will cost their company a significant portion of their operating expenses. I provide the information and guidance so that the decision-makers have the tools necessary to make this big decision.

For landlords, I deliver results. For example, in March 2012, I began representing the Offices at Palmetto Bay Village Center, a 280,000 sq. ft. office park in Palmetto Bay. In just a year, I (with my partner Scott Sime) leased over 78,000 sq. ft. at the property. I have expertise in space planning, lease negotiation, financial analysis, budgeting and construction management.

I achieve great results for both landlords and tenants by being an expert on office space in South Florida. With over 20 years as an office broker in Miami, my experience and connections are strong and deep. If I don't have the answer, I can easily find it in a couple of phone calls.

If you need an outside opinion about your particular situation, give me a call.

Senior Associate

CB Richard Ellis

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; CBG; Real Estate industry

October 2002 – July 2011 (8 years 10 months)

I focused primarily on tenant representation for office users primarily in the Miami, but I have been known to wander into Ft. Lauderdale and represent the occasional landlord.

My landlord work while at CBRE included representing Airport Financial Center, a 100,000 sq. ft. office building in Miami Springs. I also represented Corporate Park of Doral, a 200,000 sq. ft. office park in the heart of Doral from 2006 - 2010.

I provide a full range of services to clients including market surveys and tours, financial analysis of proposals from various landlords, negotiation of lease terms, construction management and abstract of new lease terms. My goal is to help clients make informed real estate decisions that best fit their unique situation.

I act as a liaison between tenant and landlord. I will even attend space planning meetings and follow up with each party to assure timely construction process.

During my tenure at CBRE, I completed over 500,000 SF of lease transactions within Miami Dade County with a focus on the Airport West submarket. I am an acknowledged expert in this submarket and have negotiated multiple transactions with every major landlord in the submarket.

Leasing Manager

Jones Lang LaSalle

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; JLL; Commercial Real Estate industry

October 2001 – August 2002 (11 months)

My tenure at JLL was to faciliate the transfer of the management & leasing program from Koger to JLL, the new third-party management & leasing team.

Senior Leasing Manager

Koger Realty Services, Inc.

March 1994 – September 2001 (7 years 7 months)

As the Leasing Manager for the Koger Center (now called the Downtown Doral), I was responsible for the leasing activity of the Koger Center, a 30-building, 1.2 million sf office park. My financial duties included projecting income, leasing commissions and tenant improvements for annual budgets and I prepared the financial analysis of each proposed lease transaction.

While with Koger, my accomplishments included securing the largest lease (27,000 sf) for the Davenport Building, a 74,000 sf speculative office building, completed in January 2000. I also had a successful track record with government leases resulting in over 30,000 sf of new leases plus renewals of over 120,000 sf. My lease renewal rate had an overall average of 80% retention.

I also developed all the marketing materials for the property as well as a quarterly newsletter for the office park. I also began a series of broker golf incentives to raise broker awareness of the property. This was all creatively accomplished without any formal marketing budget.

In 1996, I was instrumental in due diligence tasks relating to the sale of the property in 1995 to a co-mingled pension trust fund administered by J.P. Morgan Investments. This included doggedly tracking down over 75 tenant estoppel letters one by one.
