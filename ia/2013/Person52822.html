Henry Rock's Summary

Over the years, working as a professional in media, advertising, marketing, business development and strategic planning mostly with small businesses, including my own, I’ve seen a need that’s reached a critical mass — the need to create sustained economic vitality in inner-city America. City Startup Labs was started, in part to help motivate and drive entrepreneurial involvement amongst young black men to do just that.

Henry Rock's Experience

Executive Director

City Startup Labs, Inc. | Non-Profit

Nonprofit; 1-10 employees; Program Development industry

April 2012 – Present (1 year 6 months) Charlotte, NC & New York, NY

The City Startup Labs mission is to stimulate entrepreneurial activity and innovation within inner-city America. We’re launching a demonstration pilot Academy to teach entrepreneurship, primarily to young men of color. The intention is to target accelerated instruction to the best and brightest within this population, who have the latent potential to be productive small business owners. The principal foci include ex-offenders, former gang members and other “at-risk” young adult males, on the front-end of possibilities, rather than the back-end of the criminal justice system.

The City Startup Labs - Entrepreneurs Academy will conduct a unique 15-week instructional intensive for aspiring entrepreneurs from within this population. The students will be paced through a progressive series of modules designed to hone a well prepared cadre of young black men, equipped to use their ingenuity, creativity, inspiration and drive to deploy a new series of inner-city businesses.

CSL is working with the Urban League of the Central Carolinas, in Charlotte, NC on the first installation of the academy.

ComLoc, Corp. [http://www.comloc.com] helps small businesses address their core sales and marketing issues through advisory, strategic and action planning services. The ComLoc process starts with a needs assessment, then a set of results-oriented strategic and action plans. These turn-key plans are designed to be easily implemented with or without ComLoc's ongoing involvement.

ComLoc was also involved with the design, launch and marketing of new media and internet properties, including CowrieCards.com a peer-to-peer greetings website targeting the African-American niche market.

During the early to mid 2000's, while I was working in commercial real estate, I refocused ComLoc on the promotion of the alternate use of intermodal shipping containers for live/work space.

ComLoc's first project was in 2002. It included doing business acquisition planning and developing buy-out and business plans for the acquisition of computer training centers in New York City, including the Career Center.
