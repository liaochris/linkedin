Corey Krall's Summary

I have been lucky to be in the right place at the right time, twice. I started on my career path as an education curriculum researcher with the Kennedy Center and all of our research was posted online on a gopher site. We started tinkering with this new technology called the World Wide Web playing with Lynx and NCSA Mosaic. The need to quickly migrate our content to the web was apparent and I changed gears in learning how to create a website from the ground up. Twenty years later.

After a few job transitions, I found myself with this little start-up called Blackboard. We were a small start-up of about 25 people. I remember our 10th client, or first million in revenue and the late nights developing prototype courseware. Toward the end of my tenure, we were on the cusp of going public with over 750 employees and over 125 million in revenue with multiple office locations across the country and the company with a market cap of $1.25 billion.

Throughout my career I have worn many hats; designer, developer, creative director, researcher, analyst, and all around tinkerer. I have experienced many different environments from performing arts institutions, telecommunications, federal government contracting, start-ups, small businesses, higher education and health care, to vineyards.

My strengths may be design and finding creative solutions, but at the end of the day, I am a problem solver. I love to create and build and take experiences from other areas and put them together in different ways to develop new things.
