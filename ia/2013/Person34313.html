Silicon Valley Open Doors Conference

March 2006 – Present (7 years 9 months)

Despite the significant scientific and innovation potential of the Former Soviet republics, the gap still exists between the technology entrepreneurship in these countries and the global market. SVOD conference is the only event of its kind bringing dozens of high-tech innovators from Russia and Ukraine and other FSU countries together with US sources of capital. On the one hand, it introduces American venture capital community to ideas, technologies, and entrepreneurial talent that come out of universities and research labs in the Former Soviet Union. On the other, SVOD provides an unparalleled opportunity for the Russian and Ukrainian entrepreneurs to commercialize their ideas globally, learn American venture fundraising practices, acquire necessary business acumen and skills, meet US investors, and create partnership ties with Silicon Valley’s ecosystem.

www.svod.org

Yam Labs

October 2010 – August 2011 (11 months)

Meetings are an integral part of everyday business life, as all modern workplaces are built on teams (often geographically spread out), idea sharing, and effective project coordination and communication. Meetings create the workspace where we share our ideas and develop new direction, where deals are won or lost, where strategies are debated - in short - where we collaborate with others. That's what it's all about, people meeting with people - board sessions, committees, sales and project status meetings, seminars, other appointments as well as major conferences. Moreover, meetings can now be face-to-face, teleconference, videoconference, or online via the Internet. Love them or hate them, you can’t do business without them. However, despite these facts, many meetings are typically thought of as inefficient due to lack of clear objectives, poor management and unprepared participants.

Since we are fans of efficacy, we, at yaM Labs, are here to provide you with an online platform to exchange your real-life experience of why meetings go wrong, how to avoid wasting time while arranging, executing and closing meetings, and what exactly can help make meetings become efficient. Being software geeks (in a good way), we are currently working on a web application that delivers full-cycle support for meeting management activities – from meeting conception to meeting closure. mCloud (as we affectionately call this application), while being useful, will be free for its users. We hope that one day you’ll enjoy mCloud to the fullest extent, we’ll keep you updated on this project status.
Meanwhile, feel free to browse or contribute to our library of meeting-related articles, exchange or discuss ideas or ask any questions you might have regarding our website and product.
