Libby Davy's Summary

I enable people at all stages of life to use creative expression. This supports their wellbeing + contributes to a more compassionate, resilient + productive society. I have 20 year's experience in multi-arts practice + facilitation, project management, research, education, stakeholder relations + communications. Specialisation in working with people, communication, writing, photography, short film, online, visual arts + events. Experience with vulnerable groups.

My research interests + practice includes: compassion + empathy in society, arts at the end-of-life, early years, mindfulness, therapeutic outcomes in art, social movements, relational aesthetic, dialogical art, community arts, social enterprise, deep ecology, strategic planning, sustainability + person-centred education.

I am a Fellow of the Royal Society for the Arts, a member of Blank Studios and have exhibited in Australia + the UK. My work has been broadcast + published nationally + I have won national + international awards + commendations.

Triratna (was Friends of the Western Buddhist Order)

Buddhist philosophy + practice

2006 – 2020 (expected)

Currently in third year of Dharma studies course, based at Brighton Buddhist Centre. In a weekly group to reflect on practice + progress + receive regular mentoring from very experienced practitioners.

Triratna have a commitment to holistic growth integrating mind, body, spirit through person-centred, humanistic, transpersonal approaches and a focus on creative expression. Centres throughout UK, Europe, Australia, USA, India.

Prior to joining the Triratna community I have been involved with various other forms of Buddhism and was introduced to meditation and Buddhism by my mother when I was young.

I see it as a philosophy, a mind-training + way of life rather than as a religion, as I can be sceptical about religion (or fundamentalism in any form). All major religions + belief systems have compassion or kindness as their golden rule. This is essentially what I follow.
