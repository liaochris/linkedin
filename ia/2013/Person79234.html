Laurie Morgan's Publications

  • Get the Best From Your Medical Billing Service (a Management Rx ebook)

    • Working Strategy
    Authors: Laurie Morgan

    Whether you've already hired a medical billing service or are just considering outsourcing, your reasons are likely the same: you're looking for more revenue, more reliability, and a lot less hassle. Replacing your in-house billing team with a service can be a big step towards these goals, and towards a much stronger, more profitable practice -- if you do it right.

    In this easy-to-read, focused guide, you'll learn the essential keys to getting maximum value from your decision to work with a billing service -- and, how to avoid pitfalls that could cost your practice thousands of dollars. Managing your medical billing is one of a handful of major decisions that mean the difference between a thriving practice and one that struggles -- investing just a little bit of time with this information-packed e-book will help you make your best decision and set your practice on a path to strength and profitability.

    "Get the Best From Your Medical Billing Service" is the first volume in the MANAGEMENT RX line of focused business e-books for physicians and practice managers. MANAGEMENT RX is a series of concise guides for busy physicians and practice administrators. Each e-book tackles a very specific medical practice management issue in a ‘single dose’ that can be read easily in one sitting.

  • Doing Things the MBA Way

    • Practice Link
    • November 1, 2011
    Authors: Laurie Morgan, Joe Capko

    Medicine is a business, yet today's physicians receive no business training in medical school. Running a practice -- or managing a career within a large healthcare organization -- may not require an MBA, but developing some of the skills that are part of MBA training is a must. This article outlines some of the ways in which physicians' training actually runs counter-intuitive to business interactions, such as in negotiating, marketing and networking. It also offers some ideas for basic business skills doctors can pick up without pursuing academic business training -- and, some ideas for choosing an MBA program for physicians who want to go that route.

  • Watch Their Backs

    • Repertoire Magazine
    • September 15, 2012
    Authors: Laurie Morgan

    It's a sad fact that physicians get ripped off every day. Some sources estimate that more than 90% of medical practices are victims of internal theft -- and that's not the only way doctors are robbed. Records theft, overcharging and even insurance fraud bite into physician compensation every day.

    This article explains some of the ways doctors suffer losses at the hands of cheaters and criminals -- and how reps can help them avoid unnecessary victimization.

  • The End of the World as We Know It?

    • Repertoire Magazine
    • November 15, 2012
    Authors: Laurie Morgan

    Physicians and industry watchers alike have been predicting the imminent demise of the private practice for what seems like forever now -- and the drumbeat has only grown stronger in 2012. Should we be worried – or write off the hype as hyperbole?

    With examples from our experiences at Capko & Company, this article suggests that, like the old saying goes, we should never mistake a clear view for a short distance. The private practice side of medicine is clearly undergoing transformation -- but, in the short term, that change can take many forms. By spotlighting some of the unexpected ways the private practice consolidation movement has played out, this article aims to help reps take advantage of the changing environment and better serve their clients.
