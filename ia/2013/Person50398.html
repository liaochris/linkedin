Greg Rubenstein's Summary

As communication and marketing vice president for the founding institution of osteopathic medicine, I strive to reflect in my work the tenants of whole person healthcare. Defining how that occurs is perhaps as challenging as it sometimes is to convey how an online master’s or doctoral health administration, public health or health education program imbues that same philosophy; the answer is found in the process—in the desire and commitment—as much as in the actual application.

I am truly blessed that each day I am able to pursue my professional passions; convey communication, marshal marketing, clout social media, strategize, optimize, mediate, mitigate, facilitate and determinate, all while working with extremely talented colleagues in an environment that nurtures innovation while serving a noble social mission.

About A.T. Still University of Health Sciences
A.T. Still University (ATSU) is home of the world’s first osteopathic medical school, established in 1892 by Andrew Taylor Still. ATSU is comprised of two campuses (Kirksville, Missouri and Mesa, Arizona) on more than 200 acres with six prestigious schools. Learning environments include online and on campus options as well as community based partnerships worldwide. Renowned for its preeminence as a multidisciplinary healthcare educator, ATSU is focused on integrating the founding tenants of osteopathic medicine and the advancing knowledge of today’s science. ATSU instills within students the compassion, experience and knowledge required to address the whole person and shape healthcare in communities where needs are greatest. The University is consistently ranked in U.S. News and World Report’s “Best Graduate Schools” guide.
