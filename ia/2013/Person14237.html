Bill Creighton's Summary

Bill Creighton is a senior business executive with significant experience transforming news, information technology and non-profit companies into successful, innovative online and interactive businesses. His career spans more than 25 years and 76 countries and five continents.

Bill is a turnaround specialist, a visionary digital strategist, organizational leader, innovative deal-maker and award-winning journalist with two Pulitzer Prize nominations. He has an impressive record of successfully building, restructuring and repositioning both start-up and established businesses.

His skills and talent include: Executive Leadership. Turnaround/Restructuring, Strategic Planning, Intellectual Property, Global Business Development, Sales and Marketing Leadership, Financial Management , Strategic Communications, Strategic Partnerships, Start-up Expansion, Data Analytics, Emerging Technologies, SaaS

Bill Creighton's Experience

Managing Director

Dakota Partners

2001 – Present (12 years) Middleburg, Virginia

Dakota Partners is a strategic advisory firm providing consulting engagements for start-up and establish media, entertainment, non-profit and technology companies advising firms on business planning, strategic partnerships, technology development, go-to-market strategies, fund-raising and acquisition strategies.

Board Member

Airborne Lifeline Foundation

Nonprofit; Myself Only; Nonprofit Organization Management industry

2011 – Present (2 years) Washington D.C. Metro Area

Airborne Lifeline Foundation, a 501(c)(3) charitable organization working in a public-private partnership with the governments of Botswana and Malawi in southern Africa.

The charity provides a critical link flying doctors, medical personnel and vital life-saving medicines to address major health care issues including the prevention and treatment of HIV/AIDS, TB, malaria, hepatitis A, and typhoid fever.

Airborne’s service offers important benefits to health care professionals that include:

• Travel time for health care professionals limited to a few hours by air rather than a few days by road

• Quick transport of time- and temperature-sensitive medications and tissue samples

• A regular schedule so that patients can plan visits to see specialists and receive treatments at local clinics when the specialists and supplies are available

Many of the health care professionals are citizens of Botswana and are readily trusted by patients living in remote areas. Among the health care professionals visiting the regional clinics each week are specialist doctors, physical and occupational therapists, nutritionists, senior midwives, and medical trainers.

President and CEO

Council to Advance Hunting and the Shooting Sports

2011 – 2013 (2 years) Washington D.C. Metro Area

The Council to Advance Hunting and the Shooting Sports was established to bring state fish and wildlife agencies, major conservation groups plus archery and firearms manufacturers together to look at ways to grow hunting and shooting sports participation.

As the first CEO of the Washington, D.C.-based start-up 501 (c)(3) my mission was to identify and develop non-traditional efforts to recruit, engage and retain Customers in the Hunting and Shooting market segments.

We executed parallel efforts to put “boots on the ground” within the first six months providing immediate Customer benefit while developing and leading the Board of 32 in long-range strategic planning.

• Provided critical thought leadership to state Fish and Wildlife Agency Directors empowering them to begin transitioning toward a Customer-centric business model

• Delivered a comprehensive Customer Experience Evaluation for all 50 state Fish and Wildlife Agencies as a launching pad for improving their Customer Engagement

• Developed a social research database and a real time Conservation newswire to provide Fish and Wildlife agencies and NGO staff with important Customer intelligence and new engagement tools

• Designed the framework for the Council’s long-term strategic direction

President and CEO

Fotoglif

2010 – 2011 (1 year) Toronto, Canada Area

Fotoglif was created to address an ever-growing problem of image copyright infringement. The business provided bloggers and other Web publishers images taken by the same professional shooters who supply news agencies around the world in exchange for advertising inventory attached to the image or micro-licensing fees.

I was recruited to commercialize Toronto-based start-up company that provided rich visual content to electronic publishers and bloggers. This involved analysis of operations, staff, technology, content alliances, legal, sales and marketing and developed plan to effect the change.

A strategic repositioning plan and Go-To-Market plan was developed focusing the business on the growing blogging network industry.

• Designed and deployed new e-commerce website and back-end infrastructure to enable self-service transactions reducing cost of sale and improving margins

• Grew customer base from 0 to 600+ in 60 days using innovative marketing and outreach strategies

I was the transformational leader of this profitable digital content marketplace with P&L and global operational responsibilities as well as development and implementation of long-term corporate strategies.

• Signed lucrative video distribution agreements with Reuters, BBC, UPI, Splash News, others

• Focused sales efforts on online communities generating $250,000 in new revenue in six months

• Orchestrated the successful sale of the Company to private investors in 2007
