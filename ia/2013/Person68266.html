Jordan Melnick's Summary

Jordan Melnick led development of the Sktchy iPhone app, featured as a "Best New Social Networking App" in the App Store. Using the Sktchy iPhone app, artists around the world have created and shared thousands of original portraits, each inspired by a face in the Sktchy photo gallery. Download it for free at sktchy.com/app.

Jordan is a Miami-based freelance journalist with clips from The New York Times, The Atlantic Cities, The Miami Herald, The Huffington Post and Ocean Drive.

He co-founded the award-winning culture blog Beached Miami and the community art project Sketchy Miami.

Jordan Melnick's Projects

  • Sketchy Miami

    • June 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Jordan Melnick, Robby Campbell

    Sketchy Miami brings Miami artists and the general public together through portraiture.

  • Instapix

    • March 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Jordan Melnick

    Instapix is a gallery of crowdsourced photos shared via Instagram by Miami photographers. The goal is to provide a panoramic view of Miami through the eyes of its most passionate observers.

  • The O, Miami Open Source Epic Poem

    • April 2011 to May 2011
    Team Members: Jordan Melnick

    The O, Miami Open Source Epic Poem was a Beached Miami side project that coincided with the inaugural O, Miami poetry festival and National Poetry Month in 2011. It comprises hundreds of lines of crowd-sourced poetry, each a response to a previous line (except, of course, the very first line -- "O Miami, like a river or a boulevard we begin somewhere" -- which was contributed by acclaimed poet Matthew Zapruder).
