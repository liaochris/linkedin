Janet Sanders' Experience

Owner

Sanders Copy, LLC

2007 – Present (6 years) Albany, NY

Independent marketing copywriter serving clients in a wide range of industries. Content provider for small business web sites; contributor to Honda and Toyota in-house publications; lead writer for newsletter of a major West Coast Foundation. Extensive experience writing for law firms, financial services and wealth management firms.

Sole Proprietor

Sanders & Associates

1983 – 2007 (24 years) Greater Los Angeles Area

Leveraging more than 10 years of professional experience at advertising & PR agencies and in the Marketing Departments of two major financial institutions, I formed my own copywriting business in Los Angeles. Over the years, my clients included most major banks and savings institutions in Los Angeles (until they were acquired by larger institutions and left town), Experian Credit Services, nearly a dozen law firms, a CPA and business management firm serving the entertainment industry, PacifiCare Health Systems, the Jewish Community Foundation of Los Angeles, and Toyota Motor Sales, USA. I ran this business for 14 years, before moving to Albany, NY in 2007.

Advertising and Public Relations Manager

Ticor Title Insurance Company

Public Company; 51-200 employees; fnf; Real Estate industry

1980 – 1983 (3 years) Los Angeles, CA

Managed advertising, marketing and public relations campaigns; wrote and oversaw production of collateral and annual reports; liaison to company's advertising agency; worked with senior management to develop position statements on major issues of interest to the media.

Public Relations Manager

Allstate Savings

1980 – 1982 (2 years)

Handled all media calls, prepared position statements for senior management, wrote press releases, publicized events, arranged media tours, supervised editor of employee newsletter, wrote speeches for senior management.

Copywriter

Mefford Weir Advertising, Denver CO

1974 – 1978 (4 years)

With a MA degree in English Literature and my master’s thesis for a writing sample, I was extremely fortunate to land a copywriting job with the owner of a Denver advertising agency, who was also an award-winning copywriter. During my years at Mefford Weir, I learned that writing effective advertising also meant using incomplete sentences, beginning sentences with "and" and "but," being concise and plain-spoken, rather than complex and erudite. Putting my plans for an academic career (not to mention my academic vocabulary) behind me, I spent eight years writing ads, brochures, radio scripts and PR stories for Denver banks, real estate firms, housing developments, colleges and more--winning numerous awards in the process.

None of this would have happened without my mentor, Jan Weir, to whom I am eternally grateful.
