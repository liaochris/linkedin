Andrea Vollf, NCIDQ, LEED AP ID+C's Summary

Brazilian born, Andrea Vollf is a registered interior designer and sustainability professional with over fifteen years of experience in the design and marketing industries. Upon receiving her Bachelor’s Degree in Social Communication from Escola Superior de Propaganda e Marketing in São Paulo, Brazil, Andrea quit her job at a local advertising agency and moved to Fort Lauderdale, Florida to continue her studies in graphic design and to learn English. A year later she moved to New York City to work as graphic design for a large advertising agency.

After working in New York City as a graphic designer and art director for a few years, Andrea’s passion for design and sustainability led her to a new career in the interior design industry. She expanded her educational background at Parsons The New School for Design and after moving to Schaumburg, she attended The Illinois Institute of Art. Ms. Vollf’s expertise in design and sustainability and her fluency in both English and Portuguese have resulted in her being quoted in several articles across US and Canada and in a book.

Andrea Vollf, NCIDQ, LEED AP ID+C, is an active member of the Elgin Chamber of Commerce and the U.S. Green Building Council - Illinois Chapter, with in‐depth knowledge of all aspects of Sustainability – Social, Environmental and Economic. Ms. Vollf believes in giving back to the community and her volunteer work includes: IDCEC program reviewer, USGBC‐IL Chapter, N/NW Branch Advocacy Chair, USGBC SPWG, Greenbuild Committee, and the Village of Schaumburg Environmental Committee.
