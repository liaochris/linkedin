Jonathan Dube's Summary

Jonathan Dube is an award-winning leader and pioneer in digital media. He has run major digital media businesses in the U.S and Canada for AOL, ABC, CBS and CBC, and served as President of the international Online News Association. He currently runs CBSSports.com, sits on several boards and serves as an advisor to digital media startups.

During his career, Dube has led news, sports, finance, tech, lifestyle, local, mobile & video digital businesses. He has a strong track record in growing traffic & revenue, and developing innovative new products and sites. As Senior Vice President & General Manger for AOL's News & Information division, he ran more than a dozen sites, spanning everything from sports to consumer tech. He has also served as the VP & GM of ABCNews.com, the head of the Canadian Broadcasting Corporation's news and sports sites, and the Managing Producer of MSNBC.com. He has also written for publications ranging from The New York Times to the Columbia Journalism Review.

For the past decade, Dube has played a prominent leadership role in the digital media industry. He frequently speaks at industry conferences and has served on the boards of the SXSW Interactive Festival, the Online Publishers Association, the Online News Association and Landmark Ventures.

Dube has been described as "a major figure in the online news business" by Editor & Publisher and "a web reporting pioneer" by The Poynter Institute. Among his accomplishments, he was an early pioneer in TV-Web convergence and the first journalist to use the blog format on a news site.

Among his many individual honors, he won the Online News Association's first Breaking News Award in 2000 for his coverage of the World Trade Organization protests. He also helped lead ABCNews.com, CBC.ca and MSNBC.com to numerous awards. He has a master's degree in digital media from Columbia University, which awarded him its first-ever New Media Award.
