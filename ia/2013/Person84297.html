Co-Founder

LeaderSHIFT, Inc

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Management Consulting industry

Co-Founder, with Darla Klein, of LeaderSHIFT, Inc, a people-centered workforce solutions company in northeast Ohio. Co-author of the 8-day Supervisory course, Leadership Essentials.

LeaderShift's mission is to help organizations thrive through the influence of their leaders and generate business results through relationships.

Together, Darla and I created the University of Akron's Supervisory Training Course, Leadership Essentials, using the best of each of our skills and experiences. For Darla it was the systems, structures, processes and procedures of leadership; for me it was all of the interpersonal skills required of 21st century leaders working with a wide variety of employees. My specialties in the course were the interpersonal skills that allow you to create an empowering conversation with all employees as well as overcome listening barriers and manage the challenges and opportunities of conflict and negotiation.

Until December 2010, Darla and I provided our clients with a creative and supportive environment in which to develop the knowledge and skill to implement the people-centered solutions that resolve their workforce issues. Starting in January 2010, Darla continues that work when I left to pursue my work with Powerful Presence full time.

President & Owner

Maura Beatty & Associates

March 1990 – June 2006 (16 years 4 months)

This is the same company whose current form is Maia Beatty & Associates. When I legally changed my first name to Maia in June 2006, I changed my company name as well.

This company originally started in as a sole proprietorship in 1987: MEA Productions. I changed the company name in October 1990, when I married Chuck Beatty, and incorporated it in June 2002.

I began my civilian career as a business owner when I became motivational keynote speaker, although I was still employed as a Substance Abuse Counselor & EAP with Charter Hospital.

When I went into business full-time in March 1990, my other business services grew with client requests and my own development as a speaker, trainer and coach.

What began with my background in Personal Health & Well-being (stress management, resolving conflict, strategies for leading a positive life in the face of adversity) and Communicating Effectively became the seeds from which my current work in Powerful Presence grew.

PNSN ~ BM3 ~ IC1

US Navy

Government Agency; 10,001+ employees; Military industry

May 1973 – August 1987 (14 years 4 months)

This is where I honed my Leadership skills and my ability to communicate effectively with anyone, especially those who can appear "difficult" at first glance. Being one of the few women at my duty stations in the 1970s and 80s allowed me to stretch my interpersonal skills and develop the strategies for Powerful Presence that I teach today.

I spent 11 & 1/2 of these 14 years on active duty, in three major categories of work:

1. 1973: a PNSN at CINCLANTFLT (a junior enlisted secretary to high-ranking officers in Navy Atlantic Fleet HQ).

2. 1975: a BMSN & BM3 in San Diego where I sandblasted and painted submarines aboard the USS San Onofre (ARD 30) at Ballast Point Submarine Base and was the Repair Parts Petty Officer and Planned Maintenance System Petty Officer. (Honorable Discharge, BM3, March 1978).

3. 1979: After 18 months in a variety of non-traditional jobs, I returned to my final tour of duty, starting with a rate change from BM3 to IC3 after Basic Electricity & Electronics School & Interior Communications "A" School. For the next 2 years, I completed a range of work as an IC3 and IC2 from Interior Communications on The USS Vulcan (AR5), to advanced training at Closed Circuit TV School and a tour of general duty at NAS Chase Field in Beeville TX.

In 1982, I began training as a Certified Alcohol & Drug Abuse Counselor and completed my final three years in the Navy as a Counseling and Assistance Center Counselor at NAS Cecil Field, Jacksonville & NAS Chase Field, Beeville, TX. (Honorable Discharge, IC1, August 1987).
