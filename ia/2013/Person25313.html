Callum Langstroth's Summary

Having finished my final year studying Computer Games Design Enterprise at the University of Central Lancashire, I am qualified and hungry for work.

I have a good understanding of game development kits such as Epic’s UDK and a basic knowledge of programming principles which I am always trying to build on. My Game Design methodology is one of constant idea generation and refining of my existing designs to help me to realise and implement my ideas. I also have a good grasp of business, enterprise ownership and start-up procedures which is backed up with a good amount of general business knowledge and practise. I am ready and willing to start at the bottom proving my worth, improving my skills and steadily working my way up to become a Lead Designer.

This unique skill set gives me all I need to help design great games, work as a team member and see the impact of any decision for a game in both terms of the developer making the game and the industry as a whole. Outside of the games industry it means that I am a strong team player and I have developed key skills such as public speaking and being able to explain my ideas in a variety of ways. Such as in presentations, documentation or just simply talking about them. It has also given me a strong creative streak for both the visual and written arts.

In my spare time I write about my two great passions, comic books and video games.

Specialties

Idea generation, game design, UDK (Unreal Development Kit), GDD Writing, Presentations, team leadership, gaming, game design documentation, writing, Adobe Creative Suite, Photoshop, Illustrator, Premiere

Callum Langstroth's Projects

  • Simple RPG

    • February 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Callum Langstroth

    A small and simple RPG I am making as part of the learning process for using RPG Maker VX Ace. The plan is to include at least one instance of the key areas of RPG Maker game development if not multiples.

    Focusing on:
    - Map and Level Design
    - Basic Story Development
    - Eventing (in game story moments, conversations, effect and more)
    - Character Design (class construction, player characters, NPCs, enemies, stats, figures, etc. basically everything that is not character art)
    - Balancing (for battles, level progression, etc.)
    - Script Importing and Basic Editing (starting to learn programming language, Ruby is part of this)

    The game is being made with the pre-package art materials and assets that come with RPG Maker VX Ace.

    Once I discovered One Game A Month I decided to turn this into my first game for the challenge. So as it stands I have just over one week to ge the game finished. Wish me luck!

  • One Game A Month

    Team Members: Callum Langstroth

    One Game a Month is an indie development challenge to create one game in whatever form once a month for as long as possible. You can find out all about it by heading here: http://www.onegameamonth.com/

I will be updating this Project Post with basic game info as time goes on but will be making separate Project Posts for each individual game.

  • Intellectual Property/Franchise Development - Hunters of the New Dawn

    • September 2011 to May 2012
    Team Members: Callum Langstroth

    The Franchise Development project was all about coming up with a new IP that would be a multi game franchise. During the module we would be creating documentation to reflect the game IP in a few stages in its lifespan as a franchise.

    Market Research:

    Being a fan of the research process I jumped on the opportunity of doing an in-depth report on the current games industry market. So I set off on my research journey looking for as much data as possible to do with the most popular franchises in gaming, what games people like to play and the most popular new franchise. Actual solid data on the areas I wanted to explore was thin on the ground so I ended up compiling my own heavily researched and referenced data.


    IP Statement:

    The actual IP idea for this module came together fairly quickly. This is because the basics of it are something I had been sitting on for a long time, so all that was required was to make a few tweaks and changes to fit my target market and then I could just run with it. The sci-fi/fantasy mix has been done before but I think I managed to find a unique spin on things.


    Franchise Development:

    This final document for the Franchise Development module was an interesting task. We had to work on it from the point of view that the first game in the franchise had been a success and now we were coming up with a plan to take the IP further and beyond. It was a fun document to write and made me look into how game developers and publishers exploit their IP's for profit.

    Overall it was a really good project and the amount of work I put into it paid off as I received my highest grade for this particular module.

  • Honours Project - Superman: Man of Steel and Time Travel Game

    • September 2011 to May 2012
    Team Members: Callum Langstroth

    Like any big undertaking the Honours Project had its highs and lows. There was a lot to do for it and in the end I think I managed to pull it off even when I had to cut a portion of the project’s content towards the end.

    The project focused on two very different ways of presenting game design documentation and ideas. One in a very visual way and the other in a Wiki format. While the project was happening I was tracking its development and drawing comparisons between the two formats ready for a final compare and contrast at the end.

    The visual game design was my idea for a game based on DC Comic's Superman:

    Game Design Breakdown:
    - Pitch
    - Visual Design Document
    - Visual Design Process
    - Physical Prototype

    Superman: Man of Steel is one of my most realised designs to date with the prototype playing a big role in that. It is a design I am going to go back to when I get the time and really flesh out. The Visual Game Design Document idea fits the design perfectly and I want to see how far I can push it.


    The wiki game design is an for a game based on the idea of time travel:

    Game Design Breakdown:
    - Pitch
    - Time Travel Game Wiki
    - Game Design Wiki Process
    - Prototype

    The Time Travel game while a sound idea still needs a lot of work in my opinion. The focus for it was the use of the Wiki to document the design rather than the design itself but I wish looking back on it now that I had dedicated a bit more time to it.

    Overall the Honours Project was one of the most rewarding pieces of work I have done simply because there is so much of it and I put a lot into it. The conclusion I came to, that in future mashing the wiki documentation and visual document together could prove to be interesting at some point further down the line.
