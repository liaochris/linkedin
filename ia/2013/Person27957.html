Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Translation and Localization industry

December 2012 – Present (11 months)

As Head of Technology, Innovation and Best Practice my role at thebigword has evolved from the Programme Manager role into one which encompasses working closely with key stakeholders within the business including the Group CTO and The Board to develop and implement our technology strategy.

I am tasked with developing new solutions which add value to our service proposition to benefit our clients and the business at large. This has seen me develop new skills within the Localization sector and look at new ways of developing multi-tenanted high volume online transactional systems capable of dealing with a large amount of throughput.

I work with the Technology Department to productise our solutions and services enabling us to sell our technology to other service providers. We are also extending our client reach through building channels with other complementary technology solutions such as content management systems.

I represent the Technology department in client meetings and have been working as a key resource with the Global Sales Teams on client meetings (face to face and via teleconference) and authoring bids and tenders across Europe, the USA and Asia. This has seen the business realise new opportunities within existing clients and grow its client base through positioning itself more effectively to its Multi-National Corporate Clients within the Finance, Automotive, Aerospace, Technology, Healthcare, B2B Service Provider and Government Sectors.

The best practice element incorporates information security and compliance working with the Global Compliance and BMS Manager to improve compliance and information security across the organisation. This has included introducing a Corporate Risk Management system and incremental updates to our Information Security Management System which has resulted in retention of ISO 27001 Certification and passing numerous external audits since my employment commenced.

Director

Information Sharing Solutions Ltd

April 2008 – April 2011 (3 years 1 month)

Information Sharing Solutions (ISS) is a specialist software development and consultancy company focussed on data management, data feed, reporting and analysis applications.

Our short-term business objective was to provide support to Local Authorities and their partners in the implementation of the Department of Children, Schools and Families (DCSF) national ContactPoint system.

Having completed and delivered our first product a data supply adapter called CPAd, which has been deployed to a variety of Local Authorities, we have continued to expand our product set with development of UserPoint and ISSHub. These will ultimately enable the business to target the general social care sector opening up another market for the company.

These two products form our expanded product set to provide comprehensive implementation support for the DCSF’s wider Improving Information Sharing and Management (IISaM) programme, and ultimately to support any local government implementations of central government IT systems.

Our client base consists of Local Authorities (LAs), and their partner organisations such as health, independent schools and the Voluntary sector. Our products are also deployed by ContactPoint National Partners which include the largest national children's charities. We have also completed supply deals with some of the UK's largest outsourcing companies which operate in the local Government sector.

Our approach is to work jointly with our clients to understand their requirements, and then deliver a high-quality product and service solution that meets their requirements in the most efficient and effective way.
