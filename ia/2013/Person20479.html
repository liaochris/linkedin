Carol Greenwald's Summary

We all know that clients are the most important business resource, yet most of us tend to neglect them or take them for granted. In my book [written with Stephen Skyles-Mulligan] Build Your Practice the Logical Way: Maximize Your Client Relationships [ABA, First Chair Press, 2012] we explain how to grow your practice by creating a client-centric firm. The book reflects my 25 years of experience.I enable my clients to use client-centricity to grow their firm.

I work with my clients to identify their key clients and most lucrative practice areas. After finding their sweet spot, i teach them how to make it the engine of their growth. Whether the technique is called strategy, research or coaching the purpose is the same - to build solid business and marketing/business development skills and reap the rewards thereof.

Carol Greenwald's Publications

  • Build Your Practice the Logical Way: Maximize Your Client Relationships

    • American Bar Association, First Chair Press
    • 2012
    Authors: Carol Greenwald, Steven Skyles-Mulligan

    Clients will fuel your firm's growth. Consistent, strategic, and sustainable firm growth depends on strong client relationships rooted in practices that are designed to meet not only clients' expressed needs but also their expectations. This how-to guide helps professionals tend what is already most rewarding, both intellectually and financially, in their practice: the clients who value them most and with whom they most value working.
    The growth steps outlined in the book are straightforward, strategic, and significantly important for the long-term health and viability of an individual's book of business, a practice area's clients and/or a firm's clients.
    We identify strategies that keep and grow relationships with key clients as the foundation for
    growth. For example,
    • develop a client-centricity program including client guidelines and programs such as alternative pricing, client teams, knowledge-sharing programs, etc.
    • use client-defined value to create a foundation of loyalty
    • master communication techniques to build a client-centric practice
    • use research to understand the firm's growth areas
    • develop the personal skills to build and grow a practice.
    The worksheets in each chapter are collected on an included CD for individuals to use when following the strategies recommended in the book.
