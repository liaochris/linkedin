Mario Ballesteros' Summary

Mario Ballesteros is Director of Strategic Communication and Editorial Projects at the Lab for the City (Laboratorio para la Ciudad), the office for civic innovation and urban creativity of the Mexico City government, launched in February 2013.

As an independent editor and strategic advisor, Mario has conducted research with FREE/Fernando Romero and Grupo de Diseño Urbano, worked as an in-house editor at Actar (where he coordinated and co-edited the sixth installment of the Verb architecture boogazine series: Crisis) and acted as curator for the Design Hub Barcelona (DHUB), head of Digital Platforms and Communications at the Barcelona Institute of Architecture (BIArch) and an editorial and research consultant for Strelka Institute for Media, Architecture and Design in Moscow. He was part of the editorial team selected to direct Quaderns d’arquitectura i urbanisme, the journal of the Col.legi d’Arquitectes de Catalunya (Association of Architects of Catalonia) in February 2011. In 2012 he returned to Mexico City, as the first Editor in Chief of Domus Mexico, a local, Spanish-language edition of Domus, the legendary Italian design magazine founded in 1928 by Gio Ponti.

Alongside his editorial and consulting work, Mario has been a guest lecturer at the Architecture Faculty of the National Autonomous University (UNAM) in Mexico City, Elisava School of Design and the Metropolis Master in Architecture and Urban Culture program at the Centre de Cultura Contemporània de Barcelona (CCCB). His work has been published in Domus, Domus Mexico, Perspecta, Verb, Architecture Australia, Baumeister, Jornal de Arquitectos, Gatopardo and Celeste, among others.

Mario received his Master degree in Architecture and Urban Culture from the Metropolis program Universitat Politècnica de Catalunya (UPC) and a Bachelor’s degree in Foreign Affairs from El Colegio de México (Colmex).
