Graham Giller's Summary

Whatever the venue, I seek to build statistically based models that are robust predictors of behaviour out-of-sample.

Since 1995, I have worked on the quantitative analysis of financial data and the application of that information to systematic trading.

I also work on non-finance related large scale ("big data") statistical analysis including time-series analysis, geospatial analysis and geostatistics, natural language processing and the statistical analysis of social networks and other online activity.

I developed the first systems used for trading interest rate derivatives and financial futures in the Process Driven Trading (PDT) group at Morgan Stanley and also worked on the theoretical modeling of optimal trading strategies while there.

Subsequently, I created, managed, and operated several private investment companies and partnerships.I also set aside time to work on quantitative finance and statistics from a more abstract academic point of view.

For the past two years I worked full time in a private partnership developing high frequency alpha and volatility models directly exploiting recent cutting edge results in estimation and inference theory. We set up a company to operate a trading system derived from this work as a joint venture with a New York based hedge fund.

I am currently building the geostatistical content to support the Maps product for Search Engine Marketing with Didit and also leading the effort to monetize data resources for we-care.com.

Specialties: statistical analysis, data management, futures trading, equity trading, quantitative trading, optimal trading theory, dynamic processes, adaptive models, machine learning, information discovery, project initialization, start-ups, predictive modeling, geostatistics, natural language processing

Joined a California based hedge-fund start-up, VN7 Dynamic Capital, LP, as Chief Scientist. My role was to oversee the development high frequency conditional mean and volatility forecasting systems (on timescales of order 50 milliseconds to around 15 minutes) in coordination with other researchers and firm management. We focussed our research on the interactions between asset prices and their volatilities, both empirical volatility and implied volatility.

Graham Giller's Education

University of Oxford

D.Phil., Experimental Elementary Particle Physics

1990 – 1994

Doctoral thesis: On the Construction and Analysis of a Whole Sky Map Using
Underground Muons (supervisor, Dr. J.H. Cobb, Oxford University). Doctorate awarded:
1994. As a member of the Soundan II Proton Decay Experiment, using experimental
apparatus located deep underground in the Tower-Soudan Mine, MN, we conducted a
statistical analysis of the background muon flux through the experiment’s tracking
calorimeter. These muons are daughter particles from ultra high energy (TeV to PeV
scale) extensive air showers initiated by cosmic rays in the upper atmosphere. We pointed
the origin direction of the muons onto the celestial sphere with an instrumental resolution
of around 1°. I constructed and searched a smoothed whole-sky map of the muon flux for
anomalous excess event counts within the region of interesting astrophysical objects. This
was a search for extrema within a spatially correlated two dimensional random field.

1987 – 1990

I read Physics at Lincoln College, Oxford, receiving 1st Class Honors in 1990. My final year options were: Particle & Nuclear Physics, Electricity & Magnetism, Electronics, Theoretical Physics.
