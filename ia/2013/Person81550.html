Lisa Hrabluk's Summary

I tell a persuasive story. Whether through my journalism or my leadership role in creating social organizations I use storytelling techniques to make information relevant to my audience so it may impact their actions.

My roots are in journalism and the craft of storytelling for a broad audience. As a business reporter and provincial affairs columnist I broke national stories about ICT innovations, energy developments, Aboriginal rights and the ambitions of the Maritimes' political class.

In 2003 I began exploring how storytelling techniques can provoke institutional and community change by engaging audiences and building social networks that strengthen trust and deepen relationships.

Since then I have led the creation of three social organizations.

2004: Next NB
2005: 21 Leaders for the 21st Century (precursor of 21inc)
2010: Saint John 225, The Original City

Please see below for details on each project.

Founding Director, Next NB & 21 Leaders for the 21st Century

University of New Brunswick

Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry

June 2003 – June 2005 (2 years 1 month) Saint John, New Brunswick

In 2003 UNB President Dr. John McLaughlin asked me to help him provoke a change in thinking and direction in New Brunswick.

We agreed this was not a job for government; the changes required too challenging for it to tackle. It needed to be citizen-led.

Dr. McLaughlin believed to provoke the public to act, we needed to tell a persuasive story that laid out the facts and recommended solutions. He needed a writer; I needed a cause.

Together we created Next NB, which sought to create a network of new leaders. We did this through four primary channels:
1. Publication of nine essays on public policy issues;
2. 16 public forums around the province to initiate the conversation;
3. A final conference, SeaChange, in 2005 to encourage the development of tangible solutions; and,
4. The creation of two networks - 21inc and the New Brunswick Business Council.

In 2004 we created 21 Leaders for the 21st Century, the precursor to 21inc. We invited a group of young people between the ages of 20 and 35 to become citizen journalists in a seven-month project, the highlight of which was a week-long tour of New Brunswick that featured conversations with business, social, cultural and political leaders.

At the conclusion of Next NB in 2005, the original 21 Leaders decided that they would answer our call to action by continuing the program on their own, as 21inc.
