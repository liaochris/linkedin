Craig Peters' Summary

I've developed and implemented award-winning content and successful online marketing programs for a wide variety of companies -- including some of the best-known entertainment brands on the planet.

Imagine going into the nation's top Web agencies at the height of the mid-'90s Internet bubble and saying: "We're The Greatest Show On Earth and we want to build a Web site." Then subsequently developing and managing site content and marketing plans from scratch. An MBA in Web? You bet.

My online content has received A and A+ ratings from Entertainment Weekly. My e-mail marketing programs for Feld Entertainment delivered returns that typically tripled industry averages. My online marketing plans are devoid of smoke and mirrors and built to succeed.

I've been living online since Prodigy was hailed as the Next Big Thing. Before that I did everything and then some during my 15 years in newsstand magazine publishing, including plenty of writing, editing, advertising, marketing and photography.

I've written about three dozen books (mostly for the grade school market), hundreds of magazine articles, press kits, DVD scripts, speeches and the proverbial much more.

As director of online marketing and communications for kajeet I took a ride on the startup rollercoaster. It was definitely an E-ticket attraction and would make a great business case study for someone someday.

In 2004 I founded CKPcreative, an agency specializing in copywriting, marketing, PR and social media management.

In 2008 I began developing and implementing digital media strategies and services for clients of BP Media Relations.

In 2011 I was approached by Bank of America to provide editorial services for their user experience division.

I blog daily at lohad.com.

(last updated: August 2011)
