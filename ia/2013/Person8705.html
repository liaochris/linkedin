Anthony DaSilva Jr's Summary

Over the years, I've acquired lots of experience in the areas of embedded and distributed real-time software for large and long-lived, safety-critical, sensor systems. I've developed and documented SW requirements, architectures, detailed-designs and programmer documentation. I've performed unit testing, integration testing, and system testing. I was the software lead on two "aggressively scheduled" product development efforts, each with a team size of 8 people. I've developed software build schedules, allocated resources to design and coding tasks, and managed small development teams.

I'm currently deeply involved in the development of a set of reusable, distributable, layered, and composable SW components that employ Poco, an OMG DDS implementation, googletest, and C++11.

With my electrical engineering background, I can absorb new application domain area expertise relatively quickly. I'm comfortable working at all levels of abstraction; using SysML and UML at the system and software architecture and design levels; and writing source code at ground zero. I've written a boatload of multi-threaded C and C++ production code for a multitude of fielded products.

I have solid experience in both the structured analysis/design and object-oriented analysis/design methods. I'm familiar with most major software development methodologies from waterfall to XP, and I have designed and documented a "balanced" large-scale system product development process that covers "big systems" comprised of custom hardware, software, and network components.

My goal is to lead a large group of people throughout the creative development of a system with enduring value that is either directly, or indirectly useful and helpful to millions of people.

Specialties

I specialize in discovering, exposing, and resolving ambiguity through systems thinking. I know how to create a common language and shared understanding within a project team. I can dive deep into the murky details of software specialty work when it has to be done. I can learn a new software technology fast because of my insatiable yearning to discover and learn new things. I also specialize in *trying* to destroy corpo roadblocks and distractions that stand in the way of technical team success.

Anthony DaSilva Jr's Experience

Experienced Software Engineer

Saab Sensis Corporation

Public Company; 501-1000 employees; Defense & Space industry

November 1996 – Present (16 years 10 months) Syracuse, New York Area

Upon my "triumphant" return to Sensis, I achieved the following results:

I was a co-architect during the design, coding, and testing effort of the server side of a large, distributed, embedded, real-time, multiprocess, multithreaded sensor system using C++, boost, STL, Xerces/XQilla/XML, OMG DDS.

I served as the SW lead for an 8 person team charged with developing the Radar Data Processor SW for the ASDE-3 surface movement radar (RDP3X). The RDP3X is a 20+ thread, single process, program running over Linux using glib and other OSS libraries to perform its mission. I created the architecture, wrote a bunch of the code, scheduled/assigned coding tasks, and monitored/steered the development effort.

I served as the SW AND SYS lead for an 8 person team during the development of the embedded control SW for the UAT GBT air traffic control system radio. The SW is a 20+ task program running under the VxWorks 5.5. RTOS. I created the architecture, allocated functionality to RTOS tasks and internal data structures, wrote a bunch of the code, scheduled/assigned coding tasks, and monitored/steered the development effort. Even though it's not kosher to purists, I created and wrote both the Software Design Document AND the Software Requirements Specification for this 40K SLOC embedded program.

I served a dual role as the overall product manager and lead SW developer during the development of the BEacon eXtractor and Recorder (BEXR) SSR diagnostic product. I wrote the Win3.1 GUI based control SW component of BEXR along with the device drivers for two custom designed signal and target processing boards. The BEXR boards and control SW were packaged in a standard, portable Wintel PC for easy customer transfer between SSR radar sites that were experiencing performance problems.

Embedded Software Engineer

Carrier Transicold

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; UTC; Automotive industry

March 1995 – November 1996 (1 year 9 months)

I designed, wrote, and tested software functionality for the embedded air conditioner controllers deployed inside of our Carrier customer's cargo shipping containers. The C code ran under the control of a home grown, proprietary, co-operative mutlitasking, Real Time Operating System (RTOS). The RTOS was developed for a custom designed, Intel 8088 CPU board that contained a plethora of digital and analog IO peripherals for interfacing with the mechanical and electrical air conditioning subsystem components.

Software Engineer

Sensis Corporation

Public Company; 501-1000 employees; Defense & Space industry

1987 – 1995 (8 years)

I moved from GE over to the Sensis Corporation in 1987. I was employee number 13 and now we're up to 700 people!. During the period of 1987 through 1995, here's a sampling of *some* of the projects that I worked on (that I remember!):

I worked with senior radar engineer mentors on analyzing the target detection performance of the AN/TPS-59 long range, ground based radar, in the presence of enemy jamming (ECM). I helped in the design of, and performance anaylsis of, an ECCM antenna sidelobe canceler subsystem addition to the radar. I wrote, integrated, and debugged, the ECCM simulator portions of the code.

I worked on the development of a Radar Environment Simulator (RES) tool for testing the Phalanx shipboard radar system. I designed the GUI display screens and processing functionality for the Online Display and Control (OLCD) subsystem of the RES. I recorded the functional and performance requirements in a Software Requirements Specification (SRS). I worked closely with the OLCD software developer ( Unix-SunOS, OpenWindows GUI, C source code) during the post-requirements development and testing phases of the project.

I developed the functional requirements for, and software design for, the Coherent DAta Collection (CDACS) test tool whose purpose was to tap into the AN/TPS-59 radar for real-time signal collection and recording. I also wrote the C code for the character-based CDACS GUI and interrupt driven real-time data capture/recording functionality.

I designed and wrote the embedded C code for a PC-based SIMulation SYNthesizer (SIMSYN) for testing the Sensis real-time Multi-Scan Correlator product. The MSC was designed to attach to existing radar processors and provide enhanced target tracking and false target removal capabilities. SYMSYN provided a user friendly way for system testers to design and generate multi-target test scenarios that injected the MSC with test data streams.

System Engineer

General Electric Co.

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; GE; Electrical/Electronic Manufacturing industry

1980 – 1987 (7 years)

I joined GE's defense systems business right out of UConn in June of 1980. I joined their "Advanced Course In Engineering" program which culminated in an MSEE from Syracuse University in 1983. For 3 years, the program required 6 month rotating assignments throughout the radar and sonar business in addition to taking accredited in-house and Syracuse University engineering courses. The program was incredibly hard but well worth the effort.

In my seven years at GE, I worked on:

The Over-the-Horizon radar product; doing system performance anaylsis.

The AN/SQR-19 passive towed array sonar system; doing system detection and tracking performance prediction,writing and running system test procedures, and writing test tool software requirements specifications.I also ran customer-witnessed tests and wrote deliverable test reports.

The AN/TPS-59 ground-based long range radar; doing embedded PM/FL diagnostic test design and coding.

In addition, I worked in the advanced radar development group running and modifying phased array antenna design programs (FORTRAN !).
