Jonahlyn Gilstrap's Projects

  • Students.unm.edu

    • December 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Jonahlyn Gilstrap

    The purpose of the E-Stop project (since renamed Students.unm.edu) is to create an aggregation of key information and resources available to students at the University of New Mexico with the goal of increasing student engagement and presenting a holistic view of UNM.

    My contribution included assisting with various need-finding activities with students. I co-led several focus group exercises including process mapping to understand the student experience and problems encountered and a usability study to benchmark how effectively current offerings met student needs.

    Additionally, I used my python programming skills to help gather website analytics from various sources including the Urchin 6 API, the Google Search Appliance, and custom, password-protected sites to produce reports for analysis.

  • SearchUNM

    • August 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Jonahlyn Gilstrap

    The goal of the SearchUNM was to relaunch the University Google Search Appliance and address the problems and complaints users had with the UNM search product.

    I filled in as front end developer on the project for a coworker that left the university mid-project. With the help of the skills I contributed, the project was completed on schedule. The finished product doubled the rate of customer satisfaction and was a success for the whole team.

    The project was a collaboration between Information Technologies and University Libraries. If you would like to learn more about this project, the following publication was written by University Libraries project team members about the project and the positive experience of the collaboration.

    S. Y. Zoe Chao & Rebecca Lubas (2013): Collaborating with Information
    Technology: Implementing Web Search at the University of New Mexico, Cataloging & Classification
    Quarterly, 51:1-3, 251-264
    http://dx.doi.org/10.1080/01639374.2012.722590
    A subscription is required and is viewable on UNM's campus.

  • Provisioning Feeds Project

    • August 2006 to Present
    Team Members: Jonahlyn Gilstrap

    The goal of the Provisioning Feeds Project (since renamed to Person Import, Classification and Export System or P.I.C.E.S) was to provide plug-and-play support for external systems that depended on data in the UNM ERP system.

    My contribution included writing programs to take information about people in an external hospital system and, using Banner Common Matching APIs, import them into the ERP database. I also wrote a program that took information about all current students, faculty, staff and affiliates to send that information to UNM's central LDAP system, which was used for authentication and authorization purposes institute wide.

    I find it so awesome when project collaborators and stakeholders are so excited about the result that they write about it! See the following article if you are interested in learning more about this project.

    Carolyn Hartley (Oct. 2009). The Provisioning Feeds Project: How the University of New Mexico Migrated from MVS to Banner. CARDtalk Newsletter for the National Association of Campus Card Users, 12(9), pp. 1, 4-5.
