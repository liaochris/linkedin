Jay Shepherd's Summary

Prefix, LLC helps professionals learn how to price their knowledge instead of just billing their time. As a recovering lawyer, I know how demeaning and depersonalizing it is to track your hours in six-minute increments. Under the old time-based business model — which was started in 1919 — lawyers focus on the activity they do rather than on the knowledge they provide. But it's that knowledge that creates the value for the clients, not the activity. Other professionals, in accounting, advertising, IT, and consulting, face the same problem.

In my own law firm, I solved this problem in 2006, trashed my timesheets and started pricing my knowledge. My clients loved it, and my lawyers loved it. And this was in a litigation practice, which "experts" said couldn't be done. Newsflash: it can be.

By learning how to value and price your knowledge, you can have a more profitable and fulfilling professional practice. Or you can clutch your 100-year-old timesheet and be just another timekeeper. Your choice.

Come learn more at http://prefixllc.com

Jay Shepherd's Projects

  • Firing at Will: A Manager's Guide

    • April 2011 to October 2011
    Team Members: Jay Shepherd

    Firing at Will shows managers and employers how to do the most difficult part of their jobs: firing employees. Written by a leading employment lawyer in a refreshingly unlawyerly style, this guide takes the reader through the always-risky process of letting an employee go. Many employers and managers are afraid to pull the trigger when the employment relationship has broken down, and will postpone the decision by using progressive discipline and performance-improvement plans. However, an employer must be able to unload employees who threaten to undermine the company and its prospects, regardless of the risks involved in a termination. This book explains how to do it, how not to do it, and how to minimize the danger of an expensive employee lawsuit.

Jay Shepherd's Publications

  • Firing at Will: A Manager's Guide

    • Apress
    • November 11, 2011
    Authors: Jay Shepherd

    It's the riskiest thing you can do at work with your clothes on.

    Firing at Will gives you real-world advice on how to fire employees. Offering you a roadmap for making and carrying out one of a manager’s most difficult decisions, it covers the steps you need to take for a smooth, drama-free — and lawsuit-free — termination.

    Based on author and attorney Jay Shepherd’s experience protecting employers in and out of court, this book:

    • Shows how to make the ultimate decision to fire an employee and how to carry out that decision.
    • Explains why progressive discipline and performance appraisals are ineffective.
    • Guides you toward building a workplace in which you’ll need to fire fewer employees.
    • Includes samples to help you draft job-offer letters, severance agreements, employment agreements, and more.

    Filled with plain-English common sense, Firing at Will gives you battle-tested tips that help take the stress and risks out of firing an employee.
