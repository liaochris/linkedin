Damian Rakowsky's Summary

Damian Rakowsky has over ten years of professional work experience and is well versed in the fields of Environmental Graphic Design and Information, Interaction and Wayfinding Design. He has over ten years of professional experience collaborating with architects and designers on international projects with Royal Caribbean International (RCI), Celebrity Cruise Lines, Azamara Cruise Lines, W Hotels, Marriott Hotels and Hyatt Hotels. His work has also included master plans and sign design for urban development projects for the Port of Miami, the City of Miami and the City of Syracuse. His experience has also led to teaching design courses at the Miami Ad School, ITT Technical Institute, and Syracuse University.

During his employment with Tom Graboski Associates Design in Coral Gables, FL, he served as Lead Manager from 2007 to 2009 for RCI’s cruise ship, Oasis of the Seas. For this project he worked with a design team to develop and execute wayfinding and venue sign systems and graphics. He also oversaw the assembly of the sign package that cataloged over 34,000 signs and his team’s work was integral in the conception and early development of the first ever wayfinding touch screen system used on-board any cruise ship.

In recent years Damian has collaborated with Ribbit Inc. and Levin/Silbert of New York City on a number of projects. He also has worked with Syracuse University, where he assisted in the development of a transportation application for its bus services, designed a new wayfinding sign system and managed a collaborative project to create an interactive experience along the Civic Strip, part of a citywide urban development initiative.

Adjunct Professor - VPA - Industrial & Interaction Design Program

Syracuse University

Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry

August 2012 – Present (1 year 1 month) Syracuse, New York Area

Communication Design Program:
Design Methods - First year communication design students learn fundamental design principles that create a foundation for more complex graphic projects further in the program.

Industrial and Interaction Design Program:
Industrial Design Interface – This course for fourth year students focuses on the human user interfaces with both the physical objects around them and the words/images on screen.

Symbology and Semiotics – This course for third year students focuses on words, symbols, and icons and how they relate to one another. The goal of the course is to be able to communicate scenarios that are not dependent on linguistic understanding.

The spring 2013 class worked with two real clients: The City of Syracuse to design signs for local parks that promoted safe usage; and the School of Information Studies to design data visualization for the their graduate program booklet. Both clients chose student works to produce.

Interdisciplinary Product Development – This course focuses on the approach to create a product and stresses the collective efforts needed to bring a successful product to the marketplace. Fourth year students team up to mimic a workplace scenario to solve a single design brief that has real world implications. They also collaborate with mentors from various industries to assist them in the process.
At the end of the semester each team presents its project at a juried competition.

Fall 2012 Project: Oval Wood Dish Factory, Tupper Lake, NY.
Students took an existing empty factory and envisioned a creative new use for the facility. Their concepts took into account local resources, the community and its economy. The factory is located deep within the Adirondack Park of New York State and in recent years has been the subject of a long-standing debate about land development. Investors are currently considering several of the student proposals.
