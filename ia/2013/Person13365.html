Ben Werdmuller's Summary

I'm a web product manager, platform architect and developer, specializing in social software platforms for business.

For much of my career, I've thrived in startups, leading technical teams while also fulfilling other roles: product manager, developer, designer, copywriter, evangelist. I'm heavily invested in creating great products that serve real user needs, and I'm comfortable applying my skills wherever is required to serve them - or learning new skills on the fly to make sure the product ships.

I've developed and released products that are used in Fortune 500 companies, top-tier universities and national governments. I'm also an advocate for open source software and data ownership, and have spoken widely about these issues, as well as the products I've helped create.

Ben Werdmuller's Experience

Chief Technology Officer

latakoo

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry

May 2010 – Present (3 years 8 months) San Francisco Bay Area

latakoo is an enterprise video transfer and management service, used primarily for television news operations.

I lead the technical team, sit on the company board, and am heavily involved in both technical and non-technical decision-making across the company, including the definition and scope of products and services. I architected the web platform, writing a large portion of the code for the API that powers video delivery, as well as access control management and functionality across the web stack. I also participate in sales and customer meetings, providing advice and ideas from a technical perspective. I contribute to sales materials and resources across the company.

The platform supports national networks and major industry advocacy organizations. Users have sent footage from places like Syria and Mount Everest using the product (as well as hundreds of news stories a day within the US).

Founder

Idno

April 2013 – Present (9 months)

Idno gives you a powerful, customizable social network engine with full access controls, right out of the box. It's designed to be flexible for enterprise, commercial and individual hobbyist users. I founded the project and am its core developer.

Built with a PHP and MongoDB back-end, and an HTML5 / CSS3 front-end using Microformats, Boostrap and jQuery, Idno fully embraces Indieweb decentralized social web technologies. I have presented it at events including IndieWebCamp and the W3C Social Software in Business Workshop.

Geek in Residence

Edinburgh Festivals

September 2010 – September 2011 (1 year 1 month) Edinburgh, United Kingdom

The Edinburgh Festivals Innovation Lab is a new experiment and resource for the twelve Edinburgh festivals to explore how they can use digital technology to create new value for audiences, artists, the city and the festivals themselves.

The Geek in Residence was an experimental new role in which I helped spot and develop project opportunities and brought my expertise and experience to explore what it is to be a festival in the 21st century. This included creating a festivals API, and participating in Culture Hack Scotland.

Web strategist and developer

Ben Werdmuller Consulting

April 2009 – August 2010 (1 year 5 months) Oxford, United Kingdom

I provided web development and strategy services to startups, established companies and institutions.

Using an in-depth knowledge of the web application market and a network of inside contacts, I provided advice on relevant technologies and best practices. I brought a creative, technical perspective to a range of projects, building innovative, efficient web strategies. Often, I remained involved throughout the production process, including architecture design and supervision of the development process. I also developed web applications using PHP, C#, Symfony, MySQL and jQuery.

Organizer

Intersection: Publishing

January 2010 – June 2010 (6 months) London, United Kingdom

I was joint event organizer for Intersection: Publishing, an informal, one-day BarCamp-style event where professionals from the worlds of print, technology and the law came together to discuss the future of publishing. It was held on April 17, 2010, just prior to London Book Fair. It subsequently evolved into an evolving series of events on the same topic.

Chief Technology Officer / Director / Co-founder

Curverider Ltd

June 2006 – April 2009 (2 years 11 months) Oxford, United Kingdom

In 2006, I co-founded Curverider Ltd in order to provide commercial Elgg solutions. I served as Chief Technical Officer and board member, and was jointly responsible for determining company direction. In this role, I assisted the University of Brighton in becoming the first university in the world to provide a campus-wide social network, attracted angel investment, grew the business into a small team, and developed solutions for a wide variety of organizations including global banks, world-renowned educational institutions and fast-growing corporations. It was subsequently acquired by Thematic Networks in 2010.

Co-founder / Technical lead

Elgg

January 2004 – April 2009 (5 years 4 months) Oxford, United Kingdom

Elgg is a popular open source social networking platform, based on PHP and MySQL, with a front-end built using jQuery. I co-founded the platform with a colleague at the University of Edinburgh, and was instrumental in growing it from a small educational tool to a market-leading product. Elgg is used internally in national governments, Fortune 500 companies, international charities and many universities.

As well as being jointly responsible for its direction and ethos, I designed the architecture for the system and was its technical driving force. I spoke at conferences around the world and published papers on the platform and related subjects. In 2007, Elgg was a finalist in the New Statesman New Media Awards; in 2008, InfoWorld named it the best open source social networking platform.

Web administrator

Saïd Business School

Educational Institution; 201-500 employees; Education Management industry

March 2005 – June 2006 (1 year 4 months) Oxford, United Kingdom

I was responsible for developing a new web presence in order to better advertise the work of the school and attract prospective students. I liaised with marketing, IT and the overall management of the school, as well as external design consultants. Additionally, I built learning environments for executive education courses, provided training in web technologies, advised on official podcasts, assisted with event management, and provided web technology advice to entrepreneurial MBA students.

Web Developer

The University of Edinburgh

Educational Institution; 5001-10,000 employees; Research industry

June 2003 – September 2004 (1 year 4 months) Edinburgh, United Kingdom

I redeveloped the Coaches’ Info Service, an online journal for sharing sports science information with coaches, for the department of Physical Education, Sports and Leisure Studies. I created a bespoke content management system including a consultant booking tool and a sports science discussion community, as well as a distance learning and certification system for the Centre for Aquatics Research and Education, an advanced facility for exploring the bio-mechanics of swimming.

For the Media and Learning Technology Service, I produced a large number of interactive web tools for a variety of academics and departments, including a land remediation simulation for the department of engineering, and a complete blogging system for a number of separate courses. I also consulted on social media and web application issues.
