Carter Trout's Patents

  • Two Patent Applications: Dynamic Local Commerce Promotions

    • United States Patent Application 11/944,958; 11/944,935
    • Filed November 26, 2007
    Inventors: Carter Trout, Christopher Higgins, Marc Davis

    Similar to Groupon’s Now product, the filings describe a system for creating, targeting, and distributing promotions. The engine performs real-time pricing based on merchants’ dynamic business data.

    • "Redistribution And Redemption Of Commercial Incentives," U.S. Patent Application 11/944,958, filed November 26, 2007.
    • "Dynamic Augmentation Of Commercial Incentives," U.S. Patent Application 11/944,935, filed November 26, 2007.

  • Two Patent Applications: Social Media Monetization

    • United States Patent Application 12/112,843; 12/112,628
    • Filed April 30, 2008
    Inventors: Carter Trout, Christopher Higgins, Joseph James O'Sullivan, Marc Davis, Ronald Martinez

    Similar to Facebook’s Facepile product, the filings describe a system for enabling word-of-mouth advertising in a network. The system matches a person with a stated or demonstrated interest in a brand to another person.

    • "Modification Of Content Representation By A Brand Engine In A Social Network," U.S. Patent Application 12/112,843, filed April 30, 2008.
    • "Modification Of Brand Representations By A Brand Engine In A Social Network," U.S. Patent Application 12/112,628, filed April 30, 2008.

  • Four Patent Applications: Messaging and Personal Information Management

    • United States Patent Application 11/953,454; 11/952,875; 11/953,494; 12/038,716; 11/960,368
    • Filed December 17, 2007
    Inventors: Carter Trout, Marc Davis, Julie Herendeen, Marco Boerries, Chistopher Higgins, Joseph O'Sullivan, Ronald Martinez

    The filings describe a system that uses contextual metadata (who, what, when, where) to identify and manage information objects, such as people, messages, or events. This approach enables novel forms of communication. For example, the system can dramatically reduce email spam. As a second example, the system can automatically schedule appointments by optimizing common meeting dates among contending calendars, with minimal coordination by the attendees.

    • "System And Method For Contextual Addressing Of Communications On A Network," U.S. Patent Application 11/953,454, filed December 10, 2007.
    • "System And Method For Prioritizing Delivery Of Communications Via Different Communication Channels," U.S. Patent Application 11/952,875, filed December 7, 2007.
    • "Event Communication Platform For Mobile Device Users," U.S. Patent Application 12/038,716, filed February 27, 2008.
    • "System And Method For Scheduling Electronic Events," U.S. Patent Application 11/960,368, filed December 19, 2007.

  • Patent Issued: Mobile Monetization

    • United States Patent Application 8,521,832
    • Filed August 27, 2013
    Inventors: Carter Trout, Cynthia Chu, Thomas Whittaker, Charlie Jiang, Russell Beattie

    The filing describes a system that uses a number of factors, such as context and user behavior, to optimize the click action(s) that should be presented to the user as part a mobile advertisement. For example, the system might present a known shopper the option to transact through the mobile advertisement.

  • Patent Issued: System And Method For Disambiguating Non-Unique Identifiers Using Information Obtained From Disparate Communication Channels

    • United States Patent 8,166,168
    • Issued April 24, 2012
    Inventors: Carter Trout, Marc Davis, Marco Boerries, Joseph James O'Sullivan, Ron Martinez, Chris Higgins, Joe Hayashi

    The filing describes a system that uses contextual metadata (who, what, when, where) to identify and manage information objects, such as people, messages, or events. In particular, the disclosure describes systems and methods for disambiguating non-unique identifiers of real world entities (RWEs) detected by a computing network by dynamically identifying relationships between RWEs known to the network. The relationships are determined based on social, spatial, temporal and logical information known about the RWEs based on the RWEs previous interactions with the network. These relationships are then used to generate a probability for each RWE that the non-unique identifier identifies that RWE. Based on the probabilities an RWE is selected and the proper network identifier may be used in place of the non-unique identifier.

  • Patent Issued: System And Method For Synchronizing Data On A Network

    • United States Patent 8,069,142
    • Issued November 29, 2011
    Inventors: Carter Trout, Marc Davis, George Grinsted, Lee J. Parry, Marco Boerries, Christopher Higgins, Joseph James O'Sullivan, Ronald Martinez

    The filing describes a system that uses contextual metadata (who, what, when, where) to identify and manage information objects, such as people, messages, or events. This approach enables the synchronization of data on a network based on temporal, spatial, social and logical data available to the network.

  • Patent Issued: Targeted distribution of electronic coupons

    • United States Patent 8,280,766
    • Issued October 2, 2012
    Inventors: Carter Trout, Marc Davis, Christopher Higgins

    The filing describes a system and method and computer program product that matches commercial incentives to consumers based on targeting data associated with the commercial incentive and user data associated with consumers in a manner that advantageously allows for highly-targeted delivery of commercial incentives to the consumers that are most likely to use them. Because both the targeting data and the user data may include spatial, temporal, social and topical data, matching may be performed based on any or all of these data types, as well as based on any combination of these data types.

  • Patent Issued: System and method for conditional delivery of messages

    • United States Patent 8,307,029
    • Issued November 6, 2012
    Inventors: Carter Trout, Marc Davis, Marco Boerries, Christopher Higgins, Joseph James O'Sullivan, Ronald Martinez

    The filing describes a system and method for delivering communications based on delivery conditions, whereby the delivery conditions are determined by monitoring information received from multiple sources. The message delivery systems allow messages to be sent to any "Who, What, When, Where" from any "Who, What, When, Where" upon the detection of certain delivery conditions. A message may be any data object including text-based messages, audio-based message such as voicemail or other audio such as music or video-based prerecorded messages. The message is delivered based on certain conditions including topical, spatial, temporal, and/or social data. Furthermore, because the systems coordinate delivery of messages via multiple communication channels and through multiple devices, the communication channel for delivery of a message may be dynamically determined based on the delivery conditions.
