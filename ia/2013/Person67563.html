Jonathan Kalan's Summary

A multi-media storyteller with a background in technology, innovation, entrepreneurship and development in emerging markets. Currently covering innovation ecosystems and startups across Africa and the Middle East.

http://kalan.me
-------------------------

Jonathan Kalan is journalist and photographer specializing in social innovation, technology & startups in emerging markets. He has traveled to over 50 countries and worked with nonproﬁts, social enterprise start-ups, and media companies in a wide range of capacities - from business development to mobile technology and visual communications.

He is currently a columnist for BBC Future, and also contributes to BBC Business, The Atlantic, The Boston Globe, Global Post, Makeshift Magazine, Huffington Post, Christian Science Monitor, Wamda, The Toronto Star and more.

He has photographed assignments for The New York Times (front page feature), The Guardian (UK), Financial Times, NPR.org, Associated Press, BBC, International Federation of the Red Cross, among others

As founder of The (BoP) Project, Jonathan spent two years reporting on the narrative of "potential" behind the veil of poverty in emerging economies, through the lens of social innovations and entrepreneurs across East Africa.

He was a Finalist for the 2011 Diageo Africa Business Reporting Awards, and Winner of the "Africa Regional Award" for the World Bank CGAP Microfinance Photography Competition.

He is Nairobi's Sandbox Co-Ambassador, and a Staff Writer for NextBillion.net. He has presented at conferences across the U.S. from Yale University to NTen, and taught a seminar on strategic visual communications for social entrepreneurship for Columbia University in Amman, Jordan.


Specialties: Connecting, enabling, inspiring, teaching. Investigating, documenting, communicating. Social enterprises, BoP ventures, and innovations in east Africa.
