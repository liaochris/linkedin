Larry Diamond, FACHE's Summary

Versatile healthcare consultant with more than 28 years of experience and significant success as a:
• Sales and marketing EVP for a healthcare consulting firm - developed several new lines of business..
• Hospital administrator, overseeing a variety of operational areas.
• CIO for both non-profit and for-profit hospitals.
• Certified Project Manager (PMP) for many large projects.

I’ve dedicated my entire career to building teams and driving significant organizational change within healthcare organizations, beginning with a 24-year military career that included implementation of an Air Force-wide healthcare system. I have helped hospitals and blue-chip healthcare clients (and others):
• Execute large-scale projects within tight timeframes.
• Design and implement turnaround strategies for their departments.
• Find qualified consultants for challenges outside my expertise.

I build strong relationships among C-level hospital executives, marrying my technical expertise with the ability to encourage collaboration between impacted business areas. I have been praised for how I apply my for-profit hospital experience and understanding of my clients’ critical business needs to the non-profit world, identifying solutions and implementing changes without alienating the staff and leadership.

My experience implementing complex solutions and other large-scale hospital projects should also pay significant dividends for clients over the next few years as hospitals work to comply with new federal requirements regarding the Affordable Care Act.

After three decades working with hospitals and healthcare organizations around the country, I can help you in improving your operations, increasing sales for a consulting firm, or achieving success in managing your projects. My philosophy is simple: Do the right thing right the first time.

Program/Project Manager

U.S. Air Force Surgeon's Office

July 1963 – October 1987 (24 years 4 months)

Enlisted in the U.S. Air Force at age 17 as an airman and retired after 24 years as a Major with a long record of success in a variety of hospital operational positions, including medical-records abstracting; clinic administration; productivity management; admission, discharge, and transfer; aero-medical evacuation; chief financial officer; and director of hospital information technology.

As Program/Project Manager for the Information Services Division within the Air Force Surgeon’s office from 1983 through 1987, I evaluated new information technology systems for the Air Force, implementing and installing automated I.T. systems that supported healthcare and services delivery through the Air Force Medical Service. In this role, I developed a branch-wide $1.6B Composite Healthcare System implementation plan that saved the Air Force more than $50 million over the five-year lifespan of the system.

Funded undergraduate, graduate, and post-graduate education with Air Force educational scholarships, completing all while on active duty. Volunteered for service in Vietnam as an Air Force medic and extended my assignment for a total of 18 months. Earned a variety of medals during service, including the Bronze Star, Meritorious Service Medal (with one cluster) and Air Force Commendation Medal (with two clusters).
