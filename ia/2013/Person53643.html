Stephen Horner's Projects

  • Crash Test Eggs

    • June 2010 to September 2010
    Team Members: Stephen Horner, Mike Gwyther, Mark Dalton, Brett Hoskin

    BRIEF
    We needed to get both active car buyers, and passive car browsers, to 'put safety first' when considering their next purchase.

    That means getting them to put Vehicle Stability Control and side-curtain airbags ahead of flashier stuff like leather seats and a cool stereo.

    Of course, safety is as boring as sin. That's why our goal was to try and entertain people, whilst still getting across our safety message.

    Our goals were to increase traffic to the rightcar.govt.nz website by 23%, or 8,956 discreet visits.

    THE IDEA
    The first thing we needed was a strong concept. It came to us in the unusual form of eggs.

    Eggs are the perfect metaphor for car safety. They're fragile – just like human beings. And car-tons are like cars. Both exist to stop their contents getting smashed.

    A number of videos had to be copywritten and art directed, each telling the animated story of Shelby and Gregg, the crash-test eggs. The videos would sit on our rightcar.govt.nz website as information in their own right, and to lure people into using the site's car safety search tools. There would also be a banner campaign to drive people there.

    ART DIRECTION
    The videos look had to be funny and yet informative. They had to clearly get across the idea that safety was important, whilst telling a humorous story.

    The art direction of the videos centred around a logical thought – in egg-world, everything must be made out of cardboard.

    The art direction also extended into the characterisation of the eggs. Shelby was sensible and cute looking. Gregg had a greaser's haircut and wore a perpetual smirk or bad-willed grimace. The boffins in the lab had moustaches that wiggled and lab-coats.

    RESULTS
    Shelby and Gregg created a 71% uplift in traffic (38,940 total visits) and an average time spent on site of over 5 minutes per person.
