Andrew Rose, EdD's Summary

As a career school administrator of distinction, Andy Rose has acquired considerable expertise from 42 years spent on the front line of America's public education system.

He has worked to shape how public education delivers instruction, provides professional development for its teachers, holds students and schools accountable, and affects the way students learn.

In his 25 years as a chief school district administrator, four years as an elementary/middle school principal, and over twelve years as a teacher of inner-city children on Manhattan's lower east side, Andy has worked with an array of people - from administrators, school boards, teachers and their unions to parents, communities, government entities and politicians, corporations and students.

Now retired from his administrative career, "Dr. Andy" has embarked upon an active second professional life as an author of fiction while continuing as an education consultant, speaker, and workshop facilitator.

Newly published on Amazon (see the book trailer and link to Amazon: http://www.drandyrose.com/Lilys-Payback.html), Dr. Andy draws on his extensive background as a public school teacher and career school district administrator to craft a unique romantic thriller that redefines the traditional genre: Introducing "Lily’s Payback".

It’s a tale of a middle school science teacher who enlists three of her colleagues to join her in a risky plot to avenge the murder of her twin brother. They end up taking on mobsters, the New York City police, the FBI, and even the CIA!

Riveting, insightful and spicy, Lily’s Payback is a crime thriller whose heroes come from the unlikeliest of places – the teacher’s lounge! Take a peek at the book trailer: http://www.drandyrose.com/Lilys-Payback.html

Dr. Andy and his wife Vivian are active as workshop presenters and conference speakers.

Specialties

Deep knowledge of public school operations and curriculum development; the challenges of parent education; fostering life-long learners; guiding college-bound students in making smart choices; job market preparation. Also - facilitation of mission-critical negotiations (labor and management).

Education Director

JEWEL (Joining East and West in Educational Liaisons)

2009 – December 2010 (1 year)

JEWEL is Joining East & West in Educational Liaisons. Their core mission is to help Korean American families by effectively advocating for their children in schools. JEWEL provides critically needed services, including:

* Working with American educators to improve their understanding of the perspective, challenges, and needs of new American families arriving in the country and assimilating into the school system;
* Assisting New American families as they integrate with U.S. culture and the local community

A central component to JEWEL is the facilitation of the New Language Educator’s Project, which is currently focused on promoting Korean teachers of English learning about the American educational system; American teaching methodologies, including ‘cooperative learning’; brain research, such as Benjamin Bloom’s Taxonomy of Learning Domains; the traditions of American families as well as American culture and “way of life”.

Chief School Administrator

Norwood Public Schools

1984 – 2009 (25 years)

Retired Chief School Administrator after 25 years of service in the district of Norwood, Bergen County, the most populous county in New Jersey. Part of the New York metropolitan area, Bergen ranks 16th among the highest per capita income counties in the U.S.

Norwood career was distinguished by the forging of stronger ties between the school and community, raising student academic scores and teaching standards, helping to pass budgets during often challenging circumstances, continuously building new projects and expanding community outreach, recruiting more qualified teachers, and enhancing special education programs. Also helped to secure more than $1 million in donations to the schools from local organizations.

In 1990 the district received special recognition by the New Jersey Department of Education for its overall organization scheme, particularly its multicultural programs, and for teacher record keeping in the special education area.
