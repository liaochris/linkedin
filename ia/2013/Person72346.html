Karissa Wingate's Summary

"Find something you are passionate about and stay tremendously interested in it" - Julia Child

I've been interested in web development since middle school. My first website was made using Angelfire for a friends band. Even though the band never went anywhere, building it opened up a door for me that just wouldn't close.

Having graduated from design school and also having a keen interest in marketing and psychology, I have developed strong beliefs about process and quality. I believe strongly in the design process, including through requirements gathering, user testing, responsive design, and giving the client and end users an optimal experience. I consider myself a front end code quality evangelist. I believe that everyone should have a good experience on the web, from performance to accessibility. I believe in coding standards and keeping up with new technology.

Keeping up with the latest frameworks and concepts in web development is almost a full time job within itself, but using stable tools and having a pre-built toolset can seriously reduce development time and increase efficiency. Writing clean, future friendly code and not creating a lot of technical debt is the only good practice.

I am currently working on a series of blog posts to help other people get started with or at least understand some of the complexities of HTML and CSS.
