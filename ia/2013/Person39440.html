Eddie Osterland M.S.'s Summary

Eddie Osterland, America’s 1st Master Sommelier is an internationally acclaimed expert in the culinary arena of food and wine.

For over 25 years he has educated executives in Power Entertaining with Food & Wine. Clients use Eddie’s advice to strengthen customer relationships, design annual client-appreciation events and execute corporate events that go beyond people’s expectations. They realize how today, more than ever, you cannot afford to be naïve about food and wines—the international currency for connection.
Over the course of his career Eddie has worked with organizations and leaders from the following companies: Citibank, IBM, General Mills, Merrill Lynch, UBS, Esquire magazine, Food & Wines from France, Procter & Gamble, Discover Card, SPRINT, Qwest, Vistage International (225 programs), Crystal Cruises, National Australian Bank, Shoprite, Ritz Carlton, Ford, Discover Card, and Association for Financial Professionals. He participated in some of the major corporate entertaining events for the 2008 Beijing Olympics.

Audiences everywhere know him as funny, approachable and non-intimidating. He gives them the knowledge, confidence and skills they need to enjoy food and wine, and to entertain impressively.

Specialties: Eddie’s educational background evolved from a degree in behavioral psychology to graduating from L’Institut d’oenologie at Université de Bordeaux with the D.U.A.D. degree. He went on to be the 1st American to pass the grueling Master Sommelier Diploma in London in 1973. Upon returning to the US, Eddie worked as the director of trade education at the International Wine Center. He also authored the book “Wine and the Bottom Line” for the National Restaurant Association.
