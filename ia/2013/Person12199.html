Bas Zuiderent's Summary

Procurement Professional with thorough knowledge of Contract Management, Contract Governance and Supplier Relationship Management, very much interested in looking for innovations and innovative procurement methods, Lean Procurement and Best Value Procurement.

International experience by giving presentations on procurement in Europe.

Due to more than 20 years of experience in procurement I know how to act as a go-between and how to work with the client to define the exact need, with the procurement lawyer to establish the framework and with the contract manager to safeguard the business objectives. For me, the means of a successful process include clearly defined and legally correct tendering conditions, a functional and lawful assessment model and audit-proof procurement project.

Bas Zuiderent's Publications

  • How to legitimize the communication in public procurement

    • Zuiderent.nl/blog
    • February 2013
    Authors: Bas Zuiderent, Rui Rompante

    Better communication should lead to alignment of mutual goals, reduction of risk, which consequently could result in reduction of costs and improved cooperation. In other words better communication should be beneficial to both processes strategy and lower the risk. Thus a properly defined procurement strategy is essential for both the procuring party and supplier.


    There are various procurement strategies like Market Consultation, Best Value Procurement and Vested Outsourcing or a tender procedure like Competitive Dialogue. These call for a form of communication, which resembles a joint venture type of cooperation, as these strategies require a process with a lot of interaction between the buyer’s team and the vendor’s team. In fact they might need a project team in which both the vendor as well as the buyer work together. Interaction and communication is required to understand each other, to solve “mutual” problems together. In fact, such strategy might be appropriate in advance of the tender notice.

    This is an effective strategy, but is it legitimate for the public sector as well?
