Kurt Knotts' Summary

Kurt Knotts grew up in central Maine, enjoying the outdoors and rural environment. During High School he worked summer jobs including Parks and Rec, building construction, and landscaping. He studied Geology at Hartwick College in upstate New York, including geological studies of Arizona, Hawaii, and the Catskills and Adirondacks.

After college Mr. Knotts was hired as a Field Scientist at Fuss & O'Neill Consulting Engineers in Manchester, CT. He spent several years sampling groundwater, soil, and wastes at environmentally impacted sites around southern New England. His many and varied duties included overseeing the installation of sewers in Newtown and Lake Besek, overseeing construction and remediation projects, landfill closures, soil gas surveys, and drilling of soil borings and groundwater monitoring wells.

Mr. Knotts was promoted up to the position of Project Hydrogeologist and managed several large environmental investigation and remediation projects. Mr. Knotts earned a non-water well driller license and operated a Geoprobe direct-push drill rig. Over the years he became the manager of Field Operations, Field Training and SOPs, Geoprobe Services, and managed the company fleet of vehicles. He is proficient in computers, Microsoft Office, GISKey, and AutoCAD.

In 2010, Kurt and Kelly Knotts lost their 2nd child, Jamie Lynne, to a rare liver cancer at 5 1/2 months old. Their experience at Connecticut Children's Medical Center led them to want to give back to the staff and families there. The Knotts started a 501(c)(3) Jamie's Run Inc. and have held 3 annual events including a 5K, charity walk, and kids' fun run in Wethersfeild, CT. They have raised over $85,000 for the medical center in three years.

In his spare time, Kurt Knotts is President of Jamie's Run Inc. and is responsible for non-profit management, fundraising, corporate sponsorship, and event coordination.
