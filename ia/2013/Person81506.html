Lisa Fields' Summary

Lisa Fields, founder of Lisa Fields & Associates, is a creative trainer, public speaker and social media consultant. Her passion for visual storytelling, customer relationship management, digital communication, leadership development and conflict resolution enables her to produce results that strengthen organizations and motivate leaders and employees.

For more than twenty years, Lisa has empowered individuals and challenged organizations to reach their full potential. An adult education scholar with a master’s degree in adult education from North Carolina A&T State University, her clients have included small businesses and nonprofit organizations as well as Fortune 500 companies.

Her strong dedication to needs analysis and defining growth opportunities has led her to explore under homes with pest control specialists, shadow paramedics on medical assistance calls and work side-by-side with 911 operators.

Lisa has completed post-graduate work in the study of exceptional learners and is certified in the Myers-Briggs Type Indicator assessment. She holds a paralegal certificate and is a scholarship recipient for the Center for Creative Leadership. Lisa also serves as an instructor for the American Management Association and an adjunct professor at a number of higher education institutions in North Carolina.

Specialties

Social media, customer relationship management, public speaking, presentation design, leadership development, conflict resolution, training, Myers-Briggs Type Indicator.

Lisa Fields' Honors and Awards

  • Top 10 Healthcare Communications and Social Media Tweeters

    Health Care Social Media
    • April 2011

    Greensboro, NC – Lisa Fields, a Greensboro-based adult educator and communications specialist, has been named one of the Top 10 Healthcare Communications and Social Media Tweeters of 2011 by a leading industry group that supports the adoption of social media as an education and communications resource for the global healthcare industry.

    Lisa Fields joins three other Americans, five Australians and a contributor from the United Kingdom in recognition by Health Care Social Media, an organization founded in 2009 to join healthcare professionals in regular dialogue on topics ranging from medical breakthroughs to enhanced patient care procedures.

    By harnessing the real-time communications power of Twitter, the HCSM movement is revolutionizing the exchange of ideas in the medical community through regularly scheduled tweet chats. Participants include doctors, nurses, researchers and other health care specialists in chat sessions sponsored by chapters in the United States, Latin America, Europe and Australia-New Zealand.

    Fields is a regular contributor and facilitator of chats for HCSM, and leads a weekly tweet chat session (#HCSMAZN) for the Australia-New Zealand chapter from her home in Greensboro.

    “I have been amazed by the power of the Twitter platform to educate, stimulate and engage professionals in the sharing of vital information, and it was quite an honor for me to be recognized by the HCSM in this way,” said Fields, who has served as a speaker, trainer, presentation specialist and adult educator in the Piedmont Triad region for more than 15 years.

    She is also a regular contributor to Health Care Communications News and the HCSM on the subject of immunization awareness and serves related content from her website lisafieldsassociates.com and Twitter with the personal ID of PracticalWisdom.
