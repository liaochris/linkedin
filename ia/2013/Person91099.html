Melanie J. Teems' Summary

We crave human touch. We use a hand to draw soothing circles on the backs of our restless infants, place a reassuring hand on the shoulders of our youth, high-five, knuckle bump in celebration. We hug in joy and in sorrow. Even our pets seek out touch from their human companions.
Massage is defined as the manipulation of soft tissue structures of the body to prevent and alleviate pain, discomfort, muscle spasm and stress as well as promote health and wellness.
This, we crave.
My massage methods are deeply rooted in over 24 years of working as a Health Care Professional. Working with athletes is my primary passion, having a keen interest in the body and how it responds to the physical demands caused by training and competing. I enjoy preparing athletes for events and helping them recover from strenuous activity as well as "trouble shooting" discomforts, thus helping these athletes improve their game.
To be of optimal assistance I am continually studying the human body and the pathology that can interfere with movement and the accompanied frustrations.
I also consider the emotional aspect of the athlete. Stress can adversly affect the quality of sleep which may lower the body's immune system, becoming vulnerable to communicable diseases and further weakening the system.
Rest, proper nutrition, healthy relationships and appropriate training are keys to peak performance. As we age we must also adjust to these needs and recognize them when they appear.
Knowing when to walk away from the seriousness of life and have fun is just as important as the strive for excellence.
Massage is not just for athletes. A soothing touch is effective for relieving tension and stress or just working out the kinks from a long car ride! Deep tissue can relieve chronic stress from the challenges of life itself and Pregnancy-Prenatal massage can help mom adjust to her growing shape while her baby is nutured inside.

Specialties

My massage practice focuses on a handful of modalities; Sports, Hot Stone, Relaxation, Swedish, Deep-Tissue, Pregnancy-Prenatal, Body Scrub and Chair massage. I am currently studying Equine Sports Massage through Equissage and practicing on some very well-deserving and happy horses!

The Art Of Teaching provides workshops for licensed massage therapists in the form of continuing education uints (CEUs). My speciality is teaching Sports Massage. My love of ballet dancing, belly dancing, horseback riding, swimming, ice skating and long-distance cycling inspire me to teach the massage technique I rely on and utilize on athletes.
Deep Tissue massage (the other workshop I teach) can be combined with Sports massage giving a thorough massage for the athlete. Deep Tissue massage is also effective on it's own. It's purpose is to relieve chronic muscle tension in the deeper fascia and muscle tissues primarily in the back, neck and shoulders but can be performed everywhere. This modality is practiced with care in order to keep both receiver and therapist from harm.
