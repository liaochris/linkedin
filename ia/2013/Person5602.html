Ameet Mehta's Experience

Co-Founder

Azadi

Partnership; 1-10 employees; Health, Wellness and Fitness industry

March 2012 – Present (1 year 6 months) Chicago/Lucknow

Azadi is the Avon of Rural India for high impact women's health and lifestyle products. Male dominance of the rural retail sector and cultural sensitivities result in products such as sanitary pads and pregnancy tests to be unavailable in rural India. Women can now access such products through the our network of Female Entrepreneurs. Azadi is an Impact Engine backed company.

Summer Analyst

Excelerate Labs

Venture Capital & Private Equity industry

June 2011 – August 2011 (3 months) Greater Chicago Area

Excelerate Labs is the best place in Chicago to learn about digital startups and entrepreneurship. As part of the EXL team, I helped the 10 incubating startups in every way I could. This included everything from sitting through strategy meetings, conducting market research to data entry. The most important lessons learned are - go after a BIG market, clearly differentiate from your competitors and build an effective customer acquisition strategy.

Operations Lead India/Product Manager

Vidya Foundation

March 2010 – May 2011 (1 year 3 months) Hyderabad Area, India

I joined Vidya Grants, a social startup in the education space, a few months after its inception. We secured $1 million in seed funding from the Beck Foundation, funders of Wikipedia and Room to Read, in 2010.

As part of the core team at Vidya, I wear a few different hats. I spent 7 months in India to understand the market, launch our operations and implement the pilot program. In May, 2011, I moved from from the Operations to the Product team. I am helping build our website, which will become our primary source of funding as we scale in the coming years.

Transaction Services Summer Associate

KPMG Advisory

Partnership; 10,001+ employees; Accounting industry

June 2010 – August 2010 (3 months)

We identified value drivers, risks and opportunities based on the target company's financials. I worked on a corporate merger and a private equity deal.

Co-Founder

College Economy Inc.

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Internet industry

January 2009 – June 2010 (1 year 6 months) Greater Chicago Area

We developed web products with a mission to lower the cost of education. Our first attempt, Beyondtheclassroom.com, was a website that crowd sourced video lectures for Math and Science and provided a platform for online tutoring. Our second attempt, Redeal.me, was a Facebook application for students to buy and sell textbooks within their university network.

Associate

Cambridge Realty Capital Companies

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Financial Services industry

February 2009 – August 2009 (7 months) Greater Chicago Area

Cambridge Capital mainly focused on financing Senior Health Care Facilities. I built financial models and provided the initial "deal score". The position gave me an understanding of the all steps that a deal goes through before closing.

Resident Advisor

DePaul University

Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry

September 2008 – March 2009 (7 months) Greater Chicago Area

Our team of RAs built a community for 330 incoming freshman students that addressed their social, academic and cultural needs. The position gave me a chance to understand people and develop leadership and crisis management skills. Lessons learned as an RA have proved to be invaluable throughout my career.
