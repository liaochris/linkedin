Eric Fisher's Summary

I'm a storyteller turned product architect with a passion for communicating ideas and a love of building projects that connect people in meaningful ways.

From an early age, I was very interested in how things fit together to form something bigger and more meaningful. During and since college, my professional focus is in utilizing social technology to build and strengthen communities, helping to enhance the clarity of our expressions through our interactions within them.

Although I'm both a designer and programmer (BSE degree), I consider myself an applied generalist. My specialty lies broadly in understanding people and translating their needs into product design concepts that can help make the world a little bit more of the place I wish it were.

Eric Fisher's Experience

Founder, Product + Design

Voxle

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Internet industry

January 2012 – Present (1 year 6 months) New York, New York

Turn Content into Conversation; Foster Community

With conversation fragmented online across a multitude of services, it's difficult to organize all the people, objects and ideas in your community in one place. More importantly, even the most powerful admin tools still require you alone to do all the curatorial work.

Voxle is a social publishing platform matching a high quality social front-end experience with robust content management tools behind the scenes, giving you the tools to define exactly what your community is about and enabling everyone to publish and curate content together, all while learning from and interacting with one another in conversation.

Social Design Evangelist + Product Designer

Facebook

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Internet industry

February 2010 – August 2011 (1 year 7 months) Palo Alto, CA

I was the design mind behind platform efforts, serving as a communicator between the external and the internal. Externally, I educated and collaborated with all companies big and small on social design and how to build Facebook into their core product strategy. Internally, I redesigned and redefined the developer site and developer tools while also devising better conceptual documentation (including authoring the Facebook Social Design Guidelines - http://developers.facebook.com/blog/post/541 ).

I also worked on the product design team where I lead the design and strategy on numerous projects - including Insights, Friend Lists, News Feed story aggregation, the all-in-one publisher - and contributed designs to Places, Events and the 2010 profile revamp.

Interaction Designer

Google

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; GOOG; Internet industry

2009 – 2009 (less than a year) Mountain View, CA

Looking to broaden my experiences, I joined Google as a member of the Search design team which contributed the various elements and features of the search interface and logo redesign. I focused most of my attention on prototyping new interfaces for structured data in search, collaborating with the then-small Google Squared team (which has since acquired Freebase, grown tremendously and are more integral in search quality). Working with engineers and product managers, I aimed to make search results more relevant and the search experience more interactive and engaging. I also designed new features for Webmaster Tools to enhance its use and functionality.

User Interface Engineer

Apple Inc.

2007 – 2008 (1 year) Cupertino, CA

A mix between design and engineering, I restructured and redesigned Apple’s then-small internal project management website, overhauling the original, static system to create a more dynamic, user-friendly version with customizable and intuitive web front-ends to display & edit a variety of project data.
