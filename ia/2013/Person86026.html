Marina Klima Goldberg, allied ASID, CAPS's Summary

I am passionate about making people’s homes reflect who they really are. We all change but surprisingly, our environments do not always change with us. My goal is to make homes more accessible, comfortable, universal and timeless so that as people get older or their physical abilities change they can still enjoy their homes. I research the architectural materials and markets globally just to find glamorous, beautiful and unique materials that are also more friendly and safe for people.

I believe that interiors should be accessible and inclusive not only for people on wheelchair or certain disabilities but as part of any design process. For example, even when I design a formal great room I include at least one chair with a higher seat for a person with troubled knees. A wheelchair bathroom does not have to look different. It can include glamorous combination of classic and contemporary forms and materials and still have accessible vanities and a spacious floor plan.

I see my contribution in creating designs that are friendly and unique, colors that are harmonious with nature, objects that create unity and rhythm.

I want to contribute to my community by helping people stay in the neighborhoods rather than being forced to move out of their homes when they are older.

Because our vendors are our dedicated industry partners that work with us every step of the way, our clients never have to worry about discontinued, damaged or undelivered items. We take care of any situation keeping our clients informed and excited until the day of the final installation!

DESIGN STYLE

Residential Interiors: Single space to 7,000 square feet homes.
