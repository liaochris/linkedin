Alessia Messuti's Summary

I have a background in communication with a special focus on the use of new media for social inclusion and local development.

Since 2009, I am working as training specialist in both private and development sector, where I have gained experience in instructional design and quality assurance for e-Learning, facilitation of online and face-to-face trainings, educational research and web 2.0 for learning and social interaction.

Fascinated by the idea that distance education and the use of new technology increases access to learning and promote life-long learning, I keep exploring innovative pedagogical approaches for adult learners.

The International Training Centre of the ILO

Non profit; 51-200 dipendenti; Settore: Nonprofit Organization Management

novembre 2013 – Presente (2 mesi) Turin, Italy/ Heerlen, The Netherlands

“Mobile Workplace Learning: innovative interventions for continuous skills’ development”

The overall objective of the project is to encourage experimentation in education, and design innovative learning environments that will help building skills useful in a world where jobs are increasingly knowledge and information-based.

In particular, the project addresses learning and development professionals of training institutions who have little time to attend formal training sessions and rely more on informal and mobile solutions.

Objectives:

- Implementing a micro mobile learning framework for accelerated social and collaborative workplace learning; This framework will constitute guidance for introducing micro mobile learning in workplace settings encapsulating the dimensions of effective learning, mobile learning and workplace learning.

- Launching a pilot m-Workplace Learning initiative for learning and development staff.
The pilot target of the project will be constituted by staff of the International Training Centre of the ILO in Turin.

I will work for 12 months at the International Training Centre of the ILO in Turin with the Distance Education & Learning Technology Application unit (DELTA), specialized in learning and knowledge sharing methodologies. Tom Wambeke, Senior Programme Officer with comprehensive experience in the field will provide methodological support and overall supervision to the project. On the other hand, I will also receive regular feedback from the academic tutors of the project Barbara Bruschi, associate professor in Teaching & Learning Technologies at the University of Turin and Marco Kalz, associate professor at the centre for Learning Sciences and Technologies (CELSTEC) of the Open University of the Netherlands.

Finally, in November 2014 I will be hosted by CELSTEC centre based in Heerlen for a 6-month period of substantial research and concrete application of the last phases of the project.
