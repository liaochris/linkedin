DLIST MAGAZINE

Apparel & Fashion industry

March 2010 – January 2012 (1 year 11 months) Downtown Seattle, WA

Established base look & feel for 2012 issues. Established a new brand look and feel for the publication starting January 2011. Established new website design and backend CMS starting April 2011. With the assistance from a select group of designers, I create various spread designs, layouts, typography & illustrations on a monthly basis. Founder

SEVENTY EIGHT

March 2005 – March 2010 (5 years 1 month) World

Seventy Eight was my freelance design studio in LA. In its 5 year existence, I have designed for various clients such as boutique and shop owners, fashion brands, charity foundations & magazines. I oversaw project management, made sure communication was open between all parties and that deadlines are met and kept tight. It has given me opportunities to keep my design skills sharp and always present and to be active in the community wherever I traveled.

Creative Director

EARLY MORNING PRINT BOUTIQUE

January 2009 – February 2010 (1 year 2 months) Tukwila, WA

As the leader of a 3 man design team, I assisted in all aspects of running a concept tee shirt print shop that featured live design sessions in a retail mall setting. I helped customers create a one of a kind tee shirt & garments and also assisted in transferring their artwork to print ready files. I also oversaw quality print control of our in house direct to garment printer on a day to day basis. Created the company web site, print material, in store prints, & multitudes of apparel graphics.

Web Designer / E-commerce Administrator

HELLZ BELLZ

April 2009 – January 2010 (10 months) Downtown Los Angeles, CA

Design, coding, animation, and implementation of a new & improved web site for the urban fashion women’s line Hellz Bellz. It was a flash based site with a clean cut & sleek approach. When launched in late June, the site was received highly from their fan base and garnered attention from Kanye West which he featured on his personal blog. I created their e-commerce shop using zen-cart doing both front end and back end work of the site. It was officially launched on Black Friday 2009 and generated thousands of dollars in sales. Due to me moving from Los Angeles, it was decided to switch to a local web designer for accessibility.

Graphic Designer

BACKSIDE MUSIC & APPAREL SHOP

April 2008 – July 2009 (1 year 4 months) Burbank, CA

A 15 year old shop that sells music & urban apparel located in the Burbank Valley, I helped establish an official logo design for the shop, created their 2008 summer tee shirt line, website, their supporting e-commerce site www.dropping-styles.com, and designed various in store print material. Through Backside I was able to collaborate on projects with brands such as Obey, Freshjive, & Akomplice.

Co-owner / Graphic Designer

ONE MAN STAND

January 2008 – December 2008 (1 year) Los Angeles & Hong Kong

I had the opportunity to start up a tee shirt line with a business colleague to create an urban apparel clothing brand. I took care of the apparel graphics, web site, e-commerce site, social site profiles, look books and photography. In December 2008 I had chosen to sell my half of the company to my business partner to pursue other interests in the design field.

Web Designer / Administrator

Fashion Institute of Design and Merchandising

Educational Institution; 501-1000 employees; Higher Education industry

March 2005 – December 2006 (1 year 10 months) Downtown Los Angeles, CA

I was appointed by the tech team to work for FIDM's Museum Shop & Galleries. As the web site designer, I helped establish their e-commerce site at www.fidmmuseumshop.com. The official site launch was coincided with the 2005 premiere exhibition "Star Wars: Dressing A Galaxy". The site was critical to the success and sell of the exclusive book that went along with the exhibition. I also worked closely with the museum department creating print graphics for monthly gallery exhibitions and also in store print graphics for the physical museum shop.
