Karen Karo's Summary

Being a mentor has always been a big part of any job I have ever had. I can't think of any job where I did not mentor new employees or introduce and train people on new systems. I have worked many years as a Registered Nurse. As a Visiting Nurse I needed to mentor patients, their family and their friends. As a Charge Nurse in hospitals I needed to mentor Graduate Nurses, Nurses new to the hospital, Nursing Assistants, and more.

I also come from a family of Authors so writing has also been one of my strengths. One of my biggest accomplishments was when my writing skills acquired funding for more nursing hours to care for special needs clients.

When I started my Internet Marketing Career my writing also came in handy. I have Authored many articles for myself as well as for others. I wish I had more time to author even more articles.

Visiting Nurse

Many VNA Groups

January 2007 – December 2009 (3 years) Lehigh Valley

I have traveled all over the Lehigh Valley to care for and mentor patients and their families in their homes. This was a very rewarding type of nursing. I the hospitals you are always trying to balance the needs of each of your patients. As a Visiting Nurse you can give that one on one mentoring that is so beneficial to the patient. As a Hospice Visiting Nurse the mentoring was mostly for the family to learn how to care for their loved one. This was the most rewarding nursing. You became part of their family. As you helped them through their grieving process you grieved as well. I remember these days well.

Charge Nurse

LV Hospital Muhlenberg

January 1997 – September 2006 (9 years 9 months) Bethlehem, PA

I started working at Muhlenberg Hospital shortly after graduating nursing school. Within two weeks of starting I became the one and only Charge Nurse on the night shift for my floor. All the RNs I worked with were PRN or agency. If it wasn't for a long time LPN I would have been lost. It wasn't right that I was in charge of her. She had so much experience. All I had was different letters after my name. She was my mentor and made it possible for me to mentor all the nurses that did eventually get hired to help on our very understaffed floor. I learned fast to take charge and make decisions at a moment notice. This job shaped my career as a nurse and as a mentor. I was never the same.
