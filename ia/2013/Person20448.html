Dr Carol Marsh's Summary

I am the Firmware Process Lead (UK) at Selex ES.

In November 2012 I became a Chartered Engineer (CEng).

In July 2012 I became a Fellow of the Institution of Engineering and Technology (IET).

On 30 June 2011 I was awarded the degree of Doctor of Engineering in System Level Integration (EngD) from the Universities of Glasgow, Edinburgh, Heriot-Watt and Strathclyde.

A summary of my career history is as follows:
I've had over 27 years experience in the Electronics Industry primarily in the Firmware field, currently as a functional manager. I've worked in the defence, telecommunications and security intellectual property (IP) industries with both large and small companies. I have a wide range of expertise including technical, leadership, research and communication skills.

I've been a team lead responsible for a small team of engineers and for ensuring that projects from concept to delivery were completed on time and within budget. I've produced Firmware designs for all major vendor devices; Xilinx, Altera, Actel and Lattice and designed modules using Mentor schematic design capture tools.

I've presented at 7 conferences worldwide and published 8 papers, including 2 journal papers.

I'm vice-president (Scotland) for the Women's Engineering Society (WES).

Specialties: I'm proficient at writing VHDL code for all the major FPGA and CPLD devices. I have extensive knowledge of FPGA simulation, synthesis and place and route tools.
I am conversant with PERL script language and also have experience with Verilog, System Verilog, e, C, Spec C
