Donna Johnson-Klonsky, MBA's Summary

Business & Practice Strategist and Executive & Leadership Consulting Coach has over twenty-five years experience in organizational, personnel, program, project and curriculum development, administration, executive and leadership development, and change and transition management. She received her MBA from SUNY Empire State College in June, 2011. Her MBA concentration was in Marketing & Small Business Entrepreneurship.

Donna has extensive experience as a business consultant coach for numerous mid-high level executives and their companies in various industries. In the dental industry, she has been retained as a practice management consultant, to provide business analysis, human resource (HR) management, marketing, patient retention and growth and implement patient management software and the National Health Infrastructure Information initiative (NHII). She has expertise as well as an ad-hoc advisor to various dentists in the Tri-State Area and has successfully implemented programs that have increased levels of productivity and profitability.

Donna has strong professional ethics and excels in leadership, partnership, supervisory, and team roles. She enjoys partnerships and collaborative contractual project assignments. Donna works with all levels of personnel in a manner which motivates team work and the achievement of collective goals. She is enthusiastic, dedicated, and self-motivated and demonstrates a strong dedication to her community.

  • Yes, You can Master Change!

    • Fashion Manuscript
    • May 2012
    Authors: Donna Johnson-Klonsky, MBA

    What does the word MASTER bring to mind? How does that word resonate with you? For me, it brings to mind Golf Masters, Master Chefs and Master Degrees. Individuals who pursue levels of mastery have something in common. They distinguish themselves through dedication, learning and perseverance. They continue to improve and are never satisfied with mediocrity. They accept that what they desire comes with many challenges, which includes not always being successful, at first attempts, and always learning from their mistakes.

    A quick look in the Encarta Dictionary offered these definitions for the word master: boss, somebody in control, somebody highly skilled, player at high level and leader. Let’s take a moment to reflect on an athlete, about whom there are no adjectives that can fully describe their historic performance. It would be hard for anyone, probably in the world, to deny that Tiger Woods is a master when it comes to golf. Tiger is very much his own boss and demands, from himself, high performance and results, not excuses. He exhibits incredible control when it comes to focusing on a specific hole or round. He is without a doubt one of the most highly skilled athletes. There are few who would deny that his actions on the field portray him as someone who’s constantly on the lookout for how changes will affect his game. He persistently attempts to modify his actions to master change.
