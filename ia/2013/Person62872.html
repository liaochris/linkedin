Jessica Dervin-Ackerman's Summary

Jess Dervin-Ackerman is the Conservation Program Coordinator for the SF Bay Chapter of the Sierra Club, where she is solely focused on working with Sierra Club volunteers, and interns to mobilize the Bay Area Community around conservation issues. One of Jess's core campaigns is to create robust Community Choice energy programs in San Francisco and the East Bay that will bring green jobs and economic development; she serves on the steering committee for Clean Energy and Jobs Oakland Campaign. Jess also works on important issues such as waste diversion, energy efficiency upgrades, land use and transportation.

Prior to that, Jess worked with the international organization 350.org, where she mobilized citizens through grassroots organizing to take bold action to fight the climate crisis. Jess is also currently on the steering committee for 350 Bay Area, a community activist group working to reduce CO2 emissions and create a powerful climate action movement in the SF Bay Area.

Jess received a B.S. in Environmental Geology from Northeastern University, where she helped create a student-generated renewable energy fund and led the student effort to advocate for policy changes to address climate change and resource allocation for operational, academic, and institutional sustainability programs. During her time at Northeastern, Jess worked to recruit signatories for the American College and University Presidents' Climate Commitment and assisted in creating more substantial framework for the commitment.
