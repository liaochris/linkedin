melinda walker's Summary

The two most influential books in my life were well worn by the time I started kindergarten. The first, Ed Emberley's Drawing Book of Animals, gave me the skills to make my thoughts visible to others. In the other, Norman Rockwell's Americana ABC, I discovered one of the most powerful ways to deeply understand and truly remember things - through pictures. And so began my life-long adventure in creative and visual thinking!

As an art teacher, I shared the very same books, and lessons learned from them, with my students. My goal was not to produce great artists, but great creative thinkers. I taught my students how to make their thoughts visible, flesh them out, and articulate them to others. I also taught them to look for patterns and relationships, to make new connections, and how to find ideas pretty much anywhere.

In turn, my students taught me plenty: Simplicity makes things understandable, memorable, and doable; clear communication is paramount to success; and the best stories are usually the ones we discover ourselves, rarely the ones that are told to us.

That's what my business is all about. Using pictures to make things simple, engaging, and memorable so you can understand, decide, communicate, and move to action more quickly. Once things are visible, patterns and relationships are more readily discovered, connections more easily made, and new ideas more likely to surface. Your story surfaces, too, so you can write your happy ending and determine the steps needed to make it real.

Visit my website www.OneSquigglyLine.com for more info or email your questions to info@OneSquigglyLine.com. Also follow me on Twitter @OneSquigglyLine to see what interesting things I've discovered related to visual thinking, creativity, leadership, education, and also some of my work.
