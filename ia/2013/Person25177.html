Courtney (C.J.) Sinner's Summary

As Multimedia Web Producer/Editor at the Pioneer Press, I wear quite a few digital journalism hats. My boss has called me "Expandable Foam Insulation" because I "fill in the gaps" of whatever needs to be done, from helping out with daily web producing to homepage management and social media strategy. Mostly, though, I love to shoot and edit beautiful video features to pair with reporters' stories, and building web interactives to add a digital layer to the Pioneer Press' journalism. I'm still a novice when it comes to hardcore data work and coding, but keen to learn as time permits.

Courtney (C.J.) Sinner's Projects

  • Target Field: A Day in the Life

    • June 2013 to July 2013
    Team Members: Courtney (C.J.) Sinner, John Autey, Brian Murphy

    What goes on behind closed doors and in corridors beneath the seats? What does it mean to play host to 30,000-plus guests? For three games during the July 1-3 Twins-Yankees series, the Pioneer Press recorded, photographed and interviewed Target Field employees to chronicle a typical day in the life of the 3-year-old ballpark.

  • Public pension 'spiking': Overtime hours soar for St. Paul fire supervisors

    • November 2012 to December 2012
    Team Members: Courtney (C.J.) Sinner, MaryJo Webster, Mara Gottfried, Christopher Magan

    The Pioneer Press examined how deputy and district fire chiefs amassed huge amounts of overtime their final years on the public payroll, inflating the salaries on which their pensions would be based. The eight top-paid city employees were all in the fire department making more than the fire chief, police chief and mayor.

    I helped create the data visualizations that partnered with the reported story.

  • Finding fawns in Wisconsin's forests

    • June 2013 to June 2013
    Team Members: Courtney (C.J.) Sinner, Nick Woltman

    I reported and filmed a story on the Wisconsin DNR's research on fawn survival, going out with a group of volunteers to track down the newborns to give them tags and radio collars.

  • Interactive: The Lost Department Stores of Downtown St. Paul

    • January 2013 to March 2013
    Team Members: Courtney (C.J.) Sinner, Tom Webb

    I hand-coded this -- admiteddly buggy -- interacive after business reporter Tom Webb came to me with the concept. We timepegged it to Macy's closing in downtown St. Paul, and the feature was extremely popular.
