meg studer's Summary

Trained as both a ‘researcher’ and a ‘maker,’ I aspire to craft visualizations, interfaces, and animations that both inform audiences and invite innovative engagement. I particularly enjoy explaining socio-material and spatial processes, but am interested in collaborative projects and inquisitive peers.

My areas of specialty are landscape logistics and media ecologies. The former explores the operations and resource regimes that underly public works and private consumption (1820-present). The latter examines the territorial traces of land-art and the post-war communications/infrastructural networks that they index (1940s-70s).
