Katy Dickinson's Summary

2010-now: Katy Dickinson Consulting creates measurably effective long-lasting infrastructure and processes in a professional and technical environment. Successful large programs have included global mentoring, innovation and product life cycle, and corporate intellectual property management systems. Mentoring Process Architect for the first class of the U.S. State Department's TechWomen mentoring program (2010-2011).

2012-2013: Vice President, Mentoring Solutions, MentorCloud, Sunnyvale, California. Designed and supported development of new mentoring programs for customers in Afghanistan, Ethiopia, Kenya, Lebanon, Palestine, South Africa, and the USA.

2010-2012: Director of Huawei Technologies (Futurewei), Santa Clara, California. Created and managed R&D operational infrastructure.

1984-2010: Sun Microsystems, Director of Business Process Architecture (and Six Sigma Master Black Belt) in the CTO & Sun Labs organizations. In 2001-2010, created and ran Sun Microsystems’ global Engineering mentoring program. Worked for Sun in Engineering, Marketing, Quality, Operations, Legal, Standards, Strategy, the Chief Technologist’s Office, and Sun Labs. Patent in electronic commerce (2000) and a defensive patent publication on the SEED mentoring process (2004) as well as two other patent applications.

1992-2004: Volunteer lecturer and reviewer for the University of California at Berkeley’s Engineering-110 class called “Venture Design: The Start-up Company”.
