Larry Weiss ("Larry the Phone Guy")'s Experience

President/CEO

ACGTele.com ~ Atlantic Computer Group, Inc.

2003 – Present (10 years)

Formerly the provisioning arm of Larry Weiss Associates, Inc., and taking advantage of our vast experience in the telecom arena, Atlantic Computer Group has launched ACGTele.com.

ACGTele.com offers extremely low cost telephone services to small businesses and professional practices, usually with savings of up to 50% (sometimes up to 90%) over phone company and cable telephone services. Multi-line business phone service starts at $2.95 a month (yes, the decimal point is in the right place).

ACGTele.com also offers a variety of extremely low cost ancillary services and virtual office solutions such as virtual PBX, call queues, ring groups, voice mail to email, DISA and much more, as well as toll free numbers and local phone numbers in almost every North American market plus 50 countries – all of which can route right to your business – and even your cell phone.

Executive and Personal Coach

Larry Weiss Coaching

1992 – Present (21 years)

Personal "whole life" coaching practice with specializations in entrepreneurial and small business empowerment and success, relationships, addiction recovery and a focus on life management, balance and enjoyment.

Coaching is a multifaceted peer process that includes, among other things, the identification and achievement of goals, improvement of performance, the balancing and enjoyment of life, relationship building, unblocking obstacles, brainstorming and bringing out the absolute best in people.

Current clients include health care and business professionals, as well as recent retirees, young entrepreneurs and people in transition. My practice is limited to only a half dozen individuals at a time so that my clients can get full attention.

As an active credentialed coach I serve as the Chairman of the International Coach Federation Regulatory Framework Taskforce, and also serve on the board of the Long Island Coaching Alliance. I am exceedingly well trained and am credentialed by the International Coach Federation.

President/CEO

Larry Weiss Associates, Inc.

1983 – Present (30 years)

Company provides Imaging and Communications Products and Services for business, community, institutions and individuals.

Services include advertising, marketing, corporate imaging, media support, telecommunications, Web design, and Interactive Telemedia.

Notable products include Weather-by-Phone community weather services, History Phone community walking tours, Cellbee-The Cell Phone Audio Guide – all based on the company’s proprietary, award winning Cellbee audiotex platform.

The company also provides consulting and coaching services to entrepreneurs and small business, and has a focus on developing projects that provide community benefit.

· VoIP and Audiotex telecom system and service bureau design, implementation and operation - consulting and sales.

· Telecommunications technology and regulatory consultant.

· Full service advertising and public relations agency providing broadcast, print, Internet, audiotex and electronic advertising services and support.

Commander & Certified Safe Boating Instructor

United States Power Squadrons

Nonprofit; 11-50 employees; Maritime industry

1999 – 2009 (10 years)

While this was a volunteer position administering a not-for-profit organization, the 20 odd hour per week position of United States Power Squadrons commander warrants resume status. The United States Power Squadrons Operations Manual describes the role of the Commander thusly:

"The principal officer of each squadron of USPS is the commander (Cdr) who is elected by the active members of the squadron. The commander presides at all meetings of the squadron, including executive committee meetings, and must be fully acquainted with all the activities and operations of the squadron. While the commander is assisted by a staff of officers, committees and educational boards, he is responsible for the success of squadron administration. He is ex officio a member of all squadron departments and committees except as provided in the squadron’s bylaws."

To say that the above is an understatement would be, in and of itself, an understatement.
