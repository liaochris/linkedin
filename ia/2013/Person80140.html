Leonard Murphy's Summary

I'm an advocate for self-service Business Intelligence. No successful business can afford to wait until the end of the month to find out how they're doing on key metrics such as sales. No business area can be successful if every report has to be hand-written by IT. My goal is to unlock the value in the data so that trends can be identified, problems can be spotted and questions answered.

In addition to my day job, I'm a regular presenter on Business Intelligence topics at Madison's local SQL Server user group (MADPASS) and have also spoken at SQL Saturday events in both Madison and Chicago. (I also help organize the SQL Saturday Madison event each year).

Specialties: Microsoft Business Intelligence Stack including SQL Server 2012 Analysis Services (Multi-dimensional OLAP and Tabular), PowerPivot, Power View and Sharepoint as well as T-SQL development, Integration Services and Reporting Services (Native & Integrated Mode).

Leonard Murphy's Experience

Business Intelligence Business Analyst

Trek Bicycle Corporation

Privately Held; 1001-5000 employees; Sporting Goods industry

October 2011 – Present (2 years 1 month) Madison, Wisconsin Area

At Trek, my job is to work with business areas including Sales, Customer Service, Quality, Manufacturing and Warranty to help them leverage the full power of Trek's Business Intelligence infrastructure (which includes 18 OLAP cubes and 4 tabular cubes covering areas such as sales, warranty, dealer analytics, market share, call center and help-desk metrics). I also work with BI developers, writing agile stories and providing validation of completed development work. I promote the use of self-service Business Intelligence tools across the entire enterprise, including PowerPivot, Power View, Excel pivot tables and charts and Report Builder. I provide one-on-one support on these tools and also host trainings.

Sr Application Engineer

Summit Credit Union

Nonprofit; 201-500 employees; Banking industry

December 2004 – September 2011 (6 years 10 months) Madison, WI

I started at Summit as a 2nd shift IT operator and within a year was promoted to be the team lead for IT data processing. In that role, I set the standard for automation (for example automating overdraft protection and line of credit renewal decisions) and was frequently selected to be the technical lead on projects, including several mergers. After the 2008 merger with Great Wisconsin Credit Union, I transitioned into an Application Engineer role. I designed & built a data warehouse & reporting solution using SQL Server, combining data from multiple systems and providing interactive, parameterized SSRS reports that had no comparison to the previous Access based reports that were the norm.
