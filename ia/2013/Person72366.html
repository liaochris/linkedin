Karl Baehr's Summary

I’m an entrepreneur, and an educator. I’ve been both for a while now, some 30+ years as an entrepreneur, mostly in media, and related technologies. Close to 15 years in higher education where I have focused on the development, and administration of academic programs, and teaching. For most of the last decade I have been the Director of Business & Entrepreneurial Studies at Emerson College in Boston. I love what I do, and I’m pretty damn good at it too. Fortune Magazine selected me a Top Professor of Entrepreneurship for my work at Emerson. That was cool. The success of business education at Emerson has fueled larger initiatives in enterprise development, and innovation at the College, also very cool. I remain an active entrepreneur, and have done quite a bit professionally. A peek at my latest effort, Airbridge Labs, is provided under "Patents" below. My work, and that of my students, has been featured in many media including: CNN, NBC, USA Today, The WSJ, Financial Times, The Boston Globe, and Herald, Inc., Entrepreneur, and Billboard Magazine, the Travel Channel, and the Food Network!

My B.A. is in Radio, Television, and Film from Stephen F. Austin State University. My graduate work (M.A.) in Diffusion of Innovations was completed at the University of New Mexico with noted scholar Everett Rogers. My Ph.D. in Communication is from Regent University.

While there is a lot of information about me here on LinkedIn, I invite you to visit karlbaehr.com for all the latest, and greatest.

I am a LinkedIn Open Networker (LION), and invite you to connect.

My Specialties: Movin’, chakin’, bringin’ home the bacon. I grill a mean steak.

Karl Baehr's Experience

Managing Director

InterTerra New Media Group

2006 – Present (7 years) Greater Atlanta Area

InterTerraNMG is a business development firm active in media, publishing, entertainment, medical technology marketing, and investment banking. To date the Company has published two books: 3Principles: Building a Future in Peru and Latin America, a book about the economic, and socio-political dynamics throughout Latin America, and The Next Paradigm: Common Sense for This Generation, a work of words and music, TNP is a socio-political treatise addressing needed change in the world today. InterTerra also produced The Music Room, a world-music program originally aired on CNNi. The Company owned Jack's Restaurant on St. Thomas (Best damn wings in the Caribbean!), but sold its interest in 2011.

Current Company initiatives focus on InterTerra Medical Technologies, a medical enterprise development and marketing initiative built around the Cyberknife oncological-surgery technology, and InterTerra Medical Espanol, a Spanish-language website that will provide detailed cancer information, links, and resources. Also in development is a similar site, InterTerra Medical India, and Books & Writers, a television program in the genre of Inside the Actors Studio, for authors.

Director, Business & Entrepreneurial Studies

Emerson College

Educational Institution; 201-500 employees; Higher Education industry

2004 – Present (9 years) Boston, MA

I came to Emerson to build a college-wide program in entrepreneurial studies. That program, the Emerson Experience in Entrepreneurship (E3) was launched Fall 2005. Today over 20% of ALL the students who have completed the E3 Program are involved in a business of their own creation. Boom!

The Business Studies in Communication & the Arts Industries Program was implemented Fall 2008. Over 200 students are enrolled in Business Education at Emerson. This success has served as the catalyst for new, college-wide initiatives including the Office of Enterprise Development and Innovation, and expanded business and entrepreneurship programs, and related events planned for Emerson's new LA campus when it opens in 2014.

Our business programs feature immersive academics, and applied learning provided by faculty experienced in business, and teaching. As Director I remain active in the classroom, as well as managing budgets, curriculum, and fund development, partner and mentor programs within the business, and college communities, faculty recruitment, and development.

My service to the institution includes participation on standing college-wide governing, and funding bodies, the NEASC Reaccreditation Committee, Academic Policy Committee, Faculty Funding and Grant Committee, departmental, curriculum, and search committees. I also serve on the Athletics Workgroup, the LA Center Planning Committee, the Office of Enterprise Development and Innovation Planning Committee, served on the President's Committee on Diversity and Inclusion, and as a mentor in the Viacom Scholarship Program.

To find out why Emerson College Means Business visit us Emerson.edu!

President & Principal

Knowledge Based Evolution (KBE)

1984 – 2006 (22 years) Albuquerque, New Mexico Area

KBE worked with small emerging, and growth-stage businesses, primarily in the media, and technology spaces, helping to evolve their business strategy, secure funding, and assist those companies that had strayed off course to get back on track.

KBE also developed, and commercialized its own products and services, spinning off several companies. KBE's innovations included: The MFlow music scheduling software, RTx a remote-broadcast-transmitter monitoring service, launched with the Morningstar Radio Network, and Actual Radio Measurement, Inc., a technology-based radio ratings company (see below). Other initiatives included: Virtual Ventures Group International, an early online community helping to match startups with investors, Videon, a company developing test-prep tutorial videos for standardized tests, the award-winning Addictions radio program syndicated via satellite from studios in Detroit, Radio Tropico Internacional, an early satellite radio venture in Mexico serving the tourism industry, and Innova Internet Research Technologies, an early web-based research system (see below).

During the KBE Years, and a bit beyond, (1992 - 2008) I served as COO of Broadcast Entertainment Corporation. In this capacity I helped build a group of 12 radio stations in Texas, New Mexico, and California. After almost 20 years of broadcasting in the wild wild west, BEC sold the last of its holdings to Tall Grass Communications in 2008. For me this capped a 30 year career in radio that started on the air in 1978 at 92K in Nacogdoches, Texas.

All-in-all KBE enjoyed a 22 year run, not bad!

President & CEO

Actual Radio Measurement, Inc. (ARM)

1991 – 1998 (7 years) Naples, Florida Area

Actual Radio Measurement was a radio ratings company that Electronic Media Magazine called, "Radio's reality check." ARM employed proprietary technology to detect listening events in cars as they passed by a roadside device called a Traffic Survey Unit (TSU). At its peak ARM maintained research operations in 10 cities, providing its research to over 100 subscribers in the media, and advertising industries.

Karl Baehr's Patents

  • System for determining radio listenership

    • United States Patent 5839050
    • Issued November 17, 1998
    Inventors: Karl Baehr

    The Traffic Survey Unit technology was developed, and deployed by Actual Radio Measurement, Inc., a spinoff from KBE. The TSU, when placed roadside, would detect, and log radio listening events in cars as they passed by. The TSU encompassed proprietary hardware and software systems, and was further refined through partnerships with Technology Ventures Corporation in Albuquerque, and NASA's JPL.

  • Method of controlling a computer device over existing broadcast media acoustic channels

    • United States Patent Application 131750414
    • Filed January 25, 2013
    Inventors: Karl Baehr, Kerry Davis

    The technology of Airbridge Labs provides a direct, active connection between traditional media, and mobile users by accessing smart-device applications, or services acoustically. This represents instant, seamless engagement between brands, and consumers. Airbridge "Beacon Servers" turn static structures like signs, displays, and walls into active communication tools. Pretty cool stuff.
