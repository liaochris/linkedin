JG Wentworth's Summary

J.G. Wentworth was founded in 1991 as a merchant banking firm and shortly thereafter began to exploit opportunities in the market for purchasing deferred payment streams.

By the latter 1990s, J.G. Wentworth was focused almost exclusively on the purchase of payment streams from structured settlements and annuities. The company brought institutional capital and national advertising to the marketplace, and in doing so, helped transform the secondary market for structured settlements from a cottage industry into a bona fide specialty finance business.

J.G. Wentworth played an instrumental role in crafting model legislation for the regulation of the sale of structured settlement payment streams. Subsequently, 47 states adopted legislation regulating the sale of structured settlement payments and today, all purchases conducted by J.G. Wentworth are approved by a judge.

Since the firm’s founding, J.G. Wentworth has relied on the same spokesperson, the eponymous and fictional Mr. Wentworth. Over the years commercials featuring Mr. Wentworth have become increasingly creative and the firm is now well known to its’ customers as wells as many fans on the Internet.

J.G. Wentworth’s provocative and award winning commercials began in 2008 with the release of Opera, which featured Viking characters singing a Wagnerian composition about their need for cash now. The ad utilized the J.G. Wentworth’s ubiquitous vanity phone number, 877-CASH-NOW. Opera won two International Summit Awards, one Davey Award which honors outstanding local, regional, and cable TV commercials.

Today, J.G. Wentworth is owned by private equity firm JLL Partners in New York City. Since it’s founding J.G. Wentworth has purchased approximately $4 billion of future payment obligations from consumers, and has issued 22 securitizations of structured settlement and annuity-backed notes totaling approximately $2.3 billion.
