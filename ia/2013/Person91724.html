Co-Author

The EFactor: Entrepreneurship in the Social Media Age

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Information Technology and Services industry

November 2012 – Present (1 year 2 months) San Francisco Bay Area

#1 in Marketing and #1 in Web Marketing on Amazon!

In their latest book, The E-Factor: Entrepreneurship in the Social Media Age, Adrie Reinders and Marion Freijsen use real world examples and case studies of companies from around the world to examine everything today’s entrepreneur needs to think about and know how to deal with, including current challenges and issues, funding alternatives, social media, virtual and traditional networking, new working environments and strategies for the ongoing evolution of the entrepreneurial environment.

Today’s entrepreneurs share many of the same qualities as those before them. They have passion, great ideas, and a drive to pursue these ideas. They think outside of the box and take a non-linear approach to problems and solutions. They thrive in complex and challenging environments.

However, today’s entrepreneurs automatically have to think international - with the rapid increase of the Internet, technology, blogs and social networks, entrepreneurs cannot afford to simply think about their local audience. They ought to be aware of the global aspects of their business and plugged into the virtual world through social networking and social media.
