Lynn Loschin's Summary

I research and draft summaries of the facts and law in criminal, civil, and dependency appellate cases. The subjects cover just about anything you could imagine in state law.

Specialties

appellate law, appellate procedure, civil procedure, criminal procedure, complex litigation, real estate law, tort law, contracts, juvenile law, dependency law, sentencing law, legal writing

Lynn Loschin's Experience

Senior Research Attorney

California Court of Appeal

April 2001 – Present (12 years 6 months)

Research and draft summaries of facts and legal issues in criminal, civil, and juvenile appellate cases. The subject areas cover a vast range of substantive and procedural issues.

Adjunct Professor of Legal Writing

Whittier Law School

Educational Institution; 201-500 employees; Higher Education industry

August 2003 – December 2005 (2 years 5 months)

Taught an upper division legal writing class that took students through a simulated civil litigation case, integrating an interviewing, counseling, and negotiating component with a litigation component. Emphasis was placed on applying ethical principles to the pretrial litigation process.

Associate

Rutan & Tucker, LLP

Partnership; 201-500 employees; Law Practice industry

August 1997 – April 2001 (3 years 9 months)

Case experience included intellectual property, unfair competition, commercial landlord/tenant litigation, and contract/licensing disputes. Significant experience in complex civil litigation (including consumer class actions), Internet-related disputes, and litigation involving private institutions of higher education. Served as a Work Mentor for summer associates and provided detailed feedback on their written work product.

Research Assistant to Prof. Edward J. Imwinkelried

UC Davis School of Law

Educational Institution; 10,001+ employees; Higher Education industry

June 1995 – May 1997 (2 years)

Wrote research memoranda and updated prior research on various civil and criminal evidence issues, including character evidence and scientific evidence.
