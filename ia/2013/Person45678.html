Frank Gullo's Projects

  • Date on Purpose

    • April 2013 to October 2013

    In 2013, I worked with a small team to launch Date on Purpose, a platform that brings bachelor and bachelorette auctions online. Date on Purpose went live in September with online auctions and celebrated a launch event in October. With a platform and process in place, Date on Purpose plans to host additional auctions and events in the future.

  • Superior Group Brand Refresh

    • August 2011 to February 2013

    In 2011, Superior launched a major Brand Refresh project. The initiative was conceived to explore the organization’s brand identity, business lines, strategy, customers, and values, with the goal of obtaining greater clarity around customer needs, brand strengths, core attributes, and a path of continual improvement for the company.

    To help navigate the process, Superior partnered with a top external brand strategy agency, which led the research phase and conducted interviews with customers, employees, and other stakeholders. The assessment provided key insight and revealed Superior’s brand strengths and challenges, ultimately leading to a brand positioning recommendation.

    During the next phase of the project, Superior partnered with a creative services company to bring the refreshed Superior brand to life in the marketplace. Outputs of the brand launch included a refreshed logo and tagline, new website designs, print and digital assets, marketing materials, internal/external communication templates, and other collaterals.

  • Superior Group Career Site

    • March 2012 to February 2013

    In February 2013, Superior launched a new streamlined career website that was designed to function on any internet-ready device. The site is accessible at http://www.superiorjobs.com/ via web browsers and mobile devices and is promoted through major job board partnerships, traditional direct marketing campaigns, job fairs, and other outreach efforts.

    The project involved several internal Superior staff and three vendor partners, including an internet business and software development studio specializing in accessibility and mobile-friendly websites.

    Superior's goals in developing the new site were not only to build a usable, well-designed career site, but also to pilot new features, such as video job posts and job-related text messaging alerts.

  • Connections

    • December 2011 to March 2013

    Superior Group launched a major organizational initiative when it selected IBM Connections to anchor its social business platform. Connections was chosen because of how well the software modeled popular social networking sites like Facebook and LinkedIn and enabled business networking and sharing within the enterprise. Connections was also an appealing option because it was mobile-ready, with apps for company-supported mobile devices.

    Beginning as a pilot and later launched to all employees, Connections now provides Superior Group users with effective networking, collaboration, content authoring, document management, and business intelligence tools.

    As the Connections Project Manager for Superior Group, I collaborated extensively with technology implementation consultants and all company departments, especially Information Technology and Human Resources.

  • Moog Website Redesign

    • June 2008 to August 2009

    The Moog Website Redesign project was a massive initiative to reflect all aspects of Moog's growing, nearly $2 billion business in one global website, clearing demonstrating the depth and breadth of the company's offerings in precision motion control across multiple market and product categories. The site brought together what were previously at least six separate web properties under a unified moog.com umbrella.

    At launch, the main moog.com site contained approximately 1,500 pages of content, and over 1,000 PDF and graphic files. Simultaneously, four country sites were launched (China, Japan, Germany and Italy), each containing approximately 400 pages of content (in English and translation).

    Working as a Technical Lead on the core project team, I worked extensively with internal Moog staff as well as external resources, all of whom worked collaboratively toward a shared vision of Moog's online presence.

  • Voice of Customer

    • June 2010 to November 2010

    Following the release of its new global website, I worked with internal Moog staff to dimension the need for analytics beyond clickstream data, to measure user intent and satisfaction. Voice of customer analytics was identified as a desired solution and as a means to gain intelligence about site visitors and make some hypotheses to project future behaviors.

    Working as the Project Manager, I led the solution selection process through initial research, demos, RFPs, evaluation, and contract agreement.
