Lawrence O'Toole's Summary

Lawrence O'Toole is a creative director/design director with experience that spans many industries and disciplines. His specialties include brand strategy, typography, visual identity, interaction design and new product development.

Lawrence began his career Philadelphia, where he worked at firms such as Vitetta Group, 160over90, and Scünci International. In 2005, he co-founded the branding firm PRIMER. As partner, Lawrence oversaw the studio’s creative for clients such as the Wharton School of Business, SPEED Channel, Dickies Workwear, adidas Golf, and Mercedes Benz USA. In 2010, Lawrence sold his share of PRIMER and moved to New York to develop digital and visual experiences for the likes of Google, Interbrand, Fahrenheit 212, Rokkan, Euro RSCG, StrawberryFrog, and others.

In September of 2012, Lawrence joined Momentum Worldwide in the capacity of design director. He currently leads creative for clients such as Porsche (Petit Le Mans, the Pebble Beach Concours d'Elegance) and American Express (Tribeca Film Festival, Mercedes Benz Fashion Week, the US Open) as well as new business development.

Lawrence's work has been featured in numerous blogs and publications on design, including Design and Design, Under Consideration, Art of the Menu, Behance and Communication Arts Magazine. Lawrence was a member of the 2012 NYC Startup Bus, is currently a member of the TechCrunch Design Trust, and serves as a volunteer at the New York Art Directors' Club. He recently authored "Fading Ads of Philadelphia", a book about the history of ghost signs and advertisements in the city, and has developed a companion mobile application to the book.

Specialties: Branding, concept generation, visual design, design platforms & principles, brand strategy, interaction design

Lawrence O'Toole's Publications

  • Fading Ads of Philadelphia

    • The History Press
    • November 2012
    Authors: Lawrence O'Toole

    Philadelphia’s faded ads are history in plain sight. They are tangible remnants of changing neighborhoods and industries, and Fading Ads of Philadelphia presents a new way to view these forgotten urban stories. Join author and photographer Lawrence O’Toole as he explores these physical touchstones of the city’s history—a sign for a bygone family business seen only from the elevated train tracks, the Gretz smokestack advertising the now defunct Kensington brewery and an ad for the Midtown Theater that is slowly reappearing from behind layers of whitewash. O’Toole re-creates this lost urban landscape as he hunts signs from Center City to the River Wards and from South Philadelphia to West Philadelphia. Through this stunningly illustrated book, urbanites will again view these too often overlooked ads—and their stories—with fresh eyes.
