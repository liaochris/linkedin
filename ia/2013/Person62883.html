Jesse Berst's Summary

Thought leader in smart cities and smart grids. I provide strategic guidance to corporations and startups in those sectors. I serve on the advisory boards of national laboratories, venture capital firms and for-profit companies.

Jesse Berst's Experience

Chairman

Smart Cities Council

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Information Technology and Services industry

April 2012 – Present (1 year 9 months)

The Smart Cities Council is an industry coalition led by some of the world's top technology companies, including AT&T, Cisco, Electricite de France, General Electric, IBM, Itron, Microsoft and others. Its goal is to accelerate the move to smart, sustainable cities. Its motto is "Livability, Workability, Sustainability."

Managing Director

Global Smart Energy

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Renewables & Environment industry

2003 – Present (10 years)

Jesse Berst is the Managing Director of Global Smart Energy (GSE), an internationally recognized research and consulting firm. GSE publishes SmartGridNews.com and administers the Smart Cities Council, the leading industry coalition in that emerging sector.

In recent years, Berst has mapped an acquisitions plan for a large corporation; advised a smart energy startup that was successfully acquired; conceived a communications strategy for the Modern Grid Initiative; crafted a clean-energy economic development plan for a regional coalition of states; developed a market entry strategy for a Fortune 100 systems integrator; and chaired the Advisory Council of Pacific Northwest National Laboratory's Energy and Environment Directorate.

Founder and Chief Analyst

Smart Grid News

Public Company; 1-10 employees; Renewables & Environment industry

2003 – Present (10 years)

As Chief Analyst at Smart Grid News, Jesse receives regular executive briefings from the sector's leading firms. He analyzes trends, technologies and announcements and reports on their long-term impact on the electricity infrastructure. Smart Grid News is the Internet's oldest, largest and highest-ranked smart grid site.

Board of Directors

Renomics Corporation

February 2008 – July 2010 (2 years 6 months)

Renomics Corporation produces software and simulation tools that permit business to successfully achieve a "triple bottom line."

Executive Editor, AnchorDesk

ZDNet

Public Company; 501-1000 employees; ZDZ; Online Media industry

1995 – 2002 (7 years)

Jesse Berst conceived ZDNet AnchorDesk, which reigned for many years as the Internet's leading email newsletter, with nearly 3,000,000 daily subscribers. AnchorDesk pioneered many of the techniques now incorporated into popular blogs, including daily publication and "TalkBack" technology for comments.

In the '80s and '90s, Jesse Berst was one of the country's most well-known technology analysts. He was a featured columnist at leading magazines, including Computerworld, Computer Reseller News, InformationWeek and PC Week, authoring more than 1700 columns and articles analyzing technology and business trends. He authored or co-authored ten books on personal computing and software, and one on high-tech business concepts, titled The Magnet Effect (McGraw-Hill, 2001). His analysis and commentary were featured widely on national radio and television, including CNNfn, Good Morning America, ABC Nightly News, CBS Radio News, Business Talk Radio and NorthWest CableNews.

Founder

New Riders Publishing/Micropublishing/CompuTh!nk

1980 – 1999 (19 years)

During this period, Jesse founded or co-founded three high-tech publishing companies, New Riders Publishing (acquired by Macmillan), Micropublishing (acquired by Cygnus Business Media), and CompuTh!nk (acquired by Ziff-Davis). He then became a Vice President and "intrapraneur" at Ziff-Davis, where he conceived and founded "AnchorDesk," which became the company's most popular and profitable Internet property, reigning for many years as the world’s largest email newsletter with nearly 3,000,000 daily subscribers.
