Aravind Krishnaswamy's Projects

  • Eazel

    • June 2010 to April 2011
    Team Members: Aravind Krishnaswamy, Stephen DiVerdi

    Eazel is a simple painting application for the iPad which produces strokes that have watercolor-like appearance using a procedural simulation algorithm. Eazel also sports a novel user interface that makes efficient use of screen real estate and the touch capabilities of the tablet.

  • Adobe Ray Tracer

    • April 2007 to May 2010
    Team Members: Aravind Krishnaswamy, Nathan Carr, Radomir Mech, Gavin Miller

    The Adobe Ray Tracer (or ART) is a realtime and final quality ray tracer architected by Nathan Carr and by myself. It uses Monte Carlo ray tracing to simulate light transport for global illumination effects. It was first shipped in Photoshop CS4 Extended and has been improved and extended in every subsequent Photoshop release.

  • Bristle Brush

    • September 2008 to May 2010
    Team Members: Aravind Krishnaswamy, Stephen DiVerdi, Sunil Hadap

    Bristle Brush was a project which simulated the interaction of a brush with a canvas and the resulting paint flow. With the system we are capable of generating arbitrary brush tip shapes in raster or vector output. The technology was shipped in Photoshop CS5.

Aravind Krishnaswamy's Publications

  • Light and Skin Interactions: Simulations for Computer Graphics Applications

    • Morgan Kaufmann
    • May 2010
    Authors: Aravind Krishnaswamy, Gladimir Baranoski

    Light and Skin Interactions immerses you in one of the most fascinating application areas of computer graphics: appearance simulation. The book first illuminates the fundamental biophysical processes that affect skin appearance, and reviews seminal related works aimed at applications in life and health sciences. It then examines four exemplary modeling approaches as well as definitive algorithms that can be used to generate realistic images depicting skin appearance.
