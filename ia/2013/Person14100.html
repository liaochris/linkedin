Bieneosa Ebite's Summary

I have been working in PR for over 12 years and have had the opportunity to work on a diverse range of campaigns and projects across a number of sectors.

One of my most challenging and rewarding experiences to date was the time I spent working as a Regional Communication Manager for McDonald's Restaurants. McDonald's was, at the time, one of the most challenging environments for a communications professionaI. The brand was under considerable attack from the media and sales were down.

Each day was very different but the need for people skills, tenacity, empathy and the ability to handle issues with a cool head was constant. I remain proud to have been part of an award-winning communications team that contributed to the revitalisation of the brand by developing and sharing McDonald’s story of change.

After helping to play a part in McDonald’s story of change, I left the company to pursue a personal goal to start my own public relations consultancy.

Bright Star Public Relations came to life in 2007 and embodies my passion for helping organisations to tell their story, raise their profile and connect with the people that matter the most to them.

Diversity is an ethos at the heart of Bright Star; appreciating that one-size does not fit all. This has enabled me to work on campaigns for a range of organisations including the Home Office,Thomas Eggar LLP, Oxfam, Costa Coffee,Texas Chicken and the Office for National Statistics (2011 Census).

Bright Star is also one of the few PR consultancys in the UK that offers strategic guidance to help organisations understand and connect with the UK's Black and Minority Ethnic communities.

Specialties

- PR strategy and campaign planning
- Profile building and PR for entreprenuers
- Media training
- Employee engagement
- Community engagement
- Multicultural communication (strategy and training)
- Leveraging cultural intelligence for organisational success
