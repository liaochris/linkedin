Larry W. Jones' Summary

My passion is teaching Biblical Stewardship as well as living out these principles in my own life. Every day, I ask myself this question, "how can I be a better manager of what God has given me this day?"

My stewardship story began in 2001 when I attended my first Crown Financial Study and really began to grasp the Biblical principles surrounding money and the Christian life. In 2005, I read Dave Ramsey's original version of his Financial Peace book. Dave's financial teaching has revolutionized my money view forever. I became debt free in July of 2007 after paying off $25,000 in 19 months. I continue to work through Dave's seven financial baby steps.

Now, you may be asking yourself, "what is stewardship?" Christian stewardship is simply the management of God's wealth. As a believer in Jesus, I recognize that God owns all things. He has not only placed in my trust financial resources, but also time, energy, talents, relationships, physical health, mental ability, and emotional capacity.

This personal passion has manifested itself through giving generously beyond the tithe, creating two stewardship focused blogs, writing a self-published ebook, teaching Crown Financial studies, coordinating Financial Peace University classes, and striving to live my life to the max in all areas: physically, spiritually, emotionally, and mentally.

I have a vision of seeing God's people intentionally focused on using everything God has entrusted into their care for the ultimate purpose of building up His Kingdom.

Specialties: leadership, administration, teaching, writing, blogging, volunteer ministry management, donor development, and church worship ministry.

Associate Pastor - Stewardship

Nonprofit; 11-50 employees; Religious Institutions industry

- launched and maintained Financial Peace University in our church. Since 2008, we have enrolled over 600 people in FPU. I have also mobilized the help of three additional volunteer coordinators at various times in the last 5 years.

This campaign included 4 weekly sermons, 4 video testimonies from church members, and a commitment card response at the end of the campaign. One positive outcome of this campaign was a smaller budget shortfall than the previous year during a very difficult time in our church's history.
