Lewis Perdue's Summary

I have founded four companies including a wine importer/wholesaler, a magazine and two technology firms.

Wine Business Publications, which I founded in 1991, remain the largest circulation trade publications for the North American wine industry.

Pocketpass, an internet micropayments and content sales system which I founded in 1999 went public in 2005. Unfortunately the management team who replaced me fumbled the opportunity and drove the stock price into the ground. Information about Pocketpass can be found at: http://www.pocketpass.net and http://www.tibanna.net

I have been fortunate to serve as a top aide to a U.S. Senator and a state governor, run political races for Congress, work as a Washington correspondent (Ottaway/Dow-Jones, States News Service), a columnist for Gannett, The Wall Street Journal Online, CBS Marketwatch and TheStreet.Com.

I've taught writing and journalism at Cornell University and UCLA, and am the author of 20 published books including several bestsellers. My books which have sold more than 4 million copies.

My most recent novel is Perfect Killer. Other novels include The Da Vinci Legacy and Daughter of God. A complete list of my books can be found at LewisPerdue.com

Currently, I write novels and nonfiction books and book reviews for Barron's. I am involved in two Web 2.0 startups.

Specialties

Software and system design, marketing, and writing, wine, wine business, entrepreneurship.
