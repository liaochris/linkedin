Amy Martin's Summary

I am an Independent Producer based in Birmingham who works across the creative and cultural sector. I am interested in the creative potential of young people and my work focuses on Youth Empowerment, Audience Development, Participation, Creative Learning, Professional Development and Cultural Entitlement. I work with cultural organisations, young people and emerging artists to produce dynamic creative projects, festivals and events that include Arts, Culture and Digital Media.

I hold the position of Community Governor for a Looked after Children in Education Services (LACES) Pupil Referral Unit (PRU) in Birmingham, an RSA Fellow and I am on the board of trustees for Graeae Theatre Company. Graeae is a force for change in world-class theatre, breaking down barriers, challenging preconceptions and boldly placing disabled artists centre stage.

Specialties: Project Management, Consultation and Facilitation for the creative and cultural sector.

Amy Martin's Experience

Consultant

Arts Connect WM

February 2013 – Present (6 months) Midlands

As part of Arts Council England's mission, 'achieving great art for everyone', goal 5 states that every child and young person has the opportunity to experience the richness of the arts and culture.

To help ACE achieve this, they set up a network of 10 'bridge' organisations (as part of the National portfolio) that will use their experience and expertise to connect children and young people, schools and communities with art and culture.

The bridging organisation for the West Midlands is Arts Connect WM. My role is to look at how we can better engage young people as participants and audience for arts and culture outside of school post 16. This work involves a partnership project with mac, birmingham that investigates current provision and where improvements can be made.

Researcher/Producer

Ideas Vendor

January 2013 – Present (7 months) Birmingham / Nationwide

Amy Martin a young people’s creative producer based in Birmingham has received funding from Arts Council England for an R&D project called Ideas Vendor. ​​
​
Ideas Vendor will investigate and research into how cultural organisations can improve their use of digital technology and the Internet in engaging young people with arts and culture. Encouraging the development of future audiences, future artists and utilising the skills, expertise and talents of a digital generation.
​
The project will encompasses a creative project called Birmingham 2022, which will devise a re-imagined vision for the future of arts and culture in Birmingham for the year 2022 created, written and produced by young people.

BearHeART﻿ is the dynamic creative agency for young people by Spoken Word Artist Polarbear and Creative Producer Amy Martin.

BearHeART supports creative development through leading story based projects across art forms that promote genuine collaboration and provide a platform for ongoing artistic development.

We aim to:
﻿
Inspire young creatives and provide a solid and quality context for multi-faceted artistic expression

To instil an emphasis on artistic process, showcasing quality work in whatever form it takes, to inspire individuals to find their creative voice

Encourage and develop an awareness of professional practice and income generation in young creatives, enabling them to understand and manage their place within the creative sector

Build and develop collaborations between artists and educational staff to break down the artist/teacher separation

Lead projects that compliment and explore the evolving national curriculum and teaching methods to improve the relevance, impact and involvement of students to their subjects

Maintain a focus on projects that have a sustained life span and potential to evolve with participant needs and motivations

Approach and appraise ideas on their own merit regardless of status or experience

Amy Martin's Projects

  • Project Platform

    • August 2010 to July 2011
    Team Members: Amy Martin, Alex McCorkindale, Marcia Springer

    A social media project for 15-19 year olds that represents a unique perspective on the arts in Birmingham

    Bright Space recruited 17 young people aged between 15-19yrs from Birmingham and the West Midlands to be the authors and editors of the Platform blog.

    Over ten months the group received training, support and guidance from industry experts and creative professionals in multimedia journalism. The Platform journalists shaped the voice, content and direction of the Platform blog by attending, discussing, reviewing and examining the creative opportunities available to them and other young people in their city.

    Platform amplified young people as unique, dynamic, cultural commentators who successfully communicated, inspired and engaged other young people. Platform became a vehicle that cultural organisations, venues and promoters connected with to ensure their programming reflects the breadth and individuality of young people in Birmingham and the West Midlands.

    Project Platform was selected for a national campaign resource ‘Youth Arts Transforms Lives – Fact’. This accolade demonstrates further recognition for Platform amongst youth arts professionals and the youth sector as a whole.

    Supported by the voices and views of young people and endorsed by leading national and international arts, creative and cultural figures, ‘Youth Arts Transforms Lives’ - illustrates the real impact of participation in the arts and culture with, for and by young people.

    Project Platform ran from August 2010 – July 2011 and was funded by CCE (Creativity, Culture & Education) delivered by Bright Space.

  • Future Poets' Festival

    • April 2012 to September 2012
    Team Members: Amy Martin, Marcia Springer, Kimberly Trusty

    On 4th August 2012 Writing West Midlands in partnership with mac and IdeasTap mounted the 1st ever Future Poets’ Festival. Funded by Clore Duffield Foundation, a group of young people aged 16-19ys from the West Midlands worked together over 5 months to create, design and produce the Future Poets’ Festival from scratch. Lead artist and published poet; Kim Trusty along with Independent Producer Amy Martin supported the process along with professional poets and industry experts. The group explored poetry in its broadest sense and the free festival including a programme of workshops, exhibitions, installations, screenings, games, an open mic and performances from some of the best performance poets in the UK. Including; PolarBear, John Berkavitch, Jodi Ann Bickley, Al Hutchins, Musa Okwonga, Bohdan Piasecki and Kim Trusty. We received some fantastic feedback at the event, for a summary please check the Storify -

    http://storify.com/amyrozelmartin/future-poets-festival

    Future Poets’ Festival was on Saturday 4th August 2012 at mac and was completely FREE.

She is an effective project manager with a real gift for working with young people, often those who are least inclined to engage with cultural activities. She is also adept at working with partner organisations, helping to secure additional support and to create the ‘critical mass’ of support and enthusiasm necessary for a successful project. Despite working to tight deadlines and with limited budgets, she keeps a cool head and produces events and activities with real flair.”
