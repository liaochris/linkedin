Brian W. Gibson's Summary

How does a mechanical engineer end up as a software developer? By not being limited by artificial constraints, such as labels, and simply focusing on the problem at hand. Having worked in small to medium organizations throughout my career, problems don't get solved by limiting yourself to what you know or what your job description outlines. The fact of the matter is that when a problem presents itself, the answer may not be readily available. That's where I come in.

Many a time, I've been tasked with finding a solution that involved learning something completely new. It's my ability to quickly do this that has made me a dependable asset. And I love learning new things. So it's no surprise that I've gone from a mechanical engineer to a database application developer to a web developer to currently a mobile software developer for iOS and Android.

Let me know if you have a problem that needs solving.
