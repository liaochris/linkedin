Danny Miller's Publications

  • Baselining Automated Controls (White Paper - 2008)

    • Grant Thornton
    • July 7, 2008
    Authors: Danny Miller

    Automated controls exist in most IT applications, interfaces
    and appliances. All Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP)
    systems contain them in one form or another. Automated
    controls are generally highly configurable to the client’s
    environment to meet its control and automation needs.
    Some key questions to ask with automated controls
    include: (1) where do they exist? (2) how do they match up
    with my business process so that I can use them? (3) are they
    reasonable or worthwhile to turn on and use? and (4) are they
    hard to configure and maintain?

  • Information Overload: Using Data Analytics in Internal Audit Functions(White Paper – 2010)

    • Grant Thornton, LLP (white paper)
    • September 10, 2010
    Authors: Danny Miller, Scott Higgins CISA, CRISC, CRMA

    It is difficult to overstate the impact that data analytics technologies — also known as
    business intelligence — have had on customer relationship management, finance and other parts of today’s organizations.

    Now it is the internal audit department’s turn.

    The challenge is to find ways to incorporate data analytics into the internal audit department’s
    key activities. A structured approach to data analytics is essential.

    A prerequisite for using data analytics effectively is a well-defined set of audit goals and objectives covering topics such as the following:

    - Detecting and monitoring key risk indicator (KRI) thresholds and events
    - Adherence to company policies and procedures
    - Material cost fluctuations
    - Performance thresholds such as customer complaint levels

  • Supply Chain Solutions: The supply chain today: Achieving efficiencies, managing uncertainties (White Paper – 2010)

    • Grant Thornton, LLP
    • May 3, 2011
    Authors: Danny Miller, Wally Gruenes, Ralph Nefdt

    After a few years of limited information technology investment, many manufacturing companies are beginning to invest anew in IT solutions to improve supply chain efficiency. Companies are turning to IT solutions such as customer relationship management (CRM) and sales force automation; mobility applications; and warehouse management systems. They are also beginning to embrace cloud-based computing — a good choice given relatively lean technology departments, but one that calls for disciplined attention to potential privacy and security risks.

    How companies are using IT to improve supply chain efficiency is the first of our three Supply Chain Solutions surveys for 2011, conducted in partnership with World Trade magazine. The report highlights some noteworthy trends in supply chain executives' decisions over the past year, as well as their plans for the future. Key findings in this survey include:

    Survey respondents are evenly split among three priorities for IT in 2011: new mobility applications (34%); sales force automation (SFA) or other CRM solutions (31%); and replacing departmental and support systems, such as logistics or warehouse management (30%).
    Just over a fifth (22%) of survey respondents have already deployed SaaS applications, and 30% are considering SaaS for the future.
    In the past 12 months, companies implemented a number of supply chain applications. Most popular were demand forecasting and planning (27%); warehouse management systems (24%); advanced inventory optimization (19%); and transportation management systems (19%).
