Keita Wangari's Summary

My strongest interests are in researching the effects of motivation, trust, context and culture on human interaction with technology, and using the discoveries to positively influence the development of search, learning and mapping technologies.

As a co-founder and advisory team member of Esri's grassroots User Experience Group, I lead efforts to instill user-centered concepts and practices at all levels and in all areas of the company. I believe collaborative, insistent and persistent advocacy for users exposes the gaps in product and service offerings where key opportunities for innovation may be hiding.

Keita Wangari's Projects

  • Math Recognition and Retrieval Project (NSF-funded)

    • September 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Keita Wangari, Awelemdy Orakwue, David Stalnaker

    Working on the HCI aspects of math recognition and retrieval. More specifically: identifying scenarios and tasks where a mathematical non-expert (e.g.middle-school or college student) would benefit from being able to search documents or the web using math expressions, along with text. As an initial example, a student might try to locate an expression they are using in their homework within a .pdf of a textbook by drawing/typing/taking a picture of a math expression.

    My portion of the project requires a literature review, description of use cases, and an observational study designed to elicit from participants whether and how they would use math search interfaces, perhaps using the 'min' (*m*ath *in*put) prototype as one point of discussion (Link to 'min' prototype: http://saskatoon.cs.rit.edu/min).

    My deliverables will be a 15-20 page report summarizing the literature review and description of use cases, as well the protocol and results from the human study and recommendations for the design of math search interfaces.
