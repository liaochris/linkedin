Janna Chan's Summary

As the Manager of Online Strategy and Response at the Center for Reproductive Rights, an international legal advocacy organization working to advance reproductive freedom as a fundamental human right, I directed online communications and integrated marketing strategies. My responsibilities included content development, data analysis, direct mail and telemarketing communications, and implementation of all online communications and web properties (outreach via website, blogs, email campaigns, social networking, videos, and advertising).

Prior to CRR, I was a client manager at M+R Strategic Services. I led client teams to create and implement effective online campaigns for progressive, public-interest nonprofit organizations to recruit online activists, influence public policy, raise money online, and deepen relationships with online supporters. My clients included AARP, The Smithsonian and The Wilderness Society.

I honed my interest in digital strategy during my time as the Online Content Specialist for Lambda Legal. I helped develop the organization's online program and managed the website, email, and social networks. Lambda Legal is a national legal advocacy organization fighting for full recognition of civil rights for lesbians, gay men, bisexuals and transgender people and those living with HIV.

Trained as a journalist, I have a decade of experience freelancing for publications such as the Village Voice, Seattle Magazine, Seattle Weekly, West Coast Performer magazine, and AsianAvenue.com.

Specialties: Creative Integrated Online Marketing for Non-Profits; Multi-Channel Strategy Planning (Direct Response, Telemarketing, Email Advocacy & Fundraising, Social Media); Campaign Strategy, Conception and Execution; CMS/Content Management Systems (Convio, Drupal); eCRM/Customer Relationship Management Systems (Salsa, Convio, Blue State Digital); Adobe Suite (Photoshop, Acrobat, Dreamweaver); Microsoft Suite; Basic HTML; Associated Press Stylebook; Fluent in Cantonese
