Betsy Trapasso's Summary

As an end of life guide, I assist the dying in making a peaceful transition from this world. I guide the dying person, their family, friends and caregivers through the dying process. Through education and support, I allow for more open connections with loved ones. I help with personal, spiritual and practical issues.

My work as a guide can be just one session or we can work together over a period of days, weeks or months. I offer my end of life guide services in person, over the phone and on Skype.

End of life issues and care are my passion so I am working to change how we view and do death in the United States. I educate, consult and speak to people about end of life issues and the need to improve the quality of care that dying people receive in the US. It is paramount that the families, friends and caregivers of the dying receive support.

I share the wisdom of the dying so we may be inspired to live our best lives. These precious insights I have gained can be shared through speaking engagements.

I believe that people may be less afraid of dying if they see how other cultures view death and dying. Because of this, I am producing a documentary about the death rituals and ceremonies of other cultures and countries.

As a result of my years of working with the dying and their families, I bring you my rich experience and deep compassion at one of the most tender times of your life.

I am the hostess of Death Cafe Los Angeles.


My other love is to teach the 2500 year old art of Traditional Thai Massage. I studied Thai Massage with some of the most respected teachers in Thailand, the US and Canada. I have been an instructor of Thai massage since 2004 and a practitioner since 2003.

Hospice Social Worker/Community Liasion

Vitas Hospice; Assisted Home Hospice; Olsten Kimberly Qualitycare Hospice; Los Angeles, CA

January 1994 – June 2004 (10 years 6 months)

I worked at these hospices for some of the years between 1994 and 2004. I was a hospice social worker and community liasion and I helped hundreds of patients and their families through the death and dying process.

As a social worker, I educated and admitted patients to hospice; counseled patients about their issues and fears; took care of the patients' needs; dealt with all the practical matters for each patient and family; advocated for the patients; counseled families, friends and caregivers; and trained and educated hospice volunteers.

As a community liasion, I educated doctors, nurses, medical personnel and staff of hospitals and medical centers about hospice care; represented the various hospices at health fairs, community events, nursing homes and schools; and worked to increase the number of referrals to the hospices from hospitals and physicians in the community.
