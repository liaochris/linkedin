André Lambelet's Publications

  • Social Media Strategy of Multinational Companies

    • September 1, 2011
    Authors: André Lambelet

    This study presents a qualitative analysis of 25 interviews with Heads of Communications, Digital Directors, Social Media Managers and other people from 21 different multinational companies present in Switzerland. A new framework defining the key elements for the social media success, which is leading to the company success, as well as the internal social media usage, was developed.

  • From Green IT to IT for Green

    • June 25, 2010
    Authors: André Lambelet

    The goal of this research paper is to present and explain a new way of using the technology to reduce and optimize the energy consumption of different technologies.

    First, you will find some aspects about the Green IT in general and the three different types of Green IT, Green IT 1.0, 1.5 and 2.0. Many researchers and individuals are more talking about Green IT (Green IT 1.0 and 1.5), which takes in consideration the reduction of the 2% of energy consumption and pollution caused by Information Technologies. Why don’t we think about using the Information Technologies to become greener? This aspect of IT for Green or in other words Green IT 2.0 is also discussed in this paper, and highlights how things can be done differently to reduce the impact of the remaining 98% greenhouse gas which are produced by all other industries than IT. Green IT 2.0 talks more about the optimization of domains and its use of energy. Further we will explain the actual situation and usage of the different types of Green IT and how companies in Switzerland and other countries already use those tools to reduce and optimize the energy consumption and act against the environmental issues.

    Finally, there are different opportunities and trends that could be interesting for Swiss and worldwide companies. We will see how we can all make an effort in order to make our world a little bit greener.

  • Lada in Switzerland - Market potential and outline of an entry strategy and requirements

    • February 2010
    Authors: André Lambelet

    The purpose of this study is to estimate the market potential and an entry strategy and requirements for the Lada Company in Switzerland.

    Lada is the European name of the Russian company AvtoVAZ. Created in 1967 with the help of Fiat, AvtoVAZ made a joint venture with GM in the nineties and found investment from Renault in 1999. After a fall from 50% of their production in 2008, they are planning on a +300% rise between 2009 and 2015.

    The Swiss market is still affected by the crisis. The low-cost segment is not overcrowded with only 13% of the new available vehicles under 20’000 CHF. Lada has some strength which will help them to fill in their opportunities and lower their weaknesses.

    Two different entry strategies are conceivable. The first one is to sell their cars under their own brand while making a special effort on the communication and offering a very good guarantee even with a price in the low-cost segment. The second alternative is to sell their cars under the Chevrolet brand and use their distribution centers already running. This will decrease their distribution costs and the prospect will not be affected by the Lada corporate image.

  • The world car industry crisis - Estimation of its impact on the Swiss economy

    • December 2009
    Authors: André Lambelet

    The purpose of this study is to estimate the impact of the world car industry crisis on the Swiss economy.

    Until the years 2000, the Triad (USA, Western Europe and Japan) was almost alone in the world car market. Then China grew up to the 3rd place in the new car producers ranking. With a growing global market, the BRIC countries took a large part of the Triad production since 2000 and are now in the 2nd place after Europe and before Japan and the USA.

    The most important part of the Swiss car industry is the spare parts. After a 40% fall in 2008 the upturn began in the first quarter of 2009 to reach the same level as in 2004. All the sectors of the industry are hit except the tanks and armored vehicles.

    The difficulty for Switzerland is to keep its market share. The orientation has to be made towards the green cars and clean technologies to remain competitive.
