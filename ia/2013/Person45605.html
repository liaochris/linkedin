Frances Fahey's Summary

Through my business, Fran's Fine Editing, I put my 33+ years of business writing experience to work for you. I provide copywriting, editing, proofreading and resume services for businesses and individuals. I'll meet your short deadlines. I offer competitive rates and I provide my services with integrity and confidentiality. You'll also find me personable and fun to work with. "I'll keep you composed!"

I'm a Certified Professional Resume Writer from the Professional Association of Resume Writers and Career Coaches; a board member of my local Chamber of Commerce; and a member of the Editorial Freelancers Association, the Women Business Owners Alliance of Pioneer Valley, and the Women's Business Network of SE Mass.

Specialties

Research, write and edit copy for websites, marketing materials, reports, manuals, and all other types of business documents.
Design and prepare resumes and cover letters.
Copyedit books, screenplays, e-books, journals, theses, dissertations, manuals, etc.

Human Resource Management Career

Commonwealth of Massachusetts

Government Agency; 10,001+ employees; Government Administration industry

July 1980 – September 2006 (26 years 3 months) Boston, MA

Held professional and management HR positions in the Massachusetts Human Resources Division, researching and writing new and revised policies, manuals, training programs, contracts, proposals, and website materials. Managed a wide variety of programs and personnel for an HR organization that provided services for 60,000 state employees. Twenty years of experience operating personal computers using all of the major Microsoft products. Received the state's highest performance award, the Manuel Carballo Governor's Award for Excellence in Public Service, 1992.

Co-Founder, Board Member and Secretary

Committee of Ten Thousand (COTT)

June 1990 – June 1995 (5 years 1 month)

Co-founded a volunteer, peer-led non-profit organization to provide advocacy and education to people with Hemophilia who were also infected with HIV (approximately 10,000 people during these years in the US). Served as Board Member and Secretary; managed an international mailing list of 6,000 people; wrote articles for newsletter; handled all correspondence and meeting minutes; made arrangements for conferences and other events. COTT was successful in winning settlements from 4 pharmaceutical companies and the FDA for all those infected and their survivors.
