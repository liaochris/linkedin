Angel McCormack, MS's Summary

As an expert in the field of delivering both in-person and on-line career counseling, I pioneered the first ever distance counseling strategy/process for the UC Davis Graduate School of Management in 2006. My primary goal is to impart life-changing career self efficacy and inspiration to my clients by providing empathic coaching, personalized guidance and finely tuned career search resources. Helping people create manageable action plans, achieve greater understanding of the job market and how to best present their accomplishments has resulted in 100’s of satisfying careers for my clients.

I hold a Master of Science degree in Career Counseling, certified in the Myers Briggs Type Indicator and have authored a Career Counseling Theory as well as articles regarding how to deliver the most effective distance counseling. My full theoretical approach is a Humanistic mix of Existential, Person Centered, Gestalt, and Behaviorism schools of thought viewable at angelmc.org.

Cornerstone to my success is the distance style of counseling I have pioneered for the busy schedules of the WPs: a highly effective system of counseling that takes each client through every step of the career development process including self assessments, marketing materials, networking strategies, job search, interviewing and negotiations. Keeping meticulous case notes is key to effective counseling of large case loads over time, as I am working with approximately 150 students at any given time, in all stages of their career development processes.
