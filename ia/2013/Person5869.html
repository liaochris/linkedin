Jim Walls' Summary

Jim has over 25 years of medical device R&D and product development experience; 15 years of which have been devoted to 'combination medical products' which include antimicrobial/bioactive technologies in an attempt to prevent or reduce Hospital Acquired Infections [HAIs]. Commercial new product contributions include:

* Kerlix AMD, K990530 - an antimicrobial wound dressing
* Dover Silver, K020410 - an antimicrobial Foley catheter
* Agento IC, K0771365 - an antimicrobial endotracheal tube
* Elutia, K063245 - an antimicrobial surgical wound drain

Jim formed AMxTek in 2008 in an effort to continue this activity and assist corporate clients achieve similar strategic objectives

Specialties

Antimicrobial medical device product development; antimicrobial technology due diligence & sourcing; in-vitro & In-vivo antimicrobial test methods & protocol development; and FDA/regulatory pathway strategy & claim development

Validated second generation infection control technology for urological and respiratory applications and managed industry first, antimicrobial endotracheal tube clinical trial [NASCENT: 54 hospitals; 2,000+ patents] to reduce the incidence of ventilator associated pneumonia [VAP]; Agento™I.C., K071365; NASCENT study yielded 36% reduction of VAP [p = .05] and delayed VAP onset [p = .05].
