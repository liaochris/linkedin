Alex Proaps' Summary

I am a Human Factors Psychology Doctoral candidate (ABD) working with Dr. James Bliss in the REACTS (Research Environment for Alarms and Complex Task Simulation) lab at Old Dominion University. My main research interest has been individual and distributed team training in virtual environments and serious games, specifically the assessment of distributed leadership and collaborative communication behaviors. I am also involved in a number of research projects investigating Human-Computer and Robot Interaction issues: avatar and robot trust and etiquette, rapid serial visual presentation, computer versus mobile task performance, and gesture-based interfaces. In addition to research and design work, I teach Introduction to Psychology and Perception courses in the Psychology department at Old Dominion University.

Specialties: Adobe, Adobe Photoshop, HCI, human factors, human-systems integration, Microsoft Powerpoint, Microsoft Windows, Microsoft Word, virtual environments, SPSS, simulation, task analysis, training, usability
