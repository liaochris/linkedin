Howard Franklin's Summary

Howard Franklin is one of the American South's most sought-after strategists. Splitting his time between state capitols and the campaign trail, he's helped advance the policies and elect some of the region's leading lawmakers, including:

Mayor Shirley Franklin, the first African-American woman to be elected mayor of a major Southern city, Minority Leader Stacey Abrams, the first woman to lead either party in the Georgia General Assembly, and Percy Bland, the first African-American mayor of Meridian, MS.

At 23, Maynard Jackson tapped him to lead a youth voting nonprofit. He turned that experience into a trio of political companies under the moniker iRUNcampaigns.com, helping government and elected officials communicate on policy decisions, engage key stakeholders, win and hold elected office.

One of those companies, Firehouse Phones, has led 300 telephone campaigns in 14 states since 2008. Another, Georgia10x10.com, delivers the top 10 political stories to more than 5000 lawmakers and lobbyists by 10 a.m. daily. Influence Factory shapes public opinion on major policy initiatives.

Howard has taught political organizing for Wellstone Action, helping train more than 55,000 activists around the country since 2004. He earned degrees in English and sociology from Morehouse College in 2001 and was elected the first male president of the Georgia League of Women Voters in 2010.
