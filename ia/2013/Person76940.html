Kristen Brown's Summary

My gig = helping clients buzz up their work and life through my company, Happy Hour Effect LLC. I am a speaker, brand strategist, bestselling author, entrepreneur and publisher. I'm also a widow mom which is priority and job #1. I've been featured in the media over 100 times including appearances on "Live with Kelly & Michael," HLN, Pschology Today, the covers of March and Savvy magazines and I'm an ongoing Huffington Post blogger.

I've spoken for and worked with diverse clients including: Mayo Health, Cargill, Disney, Target, MN West Colleges, American Heart Association, Sony, Fox, Walmart, Sam's Club, LifeSource and many more.

Plus I'm a huge social media geek! I see the power of connecting in the virtual world and try to balance digital interaction with personal, one-on-one communication.

I'm a huge movie-buff and love the art of film. I even have a documentary and a couple of screenplays in the works. I also love food, travel, writing and helping others see things in a new way.

Most importantly, I am a mom and do all I can to be a role model for my daughter by living a life of harmony, low-stress, fun and health despite being widowed at a young age.

As the founder of Happy Hour Effect LLC, I help organizations and individuals buzz up their work and life through business and author coaching, speaking and writing. General Manager, Minneapolis Office

Collective Bias

Collective Bias® is a social shopper media company. Through Social Fabric®, a proprietary community consisting of approximately 1,300 shopping-focused influencers with an aggregate multichannel reach of more than 40 million, we weave organic social content into engaging, real-life stories to create millions of impressions leading to increased share of voice, SEO, and ultimately sales for brands and retailers. Collective Bias was named one of America's Most Promising Companies in 2013 by Forbes.

VP - Healthy Living Division

Marketing Architects

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry

November 2010 – August 2011 (10 months)

Marketing Architects is the largest direct response radio advertising agency in the U.S. As the VP of the Healthy Living division, I helped our clients create winning campaigns that captured the essence of their brand while implementing winning DR creativity - all to improve the lifetime value of every customer and improve the bottom line.

Retail Insights Director

The NPD Group

Privately Held; 1001-5000 employees; Market Research industry

March 2005 – September 2009 (4 years 7 months) Port Washington, NY

While at the NPD Group, I was promoted three times from the Target Account Manager to Target Team Leader to the overall company Retail Insights Director. In this role I led trend and market research initiatives for the creation of new reporting and processes that helped retail and manufacturer clients make more informed decisions about product direction, merchandising, marketing, brand and promotional strategies. I was responsible for leading NPD’s retail-centricity efforts across the company and translating data into actionable insights for apparel, sporting goods, beauty, footwear, electronics, toys and housewares vendors and retail teams.

Product Marketing Manager

BOTO Limited

September 2002 – December 2004 (2 years 4 months) Hong Kong

As the Product Marketing Manager for BOTO Limited and Boli Furniture, I was one of five people who built the U.S. office to serve retail clients in the U.S. I worked with our Hong Kong and China-based teams to create marketing, product and sales insights to help drive sales and secure partnerships with mass, department store, home improvement, drug, grocery and specialty retailers. I was responsible for all store and trend shops as well as ongoing reporting and presentations on product direction, design elements and licensing opportunities.

National Accounts Manager

Garven inc.

1999 – 2002 (3 years)

At Garven Inc., I received three promotions in three years allowing me to integrate with many areas of the company including Creative, Sourcing, Accounting, Warehouse and the Executive team. This cross-training gave me a solid base of knowledge in all areas of retail merchandising including factory negotiations, packaging, tradeshow planning, pricing, trend development, creative execution, marketing, budgets and sales. I managed the trend and product development function through market trips, reporting, presentations and tradeshow work that helped the company develop marketable and modern products.

Manager/Assistant Buyer

Sunflower Hill Gift Shop and Cafe

1996 – 1998 (2 years)

As the Manager and Assistant Buyer for Sunflower Hill, I assisted the owner with product selection, seasonal planning, scheduling, employee supervision, inventory management and store design.
