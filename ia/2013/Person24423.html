Christopher Huffman's Summary

For fourteen years I have worked with companies of all sizes to design and develop internet-based business solutions. Incorporating customer research and user advocacy, I've collaborated with teams of all sizes to build products with measurable success.

My methods are simple: by pairing user needs & expectations with business goals, I set out to craft experiences, not simply transactions.

Rapid iteration prototyping, usability testing, heuristic evaluation, task completion analysis, and persona/scenario development are more of the methods that I utilize regularly to ensure that projects meet their goals from both a customer and business perspective.

- - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -

I enjoy reading about human behavior, browsing UI frameworks & design patterns, and collecting infographics.

My influences are Edward Tufte, Jonathan Ive, Richard Feynman, Luke Wroblewski, Eric Meyer, Dan Cederholm, Ethan Marcotte, and Carl Sagan.

Specialties: User Experience, Interface Design, Human-Computer Interaction and Graphical Information Design
