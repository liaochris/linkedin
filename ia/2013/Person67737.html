Jonathan Tan's Summary

Jonathan Tan is a recognized authority on Asian and Asian American issues whose broad expertise is highly sought after by various academic institutions, publishers, professional associations, as well as community and religious leaders. An accomplished speaker with extensive experience preparing presentations in multiple formats for a variety of audiences, Jonathan has addressed numerous conferences, seminars, and panel discussions across the globe.

At Australian Catholic University (ACU), Jonathan has developed new face-to-face undergraduate courses for the Studies of Religion minor and fully online graduate courses in world religions, christology, and research methodologies. In addition to supervising higher degree research students, Jonathan is also a recipient of a grant from ACU's Research Office for the Asian Christianity Research Support Team Project for 2012-2013.

At Xavier University, Jonathan developed the Religion and Culture focus area for Xavier University's liberal arts core curriculum and created new courses and honors seminars for this focus area that fulfill the university's diversity requirements. A visionary in integrating web 2.0 and social media tools to higher education, Jonathan has been praised by his students and colleagues for his innovative course websites that demonstrate his creative and intuitive skills in synthesizing technology, multimedia, web 2.0 tools, and content.

As Editor of the Proceedings of the Catholic Theological Society of America (CTSA), Jonathan successfully negotiated and implemented the CTSA Proceedings digitization project with Backstage Library Works.

Jonathan holds a Ph.D. from The Catholic University of America, a M.A. from the Graduate Theological Union, and a LL.B. (Honours) from the National University of Singapore Law School.
