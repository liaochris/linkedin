Lauren Frohne's Summary

Lauren Frohne is a New York-based visual storyteller and currently the multimedia producer for the Open Society Foundations. She was previously a video journalist at The Boston Globe where she worked on a range of video projects from breaking news to feature stories to short documentary films. Before that, she interned with the online team at The Roanoke Times in Virginia. In 2010, she earned a master's degree in Journalism from University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, where she focused on multimedia and documentary storytelling. She has experience leading, managing and collaborating with teams on ambitious multimedia projects, as well as teaching audio/video content gathering and editing workflow to undergrads, graduate students and professionals. Additionally, she has served on the faculty of the NPPA Multimedia Immersion Workshop. Her work has been recognized by industry organizations including World Press Photo, NPPA Best of Photojournalism, Online News Association, SXSW, National Edward R. Murrow Awards, and New England Emmys, among others.

Specialties: Shooting, editing, story development, production, FCP 7, FCP X, Adobe, basic web dev and design, user interface design and interactive multimedia, concept to execution project management.
