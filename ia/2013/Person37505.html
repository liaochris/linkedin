Prof. Mohammed Ahmed's Publications

  • Emotional Intelligence Model for Managers

    • HRM Review
    • October 2010
    Authors: Prof. Mohammed Ahmed

    Emotional intelligence refers to those abilities and capabilities that help individuals feel and manage emotions in order to achieve their goals. This article presents a simple and clear explanation of emotional intelligence, and suggests it is a synergy that occurs between the abilities and capabilities that individuals possess for managing emotions. The model presented in this article puts forward three possible outcomes of emotional intelligence. The article claims that the best way to measure the impact of emotional intelligence is to feel and observe it, and develop internal measuring tools based on the industry.

  • TEN STEPS FOR PREPARING MBA LEARNERS FOR BUSINESS AND MANAGEMENT

    • Journal of Global Business and Management
    • August 2010
    Authors: Prof. Mohammed Ahmed

    In the past few decades, business schools have realized that traditional graduate degrees in business such as an M.A. in business administration are not marketable because of their program design. Therefore, most business schools around the world are now focusing on the star of the business school’s product portfolio, the MBA program. The goal of the MBA program is to prepare learners for business and management. This paper presents the ten steps that business school must take to prepare the learner for business and management. The paper is a triggered research paper, and the research concludes that business schools must foster a culture of continuous learning, and work with facilitators (faculty), corporate partners, and accrediting agencies as a team to prepare MBA learners for the world of business and management.

  • New Six Sigma : A Business Improvement Process for Human Resource Management

    • HRM Review
    • June 2010
    Authors: Prof. Mohammed Ahmed

    In the changing business environment, companies constantly look for new ways to improve their business processes, and satisfy the customers’ wants and needs. In the nineties, several businesses deployed a process called Six Sigma, which was a way to reduce the error or failure level in manufacturing. It was a better quantitative measure of performance compared to the past in the manufacturing industry. The total business performance of the firm depends on the five functions of the organization. New Six Sigma suggests that business improvement processes should be deployed in all the five functions of business including human resources management to improve the total business performance. Also, New Six Sigma should be embedded as a corporate culture rather than as a functional culture

  • A Value Based Model for Executive Compensation

    • International Academy of Management and Business, Orlando, Florida.
    • January 2011
    Authors: Prof. Mohammed Ahmed, Dr. Pedro Pellet, Dr. Bahaudin Mujtaba

    Executive compensation has been the topic of discussion in management research for the last few decades. The financial crises of the financial service industry and the economic slowdown has once again raised the issue of executive compensation in both research and the investment community. Corporate America is creatively claiming a large amount of benefits for the top executives from the wealth that was generated from the stockholders capital, borrowed capital on behalf of the stockholders, and creative human capital invested by the employees. The goal of this paper was to develop a compensation model for the corporate executives based on the business performance.

    The model presented in the paper includes four variables that are needed to create an environment where a fair compensation plan for the executive can be developed, implemented, and monitored. The four variables are 1) culture, 2) governance, 3) disclosure, and 4) oversight. Once the corporate framework is designed based on these four variables, the next step is to develop a measure to compensate executives based on the overall performance of the company. The variable used in the model are revenues, earnings before interest and taxes, net income, earning per share, free cash flows, invested capital, cost of capital, return on equity, and market value of the firm. The model based on these variables will generate a fair compensation plan for executives, and a compensation that is based on the performance of the company.

  • An Investment Performance of High-Intensity Firms In Relation to New Product Information

    • Global Finance Conference, Bangkok, Thailand
    • April 4, 2011
    Authors: Prof. Mohammed Ahmed

    The growth and profits of the firms depend on the success of new products at the marketplace. New product development involves large investment and the failure of the product at the marketplace will have a severe impact on the financial health of the firm. The products in the technology-based industries have become so complex that the industries have changed their production strategy from manufacturing to sub-assembling the products. A new product development involves sub-assembly of components from several industries. It is difficult for the average investor to understand the technological complexity of the product. This raises the question, whether the market has the ability to digest the technological complex information to reflect the fair value of the security. The market’s ability to digest all the available information to reflect the implicit value of the security is an important concern to both the management and the investors. The management is investing in new product development to generate growth and create value for the stockholders, and if the market is not absorbing the information about the new product due to the technical complexity of the information, management will not be successful in maximizing the stockholders wealth. Also, it will be difficult for investors to determine the fair value of the security. A sample of 10 high-intensity firms was used to determine whether the market was absorbing all the available information regarding new product development to reflect the fair value of the security. The actual and expected returns surrounding the new product information were calculated and a t-test was used to determine the significance of the abnormal returns. The results of the study suggest that there were opportunities for investors to make abnormal profits surrounding the release of the new product information. This suggests that the market was not absorbing all the available information to reflect the implicit value of the security.
