Michael Schwob's Summary

I believe that the acoustics of a space should support the use of that space.

● Theaters should deliver an immersive acoustic experience.
● Hotel guestrooms should provide privacy and have low noise levels to allow restful sleep.
● Nightclubs should sound great on the inside and be silent on the outside.
● Auditoriums and classrooms should improve speech comprehension and learning.
● Convention spaces should have the flexibility to sound good under a variety of uses.
● Offices should provide privacy and have noise levels that facilitate productivity.

  • Las Vegas City Hall

    • July 2008 to February 2012
    Team Members: Michael Schwob, Michael Crowe

    A seven-story, 310,000 sq foot, LEED GOLD facility designed to serve as the cornerstone of a downtown revitalization. Since its occupation in the spring of 2012, utility data has shown annual savings of more than $500,000 in energy costs. That’s just 50% of the old facility’s energy use and 30% better than the city’s goal. The JBA team designed the acoustics, solar power, electrical and HVAC systems, as well as the plumbing, telecommunications, data, security, surveillance, fire protection and many of the audiovisual systems for the building.

JBA’s Trusted Advisors™ designed and engineered the acoustical, mechanical, electrical, plumbing, telecommunications, audiovisual, security and surveillance systems . Among the challenges: making sure noise from a nearby container processing facility would not disturb guests.
