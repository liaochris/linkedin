Mike Barnsback's Summary

Mr. Barnsback counsels and represents Virginia employers in all aspects of employment law. He has frequently lectured on and assisted employers with disability accommodation and leave issues under the ADA and FMLA. He is experienced defending employers in Department of Labor wage/hour audits and FLSA individual and collective action overtime cases. He also has significant background representing employers before the EEOC and state/local administrative agencies. He has over twenty years of courtroom experience litigating employment related cases in the Eastern District of Virginia ("Rocket Docket") and Virginia Circuit Courts in the City Alexandria, and Arlington and Fairfax counties.

His practice also focuses on protecting employers from unfair competition and theft of trade secrets and confidential information, and breaches of fiduciary duties by employees, officers and directors. He works closely with employers to draft effective non-compete/non-soliciation agreements. He also litigates the enforceabilty of such agreements.

Mr. Barnsback has worked extensively with federal government contractors and employers in the healthcare and construction fields.

He is an editor of the Virginia Employment Law Letter, a monthly newsletter published by M. Lee Smith Publishers, LLC. Mr. Barnsback also lectures frequently on employment law and human resources issues.

Specialties: The interplay between the ADA and FMLA; wage-hour/overtime issues; DoL/ICE audits; defending against EEOC charges of discrimination; employment contracts (non-compete/non-solicitation); drafting/reviewing employee handbooks; human resources counseling.

Mike Barnsback's Publications

  • Attorney Fees and Sanctions - Virginia and Federal Courts

    • Virginia CLE Publications
    • July 2012
    Authors: Mike Barnsback, Bernard DiMuro and Michael Lieberman

    Under the "American Rule" the losing party generally is not liable for attorney fees, but many federal and Virginia statutes provide for award of attorney fees and parties may agree to fee awards. This publication covers the law of both attorney fees and sanctions in federal and Virginia courts. It provides numerous practice tips as well as example pleadings.

  • "ADA" chapter in HR Guide To Employment Law: A Practical Compliance Reference

    • BLR-Business & Legal Resources
    • 2013
    Authors: Mike Barnsback, Jonathan Mook, Audra Hamilton

    The chapter provides an overview of the coverage of the ADA and the ADA Amendments Act, along with a practical guide for employers to help them undertake the "interactive process" to identify and provide reasonable accommondations for qualified individuals with disabilities.

  • "Other Laws That Impact Benefit Plans" chapter in Benefits Compliance Strategies For Plans Program & Policies

    • M. Lee Smith Publishers LLC
    • 2010
    Authors: Mike Barnsback

    The chapter discusses the federal discrimination laws that impact employee benefit plans, such as the Age Discrimination in Employment Act, the Americans with Disabilities Act, Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964 and the Family and Medical Leave Act.
