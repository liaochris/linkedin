Kevin Brozovich's Experience

President

HRM Innovations, LLC

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Human Resources industry

August 2009 – Present (4 years 3 months)

Based in Kalamazoo, Michigan, HRM Innovations, LLC is a Human Resources and Management consulting organization dedicated to helping businesses thrive. Some of our products include:

- Recruiting Services
- Human Resources Consulting
- Leadership training and development
- Management Consulting
- New company start up packages
- Performance management
- Culture change/improvement
- Business ethics/harassment complaint investigations
- Hiring and selection system design
- Union avoidance strategies
- Employee relations audits
- Lean office strategies

With a proven track record of successful results, HRM Innovations, LLC is ready to help your business thrive.

EHS and Human Resources Manager

Kaiser Aluminum

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; KALU; Mining & Metals industry

November 2008 – July 2009 (9 months)

Responsible for establishing all HR and EHS systems and policies for a new aluminum casting and extrusion plant start-up.

• Designed best in class HR practices, including compensation, performance management, communications, goal setting, training, and development systems. Wrote an employee handbook and related HR systems to support a positive, continuous learning culture.

• Created and implemented a recruiting and selection campaign utilizing multiple advertising methods including the addition of Kaiser's first online job posting process that attracted more than 1,000 applicants during the first two weeks of use. Developed an extensive training and selection process to ensure that all new hires had the skills necessary to succeed.

Sr. Admin. Mgr.

ASMO Manufacturing, Inc

September 1997 – October 2008 (11 years 2 months)

A 350 employee Toyota group company and supplier of windshield washer systems. Hired as HR Manager and was promoted to Senior Administration Manager with responsibility for both positions starting in 2001.

Responsible for the Human Resources, Environmental, Accounting, Purchasing, and Business Control departments for plants located in Michigan and Texas. Supervised a staff of fourteen plus a contract security staff of five employees. Functioned as Business Ethics Compliance Officer, Legal Compliance Officer, and Environmental System Management Representative.

• Reduced union activity from an active UAW campaign in 1997 to non-existent by 2001. Implemented a positive associate relations program, reorganized the operations leadership structure, and implemented a leadership training program. Due to these improvements, ASMO ranked in the top 10% of DENSO Group Companies for associate relations during the 2003, 2005, and 2007 review processes.

• Successfully started a new manufacturing facility near Dallas, Texas. Activities included: Labor market analysis, environmental site assessment, development of a staffing plan, policy and organizational structure development. Coordinated public relations events, press releases, and assured the smooth start-up of the new operation. The new plant was launched on time and within budget.

• Saved two existing programs and 18 jobs by developing a sales project team to improve our plant sales condition and coordinate communications with our sales organization. Led the team through the successful creation of a future sales strategy.

Human Resources Manager

Geiger Technic, Inc

June 1994 – September 1997 (3 years 4 months)

Responsible for the HR activities and administrative services for a 100 employee German tier 1 automotive supplier of surge tanks. Joined the company six weeks after the implementation of the first contract with the Teamsters Union.

• Immediately began the implementation of a successful labor relations program. Only one grievance was ever filed, and after one year the employees deauthorized the Teamsters and dissolved the union. The company remains union free.

• Created and conducted training programs for basic supervision, drug and alcohol awareness, sexual harassment, and how to conduct a performance evaluation.

• Successfully created and facilitated a cross-functional team to create a manufacturing work cell for one of our highest volume products. Eliminated the need for material handling within the work cell and decreased the opportunity for the creation of multiple defective parts saving approximately $35,000 a year. Developed a training program for affected employees.

Sr. Human Resources Consultant

Zenith Data Systems

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Computer Hardware industry

June 1988 – June 1994 (6 years 1 month)

Was promoted three times while working at Zenith, a worldwide producer of mobile, portable, and desktop computer systems.

Senior Human Resources Consultant: Provided HR generalist support to the 180 employee engineering organization with locations in Billerica, MA; St. Joseph, MI; and Santa Clara, CA. Responsibilities included: technical recruiting, training and development, salary planning, and employee relations.

Senior Human Resources Representative: Responsible for the human resource activities of a workforce of 175 unionized and 50 salaried employees in the personal computer manufacturing operation, including: Grievance resolution, contract administration, skilled trades recruiting, and other labor relations duties. Supervised an assistant.

Human Resources Representative and Human Resources Specialist: Responsible for handling grievances and discipline for a unionized (Steelworker) workforce of 1,000. Due to a promotion and corporate restructuring, assumed responsibility for overall labor relations and skilled trades recruiting. Reduced grievances from an average of 150 per year to 16 by negotiating improvements to the grievance process with the union.
