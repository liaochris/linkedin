Brian O'Keefe's Summary

Brian O’Keefe, Ph.D. specializes in user experience design solutions for current, emerging and future product design and development. Brian is a Visiting Assistant Professor in the Masters, Human Computer Interaction (HCI) program in the Golisano College of Computing and Information Sciences at Rochester Institute of Technology. He has been developing new MS HCI courses to prepare students for the continuous shifts in mobile technologies, all the while, instructing the fundamental skill sets of a human factors specialist and interaction designer.

Brian's skills have been utilized by Eastman Kodak Company’s consumer electronics and advanced technologies generation teams. He has been working on Mobile Tourism projects in the greater Rochester area e.g. VistRochester, Finger Lakes Visitors and Connection, Genesee Village Country and Museum, Eastern National Parks. Brian has worked in the United Kingdom on European Union research projects specializing in interaction design for conversational interfaces, and he designed museum installations for the University of Siena in Tuscany, Italy. Brian holds a Ph.D. in Human Computer Interaction from the University of Florence, Italy and a BFA in Interactive and Graphic Arts from Tyler School of Art, Temple University.

Brian O'Keefe's Projects

  • NY Council on the Arts Mobile Tourism Project

    • January 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Brian O'Keefe, Stephen Mokey, Nicholas Iuliucci, Alex Nalbandian, Gaurav Chandwani

    The Mobile Experiences for Tourism Project is funded by the New York Council on the Arts, Rochester Institute of Technology and Visit Rochester. We are working to create mobile interactions which focus on historical education through digital storytelling and augmented reality. Our goal is to inspire adults to interact by designing engaging mobile experiences for their children.

  • Brick City Tours

    • August 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Brian O'Keefe, , Brianna Slutsky, Alex Nalbandian, Nicholas Iuliucci, Anushri Thanedar

    MS students and faculty have been researching and developing Brick City Tours (BCT). BCT is a host of tourisms services that connects prospective RIT students and their families to our university. After ten weeks of empirical studies and interviews with the RIT admissions office, we have uncovered that there is not a strategic plan to build and maintain relationships with prospective RIT students. Tour information is often too much to document and comprehend in an one hour campus tour. In short, the our research enables the prospective RIT student to directly experience the innovations of RIT, its students and faculty.
