Hanna Teräs' Summary

I am Learning Designer at University of Wollongong. My professional key interests include transformation of higher education in order to meet the challenges posed by the evolving 21st century knowledge society. I am currently on leave from a Senior Lecturer position at Tampere University of Applied Sciences, where I was the leading developer and Program Director for the international, fully online 21st Century Educators Postgraduate Certificate for Teaching in Higher Education. For the past decade I have taught online pedagogy to pre- and in-service teachers and implemented professional development for teaching faculty in this field in different parts of the world.

I am a PhD (Education) candidate at University of Tampere, expecting to defend my dissertation in 2014. I conduct educational design research on developing international, fully online, authentic e-learning based professional development for teaching faculty.

Research interests: Teaching faculty professional development, 21st century pedagogy, authentic e-Learning, social media in education, virtual learning communities, gamified online learning environments.

  • The role of gamification and game-based learning in authentic assessment within virtual environments

    • HERDSA
    • June 1, 2013
    Authors: Hanna Teräs, Lincoln C. Wood, Torsten Reiners, Sue Gregory

    Gamification is the application of game-based elements to non-game systems. Within education this can incorporate many game-based mechanisms. We review the use of some of these specifically: rewind, ghost images, save points and multiple lives, and time and space control. The opportunities to use these game-based mechanisms to assist assessment have remained under-examined and under-exploited. However, these game-based elements are particularly pertinent within an authentic learning framework supported by virtual environments, or virtual places. Thus, we identify how these game-based mechanisms can improve assessment while increasing efficiency and providing new opportunities for educators. This longitudinal study spans from 2012 to 2015.
