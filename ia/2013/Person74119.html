Ken Brenner's Summary

Brenner is a seasoned entrepreneur, executive, and sports marketing professional with 20+ years of experience. Ken has built several new media, technology companies that have dramatically impacted sports and entertainment including: Professional Sports Care, a revolutionary sports medicine company (PSCM:NASDAQ); Television Games Network (TVG), the first interactive television network in sports that was sold to Newscorp/TCI; Sponsor Direct, a leading Web solutions company for Fortune 500 corporate sponsors; Legacy Direct, a company devoted to providing additional resources and digital tools to help athletes, entertainers and celebrities "give back," and most recently Legacy Connect with his partner Mark Rockefeller.

"Baby Allie" the Genesis of Legacy Connect
In 1998, after losing his 11-month old daughter, "Baby Allie" to Spinal Muscular Atrophy (SMA), the #1 inherited cause of infant death, Brenner started his own foundation called Eyes of Hope, now part of FightSMA. Ken learned first-hand how celebrity star power and corporate brands can mobilize fans and audiences globally to support important causes. Brenner's insight and knowledge of the sports and entertainment industry combined with his personal passion for philanthropy led Brenner to launch Legacy Connect. Legacy uses proprietary technology, social networking and modern online marketing techniques to help non-profits raise incremental dollars globally by leveraging celebrity star power and corporate sponsors.

Brenner has served as a guest lecturer and speaker at NYU, Princeton, Georgetown, SBJ's Sports Sponsorship Summit and hosts two industry-wide summits annually on the topic of Technology's Impact on the Sponsorship Industry.

Ken is married to his wife Amy and has three children, Jake who is 14 (Baby Allie’s twin brother), and a new set of twins Kailey and Kolby who are 11 years-old.
