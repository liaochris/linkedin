Jay Hershman's Summary

Jay has over fifteen years of business development experience and has expertise across a variety of industries including financial services, information technology, social business and digital media. He has a history of spearheading innovation as a business leader through all stages of a corporate lifecycle from start-up through global Fortune 100 institution. Jay currently works as an entrepreneur and operational advisor across several new technology ventures. His primary role within these organizations is to identify and execute on a business development and sales strategy to drive early stage revenue growth.

Most recently, Jay served as the Citi Prepaid Sales Head across Europe, Middle East and Africa, responsible for multi-channel sales, origination, pipeline development, and customer revenue growth. As a pioneer in International Prepaid, his focus was also on understanding complex market dynamics for targeting core applications with existing, market-specific payment flows, while working to establish Citi as the dominant provider of corporate-funded Prepaid solutions in the region. During his time at Citi, Jay managed driving revenue growth by over 150% across 23 new markets for Citi Prepaid. He represented Citi at industry events, press interviews, and news releases related to Citi’s Prepaid solutions.

Jay served as VP, Partnership Development at Ecount, the leading provider of electronic payment solutions, prior to the Ecount’s acquisition by Citi in 2007. During this time, Jay was instrumental in executing on direct strategic account sales and indirect channel strategy, which included 100+ partners responsible for approximately 40% of company revenues.

Preceding Citi Prepaid Services, Jay was a founding team member of Sapias, Inc. (acquired by Wireless Matrix), Mediabolic (acquired by Rovi - formerly Macrovision), and FortuneCity.com ($125MM IPO on the Frankfurt Stock Exchange).

Specialties

Global Partnerships, Sales, Negotiation, Industry Insight, Competitive Analysis, Marketing Strategies.
