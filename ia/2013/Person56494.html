J.W. (Jackie) Daniel's Summary

In 1991, Daniel joined the Society of Competitive Intelligence Professionals, where he developed his marketing and competitive intelligence expertise on an international scale, receiving specialized training regarding Korean and Japanese markets under such leaders as Michael E. Porter, PhD, of the Harvard Business School. He was later responsible for consulting on construction of six automotive plants in Japan, Australia and Korea for KIA and TOYOTA in collaboration with a tier one supplier.

As either a consultant or employee, he has assisted revenue generation for over 50 of the Fortune 500 companies, including Coca Cola, Dow Chemical, DuPont, FedEx, General Mills, Glaxo Wellcom, Northwest Airlines, Pillsbury, 3M and Turner Broadcasting. His most recent engagement was with Best Buy, where he was responsible for their pricing strategy in the US domestic market. Also serving as a corporate intelligence officer, he assisted with their war room strategy and strategic planning process. While at Best Buy, revenues increased from $24.6 Billion to $45 Billion. He returned home to the family business full time in December of 2010 to augment his legacy in industrial minerals, fossil fuels, renewable energy, agribusiness, commodities and exports. His prominent background in agribusiness, competitive intelligence and international marketing provides the foundation that brings success to all of his business endeavors.

Specialties

Daniel has been ranked as one of the top ten consultants for the U.S. Department of Agriculture and was on the staff at Texas A&M University for eight years.
