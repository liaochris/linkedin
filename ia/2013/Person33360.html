Debbie Elmer's Experience

Owner and Sculptor

Debbie Elmer Studios

Public Company; Myself Only; Fine Art industry

January 2000 – Present (13 years 8 months)

Established a fine art business, which specializes in creating unique bronze sculptures. Debbie Elmer is familiar with every aspect of the creation of large-scale bronze sculpture from concept to delivery. Experienced in contracting with architectural firms to design and create custom architectural and interior elements. Sculpture work is included in many notable private and public collections including:

•William Randolph Hearst Family Collection
•City of Beverly Hills Permanent Public Collection.

Project Coordinator and Resident Artist

Carver and Schicketanz Architects

February 2005 – July 2007 (2 years 6 months)

Created custom bronze work for architectural settings as a resident figurative artist with this prestigious firm. Also assisted the company Principals and project Interior Designers to facilitate the progress of major residential jobs to completion. Primarily responsible for the coordination of a multimillion dollar interiors project. Created elaborate and informative spreadsheets to monitor project workflow and spending. Worked closely with accounting to ensure accurate records, communicated with subcontractors, resolved vendor disputes, reviewed contracts, lead weekly staff meetings, traveled out-of-state regularly to hold meetings, inspect goods, arrange storage, resolve problems, and coordinate installation. Evaluated department workflow, defined needs, and interviewed potential candidates for additional staff as necessary. All while maintaining an active roll within the company as an artist and as a small studio owner.

Assistant Production Manager and Apprentice

Richard MacDonald Studios

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Fine Art industry

March 2004 – August 2004 (6 months)

Assisted world-renowned artist Richard Macdonald in his fine art studio. Maintained quality control, work flow schedules, vendor relations, and database organization. Helped remedy the production problems in prominent bronze foundries throughout the state of California.
