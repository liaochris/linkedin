Judd Rattner's Summary

In 2007 I co-founded Intellidemia, a firm specializing in enterprise systems for higher education. My company developed Concourse, the industry’s first syllabus management platform for organizing, sharing, and analyzing course information. In many ways, Concourse is to syllabi what LinkedIn is to resumes.

As CEO, I took Intellidemia’s vision from concept to commercialization by overseeing every decision tied to system architecture, interface design, quality assurance, marketing, sales, hiring, performance review, financing, intellectual property, and strategy.

My greatest passion and area of expertise is in the arena of product development and management, and sales and distribution. I continue to play an integral part in translating customer requirements into technical specifications, designing implementations and deployments, and providing post-installation consulting and support. In addition to focusing on our current clients, I spend my time generating leads, working trade shows, advancing prospects, and negotiating deals.

Prior to founding Intellidemia, I worked for General Motors as an electrical engineer in five different product development areas: concepts, development, testing, manufacturing, and program management. I also spent time as both a radio frequency and test engineer for Lutron Electronics and a lighting control manufacturer.

I graduated first in my class from Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute with bachelor’s degrees in electrical engineering and management. I then completed a distinctive MBA program between Rensselaer and Albany Law where I focused on technology commercialization and business law.

I’ve always been told that entrepreneurship runs in my blood, and I can think of no better or more rewarding career than to dedicate myself to commercializing technologies and turning entrepreneurial visions into viable, successful realities.

Specialties

Technology commercialization, New product management, Strategic planning and execution, Business development, Enterprise sales, Requirement definition and specification writing, Client relationships, Prototyping and user experience, System architecture and design, Test engineering and quality assurance
