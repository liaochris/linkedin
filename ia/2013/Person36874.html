Don Packett's Summary

Seller of Perspective. Slasher of Legacies.

Our methodology of innovation-based problem-solving is simple:
• Identify legacies
• Kill them
• Do better business

Don co-founded 21Tanks in 2008 with the primary objective of selling perspective to large corporations. Through the years, they've refined their tactics and methodologies to solve in-depth business problems for the likes of FNB, J&J, BMW and other large organisations (acronym'd or otherwise).

Through working with organisations and clients from all sectors and all walks of life, the Power of Perspective grows and allows 21Tanks the opportunity to identify, in real-time, problems within organisations. They then work with teams to solving those problems, allowing every member present to commit to a new way of thinking, and changing their business for the better.

Don Packett's Experience

Co-Founder / MD

21Tanks (Sole Proprietorship)

Sole Proprietorship; 1-10 employees; Think Tanks industry

June 2008 – Present (5 years 4 months) Johannesburg Area, South Africa

When a group of smart people are struggling with an innovation based problem, it's never a lack of knowledge that's holding them back - it's a lack of perspective.

We sell perspective.

So...

What do we do?
We fix shit. Every business has problems, and within those problems lie a number of complexities that, whoever is suffering from said problem, finds difficult to identify. So we do just that. We identify true problems in organisations, and help them fix them. Simple.

How do we do it?
We talk to businesses. Lots of businesses. We run QDFs with a wide range of organisations, through a number of different industries, from around the world. A QDF is a Qualified Deconstructive Facilitation, and is manifested in a number of ways, depending on the problem at hand.

Why do we do it?
Because, firstly, we really enjoy it. Secondly, we’re really, really good at it. Really. And thirdly, we understand the Power of Perspective. When a group of smart people are sitting around a table trying to solve a problem, it’s not a lack of knowledge that holds them back, it’s a lack of perspective.

We sell perspective. And that’s the key ingredient.

Don Packett's Publications

  • Do Ideas

    • Amazon
    • June 1, 2012
    Authors: Don Packett

    Ideas are everywhere. Everyone has them. If you look at something that isn’t working correctly, or optimally, and consider a possible solution to fixing it, you’ve had an idea. You have them all day, every day.

    The problem, however, is that the percentage of these ideas that we actually act upon and execute is minimal. Extremely minimal. Why? Well, everyone has their own reasons why, of course. Time, resources, fear of failure. The list goes on. So ideas sit in our heads, stagnating, and are filed away in the “One day…” cabinet of our brains. A very dark place to be.

    Do Ideas is designed to encourage every person with an idea, to do that idea.

    Through a number of extreme varying perspectives on how to get things done, the aim of this book is for any person reading it to resonate with at least one contribution, and realise that people from all walks of life, from all around the world, are doing ideas.

    And so can you.
