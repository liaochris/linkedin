Kathy Sipple's Summary

Hi, I’m Kathy Sipple. Thanks for visiting my LinkedIn profile today.

As a social media coach, I’m a fusion of business coach, story teller, public speaker, tree hugger and marketer. It’s a unique blend that usually works very well for the clients I am most drawn to serve: forward thinking people with a do-it-yourself spirit--entrepreneurs, non-profits, innovators, artists, and green companies. I also work with banks, law firms and ad agencies open to a fresh approach. Someone once described me as a hippie in Talbott’s clothing. I took that as a compliment.

I think about sustainability. A lot. Sustainability from the standpoint of time, resources, energy and spirit as well as its environmental impact. This focus informs my approach to working with clients’ marketing programs – many of them are busy with minimal time to work on marketing, much less to research the “how to’s” of the ever changing social media landscape.

I relish discovering the opportunities that exist with today’s marketing tools that didn't exist a short while ago and coaching clients to use these ideas. There has never been a better time for creative people to tell their story at such an affordable price.

The value I provide lies in inspiring others to tell their story, helping them connect to their target audience, creating sustainable marketing systems and growing their business. I empower my clients to create their best marketing plans. I have a vast referral network for services required beyond social media and online marketing.

I look forward to connecting!

Kathy Sipple's Education

University of Michigan

B.A., Economics

1984 – 1988

I started out in the College of Engineering, but quickly figured out a steady diet of Math, Science and Computer Programming was not for me if it was missing English, Psychology, Marketing, and Art History.

I wanted to major in Marketing, but didn't want the hassle of transferring into Business School. An Economics degree (through the College of Literature, Science and the Arts) was a bit of a compromise. It felt "marketable" back in the 80's. It wasn't, but it provided an interesting lens through which to see the world. Besides typical classes like Money & Banking and Econometrics, I also took a lot of Economics of the Environment classes through the School of Natural Resources, which stoked my passion for environmental matters.

I learned as much or more outside the classroom at the University of Michigan as I did in class. Ann Arbor provided a rich, multi-cultural experience that I will forever treasure.

Activities and Societies: Alpha Chi Omega
