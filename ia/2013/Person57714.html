Jameson Fink's Summary

Nominated for a 2013 Saveur Best Food Blog Award in the Beer/Wine category.

Nominated for "Best Writing on a Wine Blog" for the 2013 Wine Blog Awards.

Nominated for "Best Overall Wine Blog" for the 2012 Wine Blog Awards.

Named "One to Watch" in the 9 Most Important Wine Bloggers in the US by Paul Mabray of VinTank.

Created Jameson Fink LLC in March of 2012 offering social media consulting and marketing guidance, as well as wine writing, blogging, and education services.

Working for a locally owned, independent wine bar and shop in Seattle's Madrona neighborhood, Bottlehouse. Bringing my retail experience, social media marketing, and wine writing experience to assist in growing and developing the business.

Ran an extremely busy, eclectic, and unique beer and wine department within the QFC Broadway Market in Capitol Hill for four years. In charge of purchasing and department merchandising. Built a loyal following with customers by providing a dynamic selection and outstanding customer service. Joined Esquin Wine Merchants to expand my involvement in social media and blogging and am now working on taking my experience to new clients. Sharing my fun and informative take on food and wine via jamesonfink.com. I am also the Wine Editor for Foodista and a Food and Wine Contributor to Dabble Magazine.

I have traveled extensively to visit vineyards and wineries in Australia, New Zealand, South Africa, Chile, Argentina, France, Greece, Portugal, and Italy.

Specialties: Social media, blogging, wine buying, wine selection, purchasing, building a wine cellar, wine consulting, cooking, food and wine pairings.

Jameson Fink's Publications

  • Wine Without Worry Podcast

    Authors: Jameson Fink

    Bringing my accessible, low-key, and irreverent style of writing about wine to talking about wine. Join me and a special guest as we laugh and learn together.

  • Chicken Friends and Mummy Berries: How a City Boy Reacts to a Month of Rural Life

    • Edible Seattle
    • October 2012
    Authors: Jameson Fink

    My feature detailing a month I spent living and volunteering on an organic family farm and cidery on Washington State's Olympic Peninsula.

  • Melon de Bourgogne: Beginngers Guide to Wine

    • 101 Publishing
    • July 5, 2012
    Authors: Jameson Fink

    Looking for something to beat summer’s heat and accompany that seafood or salad? A well-made Muscadet from the Melon de Bourgogne grape stands up to the toughest meal challenge. From crisp summer sippers to more cellar-worthy styles, this light-bodied white is one of the world’s best wine values. Wine writer Jameson Fink takes a look at the grape’s history and the unique technique that draws out its mineral-like qualities. “Melon de Bourgogne: Beginners Guide to Wine,” offers practical tips for buying and enjoying Muscadet, from foods it pairs with — think shellfish and more — to identifying a quality bottle and what you will pay for it.

  • The Modern Barista: A Sommelier By Another Name

    • SIP Northwest
    • November 2012
    Authors: Jameson Fink

    The unsung work of the barista, as well as the roaster, to express single-origin coffees in a manner that reflects their uniqueness and sense of place. Dare I call it coffee terroir? Steve Smith of Seattle's Fonte Coffee Roaster and Jared Rennie of Ashland, Oregon's Noble Coffee Roasting provide their expert perspective and philosophical thoughts.
