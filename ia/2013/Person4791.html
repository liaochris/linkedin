Allan Schoenberg's Summary

I am an accomplished public relations executive with a successful track record in all aspects of business-to-business communications with an emphasis in brand management, international communications, public relations and social media. My strengths are that I am detail-oriented and an energetic self-starter with a proven track record to lead teams and large, complex projects. With my academic focus in economics, I often look at the business and behavioral aspects of communications, which has been a key component to my success.

I am an experienced public speaker and have presented at Blogwell, Business Marketing Association, Finextra, FIA Expo, Ragan Communications, University of Chicago and other events in Hong Kong, Holland, England and throughout the United States, discussing communication strategies, content management, social media and evaluation.

I was an adjunct professor with DePaul University and Loyola University Chicago from 2005 to 2010 lecturing on reputation management, crisis management, social media and ethics in the graduate and undergraduate schools.

  • What it Means to Lead During a Crisis: An Exploratory Examination

    • May 2004
    Authors: Allan Schoenberg

    This research project was conducted to examine the concept of crisis leadership. Many articles have been written on leadership, crisis preparation, crisis management and the tactical elements involved in addressing a crisis scenario, but very little research exists on the skills and expertise to succeed as a crisis leader. This exploratory research project demonstrated that preparation may not be the key to managing a crisis; instead, organizations should focus on developing leadership skills and top communicators should identify who are the most effective individuals to lead during a crisis and enlist them in an organization’s planning and ongoing crisis management efforts. Using a mixed methods research approach, this project introduces a model of crisis leadership to illustrate what it takes to lead during a crisis and possibly facilitate future research on this topic. The Crisis Leadership Model suggests that crisis leadership involves a combination of four external factors (information gathering, external conscience, preparation, experience) and a wide array of personal and leadership attributes, built on a foundation of communications reinforced by authenticity and influence.
