Martha Newman, J.D., PCC Professional Coach's Summary

Martha M. Newman, J.D., PCC is an Executive and Professional Coach specializing in goal-setting and action-planning on a wide array of business and personal issues that transform her clients’ careers and bring success to their lives. She is one of a small percentage of coaches in the country who has earned the coveted Professional Certified Coach (PCC) credential from the International Coach Federation.

Having received her academic training in coaching from the prestigious Graduate School of Management at UT Dallas, Martha’s coaching and workshops are outcome-oriented with a focus on positive change that produces tangible results for her clients. She was formerly a business attorney, broadcast journalist and Fort Worth Zoning Commission Vice-Chair. Her corporate work experience is in the fields of sales training, technical writing, public relations, and advertising.

Specialties: Leadership training, team building, communication skills, marketing, interpersonal skills, business development, networking, career transitions.

Martha Newman, J.D., PCC Professional Coach's Honors and Awards

  • Professional Certified Coach (PCC)

    International Coach Federation
    • October 2009

    The only legitimate CREDENTIALS that exist for coaches are the credentials earned through the International Coach Federation, the esteemed organization universally recognized the world over for its tough requirements and high standards. http://www.coachfederation.org/icfcredentials/core-competencies/
    IN ORDER TO EARN the PCC (Professional Coaching Credential,) a coach must:
    (1) Be rigorously judged according to a defined competency model by ICF Master Coaches who listen to recorded coaching sessions conducted by the applicant coach and determine whether the applicant performs in accordance with the demanding standards set by the ICF.
    (2) Record and substantiate at least 750 hours of coaching individual clients.
    (3) Undergo 125 hours of coach-specific training through an accredited coaching program.
    (4) Perform a minimum of 6 observed-coaching sessions with an experienced coach.
    (5) Pass a comprehensive final exam that evaluates a student's coaching competency.
