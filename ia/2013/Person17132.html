Boris Reuderink's Summary

My passion is to identify simplicity in apparent complexity. My tools of choice are machine learning methods — a branch of artificial intelligence that involves automatically learning structure from data. Besides this math heavy interest, I like to work with people, to jointly explore uncharted territories.

In the past years I have obtained copious experience in creating systems that can reason about the real world. For example, I have worked on recognition of handwritten text, detection of facial expressions in audio and video, and on decoding brain activity from EEG recordings, such that raw thoughts can be used to control games.

Now it is time to capitalize on the potential of these learnings systems, that are best applied when there is an abundance of data, and a lack of time or interest work with this data manually. I offer machine learning consultancy with my company Cortext, ranging from simple advice to jointly developed solutions.

Boris Reuderink's Patents

  • Method and System for a Brain-Computer Interface

    • Netherlands Patent 12190426.2
    • Issued October 29, 2012
    Inventors: Boris Reuderink

    One of the difficulties in creating brain-computer interfaces (BCIs) is that one needs to have extensive knowledge of the brain and of signal processing methods. This slows scientific progress and dissemination of BCI technology. To alleviate this issue, we propose a new method, that can learn detectors for characteristic brain activity fully from data. This means that a layperson can record his brain signals, and note at what time points he did something interesting with this brain. Based on only these recorded signals and time points, this method can create a piece of software that can detect the presence of this interesting brain activity in real-time. This allows anyone to build BCIs. The method is based on a polynomial kernel, which can implicitly use time domain, band-power and even cross-frequency coupling features, combined with a regularizer that promotes solutions that are based on recently recorded samples. A one-class kernel machine is subsequently used to optimize detectors that selectively fire when the detector’s target brain activity is produced. We show that the detectors learned with this generic method can match, and outperform conventional methods that were developed and optimized for specific neuronal signatures.

Boris Reuderink's Projects

  • EEGtools

    • 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Boris Reuderink

    EEGtools is an open source software project containing signal processing processing methods for EEG analysis. EEGtools is written in the Python language, and can automatically download and import a wide variety of public EEG datasets into a common format. The signal processing methods are designed for the construction of brain-computer interfaces, that require real-time decoding of brain activity.
