Leon Degeling's Experience

Consultant and Architect

Devoto & Vitale

Public Company; Myself Only; Internet industry

Devoto & Vitale is a company under construction. My company invents, designs and realizes and operates smart online applications. Some are our own, other we develop for our valued customers. Director Development

Mirabeau

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Internet industry

September 2007 – September 2010 (3 years 1 month) Amsterdam, Eindhoven, Rotterdam, Hoorn

As Director Development I was responsible for software Development within Mirabeau. I headed a decentralized team of 110+ software engineers, technical specialists, consultants, information analysts, frontenders and testers.In this role, I was constantly trying to further optimise the process of software Development, despite the growing organization to keep the teams lean and strong, and our increasingly demanding customers with good solutions within time and budget.

I had a large role in the pre-sales activities, I determined pitch strategies, headed numerous pitches, spoke at fairs and congresses and had an ongoing relationship with most customers. I was considered a trusted advisor and partner in good and in bad times. I was responsible for the partner policy of Mirabeau. I had therefore a close relationship with many technology-suppliers, the competition and other organisations.

As a member of the management team, I attempted to steer the growth and development of Mirabeau. We did this by broadening our portfolio with new innovative services and by purposely working on attracting a broad set of clients. Working on this was a very rewarding experience.

Technical Consultant

Mirabeau

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Internet industry

June 2001 – September 2007 (6 years 4 months) Amsterdam, Eindhoven, Rotterdam

From the start of full-service internet agency Mirabeau I was employed there. As a technical project lead I realized the first versions of Funda.nl, new versions of Consumentenbond.nl and Triodos.com and the websites of Senter-Novem. In the following years I worked as a technical consultant in pre-sales situations, technical substantive interlocutor of existing customers. I gave substantive guidance to the relevant construction teams, advised the customers asked and unasked, and generated this much new revenue. Besides for the above customers I worked for, among others, Connexxion, ABN-AMRO, Manpower, Daf Trucks, Aegon, Unive, TLS, Essent energy, UMC Utrecht, Transavia and Vodafone.For these customers, we implemented a wide range of technical components, such as content management systems, search solutions, E-commercepaketten and Portals enterprice. As technical consultant I advised customers frequently used in selecting these solutions and I maintained a wide network with the respective suppliers.

Technical Analyst

PGGM

Nonprofit; 501-1000 employees; Financial Services industry

August 1999 – March 2001 (1 year 8 months) Zeist

I joined a huge team within PGGM (200 +) that a built a Cool: GEN client server application. I maintained and documented their middleware layer based on TIBCO.
When PGGM started their E-Business competence centre I was it's technical team lead. Besides the deployment of SDL Tridion for the PGGM website my team built a basic CRM and a dozen smaller applications, focused on taking complex data from their mainframes and making the data usefull on the web or on intranet.

Software engineer

Vrij Uit

September 1989 – August 1999 (10 years) Badhoevedorp, Hoofddorp

Vrij Uit was a more or less independent tour operator. As a COBOL programmer I maintained their internal reservation systems and complex reporting systems. Then I developed a reservation system for travel agencies based on remote viditel terminals. This wildly popular application processed more than 50% of the bookings after being live for a couple of months.
After the public introduction of the world wide web I developed the first websites of Vrij Uit and their first online booking site (global city) in 1996. In the following years in migrated the reservation system for travel agents to internet technology, in a hybrid Java/COBOL/c++ design.
Over the years at Vrij Uit I developed into a result-driven pragmatic Builder with especially eye for usable comprehensible results. In an environment without versioning, specifications or test capacity where a high availability is required I learned when to cut corners and when not. My passion for online applications and Internet technology developed in this period.
