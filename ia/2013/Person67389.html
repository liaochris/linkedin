Jon Ascher's Summary

I have over a decade of experience in the professional graphic arts field, spanning web design, print/prepress graphic design, as well as traditional and digital illustration.

I've published a full-lenglth, full-color, painted 110 page original graphic novel, NeIL (Cackling Imp Press, 7/11). I wrote (and rewrote and rewrote...) the story, and illustrated the book. So far the press response has been terrific, including some outstanding critical reviews. The book is available at cacklingimppress.com as well as barnesandnoble.com, thus far, and soon to be in digital format for e-reader devices.

In addition to (always) looking for contract illustration and design work, I'm seeking to have NeIL considered for a motion picture, either by way of a rep or through a direct contact.

NeIL is a fully painted, full color graphic novel. 110 pages, for mature readers.
Meet Neil, an ordinary punk, who hits more than a few bumps in the road. Drugs do funny things, and some funny things do happen. But as Neil feels increasingly taunted by his life and environment, his spiraling paranoia begs the question: Is Neil going mad? Or is someone, or some thing, out to get him?This modern urban seemingly-slice-of-life tale sneaks up to play more like an episode of the Twilight Zone. It is a drama you feel guilty for belly-laughing at, and it is a comedy that gets you a little choked up.

Jon Ascher's Publications

  • NeIL

    • Cackling Imp Press
    • July 20, 2011
    Authors: Jon Ascher

    The first graphic novel written and illustrated by Jon Ascher. 110 pages, fully painted, for mature readers.

    Neil, our anti-hero, sifts through the maelstrom of his troubled life, looking for meaning in this modern-day fable, a black “dramedy.” His life is full of high jinks, love and loss. Mostly loss. As Neil feels increasingly taunted by his own life and surrounding environment, is he going mad? Or is someone, or some thing, out to get him? As his world starts to unravel, Neil unwittingly finds himself in the drivers’ seat of his own storybook, and he must choose how to end it!

  • Tales of Hot Rod Horror Vol. 1

    • Cackling Imp Press
    • 2006
    Authors: Jon Ascher, , Rick Geary, Christopher Higginson, Attaboy, David Quinn, Adam Gallardo, (others)

Volume 1 features 14 original stories that have their own unique take on a supernatural Hot Rod theme. The book features a variety of funny, scary and fantastic art. If you dig Twilight Zone, Tales from the Crypt, Ed “Daddy” Roth or just cool things in general, then you will dig this book.
