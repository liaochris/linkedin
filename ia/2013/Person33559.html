Jane Schaffer Writing Program®

Public Company; Myself Only; Professional Training & Coaching industry

August 2007 – Present (6 years 5 months) Regional, National, International

The Jane Schaffer Writing Program® is a system for students and teachers. The program includes an initial structured, organized, and logical method for struggling and beginning writers K-12. That same method transforms into sophisticated weaving and syntactical techniques as students evolve in their writing ability. Leading workshops include Expository, Argumentation, Response to Literature, Narrative, and Style Analysis. Novel and Drama guides are available for online purchase. For teachers, live workshops and webinars are available in 1-, 2-, and 3-day formats that align with state and common core standards. The program is currently implemented in districts across the country and in many foreign countries.

The Center For Educational ReVision™ (CerV™)

Nonprofit; 1-10 employees; E-Learning industry

May 2011 – Present (2 years 8 months) Dallas, Texas

The Center for Educational ReVision™ increases student achievement through innovative teacher mentor programs, instructional technology integration, professional development, and advanced academic readiness preparation.

To “ReVision” the American educational industry educators must face the demands of globalization and educational settings where students learn core disciplines from an interdisciplinary approach; according to their individual cognitive development; and across their own and others’ cultural and social mores. However, the new generation of students are also digitally minded and technologically adept, as they should be in our ever-progressive society. Teachers must receive the tools and, more importantly, the instruction to engage those learning styles effectively. Thus, innovation in teaching methods, as well as leading edge technology integration are the principal elements required to effectively teach today’s student to become a productive citizen.

CerV™ works closely with foundations, educational institutions, and individual master educators to produce new pedagogical styles and approaches to learning to contribute to a new global education system. Our divisions include Star Quality Resources, Confer® Mentoring Program, and EdTech. Scroll down to learn more about these specialized programs.

Deborah E. Louis, PhD's Projects

  • Star Quality Resources

    • August 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Deborah E. Louis, PhD, Kevin Breeding, Amy Cote, Denise Pierce, Brenda Bradford

    Star Quality Resources™ is online, on-demand professional development for teachers.

    The program consists of 12 workshops, each covering one of the tested subjects for the Texas End-of-Course Exams. For each course in a given subject, there are 24 instructional videos, which include not only content information aligned with the EOC reporting categories and TEKS, but also proven strategies that teachers may use with their students. Every reporting category in the series includes a video reviewing sample multiple-choice problems. Within a “click” are ancillary materials that include Teacher Notes, a Student Galaxy of “Think Sheets,” and Multiple-Choice exercises that emulate the actual exams. Texas Master teachers, with proven and recognized success, author and present these workshops in an interactive multimedia/video format.

  • Confer Mentoring

    • February 2007 to Present
    Team Members: Deborah E. Louis, PhD

    Confer® Mentoring begins with the understanding that most secondary teachers impact 150-200 children each day. Through funding by districts and foundations, CerV™ provides positive and productive support to these teachers through caring, compassionate, and highly qualified mentors. These mentors first listen closely to the everyday situations and challenges teachers face and the goals to which they aspire.

    Our mentors work with teachers to revise syllabi, design lessons, provide proven resources, discuss and practice pacing, address classroom management and organization, model lessons and interaction with students, suggest scoring techniques, collaborate in rubric, and model student conferencing. The result for teachers is an increase in teacher confidence and professionalism; for our students, success and college readiness.

  • EdTech

    • February 2007 to Present
    Team Members: Deborah E. Louis, PhD, Denise Pierce

    CerV™ is committed to identifying and placing the most effective technology solutions coupled with specific professional development and mentoring for teachers. This dual approach has been shown to result in significant increases in achievement in classrooms.

    As schools rush to put a computer, tablet, or smart phone in every student’s hand, virtually all fail to recognize the instructional needs that allow technology to be fully realized. By pairing technology with leading edge professional development and essential integration of technology in the curriculum, students and teachers learn how to use the tools, understand the analytics, research, and deductive reasoning skills that make the technology a true difference maker in the classroom.
