Jerry Pico's Summary

Jerry Pico is the Head of Performance Management and a senior member of the Global Talent Management team for the Daniel Swarovski Corporation. He is developing and implementing world-class learning and talent management solutions for the world’s leading designer, manufacturer and distributor of fine cut crystal, Daniel Swarovski Corporation AG.

Prior to coming to Swarovski Jerry worked as a Talent Management, Learning and Organization Development Consultant for 20 years, and gained international experience with industry leading companies in the technology, financial services, premium retail (FMCG) and manufacturing sectors: Google, Wells Fargo and W.L. Gore. Highly effectively in complex, global and matrixed organizations.

Expert in applying best practices in performance management, learning, leadership and organization development to create effective and scalable talent management programs that engage, enable and mobilize diverse employee groups. Skilled at coaching and facilitation for leaders at all levels including senior executives.

Through his consulting practice, Jerry has worked with executives, managers and their teams in Fortune 100 and Inc. 100 companies, non-profit organizations, entrepreneurs and start-up ventures in the technology, life sciences and financial services industries. His clients include Google, Wells Fargo, Ernst & Young, Charles Schwab & Company, Korn/Ferry International, Genomic Health, Inc. and Stanford University.

Specialties: Learning, Leadership Development, Talent Management, Performance Management, Succession Planning, Career Navigation, Organization Development, Diversity, Facilitation, Team Effectiveness, Talent Acquisition, Competency Modeling, Organizational Alignment, Coaching, Emotional Intelligence, Career Development, Creativity

Jerry Pico's Projects

  • Swarovski Performance Dialogue Enablement Project

    • January 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Jerry Pico

    This engaging and interactive learning experience consists of approximately 60 minutes of professionally produced video content broken up into 6 modules. The ILT sessions are four hours long (but can be broken up into smaller sessions for more flexible use), after each video module the moderators facilitate an interactive session with the participants to practice and integrate the techniques demonstrated in the videos. The online platform also makes web based ILT possible.

    The videos have been subtitled in 13 different languages and contain the following content:
    • Understanding the Full Performance Cycle
    • Preparing for the Performance Dialogue
    • Effectively Giving and Receiving Feedback (Feedback is a Gift :)
    • Setting S.M.A.R.T. Objectives
    • Partnering to Create a Development Plan
    • Keeping Performance Management Alive Throughout the Year

    Each of the modules contained mixed modalities to keep the content moving and engaging:
    − Interviews with a broad cross-section of Swarovski employees from first-time Line Managers to
    Senior Executives across various functions and business units
    − Role-plays with Swarovski Employees and Managers acting in improvisational scenarios of
    typical situations that arise in conversations between Managers and Employees, with an
    emphasis on effectively handling difficult situations
    − Best practices and advice from a professional Performance Coach and Consultant
    − Interesting animated graphical illustrations of technical information

    Though just launched the project is being extremely well received and hailed as a huge success in the organization. In particular, demonstrating that Human Resources can deliver high quality, high touch projects that enable its clients in the business units to function more effectively, and thereby positively influence business results by more effectively engaging and managing talent.
