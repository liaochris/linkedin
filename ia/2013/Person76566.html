Konrad Rymczak's Publications

  • Improved (Non)fixed TSS method for promoter recognition

    • Proceedings of the International Multiconference on Computer Science and Information Technology
    • October 12, 2009
    Authors: Konrad Rymczak, Olgierd Unold

    The key concept of molecular biology, called the central
    dogma, is that DNA is transcribed into mRNA which is
    then translated to make protein molecules [19]. The control
    regions in DNA, at which RNA polymerase binds to initiate
    transcription, are called promoters. Promoter prediction (PP)
    is an important and complex problem. Although a great deal
    of research has been undertaken in the area of PP, prediction
    techniques are still not fully developed. The PP research uses
    hidden markov models [10], artiﬁcial neural networks [16],
    grammatical inference methods [14], [17], weight matrix [4].
    In this paper, we address the problem of predicting the
    location of promoters and their transcription start sites (TSSs)
    in prokaryotic genome of Escherichia coli (E.coli). Our approaches to TSS prediction are based upon Fixed and Non
    Fixed TSS methods (FTSS and NTSS) [5]. FTSS and NTSS
    methods apply scoring matrix containing the frequencies of A,
    G, C ad T at each position in DNA sequences.
