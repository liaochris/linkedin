Colin Carmichael's Experience

Communications Manager

Waterloo Wellington CCAC

Government Agency; 201-500 employees; Hospital & Health Care industry

June 2012 – December 2013 (1 year 7 months) Waterloo, Ontario

In a strategic role reporting to the Senior Director, Partnerships & Performance, I am responsible for all formal communications activities for the organization. I work closely with the CEO and senior team on issues management and am the primary contact for all media relations. I also work collaboratively with my colleagues at other CCACs and the Ontario Association of CCACs on communications issues with a provincial scope.

I am a trusted advisor on matters of communications to various project and departmental teams within the CCAC as well as inter-agency teams. My role is primarily consultative: offering advice and strategic support to project-specific communications activities. I also lead the implementation of the Digital Integration Strategy that seeks to expand and strengthen the organization's digital information resources with a focus on internal communications. Additionally, the internal and external newsletters are in my portfolio and I have been instrumental in streamlining the process and content of these publications to ensure appropriate timing and strategic alignment.

I lead the community outreach program: making presentations to community and support groups throughout Waterloo Wellington on the role and function of the CCAC and how to access our services. I manage a team of four direct reports who perform a variety of administrative duties including graphic design, managing printed materials inventory and distribution, and front desk/reception duties.

Director, Communications

The Presbyterian Church in Canada

October 2008 – May 2012 (3 years 8 months) Toronto, Ontario

I was the executive responsible for all communications activities of The Presbyterian Church in Canada including digital, print and social media programs.

Specifically, I was responsible for media relations for the national church and, by request, for regional agencies and congregations. I managed the denomination's main corporate website in addition to a network of over one hundred national, regional and local websites. I also established and maintained the PCC’s presence on various social media sites such as Facebook, Twitter, Youtube, and Flickr.

In addition to supervising graphic design and print production for national agencies, departments and committees, I coordinated several video production projects.

As the national executive for communications, I led workshops and seminars on communications and digital media best practices and spoke at conferences and other events about the evolution of communications technologies in the twentieth and twenty-first centuries.

Education & Training: Due to my facility with both the technical underpinnings and the strategy/business implications of the social web, I was tasked with bringing the North American Communications team at Ford Motor Company up to speed on impact of social media on their work. Through a series of webinars, that I developed, I gave helped Ford’s communications team develop the basic social media understanding they needed to move forward.
