Jason Anson's Summary

I'm a Sport Technologist living with Asperger Syndrome who is passionate about technology and how it can help all those involved in an athlete’s training. I have co-founded the Virtual Alliance for Sport Technology (V.A.S.T.) to provide a framework for the generation, sharing and application of knowledge about how technology can enhance sporting performance, and I am currently using this to help First Nation pullers in Canada to pursue their Olympic dreams. These projects complement educational activities in which I contribute articles and e-books and present workshops on current and emerging trends in coaching, sport science and technology.

Jason Anson's Experience

Sport Technologist

The Virtual Alliance for Sport Technology

June 2013 – Present (7 months) Online

The Virtual Alliance for Sport Technology (V.A.S.T.) is a community of people who use technology – of whatever kind – in sport. Its aim is to facilitate the generation and sharing of knowledge. To do this it will use online tools to connect people from around the world, allowing them to interact freely and productively.

The intention is that V.A.S.T. will generate educational resources, promote the use of technology in sport, and stimulate positive developments through sport in communities everywhere. V.A.S.T. provides a platform for doing this, but the way it evolves will depend on its members and their ideas and efforts.

Jason Anson's Projects

  • Cycling Training Software: Scientific Research and Experimental Development Project

    • August 2004 to December 2006
    Team Members: Jason Anson

    To develop training methods and devices for competitive cyclists based on improved data collection and tracking systems, incorporation of improved performance monitoring devices and integration of scientific training tools for endurance sports.

  • Pullers Canada

    • 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Jason Anson, Sandy Willmott, Adam Evans, Tamu Miles

    Pullers Canada is the first Virtual Alliance for Sport Technology (V.A.S.T.) cultural distance education program in development. We seek to provide First Nation Skippers and Pullers with world-class support and training to prepare for the annual Tribal Canoe Journeys, the upcoming North American Pullers Championships and the Olympic summer games.

    The program demonstrates the potential for the sport technology community to come together, using widely-available internet tools, to support positive change in communities anywhere in the world. At the heart of the approach is the V.A.S.T. Online Learning Circles that have a goal to build, share and express knowledge through a process of open dialogue and deep reflection around issues or problems with a focus on a shared outcome.
