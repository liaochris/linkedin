Jay Whitney's Experience

Chief Software Architect, Site Products

The Nielsen Company

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; Information Services industry

July 2007 – December 2009 (2 years 6 months) San Diego, California

Performed as Chief Architect for The Nielsen Company’s site and demographics product line. The suite consisted of a Windows desktop solution based upon Microsoft C#, .net and MapInfo; and a web-based active content delivery based upon Java, servlets, and MapInfo MapXtreme. Both shared a common Oracle 10g back-end with the Spatial Option and PL/SQL stored procedures. My approach was as a hands-on Chief Architect accountable for developing technical architecture and leading and mentoring others in the creation of the overall design and technical specifications for for the product line. Key accomplishments and elements of this role included:

* Produced, documented, maintained, and enforced software, system, and component architecture

* Directly managed the development work-product of off-shore resources to ensure delivery quality and acceptance, schedule management, compliance with Nielsen standards and proper documentation

* Determined the major software and implementation patterns and mechanisms

* Enforced the integrity of the software architecture by collaborating with and mentoring development staff

* Recommended and reviewed specific application integration techniques and available technologies (TIBCO BusinessWorks, IBM SOA Foundation, and Software AG webMethods). The mission was to integrate the site and demographic solutions with products throughout Nielsen and our business partners

* Led proof-of-concept exercises with sister companies

* Validated the technology's ability to meet business, performance, integration, and scalability requirements for all site platforms

* Personally and directly established low-level/kernel Oracle and Linux host tuning parameters delivering a 3x response improvement. Without this, acceptance would have failed.

* Spearheaded the integration of new systems and applications and the retirement of legacy systems

* Was an effective coach, building the design and development skills of my teammates

Technology contributions evolved from ad-hoc design to formal methods and associated documentation.

Selected Technology Accomplishments

This integrated product line is a framework-based suite of products that include network management and discovery; operating system and application problem identification, notification, and correction; asset management; inventory and configuration; and hardware console management.

* Designed a TCP/IP-based, client/server, cross-platform, object-oriented management protocol. Registered a well-known port (144) with the Internet Assigned Numbers Authority using Internet internals understanding and technology justification.
