Mike McCandless' Publications

  • The Divine Storyteller

    • Touchwood Press
    • May 2013
    Authors: Mike McCandless, William McCandless

    For fans worldwide of author Nevil Shute, The Divine Storyteller offers a new viewpoint on the novelist's life during the years 1944 to 1955 and the ideas that shaped his widely popular books. Written as a series of imagined, present-tense conversations between Nevil Shute Norway and his friends and associates, The Divine Storyteller puts you in the same room, yacht, automobile, or airplane as the author and his companions. Shute's approaches to theme, plot and character development are portrayed against the backdrop of his active life as a war correspondent, a pilot adventurer, and an Australian immigrant after World War II. Written with the greatest admiration for Shute, The Divine Storyteller is a must for long-time devotees or quick introduction to the sources of the author's work and thought. Shute helped a generation process the changing face of English society before and after World War II and the post-war specter of the nuclear threat in his novel On the Beach. The Divine Storyteller is a fast read written with gratitude and respect for the man and his contributions to popular 20th century literature globally. It will entertain and enlighten a new generation to Shute's body of work.

  • When War Comes Home

    • The Military Ministry Press
    • October 2008
    Authors: Mike McCandless, Adsit, Adsit, and Waddell

    When War Comes Home offers comfort and practical help to the wives of combat veterans struggling with the hidden wounds of war, including Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD). Insights from the medical and counseling community are wrapped in biblical principles and the shared experiences of other military wives. More than 75,000 copies in print. Edited and published by Mike McCandless.

  • The Spiritual Fitness Handbook

    • The Military Ministry Press
    • September 2010
    Authors: Mike McCandless, Chris Adsit

    An unprecedented field guide to personal faith in Jesus Christ specifically written for today’s soldier. First edition edited and published by Mike McCandless.

  • Care & Counsel for Combat Trauma

    • The Military Ministry Press
    • June 2009
    Authors: Mike McCandless, Chris Adsit

    This training and certification program provides video courseware that helps equip professional and lay counselors for ministry to military members, veterans, and military families experiencing the effects of combat trauma.
    Co-Produced by Military Ministry and the American Association of Christian Counselors. Edited and published by Mike McCandless for Military Ministry.

  • The Combat Trauma Healing Manual

    • The Military Ministry Press
    • July 2007
    Authors: Mike McCandless, Chris Adsit

    The Combat Trauma Healing Manual from Military Ministry Press offers spiritual solutions for struggles with PTSD by helping construct an environment that will give God optimal access to the wounded soul. Designed for individual or group study, the Combat Trauma Healing Manual combines the latest insights of the medical and counseling communities with the timeless principles of God’s Word. More than 60,000 copies in print. Edited and published by Mike McCandless.

  • Prayers for Our Nation's Defenders

    • Military Ministry Press
    • 2011
    Authors: Mike McCandless, Anthony Gray

    Mike McCandless developed and published the revision of this book for Military Ministry Press.

  • Angel Tree Mentoring: Program Guide and Training Seminar

    • Prison Fellowship, Inc.
    • January 2004
    Authors: Mike McCandless, Staff of Prison Fellowship and The Institute

    Angel Tree Mentoring (ATM) enables churches to build and run an after school mentoring program for prisoners' children. The ATM Program Guide and Training Seminar guides professional and lay leaders through the process of planning and implementing an ATM program in their parish or organization. The guide was developed by a team of consultants working in conjunction with Prison Fellowship staff. Mike McCandless led the production team and was the editor of the final deliverables. Mike was the sole developer of the Training Seminar delivered to and used by prison Fellowship.

  • Personal COBOL for Windows

    • Micro Focus Publishing
    • June 1993
    Authors: Mike McCandless

    This book/software bundle brought ANSI standard COBOL and a comprehensive development and testing environment to Windows. More than 50,000 university students and other learners from eight countries bought Personal COBOL in the mid-90s. Edited and published by Mike McCandless.

  • Object Orientation: An Introduction for COBOL Programmers

    • Micro Focus Publishing
    • April 1993
    Authors: Mike McCandless, Raymond Obin

    This book documented a milestone in the history of computer programming languages. In the early 1990's, Micro Focus introduced the world's first object-oriented COBOL, the new foundation that has kept COBOL in use for development and maintenance through to today. Edited and published by Mike McCandless.

  • Source Code Management Facility

    • Micro Focus Publishing
    • January 1996
    Authors: Mike McCandless, Jeffrey Douglas

    Aimed a t beginners and advanced users, this book is a detailed technical guide to SCMF with many helpful cross-references for the busy, professional reader. Edited and published by Mike McCandless.

  • Personal COBOL 2.0 for DOS

    • Micro Focus Publishing
    • 1992
    Authors: Mike McCandless

    Personal COBOL 2.0 for DOS represented an updating and re-packaging of the original Personal COBOL, introduced in 1983 as the world's first integrated programming environment, combining a compiler, debugger, code generator for interactive forms, interpreter and run-time system in a menu-driven bundle. With Personal COBOL for DOS, a new generation of COBOL programmers were introduced to the language, still critical for maintaining millions of lines of enterprise application code across the globe. Personal COBOL 2.0 for DOS was created through a "skunk works" project led by Mike McCandless, who then founded Micro Focus Publishing to take the resultant product to market.
