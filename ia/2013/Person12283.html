Bill Beckett's Experience

President & CEO

Chrysalis Packaging & Assembly Corp., dba CHRYSPAC

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Packaging and Containers industry

August 2001 – Present (12 years 1 month)

CHRYSPAC provides customized light assembly, kitting, packaging, inspection, sorting, warehousing, distribution and fulfillment for services to manufacturers, distributors, and commercial businesses.

We employ lean manufacturing techniques to labor-intensive projects to achieve cost savings for customers, with exceptional quality, seamless service, and reliable delivery.

Our plant is located in the city of Milwaukee where we create jobs, hire local residents, bring economic and social value to our community.

Certifications:

President & CEO

Future Value Ventures, Inc.

June 1985 – December 1998 (13 years 7 months)

Was a shareholder, founder and principal organizer of Future Value Ventures, Inc., a venture capital fund which invested in expanding early-stage businesses owned by minorities and women. The fund had 22 shareholders consisting of many of Wisconsin’s premier corporations.

Invested in 12 small minority and women-owned businesses throughout the United States, such as WKKV-FM radio "V-100", Prostaff Personnel Services, Sensor Devices, and Manny's Products.

Syndicated investments to other private investors and lenders to raise over $8.7 million in capital for FVV's portfolio companies, including $2 million of FVV’s own capital, plus an additional $7 million from individuals, lending institutions and venture capital funds.

Director, Minority Business Development

Wisconsin Department of Development (Commerce)

1980 – 1984 (4 years)

Assumed position following three years with Wells Fargo Bank in San Francisco as an opportunity to apply knowledge of business and further desire to have positive community impact. The federally funded job suited business experience and program development skills acquired working for the Urban Leagues in San Francisco and Milwaukee.

With a small team experienced in business, successfully created Wisconsin's first venture captial fund for minority and women business owners. The fund was entirely capitalized, owned and controlled by 22 large Wisconsin corporations. Also initiated a no-fee business acqusition brokerage service for minority entreprenuers; identified, certified credentials and published the first list of more than 400 minority-owned Wisconsin businesses. Created "Marketplace", an annual trade fair linking minority business owners to private and public buyers.

Managed turnaround of failing program and enabled funding by the Wisconsin legislature as an on-going service of the State of Wisconsin.

Assistant Manager

Wells Fargo Bank

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; WFC; Financial Services industry

1977 – 1980 (3 years)

Was second in charge of the South San Francisco Industrial Branch of Wells Fargo Bank in California. Was responsible for commercial, real estate and consumer lending, as well as assisting in overall management of the retail Bank.
