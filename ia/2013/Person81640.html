Lisanne Lentink's Projects

  • Websites @ Wolff cinema group, MustSee and Movie Unlimited

    • 2003 to 2011
    Team Members: Lisanne Lentink, Jochem Broersen, Maaike Foks, Marcel Kieboom, Jelle Foks

    We have created multiple websites for movie theaters and cinema groups like Wolff cinema group and Movie Unlimited. These projects consisted of rapid development, strong customer minded designs and integration with back-end ticketing systems. After delivering the project, Going Virtual stayed involved in maintaining the website content. Manageble content was edited by the customer through a content management system build by Going Virtual. This system was tailored to the movie industry.

    My role in this project:
    - Front-end developer (HTML, CSS, JavaScript, JQuery)

  • 10th Anniversary Social Media Campaign

    • March 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Lisanne Lentink, Jochem Broersen, Maaike Foks, Steffie Bastiaanse

    Every anniversary it was tradition to create a movie poster with a theme. For the 10th anniversary of Going Virtual we thought it would be to be a good idea to have a contest. We challenged creative people, through social media, to create a poster for us. We posted a short briefing on Facebook and targeted our audience through Twitter, Facebook banners, Google Adwords and distributing flyers at schools. The poster of the winner was used in our marketing campaigns and got an iPad. Second place was for most likes, with an iPod as price.

    The campaign was a big success and got us a great poster design and lots of Facebook likes and twitter followers.

    My role in this project was:
    - Coordination of the campaign
    - Jurying

  • Social Media @ Kinderrechtenhuis

    • May 2011 to May 2011
    Team Members: Lisanne Lentink, Jochem Broersen, Steffie Bastiaanse

    Going Virtual has supported the Child Right House (Kinderrechtenhuis) making its first steps in Social Media. From 23 to 27 May seven young child right activists from violent countries visited the Netherlands on invitation of Kinderrechtenhuis. Going Virtual helped promoting this event by coverage through Facebook and Twitter. Some of the kids wrote blogs and we posted relevant information on Facebook.

    My role in this project was:
    - Orchestrating and managing the campaign
