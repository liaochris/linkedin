Michael Bernard's Summary

Over 17 years experience developing security sensor products and accessories from design concept through tooling and manufacturing.

Expertise includes the design and development of:

* Analog and digital circuits, including linear and SMP converters.
* Very low current circuits for battery powered products.
* PIR (Passive-Infra-Red) and microwave Doppler motion sensors.
* LWIR (Long-Wave-Infra-Red) Fresnel lens arrays and tooling.
* Overseas tooling for formed metal and molded plastic parts.
* Mixed signal custom ASICs to reduce product cost and complexity.
* Feedback control systems, AC/DC motors, and motor drives.

Also familiar with:

* Project planning and development cost analysis.
* Product approval testing for EN, UL, FCC, and CE certifications.
* Reliability validation using FMEA and MTBF.
* Assembly language coding of low cost RISC microprocessors.
* Efficient signal processing algorithms for RISC microprocessors.
* PCB CAD design and layout.

Previous research and work experience with design and construction of pulsed power systems including:

* Very low inductance 500J to 500kJ capacitor discharge circuits.
* Small electromagnetic launchers, both inductive and rail-gun designs.
* Repulsive and attractive magnetic levitation demonstrators.
* Grounding and shielding of EMI in pulsed power systems.

Specialties

Analog circuit design and construction from micro-Watt to Mega-Joule applications. Broad knowledge of material properties for mechanical and optical design. Excellent math and computer skills. Highly adaptable with a strong drive to solve complex engineering design problems.
