Kristine Simpson's Summary

Kristine Simpson is a public relations and communications professional who builds her career one new opportunity at a time. As an active social media user, Kristine has built a worldwide network connecting with professionals across the globe. She never stops learning from the bright minds of her industry, and, of course, is determined to one day become a leader in her profession.

For over a year now, she has been producing the popular weekly podcast Inside PR, a weekly podcast about public relations and social media. It is no surprise that after a year of podcast producing, Kristine was ready to switch roles. Although she still produces Inside PR, she now sits in front of the mic as host of Young PR Pros, a weekly podcast for the young and young at heart PR pros looking for tips and advice on how to build and advance their careers.

Kristine works for the Canadian Automobile Association as Manager, Public Affairs. Her role includes: stakeholder relations, media relations, writing, social media strategies and marketing, and more.

Kristine is heavily involved with the International Association of Business Communicators, Ottawa Chapter, as the Jobline Manager. For her volunteerism, she is the proud recipient of the Rick Green Award, awarded to one student every year who excels in the field of communications and show interest and dedication to volunteering. She is also the publicist for Kevin Frost, a deaf-blind speed skater from Ottawa. And she also eagerly joined the Queen’s University Ottawa Branch as the vice-president of Communications.

Specialties

public relations, social media, media relations, online community engagement, writing, podcasting, event planning and management

Canadian speed skater Kevin Frost has Olympic ambitions and there are only two things in his way -- Usher Syndrome and the International Paralympic Committee's failure to recognize deaf-blind speed skating as a sport.

Usher Syndrome is a rare genetic disorder that causes progressive hearing and vision loss (there is more than one type of Usher Syndrome; for more information, please visit the links page). In Kevin's case, he has tunnel vision (only 8% of normal visual range) and a sound has to register about 90 decibels for him to hear it. But being deaf-blind is not stopping him.

As Kevin's publicist, I put together media packages to send off to various media outlets. Our goal is to raise awareness and one day have speed skating recognized in the Paralympics.
