Matthew Steger's Summary

WIN Home Inspection provides a detailed and comprehensive inspection for our clients. Our inspection reports are a computer generated narrative format that are very easy to read and understand. The report also includes digital photos of areas of concern. While some inspectors provide only a vague checklist report of little value, the WIN Home Inspection report provides the necessary detailed information that our clients need when making an informed home buying decision. The report is emailed shortly after the inspection to the client & their Realtor and is also accessible via our website.

The onsite home inspection typically lasts 2.5~3 hours. Afterwards, our client has a clear understanding of the home's true condition as well as preventive maintenance recommendations to help preserve their new investment & lower energy and repair costs. We encourage my clients to attend the inspection. We want to ensure that the client has received a thorough home inspection, received true value for their inspection fee, and have the required information they need to make a home buying decision.

WIN Home Inspection provides a FREE 90 Day Limited Home Warranty & a Free Appliance RecallChek with our home buyer inspections. WIN Home Inspection also offers Termite/WDI Inspections (PA Pesticide Applicator licensed), Radon Testing (PA DEP licensed for continuous radon monitors), Water Quality Testing, and Well Flow Analysis.

WIN Home Inspection also now offers Infrared (IR) Thermography to help find hidden roof & plumbing leaks, missing insulation, heating/cooling system issues, electrical issues, water infiltration, and more. Matthew Steger, owner/inspector, is a Certified Level 1 Infrared (IR) Thermographer.

As an ASHI Certified Inspector, we follow the American Society of Home Inspectors (ASHI) standards of practice, the nationally recognized GOLD standard. ASHI is the only national home inspector association with an accredited 3rd party certification program.

Instructor

The Real Estate School of LCAR

2009 – Present (4 years) Lancaster, PA

I have created and taught "Home Inspection 101" and "Radon 101" courses to LCAR (Lancaster County Association of Realtors) members for their continuing education.

The "Home Inspection 101" course is a detailed course to cover the entire home inspection process covering roofing, electrical, plumbing, attic, insulation, foundation, etc. It reviews what a home inspector inspects, how they inspect each system, as well as commonly found deficiencies and safety concerns. PA Act 114 and the ASHI/NAHI Standards of Practice are also covered. Wood Destroying Insects (WDI), Radon, etc. are also discussed.

The "Radon 101" course is a detailed course that covers radon nuclear theory, the potential health hazards related to radon exposure, how radon enters a home, radon remediation techniques, as well as PA DEP requirements for those performing radon testing or remediation.

The attendees of these classes leave with a detailed and comprehensive understanding of the home inspection process and radon to help better educate their home buyers and sellers.

  • Radon - Hazard or Hype

    • LCAR Closing Comments
    Authors: Matthew Steger

    According to the US EPA, south central PA has the highest radon concentrations in homes. Radon is known to be the 2nd leading cause of lung cancer after smoking (leading cause for non-smokers!). Most people are misinformed about what radon is, why it is a hazard, and where/when radon testing should be done. In PA, all professionals who offer Radon testing or mitigation must be properly trained and licensed by the PA DEP. This article details the many misconceptions of radon as well as how a proper radon test is performed as well as the PA DEP Closed House Requirements that must be in place in order for a radon test to be performed and validated.
