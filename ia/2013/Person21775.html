Chad Massaker's Summary

While pursuing a degree in psychology at Kennesaw State University, I got caught up in the information technology bubble and skipped my plans to pursue a terminal degree in psychology. When I graduated, I went to work for a New York-based hospitality management company called Sentry Hospitality, that specialized in managing 4-star+ conference centers and resorts. My first mission was to prepare them for Y2K compliance for the one property that they managed in Georgia. Four years later, I had designed, installed and managed the networks of several other large resorts and conference centers across the country.

Then 9-11 happened and the hospitality market tanked and I found myself without a job. I essentially was forced to resign and become a vendor to Sentry which was how Carceron was born. I partnered up with the man who had gotten me into computers in college, Jeffrey Lee, and we never looked back.

Now, well over 11 years later, we serve clients all over the country, primarily in the professional services industries such as healthcare, financial services, legal services, etc. We specialize in helping businesses maximize productivity and collaboration with their IT investments. We accomplish this by providing a suite of various managed IT services, including:

► Outsourced IT Department / Helpdesk Services
► Outsourced CTO Services
► Cloud Services (SaaS & IaaS)
► Telecom / VoIP Services

Director, Corporate Connections

Referrals4Life

2008 – 2011 (3 years) Marietta, GA

I served as a director for a pilot program from BNI (Business Networking International) called Corporate Connections, a closed contact business development group that focuses on generating referrals for its members. Corporate Connections differentiats itself from traditional BNI chapters in that it has a higher level of membership criteria, which is the following:

* Own or Sell for a Business That Only Sells to Other Businesses (No MLM or B2C Companies)
* Sell to Clients that generate $5,000,000 or more per year in annual revenue.

My role, as a director of this program was to create new chapters and mentor existing ones. I also innovated many of the new features of the program, especially the Power Team concept. Power Teams, a concept started in traditional BNI, are comprised of a subset of chapter members that all cross refer to one another based on industry vertical or business process. For example, you might have a marketing power team comprised of a web designer and printer, or, you might have a Power Team focused on office moves whose members would include a commercial real estate agent and a data cabling company.

Government Agency; 10,001+ employees; Military industry

I was named Top Gun in my AIT class. Because of this I was on the Fire Direction Control (FDC) squad of every mortar platoon I ever served on (Korea and Ft. Strewart, GA). My job was to take fire requests from forward observers and run those requests through a ballistics computer which would convert the request information (coordinates, win speed, spread of fire, types of rounds, etc.) into numbers that the firing line needed to aim their mortars. Being on FDC, we were also in charge for communications for the platoon and maintained several radios in our M-577.
