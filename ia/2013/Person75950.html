Scott Kinnaird's Experience

Executive Chairman

a la mode

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Computer Software industry

2001 – Present (12 years)

a la mode is a pioneer in workflow technology. We've been solving digital supply chain and data gathering problems in one of the broadest sectors of the financial services industry for over a quarter century. We’ve been deploying forms on handheld devices, en masse, and routing form-data via the Internet since the mid 1990s.

Our products are used by hundreds of thousands of real estate appraisers, agents, inspectors, and mortgage lenders to complete the nation’s real estate transactions.

Our state-of-the-art offices are located in Naples, Florida, Salt Lake City, Oklahoma City, and Washington, DC.

Chief Operating Officer

Advanced Academics

Public Company; 201-500 employees; DV; E-Learning industry

2000 – 2001 (1 year) Oklahoma City, Oklahoma Area

My business partner, Brad Eaton and I were introduced to our co-founders of Advanced Academics by the partners at Chisholm Private Capital. Ultimately, I chose this project over others Chisholm offered us because I had young children in private school at the time and was just becoming fully aware of the gaps in our secondary education system. I was confident Advanced Academics could leverage readily available technologies to fill many of those gaps.

Brad and I joined Advanced Academics as CTO and COO respectively when the company was little more than a concept. I negotiated the company's first office lease, designed our first sales CRM and introduced the partners to our first advertising agency. Brad built our first website, purchased our first servers and hired the company's first developers.

We were co-founders in the most literal sense of the word, but joined the team with the understanding that it was a short-term assignment on behalf of Chisholm. We helped Chisholm get the company launched then moved on to our next project.

Today, Advanced Academics continues to be a thriving and innovative online learning company that provides accredited curricula for the secondary education market. We were extremely early to market with innovative concepts and the company continues to be successful providing thousands of kids with flexible access to quality education.

Business Unit President

Condor Technology Solutions

1998 – 2000 (2 years) Oklahoma City, Oklahoma Area

Condor Technology Solutions was a publicly traded IT software and services company. In 1997, Ex-EDS and Booz Allen execs were consolidating technology companies from around the United States and pitching financial underwriters, hoping to take their consolidated company public.

Software engineering talent was in short supply at the time and the underwriters perceived Condor's lack of IT recruiting capacity as a vulnerability. During the late stages of their road show a Condor founder approached me about selling my staff augmentation company and participating in their roll-up IPO.

I joined the Condor team and also brokered a merger of my company, Kinnaird Technical Resources with another IT services company which closed when Condor went public in February of 1998.

Selling my company and participating in an IPO was a great learning experience. As a Business Unit President for Condor, I enjoyed building and leading Condor's National Recruiting Center and was a member of Condor's Executive Leadership Team throughout the duration of my post-IPO contract.

Managing Partner

Kinnaird Technical Resources

1995 – 1998 (3 years) Oklahoma City, Oklahoma Area

KTR was a company I started to provide IT staff augmentation services.

I focused on niche assignments as well as large "in-sourced" projects using creative and flexible search and acquisition processes.

I started KTR from nothing more than an idea and built it into something people wanted to buy, which had been a professional objective of mine for a long time.
