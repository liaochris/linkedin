Melinda Rae's Summary

Focusing on self-value, Melinda has positioned herself as a highly sought after speaker, coach, author, and empowerment leader. She has been led down a divinely orchestrated path to not only help youth, but inspire and encourage their mothers as well, creating a Revolution for all mothers and daughters who are excited to make changes in their world.

Melinda lives in Southern California with her three daughters. In between motherhood, teenagers and running a foundation for girls, she writes informative and helpful books incorporating strategies, life experience and positive coping tools to guide women, parents, and youth for everyday life.

Her first book, Empower Your Girl, is helping women, parents and girls reflect on themselves, become aware, and provide them practical life-skills to help navigate their past and move forward.

Melinda is an active leader in her community. She has served in various capacities of school parent associations, the County of Los Angeles Child Care Planning Committee, CSUN Northridge Crisis Hot Line, Rotary International, and has been of assistance to several non-profit organizations. She acts as a facilitator for the TO Youth Commission Implementation team. This year her team was established with the initiative of “Empowerment.” GIP Clubs have been founded at Sycamore Middle School.

Although Melinda was raised in a middle class home and well provided for with physical necessities, Melinda’s childhood was devoid of love, support and guidance. She often had to advocate for herself which at times lead her to tread into dangerous waters. A deep desire was born in her through these hard life experiences: a desire to help, intervene and advocate for the disadvantaged and at-risk youth. After a long journey of self-discovery, Melinda contemplated during the summer of 2010 her next step and developed GIP for preteen and teen girls.

Melinda graduated in October 2012 with a BA in Public Relations and Marketing.

Founder

Girls in Power

July 2010 – Present (3 years 6 months)

Girls in Power ("GIP") is a developing nonprofit organization that is dedicated to providing innovative hands-on programs to develop young girls (ages 9-12 and 13-16), so that they can best realize their full potential in life.
A significant development void exists in today's education, home/parenting, and community systems. Most schools focus chiefly on academics and high-level interpersonal development. Furthermore, many parents increasingly believe that they do not have the tools, time, or temperament to provide their children with the edge in life. Negative media, substance abuse pressure, and other negative environmental influences are not helping the situation either.
As a result, a growing number of high potential young girls are quietly slipping into the "at-risk" category without notice as they develop into young adults. An increasing number of teenage girls suffer from symptoms of low self-esteem, lack of direction and motivation, peer pressure, negative habits and thoughts, addictions, and abuse. Contrary to popular public perception, all of youth are potentially at-risk of developing these symptoms of our times, regardless of socioeconomic background.
GIP's solution to this problem is delivering innovative education and support programs that fill the development void, and act as a glue for other community programs. GIP is building thoughtful purpose-driven girls and providing the missing roadmap for success throughout their lives.
