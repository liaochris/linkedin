John C. Ronquillo's Summary

John C. Ronquillo is an assistant professor at the DePaul University School of Public Service where he is director of the Master of Public Administration program. His research focuses on new legal forms of social enterprise and innovation in nonprofit and public organizations and its impact on performance. He has manuscripts published or forthcoming in Public Administration Review, Handbook of Decision Making (John Wiley & Sons), Leadership in Nonprofit Organizations (Sage), and Human Resource Management in the Nonprofit Sector: Purpose and Passion (Edward Elgar).

He has lectured or presented his research in Canada, Cuba, Ireland, Italy, and the United Kingdom in addition to annual conferences of the Academy of Management (AOM), the Public Management Research Association (PMRA), the Association for Research on Nonprofit Organizations and Voluntary Action (ARNOVA), the International Society for Third Sector Research (ISTR), and the American Society for Public Administration (ASPA) among many others. In 2009 he received the ARNOVA Emerging Scholar Award and in 2010 was an ARNOVA Dissertation Fellow. He was also a Summer PhD Fellow at the RGK Center for Philanthropy and Community Service at the University of Texas at Austin in 2009.

He previously worked in various capacities in policy analysis and applied research, as well as social impact assessment, government relations and community outreach.

Ronquillo holds a PhD in public administration and policy from the University of Georgia School of Public and International Affairs, an MPA from the Arizona State University School of Public Affairs, and a BA cum laude in journalism and mass communication from the Walter Cronkite School of Journalism and Mass Communication, also at ASU.
