Matt Bukaty's Summary

Matthew John Bukaty is an American composer of music for film, television and multimedia. His emotionally-charged music has accompanied a number of documentary and narrative films that have screened at festivals worldwide.

He received his Bachelor's degree in music composition from the University of North Texas in 2009, studying under composers David Bithell and Cindy McTee. He went on to receive his Master's degree in film and multimedia scoring from New York University, where he studied under film composer Mark Suozzo and renowned film orchestrator Sonny Kompanek.

Matt is also part of THE HUNGER GAMES MUSIC PROJECT, a collaboration with friend and fellow composer Edward Underhill. They have completed two of three planned albums inspired by the novels of Suzanne Collins, the music of which exhibits hybrid scoring techniques, fusing synthetic and sampled instruments with live musicians. The project has received international attention and is swiftly developing a worldwide fan base. You can listen to the music and read about the project online at: http://hungergamesmusic.weebly.com/.

The music is available for purchase on iTunes, Amazon MP3 and on our Bandcamp site (search "The H.G. Music Project"). It is also available to stream on Spotify.

Recent projects include:

"Kicking All Odds" (documentary) — dir. Hanny Lee (TBA)
"Miasma" (2D side-scrolling platform video game) — SMU Guildhall
"The Fault In Our Stars" (short) — dir. John Lyde, Mainstay Productions (2012)
"Vermilion Cliffs" (documentary) — dir. Hanny Lee (2012)
"Into The Fire" (short) — dir. Bettis Morelock (2012)

"The Hunger Games Music Project" — co-composer with Edward Underhill
