Belmer Negrillo's Summary

Belmer’s career as an explorer and innovator began growing up with his aspirations to be a mad scientist -- experimenting and mixing things to see what would happen. That curiosity and will to explore is still a passion today, and he loves to embrace the unknown, but not before evaluating the risks.

Belmer Negrillo is a Principal Interaction Designer at IDEO San Francisco. He has more than a dozen years of experience creating and producing multimedia experiences. Belmer integrates systemic thinking, which is required to see the big picture and future scenarios, with the sensibility and care needed to master details.

Having worked in design consulting (IDEO), in digital advertising agencies (Ogilvy Interactive and DM9DDB), in rapid and iterative development (startup VUVOX), and in a complex multibillion-dollar ecosystem with multiple stakeholder management and interdependencies (eBay), Belmer has built a holistic understanding of product creation and life-cycle management.

His work has received awards from Cannes Lions International Festival of Creativity, The One Show, and the Danish Index:2005. His creations have appeared in the Italian Fabrica Mail Me project, the German book Dos Logos and in the Japanese magazine Web Designing. Since 2008, Belmer has applied for eight patents.

Belmer holds a master’s in interaction design from the Interaction Design Institute Ivrea, Italy, and a bachelor’s in social communication from Escola Superior de Propaganda e Marketing, Brazil.

These days he also enjoys a different kind of mixing, using Isadora software to create visual experiences for music festivals and DJs such as Peter Kruder & Dorfmeister and DJ Koala. As a hobby, Belmer likes gardening and collecting succulents.
