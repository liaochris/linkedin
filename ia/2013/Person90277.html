Michael Boezi's Summary

Good content connects. It empowers, educates, entertains. It inspires, instructs, impels.

But just producing it and perfecting it are not enough. Even the best content will be robbed of the opportunity to do these things if it's not delivered to the right person(s), at the right time, in the right way. You need a strategy, or your good work will not perform as intended.

My career has been about creating, curating, developing, and propagating good content. As a 17-year publishing industry veteran who successfully navigated the Shift to Digital, I am here to help content creators of all types make the right choices about their content.

I write a blog about current issues and trends in the EdTech industry at http://michaelboezi.com (originally posted at http://trackedchange.tumblr.com).

I have a regular column on The EdTech Times called “The Good Content Series” at http://bit.ly/GoodContentSeries.

I was a guest contributor to Publishing Perspectives on 2/22/13 with a piece called, “Digital Textbooks: Publishers and the Unrealized Promise.” http://bit.ly/UnrealizedPromise

Creating change is important work. It is most fulfilling when it leads to obvious improvement and tangible progress. It almost always requires the help of others, and it is often built around good content.

Like you, I am more than just my job or a list of the positions I've held. I am what I contribute, and I've got a lot more work to do.

Michael Boezi's Experience

Vice President / Minister of Strategy

Libboo

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Publishing industry

July 2013 – Present (4 months) Cambridge, MA

Libboo is an early-stage startup that's seeking to help talented authors of all types have the careers they choose in the digital age. Libboo is an advocacy marketing platform that empowers authors by providing intelligence about their most engaged fans and readers. Authors get a first-of-its-kind data dashboard with real-time analytics and insight into their book and its advocates, including how, where, and why it is shared online—kind of a "Google Analytics for Authors." For the first time, authors have useable insight into their audience based on real behavioral data and can "tune" their campaigns accordingly.

I was an early advisor to the company, but joined full time at a pivotal point in its development. My focus:
• Brand management. Drive Libboo's strategic direction. Unify, clarify, and reduce the message about the mission.
• Content strategy. Establish Libboo as thought leaders. Create a movement. Inspire underserved authors to build their own brand.
• Community growth. Employ inbound marketing and growth hacking techniques to attract author partners and their reader advocates.
• Business Development. Transform the product into a scalable business. Direct the go-to-market strategy for both B2C and B2B.

Independent Advisor and Content Strategist

Self Employed

April 2013 – Present (7 months) Boston, MA

For Content Creators:
While you were writing, the rules changed. In your favor. That is, if you know how to harness the power of the New Tools.

Published authors: You can't depend on your publisher to market your work. You are your own brand. You are the only one who can find and connect with your audience.

Independent authors, musicians, and artists: You don't need a publisher, you need a strategist. Someone who knows the whole industry, from Old to New, and can help you make sure that your content connects you to your audience.

---

For Content Owners and Investors:
This is the best time to be in publishing. There are untold opportunities for you, if you choose to embrace them. 

Publishers: Is the world changing faster than you are? I can help you rebuild your publishing processes through:
• Investment Strategy – Rethink what will you spend, and how you will spend it.
• Author Acquisition – Find the right talent, and then seek to empower, not control.
• Content Development – Being more efficient does not mean sacrificing quality.
• Asset Curation – Advise on copyright allowances/issues, including images/figures.
• Product Validation – Essential to a quality product. Community focused.
• Production – Overthrow and rebuild the traditional design/make-ready process.
• Content Delivery – This is no longer uniform. What's the best platform for your content?
• Marketing Strategy – No more spray-and-pray. Connection happens around content.
• Return on Investment – Improve cost structures and margins.

Investors: What lies ahead in this sector? It's in full churn right now, which means opportunity. Exactly where, and at what pace?

Vice President, Content & Community

Flat World Knowledge

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; E-Learning industry

March 2009 – March 2013 (4 years 1 month) Boston, MA

When I joined, Flat World Knowledge was a brand new entrant in the higher education textbook industry. I was responsible for all aspects of content, from acquiring, developing, and licensing textbook content to inbound marketing and content marketing.

Editorial Director, 2009–2011
I was a part of the core group that built the company from the ground up. I was hired at the Series A round, charged with building a brand new catalog of college textbooks from scratch:
• Acquired 82 core textbooks in under 3 years, including 22 of the top 25 enrolling markets.
• Designed and implemented a book development process from scratch to publish quality content efficiently—in about half the time and at nearly a tenth of the cost of the legacy publishers.
• Produced and delivered 57 first editions in under 3 years. The catalog contains over 100 professionally developed, peer-reviewed titles now.
• Made a tangible impact on the industry. By fall semester 2012, our books were actively in use in over 4,000 classrooms, and had served 725,000+ students.

VP of Content and Community, 2011–2013
After building the foundation of a strong product catalog, I expanded my role to shape the company’s future by:
• Building out my concept of Community Around Content, cultivating invested curators to improve existing content and create new content.
• Transforming the marketing philosophy of customer acquisition from purely outbound strategies to more inbound and content marketing.
• Opening more channels for direct user feedback, and rebuilding the review process so that it was ongoing rather than episodic.
• Redesigning the platform to transfer more control of our content to our users, and atomizing our content to enable more remixing and reuse.
• Licensing our content to innovative third party distributors and course providers, and establishing a consistent "born digital" content architecture that made such deals easy and efficient.
