Dana Greenlee's Summary

I have been creating and maintaining websites since the late '90's. My focus for the past decade is new media web production. I design, maintain, site host, teach, evaluate and improve interfaces for clients websites and maintains their social media presence, primarily for professional and trade associations in Puget Sound. My work as a copywriter for websites extends back to six years writing a weekly tech column for the Tacoma Daily Index newspaper, both print and online. Additionally, I worked at Amazon.com in customer service and website editorial writing marketing copy for products in Amazon's Kitchen & Home Store. I know how to socially engage a virtual community when I built and moderated a large, active community forum and site around “Boston Legal” TV series with 5 million visitors.

I am a pioneering podcaster, having the first terrestrial broadcast radio program, Web Talk Radio, to be distributed as a podcast using an RSS feed in September, 2004. [source: Wikipedia]. I extended my new media experience by producing and co-hosting "Web Talk Radio" for seven years, hosting the "Boston Legal Podcast" for five years, producing "The Streaming Media Show" and helping podcast client "Real Estate Radio" from 2004 - 2009. I'm an advocate to bringing the excitement of the web to others by freely sharing tips and advice with clients, as well as teaching continuing education courses for several semesters at Tacoma Community College. (Courses taught: Streaming Media Production, Website Marketing and Promotions, Website Usability Techniques, Intro to Internet for Seniors, Search Engine Strategies and Intro to FrontPage.) I stay involved with the tech industry as a voting member of The International Academy of Digital Arts & Sciences, the 550-member body responsible for judging The Webby Awards.
