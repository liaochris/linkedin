Kara Angotti's Experience

Center for Green Schools Fellow

U.S. Green Building Council

Nonprofit; 201-500 employees; Nonprofit Organization Management industry

June 2013 – Present (7 months) Trenton, NJ

In my current role as Fellow for the Center for Green Schools of the U.S. Green Building Council, I am focused on leading large-scale sustainability programs and initiatives from inception to deployment. I am based at New Jersey School Boards Association (NJSBA), and over the next three years I am responsible for guiding 586 public school districts that serve nearly 1.4 million students. By the end of my fellowship, I aim to reduce operating expenses in twenty percent of New Jersey public school districts. Toward this goal I will train school leaders on best practices related to facilities, craft policy, and develop district-scale strategic sustainability plans. In collaboration with NJSBA, I am committed to a 3-year, 12-district research project which is designed to track data linked to measurable sustainability initiatives, quantify student achievement resulting from these actions, and observe school board officials and their ability to affect long-term change.

Owner

KO Angotti (Sole Proprietorship)

Sole Proprietorship; Myself Only; Design industry

May 2009 – June 2013 (4 years 2 months)

I’ve managed multiple projects where I achieved a symbiotic relationship between design aesthetics and high performance functionality. As an environmental design consultant, I presented recommendations for improved use of space, waste reduction, responsible material selection, and applications to lower operating expenses. My recommendations included projected savings, estimated costs, and ROI associated with efficiency upgrades which prove their value instead of relying on “green” marketing.

True sustainability requires vision, innovation, and commitment. My dedication to local economy and environmentally-conscious resources allowed me to deliver projects with a triple bottom line approach. As I discovered product and material manufacturers, I shared these connections on targeted social media platforms in the form of frequent video posts, regular blog entries, and detailed product information. These online outlets updated my followers and the design community on current projects, provides additional marketing to resource connections, and strengthened my position as an engaged and forward-looking industry professional. Client’s seeking LEED credits and those interested in progressive and unique applications found the benefit of working with a designer who understands the balance between function and form very rewarding.
