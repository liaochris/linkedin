Leonardo Giusti's Summary

I am a UX Design Lead at the Mobile UX Lab, Samsung Research America, in San Francisco.

From 2010 to 2012, I have been an interaction designer at the MIT - Mobile Experience Lab, in Cambridge, MA. I led human-centered research and design in a wide range of industries, transforming deep insights and opportunities into tangible design directions.

Since 2005, I have been designing interactions in challenging contexts, carefully considering social, cultural, and aesthetic qualities. My experience includes extensive research and design for health-care and wellness, financial services, urban mobility, transportation, cultural heritage, and education. I have worked with both public institutions (European Commission, Province of Trento, Chamber of Commerce of Prato) and private companies (Samsung, Alcatel-Lucent Bell-Labs, Banca Intesa San Paolo, Bradesco, Avea Labs, Marriott Hotels).

In addition to my industrial experience, I have a long-standing academic background. I have a Ph.D in Human-Computer Interaction, held lecturer positions at several universities (University of Siena, University of Trento, MIT), and served as an external examiner in the Interaction Design Master Program at Domus Academy in Milan.

 

Specialties: - Design research
- Product ideation and concept development
- Design strategy and process
- User experience design
- Interface design
- Interaction models for robotic technologies
