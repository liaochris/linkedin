Consultant

The World Bank

Nonprofit; 5001-10,000 employees; International Trade and Development industry

September 2008 – February 2011 (2 years 6 months)

Wrote policy briefs for South Asia region’s social protection program on how shared decision-making in Pakistani households effect poverty reduction.

Advisor to the Africa Health unit’s eHealth program. The program’s goal was to mainstream technology into the Bank’s health system strengthening efforts, build capacity and be a knowledge center for eHealth. By collaborating with development partners, governments and private sector technology shops, I integrated key stakeholder information to reflect the dynamics of eHealth.

Developed eHealth strategy for the Africa eHealth unit in addition supervised two papers

Led Resource Requirement Tool (RRT) analysis and implementation in Ghana and Sierra Leone. RRT enables countries to estimate and project the resources required for HRH employment and pre-service training, analyze the plan’s affordability, simulate “what-if” policy scenarios and facilitate the monitoring of the country’s proposed scale-up plan. Working closely with the Ministry of Health, Education and Finance, a report was submitted demonstrating the HRH situation in Ghana with policy recommendations.

Lead author and co-author to five chapters in the Ghana MOH/World Bank Country Status Report on Human Resources for Health.

Designed an HRH activity-based costing model to be used to cost prioritized HRH activities for the Lesotho Ministry of Health’s HRH strategy. I designed the costing model using Microsoft Excel 2007 with visual basic macros enabled
