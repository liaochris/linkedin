Heather Britt's Summary

I’m a Monitoring & Evaluation expert with 15 years of experience in the field of international development and humanitarian aid.

My services include developing performance monitoring systems, leading evaluations, training and capacity building. I specialize in practical solutions for challenging operating environments and have a variety of international clients including CARE, World Vision, Doctors Without Borders (MSF) Catholic Relief Services, and the World Health Organization.

With a focus on rigorous results through flexible approaches, I design M&E systems using a variety of research methods including focus groups, surveys, Participatory Impact Assessments, and Most Significant Change. I have experience across the following sectors: democracy and governance, livelihoods and agriculture, gender empowerment, health, and education.

As a skilled capacity builder and trainer, I work to promote intentional knowledge management among all stakeholders including field staff, upper management, and community partners. I regularly lead "hands-on" workshops teaching staff to develop logframes and M&E plans to submit to donors such as United States Agency for International Development (USAID), the World Bank, and the European Commission’s Humanitarian Office (ECHO) among others.

I have a Masters in Sociology from Boston College and am moderately fluent in Arabic.

Specialties

Skills include performance monitoring and M&E frameworks, program design, logical framework analysis, evaluations, impact assessments, training and capacity building; Proficiency in a range of applied research methods including focus groups, interviews, Most Significant Change, various participatory methods, surveys, basic statistical analysis; Sectors include agriculture, democracy and governance, education, health, and gender empowerment; Extensive experience in Middle East.
