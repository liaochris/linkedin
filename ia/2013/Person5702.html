Michelle May MD's Summary

I am a physician, recovered yoyo dieter, and the founder and CEO of the Am I Hungry?® Mindful Eating Workshops and Facilitator Training Program (http://www.amihungry.com/). Am I Hungry? is a novel wellness program that employs mindfulness-based and cognitive-behavioral strategies to identify and resolve the mindless and emotional eating habits that sabotage most attempts at sustainable lifestyle change. Am I Hungry? offers experiential mindful eating workshops and retreats, motivational keynotes, corporate and worksite wellness programs, facilitator training and licensing, books, DVDs, and online learning.

TIME.com called my book, "Eat What You Love, Love What You Eat: How to Break Your Eat-Repent-Repeat Cycle" one of the Top 10 Notable Diet Books of 2010 (which is ironic since it is a how-not-to-diet book!). It received seven awards for publishing, including "Best Book" in Health, Mind-Body-Spirit, Nutrition, and Self-Help categories. I am also the author of "Eat What You Love, Love What You Eat with Diabetes" and several other books, workbooks, DVDs, and other support materials for individuals, families, groups, and health professionals.

As a professional speaker, I deliver interactive, paradigm-shifting keynotes and workshops for corporations, communities, hospitals, associations, and organizations interested in promoting a healthier, vibrant, productive community.

I blog for Huffington Post and several other popular websites. I love working with the media to counter conventional restrictive approaches to eating and exercise including CNN Health, Discovery Health Channel, Dr. Oz, Fitness, Fox News Radio, Glamour, Health, Oprah Radio, Parents, Prevention, Self, Shape, TIME.com, USA Today, US News & World Report, WebMD, Woman’s Day, Woman's World, and many others.

Specialties: Board certified Family Physician (retired); mindful eating, intuitive eating, non-diet weight management, Health at Every Size concepts.
