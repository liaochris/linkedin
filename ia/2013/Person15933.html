Boyd Coleman, RLA, ASLA's Summary

Mr. Coleman has more than 15 years experience in landscape design and project management. Most recently, Mr. Coleman's experience has included project management and coordination for transmission line projects in New Mexico and Arizona. Mr. Coleman also specializes in land use planning, National Environmental Policy Act (NEPA) project management and documentation support services for renewable energy, transmission lines, and other energy facility projects.

Mr. Coleman is a Registered Landscape Architect (RLA) in the State of Arizona and has experience in designing public parks, master planned communities, and residential landscape design. Mr. Coleman is experienced in providing conceptual landscape designs, construction documents, and construction administration services.

Specialties: Technical writing, Sketchup, AutoCAD, Photoshop, and hand graphic skills

Landscape Architect / Environmental Planner

Environmental Planning Group

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Architecture & Planning industry

September 2007 – January 2012 (4 years 5 months)

Landscape Architecture and Environmental Planning

While at EPG Mr. Coleman worked as an Environmental Planner on an interstate renewable energy project. As Project Coordinator, Mr. Coleman's responsibilities included working directly with the firm's Project Manager; the client; federal, state, and municipal agencies. Mr. Coleman was responsible for writing multiple sections of the Environmental Impact Statement, including all sections relating to land use, military affairs, agriculture, special designations, the regional overview, and the introduction, methods, and regulatory framework section; as well as being responsible for managing the day to day operations of the land use team.

Mr. Coleman's team was responsible for mapping land use features throughout the study area. This included utilizing aerial imagery to preliminarily identify the location of land use entities within the study area, field verify the location of the identified entities within all project study corridors, and use this data to coordinate with other resources to create needed GIS maps and write the land use chapters for required environmental documents.

Mr. Coleman was also the project manager for multiple transmission line projects in eastern New Mexico.

Mr. Coleman's previous experience as a registered landscape architect with EPG includes municipal park master planning, preliminary design, and construction document preparation.

Mr. Coleman also has previous experience with master planning and construction document preparation for multiple master-planned communities.
