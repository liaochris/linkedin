John Myer's Summary

In his corporate law practice, John Myer advises on all aspects of business transactions, finance, securities, mergers and acquisitions, corporate operations, and corporate governance.

He drafts business plans and offering memoranda, negotiates angel or venture capital for start-up companies, negotiates bank or other institutional financing for established businesses and drafts contracts with companies’ key employees, customers and vendors. He also represents investment advisers who operate hedge funds and others who manage large public mutual funds.

Prior to moving to Seattle, John Myer was an associate at Sullivan & Cromwell (New York and Frankfurt), Paul Weiss (London) and White & Case (Miami).

Before his career in law, John Myer received a master’s degree in management and finance from MIT and worked as a financial analyst and management consultant with Arthur D. Little, Inc.

Specialties

Practices areas: drafting and negotiating business contracts, forming start-up companies, advising on private equity placements and forming hedge funds.
