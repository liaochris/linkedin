Mike Dion's Summary

Mike Dion recently produced the award winning documentary Ride The Divide. The film won Best Adventure Film at the Vail Film Festival, has played on over 120 theater screens across the country, and can currently be found on iTunes, Netflix, Amazon & the Documentary Channel.

Mike is currently producing the documentary films Reveal the Path and Where the Yellowstone Goes. Both set for a summer 2012 release.

Mike has been producing and directing content for over 18 years holding top level creative positions at Starz Entertainment and The Hallmark Channel.
Prior to working for broadcast networks, Mike spent many years working for independent production companies and on the feature films Far and Away, A River Runs Through It, Dante’s Peak, The Brave and Reggie’s Prayer.

Mike’s passion is creating digital motion pictures that tell inspiring stories in adventurous locations.

Mike Dion's Projects

  • Ride the Divide

    • May 2008 to Present
    Team Members: Mike Dion, Hunter Weeks, Joseph Cantwell

    Ride The Divide is a feature-length documentary film about the world's toughest bicycle race. Released in April, 2010 at the Vail Film Festival where it won the Best Adventure Film prize, Ride The Divide went on to play theatrically in over 120 cities in North America, screen at numerous film festivals, crack the Top 30 Documentaries list on iTunes and have its world TV premiere on the Documentary Channel.

  • Reveal the Path

    • February 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Mike Dion, Hunter Weeks

    Reveal the Path is a genre-defying adventure film that contemplates what it means to live an inspired life using the bicycle as a mechanism to explore, dream and discover.

  • Where the Yellowstone Goes

    • May 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Mike Dion, Hunter Weeks, Sarah E. Hall

    Where the Yellowstone Goes follows a 30-day drift boat journey down the longest “undammed” river in the lower 48. Intimate portraits of locals in both booming cities and dusty, dwindling towns along the Yellowstone River, illustrate the history and controversies surrounding this enigmatic watershed leading to questions about its future. Connect with colorful characters, get lost in the hypnotic cast of a fly rod, and experience silhouetted moments of fireside stories on this heartfelt river adventure.

  • Hair I Go Again

    • September 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Mike Dion, Steve McClure, Kyle Kruger

    What happens when two middle-aged ex-rockers try to put the band back together? Well, it ain't easy, especially when you've lost your hair, can't play guitar anymore and the wife doesn't want you to go out and play with the boys. But when you have a dream and music is in your blood, well, even if the pressure and cholesterol is high – you gotta do what you gotta do.
