Business Intelligence Manager

Berkley Mid-Atlantic Group

Public Company; 201-500 employees; ber; Insurance industry

January 2004 – Present (9 years 9 months)

I was brought in to build a data warehousing/business intelligence team and solution from scratch. Over the past 6 years, I've used the Ralph Kimball design patterns and methodologies to deliver multiple business intelligence solutions to the BMAG organization at all levels of decision making.

Our design follows a classic Kimball structure. We have an SQL Server Integration Services(SSIS) Extract Transform and Load(ETL) layer that pulls information from SQL Server, DB2, Informix, message queues, ftp sources and file folders. This data is loaded into a staging area where we clean, balance and add business logic to the data.

From there we load the data into a series of relational star schema data structures for each operational unit and product portfolio. These data structures are fed into SQL Server Analysis Services.

The relational and OLAP sources are delivered to our users through a business intelligence portal. This technology utilizes SQL Server Reporting Services objects as well as custom portlets to query the OLAP or relational source.

This data is used operationally by our Claims, Billing, Underwriting, Risk Management, Underwriting Services, HR, etc… All of this operational information aggregates up, allowing Actuarial and Finance to create their own ad hoc queries or feed predictive analytic efforts. The predictive factors are then brought back down to the operational level in the form of decision guidance.
