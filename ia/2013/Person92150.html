Michael Bungay Stanier's Summary

If you're a Time Crunched Manager, you want to have more impact & do less work. We can help with that.


Meantime...

Michael is the Senior Partner of Box of Crayons.

Box of Crayons works with organizations around the world to help them do less Good Work and more Great Work. Their clients range from American Express to Xerox and across all major market sectors.

Michael has created:
- The Coaching for Great Work training program

- *End Malaria*, a collection of essays from thought leaders about Great Work. $20 from each book sold goes to Malaria No More.

- *Do More Great Work: stop the busywork and start the work that matters* (www.DoMoreGreatWork.com)

- The internet movies *The Eight Irresistible Principles of Fun* (www.eightprinciples.com) and *The 5.75 Questions You've Been Avoiding* (www.fivebigquesitons.com)

- The award winning coaching tool, Get Unstuck & Get Going on the stuff that matters (www.getunstuckandgetgoing.com)

Before founding Box of Crayons, Michael held senior positions in the corporate, consultancy and agency worlds He has lived and worked in Australia, the UK, the US and Canada.

He is a Rhodes Scholar and holds an M Phil from Oxford, a law degree and a BA with highest honours from the Australian National University. He is a certified coach.

Specialties

Helping people and organizations do less Good Work and more Great Work.

*Coaching for Great Work* gives busy managers and leaders practical coaching skills.

*Do More Great Work* is a book for managers and leaders. As well as 12 practical "maps", it includes free mp3 interviews with over 100 leading business thinkers and practitioners.

Michael Bungay Stanier's Publications

  • Do More Great Work: Stop the Busywork and Start the Work that Matters

    • Workman
    • February 2010
    Authors: Michael Bungay Stanier

    A best-selling book with 15 "maps" - tools to help you find, start and sustain your quest to do more of the work that matters ... and less of all the other stuff.

  • End Malaria

    • The Domino Project
    • September 2011
    Authors: Michael Bungay Stanier

    Not a book about malaria at all - but a collection of 62 essays from the leading business thinkers of our time. The real magic? $20 from every book goes to Malaria No More. Reached #2 on Amazon.com on launch and has raised more than $350k so far.

  • Get Unstuck & Get Going

    • Box of Crayons Press
    • November 2009
    Authors: Michael Bungay Stanier

    A self-coaching tool, the "flip" design allows you to generate 50,000 different question combinations to provoke you and inspire you to get unstuck. A multi-award winner.

  • Presentation Genius

    • Box of Crayons Press
    • March 15, 2011
    Authors: Michael Bungay Stanier, Mark Bowden

    108 presentation tools, tips and techniques that actually work.

    Presentation Genius is designed to put an end to dull, uninspired presentations. This set of cards contains 108 tools, tips and techniques about how to run better meetings, more useful presentations and workshops and training sessions that everyone enjoys.

    LEARN HOW TO:

    Understand and prepare for your audience and where their heads (and hearts) will be.
    Manage how the room is set up and understand its impact on the group energy.
    Increase your awareness of your own state of mind (anxious, excited), and learn how to manage and channel that energy.

    The presentation skills cards are divided into five broad topics, levers to increase the impact and usefulness of your meetings, presentations or workshops.

    Planning & Design: How to prepare and structure a session for most impact.
    Visuals: How to do away with the death by Powerpoint approach and make your slides count (and how not to use slides at all).
    Space: Using the physical environment to help and not hinder your presentations or meetings.
    Facilitation: Tried and true ways to be a better facilitator to engage and manage a group.
    Self-management: How to manage your own uncertainty and do a better job at having a stronger presentation presence in front of a group.

    Each card presents an idea or teaches you a tactic that you can use right away to make your next session work in a way it never has before.

    Presentation Genius is flexible and can be customized to your needs. Some people pick three or four key cards that really resonate with them and use the insights to redesign their presentations and sessions. Others pick a single card to focus on to run a better meeting. And still others use the I Ching approach, picking a card at random as inspiration.
