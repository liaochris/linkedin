My mission was to solicit and secure seed financing for the start-up company to finance further software development and establish global licensing and distribution. During the course of my tenure as President we closed license agreements with a corporate member of a Japanese Big Four 'zaibatsu' and a South Korean software entrepreneur. My role at Hyperwave included engagement and consultations with our law firm, McCarthy Tetrault, to secure intellectual property (IP) rights and develop the licensing agreements for global distribution of the application. I organized meetings with selected US and first-tier Canadian venture capital (banks, major corporations and an investment blind trust of a former Prime Minister of Canada). After a series of roadshows and presentations to global venture capital firms I sourced seed investment from Atlas Ventures (Boston). A condition precedent to the deal was that Hyperwave was to exit Canada and reorganize in Germany closer to its Austrian R&D at TUG, its German customers and first round investors. The company was reorganized in Germany subsequent to my departure in July 1996.
