Greg Munno's Summary

I am a communications researcher and practitioner with a focus on communication that benefits the public interest.

This communication takes many forms, including journalism, facilitated dialog, social media engagement, and persuasive messaging on issues such as health, risk and the environment.

Current projects include management of ABC Cayuga, an early childhood development public health campaign funded by the Allyn Foundation; and a quantitative evaluation of Reclaim November Ohio, a citizens' jury process funded by the Jefferson Center for New Democratic Processes.

I am not currently accepting new clients.

My professional work greatly benefits from, and contributes to, my work as a communications researcher in the doctoral program at the S.I. Newhouse School of Public Communications.

At Newhouse, I study with renowned scholars, including my adviser, Dr. Pamela Shoemaker. The program offers a rigorous social-scientific education with a focus on quantitative methods. Studies completed at Newhouse include:

* an experiment that examines how existing comments affect subsequent comments on online news articles (presented at AEJMC Mid-Winter);

* a content analysis on the framing of Latin America on the Op-Ed pages of elite American newspapers (with Dr. Guy Golan, under journal review); and,

* a regression analysis of Pew data on why online news readers share and comment (under conference review).

I have also had the opportunity to teach Intermediate News Writing, Interpersonal Conflict Resolution and the skills portion of Multimedia Storytelling.

I serve as the president of the Doctoral Student Association and sit on both the Doctoral Program Review Committee and a search committee for a new Environmental Communications professor.

I expect to complete the qualifying exams for a Ph.D. in Mass Communications in May 2013, and to defend my dissertation in April 2014. I will be seeking an assistant professorship that starts in the Summer or Fall of 2014.

Specialties

Making complex issues understandable in contexts that include feature-length journalistic articles, blog posts, grant proposals and business communications. Civic engagement and facilitation. Social media management and e-participation. Strategic planning. Communications research utilizing methods that include experiments, surveys, focus groups, content analysis, in-depth interviews, and statistical analysis.

I held a wonderful variety of positions during my 14 years at Syracuse's daily newspaper, The Post-Standard.

During my last year at the paper, I was both Civic Engagement Editor and Government Team Leader.

As Civic Engagement Editor, I developed and directed the CNYSpeaks initiative. Working with professors from Syracuse University's Maxwell School, we sparked a conversation with Central New Yorkers on how to improve Downtown Syracuse. Using a blog, surveys and facilitated public forums, we gathered thousands of comments on how to improve downtown. The comments and data were used for journalistic articles and for the creation of a "citizens' agenda" that was endorsed by local elected officials.

The project won the 2009 New York State Associated Press Award for Best Online Content.

As Government Team Leader, I managed and edited the paper's city, county, state and federal government reporters. I worked with them to develop story ideas and determine priorities, and then worked with other departments to build those stories into front-page presentations and multimedia Web packages. I represented the team at daily meetings with senior editors that shaped the paper's Web and print coverage. I also supervised their schedules and other managerial functions.

Prior to those responsibilities, I ran a satellite office as the Madison County Bureau Chief; won awards for my coverage of Syracuse City Hall and Onondaga County Government; spent a week camping on the Gulf Coast covering Katrina's aftermath; worked with interns on developing their promise; and oversaw award-winning investigative projects by the paper's best reporters.
