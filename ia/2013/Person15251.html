Bob Bradley's Summary

You can read the formal stuff about my career below. I’d like to say it was perfectly planned as it has worked out well, but I know the reality is it took me over twenty years to find what I loved to do.

In simple terms my career was in three parts.

Phase one was finding my feet – After leaving Imperial College with an Engineering Degree in Computing I began by alternating between big blue chip consultancy and small businesses, including my own. Although I was later criticised for the inconsistency, with the benefit of hindsight I gained really valuable breadth of experience.

I learnt to be both streetwise and strategic. To drive immediate revenues and cash to survive and yet be able to work through structures working to a long term plan. And by working in consultancy, project management and IT implementation I got to work with a wide variety of functions across a vast range of industries.

Phase two was the official pinnacle of my career. Leading three medium sized businesses (up to £50M revenues, £10M profit) through periods of significant transition. A corporate subsidiary, an AIM listed company and a family business – I led them all. Managing mergers, acquisitions, restructuring and turnarounds. Ensuring we got everything right for 500 course delegates every single day – well nearly ensuring. And learning to inspire up to 500 bright people to drive in the same direction for the same goals. Well as much the same as I could persuade them!

Phase three was me deciding that I’d had enough rushing around the country building other people’s business and wanting to do something myself. So since 2004, my income has come from using all my education, training and experience to help well over 100 SME business leaders grow and develop their business. Whilst coaching, mentoring and non executive roles helped me get established, over the last few years MD2MD, my leadership development peer groups business, has grown rapidly and now consumes most of my time.

Specialties

Business leadership development, leadership skills and confidence, Managing Director and senior management training, peer-to-peer support and challenge, increased business success, increased team management skills, coaching, mentoring, CPD, Continuous Professional Development

Bob Bradley's Experience

Chairman

MD2MD - Real world leaders learning WITH and FROM each other and top class speakers

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Professional Training & Coaching industry

October 2004 – Present (9 years) United Kingdom

In my experience many MD’s ‘grow’ into the role by accident; you have a great idea, start a business and before you know it (gulp!) you’re employing a group of people.

And I find many leaders have never been trained to lead and develop a business. It's not surprising: most real leaders can't afford the time and money for a leadership development course at a business school - after all who will run the business while you’re away?

So MD2MD is Continuing Professional Development (CPD) for business leaders. We aim to create the same (or better) outcome by delivering great insights in bite size chunks of one day each month.

From the perspective of a member:

"Engaging in a small group with top speakers, of the sort I’d normally only see on stage at a distance, provokes me with new ideas and motivates me to take action to continually improve the performance of my business.

Developing my thinking with other leaders who support me when I feel challenged and who challenge me when I don’t means that I make better decisions and implement them more confidently.

My greater confidence and competence as a leader improves the performance of my business."

See our company pages for more details or even better if you are interested in becoming a better leader and achieving more success AND QUALIFY (see below) email me to arrange a guest place with MD2MD so you can experience for yourself the power of learning with and from other leaders and from great inspirational expert speakers.

We're pretty generous with guest places - as long as you are a real Managing Director with staff (say 10+) to manage who is attending to learn with and from other leaders, not to pitch.

If at the end of the day you like what you see and want to join MD2MD, great, you gain a leadership development programme, we gain a new colleague. If not, we've all learnt something and gained new contacts, and the MD2MD brand profile has gone up by one person - who really understands what we're about.

Bob Bradley's Projects

  • Formative years: Learning about business and leadership

    • October 1964 to June 1986
    Team Members: Bob Bradley

    I never thought anything about business and leadership. I just did it. Strange that really as looking back the signs seem obvious.

    The earliest example I can remember is growing and selling plants for charity at my Sunday School Fete aged 6 (I think) and then starting a tuckshop at my secondary school and causing chaos by filling the school minibus full to the brim with products bought from the cash and carry and causing queues that filled the corridors all break time.

    Looking back it was there at college too. In my final year I was elected the most senior non-sabbatical in my students union. Not to do anything studentish though – I was too geeky. I led the negotiations with college over the course curricula, failure rates and the like. And sat on Governing body. Oh and I produced an 80 page Alternative prospectus and was one of the organisers of INCOST - The International Conference of Students of Science and Technology that year. All while captaining a Water Polo team and in my spare time studying for my finals three weeks later.

    I suppose the first time I realised I could actually lead was about five years later when the swimming club coach in my home town retired. Being somewhat naive and with more enthusiasm than sense I packed in my blue chip job with Accenture to join a 20 person typewriter sales business in my home town. That meant I could spend 40 hours a week of my spare time taking 300 children from third division to first in two seasons. The big learning for me was that each child seemed to have two parents (and some grandparents) who for some reason felt their child should get special attention. I innocently ignored them and used pure logic and analysis to pick the team most likely to win. Luckily the success kept me going for a couple of years before I succumbed to the pressure and in a fit of pique told them to find someone else to give up 40 hours a week of their spare time to coach their precious ones!
