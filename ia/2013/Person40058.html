Egil Andre Fagerland's Publications

  • Founding-Family Firms and the Creation of Value: Swedish evidence

    • Managerial Finance
    • June 2013
    Authors: Egil Andre Fagerland, Mattias Hamberg, Kristoffer Kvamme Nilsen

    Many publicly listed firms are owned and controlled by their founding families. For the US, Anderson and Reeb (2003) report that 35% of S&P 500 firms are influenced by their founding family, and Chen et al. (2008) report this figure as 46% for S&P 1500 firms. However, despite the abundance of family firms, we know quite little about them, mainly because data on founders are not readily available in public databases. To extend knowledge of how founding families create shareholder value, we use hand- collected data on all Swedish publicly listed firms. These firms constitute a particularly interesting setting given that founding families often want to retain control, and the use of control-enhancing mechanisms, such as differentiated voting rights, is more extensive in Sweden than in other countries.

    The analysis supports previous findings reporting that performance is higher for firms with founding-family ownership. When we separately analyze the effects of different levels of founding-family ownership on performance, we find that firms perform significantly better when founding-family owners control more than 60% of the voting rights. This result is unexpected given that Swedish firms frequently employ control-enhancing mechanisms that give owners opportunities to act opportunistically. It also contradicts earlier research.

    In addition, we find that family firms with long-term non-founding owners perform significantly worse than founding-family firms and perform no differently than other firms. In most firms in which the largest owner is a non-founding family, the largest owner takes an active role. If so, our results suggest that the founders’ superior monitoring abilities is not causing the better performance of founding-family firms. Instead we believe that other factors, such as entrepreneurial abilities, explain why founders create more value than other type of owners.
