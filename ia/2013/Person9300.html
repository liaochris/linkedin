April Yvonne Garrett's Summary

April Yvonne Garrett is the curator of the lifestyle, inspiration, and social commentary blog, The AYG List and host of The AYG List with April Yvonne Garrett on the Tavis Smiley Network on BlogTalkRadio. She has made an indelible mark on the world in three distinct fields: social entrepreneurship, civil rights, and higher education. In each arena, April has been a standard-bearing presence, making use of her unique personal history, impeccable leadership skills, and tenacious and enormously effective grassroots organizing ability, to make individuals, organizations and communities better.

April earned degrees in Islamic Studies from Kenyon College, Higher and Adult Education/Multicultural Affairs from Columbia University, and African American Religious History from Harvard University.

She has thrived in the education and nonprofit arenas, often as the only African American woman making major decisions that shaped the intellectual and campus life at Columbia, Emory, and Harvard Universities as well as the civil rights agenda of the NAACP.

For a decade, April served as the founder and president of Civic Frame, Inc., a national nonprofit organization that used documentary film and accessible intellectual work to encourage civic engagement, reaching over 50,000 people through public programs in 100 venues working with 150 unique community partners. In its final year, Garrett expanded this platform by serving as the producer, writer and host of a series of public conversations about the future of her hometown that became the weekly, 30-minute television show, Civic Frame Presents Amplify Baltimore.

With over 20 years of experience, April is in high demand as a powerful and catalyzing keynote speaker. Her common sense and unparalleled experience encourages her audiences to think creatively and benevolently, build diverse and cogent coalitions and use simple strategies to achieve personal and collective efficacy.
