Executive Director of “Start-Up” Clackamas County 501 ( C ) 3 Relief Nursery that serves children from birth to 5 years old who are in vulnerable home situations. A Relief Nursery is designed to stop the cycle of abuse, offer safety and resources to both the children and parents in order to strengthen the family. Currently, I serve a Board of 15 professionals that I work with to develop this new “start-up” non-profit organization. When hired we had nothing but the name and the 501 (C ) 3 letter. I was hired to create the strategy and implement it for development of the organization. This included: fundraising strategy and events, marketing and branding, creating a significant website, building relationships throughout the industry and within the state and county governments, program development, collaboration with all other early childhood programs and agencies, hiring of staff, orientations and trainings, program and operations policies and procedures, government regulations compliance standards securing contracts, grant writing, financials and budgets, securing a temporary site and developing it into a fully functioning relief nursery and the physical plant design remodel of a permanent building. I also built relationships with State government officials, our lobbyist, and local state and county agencies and presented to the House Human Services Committee and House Ways and Means Committees in Salem. We opened the program temporarily in the basement of the Gladstone Christian Church in September 2010 and moved into our permanent building in June of 2011. Family Stepping Stones relief nursery is now fully certified, and licensed with both an “In-Center” program and a “Rural home based” program, Mental Health therapy program and a significant Parent Education program. www.familysteppingstones.org
