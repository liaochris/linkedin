Joe Treese's Summary

Senior adviser leveraging 30 years’ experience as in-house IT executive and information-risk consultant to quantify risks in parties' information-governance and litigation-hold policies, practices and technology. Integrates established IT and legal-industry best-practices/emerging cases to develop practical strategies for mitigating issues of possession, custody and control of electronically-stored information (ESI) throughout the litigation life-cycle.

Understands the challenges created by gaps between the IT skills needed to manage big-data ESI, and those required of legal representation of its owners. Provides a bridge between two very different professions when reasonable expectation of litigation transforms ESI into potential evidence.

Specialties

ESI and Electronic Evidence case strategy, Business Legal Process Measurement and Improvement, ESI Cost and Complexity, Custodian Practices Affecting e-Discovery, Corporate Information Governance - Policy, Practices, Compliance, Metrics and Accountability

Joe Treese's Publications

  • Mitigating Malpractice Risks in e-Discovery

    Authors: Joe Treese

    Practical approach for lawyers to consider when faced with enormous electronically-stored information (ESI) repositories in civil litigation. Draws on decades of experience in corporate information-management experience across multiple industries as basis for establishing strong partnerships between legal teams and IT executives. Tailored to e-discovery as an outgrowth of information security work and overall ownership of information-related risk management, as in-house IT executive and management consultant.

  • Survive a Disaster: Plan Ahead to Protect Your Data and Systems

    • ABA Section of Litigation
    • September 2009
    Authors: Joe Treese, Jon Talotta

    In this article, we examine widely-accepted measures taken to mitigate the risk and adverse consequences of a data or systems disaster, and common mistakes that can make the difference between a successful and failed recovery.

    Included is our "Ten Ways to Better Prepare for a Disaster (and Best Practice Examples from Real Disaster Recovery Drills)"

  • Long Arm of the FRCP: Is International Compliance Even Possible?

    • Association of Corporate Counsel Washington DC Chapter
    • October 2008
    Authors: Joe Treese, Jon Talotta, Alvin Lindsay, Frank T. Spano

    This webinar explores the linkages and conflicts between the Federal Rules of Civil Procedure (FRCP) and various international requirements such as the European Union's Privacy Directives. Using a scenario-based approach, the presentation explores the issues faced by multinational firms when e-Discovery requirements in U.S.-based litigation present potential violations of privacy laws in other jurisdictions.

  • Litigation Lifecycle Management: Analyzing Critical Areas of Concern for Today’s Corporations

    • Effectively using early warning systems to avoid disputes and litigation
    • Evaluating unconventional and creative options for alternative fee arrangements
    • Identifying the right law firm and lawyers for your case and your business
    • Use of plaintiff counsel for efficient and aggressive protection of corporate rights
