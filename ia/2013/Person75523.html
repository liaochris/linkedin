Roman Kikta's Summary

Roman Kikta is a venture capitalist, wireless pioneer, entrepreneur, anthropologist and author. He has held senior executive positions in strategy, corporate development, market and product development, playing instrumental roles in building multi-billion dollar business units and divisions from start-ups to global corporations including Nokia and others. Roman is the founder of Mobility Ventures LLC, and co-founder at Genesis Campus Funds, Global Wireless Holdings (GWH), E-Life, & SpeedLink Communications.

Roman is a recognized authority on market trends and the role of technologies on society from anthropological, sociological and psychological perspectives. He led the development and commercialization of mobile technology and convergence, creating several wireless industry firsts: cellular payphones, voice recognition dialers, cellular in-building systems, several generations of mobile transportable and portable phone designs and involved in the initial launch of cellular (analog) and PCS (digital) in the U.S.

As a venture capitalist, Roman works closely with entrepreneurs, providing the experience, global perspective, and extensive network of relationships thereby increasing their probability of success. With a perceptive sense of market trends globally and an ability to identify and invest in emerging opportunities, as well as to scale opportunities across geographies, Roman has made over 20 seed & early stage investments in pioneering tech companies and played active roles in their growth, including, InnerWireless (acquired by BlackBox Networks, NASDAQ:BBOX) Spatial Wireless (acquired Alcatel, NYSE: ALU), Now Public (acquired by Clarity Media), & Arrowping Networks (acquired by Beijing XinWei).

Roman has authored four books published by McGraw-Hill and over 100 articles. He is a frequent speaker at conferences and industry events and a lecturer at universities around the world, on innovation, entrepreneurship, and the future of mobility & society.

Roman Kikta's Publications

  • Delivering xDSL

    • McGraw-Hill
    • November 21, 2000
    Authors: Roman Kikta, Lawrence Harte

    Deliver money-making bundled service packages with xDSL! A feature-focused, state-of-the-art guide, Delivering xDSL, by Lawrence Harte & Roman Kikta, tells you exactly why DSL is your best bet for profitably delivering a wide array of services to public phone network users. You'll find out how xDSL stacks up against competing technologies and how DSL technologies differ. Readers will find everything they need to know about CDMA for wireless implementations, a concise listing of all CDMA radio and network specifications; a directory of major CDMA equipment suppliers; and more.

Roman Kikta's Patents

  • Location-based novelty index value and recommendation system and method

    • United States Patent 7,529,639
    • Issued May 5, 2009
    Inventors: Roman Kikta, Eero Rasanen, Antti Sorvari

    The invention is a system and method for computing a result for a location, wherein the result indicates how novel it is for a wireless device to occupy a specific location. After determining the location of a wireless device through various means, a Novelty Index Value (NIV) is calculated for the location, and the NIV is then stored into a database. The NIV may then be subsequently used by application programs to compute a desired result from the NIV. Multiple users may utilize and/or share the same NIV values. The NIV may also be used to alter the configuration of a wireless device as well. A recommendation system is also disclosed, wherein user context is utilized along with NIV values to compute particular results for a user.
