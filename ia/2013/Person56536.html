Jack Kelly's Summary

I am a computer scientist with strong interests in energy efficiency, renewable generation and green tech. I started a PhD at the Department of Computing at Imperial in Oct 2011 (due to finish in Oct 2014). My PhD is on "Disaggregating Smart Meter Signals".

My interests include smart meters, energy efficient heating control, control systems for renewable generation, electric vehicles and making existing fossil-fuel powered generation capacity more efficient.

I was recently a trustee of a volunteer-driven, community organisation called Peckham Power (http://peckhampower.org) which aims to help local individuals to reduce their energy consumption.

I have gained valuable insight into the challenges involved in improving the energy performance of the UK's domestic housing stock after having invested an uncomfortably large amount of time into improving the energy performance of our Victorian house. I have blogged about my attempts to make our home more energy efficient at http://jack-kelly.com/tags/1010_diary

Specialties

Energy efficiency, domestic heating control, renewable generation
