The Food Evolution

May 2010 – Present (3 years 8 months)

The Food Evolution is a Nutrition and Cooking Center designed to teach people how to prepare delicious and nutritious "whole foods" for themselves and their families. We teach students the general knowledge and skills necessary to shop for, prepare, and serve healthy and beautiful! Our belief is that food is medicine, and what we eat directly affects our physical, spiritual, and mental well being. We believe in a "whole foods" approach to nutrition and support local, seasonal, and sustainable agriculture.

Diane Hoch's Projects

  • Rockland Coalition for Better School Food

    • October 2005 to August 2007
    Team Members: Diane Hoch, Becky Holt, Susan Rubin, DMD, HHC, Sheryl Leventhal

    The Rockland Coalition for Better School Food consists of health
    professionals, educators and concerned parents. This group provides Rockland County residents who are fed up with the school cafeteria food a forum to affect change. The Coalition sees a strong correlation between the low quality food available to children at school and their deteriorating health, wellness, behavior, and learning.

Diane Hoch's Publications

  • Lunchtime: Changing One School at a Time

    • The Valley Table
    • June 2007
    Authors: Diane Hoch, Becky Holt, Abby Luby, Sheryl Leventhal

    Working with schools to change their food programs is a challenge for groups like the Rockland Coalition. Today,
    most school districts have a suburban School Health Advisory Council, or SHAC, which creates district-wide wellness policies. “We don’t want to storm into schools and take over the cafeteria,” says Sheryl Leventhal, parent of two
    teens in the Suffern School District. ”We want to help make the transition easier by giving information to wellness committees in the schools—we want to partner with schools to help them accomplish the things to help them in getting it done."
