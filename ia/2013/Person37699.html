Drumm McNaughton, PhD, CMC, FIMC's Summary

Drumm McNaughton, Ph.D., CMC®, FIMC, turns around low performing organizations and makes good organizations great through holistic, collaborative, values-based approaches to strategy, people, processes and performance. A recognized leader in large scale organizational redesign and change, he works with governments, nonprofit, and educational organizations, transforming them from ”possibilities” to exemplars of excellence.

Specifically, Dr. McNaughton:

• Builds high-performing organizations
• Transforms organizational culture
• Improves employee performance
• Develops performance metrics and accountability standards
• Builds leaders and leadership capacity
• Drives accountability throughout a culture
• Turns around troubled organizations
• Creates public-private partnerships with business and government
• Develops alternative sources of funding
• Improves innovation and builds a culture of innovators

All told, he has added $400 million to organizations’ bottom lines.

His clients includes government organizations such as the European Bank for Reconstruction and Development, U.S. Air Force Reserve, U.S. Navy and the Government of Guam; large urban school districts such as Los Angeles Unified School District; institutions of higher learning such as King Saud University, Capella University, and Chapman University; nonprofits such as IMC USA’s Academy for Professional Development; and large and mid-cap companies in the high-tech, telecomm, biotech, chemical, defense, and financial services industries.

He holds a Ph.D. and an M.A. in Human and Organizational Systems (Fielding Graduate University), and a B.S. in Physics (U.S. Naval Academy). He has served as the Chairman and CEO of the Institute of Management Consultants USA, the certifying body and professional organization for management consultants in the U.S., and has earned their Certified Management Consultant® (CMC®) designation. He teaches at Trident University in their MBA and Ph.D. programs.

Specialties

Organization development, redesign, and change; culture change; organization and strategy alignment; turnarounds; business strategy and implementation; executive coaching; and innovation and creativity. Ability to work at all levels of the organization, but focuses on senior levels including boards of directors.

Drumm McNaughton, PhD, CMC, FIMC's Publications

  • The role of values and leadership in organizational transformation

    • Journal of Human Values
    • 2004
    Authors: Drumm McNaughton, PhD, CMC, FIMC

    This is an analytic study of organizational transformational change; the values that must be present and operationalized for organizations to successfully change, and the role leadership has in manifesting that change. Specifically, using De Geus’ model of the living and economic companies, and taking input from key theorists such as Senge, Quinn, Bolman and Deal, and others, this study examines and analyzes the values and guiding principles that facilitate an organization’s ability to transform, the qualities leadership must manifest, and how leadership must function as catalysts to facilitate and energize successful, positive change.

  • Using relationships to drive rigor and relevance: A systemic approach to achieving the positive classroom

    • 2008
    Authors: Drumm McNaughton, PhD, CMC, FIMC

    The “buzz” is apparent in the “store” as the “workers” prepare for their daily onslaught of lunch customers. People scurry about prepare for the store opening, stocking inventory, counting cash and cleaning counters, while others count inventory, and develop cost models and marketing plans for the new menu.

    Rick, the store’s “proprietor,” oversees the teams of workers, stopping by each group to give them encouragement and instruction. For the marketing group, he challenges them to think outside the box when considering new items for the menu. For the public relations group, he helps them format the store’s new menu on the computer. For the finance group, he helps them figure out profit margins for Tuesday’s pizza lunch.

    A small business – yes, but not the type business you would expect. This is the business of education – the San Diego High School of Business (SofB) senior entrepreneurship class is preparing for its daily task of running the school's snack bar, and demonstrating how their slogan of "rigor, relevance, and relationships" applies to this unique inner-city school.
