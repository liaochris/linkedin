Jade Blackwater's Summary

Read her prose and poetry in WILD RIVER REVIEW, LINE ZERO, THE MONONGAHELA REVIEW, and others. When Jade isn’t writing she’s usually out getting dirty in the garden, or hiking the local forests.

Jade Blackwater's Projects

  • White Center Chamber of Commerce: Visit White Center / Growing a Global Village

    • June 2012 to September 2012
    Team Members: Jade Blackwater, Kathi "george" Wheeler, Mark Root-Wiley

    White Center Seattle is a unique and diverse community with a hard working history. In recent decades White Center has become a welcoming gateway for immigrants and refugees who enrich the neighborhood with growing families, businesses, and cultural institutions. The top languages spoken here include English, Spanish, Vietnamese, Khmer/Cambodian, Tigrinya, Amharic, Arabic, Somali and Samoan.

    More than 200+ local retail businesses provide essential neighborhood services within walking/busing distance. VisitWhiteCenter.com is a welcoming online space for neighborhood events, retail business profiles, and local news. New content includes information about neighborhood goods, services, and history. This website can be used by the White Center Chamber of Commerce for years to come to promote local retail businesses and events.

  • South Park Business District: Catch the Culture / Captura la Cultura

    • April 2011 to September 2011
    Team Members: Jade Blackwater, Kathi "george" Wheeler, Mark Root-Wiley

    South Park Seattle is a diverse neighborhood isolated by highways and industrial development in South Seattle. More than 30+ retail businesses were cut off from their usual customer flow when the bridge leading into South Park was closed in 2010 for rebuilding.

    CatchTheCulture.com was launched in 2011 to promote South Park’s family retail businesses. CatchTheCulture.com is an appealing online space for neighborhood events, retail business profiles, and local news. Each participating retail merchant receives a detailed web page to promote their products and services to residents and visitors in the greater Seattle area.

  • Healthy Foods Here: Buy Fresh Southwest Seattle and Fresh Produce Marketing Guide

    • September 2010 to March 2012
    Team Members: Jade Blackwater, Kathi "george" Wheeler

    Southwest Seattle neighborhoods are home to families that hail from around the world. Vulnerable children in the Delridge and White Center neighborhoods are less likely to have equal access to healthy environments, less opportunity for adopting healthy behaviors, and less chance for lifelong success compared to children from more affluent communities.

    Healthy Foods Here and the King County Food and Fitness Initiative support efforts to build demand for healthy (and culturally appropriate) foods, as well as safe, inviting spaces to be active, plus safe routes to school. Ten stores enrolled in the Healthy Foods Here Program to increase access to healthy foods in Delridge and White Center neighborhoods. Complete Merchant Profiles are published at BuyFreshSW.wccda.org. A full-color Fresh Produce Marketing Guide was printed and distributed for each merchant, and is available online for free download. Our overall goals for Healthy Foods Here efforts are to impact the quality of life and future success of children and families in Southwest Seattle neighborhoods.

  • Festival of the Trees: A Monthly Blog Carnival of All Things Arboreal

    • November 2006 to December 2011
    Team Members: Jade Blackwater

    Leafy brainchild of Dave Bonta and Pablo Roundrocker, the Festival of the Trees was a monthly blog carnival that ran from 2006 to 2011 celebrating trees and forests with bloggers around the world. The final edition #66 was hosted Dec. 1, 2011 at the project's new home: treeblogging.com. Jade served as a volunteer contributor and coordinator.
