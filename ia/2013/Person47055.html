Gary Braasch's Summary

Environmental photojournalist and conservation photographer for 30 years. Concerned about the big connections between humans and the natural world including global warming and resource mis-use and over use. Known for powerful and richly detailed nature images. Goals: More of the same, with increasing use of my work to reach public and leaders through media and academic collaborations. Strongly focused on global warming with publication of Earth Under Fire: How Global Warming is Changing the World; a kids book written with Lynne Cherry, How We Know What We Know about Our Changing Climate; and an IPad/IPhone app Painting With Time: Climate Change. Images used by United Nations for issue of 6 postage stamps educating about climate change. Current work with the Boston Museum of Science on an exhibition of climate change photographs, and with a foundation concerned with climate change effects on people in the Himalayas.

Specialties: Location photography -- wilderness, landscape, ecosystems in their richness, field science, people and planet, cities, night and motion. Highly experienced assignment photographer for world's major magazines. Qualified journalist and reporter (Northwestern's Medill School). Public speaking.

Owner

Gary Braasch Photography

January 1975 – Present (38 years 8 months)

Environmental photojournalism and very powerful nature and Earth photography.
I have been involved in conservation campaigns since the 1980s with the early fights for the old growth forest, and with amazing natural events like the eruption of Mt St Helens.

My work is internationally known for dramatic, artistic and science-based nature photography, with awards from Communication Arts and New York Art Directors Club, and the Ansel Adams Award for Conservation photography. 2013, opened major exhibition on climate change at Boston Museum of Science, and was a speaker for World Environment Day (UNEP). In 2011 and 2012, extensive use of images and research used in Koshland Science Museum, Washington DC, and NC Museum of Natural Science. IPad/iPhone app "Painting With Time: Climate Change" launched March 2012 with RedHillStudios. My climate documentation in What Matters (Sterling Press, 2008). Climate change work was a feature at Visa pour l'Image in Perpignan. I've made illustrated presentations in 2010 at the Phoenix Art Museum; in 2007 at the Explorers Club Hq in New York; and previously at the Smithsonian. Exhibits have been mounted at the American Assn for the Advancement of Science in Washington DC, Field Museum in Chicago, and Minnesota Science Museum.

Major assigned articles and portfolios have appeared during recent years in Time, LIFE, Discover, Audubon, National Wildlife, Smithsonian, Scientific American, International Wildlife, Natural History, Sierra, and National Geographic, among many others. The United Nations has published my climate photography as postage stamps, booklets and calendars.

Now I have completed 11 years of work documenting global warming and what to do about it. Book, EARTH UNDER FIRE: HOW GLOBAL WARMING IS CHANGING THE WORLD (University of California Press, paperback and ebook 2009). And also a companion kids book. For more information, besides the websites listed above, please see http://www.earthunderfire.com
