Adam Jackson's Summary

I'm a Florida native living in New Hampshire via San Francisco. As an early adopter and strategist by nature, technology inspired me to take risks and jump into things head first. I'm currently a Global Project Manager with TomTom and Owner of TweetForMyBiz.

I began blogging in 1999 at the age of 12 on a dial-up connection and leveraged the opportunities associated with tech blogging to travel to cities around the world speaking at conferences, hosting events and meeting amazing people. After high school, I received a call from Apple inviting me to management training and to help them open a retail store near my home town. Following a career at Apple, my interest shifted to Information Technology. I acquired an MCSA and various Cisco certifications and began an IT job at a consulting firm. I climbed the ranks and became Service Manager and remained in IT for 3 years before making the life-changing move to San Francisco at age 21.

On the west coast, I took risks, started 3 companies, co-authored a book, grew my network and polished my skills. At OurBlock, I helped bring a vision of fighting crime via crowd-sourcing to reality. We saved lives and reached millions of viewers and I was able to discuss the issues of crime on CNN, NPR and other news outlets. At TweetForMyBiz, I recruited a team of hardworking social media experts to represent growing startups online. We operated over 30 Twitter & Facebook accounts for companies 24/7 and this business continues today with the National Football League and others. With Dom Sagolla, I helped author 140 Characters and it was the most exciting project of my life.

After 3 years in San Francisco, I was lured to New Hampshire by Pat McDevitt and the TomTom team to work on community endeavors in map making. Currently, I work with dozens of people around the world. This job has taken me to countries around Europe and India finding new ways to improve our map making initiatives.

Specialties

Technical writing, proposal design, social media marketing, web 2.0, information technology, mcse, mcsa, cisco, viral marketing, internet
