David All's Summary

David All is the Founder and CEO of MADE I/O (madeio.com) which works with entrepreneurs, brands, and organizations to help them effectively communicate the good they are doing in the world and our communities. It strategically directs philanthropic and social responsibility efforts to better achieve business objectives through both online and offline storytelling opportunities.

In less than a decade, David managed a Congressional political campaign, learned the art of rhetoric as a speechwriter for a US Senator, pioneered the use of social media in advocacy and politics, built an award-winning social media agency (David All Group LLC), and co-founded a company to release a consumer mobile iPhone application (Crumbly, Inc.).

David is recognized as a thought leader in social media, marketing and public relations. He is the co-author of several social media guides (Pinterest 101, Google+ 101, The Essential Guide to Twitter) that have been downloaded tens of thousands of times. He has been published or appeared in The New York Times, CNN, Wired, Fast Company, Fox News, Washington Post, ABC News, The Huffington Post, PR News, and others.

David along with his work have received a dozen awards including being named one of eDemocracy's "Top 10 Who Are Changing the World of Internet and Politics," Campaigns & Elections' "Rising Star," a Golden Dot Award as founder of the "Best Blog National Politics," and more.

David organized The AMP Summit (Advocacy, Media, and Policy) that aired on C-SPAN. David was the visionary behind award-winning products, including: act.ivi.st, software which applied gaming mechanics to advocacy campaigns; Tweet Watch Report, a daily snapshot of the most newsworthy, informative and entertaining tweets; and DirectConnect, an online advocacy tool for supporters to efficiently communicate with government stakeholders.

David lives in Chicago, IL with his French Bulldog, Jackson.

Founder / CEO

David All Group

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Public Relations and Communications industry

January 2007 – October 2012 (5 years 10 months) Washington DC, San Francisco, Chicago, Columbus

David All Group (DAG) was an online communications and branding firm with offices in Washington, San Francisco, Chicago and Columbus. I founded the firm in 2007. DAG offered marketing, reputation management, online development services and campaign management to various corporate clients, issue-advocacy campaigns, professional trade associations, and cultural movements. Fiscal Year 2010 was the strongest ever for the company. The team reached its height at 20 colleagues with gross revenue of more than $4.6 million with a profit margin of nearly 20 percent.

Founder

AMP Summit

May 2010 – October 2011 (1 year 6 months) Washington, DC

Featured on C-SPAN, AMP Summit (Advocacy, Media, and Policy) was an annual forum for influentials and thought leaders in the advocacy, marketing and policy spheres. Public officials and regulators, experts from think tanks, trade associations, and public relations, and members of the media attended. This conference was held in our nation’s capitol, Washington, DC, in September 2011 and 2012. The intention of this conference was to bring together respected colleagues and new friends to inspire new thinking, challenge traditional strategies, and create opportunities to learn from each other.

Key sponsors included Google, YouTube, AOL/Huffington Post, and Microsoft.

Communications Director

U.S. Representative Jack Kingston

April 2005 – December 2006 (1 year 9 months) Washington, DC

David served as communications director for U.S. Rep. Jack Kingston of Georgia, whose communications and leadership skills were widely recognized. Together, they evolved political communications by developing the first blog on Capitol Hill, pioneering the use of YouTube to improve communications with constituents, bridging relations with third-party bloggers, and organizing widely attended "New Media" workshops on best practices and learning. In 2008, Kingston said, "At that time it was like explaining water to people who had only lived in the desert. It was such a foreign concept, but David changed all that."

Communications Director

Mike Bouchard for U.S. Senate

August 2006 – November 2006 (4 months) Detroit, Michigan

David served as communications director for a U.S. Senate candidate in Michigan during 2006 cycle. This campaign provided him an opportunity to blend his communication learnings from Capitol Hill with the fast-paced speed of a political campaign. There David built the first campaign website on an open-source blogging platform (WordPress), was one of the first to build communities in online social networks, used YouTube in direct, innovative ways, and organized supporters online.

Deputy Communications Director/Speechwriter

U.S. Senator George V. Voinovich

April 2002 – April 2005 (3 years 1 month) Washington, DC

David served his home state of Ohio as deputy communications director and speechwriter to U.S. Sen. George V. Voinovich. This opportunity provided him a chance to go to Washington, DC and develop classic training in the art of rhetoric, messaging and storytelling. Senator Voinovich's mantra, "Work Harder and Smarter and Do More With Less," is a lesson David has carried with him ever since.

Campaign Manager

Ann Womer Benjamin for Congress

2001 – 2002 (1 year) Ravenna, Ohio

David served as the campaign manager for a closely-watched Congressional campaign in Northern Ohio. The seat was interesting to all observers due to unusual circumstance that the current Member of Congress that represented the district was running for office from jail. Though the race was lost, four out of five local daily newspapers endorsed Ann Womer Benjamin and David’s work was noticed by U.S. Senator George V. Voinovich.

Members of the association’s board of industry leaders met with members of the House of Representatives and congressional staff to advocate action on key issues.
