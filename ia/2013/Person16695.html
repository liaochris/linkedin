Bree Richmond's Summary

Visual communication is my life’s passion, and my past experience at places such as Realm and Frank Williams Design, combined with my success at owning and operating my own design company, as well as my course of study at the University of Arizona, both in Tucson and abroad, have prepared me for most any design challenge I might encounter. I have been working in public relations in some capacity for over fifteen years, and it has given me the ability to multi-task, effectively communicate, and meet deadlines.

I am confident in my design and illustration capabilities which have been used to produce logos, brand identities, letterhead, business cards, newsletters, brochures, booklets, fliers, posters, vehicle graphics, tradeshow materials, t-shirts, and banners, as well as multiple websites and social networking campaigns. I have strong knowledge of programs such as InDesign, Photoshop, Illustrator, PageMaker, QuarkXPress, Dreamweaver, and Acrobat. I have maintained company files, catalogued stock art, and performed accounting responsibilities. I also have experience logging hours worked and calculating time devoted to specific jobs. I am an avid digital photographer and have experience scanning, editing, and optimizing photos.

My track record proves that I truly enjoy the pleasure that comes from doing a job well, and I look forward to discovering more networking and career opportunities.

Specialties

Logo design, website design, print design, product design, tradeshow/event design, vehicle graphics design, marketing, photography, and social networking consultation.

Bree Richmond's Projects

  • KD Designs Business Identity

    • October 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Bree Richmond

    Kimberly came to us needing a logo for her LEED design company based out of Iowa. Being that the company is focused on environmental design, we created a mark that incorporates the feeling of environmental friendliness without being too literal. By deconstructing her initials, we were able to imply forward movement as well as imbue an architectural feeling to the design. The result is a brand that is clean and sophisticated that will unify all future projects and present a refined image of sustainability. We also just designed a website for Kimberly which can be found at:
    http://kimberlydickeydesigns.com/

  • Robert Groll Business Identity

    • June 2011 to January 2012
    Team Members: Bree Richmond

    Robert Groll, CPA came to us in desperate need of a visual identity. They wanted a logo that would be traditional enough to communicate their solid business ideals common amongst most accounting firms, but they still wanted to create a unique aesthetic personality, giving them an opportunity to stand out from the crowd. We forged a mark for them that has been extremely successful in unifying their marketing endeavors and has helped reinforce an easily recognizable company brand. They were so happy with our work that we have gone on to design letterhead, business cards, flash drives and report covers as part of their identity package. We have also created brochures, and tradeshow materials for them.

  • Realm Graphic Design

    • January 2010 to June 2011
    Team Members: Bree Richmond, Jason Isenberg

    Realm™ is an innovative landscape design firm in Tucson that is reinventing the notion of sustainable design. Their main mission is to shed light on the fact that sustainability does not have to be about corrugated cisterns and cactus. It can incorporate an extremely high level of aesthetic refinement and virtually disappear into the background, bringing sophisticated composition to the forefront. Having worked hand-in-hand with the company and its owner Jason Isenberg for over a year, we learned how intricate and important that message was and was able to foster a company brand that would communicate this complex ideal.

    Realm already had a strong logo in place, but we helped to further create and maintain a solid identity by extending their branding company-wide and incorporating it into everything from vehicle design, to in-house documents, office design, uniforms, and of course web and print design. We were also charged with all marketing endeavors, from creating classes and presentations for the Tucson Botanical Gardens and REI, to participating in community outreach, to assisting with the launch of new company divisions and creating and maintaining their social networking circles. Photographed landscape designs, some of which have appeared in the likes of "Tucson Lifestyle Home & Garden" and other various publications. In addition, we assisted with office organization and restructuring
