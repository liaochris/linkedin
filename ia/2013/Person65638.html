Joe M. Turner's Education

Mississippi State University

Bachelor of Science, Physics/Chemistry Education;

1987 – 1993

One of the first four Schillig Leadership Scholars, the most prestigious full honors scholarship offered at Mississippi State. Later received the President George H.W. Bush Education Scholarship.

Graduated Summa Cum Laude in 1992 with a B.S. in Physics/Chemistry Education, completing significant additional coursework in astronomy, mathematics, music, and drama. Completed most of a second degree in Communication with an emphasis in theatre before accepting a corporate position in 1993.

Named to the University Hall of Fame, the Society of Scholars in the Arts & Sciences, Who's Who Among American Colleges and Universities, Who's Who in Mississippi, Phi Kappa Phi and several other academic and leadership societies.

Member of the Famous Maroon Band and Phi Mu Alpha Sinfonia music fraternity; MSU Opera Theatre; Blackfriars Drama Society.

Received artistic awards for acting, directing, and playwriting at the University and in regional and national competitions.

Activities and Societies: Schillig Scholar (Distinguished Scholars Program) University Honors Program University Honors Council Famous Maroon Band (trumpet) Jazz Band (piano) German Club - President Gamma Beta Phi - President Society of Physics Students Blackfriars Drama Society MSU Opera Theatre Phi Kappa Phi Society of Scholars in Arts & Sciences
