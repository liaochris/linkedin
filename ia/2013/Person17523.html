Brian Yap's Summary

I am a designer and illustrator, currently working as a creative director at Boxing Clever, a boutique, advertising agency in the Midwest. After graduating from VCU’s School of Communication Arts and Design, I moved to the Saint Louis, MO to work as everything from a toy designer to apparel line-builder before moving into advertising. As a creative director, I work for clients like Brown Forman, Saint Louis International Airport, Accenture, and recently Adobe Systems. At the core of what I do is a respect for what illustrative styles can do for the visual impact of any design, whether it is a poster for a local rock venue or a national promotions program. As national music acts come through the Midwest, they often do appearances at one of the country’s largest non-chain music stores – Vintage Vinyl. The posters for these events have helped to show off my work to a larger audience.

Recently I have started working with the Adobe touch pad division after creating illustrations using an app on the Ipad called Adobe Ideas. The ornately graphic illustrations using new technology helped create award-winning illustrations for Adobe and posters for bands like Rise Against and Talib Kweli.

Specialties

Illustration, Design, Creative Direction

As the agency grew I took on the role of creative director, developing a powerful new type of creative team built on members that have multiple sides to their talent and work at a higher level of output than the average creative. As the role of "designer" has changed so has the need to build for creative teams that can tackle a variety of projects and direct outside resources and still continue to keep a very high level of consistency in the work.

This approach has lead to continued growth within the scope of our original clients, winning more business among companies we already had a relationship with like Brown-Forman, and creating new opportunities with exciting new clients like Adobe systems and the newly created Touch Tooling group.
