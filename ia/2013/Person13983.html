Baoguang Zhai's Summary

My idea for the future integrates two primary and related themes: seasteading and global entrepreneurship, for which twin interests I have dedicated myself to in the past and will continue to do so for my life and career.

I was born in northwestern China, in a province with per capita GDP roughly equivalent to that of Armenia – the poorest country in Europe. My parents are among those migrants who sought a better life in Beijing, where I lived for 9 years until discriminative education policy drove me to US for college. I have been in a perpetual search for frontiers, which is why I became one of the most adamant supporters for the seasteading movement. You can see my vision of seasteading at (http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HIRwETth1QM). Seasteaders are 21st Century equivalent for Christopher Columbus and Vasco Da Gama, driving humanity to new frontiers. Patri Friedman & Joe Quirk’s new book “Seasteading: How Ocean City-States Will Save The Environment, Cure Diseases, and End Poverty for Millions” also featured my story for a chapter as a paradigmatic seasteader.

Another theme that I am pursing in my work is global entrepreneurship. Coming from a huge emerging market and as both an outsider and an insider in the US, I realized that in an increasingly globalized economy, not all of the best ideas will originate in Silicon Valley. With that in mind, I work as a tech journalist between China and US, spreading ideas and covering transformative technologies. I was the first Silicon Valley-based reporter for 36kr.com, the most influential tech blog in China. I am also a founding member of Pingwest.com, the first Chinese online tech media with a global mindset. Having worked with 1284 entrepreneurs from 67 countries in Blueseed project, I am convinced that we are entering into an era that would allow international entrepreneurs to benefit unprecedentedly from these global economic trends.

May 2013 – Present (2 months) Greater Boston Area

I am selected as an undergraduate fellow by the Tufts Institute of Environment (TIE) and awarded a grant to conduct a research project that intends to identify the key environmental and social elements that would contribute to the prevalence of malaria in the developing world, particularly in India. I work closely with Professor Elena Naumova from Tufts Engineering School, utilizing remote sensing technology as a tool. Navigator

Blueseed

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Venture Capital & Private Equity industry

August 2012 – Present (11 months) Sunnyvale,CA

Blueseed is a project to station a ship 12 nautical miles from the coast of San Francisco, in international waters. The location will allow startup entrepreneurs from anywhere in the world to start or grow their company near Silicon Valley, without the need for a US work visa. The ship will be converted into a coworking and co-living space, and will have high-speed Internet access and daily transportation to the mainland via ferry boat. So far, over 1000 entrepreneurs from 60+ countries expressed interest in living on the ship.

I am among the earliest evangelists to join a team of extremely diverse background, with the vision to host international entrepreneurs in international waters. I research various operational issues, including a complete study on the possibility of turning public space on a cruise ship into co-working spaces, presenting at WORKTECH conference 2012 West Coast. As the first & only Chinese member on Blueseed team, I work on marketing, business development, public relations, investor relations towards the Chinese market, and have helped raised angel investment from Xu Xiaoping of ZhenFund, the most active angel fund in China.
