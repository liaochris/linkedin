Drawing from many years of recruiting industry experience, Coral Mesa Consulting was developed around a simple idea. Regional Recruiting Director

e-commerce Network Resource Group

1999 – 2000 (1 year)

At eNRG, I developed the regional structure of the firm and managed the recruiting staff for the Midwestern US Region. I recruited, trained and supervised the regional staff and aligned our recruiting strategy with the overall business strategy. As Regional Recruiting Director I constantly exceeded regional goals and expectations and kept pushing my limits. I learned that everything can be improved, refined and systemized out of the “fireman mentality” that seemed to be everywhere in the recruiting industry. At eNRG, I refined my skills and started thinking about creating something rather than just playing a part in it.

Recruiting Director

TSS Corporation

1998 – 1999 (1 year)

Originally an IT Tandem shop, I was brought into TSS to expand the company into other technologies. As Recruiting Director, I acted as the primary for recruiting and admin hiring and led a full life-cycle recruiting team. I consistently surpassed goals at TSS by over 175%. This was a period of rapid professional growth for me, I pushed my limits daily and realized how far I could go in this industry.

Account Executive | Recruiter | Executive Assistant

Tech-Pro

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Information Technology and Services industry

1995 – 1998 (3 years)

Tech-Pro was my introduction to the recruiting industry. When I was hired as an executive assistant in 1995, I had no idea that great things were about to happen. There was less than a dozen of us and at the time, Tech-Pro “officed” out of a converted hotel. Because we were small, as the EA I had exposure to both the recruiting and sales departments. I worked with scheduling, records, resume rewrites, job postings/descriptions and handled travel arrangements for Tech-Pro employees and our sponsored overseas contractors.

As Tech-Pro exploded on the scene I moved up to the position of recruiter and was responsible for our contract IT candidates. I learned the right way to develop candidates and built a solid database of current and past connections. I had a successful run where I placed a number of active candidates by cold calling local companies. Eventually, I again transitioned, this time into an Account Executive role. As Account Executive I placed candidates with new accounts and maintained existing clients with our candidates on contract.

By the time I left Tech-Pro in 1998, we had five office locations, our own building with a staff of about 40 and 300 employees on contract. Witnessing the rapid growth of Tech-Pro gave me a perspective that will always be a part of my identity in this industry. Big or small, in good times or bad, you have to stay focused on the fundamentals- the solid relationships you build will carry you through.
