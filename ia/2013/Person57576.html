James Burke's Experience

Division Vice President and HSHS Corporate Counsel

Hospital Sisters Health System

Nonprofit; Myself Only; Hospital & Health Care industry

April 2009 – Present (4 years 6 months)

From ASC acquisitions to developer built MOB facilities; from closing service lines to acquiring physician practices – consistently turned to as the “go-to” person for legally compliant, financially sound, and operationally sustainable business solutions.

Led the hiring of 4 staff attorneys and 1 paralegal to build an Office of General Counsel for the Southern Illinois Division. The “in-sourcing” of the Division's legal work involved the creation of a complete in-house “law firm” with policies, procedures, matters management and budget accountability. This process led to comprehensive, embedded legal services, while simultaneously creating a net reduction in total legal spend of over $2.5 Million per year.

Led a restructuring of the Southern Illinois Division compliance program, which centered on new systems and processes to assure regulatory compliance and preceded the eventual restructuring of the entire HSHS compliance program. Issues centered around financial transactions with physicians and proper compliance with Stark, Anti-Kickback, False Claims Act and other state and federal fraud and abuse laws.

Adjunct Associate Professor

Saint Louis University School of Law

Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry

January 2013 – Present (9 months) Greater St. Louis Area

Co-Teaching "Legal Issues in Hospital Governance and Patient Care" with Anne C. Justice.

Course Covers: Hospital Governance Structure; Medical Staff Privileging (Credentialing) & Peer Review; Corrective Action, Fair Hearings, and National Practitioner Database Reporting; Conditions of Participation and Hospital Accreditation; State and Federal Regulatory Surveys; Patient Safety Events, Adverse/Sentinel Event Reporting; EMTALA and Call Coverage; Advanced Directives, Capacity, and Guardianship; Patient Restraint, Behavioral Health/Involuntary Commitment, Forensic Patients, Drug-seeking Patients, ERDs, AMA and Elopement.

Duties included strategic planning, team building, and maintaining accountability. As CEO, maintained dialogue with Board of Directors and ensured executive team worked together toward mission endorsed by Board.

Captain
