Kyahn Kamali's Summary

Mr. Kamali is an extremely versatile professional with expertise in IT, data informatics, analysis and the use of data for driving business outcomes optimization. Over the past 20 years he has applied this expertise in a number of fields including health care, finance/lending, Internet and retail environments. Kyahn is experienced in playing the role of business SME/lead in enterprise data warehousing and business intelligence development efforts, including the development of enterprise reporting capabilities. Kyahn has hands-on experience across many analytic areas, including financial modeling, predictive modeling, marketing effectiveness evaluation, call center evaluations, return on investment, and other impact analyses.

Kyahn was the Director of Research and Development at Nationwide Better Health (NBH), a subsidiary of Nationwide Mutual Insurance Co. In this role Kyahn led a team of senior analysts in efforts to validate the value of NBH health management programs through client reporting and research studies. Clients included many Fortune 500 companies, in addition to clients in the healthcare and education sectors.

Kyahn also owns and represents one of the leading web-based health information companies in South America, Bibliomed. Bibliomed is a 10-year-old company which operates numerous foreign language web properties reaching several million monthly visitors and Kyahn provides market research and consulting services based upon this exposure.

Kyahn holds a Masters Degree in Health Sciences from the Johns Hopkins University School of Public Health with a focus on Health Finance and Administration. He also was awarded a Masters Degree in Astrophysics from the Johns Hopkins University. Kyahn has been recognized as a Certified Professional in Healthcare Information and Management Systems (CPHIMS 2007-2010).

Kyahn Kamali's Publications

  • Random fluctuations and validity in measuring disease management effectiveness for small populations

    • Population Health Management
    • January 1, 2009
    Authors: Kyahn Kamali, Ian Duncan, thomas Messer, Jeff Harner

    One objective of a disease management (DM) program is the reduction of members' claims costs. A considerable amount of effort has been dedicated to standardizing the outcomes of DM measurement. An area that has not received as much attention is that of random fluctuations in measured outcomes and the related issue of the validity of outcomes subject to random fluctuation. From year to year, large random fluctuations in claims costs can increase or reduce actual savings from a DM program.

  • Better Compliance with the Lifestyle Health Coaching Program Leads to Stronger and Better Results

    • Care Continuum Alliance
    • October 13, 2010
    Authors: Kyahn Kamali, Melissa Peters, Neil Gordon, Jeff Harner

    This study was conducted with 3,539 participants over the age of 18 who completed the Nationwide Better Health Check,(SM) an online health risk assessment, and participated in the company's telephonic lifestyle coaching program. The study demonstrates that compliance with an effective telephonic lifestyle health coaching program results in improvements in multiple health risk factors. Improvements in health risk factors of the magnitude observed in the study can be expected to help employers protect the health and well-being of their employees while lowering healthcare costs and boosting productivity.

    The study's results revealed that the Nationwide Better Health program helped participants reduce a number of their health risk factors they had in a dose response pattern. This lends scientific credibility to the notion that health coaching effectively reduces health risks. Further, each additional coaching session that a participant completed reduced his/her existing number of health risk factors by 0.5 risks. For example, someone who had three health risks before starting the program (e.g. smoking, overweight and high stress) reduced one of those health risks for every two coaching calls he/she completed.

    This relationship demonstrates that the Nationwide Better Health program had a direct influence on the observed pattern of risk reduction, which included:
    * 36.5 percent of smokers quitting their habit
    * 25 percent of participants lowering their stress levels
    * Prevalence of obesity and overweight body mass index (BMI) dropped by 10 percent
    * 36 percent of participants began exercising regularly
