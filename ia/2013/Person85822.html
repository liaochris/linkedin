Maria Piscopo's Summary

Most popular marketing workshop, "Taking Charge of What You Charge" for freelancers, creative services professionals, associations and conferences to learn how to get paid what you are worth!

Now a member of the Eyeist Review Team and hopes you come for a visit, Eyeist is an online photography review service that connects you with the top experts in the industry, providing the feedback necessary to take your images to the next level. Visit us at www.eyeist.com

Self-promotion marketing seminars and workshops for artists, illustrators, animators, graphic designers,photographers and other creative professionals. Author of The Photographer's Guide to Marketing & Self-Promotion, 4th Edition and The Graphic Designer and Illustrator's Guide to Marketing and Promotion both published by Allworth Press.

Twenty five years experience teaching at art and design colleges such as Art Center College of Design in Pasadena, Laguna College of Art & Design and for Dynamic Graphics Training as well as Academy of Art in San Francisco.

Speaker at industry conferences and professional associations. Writer for industry magazines such as Dynamic Graphics Magazine, Graphic Artists Guild newsletter, Adbase Newsletter, CAPIC newsletter, Rangefinder, Step inside design, Shutterbug magazine and Communication Arts magazine.

Self-promotion articles, marketing seminars, managing creative services workshops, sales strategies seminars, pricing and negotiating workshops, increasing personal productivity workshops and seminars.

Maria Piscopo's Volunteer Experience & Causes

  • Volunteer Experience

    • Volunteer

      Project Cuddle
      • Children
      March 1996 – present (17 years 8 months)

      Project Cuddle is a non-profit organization that offers safe and legal alternatives to baby abandonment. We run a confidential toll-free 24-hour hotline for pregnant girls and women who find themselves scared and feel that they have no hope or that anyone can help them through this frightening time in their lives. Many are hiding their pregnancies, are near birth term, have no plans for their babies once born, and need prenatal care.
      Project Cuddle provides educational materials at no cost to schools that are interested in sharing them with their students. Our “Believe Campaign” helps make students aware of options available to them other than abandonment of a newborn. Our school video features our national spokesperson, John Stamos, as well as Mary Stewart Masterson, Kathy Najimy and Paula Abdul. It has been distributed across America. We participate in assemblies conducted to help educate high school and college students.
      Project Cuddle helps frightened girls/women through a difficult time in their lives. Some will call 1-888-628-3353 and speak with a caring volunteer, while others will email us via our web site. Once we are contacted, we listen and offer ways we can help. Many need guidance to medical care and counseling. Some just need emotional support. We tailor each case to the specific girl or woman as needed. Many choose adoptive families who have been carefully screened, while the rest will get support, guidance and confidence to raise their babies.
