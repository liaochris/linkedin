Carla Franklin's Summary

Carla is a business strategy and information technology professional with substantial experience in strategy, business development, digital media/marketing and project management. With over 14 years of expertise honed at Deloitte Consulting, IBM, and Bain Consulting, she is adept at delivering innovative, client-focused solutions. Carla has significant background in managing teams and department P&L, and is a seasoned public speaker. Before graduating from Columbia Business School in 2008, she led several Africa-based consulting projects and conducted field analysis in Nigeria, Rwanda, and Ethiopia. Currently, Carla runs her own consulting firm, providing strategy, technology and organizational solutions for clients including the NY State Senate, Pfizer Pharmaceuticals, Penguin Books and a subsidiary of the Publicis Group. Carla is also a social media safety expert who speaks frequently on trends in social media, cyberbullying & cyberstalking, and reputation management. She has been a featured guest on NPR, Hot97 FM (New York City), AOL's "You've Got" series, and the CBS Morning show. Additionally, her work in against cyberbullying has been covered by Newsweek/The DailyBeast, Forbes.com and Time.com.

In her free time, Carla is actively involved in volunteerism and sits on several nonprofit boards. She is an active member of the Duke University Alumni network and a blogger on the topics of technology, social media safety and pop culture. In 2011, Carla was one of 50 women featured on More Magazine's "Fierce List" for her fight against cybercrimes in New York State. In 2012, she was featured as one of Newsweek/Daily Beast's Women in the World.
