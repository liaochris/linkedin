David Tamaroff's Publications

  • Bottling the Free Flow of Information: A Comparative Analysis of U.S. and EU Database Protection

    • Wake Forest Journal of Business and Intellectual Property Law
    • April 2012
    Authors: David Tamaroff

    This article analyzes the current protection of databases in the United States and the European Union, and tackles the issue of harmonization.

    In today’s information age, databases are created to deal with the specific problem of information organization. Databases allow for the compilation of massive amounts of information and rapid retrieval of specific items. Both individuals and society as a whole benefit from the role of databases in providing for the access of information and the creation of new information.

    In the United States, databases are currently afforded a combination of copyright and other common law protections. The European Union has provided the database owners further rights through the EU Database Directive. There have been several proposals for harmonization through the establishment of an American sui generis database right. However, Tamaroff concludes against the enactment of such legislation.
