Jason Mitchell's Publications

  • An Architectural Pattern for Adaptable Middleware Infrastructure

    • IEEE-IRI'2003
    • October 25, 2003
    Authors: Jason Mitchell, Arturo Sanchez

    Abstract— Middleware technologies change so rapidly that designers must adapt existing software architectures to incorporate new emerging ones. This paper proposes an architectural pattern and guidelines to abstract the communication barrier whereby allowing the developer to concentrate on the application logic.
    We demonstrate our approach and the feasibility of easily upgrading the middleware infrastructure in the context of a sample application and three case studies using three different middlewares on the .NET framework.

  • Performance Comparison Between Commercial Implementations of Relation Data Storage and Tuple Storage

    • PDPTA(2003)1186-1191
    • June 2, 2003
    Authors: Jason Mitchell, Roger Eggen

    Distributed applications typically rely on commercial databases such as Oracle or Sybase for long-term storage and retrieval. In this paper, we consider using a tuple space for long-term storage and retrieval which provides a greater degree of flexibility and options for data reads, writes, join, select, and deletes. The commercial implementation of JavaSpaces by GigaSpaces is compared to Oracle for both ease of use and efficiency.
