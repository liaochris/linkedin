Steve Dunlop's Summary

As a news editor, Steve Dunlop directed coverage of the shootings of Pope John Paul II and John Lennon. As a New York radio and television reporter, he covered the infamous Amityville Horror murders and Mafia Commission trial, the cases against Wall Street figures Ivan Boesky and Michael Milken, and he reported from the New York Stock Exchange on the day of the 1987 market crash. He covered three presidential campaigns.

As a media and communications specialist since 1991, Dunlop has worked with high-level executives, celebrities, professional athletes, doctors, attorneys, and other journalists. He keeps his own journalism skills sharp by working part-time at CBS News, where he is a standby national correspondent responsible for late updates to the CBS Evening News, and a substitute anchor for the CBS News program "Up To The Minute."

In 1992, Dunlop was elected president of the Deadline Club, the New York City chapter of the Society of Professional Journalists (www.spj.org). His awards roster includes three Emmys, one of them for Outstanding On-Camera Achievement.

Prior to joining CBS in 1999, Dunlop worked for such media organizations as the Associated Press, Reuters, and Fox Television. Dunlop’s many interview subjects have included public figures such as Richard Nixon, Jimmy Carter, Al Gore and Alan Greenspan, and celebrities such as Frank Sinatra, Steven Spielberg, Christopher Reeve, and Andy Warhol.

Among his clients and colleagues, Dunlop is known as a highly effective teacher. His journalism teaching experience includes adjunct professorships at New York University and Marymount Manhattan College.

A lifelong New Yorker, Dunlop began his career at WFUV, the Fordham University radio station in New York, where he served as political reporter and assistant news director. He also holds a B.A. degree in Political Economics from Fordham. Dunlop lives with his wife and son in New York.
