Gail M. Romero, CFRE's Summary

Gail is passionate about global education and leadership for women of diversity. She is a published author, interviewer, speaker, strategic connector and networks at the highest levels with global thought leaders, corporate and political leaders, academics, and entrepreneurs to build leadership pipelines of talented women around the world. Mentoring program development founder for women throughout the world who will enhance their small to medium sized business by working with professional women and global MBA graduates from top schools in the US and abroad.

Working throughout the world for over 30 years she brings expertise and experience to support women’s and girls efforts to empower them to gain a voice. Gail is a sought after media commentary on women in leadership "The Whole Brain". Gail has served as executive video producer for "Rainmakers TV and NGOS TV. Invited to CGI, Hilton International Symposium, CGDC and other notable summits and forums she has interviewed Nobel Laureate Muhammad Yunus, Bill Gates Sr. Senator Sam Nunn, Secretary of State Condoleezza Rice, George Russell of Russell Investments, World Banks Robert Zoellick, CEO of P & G Robert McDonald, Reeta Roy CEO of Mastercard Foundation, Helene Gayle of CARE International, CEO of American Cancer Society among others Gail has advised on white papers, written op-eds and was the designer and author of MBA Women's Guide to Success at Amazon.

Romero is Vice-Chair of the Advisory Council for Seattle University’s Albers School of Business and Economics, Advisory Board for Global Give Back Circle, judges for the Global Social Entrepreneurs Competition at UW and will be moderating panels throughout the year. She is a Paul Harris Fellow, Seattle University Alumni member and graduate of Northpark University’s Graduate School of Nonprofit Management and Certification and University of Wisconsin’s Madison School of Business, Madison Institute- AHP program. She received her CFRE confirmation in 2005.

Specialties

Connecting externally with new relationships - governmental, other NGOs, media, corporations, individual philanthropy and foundations to integrate cohesive strategies for building mission and vision around women leaders in the "C" Suite and on global corporate boards. Education and literacy, international programs, women and girls initiatives are of specific interest.
