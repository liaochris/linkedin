Harold Hovagimian's Projects

  • Development of a Videographic Pupillometer System

    • August 2011 to May 2012
    Team Members: Harold Hovagimian, Chris Walker

    The goal of this project was to develop a remote system capable of obtaining measurements of pupillary diameter of a subject from a distance of two meters. The system was realized by
    acquiring images using various optical techniques. The image data is processed by a robust algorithm, and returns the current pupil diameter and percentage change.

  • Osteoporotic equine bone modeling: 3-point bending of deer metacarpal bone

    • November 2010 to February 2011
    Team Members: Harold Hovagimian, Chris Molica

    Studied the mechanical properties of deer metacarpal bones following a 0 to 4 hour decalcification treatment as an analogue for equine osteoporotic modeling. Deer metacarpal bone ultimate tensile strength (UTS) was found to be within 18% of the value reported for equine third metacarpal bone. Additionally, a relationship between acid treatment time and decrease of UTS due to decalcification was established.
