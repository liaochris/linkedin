Los Angeles School of Gymnastics' Summary

We provide an Olympic caliber of gymnastics to everyone. Coaching to assist all gymnasts of all skill level to achieve their goals. We believe anyone can be a Gymnasts and gymnastics is the bases for all sports.

Specialties

The LASG draws on a distinguished and diverse group of nationally and internationally trained coaches from over 14 countries. All coaches are required to complete the USA Gymnastics (national governing body of gymnastics in the United States ) and Osha Infant & CPR certification courses. Ten LASG coaches have Master’s Degree in Physical Education, Anatomy and Physiology, or are former professional athletes. Together, its 27 coaches have over 120 years of combined coaching experience.

Los Angeles School of Gymnastics' Publications

  • Los Angeles School of Gymnastics

    • February 14, 2013
    Authors: Los Angeles School of Gymnastics, tanya berenson

    Promoting gymnastics as a comprehensive activity for children’s development and growth, the LA School of Gymnastics occupies a prominent position both as a local resource for young people and as a national center for training world-class athletes. Area parents enroll their children to take advantage not only of the obvious physical benefits, but also of the accompanying benefits like enhanced self-confidence, concentration, and strengthening of cooperative learning skills. The LA School of Gymnastics also serves as a national training center for both the USA National and Olympic Gymnastics Teams.

    In addition to its many gymnastics programs, the LA School of Gymnastics offers a broad range of related programs, including karate, ballet, tumbling and trampoline instruction at the competitive level, seasonal camps during school vacations, and gymnastics instruction for students with special needs. LASG’s Care Program, a fully-funded fitness-oriented program, is designed for young people age 12 – 20 in minority and low-income communities. The school also serves the community by offering several clinics, among them the Back Hand Spring Clinic and the Tumbling Basics program. These clinics offer area residents the opportunity to learn from visiting coaches from around the country. Most clinics also feature gymnastics celebrity guest speakers, including Olympic Gold Medalists Svetlana Khorkina and Dominique Dawes, and 2-time Olympian Yvonne Tousak.

    Students and others can have birthday parties and other celebrations at the LA School of Gymnastics’ facilities. These parties, themed toward gymnastics, give guests the opportunity to engage in a host of age-appropriate activities, like obstacle courses, tumbling, and climbing. The facility also sponsors themed gym sleepovers at various times of the year, featuring gymnastics, dance contests, costume contests, and more.
