Dustin McQuay's Summary

The Internet is absolutely fantastic. This giant network of real people makes for endless possibilities and is constantly making me want to try new things. And so I do. Many of my ideas are crazy or even stupid and fail. But I love it.

I find a great deal of satisfaction from taking something complex, breaking it down, and writing a nice chunk of well encapsulated code to handle it. That problem is now solved. You can just call your little (or big) API and trust that it will work.

I believe in simple code. It's great to be smart, but not if you prove it by writing code nobody can understand. That is not impressive. I think you should design something, then try to simplify it.

I believe comments are often misused and evil. It is better to write simple code with very descriptive class, function and property names. Comments have a long track record of getting stale and inaccurate and they are also often a red flag that your code is not written well enough.

When I code in Java, I love how clean and exact it is, but I really love to see grassroots technologies compete with the big boys. Right now I have my eye on node.js. It's inspiring to see what that community is accomplishing.

I've been fortunate enough to attend a fantastic University where I learned not only how to code, but also how business works. I owe much to Backcountry.com who took me as a fresh graduate and taught me most of what I know. I've learned real world software development, lead large projects and managed a team of absolute rock stars. Backcountry.com is a very cool place to work.

The next chapter for me is to be a part of something from inception or near inception. I want to have the opportunity to be mostly an engineer but partly also a product and project manager. And I want to feel ownership of what I'm working on.

Longer term I have aspirations to be something akin to VP of Software Development or a CTO, but I haven't worked all of that out in my head just yet.
