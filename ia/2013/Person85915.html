Marie Maseng, LEED AP's Summary

As a teacher, researcher, listener, believer, designer and artist my career objective is to keep building my collection of tools and resources to help others develop their own path in understanding the world around them through the visual arts.

My educational path has always been interdisciplinary in nature. The visual arts is my vessel for understanding other aspects about the world that I find an intrest in, including business, engineering, and teaching. I am continually studying and amazed by the world that we live in, gaining different experiences and finding the ways they connect. Coming from a family of engineers, pilots, and business professionals, my upbringing has always emphasized that anything is possible, followed by the phrase, “now lets figure out how…” My goal is to provide an education for students that is personalized to their development and worldly perspective guiding them towards personal empowerment and vision for life long learning, resilience and success.

Specialties

AutoCAD, Vectorworks(BIM), Space Planning, Material Selection and Specification, Customer Relations, Facilities Management, Graphic Design, Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator, Indesign, Hand Drafting and Rendering, Macintosh and PC proficient, Marketing, Materials Management, Meeting Facilitation, Microsoft Office, Powerpoint, Sales

Marie Maseng, LEED AP's Organizations

  • Community Arts for Education

    President, Co-Founder
    • August 2012 to Present

    CAFE (Community Arts for Education) is a project of the Masters of Arts in Teaching in Art Education program at Tufts & the School for the Museum of Fine Arts, Boston.

    The purpose of CAFE is to promote the importance of art in education. CAFE will encourage a dialogue between artists, educators, and students. CAFE will design projects that intersect with local organizations or companies to demonstrate how art involves entrepreneurship, networking and the engagement of our communities.
