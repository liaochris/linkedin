Maya Francis' Summary

Maya Francis is a writer and independent communications/public relations professional with agency account and project management experience. She is a weekly columnist for two widely-read digital publications, and her work has routinely appeared in multiple top-tier media outlets. As a communications strategist, she has worked on behalf of lifestyle and corporate brands as well as healthcare, commercial, non-profit, and grassroots organizations.

Francis' work as a culture writer and blogger has been featured in print for The Philadelphia Inquirer and Philadelphia Magazine. She is a weekly columnist for Philadelphia Magazine's "The Philly Post" and for Clutch Magazine; additionally, she is monthly live segment contributor for Philadelphia's 900 AM WURD. Francis also writes for digital publications including xoJane, Esquire, Ebony, The Root, and more. She has appeared on CNN's "Piers Morgan Live," "Huffington Post Live," and other television and radio broadcasts. She is also a contributing writer for "Bet on Black: African-American Women Celebrate Fatherhood in the Age of Barack Obama," a book of essays scheduled for release by Kifani Press in October 2013.

As a communications consultant, Francis specializes in multimedia content management, brand management and communications strategy. Her work history includes copywriting, media outreach, scope development, research, budget oversight, event management, public relations strategy and marketing.

Her work also includes public speaking on issues related to culture and media, in addition to an educator role as the Youth Program Coordinator for Mighty Writers in West Philadelphia.
