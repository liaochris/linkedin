Christine Michel's Summary

Have you ever thought about how important and how powerful voice can be as a tool in business relationships?

Whether you choose to learn to control your voice to improve your professional efficiency, to sing as a recreational tool to build emotional wellness and social skills, or as part of a team-building session, voice control is proven to improve corporate communication and performance.

> Professional efficiency
Voice tells more about us, our psychology and our state of mind, than the words we say. Revealing so much of us, it conditions the nature of our professional relationships and places us and our interlocutors in roles, which, very often, we are not even conscious about.
Learning to control our voice will help us choose which emotions we reveal, in order to control our image better, thereby always adopting the right and optimal vocal attitude to carry our message with the most impact.

> Well-being and social links
Voice is an excellent tool for both personal fulfillment (especially for stress self management) and social ties. Choir singing brings up human values (such as assertiveness within a group, ability to listen and take into account other people's positions, ability to adapt to a context, feeling of belonging, solidarity...), that are interesting to transpose to the world of business, in order to reveal employees' professional potential and know-how.

> Team-building
Within the context of a team-building session, choral singing may be used in a very recreational way, in order to reveal each team-member's personality (stimulating one's creativity and another's spirit of leadership for instance) and bring the group to a conscience that is even more powerful when united towards one same goal.

Business voice coach, spoken voice trainer, public speaking trainer, assertiveness for women through voice.
Well-being through voice and singing, team-building through singing, singing, choir, teaching.
