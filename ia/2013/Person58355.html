Jane Williams' Summary

Jane is an independent adviser with a portfolio of clients including FE colleges, training providers, Ofsted and the Open University. She is Vice Chair of the Corporation of Birmingham Metropolitan College. She is known as an experienced leader in Further Education in local and national settings with a reputation for effective management of large scale change and for her focus on learners and teachers.

Having begun her FE career as an adult literacy volunteer, she became Principal of Wulfrun College, Wolverhampton in 1996 and then led the creation of a new college, City of Wolverhampton College, through a period of development , investment and steep improvement in student success.
In 2003 she joined the Department for Education and Skills to create the FE Standards Unit. She led the national teaching and learning change programme , the reform of FE Teacher Training Standards and policy and set up the Centre for Excellence in leadership and the Quality Improvement Agency. She was also the Dfes Champion for Russia.
She moved to Becta in 2003 to develop a new strategy to embed Technology across the FE and Skills system which has led to sustainable progress in the technology effectiveness of many colleges and providers.

She has held a number of Non Executive roles including the Chair of Focus Housing, Chair of the Wolverhampton Strategic Partnership and Trustee of the Ufi Charitable Trust.
In 2003 she was made an Honorary Doctor of Education by Wolverhampton University. She is an Ambassador for the Association of Learning Technology,a member of the Rialto Hub Steering Committee at the Institute of Education, a member of the Institute for Learning and a Fellow of the Royal Society of Arts.
Her current interests include advice for Boards and leadership teams on strategy, quality improvement and change management.She is also the lead author of the Ofsted report on ICT in Education 2008 -2011 which was published in December 2011.
