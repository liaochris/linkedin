Ayush Ahuja's Summary

I am a freelance sound recordist and sound editor and have worked in varying roles from sound supervision to that of dialog, foley and sound effects editing and recording.

I was educated in Sound Post Production at the National Film School and i also have a Masters in Electronic Engineering from the University of Bristol where i wrote a research paper on digital noise reduction.

My recent credits include ‘Dhobi Ghat [Mumbai Diaries]’; which premiered at TIFF 2010 and was hailed as a new wave of Indian independent cinema. I was the supervising sound editor on the film, as well as the sound recordist.

In 2010 I also worked as a sound utility technician on Peter Weir’s ‘The Way Back’ and helped record the production sound and ambience effects on location in the hilly tea plantations of Darjeeling, India.

Since 2007 I’ve worked on a few features as sound effects editor, most notably ‘Taare Zameen Par [Stars on the Earth]’ which was India’s official entry to the Oscars in 2008.

When not working on films or documentaries, i have been sound recording and mixing with sony gold award winning director John Dryden for Radio 4 in producing his specific brand of on-location radio drama, recorded in locations as diverse as Istanbul and Mumbai.

In 2010 I also designed a binaural sound installation for O2 to advertise their international favourites scheme.

I have a very good understanding of sound recording and post production, and its various workflows, and am an experienced user of the various tools for dialog mixing, editing and de-noising. I own a variety of sound equipment for the recording of dialog, ambiences and effects, as well as a complete Pro Tools setup for sound editing and design.

Specialties

Sound Design, Sound Effects Editing, Dialogue Editing, Foley Recording and Editing, Sync Sound Recording, Surround Mixing, Music Recording.
