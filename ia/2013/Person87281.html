Marla Gerein's Summary

Marla Gerein joined the British Columbia Real Estate Association (BCREA) as their Director of Education in January 2011, where she oversees professional and continuing education for the province's 19000 REALTORS.

See Marla's website for a list of current projects: http://marlagerein.net

Prior to joining the BCREA, Marla was the first Head of Virtual Communities with the International Baccalaureate (IB). Before that, she was the Director of Academic Technology Services at Colorado College.

Marla holds a Masters in Educational Technology Leadership from George Washington University. Her undergraduate degrees are in Art History and History, and she did some MA work on Canadian women activists and computer-based technologies at the University of Saskatchewan before moving to the US.

Marla is involved with the new Canadian National Real Estate Virtual College as the Education Co-Chair for the Governing Committee. She chaired the feasibility study for the Virtual College in 2011-2012. Marla is also a member of the Canadian Real Estate Association's Futures Implementation Team for Professional Development. While living and working in the US, Marla was active in many NITLE initiatives and EDUCAUSE planning groups. Her research interests include:

* design of formal and informal educational programming for diverse learning communities
* fostering an empowered and engaged digital citizenry
* the intersections of the physical and the digital in framing “communities of practice”
* exploring the social and cultural contexts for technology in learning organizations
* adaptive learning
* performance support
* competency-based education

Specialties: curriculum design, technology leadership, learner profile analysis, building learning communities around the use of technology, the ethical and empowered use and safe-guarding of information and technology in the digital age, instructional design, user interface design, and digital literacy.
