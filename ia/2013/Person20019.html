Carl Morris' Summary

Mae gen i ddiddordeb yn y we gymdeithasol, meddalwedd rydd a'r defnydd o gyfryngau digidol, yn y diwydiannau diwylliannol a'r sector menter gymdeithasol.

Rwyf i'n bartner yn NativeHQ. Rydym ni'n datblygu strategaethau gwe a chyfryngau cymdeithasol i glientiaid fel National Theatre Wales, Theatr Genedlaethol Cymru, Llenyddiaeth Cymru, Canolfan Celfyddydau Battersea a'r Cynulliad.

Rydym ni'n darparu cyrsiau cyfryngau cymdeithasol ac wedi hyfforddi pobol o Rondo, Sain, BBC a Chyngor Gwynedd.

Rwyf i wedi ymgynghori’r Awdurdod S4C fel aelod o’u Fforwm Cyfryngau Newydd.

O bryd i'w gilydd rwyf i'n siarad ar BBC Radio Cymru a S4C am dechnoleg a phrosiectau ar-lein.


My work interests lie in the social web, open source and how digital media can be used, particularly in the cultural industries and social enterprise sector.

I am a partner at NativeHQ where we develop web and social media strategies for clients such as National Theatre Wales, Theatr Genedlaethol Cymru, Literature Wales, Battersea Arts Centre and the National Assembly for Wales.

We run social media training courses and have trained people from Rondo, BBC and Gwynedd Council.

I have advised the S4C Authority as a member of the New Media Forum.

I occasionally appear on BBC Radio Cymru and S4C to discuss technology and online projects.

Carl Morris' Projects

  • National Theatre Wales Community

    • January 2009 to Present
    Team Members: Carl Morris, Tom Beardshaw

    National Theatre Wales community is a social networking site for the theatre company and the community of theatre makers in and of Wales. The site hosts community discussions about all aspects of theatre and art, as well as showcasing the work of the company.

  • The Radicalisation of Bradley Manning

    • September 2011 to April 2012
    Team Members: Carl Morris, Tom Beardshaw

    A multiplatform project working with the National Theatre Wales' play The Radicalisation of Bradley Manning. The play was livestreamed globally on the web with an acclaimed multiplatform design that was all connecting audiences with Bradley Manning's story.

  • Scratchr

    • June 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Carl Morris, Marc Heatley, Tom Beardshaw

    Scratchr is a social network space for artists to develop, collaborate and support each other as they develop performance art pieces as part of the Scratch theatre process. In partnership with Battersea Arts Centre, Arts Council England, Nesta, EHRC and the Oxford Internet Institute.

  • Y Bont

    • February 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Carl Morris, Tom Beardshaw

    Dylunion ni prosiect aml-blatfform i Theatr Genedlaethol Cymru ar gyfer eu cynhyrchiad Y Bont, sioe awyr agored am brotest di-drais Cymdeithas yr Iaith Gymraeg ar Bont Trefechan, Aberystwyth. Roedd y prosiect yn cynnwys straeon cefn ar-lein am y prif gymeriadau a rheolaeth o dîm o fyfyrwyr y gwirfoddolodd i gynhyrchu a chyhoeddi fideo, delweddau, awdio a thestun ar draws rhwydweithiau cymdeithasol ar ddiwrnod y sioe.

    We designed and delivered a multiplatform project for Theatr Genedlaethol Cymru for its production of Y Bont, an outdoor show depicting Cymdeithas yr Iaith Gymraeg's non-violent protest on Trefechan Bridge in Aberystwyth. The project included creating online backstories for the lead characters and managing a team of volunteer students to produce and publish video, images, audio and text across social networks on the day of the show.
