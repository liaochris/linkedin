Harlan Ullman's Summary

An author and political commentator, Dr. Harlan Ullman is past and present columnist for a number of national and international publications, including The Washington Times, U.S. Naval Institute Proceedings and on a weekly basis a column for UPI and the Daily Times of Pakistan.

Over the years, Ullman has written hundreds of op-ed pieces that cover a variety of critical national and international topics ranging from advice to leading politicians to reforming the financial system. For example, in a 2008 article titled “Too Many National Wolves,” Harlan Ullman stressed that whoever won that year’s presidential race—whether Barrack Obama or John McCain—the next president must begin working to prevent the financial collapse that threatened the US. Ullman argued that the next president could not afford to wait until officially assuming office before his administration began to deal with the crisis, as the delay would be catastrophic to any kind of sustained recovery.

Earlier that same year, Ullman wrote a touching tribute in the Times about the death of his friend and former Pakistani Prime Minister Benazir Bhutto, whom he used to advise, titled “Loss of a Very Great Lady.” Ullman’s article eloquently expressed his feelings of loss over his friend, and the blow her assassination was to the people of Pakistan.

In March of 2012, Harlan Ullman wrote a piece for United Press International that examined former British Prime Minister Winston Churchill’s famous comment about truth needing a “bodyguard of lies” for its protection. While Churchill used lies to mislead the Nazis during World War II, Ullman lamented the use of lies by US politicians to cover the truth in the decades following the war. Aiming his critique at both sides of the isle, Harlan Ullman cautioned that continually using lies to mask the truth about politically significant topics would only further undermine the credibility of the nation’s leaders in the eyes of the people.
