Dore Hammond's Summary

Currently writing pieces illustrated with my photography for 'Women's Voices for Change' and 'Cowbird.'

The writing reflects my continued interest in art and culture as well as the broader topics of travel, health, family and women's issues.

In my award winning work as a producer and director of documentary films I explore the similar themes that I cover for 'Women's Voices for Change' but in film my emphasis is more directly focused on art and culture.

I am experienced in film project management from concept to completion. My strong verbal and interpersonal abilities enable me to work as a collaborator on a creative team and gain the confidence of those I interview for documentary films. My excellent research and on-camera interview skills have produced award winning interviews with many notable personalities such William F. Buckley, Eileen Ford, Dick Hyman among others.

My work in film and photography is in the permanent archives of The Hoover Institute at Stanford University, Columbia University Butler Library,Trinity College Library,The Dubin Center at Mount Sinai Hospital,Magnum Photography Archives and Art Watch International.

I am an active member of New York Women in Film and Television, The National Academy of Television Arts and Sciences and The National Arts Club.

As a producer, writer or photographer I bring a creative vision with a keen appreciation for the business aspects of each project.

Specialties:

Writing, documentary production, on-camera interviews and photography.
