Brad Lewis' Summary

Everyone wants to buy. No one wants to be sold.

Clients buy for THEIR reasons with THEIR data; not yours. It is more important to know definitively what your clients want to buy instead of what you think you want to sell.

The internet changed the world and empowered the buyer with relevant information to “educate themselves and self qualify”. The sales cycle is shrinking; the chest thumping and big egos of the sales world will NOT survive in this new model.

Growth strategy is about using emotional and rational client data “buying behavior” to package, price, differentiate, and sell your offering. Sound easy? Many companies lack the objectivity and skills necessary to gather these critical building blocks before investing a lot of time money and energy in new markets.

Ask yourself:

1) Does our Growth strategy reflect real market opportunity?
2) Do we truly understand our Customers buying behavior?
3) Can we simply and clearly articulate specific Competitive advantage?
4) Can we easily validate the problems we solve in New Markets?
5) Are we using real prospect and client data as content for the Web and Social media?
6) Do we have a plan to retain our top sales Talent and Leaders?
7) Are we measuring and validating our Investment in sales and marketing Infrastructure?
8) Do we know what our clients are saying about us?

Your clients will likely tell us things that they will not tell you. Explaining the steps - easy; HOW you objectively question and get to root cause is a skill.
