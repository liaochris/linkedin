Max McClure's Summary

A deep interest in social expression through visual means lead Max to complete a Bachelor of Arts (Hons) Degree in Photography in Bristol.

His professional career commenced immediately after graduating, when Max began photographing artworks and gallery spaces for advertising and documentation purposes in arts organisations across the city including Bristol City Council, Arnolfini, Spike Island, The Royal West of England Academy and Field Arts Projects.

A meticulous eye for detail lead Max to be much sought after within the visual arts and in commercial advertising within the city.

In the years following this, Max worked as the lead photographer on a number of high profile projects, securing working contracts with Arts Council England, The National Health Service, The BBC and iFeatures2 with a number of selected works being published across major newspapers in the UK and abroad.

In 2011 Max travelled to the High Arctic island archipelago of Svalbard with Situations as the official photographer on the Artists Taking the Lead project Nowhereisland for artist Alex Hartley as part of the London 2012 Cultural Olympiad funded by Arts Council England.

In 2012, Max worked for artist Larissa Sansour on her critically acclaimed concept based film and photographic project Nation Estate, in collaboration with Redeye Films and Beofilm Productions in Copenhagen.

Alongside photography, Max started up the partnership production company Wide Awake Productions and also co-runs the nonprofit organisation, Bristol Drawing Club.

Parallel to his commercial work, Max continues to develop his own practice as a fine art photographer and exhibit work internationally.
