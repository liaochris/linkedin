Michael Read's Summary

Highly resourceful, creative and versatile Creative Director. Seasoned editor, writer, art director and graphic designer with demonstrable management experience at every stage of the design and publishing process.

CAREER HIGHLIGHTS
As Creative Director at Fandor, presided over a top-to-bottom redesign and rebranding of the popular streaming movie subscription service. Charged with defining the visual and interaction design of the company.

As Director of Digital Content for the San Francisco Film Society, I was responsible for implementing communication strategies to promote the mission, vision, reputation and brand of the Society through printed publications, websites and social media. Publisher of a complex suite of initiatives including websites and printed publications. Senior editor, writer and author of an array of written materials including film notes, articles, newsletters, email communiques and grant narratives.

Breathed new life into Release Print, a 30-year-old magazine for independent filmmakers. Presided over a successful rebranding and redesign, crafted a marketing campaign, and recalibrated the editorial vision of the publication to better address a national audience.

Crafted nearly half a million words as a travel expert for Lonely Planet guidebooks and conducted in-the-field research in the Caribbean, Mexico, and across the United States.

As Senior Designer, presided over the “look and feel” of lonelyplanet.com, which won two consecutive Webby Awards during my tenure. Created front end design for many websites including e-commerce, corporate, artistic and content-driven news sites.

Designed seven books of fine art and photography, including three titles with San Francisco's Chronicle Books. Served as Editor for four books of fine art photography.

Specialties

Content creation and management for web. Editing for books and periodicals. Art direction and graphic design for print and web. Production and printing coordination. Technical and feature writing.

Computer-base competencies: Adobe Creative Suite with an emphasis on InDesign, Dreamweaver and Photoshop. Broad knowledge of electronic pre-press, offset printing, and typography. Competent coder of HTML and CSS. Familiarity with a wide range of Internet publishing technologies.
