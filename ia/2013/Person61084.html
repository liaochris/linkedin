Jeff Nemecek's Summary

What's your trajectory?

So much data in and around your organization. Operational databases, spreadsheets from finance, web logs, e-mail marketing audits, search engine spend and performance information, customer support activity, bank statements, vendor feeds...

Getting sales numbers is easy. Knowing how many customers you had last month, cake.

Can you get answers to the harder questions of consumer behavior and marketing effectiveness? Do the answers inform future decisions as well as they show past results? Can you get answers to your questions in a clear and consistent manner across the organization?

Remember the old days, when a relational database was good enough for storage and access? SQL Server, Oracle, MySQL and Postgres were the weapons of choice. Add in Netezza, Teradata, Aster and Greenplum and we still had an extension of the relational paradigm to a more scalable form.

Now, there are document stores, key-value stores, column stores, row/column hybrids, graph databases and distributed in-memory object stores, most with funny names like Mongo, Couch, Hadoop, Cassandra and Riak.

Toys and lab experiments have become enterprise-class tools. Unlike the relational models, which have a canonical set of features, non-relational stores (nosql) vary widely in features, scalability and defined purpose.

Which platform is going to best meet your needs? What's the reality behind the hype? How can you tell if it's just a fad or your future?

I can help you, there.

--------------------------------------
In short, I build cross-platform data management teams with a high value:cost ratio, am adept at managing and collaborating with the entire range of difficult personalities up and down the chain, and consistently deliver direction-changing insight to projects and teams stuck in that trough of uncertainty.
