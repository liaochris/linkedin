Gary Lerner's Publications

  • Enabling item level serialization: Ten key steps to ensure success

    • Pharmaceutical Commerce
    • April 30, 2013
    Authors: Gary Lerner

    Item level serialization (ILS) and traceability fosters supply chain integrity. Whether to expose counterfeits, identify sources of product diversion, improve integrity in the reconciliation of returns or comply with increasing regulation, the ability to establish uniqueness at the item level provides unparalleled supply chain accountability and visibility benefits.
    Attaining a common, yet integrated, approach to ILS requires participation and consensus of diverse stakeholders. Consensus building across divisions, geographic regions and corporate structures, is difficult to manage and control.
    Adherence to a comprehensive, disciplined approach is necessary to clarify objectives, align priorities and facilitate a successful project outcome.

  • Serialization in a CMO Environment

    • Contract Pharma
    • May 30, 2012
    Authors: Gary Lerner, Todd Applebaum, Jim Dougherty

    Strategic approaches for becoming serialization-ready
    Many healthcare companies are currently in varying stages of analyzing or implementing serialization. Implementation of unit-level serialization is a complex undertaking, and very few pharma companies have fully functional serialization systems in place today. As a result, established models and approaches are difficult to come by. In order to enable tracking of products across the supply chain and authenticate questionable products wherever they appear, these solutions must go beyond the packaging and also incorporate distribution operations, quality, compliance, and information systems components. Bridging these various groups across the organization can be a challenging task, even for companies that conduct all of their manufacturing internally. However, companies that outsource components of their operations face additional and unique challenges in developing serialization strategies and designs, implementing the solutions across multiple enterprises, and coordinating ongoing management of an operating solution.

  • SERIALIZATION VAULT—MISSING LINK IN SUPPLY CHAIN INTEGRITY

    • Pharmaceutical Commerce
    • November 2008
    Authors: Gary Lerner

    Attaining Supply Chain Integrity, where the customer experience matches the key values and ideals of the Brand, should be high on the priority list of the C-level executive who is seeking to establish and retain a market-leading position in the pharmaceutical industry. Now more than ever, there is a compelling need for pharmaceutical manufacturers, distributors, pharmacists and retailers to sharpen their focus on supply chain integrity. Threats to the safety, security and success of pharmaceuticals are everywhere.
    Serialization enables integrity Supply Chain Integrity illuminates item level activity and enables the consumer, retailer and/or regulator to question the integrity of a packaged good and extract a detailed understanding of authenticity, chain of custody and channel integrity through item level serialization. Without serialization at the item level, similar transactions from the same lot would be indistinguishable and resultant queries would be ambiguous at best and potentially misleading or leading to erroneous allegations in worst case scenarios. Recent episodes of tampering, tainted raw materials and counterfeiting which significantly impacted the safety and well being of consumer have raised the stakes for brands not well protected.
