Erland Serra Lind Jacobsen's Summary

Currently Strategic Consultant in Online Brand Protection at Zeusmark Consulting Group, providing strategic support and planning for a range of Zeusmark clients and responsible for developing markets in the Nordic and Latin regions.

Have prior to Zeusmark Consulting Group, served as Executive Secretary of the Danish-Brazilian Chamber of Commerce in São Paolo, as responsible for the Chamber’s daily administration and for establishing and strengthening business relations between Senior Executives of Danish and Brazilian corporates. 

Furthermore, served as Business Development Consultant for international companies such as SuccessFactors and Mediaplanet, and lately as Director for INK Group, a niche marketing agency.

Hold a Masters degree in International Marketing and Management from Copenhagen Business School and a Bachelors degree in International Business Administration from Escuela Superior in Barcelona, and have lived in numerous countries including France, Spain, Mexico & Brazil.

Erland Serra Lind Jacobsen's Publications

  • "Trade & Investments in Latin America – A guide for new growth markets"

    • Mediaplanet
    • December 17, 2007
    Authors: Erland Serra Lind Jacobsen

    While Danish companies are focused on their own expanding markets, and near Eastern European markets, our Scandinavian neighbors are investing billions in distant emerging markets. Economical growth, free trade agreements, tax reforms and improving infrastructure has made Latin America an appealing market, and should for the next coming years attract and become more accessible for foreign investments.

    Therefore the time is now for Danish companies to get a share of the new growth opportunities. With 550 million inhabitants in the region, and a fast growing number of people with same purchasing power as European consumers (approx. 100 million), Latin America possesses an enormous economical potential for the future. The countries demand high-quality and fair-priced industrial products and know-how for extraction and processing of resources within agriculture, environment, oil and gas. Likewise there is a huge potential for Danish investments in IT, telecom, construction and the health sector.
