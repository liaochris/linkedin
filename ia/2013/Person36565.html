Donald Scarinci's Summary

I represent businesses as general counsel and in a broad range of matters including corporate transactions, real estate development, and litigation. I have also represented artists, galleries and entertainers in a variety of matters as well as hospitals, charter schools, and not for profit corporations.

Since 1985, my reputation has been earned representing municipalities, public authorities and school boards with experience ranging from corporate counsel to cities, colleges, schools, counties, and regional authorities to advising the New Jersey State Assembly and New Jersey congressional and legislative redistricting commissions.

I have litigated complex commercial cases as well as civil rights cases and matters involving constitutional issues. I have appeared before the New Jersey Supreme Court, as well as trial courts in most of the northern New Jersey counties and the federal courts in New York, New Jersey, Pennsylvania, the District of Columbia and California, pro hoc.

Donald Scarinci's Publications

  • David Brearley and the Making of the United States Constitution

    • New Jersey Heritage Press
    • June 1, 2005
    Authors: Donald Scarinci

    In August 1787, with the Constitutional Convention near collapse, the deeply divided delegates turned to David Brearley for leadership. In just five days, the Brearley Committee created a powerful president, invented the vice-presidency, and concocted the Electoral College. It was a crowning accomplishment for New Jersey’s Supreme Court Chief Justice, who had already fought stubbornly and successfully for a Senate in which each state would have equal votes. David Brearley was born to revolution, the son of a radical who was jailed twice for his leadership of the anti-Proprietary land riots in the 1740s. Brearley served with Washington at Valley Forge, wooed two beautiful wives, and was the first American judge to declare a law unconstitutional – establishing the principal of judicial review a quarter-century before Chief Justice Marshall’s famous Marbury v. Madison ruling. This is his story.

  • Redistricting and the Politics of Reform

    • Independent Books
    • December 1, 2010
    Authors: Donald Scarinci

    Fifteen states entrust redistricting to bipartisan or independent commissions, including California, where voters just expanded the power of their new reform panel after a referendum that pitted Governor Arnold Schwarzenegger against House Speaker Nancy Pelosi. But even the best-intentioned reforms can get snarled in politics. This book tells the story.
