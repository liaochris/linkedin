Matthijs Hoekstra's Summary

Currently I am a Sr. Product Marketing Manager in the Windows Phone Division at Microsoft Corp in Redmond. I work in the Apps Team (which is responsible to run the Phone store and for Developer Marketing). I am responsible for the Enterprise and LOB app value proposition towards developers.

Before this role I worked as Evangelist with DPE (Developer and Platform Evangelism group) at Microsoft Netherlands. I was responsible for everything around Windows Phone 7 development. It was my job to get developers enthusiastic, make sure the application marketplace was filled with great Windows Phone applications and start an ecosystem around our Windows Phone 7 platform.

Before Windows Phone I was mostly concentrating my focus around our productivity suite like Office, SharePoint, Unified Communications and topics like New World of Work.

I regularly present in front of small and large audiences. I’ve presented at events like Developer Days, TechEd and Techready (Microsoft internal event).

Before I started working within DPE as evangelist I worked several years as a consultant with Microsoft Services.

The focus was on Information Worker technology but with both a Development and Infrastructure background I was able to cover a very broad area during projects. I have experience in large Microsoft development projects as architect and development lead, large deployments with Microsoft SharePoint and Office Communications Server.

Specialties: Information Worker, Portals, Collaboration, .NET Development, Microsoft products, Windows Phone, Presenting, SharePoint, Building communities

Matthijs Hoekstra's Projects

  • Windows Phone Evangelism

    • July 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Matthijs Hoekstra, Amina Waters, Andreas de Ruiter, Bsc MBA, Wilfried Heermann

    Context: In recent years the competitive pressure on Microsoft has been growing due to trends such as the rise of smartphones based on Android and iOS, the emerging category of tablet computers and the gradual shift from on-premise to cloud computing. To counter these threats, Microsoft is introducing new products and platforms such as Windows Phone, Windows 8 and Windows Azure. The degree to which these platforms become successful largely depends on the availability of third-party apps and games.

    The challenge: how to get many third-party apps developed for Windows Phone.

    The approach: Andreas managed to build and execute a solid global program which was executed in over 40 countries. Part of the program was organizing 1000 training events for developers and designers in a period of one year. Local Microsoft employees were trained to become “Phone champs” in their country after which they could deliver their own training and engage local networks. The success of the program hinged on the way the it was executed:

    • An international community of “phone champs” was formed. They were facilitated to learn from each other and directly interact with the Windows Phone product group at HQ.
    • Processes were optimized to work well in a wide variety of countries.
    • A business intelligence system was put in place to keep all stakeholders instantly informed about goals, activities and results.

    The result: In one year time the number of apps for Windows Phone 7 increased from 20.000 to 100.000
