Javier González's Projects

  • PDS4NRJ

    • agosto de 2013 a Actualidad
    Miembros del equipo: Javier González, Philippe Bonnet, luc bouganim, philippe pucheral, Irina Shklovski, suzan boztepe

    This Marie Curie Action, called PDS4NRJ, is based on the insights that (a) secure personal data management can be radically improved with the advent of secure hardware embedded on personal devices at the edges of the Internet, and that (b) a secure personal data management infrastructure should be applied in the context of smart (residential) buildings. Our overall objective is to define a new form of decentralized infrastructure for sharing smart meter data with access and usage control guarantees. To meet this ambitious objective, the fellow will rely on the synergy that exists between this Marie Curie project, the newly started Energy Futures initiative at IT University of Copenhagen (ITU) focusing on the socio-technical aspects of Energy&IT, and the large-scale, multi-disciplinary INRIA initiative CAPPRIS focusing on privacy by design and the application of privacy enhancing technologies, started in March 2012.

  • INTERACT

    • noviembre de 2011 a Actualidad
    Miembros del equipo: Javier González

    INTERACT - International Network for Terrestrial Research and Monitoring in the Arctic- is an infrastructure project under the auspices of SCANNET, a circumarctic network of 33 terrestrial field bases in northern Europe, Russia, US, Canada, Greenland, Iceland, the Faroe Islands and Scotland. INTERACT specifically seeks to build capacity for research and monitoring in the European Arctic and beyond, and is offering access to numerous research stations through the Transnational Access program.

    We are in charge of the IT-related Work Package "Virtual Instrumentation" (WP5) and we collaborate with "Data Management" (WP7).

    Read more on our involvement in INTERACT here: http://javigon.com/interact/

  • BISP - Building Information Service Platform

    • diciembre de 2012 a junio de 2013
    Miembros del equipo: Javier González, Aslak Johansen, Matias Bjørling

    BISP (Building Information Service Platform) is a framework for working services (lightning, watering, heating, etc.) in a building. It acts as a repository for sensor data, and it export the measurement and building information using a coherent interface (RestLess). It has been developed as a platform for students to use, in the context of the Global Software Development project cluster at the IT University of Copenhagen.

    Read more on BISP here: http://javigon.com/bisp-building-information-service-platform/

  • MANA Project

    • agosto de 2010 a Actualidad
    Miembros del equipo: Javier González, Philippe Bonnet

    Design, Implementation and Deployment of the Capoh System in Zackenberg Arctic Station - Northern Greenland.

    The main goal of the system is to autonomously and wirelessly (802.15.4) gather temperature, chlorophyl, fluorescence and other scientific data from Langemandsø lake and transfer it to the Base Station, which is 5Km far from it, by means of a 802.11 link.

    The Buoy in the lake, the base station at the shore and the wireless links are deployed at the moment.

    Read more on MANA here: http://javigon.com/manaproject/

  • ITU/PitLab Testbed

    • junio de 2012 a Actualidad
    Miembros del equipo: Javier González

    In the context of Smart Buildings and efficient monitoring in indoors environments, we started the development of the so-called ITU/PITLab Testbed. The initial intention is to create a simple wireless multi-hop network that allows us to collect data related to power consumption and process it in order to actuate in the electrical devices themselves.

    Initially, we designed and programmed the base for this project. Since it grew dramatically in size, we have defined a set of individual projects for Master Students at ITU in order to carry out the different sub-problems associated to this project. THerefore, my labour in this project will be a close supervision of the projects, acting both as a Project Manager/Supervisor and as a Programmer.

    Miembros del equipo: Javier González

As a result of the success of the project, I became a member of the UVa Moodle Work Group (University of Valladolid, Spain), maintaining the project for over a year after the Thesis was finished, and polishing it until it became a Moodle Contributed Plugin.
