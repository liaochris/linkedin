Jyoti Banerjee's Summary

I specialise in creating and driving new strategic initiatives in technology companies, with most of my experience being in business application software. KiteBlue Limited is the company through which I do my consulting work.

Some of my noteworthy clients (since 1991) have included leading application software providers such as Microsoft, SAP, JD Edwards, Coda and PeopleSoft. In this area, I have helped companies such as Navision and Great Plains build UK market entry strategies.

Since 2009, I have added an extra element of focus: corporate responsibility. In this context, I have helped Trucost, the holder of the world's largest database on corporate environmental impacts, create a new business strategy. I am keen to see environmental information used by business managers in their day-to-day processes. As a result, in 2011, I co-founded Fronesys, a sustainability advisory organisation.

I am involved in Ibex, a venture fund that invests in medium-sized businesses that are committed to ethical practice - all these investments are in third-world, emerging markets settings. And I am on the board of Interserve, an international faith-based charitable organisation where I have plenty of exposure to the Carver Policy Governance Model.

Since the summer of 2005, I have researched the performance of mid-sized organisations. As a result of the research, I co-founded M Institute, a think tank focused on issues that are key to mid-sized organisations.

As part of my efforts in entrepreneurship education, I am co-teaching an MBA elective class on Software Entrepreneurship at Said Business School, Oxford University.
