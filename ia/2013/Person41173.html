Emil Hauch Jensen's Summary

I have met so many amazing people who have achieved unbelievable things that I have come to firmly believe that anything is achievable to anyone; the limiting factor is our own imagination. Of course truly great things are never done by individuals alone, they are done by people touching the lives of other people. Because we are all connected, what we do sends ripples through the system that causes other people to react, and through this ripple effect, each of us hold the power to make incredibly big changes in the world.

Simply put I believe that we have a responsibility to contribute to the greater good of all. I am no saint, but I like to aim for being better, and I like to think that the sum of all my actions is a net positive. That when the end comes I have helped created ripples that made the world a better place – this goal is what gives meaning to my life.

Here are a few other things I also believe in:

• I believe that once you know how to do something 100% you should teach it to someone else and let them do it instead. It will help both of you grow.

• I believe in sharing any kind of knowledge that would have made me irreplaceable. It is the right thing to do, and it is a challenge to myself not to get lazy and complacent.

• I believe hiring people is one of the highest impact decisions a manager makes, it should never be done in a hurry, you should never compromise on the quality of the candidate nor should you hire people who are overqualified.

• I believe everyone starts the first day on their job with high levels of genuine enthusiasm, and it is the responsibility of the company to feed the fire and not let the energy be lost or wasted.

• I believe business is about achieving something as a company of people; the bottom line number is a measurement, not the goal.

Specialties

Company Start-up and Market Entry (China Specific).

Emil Hauch Jensen's Experience

General Manager

Nilorn Shanghai Ltd.

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Apparel & Fashion industry

November 2010 – Present (2 years 10 months) Shanghai, China

I was given the opportunity to lead Nilorn Groups entry into China. What I like about Nilorn is that we turn something cheap and functional, like a price tag, into something more and something meaningful. We make the wrapping a part of the buying experience, and we do it, not by adding layers of expensive materials, but by design choices that are driven by attention to our customer – looking at the value, not just the cost.

At a more personal level I like my job because it combines my passion for developing and growing a new business with my interest in leading people. From The Group I get the basic ingredients, products, procedures, IT systems etc. but I also get the freedom to decide myself how to mix these with Chinese flavors. More importantly I got the opportunity to build up from scratch the company culture I believed would work best in China and fill it with the best matching people. So far it has been an absolute thrill.

Job Description:
Full P/L responsibility and custody of company chops.
Reporting to Managing Director of Asia (Hong Kong) and Group CEO (Sweden).
Overseeing all aspects of daily operations (Customer Service Department, Sales Department, Logistics Department, Production Department, Finance and Administration Department).

Local Manager

A-TEX International Trading (Shanghai) Ltd.

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Textiles industry

January 2007 – November 2010 (3 years 11 months) Shanghai, China

I just graduated when I joined A-TEX, but they offered me a great deal of responsibility and opportunity. I was a big fish in a small pond, and what I really liked about the job was that in my four years there I was able to reach a level of responsibility that would have taken 10 to 15 years back in Denmark.

I had a good boss who believed in me, and great colleagues who supported me when I occasionally got in too deep with escalating responsibilities while at the same time learning about a new culture in a strange far away land.

Job Description:
Reporting to Managing Director (in Hong Kong) and working together with Financial Manager and Daily Manager in Shanghai.
Responsible for sales to all customers in China
Responsible for developing new suppliers and sourcing in Eastern and Northern China
Responsible for optimizing performance in customer service and logistics department
Responsible for in-house production department

Business Developer

A-TEX Shanghai Trading International Co.,Ltd.

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Textiles industry

October 2006 – July 2007 (10 months) Shanghai City, China

I worked as an intern in A-TEX Shanghai the last year of my studies. It was an excellent opportunity to get my feet wet. I was put on a project to build up a new Business Unit for sales to local customers in China – where previously all sales had been done in Europe, the Shanghai office till that time had only been a logistics center. It was an exciting project that I eventually got to also bring to life when I stayed on with A-TEX after graduating.

Founder and CEO

Prodigium Media

January 2002 – October 2006 (4 years 10 months) Denmark

I started my own company before I was legally allowed to drive or go to a bar. It was the dot-com boom, and I launched a web design company with two high school friends. It was about freedom, about being in charge of your own destiny, about doing something exciting instead of dealing papers or cleaning trains (which had been my jobs up to that point). I absolutely loved it, and it was this early experience that got me hooked on later studying business development. We never made it truly big, and were soon hit by the dot-com bust. So after high school when we each moved on to different cities to study elsewhere I took over the company by myself. I kept it running throughout university where despite still being a small operation it sustained me, and was a whole lot cooler than other student jobs.
