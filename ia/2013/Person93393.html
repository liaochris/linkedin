The Dempster Family Foundation

Nonprofit; 1-10 employees; Nonprofit Organization Management industry

2009 – Present (4 years)

The Dempster Family Foundation was founded by Chicago Cubs pitcher Ryan Dempster in honor of his daughter Riley, born in 2009 with Di George Syndrome (22q/VCFS) a common but rarely diagnosed genetic disorder, which for her has meant a feeding tube and trach since birth. Riley is fine, but the Dempster Foundation exists to bring education and awareness to Healthcare providers, as well as resources to families facing the very difficult 22q11.2 Deletion Syndrome diagnosis.
The Dempster Family Foundation strives to bring greater availability of exceptional patient care in a collaborative health care setting to the children and the parents that come to face the very board and difficult 22q11.2 deletion.
With our combined experience, audience, and work ethic we intend to make the 22q11.2 deletion better understood, and the experts more widely accessible to every patient, by establishing 22q11.2 Centers of Excellence and encouraging early detection in children. We take an active role in driving public awareness of the syndrome and encouraging educational opportunities for health care providers and parents alike, through the foundations conference sponsorship program.
“The Parent Empowerment Program” that teaches families how to better communicate with physicians and educators, as well as provides the tools and dialogue for parent to take personal responsibility in driving awareness within their local communities.
See our website for details on all foundation sponsored programs. www.dempsterfamilyfoundation.org
