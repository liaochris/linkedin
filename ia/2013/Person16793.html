Brendan Peters' Summary

As a recent graduate of OCAD University in Toronto, Ontario, I’ve developed a diverse skillset and creative thinking process that allows me to produce intelligent and original design solutions. My critical approach to problem solving remains one of the strengths of my design process. I’ve been able to successfully exemplify these skills throughout a variety of corporate, academic, and self-motivated projects. I'm now looking for a full-time salary position.

I’m an observer of the world around me. I like asking questions. I’m active, creative, passionate, detail-oriented, motivated, complex, humorous, intelligent, and honest.

My objective is to provide passionate and exceptional design solutions to those dedicated to the innovation and continuous improvement in the quality of life for all living things. Areas of interest include, but are not limited to: education, physical and mental health, child development, scientific research, technology, entrepreneurship, clean energy, and economic development.

Feel free to contact me or visit my personal website at www.brendanpeters.ca

Graphic Designer

Macha Health Clinic

November 2012 – November 2012 (1 month) Macha, Zambia

I had an opportunity to do some pro bono work for a health clinic in Zambia on a PMTCT HIV campaign. PMTCT HIV stands for Preventing Mother-to-Child Transmission of HIV, a huge problem in Zambia because of the low status of women, the risk to the child, and social stigma. Another issue is the lack of accessible information to the public, as the majority of the brochures and posters are in English. With the help of a few nurses, the brochure and poster information was translated into Tonga, the local language in Macha.

Volunteer

Friends of Honduran Children

August 2011 – August 2011 (1 month) Tegucigalpa, Honduras

In the summer of 2011, I spent two weeks at an orphanage in Honduras. A lack of government support for health care, and a serious shortage of adequate facilities, potable water and sanitation services have led to a serious health crisis throughout Honduras. I assisted with FOHC Medical Brigades, where a team of 16-18 medical professionals provided free care to 1,500 medical patients and 500 dental patients during our visit. I also spent time visiting elementary schools in the mountains and countryside, where we provided new lessons and instructional techniques and delivered much needed supplies and educational resources to the Honduran teachers and students.
