Anthony E. Alvarez's Summary

Use of information systems to generate insights are particularly important in business. Data and systems allow managers to gain insights and deep and thorough understandings of what is driving their businesses. Transforming raw marketing data and processing it into a more meaningful form.

Data and systems are used to answer specific questions. For example: Track performance of brands and items in different markets over time, assess the impact of past programs and events on performance, understand what is driving a brand's business in each market gain a competitive advantage, perhaps even a strategic, competitive advantage.

In addition to being a marketing manager, Anthony is also computer consultant who specializes in web projects involving following technologies: Linux, Apache, MySQL, PHP, Javacript, Jquery, GiT, CSS, SASS, Ruby on Rails, Joomla, Wordpress, Drupal, ASP, Perl, JAVA, REST, MVC, ColdFusion, Python

Anthony has worked all over the US and in foreign cities like Tokyo, Guangzhou and Hong Kong. He has worked for Major Multinational Corporations such as IBM, Pitney Bowes, Panasonic, Casio, Taisei Construction, Nippon Telephone and Telegraph (NTT), Budweiser and Polaroid. He has held titles such as Software Engineer, Webmaster, Unix Administrator, Customer Service Representative, Marketing Systems Manager and Salesforce.com Database Administrator.

He is an evangelist for open source software. Currently he is a copy editor for Wikipedia project, an online encyclopedia and a technical book reviewer for Packt Publishing.

In addition to web software Anthony is learning how to make mobile apps for Windows Phone 8 and Android platform.
