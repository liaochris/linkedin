Mark Mittlesteadt's Summary

I am inspired by the natural world and the people and animals we share this planet with. I believe in possibilities where some may see limitations. I believe in dreaming big as one can never exceed their own self-proscribed limitations. Possibilities are endless when one looks beyond conventional thinking and expands their world view.

I've been an artist, musician, writer, inventor and explorer my entire life. I am fascinated with everything in life and find it all inspiring to create works of art, in all the many forms.

My experiences in life have led me to meet many fascinating people and to work on many great projects. From the years of life on the road as a musician (which I continue to pursue), to my love of astronomy, my wilderness explorations and studies, and the rich, varied life I've lived it's given me an outlook on creation that allows me to see possibility in everything. My life long study of our natural world, the plants and animals living in harmony with it and more importantly our place in it, has given me a unique perspective on life that fuels my passion to create all that I do. My passion is to create art that is bigger than the object itself. I consider myself in service to others for a greater good in the world.

My major goals are to create for the Wausau School District, what I did for the DC Everest School District, and that is an educational natural history museum at their School Forest.

My biggest dream is to create for the Wausau area, a world class cultural and natural history museum that will be a destination for people all over the country and a source of local pride and a legacy left for future generations.

To inspire others to realize the beautiful world we live in and to help them see what a difference living an inspired life makes gives me the most satisfaction, whether it is in my actions or the art I create.

  • DC Everest School Forest Earth Geological Timeline mural

    • March 2012 to May 2012
    Team Members: Mark Mittlesteadt

    18ft. x 17 ft. stairwell mural at the DC Everest School Forest

    The mural depicts the Earth's Geological Timeline showing the Earth's Epochs and representing all of the major plant and animal species that inhabited Earth that are now extinct. It covers every time period from the Ice Age (Pleistocene) all the way through the Age of the Dinosaurs (Mesozoic Era - Cretaceous, Jurassic and Triassic) all the way down to Earth's beginnings in the ocean of the Pre-Cambrian Epoch.

  • DC Everest School Forest "Wild Wisconsin" Museum

    • June 2009 to March 2011
    Team Members: Mark Mittlesteadt, Cindy Damrow (School Forest Director), Randy Schwalbach (School Forest Naturalist)), Sam Champine (3D Construction Assistant)

    A one of a kind School Forest Natural History Museum exhibiting Wisconsin nature by using taxidermy mounts along with painted murals and 3D sculpted diorama habitats. The museum takes one on a journey throughout northern Wisconsin with the ability to see native species in their natural environments, how they live and predator/prey relationships and interactions among different species.
