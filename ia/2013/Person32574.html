David Shechtman's Summary

David is the founder and president of Tru Progress Consulting based in Los Angeles. His coaching, consulting, and training experience includes work with Fortune 500 companies in the financial services, healthcare, travel & hospitality, and telecommunications industries. Specific clients include Northwestern Mutual, ADP, DaVita, Virtuoso, and the Entrepreneurs Organization (EO).

David's main focus is helping clients transform adversities into victories. His clients work in high growth, high challenge environments that require real-time learning and bold action. David helps his clients navigate difficult circumstances, identify golden learning opportunities, and establish straight-forward action plans for success. His expertise comes from over a decade of studying the art and science of behavior change.

David’s education includes a Master of Science in Organization Development from Pepperdine University. He also has taught Organization and Strategy Consulting at the University of Southern California’s Marshall School of Business. David is a regular presenter at the Organization Development Network annual conference. David's writing has appeared in the OD Practitioner journal and ASTD's T&D magazine.

Author of The Adversity Curve and The Seven Deadly Sales Sins.

David Shechtman's Projects

  • Improving the performance of a sales group

    Team Members: David Shechtman

    The Challenge
    - The organization had an underperforming group of salespeople.
    - The people knew what they needed to do; yet they lacked the structure, confidence, and motivation to take action.

    The Solution
    - Create monthly roundtable groups focused on Learning New Skills, Sharing Best Practices, and Forming Accountability to an Action Plan.
    - Give people the structure, guidance, and support to individually change in a group environment. Set the tone for growth.

  • Developing the skills of a high-potential leadership group

    Team Members: David Shechtman

    The Challenge
    - A methodology that felt flat and one-directional, a parade of disjointed subject matter experts.
    • A deficit of new, boundary-pushing ideas in the curriculum.

    The Solution
    - Establish an exciting, competitive approach to learning new material. Engage participants in new content, interactive exercise, and a competitive case-study challenge.
    - Organize a series of workshops focused on unexplored skills that participants will need years down the road to be effective. Significantly challenge people intellectually.

  • Reinventing how an industry does business

    Team Members: David Shechtman

    The Challenge
    - An organization part of an industry dying a slow death financially.
    - A need to change how its members engage with the marketplace, moving from a transactional model to an advisory model.

    The Solution
    - Create a pilot program of early adopters to chart the course for future changes to the industry.
    - Establish an innovation laboratory for activated members to create new tools and resources for changing how business gets done.

  • Future Search Project in Beijing, China

    Team Members: David Shechtman

    Co-designed and co-facilitated a three-day future-search conference with water-based NGOs in Beijing. The conference brought together a variety of environmental stakeholders (business, education, non-profits, and government) all focused on improving the state of water quality and conservation in China.

  • Get JOB READY Pasadena

    • April 2013 to Present
    Team Members: David Shechtman

    Organized and facilitated a three-day event for 16-21 year-olds in greater Pasadena, CA. The goal was delivering workshops and seminars that would help underserved youth prepare for the job force. 42 individuals completed the program and earned a certificate.
