James Eggers' Projects

  • The Vibes of Ireland: Realtime Mood Tracking via Twitter

    • August 2010 to December 2010
    Team Members: James Eggers

    In this experiment, approximately four million Tweets originating from Ireland were collected between September 21st and December 14th 2010. Tweets are short, information-rich messages that people around the world (including Ireland) post to Twitter. I mined Twitter and built algorithms that tagged the Tweets as being positive, negative or neutral. In doing this, I created a very accurate picture of how the emotions and moods of people in Ireland change and fluctuate over time.

  • FreeFlow: Automatic, Realtime Road Traffic Monitor, using traffic cameras + twitter.

    • August 2011 to December 2011
    Team Members: James Eggers

    The aim of this project is firstly to detect the level of traffic present on roads and, secondly to disseminate the information efficiently so as to help relieve traffic congestion. This is accomplished by using live data from existing traffic cameras in metropolitan areas in conjunction with data from Twitter. The algorithm I wrote detects current traffic conditions, and displays results on freeflowapp.com for everybody to see.

  • Better Examinations.ie: A really easy way to get past exam papers for Irish State Exams.

    • January 2012 to Present
    Team Members: James Eggers

    Accessing past exam papers for Irish Government Examinations was tedious at best when done from examinations.ie, so I decided to make betterexaminations.ie, which makes accessing past exam papers really, really easy! Better Examinations.ie has is also a finalist for an Irish Web Award.
