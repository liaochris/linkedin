Elizabeth Lynch's Summary

I am an experienced arts leader and consultant, pioneering and developing imaginative approaches to engagement and participation. I have successfully led creative organisations that place young people’s participation at the heart of their vision and now practice as a freelance producer, consultant and coach. Current and recent clients include: Oily Cart Theatre Company, Alinah Azadeh, Bush Theatre, Made by Brick, Institute of Physics, Wellcome Collection, Royal Opera House Bridge, Rajni Shah Projects, People's Palace Projects, Mc Mc Arts, Royal Opera House Education, and Frantic Assembly. I coach and mentor mid-career and senior arts professionals.

I led on the vision for young people as a Director at the Roundhouse 2001-8. Prior to that I established Tower Hamlets Summer University as the brand leader for summer learning for 12-25 year olds, as Director 1996-2001 and was Director of 'A' Team Arts 1984-1996.

Chair of Board of Trustees for The Arts Catalyst, Board member for Kinetika Carnival Arts, founding faculty member of The School of Life. Freelance clients have included leading performing arts companies and arts education organisations, advising, producing, strategic development, research and evaluation.

Specialties: Making things happen. Creative thinking. Project direction and management. Writing and research. Brokering cross-sector and inter-disciplinary partnerships, managing diverse interests and complex budgets. Great professional networks and inter-personal relationships.

Elizabeth Lynch's Publications

  • Dear Stranger I Love You

    • Dear Stranger, I love you: the ethics of community in Rajni Shah Projects' Glorious Special launch price £15 www.rajnishah.com/dear-stranger-i-love-you
    • May 30, 2013
    Authors: Elizabeth Lynch, Mary Paterson, Lucy Cash, Becky Edmunds

    Dear Stranger, I love you offers an in-depth exploration of artist Rajni Shah’s Glorious, an experimental performance project that began with a series of conversations between strangers and ended in a large-scale theatre production involving local residents and musicians in each location where it was presented.
    The publication brings together four ways of looking at Glorious: a short film made in response to six performances of Glorious by filmmaker Becky Edmunds; a music video shot in and around Lancaster and Morecambe by Lucy Cash; a critical overview of the process behind two iterations of the project by Elizabeth Lynch; and The Glorious Storybook, a collection of memories from throughout the process, edited and contextualised by writer Mary Paterson.
    The publication is designed to reflect a process of letter-writing between strangers that lay at the heart of the project, and is packaged within a cover that can be posted directly to each recipient, resulting in a uniquely personalised book cover each time it is sent out.
