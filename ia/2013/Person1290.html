Adam Bourn's Summary

At Adam Bourn Creative Services Ltd, we supply and install graphics for work spaces, exteriors and events. We offer a huge range of products and have 16 years experience in all types of sign systems and methods.
From manifestation to digital wallpaper, from acrylic LED signage to building wraps, we cover all things graphic.

We have worked alongside some of the biggest names in construction, architecture and events and have always delivered a first class service. We have a Zero Snag policy that we achieve time and again, improving client relations with every installation.

We also run a full creative studio, which means we are well placed for design and build works and offer branding solutions to business of all sizes.

Please feel free to get in touch and discuss your next project, we would be very pleased to help.

  • Burges Salmon, Bristol

    • July 2010 to October 2010
    Team Members: Adam Bourn

    The Burges Salmon project in Bristol’s Temple Quay is one of the most prestigious and sort-after graphic projects within the industry of 2010.
    Graphic Image were successful in securing this project as a result of their advanced techniques and applications of Optically Clear Digital Film. Within the Burges Salmon project, Graphic Image were commissioned to supply and install a wide range of graphics including;
    • Optically Clear Digital Film
    • CAD-Cut vinyl
    • Etch effect films
    • Bespoke commissioned artworks
    We installed over 3000 square meters of Optically Clear Digital Film, 350 square meters of Etch effect film, 450 square meters of CAD-Cut vinyls and 12 bespoke frameless artworks. All installed within a programme of just six weeks.
    The executive level on the 5th floor was the main focal point for the manifestation. The double glazed offices were turned into a design feature where each side of the glass had different printed patterns, which, when installed, formed a privacy screen in various densities of white.
    The 5th floor study room was a particularly creative installation. The layers of white print with bursts of colour from hanging glass panels within the double glazed cavity. All bought to life with discreet LED lighting to make the whole area ‘glow’.
    Graphic Image worked with photographer Kate Rogers (via Sheppard Robson) to create bespoke artworks for the executive offices. These ‘one-off’ pieces of photographic art were flush-mounted to a matt black Perspex frame. The artworks injected colour and tranquility into the office environments.
