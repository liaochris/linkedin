Michael Doret's Summary

I grew up in Brooklyn near Coney Island—the influence of which, I believe, is permanently etched in my work. After graduating from Cooper I went on to start my own design studio in New York. I've been told that my work revitalized the art of hand-lettering by expanding it into areas of illustration and graphic design—initially inspiring many imitators, many of whom have moved on to making their own contributions to this art form. Other than the Coney Island midway, the inspiration for my work has come from such diverse sources as matchbook covers, theater marquees, enamel signs, early and mid-20th century packaging, and various other American artifacts. Currently I run my studio out of Hollywood, CA, sharing it with wife, illustrator Laura Smith.

Michael Doret's Experience

Owner

Alphabet Soup Type Founders

January 2003 – Present (10 years 10 months)

I expanded the scope of my work to include font design with the advent of Alphabet Soup, my digital type foundry. Currently I have completed six unique font designs:

* Orion AS - my first complete font. This is a very "Art Deco" geometric, connecting script font.
* PowerStation Family - this font family was inspired by a signage project I worked on for Hershey's Chocolate. It's comprised of eight fonts, four of which have the unique ability of being set in 2 colors.
* Metroscript - an OpenType font that is chock-full of unique features, including tails that conform to the length of the word.
* Bank Gothic AS - an update of the classic 1930s font–now with a unique new set of lower case characters–in two widths.
* Deliscript - like Metroscript, Deliscript Upright and Slant have never before seen font features including the variable-length lowercase "t" crossbars.
• Grafika - an extreme Art Deco influenced font which I first created many years ago for the Merchant/Ivory feature film "Savages", and recently updated and digitized for your font-setting pleasure.

Simultaneous with the release of PowerStation and Metroscript I published limited edition serigraph posters celebrating the new designs.

The posters can be found through the Store on my website and the fonts can be found either through my website or on FontBros.com, FontShop.com, MyFonts.com, or Veer.com.
