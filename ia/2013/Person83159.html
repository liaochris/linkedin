Luc Morin, T.P.'s Projects

  • Coffee recipe batching software

    • September 2007 to Present
    Team Members: Luc Morin, T.P.

    Design and implement a coffee recipe batching system, from user front-end to hardware back-end.

    Customer was in need of a system to allow him to manage all his coffee recipes, that would mix the beans from a dozen silos, and finally send the mix to its destination, whether roaster ovens or green beans packaging machine.

    The recipe management software was developed using .NET/C#, and it used a MS SQL database to persist the recipes. If offers a series of screens to manage the coffee beans origins, the silos and the recipes.

    The batching system was implemented with rotary feeders on each silo. Budgetary constraints prevented the use of load cells on each silo, so timers were used to measure the quantity of coffee dispensed for each silo.

    The rotary feeders were controlled by a Panasonic FP2 PLC, for which we developed the program in a mix of ladder and SFC languages, using the Panasonic FPWIN-PRO software.

    Finally, a batch selection software, also developed in .NET/C#, was provided to the coffee roasting plant employees. It made use of the OPC protocol to communicate with the Panasonic PLC. We used KepServerEX as the OPC server.

    System is now in place at customer's site.
