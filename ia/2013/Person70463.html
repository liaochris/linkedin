Juha van 't Zelfde's Summary

I am a writer, developer and exhibition maker of art, music and technology. I try to make things public. I am co-founder of Non-fiction, office for cultural innovation, and of special purpose vehicles Viral Radio, VURB and Shippr.

Juha van 't Zelfde's Experience

Co-founder

Non-fiction

Partnership; 1-10 employees; Research industry

November 2008 – Present (5 years) Amsterdam

Non-fiction is an office for cultural innovation founded by Michiel van Iersel and Juha van ‘t Zelfde in 2008. Based in Amsterdam, it is a collaborative vehicle for making things public, that synthesizes interests in ubiquitous culture, urban informatics, spatial appropriaton, vanguard art and music.

Innovative strategies are being articulated through a growing international network of cutting-edge practitioners with expertise in architecture, datavisualisation, urban planning, spatial computing, game design, contemporary art and experimental music. We believe in open collaboration, scanning specific contexts and environments and finding fitting partners for the particular needs of our clients.

Non-fiction works for and with cities, museums, libraries, concert halls, archives, schools, galleries, artists, designers, scientists, and even one medieval castle. We (help) build, organise, think, analyse, communicate, position, improve, curate, develop and innovate.

Our office is situated in the former chemistry lab of the Netherlands Institute of Cultural Heritage, right in the middle of the Amsterdam museum quarter at Museumplein.

Co-founder

Shippr

September 2012 – Present (1 year 2 months) Rotterdam

Shippr is a research foundation for maritime computation based in Rotterdam. It was founded by Maurits de Bruijn and Juha van 't Zelfde in 2012, with funding from the Netherlands Mediafonds and support from the School of Geospatial Sciences of the University of Salzburg.

Shippr is dedicated to making maritime data more visible to public institutions as researchers and journalists, and more social for the general public. Shippr is developing a framework for the aggregation and dissemination of maritime data with its Shippr Datastore. In 2013 it will develop a first prototype checkin service to show the behaviour of ships, and to visualise the social patterns of dynamic objects.

Founder

Viral Radio

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Music industry

January 2007 – Present (6 years 10 months)

Viral Radio is an Amsterdam based music network that tries to connect the nodes between the ever-changing shapes and forms of contemporary rhythmculture. It serves as a vehicle for investigations into hyperrhythms, bass experimentation and computer improvisation, and tries to draw maps of all the many contemporary music practices.

It was founded in 2007, inspired by a paper by MIT Media Lab co-founder Andrew Lippman on autonomous networks and scalable, collaborative systems. His idea of ‘social mobility’ is a fitting metaphore for how dubstep, beats and other new forms of electronic music are shared and distributed online via informal and social channels like AIM, Soundcloud and Ustream.

Binger Filmlab

Public Company; 11-50 employees; Motion Pictures and Film industry

January 2013 – Present (10 months) Amsterdam

Binger Filmlab is an Amsterdam based international feature-film development centre where talented writers, directors, producers and script editors from around the world can place both their projects and their usual working practices within an inspiring environment of fellow film-makers, to be coached and supported by internationally acclaimed mentors and advisors.

Binger opened its doors in 1996 as a post academic training facility for film professionals and now - in these ten years - has matured into a challenging and unique film lab with intensive five-month programmes that focus on the development of projects and the individual talents that create them. In that time the world film community discovered this rich resource and we've welcomed to our global crossroads, film-makers from Western, Central and Eastern Europe, Africa, The Middle East, Asia, Latin and North America, and the Australia/ Pacific region.

  • Hyperrhythm at North Sea Jazz

    • July 2010 to July 2010
    Team Members: Juha van 't Zelfde

    Dimlite (Zürich) / Dorian Concept (Wien) / Hudson Mohawke (London) / Mike Slott (New York)

    In the past few years in various cities around the world, scenes of musicians have popped up with a heightened interest in complex rhythmstructures of changing speeds and intensities. These hyperrhythms are created by producers who have grown up in an ocean of sound of ubiquitous media, from computer games to iPhones, and who are connected through a myriad of online networks. More than once, these producers know the hardware and software better than their developers, mutating into architects of complex, gravity-defying structures. Cities as Los Angeles, Glasgow and London are their main protagonists, with labels as Brainfeeder, LuckyMe and Warp. In Amsterdam, Viral Radio has been active as a vehicle for the dissemination of these new electronic musics since 2007, with regular programmes at Trouw and the Bimhuis.

  • Urbanode

    • December 2010 to Present
    Team Members: Juha van 't Zelfde, Ben Cerveny, James Burke, Andy Smith

    Urbanode lets web developers connect to hardware and control it via javascript. Urbanode lets you declare a local “node” or service. Let’s say that our node in this case is “Melkweg”, which is a club in Amsterdam. We now declare a list of resources that are available inside our node. These might be hardware like having access to lighting or can also be applications using more than one hardware system to create more complex services.
    Urbanode also integrates features of the real-time web, including websockets and node.js. This means that changes to hardware will be published simultaneously, rapidly across devices enabling new types of interactions and applications.

  • Tussen-ruimte

    • August 2013 to September 2013
    Team Members: Juha van 't Zelfde, Rutger Zuydervelt, Radna Rumping, TAAK, Jarrik Ouburg, Michiel van Iersel

    In the center of Amsterdam, there are alot of narrow, unused spaces between buildings. The project 'Tussen-ruimte' (an initiative of Jarrik Ouburg, Non-Fiction and TAAK) gives these hidden alleys a (new) purpose, with interventions that range from visual art and architecture to sound and events.

    The sound piece 'Stiltetonen' was composed for the space between Prinsengracht 769 and 771. A series of slow piano phrases is played in a constant loop. There's alot of silence between the notes, giving the environmnet the room to play a part in the piece.

    The music is loosely based on the singalong-song 'Aan de Amsterdamse Grachten', but reconstructed to something completely different, more reminiscent to the piano works of Morton Feldman.

    With it's slow pace and unctuous chords, 'Stiltetonen' tries to create a place where the speed of everyday live is put on hold for a moment.

  • HIAP Suomenlinna Residency

    Helsinki International Artists Programme
    • July 2013

    Since the beginning of 2009 HIAP is coordinating the international residency activities at the Palmstierna studio complex on the historical fortress island of Suomenlinna in Helsinki. A Unesco World Heritage -site, Suomenlinna is a unique combination of diverse cultural activities, maritime environment and layers of history spanning over 250 years. Yet it is only a 15-minute ferry ride from the heart of the city.

    The Palmstierna studio complex building is rich in historical details and atmosphere and offers all modern amenities with full privacy within each of the studios and guest rooms. The Suomenlinna Studios, together with the Cable Factory Studios, form a lively international community for artists and other art practitioners visiting Helsinki.
