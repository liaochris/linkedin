Clovia Hamilton, JD, LLM's Summary

Clovia Hamilton is a member of the Georgia bar and is a registered patent attorney. Clovia is a TRAINER. She has counseled and assisted hundreds of individuals and small businesses including faculty start-ups. Clovia has 30 years of professional work experience!

Clovia founded Lemongrass Consulting in 2005 to help business organizations with DEVELOPMENT - Business Development, Professional Development, and Workforce Development:

* strategic planning
* business training
* business development
* community outreach

Lemongrass offers clients a broad range of services that optimize their return on investment and end results.

Lemongrass services include:

* private and group training
* writing business plans
* conducting organizational assessments
* conducting business process improvements
* offering intellectual property strategy
* planning community outreach initiatives (including social media marketing)
* helping businesses with marketing to government agencies for grants and contracts.

We help both public and private entities with their needs.

Clovia teaches business development, professional development, and workforce development topics. She has taught business law, ethics, and legal compliance at Mercer University, Clark Atlanta University, Strayer, and Atlanta Metro College. Clovia was an Assistant Professor of Zoning Law and Land Use at East Carolina University. She has also lectured on government contracting, planning, & intellectual property at chambers of commerce, a Small Business Development Center (SBDC) and Procurement Technical Assistance Centers (PTACs).

Clovia Hamilton has a passion for:

* women, minority, and faculty owned small business, workforce & professional development
* STEM (science, technology, engineering and math) and the transfer of STEM research results into the marketplace for economic development (university & federal lab technology transfer)
* the advancement of women & minorities in higher education and in STEM fields
