Phil Collins' Summary

Anyone who believes their software development role begins and ends with their job title is far from the truth in my opinion. As a Development Director it's my responsibility to oversee the direction and roadmap of the products myself and my team develop but as part of the team it's my responsibility to ensure that everyone is happy and fulfilled in their roles. I firmly believe that an unhappy team does not create brilliant software and brilliant software is what developers want to make. Many managers and directors take a back-step following promotion from the core dev team, personally I believe they need to be more in-step with their developers, taking every opportunity to be hands-on with the code, to guide and lead new developers whilst ensuring their seniors are continuously challenged and still learning.

On a personal nature I am a recognised and requested public speaker in the field of software development and have given talks on how to manage rewrite projects at community events at Microsoft’s Campus in Reading as well as at the University of Scotland in Glasgow, University of the West of England in Bristol and Bradford University. I have also given talks on Build Radiators and Continuous Integration.
