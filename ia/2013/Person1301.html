Graduate Placement (Analyst Programmer)

Barclays

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; BARC; Financial Services industry

May 2009 – September 2009 (5 months)

My work on this team was completely project-focussed. The first project I worked on was the implementation of a new content management system for Barclays' retail online banking site. This project was driven by a need to give more control to the business over content on this site. I worked on the design phase of the project, involving solutioning for multiple aspects of the new environment, in particular navigation, application interface layer and the preview environment. I collaborated with multiple designers and developers on the project, delivering or contributing to several solutions which will appear in the final design.

The second project I briefly worked was an enterprise code review of an account servicing platform for Barclaycard. Working largely independently, I delivered a review of the menu and general navigation against architectural principles such as maintainability, security and flexibility. This web application used Spring Web MVC and was my first real introduction to any Java EE application.

Thirdly I worked on a project to encourage best-practices in offshore resources to help prevent security vulnerabilities from arising. I was a key resource on the initial phase, to transfer knowledge about the vulnerabilities. I prepared a presentation and a sample application to give to the target audience, and delivered the presentation on two occasions to members of my team, making modifications and undertaking internal knowledge transfer where appropriate. The sample application is written in Java and utilises Java Spring Web MVC and also Hibernate to some extent - my work on this involved modifications and code review for which my work was largely independent. I then got involved to some extent with the writing of "recipes" to plug these vulnerabilities, focussing initially on whitelisting component frameworks such as HDIV and ESAPI.

Barclays

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; BARC; Financial Services industry

January 2009 – May 2009 (5 months)

Reporting to the head of UK technology governance, managed the Online Design Assessment (ODA) tool for the Architecture & Strategy Group. This key tool is used for all governance assessments against defined architectures. Worked on the day-to-day management of the tool, involving assisting end-users with issues and managing a wish list of user requirements.

Worked on two major code releases for the ODA tool end-to-end, involving requirements gathering, design, planning, development, problem-solving and solutioning, deployment, documentation and knowledge transfer. The first of these implemented a large number of bug fixes and "quick win" functionality to satisfy team-internal and -external requirements. This delivered greater productivity and reliability for both the end-users and administrators. The second of these represented a paradigm shift in the way the tool will be owned and managed in future. As part of this work, approximately 70% of the codebase for the tool was rewritten.

Had a number of other minor responsibilities including managing an instant messenger collaboration tool, performing urgent tasks around the migration to a new project management tool and working on the organisation of a conference, which involved directly working with the Architecture & Strategy Director.

Disaster Recovery Analyst

Barclays

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; BARC; Financial Services industry

September 2008 – January 2009 (5 months)

My main piece of work was on the dispensation process, where I developed a process document still in use today, almost from scratch (existing documentation was very out of date) and involving liaison with a large number of key stakeholders. Also helped with managing the process itself.

Worked on a full disaster recovery test of Barclays' main data centre, taking place over a weekend in 2008. Managed infrastructure resources from three areas, collated and delivered timing information and reported back on progress and issues during the test. Was also involved with a further, smaller test focussing on tape backup information-gathering and stakeholder reporting. I also chaired a mainframe pre-test meeting for this test.

Other major pieces of work I was involved in included gathering information about third-party contractual obligations from multiple sources, and assessing an internally-developed invocation-management tool against team requirements.
