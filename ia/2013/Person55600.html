Michael Young's Experience

Internet Lawyer for Business

Law Office of Michael E. Young PLLC

1994 – Present (19 years)

As an Internet lawyer, I help privately held companies protect and grow their business online. Unlike others who practice Internet law, my firm focuses on business transactions (no lawsuits). If you're looking for an Internet attorney to draft website legal documents, ecommerce agreements, or help you buy/sell online businesses, my Internet law firm may be able to help you. If you're looking to sue someone (or have been sued), you'll want to look elsewhere for a litigation attorney.

Michael Young's Education

Georgetown University Law Center

LL.M., International Law

1993 – 1994

Internet law wasn't an issue at the time because we were still using BBSs. However, tech advances made it clear the new library they had just built for dead tree law books was obsolete before they laid the cornerstone.
The international lawyers who taught as adjunct professors were excellent. Each was successful in private practice so their input wasn't academic theory. By the time I went to the law center, it wasn't really a Catholic institution. They had a liberation theologist (Marxist) priest as a teacher on faculty. Other than that, it was secular. Downstairs there was an inter-denominational chapel to be used by different faiths. The Muslims had a separate adjoining prayer room. Coolest non-academic feature was an in-school pub and the ability to take a beer to class. Not sure what's changed because I haven't been back to the school since graduating in '94.

Activities and Societies: international law, business law, computers, technology

Southern Methodist University Dedman School of Law

J.D., Business Law

1990 – 1993

Remember taking a Grid 286 laptop to class (dial up modem) for taking notes...and having the battery die frequently. Internet law wasn't a specialty yet. Cyberlife was connecting to one of the many BBSs in the DFW metroplex and even attending a few social get togethers put together by board sysops (old school social networking). International law, contracts/sales of goods, and real estate law classes delivered the biggest bang for the buck at SMU's law school. The rest was simply passing time for 3 years. Found the undergrad scene to be in turmoil coming off the school's football death penalty. Plenty of Biffs and Buffys trust fund babies partying on the parents' dime. School shirts with slogans like "Southern Millionaire University" and "SMU - We're Not Snobs. We're Just Better Than You" aptly described about half the campus.

Activities and Societies: cyber law, business law, computers, technology
