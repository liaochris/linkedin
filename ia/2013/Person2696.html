Andrew Ambrosino's Projects

  • SA2 Web

    • May 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Andrew Ambrosino

    SA2, the SATCOM Availability Analyst, is a Java application that allows operators of shipboard SATCOM terminals to visualize and manage the impact of superstructure blockage, on their satellite radio, navigation and television links. It is deployed on US Coast Guard cutters, Military Sealift Command ships, and ships of the US Navy. SA2/web is the newest incarnation of SA2, which centralizes provision of satellite and antenna data, along with SA2's orbital geometry engine, on large servers, and features modern, client-side rendering of the resulting antenna views and other diagnostic plots. Current clients include a Javascript-based application for browsers, as well as native clients for iOS and Android devices.

  • WhiteBoardr

    • June 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Andrew Ambrosino, John Moore, Elizabeth Ferrao, Heemin Seog, Catherine Mullings

    Whiteboardr is an interactive, collaborative HTML5 application aimed at educational markets. Whiteboardr can be used in-class with a classroom management system or used to augment online courses. Winner of "Best Overall App" at Google Chrome Academy 2013.

  • Aprenda

    • December 2012 to April 2013
    Team Members: Andrew Ambrosino

    $20,000 Winner of the Innocentive/Lumina Challenge "Design of Student-centric Websites for Open-Enrollment Colleges and Institutions"

    Aprenda is a conceptual web application designed for current and potential students. It features sections for finding a degree program, finding classes, applying for jobs, managing finances, and monitoring trends. As the Lumina Foundation specified, Aprenda makes a user-friendly program choosing interface a priority. Aprenda offers the ability to compare multiple programs, see their payoff, and see their classes. The application uses a “cart” metaphor that allows students to enroll, add courses to their cart, and checkout. This familiar process is designed to be quick and intuitive for any student user. In addition to programmed utility, the application features sections where students can ask questions and view other students’ questions and answers.
