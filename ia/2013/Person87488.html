Martha Lee Turner's Publications

  • The Gospel According to Philip: The Sources and Coherence of an Early Christian Collection

    • E. J. Brill (Leiden, The Netherlands)
    • 1996
    Authors: Martha Lee Turner

    Found at Nag Hammadi in Egypt, the so-called "Gospel According to Philip" is not a gospel in any known sense of the word and contains only a passing reference to someone named Philip. What is it?
    The first scholars who examined it thought it was a collection of diverse excerpts. Later scholars thought it contained some hidden order--but could not agree on what that order was.
    In this book (a revision of my PhD dissertation), I first re-examined the "collection" hypothesis in light of the characteristics of known excerpt collections from its period. I found that the purported evidences of a mysterious order were all typical characteristics of the collection genre. Then I examined the theological commitments of the work's contents; tracing them to a few traditions including the main stream of early Syrian Christianity, a mild and possibly early form of Valentinianism, and some more radical Gnostic traditions. I concluded that the document was an excerpt collection, the work of a collector more interested in the insight-generating potential of the excerpts than in their literal meaning.

  • "Cultivating Mindfulness in Relationship: Insight Dialogue and the Interpersonal Mindfulness Program" in Mindfulness and the Therapeutic Relationship ed. S. Hick and T. Bien

    • The Guilford Press, New York
    • 2008
    Authors: Martha Lee Turner, Gregory Kramer, Florence Meleo-Meyer

    The book in which this essay appears, "Mindfulness and the Therapeutic Relationship," is a collection of invited essays presenting ways in which meditative techniques can enhance the therapist's effectiveness and enhance the therapeutic relationship itself.
    Our 19-page chapter described Insight Dialogue (as developed and taught by Gregory Kramer) and an adaptation of the practice, the Interpersonal Mindfulness Program (patterned loosely on the Mindfulness Based Stress Reduction program and taught by Florence Meleo-Meyer and others). Insight Dialogue and its derivatives make the interpersonal situation the context and focus of mindfulness practice, contributing much to therapist training and to therapeutic practice.
    The programs in this chapter were developed by Kramer and Meleo-Meyer. I drafted the chapter (including developing its organization), revised it to meet focus and content expectations for the volume (which included soliciting new data from the program developers and from others), and handled most interactions with the volume editors and publisher.
