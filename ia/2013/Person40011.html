Chandra Kant's Summary

I train and speak on transferable corporate skills, management skills, and life skills. I mentor young adults with less than 10 years corporate experience in their careers and corporate survival skills.

I have authored "MBA Blues: What Recruiters Want", a book for MBAs who wish to learn transferable skills. I am an active blogger on life skills and corporate survival skills for young adults.

I have a B-Tech Degree from I.I.T. Kanpur and MBA degree from I.I.M. Calcutta.

I have helped in creating a unique marketing positioning for each organisation I worked in. I have helped in defining and creating appropriate service offerings and collateral based on current market demand and the organisation's past experience.

I now help in creating scale in an organisation by mentoring a second level of managers capable of running business units. I train them in soft skills, corporate skills and business sense.

I believe that people need to periodically revisit and update their transferable skills in order to succeed at the next level as past performance is no guarantee for future success. I help people who are stuck in their careers by:
(a) doing a personal current state analysis,
(b) determining their needs for growth,
(c) helping them create a path to acheive their goals, and
(d) empower them with the right skills to travel that path.

I teach in a Business School, keeping myself updated on new theories and ideas across the academic world. I use innovative ways to inculcate corporate survival skills in young graduates. I actively interact with my ex-students of my college to get feedback on the skills required for corporate survival and career growth so that I can refine the skills that I need to teach.

Specialties

Corporate Re-positioning, IT in Banking and Financial Services, Change Management, Executive Mentoring and Training, Career Counselling, Emotional Counselling, Life Skills and Corporate Skills Training.

You can obtain the first 3 chapters of my book by registering at www.chandra-kant.com
