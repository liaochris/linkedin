Julia Dando's Summary

Since joining the Society for Companion Animal Studies in 2010 my responsibilities have included managing current training provision and developing new courses in subjects such as animal-assisted interventions, the human-animal bond and pet bereavement support. I deliver and support training in a variety of mediums including face-to-face workshops and seminars, online and offline distance learning programmes and live webinars.

I have been involved in teaching and learning since 1996, with a particular passion for Information and Learning Technologies and eLearning.

I have worked on and led many projects related to learning technologies including electronic learning materials, VLEs, mobile learning technologies and the use of virtual worlds in training and education.

My early training was in equine science and more recently my research has been again focussed on animals and specifically human-animal interactions and the importance of the bond between people and their pets.

Specialties

Project management, presentation and public speaking, using virtual worlds in education, Second Life, Information and Learning Technologies, ICT, pet bereavement, human-animal bond, human-animal interactions.
