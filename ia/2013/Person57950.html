East Africa Program Officer

Teach A Man To Fish

Nonprofit; 1-10 employees; International Trade and Development industry

April 2010 – March 2011 (1 year) Uganda/Kenya

Teach A Man To Fish is a British NGO working to promote a new model of self-sustainable education in secondary schools across the world. The idea is two-fold – to introduce a more practical, experiential curriculum and also help schools develop businesses and entrepreneurial education so schools can generate their own income. Students can thus learn hands-on skills and graduate with needed experience to start their own businesses in the future.

As East African Project Officer, I worked in southern Uganda and currently western Kenya with two secondary schools to implement this new model. My duties included:
● Community organizing
● Teacher and educational developments
● Monitoring and evaluation of businesses
● Business development, marketing, and capacity building
● Accounting for all project money used by businesses
● Budget and business plan development
● Microsavings development
● Community education on leadership, entrepreneurship, education, and business development
● Organizational capacity building
● Developing East African network of schools and organizations working to implement the Teach A Man To Fish philosophy and model
● Documenting successes through photography and interviews
● Program development with Teach A Man To Fish organizational leadership

Program Officer & Volunteer Program Manager

Support for International Change

Nonprofit; 11-50 employees; Nonprofit Organization Management industry

April 2009 – March 2010 (1 year) Arusha, Tanzania

Support for International Change is a NGO with the dual mission of working to limit the impact of HIV/AIDS in Northern Tanzania and train a new generation of leaders in international health and development. I held two positions within SIC. As Program Officer my duties included:
● Overseeing and monitoring data collection for SIC evaluations, such as supervising survey teams, conducting focus group discussions, etc.;
● Developed and implemented program wide evaluation, http://sichange.org/test/wp-content/uploads/SIC-CHW-Program-Evaluation-2009.pdf.
● Identifying evaluation needs and design appropriate assessments in order to gather data to inform program developments;
● Documenting program successes through photography, interviews, and field visits;
● Assisting with monthly and quarterly reports to funders and governmental officials;
● Improving and developing ongoing monitoring systems and forms;
● Assisting with grant research and writing;
● Contributing to the monthly newsletter;
● Assisting with day to day activities in the SIC office and to perform other tasks
As the Volunteer Program Manager (May-August 2009), my duties included:
● Having overall responsibility for all Tanzania-based aspects of the development, planning, execution and evaluation of a volunteer-run HIV awareness campaign;
● Accounting appropriately for money used in the administration of the volunteer program, and follow budget guidelines;
● Working to achieve high levels of volunteer satisfaction and a high quality learning experience;
● Supervising and supporting volunteer program coordinators and Tanzanian teaching partners.
