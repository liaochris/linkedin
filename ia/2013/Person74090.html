Kenneth Brown's Summary

Kenneth Brown is currently a member of the Governing Board of Trustees at El Camino Community College District in Torrance, California. He was appointed by the Board of Trustees in December 2010 to fill the vacated seat of longtime Board of Trustee member, Dr. Nathaniel Jackson. He gained over 23,000 votes to hold his seat in the November, 2011 elections.

During the day, Ken is an Senior Systems Engineer at Booz Allen Hamilton. He is currently overseeing efforts in the Integrated Verification and Test (IV&T) area to help direct a team to execute requirements close-out and verification.

Ken has also supported the GPS-Based, Positioning, Navigation, and Timing Service (GPNTS) as PMW/A 170’s Liaison to Space Missile Command’s GPS Directorate for the MGUE Program as they complete GPNTS integration for the Navy’s lead platform, Arleigh Burke class destroyers (DDG).

Ken received his B.S. in Computer Science and Physics from Morehouse College in Atlanta, and a M.S. in Applied Physics from Clark Atlanta University. Brown joined Booz Allen Hamilton in 2011 after serving for more than 20 years as a Senior Engineer and Project Controls Liaison at NASA’s Jet Propulsion Laboratory. Brown last worked as a System Engineering Analyst for the Mars Science Laboratory (MSL) External Metrology.

Ken has directly served California Superintendent of Public Instruction Jack O’Connell as a Technology Advisor and sits on various advisory boards in the L.A. area. In 2010 he served the California State Board of Education as a Content Review Panel expert during California’s State Board Science Primary Adoption. Brown was approved by the CDE to serve as a member the Curriculum Framework and Evaluation Criteria Committee for the update of the Science Framework for California Public Schools.

Specialties

Remote Sensing; Metrology; Interferometry; Integration & Testing; Interferometric Nulling; Cryogenic Nulling; Optics; In-Situ Measurement; Science & Technology Education; Aerospace-Education Partnerships; School-to-Career and Workforce Development; School Restructuring and Technology; Education Reform.
