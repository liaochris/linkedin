Ariane Zurcher's Summary

“My jewelry is designed to be an extension of the body. It is essential for me that the jewelry and the wearer reflect each other… like a second skin … my jewelry is designed to move fluidly and temper to the body.”
Liquid gold is seemingly poured into flowing ribbons festooned with the highest quality of fashion-shaded gemstones and diamonds. Ariane’s signature clasps and fastenings asymmetrically lace together her contemporary designs, influenced by her appreciation of primitive art and nature. Many of her pieces are one-of-a-kind or limited editions. A graduate of Parson’s School of Design, and now a master jeweler, Ariane envisions each piece with a sketch and is equally able to make a wax or metal model. She lovingly selects every gemstone and cut.
Ariane grew up surrounded by art, spanning many cultures and periods. Her parents were avid collectors of primitive art and artifacts. Her maternal grandparents, Elizabeth and Walter Paepcke, played a major role in founding modern day Aspen and began the Aspen Institute, and the Aspen Design Conference. Trained in the fine arts, Ariane quickly segued into the world of fashion. Freelancing for design houses in London and New York, along with a stint at Elle Magazine, led to her discovery of jewelry.
“I am as much in love with the process of making jewelry as I am in designing it.”
