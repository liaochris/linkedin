Jason Onur Gok's Projects

  • Design and Build a Microcomputer Controlled Mobile Robot

    • February 2004 to June 2004
    Team Members: Jason Onur Gok

    The aim of this project was to familiarize students with real time systems. Real time systems can be defined as machines, which require the intervention of a computer to fulfill their duty and function properly. Real time systems are widely used in daily life in both household and industrial applications.
    The project was a hands-on construction workshop. We designed and built a microcomputer controlled mobile robot that fulfilled specified tasks. At the end of the project, our robot entered a competition against other such robots.
    Other goals of the project was to introduce microcomputer hardware, microcomputer programming and sensor technology, and to explore the methods of creative thinking through designing a real system that is expected to perform in the real world, from scratch.

  • ENS 491 - Capstone Design Project

    • September 2006 to January 2007
    Team Members: Jason Onur Gok

    Sound waves have been extensively used in underwater observations, such as depth measurements, locating underwater objects, and seeking fish. In this project, we would like to construct an underwater imaging system that uses principles associated with the Sonar. A transducer of reasonable size would be used as the sound source and detector. The return information will be processed such that several measurements will yield a map of underwater objects.

    This project involves:

    - The design of drive and detection electronics for a Sonar.
    - The design of transducer housing and mechanical steering mechanisms.
    - Computer interfacing for data acquisition.
    - Signal processing and data visualization.

  • ENS 492 - Capstone Project Implementation

    • February 2007 to June 2007
    Team Members: Jason Onur Gok

    In this project, we would like to construct the underwater imaging system that we designed in the ENS 491 Project. A sizable transducer will be used as the sound source and detector, and the return information will be processed such that several measurements will yield a map of underwater objects. The device will be tested at the reservoir on our campus, with the aim of mapping the reservoir floor, and identifying objects floating in the reservoir.

    This project involves:

    - The construction of drive and detection electronics for a Sonar.
    - The construction of transducer housing and mechanical steering mechanisms.
    - Computer interfacing for data acquisition.
    - Signal processing and data visualization.

    • September 2008 to April 2009
    Team Members: Jason Onur Gok

    In the capstone project, students select or are assigned to a project. The initial work involves the development of a needs and requirements statement for their project. Incorporates all of the literature search and groundwork for the main capstone project.

    The second phase of the project requires students, working with an adviser to develop the initial phase of the project, to test concepts and build prototypes of the product/technology they plan on developing for their business. An important component of the course is the requirement that students develop a proof of concept to present to potential investors.

  • Boeing and Climate Change

    • September 2011 to December 2011
    Team Members: Jason Onur Gok

    This project looks at how climate change will affect The Boeing Company 25 years from today (2011), as an outcome of four possible scenarios. As one of the largest aircraft manufacturers in the world, Boeing will be susceptible to the macro trends related to climate change.

    In this project, four macro trends will be identified as; Boeing's customers, the airlines, demanding new low emission aircraft; the legislators regulating the aviation industry into low emission and low carbon footprint aircraft; similar regulation forcing manufacturing and logistics operations into being more efficient; and finally increase in manufacturing costs due to rising fuel costs. Once these trends are properly identified and ranked according to their improbability and impact, they will be used to create four scenarios.

    The final recommendation of this project, for The Boeing Company to be fully prepared for the outcome of any of these scenarios, is to focus on multiple ways of reducing emissions, most importantly through research in to alternative non-fossil fuels like sustainable bio-fuels.
