Joseph Ferry's Summary

Home inspectors need to minimize risk and maximize their business’ protection against meritless claims. I provide a strategic go-to market response to meritless claims through ClaimIntercept™, a proprietary method of early intervention that covers your back and puts a professional advocate on your side against potential professional liability claims.

I have successfully terminated 97% of the over 500 cases wherein I have intervened.

I provide home inspector training through educational videos and corresponding case studies at joeferry.com, and a detailed look at the industry from a legal perspective with the online Law and Disorder Seminar, a 35-video home inspector training course available at my website.

Since 2006, I have also been touring the nation and Canada, providing this 4-hour home inspection training seminar that teaches professional home inspectors how to reduce their risk profile and how to respond effectively to professional negligence claims.

Both the InterNational Association of Certified Home Inspectors (InterNACHI) and the American Society of Home Inspectors (ASHI) have approved this acclaimed home inspection training seminar for four continuing education credits, as have the states of New Jersey, Texas, Illinois and Tennessee and the Commonwealth of Massachusetts.

I am also General Counsel for the InterNational Association of Certified Home Inspectors (InterNACHI) and have written extensively and lectured widely on the issues facing home inspectors in our litigious culture. You can find over 100 articles on a wide range of issues facing home inspectors at http://joeferry.com.

I am a graduate of the University of Notre Dame and the Temple University - James E. Beasely School of Law. I am a former Army officer [FAOCS Class 8-69] and served as a Company Commander in Korea at age 23 and as a member of the Commanding General's Staff at Fort Campbell, Kentucky. I also hold a commercial pilot’s license with multi-engine and instrument ratings.
