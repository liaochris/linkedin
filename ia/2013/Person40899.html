Elliott Cennamo's Summary

Elliott Cennamo is a Motion Graphics designer living in and working out of Columbus, Ohio. Elliott studied Motion Graphics Design and Typography at East Tennessee State University, one of the country's top schools for Digital Medias. Since his gradation with honors in 2009, Elliott has had the pleasure of working with some of the world's top brands including Eli Lilly, Bayer, Nationwide Insurance, Battellle, Elmers, Abbott, United Way, Alere, and the State of Ohio. In addition to being Emmy nominated, Elliott's work has entertained in major sports arenas, been broadcast nationwide, and been used to spur changes in public policy.

Specialties

Motion Graphics, Compositing, Typography, Particles and Dynamics, Editing, Art Direction and Design

Elliott Cennamo's Projects

  • Ohio Opiate Awareness

    • October 2011 to November 2011
    Team Members: Elliott Cennamo, ryan brinkerhoff, Martim Thomaz

    When the State of Ohio's Department of Alcohol and Drug Addiction Services wanted to convey to lawmakers the seriousness of Ohio's Opiate problem in a plea for action, they turned to a motion infographic to make their case. Images and type transition into one another while toned down colors and music convey the seriousness of the situation in this colaboration with Origo Branding Company.

    This piece presented the interesting challenge of turning a lot of information into a piece that not only informs the viewer, but keeps their attention as well. Kinetic elements and unique transitions not only provide visual interest, but help to make connections between disparate pieces of information.

  • Flying Horse Farms' "Quarters"

    • February 2012 to February 2012
    Team Members: Elliott Cennamo, Victor Matkovic

    Flying Horse Farms is a getaway camp for children with serious illnesses. As part of their giving campaign, they collect the proceeds for two months from the parking meters at a local shopping center. This video explains not only how the idea works but it's expected impact as well. Scenes depicting the campaign transform into graphs and back again in this fun example of motion data visualization.

  • Nationwide Jack Hanna Promo

    • April 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Elliott Cennamo, Vicki Casa-Egan

    Being involved in a shoot with local animal-man and celebrity Jack Hanna was exciting enough, but working on the art direction and graphics for it was a real pleasure as well. The nature video like quality of it lent itself to graphics that were fully integrated into the scene as virtual prop pieces. Camera motion was tracked and solved and digital were put into place complete with shadows, reflections and perspective.

  • "Why Nationwide?" Campaign

    • January 2011 to February 2011
    Team Members: Elliott Cennamo, Vicki Casa-Egan, Will Woolf, Natalya Borack

    As Nationwide Insurance sought to expand its talented workforce, it made this series of Emmy Nominated videos highlighting different departments within their organizational structure. Figures are drawn in, then come to life as employees talking about their positive experience working for the company.

    Each face was traced by hand, then those drawings were scanned in and each stroke was re-created into an animation. While sometimes tedious work, I'm certainly happy with the results. A few unique challenges presented themselves in making the transition from purely motion elements to the the live videos. Camera motion is matched before and after, and each face even fills in with a bleed of that departments characteristic color.

  • Battelle For Kids' "Celebrate Teaching"

    • June 2012 to June 2012
    Team Members: Elliott Cennamo, Ryan Brinkerhoff, Nicole Fansler

    To further Battelle's Committment to honoring great education, an award ceremony was thrown to honor 83 excellent teachers at which this video was played to introduce the event and describe the how and why these teachers were chosen. Using a simple but colorful pallette and featuring complex movement giving life to elements from their logo, this video keeps viewer interest by providing powerful visual metaphors and dynamic action.
