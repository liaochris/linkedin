Matthew Goldstein's Summary

Seasoned International Trade Attorney with a practice focused on export controls and economic sanctions law, including enforcement, corporate compliance, and investigations involving export controls (ITAR & EAR) and economic sanctions (OFAC).

Specific expertise in:

• interacting with government agencies on behalf of clients

• performing internal audits and investigations for voluntary disclosures

• effectively managing violations of U.S. international trade law and regulations

• representing companies in due diligence for mergers, acquisitions, and divestitures

• compliance program development and implementation

• commodity jurisdiction, advisory opinion, and other agency submissions

• internal company training

Matthew Goldstein's Publications

  • U.S. Trade with North Korea: the Sanctions, Recent Transactions, and the Contradictions

    • WorldECR: The Journal of Export Controls and Sanctions
    • September 2013
    Authors: Matthew Goldstein

    United States sanctions against North Korea have not curbed North Korea’s pursuit of weapons of mass destruction (‘WMD’), criminal activities, or its ongoing human rights violations. However, perhaps indicating some effects of the sanctions on behaviour, recent licence requests highlight actions taken by the North Korean government that contradict the government’s core ideology, known as ‘Juche’.
