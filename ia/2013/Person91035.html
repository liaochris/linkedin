Bill Meili's Summary

Military law and criminal defense attorney with national and international reach. Extensive experience and success in saving or extending clients' careers, or preserving and protecting a client's future. More than 26 years of active duty and reserve legal experience as an Army Judge Advocate. Retired Colonel 0-6. Former commander and felony prosecutor, now in my 20th year of private practice.

Specialties: Professional license defense for health care providers, commercial pilots, and other licensed professionals; Show Cause Boards of Inquiry, Involuntary Mobilization Defense, Officer Resignations; hardship discharges; LOD Re-Investigations and Determinations, Security Clearance defense and responses to LOIs and SORs; IRR involuntary mobilization Defense, defense of any adverse military action which could result in an involuntary separation with a less than honorable discharge; and comprehensive criminal defense in federal, state courts nationally, and military courts-martial worldwide.

Bill Meili's Experience

Owner

Law Offices Of William C. Meili & Associates

February 1993 – Present (20 years 6 months)

Firm Profile:

Our firm handles significant and often complex military and civilian legal matters in the following core areas: (1) Comprehensive Military Law representation, including Boards of Inquiry, Intent to Eliminate, and Admin Separation Boards in all services; unqualified resignations for both obligated and non-obligated officers, hardship discharges for all ranks, career-saving, administrative separation defense, GOMOR relief, defense from involuntary mobilizations, and court-martial criminal defense for all services; (2) Civilian Law representation covering virtually all aspects of Security Clearance defense and Professional License protection for health care professionals, commercial pilots, and other professional license holders nationwide; and (3) Civilian Criminal Defense (in federal and state courts nationwide).

While our principal offices are in Dallas and Fort Worth, we have the capability, resources, and networks to handle cases successfully throughout the United States and globally.

Judge Advocate

United States Army Reserve

Government Agency; 10,001+ employees; Military industry

May 1987 – December 2007 (20 years 8 months)

Bill Meili is a graduate of the Army Judge Advocate General's Basic and Advanced Courses, The Army Command and General Staff College, and the Army Civil Affairs Officer Advanced Course. He was twice selected to attend the Army War College before he retired in December 2007 with the rank of Colonel. He served more than 26 years as an active duty and reserve Judge Advocate, and held a number of leadership positions within the organization. His last active U.S. Army Reserve assignment was Commander, 2D Legal Support Organization, New Orleans, LA, from 2 April 2005 to 8 December 2007. He transfered to the Retired Reserve effective 31 December 2007.

Judge Advocate

US Army

Government Agency; 10,001+ employees; Military industry

January 1982 – January 1985 (3 years 1 month)

Military Affiliation:
Bill Meili served on active duty as a Trial Defense Counsel (military defense lawyer) with the Army's Trial Defense Service (TDS) in Yongsan, Seoul, South Korea, then PCSd to the 25th Infantry Division at Schofield Barracks, Hawaii where he served as Brigade Legal Advisor and Trial Counsel (military prosecutor). He prosecuted Uniform Code of Military Justice (UCMJ) offenses as a military lawyer and court-martial trial counsel. His last active reserve assignment was as Commander, 2D Legal Support Organization, (LSO), New Orleans, Louisiana.
