Karin Klenke, Ph.D.'s Publications

  • Integrating Leadership and Spirituality in the Workplace through Coalescing Values and Identity Transformations

    • International Leadership Journal
    • August 15, 2013
    Authors: Karin Klenke, Ph.D.

    This article delivers, through a series of increasingly complex models, a theoretical franework aimed at the integration of leadership and spirituality. The integration of leadership and spirituality is attained through coalescing, hierarchically structured values ranging from first- to higher-order values that reflect the leader's cognitive, psychological and emotional maturity which combine with transformations from self identity,to leader identity, and spiritual identity. Contributions of this research to the extant leadership and spirituality literature are discussed.

  • Women in Leadership: Contextual dynamics and boundaries

    • Emerald Limited
    • April 25, 2011
    Authors: Karin Klenke, Ph.D.

    This book examines women leaders in different contexts including politics, corporate, technology, media, sports, military and global. Each chapter opens with a vignette of an extraordinary women leader illustrating the contextual dynamics of her setting, presents a contextual analysis, and discusses challenges, controversies and paradxes germane to the context.

  • Leadership at the core of corporate social responsibility: A multi-level process model.

    • International Leadership Journal
    • July 1, 2011
    Authors: Karin Klenke, Ph.D.

    The basic premise of this article is that leadership not been sufficiently examined as a central construct in theoretical formulations of corporate social responsibility (CSR) or in empirical research. Therefore, the purpose of this article is to fill this gap by articulating a theoretical model which incorporates the following constructs as antecedents of corporate social responsibility; (1) leader integrity; (2) ethical work climates; and (3) authentic leadership with accountability serving as the moderator variable. The model is intended to broaden existing models CSR and contribute to authentic leadership theory which is in the early stages of conceptual development by providing a set of antecedent constructs for which reliable and valid measures exist. Recommendations for empirical tests of the model are discussed.

  • Coaching and leadership fitness: An Adlerian holistic approach to leadership effectiveness.

    • Leadership review
    • April 20, 2011
    Authors: Karin Klenke, Ph.D., Barrett, P., Haugh, J.

    This paper presents the current thinking from the literature on holistic coaching techniques starting with a review of the basic concepts of Adlerian therapy in support of today’s coaching techniques, a body of interventions that emphasize individual growth, rather than remediating deficiencies. We also present a discussion of the central concepts of the positive psychology movement identifying parallels between principles of Adlerian therapy and positive psychology constructs, such as optimism and resilience. In the last section of the paper, we discuss various aspects of leadership fitness including physical and psychological fitness to establish the linkage between leadership fitness and leadership effectiveness. A comprehensive model is presented that integrates the constructs of interest, and future research directions are addressed.

  • Trilogy of the leader’s mind: Emotional, spiritual, and cultural intelligences.

    • Journal of Management Systems
    • January 30, 2011
    Authors: Karin Klenke, Ph.D.

    A tripartite classification of the leader’s mind is presented here to include emotional, cultural and spiritual intelligences as key elements of leadership effectiveness. This paper traces the intelligence – leadership relationship from early conceptualization of intelligence as an analytical competence through Gardner’s model of multiple intelligences to the current proposal of a tripartite model of the leader’s mind that combines traditional analytic ability with three different non-IQ intelligences – emotional intelligence, cultural intelligence, and spiritual intelligence.

  • Failed Moral Decision Making in High Profile Leaders: A Social Cognitive Study of William Jefferson Clinton’s Political Leadership

    • International Leadership Journal
    • March 25, 2009
    Authors: Karin Klenke, Ph.D., Allen, V.

    High profile or exceptional leaders garner recognition, wield extraordinary influence, and possess the potential to mobilize human work and resources toward epic achievement; yet for some, their failure can be as spectacular as their success. Ironically, the exhilarating and chaotic psychological and social dynamics of high profile leadership create an ideal medium for not only success but also failure. This article explores psychological, social, and organizational variables related to moral failure of a high profile political leader, former US President William Clinton. Clinton’s failed moral decision making was examined through a qualitative content analysis of self-exonerating and non self-exonerating statements made over a period of time starting 12 months prior to his impeachment trial until several years afterwards. The results of content analysis revealed that the majority of Clinton’s statement served self-exonerating rather than non self-exonerating statements. Implications for leadership theory and future research are discussed.

  • Qualitative Research in the Study of Leadership

    • Emerald Limited
    • March 1, 2008
    Authors: Karin Klenke, Ph.D.

    This book chronicles the gradually increasing legitimacy of qualitative inquiry in leadership studies, a discipline in which the hegemony of the quantitative paradigm continues to prevail. A variety of qualitative methods are presented including case study, interviews, content analysis and mixed methods along with underutilized methods such as phenomenology, ethnography, grounded theory and image/arts based research methods.
