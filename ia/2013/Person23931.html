Christian Kettmann's Summary

My passion for IT began with playing around as a child with Commodore VC20, ZX81, Spectrum and C64. Following actively the domestication of computers, I learned myself how to install and tune first versions of DOS, Windows... Besides exchanging messages over BBS and internet, I developed all kind of applications with Basic, Visual Basic and Turbo Pascal. As a student, I worked in my holidays as an IT developer for an assurance company.

It was obvious - I wanted to make a profession out of my passion.

As everybody told me that I had the ability and the necessary nerves for explaining IT to users, I found the adequate job at CFL as an internal IT instructor. In addition to this, I did helpdesk for a variety of applications and developed some client-server applications.

After 4 years near "userland", I needed a new challenge and did a career change towards system administration - a change I will never regret.

As a member of a small team, I learned almost every aspect of administration : unix systems, database, backup and storage. My ability to focus 100% on every given task and my talent for evaluating problems and finding solutions helped me a lot during these years. Dealing with unexpected hardware failures, battling against built-in flaws of software layers, recovering from accidental data loss in databases and leading several IT projects, built up my strong IT experience.

Besides my role as a head dba, I was participating intensively in the design process of current/future CFL IT systems for central applications, backup/recover/archiving infrastructure, storage needs and disaster recovery plans.

In 2012, I was given the opportunity to take over lead of production systems departement and I accepted this position ready to face the responsibilities for that post.
