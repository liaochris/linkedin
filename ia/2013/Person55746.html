Rajat Kumar's Experience

Innovation Research & Robotic-Interface Design Intern

Vorwerk & Co. KG

Privately Held; 10,001+ employees; Consumer Goods industry

January 2011 – May 2011 (5 months) Wuppertal, Germany

Intern at Vorwerk Elektrowerke's (GmbH) R&D Center, working in the area of technology management and programming. Responsible for development of a computer interface to communicate with the Kobold VR100, a new robotic product offering from Vorwerk. The interface was created using LabVIEW and was intended for testing commercial prototypes before market introduction of the product.

Carried out project-work towards bachelor thesis in the sphere of innovation management. The objective of the study was to carry out technology-maturation analysis in light of the Kobold VR100, employing Gartner Inc.'s Hype Cycle model. The hype cycle is a technology management instrument that is unique in it’s capability of permitting analysis of technology maturity, under the premise of the market visibility of the innovation. This model is revered to be a critical tool for technology adoption and in-house development, in light of market expectations. The research also entailed in a review of six other technology management frameworks, in order to establish the utility of the hype cycle model. This was concluded by a presentation to senior management, sketching out a strategy to implement the instrument in technology management by the company.

Intern

PPC S.A.

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; Oil & Energy industry

July 2009 – August 2009 (2 months) Florina, Greece

Employed as an intern at the Operations Department of the Meliti Thermal power-station in Western Macedonia, Greece. Followed lectures and practical training, intended to develop a basic understanding of the schema of the power-plant systems and installations. Detailed study of the Procontrol-P system was conducted through manuals and provided literature, to develop an operational knowledge of the system. The Procontrol-P system is a digital process control system which combines innovation and broad functionality with established operational reliability. It’s a software interface designed by ABB which facilitates the transfer of data between process operating stations and the Plant Information (PI) System, and is utilized in the automation of plant control systems. Also, a brief study of the various carbon capture and sequestration (CCS) technologies and processes was also conducted towards the end of the internship period.
