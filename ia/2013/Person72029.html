Karen Batten's Summary

Karen Batten blends visual art, print and web design using a participatory approach with groups and individuals. This process happens by means of dialogue, workshops and training to facilitate concept creation through to the final product.
Drawing from 19 years experience, Karen has developed a highly effective approach to consulting with clients and collaborating with their service users to provide skill, concept and image development. This process can involve mentoring and/or training with staff or service users.
Karen is based in Turrbal and Jagera Country (Brisbane) and works throughout Brisbane and Australia.

Specialties

Collaborative processes in Marketing, Visual Arts, Circus, Graphic and Web Design.

Karen Batten uses a distinct process of service, involving consultation and collaboration in developing creative products. This process places clients and service users at the centre of the creative process through dialogue, workshops and training which is applicable to a range of clients including individuals, organisations and businesses. Karen creates opportunities for the service users, members and staff to be involved in the creative process, where-ever the interest lies. This process of greater consultation and collaboration has proven to not only yield greater results, but also creates a climate of creativity within organisations, ensures client’s ideas are utilised and fosters a sense of creative ownership over the final product. Karen weaves evaluation and reflection into every project to continuously improve the process.

Karen Batten's Projects

  • Inala Photo Mural

    • July 2010 to September 2010
    Team Members: Karen Batten, Melly Nio

    The Changing Face of Inala Mural is a collaborative artwork celebrating Inala and its vibrant community.
    Seven young artists from Glenala and Forest Lake High School collaborated with established artists in concept development, photography and design. The eight large portraits represent a cross section of the Inala community.
    Collaborating artists: Jessica Bell, Shaun Rogerson, Hao (Alex Tran) Luong, Andre Jeremy Poutasi, Jayd Pugh, Cody Wood, Samantha Holzberger, Melly Nio, Karen Batten and Anthony Anderton.
    An initiative of Brisbane City Council supported by The Lord Mayor’s Suburban Initiative Fund and Richlands Ward, in collaboration with Glenala State High School and Forest Lake State High School.

  • Bowral Yoga Studio Website

    • April 2012 to May 2012
    Team Members: Karen Batten, Kate Pell

    Bowral Yoga Studio Website was created to establish Kate Pell's new studio and classes. Created in wordpress, Kate is now able to make all the timetable and text edits in her own time.
