Fernando Martins' Summary

With a large background on hardware and computer systems administration, and after starting a small company specialized in high-quality, custom-made computers for designers, architects and engineers, held a "Web Project Manager" position at Seara.com (www.seara.com), for 3 years, gaining a whole lot of experience in that area - the web.

In June 2008 co-founded a web company - www.muchBeta.com - specialized in SaaS (http://en.wikipedia.com/wiki/SaaS). muchBeta has now 3 products on the market: LawRD, for law firms (www.lawrd.com) Teepin, an idea management system (www.teepin.com) and Yoomit, so you can organize your meetings (www.yoomit.com).
Before leaving the company wrote the specification of a 4th product, but at the time it wasn't in true SaaS model.

In early 2011 started at one of the largest portuguese groups - Sonae (www.sonae.pt), which business relies mostly in consumer goods retail. It's a bricks-and-mortar group, but with lots of other companies too, like IPTV services, newspapers and an auctions website.

Held the position of E-Commerce Product Manager, regarding the specialized retail brands. Was the technical manager for the new internationalization project, from market research and analysis to the implementation phase.

Now bridges the Electronics division roadmap with the IT designs and efforts needed to accomplish it, with the position of Business Partner (for E-commerce).

Specialties: E-commerce, SaaS, internet, usability, UX - user experience, design, UI - user interface specification and design, information technology, e-solutions, web tools, social networks, professional productivity tools, business management tools, innovation, quality, project management, product management.

Co-Founder, Co-Owner, CTO

Prostation Systems

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Computer Hardware industry

June 2002 – May 2005 (3 years) Porto Area, Portugal

A close friend and I decided we could do better on high-end personal computers for designers, architects, engineers and gamers. So we started "Prostation Systems". "Prostation" stands for "professional workstation" and we based it on a sturdy concept that put customization, customer service and quality above anything else.

We "thought" there was a market for those high-quality dedicated machines in our country - Portugal - but we were wrong. At the time there was a market for it in the USA and in the UK, definitely, and perhaps in other countries, but not in Portugal, unfortunately.
Here, gamers built their own machines because it was cheaper and 99% of image professionals worked with "white boxes" - no-brand, generic machines, because they were really cheap.
The other 1% bought only wide accepted and recognized brands like HP and Dell.

So we went out of business, after less than 3 years, and went on pursuing different careers.
