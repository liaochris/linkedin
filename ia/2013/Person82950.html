Luan Wise's Projects

  • Pharmacy Management National Forum

    • 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Luan Wise

    Pharmacy Management is a long-standing and highly regarded quarterly publication for healthcare professionals, first published in 1985.

    In 2012 the team launched their inaugural national forum, in collaboration with the Department of Health. The forum was established so the people setting the pace and direction of the NHS could directly inform professionals working in both the NHS and the pharmaceutical industry.

    I was commissioned by Bright, the event production and management company, to provide digital marketing support.

    I coordinated the development of a forum microsite, which incorporated a blog, and posts were used to generate discussions in a newly formed LinkedIn group. Both the microsite and the blog were promoted on Twitter.

    Email campaigns were created to communicate the details of the forum, such as registration and session bookings, and over 700 delegates attended the event on 15 November 2012.

  • Homerton College, University of Cambridge

    • 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Luan Wise

    The newest college in the Cambridge collegiate system, Homerton College, is the largest in terms of student numbers. It also has more green space than any other college (and they allow you to walk on the grass!).

    An outdated website called for a new strategy and design solution, to be based around a content management system (Drupal) for ease of updating.

    University websites need to appeal to a number of key stakeholders, and include a lot of information. Their audience includes:

    - Potential students (plus their parents and teachers)

    - Existing students

    - Alumni

    The project began with a review of content and a redesign of the navigational structure to satify the needs of these three key groups.

    The home page was designed to direct visitors quickly and easily to the information they were seeking.

    Video was used to enhance the site’s written content, including vox pops with staff and students, showcased college facilities and events, and highlights of the city. The college’s YouTube channel attracted over 7,000 views within the first month.

  • Cambridge Ambassador Programme

    • 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Luan Wise

    Conference Cambridge is a free venue-finding service for the University, Colleges, hotels and other unique venues in and around Cambridge

    Ambassador Programmes have proved successful in cities such as Newcastle, Gateshead, Edinburgh and Liverpool, where they have attracted events, visitors and significant revenue to the area, and Conference Cambridge decided to establish a similar programme.

    The Cambridge Ambassador Programme, open to members of professional bodies, associations, societies, institutes and federations, launched on 14 June 2012.

    I managed the design and production of brochures, and a short video to promote the programme.

    Prior to the launch, Conference Cambridge secured a series of bids to hold events in the city. These events are collectively worth £1.2 million, and a further £5 million to the local economy. The Cambridge Ambassador Programme aims to exceed this over the next few years.

Luan Wise's Publications

  • Sales Enablement Best Practice Guide

    • B2B Marketing
    • July 8, 2013
    Authors: Luan Wise, Gemma Huckle, Charlotte Graham-Cumming, Bjorn Willemsens, Graham Wylie, Adam Sharp

    The term ‘sales enablement’ has been around in the US since 2009, with the concept resonating in Europe over the last few years. But what is it, why is it needed and how does it work? Sales enablement is a cultural shift in the way sales and marketing interact; providing a unique opportunity to redefine their relationship with sales and increase their strategic value.

    This best practice guide looks at the origins and definitions of sales enablement, how to leverage buyer insights, challenges marketers are likely to face, establishing solid relationships to make enablement successful and what technology to implement and when. Five case studies showcase the successful sales enablement in practice.
