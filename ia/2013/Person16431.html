Brandon Alderman's Summary

Ever since my childhood, my mind has raced with ideas to make processes simpler, more efficient, to simply make more sense. It was the mix of pulling apart the family computer to see how it functioned and then convincing my parents to take me down to buy some new parts so I could make it run even better.

My mind still races with this idea when I see new companies coming and going, new ideas rising and falling and I realize: it's all part of the design and development process. Could the project have been managed better? Maybe the production line could have been simplified? Maybe the team wasn't a team at all...

No organization is perfect. Not all teams have everything they need to succeed in every project.

But I come in where those companies need a solid mixture of soft-technical skills to fuse with their mostly technical team. My passion is to continue oiling the machine or if necessary, to invent a new oil.

In every company, we should all be Producers. Staying organized and on task is an individual effort but it's always great to have the accountability of an official Producer. From my experience I've learned the following ideas that add to my professional value:

● Execute. Without execution, ideas die. Focus on execution.
● Those who are stagnant, stink. Believe in constant research and improvement.
● Taking credit is overrated. Success in the journey and project is foremost.
● A team without leaders is D&D without a DM. There is no game.
● Be adaptable. Changes will come, don't be left behind.
● Prepare for the future, don't put all of your resources into one project all of the time.
● Enjoy what you do or move on. Others who do enjoy it will fill in for you and do a better job.

If your team is in need of a producer, feel free to email me at Alderman.Brandon@Gmail.com

Specialties: Agile Development, Project Management, Team Development, Composure, Design, Organized, Expedient, Adaptable, Task Management
