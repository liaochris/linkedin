Denise Pietzsch - Director, EPIC, LLC's Summary

PROVIDING PERSONAL SKILLS FOR GLOBAL SUCCESS

Incivility has reached "epic" proportions. Embrace the Power of Civility!

The "e.p.i.c." mission is to bring confidence, civility, refinement, and success to this global generation through etiquette education.

TRAINER, SPEAKER, AUTHOR, CONSULTANT - Denise creates positive energy and promotes excellence working across cultures and generations. She assesses the needs and personalizes her work for businesses, individuals, colleges, and major organizations. She works with associations and groups for their conventions, conferences, trade shows, and international events.
Her passion is to make a difference in the individual brand that people project and in the brand of the organization they represent.

As an etiquette expert, Denise has personally seen the competitive edge that etiquette and image skills bring to clients such as the IT professional, CPA, Engineer, Lawyer, those in Banking and many others. The ETIQUETTE EDGE MAKES A DIFFERENCE!

As co-author of “The Power of Civility” from Thrive Publishing, Denise’s heart for the university student cannot be denied when reading her chapter, “Civility on Campus; How to get an ‘A’ in Conduct”. Ms. Pietzsch has worked with several different universities, including The Ohio State University, Ohio University, and Kent State University. She has also enjoyed working with campus groups, including International students and sororities.

TRAINING: BA in Social and Behavioral Sciences from The Ohio State University; Certification as a Corporate Etiquette and International Protocol Consultant through The Protocol School of `Washington, the only accredited school of its kind, known world-wide for being the "experts who train the experts" and founded by Dorthea Johnson. Denise’s esteemed teachers have included Pamela Eyring (PSOW), Nicole Krakoka (Smithsonian Institute), Sue Gaulke (Successworks), and most recently, Juanita Ecker (Professional Image Management).

Specialties

THE FAB FIVE: The most frequently requested "epic" topics:

“Enter Like an Ambassador and Mingle Like a Pro”™

Do you know how to make small talk? Want to learn how to make an entrance and a great first impression? Do you know how to enter a conversation or get out of one? This session will take the anxiety out of attending business events, gathering, and other networking opportunities. Interactive,informative, power packed.

“Maximize Your Impact”™

Did you ever wonder why the classmate with the highest grades and the first to get hired was the first to get fired? Technical skills account for less than 15% of the reason you get a job, keep a job, and advance in a job. Your business manners and professional presence make the difference. You will learn, laugh, and take home "million dollar manners" in this exciting workshop.

“Dine Like a Diplomat”®

This course is the definite answer to your dining etiquette questions, designed by The Protocol School of Washington. The course may be presented with or without a meal.

"Cell Phone Civility &
Technology Etiquette”™

We may be connected, but are we civilized? Are your colleagues texting during meetings? Are you answering your phone at the table? Do you put emoticons in your emails? This sessions covers the etiquette minefield of the electronic era and provides the tools to become a professional in this often overlooked area. This course is fast becoming the most requested session. You won't want to miss it!

"The Power of Civility"
According to research in 2010 “94% of Americans consider the general tone and level of civility in the country today to be a problem, with 65% considering it to be a major problem.” The power of civility is contagious! Acknowledge the fact that you can spread something positive-civility! You have the power to make it happen. You will be challenged to make one simple change as you leave the presentation It’s time to make a change! Embrace the power of civility.

    The Power of Civility is packed with thought-provoking perspectives on what civility really means, as well as practical solutions for incorporating civility best practices into your work and life. In this comprehensive book, eighteen top civility and etiquette professionals go beyond the typical "please and thank you" conversations about civility and challenge you to think about your personal standards, accountability, values, and what it means to be committed to choosing civility, whether at home, at work, in your community, out in public - at home and abroad. This book provides the tools you need to boost your social intelligence and build your cultural competence, giving you the confidence and poise to go anywhere and be welcomed as a caring and considerate citizen of the world.
