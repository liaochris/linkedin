Ernie Brogdon's Experience

API Technical Support

Endicia

Public Company; 51-200 employees; Computer Software industry

October 2008 – Present (5 years) Palo Alto, CA

My two favorite responsibilities at Endicia are answering questions from our partner developers, and working on the technical documentation. Both circle back to one another - customer questions influence the changes in the documentation, and the documentation is improved for future partner integrations. Maybe someday the docs will be so clear and complete that we won't need technical support... well, I can dream!

Our partners not only ask technical questions about the Label Server API, but they also ask about US Postal Service rules and regulations. A lot has changed since I first assumed the role of Liaison to the USPS for Intuit in 1992, and keeping up is almost a full-time job in itself. Fortunately, I'm surrounded by many other "mail geeks" at Endicia, and we can quickly clarify even the most complicated issues that come before us.

Early in my Endicia career, I developed a process for reconciling USPS chargebacks that is used successfully today to reduce related costs by up to 49%. I also worked on a team to reduce fraud, and still occasionally assist in fraud prevention projects.

Joomla Web Developer

Flapdoodle Web Design & Hosting (Sole Proprietorship)

Sole Proprietorship; Myself Only; Internet industry

June 2007 – Present (6 years 4 months) San Leandro, CA

Web design and hosting service primarily supporting Postal Customer Councils (PCC's) using Joomla Content Management System (CMS) and 3rd party extensions.

With over two dozen PCC web sites, I sometimes feel that I've reached my limit with a full-time day job. But Joomla and the extensions keep getting better, and my customers are growing more adventurous to make their own updates, add their own events and take full advantage of all the power available to them through the Joomla Administrator.

Direct Distributor

NSA Juice Plus (Self-employed)

Self-Employed; 1-10 employees; Health, Wellness and Fitness industry

September 2004 – Present (9 years 1 month) San Leandro, CA

I first learned about Juice Plus capsules in 2002 when they had a booth at a Taking Control of Your Diabetes conference at the San Diego Convention Center. I took some materials, but didn't pursue it any further.

A few years later, while attending a business seminar, I saw that Juice Plus distributors were meeting in another room, so I poked my head inside to take a look. During the break, I chatted with Allen Fisher, the world champion arm wrestler, and decided to try Juice Plus. The company, NSA, has an excellent business model, so I also signed up as a distributor.

I don't have the best eating habits, so Juice Plus fills the gap - two each of the Orchard Blend with breakfast, the Garden Blend with lunch and the Vineyard Blend with dinner - so that when I don't eat the right things (which is pretty much all the time), I know that I'm getting a baseline, wide variety of real fruits, vegetables, grains and antioxidants every day.

I've been happily taking Juice Plus since 2004, and cannot imagine what my health would be like today if it wasn't part of my diet.

Corporate Postal Advisor

Intuit

Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; INTU; Computer Software industry

October 1993 – April 2003 (9 years 7 months) Greater San Diego Area

I started as one of the first 100 employees at ChipSoft, maker of TurboTax, in 1988 and not only survived the Intuit merger, but thrived with a new, expanded position. After much success and growth at Intuit, I decided that it was in Intuit's best interests to divide my responsibilities among multiple departments within the company. I worked with a Six-Sigma team to plan the process, which we successfully completed nine months later. At that point, I was offered several positions within Intuit, but chose to pursue my entrepreneurial passion.

During my 14 year career at ChipSoft/Intuit, I advised all marketing groups, operations and functional units on mailpiece and small parcel design. I established policy for Corporate Finance, Corporate Services, and Manufacturing & Distribution regarding postage payment, forecasting and postage payment methods. I was also a seminar speaker with U.S. Postal Service at many National Postal Forums held nationwide.

Quoted in the Wall Street Journal January 24, 2001, Section B-1, “A Deal No More, Priority Mail is Prey for Rivals”

Profiled in MAIL, The Journal of Communications Distribution December 1996 ,profile p. 81-82 “Management Strategies” Vol. 8, No. 9

Print Buyer

ChipSoft, Inc.

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Hospital & Health Care industry

June 1990 – October 1993 (3 years 5 months) Greater San Diego Area

I sought out and developed new printing vendors and designed new packaging for our products sold in retail stores.

I worked extensively with the documentation group to migrate from one-color, word-processing documents to two-color, desktop publishing documents without increasing our unit cost for printing. This greatly improved the professional appearance of documentation included with our products.

It was during this time that I gained expertise in paper and small parcel/flat mail piece design. I used these skills to produce more attractive, professional product components at lower costs for both manufacturing and shipping.

I also implemented early OCR technology to scan IRS instructions for use in our products to enhance the context-sensitive help in the product.

Press Operator

The Duplicating Center

May 1978 – August 1980 (2 years 4 months) San Leandro, CA

This was my first full-time job after high school. I had carefully typed a resume, stopped at The Duplicating Center to make copies, and accidentally left the original in the copier. The owner called me and hired me as his first press operator to work alongside he and his wife, who ran the little 'mom & pop' shop.

During this time I learned how to operate and perform maintenance on the A.B. Dick 360 and Ryobi 960 offset presses, and learned how to use all the training I had received in graphic arts classes.

Amazing how these things work out, isn't it?
