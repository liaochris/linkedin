Alan de Sousa Caires' Summary

Alan is Director and Editor in Chief of Management Today Magazine, a role he assumed in February 2011 after relocating from London where he amassed an enviable track record of over 25 years in Finance.

Alan is also a Managing Partner at the digital publishing solutions provider Eish Digital, which enables publishers to quickly and easily utilise the iPad and Android devices to grow global distribution for their publications.

A Chartered Accountant by trade, and a Fellow Member of ACCA (Certified Association of Chartered Accountants), Alan worked for global giant General Electric for over 14 years. During this time he gained extensive international experience, working across the US, UK, Europe and Africa. He also spent 18 months on assignment in Florence, Italy. Before leaving London, Alan expanded his breadth of experience through taking on two roles in the Not for Profit sector, working for Notting Hill Housing Association and the Chartered Insurance Institute.

Alan has worked with numerous finance systems including SAP, Oracle, Great Plains, SunSystems and Pastel, and has also implemented both SAP and Oracle. In addition to his specialist implementation skills, he is also strong on business process improvement. He holds a Six Sigma Blackbelt – Manager level.

This considerable track record positions Alan with an in-depth knowledge of the critical issues facing businesses globally, across a range of industry sectors and functions.

Since taking over the helm from retiring editor Richard Havenga, who built Management Today Magazine over the course of 28 years since launch in 1983, Alan has been focussed on growing the magazine into a fully accessible, digital information source which taps into the challenges of a truly global operating context.

Alan still provides specialist consulting services and advisory on systems implementation and business process improvement.

Specialties

Business Process Improvement and Six Sigma, Financial Systems Implementations (ledger systems, PO systems, Expenses systems), Sarbannes & Oxley, Management Accounting, Financial Reporting and Consolidations, VAT, delivering training and Advanced Excel.
