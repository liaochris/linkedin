Cartoon and Fine Artist

Marti McGinnis Art

July 1994 – December 2000 (6 years 6 months)

In 1989 I started a venture on a shoestring that grew to support me fully and well through the 1990's as I designed, created and sold an entire line of fine crafts first called Martoons, which evolved into HappyArt. I had the great good fortune to sell Happy Art through gorgeous galleries and other happy places all over the United States and Germany and France! After selling over a million dollars worth of creations made entirely by my own hands I started learning digital arts to expand my scope and and personal reach, while addressing the eventual depletion of my motor adeptness at what had been up to that point, break-neck -no, break-arm- speed. Mission accomplished - I now make paintings at much more manageable pace in between applying the rest my artistic drive towards ever more fascinating digital projects.

Marti McGinnis - www.martimcginnis.com's Publications

  • Cans: The Life's Work of an Optimistic Dog

    • Amazon.com
    • September 1, 2010
    Authors: Marti McGinnis - www.martimcginnis.com

    This book contains the creative output of one very talented cattledog. Using what he has available, this full-on visionary artist creates imaginative sculptures out of discarded cat food cans. Maybe you're thinking "Yeah well just because a dog chews cans doesn't mean it's art.". But you know what we say? It IS when it's considered as such regardless of the intention. Then throw in an ongoing pursuit, a dedication to detail and commitment to the craft with individuated results - and JoJo's output has all the earmarks of not just being craft, but it is art as art is defined by people a whole lot smarter than me. As a visionary, outsider artist JoJo has all the qualifications. His art is unique and unparalleled. No two pieces are alike though they definitely fall into categories; such as, crushed, flattened, pierced, bitten through and so on.

Author, McGinnis, describes a world of wonder she claims to visit in her dreams - or does she visit in fact? Her inventive and entertaining musings make a compelling case for alternative realities.

  • Who's Who in Equine Compassion: A Compendium of People and Places Who say They Care

    • amazon.com
    • May 6, 2012
    Authors: Marti McGinnis - www.martimcginnis.com

    A llisting of people, places and organizations that are self described as or focus on advocacy issues surrounding equines, rescue groups or other horse, pony, mini, mule, burro and donkey advocates. With an online persona named "Merry Horses", the Editor of this professionally designed compendium brings together people and organizations throughout the United States and beyond; all involved in horse rescue or advocacy of one kind or another. Divided into geographical regions (by state, country or region) the listings include name and contact info (as provided) as well as quotes and other salient information when included. As a first in what is slated to become an annual series, this volume has been designed to inspire those who read it to become involved in whatever aspect of equine stewardship they like and make it easy for them to do so. Also included are a scholarly discussion by writer Mary McDaniels on the controversial issues surrounding equine slaughter along with suggestions for solutions as well as original content contributed by several other authors involved in diverse aspects of equine stewardship. People and organizations are invited to include themselves in future volumes
