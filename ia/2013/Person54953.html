Donald Ramsell's Summary

Donald Ramsell has been an Illinois DUI Attorney for 26 years. He is the author of "Illinois DUI Law and Practice Guidebook." His firm has defended over 14,000 cases since 1986. He is a highly respected leader in the field of DUI Defense, having lectured before thousands of attorneys in Illinois, Michigan, Missouri, Texas, New York, Arkansas, Louisiana, Ohio and Wisconsin.

Specialties

Specializing in Illinois DUI Defense, Criminal Defense and Traffic Laws.

Donald Ramsell's Publications

  • Illinois DUI Law and Practice Guidebook

    • Thomson-Reuters Publishing
    • April 8, 2012
    Authors: Donald Ramsell

    Considered the "Bible of Illinois DUI Law", this 1,000-plus page book contains everything related to Illinois DUI - laws, suspensions, penalties, breath tests, blood tests, field sobriety tests, defenses, sample motions, transcripts, charts, and discovery techniques.

  • Nystagmus Testing for Alcohol and Drugs

    • Intoxication Test Evidence (2d Edition)
    • January 2011
    Authors: Donald Ramsell

    Intoxication Test Evidence is the leading publication on issues involving alcohol testing in the legal system, most notably in DUI, DWI, drunk driving and civil litigation. Ed Fitzgerald is the Editor and leading author.

  • Understanding DUI Scientific Evidence, 2nd Edition: Leading Defense Attorneys on Understanding Chemical and Field Sobriety Testing Procedures

    • Aspatore Publishers
    • 2009
    Authors: Donald Ramsell, George Bianchi, Victor Carmody, Jr., Alfred Staubus, Ted Vosk, Lawrence Wines

    This book is devoted to discussing legal issues for attorneys and judges arising in DUI DWI cases where science is involved, such as the use of experts, sobriety testing, breath testing, etc.

  • Understanding DUI Scientific Evidence: Leading DUI Defense Lawyers on Utilizing Expert Witnesses, Understanding Chemical and Field Sobriety Testing Procedures, ... Trial Strategies !st Edition

    • Aspatore Publishing
    • 2008
    Authors: Donald Ramsell, Linda Callahan, Victor Carmody, Jr., Lawrence Wines

    This book discusses the use of chemical and field sobriety testing procedures, experts and trial strategies by attorneys and judges.
