  • Systemic Review-Comparison of Chlorhexidine for Vascular Catheter site Care

    • May 2009 to Present
    Team Members: Charles Kaluwasha, Rebecca & Alex,

    Does using chlorhexidine gluconate solution for cleansing insertion site before vascular catherter insertion reduce catheter related blood stream infections in comparison with povidone-iodine solutions? To answer this question,a systemic review of Randomised Controlled Trials(Meta-Analysis) comparing chloherxidine gluconate with povidone-iodine solution for catheter was undertaken. 8 eligible studies were selected from 302,several databases were searched,consulted product manufactures,reviewed reference lists of retrieved articles and identified studies presented at recent scientific meetings in the area of infection control and we contacted authors f relevant studies and experts in the field.
    RESULTS:7 studies identified chloherxidine solution as having lower infection rates than povidone-iodine,indicating a 50% reduction rate. The hospital stay was shorter saving million dollars. Therefore chloherxidine gluconate use was implemented in the hospital settings.
