Leighton Oosthuisen's Experience

Founder/Training Director

Partners Working Dogs, Inc

2010 – Present (3 years)

Partners Working Dogs provides Personal Protection dogs for families, as well as Military Working Dogs trained in explosive detection.

Training Director

Partners Dog Training School

January 1995 – Present (18 years 10 months)

Leighton founded Partners Dog Training School to help people understand their dogs.
Partners is Arizona's largest dog behavioral school and specializes in aggression, obedience, agility and snake proofing of dogs.
Leighton has been working in the canine field for 30 years, and has worked with over 25,000 dogs and clients.
He has appeared on TV and Radio, as well as numerous movie, television and commercial productions.

Founder / Instructor

Partners Service Dog Division

January 1989 – Present (24 years 10 months) Cave Creek AZ

Leighton has been training / supplying handicap assistance dogs since 1989, many sponsored by him. These dogs assist handicapped recipients with daily functions such as stability, mobility and hearing assistance.

Producer / Videographer

Partners Movie Production Division

January 1986 – Present (27 years 10 months)

Aside from a professional dog trainer and behaviorist. Leighton has a passion for photography and videography. His work frequently appears through print, online and on television mediums. Some of the movies he has worked on are Howling IV, Purgatory and Winged Migration. Commercials include Petsmart, Titleist, Toyota, BBC, Animal Planet and Discovery.

Founder/Training Director

Partners Personal Protection

January 1985 – Present (28 years 10 months)

Over the past twenty years, Leighton has trained over 200 personal and sports protection dogs for clients. These ranged from advanced dogs trained to search, apprehend and guard; to the basic bark, bite and hold dogs. Clients include law enforcement, private individuals, celebrities, coaches and players for the Phoenix Suns, Arizona Cardinals, Diamondbacks and Coyotes. Leighton was featured on Arizona's local Channel 5 with his protection dog program, and has appeared on local and national television.

Instructor / Handler

Search & Rescue K-9's

January 1998 – January 2004 (6 years 1 month)

Leighton trained with the PFOC search dog group where he trained, instructed and competed with his German Shepherd, Izzy. This group included members of law enforcement and FEMA. In 1999 Leighton competed in and won the Tracking Dog Trial with dogs from all over the Southwest.

Founder / Instructor

Narcotic Detection Canines (Prosec Investigations Agency)

January 1996 – January 1999 (3 years 1 month)

In 1996 Leighton founded the Narcotics Detection & Prevention program, in conjunction with a well known Juvenile COurt Judge and the Paradise Valley School District. He trained canines and supervised the daily operations, resulting in hundreds of successful searches and no incidents. The program appeared on Good Morning America in the Arizona Republic.

Reserve Officer

SAPS

Government Agency; 10,001+ employees; Law Enforcement industry

January 1986 – August 1989 (3 years 8 months)

Served in the uniformed patrol division. Also assisted Detective Unit working undercover. Heavy Duty rated driver. First place in the Police Shoot. Member of the Anti-Terrorism Task Force.

Captain

NSRI (National Sea Rescue Institute)

January 1986 – August 1989 (3 years 8 months)

Leighton was a member of this Search & Rescue organization, reaching the rank of Captain. He worked on approximately 210 rescue missions, commanding approximately a dozen.
He was also a Trauma Medic, instructor in boat handling, radio procedures and life-saving.

Founder / Instructor

ProDog Security

January 1985 – January 1988 (3 years 1 month)

Leighton was the founder and Training Director of ProDog Security, a company that specialized in training of working canines.
These dogs were trained to detect explosives, narcotics and other substances for the private and law enforcement sectors.
In addition, the school worked with dogs with behavioral problems. With a staff of twelve, they taught eight classes per week with about thirty dogs in training.

Instructor (Special Forces Dog Unit) Bourkes Luck

South African Military

July 1983 – June 1985 (2 years)

Operational Wing, Handler and Trainer of Explosive Detection Canines, Narcotic Detection Canines. Assisted with Tracker Dogs and Patrol Dogs. OPS Wing consisted of about 400 working canines, and were deployed on border patrol and in the cities.
In 1984 Leighton deployed to Johannesburg as Section Leader of the Anti-Terrorism Task Force, tasked with supervising a team of bomb detection canines.

Military Service

South African Defense Force

January 1983 – January 1985 (2 years 1 month)

Leighton served in the Military Working Dog Unit, of the SADF Special Forces. During this
time he trained and handled explosive detection, narcotic detection and tracker dogs. He
was deployed as the Section Leader of the Bomb Dog team, working with the Police
