Billie Bryan's Projects

  • Magazine Ads for Walk Good Caribbean

    • 2010 to 2011
    Team Members: Billie Bryan

    Walk Good Caribbean, operating in Jamaica, The Bahamas and The Cayman Islands and founded by André Steiner and Vanessa Taylor, is the official distributor of Havaianas®—a fashionable and popular Brazilian brand of flip flops—in the Caribbean. I was working as a freelancer in 2010, employed part-time as a sales associate at their retail store in the Cayman Islands, when the store owner expressed interest in my services. After putting together in-store signage, promotional copywriting, fliers and window displays for their business, the founders, completely satisfied with my work, decided to take me on as head of their marketing department.

    Since then, I’ve provided myriad creative services for them, including designing, launching and monitoring email campaigns through MailChimp, social media marketing through Facebook, magazine ads, business cards, catalogues—even developing new brand standards for the business, covering everything from logo usage, colours and typography, all the way down to the employee apparel.

  • Teaser Ad Campaign for Sapphire Lounge & Bar

    • May 2004 to May 2004
    Team Members: Billie Bryan

    During my time at Elite Visual Advertising, I was the sole designer employed, in charge of branding our clients, crafting signage, putting together newspaper and Yellow Pages advertisements for them and, sometimes, even creating television and radio broadcasts. In other words, I often had complete creative control. Sapphire Lounge & Bar was no exception. In addition to creating a look and identity for the new business, I also put together a teaser ad campaign, consisting of radio jingles, display boards and a newspaper print series (shown above) featured every week for four weeks in the Caymanian Compass. These newspaper teasers were clean cut and classy, implementing high contrast black and white photography with subtle spot colour
    highlights, derived from the logo. Sapphire was a huge success when they finally opened.

    Unfortunately, 2004’s Hurricane Ivan destroyed their initial operating space later that year. The business has, however, reopened since then and now operates under the name Nectar.

  • Self-Promotional Campaign Display Boards for E.V.A.

    • December 2004 to December 2004
    Team Members: Billie Bryan

    The Managing Director of Elite Visual Advertising requested that I piece together a series of promotional artwork for our display boards to help attract additional clientele. I wanted to showcase a series that was solid, consistent and spoke clearly to the public in a creative way, as these were to be displayed in high-traffic locations.

    The resultant design incorporates little more than a bold and sizable caption bubble, some promotional verbiage and the Elite Visual Advertising logo, slogan and contact information. The creative quirk lies in the varying twists on the lettering in the speech bubble, intended to spark interest in the viewer. Some are humourous, some puzzling and some deliberately break the mould of consistency.

    The message is clear and effectively illustrates how potential clients can benefit from marketing with Elite Visual Advertising.

  • Catalogue and Stationery Package for Office Worx

    • January 2005 to January 2005
    Team Members: Billie Bryan

    After severely revamping their logo, putting together a stationery package and designing decals for their business vehicles, Office Worx then assigned me the task of designing their printed catalogue exterior for the new year. Time was of the essence and, while channeling information back and forth from Office Worx and the printers overseas, I was under extreme pressure to arrange everything according to print specifications, gather up all the information from the client and confirm the final proof all before the impending deadline.

    With little time to spare, the project was successfully completed, all artwork was submitted and the catalogue was in circulation year round.

  • Miscellaneous Advertisements for Seaboard Marine

    • 2004 to 2005
    Team Members: Billie Bryan

    Over the course of about a year or so, I put together numerous newspaper ads, magazine ads, signage and a website for Seaboard Marine (Cayman). These were all based on a strong visual identity I created for them, derived from the nature of their business and the message they wanted to portray: ease of use and professionalism.

    Though they are no longer one of my clients, that same visual identity has remained consistent throughout their branding and advertising thereafter.
