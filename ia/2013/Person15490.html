Bob Roffman's Summary

Performance Massage for Horse & Rider

How would you like to optimize you and your horse’s performance? We all know about the connection you share when you are riding. Whether it is for pleasure or competition, the quality of your experience depends on how you feel. Being the intuitive animals that they are, horses feels any discomfort you are in by the subtle or not so subtle changes in your body. The reverse is also true. Just like you, your horse communicates by its behavior, regarding what is transpiring with its body. Since I have obtained my Medical Massage Certification I have been dedicated to helping people manage their physical challenges, which more often than not include pain and range of motion issues.

Most horses, like humans, enjoy massage when it is done with skilled hands and a good understanding of anatomy. More than 60% of the horse's body weight is muscle. Small muscle injuries can take up to 90 days to become apparent
(by change in attitude, decreased performance and lameness). By this time they may have caused more serious injury. Muscle tightening is transmitted from one
muscle group to another. For example, shoulder tension might appear as hind end lameness. Muscles may also enlarge with tension. Prompt attention to these injuries not only enhances a horse's performance but also may prevent future problems.
