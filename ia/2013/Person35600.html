Mon relevé de carrière...

Career Statement

January 1970 – Present (43 years 8 months) Montreal, Canada Area

MICHEL A. DI IORIO, publiciste et spécialiste de la communication, a troqué, au tournant du siècle dernier, la vie publique et la notoriété du lobbysme pour la pratique privée d’une carrière quelque peu moins flamboyante. À ces fins, il a fondé « LES MOTS JUSTES » comme véhicule pour exercer la profession d'artisan des mots et traducteur, répondant aux besoins de ses clients; les grands et les petits... les riches et les pauvres... publics et privés. Il écrit et traduit pour ceux qui ne savent pas comment, qui ne peuvent pas, qui n’ont pas le temps ou qui ne sont tout simplement pas de très bons rédacteurs. La nature de son métier en fait un conseiller judicieux.

Un visionnaire de la communication, Michel s’est adapté au marché évolutif à l'aube du nouveau millénaire en offrant un ensemble de services en communication, conçu pour les petites et moyennes entreprises, en misant sur une approche personnalisée et le respect absolu des délais de livraison.

Il comprend parfaitement les enjeux politiques et les défis linguistiques associés aux affaires dans sa province natale, ainsi que dans le reste du Canada. Pour « Les mots justes », il n’y a tout simplement pas de meilleur choix!

Spécialités :
Conception-rédaction / traduction / révision de texte / réécriture (français – anglais - français).

Michel possède une solide expérience de la communication commerciale (à la fois virtuelle et réelle), ainsi que des services médicaux d’urgence, et de la politique. Originaire du Québec, il maitrise les deux langues officielles du Canada avec autant de flair.

Que ce soit à Saint-Eustache, Lachine, les Cantons de l’Est ou ailleurs dans le monde, « Les mots justes » de Michel sont là pour véhiculer votre message...

Crew Leader - Teams 1727 and 1733

Statistics Canada

Government Agency; 1001-5000 employees; Government Administration industry

April 2011 – August 2011 (5 months) Lachine, NDG, Ville Saint Pierre, Montreal West

During the Canadian Census, I was contracted as a Crew Leader and asked to accept an outside territory, to which an additional one was eventually grafted. I supervised 2 teams (up to 30 enumerators) to handle these 2 areas, which was a bit of a logistical juggling act. In any event, the juggling act worked out pretty well for us, as we became one of the top producing teams during the 2011 Canadian Census.

I unpacked a project that had been shelved several years before, and pitched the municipal council of the day to approve a plan to develop the project, using the resources at hand. With the help of 25 hirees, including a theatrical and musical director, actors, seamstresses, musicians, acrobats, artists and volunteers, the team's efforts over a six month period drew a record crowd of 34,000 visitors within a four day timeframe, making the event a huge success.

President

Gizmania RLD inc.

1987 – 1995 (8 years) St-Eustache

Based in St. Eustache, Gizmania RLD inc. was a holding company founded to group the eight complementary divisions that were subsequently developed to service a demanding clientèle. The company provided a comprehensive communication package to a local and regional client base, including political candidates, business development interests, local industry, the provincial government and private sectors alike.

Gizmania RLD inc. provided conventional print shop products and services, as well as a specialized business section that catered to a marginal client base, a publicity club, an advertising specialties company, a comprehensive communications consulting division, specialized text services in both official languages, sublimation specialties, a specialized portrait studio and a textile division.

During this same period, Michel sat on a number of boards and commitees, which attracted requests from interested political groups to forge a media-friendly persona for various local candidates.

EMT - Paramedic

Urgences Santé

1977 – 1991 (14 years) Région 06A, Greater Montreal and Laval area

He worked for various agencies as an EMT - Paramedic, CPR Instructor and Rescue personnel.

An interesting and passionate career choice led to serious vocational training for this young father of a future heart transplant recipient. As a result, he pioneered the modern prehospital care model, along with 87 other graduates of Quebec's first prehospital care pilot project, which yielded a total of 88 graduates. As a paramedic in Quebec's fledgling prehospital care system, Michel personally responded to more than 20,000 calls for help in a variety of emergency situations.

As a paramedic, Michel was approached to deliver conferences in local elementary schools to provide school children with a practical working knowledge of prehospital care resources available to them in case of need. He also provided CPR and other related training to both the lay public and other health care providers during this time.
