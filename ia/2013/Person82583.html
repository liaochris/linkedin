Lorraine Ranalli's Summary

Never has there been a more important time in the corporate world than now to add someone with skills to communicate both internally and externally.

Lorraine Ranalli has over twenty years experience in the field of Communication as a broadcaster, published author, and corporate trainer. Applying her expertise, Lorraine has worked with organizations to develop from the ground up uniquely tailored marketing and communication strategies. Lorraine’s passion for the art and science of effective communication is not only evident in her PR and marketing efforts but also in her "Speaking for Success" and "Business Writing That Works" seminars and workshops.

Specialties

Branding, creative promotions, project management, publishing

www.LorraineRanalli.com

2006 – Present (7 years)

Lorraine Ranalli spent almost two decades writing, editing, and reporting news on prominent Philadelphia radio stations such as KYW, Talk Radio 1210, and B101. As demand increased for her services as a Communication Consultant, Lorraine stepped away from the microphone in 2006 and carved a niche in Guerrilla Marketing.

Employing an unconventional approach to promotions that capitalizes on the strength of partnerships has proven successful for Lorraine’s clients, for her humorous cultural tome "Gravy Wars: South Philly Foods, Feuds & Attytudes" (Folger Ross Publications 2009) and for Cucina Chatter, her syndicated weekly talk show.

Lorraine's career has come full circle. The marriage of Guerrilla Marketing and Social Media produced Cucina Chatter, which features a unique cast of characters that includes professional chefs, comedians, entertainers, and writers, and conversations that vary as broadly as the topics discussed around your kitchen table.

Drawing upon her media experience and marketing savvy, Lorraine developed and delivers these soft skills training programs to professionals and to college students:

BE ODD®
Write first, think later. This five-step method to breaking writer's block will help you overcome writing anxieties. With more and more of us communicating through e-mail, writing is a necessity, and good writing is a commodity. Well-written communiqués give us an edge in business and improve our bottom line.

Business Writing That Works
Write for revenue. Discover the difference in your organization when written communication is clear, concise, and correct.

Speaking for Success
Research shows that most people would rather die than speak in public. Learn how the proper use of body language and vocal variety can quell your nerves and empower you to present with confidence.
