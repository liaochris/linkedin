Chief Executive Officer

Thrive (Sold to LendingTree)

April 2006 – January 2009 (2 years 10 months) New York

CEO and co-founder of Thrive, a startup that helped people manage and improve their financial lives through free, online, tailored advice.

People who used Thrive built about $6K in assets over a 9 month period ($3K in reduced debt, $2K in a savings account, $1K in retirement account). Active users meaningfully improved their credit scores over the same period.

Over 3 years, built a team of twenty focused on engineering, product, marketing, and fantastic customer service. Raised two rounds of funding and sold the company to LendingTree in 2009.

I created Thrive when I saw my twenty and thirty-something friends had serious financial decisions to wrestle with and little in the way of education or tools to help them do that effectively. Banks were busy trying to get baby boomers to let them manage their nest eggs, while young people were putting off getting married or buying homes, spending themselves into debt, choosing not to invest rather than choosing what to invest in, and losing money on fees.

So, I left the world of big finance to build a tool that would help them make those decisions. Along the way, my team and I figured out how to change the financial behavior of young people and proved that people saved more, spent less, and improved their credit scores just by using Thrive.
