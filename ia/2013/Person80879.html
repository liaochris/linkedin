Lillian Lincoln Lambert, MBA's Summary

*First African American woman to receive a Harvard MBA, an experience that taught me how to "bounce back" when faced with adversity, obstacles and isolation.

*Founded a business during the tumultuous 1960's when it was not customary for women to become entrepreneurs. Operated the company for 25 years and grew it from $150,000 to $20 million in sales with more than 1,200 employees. After selling the company I started two other ventures then used the combination of all those experiences to develop a coaching business. Shortly thereafter I was certified. .

*Authored my memoir, "The Road to Someplace Better: From the Segregated South to Harvard Business School and Beyond"

*Awarded Alumni Achievement Award by Harvard Business School, its highest award bestowed on alumni.

Lillian Lincoln Lambert, MBA's Publications

  • The Road to Someplace Better: From the Segregated South to Harvard Business School and Beyond

    • John Wiley & Sons, Inc
    • January 25, 2010
    Authors: Lillian Lincoln Lambert, MBA

    This is an old-fashioned rags-to-riches story that traces Lambert's upbringing as the daughter of God-fearing Virginia subsistence farmers to becoming the first black woman to graduate from Harvard Business School, in year 1969 and later attaining success as a Maryland entrepreneur. Told in straightforward, no-nonsense prose, Lambert's memoir begins backward, from the shocking anecdote about arriving for a meeting of a group of powerful businesswomen in New York City in 1986 and being ushered to the kitchen.In fact, Lambert worked as a maid when she first arrived in New York City fresh out of high school from Ballsville, Va. (Her 1976 startup of a janitorial service in Maryland provides another irony.) Her account captures a historic epoch and offers some business strategies.

  • Sister to Sister: A Guide for African American Girls

    • PWN Publishing
    • August 1, 2011
    Authors: Lillian Lincoln Lambert, MBA, Co-authored with 21 women

    A must-have book for teenage girls of color involving self-esteem, dating, etiquette, preparing for college, personal image, relationships, and self-awareness. Written by professional coaches, consultants, and educators. The type of book to be included in personal libraries of African American families, youth ministry and young African American women.

  • The Daily Devotional

    • Professional Woman Network
    • December 15, 2012
    Authors: Lillian Lincoln Lambert, MBA

    Lillian Lambert and other inspirational writers have provided encouraging and spiritually moving words for the reader. Their writing is deeply inspirational with special real-life stories combined with a daily scripture reading.
