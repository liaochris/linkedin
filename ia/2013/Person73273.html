Kaveh Cohen's Summary

A veritable force in film, television, motion picture advertising and video games, Kaveh has quickly become the first choice for producers and directors to create cutting edge and memorable scores across all genres. With literally hundreds of advertising campaigns for Hollywoodʼs biggest blockbuster films to his credit, Kaveh continues to thrill audiences on both the large and small screen. Recent credits include The Wolverine, Oblivion, The Lone Ranger, Good Day To Die Hard, Oz The Great and Powerful, Lincoln, Total Recall, The Hunger Games: Catching Fire, The Bourne Legacy, Taken 2, Looper, Red Dawn, The Dark Knight Rises, Prometheus, Rise of the Guardians, The Avengers and countless others.

Recently Kaveh completed the score for Crawlspace, a feature from writer/director Josh Stolberg (Sorority Row, Piranha 3D. Prior to that, Kaveh scored Victor Ginsburg’s critically acclaimed feature adaptation of Viktor Pelevin’s book Generation “P”. Kaveh co-scored Marvelʼs hit animated series Wolverine and the X-Men which garnered Nicktoons their highest ratings to date. He's composed scores and themes for dozens of award winning television shows and mini series such as America: The Story of Us, Ocean Adventures, The Science of Star Wars, The Crash of Flight 191, Biography: John Travolta, Red Flag, Above and Beyond, Probable Cause and many others. Not stopping there, Kaveh has scored numerous commercials for clients including AT&T, Sony, SEGA, NBC, Mattel and Six Flags.

2010 saw the release of Tom Clancy's Splinter Cell Conviction, one of the highest grossing and most anticipated video games of the year with over two hours of cinematic score. Now in demand in the growing world of video games, Kaveh has provided scores for Warner Bros. Interactive’s video game tie-in to the Green Lantern movie as well as Activision’s Battleship video game based on the 2012 summer blockbuster. Kaveh is currently hard at work on exciting upcoming titles from Ubisoft and Riot Games.
