John Bates; the Job Guy's Summary

I am the Job Guy, a Career Coach and Advisor who leverages expertise and perspectives gained from 4000+ hiring decision makers to help clients develop personal brands that pave the way to rewarding and productive careers.

I have helped hundreds of:

W-2 employees and solopreneurs under age 65 who don't already own the 3 basics of modern survival: indoor plumbing, clothing, and a personal branding or career strategy.

Miserably employed professionals who run the risk of jumping from the frying pan into the fire just to escape a bad boss or job.

Those who need/want a stronger professional network.

Mid-career (aren't we all in the new economy?) professionals who wonder "is this all there is?"

Solopreneurs who want to return to traditional employment; traditionally employed who want to be solopreneurs (efforts to look for jobs and clients succeed and fail for the same reasons, you know).

Really talented people who have horrible resumes or freak out about interviews.

Those who would like to have fun doing something that isn't supposed to be (yeah, that would be job stuff with a really upbeat career coach)

Professionals who feel about their careers as they do about a 15 year old car: nothing wrong with it, but might just be time for a new one anyway!

Anyone who wants to make a career coach happy by allowing him to help him/her make good money doing something fun.

Specialties: Career Coach, Career Coaching, Personal Brand Management, LinkedIn Profile Building, Resume Development, Interview Training, Career Planning, Professional Network Development, Salary Negotiations, Job Search Strategy.

John Bates; the Job Guy's Experience

Career Coach

Job Guy (Sole Proprietorship)

Sole Proprietorship; Myself Only; Professional Training & Coaching industry

September 2004 – Present (9 years 5 months) Mansfield, MA

I am a high-energy Career Coach who helps vocationally-challenged professionals figure out what type of work they should be doing and in which work environment. I believe that, like me, everyone should enjoy what they do for 40+ hours a week. It is fun for me to help others recognize where they belong and/or assist them to get there.

Clients can save money by opting for a full array of career services from me. But I can also be hired to help with specific areas of need including search techniques, resumes, interviewing, online networking, face-to-face networking, branding, salary negotiations, and career planning.

I am fortunate to be blessed with really good career coaching skills, because I have yet to identify talents in other areas. I can’t sing or dance, I can’t hit a baseball, and I invariably draw my own blood doing home improvement projects.

So my first piece of advice would be to hire me to coach you, but not on those other things.

This was my training ground as a career coach.

I started here as an executive career coach and campaign troubleshooter.

Here I oversaw contingent recruiting activities targeted toward the retail industry (I hunted heads!)

Simultaneously, I managed delivery and operational programs for all divisions of the Boston office of this national firm.

I was also resident career coach, retained to develop the company's executive career coaching business.

District Manager

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; TJX; Retail industry

1978 – 1996 (18 years) Framingham, MA

I traveled from Maine to Connecticut making sure that TJ Maxx stores operated properly. The territories I supervised in the mid 90s produced about $60M in sales volume and employed 350 people. This company is well known for best practices in human resources management. So I learned a lot about interview selection critieria, training, people development, performance feedback and succession planning.

This bedrock of training serves me well in my current role as career coach.
