Donna Medina's Summary

I have extensive print and communication experience. I am in my element working with clients and coworkers, I enjoy the challenge and rewards of making the customer's needs job one, because a satisfied customer is a return customer and a walking referral!

Attention to detail is another forte of mine. Cross all your "t"s and dot all your "i"s is my attitude. It is easier to do a job right than to just "get it done". I strongly believe in efficiency, that means being an expert in multi-tasking and as long as you have a plan, a method and an open line of communication, optimum work can be successfully accomplished.

I have been in the publishing and printing business for over 30 years, I pride myself in staying abreast of the latest software and design techniques. I work well under pressure and am deadline driven. I am very experienced on Macs, matter-of-fact, my first one was a 512k way back when.

I am a true team player and am willing to roll up my sleeves to do whatever it takes to get a job done. Though I do like the creative side, I equally enjoy the production faction as well. Designing all the time is like having dessert three meals a day, fun at first, but is soon becomes mundane!

I am a people person and love sales, though you won't find me just "pushing" a product or concept on a client. I prefer to consider myself a consultant/assistant to a customer and assure what I am offering is a great fit for them and their company. The bottom line is: I DO care about my business associates!

Specialties

All phases of creative design, typography, print, advertising specialty items, signage, POP, fulfillment, web, social networking, consulting, marketing, education and sales.

Typesetter/Crisis Consultant

Tune-In Publications

September 1987 – July 1989 (1 year 11 months)

The interview for this job was crazy, didn't want to see my resume, nor portfolio, they just liked the idea I was a warm body, knew how to use a Compugraphic Editwriter and could start the next day. That started the beginning of the next era of my life. We produced a magazine in three different music genres, rock, urban and the most popular, country. Radio stations across the country would contract with us and we would put our interviews with the actual artists, with their contracted pages with their ads, promotions, or whatever they deemed fit. In turn, the radio stations would distribute them to their listenership. Our tools of communication, receiving copy and proofing were done via phone and FedX. There were no websites (until later date) email (ditto on that) or fax machines. Everything was cut and paste, stat camera and spray mount. In hindsight, I just can't imagine how we used to do work this way. Because it was the only way we knew how and it was cutting edge at the time. Phone and overnight, that was our basics, but between that and 45 radio markets, our track record was excellent.

Donna Medina's Publications

  • Where The Hell Is Podunk, Texas?

    • Bayou City Publishing
    Authors: Donna Medina, Dr. Jay Grady

    For the "Where The Hell Is Podunk, Texas" book, I did the cover design and interior layout.

    If you like Texas trivia, the mystical, the historical or just plain sports, you will enjoy reading "Where the Hell is "Podunk, Texas?" The book has something for everyone. This book is a nostalgic look at an East Houston community that for over sixty plus years has been referred to as “Podunk”.
    You will be introduced to the members of the legendary “Podunk” Skunks basketball team and the details of a basketball game played on March 9th, 1948, which received national attention and set several records for amateur basketball. The story is true and the characters real. You will follow them into the record-breaking 1948 game.

  • Edgar Allan Poe Elementary School Yearbook

    • Elementary School Yearbook, Houston, Texas
    • May 2004
    Authors: Donna Medina, David Medina

    The Poe Elementary yearbook was a joke. It was the same handful of children page after page with a two color cover and copied at Kinko's. Poe is a fine arts magnet school and this publication did NOTHING to represent that. David and I came into this project late this year, taking it over from a parent that was overwhelmed by the whole situation. In two months time, we were able to accumulate a year's worth of photos (David being the avid photobug and proud parent of our Poe student had plenty), organize class group shots, create fun and interesting fifth grade ads and get it printed and distributed all before the close of the school year. The quality was excellent, the students, parents and faculty were delighted.

  • Edgar Allan Poe Elementary School Yearbook

    • Elementary School Yearbook, Houston, Texas
    • May 2005
    Authors: Donna Medina, David Medina

    This year we had a head start, we had actually started in May of 2004 as soon as we had the previous one in everyone's hands. We were able to incorporate more design features, sell more fifth grade advertising (said advertising is purchased by family and well-wishers of graduating fifth graders, they are personal tribute advertisements), get a million more photos (enlisted the help of every parent in the school through an email network and started yearbook sales immediately. We were able to increase our run, increase the number of pages, ad more personal interest sections and keep the price so affordable that the school made money, but parents could buy more than one copy for multiple children. We also set up a fund to where children could buy a teacher a book or donate to another child that perhaps would not be financially able to purchase one. We also offered the yearbook on CD that year as well as a marketing experiment. Being 2005, the concept was just a little ahead of many people's thinking. It did keep my faith in the printed page!

  • Edgar Allan Poe Elementary School Yearbook

    • Elementary School Yearbook, Houston, Texas
    • May 2006
    Authors: Donna Medina, David Medina

    This our last year of doing the yearbook started with the PTO wanting to use a publication company to do the book. After reviewing all the information, there was just too many cons. Though the book would be full color, the price would be thrice what it had been, it would be hardcover, but hey, this is elementary school. No independent designs, you were required to use the company's cookie cutter templates, plus ALL the information for the book had to be submitted by the beginning of February! Most of the events that make Poe great happen after that. I stuck by my guns and we produced yet another amazing book. Our Poe yearbook experience was truly a labor of love and this is where we ended our time. The following year our child was to be a fifth grader and he wanted that special time with us, not with every kids in the school! I must say that his and several of his friends fifth grade ads were fantastic that year, I wonder who the artist was?
