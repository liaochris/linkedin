Matthew Damp's Projects

  • Evolve Asia Data Center Operations

    • June 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Matthew Damp

    This project has been evaluating all current services hosted in the Asia Data Center and ensuring that they are offering the best value to support the business. Standardized Service Level Agreement (SLA) documents, support procedures, crisis response plans have been created and are being applied to all existing services. Wherever possible, monitoring, trend analysis, and charge-back/show-back report has been put in place so that business groups can see the value provided.

    The data from this project has been incorporated into an internal information sharing portal and development continues to ensure that the output can be reused by other systems. Regular blog posts, user guides, technical summaries, and other data center planning material is also published as part of this project.

  • Asia Data Center Rack Consolidation

    • January 2012 to April 2012
    Team Members: Matthew Damp

    With the Cisco UCS systems being deployed, it was decided to shrink our data center footprint to take advantage of the greater density available. Working with all key stakeholders, we redesigned the data center layout to eliminate entire racks of equipment, reducing amount of equipment in operation, and completely relocating core network gear. Total data center operational costs were reduced 11.4% even though an entirely new NetApp SAN was installed, the number of services offered increased, and the number of users supported by the data center grew over 10%.

  • Asia Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS) - Phase 2

    • December 2011 to August 2012
    Team Members: Matthew Damp

    The second phase upgraded both the VMware hosts and the attached storage. The hosts were migrated onto a Cisco UCS system with blade servers and fabric interconnects. Initially connected to the existing EMC SAN, a new NetApp SAN was purchased and all virtual machines and templates were migrated across.

    The NetApp is now a shared storage device and is supporting source-code distribution via the NetApp SnapMirror software. The built-in de-duplication and snapshot backup system allows us to offer a 10-day backup protection while reducing disk consumption in half. Combined with VMware thin provisioning, we are maintaining a 4:2:1 ratio of requested, used by virtual machine, and actual disk usage.

    SolarWinds Network Performance Monitor, Virtualization Manager, Mobile Admin, and Storage Manager were also deployed so allow for better usage tracking and trend analysis. This also allows us to generate “show back” reports to our internal business groups.

  • Asia Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS) - Phase 1

    • October 2010 to May 2011
    Team Members: Matthew Damp

    Used Dell PowerEdge 2U servers, VMware Infrastructure 4.0, and an EMC fiber channel SAN at a data center in Japan to provide virtual servers to people across Asia. Provided a wide variety of operating systems and application templates for software testing, pre-deployment scenario walk-throughs, training, proof of concept, and solution demonstrations.

    Also used the IaaS system and Microsoft Terminal Server Gateway to provide business continuity after the March 2011 earthquake in Japan.

    At deployment, the Asia IaaS system used 20 Dell servers and over 18 terabytes of disk storage. The Phase 2 project replaced the Dell 2U servers with the Cisco UCS platform and connect to NetApp SAN with FCoE.

  • Global IT Information Portal and Knowledge Base

    Team Members: Matthew Damp

    Created a Microsoft Office SharePoint Server 2007 (MOSS 2007) based publishing site to act as a centralized information portal for IT products. Used SharePoint Designer and minor Java Scripts to re-brand the SharePoint site to match the public facing corporate websites.

    As part of a multi-phase, multi-region, multi-language project, leveraged the publishing features of MOSS 2007 to create a Knowledge Base with technical review and publishing workflows. Also created a centralized policies and processes repository for official documents with translation, publishing, and approval workflows. Worked with an outsourced programming team to create customized translation workflows, web parts, and improved site features.

    Worked with owners of other applications to use the SharePoint web services so that Knowledge Base content could be displayed in other systems such as the IT incident management / service ticketing system.

  • Global Groove Deployment

    • March 2008 to October 2008
    Team Members: Matthew Damp, , Eric Richeux

    Worked with a global team of system engineers to deploy Microsoft Office Groove 2007 infrastructure.
