Bob Kodrzycki's Summary

I am a biotechnology consultant that works with executives and managers in biotechnology companies to evaluate and commercialize technology, identify new markets and to optimize R&D programs.

I work with senior management (C-level, directors, managers) and their teams to:
- Solve technology problems that hinder R&D or access to new markets,
- Analyze technology or intellectual property landscapes,
- Develop technology roadmaps and implementation plans,
- Evaluate markets for bioproducts (US and Europe),
- Identify the right collaborators, partners and customers for biotech R&D companies,
- Bio-based technology development and
- Development of bioproduct manufacturing facilities.

Examples include:
- Wrote several white papers for US ag biotech companies detailing opportunities for extraction of high value products from commodity crops, energy crop bioprocessing and logistics improvements, non-destructive analysis techniques and market analysis for bioenergy crops.
- For a US ag biotech company, developed a detailed roadmap for fuel pellet production from energy crops.
- Working with the Jamaican Ministry of Energy and Mining, developed a plan for the sugar industry to increase efficiency of biopower generation from bagasse and sustainable forestry.
- Patent landscape analysis for a agricultural biotechnology companies.
- Working with an entrepreneurial team to establish a wood pellet operation in the southeast USA for export of product to European utilities.

For a confidential discussion of your specific situation or project please contact me by phone +1.843.509.6492 or email bobk@encompassbiotech.com

Bob Kodrzycki's Experience

Principal

Encompass Biotech LLC

2007 – Present (6 years) Summerville, SC

Encompass Biotech: Innovation…Technology…Intelligence.

Understanding how science meets business and what it takes to build a successful biotechnology venture requires more than dedication and financing. As a technology consulting firm Encompass Biotech specializes in helping you understand your competitive position and how to move your technology into a successful business venture. More info at EncompassBiotech.com

Encompass Biotech specializes in the interface of technology and business.

• Monitor the changing competitive landscape of biotechnology to identify key players, technology trends, and the changing political environment.
• Provide critical technology consulting to agricultural biotech companies in R&D operations with an emphasis on creating breakthrough biotech products.
• Conduct patent strategic planning and landscape analysis.
• Provide technical expertise in patent prosecution, litigation, and infringement cases.

Chief Scientist

Phenotype Screening Corporation

2006 – 2009 (3 years)

Developers of revolutionary technology for non-destructive imaging of living plant root systems. Phenotype Screening Corp's low energy x-ray imaging technology allows researchers in agricultural biotechnology to repeatedly image developing roots under a variety of experimental environments without removing the plant from their growth container. Check them out at phenotypescreening.com.

As Chief Scientist of Phenotype Screening Corporation:

• Directed R&D for adapting plant growth systems to analysis using x-ray technology for seamless integration into existing agricultural biotechnology operations in industry and academia.
• Developed innovative granular rooting medium that allows natural root development while significantly improving image quality.
• Developed novel software-based methods for extraction of quantitative data from images and analysis of developing root systems.
• Wrote a commercialization plan for RootViz FS, the flagship digital x-ray imaging product.
• Served as company liaison to industrial and academic customers as well as trade organizations.
• Raised capital and operating funds through federal grants.
• Managed regulatory affairs.
• Conducted strategic planning and opportunity assessment that resulted in more than doubling the size of the business over two years.

Senior Research Scientist

ArborGen LLC

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Biotechnology industry

2002 – 2005 (3 years)

ArborGen is the premier forest tree biotechnology company developing advanced plantation stock for sustainable forestry for bioenergy and forestry applications. See ArborGen.com for more info.

As a founding employee of ArborGen:

• Developed the core elements of an international research program focusing on growth and biomass productivity of trees.
• Established scientific approaches to improved biomass productivity in trees that are currently in various stages of product development.
• Used a systems biology approach to transfer a knowledge base from Arabidopsis genomics and developmental biology directly to R&D programs in several species of forest trees.
• Established a functional genomics gene testing pipeline for hardwood and softwood trees that spanned laboratory, greenhouse, and field testing.
• Developed a technique for rapid biomass chemical analysis in collaboration with scientists at the National Renewable Energy Laboratory.
• Used bioinformatics to identify pine and Eucalyptus genes for improved biomass, bioenergy and bioproduct applications.
• Inventor on several US and World patent applications.
• Conducted ongoing competitive intelligence and strategic planning.
• Identified critical outside technology and negotiated licensing agreements.

Bob Kodrzycki's Education

Purdue University

PhD, Biochemistry, Molecular Biology, Plant Sciences

1985 – 1989

My PhD research at Purdue was in gene regulation in Brian Larkin’s lab. My project showed that the opaque-2 mutation in corn resulted in a transcriptional defect of maize zein genes. Zein is the major storage protein in corn seeds. My work was published in the inaugural issue of The Plant Cell:

Kodrzycki, R, Boston, RS, and Larkins, BA. (1989). The opaque-2 Mutation of Maize Differentially Reduces Zein Gene Transcription. Plant Cell 1:105-114.

Another interesting paper that my transcriptional work lead to is this one:

Lopes, MA, Coleman, CE, Kodrzycki, R, Lending, CR, and Larkins, BA. (1994). Synthesis of an Unusual Alpha-Zein Protein is Correlated with the Phenotypic Effects of the floury-2 Mutation in Maize. Molecular and General Genetics 245:537-547.
