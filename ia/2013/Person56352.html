anonymous bodies || art collective

June 2006 – Present (7 years 5 months)

anonymous bodies || art collective is an interdisciplinary performance company co-directed by Kate Watson-Wallace and Jaamil Olawale Kosoko, based in New York and Philadelphia. As a collective, the two co-directors create work independently and collaboratively, creating an array of art projects, that range from solo performances in gallery settings, to larger performance installations on-site. It is a project-based company that collaborates with a diverse group of dancers, visual artists, musicians, stylists, and DJs. The company draws upon the traditions of visual art, post-modern dance, site-specific study, conceptual and performance art, audience participation and public spectacle. Known for its creative use of ordinary space, anonymous bodies “has a gift for making pockets of space feel infinite. Wild imagination ruled.” (The New York Times)

Jaamil Olawale Kosoko's Projects

  • other.explicit.bodies.

    • March 2012 to January 2013
    Team Members: Jaamil Olawale Kosoko, Catherine Peila, Dance New Amsterdam, Art in Odd Places

    other.explicit.bodies is part of Dance New Amsterdam’s (DNA) LateNite series, a triannual event that is presented as a late evening venue and was created to give voice to artists working in performance art, experimental theater, and burlesque. The title of 2013 triannual, presented during APAP, is taken from that of the curator Jaamil Olawale Kosoko’s performance of the same name. The event took place over two nights, on January 11th and 12th, 2013.

  • JUXTaPOSE - A Performance Series

    • January 2012 to May 2012
    Team Members: Jaamil Olawale Kosoko, Studio 34, Marcel Foster, Zachary Svoboda, James Peniston

    JUXTaPOSE is a performance series curated by Jaamil Olawale Kosoko, and produced by James Peniston, the Co-Founder of Studio 34. Picking up where Nicole Bindler left off over a year ago with her StudioSeries, Kosoko has created a showcase whose mission is to support the work of more matured artists. Kosoko writes, “When I say matured, I mean artists who have been making professional work for 8 to 10 years. All of the artists featured in JUXTaPOSE are veteran performers and artmakers. They all have a detailed understanding of their process, their vision, and the role their voices hold in the overall landscape of regional dance and performance.”
