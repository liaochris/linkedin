Kern Marshall's Summary

I have a breadth and depth of experience that includes entrepreneurial and large company. After completing my MBA in 2004, I joined technology services startup TriFusion. My work centered around continual process improvement by identifying challenges, designing and implementing measurement systems, and developing scalable and repeatable processes.

During the four years I spent at TriFusion, I held multiple leadership roles including Director of Operations and General Manager, with P&L responsibility for the larger of two divisions. I spent more than a year onsite with our largest customer, Dell Computers.

My experience with Procter & Gamble began at global headquarters in Cincinnati, where I worked in the Pet Care division (Iams and Eukanuba). I now work in the Personal Power division (Duracell). In both areas, I have been able to solve complex problems using a financial framework. I am quick to tell you that I’m not just a finance guy, and the other problem-solving tools I use include process analysis, sales strategy, engineering, and human capital development.

One of my key professional phrases is, “You cannot manage anything that you cannot or do not measure.” Throughout my career, I have proven my ability to turn complexity into manageable pieces that can be measured, optimized, documented, and replicated. I speak fluent marketing, R&D, sales, operations, and IT, which allows me to collaborate across departments to develop bottom-line-enhancing initiatives.

I require challenge in my professional life, and this has carried through to my personal life, as well. Recently, I ran with the bulls in Pamplona, Spain, and I climbed a glacier/volcano in Iceland. I’m an adventurer, and I’m looking for treasure. In my work, that might mean that if I climb a big mountain, my treasure will be millions added to the company’s bottom line, or reduced wasteful spend. Looking for treasure – personally or professionally – is intellectually stimulating and energizing to me.

Global Cost Forecaster, Duracell

Procter & Gamble

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; PG; Consumer Goods industry

July 2012 – Present (1 year 4 months) Bethel, CT

In this forecasting role, I own quarterly and long-term forecasting for $1.5B Cost of Goods Sold budget of the global supply chain for Duracell batteries. This includes seven manufacturing plants in the U.S., Belgium, and China. I spearhead continuous improvements in the supply chain, recommending gap-close opportunities versus targets.

--I built a sustainable, repeatable, and auditable process for the FY 2014 budget and forecast that removed as much as three days from the delivery of each month’s financial forecast. The new process will result in a cumulative savings of nearly one month per year.

By incorporating this system into all management reporting, managers now have leaner and more efficient reporting tools with which to make business-critical decisions.

Senior Financial Analyst, P&G Pet Care (Iams & Eukanuba)

Procter & Gamble

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; PG; Consumer Goods industry

December 2008 – July 2011 (2 years 8 months) Cincinnati, OH

This position was in the Pet Care division, focused on long-term business growth strategy and innovation planning for Iams and Eukanuba.

--Pet Care is the single largest category globally in which P&G plays, with more than $50B in annual sales. Although P&G has a relatively small share of this category, I saw a tremendous opportunity for growth. It was important to identify the competition’s key advantages, cost structures, and margins. I led the division’s first comprehensive Pet Care Competitive Profit Pool analysis, resulting in a top-down income statement for key brands. We also generated trend-analysis reports that showed profitability of premium versus mid-tier food and rates of cannibalization when one brand has both tiers.

The cross-functional team I assembled for this analysis included:
-Sales (retail trade funds)
-R&D (product formulation)
-Procurement (cost of formulations and packaging)
-Marketing (trade dollars and media spend)

As a result, the analyses we conducted became pivotal for numerous projects, and the model I designed has been used by several other P&G brands for competitive intelligence work.

--By collaborating with multiple business units, I analyzed data to determine the potential costs and profits for each Pet Care division’s future projects. As a result, I was able to lock in a five-year innovation strategy plan that provided the division with long-term master plan sufficiency and better choices for future projects.

--I led an analysis of three years of product launches. This included identifying attractive investment opportunities and key growth possibilities; I also made kill or cure recommendations on bleeder SKUs.

--When the Pet Care division introduced a new product line specifically for senior pets (11 years of age or older), I played a significant role in increasing retailer margin and enhancing P&G’s profitability by making sizing and pricing recommendations.

Operations Manager, Dell Computers

TriFusion

Partnership; 51-200 employees; Information Technology and Services industry

July 2005 – October 2006 (1 year 4 months) Austin, Texas Area

In this unique role, I was onsite for TriFusion’s largest customer, Dell Computers. I managed operations including implementing measurement systems to identify and eliminate systemic waste, instituting documented processes, and developing training manuals. I managed a headcount of as many as 75 through four direct reports.

--TriFusion’s role at Dell was to provide additional customization beyond Dell’s capability. One of our first major projects was a laptop data migration, which involved receiving laptop boxes from a client on Friday evening, making the necessary changes, and having it back on the client’s desk by Monday morning. I took over this operation soon after it began, and we were struggling to meet the deadlines.

I immediately implemented a time-and-motion study. This allowed me to quickly identify gaps and bottlenecks, and I then rearranged the production area’s floor plan layout to mitigate the problems. As a result of these changes, we were able to ship 100% of received laptops on time, with fewer total FTEs. Most importantly, the changes left a very favorable impression with Dell, and were instrumental in our dramatic growth of business with them.

--One of our key challenges onsite with Dell was achieving appropriate staffing levels for the two-shift operation, which had highly variable volume and needed a wide range of skill sets. We were constantly over staffing to avoid capacity misses, which was significantly lowering our profit margin.

I built partnerships with staffing agencies to create an on-demand queue of labor on short notice. As a result, we were able to improve service levels to more than 95% shipped to commit and improve gross margins.

--Dell and TriFusion developed a “Rack & Stack” program, where servers purchased from Dell could be shipped to customer Data Centers fully configured and connected. I oversaw the launch and execution of this program, which reduced data center install and deployment times from 2-3 weeks to 1-3 days.
