Claire A. Murray's Summary

Seasoned consultant and admitted Geek (even have the mug) who loves human services.

* Bringing people and technology together is part of what I do.
* Paying it forward is how I have always lived even before I had a name for it.
* My multiple roles and skills have converged in this position at Tri-CAP where I shift from human specialist to technologist constantly.

Tax Program: Developed and grew Volunteer Income Tax Assistance (VITA) program in 4 years from preparing 35 tax returns to more than 200. 70% increase from year 3 to year 4; IRS recognition and accolades for increasing service while maintaining their high quality standards. Coordinate and manage all-volunteer crew; train volunteers in applying tax software properly to tax law; resolve taxpayer issues; and oversee day-to-day operation of intense, 4-month tax preparation season. Assist taxpayers, year-round, who have past tax returns due.

Asset Formation/Financial Literacy: Coordinate volunteer instructor crew of bankers, credit union staff, and community volunteers; teach topics on the competencies of earning, spending, saving, borrowing, and protection against risk; developed special session on budgeting; developed 2nd Chance Banking Program.

Cyber Cafe @ Malden Square: Co-manage day-to-day operations and oversee all technology needs; upgraded all equipment in 2008, saving $6,000 by using thin client (virtual) workstations instead of actual computers. Serve 50+ members daily; have served more than 7,000 unique individuals since 2001; offer basic and intermediate computer skills courses; host the VITA tax preparation program; and provide technology and space for other organizations to provide their staff/clients with technology training.

Benefits Specialist: helped clients apply for or get advocacy on health insurance, SNAP (food stamps), social security, and other public benefits. Served on coalitions & networks to address public policy issues and improve advocacy results.

Specialties

Helping solopreneurs and small business owners/managers link their business needs to their human and technology needs; workforce development; curriculum management, career development, and training; web design, Web 2.0 tools, desktop support for end users, technology planning and integration, networked systems (LAN), and end user training
