Jeff Hunter's Summary

Jeff is a process control engineering technologist with a Bachelor of Arts degree in Business Management and Computer Science from Wilmington College and an Associate degree in Robotics and Machine Technology from Southern Ohio College. After serving 5 years in the Navy as an Aviation Electrician, Jeff joined the Department of Energy and became a process control engineering technologist at the Fernald Feed Materials facility, a nuclear weapons processing plant. Along with process control instrument asset management and calibration, Jeff was responsible for Site Communications including Fernalds own Fire Department site and county mutual aid communications.

After 8 years of service with the Department of Energy and two college degrees, facing the prospect of the Fernald plant closing, Jeff left to join the pharmaceutical industry and spent another 8 years perfecting his process control and machine technology experience. He also started several entrepreneurial ventures including businesses involved in small business services and real estate ventures, now all under one business entity called Aletheia Management LLC with some 20 plus years experience helping other business owners succeed. While working with another entrepreneurial business, Jeff was introduced to an Indoor Organic growing process. From this introduction Jeff spent the next 3+ years developing and integrating advanced technology to create what has become Safe Harvest.

Small Business services, Small Business Funding, Risk Management, Group Voluntary Benefits, Identity Theft Protection & Restoration services.

The Customer Advantage (TCA)

Marketing and Advertising industry

July 2011 – Present (2 years 6 months) Cincinnati Area

TCA is a tool for consumers to save money in their local community, providing local merchants a risk-free and "controlled" advertising medium, and for families, nonprofits and entrepreneurs, a cost-free opportunity to earn a passive residual income. It is modeled after mega success stories Groupon and Living Social, so there is no doubt the business model works.

We often wonder how a merchant can afford to offer such substantial savings. There are 5 primary reasons why this is an attractive advertising tool:
1. Acquire new and potential "repeat" customers

2. Bring back old customers with an offer they can't refuse

3. Exposure to thousands of local consumers via the email (if 99.9% of recipients do not purchase the discount, at the very least, they are now aware of the business and may visit in the future).

4. No risk...paid ads have no assurance of any customers and are quite expensive. If the TCA offer is not accepted by any consumer, the merchant marketing cost is zero!

5. Controlled growth- the merchant limits these discounts. If target new customer growth is 100, than they cap the offer at that amount. If there was no cap, they could have 1,500 customers which would be cost prohibitive

Just imagine the potential financial difference TCA can make in the nonprofit world. This totally passive fundraising opportunity leverages the power of word of mouth marketing and Social Media, in the same way the pioneer companies grew to 40M users without a "residual" financial reward attached.
