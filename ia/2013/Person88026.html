Mary Campbell Gallagher, J.D., Ph.D.'s Summary

Trained in law at Harvard and in theoretical linguistics at the University of Illinois, Mary Campbell Gallagher, J.D., Ph.D., is a professional speaker and writer and the founder and president of BarWrite® and BarWrite Press. BarWrite® offers large-group classes that prepare candidates for the bar exam, specializing in training foreign-educated lawyers, and it trains practicing lawyers to write efficiently. BarWrite® Press publishes study guides, including Dr. Gallagher's books Scoring High on Bar Exam Essays, originally published by Simon & Schuster and now in its Third Edition, and Perform Your Best on the Bar Exam Performance Test (MPT). Her books have been adopted as course texts in law schools and are best-selling bar-exam resources.

Aided by an advisory committee of successful foreign-educated BarWrite® alumni, Dr. Gallagher brings to bear her training in linguistic theory, her experience as an active member of Paris-based architectural preservationist association SOS Paris, and her own more recent study of civil law, to create bar-preparation classes for foreign-trained lawyers in New York and Houston. She coaches lawyers privately, and she lectures nation-wide and in Europe.

Dr. Gallagher is a member of the New York State Bar Association Committee on Legal Education and Admission to the Profession, of the New York City Bar Association, of the Women's Bar Association, and of the Harvard Club of New York City, the National Speakers Association (Board of Directors of the New York City Chapter, 2002-2010), the Authors Guild, the American Society of Journalists and Authors, the National Arts Club, the Society for the Advancement of Consulting, the Association des Journalistes du Patrimoine, the French-American Bar Association, the Harvard Club of France, and numerous other associations for lawyers, urbanists, consultants, and writers.

Specialties: CLE, Legal Writing, Preparing Foreign-Trained Attorneys, Speaking, Bar Preparation, Publishing Study Guides
