Hèlen A. Vink's Summary

It is wonderful to combine the modern invention of photography and the eastern approach on the nature of perception into a path, photographic processes and resulting photographs. Allowing ourselves to become more available to the things around us without the biases, filters and formulas often associated with photography, our experience and expression of day-to-day moments becomes more rich and endlessly varied—beyond what we think.
One moment, one shot.

About Workshops - 1 evening introductory talk on contemplative photography with photo presentation, followed by 3 days of visual exercises, photographic assignments and photo reviews.
We can express our experiences of seeing things as they are, of our direct rich visual world, with the medium of photography. There is a moment of pure perception before we start thinking about what it is what we see and what is means, before we like it or don't like it. When we relax all these beliefs, preoccupations and are free of interpretation, we can be completely present in the moment. We express this moment, our experience of seeing, elegantly and precisely with our cameras. Free and joyful.

Module 'Slow Down, See Clearly'.
1/2 day module on experiencing one's own pure perception: includes a photo presentation, visual exercises and individual and group reflection. Especially suitable for corporations, groups and as part of team building, seminars and private groups.

Miksang Photos
The resulting photos can be of great benefit in a variety of environments. By looking at the photos anyone can in any setting in a direct and simple way connect with the qualities of pure perception being expressed, as the person who has engaged into this process and formed the pure perception into a photo.

Specialismen: teacher, photographer, nature of perception, perception, meditation instructor

Gagaku & Bugaku

Bugaku & Gagaku

2002 – 2005

Bugaku, the ancient dance and music of the Japanese Imperial Court, is more than fourteen hundred years old. This stately dance is performed in richly brocaded and highly stylized costumes, expressing contemplative mind in a cultural context. Gagaku is the oldest existing orchestral music in the world, introduced into Japan from China in the 8th century AD. Bugaku, the dance form, is characterized by its elegance, solidity and space. Together, the music and the dance radiate timelessness and peace. The several active bugaku groups within Nalanda study with master musician and dancer Togi Sensei, a Living National Treasure of Japan. Togi's family has been part of the Japanese Imperial Music Department for 1000 years. He is presently a priest-musician at the Meiji Shrine in Tokyo.

Activities and Societies: Bugaku training with Dessie Howard in Dechen Chöling, France and in Boulder, CO USA. Bugaku training with Arawana Hayashi in Dechen Chöling, France and Sky Lake Lodge, NY State.
