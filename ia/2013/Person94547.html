Michael Rychener's Summary

I am a software engineer with over 15 years experience in the Research Triangle Area of NC. My most recent work has been as a contractor on short-term software assignments, including Java software development in these areas: airport optimization, interfacing SaaS with an SAP purchase order system, and change team for IBM Systems Director.

Before that, I worked on a multi-year project, a web-based application for IBM's CIO. It included tracking and evaluating corporate initiatives and projects, combining ETL database integration with Business Intelligence. This resulted in a web site with over 25 custom web pages for entering data for various internal customers.

The IBM internal web site featured data gathering (custom ETL), pulling and combining data from various sources, then reporting in multiple formats (eg, Dashboards, Scorecards, PDF files, spreadsheets). I was active in all phases of definition and development, along with leading a team of developers in China.

I submitted two patents and previously developed a search engine for the PC Company's global eSupport site.

Before IBM, I worked as a consultant on a handful of desktop and database applications for several RTP companies. My academic background includes expert systems, artificial intelligence and robotics. I am proficient in Java, Perl, Websphere, Python, C, HTML, XML, Javascript, Linux / Unix shell scripts and various open source packages.

My main interests are: database applications and their user interfaces; business rules, inferencing and expert systems; knowledge management; ETL data integration and analytics; web applications and services (incl. web design). As a software engineer, I enjoy learning new software approaches; I can solve difficult problems and develop innovative approaches. I especially like working with open source frameworks and search engines.
