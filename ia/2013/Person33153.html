Darryll DeCoster's Summary

I've been bending the web to my will for more than a dozen years. Whether it's been building enterprise e-commerce sites, customizing Wordpress, writing custom JavaScript or making use of jQuery to fine tune interaction, building and design for the web is my passion.

In my grab-bag of tools you'll find HTML5, CSS3, jQuery, Wordpress, PHP, and others all being convinced to play nicely on many platforms including Facebook and Twitter, and even Salesforce and Jive.

I don't ignore our mobile friends either. I build responsive sites that are designed for a cohesive, singular experience regardless of the size of the screen you happen to be using. I also build iOS native apps, and have a couple for sale in the App Store.

All the while keep them Social aspect close at hand, working with Marketing teams to incorporate the web presence into the social markets to further enhance reach and expand the conversation.

Darryll DeCoster's Projects

  • Adaptu.com

    • March 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Darryll DeCoster, Verne Lindner, Vijay Balakrishnan, Fredrick Roberts, Gretchen Cummings, Lisa Hannah, Benjamin Barniskis, Jenna Forstrom, Debra Paynter

    Adaptu is an online and mobile service that puts you in charge of your financial future.
    Development spanned several platforms and included custom Salesforce.com Apex pages to deliver a secure logged in user experience and secure access to the members personal finance information, as well as a highly customized and secure Wordpress site to deliver financial content from industry experts and the personal finance community.

  • Adaptu Financial Quiz

    • April 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Darryll DeCoster, Verne Lindner, Jenna Forstrom

    Verne and I created a Flash quiz to be hosted on Facebook to help engage Adaptu's Facebook page fans with Adaptu.com. Developed in Flash, using AS3, the quiz was integrated with custom landing pages designed specifically for each of the personalities that were associated with the players answers, and then tied back to Adaptu.com with hooks into the registration process of the Salesforce backend.

    The Facebook app was hosted through Salesforce, and then directed towards static HTML5 based landing pages.

  • Zippo Encore Multinational Music Site

    • 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Darryll DeCoster, Brent Tyler

    The website for the Zippo Encore music program began as a .NET, database driven application that supported the US market only, and offered bands and musicians a front door to the Zippo Encore program while also giving visibility to the bands fan-base.

    In 2011 the project expanded to incorporate several countries, and it became clear that existing site wouldn't handle the localization requirements needed. Over the course of 2.5 months, the site was rebuilt using the robust Wordpress platform, incorporating many jQuery interactive enhancements as well as social integration to give both Zippo and their partners easy access to the administrative tools needed to run their programs in each of the supported countries.

    The core structure and Wordpress theme was built on an HTML5 backbone, and utilized popular Wordpress plugins to allow the site to be easily accessible on mobile devices.

    On this project I was the designer and developer, and worked closely with the marketing team and several national and international partners to get the sites localized and launched on time.

  • WestBeer.com

    • 2006 to Present
    Team Members: Darryll DeCoster, Litsa Decoster, Gordon Wetzel-Stewart

    WestBeer.com was a web site project for a Glasgow, Scotland based micro-brew, West Brewing. I designed and developed this website using standards oriented practices in a series of HTML pages and minimal CSS to allow for fast loading times.

    The site integrated photographs of the brewing facility and restaurant taken by the owner and brewmaster, Gordon Stewart. Other aspects of the project also included integrating Flash for image slideshow as well as Google Analytics for tracking.

  • HP Real Estate Document Assistant Interactive Help

    • 2001 to Present
    Team Members: Darryll DeCoster, Gwyn Mauritz

    This was a companion to the launch of the HP REDA (Real Estate Document Assistant). It was an interactive, Flash-based help app that incorporated product animation for tutorials simulating actual usage of the product that the custom would receive. The interactive pieces work in tandem with technical documentation and links directly to the HP REDA software to make it easy for the customers to find the help they needed and get back into the application quickly.
