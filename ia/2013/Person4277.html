During my time at Full Tilt Poker, I was responsible for the design of a number of major new software features (in particular, poker features which are customer facing and reach an audience of millions of poker players). Poker Room Analyst

PokerStars

Privately Held; 1001-5000 employees; Gambling & Casinos industry

April 2006 – October 2010 (4 years 7 months) Isle of Man

I joined PokerStars in April 2006 as a Poker Support Specialist, and was promoted to Poker Room Analyst in November 2007, working as part of the Poker Room Management Team. In my most recent role, I had two key functions:

- Managing the PokerStars.it site aimed at the Italian market. I was responsible for managing the tournament schedule, developing promotions in conjunction with the regional marketing teams, and general management of the site. In less than a year, PokerStars.it went from being a newcomer to the market to the largest independent operator in Italy.

- Working with developers to build new features for the PokerStars software. I worked with the Project Management Office in Canada to clearly define requirements for each new feature, and manage the development priorities for software improvements. Some notable features I have worked on include multi-currency support, auto-rebuy for ring games, Team Pro visibility, time zone support, synchronised breaks, improved game filtering, and some responsible gaming functions.

In my time at PokerStars, I lent my skills to many other areas of the business, including Game Security (in particular, the prevention of collusion and bot usage), Content (writing and reviewing copy for the PokerStars web estate) and Poker Room Management (acting as an escalation point for poker issues, developing policies, and performing other general management tasks).
