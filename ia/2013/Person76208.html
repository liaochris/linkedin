About Curt's book:

The 1991 Ryder Cup epic journey and tale really began in 1985. At that point, Team Europe wrested a victory away from Team USA for the first time in years in this biannual match-up of golf professionals representing the US/Europe. Following another European victory in 1987 and a draw in 1989 - in 1991, the US team was ready to win.

The 1991 Ryder Cup also occurred during a time of heightened patriotism in the United States as the country was emerging victorious in the Persian Gulf War and the Cold War. During the match, fans forgot golf's gentlemanly code of conduct. Loud boos, jeers and cheers of "USA!" could be heard from the gallery and it quickly became evident that this match was about more than just golf.

In The War By The Shore, Curt chronicles this pivotal competition. He interviewed dozens of key players from both teams and provides historical context to explain why the tension was so high at this particular Ryder Cup venue.
