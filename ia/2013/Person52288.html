Heather Rushall's Summary

Heather is an ambitious, confident goal-driven young woman who is highly skilled and passionately pursuing a career in the field of journalism. A self-described "looky-loo," she admits to holding up traffic to catch a glimpse of the scene of an accident and spends hours listening to the police scanner, attempting to decipher and memorize police code and listening in on local drama.

Although her interest is in crime, politics and the courts, she has experience with breaking news, editorial and features writing. She is currently working as a freelance reporter for the North Coast Current out of Encinitas - a city in the northern part of San Diego county. She spent 18 months as an opinion columnist at The Daily Aztec and gained experience during her last semester at SDSU as an editorial intern at Patch.com covering anything from entertainment to news, as well as taking on the weekly small business column, "Behind the Biz."

Her experience extends beyond basic news gathering and story writing to include fieldwork, conducting interviews, taking photographs and video, as well as online editing and publishing. While acting as the web editor for The Daily Aztec for two semesters, she worked extensively with WordPress, Drop Box and Hootsuite to edit, post and maintain the online version of the newspaper. Her position required both web editing and social media expertise.

While writing opinion editorials has been Heather's favorite experience as a journalist so far, her true passion is in dramatic, long-form stories and her "dream careers" include crime reporting, embedded journalism and correspondence. Regardless where journalism takes her, Heather's passion for a good story will always be what drives her.
