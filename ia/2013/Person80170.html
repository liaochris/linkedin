Leon Benjamin's Summary

Social business designer. Managed a number of successful social network/community implementations over the past 13 years with organisations counting British Airways, Microsoft & BT. Co-founder of Sei Mani, an enterprise social business services company that helps organisations change the way they work. Sei Mani currently leads a number of enterprise Social Collaboration initiatives delivering ‘Facebook inside’ technologies that enable organisations to transform the way they work.

Previously, a twenty-year track record of complex, multi-project delivery, the management of change and the formulation of business and technology strategy, in financial services, telecommunications and retail.

Unusual ability to straddle business and IT disciplines and comfortable with the detail in each. Passionate about value driven performance cultures, leadership, the power of community and the culturally sensitive treatment of people.

Attributes: Fresh ideas with the ability to translate them into the art of the practical, deliver and complete. Experienced both sides of supply and demand and felt the challenges at the customer end. The ability to listen. Civility.

Specialties: Collaboration, Social business, design, social media, adoption, programme manager, project manager, change agent, agile, software development, scrum, ecommerce, retail, web applications, social media, social software, offshore, community, multi-channel, insurance, customer experience, flexible working

Leon Benjamin's Projects

  • Flexible Working enabled by enterprise social media

    • February 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Leon Benjamin, Elisa Nardi, Colin Miles

    One of the greatest challenges facing organisations in the 21st century is how to manage people at a distance.

    Managers and employees spend hours filling out activity reports. Now they can just look at activity streams to see what people are doing and so don't care where they are. This makes people more productive, more connected and more engaged. There are some startling bottom line financial benefits that support the idea that companies can make more money if money isn't the only thing on their mind.
