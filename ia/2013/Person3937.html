Alex Czajkowski's Summary

There’s a perfect storm brewing-where entertainment brands collide with social and mobile right at their intersection with gaming. The new Gala Interactive is at the centre of it.

Gala Interactive (Gibraltar) Limited is a newly formed division of Gala Coral Group, coming together in 2012 and launching the all new GalaCasino.com and GalaBingo.com under the leadership of a team from Bwin.Party, William Hill, Sportingbet, GTECH, 888, Mansion, Sky, Victor Chandler and other top tier gaming brands.

In order to compete in an already crowded market with similar products, the team decided to tie to brands that resonated with their prospective player base. So, they launched GalaBingo.com with popular reality-TV celebrity Peter Andre. Selected, much to the surprise of the group’s CEO, by surveying the player base and, importantly, by also overlaying the celebrity’s social footprint.

We’ve seen entertainment brands meet gaming before, Red or Black or Britain’s Got Talent games on TV and online. Brands, but not celebrities. And we’ve all seen celebs meet gaming, but with no real social component. What happens when all three collide—TV-based entertainment, celeb-led social and gaming—both online and offline?

The numbers confirmed the team’s belief that exclusive, branded, content, leveraged over social networks and into retail would further differentiate the product. So, now Gala Interactive has a roadmap of celebrity-led products and promotions that would fill a summer festival’s line-up. Driven directly by player input, the line-up includes a few oldies, but also others as fresh as a Saturday night.

And since the deals are exclusive, there is only one place in town to play those games, to ‘touch fame’, either through the games or literally at one of our celeb-laden parties.

Perhaps best of all, Gala’s efforts are reaching new players, bringing many of them to online gaming for the first time. They know the Gala brand, trust it, and they certainly love their TV idols.
