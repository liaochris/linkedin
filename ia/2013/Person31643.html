David Cooke's Summary

We are in an age where nothing is more important that delivering (insert buzzword here). We live in a buzzword environment where fear influences too many behaviors, decisions, management styles, lives, and outcomes.

I have been a sales guy, a dad, a coach, and a business adviser. I have celebrated the highest of highs and the witnessed the lowest of lows. I have watched businesses cave before me and I have participated in incredible, powerful and profitable turnarounds.

Business, life, happiness, success, and accomplishment is all about people. Your customers, your team, your family, your friends, and your advisers are all valued, necessary, and critical resources.

I know how to sell. I have developed and educated thousands of sales people to be successful, energized, and productive.

I know how to coach. I have guided, mentored, and groomed business managers into wonderfully inspiring leaders.

I know profit and strategy. I have participated in and have lead several incredible turnaround projects that turned sinking ships into shining stars.

I know how to lead. I have built teams that discovered the power of respect, integrity, and communication to accomplish big outcomes.

More than anything else, I know what is important. I have learned how to be a loving, committed dad and a successful, accomplished business leader in the midst of life's most painful, challenging, and difficult experiences -- a son's heroin addiction.

Nothing prepared me for the struggle. Yet, nothing has blessed, taught, or influenced my life in a more positive, effective way than this experience. The lessons learned have become a guide for all that I do, share, teach, and offer others as they deal with the personal and professional struggles everyone faces daily.

Whatever the challenge, whatever the problem, whatever the situation -- the Four Wheels of Personal Leadership provides the path to the outcome of any difficult journey.
