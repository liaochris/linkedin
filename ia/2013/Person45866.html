Fran Severn's Summary

Travel & lifestyle writer with credits in regional, national, and international publications. Focus on Mid-Atlantic, but travel wherever assignments and curiosity take me. My best and latest articles are at http://franstravels.wordpress.com/

Staff writer for Striped Pot, on-line travel magazine. (www.stripedpot.com) The magazine currently attracts 50,000 unique visits per month.

Public Relations professional with over 20 years' experience, including PR Director for Maryland Office of Tourism and Community Relations Director for Upper Chesapeake Health System. Specializing in working with small businesses and self-employed to use traditional and social media techniques to promote their businesses, particularly tourism businesses and bureaus.

Specialties: Articles for consumer and trade publications and websites. Consulting and writing especially for tourism offices and CVBs. Copywriting and editing for businesses and associations.

Fran Severn's Experience

Freelance Writer

Various Publications

January 1983 – Present (30 years 11 months)

I've been freelancing since 1983 and have over 300 credits in everything from Smithsonian Air & Space and Delta Sky to the Harford County Business Ledger. I've written about pilots who tow advertising banners, being a "lab rat" for medical testing for a vaccine for cat allergies, the challenges of running a family business, insurance issues for equestrians, and a pow-wow with members of the Sioux Nation on the anniversary of Little Big Horn. I am equally comfortable writing about the moral implications of in utero surgery and zip-lining in the Carolinas. An archive of my work is at www.severnwriter.com.

    The range of my freelance articles is vast. I've written about the final flight of the F-111 fighter/bomber, a school for butlers in London, and oyster farms on the Chesapeake Bay. I've tried street luge, toured the only tea plantation in the US, ridden a camel in Morocco and a hot air balloon over Amish country. When traveling, I try to find the spirit of the place, and that means spending my time with the locals -- where they eat, where they shop, where they hang out. You'll find me at the local hardware store and market, not the souvenir shop. The world is endlessly fascinating; learning about it and understanding the people who create it is my "continuing mission."

Fran Severn's Projects

  • USA on the Road

    • June 2012 to September 2012
    Team Members: Fran Severn

    This is a guidebook of road trips across the USA. The primary market is US, British, and Australian tourists. I was hired to drive and update two of the trips: one from New Jersey, through Virginia and North Carolina, ending in Savannah. The other from Northern Virginia through the Blue Ridge, to Tennessee and Arkansas. I verified the existing information, changing and updating as needed; adding new attractions, dropping sub-standard ones, and gathering plenty of material that could not fit into the book.

  • Insight Travel Guide New England

    • June 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Fran Severn

    Updated, revised, and rewrote Insight's Guidebook to New England. This required verifying the existing information, dropping those which no longer existed, updating old material, finding and profiling new attractions. I wrote front-of-the-book essays about the culture and history of the region, including education, politics, literature and the arts, and ecological initiatives. The project also required verification and proper identification of locations on a series of maps.
