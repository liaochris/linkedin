Greg May's Summary

I am an attorney focusing on civil appeals and select civil litigation, as full time "of counsel" to Jones & Lester, LLP.

My appellate practice leverages the research and writing experience I've gained in more than a decade of litigating high stakes business cases, much of it in "David versus Goliath" scenarios, pitting the resources of the small firms I worked with against those of much larger firms, like those at where I started my career. I now put that experience to work on appeals in all areas of substantive law, be it business, personal injury, or family. The nuances of appellate practice transcend specific subject areas of practice.

My practice also includes select civil litigation. Interesting (and monetarily significant) cases get my attention.

To both aspects of my practice, I bring the perspective of a former client. As former general counsel of a software development company, I know full well the stresses and disruption caused by litigation, and closely counsel clients with an aim toward minimizing the inconvenience of litigation while still providing aggressive representation.

Specialties: My practice focuses on civil appeals and select civil litigation. Please see the summary under my business, The Law Office of Greg May, for details.

Greg May's Projects

  • Christie v. Kimball

    • January 2011 to January 2012
    Team Members: Greg May, Mark Lester

    Defending against the appeal in a trust matter, my firm obtained a favorable opinion establishing new precedent. The Published decision, Christie v. Kimball (2012) 202 Cal.App.4th 1407, establishes that a probate judge's authority to supervise the administration of a trust includes the authority to order an interim accounting, where the circumstances warrant one, even if the judge orders the accounting without a request from any party.
