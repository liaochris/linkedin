  • We Can't Fix It If We Don't Know What's Broken...An MRP Exercise

    • Professional Development Meeting Presentation
    • 2012
    Authors: John Capron, CPIM

    Many Production & Inventory Management folks work with MRP output. Unfortunately, for many people, this is a "black box" output. They work with the results, but have not been trained in how those results are calculated. The evidence of this can be seen with more and more business entities setting the planning parameters globally for their MRP processing. An opportunity for more effective planning is being lost. This exercise is going to take a reverse approach with the PDM attendees. Everyone will be asked to calculate a set of MRP outputs. To do this, everyone will be given all the planning data necessary to do this, and without further guidance, will execute the calculations. People will be free to work together, or separately. Following this, the actual results will be shared, and in the process, problems will be highlighted. The goal of this exercise is not to embarrass anyone. It's to expose what people don't know, which will provide us with the opportunity to fix what is broken.

  • Naive Forecasting

    • Professional Development Meeting Presentation
    Authors: John Capron, CPIM

    A naive forecasting model is a special case of the moving average forecasting model where the number of periods used for smoothing is 1. Therefore, the forecast for a period, t, is simply the observed value for the previous period, t-1. It also can be defined as Ft + 1 = At which is also known as the do-nothing forecast. So what is its value? The naive forecast provides a baseline level of accuracy against which all other forecasting efforts can be compared.
