Ian Aspinall's Summary

As Retail Broker Solutions Manager at GBST I am responsible for the distribution and enhancement of;

GBST FrontOffice™ - A dynamic, browser based platform offering consolidated client views via an Adviser Desktop with real-time information from GBST Shares™, DCA, Margin Lending and other back office sources.

Business Information Reporting (BIR) - Offers consolidated reporting via a number of prebuilt reports and dashboards that can be scheduled, emailed, exported and/or printed directly from the application. BIR Professional offers additional functionality such as the ability to create ad-hoc reports across a comprehensive data set that can be scheduled, emailed, exported and/or printed.

GBST Market Access™ – Offering both the GBST Real Time CMT Interface™ and GBST Margin Lending Interface™, Market Access provides a single point of interaction for financial institutions to integrate with Australia’s largest trading network. GBST enjoys over 50% of Australian brokers and lenders who use either GBST Shares™ or GBST Margin Lending™

Prior to this I had the responsibility of Head of Wholesale Platforms / Group Marketing for Investorfirst Limited (INQ.AX), working at a provider of OTC and CFD products where I had the responsibility of product development for a multi exchange, multi-currency, multi participant platform for wholesale financial, institutional and SME advisory services, and Divisional Operating Officer Retail Wealth at IWL Limited – which was purchased by CBA (ASX:CBA).

Included in my role at IWL was product development which included Virtual Broker Wrap (http://www.virtualbroker.com.au) which involved integrating our Echelon & VirtualBroker™ solutions as well as Sanford2Go (http://www.itwire.com/content/view/14834/545/) Australia’s first dedicated mobile share trading website for both 2G and 3G Internet enabled mobile phones and PDA’s

Ian Aspinall's Projects

  • VB Wrap

    • June 2007 to November 2007
    Team Members: Ian Aspinall, Anthony Brittain, Marnie Shervey

    Virtual Broker Wrap (VB Wrap) was a portfolio administration and tax reporting service which combined the Virtual Broker trading platform with the high-end wholesale Echelon portfolio administration and tax platform to provide and end-to-end solution for shadow brokers, financial planners and wealth managers.

    The result of the integration was that (a) Advisers and clients no longer have to battle with manually updating portfolios, downloading files or emailing contract notes as transactions were processed directly into the portfolio administration service and (b) Virtual Broker clients benefited from the provision of an online corporate actions solutions tailored to cope with today’s complex offerings.

  • Sanford2Go

    • August 2007 to October 2007
    Team Members: Ian Aspinall, Cliff Richards, Ruwan Weerasooriya

    While I was Divisional Operating Officer - Retail Operations at IWL it was identified that many short-term share traders needed to stay closely in touch with the market, not only to know what's going on but also with the ability to buy and sell. Further to this many Medium to Long Term Traders in some corporate environments had access to share trading websites restricted, removing their ability to trade shares and options as well as view stock quotes and watch lists.
    In 2007 the App revolution had not taken off yet and the mobile market was dominated by the Nokia Symbian Platform, Windows Mobile 5.0 devices and the Blackberry. The iPhone at that stage had ONLY been launched in the US (July 2007).
    To meet the need of Sanford Securities clients we launched Australia's first dedicated mobile share-trading website branded "Sanford2GO".
    It was accessible by Sanford clients who had either 2G or 3G internet-enabled mobile phones and PDAs and the service was trailed on a range of internet-enabled mobile devices such as the BlackBerry, i-mate JASJAM, PALM Treo 750, SAMSUNG I601 (Blackjack) as well as the Nokia N95.
    It was made available to Sanford's premium members who were already paying an existing monthly charge of $9.95 - a fee that could be waived at the time if they make at least two trades a month.
    The site was able to define the mobile device it was being viewed on by qualifying the borwser and optimise the experience to the screen size. It provided client security by employing 128-bit secure encryption (SSL) access while the client needed only to enter their existing username and password.
    Once logged on, Sanford members had the ability to;
    - trade both ASX listed equities and options via straight through processing (STP)
    - amend and cancel existing orders
    - review their portfolios
    - create, amend or cancel SMS alerts
    - review their equities or options watch screens
    - access a live equities or options quote
