Brandon Smith's Summary

Brandon Smith is a leading expert in workplace health and dysfunction.

He is the founder of www.theworkplacetherapist.com – a resource dedicated to eliminating dysfunction at work, improving workplace health and restoring focus and productivity in the workplace. Brandon also currently serves as faculty at Emory University’s Goizueta Business School where he teaches and researches on topics related to healthy workplace dynamics, leadership and communication.

Brandon has consulted with organizations across a broad spectrum ranging from for-profit corporations to non-profit institutions. Many of Brandon’s clients include numerous Fortune 100 “Best Companies To Work For.” In addition, Brandon Smith has spent nearly 15 years working with thousands of individuals in a counseling and coaching capacity.

Brandon resides in Atlanta with his wife and their three children.

Specialties:
- Executive coaching
- Leadership team alignment
- Corporate culture consulting
- Leadership programs and workshops
- Keynote addresses

... And fighting dysfunction at every turn.

Brandon Smith's Experience

Founder & Chief Therapy Officer

The Workplace Therapist (Sole Proprietorship)

Sole Proprietorship; 1-10 employees; Professional Training & Coaching industry

January 2011 – Present (3 years)

As a "workplace therapist" I work with individuals ranging from senior leaders to rising "rock stars" to produce the relationships, results, and impact they need and want. And in those cases where dysfunction in the workplace raises its ugly head, my job is to eliminate it. From nasty team disputes to coworkers that don't "play well in the sandbox" with others, curing dysfunction is what being a workplace therapist is all about.

For each of us, our workplace journey is about finding that right job that gives us meaning and making the choice to develop healthy relationships with the people we work with everyday. Somewhere along the way we've forgotten how simple work really is. I'm here to remind us and to make life and work just a little bit easier for all of us.

Check out my blog (www.theworkplacetherapist.com) where I take on a different "dysfunction of the month" and my radio segments on GPB as I combat dysfunction weekly.
