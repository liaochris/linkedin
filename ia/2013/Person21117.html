MJ Petroni's Summary

MJ Petroni is a consultant based in San Francisco, California. He is the owner and founder of Causeit, Inc., where he leads a team supporting innovation companies through alignment coaching, thought leadership strategy and ecosystem research. Here, he has offered tangible startup support, strategic product creation, brand strategy and coaching to over 250 clients, approximately 85% of whom are minorities. He studied a combination of Sociology, Anthropology, English and Latin American Studies at Lewis and Clark College, where he conducted ethnographic research in the emerging field of Cyborg Anthropology, examining the complex relationship between humans and technology. Recently, he has been focusing on community engagement work through branded innovation and content, offering his brand strategy and executive facilitation expertise in support of a major university and a major tech company. A lifelong learner, Petroni regularly attends thought leadership conferences like the TEDxSummit, Compass Summit, the Wisdom 2.0 Summit, Women 2.0 Conference and BlogHer Entrepreneurship. He also has spoken at SIBOS and will be teaching at Lewis and Clark College, as well as authoring the living Field Guide to Creating Culture of Innovation. He has also produced TEDxBellevue in coordination with other Causeit team members and is currently producing PurpleBeach in London with Annemie Ress, former Head of People Innovation at eBay Global.

Petroni is mentored by Mark Bonchek, PhD, former SVP of Networks and Communities at Sears, a recognized innovator in citizen engagement, social empowerment and world-class business innovation.

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Professional Training & Coaching industry

August 2005 – Present (8 years 2 months)

Causeit helps innovative ideas become real by empowering your team.

Causeit helps your teams make better, more creative decisions and, most importantly, actually execute on them. We help you tap into collective wisdom by engaging broad, sometimes complex ecosystems. We help paradigms shift by helping thought leaders share their Big Ideas with people who can make them real. We do all of this because we know that you have brilliant, world-changing ideas just waiting for the right place, at the right time.

Many companies seek to increase their capacity for innovation, and many consultants claim to be able to help them. This quest is narrowly focused on product development, with the aim of creating a better widget, resulting in a certain, iterative kind of innovation. It's not bad, but it's also not the only kind of innovation—and it's not enough to have you thrive.

Innovation with the power to transform markets and the larger world requires engaging a much broader spectrum—not just engineers, but educators, not just coders, but customers and even competitors. Deep change must occur across the entire context of your organization and the ecosystems you function in. Causeit's here to help.
