Marcia Eckerd's Summary

I have dedicated 25 years to helping children, teens and young adults with Aspergers Syndrome, Nonverbal LD and PDD NOS. For two years I've also been advocating for the needs of low income children with these special needs. I've been appointed by the CT State Legislature to the newly formed Autism Spectrum Disorder Advisory Council as one of two clinicians working directly with individuals with ASD to be included.

In my work, I find the parents of these children are relieved to find a therapist who "gets" their child. Adults respond to an approach that develops cognitive flexibility, social cognition and uses cognitive and mindfulness techniques.

I provide therapy, social skills coaching, neuropsychological evaluations and consultations for parents and adults. I work closely with parents, schools, advocates and attorneys so that children achieve success in a safe environment with adults who understand them.

I also provide professional education to teachers and other school professionals to help them gain a better understanding of "spectrum" kids and young adults.

One of my parents described my work: "Dr. Eckerd's insights have changed the way we parent, and have eliminated a lot of frustration at home. Her work with our school has had considerable impact. The result is a less anxious, more confident kid."

Coming from my background in neuropsychological evaluation and therapy, I understand the unique social and conceptual needs of these clients, young and older. I developed a social skills curriculum to teach social understanding to kids through young adults, and to teach families how to model and support these skills.. Social skills are the subject of my blog, http://www.psychologytoday.com/blog/people-skills".

Goals: to increase my practice and consulting, to stay informed about colleagues and "what's new"and network with others re practice, resources and writing.
