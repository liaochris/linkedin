Andrew Lavespere's Summary

Andrew Lavespere started his own company implementing SAP Portals on a contract basis in October 2004. Recent clients include Sysco, Newell Rubbermaid, The Home Depot, Anadarko Petroleum, Lennox International, Inc., BHP Billiton and the US Navy. Before opening his company, Mr. Lavespere was a Senior Technical Consultant for SAP America with close to four years of service and has been in consulting since 1991. The vast majority of his business as an independent contractor has been gained from former SAP colleagues that have moved to new companies or from client references. Since he had done work for them or someone whom they respected, they realized they were getting the Platinum level SAP experience at a fraction of the cost.

As a Senior Consultant with at SAP billing at a Platinum K6 rate, he specialized in the implementation of SAP Portals for some of SAP’s largest customers. He was recognized as a technical leader in his peer group and routinely mentored junior consultants while at SAP. His many years of experience as a consultant along with his analytical and troubleshooting skills have enabled him to succeed on every project in which he has been involved.

While at SAP, he lead SAP Portal implementation projects for Fortune 100 companies such as Raytheon, Lockheed Martin, Marathon Oil, Kindred Healthcare, Halliburton, International Paper, Weyerhaeuser and Delphi Automotive to name a few. His responsibilities included installing and configuring the Enterprise Portal environment, managing the development and implementation of portal content, creating content management processes, developing portal strategies, troubleshooting and escalating urgent technical Portal issues for SAP customers, and supporting SAP Portals Sales and Consulting Team. His last project with SAP was a technical and content lead on a highly visible cradle to grave implementation of SAP’s largest EP 6.0 deployment in North America at the time for Raytheon.

Specialties

SAP Portals implementation and integration. Architecture, System Administration, Content Administration and Development.

Sysco Foods

See my website for details of this project

A large SAP implementation to integrate business processes and systems across functions, reduce or remove unnecessary duplication of work and deliver new business capabilities. The project will implement three portals: one each for Customers, Suppliers and Associates. The projected named users are around 600,000 with an estimated 50,000 concurrent users when fully live with all operating companies.

As the SAP Portal Architect for the project working on the Basis team, I architected and implemented a Federated Portal Network (FPN). The architecture consists of a dedicated consumer portal (NW7.01) and multiple producer portals (NW70, NW7.01, CE 7.1.1, CE 7.2, CE 7.3) and supports the implementation of three separate portals for customers, suppliers and employees on a single portal platform.

The FPN landscape consists of a Consumer portal and ten Producer portals for sandbox, development, QA, training, performance and production. The landscape also includes an "N+1" environment for development and QA to allow for production support as well as ongoing project work. The portal integrates with OpenText as the Web Content Management system.

All requests are federated through the consumer to the producer portals utilizing the federated portal network with a combination of Remote Role Assignment, Remote Delta links and Remote Application Integration.

Highlights:
• Architected one of the largest SAP Federated Portals in existence
• Maintained and managed patching of over 120 java installations
• I was involved with architecting the Sybase Unwired Platform (SUP) system for an iPhone/Android based application allowing Sysco customers to maintain their inventories.
• Installed and configured NWDI using CTS+ allowing for production support and project work using 28 tracks.

Contractor

Newell Rubbermaid

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; NWL; Consumer Goods industry

April 2008 – August 2009 (1 year 5 months)

As the SAP Portal Architect for the Catapult project working on the Basis team, I designed and implemented the EP 7.0 Federated Portal Network (FPN) running on IBM AIX/DB2 with Microsoft Active Directory as the central user repository consisting of a dedicated consumer and multiple producer portals. This architecture is the platform for multiple portals for customers, employees, project teams and suppliers. By sharing a common architecture, maintenance and licensing costs are reduced while allowing for different content and user interfaces for each portal. Two of these portals are externally facing which I architected utilizing Apache as a reverse proxy

The FPN landscape consists of a Consumer portal and two Producer portals behind SAP Web Dispatchers for sandbox, development, QA and production:


•Both the QA and production Consumer portals are clustered.
•The consumer portal is the main KM infrastructure for shared documents as well as the authenticating portal.
•All requests are federated through the consumer to the producer portals utilizing the federated portal network via Remote Role Assignment.
•The “Apps” producer portal runs Java Dynpro applications not tied to a particular back end system including a Time Tracking application for the intranet/extranet portal and a Certificate of Compliance application for the customer portal. Future plans are to move the current Newell Rubbermaid intranet to this portal.
•The second producer portal is running on the Java stack for the BI 7.0 environment which is used to present reports and also interface with reports running on Business Objects (BOBJ).
