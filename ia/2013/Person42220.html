Eric Lawson's Summary

Originally from the Washington, DC area, Eric was interested in becoming an architect, studying three years of Mechanical Drafting in H.S., pre-architecture early in college, and returning years later to study AutoCAD, Advertising and Sales Promotion, Accounting and MS Office.

Over the years, Eric parlayed his hobbies in high-end home and car audio, IASCA Sound-Off Competitions, import drag, auto-x, and road racing, and graphic design into several career opportunities working as Performance Parts Mgr for two different Honda/Acura dealerships in CT and DE, Customer Service Manager for a German speaker manufacturer in Massachusetts, all the while competitively campaigning a 1993 Pro All-Motor Drag Honda Civic Hybrid in NHRA, IDRC, and BOTI drag racing events, SCCA auto-x racing events, and IASCA sound-off events all over the United States.

Along the way, Eric dabbled in graphic design as far back as Corel Draw 3 and always had an interest in corporate identity, branding, advertising, and marketing. Eric continuously found himself to be more involved and interested in the image and identities of the companies he was employed by and worked with. Many of these positions required Eric to work with designers to develop corporate identities, logos, brochures, advertising campaigns, catalogs, tee shirts and more. Eventually he decided to parlay that interest into a career by opening a sign & graphics business, Red Dog Designs, in Lewes, Delaware in 2005.

Running a sign & graphics business was such a thrill to Eric, being able to be involved with customers, helping bring their visions to life, that he decided to pursue additional training and returned to college at The Center for Digital Imaging Arts at Boston University (CDIA @ BU). After graduating from the Graphic and Web Program, Eric realized that he enjoyed the return to the Boston area and decided to remain and continue freelancing and to pursue career opportunities.
