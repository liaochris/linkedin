Marcus Enrico Williams II's Summary

Art Director & UI/UX Designer with extensive experience in identity development, publication design, HTML/CSS web design with basic Javascript implementation, creating a curriculum and instructing adult classes of 15+, 1-on-1 tutelage, photo editing, outdoor/indoor signage design, interior design, furniture design, and project management.

Marcus Enrico Williams II is a creative consultant and hands-on graphic designer/entrepreneur who founded two separately successful companies within six months of completing his undergraduate work. He has built a reputation for providing quality design among Atlanta-based, arts-related organizations via WE Design Atlanta, Inc., a non-profit organization he co-founded in 2007.

Williams is interested in User Interface design and how it is directly related to User Experience design.

Marcus freelances under the name Ectonym but is seeking meaningful employment in a corporate environment.

Williams is extensively involved in non-profit work and has logged over 1032 hours of volunteer service time providing administrative assistance and pro-bono Adobe Illustrator instruction since May 1, 2008.

The non-profit Williams co-founded, WE Design Atlanta, Inc. acts as liaison between 501 (c) (3) organizations and volunteer designers willing to offer their services pro bono and is one of three known not-for-profit design firms in the United States. WE Design offers free print, web, and interior design to organizations whose budget would better be spent hands-on in the community.

Among the pro bono designs Ectonym and WE Design have provided for non-profit organizations are logos and identity guides for numerous local institutions, web design and coding for PebbleTossers, Inc., and web design and accompanying company report design for the Genocide Intervention Network's Sudan Divestment Taskforce.

Specialties

Branding and Identity, UI/UX Design, Art Direction, HTML/CSS, Publication Design, Interactive Design

Marcus Enrico Williams II's Publications

  • FORM:artistic independence 2009 Special Edition

    • Williams-England Design
    Authors: Marcus Enrico Williams II, Jamie England

    Special Edition featuring cover art by David Baerwalde.

    It is WE Design Atlanta's endeavor to weave FORM: artistic independence 2009 and its contributors into the vibrant cloth that is Atlanta's arts community. This sophomore effort is a culmination of inspired creations from artists of all stripes: graphic designers, photographers, sculptors, painters and other mediums, all unified with a common thread of creating sans borders, constraints or impediments.

    The creatives featured within these pages propagate WE Design Atlanta's intent to place both the established and emerging artist on common ground. In our declaration of artistic independence, we support a collaborative environment with a singular goal of increasing involvement in the Atlanta arts community.

  • FORM:artistic independence 2008

    • Williams-England Design
    Authors: Marcus Enrico Williams II, Jamie England

    46 Atlanta area creative minds show their talents in this one-of-a-kind anthology. Williams-England's commitment to support the community and other emerging designers/artists is at the core of our philosophy. "We believe in designing a better world by coming together, sharing together, creating together and ultimately succeeding together!" This is the goal of FORM: Artistic Independence 2008, Atlanta. This is a published body of works from Atlanta's local designers, artists, photographers and other creative media. Included within are submissions from emerging and established creatives. FORM is presented as a collective pictorial/provocative survey of where the arts stand today in Atlanta and where they are headed in the future.

  • FORM: artistic independence 2010

    • Williams-England Design
    • December 10, 2010
    Authors: Marcus Enrico Williams II, Jamie England

    Special Edition featuring cover art by Lucha Rodriguez.

    The central purpose of FORM: artistic independence is to provide exposure for artists who live and work in Atlanta, and to chronicle the creative forces driving the city's dynamic artistic community.

    With FORM: artistic independence, WE Design Atlanta aims to provide equal space to artists both established and emerging who believe in creating art on their own terms. Artistic independence takes various forms in this third edition, from performance art to photography, book design to sculpture. The artists speak in their own words on the creative process, inspiration and methodology.

    Founded in 2007, WE Design Atlanta has grown in tandem with Atlanta's grassroots arts community. The organization's mission is to design a more vivid world by coming together, sharing together, creating together and ultimately succeeding together.
