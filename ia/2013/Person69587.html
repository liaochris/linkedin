Joe Pantuso's Summary

For the past 20 years I have been inventing, originating and otherwise shepherding software products and services into existence. I have moved back and forth between the realms of compression, network security and games.

This cross-pollinisation has been very useful in bringing outside strengths to each area. Bringing "game sensibility" to otherwise mundane activity has been a cornerstone of how I approach things since long before "gamification" became a buzzword.

I am a cheerleader for other startups and do advising, angel investing and other startup related 'hobby' activity to help support and encourage entrepreneurship.

Specialties

Building top-tier development teams and culture. Mass-market/'shrink-wrap' quality software. User experience (UX) design, social dynamics, simplification of complex systems, security, compression, performance, games & interactive UI. Agile and behavior driven methodologies.

Fortezza Technology

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Computer Software industry

May 2009 – April 2011 (2 years)

Fortezza was designed as a new product play in consumer security. It focused on novel ways of controlling and filtering content between the Internet and a local device, and monitoring and controlling access to applications. It was abandoned due to market and technology realities caused by the ascendance of iOS devices.

VP, Engineering

CH Mack

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Information Technology and Services industry

August 2007 – May 2009 (1 year 10 months)

At CH Mack I came in as part of a 'reboot' by new investors and built a new engineering department and culture from the ground up, introducing a new technology platform and architecture to deliver core services.

Founder

TrayGames Corporation

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Computer Games industry

February 2003 – April 2008 (5 years 3 months)

I founded TrayGames to build a technology platform to deliver on my long-held belief (since demonstrated spectacularly by Zynga) that online games should be social experiences first and would also move to mobile. The team succeeded in building a powerful system for delivering multi-user applications and chat. Nearly a dozen games were published and the client was pre-installed on computers sold by a number of vendors including HSN.

Director of Engineering

McAfee/Network Associates

Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; MFE; Computer & Network Security industry

October 2001 – July 2004 (2 years 10 months)

I joined McAfee through a merger; my company NeoWorx was acquired in 2001. What was NeoWorx became a regional office in which I continued to run and expand. Products I owned development of included the McAfee Personal Firewall and Privacy products and the HackerWatch system, which are all directly descended from technology originated by me and my team at NeoWorx. Multiple patents.

Co-Founder, VP & CTO

NeoWorx

August 1997 – October 2001 (4 years 3 months)

NeoWorx created a series of popular Internet and security related programs including NeoTrace and NeoWatch (which later became McAfee Personal Firewall).

Acquired by McAfee in October of 2001

Lecturer

Ngee Ann Polytechnic, Singapore

Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry

January 1996 – February 1997 (1 year 2 months)

Gave weekly lectures on the ins and outs of game design and development, the games industry and market. Providing these lectures was part of the deal we arranged for housing the Games Online in a convenient space on the campus.

Founder & Studio Director

Games Online Pte Ltd, Singapore

November 1995 – February 1997 (1 year 4 months) Singapore

GOL was a joint-venture company located in Singapore, created by Renegade Graphics (my wholly owned company) and Sembawang Corp (Singapore). Renegade Graphics contributed 6 American team members including myself, a massively-multiplayer role playing game engine, and three original game concepts.

This studio was the first pure-play games company located in Singpore. The concepts worked on here were way before their time, we were building MMORPGs and MMO action games 6 to 8 years before the terms were coined. Being rather far ahead of the curve and in uncharted territory made it difficult to achieve investor buy-in in SE Asia. We were nearly rescued by Bandai, but failed due to this investment being blocked by their initiating merger activity with Sega. We ran out of funding before that situation was resolved, and so experienced the .COM boom/bust cycle 4 years ahead of the curve...

Founder

Renegade Graphics, inc.

October 1992 – February 1997 (4 years 5 months)

As Renegade I created the most successful Windows-based editor for the original Doom (and its derivatives). This led to the publishing of several books, the first of which was specificially about creating add-ons for Doom itself. Renegade went on to create several multiplayer Internet games and form a Joint Venture company in Singapore (Games Online Pte Ltd).

Developer

PK Ware Inc.

February 1991 – October 1992 (1 year 9 months)

This is where the .zip file was invented. I was an early member of the PKWare team and as is typical at small start-ups I wore many hats, including designing new features, writing and formatting documentation, creating and managing the publishing of magazine advertising, supporting customers, maintaining the BBS systems (pre-Internet ubiquity you know...) etc.
