Garianno Lorenzo's Honors and Awards

  • HARD DISK RECORDING

    GRAMMY
    • 1991

    Garianno helped developed the first desktop stand-alone hard disk audio recorder: the Roland DM-80.

    Garianno Lorenzo used this technology to produce 10 time grammy winner George Benson's new album, the result was honored by Naras. The composition was nominated for a Grammy Award. Garianno's recording studio won awards for innovative technology, and received a 4 page review in Musicians Magazine and in ProSound News. His new proven technology ended Studer Analog Tape Machine production, and gave way to software used today, i.e. Protools and Logic Audio.

  • SONG OF THE YEAR

    SESAC MUSIC AWARDS
    • 2001

    Garianno Lorenzo wrote and produced the theme song for the movie "Living For Love", The Natalie Cole Story. Natalie Cole is the daughter of legendary singer Nat "King" Cole. The composition was co-written by songwriter, socialite and philanthropist Denise Rich. The song was performed by Natalie Cole and release as a single. It reach #1 on the pop charts. It sold more than a million copies, and won SESAC song of the year.

  • MTV VIDEO AWARD (SALES - PLATINUM)

    MTV
    • 1995

    Garianno Lorenzo received MTV Award for Video Sales of The Grind TV Show - RIAA Certified Platinum Sales. The Grind was the longest running, most successful TV show created by MTV. It spun-off into an international tour, clothing line, record company, fitness videos and more. All of which reach #1. Garianno Lorenzo was a producer.
