Dan DeMaioNewton's Summary

As a recognized education and workforce revolutionary, Dan is helping reshape education to employment in the 21st century. Leading some of the nation’s largest and most critical educational and workforce transformations, Dan’s work has helped millions of students get on track, job seekers getting better jobs and thousands of employers hiring the best talent faster. At Houghton Mifflin Harcourt, Dan leads strategy and business development for their largest and highest impact education and workforce workforce engagements.

Among his many accomplishments, Dan was the chief architect and director who led the re-engineering of USAJOBS - the award winning career site for the Federal government. Other major engagements include OhioMeansJobs, Helmets to Hardhats, Military Spouse Career Network, Extreme Hiring Makeover, Green Jobs, DNC and RNC National Convention Volunteer Management System, Disaster Relief, Pathways Out of Poverty, BetterJobsFaster.org, Job Corps, USDA Academy, and many more.

His work generated over $180M in original revenue to Monster. His leadership helped transform Monster from an online job board to a solution-centric career company.

He was awarded the prestigious Monster MVP award in 2012 for his numerous contributions to the success of the organization, his vision and leadership, and ability to create new lines of business on a global basis. A pragmatic visionary with a razor focus on metric-driven results, Dan's spirit and passion are helping businesses, government, and workers to achieve their career potential.

Dan DeMaioNewton's Patents

  • Method for interactive employment searching and skills specification

    • United States Patent Application 20080086366
    Inventors: Dan DeMaioNewton

    The present disclosure details apparatuses, systems and methods for an Interactive Employment Search Platform enabling interactive employment searching and skills specification. The Platform enables employers and job seekers to easily identify, connect, and coordinate with one another. The disclosed systems and methods collect profile data for jobs, employers, and job seekers. This profile data includes a specification of skill levels either possessed or desired, in the case of job seekers, or required/recommended, in the case of job listings. Skill sets may then be compared to determine the congruency between job listings and job seekers, as well as to supply information about skill deficiencies. Additionally, the Platform generates extended job seeker profiles containing job listing ratings that enable future job listing searches with highly tailored specificity. By connecting job seekers with the employment opportunities most closely associated with their skills and interests, the Platform effectively increases the efficiency and success potential of hiring efforts.
