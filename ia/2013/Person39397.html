EXECUTIVE PROFILE

Strategic Planning ~ Executive Leadership ~ Systems Implementation ~ Technology Management

Dedicated leader who knows how to roll up the sleeves and work along side technology professionals to meet organizational objectives on time and within budget. Consensus builder across multiple disciplines assuring the right technology is the right fit for complex computing and care environments. Ed Collins' Experience

Vice President and Chief Information Officer

Martin Memorial Health Systems

Privately Held; 1001-5000 employees; Hospital & Health Care industry

August 2008 – Present (4 years 7 months) Stuart, FL

Led the development of a 5 year information technology strategic plan that positioned the organization to receive $13 million in additional Medicare reimbursement and to avoid $16M in Medicare penalties from the Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health Act (HITECH).

Developed and led an accelerated clinical information system selection process that included input from over 375 cross disciplinary constituents including nurses, doctors, ancillary personnel, hospital administrators, and board members. The selection was completed in 7 months and included an approved expenditure of $41M in capital funds.

Partnered with organizational Lean process leaders to develop a 10 year return on investment analysis that included $90 million in new technology spending that was offset by $87 million in savings from operational efficiencies, reductions in legacy systems, receipt of HITECH funds, and Medicare penalty avoidance. Tied Lean performance with the acquisition of technology to assure that the right process was matched with the right technology.

Dramatically improved clinical order management by achieving 80% Computerized Provider Order Entry (CPOE) at electronic medical record go-live by recommending, promoting, and implementing a sophisticated physician training program that included a minimum of 9 hours of classroom training.

Improved nursing medication administration workflow by reducing the number of missing medications in cart fills by 85% through the recommended adoption of an integrated electronic medical record that included CPOE, pharmacy robotics, and pharmacy Radio Frequency Identification (RFID) technologies.

Reduced medication errors by increasing the percentage of scanned medications from 92% to 98% through the recommended implementation of Aztec bar code technology combined with bulls-eye bar code scanner technology.

Vice President and Chief Information Officer

United Hospital Center

Nonprofit; 1001-5000 employees; Hospital & Health Care industry

February 2000 – July 2008 (8 years 6 months) Clarksburg, WV

Created accountability for technology spending through the development of a three-year Information Technology planning process that was directly tied to the capital budgeting process. Implemented a value proposition approach to technology investment that calculated true return on investment combined with non-financial benefits that were scored and weighted to arrive at a go/no go recommendation. Refreshed the three-year technology plan annually as a precursor to the capital budgeting process.

Dramatically improved health information management through the recommended acquisition and implementation of a medical record imaging system. Produced a savings of $600,000 per year in micro-film savings, maintenance fees, storage fees, and employee hours.

Saved $450,000 a year in diagnostic imaging film costs through the recommended acquisition and implementation of a Picture Archiving and Communications System - PACS. Improved patient care and physician satisfaction by reducing report turn around time from hours to minutes.

Improved patient care by recommending and implementing a clinical web portal for physicians. Provided real time access via any internet connected computer in the world to lab results, pathology results, diagnostic imaging results, EKG results, the electronic medication administration record, nursing flow sheets, and a computerized image of the historical medical record.

Reduced medication errors by 75% through the recommendation and implementation of a bar code enabled point of care system (BPOC) with electronic Medication Administration Record (e-MAR).

Saved $60,000 a year in hardware and software support costs through the information system consolidation of a twenty-five provider physician network.

Involved Physicians in technology decisions through the formation of a Portal Users Group (PUG). Formed a cross-disciplinary team of physicians who provided feedback to the McKesson WP portal technical team.
