Melissa Richardson Banks' Summary

As the founder of the consulting firm CauseConnect, Melissa Richardson Banks specializes in helping companies reach their target markets and fulfill CSR goals through signature community programs designed to engage employees, meaningfully connect with charities and causes, and uniquely incorporate the arts and the environment.

Since 1999, Melissa has taught nonprofit training workshops independently or for clients such as the National Arts Project, Center for Cultural Innovation, and InfoPeople. She often presents at national conferences such as the American Association of Museums, American Libraries Association, and Hispanic PR & Social Marketing Association. She also serves as a grant reviewer for many companies and government agencies.

As an independent cultural producer, Melissa plans, creates, funds and executes a variety of events, programs and projects such as museum exhibitions, community festivals, speaker series, classical music concerts, influencer dinners and salons. Over the course of her professional career, she has produced, marketed, managed, and/or funded nearly 100 museum exhibitions, and managed several national tours of traveling exhibitions. Notable examples include "The Chicano Collection / La Colección Chicano," "Siqueiros in L.A.: Censorship Defied," "Puro Muerto: Contemporary Imagery of Day of the Dead," and "Bravo: The Tortilla Paintings of Joe Bravo."

In addition, Melissa is an author, book editor, and art publisher. She independently funded, edited and published "Papel Chicano: Works on Paper from the Cheech Marin Collection" and "Chicanitas: Small Paintings from the Cheech Marin Collection."

Melissa is an avid urban cyclist who serves on the official rider teams for Tern Bicycles and Nutcase Helmets. She is also a photographer who documents her changing Arts District neighborhood through her Downtown Muse blog and extended social media presence.

Specialties

cause marketing; corporate social responsibility (CSR) initiatives; nonprofit-corporate partnerships; cultural productions such as museum exhibitions, speaker programs, and community festivals; special events; volunteer programs; nonprofit training

Melissa Richardson Banks' Projects

  • BloomfestLA

    • April 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Melissa Richardson Banks, Edgar Varela

    Presented at 3rd St. and Traction Ave. near Wurstküche and The Pie Hole in the Arts District of Downtown Los Angeles, this FREE community festival attracts over 25,000 attendees described as urban singles, couples, and parents who are arts-minded, creative, unpretentiously hip, and environmentally aware professionals. BloomfestLA offers live music, artisan goods, street art, and great food.

  • Downtown Muse

    • December 2005 to Present
    Team Members: Melissa Richardson Banks

    Downtown Muse is a lifestyle blog that, combined with associated social networks (Twitter, Facebook, Instagram and Tumblr), promotes people, places, and events in urban areas, particularly in the Arts District of Downtown Los Angeles (but also other downtown areas when traveling).

  • The Chicano Collection with Cheech Marin

    • August 2003 to Present
    Team Members: Melissa Richardson Banks

    This traveling exhibition originated with a project designed to advance and increase public visibility of Chicano art. Produced with Cheech Marin, it features archival-quality digital prints (gicleés) of paintings by 26 prominent Chicano artists. Depicting the Chicano experience between 1969 and 2001, the exhibition also includes linocut artist portraits and a documentary film produced by CauseConnect.
