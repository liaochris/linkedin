Gordon McLean's Summary

An integrated, ideas-driven and digitally switched-on creative planner, I've developed local, regional and global creative strategies at a number of leading agencies, including DLKW, JWT and BBDO. My work is guided by three fundamental beliefs: creativity is the most powerful force in business, ideas are nothing without effect and every point of interaction with a brand is both valuable and vulnerable, an opportunity to either build or diminish its equity. My planning style is characterized by strategic rigor and creative vision. And my planning method centers on the intersection of killer company, consumer, category and cultural truths, in the service of work that is authentic, relevant, original and timely.

I’ve worked on big and small brands, including Bing, GE, Gillette, Guinness, Holiday Inn, Halifax Bank of Scotland, HSBC, Radisson, Red Stripe, the US Marine Corps, Viagra and Vodafone amongst others. I've also helped create work that’s been recognized with two gold Effies, a gold Marketing Week Effectiveness Award, a gold David Ogilvy ARF Award and a clutch of creative awards from Lions to Circles to Pencils. One piece I’m particularly proud of even made its way into the MoMA archives via the 2009 AICP Show.

A native of Dublin, I've also lived and worked in Sheffield, London, Istanbul and New York. Beyond strategy, I'm partial to an Inci Pastanesi profiterole, the idea of smoking // the reality of not, moody images, the 22 down Piccadilly, serendipity, the memory of Guinness, Johnny Marr's guitar, early Woody Allen, the sound of rain at night, a Beşiktaş vapur @ dusk, a Sunday evening Bombay Bicycle, riding the FDR at speed, Türk kahvesi fortune finding, drifting out while driving down 5th Avenue at night, and an evening summer stroll from Seapoint to Dún Laoghaire.

Planning Partner

Ideas at the Intersection

Partnership; 1-10 employees; Design industry

2009 – 2011 (2 years) Istanbul

After 10 years on the road, my designer wife and I left New York for Istanbul, with a plan to bounce on to Europe. But shortly after we arrived we set up Ideas at the Intersection and together we helped finance Amir Naderi’s first Japanese film, rebranded the largest aluminum manufacturer in Turkey, launched a maritime security company out of the Gulf of Aden and repositioned a super premium watch retailer in Istanbul. I also continued my role as Planning Director for HSBC in North America for a further 6 months and helped CreativeFeed’s San Francisco office develop their strategic thinking for an LVMH pitch.

Planning Director

JWT

Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; WPP; Marketing and Advertising industry

2006 – 2009 (3 years) New York

In 2006 I joined JWT New York as Planning Director on the HSBC business. There I helped develop award-winning global brand work that won a Gold Epica, a Gold Circle, a Silver D&AD and a permanent place in the MoMA’s archives via the 2009 AICP show in New York City. I also strategically authored HSBC’s 2008 Math of Life campaign. This campaign played an essential role in building HSBC’s business in the midst of the global financial meltdown and was honored with a Gold Effie. Alongside my HSBC responsibilities I co-authored the pitch winning $100m US launch strategy for Microsoft Bing. The resulting Search Overload Syndrome campaign was honored with a Gold Effie, a Gold ARF David Ogilvy Award, a Bronze Jay Chiat and a Cannes Lions Shortlisting. I also managed two planners, co-authored the winning 7 Minutes To Reinvent The Internet (For Advertising) IAB pitch with Ty Montague, developed the experience brief for the redesign of the US Marine Corp’s Marines.com site, helped win a brand strategy assignment for Radisson hotels, guest lectured in strategic design at The Art Institute of New York City and qualitative research methods at Columbia University.

Based on my teamHSBC work at JWT, I was invited by Charlie Snow & Richard Warren – two of London’s most respected planners – to DLKW, one of London’s most successful agencies. In October 2005 I joined as a planner to develop brand, advertising, direct and digital strategy on the agency’s flagship Halifax Bank of Scotland (HBOS) account.  I developed a clutch of integrated HBOS campaigns, developed the digital strategy to relaunch Halifax Bank of Scotland’s first time buyer proposition and helped win the Blockbuster Entertainment retail business in partnership with Dialogue DLKW.

Planner

JWT

2003 – 2005 (2 years) London

I joined JWT’s global digital marketing network RMG Connect in March 2003. Shortly after I arrived we won the global HSBC business and I became the lead integrated planner working with JWT, RMG Connect, XM London, 141 Worldwide and Mindshare teams. I authored the creative strategies for a number of high profile HSBC campaigns, including The Power of 8, Blend In Like A Local and The January Sale, a campaign that won gold at the 2004 Marketing Week Engage Awards. I also worked on Vodafone’s first global CRM toolkit and developed the agency’s first strategic planning training program, a program that was also shared with clients at ITV and HSBC and eventually exported to JWT Milan.
