Simon Aloyts' Summary

Senior Information Technology professional with over 16 years of experience building IT-focused businesses, managing software development life cycles, teaching classes, presenting to boards and to developers, managing multi-national teams, bridging 4 time zones on 3 continents to maintain a 24-hour workflow, and deploying complex applications in the time it takes big consulting to write a use-case. And helping staff with code puzzles, Excel functions and even misbehaving printers.

Simon Aloyts' Experience

CTO

Brand Blaze Studios

October 2008 – February 2013 (4 years 5 months) Greater Los Angeles Area

Brand Blaze specialized in general software development, casual games, second-screen apps and social media solutions for the entertainment industry. In late 2012, it was acquired by a developer of second-screen apps and integrated into operations.

* Built and managed the consulting operation.

* Architect for 100+ projects using every modern stack and handling television-level traffic bursts. Customers included: Unilever, Sony, Microsoft, other Fortune clients, every major movie studio and The US Army.

* Implemented Agile workflows to integrate offshore deliverables with animation and design teams in Los Angeles. 24-hour schedules enabled shipment of full-featured solutions with extremely tight deadlines. We called it Sprinting Marathons.

* Early adopter of the cloud. Virtualization is a natural fit for project-work eliminating time for spinning up or down and making the ownership transfer as easy as an email.

CTO

Liquid Generation

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Entertainment industry

February 2002 – February 2013 (11 years 1 month) Chicago, IL and Los Angeles, CA

LG was a leading entertainment destination for 18-24 year-olds serving over 10 million unique visitors per month. LG was acquired by a developer of second screen apps in 2012.

* Managed all technology through every funding benchmark, implemented user experience standards, stickiness widgets and search engine-optimizations long before any had well-known acronyms. During tenure, time-on-site went from 40 seconds to 3+ minutes, Page Rank from 0 to 6, and overall traffic increased 20x.

* Early adopter of Omniture, Web Trends, DoubleClick, Right Media, Rubicon, AdWords and many others. Wrote proprietary analytics and ad-serving engines for special cases and campaigns.

* Developed a flash-player compatible with SWF files (interactive media) to serve ads into embedded content and re-capture millions of lost impressions every month.

* Created send-to-a-friend viral feature in 2001 with one-click forward. Average recipient sent to ~3 friends driving explosions of traffic and ad-revenues. Implemented text-mining from SPSS to analyze user comments. Except for teaching novel, new obscenities, analysis was very cool but not too useful.

* Launched LGIX, LG Information Exchange with a registered panel of 800k Gen-Y users to get early peeks at new content in exchange for demographic info and survey cooperation. Their responses influenced several major CPG and entertainment product launches aimed at Gen-Y including: AXE Deodorant Body Spray and Freddie vs. Jason.

* Pioneered the “sabotage” concept of online pranks. Many who have not otherwise heard of LG admit to having been startled by a sudden outburst while browsing a photo-comparison, a cute card, or a fake news story where a friend filled-in some embarrassing blanks.

Partner

Intraweaver Corporation

November 1997 – February 2002 (4 years 4 months)

Intraweaver was a custom software development firm, staff augmentation specialist and e-commerce consultancy specializing in workflow automation. IW ceased operations after I joined Liquid Generation.

* Ran day-to-day business operations of a 20-person development and design team. Average project earned 60 cents on every dollar of revenue.

* Goldman Sachs – lead programmer: custom 360 degree Managing Director Review application using SPSS to crunch data for tens of thousands of employee reviews, and create presentation-quality reports using the MS Office family of products. VB, VBA, SPSS, Sybase.

* Tellabs Inc. – lead programmer, architect and project manager: custom HRMS app. Processed 1,200 new hires in 4 months (tripling the organization), and made sure that no one was without a phone or email on starting day. Classic ASP, VB, C++, SQL Server.

* Colgate-Palmolive – lead programmer, architect: custom app including portable registration program to process 250K dentists and hygienists in 3 days for ADA convention and custom cavity shoot-em-up game promoting Colgate products. VB, C++, MS Access.

* Confidential client – lead (non-Flash) programmer, architect: custom shopping cart for e-commerce site built in all Flash exploiting remote scripting capabilities to make page-reloads invisible. Shopping was drag-and-drop in a VR store. Then Flash de-compiler was discovered and rendered the project somewhat insecure.
