James Foster, MA's Summary

James is a doctoral candidate in Clinical Psychology (Psy.D.) at the Union Institute & University. He has direct clinical experience with a wide variety of presenting issues and populations, but specializes in adults, young adults, identity issues, anxiety, OCD, and depression.

Interest in mindfulness-based therapies, such as Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT) and Acceptance & Commitment Therapy (ACT); therapeutic flexibility in use of other empirically supported treatments.

Successfully defended my dissertation proposal,"Holding Lightly: An exploration of the effects of Zen Buddhist Koan contemplation on psychologist narrative flexibility."

Clinical and assessment experience with common mental health concerns that emerge with the college student population: adjustment issues, substance abuse, sexual orientation and identity, trauma, stress/anxiety, depression, body image/eating disorders, identity formation/development, and others.

Committed to helping each client reach value-based personal goals and identify the ways in which choices serve to bring one closer to or further away from those goals.
