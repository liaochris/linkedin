The result was time savings for the department's researchers and administrative staff and improved adherence to the school's purchasing contract. It also resulted in a 30% increase in sales for Sigma-Aldrich. I uncovered the opportunity, sold it to the key stakeholders at the University, managed its implementation, and trained individual users to ensure a successful launch. As a result of my success in this role, I was promoted to Senior Account Manager in 1998.

Chemist

Sigma Aldrich

Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; SIAL; Biotechnology industry

December 1997 – January 2000 (2 years 2 months) Greater Milwaukee Area

I began my career as a chemist at Sigma-Aldrich chemical company in downtown Milwaukee. I was the prototypical scientist - goggles, white lab coat, and lots of wild-looking glassware. My job was to prepare small quantities of specialty chemicals that would be packaged for sale to universities, government labs, and research institutes all over the world. Often the result of my work would be a few hundred grams of white powder or clear liquid that would sell for many thousands of dollars. The work itself was like high-tech cooking. My ingredients were reactants, catalysts, and solvents, and my recipe was the "prep" that gave step by step instructions for the reaction and purification of the final product. With multiple reactions running concurrently in a lab I shared with another chemist, project planning and resource management were critical skills. It was a good first job, but not very intellectually stimulating. After a couple years, I decided to back to school for my MBA in order to pave a career path that would lead me out of the lab.
