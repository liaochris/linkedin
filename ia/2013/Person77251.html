Krisztina 'Z' Holly's Summary

***If we don't know each other and you want to reach me, please send me InMail!***

Krisztina 'Z' Holly is an engineer, entrepreneur, and innovator whose passion is identifying and amplifying undiscovered talent and ideas, and creating new organizations and scalable models that turn ideas into impact.

In 2009 she created the first ever TEDx event, TEDxUSC, which so far has inspired over 6,500 similar events globally. Most recently she served as vice provost for innovation at USC and founding executive director of two university centers for innovation - MIT Deshpande Center and USC Stevens Center - which have launched 39 funded startups and helped expand the innovation ecosystems in Boston and LA through engagement of hundreds of faculty, students, VCs, and entrepreneurs.

Early on Z was co-founder of computer telephony pioneer Stylus Innovation (acquired by Artisoft) and subsequently joined other tech and media startups including Direct Hit Technologies (acquired by Ask Jeeves). She worked on various engineering projects including the space shuttle main engine, a head-eye robot for the MIT AI Lab, and the first full-color computer generated reflection hologram at the MIT Media Lab.

Between startups Z spent nearly three years in documentary production and in her role as curator and host for TEDxUSC, she discovered and coached more than 60 presenters whose videos have garnered 4+ million views online.

Named Champion of Free Enterprise by Forbes in 2010, Z has been a contributor to Forbes, BusinessWeek, Huffington Post, CNN.com, Big Think, Strategy+Business, Science Progress, and Mountain Bike Magazine. She currently serves as advisor to the Obama administration and the World Economic Forum in innovation and entrepreneurship.

Z received her B.S. and M.S. in mechanical engineering from MIT. She is an avid backcountry/telemark skier, adventure traveler, skydiver, mountain biker, shark diver, and authentic food aficionado. She is married and resides in Los Angeles.
