David Birnbaum's Summary

I’m a design leader and inventor with background in gesture, music, haptics, and interface design. My recent work is focused on building Immersion’s new UX Design program, where we systematize implicit knowledge and patterns within our organization, build strategic approaches to emerging technologies, and evangelize tactile design practices to the wider design community. Why?

Because digital networks can’t enable authentic experiences and deep connections between people without the critical elements of human touch and physical presence. Constance Classen writes about it better than I could:

"..[W]e live in a society of the image, a markedly visual culture, in which, while there may be many representations of touch, there is often nothing actually there to feel. The attractions of advertising, television, or the Internet, are designed to be consumed by the eyes and the ears. The inability to touch the subject matter of the images that surround us, even though these have a tremendous impact on our lives, produces a sense of alienation, the feeling of being out of touch with one’s society, one’s environment and one’s cosmos — an isolated fragment in an indifferent universe."

Let’s do something about it.

Specialties: user experience architecture & design . research strategy . intellectual property development & analysis . usability analysis & validation . user interface design . rapid prototying . innovation . visionary leadership
