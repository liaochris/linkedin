Brian C. Janes' Summary

Brian C. Janes (aka BCJ) is a New Media Producer and Published Photographer.

A team leader with a penchant for solving complex problems and building efficiency into systems and processes, BCJ’s background spans a variety of industries. In recent years he has focused on his passion for developing and producing digital entertainment content.

At Buzzine Networks, BCJ led the production and post-production of more than 100 on-camera artist interviews with top music and film talent, including shooting and editing most of the weekly video content. He defined and documented processes & workflows, managed the content archives & associated backups, used his photographic abilities to capture galleries of bands live on stage, and performed all of the photo editing for galleries submitted by contributors.

From 2009-2010 BCJ traveled over 14,000 miles to photograph and interview more than 100 burlesque performers in their homes. This project culminated in a hardcover coffee table book, "It’s All That Glitters: Portraits of Burlesque Performers in Their Homes", published by Schiffer Books in April 2012 and available through major retail outlets. In 2012 he produced a book launch event that featured a photo gallery, burlesque show, and book signing to coincide with the release of the book.

Prior to his adventures in the world of new media, BCJ spent 10 years in product development and project management for software solutions in the online classifieds and travel industries. His expertise centered on electronic advertising and data reporting.

Brian C. Janes' Projects

  • A Minute With My Friends

    • May 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Brian C. Janes

    Usually, a portrait is just a single image, a moment, a mere sliver of a second out of the subject’s life frozen forever in time. But what if the portrait also captured their subtle facial muscle movements? Their smile? Their blink?

    Welcome to A Minute With My Friends, a project in which I capture a sixty-second video portrait of my friends with one simple guideline: No Rules. A new video is published every Monday.

    The videos capture more of an essence of who these people are than is possible with a single photograph. They also give the viewer an excuse to look closely, to scrutinize their face, their dimples, their eyes, all without judgment from the subject.

Brian C. Janes' Publications

  • It's All That Glitters: Portraits of Burlesque Performers in Their Homes

    • Schiffer Publishing
    • April 27, 2012
    Authors: Brian C. Janes

    What is burlesque? Who better to ask than burlesque performers themselves? With this question in mind, the author traveled over 14,000 miles across the United States visiting today s burlesque performers, photographing them in their homes, and asking them: What does burlesque mean to you? Their answers and 104 accompanying portraits may surprise you. For these performers, burlesque is so many things, including fun and empowerment on stage, an escape from life's doldrums, a fantasy, a career. This book also presents a study in contrasts between the public persona of each performer and that performer s private life.
