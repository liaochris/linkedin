Tommy Boy Entertainment (formally known as Tommy Boy Records) is a record label started in 1981 by Tom Silverman. The label is widely recognized for its significant contributions to the creation of hip hop music, the development of electronic dance music & for giving birth to numerous pop culture icons.

In 1999, Tommy Boy Records founder, Tom Silverman, in collaboration with producer-director Mic Neumann, and his company Dubtitled Entertainment, began the process of launching an independent television and film company to be branded as Tommy Boy Films that was intent on creating a developmental platform for artistic audio visual content & supporting unique causes.

Tommy Boy Film's first cause to be supported was for the "We Are Family" Foundation (WAFF) founded by legendary music producer, artist & composer Nile Rodgers, whose mission is "dedicated to the vision of a global family by creating and supporting programs that inspire and educate the next generation about respect, understanding & cultural diversity, while striving to solve global problems."

(http://www.WeAreFamilyFoundation.org)
(http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/We_Are_Family_Foundation)

Tommy Boy Films’ first audio visual art project, was a half-hour television series titled Kung Faux, that was released on TV, DVD & VOD after first being licensed to numerous countries around the world, including such television networks as FUSE & STARZ in the United States, IFC & SHOWTIME in Canada, STAR Asia, FOX Latin America, ABC & CHANNEL [V] in Australia, CANAL+ Scandinavia and SONY Entertainment Television in the Middle East, Europe & Africa just to name a few. Kung Faux has now been seen in over 150 countries worldwide.
