Matt Sheridan's Summary

Matt Sheridan's work invites participants into spaces of abstract action painting. His video installations -- paintings-in-motion -- address contemporary power relations between physical reality and the click + drag mentality by re-imagining, then re-materializing, combinations of the mediums of painting and video. Matt has been awarded a number of grants (CCI/ARC, LADCA/CEI, SECULT) and fellowships (Instituto Sacatar-Brazil, La Napoule Art Foundation-France, Santa Fe Art Institute) for his innovative work over the past three years.

Matt received his BFA in Film and Television from NYU/Tisch School of the Arts and his MFA from Art Center College of Design in Pasadena CA. Before attending Art Center Matt worked in NYC as a storyboard artist for various commercials --most notably for the NBA-- and the MTV series Beavis+Butt-Head, Daria, The Head and Celebrity Deathmatch. He also directed ten episodes of Blue's Clues and internet animation production at Heavy.com as he made animations in his off-time which received extensive global festival play.

Based in Los Angeles, Matt exhibits and teaches (NYU/TSOA/UGFTV, NYU/TischAsia/ANDA-MFA, School of Visual Arts, Pratt Institute) internationally. In 2005 one of his SVA undergraduate thesis students won a Silver Medal Student Academy Award; many of his other students have gone on to work for Pixar, Nickelodeon, Scholastic, Sundance Channel, Dreamworks Animation and Blue Sky, among many others.

In 2013 Matt exhibited in Brooklyn, Miami, and Los Angeles. Upcoming projects include: an animated interstitial about "The Future" for Miami-based Fusion network (October 2013), a commission for the Chateau de la Napoule on the Cote d'Azur in France and a major urban painting-in-motion on the streets of Salvador da Bahia Brazil courtesy Bahia's Secretary of Culture; both in early 2014.

To see Matt's exhibition history, please visit the following link for resumé and full CV: www.msheridanstudio.com/biores.html
