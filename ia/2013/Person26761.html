Conor Keegan's Summary

Software engineer and team lead. Over thirteen years of professional software development, mainly in Java, but also Perl experience and exposure to PHP and Python.

Strong back-end skills in high availability, high transactional, environments using distributed services and frameworks such as Spring, Camel/JMS and Hibernate/JPA. A broad knowledge of databases, most recently MySQL but also Progress, Informix, SQL Server and Oracle.

Strong team leading skills. Currently team lead on an agile, scrum-based team.

July 2011 – Present (2 years 3 months) London, United Kingdom

The BBC is the world's largest public service broadcaster.

I lead the architectural design, development and maintenance of a suite of applications responsible for the processing and/or creation of programme metadata. These applications feed into the BBC's central programme metadata repository, which in turn drives the backend workflow and metadata feeds behind BBC iPlayer and other parts of the our online presence.

I am also line manager of a number of engineers and am responsible for encouraging the development of their skills and supporting them in their careers.

Achievements thus far:

* Leading development of our "interlinking" application, which pulls partner feeds from other broadcasters into the BBC metadata service and ultimately provides links to other broadcasters' programming inside BBC iPlayer. Using Java, JMS, Spring, ActiveMQ, Hibernate, MySQL, SimpleXML, Quartz.

* Leading development of our internal authorisation service currently used across BBC Future Media to manage role and group permissions for user access to a swath of internal editorial applications. Using Java, Spring, Hibernate, MySQL.
