Kory G. Wells' Summary

I'm a technology and math enthusiast who believes in the power of language to improve organizations and people.

I became involved with workers' compensation almost 20 years ago as one of the first programmers of ModMaster, a market-leading software product for experience rating analysis. Now at Zywave, I'm still involved with ModMaster and have become known for my writing on the WorkCompEdge blog, my social media activity in the work comp and financial spaces, and my presentations to groups such as the National Workers’ Compensation and Disability Conference, the American Association of Safety Engineers, state and national programming of the Independent Insurance Agents and Brokers of America, and others.

I help agents, brokers, and other risk management professionals understand and communicate the story that an employer's mod reveals. I also engage with this audience on other topics of interest in the property and casualty market. My experience in a diverse range of organizations, including small business, manufacturing, government contracting, and global financial services, helps me relate to a wide variety of scenarios that these professionals encounter. With more general audiences spanning business, non-profits and creatives, I share my experience with social media and blogging.

I am a published poet and creative writer who's passionate about both literacy and literature. Nothing delights me so much as to help a reader experience art and humanity in the form of a good book - or especially a poem.

Specialties: workers compensation experience rating, creative writing, poetry (not usually all at the same time), social media, blogging, presentations

Kory G. Wells' Experience

Product Director, Data Analytics

Zywave

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Computer Software industry

October 2010 – Present (3 years 1 month)

Now with Zywave after its acquisition of Specific Software Solutions, Kory is focused on product development related to ModMaster, the ModMaster Suite of products, and their integration with Zywave's offerings in its Data Analytics family.

She is also editor of the WorkCompEdge blog, where she attempts riveting explanations of the intricacies and advantages of workers compensation mod analysis.

Creative Writing Mentor

The Writer's Loft at Middle Tennessee State University

Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry

May 2010 – Present (3 years 6 months)

The Writer's Loft is a low residency, 3 semester certificate program in creative writing. Kory and other mentors work with students on a one-on-one basis and deliver lectures and readings at the orientation/workshop weekends which are held in the spring and fall semester. The Writer's Loft is designed to help participants hone their creative writing skills, develop publishable work, and bring more structure to their writing lives and goals. Great for adults who are serious about their writing but perhaps not ready for the time or financial commitment of an MFA.

Poet and Creative Writer

korywells.com

December 1995 – Present (17 years 11 months)

Kory is author of the poetry collection Heaven Was the Moon (March Street Press) and often performs her poetry with her daughter Kelsey, a roots musician, in an act that’s been called “bluegrass rap” and “hillbilly cool.” The Tennessee duo’s first album, Decent Pan of Cornbread, was released in the fall of 2012.

The intersection of Kory’s dual careers occurs in her essay “Really Good for a Girl” which leads the anthology She’s Such a Geek: Women Write About Science, Technology and Other Nerdy Stuff. Ladies Home Journal picked Geek as one of its December 2006 “Books We Love” and singled out Kory’s writing that will “resonate with any woman, geek or not.”

Kory’s short stories, essays, and poetry have appeared or are forthcoming in Christian Science Monitor, Literary Mama, Now & Then, Ruminate, Rock & Sling, Pindeldyboz, New Southerner, Birmingham Arts Journal, Southern Women’s Review, Deep South Magazine, and numerous other publications, including obscure literary journals and a cookbook (poetry in a cookbook – yes!)

She is currently seeking a publisher for her first novel, which was a finalist in the William Faulkner competition.

Chief Communications Officer

Specific Software Solutions

January 1999 – September 2010 (11 years 9 months)

Kory was CCO for Specific Software Solutions and its affiliate SIGMA Actuarial Consulting Group, affiliates serving the insurance and risk management industry with software and actuarial services, prior to the acquisition of SSS by Zywave. Kory joined the companies as the principal software developer of the Windows versions of ModMaster software, the company's flagship product which calculates and analyzes the workers compensation experience rating modification factor.

Kory served as blog editor for the WorkCompEdge.com blog, which was recognized as a top workers comp blog in 2008 by LexisNexis.
