Jason Pijut's Summary

When asked what I do, I describe myself alternately as a Digital Mercenary or as the guy who knows how to make your website the right way. Digital Mercenary sounds much cooler, but they both mean about the same thing. If it exists on the internet, I can help you make it happen. I’m the hired gun business owners and artists bring in when they’re tired of dealing with the faceless, anonymous “web design firms” that litter the market. I try to run my business by a few basic rules.

• Be a gun for hire – I’m a consultant. That means that whatever content I generate for you, you own. I’m not going to string you along endlessly for updates. I do everything in my power to give you the power and control over your digital media.

• Be available – I limit my company's load so that when you need us, you can get in touch with us. When you need work done, it gets done.

• Be flexible – Your time is valuable. Your projects are valuable. I do everything I can to work with my clients and bring their interactive vision to life on their timetable, their budget, and with their best interests in mind. A side note to being flexible – If we don’t know how to do something, we’ll learn it, or subcontract someone that does.

I bring with me a broad array of skill sets as well as artistic and technical abilities.

I have been a website designer and consultant since 2000 and have created more than 500+ websites over those years.

I am available for speaking and teaching as well as design work and consulting.
