Joshua Heslinga's Projects

  • Episcopal property litigation

    • September 2006 to August 2011
    Team Members: Joshua Heslinga, George Somerville, Brad Davenport, Mary C. Zinsner

    I spent five years working on this complex litigation, from before its inception to just before the second appeal, representing the Episcopal Diocese of Virginia. The litigation involved multiple trials, thousands of exhibits, hundreds of pages of briefs (made into a special volume of the Va. Cir. reporter), dozens of witnesses, multiple written letter opinions, and two appeals. The litigation ended (barring unlikely & unexpected SCotUS intervention) in a favorable result for our client. See Falls Church v. Protestant Episcopal Church in the United States, 285 Va. 651, 740 S.E.2d 530 (2013); In re Multi-Circuit Church Prop. Litig., 84 Va. Cir. 105 (Fairfax 2012); Protestant Episcopal Church v. Truro Church, 280 Va. 6, 694 S.E.2d 555 (2010).

  • Riverside tax litigation

    • 2008 to 2011
    Team Members: Joshua Heslinga, Brad Davenport, Russ Palmore

    In this litigation, a property owner and anchor tenant successfully challenged the City of Richmond's policy and method of assessing the building and rehabilitation tax credit values. I was involved at all stages of the litigation and presented the oral argument before the Supreme Court of Virginia on appeal. See Riverside Owner, L.L.C. v. City of Richmond, 282 Va. 62, 711 S.E.2d 533 (2011).

Joshua Heslinga's Publications

  • Bye, Bye, Blackwelder? Recent Cases and the Standards for Preliminary Injunctions in Virginia’s Federal and State Courts

    • VSB Litigation News
    • April 2009
    Authors: Joshua Heslinga, Brad Davenport

    Discussing current case law and the standard for preliminary/temporary injunctions, including the uncertain standard that applies in Virginia state courts, and predicting that the venerable Blackwelder test was no longer good law (which the 4th Circuit confirmed later that year).

  • Update: the Real Truth is that Blackwelder's preliminary injunction test may no longer be applied

    • VSB Litigation News
    • October 2009
    Authors: Joshua Heslinga, Brad Davenport

    A brief update noting that the 4th Circuit's decision in The Real Truth about Obama confirmed that the Blackwelder test for preliminary injunctions was dead.
