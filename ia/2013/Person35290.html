Diane Marquette's Summary

I'm the author of five published novels: "Good Fridays," "Almost Mine," and the first three titles from my Chesapeake Conference Center mystery series, "In Over My Head," "Too Close For Words," and "Suitable For Framing."

Read an excerpt, a synopsis and reviews of each of my books and find out much more about my involvement in the writing community on Maryland's Eastern Shore by visiting my website www.dianemarquette.com.

More than 150 of my articles have been published in regional and national newspapers and magazines.

My work has been read on Public Radio Delmarva and I've been interviewed on television, radio, and the Internet (including the blog talk show "Murder She Writes") about my writing career and my books.

Since 2000, I've coordinated the annual Bay to Ocean Writers Conference on the Eastern Shore of Maryland.

I'm a member and former vice president of the Eastern Shore Writers Association.

I'm also a member of the Maryland Writers Association and the Chesapeake Chapter of Sisters in Crime.

Specialties

I write fiction books. There are currently three published titles ("In Over My Head," "Too Close For Words," and "Suitable For Framing") in my Chesapeake Conference Center mystery series. My two other published novels are "Good Fridays" and "Almost Mine," works of women's fiction.

Aspen Institute Wye River Conference Center

April 1997 – May 1999 (2 years 2 months)

My employment with the exclusive, world-renowned Aspen Institute's Wye River Conference Center on Maryland's Eastern Shore provided the basis for my published Chesapeake Conference Center mystery series. The first three titles are "In Over My Head," "Too Close For Words," and "Suitable For Framing."

One of these books is based on my unique experiences working at the conference center during the 1998 Mideast Peace Talks. During this event, I met President Bill Clinton, Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu, Palestinian Chairman Yasser Arafat, Secretary of State Madelaine Albright, CIA Director George Tenet, Security Advisor Sandy Berger, and many others. I worked closely with the U.S. Secret Service and the State Department during the ten days that the Peace Talks took place.
