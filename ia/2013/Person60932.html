Jeff Grindel's Summary

I am currently in my final year at Illinois Institute of Technology, pursuing a B.S/M.S in Electrical Engineering with a concentration in computers and microelectronics. Through my internships and my schooling I have mainly focused on digital hardware design.

I recently spent the summer at BAE Systems working with the ASIC Design team in charge of designing the next generation radiation hardened microelectronics for space applications. During the summer I was exposed to RTL design, functional verification, and physical layout.

In my schooling I have worked on projects that include:
• 16x16 bit multiplication using split path data driven dynamic logic (SPD3L)
• Carry ripple adder implementation using data driven dynamic logic (D3L/SPD3L)
• Coding of a HDL complier and simulator
• Design and synthesis of multi-operand adders
• Design and implementation of a MIPS CPU with a multi-cycle datapath
• Design and implementation of a CISC monitor program

I have also been able to work on two teams that have worked on real engineering problems with specific companies/organizations. The first one I was a part of was with McGill Arctic Research Station in which we were tasked with determining if a wind generation station would be a viable option to generate power needed for their research station in the arctic circle.

The second team was partnered with OMRON Automotive Electronics to help overcome issues with their manufacturing processes; specifically, the process time reduction of the manufacturing of a fuel pump assembly, and the consolidation/stream-line of a work space of made-to-order parts. It was through this team that we were awarded two awards at our school competition; Dean's Choice Award: Armour College of Engineering, and Best in Track 5: Product & Process Innovation.

Jeff Grindel's Projects

  • Data Driven Dynamic Logic Implementation of Full-Adders

    • May 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Jeff Grindel

    - Schematic design for three full-adder equations implemented in both D3L and SPD3L
    - Analysis and comparison of all different implementations in metrics of delay, power, and leakage using Synopsys HSPICE, and NanoSim

  • Design and Synthesis of Multi-Operand Adders

    • December 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Jeff Grindel, Michael Bodzay

    - VLSI design including datapath circuit design, standard cell based design flow, and design validation and verification through construction of fast multi-operand adder architectures in Verilog.
    - Utilized commercial EDA tools from Synopsys and Cadence Design Systems to perform the design and synthesis.
    - Exploring multi-operand adder architectures with four different implementation methods; 8-operand Carry Ripple Addition, 8-operand Linear Carry Save Addition ([3:2] adder), 8-operand Tree Carry Save Addition ([4:2] adder), and 10-operand Carry Save Addtion ([5:2] adder).

  • Design and Implementation of a MIPS CPU with Multicycle Datapath

    • November 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Jeff Grindel, Thomas Demeter

    - Design and Implementation of a custom RISC processor utilizing VHDL and ModelSim.
    - Processor supports the three instruction formats: R-format, I-format, and J-format and includes the implementation of the following commands; Load Word, Store Word, Add, Logical Or, Set less than, Branch, Shift Right Logical, and Load Upper Immediate.

  • Sous-Vide Arduino Build

    • October 2012 to October 2012
    Team Members: Jeff Grindel

    - Arduino built Sous-Vide (water bath) cooker.
    - Incorporated a temperature sensor, LCD screen, and DC controlled AC power switch to control the temperature inside of crock-pot in order to maintain a perfect cooking temperature for meat and other food items.

  • CISC Monitor Project

    • April 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Jeff Grindel

    - Program for the Motorola 68000 Microprocessor to perform basic debugger functions, such as memory display, memory sort, memory change, block fill, block search, block move and also exception handling.
    - Includes command interpreter to interface with the user and to recognize the commands inputted.
    - Exception handling to handle various exceptions that could be thrown by invalid instructions.
    - Built in instruction manual to help guide the user on the correct syntax of the debugger commands.
