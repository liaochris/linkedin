Jessica Dally's Summary

As someone who is always in motion, I work in a variety of different fields from technology to marketing and most everything in between, using the wide range of experiences to build more robust missions and vision for organizations and businesses. I am passionate about adding value to communities by connecting them to the resources and information necessary to make a better planet.

-I’m an idea person who can not only envision new creative social strategies but implement them while working within limited budgets.
-I believe the world rotates around networks and connection.
-I am able to work in gritty detail without losing sight of the bigger picture.
-I believe that consistency, integrity, authenticity and discipline are the key components of good leadership. I strive to constantly improve these qualities both in the organizations I work with and personally.
-I steal with my eyes, regularly searching for stories and examples of success to synthesize these into my own practices.
-I think successful engagement requires creating value for others.


Feel free to reach out if you’d like to talk about marketing strategy, innovation or adventure motorcycling.

Springwire, formally Community Voice Mail

Nonprofit; 11-50 employees; Nonprofit Organization Management industry

January 2007 – October 2013 (6 years 10 months) Greater Seattle Area

I originally started at Springwire (formally Community Voice Mail) as a help desk technician to migrate 20+ sites from stand alone servers to a enterprise level, heavily customized Cisco Unity centralized system. This included everything from insuring the data integrity of over 40k voicemail boxes and agencies to training site managers on new systems. After migration concluded I continued to work to improve data processes and insure data integrity. At one point I created a process that turned a 2+ hour job into a 1 minute job with almost no chance of errors.
As my knowledge of our system grew I moved into a Network Admin position working to insure that this system of 40k+ homeless and low income was functional with a very high uptime.

Currently I've been promoted to the manager of the very small tech team, making sure our team is working to move our US and Canadian sites to Cisco's Connection product and working with Cisco to make sure the product is customized to meet our needs. We're also moving to a new product for our customer's front end interaction and I'm building reports and working through bugs to insure the smoothest possible transition for people of various degrees of computer experience.

I am also working with the strategic planning committee for Springwire, looking forward to the next few years by increasing our product offerings and increase awareness of our brand. As a side job I'm working with marcomm to create new content program using client voices to educate the public on the realities of homelessness and the importance of the programs offered by Springwire.
