Jesse Kramer's Summary

I like to build businesses, I like cars and I like mountain biking. I have yet to figure out how to make any money mountain biking (primarily because I'm really slow), so I'm focusing on the former two.

In 1996 I co-founded an ad agency, fabGORGON. We won the most innovative business of the year from the Philadelphia Chamber of Commerce and Wharton said we were one of the 100 fastest growing businesses in the region. We had impressive clients considering we started in a boarded up second floor coffin, shared a phone line with a recording studio and my primary source of income was being a bicycle courier. Over seven years, the likes of HBO, Sungard, the City of Philadelphia and more drank our brand, enough so that our boutique was acquired in 2003 by Kanter International, founded by the former president of IKEA.

Shorts turned to suits, and we brought in World Wrestling Entertainment, Everlast Worldwide, Vans, even the Department of Defense (can I say that?). In 2008 we re-named ourselves as Finch Brands. My Kanter/Finch days went on for 6 good years after the acquisition (and still successfully continues today under the leadership of Daniel Erlbaum, Jordan Goldenberg and Bill Gullan).

After a brief and incredibly unsuccessful stint in corporate America, I put my money where my brain was and in 2010 took equity in a 20 year-old brand called AWE Tuning. We make German cars go faster. If you like cars, this is where it's at. Our biggest issue now is keeping up with demand.

In 2012, along side my "day job" at AWE Tuning, I co-founded Venturef0rth, a 10,000 square foot shared-working space, where I, along with Elliot Menschik and Jay Shah, help businesses who are on to something. Our vision is to "enable the next big thing."

In my spare time's spare time I am chair emeritus of the Chamber of Commerce's small business board, and have sat on boards for the Fox School, ESPN X Games, Breast Health Institute and others.

Thanks for reading.
