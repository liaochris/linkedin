Electoral Officer

United Nations Integrated Mission of East Timor

Nonprofit; 10,001+ employees; International Affairs industry

July 2011 – September 2012 (1 year 3 months) East Timor

United Nations mission created to support the East Timorese government gain stability during a post-conflict era. The United Nations Electoral Support Team (UNEST) specifically focused on advising the newly formed Electoral Secretariat and National Commission of Elections. Advised and provided capacity building for the following activities: voter registration, civic education, logistic planning, campaigns, election day activities and tabulation.

Key Achievements

• Successfully advised the National Commission of Elections 2012 presidential and parliamentary elections at district level, in coordination with United Nations and local election officials.
• Collaborated on developing training program for the National Commission of Elections of Aileu, East Timor: project management and requisite computer skills; encouraging participatory planning and democracy among local staff.
• Assisted in the overall institutional strengthening of the National Commission of Elections.
• Created a strategic logistic plan to monitor and evaluate 43 polling centers pre-election and election day: developed transportation plans, ensured security, organized staff deployment and monitoring of equipment distribution.
• Employed cultural sensitivity and knowledge of Tetum, achieving a positive working relationship with local suco chiefs, political parties and local authorities. Resulted in seamless collaboration between national and international stakeholders.
• Administered a UNICEF exploratory survey of over 60 young voters for 2012 Presidential and Parliamentary elections.

Economist/Urban Planner

AKRF, Inc

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Environmental Services industry

July 2007 – March 2009 (1 year 9 months) New York, New York

Planning, environmental and engineering firm evaluating the environmental impact of infrastructure projects throughout New York City, State and additional municipalities. The Economic Division focuses on analyzing each project's effect on local commerce, affordable housing and employment figures.

Key Achievements

• Published eleven socioeconomic research pieces for various Environmental Impact Statements. Presented to municipal governments.
• Managed a team to survey, analyze and compile a database of over 6,000 businesses for two business competition analyses.
• Designed over 30 Geographic Information Systems (GIS) maps to spatially illustrate socioeconomic trends and predicted changes.
• Created a transit-oriented strategic plan in conjunction with local property owners and city officials. Plan was implemented to accommodate a mixture of multi-family, mixed income housing, office space and open green space.
• Developed a focus on industrial to residential shifts in land use and effects on the welfare of local residents and businesses.
• Detected vulnerable populations of residents, businesses and employment sectors. Master plans were altered and/or downscaled based on this research.
• Presented potential impacts to resident Spanish-speaking community at public hearings to maintain up-to-date information for the local community.
• Designed a labor market study for a casino project intended to develop employment on a 60-acre/24 hectare Native American Reserve. Illustrated primary and secondary spending on behalf of these new employees, detailing positive and negative effects.

A nonprofit organization created to revitalize commercial districts; providing technical assistance to communities, businesses and individual entrepreneurs. • The Corona Community Action Network (CAN) was established in 2005.
