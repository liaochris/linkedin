David Mann's Summary

Currently I am a principal of GreenTek Consulting, LLC (a firm co-founded to focus on sustainability for built environments) and teach Alternative & Renewable Energy, Building Technology and Structures in the Architecture and BEST (Building Efficiency and Sustainable Technology) Program(s) at Norwalk Community College. I received my Masters in Architecture from Harvard University and have over 15 years of experience in design, development, construction and financial analysis. Previously, I was a principal of Jupiter Atlantic, a South East Florida real estate development firm; and have worked as a technology and business financial analyst for a New York City private equity & hedge fund. One of the most challenging and exciting times in my career was as a senior associate of Schwartz Architects/THINK - the runner-up in the World Trade Center Re-Design Competition. My other design and construction experience includes projects of virtually all types: from the Southwest Capitol of France and the Whitehall (Staten Island Ferry Terminal), to NYC offices, restaurant renovations and homes in New York’s Hamptons, Arizona and Connecticut. I am currently the Chairman of the Green Task Force and sits on the Architectural Review Board in Westport, CT. Previously I served on the Steering Committee of the Connecticut Alliance for Sustainable Enterprise.

Specialties

Green building methods and technologies, sustainable building design and construction, weatherization and energy retrofits, sustainable health care / dental facilities, green affordable housing, luxury green homes, the economics of sustainability and green building, education – focusing on sustainable built environments, alternative energy and building science.
