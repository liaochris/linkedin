Frank Theuns' Experience

Owner

Theuns Engineering B.V. +31 (0)631226636

Public Company; Myself Only; Oil & Energy industry

June 2010 – Present (3 years 7 months)

Theuns Engineering offers specialized support within the steel industry focusing on:

• QA/QC Management: assurance and control of quality.
• Welding coordination, welding engineering and all other welding related solutions.
• Construction management: project support based upon a practical and methodical approach.

Theuns Engineering is involved, result driven and flexible. Due to our helicopter view we can control quality, safety and project progress all within one oversight. We also offer short term solutions and advice for the smaller business requirements and often deal with welding ‘feasibility’ questions along with providing guidance to obtaining and attaining welding qualifications.

Theuns Engineering operates independently and upon request offshore assignments. Assignments are discussed in detailed and documented in the form of a contract. We also can contribute towards to managing and organizing project teams, based upon our wide network of industry contacts. The majority of our customers are based in the Benelux or overseas.

Current assignment: Foundation Fabrication Manager for Dredging International (part of the Belgium DEME group) during fabrication of 48 jackets at site of the main contractor (Smulders Foundations). Main tasks are the pursuit of all quality related aspects together with production progress. Examples of these tasks are: load out preparation, interface between contractor and Dredging International project directors, subcontractors follow up (partly abroad), control of all welding qualification processes, review of project documentation and inspection coordination. Project is still running within schedule.

Welding/ QC Engineer (IWE)

Heerema Zwijndrecht

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Construction industry

January 2004 – December 2010 (7 years)

As a welding engineer I was mainly responsible for managing and performance of offshore related qualification programs with respect to welding methods, production tests and welders in all kinds of ferrous and non ferrous materials.

Other tasks were:
• Point of contact for clients (mainly oil companies) concerning welding and quality matters.
• Development/implementation of robotics (full penetration welds).
• Coordination of multi discipline QC-activities, like leading inspector’s, raising ITP’s, and coordination of NDE.
• Welding and material inspections, also abroad and offshore.
• Supporting and advising of production and engineering with respect to materials and their process ability.
• Compilation of as built documentation, making deviation requests/technical queries and non conformance reports.
• Production progress control by meaning of a mechanical completion system.

Results:
• Timely delivery of welding qualifications (records) for large scale offshore projects (up to 12000 metric tons platforms). Example: 45 welding qualifications (Inconel 625) for a subsea project within one year.
• Welding repair rates always < 1,5% for piping and structural parts
• Managed to implement a welding robot in combination with a 2D laser scanner for full penetrated welds with dimensional deviations.

Material analyst

Schielab B.V.

January 2000 – January 2004 (4 years 1 month)

During this job I was responsible of destructive testing all kinds of materials and welded joints to determine if standard requirements and client specifications were met.

Example tests:
• Bending, tension and Charpy-V tests
• CTOD testing (crack initiated failing)
• Replica investigation
• Nitrogen embrittlement tests
• PMI testing

Results:
Client from all kinds of branches were provided with test results, certification and advice. In some occasions additional solutions to material, mechanical or welding issues were given. During this experience a very wide knowledge about materials and their properties was gained.
