Bart Windrum's Summary

I'm a citizen end-of-life reform advocate working to empower all of us, helping cut through the misinformation around what it really takes to die in pace. Part of it is how to advocate when hospitalized. I present nationally to both lay and professional audiences.

I also enjoy doing everyday, and unique, home improvement projects so much that I offer myself as Man Around The House to do yours.

And, keeping the old graphic design fires burning, I provide book and long document interior design.

Bart Windrum's Publications

  • Notes from the Waiting Room: Managing a Loved One's (End of Life) Hospitalization

    • Axiom Action LLC
    • May 15, 2008
    Authors: Bart Windrum

    One or more hospitalizations are in everyone's future. We're avised to bring an advocate, and to advocate on our loved one's behalf. But why? And how? Learn how to advocate during any hospitalization, and to revision end of life in to increase the likelihood of dying in peace.

    Notes has received 7 book awards and is endorsed by a range of medical providers and policymakers.

  • How to Efficiently Settle the Family Estate

    • Axiom Action LLC
    • November 15, 2008
    Authors: Bart Windrum

    Settling the family estate is very different from planning an estate. It's a part-time business that can be engaged in very efficiently or inefficiently (which can cost up to 25% of the value of the estate). Get this lay person's unique guidance based on personal experience.

  • The Option to Die in PEACE (Patient Ethical Alternative Care Elective)

    • 2007
    Authors: Bart Windrum

    PEACE, a concept original developed for and presented in the book Notes from the Waiting Room: Managing a Loved One's (End of Life) Hospitalization, is also a healthcare reform initiative accepted by Colorado's Blue Ribbon Commission for Healthcare Reform—the only 1 of 33 submissions to mention or include focus on end-of-life matters.
