John Frank Palermo, III's Volunteer Experience & Causes

  • Volunteer Experience

    • Leader of Career Coaching and Counseling Ministry

      Saddleback Church, Article on Lending a Hand
      2001 – 2011 (10 years)

      LAKE FOREST – Every Monday as he has for the past 9 years, Palermo is there working the room, with volunteers and greeting new people. He checks in with to make sure everything is on schedule and the volunteers are where they should be. The people filing the room on the sprawling Church campus for this weekly Monday night Career Coaching and Counseling Meeting are not here for a revival but are seeking guidance to help them find a job. Saddleback’s Career Coaching and Counseling program provides a number of simultaneous free training classes each Monday evening. While most attendees are taking advantage of the free classes, not too many folks know what goes on behind the scenes at one of the largest network outreach career training organizations in Southern California. Few see the army of volunteers that make all of this work in the weekly operation. The one person behind the scenes, who tirelessly monitors these activities, and keeps things running smoothly, is a casually dressed, soft spoken man who is affectionately known as the “godfather”. Not only does Palermo manage the operational side of this massive program, but he personally helps those in transition. Being well aware of the difficult job market and given his unwavering commitment to helping others, John decided to use his 25+ years in human resource experience to start his own firm; Palermo and Associates (www.palermohrconsulting.com) which provides outsourced HR consulting services to businesses and personalized career coaching for those in transition. With a sense of pride and first-hand experience, Palermo shows up every week to give back to those in the community who need his help. Despite shunning the limelight, his unwavering positive outlook and years of human resources coaching has made him a well recognized figure at Saddleback. In 2011, he received an outstanding achievement award from the Orange County Register for his volunteer work with Saddlebacks CCC Ministry.
