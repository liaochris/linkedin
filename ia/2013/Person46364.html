Paul Ford's Summary

I build digital products out of big data. I take big messy things and turn them into little fun things.

Usually I'm there when projects begin, doing strategy, information architecture, and prototyping, but I also like to stick around to help with design, architecture, integration, etc. and see projects through launch.

I started out in the late 1990s as a writer and computer programmer. I brought Harper's Magazine to the web in 2007, roping together millions of files to build an 160-year archive. I used to be a commentator on NPR. I've published essays in dozens of websites and magazines, and every now and then I'm in "best of" anthologies.

I advise, with varying degrees of formality, a number of companies and media organizations, and teach in the Interaction Design program at the School of Visual Arts in New York City.

Since 2010 I've mostly worked with a media and technology strategic consulting firm called Activate, where I'm a Director.

I live in Brooklyn, New York, with my wife and twin one-year-olds.

Specialties

Writing, programming, figuring out how the web actually works, sorting things into boxes.
