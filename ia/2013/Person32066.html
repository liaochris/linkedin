Global Technical Architect

Pcubed

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Management Consulting industry

August 2003 – April 2010 (6 years 9 months)

Put in place all major processes (Scrum, continuous integration, and so on) to help lead the developers in the delivery of data warehouse and reporting solutions for Project Server and SharePoint.

Architected and authored the core of a data warehouse product. The product aggregated data from SharePoint and other back office systems to create a database that allows almost real-time reporting against data within an organisation. This data includes snapshots and thus allowed for trending. The interface was through SharePoint web parts that encouraged reports to be constructed through a WYSIWYG tool and required no knowledge of SQL or the storage layer. Primarily worked in C#.

Performed a Technical Architect and Consultant role for major clients across all verticals, and delivered numerous projects for those clients in either a developer, architect or advisor capacity. A not insignificant number of these clients were from the financial services sector.

Worked closely with Microsoft as a DAC (Developer Advisory Council) member on the future direction of Microsoft SharePoint from STS v1 through to MOSS 2010. This position included numerous visits to Redmond to work closely with developers and program managers.

Senior Developer

Premium TV

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Internet industry

May 2001 – August 2003 (2 years 4 months)

Worked in a small team that designed and delivered a system that generated consumer video websites for football clubs.

Designed and delivered the system to receive content from live broadcasts, catalogue and archive it, as well as the consumer search interface for this content.

This single solution interfaced with numerous legacy or industry specific technologies and required the use of TCL/TK, Vignette, Java, Visual Basic, ASP, C#, C++, AJAX, Sql Server, Oracle. This diversity wasn't desired, but was pragmatic with regards to the key products that needed to be brought in for the video and publishing platforms at that time.

Senior Developer

British Telecom

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; BT.A; Telecommunications industry

August 2000 – May 2001 (10 months)

Designed and authored from scratch the CMS that powered the BT consumer portal (btinternet.com) and the business portal (btopenworld.com). These were implemented in TCL as BT used the Vignette web and cache platform.

Integrated over 300 content suppliers into the CMS, including video and audio content. This involved consuming RSS-style XML feeds, generating Django style administration tools for the data consumed and then creating a template system that display each feed as a widget on the portals.
