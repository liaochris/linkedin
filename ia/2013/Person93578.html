Michelle Rowan's Summary

I'm a digital nomad from Australia but I spend most of my time these days painting in India.

I'm best described by friends and colleagues as a generalist. My formal education includes a degree in anthropology and communications as well as interactive design. However my professional employment spans start-up consultant, product and customer development through to professional cook, cake decorator and yoga teacher.

What can I say? I love variety!

So how did I learn to paint and become an artist?
Well, I didn't go to art school. In truth, I didn't even finish high school. It was anthropology and Youtube that taught me how to paint. You see, my anthropology degree taught me how to see things in a unique way. Youtube taught me how to use watercolours. And travel is my muse.

Interestingly, every skill I've developed along the way I use every day in my painting. For example, attention to detail comes from my product management work. The concept 'now means now' required when working with watercolours (especially in the Goan heat as they dry very quickly) comes from working in kitchens. The stillness and concentration comes from years of meditation and yoga. The ability to understand space and colour combinations comes from interactive design. Cake decorating taught me about instant gratification and anthropology taught me how to teach myself anything (though my ukulele playing hasn't been quite as successful as my painting, don't be surprised if you one day, most likely in the distant future, receive a 'Kickstarter' request from me so I can record my original ukulele songs - of which I currently have written six already).

The only thing I can guarantee about my future is that I will continue to learn. Always. Oh, and it will be colourful.

If you're interested in seeing my work, you can find it here: www.talesandfails.com

Thanks for you interest & enjoy the delightful surprises which await you every day,
Mishy
