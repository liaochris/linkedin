Anand Prabhu Subramanian's Summary

My research interests span the broad area of Networked Systems, in particular, Wireless Networking, Cellular Data Networks and Mobile Computing with emphasis on efficient protocol design, high speed system design, experimentation, measurement, modeling and performance evaluation.

Anand Prabhu Subramanian's Experience

Research Staff Member

IBM Research

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; IBM; Information Technology and Services industry

June 2013 – Present (2 months)

Network analytics based protocol development in telecom domain.

Member of Technical Staff

Alcatel-Lucent USA Inc

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; ALU; Telecommunications industry

June 2009 – May 2013 (4 years)

At Alcatel-Lucent, I worked on ALU 9900 Wireless Network Guardian, a real-time network monitoring and analytics product out of Bell Labs Research venture program, to monitor and analyze cellular data network (2G/3G/4G) traffic.

I worked on architectural design, research and development of the core multi-gigabit packet processing engine that provide real-time mobile network analytics in cellular data networks. I developed new algorithms and key modules for high speed packet processing, LTE/UMTS/CDMA signaling inference, TCP based passive performance inference, anomaly and congestion detection, statistical modeling, root-cause analysis techniques and deep packet analysis. I also performed mobile data analytics on large datasets collected from cellular data networks to understand key subscriber experience, network usage and performance.

PhD Candidate

Stony Brook University

Educational Institution; 10,001+ employees; Higher Education industry

August 2004 – May 2009 (4 years 10 months)

In my PhD dissertation work, I addressed the challenges of improving wireless capacity and mobile connectivity in wireless access networks by proposing efficient MAC and network layer solutions that exploit physical layer diversities such as multi-channel support and directional communication. The results of my graduate research work are published as part of more than 10 research publications in acclaimed journals and conferences in the area of wireless networking. My most cited works include multi-channel work in wireless networks and Mobisteer work (Best Paper award winner) in vehicular access networks.

Research Intern

Thomson Paris Research Lab (Technicolor)

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; TCH; Entertainment industry

October 2008 – January 2009 (4 months)

I performed a detailed measurement study to experimentally characterize the performance of multi-sector antennas with commodity 802.11 hardware in dense wireless deployments. Based on the measurement study, I developed and implemented a sector assignment algorithm that enables multiple sectors in each node to create a minimum interference topology in the network.

Research Intern

Bell Labs, Alcatel-Lucent

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; T; Telecommunications industry

June 2008 – August 2008 (3 months)

I designed and implemented a novel collaborative spectrum sensing scheme on a reconfigurable software defined radio platform for opportunistic use of spectrum white spaces to improve capacity in 3G/4G wireless data networks.

Research Intern

Bell Labs, Alcatel-Lucent

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; T; Telecommunications industry

June 2007 – August 2007 (3 months)

I designed two efficient approximation algorithms for dynamic spectrum allocation and pricing in coordinated dynamic spectrum access based 3G/4G cellular networks.

Research Intern

Bell Labs- Lucent Technologies

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; T; Telecommunications industry

June 2006 – August 2006 (3 months)

I developed and implemented a routing protocol (AODV-MR) as a loadable Linux kernel module for multi-radio wireless mesh networks that uses a novel routing metric (iAWARE) to find highly efficient channel diverse paths.
