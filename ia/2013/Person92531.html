Head Football Coach

Mt. Hebron High School

August 1967 – June 1972 (4 years 11 months) Ellicott City, MD

After serving as an assistant football coach at St. Albans School for Boys and Woodrow Wilson High School (Washington, D.C.), I spent another year as an assistant at Atholton High School in Howard County, Maryland. I got a big break in 1967, as I had the opportunity to become the first head football coach at a new high school, Mt. Hebron in Ellicott City, MD. We built a program from the "ground up". Our first year, we played a jayvee schedule with freshman and sophomores; the following year, we hit the "big time", playing a full varsity schedule with no seniors; our juniors were our "top dogs". That year, we were pretty much humiliated in most games, with a line that averaged about 150-160# per man across the front line, winning only vs. North Carrroll 27-6.

The following year, our team, which had played together now for two solid years, was older, more experienced and out to settle some scores. That year, 1970, we won the Howard County Varsity Football Championship, beating our arch enemy Howard High School 11-8. (Howard had whipped us 68-0 the previous year).
