Dennis Goedegebuure's Summary

I'm Dennis Goedegebuure, working for AirBnB in San Francisco. With AirBnB I'm growing the business through SEO of all the AirBnB sites.

Prior to AirBnB & Geeknet I've been with eBay for 9+ years in various roles in the Internet Marketing team. I started my eBay career in The Netherlands office working for both eBay and Marktplaats, which is the local classifieds site. After 3.5 years I transferred over to the US San Jose office, to take on a global coordination role in Internet Marketing, with the emphasis on SEO. In this role I not only worked for the eBay websites, but also consulted for Paypal, Skype, Shopping.com, StumbleUpon & the classifieds portfolio.

From 2008 until 2011 I've been responsible for all SEO for the 22 global eBay marketplaces sites. The team I managed was primarily focused on the delivery of the SEO strategy, consulting with product teams on projects, training of the product & engineering teams, and custom content. One of my pet projects at eBay was to create viral stories based on eBay data with the help of Infographics. It's a lot of fun to see stories go viral and get picked up by large technology blogs.

I was involved in the purchase of Marktplaats.nl and integrating the two teams. Setting up the Internet marketing strategy for Marktplaats has been the primary focus for 2005.
Moved to the US into an international Marketing function beginning of 2006.

Organizing 2006 IM summit in Tallinn, Estonia for 80+ people, 2007 IM & RM summit in Dublin for 120+ people, 2008 IM, RM & Advertising summit in Prague for 240+ people.

Leading force behind setup of centralized in-house SEO program for eBay Marketplace. Building a team from scratch, connecting with all parts of the organization, bringing back SEO on the radar screen of executives.

Dennis Goedegebuure's Patents

  • Document Generation Based on Referral

    • United States Patent Application P1248US1
    • Filed May 27, 2011
    Inventors: Dennis Goedegebuure

    For a marketplace system like eBay, there are items which generate sufficient news and PR momentum to make it in pop-culture. Where eBay historically purged all item pages, and descriptions after 90 days of closing, the story around these special items were removed with the original document.
    This system was designed to identify those special items based on the number of distinct domains referring traffic to the document, classify the items in 3 classes, after which a decision would be made based on the class how to treat the document:
    1. No external referral traffic: Purge item
    2. Some distinct domains referring traffic: 301 redirect item to category page, as item is not special enough
    3. Referral traffic over a certain threshold from number of distinct domains: automatically recreate document with original content

    The objective for the system is:
    - Preserve user experience. Purging a document breaks the user experience for future visitors
    - Preserve Search Engine optimization value of incoming links from distinct domains
