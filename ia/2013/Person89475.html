Matt Miller's Summary

A unique mix of Business Services Consultant, and Prince2-certified Senior Project Manager, with a keen interest in the bigger picture for building the business of tomorrow, today.

Focus: how organisation structure, business processes, enterprise applications and ICT infrastructure fit strategically and operationally - and particularly, these days, for doing business over the Web, including use of the cloud and social media

Approach: One, more or all of the following:
* Analyse challenges or issues and determine what needed to integrate software solutions with key business functions (e.g. Demand Planning) or processes of the business (e.g. Plan to Fulfill).
* Interviews and workshops with key stakeholders to clarify the cost/benefits of the solution
* Consider issues and risks impacting business or solution objectives and benefits from how application has been implemented and/or supported
* Deliver application or system review independent of vendor, system integrator or outsourced service provider
* Define applications management strategy or solution
* Manage delivery, from design through to implementation and support, based on best practice for software delivery and project management principles

Typical Objectives: lower TCO, higher ROI, benefits realisation

Summary of Experience: 20+ years working direct or through/with strategic consultancies and system integrators for businesses in the Construction, Engineering, Government, Investment Banking, Retail, Telecoms sectors.

Highly experienced with aligning and integrating CRM, ERP and SCM enterprise applications to fit an integrated operating model using service-oriented architecture (SOA). E.g. recently provided a target architecture for a Supply Chain Hub that can be readily re-used to reduce cost and risk as well as provide greater visibility of physical product movement in line with financial flows

Specialties

Applications Architecture, Business Analysis, Business Process Reengineering (BPR), Cloud Services, Enterprise Applications Integration (EAI), IT strategy, Project Management (PRINCE2), Risk Management, Service Stabilisation, Turnaround, Incident Management and resolution for software solutions based on Oracle software, system integration, technical analysis (view independent of vendor and SI), SOA, Solutions Architect

Principal Business Coach

7C Strategies (formerly part of 7C Alliance Limited)

Nonprofit; 1-10 employees; Nonprofit Organization Management industry

July 2005 – Present (8 years 2 months) London, United Kingdom

In addition to independent consulting to both large corporates and SME businesses through Eco-Enable (as shown below), I provide private business coaching for individual professionals through 7C Strategies, as well as facilitate training courses for independent professionals and start up or review business models and operating practices of small- to medium-sized consulting practices.

In the past 5 years, 7C Strategies (formerly part of 7C Alliance Limited) has coached over 100 people on how to sustain themselves in IT contract work in the UK as well as improved their marketing, networking and business management skills.

It has also been used to facilitate training in standard industry practices, such as PRINCE2 and ITIL, for groups of individuals as a way of reducing costs of training for professionals operating independently. This has led to me defining and incubating several consultancies based on shared competencies, a common business practice or an IT service. In 2006 I applied the same principles to help the Professional Services Division of a large System Integrator win a key contract and get fully established as well as again in 2011 with regularising the engagement model of a large international software company in dealing with a key client.

Over the last 3 years, I have extended support for individuals through setting up the Open Practice Technology Network (OPTN) as a way of bringing all members of mine and my coachees' networks together. This is with a view to them being able to both share and discuss best practice in supporting the end-client in sourcing, analysing and managing implementation of technology independent of any brand of hardware or software, as well as help them source good and reliable contacts for ICT contract work or employment here in the UK or Europe direct with end-clients. As at 16/08/2011, this group now has over 500 members in a discussion group on LinkedIn.
