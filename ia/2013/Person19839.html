Bob Carilli's Summary

I found myself hanging out the door of a Russian Military Helicopter flying low over the Amazon Jungle. I had a digital video camera capturing everything. I'd most recently worked in a cubicle environment so this situation was a bit different. I traveled around the Amazon by planes, helicopters and canoes working as a consultant to the Ecuadorian Military for several months. My adventures included a border trip to Peru where, upon waking up, I immediately checked to make sure both my kidneys were still there.

I applied my TV & Film Production Studies to Web Production working on many "Internet Firsts" including AT&T's 1996 Summer Olympic Games Website, John Hancock's First Website, EarthCam's New Years Eve 2000 Live from Times Square and a number of WWE Streaming Media Productions. I developed my Project Management and Web Production expertise in the field while establishing lasting relationships with many of the leaders of the online world.

When the dot com bubble burst, I made the move to working client side rather then at an agency. I spent a couple of years building a business in Ecuador which forced me to test my chops operating in uncharted territory. Working at smaller businesses helped me to fine tune my ability for formulating and executing marketing strategies and development plans. I found myself leading and developing teams that were focused on optimizing sales, lead generation and monetization models.
