Lindy Dobbins' Summary

I'm a problem-solver. My super power is deductive reasoning.

My focus is meaningful design, particularly in branding and brand management. I've got more than a decade of experience in print, web, multimedia and emerging technologies. I've done work for a diverse range of clientele including nationally-recognized brands (Barnes & Noble, Sears, Best Buy, Overstock.com, Walmart.com, Lions Gate, NPR, NBC Studios, Sony BMG, EMI Music Marketing, etc) and small businesses in the Southeast (NORTEK Construction, Orange Olive Hair Gallery, AST Exhibits). I’ve also helped non-profits and placed ad work in publications ranging from The Charlotte Observer to the New England Journal of Medicine. I'm partial to MACs, but I can produce on a PC if need-be, too. I also teach courses on web development tools and basic computer literacy at Central Piedmont Community College. When I have time, I moonlight as a local rock star – I've even got albums and merch to prove it.

Specialties: Branding and identity; print media in standard and large formats; web design and information architecture; team leadership, project management and planning; effective, meaningful communication; making boring beautiful; designing and implementing elegant systems, creative solutions to common problems, teaching/training.

Graphic Designer

Baker & Taylor, Inc.

Privately Held; 1001-5000 employees; Wholesale industry

July 2006 – February 2010 (3 years 8 months) Charlotte, NC

For the first couple of years I mainly made boring beautiful. I was doing graphic design for medical textbook distribution, after all. Things started to get interesting when the B2B customers began asking B&T to help them add their logos and contact information to the pieces I designed (to market to them) for use in their stores as B2C promotional materials. That's when we started down the path of marketing asset management, and I was on the front lines.

I managed the project, first as a custom internal build and later in partnership with Saepio Technoloiges. I designed and encoded the actual marketing pieces and designed a custom portion of the database structure that allowed sales reps to gather customer branding and contact information using Excel spreadsheets, then I trained the reps on how to use the spreadsheets and the system from Saepio. The system went from adding store names, logos and web addresses to brochures and catalogs to allowing customers to rebrand our email campaigns and send those out to their own subscriber lists. I also helped figure out how to make that work with a separate ecommerce product that was already in place for our customers.

Additional Design Responsibilities: Traditional print media, standard web graphics (static images and animated GIFs), HTML/CSS coding, trade show signs and booth graphics, branding of individual products and services, initiation and execution of email campaigns, design of HTML templates, primary point-of-contact for approval of brand usage and corporate brand standards.

General Responsibilities: Authoring written communication across multiple departments (ad sales, editorial, stakeholders, third party developers, marketing) in the form of formal documentation to aid in understanding of project overviews, component details, time line requirements, standardized vocabulary, materials/data submission templates and general process definition, training associates to use design software applications when needed.
