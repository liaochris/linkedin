Chris Vetter's Summary

In 1996, Chris Vetter founded a company offering media and technology consulting to more than 100 companies including Turner Media, Intel, AOL Canada, the Portland Trail Blazers, the Portland Winter Hawks, AutoShopper and Marketing Analysts. "Chris is a remarkable communications professional with two decades of experience in business development, marketing, politics and journalism," said former Oregon Governor Vic Atiyeh. “Chris is a strategic thinker who helps growing companies reach their full potential," said Intel Executive Monique Hayward.

In September 2013, Chris launched a sports company called America Loves Golf and recently promoted White House Call Girl, the latest book by author Phil Stanford. In June 2013, Chris scored legislative victories as the Director of Save Oregon Poker and Oregonians Against Predatory Towing. In July 2011, Chris advised the Oregon Unlimited Project, an Internet forum for solving community issues. In April 2011, Hot Pepper Studios hired Chris as Communications Director for WebVisions, the influential technology conference held annually in Portland, Chicago, New York and Barcelona. In spring 2011, Chris developed a marketing plan for the Oregon Bach Festival. In fall 2010, Ptownbooks hired Chris to promote The Peyton-Allan Files. The true crime book spent weeks at the top of the regional bestseller list. In 2007, Chris shaped national marketing trends in a landmark study that persuaded CNN to invest millions in online promotions. In 2003, Chris founded Inside Portland Magazine.

Since 1992, Chris guided more than two dozen political races and presented at over 30 conferences. "Chris is a strong leader who motivates others to perform exceptional work," said former Oregon Secretary of State Bill Bradbury. “His skill set is transferable to any company or cause,” said political consultant Joe Trippi. Chris performed more than 2000 hours of community service work for non-profit groups. Visit http://chrisvetter.com.for more information.

Articles and features about the Peyton-Allen Files, City of Roses and Portland Confidential appeared in the Portland Tribune, the Oregonian, Willamette Week and Portland Monthly Magazine. Stanford appeared on all three broadcast television networks and secured interviews with multiple radio stations in the Portland market resulting in more than 750,000 regional impressions. Designed philstanford.com and launched successful social media marketing campaigns on Facebook. Created publicity materials to promote personal appearances at bookstores and literary events.
