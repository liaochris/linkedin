Dara Albright's Summary

Dara Albright serves as Crowdnetic's Chief Communications Officer. She is a thought provoker and frequent speaker on topics relating to market structure, private secondary transactions and crowdfinance. Albright founded NowStreet Wire (acquired by Crowdnetic in 2013), the primary provider of analysis and insight into the crowdfinance industry as well as the legislation and innovation currently fueling it. She is also the co-founder of LendIt, the preeminent conference organization dedicated exclusively to the burgeoning P2P and online lending industry. Albright has held a distinguished 21 year career in IPO execution, investment banking, corporate communications, financial marketing and institutional equity sales. She is a visionary who continues to introduce neoteric asset classes and ground-breaking financial products to the Wall Street community. Prior firms she worked for include: Unterberg Towbin, Morgan Stanley Dean Witter, Divine Capital and Citigate Dewe Rogerson. Dara is a co-founder of the Crowdfund Intermediary Regulatory Advocates (CFIRA) and the Crowdfunding Professional Association (CfPA). She is also a proud board member of SparkMarket, the nation’s first bona fide crowdfunding portal allowing unaccredited investors to legally crowdfund via intrastate exemption. She is a graduate of the George Washington University and holds securities industry Series 7, 24, 31 and 63 licenses.

Specialties: Crowdfunding, IPO Execution, Road Shows, Marketing, Capital Raising, Restricted Transactions, Illiquid Assets, Secondary Transactions, Emerging Private Companies, Social Media

C.E. Unterberg, Towbin

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Investment Banking industry

1994 – 1998 (4 years) New York, NY

C.E. Unterberg, Towbin, was a United States investment bank founded as C.E. Unterberg & Co. in 1932. The firm began as a niche over-the-counter trading firm. Following World War II, the firm made a name for itself as an investment banker for the technology oriented companies of the period.

The firm was known for its merchant banking investments, particularly in high-technology companies. In the early 1980s, the firm emerged as the leading underwriter of initial public offerings, surpassing the elite investment banks (at the time, including Lehman Brothers, Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley). Among the companies they took public were Intel, Cray Research and biotechnology company Cetus Corporation.
