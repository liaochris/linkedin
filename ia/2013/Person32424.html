David Maher Roberts' Summary

Experienced Executive with a proven track record in developing and executing business strategies across geographic boundaries for early to mid-stage technology and digital content businesses. 15+ years of Board-level Executive roles with P&L responsibility.

CEO & President

The Filter

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry

September 2007 – August 2012 (5 years) Bath, United Kingdom & San Francisco, CA

The Filter is a world-leading B2B personalisation system for digital media, using big data to make search, navigation and discovery of content more relevant. The company delivers 550m API calls per month to over 100m users for customers that include Nokia/Microsoft, Warner Bros, Walmart and British Telecom. The Filter is backed by Eden Ventures and Peter Gabriel and has offices in Bath (UK) and San Francisco.

Hired by the founders and investors with a brief to transform the company from a UK software developer of a music playlisting application into a successful global commercial business.

Delivered in two stages: first by developing an innovative B2C proposition reaching over 1m monthly users in less than 12 months (2008); and, secondly by offering the core tech to digital entertainment providers as a Software as a Service (SaaS) system in 2009. Making the strategic change from B2C to B2B and signing Tier 1 customers in order to achieve profitability by the end of 2011.

I was responsible for leading the triple digit revenue growth from 2009-2011, which was fuelled by a number of large deals with companies like Nokia, Orange, Dailymotion, NBC, Walmart, Warner Bros and British Telecom. The company now has customers and partners in the UK & Europe, the US, Asia, Australia and South America.

New Media Director - Europe

Future Publishing

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; FUTR; Publishing industry

June 2004 – December 2006 (2 years 7 months) London, United Kingdom & Paris, France

Future PLC is a specialist interest media company, quoted on the London Stock Exchange, that delivers compelling content across specialist sectors including technology, entertainment & video games. At the time, the company boasted 200+ magazines, 50+ websites reaching 35m monthly readers and had offices in the UK, US, Australia, France, Germany, Poland and Italy.

Developed and implemented Future’s digital media strategy for the UK and Europe, managing 35 websites and a team of over 70 people. Directed the launch of Future’s first ever US/UK games portal, GameRadar, reaching 15m monthly visitors within weeks of launch.

In the UK, I built an innovative digital sales team and delivered rapid revenue growth from £700k to over £10m within 2 years thanks to an aggressive roll out of 20+ web properties as well as the launch of Future’s online magazine subscription and ‘back issue’ store (myfavouritemagazines.co.uk).

Delivering strong growth by focussing on developing new online content offerings within Future’s core market sectors. This requires a clear understanding of and focus on the changing needs and behaviour of consumers. In two years, the business has grown from an annual turnover of £700k to over £4m and 19m to 35m monthly page impressions.

The business unit now has offices in Bath, London and San Francisco.
