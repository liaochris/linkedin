Aneta Marcheva Key's Experience

Founder and CEM

AEDEA Partners LLC

April 2009 – Present (4 years 4 months)

AEDEA Partners LLC is a strategy, organization, and leadership development consultancy helping individual executives and leadership groups manage growth, change, risk, and uncertainty.

AEDEA Partners LLC delivers nimble, versatile, pragmatic, and effective advice that helps managers and professionals with their core tasks: make informed decisions, collaborate and enlist the support of others, and continually expand one’s leadership skill set. Engagements simultaneously advance the client agenda (eg, strategy development, initiative launch, merger management, change roll out) and expand the leadership capacity of the client (eg, learning, coaching, training, establishing repeatable process).

Based on the specific client needs, Aneta adds value through
[1] Systematic managerial problem solving: structured analysis of issues and opportunities, aided by time-tested approaches and side-by-side interaction with clients
[2] Collaborative group alignment: facilitated process to get everyone behind a shared action plan, supported by rigorous situation assessment and systematic alignment approach
[3] Leadership development training: interactive events to increase leadership skills, deepen relationships, and build teams
[4] Executive advisory coaching: a personalized blend of business consulting, training, and executive coaching for individual clients; Aneta serves as a confidant, thought partner, private instructor, and coach

As needed, Aneta brings in other collaborators (e.g., research analysts, facilitators, trainers) to meet client needs.

Associate Principal

McKinsey & Co.

Partnership; 10,001+ employees; Management Consulting industry

July 2005 – April 2009 (3 years 10 months)

Aneta was an Associate Principal in the San Francisco office of McKinsey & Co -- a global management consulting firm -- and a leader in McKinsey’s West Coast Financial Institutions practice. Since joining the firm in 1999, Aneta advised executives at leading financial institutions across sectors (banking and consumer credit, asset management, brokerage, captive finance, insurance) and around the world on topics ranging from strategy and organizational performance, to risk optimization and change management.

Previously, Aneta was a fellow at the McKinsey Global Institute, McKinsey’s economic research think-tank, where she led research on the evolution of the Global Capital Market investigating the financial stock of more than 100 countries since 1980. The research resulted in numerous publications, including the highly regarded 2005 report $118 Trillion and Counting: Taking Stock of the World's Capital Market, its 2006 update Mapping the Global Capital Market (Second edition), and related articles.

Aneta also lead McKinsey’s pro-bono research on the Bay Area Economic Profile “Sustaining the Bay Area’s Competitiveness in a Globalizing World” (2008) as well as its prior issue “The Innovation Economy: Protecting The Talent Advantage” (2006). She frequently engages in discussions on the state and outlook for the Bay Area’s economy as compared to other regions in the US and around the world.

Aneta led numerous McKinsey and client training programs on problem solving, leadership, and change management.
As an an engaging speaker and large group facilitator, she was also a frequent guest to seminars, executive retreats and corporate events.
