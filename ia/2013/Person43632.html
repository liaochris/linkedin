Chris Ewels' Publications

  • Controlling fullerene addition sequences, regioselectivity and magic numbers via metal encapsulation

    • Chemical Communication - Royal Society
    • April 11, 2011
    Authors: Chris Ewels, Alexis Vlandas, Gregory Van Lier

    A systematic density functional study of chlorine addition to C70 up to C70Cl12 confirms experimental observations of regioselectivity and stability of C70Cl10. We show that K@C70 follows an alternative addition sequence with different isomers and magic numbers to C70 such as KC70Cl3. This prediction is important for controlling functionalisation behaviour via encapsulation and endofullerene purification.

  • The di-interstitial in graphite

    • Journal of Physics: Condensed Matter, 20, 395220
    • October 1, 2008
    Authors: Chris Ewels, Irene Suarez-Martinez, Malcolm Heggie, Christopher Latham, J. A. Gámez, P. R. Briddon

    Di-interstitial defects appear to play a key role in the microscopic understanding of radiation-induced damage in graphite. Their formation has been invoked as both one of the main causes of dimensional change and as an energy releasing step in annealing cryogenic radiation-induced damage. In the present work, first principles calculations are employed to examine several models for these defects. Two of the structures possess nearly equal energy, yet take very different forms. The results suggest that di-interstitial defects cannot play the principal role in radiation damage that has been assigned to them. The possibility that one of the structures may exhibit ferromagnetism is also investigated.
