Deborah Carver's Summary

As a suburban 14-year-old searching for a creative outlet in the late 1990s, I made my own webpage to share my writing and build connections with other artsy car-less teens.

Since then, I've been heavily involved in publishing, on and offline. I've been a publishing professional for nearly 10 years, from intern to editor, and I've even launched my own publishing initiatives. Now I'm heavily entrenched in the B2B publishing world at Catersource, where I've developed new content initiatives as well as written and edited a bimonthly print magazine.

But my favorite part of the job is the connections I've built -- to caterers and small business owners, to publicists and to foodservice professionals. Connect if you'd like to grow your relationship with Catersource -- or to geek out over web analytics.

Specialties:
+all things content, including development, management, strategy and measurement
+writing and communications in every medium, from the tiniest Tweet to the most in-depth whitepaper
+social media, SEO and analytics
+building connections and constantly learning in the changing digital environment

Deborah Carver's Experience

Editor/Content Manager

UBM Catersource Magazine, Conference & Tradeshow

Public Company; 11-50 employees; Professional Training & Coaching industry

I create digital and print content for Catersource Magazine, Conference & Tradeshow. In addition to our bimonthly magazine, I run our Facebook page, LinkedIn group, Pinterest boards, monthly enewsletter, Instagram, Twitter and the Catersource Carvings blog. Founding Publisher and Editor-in-Chief

Twin Cities Runoff

May 2010 – February 2012 (1 year 10 months) Greater Minneapolis-St. Paul Area

To encourage long-form journalism and storytelling from new writers, and to engage my enthusiasm for editing and organization, I began Twin Cities Runoff (TCR) in mid-2010. We published weekly from November 2010 to December 2011, and I gathered over 20 writers and illustrators to publish magazine-style creative nonfiction about the Twin Cities. With the Runoff I not only commissioned and edited over 20 pieces of fantastic writing, but I also managed a team of volunteers, created marketing and development strategies, written a monthly column of community news, planned and executed community-based storytelling and writing events, developed grant applications and wrote press releases.
