Cliff Whearley's Summary

I remember when I first started noticing the details of architecture. I was knee-high with a toothless grin. My mom would point out the properties that my grandfather built. Then, she’d tell me the story of how my grandfather came to America from Ireland as a teenager - alone and determined. After years of hard work in construction, he became a developer and built many residential, commercial and apartment buildings in the Bay Area.

I grew up in Redwood Shores, moved to San Francisco for a few years and then again back to the Peninsula. As I got older and passed by my grandfather’s buildings, I started to notice other properties too. I became intrigued with the design and the history in every skyscraper, home and building I passed. That was when I began to gravitate toward a career in Real Estate.

Growing up in a family of developers, real estate agents, lenders and appraisers, I immediately had a strong support system and network that I could count on to deliver smooth transactions. For my clients, this has meant that they have been able to benefit from the over 50 years of combined real estate wisdom and creative strategies, while also tapping into my dedication and energy.

Did you know that when a buyer's agent presents offers to a seller's agent or vice versa, the other agent is analyzed more than your offer? Real estate agents need to count on each other to make sure that buyers and sellers understand the paperwork and that all of the “t”'s are crossed and “i”'s are dotted to avoid lawsuits and nightmare transactions. If your agent has a bad reputation in the industry, it can affect the way the other agent and their clients view your offer or make them question your property on the market.
