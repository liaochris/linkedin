Michael Fergusson's Summary

I believe play is among the most productive things we can do. Currently, I am the CEO of Ayogo, an exciting new company building functional social games, working transform healthcare. The Ayogo team has won many awards (see below), and been featured in Fast Company, MIT Technology Review, and many other places, for our work on patient adherence and engagement through games and other mobile applications.

In 2012, I was Ernst & Young Social Entrepreneur of the Year and was named to the PharmaVoice 100, a list of the 100 most inspiring people in Life Sciences. Several companies I've founded or co-founded have gone on to great (or at least interesting) things, one of which is now public and the other is now part of WPP.

I built my first social networking applications in 1995, and continue to be fascinated by the power of crowdsourcing, user generated content, microblogging, and social-graph-based applications.

Specialties: That bundle of things recently called "web 2.0": social networking, game play, "gamificiation" (a term I don't love, but you can't argue with the SEO (-: ) web-based and mobile technologies, user-generated content, and so on; Product development, team building, and other aspects of initializing a new business; I have an engaging and entertaining presentation style, and often present on topics ranging from serious game design to entrepreneurship to on-line community building.
