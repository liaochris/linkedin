Bob Robertson's Summary

Born 1934, Hertford, England.
Joined the Royal Navy in 1949.
Served eight years in general service at sea and ashore and three years as aircrew in the Royal Naval Fleet Air Arm.

Married 1957. Has five children living in various parts of the world. His wife Yvonne sadly died in 2006.
In 1960 he left the Navy and joined his London-based family shipping business.

Emigrated to South Africa in June 1969.
Formed South Africa's first container shipping line Enterprise Container Lines (ECL) in February 1972, providing unique connecting carrier services to Europe, Scandinavia, the United Kingdom, Mediterranean, the USA, Canada, South America and Japan via a transhipment hub in Rotterdam.

ECL was eventually sold to South African Marine Corp. (Safmarine) in 1977.
Formed a specialised publishing company for the shipping industry in 1980, producing two alternate weekly newspapers.
The titles were eventually purchased by Lloyd's of London. At that time he had moved into the electronic media industry.

Became a founding member of the United Democratic Movement (UDM), an opposition party to the ANC in South African politics, and was chairman of the Gauteng Finance Committee for a while.

Sold his business in 1999, resigned from the UDM and slipped into retirement in 2000. Spent time researching material for his book “Money Meltdown & Climate Change – The Penalties of Infinite Economic Growth.”

    The disastrous consequences of the 2008 financial meltdown, coupled with the relentless approach of worsening climate change conditions, have combined to become mankind’s greatest survival threat ever.

It reveals their historical origins and exposes the conspiracy of greed, corruption and abuse of power behind them, which persist today.
