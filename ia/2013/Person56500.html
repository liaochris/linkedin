Jackie Harris' Summary

So here's the thing... Everyone it seems, is an expert on social media marketing. Anyone with a keyboard and spellchecker is apparently a copywriter. So what's the point in someone like me?

It all boils down to this:

- By all means get your next door neighbour's teenage son to set up your social media accounts for tuppence halfpenny. But unless they're creating decent content that reaches and engages with the RIGHT people, it's a waste of time, money and energy.

- Copywriting needs to sell. Anyone with a half decent education can pull together a coherent sentence or two. But can those words convey important messages? Can they influence and persuade? Can they keep people reading your website? Can they make sure Google loves you?

- Blogs, social media posts, email marketing and other digital content programmes always start off with a flurry of enthusiasm. But they normally run out of steam in about three months. The trick is to consistently come up with interesting and topical ideas week in, week out. Year in, year out.

- If your marketing is to have any hope of yielding decent results, all messages, content and marketing activity needs to be part of a multi-platform master plan. If you don't have a clear focus, or you don't want help defining that focus, then don't spend any money on marketing until you do. You'll just be throwing money down the drain.

I offer you a strategic mind to help get you focused. An ability to generate bold and creative ideas. And the skill to create well targeted content. If you want things done right for a reasonable rate, you know where to find me.
