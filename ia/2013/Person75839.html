Kim Plowright's Summary

I have over nine years experience of producing and project managing the delivery of high profile, user-focused web projects; both within a large public service media organization and a small commercial start-up environment.

I have extensive experience with projects where web-based content, television and the real-world intersect.

I have a strong perspective on the problems of representing both fiction and complex factual information in an online environment in order to create compelling experiences for the audience.

I have experience managing complex new media projects, including working to secure consensus among disparate stakeholders.

Kim Plowright's Projects

  • D-Day: As It Happens

    • March 2013 to June 2013
    Team Members: Kim Plowright, Digit London Ltd, Windfall Films Ltd, Channel 4

    D-Day: As It Happens was a real time multi-platform 24-hour history event. Running across TV, a responsive website and Twitter, it told the story of this pivotal event by following seven people who were there on the day.
    Each character was a real participant in the 1944 invasion. Their stories were told through a series of moment-by-moment updates using archive film, photographs, radio reports, diaries, testimonies, letters and official records.
    Each update was timed and geolocated as accurately as possible, allowing the stories told across the day to marry up across platforms.

  • REACT Books&Print Sandbox

    • January 2013 to May 2013
    Team Members: Kim Plowright, Jo Lansdowne, Clare Reddington

    Books&Print Sandbox supports eight ground-breaking collaborations between creative economy partners and academic researchers to explore Books and Print as historical, contemporary or future phenomena.

    The collaborations develop explorations around how we read, learn and publish. Books&Print Sandbox seeks to support shared learning, creative collaboration and brilliant ideas.

    Books&Print Sandbox is originated and produced by iShed, part of Watershed on behalf of REACT.

  • Pepys Rd

    • November 2011 to April 2012
    Team Members: Kim Plowright, Matt Locke, James Bridle, Christopher Thorpe, Dean Vipond, Phil Gyford

    Pepys Road was a promotional experience for John Lanchester’s book Capital.

    How will your life change in the next ten years? Will you be better or worse off? Signing up to Pepys Road delivered a simple story over ten days allowing readers to explore how the world will change over the ten years of the 'lost decade' and what this meant for them. Simple interactions affected the outcome of their stories, and realtime data pulled from the web further personalised the experience.

  • SuperMe

    • January 2010 to July 2010
    Team Members: Kim Plowright, Jo Roach, Paul Bennun, , Phil Stuart, Robert McHardy, Margaret Robertson, Phil Gyford, Tom George, Tony Sephton

    Somethin' Else, Preloaded and Coney for Channel4 Education.

    About the project - http://superme.somethinelse.com
    Full credits - http://www.playsuperme.com/makers/

    SuperMe is an online game which teaches resilience through play. It’s a meta-game made up of many parts: immersive games, short videos, quizzes and written content. Inside each piece of content are hidden achievements and experience points. Players are taken on a unique journey of self-belief and discovery, and based on their strengths and weaknesses and the SuperMe system sends them to new content via messaging inside the browser.

    SuperMe is a Guardian MEGA winner, finalist in the “best use of digital technology” category in the Broadcast Digital Awards, and nominated for two BIMAs.
