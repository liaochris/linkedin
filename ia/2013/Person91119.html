Melanie Negrin, GPC's Volunteer Experience & Causes

  • Volunteer Experience

    • Board of Advisors - Marketing and Funding Development

      Kahele Foundation
      • Health

      Through Pure Sound Therapy™, Kahele is redefining quality of life for children with autism.

      The vision of the Kahele is a world where autistic children are able to function effectively in society, do not require special education, are able to communicate clearly with their families and make new friends, and feel a sense of control over their own environment. We are working toward this vision by building a network of parents, occupational, speech, and music therapists, special needs educators, and healthcare facilities that implement Pure Sound Therapy™ with autistic children.

      The work of the Kahele Foundation complements other early invention programs designed to eliminate the impairments so common to Autism. Kahele's Pure Sound Therapy™ reduces aggressive behaviors, tantrums, outbursts associated with sensory overstimulation, repetitive behaviors and sounds, seizures, and sleep disturbances when applied as part of a regular family routine or therapeutic practice. Building on underlying harmonies of pure musical tones, simple ethereal melodies bring the brain hemispheres into balance and promote a state of active relaxation. The underlying miscommunication between hemispheres of the brain is then relieved, improving focus, enhancing learning and memory, and enabling creativity and social development.

      In addition to developing a network of providers to spread awareness and use of Pure Sound Therapy™, Kahele is working with musicians from around the world to create the one-of-a-kind music that is the foundation for the therapy and is engaged in on-going research and video documentation of the therapy's benefits.

    • Board of Advisors - Marketing and Funding Development

      NAIPEC - Native American Intellectual Property Enterprise Council
      • Economic Empowerment

      NAIPEC is a nonprofit 501c3 corporation, based in Atlanta, GA, dedicated to fostering creativity and invention among native peoples across the U.S., helping them to identify ideas and assets that can be protected and licensed for the economic benefit of the tribe. Founded by Native American inventor and entrepreneur David Petite, creator of the technology that supports today's "smart grid" energy infrastructure, NAIPEC believes that creativity, invention, and entrepreneurship are powerful tools to motivate native youth and adults and change the economic and social fabric of their communities. NAIPEC's vision is a funnel of invention and entrepreneurship that brings hope and prosperity to native communities across the U.S.

      The mission of NAIPEC is to foster creativity and new ways of thinking about native assets through education, create a funnel of innovation that identifies ideas that are most likely to result in initial and on-going investment by private parties, and facilitate the manufacture of prototype inventions while protecting them and preparing them for market.
