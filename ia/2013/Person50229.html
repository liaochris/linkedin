Greg Maxson's Summary

As an internationally recognized illustrator and author, a variety of clients have called upon Greg to provide clear and accurate visual solutions for over twenty-five years. He routinely works from sketches, photos, engineering drawings, and the actual products to illustrate complex processes and products for some of the top studios, publishers, and manufacturers in the United States.

Clients include:
AFAR magazine, Barnes & Noble, Board of Certified Safety Professionals, Eclipse Shading Systems
Bob Vila's American Home, CiviGuard, Cleveland magazine, Consumer Reports, Consumers Digest
Corvette Quarterly, Creative Publishing International, Dana Corporation, Home Buyer Publications
Furniture Lounge, Georgia-Pacific, Grolier Encyclopedia, Houghton Mifflin Harcourt, Human Kinetics
Howard, Merrell & Partners, howstuffworks.com, Illustrator WOW! books, ImageWork Technologies Kirchoff/Wohlberg, Log Home Living, Los Alamos National Laboratory, NanoIntegris, Network World Popular Science, Precision Graphics, Purdue University, ReadyMade magazine, Ryobi, Scholastic
Science World, Successful Farming, Sunset Publishing, The Family Handyman, VanDam Media
Thomas-Bradley Illustration & Design, Timber Home Living, Today's Homeowner, Workbench
UNC-Chapel Hill School of Medicine, University of Illinois, Wiley Publishing


Greg is co-author of The Complete Technical Illustrator, a college-level text/professional reference book on the subject of technical illustration, with sales world-wide. More detailed information about CTI, as well as a link to purchase the PDF version, is available here: http://gregmaxson.com/store/


Greg is also the owner/manager of the LinkedIn group tech.ART. Attracting over 500 members since 2009, tech.ART is solely focused on the technical illustration industry.
