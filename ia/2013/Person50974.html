  • Oracle Automated Scanning and Attaching of Production Documents

    • May 2012 to Present

    The AutoScan process allows Production personnel to automate the scanning of production paperwork into Oracle. Production paperwork generated for each order will usually encompass some or all of the following: Pick Slip, Sales Order Acknowledgement, Packing Slips, Commercial Invoice, Emails (Work Order Pull Notifications and notes), and the Bill of Lading. Prior to IT designing a solution this paperwork was manually scanned and attached to the Sales Order in Oracle. The process consumed several hours per day. With the new automated process this takes only a few minutes per day. All of the production paperwork for the day is placed in bulk on the scanner. The paperwork is scanned into an inbound directory where it is processed via barcode and attached to the appropriate Sales Order using a custom process and one very inexpensive application. There is no expensive document management system in place. The existing reports were migrated to XML publisher in most cases, and barcodes were added where needed. This project improved production management efficiency by at least 15 hours per week. Additional hard costs to implement this project were under $100 per year.

  • SMS and Email on the retail ATM

    • July 2012 to Present

    The ARGO is the first retail ATM with the ability to send e-receipts. The Triton ARGO 7.0 is available in a printer-less model. This model has the capability to send transaction receipts globally via SMS or email thus reducing waste paper and service calls to clear printer jams and errors. The ARGO also has the option to email the management reports necessary to track cash replenishment and ATM activity. The project is a collaboration between software engineering, IT, and the web team to utilize Triton’s own TDL protocol in combination with email and SMS gateways to route ATM receipts from anywhere the ARGO is located to any SMS or email recipient. Emailed management reports can be utilized with an email trap to automatically assimilate daily and management reporting.

  • Dynamic Currency Conversion (DCC)

    • March 2012 to Present

    DCC enables a foreign customer to see the amount debited from their bank account in their own currency and the exchange rate applied, while receiving US dollars from an ATM. DCC eases the uncertainty of money exchange for these customers when traveling.

    DCC is a very profitable business, as a markup can be applied to the actual exchange rate paid for the convenience of providing the DCC service to the ATM customer. The money made on a DCC transaction can be measured in dollars, rather than $0.20-0.50 made on some normal domestic withdrawals.

    Interest in DCC products exploded in 2012, with several projects being undertaken for development on Triton ATMs. As Director of Product Development, one of my duties was to manage Triton's custom software group. I was responsible for capturing each customer's requirements, documenting these requirements in a Product Requirements Document, and then preparing the contracts, developed a preliminary schedule and the quotation for the custom development. Once the contracts were in place, I managed the project through development and testing phases.
    Work on DCC required working with multiple parties, typically the ATM owners, the financial processor, the sponsor banks and at time the development staff of the processor's software. Coordination of all these parties was essential in minimizing the time-to-market. As Director of Product Development, I was responsible for this coordination of effort. Many conference calls, WebExs, and customer visits were necessary to accomplish this.

    Triton released its first custom DCC product on schedule in November 2012. The product was featured in a presentation given at ATMIA 2013 and was demonstrated on the trade show floor.
    Triton was the first retail ATM manufacturer to offer DCC to its customers.
