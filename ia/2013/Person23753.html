Christopher Shuler's Publications

  • Collaborative Problem Solving Agent for on Board Real-Time Systems

    • IEEE
    • August 7, 2002
    Authors: Christopher Shuler, Shikha Jain, Ohio University, Lonnie R. Welch, Ohio University, David M. Chelberg, Ohio University, Zhenyu Tan, Ohio University, David Fleeman, Ohio University, David Parrott, Ohio University, Barbara Pfarr, NASA Goddard Space Flight Center, Mei C. Liu, Computer Sciences Corporation

    Breakthrough in Earth Science Observing will occur when constellations of Earth observing satellites are able to fully collaborate together and collectively monitor the conditions of our planet through a vast array of instruments. These satellites form a network that consists of distributed processes that need to respond to perceived scientific events, the spacecraft environment, spacecraft anomalies and user commands. The requests and responses exhibit dynamic behavior. In order to handle such dynamic environments, a method is needed to guarantee the real-time quality of service constraints.
