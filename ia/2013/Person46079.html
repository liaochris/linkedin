Frederique Remy's Summary

I am a video producer with 14 years' experience in producing, editing, video camera operating, and screen writing.
I started Gone West Productions when I moved to Vancouver in 2009. Since then, I have worked on several projects for the corporate and non-profit worlds
During my time at MSD, I made several short documentaries on the issue of HIV/AIDS in sub-Saharan Africa. I also produced two short films. One of them, 'Ashes to Dust' was presented at the 2010 edition of the WIFTV short film festival in Vancouver.
Some of these videos can be found on gone West Productions' Vimeo channel.

I am also a coach and consultant for OnScreen Coaching and Teambuilding that offers smaller groups and one-one-one video workshops, as well as creative workshops (video making and screenwriting basics.)

Specialties: Experienced Final Cut Pro, Avid and Premiere Editor
Experienced HDSLR, Panasonic HPX170, Sony HVR-Z1 operator
Writer, Director, Producer
Video documentarist

OnScreen Coaching & Teambuilding

Teacher of English

French Ministry of Education

September 1998 – July 2001 (2 years 11 months)

I wrote my training thesis on the potential of team work in class. I applied my theories through one of my favorite activities which was to introduce students the fun arts of writing and acting. They would write short scenes and play them in front of their classmates. The idea was to improve both their written and spoken English. I also filmed their performances which helped them gain awareness on the way they spoke English, which in turn led them to improve their oral skills.
I also created a video club, in which students were given the opportunity to write and shoot their own short films.

Attachee at the Dpt of Economic Development

City of Marseille

July 1994 – July 1995 (1 year 1 month)

The mission of the Department of Economic Development was to promote the greater Marseille area to potential business investors. As an Attachée, I helped French and International companies to find the best opportunities to settle or develop their businesses in the greater Marseille area.

Project Manager

Euriel

May 1990 – April 1994 (4 years)

In the framework of a project led by the Port of Marseille Authority and the European Union, I managed a team of developers and performed consultancy missions in order to computerize commercial ports of the Mediterranean and of the West coast of Africa. My mission was to organize conferences to present the project to the various actors of the port communities, and to sit down with them to closely study their operations in order to try and computerize them, and also connect all the ports via a common data exchange network. Thanks to this project I traveled to Portugal, Spain, Italy, Malta, Slovenia, Croatia, Greece, Cyprus, Russia, Ukraine, Turkey, Israel, Egypt, Morocco, Mauritania, Guinea Conakry, Senegal, Ivory Coast, Benin, Ghana, Congo, Dem. Rep. of Congo (Zaire at the time) and the Philippines.

Database analyst

Unisys

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; UIS; Information Technology and Services industry

July 1987 – December 1989 (2 years 6 months)

I joined Unisys the year after Sperry and Burroughs merged. Besides trying to develop a sensible organization, one of the challenges that they were confronted with was the merger of their product databases. As a database analyst, I helped to develop the new Unisys price-and-product database, first in Blue Bell, PA, USA (87-89) then in Uxbridge, UK (90). While in the UK, I got to travel to our subsidiaries in France, Germany, Italy and Sweden to promote and help implement the new database.
