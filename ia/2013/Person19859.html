Cari Rincker's Publications

  • Field Manual: Legal Guide for New York Farmers and Food Entrepreneurs

    • CreateSpace
    • September 2, 2013
    Authors: Cari Rincker, Pat Dillon

    This book aims to be a practical guide for New York farmers and food entrepreneurs. It gives a general overview of a myriad of food and agriculture law issues including business formation, contract law, confidentiality, farm leases, environmental regulation, animal law, land use and zoning, right-to-farm law, intellectual property, farm transportation, employment and labor law, property law, wind energy leases, direct farm marketing, cottage food operations, food labeling, and on-farm poultry slaughter. It's a "must read" for New York farms, agri-business owners, and food entrepreneurs to help them understand the breadth of laws that affect their business.

  • New York State Agriculture Mediation Program

    • February 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Cari Rincker

    I am on the roster of mediators for the New York State Agriculture Mediation Program ("NYSAMP"). NYSAMP works with the CDRC's in each country throughout New York to provide free mediation services for the New York agriculture industry. NYSAMP is funded through the U.S. Department of Agriculture ("USDA") and the NYS Unified Court System's Office of Alternative Dispute Resolution. It is managed by the NYS Dispute Resolution Association ("NYSDRA"). NYSAMP can help farmers mediate disputes with the USDA with various farm programs including crop insurance, Farm Service Agency ("FSA") loan programs and servicing, Rural Development ("RD") loan programs and servicing, wetland determinations, conservation programs, neighbor complaints, nuisance suits/ Right-to-Farm, and farm landlord-tenant disputes.

  • New Jersey Agriculture Mediation Program

    • May 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Cari Rincker

    I am on the roster of mediators for the New Jersey Agriculture Mediation Program, funded in part through the U.S. Department of Agriculture. It is a free program available for those involved with the New Jersey agriculture industry. It can help farmers mediate disputes with the USDA with various farm programs including crop insurance, Farm Service Agency ("FSA") loan programs and servicing, Rural Development ("RD") loan programs and servicing, wetland determinations, conservation programs, neighbor complaints, nuisance suits/Right-to-Farm, and farm landlord-tenant disputes.
