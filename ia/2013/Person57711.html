James McCarthy's Summary

James M. McCarthy has 30+ years of real estate experience as a lender, investment/portfolio manager, adviser, and city planner being involved in $5+ billion of transactions.

Jameson/Jefferson invests for its own account and on behalf of a group of family offices and high net-worth individuals, often in partnership with institutional and private funds, combined with third-party debt or equity.

Jameson/Jefferson offers 1 to 3 year bridge/value-added financing/equity, proof of funds/soft deposits, debt/equity to IPO, and other opportunistic investing (corporate and real estate).

Jameson/Jefferson also consults on the corporate and project level, including structuring/re-structuring, management, and capitalization, and arranges both short-term and long-term capitalization.

Previously, Mr. McCarthy advised on bridge funds backed by Amaranth and Liberty Harbor/Goldman Sachs and consulted to several investment banks including Paine Webber and CIBC and was Consultant/CIO for a real estate merchant bank based in NY and London.

He co-founded Intercap Investments specializing in U.S. investments for offshore investors, including some of the first Islamic real estate transactions in the U.S.

He substantially re-established the Real Estate Investment Group of The Guardian Life Insurance Company increasing the portfolio from $100 million to over $1 billion (when a billion meant something).

At Chemical Bank, he was involved in $500+ million in transactions including structuring a major masterlease/purchase option on a NY office property, equity investments for the Trust Department, assistance on a Eurobond, and advising offshore investors.

As Supervising Principal Planner, he worked on the redevelopment of the Jersey City “Gold Coast” including planning, funding, and development; housing and economic development, historic districts, residential development, neighborhood planning, and transportation planning.
