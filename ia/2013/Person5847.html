Aaron Stauch's Projects

  • Transitioning In New Times

    • October 2010 to Present
    Team Members: Aaron Stauch, Marguerite Jacobson

    Lutherwood received a three-year grant from the Ontario Trillium Foundation to develop employment services that focused on local labour market demand in Waterloo Region. I led the development of the Transitioning In New Times program. This program focused on Demand-Side Employment Services to more effectively help those experiencing increased structural unemployment move into sustainable careers. Transitioning in New Times worked with new Canadians, youth, experienced workers, and displaced manufacturing workers; providing them training identified by area employers, increasing employed outcomes. The program met three-year goals in just over two years. In addition it has gained provincial attention for its innovative approach to employment issues, with a system level initiative to increase replication. Transitioning In New Times has also fundamentally informed the local employment sector on how to increase the effectiveness of programs.

  • Bridging the Gap Conference

    • November 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Aaron Stauch, Katrina Michielsen, Kyla Frankowski, Lynda Prior

    Guelph currently has one of the lowest unemployment rates in the province. The Workforce Planning Board has even indicated that the community is approaching full-employment. As such, many area employers are experiencing difficulties recruiting talented employees. One solution to this skills shortage is accessing Internationally Trained Professionals (ITPs) and attracting them to the Guelph Community. Working with the Guelph Chamber of Commerce, the City of Guelph, area social service providers, and the business community; my team led the development of a conference to connect employers with ITPs. With over 150 participants, the conference connected employers with the talented individuals they need to grow their workforce. The conference was a successful catalyst for community conservation and action on economic development and the inclusion of new Canadians.

  • Mental Health System Partnerships

    • February 2012 to November 2012
    Team Members: Aaron Stauch

    Working closely with the Board Chair and the organization’s Executive Director we provided leadership to an examination of potential system partnerships within the Waterloo-Wellington mental health system. We worked with the Waterloo-Wellington Local Health Integration Network and Trellis Mental Health to reach a formal agreement for an organizational partnership. This partnership sets the stage for better mental health care for all citizens of Waterloo-Wellington, and the more effective use of limited resources. I also worked closely with the Self Help Alliance to more fully integrate their operations with the Canadian Mental Heath Association. Integration in the mental health system provides better care for all, and as a Board we championed this for our community.

  • Corporate Rebranding

    • January 2008 to December 2010
    Team Members: Aaron Stauch

    D.L. Services Inc. is a small environmental consulting firm. Working with the Senior Management team, I co-led the development of a new corporate brand. This included: print advertising materials; internal/external forms; website; tradeshow materials; clothing; and social media. The new brand increased our recognition in the industry. It was designed to better communicate the work and values of our organization. As a result of the successful implementation of the new corporate identity there was an increase in client’s trust in the services we offered. The corporate rebranding was a component of an overall marketing strategy that was credited for increasing business by 50%.

  • Anaerobic Digestion Study

    • January 2010 to August 2010
    Team Members: Aaron Stauch

    Working with the Toronto Region and Area Conservation Authority (TRCA), Yield Energy, and businesses in the Pearson Eco-Business Zone; I led the study of organic waste creation and disposal. The purpose of this study was to identify the feasibility of anaerobic digestion (a energy-from-waste technology) for the disposal of the Organic-Fraction of Municipal Solid Waste in the Pearson Eco-Business Zone (an proposed eco-industrial park). The study findings were used by key stakeholders and decision makers to evaluate the viability of anaerobic digestion, and associated major capital investments.

  • Property Due Diligence

    • June 2008 to August 2008
    Team Members: Aaron Stauch

    Led priority Phase I & II Environmental Site Assessments in a series of property transactions for an up and coming waste management organization. These properties were being acquired to form the infrastructure required for major operational growth. I led a multidisciplinary team that identified significant potential risks for our client. Through this process we were able to provide the client viable options to mitigate those risks while still acquiring the infrastructure required. These properties gave our client the ability to secure a major municipal waste collection contract.
