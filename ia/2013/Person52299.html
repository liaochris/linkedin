Heather Stevens' Summary

As a Full Member of the Canine & Feline Behaviour Association, I am approved and recognised as a professional with high standards of behavioural work by pet insurance companies offering cover for pet behaviour problems.

Specialising in dog aggression, I utilise my 30 years' experience of working with "no-hopers" - those rescue dogs that would have ended up going to Rainbow Bridge, alone and unloved.

My other speciality is puppy training and socialising, and I'm in the process of writing my first book called "The Puppy Workbook", taking the reader on a journey from searching for a puppy, through their pet's first year of life, with oodles of ways on how to make training fun for both owner and pup.

Since 1979, I have competed in KC Obedience, Heelwork to Music, Agility, and showing ... with a variety of breeds, but particularly with Rotties, of which I have owned 40. I currently share my life with three Staffie crossbreeds and a Patterdale cross.

In brief:

1962 Trained my first dog, aged 6 - the family GSD, Sparky
1979 Started Obedience Competitions with my Lab cross
1985 Bought my first Rottie & trained at March DTC, Cambs
1987 Founded Fenland Dog Training Club at Murrow, Cambs
1987 Started rescuing, rehabilitating & rehoming Rotties
1988 Co-founded "Rott 'n' Shepherd" Display Team
1990 British Rottweiler Assoc Committee Member & Trainer
1995 Discovered clicker training & its benefits for dogs
2003 Founded Canine Connexon Dog Training School
2006 Kennel Club Approved Examiner - GCDS - all levels
2008 Full Member - Canine & Feline Behaviour Assoc
2008 Master Trainer Member - Guild of Dog Trainers
2009 Founded Phoenix Paws Demonstration Team
2010 Canine Connexon rebranded to We Train Any Dog
2012 Devised an 8-Level training scheme - PAWS (Progress And Wag Scheme)
