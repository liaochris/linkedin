Brad Dempsey's Experience

Senior Litigation Attorney, Finance & Restructuring Group

Faegre Baker Daniels LLP

Partnership; 1001-5000 employees; Law Practice industry

January 2012 – Present (2 years) Denver, Colorado

Brad Dempsey is an experienced trial lawyer that leads the firm’s Colorado Finance & Restructuring litigation team. Brad focuses on bank litigation, bankruptcy litigation (contested matters and adversary proceedings), receiverships, negotiation of workout and restructuring agreements, real property and Article 9 foreclosures, FDIC and bank failure matters, real estate litigation, appeals, and other complex financial matters. Brad has handled numerous cases before state, federal, bankruptcy, and appellate courts throughout the United States. Brad has represented creditors in bankruptcy cases throughout the United States such as American Airlines, Chrysler, Lehman Brothers, Enron, General Motors, WorldCom, Kmart, Provident Royalties, and Shane Co.

Brad also represents clients involved in complex disputes and litigation involving homeowners associations and Colorado’s Common Interest Ownership Act (CCIOA). Brad actively participated in the enactment of Colorado’s recent HOA reform legislation and has developed significant expertise in HOA governance, management, and CCIOA compliance through a decade of service to large residential and resort communities in Jefferson County, Summit County, and Eagle County.

Brad also has experience working with domestic and international clients operating in the wine industry.

Clients include national, regional, community banks, private investment groups, court-appointed receivers, asset purchasers, secured and unsecured lenders, utility providers, landlords, bankruptcy trustees, intellectual property licencors/licensees, homeowners and homeowners' associations. Wine industry clients include wineries, growers, wine lenders, and equipment vendors located in Colorado, Oregon, and California.

    • Litigation Magazine, Volume 33, No. 1, Fall 2006, American Bar Association
    • October 2006
    Authors: Brad Dempsey, Nancy Dempsey, Kirstin Stoll-DeBell

    While injunctive relief was once considered an extraordinary remedy, it is now far more common, especially in cases where irreparable harm is presumed, as in patent law and copyright claims. The authors examine the history of the injunction, how injunctive relief has historically been tempered, and how to most effectively plead for or against an injunction.
