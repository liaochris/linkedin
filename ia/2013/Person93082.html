Mike Dean's Projects

  • Sustainability as a Factor of Municipal Bond Ratings

    • August 2010 to December 2010
    Team Members: Mike Dean

    An empirical analysis of the effect of sustainability, or "green", practices on municipal bond ratings throughout the 50 largest cities in the United States.

  • Re-energizing the Frozen Climate of Energy Financing: Residential PACE Programs & the Impact of Sonoma v. FHFA

    • January 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Mike Dean

    Property Assessed Clean Energy (PACE) programs are a cost-effective financing tool used by governments to provide homeowners with funds to install energy efficiency improvements. However, after early success, a series of decisions by the Federal Housing Finance Agency (FHFA) and the Ninth Circuit Court of Appeals have left PACE programs with an uncertain future and few remaining pathways to realize the program’s economic and environmental savings. Part I introduces PACE programs and surrounding issues. Part II presents FHFA’s concerns regarding PACE. Part III considers three alternatives to the traditional structure: legislation reversing FHFA’s actions, on-bill repayment, and subordinate-lien PACE programs.
