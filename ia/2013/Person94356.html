I had the privilege of being one of the first artists to work on "Disney's Little Einsteins." I (along with Barrett Benica) designed the 4 kids, helped design Rocket and worked on lots of concept and development work for the show in it's very early stages.

I set up the pipeline for character animation and oversaw all character animation from boards through final compositing. As head of character animation, I managed a team of 10 animators.

Later as Director, I managed a team of over 30 artists including storyboard artists, animators (3D and 2D) and compositors. I worked with designers to create props and 3D models of Rocket and other characters. I directed all animators and worked with editors to cut animatics and final edits of shows.

I also worked directly with Disney to create character marketing artwork and helped design 3-dimensional versions of the characters to be used as guides for toy creation and Costumed Characters in the Disney Parks.

I illustrated all DVD covers, a book and most art of the characters used on branded merchandise.

Animation Director

Nickelodeon Animation Studios

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; Animation industry

January 2006 – April 2006 (4 months)

Working on "Lolly and the Pipsqueaks" pilot for Nick Jr. I had the task of creating a pipeline fir a very unique style of character animation. I set up a system for animating characters using a hybrid of drawn animation and After Effects puppeting. I conceptualized and oversaw the creation of a head system using a rig to mimic 3D looking movement on heads that maintained a painterly stylized look. I also set up a pipeline to use Flash in creating traditionally drawn animated characters which were then run through a series of filters and effects to make them look furry and 3 dimensional.

This project was a big technical challenge and a lot of fun to develop. It really broke a lot of ground for visual techniques that could be used in long form television productions.
