Joshua Nelson's Summary

As a structural engineer I have experience in a variety of methods of construction including wood, steel, concrete, post-tensioned slabs, concrete masonry and structural clay brick. I am licensed in both California and Washington, as well as LEED Accredited. My past projects have included: residential new construction & remodels; commercial and manufacturing buildings; large multi-family, low-income and senior housing; high school buildings and college housing; and mixed use structures.

My green building & design experience consists of multi-family structures, single-family residential green remodels, as well as combined residential and commercial buildings seeking LEED certification. I have experience with green roofing, designing for alternative heating and energy systems, low impact development and LEED structures. I am also interested in straw bale construction, green roofing, and on-site renewable energy systems.

Professionally I am all about communication and collaboration. I believe a open dialog with co-workers to brainstorm solutions is vital and a strong relationship with clients from project inception through construction is a crucial part of a successful business. I pride myself on being honest about my strengths and weaknesses while attempting at all times to develop myself as a better engineer.

In 2007 I started a blog about sustainable economics which lead to co-founding the Post Growth Institute in 2009, an international group of volunteers inspiring and implementing new approaches to global well-being without relying on unsustainable economic growth.

In 2011 I took my web development to the next phase and started a consulting business - building sleek, custom websites using WordPress. I have designed websites for non-profits, bloggers, and for-profits.

In my free time I enjoy being outdoors with my son and my wife. I also am a very active mead-maker, occasional home brewer, craft beer lover, localvore and cyclist. Occasionally I blog.

Specialties

Structural Engineering with wood, steel, masonry, concrete, and green design. including moment frames, braced frames, specially reinforced concrete and masonry shear walls, post-tensioned and reinforced elevated slabs, wood shear wall systems, and mat foundations.

Sustainable Economics, Sustainability, Outreach, Writing, Blogging, .

Web development experience includes WordPress, PHP, HTML, CSS and JavaScript.

Joshua Nelson's Projects

  • High Tech High - North County

    • January 2008 to June 2008
    Team Members: Joshua Nelson, Matt Schmitter

    High Tech High is a two story, 46,000 square foot high school in San Marcos, CA consisting of concrete masonry (CMU) shearwalls supporting steel beams and concrete filled metal deck floors.

  • West Village Davis Square

    • May 2011 to November 2011
    Team Members: Joshua Nelson, Matt Schmitter, Jordan Janicki, P.E.

    West Village Square consists of six 4-story mixed-use buildings and a leasing & recreation building and is the central portion of the new West Village development of the UC Davis campus.

    I worked on the mixed-use buildings, which contain approximately 42,500 square feet of ground level commercial lease space with three levels of apartments above providing 192 beds for students. The construction of these buildings include 3-stories of wood framing utilizing open web floor trusses over an elevated post-tension slab.

    The project utilizes numerous energy efficiencies such as solar water, PV panels, thermal massing, passive cooling and a "solar tower" incorporated into a paseo spanning between two of the mixed use buildings that I personally worked on as well.

  • Autumn Terrace

    • August 2009 to June 2010
    Team Members: Joshua Nelson

    Located along the newly revitalized rail link of North County, this 5 building, mixed use project was designed to meet the requirements for LEED Silver accreditation. DCG provided structural services for the 3-story wood structures.

    The project’s unique features include complicated awnings and low roofs along with open walkways and architecturally featured steel stairs. The design also incorporates merging steel moment frames into the wood framing. The project is was completed in the summer of 2010.

  • Arrowhead Gardens Senior Housing

    • June 2007 to January 2010
    Team Members: Joshua Nelson

    Arrowhead Gardens is an affordable senior housing project located in the south end of Seattle and includes 3 separately permit structures. Two of the structures were the residential buildings including 163 and 281 units. These structures were 5 stories of wood framing over two and three levels of concrete; the elevated concrete levels were post-tensioned slabs. The third structure was a 3-story, daylight basement, wood framed common recreation building.

    The unique features of the project included exposed concrete exterior walls, a 50 foot “bridge” in which the residential building spans the drive aisle and self-supporting non-bearing CMU walls and how they coordinated with the wood framing. The project was completed in 2010.

  • Whidbey Island Cargotecture

    • August 2010 to January 2011
    Team Members: Joshua Nelson

    Design of a single family residence utilizing a double bay of cargo containers, 40-foot containers cantilevered above 20-foot containers. This design required a lot of steel detailing and coordination with the client to create a unique structure.

  • Post Growth Institute

    • January 2009 to Present
    Team Members: Joshua Nelson

    A volunteer driven non profit, Post Growth Institute aspires to change the world for the better by exploring and inspiring paths to global prosperity that don’t rely on economic growth.

    The website is built on Thesis Theme for WordPress, combining customized plugins and CSS to create a sleek and modern design.

  • Davido Consulting Group

    • January 2010 to January 2010
    Team Members: Joshua Nelson

    The new website for an engineering consulting firm. Built with WordPress and Thesis Theme - it boasts multiple custom post types that allow projects to be inputted by the admin in a very user-friendly interface and displayed on the live site without knowledge of coding by the admin.

  • EnRich List

    • January 2012 to March 2012
