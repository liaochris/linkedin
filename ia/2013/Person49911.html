Gray Horton's Summary

As an operations manager, I've built solid businesses and dramatically turned around failing ones—with integrity. I have a simple formula: take care of the employees, make the customers happy, and you’ll have a successful business making good money. This formula has worked again and again in both union and non-union environments, in diverse businesses from vertical transportation to real estate development, commercial construction, industrial material handling, and IT. I’ve consistently shown that I’m a fast learner who can quickly come up to speed in new industries.

I’ve had full P&L responsibility, supervised up to 10 direct reports and 60 employees and overseen budgets of up to $14M. I form motivated teams, play by the rules, and consistently complete projects on time and on budget. I do this while making safety part of the culture. While working in a very dangerous industry, I brought about a 300% increase in safety procedure compliance.

TURNED AROUND UNDERPERFORMING BRANCH
I’ve set and met aggressive revenue targets. At Kone, Inc., I was assigned to lead an underperforming branch office at a time when the market was deteriorating rapidly. I got back to basics and implemented my formula. Working within a union environment, I instilled a strong pride of ownership. I rebuilt our reputation with our customers, who soon noticed a big difference in the quality of the workmanship and customer service.

REAL ESTATE PROJECT MANAGEMENT
At Storage Concepts, a self storage firm, I surveyed existing storage facilities to determine the feasibility of new business, then acted as general contractor to coordinate all the activities from land acquisition and construction to management of multiple properties. We were able to produce an annual ROI of over 35% for four projects totaling $50M. Under my leadership, we completed a $5M construction project $500,000 under budget.

Gray Horton's Experience

Branch Manager

Schindler Elevator Corporation (U.S.)

Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; Construction industry

September 2011 – Present (2 years 1 month) Portland, Oregon

At this $9 billion global company, I directed statewide business operations, developing and executing corporate profitability, quality, safety, environmental, and customer focus initiatives. I managed installation, sales, administrative, and service functions through department leaders; full P&L responsibility.

When I took over the branch, employee turnover was high and there was little responsiveness to customer needs. The company’s market performance was low: of the 15,000 units in the total market, my company had under 20% market share. I turned things around by doing the basics well, especially responding to customers. For example, before I arrived, customer calls went into voice mail and often were not returned. Consequently, when our mechanics went on service calls, they frequently ran into a hornets’ nest of irate customers. I personally took the calls and allowed the customers to vent, then reassured them that we would take care of them. Mechanics were grateful that I made their jobs that much easier. With this and other changes I made, net profit went from a ($32,000) loss in 2011 to $350,000 in 2012, morale increased dramatically, and the high turnover became a thing of the past.

I took aggressive action to address the serious issue of safety. In this difficult and dangerous work, hazards include electrocution, crush injuries, and falls. Ten employees industrywide had died in the last five years. I implemented the company safety program and made safety part of the culture, and received an award for a 300% improvement in adherence to safety processes.

Branch Manager

KONE Inc.

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; KNEBV; Mechanical or Industrial Engineering industry

January 2009 – September 2011 (2 years 9 months) Honolulu, Hawaii

A $6B company, one of the global leaders in the elevator and escalator industry.
As the first branch manager in ten years, I found a branch suffering from remote management and unsupervised staff. Staff was unfocused in performing business operations and customer service; and worse, some were peddling get-rich schemes while on sales calls. I improved performance by recruiting, training and coaching good people, instilling high standards and ethics, and insisting on adherence to company policies.

Our warehouse was cramped, dirty, run down and cluttered. Thirty years of parts were stacked floor to ceiling so that a forklift couldn’t even drive through. Plus, decades of installation records were chaotically stored in over 200 filing cabinets. I moved the company to a new, roomier warehouse and placed those filing cabinets in easily accessible, logical order, then eventually digitized the records. The new location cost more, but was much more efficient and paid for itself many times over.

I returned an annual net profit of 11% on revenue of $14M, despite the onset of the recession.
