MaryEllen Walsh's Experience

Teaching Artist

various universities

May 2013 – Present (6 months) Southampton

As a teaching artist, I teach creative writing on the university level in various genres: poetry, personal essay/memoir, screenplay and fiction. I recently taught the first subscribers introduction to creative writing workshop for Stony Brook Southampton's Writers Conference during Sessions I and II, summer 2013. I incorporate out-of-the box writing prompts and exercises that include various forms of art, music and therapy designed to open up the creative flow and produce flash fiction, essay pieces in five sessions. I also taught a one-day poetry workshop in Dowling College's first ever Creative Writing Conference in spring 2013.

PR strategist / social media consultant / writer / publicist

Public Relations Consultant

Educational Institution; 5001-10,000 employees; Higher Education industry

January 2005 – Present (8 years 10 months)

I am a creative media strategist for authors, musicians, non-profit organizations and academia. Utilizing social media, press contacts, event planning and out-of-the-box ideas, I leave no stone unturned in spreading awareness for my clients. Clients include: SUNY Farmingdale, LIWA, Long Island Womens Agenda, www.whatbetterlookslike.com, www.PROUDgirls.com, musician/composer of LIBERTY the musical Jonathan Goldstein and others.

Freelance journalist

Freelance

January 1995 – Present (18 years 10 months)

An award-winning journalist specializing in human interest features that touch on women's issues, lifestyle, author interviews, book reviews and news features, My work has appeared in: Newsday, The New York Daily News, Family magazine, Long Island Pulse, Long Island Press and elsewhere. As a regular feature writer for Newsday's Wellness magazine, I also penned the "Eat Out, Eat Right" column including tips from the American Heart Association. My column writing continued with the first-person yarn "Mewsings" on AOL's hyperlocal news initiative Patch.com, which won first and second place 2011 Society of Professional Journalists (PCLI) awards for online humor column. I also contribute to Newsday's business column "Made On Long Island."
