Jose Antonio Risi's Summary

José Risi, CEO of Eminent IT & AFCEA Co-Chair Emerging Technologies. A former Marine Corps Sergeant and a member of the post 9/11 generation, New Yorker, and DC Metro Area Resident, currently living in Crystal City.


The past few years have been of great pleasure, many successes, experiences and lessons learned. I continue to grow Eminent after four years.

I've compounded my experiences as a student, Marine, artist, older and younger brother, only son, leader & follower, programmer, designer, teammate, teacher & mentor, database administrator, consultant, graphic designer, developer, partner, manager and sub-contractor to mold an identity which embodies a spirit of Excellence, Maturity, Innovation, and Nobility, while also providing results, effective solutions, and desired outcomes.

My goals are to continue to make strides forward as a leader and businessman in the interest of my family, network, mentors, America, and those who need my help and experience.

Studying and working towards gaining relationships within the DOD & MHS Community, is something that I work towards each day. My desires are to help move forward the big data and cloud visions of the government. Efforts, which directly impact the vision and missions of various organizations, active military members, veterans, Wounded Warriors, and the everyday end users.

Jose Antonio Risi's Projects

  • Navy Medicine Online Migration to Spawar Systems Center Atlantic

    • August 2010 to Present
    Team Members: Jose Antonio Risi, Ronald Overby, Otis Hopson, , Perry Gao, Darren Holland

    Serving as a subcontractor for Celtic Technologies, I led a small team of contractors in successfully delivering the migration of a Military Medical Readiness System. The system consisted of several independent external and internal sub-systems (IMR-Lite, BUMED WebWave, NMO Portal, Medical Midshipmen Application, MEDBOLTS, DENCAS, MRRS, SAMS 8, CDR, & TMIP). The new system was housed and relocated to a greener and more secure data-center in New Orleans, while the retired System was located at what is now known as Walter Reed National Military Medical Center.

  • NAVY BUMED WebWave Application

    • September 2008 to Present
    Team Members: Jose Antonio Risi, Isaac Barnes, Ronald Overby

    Developed and Delivered Online Case Management System WebWave by fusing Microsoft .Net & Oracle Database. System is used for Medical Waver Processing, Reporting, & Data Tracking. BUMED received a System which, centralized and standardized the processes of satellite offices both CONUS & OCONUS in the Navy and the Marine Corps.

    The system eliminated all of the paper based processes, which bogged down the Medical Waver Designation process. Staff levels required to support the effort were also significantly reduced, one year after implementation. The effort also included training the end user and the system administrators.

  • NAVY BUMED M3 WebWave Application - INCAP version

    • February 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Jose Antonio Risi, Isaac Barnes, Darren Holland, Jessica Metcalf, Stacy Brown

    INCAP is the second generation of WebWave, which was created in 2008. INCAP including new features such as CAC Authentication, User Notifications, and Microsoft Word Integration.

    INCAP, like Webwave is a fusion of Microsoft.Net C# & Oracle Database 11g.

  • USMC Manpower Management Support Branch Tracker Application

    • April 2006 to Present
    Team Members: Jose Antonio Risi, Minor D, Christopher Taylor, Darren Holland, Jason Baker

    Developed two separate Case Management systems for separate sections of the organization, by fusing ASP.net VB and Oracle. Requirements naturally derived from User Stories, Office Processes and Microsoft Access Databases.

  • Navy NMPDC Enterprise SharePoint Portal
