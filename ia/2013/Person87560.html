Martina Watts' Summary

BANT & CNHC registered Nutritional Therapist working in private practice. Independent Nutrition Consultant for schools, local government, NHS and Nuffield Proactive Health.

Special experience working with children and adults suffering from digestive, behavioural, mood and immune conditions.

Convener and speaker at national conferences examining the increasing impact of our technological age on diet, the established links with physical and mental health problems, and how these trends can be reversed.

Newspaper columnist on nutrition for The Argus (Sussex) from 1999-2009. Various articles published in journals and magazines, including the Nutrition Practitioner, Montessori International, Mentalhealth Today, Optimum Nutrition, Foods Matter.

Editor of "Nutrition and Mental Health: a handbook. An essential guide to the relationship between diet and mental health (Pavilion Publishing).

Appearances on local and national radio and BBC Breakfast TV.

Invited by Brighton & Hove Council (2004) to visit local schools and report on the quality of school lunches. Made several recommendations regarding the content and preparations of meals, drinks and snacks, many of which were implemented following consultations with Scolarest (catering provider).

In 2004/5 implemented a cost-effective healthy eating project at a secure unit for young offenders. The unit confirm the young people are much calmer with less aggravated behaviour, and according to a monitoring report by the General Manager: "the use of physical restraints is down by 60%".

Provision of training sessions on nutrition, mental health and addictions for the corporate and voluntary sectors. Most recently for the award-winning Fareshare Community Food Network aimed at community project staff and volunteers and held in London, Manchester, Dundee, Brighton, Sheffield, Newcastle and Bristol.

Speaker on 'Nutritional Interventions for Behavioural and Mood Disorders' at Primary Care 2011, NEC Birmingham (25th May).

Martina Watts' Publications

  • Nutrition and Mental Health: a handbook

    • Pavilion Publishing (Brighton) Limited
    • June 30, 2008
    Authors: Martina Watts

    Edited by Martina Watts, this bestselling handbook explains the science behind nutrition and its effects on mental health, in a clear accessible way.
    It helps readers to think about the complex and dynamic relationship between mental health, diet and nutrition.
    Leading health practitioners have contributed their own valuable insights, experiences and nutritional strategies to create an informed, up-to-date and fully referenced resource.

  • Nutrition and Addiction: a handbook

    • Pavilion Publishing Ltd
    • November 10, 2011
    Authors: Martina Watts

    Edited by Martina Watts. This multi-authored handbook examines the underling nutritional and biochemical factors involved in addictive behaviour. whether substance addiction or food addiction, and the importance of nutrition in prevention, management and sustainable recovery. Leading researchers and nutrition practitioners explore the existing scientific evidence and newly emerging concepts in the management of addiction and also consider the role of stress, the influence of genetic and environmental factors on craving and reward systems, and discuss practical ideas for introducing safe, effective nutritional programmes. Up-to-date, fully referenced resource with a glossary and guide to drug terms.

  • 49 Ways To Eat Yourself Well - Nutritional science one bite at a time

    • Step Beach Press Ltd, Brighton
    • May 19, 2013
    Authors: Martina Watts

    Combining a nutrient-rich diet with thousands of active components creates one of the most powerful factors of achieving consistently improved physical and mental health. This book uses evidence from nutritional biochemistry to highlight the associations between our diet and our general well-being, without the reader having to have detailed understanding or knowledge of nutrition science. The book does not have to be read sequentially. The reader is free to dip in and out of topics, which include: how to boost immunity, balance blood sugar, regulate hormones, support digestion, reduce inflammation and improve concentration and mood.

    49 Ways to Eat Yourself Well is packed full of motivational and practical ideas. There are handy self- help tips and easy, tasty snack recipes included with each topic – useful for all those who want to improve their quality of life. This book can help us understand the importance of taking responsibility for our own health and take back a role of leadership with our own nutrition. It encourages us to be proactive in building knowledge and making changes to forge a better relationship with our food. An inspirational guide that encourages lifelong healthy eating habits. Its emphasis is on increasing long-term vitality and resilience, thereby improving our ability to not only survive, but to thrive in a rapidly changing environment.
