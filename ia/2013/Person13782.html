Betsy Aoki's Summary

For much of my 18+ year technical career, I've led Web teams that built social/community applications. Gamers may know me for launching the Xbox LIVE Indie games platform (now part of http://create.msdn.com/en-US/ ), which for the first time allowed gamers to write games for the Xbox, peer review each others games, and offer them for sale on Xbox LIVE marketplace.

In 2013 I produced my first kids' game, Linear 1.5, as a side project with Jackson Maynard, a Seattle startup. http://www.girlgamer.com/zine/article/2659/ Available for iOS and Facebook, what made it unique was its "kid-driven development" - we worked with a local STEM classroom of 4th and 5th graders to determine this release's features.

In 2009 I was recruited by a GM to do social media strategy for the launch of Bing, Microsoft's search engine and to serve on an evangelism/community outreach team within PR/Communications. While tweeting, I also had to launch http://bing.com/community, the blogs/forums site we used to engage our community from day one. My current team on Bing incubates outreach projects, including Web sites and Web apps, and we have a good time doing it :).

With every job I try to learn something new - about technology, social networking, community building and how to make humans connect. :)

This is a very short summary of my most recent projects at Microsoft prior to Bing.

(Xbox) XNA Indie Games Platform team - 2007-2009
Live QnA (Live Search engineering, now Bing engineering) 2006-2007
"Microsoft Blog Queen" for Microsoft.com Communities team (2005-2006)
MSDN Community program manager (2003-2005)

My twitter handle is http://www.twitter.com/baoki - I'm not pushing twitter updates into LinkedIn at this time.
