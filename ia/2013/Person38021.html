Judy Ho, Ph. D., ABPP's Summary

Judy Ho, Ph.D., ABPP is a board certified and licensed clinical psychologist and a tenure-track professor at the Graduate School of Education and Psychology at Pepperdine University. She received her specialty board certification in Clinical Child and Adolescent Psychology through the American Board of Professional Psychology in November 2011, and was previously a psychiatry fellow at the Semel Institute of Neuroscience in the Department of Psychiatry at University of California Los Angeles. She is a two-time recipient of the National Institute of Mental Health's National Research Service Award (2004 and 2008). She received her Ph.D. in May 2007 from the San Diego State University/University of California San Diego Joint Doctoral Program in Clinical Psychology and completed her clinical internship in the Department of Psychiatry at the University of California San Diego School of Medicine. Dr. Ho’s clinical experience includes conducting neuropsychological assessments, providing expert witness/forensic psychology services, and implementing empirically-driven therapies such as cognitive behavioral therapy, dialectical behavioral therapy, and parent management training to adults, youth, and families in inpatient and outpatient settings.

Dr. Ho’s research addresses the impact of sociocultural factors in the development and maintenance of mental health problems, and how these factors influence the treatment process (utilization, quality of care, and outcomes) for economically and culturally diverse populations. Her current research projects are aimed at addressing treatment retention for low-income families, and improving social, academic, and psychological outcomes for low-income, at-risk youth through the provision of a sociall skills building performance arts program. Dr. Ho hopes to combine her research and clinical efforts to develop interventions aimed at improving the quality and effectiveness of care for underprivileged and culturally diverse adults and youth.

Specialties

Mental health services for youth, Neuropsychological assessments, Forensic psychology services, cognitive behavioral therapy, dialectical behavioral therapy, parent management training, ethnic and minority mental health disparities
