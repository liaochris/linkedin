Florica Vlad's Summary

I am interested in visual media, web theory and community organization. I have an understanding of creative industries, a passion for stretching boundaries and thinking well outside the box.

I am a graduate of the Film and Television Department at the Tisch School of the Arts, NYU, with a minor in Journalism and Communications. I have have written and directed my own short films and have collaborated with other artists and professionals on short and feature film projects. Notable examples include a promotional film I helped create for the media theorist and writer Douglas Rushkoff for his book, Life Inc., and a successful Kickstarter campaign video I collaborated on for the funding of Hayes Valley Farm, an urban farm in the center of San Francisco.

In addition to my work in film, I have created interactive physical projects. Having completed my graduate studies in the Interactive Telecommunications Program at the Tisch School of the Arts, NYU, I gained exposure to physical computing, new media arts and design. The Pianocktail, an interactive cocktail-making piano, I built with my colleague Oscar Torres, was featured in MAKE Magazine’s Best In Show category at the Interactive Telecommunications Program Winter Show.

I was introduced to community building, public outreach and open-source advocacy while working with the New York collective ShiftSpace, based on an open-source meta-web platform allowing users to annotate, remix, shift and share any webpage, anywhere. And I honed these skills in outreach and community building while working with the budding Gray Area Foundation for the Arts, a new San Francisco based non-profit media arts center.

I currently work in support at Square Inc.

Specialties

customer support, outreach, communications, event management, film production management, marketing, photography, presentation skills, research and development, telecommunications.
