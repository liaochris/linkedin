Bobby Smith's Experience

President

Sword Dreams

February 2011 – Present (2 years 9 months) 1401 Vermont Detroit MI

President of the board- Sword Dreams a 501(c)(3) non-profit. Oversee the general management and early stage development of the organization.

Due to the growing financial constraints within state and local government; cultural exposure, financial literacy, and promotion of alternative physical activities have been stripped away from the public school system. However, this is not an isolated occurrence within Detroit schools, it is happening within low-wealth communities across America. Not only does this hinder the growth of young minds by depriving them of access to possibilities but, it also perpetuates the growing obesogenic environment within these communities.

By operating from a social business model we are attempting to create a sustainable means of providing access to enriching activities such as alternative sports programs (Fencing, Boxing, Golf..), Fine Arts Programs (Ballet, Photography, Sculpting, Classical Music...), Financial Literacy, Nutrition Education, and After-School Tutoring Support. Our goal is to develop high level athletes with strong academic training. We will raise to funds to offset the cost of their training and travel across the nation. We will create S.H.A.R.P kids.

Founder & CEO

En Garde! Detroit

July 2008 – Present (5 years 4 months) 6200 Second Avenue Detroit MI

En Garde! Detroit (EGD) was established in the summer of 2008 in collaboration with local fencing clubs, community partners and families across Southeast Michigan. Over the past 3 years we have exposed the community to the sport of Fencing with great success. However, we have determined that more is needed within these communities to create lasting impact. Thus, over the next 3 years we are expanding our services to match the growing needs of our clients, partners, and the youth. En Garde! Detroit is a social enterprise which utilizes the transformative effects of Fencing & other enriching after-school activities to better the lives of youth. We achieve this by providing high quality services to youth based organizations, schools, and corporations which defrays our cost for providing the same services within low-wealth communities.
