Alfred Contarino's Summary

Al Contarino is an established “out-of-the-box” thinker, inventor and entrepreneur with over 20 years of senior management experience growing and overseeing his own businesses. He currently manages two businesses - AFC-Lancer1 and KettlePizza, LLC.

AFC-Lancer 1 is a think tank dedicated to developing new products and bringing them to market. KettlePizza LLC is an American manufacturer of pizza grilling products and accessories. Both businesses are dedicated to bringing manufacturing and ingenuity back to the United States.

In the past, Al was the President of L-com, Incorporated, He was instrumental in defining the business strategy to grow the e-commerce/direct marketer of electronic cable assemblies. The company grew from $5M to $30M in sales during his tenure. He is extremely proud that L-com was “consistently profitable” for over 12 years with EBIT percentages exceeding 20%.

Al left L-com in 2006 after completing a successful sale of the company to the private equity group – The Riverside Company.

Al also has a passion for teaching college level business classes. In the past he has taught business management classes at Boston College, The University of Massachusetts – Lowell, Northeastern University and Northshore Community College. Specific classes are Operations Management, Supply Chain Management and Small Business management.

Al has an undergraduate engineering degree from University of Massachusetts – Lowell and a Masters in Business Administration from Bentley College. He is also a proven inventor with numerous patents issued under his name. In 2012 he was chosen to be on the TV program Everyday Edisons showcasing his his invention the Continuous Convection Grill.

When Al is not teaching or working on his business endevors you might find him at home playing with his two children or at Ascot Farm in Ipswich riding his horse.
