Darren W. Love's Summary

Darren W. Love's Early Career

Darren's first career started as a teenager and culminated as an award-winning graphic arts professional in 1982. He completed his liberal arts education in Ohio at The University of Findlay, being nominated as Founder's Son and Social Science Student of the Year in 1986 and finished his clinical training in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania at Temple University in 1992.

He became a licensed counselor in 1997 after working five years for a psychiatric hospital earning multiple commendations while later joining a private practice. In 1999, he returned to Northwest Ohio and served as the director of an innovative therapeutic horseback riding center helping adults and children meet the challenges of serious physical and emotional disabilities through animal-assisted therapy. While there, Darren worked with a national advocacy group and noted regional researcher improving services and seeing the program earn the state's top service awards.

Darren also completed work on the grant, Sex Offenders & Significant Others: New Initiatives for Strengthening Families and the Community and coauthored, Responsible Choices Program: Effective Strategies for Safe and Effective Relationships.

Darren served as the mental health director in 2007 - 2008 for over 600 inmates at New Mexico's female correctional facilities and provided testimony on behalf of inmate mental healthcare before the New Mexico Legislature. Additional hearings resulted in a task force to implement and design effective solutions for the state's female inmate population.

More Recent Projects

Currently Darren is in private practice while managing treatment services and the counseling internship & clinical residency program for one of the state's regional community forensic psychiatry centers.
