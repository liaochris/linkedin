Nathalie Rossette-Cazel's Summary

Nathalie Rossette Cazel is a Doctor in international law, with an emphasis on labor relations law within the Specialized Agencies of the United Nations and the United Nations . Since 2004, she has practiced as a counsel before the ILO’s International Tribunal representing employees of the Specialized Agencies.

She has been employed by the United Nations Office in Geneva, The United Nations High Commission for Refugees and the World Health Organization. She has also consulted for the Staff Unions of WHO, UNAIDS, OMPI, IOM, IARC and the Global Funds as well as environmental and human rights NGOs (Red Cross).

In addition to specialising in the field of international and labor law, Nathalie Rossette Cazel worked in a variety of roles in the practice of international law in France, Cook Islands and New Zealand where she lead collaborative projects on Foreign policy issues, Constitutional Law , Disaster management and human rights . In January 2013, she participated in a colloquia on the role of France in the Pacific in the 21st century, at the French Senate in Paris.

Specialties: International law, Constitutional Law, with an emphasis on international administrative labor law within the Specialized Agencies of the United Nations. Foreign policy issues, environmental law and human rights.
