Jeff Werner MBA, JD, PMP, PSP, CCM, CFCC's Summary

Mr. Werner has thirty five years of experience in program management/construction management, project controls and construction claims analysis/dispute resolution.

Mr. Werner’s project experience includes: Commercial/Residential Developments, Water/Wastewater Facilities, Power Generation facilities such as; Wind Energy, Nuclear Power, Fossil Fuel, Hydro Electric, Transit Projects including; Light Rail, Roads, and Bridges. Educational Facilities, Aviation, Oil and Gas Industrial Plants, Hospitality Renovation and New Development, Nationwide Wireless and Fiber Build-Out.

Titles have included; Project Manager, Program Manager, Project Controls Manager, Construction Manager, Assistant Vice President and Director/VP.

Mr. Werner’s education credentials include a JD from Concord Law School, MBA from the University of Texas, Dallas, and a Masters Certificate in Project Management from UTD. Mr. Werner is a certified Project Management Professional (PMP) and a certified Planning and Scheduling Professional (PSP) a Certified Forensic Claims Consultant (CFCC) and a Certified Construction Manager (CCM).

Specialties

Program Management, Project Management, Construction Management, Project Controls, Planning & Scheduling, Construction Law, Surety Law
Construction Claims, Contracts Manager, Dispute Resolution, Schedule Expert, Contract Administration, Primavera software user/expert.

  • Expo Phase 1 Light Rail

    • June 2006 to April 2012

    Currently under construction, the 15.2 mile Expo Line will bring light rail to the Exposition Corridor, with 19 stations serving popular destinations like USC, Exposition Park, the Mid-City Communities, the Crenshaw District, Culver City, and West Los Angeles.

    Phase 1 of the line travels from Downtown Los Angeles to Culver City. Service on Phase 1 began on April 28, 2012, with service to the Culver City station on June 20, 2012. Phase 2 will extend the line out to Santa Monica and construction is scheduled to be completed by 2015. Estimated costs for the project are $930 million for Phase 1 and $1.5 billion for Phase 2. By 2030, an estimated 64,000 passengers are expected to ride the Expo Line each day – which would make it one of the most heavily used light rail lines in the country.

    On June 14, 1996, the DART Light Rail Starter System celebrated its grand opening on time and on budget. The first light rail system in Dallas consisted of approximately 23 miles of light rail with 22 stations, a three-mile long tunnel, and a bridge over the Trinity River. Initial train ridership exceeded all expectations and DART began preparing for the first phase of the light rail Build-out.

More than 100,000 residents of the City of Decatur and the Villages of Forsyth and Mt. Zion are served.
