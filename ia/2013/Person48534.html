Gideon Strauss' Summary

I have provided executive leadership in three organizations, edited three magazines, taught at the undergraduate and graduate levels in higher education, presented at numerous conferences, done and managed research, and written both popularly and academically.

My goals are to build organizations and publications with global impact, to enable the careers of talented leaders, professionals, and artisans, and to teach effectively with insight and imagination.

Specialties: executive leadership, strategic management, networking, editing, teaching, researching, recruiting, curating, writing, public speaking, imagination

(Updated October 8, 2012)

Gideon Strauss' Experience

Executive Director and Editor of Fieldnotes Magazine

Max De Pree Center for Leadership

Nonprofit; 1-10 employees; Education Management industry

January 2012 – Present (1 year 9 months) Pasadena, California

As Executive Director of the Max De Pree Center for Leadership I cultivate the organization's mission (achieving and maintaining clarity of purpose), morale (nurturing generosity in relationships and ensuring accountability for good work), and money (raising funds, ensuring revenue flows, and practicing wise stewardship). As of April 2013 I also edit our online publication, Fieldnotes Magazine (http://www.fieldnotesmagazine.com/).

Senior Fellow

Center for Public Justice

Nonprofit; 1-10 employees; Public Policy industry

July 2011 – Present (2 years 3 months) Pasadena, CA

As Senior Fellow I speak, teach, and write on the biblical theme of justice, and work on strategic leadership development as an advisor and consultant.

Senior Fellow

Cardus

April 2000 – Present (13 years 6 months)

As a senior fellow at Cardus (formerly the Work Research Foundation), I was a participant in several research projects on the integration of faith and work by corporate executives, and co-author of the De Pree Leadership Center monograph To Honor God: Dacor’s pursuit of corporate virtue (2004). My essay “My Africa Problem … and Ours,” which first appeared in the journal The New Pantagruel, was subsequently published in the volume The Best Christian Writing of 2006, edited by John Wilson (Jossey-Bass). In 2007 I gave the Lectures in Moral Formation at Wheaton College, and served as the keynote speaker for the Interdisciplinary Studies conference at the King’s University College in Edmonton, Alberta. In 2008 I gave the Zylstra Lectures at Redeemer University College in Ancaster, Ontario, and was Fall Retreat speaker for the Manna Christian Fellowship of Princeton University.

CEO

Center for Public Justice

Nonprofit; 1-10 employees; Public Policy industry

October 2009 – June 2011 (1 year 9 months)

I extended the influence of the Center for Public Justice (in particular among young adults) through public speaking, the expansion of the Center’s online publication Capital Commentary (quadrupling content and increasing readership more than tenfold in the eight months from October 2010 to May 2011), the introduction of the use of social media, in particular Facebook (building a Facebook community of 1,000+ of whom more than 50% are monthly active users within a period of just more than 12 months) and Twitter, and by making catalytic contributions toward the emergence of local chapters. I served as primary fundraiser, and supervised a small staff.

Editor of Capital Commentary

Center for Public Justice

Nonprofit; 1-10 employees; Public Policy industry

October 2009 – June 2011 (1 year 9 months)

I curated fresh voices and provide them with a media platform in the service of public justice - a vision of political life for the common good. In this capacity I led our small editorial crew in expanding the volume of content of Capital Commentary fourfold, migrating the publication to a distinct and accessible website, and expanding both the contributor base and online readership significantly.

Adjunct faculty member in philosophy/aesthetics

Providence Christian College

Nonprofit; 11-50 employees; Higher Education industry

January 2011 – May 2011 (5 months)

In this position I taught or co-taught courses in "The philosophy and history of beauty and the imagination" and "Visions of Los Angeles: Place, People, and Power in a Global City."

Advice to college students:
Fall in love; ask big questions; read great books; forge deep friendships; be in church.

Editor of Comment magazine

Cardus

July 1999 – September 2010 (11 years 3 months)

In this position I took the magazine from an 8-page quarterly print format to a substantial journal that appears in print quarterly, with a weekly online edition, and grew the readership more than tenfold, to 5,000+ readers. Comment seeks to communicate a Christian worldview and cultural strategy to the next generation of cultural leaders. For my own writing in Comment, see http://www.cardus.ca/contributors/gstrauss/.

Research and Education Director

CLAC

Nonprofit; 51-200 employees; Human Resources industry

July 1999 – September 2009 (10 years 3 months)

During my tenure as director, the research and education department of the Christian Labour Association of Canada developed a comprehensive education program for CLAC staff, including a year-long in-service-training “CLAC College” for new staff (two week-long worldview retreats, a three-day workshop on negotiation skills, and a curriculum of interactive intranet-based supervised personal study), six-monthly two-day national educational retreats with about 100 of CLAC’s 200+ staff at a time, and an annual educational retreat for managers. The department managed or collaborated in the development of workshop-based and video-based educational programmes for CLAC members. The department engaged in a wide array of research projects, incubated innovative new programmes for the organization (including the development of new membership benefit programmes, outreach to non-traditional audiences, the development of database-based tools for staff, and the customization of a new in-house HR system), and produced an extensive series of reseach-based products (databases, publications, reports, advisory briefings). For a period of five years I also managed the head office staff of CLAC.

Adjunct teacher in philosophy

Redeemer University College

Educational Institution; 201-500 employees; Higher Education industry

July 2000 – May 2009 (8 years 11 months)

I taught full-semester courses at Tyndale College, Tyndale Seminary, Trinity Western University, and the Institute for Christian Studies. Most frequently, however, I taught philosophy at Redeemer University College, alternating between an Introduction to Philosophy course in the Fall semester and an upper level course in the Winter semester (variously aesthetics, social philosophy, and modern philosophy).

Board member (volunteer)

International Justice Mission Canada

Nonprofit; 201-500 employees; Nonprofit Organization Management industry

2002 – 2002 (less than a year)

I served on the founding board of IJM Canada, an organization committed to fighting sexual trafficking, bonded labour, and illegal land evictions.

Senior Researcher

Language Facilitation Programme, University of the Freestate

March 1995 – December 1997 (2 years 10 months)

I did research on the economics of language in support of language policy work in support of the new democratic constitutional order in South Africa, providing advice towards legislation, regulations, and service provision on the national and provincial level, and contributing to international academic conferences in the USA, Denmark, and the Netherlands. During this time I also provided simultaneous interpretation services to the provincial government in Bloemfontein, South Africa and the Truth and Reconciliation Commission (which investigating gross human rights violations) chaired by Archbishop Desmond Tutu - a formative even though traumatizing experience.

Community service

South African government

May 1986 – February 1990 (3 years 10 months)

I served just more than three years of a six year community service assignment in the Department of Labour, as a conscientious objector to military service under the apartheid government. I studied full-time at night school at the same time.

Gideon Strauss' Education

Regent College

Christian spirituality

1998 – 1999

Regent College was a time of sabbatical refreshing and personal development. I focused on learning to pray and read the Bible, tried to learn as much as I could from JI Packer and Eugene Peterson, and worked as a teaching assistant to Craig Gay. Our family loved Vancouver.

University of the Free State/Universiteit van die Vrystaat

PhD, Philosophy and ethics

1985 – 1995

I studied philosophy with good teachers, and received my BA, BA (Hons.), MA and PhD from this university, receiving the Dean's Medal and an award from the South African Association for the Advancement of the Sciences for my master's thesis. My mentor, Kobus Smit, introduced me to aesthetics, trained me in ethics, and supported my friends and me as we tried to come to grips with our political responsibilities against apartheid. There was much wrong with the university during the time I studied at it, most significantly the fact that it was an apartheid institution that excluded students on the basis of their race.

Jim Fouche Secondary School

Matriculated

1980 – 1984

Jim Fouche is an academically oriented public school in my birth town of Bloemfontein, South Africa. During the apartheid years it was complicit in the policies of racial segregation enforced by the then government.
