Greg Carley's Honors and Awards

  • AT&T Sneak Peek

    W3 Award – Gold for Best-in-Class (Telecommunication)
    • 2008

    A 'Movie Trailer' like experience to introduce site relaunch of ATT.com. The consumer face and structure of ATT.com was about to drastically change. While the changes were beneficial, there was potential for customer confusion upon initial release. Some of the significant changes included AT&T changing its primary brand color from blue to orange, many information structures were consolidating into one, and the site was shifting from being product-focused to being task-based. We saw this as the perfect opportunity to communicate to customers in a fun, engaging way what was in store for them, while mitigating any potential customer disconnect. SEE: http://boldshift.com/att-sneak-peek

  • AT&T Sneak Peek

    W3 Award – Silver (2) for Visual Appeal and Rich Media
    • 2008

    We created a movie intro and microsite that would announce the reinvention of ATT.com in an entertaining way, while at the same time, explain why the new changes are good for the customer. We decided the piece should be something the user could initially just sit back and watch, while reinforcing our customer-focused message, “We are reinventing ATT.com to be better for you.” In addition, it also visually showed how the new task-based navigation model makes shopping, getting support and exploring AT&T easier than ever. So, making a movie trailer, using motion, music, and telling a story seemed like a great way to engage the customer. The ending redirected users to a microsite with more details. SEE: http://boldshift.com/att-sneak-peek

  • Razorfish Website Redesign

    W3 Award – Gold for Self-Promotion and a People's Voice WEBBY
    • 2009

    The Razorfish site reflects its image as a pioneer in digital brand management; embodying its four core principles (experimental, simple, global and social), it’s a fitting showcase for the grown-up yet playful agency it has become. As visitors navigate a content-rich portfolio, ideas and offerings, full-screen video feeds offer sneak peeks inside twenty worldwide offices while subtle sound design, vivid color cues and decidedly human copy add depth to the experience. Despite an ambitious amount of content, the user experience is immersive, interactive and, with an intuitive navigation and a shallow hierarchy, easy to use. SEE http://www.razorfish.com
