Founder and Owner

The Irish Image Collection

July 2002 – February 2007 (4 years 8 months)

With increased use of the Internet, the stock image industry has changed radically and offered the opportunity to relocate to peaceful and lovely County Waterford where I established the Irish Image Collection. It was as its name implies, a specialised stock photography agency, internet based, and marketing a collection of photographs and illustrations covering all aspects of the island of Ireland from 1900 to the start of the new millennium.

Apart from administering the company, I also created its website and edited all incoming images. However, after three years I felt that the outlook of the industry had shifted from one of regarding photography as an art form to mere product and after some considerable soul-searching I made a decision to sell the company in 2007...

Co-Director and Co-Owner

Slidefile Stock Photography Agency

April 1979 – July 2002 (23 years 4 months)

Slidefile started life as commercial and advertising photographic business, working with major Irish clients, advertising agencies and design houses. The stock agency started - almost as an afterthought - from very humble beginnings, and co-existed with the assignment work.

Following the introduction of stock catalogues and high quality imagery, the company grew rapidly to become the major stock image agency in Ireland; with the phenomenal rise in business and turnover, we decided to concentrate on the stock agency side of our business. Over the next few years, the company grew to a quarter of a million images, a staff of seven, fifty overseas agencies and hundreds of Irish and international clients.

Apart from management responsibilities, my specific areas of interest included liaison with overseas agencies, along with the role of picture editor. A key factor in the businesses success - an agency is only as good as the images that it markets!

But all good things come to an end, and in 2002 Jaimie Blandford, my co-owner and I decided, very amicably, to follow our own interests.

Chief Photographer

the Kidderminster Shuttle Newspaper

November 1975 – March 1979 (3 years 5 months)

Chief photographer on a reasonably large provincial newspaper in the UK. Photography included news stories as they occurred, local features and some social photography.

George Munday's Summary

A passion for photography remains undiminished after almost 40 years shooting in a wide range of disciplines. It's been a photographic life of enjoyable challenges since I graduated from the Birmingham College of Photography in 1975...

After four years as chief photographer for the Kidderminster Shuttle Newspaper; ten years as a joint partner in a photographic studio shooting advertising and corporate photography; another ten establishing and growing a stock photo agency called Slidefile; four years running The Irish Image Collection and and another five teaching digital photography at Copper Coast Workshops, I've partly retired. Whew!

I still shoot landscape, and portraits in a reportage style for selected clients and I shoot for the sheer enjoyment it, my personal work can be seen in my blog - Renaissance -
http://www.georgemunday.com/renaissance/

And in my Behance portfolio at http://www.behance.net/search?search=George+Munday
