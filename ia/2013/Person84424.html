Martin Alaimo's Summary

I'm a Certified Scrum Coach (CSC) & Certified Scrum Trainer (CST) with more than 15 years of experience in software delivery projects. I deliver Agile+Scrum trainings -both from a management & leadership perspective and from an engineering & software development perspective- almost every month. In my daily work I also spend several hours inside different companies, coaching them in Scrum adoption.

I started my career within the software development industry more than 15 years ago as a Software Developer always focused in web implementations and then evolved into project manager for Java/.NET/RoR & SAP technologies, with several years of experience leading software project in international organizations.

I studied IT/System Analyst and speciallized in Project Management. I'm a certified PMP (PMI) but strongly focused in agile methodologies. I now help Organisations and Individuals achieve their goals through agile methodologies.

I’m also an airplane private pilot, thus I spend my free time flying Cessna like airplanes. I also like photography and I participate in photography contests every time I get the chance to. :)

Specialties

Expertise leading teams in a wide range of business and industrial applications. Strong orientation to project results, delivery and technology platform.

Martin Alaimo's Publications

  • Trust

    • Scrum Allaince, inc.
    • January 14, 2013
    Authors: Martin Alaimo

    I often encounter mature Agile teams, well trained in Scrum, some of them doing Scrum of Scrums and having already participated in several releases. These teams' retrospectives frequently bring in opportunities for improvement in a number of areas connected to processes, techniques, and, occasionally, impediments at the organizational level. This is very good, but I wonder why personal issues are rarely discussed in retrospectives. This I have learned personally: Teams that do not discuss personal matters openly pay the price of missing considerable opportunities for improving an already good team performance.
