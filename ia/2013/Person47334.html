Gavin Chait's Summary

I am currently Head of Services at the Open Knowledge Foundation, a non-profit organisation dedicated to promoting the creation, sharing and application of Open Knowledge in the Digital Age.

I am also a data engineer and development economist at Whythawk, which I founded in 1998. Whythawk focuses on decision support, economic modelling, market insight and data analysis.

My career started in South Africa where I created Business Beat, a student- and professionally-run development organisation aimed at micro-enterprise and small business development. This was jointly sponsored by Deloitte and the University of Cape Town.

I gradually became more interested in the analysis and presentation of data, migrating into corporate consulting in 2007. From 2009 I began teaching myself programming to automate and enhance the routine tasks of risk analysis and presentation my clients were demanding.

My experience in journalism and media includes interviews with leading thinkers such as CK Prahalad of "Fortune at the Bottom of the Pyramid", Jimmy Wales of Wikipedia, Nobel Prize-winner Dr David Baltimore of the California Institute of Technology and Sir Chris Bonington, one of the world’s most accomplished climbers.

In January 2006 I was contracted by Independent Newspapers South Africa to develop their business news desk for the Cape Argus, their Cape Town newspaper. I produced their business news for a year before becoming a bi-monthly columnist. I have worked in radio and contributed to a number of television stations, including appearing as a business pundit on CNBC Africa.

I also spent six years at the University of Cape Town teaching a Freakonomics-style economics course to undergraduates.

I have spent more than a decade researching and presenting ideas in business and economic development, and in teaching entrepreneurs and consultants.
