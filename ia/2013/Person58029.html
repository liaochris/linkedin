Jamieson Becker's Summary

On behalf of LabArts, HP, CSC, Compaq, DEC, and IBM, Jamieson has created solutions including enterprise Internet applications, Open Systems Stacks, UNIX & Linux Common Operating Environment standards, LAMP stacks, Linux desktop platforms, open systems security standards, and enterprise messaging frameworks for some of the world's biggest companies, such as Ford, GM, T-Mobile, Cargill, Eli Lilly, WalMart, Disney, United Technology Corporation (Carrier Air Conditioning, Sikorsky Helicopter, Otis Elevator), Deutsche Bank, HP, RJ Reynolds, Hertz, Sprint, Compaq, and Bank of America, as well as international firms such as ABN AMRO, Clalbit (Tel Aviv), HP Globalsoft (India), Saudi Aramco, and Roche.

Jamieson remains an active member of the open source community, speaking and contributing to industry journals and open source projects. His first Linux book came out in 1999 and is currently finishing "The Amazon Cloud for the Enterprise." He has traveled throughout the United States, Europe, Asia, and the Middle East in support of enterprises pursuing Linux migrations and Secure Internet Architecture.

Jamieson led technical due diligence teams for HP Mergers & Acquisitions, as well as leading the HP technical consulting teams for major projects such as the secure worldwide Linux COE for British Petroleum, standardized LAMP stack and Kickstart web-based toolkit for Merrill Lynch, custom deployment framework for Time-Warner's worldwide cable monitoring system, multiple projects for Wal-mart on more than 10,000 servers worldwide, Oracle RAC deployment for ABN AMRO, and a 30,000 seat thin-client deployment for Ford.

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Internet industry

January 2013 – Present (8 months) Houston, TX

Userify is a cloud security startup that provides secure administrative user administration for cloud instances and servers. It's the first-ever completely decentralized solution and does not rely on an authentication server that can be attacked or taken offline, preventing your administrators from accessing critical servers. Userify deploys SSH cryptographic keys as well as configuring sudo roles via a zero-weight agent automatically deployed via your AMI, userdata, or wget magic.
