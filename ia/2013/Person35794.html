Dirk Hayhurst's Projects

  • Out Of My League

    • May 2010 to August 2011
    Team Members: Dirk Hayhurst

    Out Of My League was meant to be an inside look at the climb from the minors to the majors from the player's perspective, and the growth a person goes through as they are stretched and challenged by the sacrifices they must make to achieve their dream. That's the inner content at least.

    On the outside, getting my book the the status of New York Times Best Seller required a massive amount of networking, coordinating, and publicity efforts. There were hundreds of interviews, for radio, web, and television. Thousands of emails, and countless follow ups.

    The success of my first book was made easier due to my active status on a major league roster. For this book, its success was more organic. Beyond the personal discipline aspects, It taught me a lot about social media and radio marketing, as well as television, public speaking, and sales.

    I also grew substantially as a writer, and proved to the literary world that I was not a fluke, but had real—and improving—talent.

  • The Bullpen Gospels

    • October 2008 to October 2009
    Team Members: Dirk Hayhurst

    My first book. This was about grinding out a life in the minors as an older player who had made the mistake of letting baseball results define his value as a person. It's a coming of age story, intensely funny and dramatically emotional. It was a massive success, reaching the top 10 in book sales on Amazon and debuting in the top 25 on The New York Times Best Sellers list.

    This was my first foray into the literary world. I had never written anything this long before, nor I was formally trained in book writing in college. I did it by sure force of will, hammering out pages in the down time I had around a full baseball season.

    But creating a book is only half the battle. Marketing it is the other, more intense issue. There are many great books out there that don't sell because they have no market clout. In order to push mine, made some major contacts during the creation of The Bullpen Gospels: Keith Olbermann, Bob Costas, Tim Kirkjian, just to name a few. Furthermore, I earned their respect since very few players author their own books from inside the game— books that are honest, poignet, and insightful and not diatribes, exposes, or self glorifying drivel.

    The Bullpen Gospels is a testament to intrepidness. I'm extremely proud of it as I think it represents my ability to jump into the unknown and come out on top.
