  • Mediating the Insurance Coverage and Bad Faith Case

    • Law360
    • April 2013
    Authors: Bruce Friedman

    In general, I believe that the approach to mediation of an insurance coverage and bad faith case is similar to mediations in other substantive areas of law. There are a few differences, which focus on the analysis of the insurance policy, the cases construing the policy, and the rules applicable to the interpretation of insurance policies. While insurance policies come with a body of law interpreting what they mean and a set of court- established rules of construction that are unique to insurance contracts, insurance policies are, without question, the most difficult and least understood contracts in general use.
