J.F. (Jeff) Taylor, Jr.'s Summary

I’m J.F. Taylor, Jr. My friends call me Jeff. I am the Director of Economic Development in Appomattox County. You've been there, right? No?!? Well, it's a very important place. The course of American history changed there in April 1865. Come visit Appomattox and the Appomattox Court House National Historical Park and just maybe we'll have lunch together. Also save the date - April 2015 - the Sesquicentennial Anniversary of the Surrender at Appomattox Courthouse. Sesquicentennial means 150th. It's a major anniversary in our local, state and American history.

I’m a real Optimist who believes life was much easier when it was just about Dick, Jane and Spot. I’m a tech savvy guy with many years of experience in broadcasting, marketing and public relations, communications, website content management and social media. I’m a former TV news director, news anchor and reporter. I’ve been a narrator for many projects, most recently the 2013 Blue Ridge Public Television special on the 200th anniversary of the Virginia Governor's Mansion - "First House: Two Centuries with Virginia’s First Families." I'm also the public address announcer for Liberty University Flames football and basketball.

In the early 1990s I was the first full-time employee for Virginia's Region 2000, what was then a regional public/private economic development partnership serving the best 2,000 square miles of Virginia in so many other ways.

For the last several years I have been the Moderator/Host of “Shaping the World: Conversations on Democracy” Series Public Television Program and Live Performance for The Corporation for Jefferson's Poplar Forest. I’m a James River Batteau Festival & Colonial Era reenactor. And I’ve done a little acting recently in the independent film "So Falls the Shadow", also as an extra in "A Little Closer" and on projects for the CW Network and the Discovery Investigations channel. I've also been in some friends' film shorts such as "Lunch With A Prophet".
