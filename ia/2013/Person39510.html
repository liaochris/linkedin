Ed Fullman's Summary

My experience includes: strategy, leadership, marketing, sales, technology development and operation, customer service operations, business transformation, corporate development, as well as analytics.

During my twenties, I developed my experience as a technologist and leader in business solutions. In my thirties I joined my first startup, built a large company, and a successful IPO. Co-Founder & CEO

Reunify

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Market Research industry

November 2009 – December 2012 (3 years 2 months) Los Angeles, CA

Reunify simplified the process of leveraging big-data to transform marketing and CRM operations.

Marketers provided Reunify with a small sample of consumers or customers that had previously activated on a marketing strategy, and sometimes an entire CRM file. Reunify responded with an in-depth psychographic segmentation, followed by lookalike segments derived from its internal house file of 220M US adults.

Reunify's deliverables were used to drive messaging, audience targeting, media buys, and attribution for direct response, and loyalty.

At the foundation of Reunify's technology is a unique data asset. Reunify's house file was aggregated from a variety of source and represented 220M US adults at multiple addresses, email, and phone numbers, designed for PII matching versus contact. Reunify enhanced the house file by modeling a psychographic profile for each consumer using the respondent-level data from Experian/Simmons. Reunify distilled terabytes of social media data and used the information to influence psychographic parameters at the individual consumer level.

Reunify was a startup, as such, my role as CEO included leading all major functions of the company from corporate development, to product, offering, sales, and client data and digital strategy. Our team included a small business group, and a contingent of engineering and data scientists.

In December 2011, I achieved a successful exit for Reunify, and left the company in December 2012.

  • A Cookie-less World may be on the Way

    • The Pin Factory
    • April 16, 2013
    Authors: Ed Fullman

    In a classic example of what Adam Smith predicted would be a free-market correcting itself, the heightened concerns that online tracking cookies violate consumer privacy has resulted in consumer demand for a solution, and that demand must be satisfied.
