Prof. Dr. G. Keith Still's Summary

Keith has over 25 years of experience in modelling crowds and crowd behaviours for major events Projects include the Royal Wedding (UK 2011), Manchester United and Manchester City Victory Parades (UK 2011), Westfield (UK 2012), Olympic Park (Sydney 2000 and UK 2012), Al Haram (Saudi Arabia 2000 - 2012), Al Jamarat (Saudi Arabia 2000 - 2005), Texas, USA (SXSW/Austin 6th Street) 2013, Kendal Torchlight Carnival, UK (2013).

He has developed several leading crowd simulations systems (including the original Legion Crowd Modelling software and the Paramics UAF interface, Myriad and the 3D RTDS tools).

Specialties:

Expert Witness (crowd related risks and crowd safety)
Teaching, Training and Education programmes (eLearning systems)
Crowd Modelling Software (Design, Development and Support)
Event Control Room Systems (Development and deployment of real-time decision support system)

Prof. Dr. G. Keith Still's Experience

G4S Professor of Crowd Science

G4S

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; GFS; Security and Investigations industry

September 2010 – Present (3 years 1 month) North West England

Keith is the G4S Professor of Crowd Science and is a Fellow of the Institute of Mathematics and its Applications (IMA) and the Institute of Civil Protection and Emergency Management (ICPEM) he supports G4S on a wide range of client projects.

Since 1999 Prof. Still has lectured at the UK Cabinet Office Emergency Planning College and developed the Crowd Dynamics and Applied Crowd Dynamics workshops as well as contributing to several other safety-related courses at the EPC.

Keith currently teaches the principles and applications of crowd risk analysis and safety engineering during normal and emergency situations around the world, both at clients sites and via eLearning systems.

His mathematical and behavioural modelling and simulation tools led to the development of a systematic blueprint for crowd safety for major events.

Director

Crowd Modelling Ltd

January 2000 – Present (13 years 9 months) Cumbria, UK

Crowd Modelling Ltd is a non-trading company that holds the IPR for Prof. Still's mathematical and crowd research activities. Specifically the XenoFractals (see www.XenoFractals.com) and core modelling techniques for complex systems behaviour and analysis.

Mathematical Modelling

Starlab

2000 – 2001 (1 year)

Modelling crowd behaviour in the complex and built environment. Focus on financial modelling, human perceptions to their environment.
