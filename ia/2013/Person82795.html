Lou Schopfer's Summary

In January 1999, Lou served as Chair of World Research Group’s Las Vegas conference entitled “Best Practices in Government Procurement: Streamlining Purchasing through Information Technology and Process Reengineering.” In May 1999, in Washington, D.C., Lou participated on a panel for the National Association of State Auditors, Comptrollers and Treasurers’ (NASACT) “Thirteenth Annual Cash Management and Electronic Commerce Conference.” In May 2000, he served as Co-Chair of a joint statewide symposium in Princeton entitled "Public Service in the New Millennium."

Lou served in the elected position of the Trenton Council for the American Society for Public Administration (ASPA) and has served as a member of the Association of Government Accountants (AGA), and the New Jersey Association of Criminal Justice Educators (NJACJE). He is a former member of the American Academy of Certified Public Managers and was elected as a Trustee in the Certified Public Managers Society of New Jersey, Inc. (CPMSNJ). Lou is listed in the International Who’s Who of Professionals, 1999 Edition.

Specialties: Lou holds a Master’s in Public Policy and a Bachelor’s in Political Science from Rutgers University in Camden and served as Founding President of the Public Administration Alumni Chapter (PAAC) and served as Chair on the Dean’s Advisory Council. While at Rutgers, he was inducted into the Athenaeum and Pi Sigma Alpha academic fraternities. He is also a member of the New Jersey Masonic Lodge and the Scottish Rite.

Interests:

Lou served as an elected Deacon/Treasurer at Kingsway Church (www.KingswayChurch.tv) where he attends with his family. He enjoys swimming, cats, music and Ocean City, New Jersey.

Groups and Associations:

Lou is an Executive Assistant 1 in the Office of Fiscal Accountability and Compliance within the Department of Education where he works at “Keeping Our Kids Safe.” Prior to this appointment, he served in the Office of the State Treasurer, Department of the Treasury, where he managed business re-engineering and efficiency initiatives for the Administration. Prior to his appointment, he was Director of Finance and Administration for New Jersey Network (NJN) and Assistant Treasurer and CFO for the NJN Foundation, the State’s public radio and television provider. Lou is a former employee of the Office of Management and Budget (OMB), as well as several other State agencies.

Honors and Awards:

On Tuesday, June 18, 1996, Louis A. Schopfer, Jr. (because of his volunteer work in the community) carried the Olympic Torch from Trenton, New Jersey across the “Trenton Makes” bridge into Morrisville, Pennsylvania.
