Laura Al-Amery's Experience

Real Estate Mentor, Coach and Investor

Laura Al-Amery LLC

July 1987 – Present (26 years 6 months)

I started my experience in real estate while I lived in Honolulu, Hawaii in 1987. I was a college student at Hawaii Pacific University and I became a real estate agent in 1987. I joined as an agent Dolman and Associates in Honolulu.

I graduated in 1991 with a Bachelor of Science in Business Administration and moved to Missouri. In Missouri I started looking at real estate investing in multi-family buildings and purchased several REO. I received my real estate Broker license in Missouri in 1993.

While I was working in real estate, I pursued a Master in Business Administration in Finance, and I started working as an assistant controller in metal commodities. By 1996 I was making more money in real estate part time than my full time corporate job, so I decided to devote myself full time to real estate.

I started my own brokerage company Midwest Realty Corp. and my core in real estate was flipping properties. I also had a property management division for my clients that purchased properties from me. In 2001 I started a real estate investment club, with a group of cash investors, where we would buy properties, mainly foreclosures on the court house steps and REO, rehab them and then sell them. We quickly became very active in this sector in St Louis, with an average turnaround of 40 buildings per year.

From 1997 I started sharing my knowledge of real estate investing, using creative financing techniques, by hosting real estate seminars around St Louis.

With the widespread use of the Internet, I decided to share my practical knowledge and experience in real estate via online mentorship. In 23 years in this business I have acquired a vast and versatile knowledge of the different real estate market trends and techniques and I also partner with other investors, whose areas of expertise vary from long term real estate investment holdings to hard money lending.
