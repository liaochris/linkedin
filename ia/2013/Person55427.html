Inna Volynskaya's Projects

  • 18 Reasons Marketing Development

    • January 2012 to May 2012
    Team Members: Inna Volynskaya, Judy Prejean, Jeff Rangel, Eric Cetnarski, Ryn Longmaid

    Our team developed a marketing plan to

    1. Increase paid attendance at events to maintain a consistent revenue stream
    2. Increase awareness of 18 Reasons' offerings beyond the Mission-Dolores area

    The marketing plan addresses four key strategies to support 18 Reasons’ current objectives
    while remaining aligned with their mission:

    1. Sharpen the brand Mission, Vision & Values through a refined web site and message testing;
    2. Expand marketing reach beyond immediate neighborhood through social media and events;
    3. Establish data collection and reporting practices to evaluate effectiveness of marketing;
    4. Increase participation and ‘stickiness’ through ambassador and loyalty programs.

  • One Percent for the Planet Business Strategy

    • August 2011 to December 2011
    Team Members: Inna Volynskaya, Joey Christiano, Aaron Israel, Gregory Kandankulam, Carrie Staller

    Our team took a deep dive into 1% for the Planet's current and future strategy. The study was conducted in partnership with 1%’s vice president of organizational development, Rebecca Callahan Klein, and examined the following questions:

    How can 1% improve its business model to earn more revenue and retain more members? What can 1% improve the quality, value and effectiveness of its work? What is its impact as an organization and the impact of its members’ philanthropy? How can 1% leverage corporate giving to advance sustainability work? What does 1% do to encourage this kind of philanthropy? What value does 1% provide its business members and NGO partners? What are the existing and emerging needs of both the corporate partners and the NGOs? How can 1% become financially self-sufficient on membership dues and earned income and free of grant funding?

  • UCSF Telemedicine Financial Evaluation

    • August 2011 to December 2011
    Team Members: Inna Volynskaya, Joey Christiano, Carrie Staller, Gregory Kandankulam, Eric Cetnarski

    The Presidio Graduate School team worked with the University of California, San Francisco (UCSF) to calculate the financial, social and environmental costs and benefits of the UCSF 360-Positive Care Center Urban HIV Telemedicine program. The telemedicine program provides pharmacy and nutrition consultations via telemedicine to HIV-positive patients at clinics run by the San Francisco Department of Public Health.
    The Presidio team analyzed scenarios with and without telemedicine to calculate the cost and benefit of each.
