Mackenzie Wesson's Projects

  • Project SEED

    • January 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Mackenzie Wesson, Ana Isabel Bermudez, Daanish Nasir

    Dr. Su Yeong Kim, an associate professor in the Human Development and Family Science department at The University of Texas at Austin, is conducting a longitudinal study on Mexican American children and families in the Austin area. Our participants include middle school children who translate for their parents on a regular basis. These children are labeled "language brokers" because they provide language translation and language interpretation for the survival of their families. Our goal is to determine how language brokering affects their development emotionally, socially, and educationally over time. After recruiting the Mexican American families through middle schools and organizations, our Spanish-speaking research assistants conduct interviews and surveys with the child, mother, and father at their homes. The participants are monetarily compensated for their time and sincerely appreciated for the participation in our study. The implementations of this project may include future changes to our public education system and even legislation.
