Alexandra Lajoux's Summary

Senior-level writer, editor, and researcher with broad knowledge of business, including accounting, finance, governance, law, and management. Involved in communication, education, and relationship-building. On-the-job experience as educator and corporate secretary. Author/coauthor of Art of M&A series for McGraw-Hill and coauthor of a book on securities valuation for Bloomberg/Wiley. Advanced degrees from Loyola University of Maryland (Sellinger School) and Princeton University. Broad interests in humanities and sciences. Multilingual. Involved in performing arts. Supportive of veterans' causes. Active in local program for youths in foster care. Certified catechist. Goals: to continue learning and serving in a setting that values education and welcomes diversity.

Specialties include:
Business: governance, finance, HR, M&A.
Arts/Education: music (voting member of The Recording Academy); drama; comparative and creative literature; special education; religion (certified volunteer catechist for 7th and 8th grade).
Technology: audio engineering (hold certificate for 30-hours - a modest beginning)
Languages: English, French, German, Spanish; some rusty Italian, nascent Portuguese, and ancient Latin. Have attempted to learn ancient Greek as well as Japanese and Mandarin. So much to learn, so little time!

Alexandra Lajoux's Publications

  • Corporate Valuation for Portfolio Investment: Analyzing Assets, Earnings, Cash Flow, Stock Price, Governance, and Special Situations

    • Bloomberg/John Wiley & Sons, Inc.
    • December 1, 2011
    Authors: Alexandra Lajoux, Robert A. G. Monks

    We wrote this book to advance world prosperity by explaining how to determine the value of corporate equity for the purpose of portfolio investment. Equity capital provides two main benefits: a flexible funding option for companies and superior returns to investors compared to debt. To take advantage of equity's power, investors must learn how to put a precise value on a specific stock for a specific investment period. This book advocates a multidimensional approach to determining equity value.

  • The Art of M&A Due Diligence: Navigating Critical Steps and Uncovering Crucial Data

    • McGraw-Hill
    • October 1, 2010
    Authors: Alexandra Lajoux, Charles Elson

    The basic function of M&A due diligence is to assess the benefits and liabilities of a proposed acquisition by inquiring into all relevant aspects of the past, present, and predictable future of the business to be purchased. Yet given the complexity of companies and of the world, an acquirer cannot -- and should not be expected to, -- discover every possible exposure to risk. The effort required to do so could bankrupt the acquirer and alienate the seller. In this book we hope to help acquirers and sellers exercise the diligence that is rightly "due" in their situations.

  • The Art of M&A Integration: A Guide to Merging Resources, Processes, and Responsibilities

    • McGraw-Hill
    • February 1, 2006
    Authors: Alexandra Lajoux

    Every year thousands of companies join forces hoping to accomplish together what they could not do alone. But will they achieve their goals? The answer depends in part on whether and how they integrate. The path to integration is set out like a decision tree, with many possible options for separate vs. combined existence. This book--drawing wisdom from a variety of experts--takes a high-level view of all the company aspects that need to be combined, and offers tools to accomplish this.
