Dr. Jeffrey David Zacko-Smith's Summary

Skilled teacher, facilitator, project manager, grant-writer, researcher and entrepreneur; I'm currently working in areas that represent my passion, experience and expertise and am always open to new opportunities and collaborations with others.

I DEVELOP LEADERS! I find value in all models of leadership, but am specifically interested in Constructionist, Authentic, Transcendent, Diversity and Creative Leadership. I want to assist others in developing awareness of self and showing them how to mentor others in this way; self-awareness has a direct impact on practice. I prefer blending skill-building, epistemology, reflection/visioning, and philosophy in my work, facilitating processes of self-discovery; promoting awareness through story and skill acquisition through service-learning.

I FACILITATE DIVERSITY! As a trainer, facilitator and speaker with expertise in multicultural and diversity education, I work with the National Coalition Building Institute (NCBI), an international organization that helps to eliminate prejudice, expose oppression and build community coalitions focused on social change. I'm passionate about issues of identity, labeling and personhood and am skilled at integrating diversity principles into curricula and classrooms; I'm educated in diversity pedagogy.

I WORSHIP WORDS! I have multiple publications to my name, including peer-reviewed journal articles, a book and 2 book chapters; I served as an editor for publishers, publications and organizations, including Sage, Norton, Emerald, Academic Exchange Quarterly and the International Leadership Association (ILA). I am also the founder of a successful online editing service (see below).

Specialties: Leadership Development; Diversity Education; Copyediting/Proofreading; Creative Thinking; Creative/Change Leadership; Career Mentoring.

Dr. Jeffrey David Zacko-Smith's Volunteer Experience & Causes

  • Volunteer Experience

    • Trainer & Facilitator

      National Coalition Building Institute International
      • Civil Rights and Social Action
      May 2011 – present (2 years 5 months)

      The National Coalition Building Institute is an international, non-profit, leadership training organization based in Washington, DC, USA. Since 1984, NCBI has worked to eliminate racism and all other forms of prejudice and discrimination throughout the world. NCBI takes a proactive approach that begins with one or more people from a variety of organizational or community settings, including schools, colleges and universities, corporations, foundations, correctional facilities, law enforcement agencies, government offices, and labor unions.

      These individuals are taught effective leadership skills in the areas of prejudice reduction, violence prevention, conflict resolution, and coalition building. NCBI programs enable leaders to take principled and courageous stands, enter the heat of emotionally-charged conflict to build bridges, and act as stalwart allies for all groups.

      When a handful of like-minded leaders from an organization or community has been trained, a local NCBI resource team is formed. These teams offer prevention-oriented strategies and programs to deal with discrimination and other inter-group tensions. They are also available to intervene when specific inter-group conflicts arise. Over the past 27 years, NCBI has developed an international network of resource teams that has grown in influence and reach. NCBI currently is represented by over 100 teams in cities, on campuses, and in organizations.

  • Social Reconstruction and Education

    • Encyclopedia of Diversity and Education, Sage Publications
    • May 2012
    Authors: Dr. Jeffrey David Zacko-Smith

    Social reconstruction is a philosophy focused on achieving social change. As a practice, it strives to achieve social justice and equity by altering the various social systems upon which society rests. It is based upon two major understandings; first, that society tends to develop systems that marginalize and oppress others and thus need to change, and second, that achieving this change requires both creating a system that serves as a change agent and is open to changing it’s own purposes and structures as the social contexts in which it exists naturally evolve.

  • The Three T's Framework for Leadership Education

    • Academic Exchange Quarterly
    • July 2010
    Authors: Dr. Jeffrey David Zacko-Smith

    The Three-T’s Framework provides leadership educators, and others interested in the subject, with an easy way to help organize and understand leadership theory, while placing it in context, and adding to knowledge about the history of the field.

  • Recognizing and Utilizing Queer Pedagogy: A Call for Teacher Education To Reconsider the Knowledge Base on Sexual Orientation for Teacher Education Programs

    • Multicultural Education - Caddo Gap Press

    Authors: Dr. Jeffrey David Zacko-Smith, G. Pritchy Smith

    This article explores how the integration of Queer Theory into the knowledge base on sexual orientation for educators (based on the work of G. Pritchy Smith at the University of North Florida) can help achieve social justice in our schools, and, thus, our society.

  • Creative Leadership: Welcome to the 21st Century

    • Academic Exchange Quarterly (Winter 2010)
    • December 2010
    Authors: Dr. Jeffrey David Zacko-Smith, Gerard Puccio, Marie Mance

    In an increasingly change-driven world, leaders are called upon to evolve their mindsets and skill-sets to reflect and serve the demands of the new millennium. A novel leadership concept, creative leadership, is introduced and defined and then placed in context within the history of the field. Additionally, skills and behaviors related to leading creatively, which are immediately applicable to leadership practice, are outlined. Creative thinking and problem solving are shown to be indispensible to leaders who want to thrive in times of complexity and change.

  • The Leader Label

    • VDM Verlag
    • 2009
    Authors: Dr. Jeffrey David Zacko-Smith

    Examining leadership through the lens of Social Constructionism, a framework that provides the flexibility necessary to ensure that leadership is available to everyone, in all contexts; essentialist understandings (such as trait theory) are shown as outdated and ill-equipped to be workable in diverse environments.

  • The Leader Label; Influencing Perceptions, Reality and Practice

    • Leadership Review
    • July 2007
    Authors: Dr. Jeffrey David Zacko-Smith

    This research challenges conventional leadership metaphors, reframes the construct of "leader" as available to everyone, and allows us to re-shape our individual and collective leadership stories.

  • The Leader Label: Using Social Constructionism and Metaphor to Influence the Leadership Perceptions of Graduate Business and Public Adminstration Students

    • Taos Institute
    • December 2007
    Authors: Dr. Jeffrey David Zacko-Smith

    This research examined the use of metaphor as a tool of discourse, applying it specifically to the field of leadership. Utilizing a post-modern social constructionist framework under which the construct “leader” was highly pliable, and was created, enhanced, mitigated or destroyed via language and interaction, this study investigated whether and in what ways the intentional use of metaphor altered the individual leadership perceptions of graduate business and public administration students.
