Clara Villarosa's Summary

Villarosa is nationally known as founder of two influential bookstores focused on the African American market: the Hue-Man Experience, established in Denver in 1984, and the Hue-Man Bookstore and Café which opened in Harlem in 2002.

Not least among its visitors was former president Clinton who chose Hue-Man as one of only two stores to host an in-store book signing on the release day his bestselling memoir, “My Life” . Seen by the publisher as one of the author’s most successful market outings, the event attracted local, national and international media including CNN, Access Hollywood and Entertainment Tonight. .

Preceding the former president’s in-store event by one year was the book signing by New York’s junior senator, Hillary Rodham-Clinton. The former First Lady spent nearly two hours at the store in 2003 signing over 750 copies of her bestseller, “Living History.”

Villarosa’s success attracting celebrity authors to the Harlem store reprised her earlier accomplishments at the Hue-Man Experience in Denver. There, first-time author, General Colin Powell signed more than 650 books during the national tour for his bestseller, “My American Journey.” And authors from James Baldwin to Alice Walker, Terry McMillan to Walter Moseley, and Maya Angelou to Bebe Moore Campbell made Hue-Man their Rocky Mountain home.

Retiring in 2004 as Managing Partner of the Harlem bookstore, Villarosa is currently a business coach who offers workshops on “The First Ten Steps to Entrepreneurship.” The 90-minute, hands-on sessions based on her published book.

The workshop has been conducted for banks JP Morgan Chase, Commerce TD, Washington Mutual, HSBC, Wachovia and Carver Federal Savings.

With clients that include realtors, restaurateurs, retailers, developers, a veterinary hospital, an art gallery and a media production company, she works in one-on-one consultation with small businesses to help plan their strategy and growth.
