Rosemarie Rossetti, Ph.D.'s Publications

  • Universal Design Handbook, Second Edition

    • McGraw-Hill Professional
    • September 17, 2010
    Authors: Rosemarie Rossetti, Ph.D., Wolfgang Preiser, Korydon Smith

    I wrote the last chapter, “My Story.” This serves as the Epilogue of this book and gives details the story of the Universal Design Living Laboratory design process. This national demonstration home and garden is in Columbus, OH and showcases universal design, green building and healthy home construction practices.

    This 496 page book covers the full scope of universal design, discussing how to develop media, products, buildings, and infrastructure for the widest range of human needs, preferences, and functioning. This pioneering work brings together a rich variety of expertise from around the world to discuss the extraordinary growth and changes in the universal design movement.

    The book provides an overview of universal design premises and perspectives, and performance-based design criteria and guidelines. Public and private spaces, products, and technologies are covered, and current and emerging research and teaching are explored. This unique resource includes analyses of historical and contemporary universal design issues from seven different countries, as well as a look at future trends. Students, advocates, policy makers, and design practitioners will get a theoretical grounding in and practical reference on the physical and social roles of design from this definitive volume.

  • Take Back Your LIfe!

    • Fortuna Press
    • August 2003
    Authors: Rosemarie Rossetti, Ph.D.

    Having the courage to face life after you’ve been thrown a curve ball is difficult at the best of times. But when life throws a 3-1/2 ton tree on top of you, and places you in a wheelchair, you barely have the strength to survive. This is a collection of 20 inspirational articles written by Rosemarie Rossetti, Ph.D. With uncommon courage and determination, Rossetti pulled herself back into life. She highlights the life lessons that she learned about coping with change while rebounding from her crisis. She teaches others how to take back their lives after facing adversity.

    Rossetti offers insightful lessons on personal growth, hope, courage, patience and perseverance. She shares her conviction that the IMPOSSIBLE IS POSSIBLE.
