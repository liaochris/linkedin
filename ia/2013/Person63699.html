Jill Kurtz, APR's Summary

Public relations is about constituent relations. An organization’s relationship with its stakeholders must be focused on serving their needs, not simply spinning a positive image. Today’s constituents are savvy consumers who crave transparency and authenticity.

I am an authentic communicator in a world of hype.

WHAT I DO:

✔ I respond to constituents’ core needs so that an organization can genuinely serve. The value of great communication is not about wordsmithing; it is about investing with an audience for a long-term relationship of give and take, share and receive.

✔ I quickly grasp the core issues of a business and its constituents to serve both authentically. Effective PR is about research first, strategy second.

✔ As a trusted advisor to executives, I am the voice of our constituents. Only when executives know their constituents' perspectives can they make an informed response. An educated executive is my best customer.

MY PUBLIC RELATIONS PHILOSOPHY:

♦ Constituents will share their needs when they trust an organization has their best interest at heart.
♦ Authenticity is communicated in an ongoing conversation, not in one-off messaging.
♦ People are overloaded with stimuli. They want on-demand information when and where they need it.
♦ Media relations is a thing of the past.
♦ Social media should be about creating community. Period.
♦ Crisis PR can be avoided by telling the truth--early.

MY NOTABLE ACCOMPLISHMENTS:

✰ I have won awards for developing more than 75 websites – one in the earliest years of the Internet.
✰ I won an innovation award for developing the first opt-in email list to serve k-12 parents in Virginia. The subscriber base exceeded the number of families in the district.
✰Through authentic relationship building I achieved “the impossible” -- convincing an environmental community to support a wastewater discharge permit for a chlorine paper mill.
