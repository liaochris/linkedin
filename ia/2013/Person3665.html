Alexander Böhn's Summary

I’m Alexander Bohn, and I am a User Experience (UX) Engineer. I have over twelve (12) years of programming experience and a Master of Fine Arts (MFA) in Graphic Design from the Rhode Island School of Design (RISD).

I have built experiences for organizations as large as Sotheby’s and MIT, and for those as small as a single person. I have a long freelance career, but at the time of writing I am looking to “come in from the cold”, as it were, and find a place to work full-time.

My long-term objective is to strategically direct and compose the user experience of applications that do wonderful things for their users, working with like-minded and talented free-thinkers who do what they love.

Design and UX consultant

Vidvox

Sole Proprietorship; Myself Only; Veterinary industry

2003 – 2004 (1 year)

In the wake of the ür-founder of the company Johnny DeKam's passing of the operational torch to Jack Turner and David Lublin, Vidvox sought to launch a fleet of 2nd-generation live-video performance apps. The concept for this new product line was a hybrid of DeKam's original VDMX, a video-mixer app, and the new collection of paradigmatic UIs yielded by Turner and Lublin's years of experimental live-video performance work.

I developed a typographic identity for Vidvox and a matching style guide. Working closely with the principal developers, I integrated this identity's graphic design concepts to produce a novel yet intuitive interface for the new software line. The fruits of our convergent design effort was a distinctive idiomatic UI suite with a tightly enmeshed colorimetric and typographic identity – several days prior to the launch of which were devoted entirely to pixel-level minutiae.

The Vidvox product offerings have remained true to the spirit of real-time video performance, cannily exploiting the many technological opportunities that have arisen in the ten years since; fittingly, my identity work has also remained the basis for the evolution of the face Vidvox apps continue to offer to their expert users today.

Web Manager

Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute

Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry

2001 – 2004 (3 years)

Oversaw the design, construction, and deployment of several turnkey full-stack web apps to serve the Art Department and its vastly multivalent subordinate concerns – from the broad strokes of the branding to the UX stories and interface minutiae, down through the many layers of the HTML/CSS/PHP/MS-SQL stack (including an antediluvian array of sub-projects – e.g. a bespoke optimized ORM and a custom RADIUS/LDAP authentication module) to the provisioning of hardware. These apps delivered a range of well-integrated services, including:

* Scheduling and CRM for the entire studio's production equipment stock
* Student and faculty collaborative blogging and discussion courseware
* Event PR workflow management – we were an early adopter of RSS syndication, which dovetailed with our ORM's internal RDFa serialization scheme
* Early support for mobile and other non-PC platforms, via such venues as the iCal and vCard standards, as they emerged
* Interactive single-page DOM-based UI elements (before the advent of jQuery!) to brighten up aspects of the site's experience for users at all levels
* Hosting infrastructure for student work – experimental graduate web projects ran sandboxed and had access to a permissioned subset of the server-side data API

... among other things.
