Jordan Friedman's Summary

Stress Management Speaker & Trainer

Jordan Friedman loves stress and helping people reduce it. Called "The Stress Coach," he combines his remarkable stress survival story with 20 years of speaking and training experience to motivate and teach audiences to deal productively with challenge, change and crisis. His unique emphasis on practical strategies that simultaneously reduce stress and promote career and academic advancement, resonates with anyone who wants to ace interviews, shine among peers, maximize performance or just feel better.

Stress Management Expert

Jordan is the author of The Stress Manager’s Manual, and you may already know him as a voice of 'Alice,' the first online source of straightforward answers to questions about stress, sex and other wellness topics on Columbia University’s acclaimed Go Ask Alice website. As long time director of Columbia’s health education program, Jordan penned hundreds of Internet and newspaper columns on everything from muscular relaxation to bedtime communication, and he co-authored the popular Go Ask Alice Book Of Answers. His work has been quoted and promoted by The New York Times, The Wall Street Journal, Entrepreneur and O Magazine among others.

Campus & Workplace Wellness Programs

Jordan's dedication to sustained stress reduction also drives his Stressbusters campus and workplace wellness program, a continuous outreach magnet that now connects nearly 250,000 students and staff with stress reduction and health promotion resources at Harvard, St. John's, and the University of Missouri among other schools.

Less Stressed Clients & Audiences

Jordan's stress management keynotes, training workshops and teaching resources have reduced stress for countless clients and audiences including Dow Chemical, Blue Cross Blue Shield, New York City Bar Association, MIT’s Sloan School of Business and the National Society of Hispanic MBAs.
