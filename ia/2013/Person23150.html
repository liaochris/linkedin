Chris Ward's Projects

  • Hurll Nu-Way

    • May 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Chris Ward, Matt McDougall

    Hurll Nu-Way are a major equipment supplier to various Australian and New Zealand industries. Their existing website had been in existence for nearly six years and the company felt it was time to create a new, more flexible website to serve their two separate Australian and New Zealand branches as well as to allow for future feature expansion into responsive design and ecommerce features for warranty and extras retail. The site was built in Drupal 7 with Domain Access to handle the different territory sites and some custom code to handle the businesses peculiar product catalogue.

    My role - I was involved in initial scoping discussions with the client, assessing their needs and requirements. I wireframed the project and created a technical specification for our development team. During development I managed task allocation, testing and liaising with the client to inform them when features were ready for testing, talking them through training and handling bug reports to pass back to our team.

  • Green Renters Workshop program

    • August 2009 to Present
    Team Members: Chris Ward, Cate Lawrence

    Green Renters workshop program has been running since mid 2009 and in that time we have delivered several hundred sessions covering a variety of diverse topics all over Australia.

    The program is by far the most successful and active Green Renters undertakes, often with an average of 2-3 sessions a week.

    To get a better idea of what we offer and when, visit the workshops page.
    During the life of the project, several workshop sub-programs have been funded by the following organisations: Opportunities for Carlton, Colac Otway shire, City of Frankston, City of Monash, City of Melbourne, City of Yarra.

    My role – Initially delivering workshops, more recently, budgeting, setting up a booking and management system, invoicing, promoting and designing presentations.

  • University of Melbourne's School of Psychology

The site was created in Drupal 7 and utilises many modules with some custom code to allow for fairly complex content creation workflow.

    My role - I was involved in initial scoping discussions with the client, assessing their needs and requirements. I helped wireframed the project and create a technical specification for our development team. During development I managed task allocation, testing and liaising with the client to inform them when features were ready for testing, talking them through training and handling bug reports to pass back to our team.
