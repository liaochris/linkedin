Kevin P. Siu's Publications

  • Technological Neutrality: Towards Copyright Convergence in the Digital Age

    • University of Toronto Faculty of Law Review (Forthcoming)
    • 2013
    Authors: Kevin P. Siu

    Abstract: This paper examines the application of the principle of technological neutrality in copyright law, focusing on recent developments in Canadian jurisprudence and legislation. The principle is central to a sustainable copyright regime in an era of technological innovation. Canadian jurisprudence has largely focused on functional equivalency between physical and digital transactions in applying the principle of technological neutrality, but has not addressed how similar technologies have been treated differently in the law. Examples from other jurisdictions show how a non-neutral regime can lead to economic inefficiencies and hinder innovation. This paper argues that the best way to protect the integrity of copyright law and promote technological development is to enhance the principle of technological neutrality through a paradigm shift from a list of enumerated statutory rights to a general right to commercially exploit copyrighted works.
