James Green's Summary

I studied Western European literature at Coimbra University, Portugal then trained as a tax accountant/attorney with Thornton Baker, London. I have always had a parallel career in publishing and taxation. Occasionally these have collided when I have authored books and articles on taxation.

I left Thornton Baker to work in the legal department of a major UK publisher and then spent two years at Birmingham completing a PhD thesis on the Portuguese writer Fernando Pessoa and working as a freelance presenter/producer for BBC Radio.

I spent the next 25 years in the publishing, printing and bookselling industry as a director and editor. I also wrote, edited or translated over 60 books. Search Amazon for J.C.R. Green for details of available titles.

In the late 1980’s having sold my publishing business I changed my main activity back to taxation and set up a corporate services company in London, later moving it and my family to Shropshire and the Isle of Man and with offices in Geneva, Luxembourg, Dublin and Amsterdam.

I am a member of the International Tax Planning Association, the International Fiscal Association and am or have been a non executive director of companies, both listed and unlisted, in several European and Caribbean jurisdictions.

Professional Qualifications
Chartered Director (CDir)
Fellow of the Institute of Directors (FIoD)
Fellow of the Chartered Institute of Management (FCMI)
Associate of the Institute of Financial Accountants (AFA)
Holder of the IoD Diploma in Company Direction (Salford University and the Isle of Man International Business School).
Holder of the Certificate in Strategic Marketing from Cornell University Graduate School of Management.

I have wide experience at board level in large and small companies and was a senior elected member of a large UK local authority. I have been a member of a number of NGO boards including the EU Objective 2 (Structural Fund) and Objective 3 (Social/Educational Fund) Program Monitoring Committees.

Specialties

Recent projects have included the reorganisation of an international group of companies to minimise tax leakage. This resulted in a first year saving of over £250,000 and ongoing savings of at least £120,000.

In addition, as a Chartered Director I believe that my professional skills as a director covering effective marketing strategies, leading strategic change, HR issues, strategic business direction, finance, company law, and board development is an asset to any type of company.

Carraghyn is based in the Isle of Man, from where we serve the UK and Ireland. Chief Executive

Nautilus Media Limited

January 2012 – Present (2 years) Isle of Man

Nautilus Media Limited was established as an independent publishing company initially to hold and exploit the IP rights to a trilogy of novels called The Socrates Project.

This trilogy does not fit easily into the confines of regular publishing companies but has support from several major writers and celebrities who have joined together to fund publication of the trilogy which we believe has the possibility of being another "Girl With The Dragon Tattoo".

The first book - "The Socrates Project" was published (hardback, trade paperback and eBook) in February 2013 followed by French and Dutch editions later in the same year.

Negotiations are in place in respect of, further translation, eBook, film and other rights.

The company has been successful in raising equity finance from private investors and our ultimate aim is to use the expected profits from the trilogy to publish fiction and non fiction titles dealing with sustainability, regeneration, and ethical issues of a wider compass. In particular we intend to publish English language editions of books originally written in other languages.

A proportion of our profits will be donated to charities active in these areas.
