Michael Kranitz's Summary

Kranitz is a proven entrepreneur, who practiced corporate reorganization law for 11 years before venturing into software development and Internet services.

Kranitz is a dynamic speaker, software design expert and business creation authority. He brings a wide array of skills and experience to bear on business, including business development, marketing, creative deal structuring, IT strategy and new product deployment.

Michael Kranitz's Experience

CEO

Kranitz Enterprises, Inc.

January 1992 – Present (21 years 9 months) Lone Tree, CO

KEI has developed a suite of community software solutions for Internet and mobile distribution.

- iOnYou - an iPad app for shooting, cataloging, annotating and sharing video
- Who's Flying Where - a smartphone app for radio control pilots to see where and when their buddies are flying
- Who's Racing Where - for the RC car racing market
- RC FlightDeck - a syndicated platform for promoting and taking registrations for RC flying events
- RC CarCal - for RC racing

KEI has been creating consumer software solutions since 1992. KEI first launched HomeColor Concepts, a design tool for envisioning your new home's colors and textures. In 1996, KEI spawned LeaseWizard Lease & Loan Software for consumers along with the book, Look Before You Lease: Secrets to Smart Vehicle Leasing (written by Michael Kranitz). In 2005, KEI launched EasyTeamManager.com, which provides sports teams with easy-to-use but highly functional websites.

KEI is currently developing an iPad application for coaches and teaching professionals. iOnYou (http://www.iOnYouVideo.com) will be released in 3nd quarter 2013.

CEO

RCUniverse.com

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Internet industry

December 2001 – August 2010 (8 years 9 months)

RCUniverse.com is the world's largest online community for radio control hobbyists. I wrote the code for roughly 80% of the site and operated it with my partner until it was acquired by Hearst Corporation and MediaNews Group in 2007. I re-acquired the site in February 2010 and sold it to Internet Brands (NASDAQ: INET) in August, 2010

President & CEO

Kaango, LLC

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Information Technology and Services industry

January 2005 – February 2010 (5 years 2 months)

Kaango is a networked online marketplace for users to buy and sell goods as well as a classifieds software platform for media companies. Kaango has more than 230 newspapers and other media partners and is rapidly growing. Kranitz created Kaango in 2005 as a spin off of hobby community site, RCUniverse.com (which he co-founded). Hearst Corporation & MediaNews Group acquired 80% of Kaango and RCUniverse in November 2007.

Vice President, Carpoint

Microsoft

Following the acquisition of DriveOff.com, I was appointed VP of Carpoint (currently MSN Autos).

President

DriveOff.com

July 1999 – February 2001 (1 year 8 months)

DriveOff.com was the first company to accept and document an automobile lease transaction entirely online. A first in many areas of online auto sales and leasing, DriveOff was acquired by Microsoft and Ford in 2001.

Partner
