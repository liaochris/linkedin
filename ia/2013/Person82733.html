Louis Hernandez, Jr's Summary

Louis Hernandez, Jr. – President and Chief Executive Officer

In his role as President and Chief Executive Officer, Louis Hernandez, Jr., will help Avid into its next phase of growth and market leadership. Building on Avid’s long heritage of technology excellence in the media industry, Mr. Hernandez oversees Avid’s strategic investments in the product innovation, solutions, and services that help content creators and broadcasters better connect with and inspire their audiences, both now and in the future.

Mr. Hernandez is a proven growth-oriented technology executive with expertise in operational execution and results-driven performance in a variety of complex technical fields including content management, e-commerce, workflow management and financial services. Prior to joining Avid, Mr. Hernandez was the Chairman of the Board and Chief Executive Officer of Open Solutions Inc., a technology provider to financial institutions worldwide, which was acquired in January 2013 by Fiserv, Inc. He has been a member of Avid’s Board of Directors since 2008 and is also currently serving on the Board of Directors for HSBC North America Holdings, Inc., HSBC USA Inc., and ScoreBig, Inc. He has also founded or advises several high-tech companies in various industries.

Mr. Hernandez has been a featured guest on Fox Business, CNBC, ABC News, and Bloomberg TV, where he discussed technology, economics, strategic planning and globalization.

He is also actively involved in the community, and supports children’s issues and advances in healthcare and education through his private foundation.

April 2010 – Present (3 years 8 months)

In Saving the American Dream Louis explores the origins, evolution and economic underpinnings of the American Dream, detailing how key government policies over-facilitated the dream’s attainment, weakened the will of the American people and drove us inexorably toward the 2007 financial crisis. He shows how policies put in place after the crisis not only failed to address the core problems America faces, but created a growing disconnect between Wall Street and Main Street that has made things far worse.

Too Small to Fail reflects on the pace of change that impacts all aspects of how we live, its implications on the financial services industry, and the unique interrelationship of recent history and the current state of the financial industry. It also lays out a path to navigate the future that will ultimately reposition the industry and individual institutions for long-term success.

Louis urges leaders of community-based financial institutions to take action today. “No one is standing up for you; you need to lead the charge and inspire your community.”
