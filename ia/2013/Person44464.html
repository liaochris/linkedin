Femke van Drooge's Honors and Awards

  • iZovator Award 2011

    Stichting iZovator
    • November 2011

    The iZovator Award is a Dutch healthcare innovation award that is granted for the best innovative idea for healthcare in the region of 'Gooi en Vechtstreek'. I won this award together with Hilde de Wit for our mobile application 'Vinni', that helps people with a cognitive disability to express their needs and feelings through the use of photos and emoticons.

  • HOT100 2011

    Virtueel Platform
    • August 2011

    The HOT100 is a list of the 100 most talented & promising new alumni of all Dutch media and art programs in higher education, created annually by Virtueel Platform. Students can be nominated in their final year before graduation by teachers and are ultimately chosen by the team of Virtueel Platform.

Femke van Drooge's Projects

  • Zebra Technologies UX Design

    • April 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Femke van Drooge, Joep Slenter

    Cooking Fox advises Zebra Technologies on the User Experience Design (UX Design) in the process of creating innovative solutions and applications in the logistics business. Cooking Fox is helping Zebra to make the use of their technology as easy, comfortable and user-friendly as possible for their customers in hospitals, airports factories, or distribution centers all over the world.

  • Sodexo Flash Enquète / Sodexo Quick Survey

    • April 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Femke van Drooge, Joep Slenter

    The Sodexo Flash Survey is a web-application that makes it easier for restaurantmanagers of Sodexo Education The Netherlands to ask students eating at their restaurants about the quality of their food and service. This gives a better insight into the points of improvement of each restaurant and will motivate the staff to work on it.

  • Vinni

    • August 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Femke van Drooge, Hilde de Wit

    Vinni is a mobile application that makes it easier for people with a cognitive disability to express their opinion and for family and caregivers to understand their needs and feelings. Vinni was my graduation project for the Master of Arts 'Design for Digital Cultures' and comes with a research paper about client-centered communication in long term care.

  • Slag om Dondervoort / Battle for Dondervoort

    • October 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Femke van Drooge, Tim Bosje, Jennemie Stoelhorst

    Slag om Dondervoort (Battle for Dondervoort) is an educational social game made for the Dutch Association of Fortified Cities. Battle for Dondervoort has been designed for the second year of Dutch Highschool (13 year-old kids). During the game the players learn about the origins of the Dutch fortified cities and the value of group dynamics and collaboration.

  • Stoomreizen

    • February 2010 to May 2010
    Team Members: Femke van Drooge, Joep Slenter, Marco Spaapen, Anne Bras, Abel de Beer, Marnix Puijker

    Stoomreizen is an educational game for children of 8-11 years old, made for Het Spoorwegmuseum in Utrecht, The Netherlands. In the game, kids go on adventure within the museum, where they can build and design their own train and test it to see how passengers would like it. It is an online game and is innovative because of its use of Augmented Reality.

  • Beroepskunstenaars in de klas

    • November 2009 to Present
    Team Members: Femke van Drooge

    Kunstenaars in de klas (artists in the classroom) is an organisation that wants to facilitate schools in finding arts & culture projects, given by professional artists that are trained and certified to teach. The website is a platform where these artists can promote themselves and show what projects they have running. For this website I have done some Interaction Design and Visual Design.

  • Hospital in the Clouds

    • May 2008 to Present
    Team Members: Femke van Drooge, Anne Bras

    The Hospital in the clouds is a flying simulator for children in a wheelchair. The main purpose of this interactive installation is to give children, who are bound to their wheelchairs in daily life, a sense of freedom through flying. It was designed for the Wilhelmina Childrens' Hospital in Utrecht.
