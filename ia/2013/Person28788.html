Dale Larson's Summary

As a veteran of ten startups and small businesses as a founder or early employee (hacker, marketer, CEO), and as a consultant and advisor, I've helped bring hundreds of products and services to market over nearly 30 years.

Now a full-time Executive Coach, I work mostly with venture-backed tech startup CEOs, helping high-performing leaders to be happier and more successful in all aspects of their work and life, continuing to learn and grow while they build outstanding products, teams and businesses.

I am certified by CTI (CPCC and Leadership), a PCC credentialed member of the International Coaching Federation, a Founding Fellow of the Institute for Coaching, and a member of the Harvard Business Review Advisory Council. I co-founded the Startup Coaches Summit.

Executive Coach

Startup Happiness

Partnership; 1-10 employees; Internet industry

2010 – Present (3 years) San Francisco and Worldwide

We help startup founders become ever more awesome versions of themselves so they can lead and grow their companies to happier and more successful outcomes.

Common topics include:
-clarifying vision
-building and leading an exec team
-managing relationships (board, cofounders, staff, and others)
-finding your voice as a leader
-delivering effective feedback
-having difficult conversations
-transparency
-making challenging decisions
-feeling overwhelmed, managing fear and doubt
-hiring and firing
-raising money
-creating accountability
-setting & tracking goals
-creating habits that reinforce company values
-what to do when your founding role (or company) doesn't still feel exciting
-what's next after a failure or exit

While the accomplishments are all theirs, we're really proud of the amazing things our clients do: they create awesome technology, products and services. They build amazing teams (staff and boards) and businesses. More than half our clients closed a venture round in the last year with over $100 million raised among them, and they're all swinging for the fences. Others own profitable businesses that just keep getting better. Sometimes, we work with someone who's still in the idea stage, especially if they've had a successful prior exit or career.

I also keep two time slots (with waiting list) to coach and mentor other coaches with high potential (sometimes while they go through training and certification).
