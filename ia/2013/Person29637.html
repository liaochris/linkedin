Daniel Kehoe's Summary

I only make LinkedIn connections with people I've actually worked with.

If you're a recruiter, there is no reason to contact me as I am no longer employable for love or money and I don't make referrals.

I always enjoy conversations with startup founders and investors and occasionally serve as an informal advisor but I no longer do paid consulting.

...

I've been part of the development of the Web since its beginnings in 1991 (I was an early contributor to Tim Berner-Lee's pioneering www-talk forum). I've lead development teams and contributed to open source software projects. Over the years, I've built web applications in Ruby on Rails, Python, PHP, Java, and Perl (you can find my current projects on GitHub).

In the past, I've written for popular technology magazines to introduce leading-edge technologies to a wider audience. I'm interested in individual as well as organizational transformation and I've worked with prominent authors in the enlightenment industry (most notably, Jerry and Esther Hicks) as a partner or consultant to build online communities focused on self-help and self-realization.

I'm interested in the potential of the Internet to transform commerce and society. From my perspective, the web is still being invented.

Daniel Kehoe's Experience

Founder

RailsApps

April 2011 – Present (2 years 5 months) San Francisco

Founder of a popular community-based project to build open source applications for the Ruby on Rails web development platform. The project includes starter applications accompanied by tutorials for beginner and intermediate level developers. The project is funded by subscription sales of advanced tutorials.

Principal

CTO Consulting

July 2002 – January 2012 (9 years 7 months) San Francisco Bay Area

Consulting CTO for small/medium enterprises (10 to 500 employees, 2 to 50 million revenue/year). Involved with early-stage startups as a cofounder or advisor.

For enterprises, I focus on business processes for innovation, project management, and new product development. A typical engagement involves developing organizational capacity for successful development of software products to support an enterprise business mission.

For startups, I'm keenly interested in solving the challenges of getting from "concept to code" and getting development done right.

For both startups and established enterprises, I see my role as bridging the space between strategic vision, business practice, and software implementation.

I'm comfortable working with founders, investors, and executives to implement a vision and execute a plan. As a consultant to management, I focus on organizational capacity for software development and technology innovation. Among entrepreneurs, I'm interested in the potential of the Internet to transform commerce and society.

Chief Technology Officer

Foodmoves.com

July 2006 – May 2008 (1 year 11 months)

CTO and lead developer for a business-to-business online auction site for the perishable foods industry. My team built this internationalized Web 2.0 auction site using Rails and Ajax in a little over a day ;-). The site launched in mid-October 2007. Funded by a small group of private investors from the produce industry.

Founder

Bookport

May 1994 – December 2006 (12 years 8 months)

Bookport was a project to work with authors and book publishers to monetize content on the web. Launched the first trade book sold online as an ebook in 1994. Early innovator in Internet marketing and social media as a means to promote and sell books. Focused on developing online communities as a source of revenue for authors and publishers. Notably, worked with best-selling authors Dan Poynter (1994-96), Joe "Mr Fire" Vitale (1995-97), and Jerry and Esther Hicks (1996-2006), among others.

Consultant

Navis LLC

Public Company; 201-500 employees; Computer Software industry

August 1999 – June 2002 (2 years 11 months)

Navis LLC, of Oakland, California, is the world leader in software for the container shipping industry. Served as team mentor, software architect, and lead implementor for the company's first web application product, which became central to the company's strategic positioning as a technology leader in its industry.

Chief Technology Officer

MediaImprint LLC

September 1999 – April 2000 (8 months)

Cofounder and CTO for an Internet start-up focused on services for advertisers, media buyers and marketing managers. The company was a dotcom failure.

Principal

Fortuity Consulting

September 1994 – September 1999 (5 years 1 month)

Development of web applications for e-commerce, as a consultant and mentor to software development teams, for five years prior to the dotcom boom. Clients included Altra and Elsa (manufacturers of computer hardware), Pulse Entertainment (3D technology for the web), Psygnosis (games), InsightsInto (economics publishing), and American President Lines (global Fortune 500 shipping company).

Senior Associate

Marble Associates

September 1993 – September 1994 (1 year 1 month)

Early use of the World Wide Web in enterprise applications. Directed methodology and practice development for a consulting firm that led in transforming information systems organizations for large enterprises engaged in business process reengineering. Company clients included CSC Index, Chase Manhattan, Consumers Power Company, Hitachi, and NCR Corporation.

Contributing Editor

NeXTWORLD Magazine

January 1991 – March 1994 (3 years 3 months)

Contributed articles to every issue of the only magazine devoted to NeXTSTEP computing. Speaker at annual NeXTWORLD Expo conferences.

Principal

Fortuity Consulting (Sole Proprietorship)

July 1986 – September 1993 (7 years 3 months)

Participated in development of the World Wide Web beginning in 1991 as a member of the original www-talk mailing list. Authored articles in leading industry publications, including cover stories in PC World and NeXTWORLD magazines. Consultant to technology companies, including Adobe Systems, IQ Engineering, Kyocera, LaserTools, and NeXT Computer, Inc.
