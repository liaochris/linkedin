Adriana Dunn's Summary

For the past six years I've worked in editorial and marketing roles at startups including StellaService, The Huffington Post, GOOD, ThingsWeStart and Participant Media. I graduated from Western Washington University in 2007 with a bachelor's degree in journalism and concentration in political science. While at WWU I served as Managing Editor for the campus newspaper, The Western Front, and Vice President of the Public Relations Organization.

In 2012 I sat on City Year Seattle's Young Professionals Host Committee, and I mentor college students through the WWU Alumni Association's ASK program. I also enjoy supporting projects that protect our environment and improve access to quality education.

Writer and Editor, TakePart.com

Participant Media

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Entertainment industry

February 2009 – July 2010 (1 year 6 months) Los Angeles

TakePart is the digital arm of Participant Media, producers of An Inconvenient Truth, Syriana, Food Inc., and The Cove. I served as editor of the TakePart blog and contributed more than 100 articles across multiple web properties.

Powered by TakePart, Hopenhagen was a global communications initiative led by the United Nations in partnership with the International Advertising Agency. In 2009, I worked closely with the OgilvyEarth team to produce a series of articles leading up to the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change in Copenhagen. I also managed syndication partnerships with leading environmental sites including HuffPost Green, TreeHugger and Ode Magazine. Contributors included Al Gore, Bill McKibben, Philippe Cousteau and other leaders of the climate movement.

In 2010, I worked with oceans expert and National Geographic Explorer Jon Bowermaster to produce and distribute daily articles and videos related to the health of our oceans. Once the Gulf Oil spill began, we focused coverage on the spill and produced the 5-part video series Voices from the Spill, which featured exclusive footage of the disaster and its impact on the local communities.

At TakePart I was proud to be a member of the team that developed and executed the digital strategy for The Cove, winner of the Academy Award for Best Documentary in 2010.
