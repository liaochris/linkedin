Keegan Hayes' Summary

I started the Capital Region Entrepreneurs Group for a very simple reason: to help entrepreneurs in the area get the resources they need to make smart decisions and grow their businesses. With the growth of Tech Valley and the focus on the big players like Global Foundries, the College of Nanoscale Science & Engineering and GE, it's important not to lose sight of the entrepreneurs and small business owners who create the majority of job growth in the region. This area is a great place to live and do business, and I'm devoted to helping it grow.

Keegan Hayes' Experience

Founder & Executive Director

Capital Region Entrepreneurs Group

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Civic & Social Organization industry

August 2011 – Present (2 years 3 months) Albany, New York Area

The mission of the Capital Region Entrepreneurs Group is to help grow the startup and small business economy in the local Capital Region, as well as across NY state through strategic partnerships and programs.

Our primary initiative as of now, in conjunction with In Focus Brands, is to build a co-development fund from which startup capital is disbursed to entrepreneurs who are facing the "death valley" stage. Death valley refers to the place between the typical family & friends funding stage and revenue generation where startups are the most vulnerable, yet have the least amount of resources available to them. It's at this stage where we step in to provide the influx of capital and guidance to reach the next level in which monetization is possible. It's a highly collaborative fund that I am proud to be a part of. If you are interested in learning more or getting involved, please visit www.infocusbrands.com/fund for a more detailed overview of the fund.

Organizer

Startup Weekend

Nonprofit; 11-50 employees; Civic & Social Organization industry

December 2012 – Present (11 months) Saratoga, NY

Startup Weekend is a global network of passionate leaders and entrepreneurs on a mission to inspire, educate, and empower individuals, teams and communities.

Startup Weekends are weekend-long, hands-on experiences where entrepreneurs and aspiring entrepreneurs can find out if startup ideas are viable. On average, half of Startup Weekend’s attendees have technical or design backgrounds, the other half have business backgrounds.

Beginning with open mic pitches on Friday, attendees bring their best ideas and inspire others to join their team. Over Saturday and Sunday teams focus on customer development, validating their ideas, practicing LEAN Startup Methodologies and building a minimal viable product. On Sunday evening teams demo their prototypes and receive valuable feedback from a panel of experts.
