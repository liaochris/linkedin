Chris Cotter's Projects

  • Oral-B "Power of Dad"

    • June 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Chris Cotter, Mark Ronquillo, Patrick Walsh, Angela Li, Rema Vasan, Heather Stiteler

    Client requested a program that would generate conversation for Oral-B on the web around Father's Day as a topical event. Hence, provided strategic direction that we should target millennial children with parents roughly 50+, and that we needed to develop a piece or pieces of content that would harness the visceral emotion of the bond between child and parent. Observing that despite the aforementioned powerful emotional bond the day is meant to celebrate, many Father's Day gifts (ties, wallets, golf clubs) are pretty impersonal, and that Dad's contribution to the family often doesn't go as noticed as Mom's, we decided to celebrate Dads, and specifically, to connect with our category, all the smiles they had for us as their children throughout our lives. So, we created the video linked above, and along with our media and PR partners, nailed down a strategic seeding and media strategy that would allow us to maximize our impact within the two weeks leading up to Father's Day.

  • Oral-B "Black"

    • April 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Chris Cotter

    Client requested work that would appeal to a more upmarket, younger male audience interested premium products for this top-of-the-line electric toothbrush. Conducted extensive desk and primary research to understand this consumer, and determined that we needed to position this brush as a gadget and not a toothbrush (ie, we were vying for a piece of our consumer's consumer tech budget). Any toothbrush will clean teeth, but for a premium electric brush like this, we had to show that it had the technological chops to have an effect commensurate with other "innovative" tech products. Thus, our communications needed to portray (in a unique and "cool" way) that this toothbrush was actually the result of much technological innovation and engineering, focusing less on the benefit of clean teeth and more on the benefit of having the most premium and advanced toothbrush available (implying that as a result it would have a superior effect).

  • Vaccines.com

    • January 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Chris Cotter, Caitlin Francke, Tony Effik

    Conducted comprehensive conversation mapping exercise in order to understand the dialog happening on the web around vaccines and vaccination. After developing personas based on what was observed, conducted exhaustive search engine keyword analysis, providing recommendations on site content that would address the points noted in the conversation. Finally, provided strategic design and content recommendations on how to maximize impact of said content, and particularly content that people might take and share with others to spread the truth about vaccines.
