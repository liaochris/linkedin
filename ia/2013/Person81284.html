  • LinkedIn Tips

    • Linked Into Business Blog
    • December 2010
    Authors: Viveka von Rosen

    1. Treat your LinkedIn profile like a website: Make sure it is formatted, clean, and free of spelling and grammatical errors.

    2. Know your keywords:
    Like any website, LinkedIn’s internal search engines weigh your keywords heavily in its searches.

    3. Keep your name clean: Put only your first name in the first name field and your last name in the last name field.

    4. Keep your photo professional: I recommend a close up and a smile.

    5. Don’t ignore the “post an update” function: LinkedIn’s update function is much more robust than it used to be.

    6. Personalize your public profile URL: Make sure your public profile reflects your name, your business, or your area of expertise

    7. Personalize your websites: When you edit your website, the drop down menu gives you the option of “other”.

    8. Juice up your “Experience” section: “Experience” is not your resume. Make sure the jobs you choose to list support each other.

    9. Utilize the “Experience” description area: Use the 1000 characters in the description section.

    10. List your “additional education”: Make sure you list your certifications and licenses as well as traditional education.

    11. Get Recommendations: I recommend at least ten recommendations.

    12. Join strategic groups: Once you join a group you can send a message to strategic members /prospects or invite strategic members to connect with you.

    13. Create a group: Consider creating an open or closed group.

    14. Limit the invitations you send out: You only get 3000 invitations in a lifetime – use them wisely.

    15. When inviting others, tell them how you know them:
    When inviting someone to connect with you, tell them how you know them or why you want to connect.

    15. Use “Answers”: The answers section is a great place to position yourself as an SME or thought leader.

    17. Always be courteous.

    18. “Give” more than “Get”.

    19. Use Applications.

    20. Listen to my interviews and read my blog!
