Bryan Long's Summary

Bryan Long, as executive director, founded Better Georgia in November 2011 along with Amy Morton, chair of the 501(c)(4) board of directors. Better Georgia advocates for progressive values in Georgia using new media and earned media.

Before founding Better Georgia, Bryan started his career as a journalist, followed by more than five years with the Atlanta-based communications firm Jackson Spalding.

At Jackson Spalding, Bryan managed crisis communications, corporate communications and social media for some of Atlanta’s largest and most influential companies.

Previously, he worked as a reporter and editor with CNN.com, the Atlanta Journal-Constitution and the Atlanta Business Chronicle.

In 2003, he launched a successful Web site to cover Atlanta's ever-expanding real estate development industry. His contributions to the community through the daily real estate report led to the honor of addressing the Atlanta Press Club as a featured speaker in January 2005.

In October 2009, Bryan spent a week studying clean water projects in Haiti for Pure Water for the World and Midtown Atlanta Rotary Club. Bryan is an alum of Leadership Atlanta’s class of 2012

A native of Troy, Alabama, Bryan graduated with a B.A. from the University of Alabama upon completion of a double-major curriculum in Journalism and American Studies. His post-graduate education includes a six-month immersion in Leadership Skills Training for News Managers at the prestigious Poynter Institute in St. Petersburg, Florida.
