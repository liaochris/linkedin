Andy Martha's Summary

Finishing up Computer Science E-76: Building Mobile Applications, a course at Harvard Extension School in 12/11. Subjects covered: html5 and javascript, phonegap, and android native development. http://cs76.tv/2011/spring/

Working through buildamodule.com 's training. So far, I've gotten through a majority (sans intros) of Drupal 7 Development Core Concepts (12 hours), and Drupal Theming Essentials (8 hours). I'm going through the Change Management and Version Control tutorials now. I highly recommend it. http://buildamodule.com/ Completed many of the Drupal Tutorials such as Rules, Views, Relations and more put forth by Nodeone, the Swedish powerhouse, which are awesome (thanks Johan!). http://dev.nodeone.se/sv Happily attended DrupalCon '11, and volunteered at DrupalCamp '11.

I started in the back-end, but as I work and study more and more I am convinced that html5+social media+ajax+api's+front end ux are what I'm actually good at. Some people I really click with what they say/do are Brad Frost, Paul Irish, LukeW, Brian LeRoux, and Remy Sharp.

One of the reasons I am self-employed is that I like customer-centered design. For example, to build a product, I like to ask questions like "Who are the users? What devices do they use? What are they searching Google for? What language do they speak? What are the top 3-5 actions they want to perform in each circumstance?" before you design and build a top-down approach all out and then say, "Ok, let's test it out at the end." However, if you employ this method, please contact me for positions.

I like public presenting but one flaw of mine is that I am an introvert and work best when I don't have to have IRC, Skype, Google Chat, Facebook Messenger, Twitter, BaseCamp, LinkedIn, phone calls and multiple email clients open at the same time as work, unless the job is customer service. I am also better at on-site work than remote working. Just letting you know my strengths and weaknesses.

Joie de vivre!
