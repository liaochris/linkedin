Erik Gibb's Summary

Erik started his career over 16 years ago as a telephone customer service rep for an internet service company. He quickly discovered that most of the problems customers encountered originated in small but crucial decisions made during the product design process. He worked his way backward in the product development timeline so that he could help build products that worked well and left users with a smile whenever possible.

He's taken this mission to internet staples (Netscape, Yahoo), entertainment dotcoms (musicbank.com), finance (Intuit), eCommerce (Safeway.com) consulting practices (Tangible UX, Digitas-ModemMedia), health care informatics (Intermap), online dating (PlanetOut), localization (eTranslate), and plenty of top shelf brands including Microsoft, Hewlett-Packard, Sephora, Netflix.

Specialties

+ Interaction design in small and large companies
+ Creating build docs for designers, engineers, product development
+ Working in various Agile frameworks
+ Thorough documentation
+ Quick and dirty documentation
+ Design management
+ Delivering on time

Erik Gibb's Patents

  • Multiple Views for Web Applications across Multiple Endpoints

    • United States Patent Application 12200545
    Inventors: Erik Gibb, Cody Simms, Micah Laaker, Matt Kozlov, Nicole Gregory, Naitik Shah, Sadaf Shahsahebi, Steven Spencer, Isaac Z. Schlueter

    In one example embodiment, a selection may be received from a user of one of a plurality of web applications. Information related to one or more views of the selected application may be assembled and transmitted to a user computing platform for display in a plurality of application areas for a respective plurality of web pages associated with the user.

  • System and Method for Customizing a User Interface

    • United States Patent Application 11676728
    • Filed February 20, 2007
    Inventors: Erik Gibb, Micah Laaker, Matt McAlister, H. Scott Roy

    Described is a system and method for customizing a user interface. A method according to one embodiment of the present invention comprises receiving a given content item and scoring the given content item. When the score exceeds a threshold, at least a portion of the given content is highlighted. The given content item is displayed with at least the portion of the given content item highlighted.
