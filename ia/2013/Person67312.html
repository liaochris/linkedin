  • Malignant Peripheral Nerve Sheath Tumour With Heterologous Differentiation: An Unusual Entity With A Poor Prognosis

    • Rev Esp Patol
    • July 2013
    Authors: Vieites Perez Quintela, M B, Joaquin R. Gonzalez-Canton, López García, M A, Biscuola, M

    Malignant peripheral nerve sheath tumour (MPNST) is a malignant neoplastic prolife- ration arising from peripheral nerves or displaying differentiation along the different elements of the nerve sheath. It includes a group of lesions with high morphologic heterogeneity and further investigations are required for their differential diagnosis.
    We present a case of a 42 year-old male presenting with a painful, pretibial mass which had an aggressive appearance on imaging studies. Histopathology showed a malignant mesenchy- mal lesion, composed of a proliferation of predominant spindle cells alternating with atypical glandular structures and groups of cells with rabdomyoblastic differentiation.
