Kevin Stube's Summary

Kevin Stube has studied in a wide range of academic areas from psychology, anthropology, theology, and management to space studies and planetary science. This wide academic background gives Kevin the ability to work with people on site, remotely, and in virtual environments with key understandings of cultural differences in the world today. Kevin also understands the general aerospace and international aerospace industries through his extensive work with the International Astronautical Federation, Space Generation Advisory Council, and The Planetary Society over the last nine years. Through excellent time management, organization, and attention to detail, Kevin will be a top level project manager.

Kevin Stube's Honors and Awards

  • Young Space Leader

    International Astronautical Federation
    • May 2012

    Berndt Feuerbacher, President of the IAF, is pleased to announce the results of the Young Space Leaders Recognition Programme selection. This inaugural programme produced considerable interest from IAF Member organisations which resulted in a large number of highly qualified nominations.

    The outstanding contributions of these individuals to the activities of the International Astronautical Federation along with their efforts to promote astronautics through their academic and/or professional activities, through outreach to other young people and their communities in sharing knowledge and experiences , and through engagement with the international space community combined to make each of them uniquely qualified to serve as the first five recipients of the Federation’s Young Space Leaders award.

    The 2012 Young Space Leaders will be inducted at the 63rd International Astronautical Congress (IAC) 1-5 October 2012 in Naples, Italy. The induction will occur during the Closing Ceremony on 5 October and will be a highlight of the IAC.
    They are also invited to attend the IAC Gala Dinner as guests of the IAF President.
