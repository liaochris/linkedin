Lynda Moe's Summary

I am an innovative Human Resource and Organisational Development Specialist with a wealth of skills and experience. I have practical and strategic expertise in working from top to bottom within many different organisations both in New Zealand and overseas. Having set up the Human Resources Department for Trust Bank, Bay of Plenty region and having been involved in the initial stages of developing the National Training Department for KPMG Chartered Accountants (New Zealand), I have expertise across the full spectrum of Human Resources and Organisational Development disciplines.

By applying my strong coaching, facilitation and HR/OD management skills, as well as my skills in the area of relationship building and communications, I am able to influence and achieve results. I've lectured in both the business management and human resources areas at polytechnic level and have a strong grounding in both these disciplines. In addition, I've worked with many individuals as a personal, business, and career planning coach helping them to identify and clarify their goals, design personal and/or business plans, and assisting them in overcoming challenges that might hinder them in achieving their goals. My experience includes working with organisations in the process of being restructured and culturally re-defining themselves.

As a consultant, I research and analyse structures, systems and people to identify their needs and produce solutions, resolve complex issues and design and implement strategies, frameworks, policies and processes to meet the goals of the Company.

I tailor my work to ensure strategic alignment with the organisation’s visions and values and to meet the specific needs of the organisation. My approach is values based, holistic and inclusive. As a professional I am committed to achieving significant results in partnership with those I work with.

2013 – 2013

Inspiring Leadership through Emotional Intelligence
Instructor: Richard Boyatzis

Great leaders establish a deep emotional connection w others called resonance. Their own levels of emotional intelligence (EI) allow them to create & nurture these relationships. They use their EI as a path to resonant leadership thru mindfulness, hope, compassion, & playfulness. Most people in leadership lose their effectiveness over time due to cumulative damage from chronic stress but humans can renew themselves, neurologically, hormonally & emotionally.

Based on decades of research into EI competencies, the course examines resonance & developing "resonant leadership" capability, EI, & the experiences of mindfulness, hope & compassion. Using the latest in neuroscience, behavioral, & organizational research, attendees will understand the theory, research, & experience of the Positive Emotional Attractor that's an essential beginning to sustained, desired change for individuals, teams & organizations.
