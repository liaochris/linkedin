Kevin Andreassend's Projects

  • FIA European F3J International Championships

    • July 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Kevin Andreassend

    ICE AV, LED Division, New Zealand was the official supplier of three 1440mm x 960mm Time Display clocks for the FIA European Turkey F3J 2013 international competitions. In addition an ICE Hang Roll Fold Flexible LED screen was used to display results, video, photos throughout the event. The F3J started 11 July and went to 20th July. It was held in the small rural Turkish town of Müsellim in the Köyü Sarayey district where teams from 17 European countries including the USA will compete. The area is suitable for aviation sports due to the wide open flat terrain. Turkey won the Championships for 2013.

  • Splash Palace Swimming Cinema Screen

    • March 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Kevin Andreassend

    ICE AV’s LED Division established a New Zealand first with a stunning aquatic cinema LED screen installation and represents a news trend of bringing the large screen experience pool side.
    The screen mounted on a swivel base is designed for viewing by wave pool viewers and from the main swim pool and race audiences.

  • The HoloDesk

    • March 2010 to Present
    Team Members: Kevin Andreassend, Warwick Johnson

    Interactive Division: The Challenge. To design and manufacture a semi transparent thin, interactive dual imaging surface for live performance DJs. The system has been designed around projection technology as the display surface which is supported at 48 degree angle. The image is viewable on both sides for the DJ and audience. Sizes are available from 32-74 inches. The system packs down for shipping. The HoloDesk is available based on two different multi touch technologies. The HoloDesk can be used by both DJ, VJ and multi media artists. The HoloDesk made its international début on the 18 December 2010 as a centre stage performance feature located in the centre of the audience in the first sell out concert in Earls Court by Deadmau5. On the 19 December 100's of videos began appearing on Youtube featuring the 74 inch HoloDesk and was the first time an international DJ, Producer, musician had performed live with such a system. The HoloDesk became the first multi touch interactive "instrument" to appear in a live concert DVD that followed the Earl's Court concert and was released in the following year.

  • Holo-Info-Desk Interactive Content Display

    • March 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Kevin Andreassend, Warwick Johnson

    The Holo-Info-Desk draws on our development based on the HoloDesk DJ system. With the increasing demand to create engaging access to media for marketing, sales process, product explanation, point of engagement, content exploration and discovery, the Holo-Info-Desk is a seamless multi touch environment that seamlessly converges content such as video, graphics, photos, PDFs, mapping, questions, time lines, contact acquisitions and web sites.

    The Holo-Info-Desk is not just a hardware solution but also a fundamental change in thinking and process, from the mouse driven or stand and watch scenario. With The Holo-Info-Desk it is all hands on - literally. Today's business world and user is about engagement and timely relevance, dynamic engagement, instant and on the fly, multiple routes to your media, instantly changing story lines, on line and off line all at your fingertips on large display surfaces.

    To achieve this, the Holo-Info-Desk uses flat panel and projection in a number of model and size configurations. They ship complete as a system prepared with your content for a true unpack and play experience.

    The Holo-Info-Desk has been designed for use in expos, exhibitions, conferences, retail, museums, tourist centres, foyers, product launches - essentially anywhere where you want users to engage with your content.

  • Real Estate Interactive Touch Window

    • June 2006 to September 2006
    Team Members: Kevin Andreassend, Spitfire Software

    The Interactive retail or office window is the digital version of Window Shopping. In this 2006 project the requirement was to display in a downstairs office window a high tech interactive display.

    The principal requirements included:
    - No equipment or technology installed on the footpath side.
    - 24/7/365 use
    - Touch interactive
    - CRM back end
    - Collection of user details
    - Dynamically display real estate information.
    - Minimalist installation appearance from both sides.

    The solution was based on projection and capacitance touch. We were able to design a inverted projection solution that meant the projector was essentially invisible behind the screen. The minimalist designed display meant that when users walked thru the doorway there was no hardware seen behind the screen and yet their was an image and touch sensitive..... The Magic is in the Screen, literally.

    We can supply systems for clients who are seeking new ways to create innovative and modern interactive touch points for their clients and potential new customers.
