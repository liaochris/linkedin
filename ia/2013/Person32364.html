David W Orr's Summary

A former long-time renter who became a homeowner and later, a real estate agent; my passion is helping others make the best choice when it comes to their home. I'm committed to making the goal of buying and selling real estate as stress-free, fun, and positive an experience as possible - both online and off.

If it's computerized or deals with technology, I'm there. With a background in technology, electronics, lasers & night vision, computers, programming, web design, multimedia & video development, residential construction, and business management - I try to incorporate all I know into my career in real estate sales and marketing.

  • Upgrade to Nearly Silent PC

    • March 2012 to Present
    Team Members: David W Orr

    Completed a new upgrade of my main PC system yesterday. Wanted to keep the cost down, so no bleeding edge motherboard or CPU.

    I did want a quiet PC, as in whisper quiet. For those who are techies or geeks at heart: The upgrade components are: the Antec Three Hundred Two case - lots of bells and whistles, especially the newly designed side rail system for installing drives. Paired with the case, I chose the Antec NEO ECO 620W power supply. It sits in the bottom of the case.

    The really quiet part is the Antec Kuhler H2O 620 liquid cooler. This is the first time I've gone with a liquid cooler - but it won't be the last. The ice maker in the kitchen is louder than my whole PC!

    Since I was keeping my original Asus M4A78 PRO motherboard, I upgraded the processor to the AMD Phenom II X4 965 Black Edition. (The heat sink and fan that came with it have been added to the parts bin for future projects.)

    I did want to finally get to DirectX 11, so the video was upgraded to the Asus (I know - I didn't realize how much Asus stuff was going into this upgrade.) Radeon HD 6670 with 1GB of DDR5 memory.

    This system should make it a lot easier in developing video tours and marketing multimedia for my clients' properties. (Posted to my Facebook page 03.26.2012)
