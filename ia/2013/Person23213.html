Chris DeLeon's Summary

Chris DeLeon is a PhD student in Digital Media at Georgia Tech. He was honored in Forbes 30 Under 30 for his work on iOS Entertainment apps, including an IndieCade 2010 finalist, feelforit, and a best-selling app in winter 2008, Topple. He's been a lead or solo developer for dozens of completed freeware games, averaging 4 or more releases annually since 1998, not including hundreds of experimental gameplay prototypes he made daily 2007-2008. Chris established prolific videogame development clubs at Carnegie Mellon and Georgia Tech, and has served as a professional videogame designer for Electronic Arts, Will Wright’s Stupid Fun Club, and as the 4th hire at ZipZapPlay, a Silicon Valley start-up later acquired by PopCap. He created and writes for HobbyGameDev.com, a videogame development blog now in its fifth year. For his Master's thesis DeLeon studied the business influences and game design parallels connecting pinball to early coin-op videogames.

Specialties: Videogame design, rapid prototyping (digital playable), C/C++/Obj-C, project management, ActionScript 3, OpenGL, Unreal 3 Engine, Unity, Photoshop, Office (Word/Excel/Visio/PPT), PC/360/Wii/iPhone development, teaching videogame development, writing for gameplay, pinball maintenance and minor repair
