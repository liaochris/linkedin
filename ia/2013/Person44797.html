Fiona Crawford's Summary

Fiona Crawford is a freelance writer, editor, blogger, and social media manager whose work spans social and environmental issues, the arts, and football.

She travels annually to write about the Homeless World Cup, an event that utilises football to create social change. Fiona is also the Queensland Book Resources Manager for the The Benjamin Andrew Footpath Library, a charitable organisation that donates high-quality books to homeless and marginalised people.

In 2013, she commenced a PhD examining how activist authors and social enterprises use transmedia writing to drive social change.

Fiona has written for such publications as The Big Issue, Peppermint, Bookseller+Publisher, Writing Queensland, the Matildas, the W-League, Girls FC, and FourFourTwo, and she co-ordinates reviewing project Critical Mass.

She blogs about books, the publishing industry, and her penchant for permanently ‘borrowing’ family members’ books under the pseudonym The Book Burglar. She edits print and online educational material for the Queensland Studies Authority. She also organises a weekly Write Club for writers and editors, which she uses as a good excuse to head to a cafe to sip tea and write.

More information, including samples of Fiona's work, are available at www.agirlcalledfred.com.

Specialties: Web content, blogging, social media management, community management, editing, proofreading, e-communication consulting, football, WordPress, Joomla, creative non-fiction writing, transmedia storytelling, social entrepreneurship, not-for-profit social media management.
