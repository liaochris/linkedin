M. David Green's Summary

I taught myself to code because I enjoy it. I get a physical thrill from puzzling out cross-browser CSS solutions, and crafting clean well-structured HTML. Because of my background in business and my interest in UX/UI optimization, I like to position myself as a developer who can interface easily between engineering and marketing in an agile and transparent manner. I am also comfortable working closely with executives and design professionals, managing teams, directing agencies, communicating with clients, presenting to senior executives, and leading or participating in cross-divisional projects.

Online social networking has been a strong interest of mine since I wrote my thesis on the subject more than two decades ago, in a self-styled Social Sciences major program at U.C. Berkeley. What I saw showed me that the human instinct to network is vital enough to thrive in any medium which allows one person to connect to another. I've worked in several industries since graduating, and applied this learning to everything I do.

I specialize in cross-browser responsive front-end web development with semantic markup, paying special attention to both UX and UI issues across platforms, with accessible content structured for Section 508 compliance as needed. I'm pretty comfortable working in Vim and Git, but flexible. I can do native HTML5 and CSS3 easily, and I'm fond of HAML and Sass/Compass/Susy when the team is game. I'm interested in test-driven Javascript with Jasmine. I appreciate the power and transparency of well-executed Scrum in an agile organization. I've watched web analytics grow from hit counting with Perl scripts to SEO with Google Analytics to Growth Hacking, and I enjoy being part of that dialogue.

On the back end I have a strong interest in Ruby/Rails/Sinatra and the potential of Node, but I'm most familiar with PHP/Smarty. I also have a casual curiosity about functional programming concepts in languages such as Haskell and Scala. Teach me something fun.
