Malcolm Marler's Summary

Director of Pastoral Care at UAB Hospital, 2009-present. Episcopalian.

A big picture visionary who develops programs to meet practical, emotional, and spiritual needs in the community. UAB Pastoral Care connects care in the hospital, outpatient clinic, and in the community through three channels: Building Relationships, Providing Education, and Developing Research.

Chaplain, Director of The Support Team Network, at The 1917 Clinic at UAB (HIV Clinic) from 1994-2009. Founded a community outreach program to teach the faith community how to organize volunteer teams to care for the practical, emotional, and spiritual needs of persons in the community. Became a national training program in 37 states, 125 two-day trainings, 5,000 persons, 10,000 teams.

A pastoral care specialist in two congregations, Baptist and UCC, in Louisville, KY and Glastonbury, CT from 1981-1994. Developed community programs to meet the emotional and spiritual needs of persons that included:

Widowed
Divorced
Stepfamilies
Singles
Nursing Home Residents
Persons who were Homebound
Hospitalized
Grief experiences of all types
HIV/AIDS
Care Teams
Support Teams -- Volunteer teams to provide practical, emotional, and spiritual support to persons with any illness or need.
Speciality is teaching others how to provide pastoral care to others.
