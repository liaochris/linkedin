Stephen Weber, MD, FACS's Summary

Dr. Stephen Weber is a double board-certified Facial Plastic and Reconstructive Surgeon with extensive experience in all facets of facial cosmetic and reconstructive surgery. My goal is to offer exceptional and timely care to patients in need of facial cosmetic, facial trauma or facial reconstructive surgery.

Specialties: Browlift, blepharoplasty, facelift, cosmetic rhinoplasty, revision rhinoplasty, otoplasty, skin cancer reconstruction, microtia repair, facial trauma reconstruction, scar revision, laser resurfacing, Sculptra (poly L Lactic acid) facial volumization treatment.

Member-At-Large

Arapahoe - Douglas - Elbert County Medical Society (ADEMS)

March 2012 – Present (1 year 10 months) Greater Denver Area

The Arapahoe-Douglas-Elbert Medical Society (ADEMS), founded in 1890, is an association of South Metro physicians which are “unified in promoting the science, art and business of medicine, the betterment of public health, the education and welfare of medical professionals and of the people served by our physicians.”

ADEMS serves as an advocate to its approximately 1,000 physician members living and working in our counties. ADEMS is not for profit and is a component organization of the Colorado Medical Society. ADEMS proudly partners with the five area hospitals and one medical school in our catchment area to coordinate and develop educational and social programs for the physicians. With frequent publications and programs advocating available resources, ADEMS helps physicians navigate through the rapidly evolving practice environment and the ever changing requirements in the practice of medicine.

Volunteer Facial Plastic & Reconstructive Surgeon

Outreach Africa

January 2012 – Present (2 years) Tanzania

Outreach is a non-profit 501c3 corporation located in Union, Iowa.
Outreach was created in 2004 when the founders, Floyd Hammer and Kathy Hamilton, returned from a trip to Tanzania on a construction mission. During that trip they experienced the death of many children from hunger and related diseases. Medical professionals from the Upper Midwest have again united for a mission of healing and hope. A team of dozens of health care professionals volunteer their time and medical skills to offer free medical, surgical and dental care to the people of Tanzania, East Africa.
