Brad Swanson's Summary

Brad Swanson is a Senior Agile Coach and Vice President at agile42. He started his software career at age 10 on the Apple IIe and is now a Certified Scrum Trainer® (CST) and Coach (CSC) with 19 years of experience in management, project and program leadership, product management, and software development in both start-ups and large companies. Brad has led agile transformations using XP, Scrum and Kanban at many organizations across the US, Europe, and Asia. He has deep experience with agile software development, starting with eXtreme Programming (XP) in 1999. Brad is President of Agile Denver and a speaker at international conferences.

In addition to his deep experience with agile software methods, Brad has a long history with software process improvement initiatives. He has led ISO 9001 and CMM initiatives for a 2000-person engineering organization. Brad previously held the positions of Director, Engineering Manager, Senior Consultant, Software Performance Engineering Lead, Technical Lead, and Senior Software Engineer.

Specialties: agile, agile coach, Certified Scrum Coach, CSC, agile training, agile trainer, scrum, scrum coach, scrum coaching, scrum master, scrummaster, scrum training, extreme programming, xp, lean, kanban, change agent, project management, project manager, program management, program manager, product management, product manager, team leader, team leadership, coaching, software development, web application development, performance tuning, software testing, testing, java, process improvement

  • Ericsson Mobile Core Agile Transformation

    Team Members: Brad Swanson, Dave Sharrock, Richard Lawrence, Bob Sarni, Ralf Kruse, Andrea Tomasini, Hendrik Esser

  • Global Manufacturing & Distribution: Agile Transformation

    • October 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Brad Swanson, Dave Sharrock

    We helped this large, global company transform it's IT delivery model using agile and lean development methods. We partnered with Senior leadership to create a strategy for adopting and scale agile development to achieve sustainable results. We trained and coached multiple teams worldwide in software development and business analytics. We turned around stalled projects and quickly got teams delivering business value in a predictable fashion. The clear success of the pilot projects has led to the expansion of agile development through larger portions of the organization, moving beyond IT to other business functions. We facilitated the alignment of diverse and competing stakeholder groups so they were able to agree on priorities and optimize their project portfolio using a combination of the Lean Canvas (Business Model Canvas) and facilitating the Business Value Game for a group of global business stakeholders. This clarity in direction enabled delivery teams to focus and deliver more value in less time. In a dramatic turnaround, the IT teams are often delivering functionality faster than the business is able to deploy it.

  • Time Warner Cable: Adopting and Scaling Agile

    • September 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Brad Swanson, Manny G Segarra 3, CSP

    This 200-person organization delivers the set-top boxes, embedded software, and back-end servers that deliver video and programming guides to cable TV customers. With 8 software teams that deliver a single product line, and a very expensive and time-consuming deployment process, tight coordination and a quality-focus are key. I helped this company successfully scale agile practices and reduce cycle time from 8+ months to 4 months, using the Scaled Agile Framework (SAFe) - one of the early applications of that now widely-recognized framework. They measurably increased quality at the same time.

  • Stampin' Up! Agile in IT, marketing, and product design

    • September 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Brad Swanson

    Brad Swanson led the transformation of Stampin' Up's 40-person IT department from traditional to agile methods. Brad guided the Agile Transformation Team in developing a long-term strategy for introducing agile methods, sustaining the change, and continuously improving. Brad trained and coached 5 teams within IT to significantly reduce project lead times and increase focus on the highest priority business needs. He coached developers and testers to improve test automation using Fitnesse and Selenium. The success within IT caught the attention of the Marketing department, where Brad trained the department and helped them reduce cycle time for producing a paper product catalog by 2 months. A team that markets digital products reduced their cycle time for launching products from 12 weeks to 4 weeks: a 66% improvement. Brad is now coaching their Product Design teams to use lean & Kanban techniques to improve their effectiveness.

  • Oppenheimer Funds: Test-Driven Development coaching

    • June 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Brad Swanson

    Brad Swanson coached 2 software development teams over a 3-week period to effectively use Test-Driven Development (TDD) and Acceptance Test Driven Development (ATDD) to improve quality not only through testing, but through better definition of User Stories (requirements) using ATDD. Brad mentored one of the engineers to become an internal mentor and champion for TDD and expand the practice to many more teams.
