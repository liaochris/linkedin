James F. Haggerty's Summary

Jim Haggerty is an innovator in communications, media, and crisis & litigation response. An attorney, writer and consultant for more than twenty years, Jim has a broad background working with some of the nation's most prominent corporate, nonprofit and individual clients. He is frequently called upon as a trusted advisor to leaders in all manner of sensitive reputation management issues.

In the area of litigation and other complex crisis communications matters, Jim is considered one of the nation’s foremost experts. His landmark book, In The Court of Public Opinion (John Wiley & Sons, 2003) was called “… the perfect handbook for this age…” by Financial Times. The book was also positively featured in publications including The Boston Herald, The New York Law Journal, American Lawyer, The Washington Post and the Law Society Gazette. The second edition was published in 2009 by the American Bar Association.

Over the years, Jim has been involved in numerous high-profile crisis matters and legal disputes — including the largest lawsuit ever filed against the United States government, the largest single-family Holocaust restitution claim in history, the largest intellectual property verdict in history and several of the largest antitrust cases in history. He has worked on a wide range of crisis matters in the U.S. and abroad involving product recalls, accidents, workplace violence, government investigations, insider trading and financial fraud.

Building upon his work as a consultant, Jim is also a well-recognized writer. In 2012 and 2013, Jim was nominated for a Neal Award, considered “the most prestigious editorial award in business-to-business journalism,” for his monthly column in American Lawyer’s Corporate Counsel magazine. He is the only full-time public relations professional ever to be honored in such a manner.
