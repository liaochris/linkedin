Edward Cazalet's Summary

An internationally recognized electric industry expert, Dr. Cazalet is a leader in the analysis and design of markets for electricity. For his industry contributions, Public Utilities Fortnightly magazine named Dr. Cazalet "Innovator of the Year".

Dr. Cazalet has over thirty-five years of electric power experience as an advisor to industry and government executives, and as a consultant, researcher, developer and entrepreneur.

He is a founder of TeMIX Inc, MegaWatt Storage Farms Inc, The Cazalet Group, Automated Power Exchange (APX), Decision Focus, Inc. (DFI) and Cazalet Systems.

Dr. Cazalet was recently a member of the Board of Governors of the California Independent System Operator (CAISO).

Specialties: Strategic management and decision analysis, market modeling, power market design and analysis, power scheduling, trading, risk analysis, and software design.

TeMIX Inc.

Ed is the CEO and founder of TeMix Inc., a transactive energy services Company. TeMix provides the TeMix Platform, a cloud based service to the electric power industry and its customers. The TeMix Platform supports standardized, automated transactions among customers and their smart devices, electricity retailers, transmission and distribution service providers, generators and system operators.

Governor

California ISO

Nonprofit; 501-1000 employees; Utilities industry

October 2004 – March 2007 (2 years 6 months)

Appointed by Governor Schwarzenegger, Dr. Cazalet was a member of the 5-person Board of Governors of the California ISO. The California ISO operates the most of the electric transmission grid in California and runs markets to procure ancillary services and balance real-time load and supply. Dr. Cazalet was Vice Char of the Board and Chair of the Operations Committee overseeing markets, MRTU development, real-time operations and transmission planning. He was also Vice Chair of the Finance Committee.

Founder, CEO, Chairman

APX, Inc

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Renewables & Environment industry

April 1996 – January 2002 (5 years 10 months)

In 1996, Dr. Cazalet founded Automated Power Exchange (APX) and served as its CEO from 1996 to 2000. APX is the largest provider of independent transaction services for scheduling and paying for electric power in the US. Dr. Cazalet conceived the business opportunity, designed the power scheduling, trading and settlements technology, created a 24x7 operations center to deploy the technology and services, obtained regulatory approvals, and raised $66 million in investment capital in three rounds. He successfully led the company into diverse power scheduling and exchange related businesses in North America, Europe and Asia with over 100 highly competent professionals. Dr. Cazalet resigned from APX in 2002 and continued to serve as a member of the APX Board of Directors until 2004.

Independent Professional

Cazalet Systems (Self-employed)

July 1985 – October 1996 (11 years 4 months)

As consultant to Bonneville Power Administration and several other electric utilities, Dr. Cazalet developed the Power Market Decision Analysis Model (PMDAM) to simulate hourly power markets and forward capacity planning over horizons up to 20 years and covering all power plants and transmission lines over the Western American continent. This advanced model was used for many years by BPA for analysis of complex capacity energy exchange contracts with California and Southwest Utilities, transmission planning, resource acquisition, and rate case pricing. He sold this modeling system to Electronic Data Systems (EDS) where it was expanded to other regions of the US and it was also in operation for ten years as a major analysis tool for many US power companies. BPA used this PMDAM model for its cost benefit analysis of the INDEGO RTO that preceded RTO West.

Founder and President

Decision Focus, Inc (DFI)

January 1975 – July 1985 (10 years 7 months)

Dr. Cazalet founded DFI in 1975 and led DFI to become a leading firm in energy and electric power system and market modeling and market consulting. The firm attracted more than 50 Ph.D.'s specializing in decision analysis and software development for the power, energy and transportation industries. Under Dr. Cazalet's direction the firm developed dozens of software products for power system planning and operation.
