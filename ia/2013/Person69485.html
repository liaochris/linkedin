Joy Carol's Summary

Over twenty-five years of diversified experience as an author, international speaker, counselor, teacher, workshop/retreat leader, spiritual director, grief and trauma counselor, and manager of community development programs in the United States and Asia, Africa, Latin America, and the Pacific. Extensive experience working with community groups, governments, and non-governmental organizations developing, managing, funding, and reporting on programs with specific emphases in community development, health and education.

Specialties

Special skills and strengths in speaking, writing, communications and teaching; effective interpersonal relations and counseling skills; highly motivated and energetic team player; strong leadership and decision-making abilities.

Save the Children USA

Responsible for negotiating, developing, funding, supervising, and reporting on national and international community development programs for Save the Children (SC) in 12 countries of Asia and the Pacific. Responsible for developing a strategy to pilot-test the first United Nations funding of regional and national non-government organizations' (NGOs) projects in the Pacific Region. Duties included providing liaison between government ministries, UN agencies and private organizations in the selection of projects. Made programmatic and funding recommendations to UN agencies.

Key Accomplishments: Wrote official reports analyzing the social and economic conditions in each of the eight countries. Assisted 30 national and regional projects in the Pacific to obtain, for the first time in history, funding from UN agencies. Developed project proposal format and established criteria for funding non-government organizations. Initiated and conducted national training workshops on project design for government ministries and NGOs in eight countries in the region.

Consultant

World Council of Churches, Church World Service

1976 – 1977 (1 year)

Evaluated the village leadership training program of the Department on Service and Development of the Dewan Gereja di Indonesia. Trained participants to identify and analyze village needs, local resources, patterns of leadership, and economic systems. Developed recommendations for a coordinated system for project design and evaluation for use in local villages.

Evaluated projects for future program planning of Church World Service in Pakistan, Indonesia, Japan, Korea, and the Philippines. Served on the World Council of Churches’ Asia Screening Committee to select development projects in Asia to be funded by the WCC. Organized first meeting of CWS and its Asian colleague agencies to promote collaboration and recommend policy and program directions.

As Acting Assistant to the Executive Director of CWS, wrote appeals for fundraising efforts in response to natural international disasters. Raised over $4 million in funds and relief supplies from private agencies and individuals.

Conducted study of the role of women in development and the role of voluntary and international agencies in India for CWS and CASA Agency of the National Council of Churches of India. Conclusions and findings published in widely circulated report But We're Not Afraid to Speak Anymore. Recommendations resulted in policy changes in several agencies regarding women's participation in development.

Director/Founder

Union Center for Women

1973 – 1976 (3 years)

Responsible for creating, developing, managing, and raising funds for the oldest, existing women's center in the world. Conducted needs assessment in the community and represented the Center to the public and the press. Developed programs in such areas as job training, abuse, finance, career development and counseling services. Helped organize Outreach Team to assist other communities to establish similar projects.

Key Accomplishments: Raised funds for first three years of Center's operation and in a 12-month period built a membership base of nearly 500. Trained members to successfully assume all financial, personnel and program functions. In 1975 during the International Women's Year, Norwegian Broadcasting Company made a documentary film about my work, which was aired throughout Scandinavia.

Counselor/Teacher

Public/Private Schools, Project Head Start

1964 – 1973 (9 years)

Taught and counseled students in junior high and elementary schools. Developed and directed counseling programs in private and public junior high schools in New York City and in Maryland. Helped organize and directed newly created Project Head Start in Colorado.

Key Accomplishments: Selected as the Outstanding Young Educator in the United States in 1966 by the U.S. Chamber of Commerce, after being chosen for that honor in the city of Boulder and in the state of Colorado.

Responsible for training teachers and administering educational system in Karachi, Pakistan providing projects with supervision, technical assistance, personnel management, and financial administration. Developed and directed adult literacy program and women's income-generating projects in four refugee areas.

Key Accomplishments: Expanded school system by six new schools increasing student population by more than 3,000. Initiated and developed adult literacy programs in eight communities reaching more than 2,000 adults each year. Organized public health clinic with mobile outreach capabilities. At completion of term, recruited and trained local staff to administer entire program.
