Marvin Smith's Summary

As a strategic talent sourcing technologist; I use analytics, technology, and social platforms to enhance talent sourcing and recruiting (talent acquisition) programs. As a talent hunter, my work relies heavily on metrics, intelligence and data to inform the talent identification and engagement strategies that are employed to accomplish this goal. Another way of thinking about this work is that it takes place at the intersection where the science of technology, intelligence and data meets the art of talent sourcing and recruiting.

My roles in strategic talent sourcing have ranged from a sourcing and recruiting practitioner, program manager, and evangelist/advocate for adding a social dimension to sourcing and recruiting. I have been blessed to be on teams that were at the forefront of resurgence of talent sourcing as a strategic weapon in recruiting. These teams piloted groundbreaking programs (ERE Award Winning) work that used intelligence, data and technology to segment the target talent audiences and build talent pipelines and communities.

  • EMEA Competitive Response

    • March 2011 to April 2011
    Team Members: Marvin Smith, Stacey Schutt, Tito Magobet

    Situational Analysis
    Major global competitor made some announcements that we thought might cause some unrest in their workforce. Our approach was to target the engineering talent that mapped to our existing openings as well as the projected needs according to our workforce plan. We wanted to make the experience very personal and guaranteed a conversation with a sourcer within 2 days of expressing interest.

    What We Did
    Created a landing page (web site) that featured our engineering opportunities and allowed prospects to explore and express interest in discussing RIM opportunities. We incorporated Google Analytics into the landing page so we could measure the results. Identified 1592 engineering prospects on LinkedIn and sent LinkedIn InMails on a staggered basis. The message was “indirect” seeking referrals as opposed to direct solicitation of the competitor’s employees.

    Results
    Over 40% of the target audience (911/1592) visited our landing page with 30% of the visitors responding by seeking additional information; referring colleagues; or advising us on the timing of their job search. Even though our message was indirect, many people “referred themselves.” Approximately 20% (187/911) of the visitors expressed interest in speaking with us about opportunities. We engaged those prospects within the 2 day promise and made 3 hires within 3 weeks. Additionally, 50 prospects were in the interview process when RIM froze hiring due to economic conditions.

  • Reliability Engineering Talent Community

    • February 2008 to March 2011
    Team Members: Marvin Smith, Dan Contreras

    The hardware team at Microsoft Entertainment & Devices had a challenge--$1 Billion worth of Xbox 360 consoles were recalled due to manufacturing flaws. E&D staffing was giving the responsibility of recruiting a world class reliability engineering team ASAP.

    What We Did
    Our goal was to fill the 9 immediate openings as well as created a talent pipeline for the anticipate growth of the reliability engineering team. Identified 2500+ reliability engineers though competitive intelligence research and aggregated that data from 17 different sources to make up the target talent audience for this initiative.

    The first phase was to launch an indirect email campaign that would iteratively messages to engage the prospects.

    The second phase was to engage this target audience going forward in order to fill future needs. We decided to pilot a Reliability Engineer Community on LinkedIn as the method of building the talent pipeline.

    Results
    The immediate/short-term results were to fill the 9 position with “world class” reliability engineers. The metrics were as follows:
    34% (866/2523) visited our landing page to learn more.
    24% (211/866) of the visitors applied for a job.
    10% joined the talent network, interested in future opportunities.
    Hired 9 key reliability engineers from 9 different sources

    The longer term results were to use our Reliability Engineering Community to fill future openings. We created the “Reliability Engineering on a Global Scale” Community. We again used an iterative (drip marketing) outreach to our initial mailing list of 2500. We curated the community with content from the leading reliability engineering sources and blogs; and moderated the conversation by enlisting our hiring teams to participate. The community grew from around 220 (April 2008) in our initial membership drive to over 1100. In 2009 and then again in 2010-2011, we were able to return to this community and make hires for the reliability engineering team.

  • Live Events: Inviting the Target Audience to Meet Your Hiring Team

    • February 2008 to February 2011
    Team Members: Marvin Smith, Dan Contreras, Tina Kettell, Erin Elsasser, Louise Cassell, Theresa de la Osa, Katie Robinson, Andy Palmer, James Temple, Megan Neumyer, Lisa Vailencour

    During my tenure at Microsoft Entertainment & Devices (E&D), I collaborated on over 20 live events. My role was to identify and invite the target talent for the event These events were in support of 17 businesses that compromised E&D including Microsoft Hardware; Windows Phone, Games, Zune etc. The metrics for the events became very predictable (numbers show are per 1000 targets: 8% (83) will RSVP with 5% (51) actually showing up at the event. 20 (20/51) prospects will evolve into 8 candidates. The respective Microsoft business will hire 2 of the 8 candidates immediately and another 2 over the next year.

    One of the interesting aspects to this approach is that begin the targeted email outreach 2 weeks prior to the event. The targets are typically from a “cold” list of names that are gather from competitor and aggregated from a variety of sources. They are segmented based on their skills, not their interest in Microsoft. In other words, we are able to reach out to total strangers that may not be looking for another job and by engaging them in a live event; they will become candidates for our teams.

  • Email Campaign for Procurement/Commodity Managers

    • January 2007 to March 2007
    Team Members: Marvin Smith, Lisa Vailencour

    Situation Analysis
    Immediate needs for procurement managers to support division level purchasing needs. New products release dates mandated swift action without lowering the “bar for talent.” The target talent need to come from a high volume consumer products background as Microsoft was ramping up it consumer products offerings.

    What We Did
    We created a target list of 5000+ procurement professional via competitive intelligence, research and name generation activities; we tapped into talent pools that our advertising was not reaching. We created an email campaign inviting the target audience to explore these opportunities by reading a online advertisement. We used the Jobster platform and monitored the results.

    Results
    38% of the target audience responded by clicking through to read the message resulting in 98 prospects and 38 referred prospects. 6% joined our talent network (for future interest) and 5% applied for the positions. We created a pool of 50+ qualified, interested and available candidates. We hired 4 Procurement Managers in 41 days (about ½ the normal time to fill) and 2 additional managers a few weeks later.
