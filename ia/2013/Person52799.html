Henry Kalaf's Experience

Festival and Development Manager

Tropfest

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Entertainment industry

June 2013 – Present (4 months) Sydney

As Festival and Development Manager I work closely with the Founder, Managing Director and Brand Manager to develop new partnerships, content initiatives and live engagement opportunities that not only integrates into the event, but also across various channels the global Tropfest brand holds (digital, broadcast and pr).

Tropfest is Australia’s most prestigious short film festival and one of its most iconic cultural events. It is also the largest short film festival in the world. At home, Tropfest is recognised for its enormous contribution to the development of the Australian film industry by providing a unique platform for emerging filmmakers to connect with new and expanded audiences for their work.

The annual short film competition selects 16 Finalists from an entry pool of an average 650 entries. Tropfest films are unique in that they have all been made specifically for Tropfest, will premiere at Tropfest and include the Tropfest Signature Item (or TSI), which changes each year. The TSI for Tropfest 22 is “CHANGE”.

The Festival attracts a live national audience of more than 150,000 people on a single night. The event is now hosted at Sydney’s Centennial Parklands, with simultaneous links to outdoor locations in major cities around the country and is also broadcast on SBS 2.

Tropfest currently has a number of opportunities for innovative, fresh and exciting brands to partner with in 2013 and beyond.

Film and Entries Manager

Tropfest

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Entertainment industry

October 2012 – June 2013 (9 months) Sydney

During my time in this role I oversaw the the entire entry process, preselection and festival selection working closely with the Festival Director/Founder. I implemented an entirely new system in which entrants could upload their films in HD and the preselection panel could watch over 700 films on any device, anytime, anywhere. I managed the day-to-day relationships with filmmakers, entry queries and curation of screenings around the world. As part of the festival, I produced the Tropfest Trailer with Director Alethea Jones and managed all content integration into the event production and the broadcast on SBS.
