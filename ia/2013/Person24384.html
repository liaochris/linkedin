Christopher Dyke, MBA's Projects

  • Hydrotracker

    • August 2011 to December 2011
    Team Members: Christopher Dyke, MBA, Sanaa Bhambri, Ayham Erekssousi, Mary Elizabeth

    In a collaborative project between NC State University and the Environmental Protection Agency, this project's objective was to create a product to modify the behavior of water consumers. The Hydrotracker monitors the flow rate through various outlets throughout the house (i.e. shower, sink, dishwasher, etc). The sensor then transmits this information to the internet where the user can view a breakdown of water usage, projected monthly water bill, and comparisons with historical data in realtime. Additionally, the website provides tailored tips on how to reduce consumption as well as the option to enter into community contests.

  • Increasing Client Participation in Foodbuy Programs

    • August 2011 to December 2011
    Team Members: Christopher Dyke, MBA, Siddharth Rastogi, MBA

    Foodbuy is a GPO for the foodservice industry. Their unique competitive advantage is the flexibility they provide to clients to use any manufacturer and distributor they desire. This unique flexibility doesn't come without it's challenges. This project conducted a strategic assessment to determine how Foodbuy could 1. increase the spend clients allocate to Foodbuy manufacturers, 2. capitalize on information that Foodbuy has on hand, 3. look into creating new value to clients to incentivize increased spend. Results of the project produced recommendations in three pivotal areas: Pricing Strategy, Contracting Practices, and Business Intelligence. Recommendations were supported through the use of Data Analysis and Information Technology.

  • Does Market Intelligence Improve Forecast Accuracy

    • August 2011 to December 2011
    Team Members: Christopher Dyke, MBA, James Wall

    This project was a collaboration between the Supply Chain Resource Cooperative (SCRC) and Novozymes. Novozymes currently has a forecast process very similar to many companies in which a forecast is produced, adjusted by sales representatives, and then re-run the forecast model. In this analysis there were several recommendations made to the process based on industry best practices.

  • The Development of a Business Intelligence Program

    • January 2011 to May 2011
    Team Members: Christopher Dyke, MBA, Gautham Paladugu, Michael Donahoe

    In the search to reduce product cost, one of the greatest advantages is intelligence. Business intelligence is the combination of quantitative information (data analytics), qualitative information (events influencing the supply chain), and the analysis to turn this information into actionable intelligence. This project created a unique system that integrates a qualitative information analysis into the data analysis process.

  • Hydro-Tracker Project

    • August 2011 to December 2011
    Team Members: Christopher Dyke, MBA, Sanaa Bhambri, Ayham Ereksousi, Kaushal Doshi, Mary Elizabeth Miller

    In a collaborative project between NC State University and the Environmental Protection Agency, this project's objective was to create a product to modify the behavior of water consumers.
    As a project for Product Design & Development course, my team was responsible to develop a product that aimed at impacting consumer behavior by providing relevant real-time information. In this semester long project, team successfully conducted market research to understand unmet needs and decide product attributes. In the end team delivered 3D model of the product and 2 stage launch strategy including financial estimates for EPA.

The sensor then transmits this information to the internet where the user can view a breakdown of water usage, projected monthly water bill, and comparisons with historical data in realtime.   • "Weather impact on grocery sales" (Dominick’s Finer Foods, Inc)

    • January 2012 to May 2012
    Team Members: Christopher Dyke, MBA, J Hollie Jones, MBA,

    Marketing Analytics course capstone project using SAS to correlate historical weather events with consumer purchase behavior patterns at 96 Dominick’s grocery stores in the Chicago area over the period from 1989-1994. This project made unique use of the renowned data set collected by University of Chicago and referenced in approximately 50 published academic papers. Team’s findings challenged popular notions including, for example, the generally accepted idea that there is a relationship between outdoor temperature and consumer soup purchase habits.
