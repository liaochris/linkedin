Darshan Sanghrajka's Summary

I believe in 3 simple things -

1) Smart people with potential, work better together rather than for each other. I want to keep working with brilliant people who rip up the rules to make things incredibly better for everyone;

2) Make people laugh as much as you can;

3) Always be learning - don't stand for mediocrity. Simple.

Though, the only thing that really matters when it comes down to it, is how we co-exist with each other...everything else is merely a means to an end. If you can convince me otherwise, I'll buy you lunch and if you can't, we split the bill.

I love solving problems (especially if it makes a difference to someone's life) by creating campaigns and/or innovation using creative, strategy, communications, technology and behavioural economics. This includes things like leading the strategy, marketing and implementation of Commonwealth Writers across the world for the Commonwealth Foundation, in order to create change through dialogue. Or for Forum For The Future & the Technology Strategy Board, branding and creating a digital tool for sustainability thinking - check it out at horizons.innovateuk.org

It doesn't always have to be serious or huge though - you can have fun at the same time - here's an example of something small but exciting that I enjoyed dreaming up and making happen - http://www.tinyurl.com/5zdsha

At the moment my focus is making State of Ambition happen as a thriving social enterprise to tackle the causes of youth unemployment and realise one million ambitions for 16-30 year olds in the UK by 2018 - (see more below about it).

I'm also available for consulting work around brand-building, creative digital comms, content and product strategy.

If you fancy a chat - I'd love to meet. Always keen to learn and share.

You can get in touch with me by emailing darshan@stateofambition.org or follow me on Twitter @chiefchimpanzee

Darshan Sanghrajka's Experience

Founder / Product & Marketing Director

State of Ambition

Public Company; 201-500 employees; ASX:AMB; Staffing and Recruiting industry

September 2012 – Present (1 year 4 months) London, United Kingdom

http://www.stateofambition.org

State of Ambition is a social enterprise designed to help young people stand out - we want to tackle unemployment in a bigger way than just finding jobs.

Our mission? To help young people stand out and create a State of Ambition - where ambitions are realised, relationships become more meaningful, talent is discoverable and communities are formed. Within 5 years, we want to make the Job Centre redundant and have realised over one million ambitions.

So, what is STATE OF AMBITION? It's a platform for aspiring young people campaign to make their ambitions a reality by building a community around them. They do this through creating a 21 day campaign to spread their 'Postcard of Ambition.

We are delighted to be supported by Brightside Trust, Big Issue Invest Salesforce Foundation, Nominet Trust, the Design Council and many other amazing people.

So far, this has been funded in three stages:

1) In August 2012, we won a grant from the Design Council and Nominet Trust for the Working Well Challenge - The task was to help reduce youth unemployment. We create a prototype from this over a 6 month period.

2) Since February 2013, I've gone full time on this, invested all my savings into turning this into a full social enterprise with a team of five full time employees and several partners.

3) In October 2013, Big Issue Invest have invested in SoA too - we are delighted to be working with them.

We were also finalists in the Wayra UnLtd 2013 application process but alas got pipped to the post - next year hey? :)

We are currently also looking for further investors too, so we can scale one region at a time.

70% of all our profits will go to provide offline support to young people, through mentoring, training and scholarships. This will happen via The State of Ambition Foundation. (If you would like to donate, please do let us know - we'd be so grateful! You can reach me at darshan@stateofambition.org . Thanks!)
