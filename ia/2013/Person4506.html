Alicia Arinella's Experience

President and CEO & Lead Producer

On the Leesh Productions

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Entertainment industry

October 2002 – Present (10 years 9 months) New York City

On the Leesh Productions is a New York City based company devoted to the production of innovative, energetic and challenging film, theater and webseries.

Producer of 11 short films and one feature, Belly. Short films include - What are the Odds?, The Muse: A Girl's Delicious Daydream About a Dangerous Man, Soap Scum, Table for Three, Compliments to Amanda, The Things We Know, Denim, Claire, Trevor's BIg Break, Kung Pow Wow and Milestone.

Our films have been accepted at over 30 film festivals - including Cannes Short Film Corner, San Francisco Independent, Sarasota Film Festival, Atlanta Film Festival, Nantucket Film Festival, and the Durango Independent Film Festival. The Muse won 2nd place Short Film Experimental at the 2006 Indie Gathering.

Producer of over ten plays and three web-series. The In-Betweens of Holly Malone, In the Can, thE SEXually. To view - please visit www.ontheleesh.com

On the Leesh's latest foray is What You Can Do, a series of 1 minute videos that show people what do do about social or environmental issues if they only have one minute of time to give. Various videos have screened on PBS WNET Affiliates - WLIW, THIRTEEN and NJTV. Over the past three years, What You Can Do has grown to partner with over 100 not for profits and has catalogued more than 300 videos. Awards received: 2012 Accolade Award for Best Use of Video for Social Change, 2012 Indie Fest Award for Best Video, Contemporary Issues/Raising Awareness, 2012 Best Documentary Series LAWebFest, and 2013 Webby Award Honoree for Public Service & Activism (Channel). The whole project can be seen at - www.whatyoucando.com

In addition, On the Leesh produces corporate videos for companies big and small. Some clients include: PRG Lighting, Pfizer, Wahl Clipper Corporation, Young Audiences New York, Christian Cota and SDI Laundry Solutions. Their video series for Super Styling Sessions has won - Best DVD Series and Best DVD from The Barkleigh Awards.

Producer - Ken Roberson Benefit

Our Time Theatre Company

November 2004 – April 2005 (6 months)

Our Time Theatre Company is an artistic home for young people who stutter. Once a year, they honor a person who serves as a role model to the community. In 2005, we honored Ken Roberson, choreographer of Avenue Q.

Lead Producer - Issue's Project: Democracy Project
Naked Angels, in collaboration with The Culture Project, joined forces to produce The Issues Project: Democracy, a collage of short plays, music and theatrical presentations written and performed by some of the country’s leading playwrights and artists. This unique and timely event will take place in between the National Conventions and the National Elections, beginning performances on September 13 – October 2, 2004 at The Culture Project’s 45 Below.
