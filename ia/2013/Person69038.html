Josh Paul's Summary

I'm a software engineer and video geek who is eager to solve problems through innovation, the implementation of new technologies, and exploring the edges of possibility. I thrive in challenging and creative environments, and endeavor to deliver solutions that help people live and work more productively and efficiently.

I have been developing iPhone/iPad applications since 2008, having a background in Cocoa/WebObjects provided an advantage to learning the SDK. I have helped develop 15+ published applications in the AppStore, as well as Enterprise solutions for a variety of markets.

I strive to provide leading-edge solutions for emerging problems, particularly in the digital video market. Since 1996 I have provided software and service solutions to entertainment production companies throughout Los Angeles and New York.

I am an active member of the Producer's Guild of America, a published author, and speaker. My books, "Digital Video Hacks" (O'Reilly; ISBN 0596009461) and "Videoblogging" (Wiley; ISBN 0470037881) are available in multiple languages, and have (hopefully) helped people understand how to produce and distribute digital video with a high level of competence.

    • United States Patent Application 11/969863
    • Filed January 4, 2008
    Inventors: Josh Paul

    A system creates interactive video advertisements, using elements provided by a client, and templates created using a node-based visual programming language. Client provided elements may include multimedia files, including but not limited to still images, text, and temporal files--such as video, music, and sound effects.

  • Verification system determining whether a predefined segment within a media program has been displayed

    • United States Patent Application 11/139300
    • Filed May 27, 2005
    Inventors: Josh Paul

    Verification system determines whether a viewer's terminal displayed a segment within an media program. The system includes embedding markings within the media program that indicate an identifier associated with the media program, the starting and ending points of the segment, and a mechanism for determining whether the segment was displayed within a predetermined time range. An observation application on the terminal where the media program is displayed may be capable of sending information regarding the display of a segment to the host server, which then may verify that the segment has been displayed and forwards this verification to a third party, such as an advertiser, who has agreed to provide compensation each time the segment is displayed on the terminal.
