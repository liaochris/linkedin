Chris Lauretani, MS,PT,CSCS,CEES,CKTP,TPI CGFI's Summary

Christopher Lauretani, MS, PT, CSCS, CEES, CKTP, TPI CGFI, is a licensed Physical Therapist in New York and Connecticut, a National Strength & Conditioning Association, Certified Strength & Conditioning Specialist (CSCS), a Board Certified Ergonomics Evaluation Specialist (CEES) a Certified Kinesio Taping Practitioner (CKTP) and a Titleist Performance Institute Golf Medical Professional (TPI CGFI). Chris has worked extensively with professional, college, and high school athletes since the start of his career when he served as a Physical Therapist for the Philadelphia 76’ers of the NBA. In addition to his other professional credentials, Chris is recognized as a Certified Coach for USA Track and Field and holds a coaching certification from the USA Weightlifting Association. He has been asked to speak locally and nationally about topics specific to rehabilitation, sports performance training and healthcare management, as well as offering his expertise in the digital media market via web contributions. Most recently, Chris has provided injury prevention and athletic enhancement online content for the Run S.M.A.R.T. Project and served on the 2013 US Open Wellness Team at Merion GC.

Chris has served as a committee member and consultant to a number of professional organizations. In addition to his position as President of Symmetry, he has worked as the Head Physical Therapist for the Long Island Lizards and New Jersey Pride Professional Lacrosse Teams of MLL. In 2010, Chris was awarded the prestegious Westchester Business Council's "Rising Stars: 40 Under 40" business award. Chris has also worked as the Physical Therapist on Spike TV's hit reality show, "Pro's vs. Joes". Throughout his career, Chris has been fortunate to work with a miriad of entertainers, sports professionals and celebrities including the Radio City Rockettes, cast members of Saturday Night Live and the Regis and Kelly Show, and executives of HBO Sports and NBC News to name a few.

Chris Lauretani, MS,PT,CSCS,CEES,CKTP,TPI CGFI's Publications

  • Comparison of motor unit action potential characteristics and hand dominance using monopolar needle electrodes in the abductor pollicis brevis and abductor digiti minimi muscles.

    • Electromyography & Clinical Neurophysiology
    • January 2003
    Authors: Chris Lauretani, MS,PT,CSCS,CEES,CKTP,TPI CGFI, Michael Fink, Roger Nelson

    This study examined specific electrical characteristics of voluntary single motor unit action potentials (SMUAPs): amplitude, duration, phase change, and rate of rise. These characteristics, which were detected from two intrinsic muscles of the hand--the abductor pollicis brevis and the abductor digiti minimi--were compared to hand dominance. Forty subjects participated in the study. Five characteristics were detected from each muscle using the quadrant technique while the subject produced a minimal isometric contraction. Based on results of an ANOVA [two-factor with replication] test, our study revealed no significant difference between muscles in the dominant and non-dominant hands. Descriptive statistics for each muscle characteristic are presented. This study has identified parameters for SMUAP characteristics detected in non-impaired individuals ranging in age from 20 to 43 years. The normative parameters serve as a valuable base from which one may examine potential neuronal damage from cumulative trauma disorders.

    Nelson RM, Cauley T, Fink M, Lauetani C, Simonson T. Comparison of motor unit action potential characteristics and hand dominance using monopolar needle electrodes in the abductor pollicis brevis and abductor digiti minimi muscles. Electromyography & Clinical Neurophysiology. 2003 Jan-Feb;43(1): 17-22.
