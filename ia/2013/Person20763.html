Casey Hall's Publications

  • The Slippery Slope and its Tendency to Result in Poor Policy Decisions

    • University of St. Thomas Journal of Law & Public Policy
    • 2007
    Authors: Casey Hall

    When evaluating a potential outcome of a disputed issue, a ‘slippery slope’ argument can almost always be made. In the abstract, it looks like this: if acceptable proposal, X, is adopted, unacceptable position, Y, will inevitably follow. A slippery slope argument urges policy makers to base their decision on fear of an avoidable result rather than the merits of the policy in question. While the slippery slope argument is often effective, its use often leads to sub-optimal policy decisions.
