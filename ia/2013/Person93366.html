Michel Heuvelmans' Summary

Michel is the current Managing Director of NetPower Solutions Belgium, an IT consultancy and software development company. As senior ICT consultant, his latest assignment is with Belfius Insurance where he is Programme Manager for the Solvency II programme.

At the same time, Michel is still responsible for Belfius' Sourcing Partnership with an Indian company, an assignment which started as Programme Manager Strategic Sourcing for Dexia Insurance Belgium and Dexia Bank Belgium.

Before that, Michel served at the Flemish employment agency VDAB where he was leading the “Strategic new projects” division within the software development department. Before that he was with the Belgian Post Group where he managed the Postpunt / Pointposte cross-ICT programme. Michel also served in several other companies in Belgium and abroad.

Michel has a lot of experience in project, programme and portfolio management (3PM), process-driven development, e-business, e-government and the synergy between traditional businesses and internet technology in general, both in the private and public sector. He can furthermore rely on his international experience, his language skills and his broad network.

Michel studied Civil Engineering, has been asked several times to give seminars in the USA and in Europe, and holds an exclusive degree from the Austrian Chamber of Commerce. He’s also served in several associations, and in 2000 was elected for one year as chairman of JCiE, representing 36000 young European entrepreneurs.

Specialties: Use of internet technologies to make business automation software; IT crisis management; change management; introduction of best practices and proven methodologies (CMMI, PMI, RUP, Prince2, V-Model, ITIL); e-business, CRM and ERP products.

Michel started this assignment by organising the change management needed for the introduction of new project management tools at Dexia Insurance Belgium.

After a number of months, he assisted in streamlining IT processes and methodology over multiple Dexia entities. This resulted in the Dexia Project Methodology based on PMI, Waterfall and Prince2, which was introduced at DIB.

Michel was responsible for coaching and training to ensure proper usage of this methodology and its corresponding tools by all IT teams.

Michel further streamlined this methodology for use with the Indian software development partner of Dexia.

Programme Manager

VDAB

Government Agency; 1001-5000 employees; Government Administration industry

April 2006 – January 2008 (1 year 10 months)

The Flemish employment agency VDAB contracted Michel to lead a development division involved in strategic new projects. In order to deliver very complex projects with a lot of integration challenges on-time and within budget, it was necessary to change the software development department into a process-driven matrix organisation.

The change management effort lasted for more than a year. CMMi was used to measure the department’s maturity level, a mix of RUP and Prince2 was introduced as project management methodology, V-Model and basics of T-MAP were introduced for testing and a PMO (Project Management Office) was set up.

The department was streamlined and made more flexible. Michel was also partly responsible for HR matters such as selection, hiring, solving resource conflicts, capacity planning, evaluations and contract endings. In this change period, continuous motivation and HR skills were crucial to achieve the set goals.

Belgian Post Group

Privately Held; 10,001+ employees; Logistics and Supply Chain industry

July 2004 – March 2006 (1 year 9 months)

Michel was asked to lead the PostPunt / Pointposte IT programme. A Postpunt / Pointposte is an independent postal sales office run by a partner company (such as a warehouse) or a self-employed individual.

This mission involved setting up a new team to alter existing applications to run at Partner companies, to design and develop new IT applications - such as the financial application which calculates Partners’ commissions and fees - and to research and solve security issues.

Necessary organisational changes were made. People management and HR skills had a big emphasis.

Michel was also involved in specific changes to make to company more customer-friendly and service-oriented. To this purpose, the helpdesk was reorganised and ITIL foundations were introduced in the Operations department.

Project Manager

Siemens Business Services

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; si; Electrical/Electronic Manufacturing industry

August 2001 – January 2002 (6 months)

SBS' biggest customer in Belgium used to be the Flemish Government, with an annual turnover of more than 50 million EUR, all departments confused.

Michel was asked to become Senior Project Manager, effectively leading those projects for the Flemish Government which had problems on a technical, financial or operational level:
- GKB (Gemeenschappelijk Klanten Bestand): The GKB was the common database between different customer entities of the Flemish Government. Michel successfully managed this first attempt to merge major government databases.
- Mecenas: this was an application to manage the different subsidies attributed to artists and cultural initiatives. Michel lead the project from start to finish.
- Kasbeheer: a global application to optimise the cash situation of the Flemish Government. The project was in a crisis phase when Michel took over but was handed over in a stable situation when Michel left.

CTO

Veridis

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry

July 2000 – December 2000 (6 months)

Veridis SA was a seed-capital start-up company. Originating from Highware (a company specialised in Apple security solutions), Veridis focused on internet-related security and trust services, such as encryption technology based on PGP keys, online notary services and ‘registered’ e-mail.

Michel was one of four Directors, and was hired in the position of CTO to do the following:
1. Select and hire the R&D team.
2. Coach this team within the R&D budget (own P&L).
3. Develop with this team a series of 4 new products as described in the Business Plan within a time frame of only 5 months.
4. Give advice on potential sales strategies.
5. Seek potential strategic partnerships to boost the company’s sales.
6. Implement ISO 9000 quality standards in development procedures.

This mission was conducted successfully and Michel handed over responsibility to a colleague once the start-up mission was fulfilled and the 4 products were ready for commercialisation.

Start-Up Manager

Econocom

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Information Technology and Services industry

December 1999 – July 2000 (8 months)

In 1999, the company made a strategic decision to be present in the ever-growing e-business market. Michel was hired to help launch a division specialising in e-business, e-procurement and e-solutions in general.

His job consisted of:
1. Finding and hiring the team of e-business developers the company needed.
2. Following up on the operational side, according to the Business Plan and the P&L status.
3. Writing the corporate “Internet products white pages”, which would cover the basic products and services, but also the marketing and sales approach, an Internet lexicon, etc…
4. Support the sales staff and teach them how to sell Internet related services, especially on the field.
5. Supervise all pricing offers towards the customers.
6. Finally, sell these services directly to the key accounts.

Michel left after 8 months, when the initial targets were reached and the start-up phase was ended, handing over final responsibility to a colleague-consultant.
