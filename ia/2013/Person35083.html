Dorothy Hoskins' Projects

  • William B. Hoskins, American Composer, legacy

    • June 2008 to Present
    Team Members: Dorothy Hoskins

    William B. Hoskins was a profoundly gifted composer and musician who had local fame (Jacksonville, Fl) but never achieved national recognition. He was a humanist and a master teacher of composition whose life work was as much recognizing and helping develop the talents of others as working on his own compositions.

    Since his death, the only legacy that the Hoskins family has been able to bring to fruition has been the website http://www.williambhoskins.org/ and publication of some of his works for trombone. He created a broad range of chamber, vocal/choral and some symphonic works, which were not much preformed outside of Florida and the southern states. However, he would wish to be known for his pacifist compositions, such as Ballad of the Trumpet Boy, Lost Lands, and Requiem for the Six Million.

    Hoskins was a pioneer in 1969 with the first electronic music studio in Florida, and worked with Bob Moog on some Moog synthesizer instruments. Most of his electronic compositions are irretrievable due to the analog synthesizer components used in them.

    His scores are archived at the Swisher Library of Jacksonville University in Jacksonville, Fl. Most of his contemporaries are gone, but his students still express their regard for him as teach, mentor and friend.
