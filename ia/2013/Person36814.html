Donna Wood's Summary

I grew up on the shores of Lake Sakakwea, on the Fort Berthold Indian Reservation, in western North Dakota a.k.a. the "Oil-Boom" region of the state. I was immersed in two cultures, Native American and Irish. I am not Native American.

I worked as a Diversity & Inclusion professional for six years as an administrator, in a nonprofit organization, spanning four cities, two counties, in two states (ND & MN.) The entire world entered my own backyard. I worked with employers and civic leaders to help build inclusive workplaces and communities for the refugee population from all over the world.

Throughout my lifetime, I have followed the path of a leader through involvement in organizations within the community: 4-H, FHA (now FBLA), The American Legion Auxillary (ND Girls' State Alumni,) listed in Who's Who in America (1986), International Order of Job's Daughters (MN) - Past Secretary of the Bethel Executive Council (2008), received the YWCA Cass Clay Women of the Year award- 2009, and served on the North Dakota State College of Science Diversity Council AYs 2011 - 2013. I draw on these experiences, as well as personal experience to assist individuals, businesses, and organizations to develop effective leadership strategies.

As an employment investigator (Registered P.I. in ND,) I was hired for one of two reasons: Risk Management or Suspected Misconduct.

I offer my experience, my time, and my talents to businesses, organizations and individuals to assist them to emerge into the global society. Let's get to work today!

Employment Investigator

Registered Private Investigator

January 2002 – January 2003 (1 year 1 month) North Dakota

As an employment investigator I was hired for one of two reasons: Risk Management or Suspected Misconduct.

This was one of the greatest learning experiences of my life. I learned to value the actions of people over their words. I learned to be observant, to assess body language, and to evaluate human relations within select groups of people. I learned that not everything, or everyone is what and who they say they are. I also learned that things and people can be so much more than they present themselves to be. I learned to identify the internal strengths of individuals and to capitalize on those strengths. I am most grateful for this opportunity of life as it has been one of the greatest personal assets I have carried with me.
