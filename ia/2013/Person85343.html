Marc Sirkin's Summary

Marc Sirkin has spent close to 20 years focused on creating digital change by enabling businesses to use new technologies in driving revenue and creating new organizational structures designed to leverage the internet. His focus on digital change and transformation has led him to work in B2B and B2C as well as inside startups and enterprise class organizations. He has led teams as far ranging and diverse as product development, marketing, branding, digital, IT, CRM and direct mail. He has the agility and maturity to both lead and manage as well as to effectively contribute individually to a wide range of opportunities.

Marc works for PwC as a Director and is as always, focused on digital strategy and engagement. He has a passion for the Maker movement and anyone doing something new and disruptive with digital technology.

Marc Sirkin's Projects

  • Hacking Autism

    • May 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Marc Sirkin, Alison Dyer

    When touch-enabled computing was introduced to the world, no one could have anticipated that this technology might help open up a new world of communication, learning and social possibilities for autistic children. Yet it has.

    Hacking Autism is a story of technology and hope and the difference it's making in the lives of some people who need it most.

    Hacking Autism doesn't seek to cure autism, but rather it aims to facilitate and accelerate technology-based ideas to help give those with autism a voice.

  • Light It Up Blue

    • March 2009 to Present
    Team Members: Marc Sirkin, Kai MacMahon, Alison Dyer, Dana Marnane

    Light It Up Blue ("LIUB") is Autism Speaks cornerstone awareness and fundraising event. Tied to World Autism Awareness Day, we celebrate by "lighting" the analog and digital world blue. Our digital platform engaged more than 70,000 registered users and our entire community through April on a variety of channels ranging from direct mail to digital/web to mobile, retail and more.
