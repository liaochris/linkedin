Carolyn Arnold's Experience

Author

Carolyn Arnold

June 2006 – Present (7 years 4 months)

Carolyn Arnold had a love for writing back in her teen years, but her passion was reignited in 2006 when a fellow employee said, "tell me a story". Since then, Carolyn has written 9 novels, 2 short stories, 1 novella, and has plans for many more.

Her Madison Knight series has become an international Kindle best-seller, with all three hitting the top 100 in Police Procedurals in the US. Set in the fictional city of Stiles, the series is designed as a stand alone, which means you can read these books out of order and still pick up on the series' characters and the storyline. The series follows Major Crimes Detective Madison Knight who is fueled by determination to find justice for the victims. She is a strong, independent female amidst a career dominated by men. If you love a female protagonist lead, she invites you to meet Madison for yourself. Three novels in the series are currently available: Ties that Bind, Justified, and Sacrifice.

Carolyn has also written Eleven, an FBI thriller, which follows a team of four while they try to stop a serial killer from reaching his eleventh victim. Eleven was noted as a recommended read on The Examiner's "Top 12 Fiction Books of 2011".

Her publications Rings of a Tree and Hart's Choice both have their profit proceeds being donated to charity. Proceeds from Rings of a Tree will go the Breast Cancer Society of Canada, and the proceeds from Hart's Choice will go to a charity near and dear to Carolyn's heart--the Alzheimer's Society of Canada in memoriam of her father-in-law.

Carolyn was born in 1976 in a rural town of Ontario, Canada. She currently lives with her husband, and two beagles in a city near the well-known Canadian center Toronto.

For the latest news on upcoming releases, please visit: http://carolynarnold.net/
