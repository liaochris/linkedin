Marla Miller's Summary

At MarlaMiller.com's 'MarketingtheMuse Workshops I shepherd authors on the road to publication. I teach annually at The Santa Barbara Writers Conference, The Southern California Writers Conference and (recently added) The Writers Digest Conference. My experience in writing for publication spans 25 years. When I started on my own road, I was an academic/psychiatric RN practitioner with three small kids. Today, my work melds my teaching/mentoring/interviewing experiences and my writing/marketing life.
I am a writer/editor/ writing workshop facilitator whose MarketingtheMuse workshops are well received by writers at every level. I am 'With author' for two indie published books-one is in fitness, the other in health care. My professional expertise in both areas make me a good match for these projects. I love our new options in e-publishing. My only concern is some folks think that now it's easy to be a writer. There are no short cuts to mastering this craft. Folks on some social media sites don't want to hear this but I tell it as I see it, which is why you like my style or you don't. Writers may get their works out there easier but they won't be read by more than a few. Poor story telling reads like poor story telling.....period!
Specialties: Finding story: My read/critique editing service zooms in on helping writers 'sculpt' their story. Testimonials on MarlaMiller.com offers solid evidence of this skill. I am a solid interviewer with excellent communication skills. My understanding of platform building & branding is solid, too. I have an innate ability to translate complex into simple, a skill honed during my nursing career.

When teaching workshops, my focus is always on 'openings'-the first 2 pages of a manuscript, the Overview of a book proposal, the query letter--all introductions to authors' writings.

MarlaMiller.com - MarketingtheMuse

January 2003 – Present (10 years 10 months)

MarketingtheMuse was founded in 2003 when I first taught writers marketing strategies I learned-or didn't- when my first book was published by Simon & Schuster. Back then, we were on the brink of a new epublishing age that is now considered mainstream.
I have built a 'indie' writing & marketing business that sprouted from my many years of being a published writer & author never quite satisfied with the lack of control and input I had over my projects.
'Be careful what you wish for' applies here. I am now in control and most of the time, that's the way I like it. That said, it's also a lot of frickin work I didn't have to do when other folks were managing my content. Because I also 'walk the walk', I understand today's writers frustrations. So much to know besides crafting a story you hope others will want to read. Yet, it's exciting to have control of your 'art'...
Ah..the 'indie' writing life!
