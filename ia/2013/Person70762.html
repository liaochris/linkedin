Juliellen Sarver's Summary

Innovative urban planning consultant in affordable housing development, equitable active transportation and housing, senior housing, aging, and active transportation and active tourism (bike-walk).

Proven leader in guiding urban planning and development projects from concept to completion, including grant-writing, securing funding, complex financial and project management.

Pioneered HUD 202 – PACE joint development with non-profit client to provide affordable senior housing and healthcare facilities to enable and promote aging-in-place, including transportation to services. Provided housing development expertise and guidance to numerous non-profit organizations to construct truly affordable housing for seniors in rural areas and small towns throughout North Carolina.

Authored numerous active transportation and mobility articles and research studies on bicycle and pedestrian planning, bikeshare, carshare, return on investment of mobility strategies for health, senior housing and healthcare.

Available for writing, project consultation, speaking, technical assistance, project management. Please see list of publications below.

Specialties: active transportation, bicycle and pedestrian planning, transportation demand management (TDM), bikeshare, carshare, senior and affordable housing development, issues of affordable housing and transportation, project management, grant writing, development finance, writing.

Member of the American Institute of Certified Planners and the American Society of Landscape Architects.

Fluent in English and French; dual national USA and France

Juliellen Sarver's Experience

Senior Planning and Development Consultant

Sarver Housing Group, Inc.

August 2007 – Present (6 years 5 months) North Carolina and Virginia

Comprehensive development consultant specializing in creating sustainable and affordable housing for people with special needs and the elderly.

Secured over $50 million in grants and other funding for development of affordable, energy-efficient housing for low-income seniors and people with disabilities. Successful in winning numerous funding awards from the HUD 202 Capital Advance program, North Carolina Housing Finance Agency Supportive Housing Development Program, and various state and local funding sources.

Work with a variety of non-profit organizations to determine successful development teams, and coordinate development process from funding through construction and rent-up. Facilitate EarthCraft green building program, funding sources, real estate, zoning, and overall development process.

Projects have provided permanent, decent homes with supportive services for hundreds, if not thousands, of low-income seniors and people with disabilities.

    I am happy to be a contributor to the Public Health and Safety portion of this important project:

    “Mobility management” or “transportation demand management” (TDM) programs provide great bang for a very modest buck. The return on investment for corporations, government agencies, and consumers can be astronomical.

    These programs provide shorter trips, more trip options, reduced travel delay, as well as benefits to business and society, such as increased economic vitality and enhanced public health and safety. This project is developing a calculation method to estimate the return on investment in “TDM” programs.
