Dragos Iorgulescu's Summary

Dr. Iorgulescu graduated from the University of Bucharest top of his year in 1993 and the same year moved to Australia. He quickly passed the Australian Medical Council examinations and then completed his general surgical training at St. Vincent's Hospital in Melbourne and obtained his Fellowship of the Royal Australasian College of Surgeons in 2004.
He has a higher degree of Mastery of Surgery by research in cancer molecular biology from the University of Melbourne in 2002. In his thesis he described a new method of detecting micro-metastases in patients with solid tumours, which would help in early detection of spread and targeted cancer treatment.

In 2004 he worked one year as the only full-time Consultant General Surgeon in Mt. Gambier Hospital, a very busy remote area of need in South Australia. He gained broad experience in General Surgery during this time and has a good understanding of the needs of country patients and medical practitioners in Australia.
Following this he spent two years of subspecialty fellowship training in Hepato-Biliary and Pancreatic Surgery in major teaching hospitals, first at Queen Elizabeth Hospital and second at Royal Adelaide Hospital. In 2007 he was appointed as a Consultant Surgeon on the Hepato-Biliary and Pancreas Unit and the Trauma Service at Royal Adelaide Hospital. During this time he acquired further expertise in complex liver and pancreas resections, gallbladder and biliary.
Dr. Iorgulescu's main interest is in gallbladder, biliary tract, liver and pancreatic disease and in minimally invasive laparoscopic surgery, but he is trained as a general surgeon, therefore is happy to manage all general surgical conditions, including hernias, upper and lower gastro-intestinal conditions, perianal, skin lesions, minor urological and vascular procedures etc. Dr. Iorgulescu performs laparoscopic hernia repairs as well as upper GI endoscopies and colonoscopies, including interventional endoscopy: polypectomy, PEG insertion, etc.
