Deborah Gohrke, Ed.D., EDAC's Summary

I'm papered: I have a B.S. in Business, a M.S. in Ministry from Pepperdine University, and a Doctorate in Educational Leadership from Seattle University. My research is published as a academic book, The Role of Hope in Leadership (2008). I received my EDAC (Evidence-Based Design Accreditation & Certification) in 2012. That's enough paper for a pretty big litter box.

I'm experienced: I've lived a long time, hopefully not simply piling on repetitive years. I like to imagine my experience contributes to great discernment. My mother thinks so. Here is a thumbnail sketch of work related adventures.

I have served on four boards, most recently on the Board of Directors of First Place, a school for children who are experiencing homelessness, located in Seattle.

Once upon a time, long ago. I was a marketing Project Manager for Puget Sound Energy; Regional Sales & Marketing go-to-gal for TY. Inc., a plush toy company and manufacturer of the phenomenally successful Beanie Babies; Regional representative for the wonderful, but ultimately unsuccessful, Better World Toys; Vice President of Sales & Marketing for Northward Construction, “the most awarded builder in the Northwest” for Marketing and Merchandising Excellence awards. Then I started a distribution company with a partner in 1998. Magellan Inc. was an industry supernova. A much larger 100 year old company made an offer to purchase Magellan after only a few years; material assets were minimal, brand equity was enormous, personal equity gained from the experience - priceless.

It was back to school after Magellan. I LOVE learning; it is bloody exciting. Either as a student or an instructor, being in a classroom full of engaged adults wrestling with concepts and exercising skills that matter is good. Real good.

In the fall of 2011, I started my current business, fractalfotos, with my partner Tim.

Specialties

Evidence-based Design (EBD) studies in healthcare show views of nature, even in artwork, can alleviate stress and promote healing. Fractalfotos provides artwork appropriate for healthcare environments.
