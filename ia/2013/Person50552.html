Griffin Hammond's Publications

  • Media search literacy: The role of access as media technologies converge online

    • August 2009
    Authors: Griffin Hammond

    To be "search literate" is to possess the skills necessary to effectively find information online via search engines like Google. In my Masters thesis, I define "media search literacy" (MSL) as the ability to find media (like videos and music) through search engines. This study gauged college students’ level of MSL and identified which attributes separated the good searchers from the not-so-good.
