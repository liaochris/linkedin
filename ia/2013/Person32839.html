David Young's Summary

David R. Young is a professional home designer, the current president of Arcdesign (an architectural firm in Clearwater, Florida), an entrepreneur, and an author and speaker on the Political Spectrum.

He first encountered politics at a young age wherein his parents, who are Republicans, had a falling out with their friends who were Democrats. Although registered as an independent, Young's political views fall between Republican and Libertarian.

He felt something was wrong with politics; which drove him to research the political spectrum. This eventually led to the book "The Political Spectrum: Freedom vs. Enslavement"

The Political Spectrum: Freedom vs. Enslavement

Confused about the political spectrum? You won't be - not after you read The Political Spectrum: Freedom vs. Enslavement. This eye opening thesis reveals the natural Political Spectrum. You can take every political spectrum chart you have ever seen and toss it out the window. Politics has never been so clear.

The Political Spectrum: Freedom vs. Enslavement is a rare book which brings the whole field of politics into an understandable and manageable affair. It is a must read for anyone who has a concern about where our country is going and what must be done to turn this ship around and put it back on course... and you'll know what the destination is.

In The Political Spectrum: Freedom vs. Enslavement you’ll discover:
- The political philosophy which actually occupies the far Right and why
- The spectrum of political philosophies in a completely understandable and logical progression from right to left.
- How and why a political system based on freedom for the individual can decay toward enforced dependence (communism).
- How and why the Right can be dragged down into fascism, and how to avoid it.
- How the hidden affect of communism is contributing to the decay of America.
- Why some capitalists support socialist policies and how they profit by so doing.
- A clear distinction between Capitalism and the Free Market Economy.

“I didn’t start out to write a book.” Young revealed. “Last August, after googling the political spectrum and becoming more confused, I started sketching out the spectrum on my computer, working out how the spectrum would look if it were based on enslavement and freedom at its extreme ends. The notes kept growing – the paper size kept growing – by the time it was 3 feet x 4 feet, I was astonished with the results and it occurred to me I should write a book."

And he did!
