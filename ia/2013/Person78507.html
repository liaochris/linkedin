Larry Tolpin's Summary

It’s a little known fact that Larry and Clark Kent are never in the same place at the same time. Coincidence?

You see, Larry is one of those unique advertising super heroes who can manage and inspire the whole agency and their clients at the same time.

After all, how many Presidents and CEOs do you know that have also served in dual roles as Chief Creative Officer at two of the largest worldwide advertising agencies?

Larry is the President and Chief Creative & Innovation Officer of Paradise Communications, Digital & Entertainment, a whole new kind of transmedia storytelling company for the digital age and one of the premier and top award-winning travel, lifestyle and luxury brand advertising agencies in the world.

His focus is on creating innovative, world-class ideas and user experiences that make consumers talk about and fall in love with the brands, no matter where or how they might encounter those brands.

Larry is a proven leader, innovator, and builder of brands and agencies.

At JWT, he served as one of the company's two Worldwide Creative Directors and CEO of JWT's Western Operations and JWT Canada.

At BBDO, Larry was the North American Creative Chairman, CEO and Chief Creative Officer of BBDO South, and Chief Creative Officer of BBDO Canada.

At Ypartnership (now MMGY Global), Larry was the President and Chief Creative Officer of the global agency.

Some of the global brands Larry has overseen include Apple, Pepsi, Nike, Walt Disney, Visa, Marriott, Delta Airlines, Fairmont Resorts, Wynn Resorts, Bahamas Tourism, Costa Rica Tourism, Mexico Tourism, Busch Entertainment, HBO, Sony, Darden Restaurants, De Beers, Diageo, FedEx, Sprint, Jeep, and Unilever.

Larry has won over 500 major creative awards from Cannes Lions to Clios for creative work across all platforms. In addition, his companies have been named Agency of the Year five times with Larry being named Creative Director of the Year three times by global trade publications.

Specialties

• A technology-driven leader with a history of engaging consumers through innovative digital and social media content and transmedia storytelling campaigns.

Larry Tolpin's Experience

President and Chief Creative & Innovation Officer

Paradise Communications, Digital and Entertainment Group

December 2012 – Present (11 months) Tampa/St. Petersburg

Larry is the President and Chief Creative & Innovation Officer of Paradise Communications, Digital & Entertainment, based in Tampa/St. Petersburg, Florida.

Paradise is a whole new kind of transmedia storytelling company for the digital age and one of the world's top-ranked creative travel, lifestyle, and luxury brand advertising agencies. Larry and the Paradise team recently dominated the New York 2013 Adrian Awards, the "Oscars" of worldwide travel, destination and hospitality marketing and creativity, winning 18 medals, including six gold and Best of Show. In addition, they also won another 16 medals at Visit Florida's 2012 - 2013 Flagler Tourism Awards, including winning an unprecedented three Best of Show for three different clients.

Besides co-managing the organization, Larry sets the creative tone and standards for the company. He oversees all of the company’s creative operations, including advertising, digital, social media, and the film studios. His role is a combination of innovation and inspiration, strategy and creative solutions, and helping their clients lead and succeed in the digital and social media age.

Larry believes creative is not just a department, but it is a way of thinking for the whole company. He strongly believes that there are no boundaries, no silos, and no rules. There are only brilliant ideas from brilliant partnerships across all disciplines. His focus is on creating world-class ideas that consumers talk about and fall in love with the brands, no matter where and how they might encounter those brands.

Starting with consumer and business insights, Larry combines creativity and innovation to inspire people and change behavior. Larry’s tramsmedia storytelling ideas embrace the most appropriate platforms, whether harnessing the power of TV and film, social media, mobile, interactive experiences or groundbreaking content creation.

President & Chief Innovation Officer

Intermark

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry

September 2010 – July 2012 (1 year 11 months) Birmingham - Los Angeles - South Florida

Larry was the President and Chief Innovation Officer of Intermark Group, one the largest digital, social media and hybrid advertising agencies in the country with over 150 digital/social media professionals and over 100 traditional brand specialists.

Larry worked in partnership with his team and clients to deliver fresh insights, innovative solutions, cutting-edge technology, and world-class creative that created powerful relationships with consumers and brands.

Through the conception, creation, and development of online communities, branded content, and rich user experiences, Larry and his team launched dozens of new brands and revived countless classic brands. Some of the global brands included Toyota, The History Channel, Nucor, Mohawk Worldwide Industries, Krispy Kreme, and Pinkberry.

The agency’s work won numerous creative awards, including being honored at the 2011 Cannes Lions Festival as one of the top integrated campaigns in the world.

President and Chief Creative Officer

Ypartnership

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Leisure, Travel & Tourism industry

2004 – September 2010 (6 years) Orlando - New York - Los Angeles - London - Honolulu - Mexico City

In 1994, Larry was recruited to YPB&R (now MMGY Global), the world's leading travel, hospitality and entertainment marketing company, as President and Chief Creative Officer. Working in partnership with Peter Yesawich, CEO, and Gary Sain, CMO, Larry led the company’s rebranding efforts to Ypartnership and transformed the agency from a research driven, traditional advertising agency into a world-class creative, 360-degree, integrated award-winning agency for the digital age.

Besides, co-managing the company, Larry set the creative tone and standards for the agency.  He oversaw all of Ypartnership’s creative operations as well as led the entire organization to think beyond the traditional process and to engage brands holistically across multiple platforms and touchpoints.   

Serving in his dual role, Larry helped Ypartnership win over 25 significant new pieces of business and put the agency and Florida on the global creative stage.

A writer by craft, Larry penned strategic integrated campaigns for select brands and clients including the Walt Disney Company, Wynn Las Vegas, the new Fontainebleau Miami Resort, the Bahamas Tourist Board, the Mexico Tourist Board, Canyon Ranch Resorts & Spas, Steamboat Ski Resort, Outrigger Hawaiian and Pacific Resorts, Hard Rock Casino & Resort, Marriott International Resorts, Fairmont Resorts Worldwide, Hilton International Hotel & Resorts, Darden Restaurants and numerous other tourist boards and CVBs in Florida as well as across the country and the world.

Worldwide Creative Director of JWT and CEO of JWT West and JWT Canada

JWT

Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; WPP; Marketing and Advertising industry

1997 – 2003 (6 years) New York City - San Francisco - Los Angeles - Toronto - Montreal

In the fall of 1997, Larry was recruited by Chris Jones, CEO of J Walter Thompson Worldwide to be one of two Worldwide Creative Directors for the entire company as well as CEO of JWT’s U.S. Western Operations and all of Canada.

Larry was also appointed a member of the Worldwide Board, the Worldwide Executive Group and the North American Management Board.

At JWT, Larry helped develop a new global creative philosophy and set of creative standards that led the entire company to think beyond the traditional agency process and to take a more holistic 360-degree approach to brand solutions, including a major emphasis on digital and new technologies.

Larry also help developed and managed a semi-annual, total communications creative review and report card for every major global brand and JWT office.

In less than two years, he helped earn JWT an overall “B” rating from Adweek up from a “C” and a creative rating of “B” up from a “D”.

Some of the global brands Larry oversaw included De Beers Diamonds, Rolex, Nike, BV Wines, Sprint Telecommunications, Bermuda Tourism, California Tourism, Princess Cruises, Diageo, Unilever, Kraft, Kellogg's, Nestle, Babystyle.com, Siemens, Chevron, Clairol, Merill Lynch, Ford, and Pepsi International.

In addition, Larry helped JWT Worldwide and JWT West win 14 major pitches including 10 new clients and 4 major new assignments from existing clients.

CEO and Chief Creative Officer of BBDO South, CCO of BBDO Canada, BBDO Toronto and BBDO Vancouver

BBDO Worldwide

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; OMC; Marketing and Advertising industry

1987 – 1997 (10 years) Atlanta - Toronto - Vancouver - New York City

Larry cut his teeth as a creative director at BBDO and made his mark in Canada, the United States and around the world.

He rose from Executive Creative Director of Baker Lovick/BBDO Vancouver to Chief Creative Officer of BBDO Canada to CEO, President and Chief Creative Officer of BBDO South. He was also named North American Creative Chairman for BBDO Worldwide and a member of the North American Board.

Under Larry's creative leadership, BBDO won over 30 new pieces of business including major brands such as Apple, A&W, BC Lottery, Bell Canada, Canadian Pacific Hotels & Resorts, Canadian Tourism, Chrysler Canada, Costa Rica Tourism, Dairy Queen, FedEx, Frito-Lay, Kraft, Jeep, M&M/Mars, Miller Brewing USA, Molson, Nike, Pizza Hut, Pepsi International, Sony, TSN Sports Network and the Yellow Pages.

The result? Larry helped double the billing and revenue BBDO Canada, winning Agency of the Year five times by major trade publications, including Advertising Age's International Agency of the Year, the first time a Canadian agency had ever won that honor. In addition, BBDO Canada became the largest agency in Canada and BBDO South became the largest agency in the Southeastern United States.

Under Larry's creative guidance, BBDO offices won over 500 creative awards including the Cannes Lions Festival, the Clios, D&AD, the London International Awards, and the New York One Show. Larry was also named Creative Director of the Year three times by trade publications.
