Judi Window's Experience

President/CEO

NH Granite State Ambassador

September 1996 – Present (17 years 1 month) New Hampshire

NHGSA is a non-profit, charitable, organization of trained residents and industry professionals dedicated to adding value to the state of NH by promoting and protecting our sense of place and supporting and enhancing the communities where we live and work.

Accomplishments: Founded the organization in 1996, incorporated it as a non-profit, charitable organization in 1999. I worked with NH College in developing a certification/training program specializing in hospitality and tourism, graduating more than 1,300 people as tourism professionals and volunteers. Along with our board of directors, I have worked closely with the State of NH and other governmental agencies and tourism organizations to provide support and value to the traveling public. Over our 18 year history I have worked to develop a team environment for our staff to ensure the operations, funding, and membership of the organization continues beyond the founders.

Customer Relations Manager

Shorty's Mexican Roadhouse

Southern New Hampshire's favorite, locally owned, Tex-Mex restaurant located in Bedford, Manchester, and Nashua.

Accomplishments: Worked collaboratively with owners to develop and execute a plan to move from a traditional marketing format to a socially engaging marketing strategy. Established, energized, and now maintain multiple social media platforms to encourage brand recognition, increase sales, and streamline daily promotions, including adding a “Single Platform” menu service to enhance visibility to both the daily and special menus.

Instructor

Nackey Loeb School of Communications

July 2009 – Present (4 years 3 months) Manchester New Hampshire

The NSLSOC is a private non-profit school founded in 1999 by Mrs. Loeb, late president and publisher of The Union Leader. The school fosters interest, integrity, and excellence in journalism and other forms of communication by educating students of all ages and providing them with the tools and knowledge to improve their skills.

Accomplishments: Developed and taught the first Social Media for Business curriculum for the school. I have engaged over 300 students of all ages and experiences, through five 6-week courses, nine workshops, and various specialty speaking engagements over the past 4 years. I also work closely with administration to raise awareness and funding for the school through committee work, speaking engagements, and interviews.

Founding Partner

311 Management Services

September 2008 – September 2012 (4 years 1 month)

311MS specializes in assisting small businesses, non-profits, and governmental agencies problem solve through information gathering, enhanced communications, social media solutions, promotions, training, staff support, and project management including e-mail marketing, bookkeeping, and special events.

Assistant Vice President

Amoskeag Bank

February 1982 – September 1993 (11 years 8 months)

First NH Bank | FDIC | Amoskeag Bank, various positions [1982 – 1993]

On October 10, 1991, seven banks failed in New Hampshire. These seven banks represented 25% of all banking assets in the state. The closing of all seven banks on one day was a significant economic event for New Hampshire.

Accomplishments: After an on again, off again career path in banking that started as a file clerk when I was 18, I never thought the most influential experience in my business career would be working with the FDIC and the Bank of Ireland to manage the operations of a separate asset pool established after the bank failures in 1991. I worked with a team of five managers, the Bank of Ireland (parent company of First NH Bank) and Price Waterhouse to ready $800 million in classified assets, reposed real estate, subsidiaries, and unwanted bank premises for resolution and ultimate acquisition by Banc One New Hampshire Asset Management, Inc. After the transition I went on to become an Assistant Vice President, managing 5 departments within the Special Assets Division for First NH Bank. My most memorable accomplishment was the development of programs and systems for reporting on special assets that moved the bank from a manual/paper process to a fully integrated desk-top computerized system for the work-out loan officer. This was monumental for the banking industry at the time.
