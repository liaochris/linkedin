H. Michael Young's Summary

Mr. Young has a wide pallet of skills that benefit healthcare simulation training environments: instructional technology support, instructional design, scenario development and programming, teaching/training, technical skills assessment and remediation, project planning and supervision, graphic design, media conversion, and finding innovative solutions.

Michael is an advocate for simulation-based training and assessment, primarily as it relates to healthcare and patient safety. As an innovator he connects technology with curricular objectives.

His latest passion is development of the technical workforce tasked with supporting educators and trainers with their use of these specialized and sophisticated technologies. Healthcare simulation programs often struggle with integrating simulation technology into course curricula, and would be able to accomplish more with a competent and experienced staff.

The workforce development theme is not limited to support staff, as faculty and administrators are sometimes the only personnel addressing simulation tasks. For many years, the simulation community relied on early adopters, who had little resources or guidance and made healthcare simulation a fast growing field. As these programs have matured, faculty and administrators doing much of the day-to-day maintenance, setup and facilitation began hiring and training staff from outside the simulation profession (RNs, EMTs, etc.). As more and more technologies were integrated, need for experienced IT professionals became apparent. Mr. Young's work with other members of the Society for Simulation in Healthcare (SSiH.org) to develop professional certification for the existing and emerging simulation operations specialists. The framework for the certification provides clarification of the knowledge, skills and abilities (KSAs) that a competent simulation operations specialist should endeavor to achieve. Launch date for this certification is first quarter of 2014.

  • WEBINAR— Scenario Innovation: Programming Patient Outcomes in Simulation

    • April 2013 to Present
    Team Members: H. Michael Young, Laurie Kerns

    As simulation matures within the context of healthcare education, training and assessment, the need for standardized case scenarios arose. Much work has been done around this topic, and a market for pre-programmed scenarios has emerged. Faculty often don’t have the time to develop scenarios from scratch, let alone program the outcomes as well. This webinar will address the challenges common to on-the-fly operation, and how programming can automate processes. Ultimately, the programmed scenario functions as a check-list. Learner interventions are “checked off” and the programming does the work, freeing the educator/operator to focus on assessing learners. At the end of the webinar, attendees will have a clearer picture of how programming can effectively reduce preparation time, standardize patient responses to learner interventions, and improve fidelity through team work.

H. Michael Young's Publications

  • What is IT's Role in Human Patient Simulation?

    • Education Management Solutions
    • November 1, 2011
    Authors: H. Michael Young

    Many institutional IT technicians and managers are intimidated by the technological systems that enables patient simulators to work. This paper seeks to demystify this system and give a broad overview of what it takes to make this platform work, and the role that IT plays in the successful use of this technology. Additionally, many administrators are unsure of how best to maintain such systems, and often are forced to hire their own technicians out of their staff budget to oversee the support of this technology. This paper offers tips and guidelines for recruiting the assistance of their institutional IT department to address ongoing infrastructural challenges. Additionally, it highlights the important role that IT, whether local, contracted, or new hire has in the planning, maintenance and operation of human patient simulators and the systems that enable them.
