Michael Sharnoff's Publications

  • Academic Perspective: Revisiting Nasser and Palestine after the 1967 War

    • Al-Arabiya
    • June 11, 2011
    Authors: Michael Sharnoff

    A closer study of Nasser’s Palestine policy after the 1967 War challenges his historical legacy as champion of Palestinian rights and calls for greater introspection and more honest debate in academic discourse.

  • Post-Mubarak Egyptian Attitudes Toward Israel

    • Foreign Policy Research Institute
    • October 2011
    Authors: Michael Sharnoff

    With the rise of anti-Israel sentiments in the post-Mubarak era, the Supreme Council of the Armed Forces, led by Field Marshal Hussein Tantawi and Prime Minister Essam Sharaf, appears caught in a complex web of satisfying popular aspirations, which oppose or have reservations about the peace treaty with Israel, while not abandoning the strategic dividends the treaty has produced.

  • Palestinian Attitudes Toward Israel

    • Foreign Policy Research Institute
    • May 2012
    Authors: Michael Sharnoff

    A survey of attitudes from secularists, Hamas, Fatah and the general public suggests that Palestinians will continue to embrace what they describe as “popular, nonviolent resistance” to gain concessions.
