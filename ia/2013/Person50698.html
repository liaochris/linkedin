George Serel's Summary

George Serel has been actively involved in various facets of real estate since 1987, when he became a licensed sales agent. He began his career in Manhattan's commercial real estate market, handling retail space as well as apartment and office buildings. George got his broker's license in 1988, became a mortgage broker in 1989, became a real estate principal in 1990 purchasing distressed 1-4-family homes for renovation and resale, then formed a licensed real estate firm handling brokerage, investment services and property management.

In the last 25 years, George has transacted over a half billion dollars worth of real estate. He recently transitioned from the Long Island market where he was quite successful, and returned to Manhattan where he specializes in, serving rentals, sales and investment clients as a member of Corcoran's Union Square office.

George is adept and knowledgeable when it comes to analyzing and capitalizing on the market. In 2006, he had the insight to sell the majority of his real estate property in anticipation of the market change. He then joined a national real estate firm, where he established and directed the foreclosure department, training a staff of 15 to effectively negotiate foreclosure sales, generating a $6 million profit in a down market.

Today he shares his expertise and residential real estate investment strategy with others, making real estate a solid investment vehicle for his clients. George works closely with his loyal client following to enhance their real estate experience with his seasoned skills and service excellence.


A New York City native, George holds a BA in Economics and Accounting from Queens College, and an MBA in Finance and Accounting from Rutgers University.
