James Nelson's Summary

-Pilot in airline and corporate aviation
-Corporate aircraft management
-Corporate aviation training management

Corporate aviation is a service business- it's not about flying, fixing, or scheduling planes, although those are all means to an end. The end is to get the customer where he needs to be when he needs to be there with the greatest utility to him. Value must exceed cost or the customer is lost.

Corporate aviation involves large capital costs and operating costs. Money must be spent, but it must be spent wisely and judiciously. Excessive expenditures can be avoided by thoughtful planning, careful analysis and decision-making. All involved must understand and practice these principles.

I have applied these principles consistently and been successful at providing a valuable service at a reasonable cost. I concentrate on careful planning and creative problem-solving for those times when the plan changes. I'm a service and solutions oriented pilot who will assure you value.
