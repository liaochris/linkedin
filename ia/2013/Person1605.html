Adam Sheppard's Summary

I am an economist with broad experience in microeconomic policy analysis, focussing on Australian domestic reform. I have a particular interest in the economics and public policy of intellectual property and the challenges that new technologies pose for the law.

My goal is to continue influencing public policy decisions through work in the public, academic or parliamentary sectors.

Selected achievements:

2011 (May): Presentation of research on the economics of Investor-state dispute settlement arbitration at the London School of Economics colloquium 'Rethinking Investment Treaty Law', London.

Adam Sheppard's Experience

Transport Economist

Department of Infrastructure and Transport

Government Agency; 11-50 employees; Executive Office industry

July 2012 – Present (1 year 2 months) Canberra Area, Australia

My work supports the Department in progressing regulatory reform and policy regarding infrastructure and transportation, and in particular work related to the COAG Heavy Vehicle Charging and Investment reforms (formerly the COAG Road Reform Plan).

Senior Research Economist

Productivity Commission

Government Agency; 51-200 employees; Public Policy industry

January 2006 – July 2012 (6 years 7 months)

As a senior research economist my work contributed to the Productivity Commission's public inquiry and research functions. I was responsible for undertaking research work, writing chapters and contributing to the analysis of issues referred by the Australian Government. I liaised with members of the public and other interested stakeholders, and represented the Commission in public forums.

I contributed to a number of the Commission's research reports and public inquiries, include reviews of Australia's Bilateral and Regional Trade Agreements, Anti-Dumping and Countervailing System, Copyright Restrictions on the Parallel Importation of Books, price regulation of airport services and Benchmarking of Business Regulation.

I also worked in the Office of Best Practice Regulation, formerly a function of the Productivity Commission (now within the Department of Finance and Deregulation).
