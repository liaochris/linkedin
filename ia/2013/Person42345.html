Eric Reiss' Summary

NB: Please look at my contact info before you send me an invitation.

Eric Reiss has been actively involved in the creation of menu-based programs, hypertext games, service programs, multimedia, and web projects for over 30 years.

Born in San Antonio, TX, in 1954, and raised in St. Louis and Chicago, he holds degrees in Political Science and Performing Arts from Washington University in St. Louis, MO. In 1976, he moved to Denmark to accept a position as a stage director at the Danish Royal Theatre in Copenhagen. In 1979, he published his first commercial program (for the Apple II) which helped engineers optimize theatrical seating systems, and in 1983, he co-authored the first Danish-language adventure game for the Sinclair Spectrum.

Eric has been a full-time writer and business strategist since 1984. He regularly conducts workshops and lectures on a wide range of multimedia issues with particular emphasis on usability and web content.

Eric is Chairman of the EuroIA Summit Committee (www.euroia.org). He also serves on the advisory boards for the Department of Informatics at the Copenhagen Business School and the Romanian Information Architecture Association. He is a past two-term President of the Information Architecture Institute and was Professor of Usability and Design at the IE Business School in Madrid, Spain for three terms.

His written works include:
Honeymoon Trail (musical, 1976)
Marionettes (one-act play, 1977)
Skabet (computer adventure game, JC Jumbo Data, 1983)
The Compleat Talking Machine (Sonoran Publishing, 5 ed. 2007)
The Egyptologist's Vademecum (publication delayed, 1990)
Practical Information Architecture (Pearson Education, 2000)
Web Dogma '06 (e-zine Boxes and Arrows, 2006)
Usable Usability (John Wiley & Sons, 2012)

More info available at www.fatdux.com
See also http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eric_Reiss

Eric Reiss' Experience

Chairman and CEO

The FatDUX Group

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Internet industry

July 2006 – Present (7 years 3 months)

FatDUX creates innovative interactive products that:

- improve the quality of people's lives
- improve our clients' bottom line.

We bridge the gap between online and offline communications that hinders effective brand-building and reduces communication effectiveness.

The DUX in our name is a popular acronym for “Design of User eXperience”. It's a term used to describe the overall experience and satisfaction a user has when using a product or system.

Our job at FatDUX is to make sure our clients make a positive impression at key brand touchpoints.

In order to accomplish this, we develop detailed blueprints to guide designers, programmers, and content providers. We design and incorporate visual identity to ensure consistency and recognition. And we conduct impartial usability evaluations to improve the value of our work.

Headquartered in Copenhagen, Denmark, you'll find FatDUX offices and associates throughout Europe and the Americas.

Eric Reiss' Publications

  • The Compleat Talking Machine

    • Sonoran Publishing, Chandler, AZ
    • 2007
    Authors: Eric Reiss

    For over 25 years, the Compleat Talking Machine has served as the definitive repair and restoration manual for antique phonographs. The updated 5th edition delivers practical step-by-step solutions to repair problems, plus includes an identification section with pictures and descriptions of over 300 early machines, and a comprehensive guide to current market values.

  • Practical Information Architecture

    • Pearson
    • 2000
    Authors: Eric Reiss

    The book is divided into three main sections: Initial Considerations, which deals with the basic business objectives that must be determined before the actual structure is created; Mechanics, which explains how to organize the information on paper and create the first draft structure; Fine-tuning, which covers those issues related to the completion/finalization of the project from a structural point of view.

  • Web Dogma

    • Boxes and Arrows
    • 2006
    Authors: Eric Reiss

    This is a set of best practices for interactive design that transcend both fashion and technology. It was modeled on Dogme 95, created by Danish filmmakers Lars von Trier and Thomas Vinterberg.

  • Usable Usability

    • John Wiley & Sons
    • 2012
    Authors: Eric Reiss

    The A-to-Z guide to spotting and fixing usability problems Frustrated by pop-ups? Forms that make you start over if you miss a field? Nonsensical error messages? You're not alone! This book helps you simply get it right the first time (or fix what's broken). Boasting a full-color interior packed with design and layout examples, this book teaches you how to understand a user's needs, divulges techniques for exceeding a user's expectations, and provides a host of hard won advice for improving the overall quality of a user's experience...all in an engaging, easy-to-apply manner.
