  • BU Alumni Association Professional Development Webinar - Reinventing Your Career & Expanding Your Options

    • October 2013 to Present
    Team Members: John Brady, Jeff Murphy

    Having been successful, you might be wondering why making or even identifying your next move is proving so difficult. After all, you’ve been solving problems and coaching others for years. So why does this time feel so much different?

    The fact is your life and career are more complex now than at any time before. You have obligations to your current employer (or you did), you have personal obligations that only seem to grow in number and complexity, and the way in which middle managers and senior executives find opportunities and are found by promising employers has changed tremendously in the last several years.

    In a knowledge economy career paths are non-linear and often not visible in obvious ways. This does not mean paths to continued professional growth do not exist. It does mean that you need to look at your professional profile in new ways, and map it such that you have a plan that is clear for you and for those who need your expertise and talent.

    Join us online at 12 noon EST on October 30th, 2013 as Mr. Brady will identify concrete steps to take when you are making a career change, including:

    - Example of ways you can effectively illustrate transferable skills for prospective employers
    - Increase your visibility to prospective employers
    - Focus on search activities that have the highest likelihood of return/ success for experienced professionals

  • “Courageous Conversation” vs. Corrosive Caution: Mitigating Conflict Avoidance in the Workplace

    • November 2013 to Present
    Team Members: John Brady, Suzanne Gleason

    Approved for 1.5 HRCI Credits
    Speaker/Facilitator: John J Brady - Executive Director, Protem Partners

    Do you ever wonder how much easier it would be if your office or the managers you support were a little less conflict avoidant? If the same issues with the same colleagues didn’t keep coming up? If managers didn’t come to your office seeking to put someone on a PIP who has glowing reviews, but an unaddressed and undocumented issue to-date that EVERYONE knows about yet does nothing about? We will talk about the cost of excessive conflict avoidance, means to address necessary conversations with professionalism and discussions to have with the managers we support about how to do the same.

    Participants will view challenging (“courageous”) conversations as way of showing support and care to colleagues as opposed to conflict, and model this type of engagement for the benefit of their own work environment as well as the culture of the organizations and people they support.

  • Preparing for February 2: Advising the Career Ambivalent

    • November 2013 to January 2014
    Team Members: John Brady, Josh Proulx

    Alumni Career Services Network (ACSN) Member Webinar

    Almost twenty-one years ago Columbia Pictures released Groundhog Day, a comedy film about a local weatherman who is forced to live February 2 (Groundhog Day) over and over again. The iconic film touched something deep in the psyche of moviegoers in the developed world and continues to inspire analysis and commentary by film critics and clergy of the world’s major religions.

    Career ambivalent alumni are those who come to us describing themselves as less than satisfied with their work and will often complain about being “stuck” (or other synonymous metaphor about a cycle of professional dissatisfaction they cannot break). Vexing for most of us is that central to our training is the premise that we begin and conclude every process with the constituent’s goals; but these individuals struggle with the answer to the simple question: “What is it that you want to do?”

    Unlike the narrative we associate with Bill Murray’s film performance, the career ambivalent aren’t interested in making us laugh. In fact, many experience endless frustration having had little luck with a problem that is seldom in the domain or interest of mental health professionals, beyond the skills or comfort level of the average career counselor, and usually too complex for career inventories and personality assessments.

    In our webinar we will identify in concrete ways the following:
    • Common profiles of the career ambivalent
    • Where we can help
    • Methods of utilizing our alumni networks in ways that are ideal for this population
    • What types of other professionals can and should augment our expertise
