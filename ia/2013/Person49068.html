Glenn Reid's Summary

If I were in a novel about CIA operatives, I'd be the guy who wanted to stay in field ops, rather than settling for a cushy desk job. I like being in the thick of it, taking risks, doing things. I'm not sure I can still shoot straight, and my shoulder hurts, but I would *not* let the bad guys blow up Skyfall, my ancestral home in Scotland.

I created iMovie 1.0 at Apple in 1998 with a team of 3 people — ahead of schedule. I built iPhoto 1.0 with a team of 5 people. I managed both products through 4 releases and tens of millions of users. I am the primary inventor on a dozen or so patents related to both of those products.

I spent hundreds of hours in front of white boards with Steve Jobs, designing software products. It was a great experience, and I'm proud to have worked with him.

I was one of the first 25 people at Adobe Systems and wrote one of their seminal books, still in print 25 years later. Worked on applications, technologies, and many aspects of the business over many years.

I have created and led several startup companies. As founding CEO of Five Across, Inc., I created a powerful networking platform with peer-to-peer, file sharing, instant messaging and social networking capabilities. It was acquired by Cisco in 2007. At RightBrain Software, in the early 90's, I created the first object-oriented page layout application for NeXT computers, called PasteUp.

Specialties: Envisioning, designing, building, shipping and supporting great products.

Director of Engineering, Consumer Applications

Apple Computer

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; AAPL; Consumer Electronics industry

1998 – 2003 (5 years)

Hired a small team and created iMovie 1.0 out of thin air in 1998, based on a whiteboard drawing.

Hand picked by Executive Staff members to create Apple's iMovie consumer video editing software from scratch. Architected and implemented the application within 9 months with a team of two other engineers. iMovie won two Eddy awards: Best Digital Video Software, and Software of the Year. Worked directly with Steve Jobs, Apple CEO, in designing iMovie features and interface details. Designed and built iMovie 2.0 close on the heels of 1.0 to cement iMovie's leadership in the consumer video marketplace, adding several innovative features including reverse, fast and slow motion in real time.

iMovie was the kick-start to the entire Applications division at Apple, which did not exist when I started. We conceived and implemented the "digital hub" media strategy which led to the iTunes acquisition and iPhoto development as well as the beginnings of the consumer electronics that became iPod, iPhone and iPad.

Created iPhoto 1.0 through 4.0 with a small team, working directly with executive staff on all aspects of design and implementation.

As Director of Engineering, I managed the iMovie and iPhoto teams, including the QA department. Built a highly successful organization, most of which is still in place more than 10 years later.

Senior Engineering Manager

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; ADBE; Computer Software industry

Created and managed the Developer Support team for 3.5 years. Developed several PostScript industry standards, including PPD (PostScript Printer Description) and EPSF (Encapsulated PostScript) file formats. Member of the PostScript Language Committee that designed PS Level 2. Wrote PostScript Distillery, predecessor to Acrobat technology.
