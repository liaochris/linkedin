George Brown's Summary

Photographer, designer, videographer and editor. After delegating between my degree and my hobby of photography for the last 3 years at Nottingham Trent University, I've become more than I ever thought I could. My work at Nottingham Trent as the university's official photographer has given me the experience and practice I need to excel in the field of entertainment photography. From stop motions to rebranding a nightclub, my work specialises in the motion of life. Experience has taught me to never be satisfied and continuously motivates me to excel at what I love. That's the beauty of photography, there is always more to learn.

George Brown's Projects

  • Magazine Me

    • January 2012 to January 2012
    Team Members: George Brown

    Magazine Me was loosely based on a course brief titled "Book of Me." For this, I looked at the growing popularity of reality shows such as TOWIE and Jersey Shore. Shows, that inspect the very fabric of our culture, exploiting our sadistic curiosities and voiding what's left of privacy in society today. My interpretation led me to conceptualise an entire magazine dedicated to a single person, with articles depicting personal opinions, characteristics and interests.

  • Graduation Ball

    • May 2012 to May 2012
    Team Members: George Brown

    Whilst the SU in previous years had merely taken just a picture every minute from one angle, I knew that there was potential for so much more. I pitched the idea of multiple angles, focal lengths and positions with 1 second interval between shots and it was successful.
    As a consequence, I single handedly took over 20,000 pictures from 100 different angles during the night. Equipped with 2 tripods, 8 lenses, 2 D-SLR's, my laptop, hard drives, card readers and a plethora of batteries and memory cards, I thought I had everything prepared for every possible obstacle. And I had, except for one. The fact that I was just one photographer, when I needed at least two angles at the same time. I found myself planting, framing and calibrating one tripod on the side of the event, then whilst that camera was snapping, I'd sprint to other side. A 200m sprint that must have contributed to at least the lengh of a full marathon by the end of the night.
    It was an invaluable experience. I learnt the real life restrictions of a time-lapse workflow, coping mechanisms for a 18 hour shift and a whole load more. I definitely look forward to any future endeavours involving lapses.

  • Climax

    • February 2012 to Present
    Team Members: George Brown

    Climax has been the testing grounds for where I refine the techniques, settings and combinations I have learnt, and in that respect, it has been a priceless experience. From starting with basic modifiers, to advancing with bouncing, light trails and diffusers, Climax has taken me further than I ever expected. Commissioned by the Student Union, my job is to capture the nights atmosphere and explosive mood. Over time I've developed my own style involving the use of candid shots, exposing and framing to best capture ambient light whilst featuring saturated, vivid colours.

  • Double Vision

    • February 2012 to Present
    Team Members: George Brown

    In addition to Climax, I am commissioned to work Double Vision every weekend. It's yet another club that I've had the privilege to utilise to the benefit of my photography. They say that you learn the foundations of lighting whilst working at a nightclub, and experience proves this to be true. These pictures are a compilation of techniques, angles and settings that I've explored during my time with Double Vision.

  • La Folie Douce

    Team Members: George Brown

    Whilst a personal excursion and not one of commission, taking pictures in the atmosphere of La Folie Douce is not one I will soon forget. These pictures showcase the colour and energy that I love to capture, and serve as an example of the kinds of environments I can thrive in. The colour correction is all based on a personalised preset that I designed for the specific event, a stage I consider vital to my process.

  • Double Vision (Halloween Special)

    • October 2012 to Present
    Team Members: George Brown

    When approached with the challenge of doing something special for halloween at Double Vision, I instantly knew of the potential. I came up with the concept of intensifying the horror atmosphere with a selection of edits. Including spiders, rotting flesh, demonic eyes and joker smiles, I went all out to make the pictures really catch your eye. To make things even more complex, I was required to edit on the same night so that the pictures could be showcased on the nightclub projector. To make this a reality, it required practicing the workflow and refining it for efficiency. In the end I couldn't be happier with the results. It was everything I planned and more, and would love to apply similar effects to Christmas and Easter.

  • Faded

    • March 2012 to Present
    Team Members: George Brown

    Faded is an up and coming group of event organisers for which I was commissioned for a short while. The work I did with them gave me a new surrounding to work with and hugely dynamic lighting that required constant awareness to control. However, whilst it did introduce new obstacles, it was a pleasure to work with such a laid back atmosphere. It made capturing 'in the moment' photographs, so much easier and natural than usual.
