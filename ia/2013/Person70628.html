Julian Ware's Projects

  • Anonyming - Curated Anonymous Blog Posting

    • February 2013 to March 2013
    Team Members: Julian Ware

    Anonyming is a personal project that I conceived, designed, developed, and authored, in three weeks. The site is my most recent work and demonstrates many of my professional strengths.

    • The design is mobile-compatible, meaning that it's designed to look the very similar in all browsers.

    • The approach to usability is what I call the 'ATM approach,' meaning that maximum usability came before a concern for aesthetics (which isn't my forte). I wanted the site to be usable even for those with limited online experience.

    • There is a high level of attention to detail in design consistency and subtle aspects of user experience. Content is not selectable preventing behaviors confusing to inexperienced users. The cursor is active on load facilitating immediate expression. Link color is consistent across the site. Textareas resize dynamically. Navigation is consistent in design and location.

    • Coding is minimized to a legible degree in order to reduce loading time. Redundancy in code and Wordpress templates has been limited.

    • The site has been optimized for SEO, with meta-descriptions, proper header tag implementation, exclusion of taxonomies from the sitemap, and the use of nofollow on redundant internal links.

    • The About content is separated into an Introduction provided for those wanting a basic overview of the project and further in-depth content for those interested in specifics.

    • Moderation strategy is comprehensive demonstrating my experience with online social venues.
