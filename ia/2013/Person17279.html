Brian Cuban's Summary

According to the web, I am the only Brian Cuban in the world so if you are here, you found me!

I am the author of "Shattered Image: My Triumph Over Body Dysmorphic Disorder". Shattered Image recently reached the #1 ranking on Amazon in the Eating Disorder category available on Amazon.com and BarnesAndNoble.com. At this time it is only available online. Hopefully in bookstores soon!

I am a 1983 graduate of Penn State University. I received my J.D in 1986 from the University of Pittsburgh School Of Law. I have passed the respective state bar exams of Pennsylvania(inactive) and Texas.

I am also currently the segment host for “Brian Cuban’s Legal Briefs” on EyeOpener Tv. EyeOpener is a different kind of morning show chocked full of entertainment, humor, and what you need to know to get your day started. It runs every weekday morning and is currently syndicated in the following locations:

KIAH-TV (Houston, TX), KDAF-TV (Dallas, TX), WSFL-TV (Miami, FL), WPHL-TV (Philadelphia, PA), KRCW-TV (Portland, OR)

I also speak on the 1st Amendment and Hate speech in Social Media. I have appeared on Fox News, NBC, and have been invited to speak at prestigious locations such as, The Commonwealth Club, South By Southwest, BlogWorld 2009 and 2011, The FPA(Financial Planning Association) Experience 2011, The Simon Wiesenthal Center, The Anti-Defamation League and Facebook Corporate to name a few.

I used to be an avid long distance runner having run 8 marathons. Now I kind of just propel myself forward in a running motion between injuries

Proud Parent to a Cat named Useless and my dog, Peanut.

Brian Cuban's Projects

  • Project HEAL

    • May 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Brian Cuban, Sara Brody, Eric van Furth, Evelyn Attia, Jane Stewart, Medha Khandelwal, Jack Britvan, Lori Fitzmaurice, Lawrence Holzberg, Wendy Sterling

    Our Mission: Project HEAL: Help to Eat, Accept and Live is a 501(c) not-for-profit organization in the United States and a federal not-for-profit organization under the Canada Not-for-profit Corporations Act (NFP Act). We provide scholarship funding for people with eating disorders who cannot afford treatment, promote healthy body image and self esteem, and serve as a testament that full recovery from an eating disorder is possible.

    "We want to diminish society’s obsession with body image by encouraging people to accept their bodies. People do not have to love everything about their bodies, but it is important that they learn to embrace their perceived flaws and ‘imperfections.’ We hope that by providing encouragement and support we can help those struggling with body image issues from developing full-blown eating disorders." Founders of Project HEAL, Kristina & Liana
