Kris Schaeffer, CPLP's Summary

I have been leading a dual life -- corporate consultant and adventurer.

From world-class service to world-wide collaboration, I have spent 30 years customizing culture-changing programs that enabled companies to increase their ability to delight and engage their customers which also increased their revenue.

I create talent-building programs for Pepsi Cola, Pacific Bell, Viacom, Comcast and the American Institute of CPAs.

As a Workplace Learning & Performance professional, I use a toolbox of methods to change organization culture, tackle thorny performance issues, and start up new companies.

I develop "customer-focused cultures" for clients who seek to get and retain their customers.

And I have also traveled the globe. Down rivers -- Nile, Irriwaddy, Danube, Missouri. Up heights -- Machu Pichhu, Alhambra, horseback riding in the Himalayas. And to the walls -- Berlin and Great Wall of China -- to see that barriers are broken and cultures mix. Today I conduct workshops based on the No Opportunity Wasted philosophy that Phil Keoghan wrote about. This mindset helps employees be more innovative, independent, and collaborative.

Specialties: Motivation: Helping people to get rid of excuses and achieve their goals.

Team building: Using collaborative strategies and tools to help people work together smarter.

Workplace Learning & Performance: Using management tools to solve thorny performance problems and teaching your experts to train others.

Customer-centric culture: Improving the customer's experience from sales, service, installation, trouble shooting. We provide analysis of customer expectations, how well you fit them, and how to close the critical gaps.

Kris Schaeffer, CPLP's Publications

  • Managing Change and Keeping It Going

    • ASTD Press,OD Division
    • 1993
    Authors: Kris Schaeffer, CPLP

    Organization Development is not an event, it's an on-going process. This book describes best practices in developing sustainable programs.

  • Best Practices in Survey Tools

    • ASTD Press
    • 1997
    Authors: Kris Schaeffer, CPLP

    Our LEADER survey used customer data to determine 1. customer's priorities, 2. how well companies and sales reps met those priorities, and 3. action steps to fill critical gaps. This was one of the first self-norming instruments that helped our clients allocate resources to the changes that would make the most impact . . . as viewed by their customers.
