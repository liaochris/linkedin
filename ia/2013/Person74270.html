Kenley Neufeld's Experience

Library Director

Santa Barbara City College

Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry

July 2006 – Present (7 years 4 months)

Build relationships. Inspire creativity. Drive innovation. Experiment. Listen and serve. Collaborate. Lead with Technology.

Between 2006-2010, the library experienced a transformation. From an under-utilized and disregarded support service, the Luria Library has become one of the leading learning spaces on campus. Information literacy has been infused into the institutional learning outcomes and we are sought out by students, faculty, and administrators. The library has doubled the visitors and doubled the number of classes taught. In 2011, we were recognized by ACRL with the Excellence in Academic Libraries Award.

As the Library Director, I am responsible for the leadership and operational management of this community college library employing 10 full-time and 5 part-time employees. The operational budget of the library is $1 million. In addition to the library budget, I serve on the Planning and Resources Committee and the College Planning Council - these two committees are responsible for reviewing and setting the college-wide budget priorities. In addition, the College Planning Council is the advisory group to the College President/Superintendent.

During my tenure, I have served (or continue to serve) on the following campus committees: Academic Senate, Information Technology Committee, District Technology Committee, Committee for Online Instruction, Planning and Resources, Committee on Teaching and Learning, Academic Policies, and College Planning Council.

Building community relationships through the Friends of the Luria Library has resulted in increased scholarships for students, extended library hours, and most recently the creation of a Library Resident Program - an rare occurrence in community colleges.
