Ian D. Muir's Summary

Ian is a Non-Resident Fellow within the German Marshall Fund of the United States' Energy & Society Program, a Johns Hopkins SAIS M.A. candidate, and Director of Technology and co-founder of Read and Prosper, a non-profit that promotes access to knowledge through the use of technology. He also serves on the advisory board of GIVEWATTS, an NGO which brings clean, safe, renewable power to schools and clinics in the developing world.

At GMF, Ian contributes research and analysis on a range of pressing energy and climate change issues, leveraging knowledge and insights gained through over five years of energy and climate change consulting experience, where he advised oil and gas companies, intergovernmental organizations, and other key energy sector players.

Ian left PFC Energy, where he managed its Carbon Strategy Service (CSS) and GHG-related consulting offerings, to pursue a master's in International Relations from Johns Hopkins SAIS and to spend more time developing Read and Prosper's pilot project in Kathiani, Kenya.

Specialties: US, EU & China carbon policy + impact assessment, alternative energy technology and investment analysis, GHG accounting & disclosure best practices, oil & gas industry GHG emissions modeling (upstream, refining, lifecycle), carbon capture & storage (CCS).
