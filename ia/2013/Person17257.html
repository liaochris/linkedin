Brian Colligan's Summary

The editorial page of The Daily Reflector intends to be a destination for area residents seeking thoughtful discussion on events and issues of local importance. For more than 13 years, I have contributed to that mission by writing editorials and columns, overseeing letters to the editor, selecting syndicated content and being an accessible and eager voice within the community. I have led the page as editor since 2004, a promotion that brought with it greater responsibilities and expectations, but also tremendous latitude to chart the goals, direction, tone and voice of a newspaper serving the city and county, and which stands proudly as a advocate for all of eastern North Carolina.

Those who believe that newspapers cannot make a difference need only look at the $160 million East Carolina Heart Institute, funding for which was won through persistent editorial pressure on local and state leaders. Arguments about the paper's relevance should consider the influence exerted on area officials over budgetary matters or education policy. Those who contend that print media is dead need only look at the public discourse we readily court, encourage and promote to see otherwise.

I came to the Reflector in 1998 following graduation from Virginia Tech, where I earned a bachelor's degree in Communications Students and Political Science. I am also a 2005 graduate of East Carolina University, where I earned a master's degree in Public Administration with a concentration in Security Studies. My professional paper topic was a study of the William S. Lee Act and how its incentive program affects job creation in North Carolina.

Specialties

My professional specialties include persuasive writing, editing, reporting, content management, social media, page design and news judgment. My academic work combined some of the above with database research and data analysis, and included a course in grant writing.
