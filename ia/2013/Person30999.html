David Boyer's Summary

I’m an accomplished and experienced UX/UI designer and developer. I conceptualize and implement user interface designs that are more than just nice to look at, they are useful and shaped by usability principles and methodologies. I've been designing and building for the web since the time a 14400 modem was cutting edge and I have a high degree of expertise in HTML5 and CSS3 with a concentration on strong visual design and intelligent user interfaces. I'm a clear communicator with a natural ability to get to the root of the problem and create workflows that involve clients in the creative process.

This is all fine and good and exactly what you would expect to see here. However before we go much farther I'm going to suggest that you consider me a "UX Detective" instead of a designer. Since the bulk of what I do is much akin to a process followed to solve a case, I believe the detective title makes more sense. The foundation for this thinking is that to be a good UX/UI designer one needs to be a keen observer and an expert listener - just like a detective (think Columbo). Also like a detective, I look for data (clues). They are all around and sometimes I need to dig them up but they are always there. I listen to the stakeholders. Somebody knows something and if I don't find and talk to those involved I will be ignoring critical information. Most importantly though, I'm an advocate, I speak for the users since they can't always speak for themselves. Lastly, I process the facts and crack the case. Doing this in a creative, artful and analytical way is what drives me and keeps me coming back for more.

 In my former life, I forecast the weather, flew airplanes and dreamed of being a rockstar. I still enjoy the weather but quit guessing and now revel in fatherhood as opposed to rockstardom.
