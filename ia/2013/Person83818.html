Lynsey Jean Henderson's Summary

Having been awarded, with distinctions, the two year Master of Fine Art postgraduate course at Edinburgh College of Art in 2010, specialising in printed textiles. I later went on to win the Deutsche Bank Business Set Up Grant for Design Award 2010 which was for £10,000. With the grant I set up my dedicated textile screen printing studio in the heart of Scotlands capital.

At the studio I specialise in hand printed textiles for luxury interiors. I have a keen interest in colour, drawing and interiors and currently have a full wallpaper and fabric collection in production entitled Flora & Fauna of Scotland. As a result of this collection I was nominated for a Homes & Gardens Designer Award in January 2011, the results being announced later in 2011.

I am enthusiastic about my work and confident in my ability to overcome any design related challenge with suitable style and flair. I like to take briefs and apply exciting design solutions to them that are relevant to the current market but are augmented with something new and fresh. My inspiration can come from anything that sparks an idea in my head. It can range from something as simple as a leaf to a complex piece of classical music.

I always have ‘one eye open’ and am constantly receptive to anything that may spark a design concept/solution. My style is highly decorative and stylized. I like to draw flora and fauna and the natural world is my main source of inspiration.

Materials wise, I enjoy using pen and ink. I am excited by the variance in the quality of line that can be achieved by using different nibs. I believe that first and foremost design should be fun and enjoyable. I will always strive to ensure my collections have this element of frivolity without forsaking top class execution and beautiful drawing.
