Charles Randall's Summary

Director of Randall Concepts Ltd. We build and supply the Ranger suitcase studios and other equipment for radio stations. These are ideally suited for disaster response teams as well as community radio stations. The series of Ranger studios is a development of the Chrysolite suitcase studios previously built by Feba Radio and now licenced to us.

I previously spent 24 years with Feba as audio engineer/consultant.

I was responsible for the overall concept of Feba's Chrysolite studio in a suitcase and the detail design of its hybrid analogue/digital mixer. This has been deployed with several disaster response teams to establish emergency radio stations. It has recently been deployed in Padang, Sumatra following the earthquake there.

The range of work has been very wide and outside Feba I have increasingly found myself returning to my sound reinforcement and AV roots whilst helping my own church and others with their systems.

I travelled extensively as Feba's International Audio Consultant designing and building studios in many developing countries. This included all aspects of acoustics, ventilation and equipment.

I was instrumental in Feba being an early adopter of a number of technologies and systems including:
low bit rate audio codecs and FTP to transfer of radio programmes, structured cabling, portable appliance testing,
and intranet based technical documentation.

I have experience of debugging heavy duty power supplies.

I learnt the audio trade with the legendary Rupert Neve and Geoff Watts in Cambridge at BTS and the Cambridge Radio Course from 1978 to 1983 designing mixers and church and cathedral sound systems.

Charles Randall's Experience

Director

Randall Concepts Ltd.

November 2009 – Present (3 years 10 months)

Randall Concepts manufactures the Ranger range of suitcase studios for emergency broadcasters and community radio with other prducts in the pipeline. The Ranger suitcase studio is a development of the Chrysolite suitcase studio originally designed by Feba Radio and manufactured under licence. It is uniquely suited for disaster response teams and temporary or emergency radio stations. It was recently used in Indonesia with a disaster response team following the Sumatra earthquake.

Principal Audio Engineer

Feba Radio

October 1985 – December 2009 (24 years 3 months)

The range of work has been very wide but I have increasingly found myself returning to my sound reinforcement and AV roots whilst helping my own church and others with their systems.

I have recently been focused on the design of Feba's Chrysolite suitcase studio. This has just been shortlisted for the AIB 2009 (Association of Christian Broadcasters) Technical Innovation award. This self contained studio is uniquely suited to disaster relief broadcasting and has been used in the Bihar floods of 2008 and the Indonesian earthquake of October 2009.

I was responsible for the overall concept and the detail design of its hybrid analogue/digital mixer.

I travelled extensively as Feba's International Audio Consultant. I designed and built studios in many developing countries. This included:
Sound isolation and soundproofing,
Internal acoustic design,
Acoustic measurement and analysis
Air-conditioning and ventilation,
Standby power,
Design, construction, testing, installation and commisioning of turnkey studio systems
Training of users.

Introduced low bit rate audio codecs and FTP to transfer radio programmes between studios and transmitters. Feba was one of the first international radio stations to move of all it its programmes via the internet.
Introduced structured cabling in the early '90s.
Developed the engineering intranet for our technical documentation.
I set up the Portable Appliance Testing system.

Test Technican

Westinghouse Davenset Rectifiers

September 1984 – September 1985 (1 year 1 month)

Testing high powered DC supplies, battery chargers and inverters. These were used in warships, rail traction supplies and power stations. This was intended as as stopgap job but gave me experience with standby power systems that later proved useful. I mostly worked on debugging prototypes.

Studio Engineer

Cambridge Radio Course /CBT

July 1978 – September 1983 (5 years 3 months)

I started as a technical assistant and ended as studio engineer, setting up and mantaining the temporary studios needed for each course, recording and copying lectures, supervising and assisting the students in the studios. I supervised students on their outside broadcast (remote) exercises as they recorded church services for broadcast. I built the studios in the permanent training centre.

Technical Assistant

Nevenco Consultants /BTS

April 1977 – September 1983 (6 years 6 months)

I worked with the legendary Rupert Neve and Geoff Watts who taught me much of what I know. I joined when the AIR Monserrat console was under development testing countless prototype amplifer stages and transformers. We designed other mixers and studio equipment. we designed and installed sound systems in chuches and cathedrals. The company started as Nevenco Consultants, but reformed as BTS (Broadcast Training and Services).
A the same time I worked on the Cambridge Radio Course also founded by Rupert Neve.
