Joanne Richardson's Summary

User Experience / Interaction Designer based in Newcastle upon Tyne with over 6 years experience in conducting design research and producing engaging, memorable and intuitive solutions for the web.

Specialties: Designing for conversion rate optimisation, Usability Testing, Independant reviews, Design Research, Competitive Analysis, Persona creation, Wireframe & prototype design.

Joanne Richardson's Experience

Head of User Experience Design

Orange Bus

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry

June 2009 – Present (4 years 3 months) Newcastle upon Tyne, United Kingdom

At Orange Bus I currently head up the UX department specialising in conversion & engagement optimisation for a variety of blue-chip clients such as Philips, Philip Morris International, CAPITA, Pearson Education and Sage.

I have worked for over a year collaboratively with Pearson, the world's leading Education publisher on ActiveLearn an online GCSE product for both students and teachers to assist with self-study online learning, homework assignment and class management. In addition to this I have also assisted Pearson with other multiple projects within their online Educational department.

I have also worked extensively with Sage conducting a variety of work from an internal interactive toolkit to encourage brand consistency across the globe, usability testing and conversion design across the Sage online store which lead to redesigns or formed part of A/B tests with impressive results.

In addition to traditional management and project work I have been dedicated in promoting UX in the community, speaking at a Mi Network event - Turning Clicks into Sales and SuperMondays - SuperUX.

User Experience Designer

TH_NK

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Internet industry

July 2007 – June 2009 (2 years) Newcastle upon Tyne, United Kingdom

At TH_NK, my biggest achievement was working as the lead Interaction Designer on the BBC TV Licensing redevelopment.

Being heavily involved throughout the project made it very personal project (and i'm sure still to date anyone can quiz me on anything to do with TV Licensing). During the year long project, I was responsible for designing and optimising the numerous payment processes then wireframing these solutions to discuss with the clients and to ensure all business and user requirements were covered. As well as the creation of a large interactive prototype of the entire website used for usability testing to improve the design. I also organised, conducted and analysed subsequent usability testing on the build phase and conducted quality assurance testing to ensure a high quality the final product.

The project achieved a 35% increase in Direct Debit conversion, 28% in card payments and automated customer change of details improved by 21.6% significantly reducing manual processes.

As well as TVL, I also had the opportunity to work on many large projects for big name clients, including T-Mobile, NHS North East, Northern Rock & Bupa. During these projects I contributed to a number of the UX tasks including:
- Audience definition through research, such as diary studies, non-directed interviews, and observations to feed into Persona creation.
- Competitor Analysis - conducting and documenting competitor analysis to feed into requirements and project understanding.
- Wireframing and prototype creation: Including documentation and demonstration to the wider team on design decisions.
- In-house usability testing (preparation, planning, conducting, analysis of observations and suggested improvements).

Research Assistant

University of Sunderland

Participated in a large EU funded games development project collaborating with teams across Europe. The project aimed to help children better understand different cultures through the use of an interactive game utilising RFID tags. During this time my MSc research and findings on photo-elicitation and participatory design were produced as a paper for the 2007 HCI conference.
