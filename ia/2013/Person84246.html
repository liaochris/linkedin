Margaret Doyle's Summary

If there is a narrative design to my life and career it would appear as a transmedia story of text, photography, gesture, film, and spoken word I wrote, directed, shot, acted in, stage managed, or designed. Story is my through line.

I have worked in regional theatres from Victoria to San Diego where I honed my storytelling skills and learned to craft narrative on stage. I wrote and produced my own plays, stage managed large productions, designed lighting plans and costumes, and created storyworlds that took shape in real-time before an audience. I took my directing and design skills into marketing positions then into my own business where I began designing and manufacturing for the hospitality industry. Working from brand concept to product manufacturing with fulfillment and logistics spanning the globe, I gained the skills required to execute a complex brand story.

Having had many years of entrepreneur experience, I took an opportunity to work with the young and evolving School of Tourism and Hospitality Management at Royal Roads University. I developed a robust social media eco-system for the School that I utilized to develop an international network for interns. I helped create a world-class internship program known for its innovation in digital literacy and for producing (in my opinion) the best interns in the tourism and hospitality industry. I taught the nine-credit course for the International Internship program, both online (Moodle) and in the classroom.

What Is Your Story? was a question I asked so often I started a business by the name. After several years consulting large enterprise on storytelling, this past summer I moved into a new role at the University of British Columbia as their first Digital Storyteller--a wonderful opportunity to develop a narrative that has been a part of my entire life.
