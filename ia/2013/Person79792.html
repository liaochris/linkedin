The Small Business Barter Network - Smarter Barter!

How does our fully automated, 24/7 business barter network work:

Let’s say you’re a printer – you put capacity into the system, and do a print job for Rob who has a web design business. This is not a direct exchange network where 'I give you this and you give me that' so you do not have to buy from Rob in return. You can buy advertising, social media marketing, massage therapy or any other business services on offer.

Using the network you can...

•Promote your business locally and nationally with no cash outlay
•Connect with other business owners locally and across the whole of the UK
•Generate sales
•Preserve margins
•Enjoy 24/7 access to the services you need or would like to try
•Improve your cash flow
•Put idle capacity to work
*No transaction fees
*When members buy from you, they pay you at your normal everyday selling price, not at a discount

Our system is fully automated and really easy to use. Buyers and sellers negotiate their own prices and raise normal invoices, including VAT, where applicable. The transaction is then captured on the Business Barter Network system, which debits and credits each account immediately.

Many of our members are self-employed and find bartering really helpful because they can try out new services, conserve cash, earn valuable currency and connect with other local businesses without any cash outlay or any transaction fees.

Members do not barter ALL of their time, appointments, goods or services - they just trade what they have spare or aren't using, transforming this otherwise lost revenue into new income. Several members have benefitted from gaining new cash clients and customers by offering their services for Action Pounds.

    It's a fact that complementary currencies improve cash flow by providing a new source of extra income that can be spent on local services and resources, saving limited cash income. Some businesses spend their Action Pounds (our unique e-currency) on resources they need, others reward their e-currency to employees, suppliers, customers or donate it to local charities - who then use it to reward their volunteers, who then spend the currency in their local community - in some cases with the original donor business!
