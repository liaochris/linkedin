Mary O'Hara-Devereaux, Ph.D's Summary

Mary is a leading global futurist,trend analyst, business forecaster and strategist helping executives to "find targets no one else can see" for over 20 years. She is a strategy advisor senior executives to create ground breaking strategies across industries including high tech, entertainment,food, financial services, consumer products, telecoms, distribution and energy. She has extensive experience in the emerging markets (China,India, Thailand, Korea, Mexico, Brazil, Chile, Ecuador, Turkey,Morocco as well U.S., Europe, Japan.(62 countries on 6 continents).

Her accurate forecasts and trend analysis is legendary and steady through the volatile times since the 1990s. She is the author of several books including Navigating the Badlands-Thriving in the Decade of Radical Transformation, The China Five Year Forecast, GlobalWork-Bridging Distance Culture and Time, China's Diverging Lifestyles and several others.

In demand as keynote speaker, she is known to provide profound, provocative and pragmatic insights on with customized implications for every group. She is frequently in the media from Bloomberg T.V., Asia Wall Street, Journal, CCTV in China and NPR.

Mary is Chair of a private CEO board of advisors affiliated with Vistage International in the San Francisco Bay Area. Membership is by invitation only to leaders who are successful & committed to profitable growth and investment in their leadership development.

Mary maintains a limited private practice of CEO/top executive advising to leaders with big visions poised to have big impacts. Clients range from start ups to multi-billion $$ global companies.

For more information, please visit Mary at www.global-foresight.net This site is updated weekly with Mary's latest profound, provocative and pragmatic thinking on Future Trends, The Future of China & Emerging Markets, and the Future of Work.

Mary O'Hara-Devereaux, Ph.D's Publications

  • Navigating the Badlands: Thriving in the Decade of Radical Transformation

    • Jossey-Bass; 1 edition
    • September 3, 2004
    Authors: Mary O'Hara-Devereaux, Ph.D

    Navigating the Badlands is filled with the strategies necessary to move forward successfully in the unforgiving business climate that lies ahead, explores the challenges of developing executive readiness and effective leadership, and describes eight Organizational Pains, New Capabilities, and Principles of Transformation vital for doing business in the Badlands. The book provides powerful diagnostic and communication tools, short-term and long-term strategic support, and new collaborative suggestions for the journey. In addition, Navigating the Badlands is filled with illustrative stories of success and failures from a wide variety of companies and organizational models around the world.

  • Globalwork: Bridging Distance, Culture and Time

    • Jossey-Bass; 1st Edition
    • June 1, 1994
    Authors: Mary O'Hara-Devereaux, Ph.D, Robert Johansen

    From "Booklist"

    While many of the books on the globalization of business, society, or telecommunications focus on technology, and others consider cultural diversity, few have dealt with both issues simultaneously. The authors discuss the problems of team building and group activities when team or group members are dispersed—separated by time, distance, and cultures. They cover cross-cultural communication, process facilitation, remote teamwork, and the use of information technology to minimize distances and take advantage of differences. While their scope is truly multinational, the authors target the U.S. and its relations with the Pacific Rim nations, Mexico, and Canada.
