Gus Strand's Summary

I'm a lifelong service practitioner and customer evangelist. I've spent the last 20+ years in a career in corporate L&D and global people management and credit my service focus to a grandfather that had an "old school" small town hardware store. You know the type - worn wood floors, china and Osterizers in the front window, a pipe threader out back and everything - including hot coffee - in between. I've a DNA-level service and learning focus with experience in companies that defined service in ways that other companies strive for: Wal-Mart in Sam's day, Coldwater Creek, Harry & David, Dell and now Hanger.

I contribute effective, practical experience in leading global teams, training and people development. I have deep experience in large scale site and initiative implementation, coaching, facilitation, design and delivery of technical and soft skills subjects at all levels within organizations, as well as human resource management and large scale hiring management experience.

I give myself a personal challenge in every learning organization I have been a part of - to lead the charge and build those departments in to business critical partners with the businesses and customers they support.

I am always looking for my next challenge and opportunity.

Gus Strand's Experience

Learning Leader

Hanger

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; HGR; Hospital & Health Care industry

December 2011 – Present (1 year 11 months) Austin, Texas Area

My current primary responsibility is to drive the learning and change management around implementing enterprise-wide electronic medical records and office systems for nearly 800 locations domestically.

I support traditional learning initiatives as needed as well - from designing and delivering Change Management sessions for the largest trade and education event in the Orthotics & Prosthetics industry to stealth individual leadership and management coaching.

Under my direction, and leveraging internal and vendor SME's, we created full classroom training suites with workbooks, a full training sandbox system supported by traveling trainers and fully mobile and connected classrooms for traditional training scenarios. We have delivered these in two pilot groups, as well as the majority of a Midwestern region. The training has been supported by electronic performance support systems, job aids, and internal communities of practice.

With the realization that traditional training methods were not scalable, consistent enough or a fiscally responsible approach to train hundreds of remote locations, we are moving to my originally proposed solution for this challenge: an eLearning and performance coaching based model that will replace the traditional training methodology.

I am currently leading the creation and implementation of the new learning solution for this implementation. The solution has been developed with a partner company that specializes in simulation based eLearning and is currently being piloted. It consists of over 100 custom role-based simulation courses with role specific and behavior measuring level 3 assessments supported by a network of specifically trained coaches, a full internal website with role specific job-aids, communities of practice and click-level reporting.

Global Services Training Operations Manager

Dell

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; DELL; Information Technology and Services industry

October 2007 – August 2010 (2 years 11 months)

My group was responsible for worldwide training process and policy, learning and content management systems and training specific IT support, training reporting and analytics, certification program management and administration, testing center management, and end-to-end training efficiency and quality for the Global Services Operations organization.

In this charge, my team members eliminated local and regionally redundant LMS' and created an internally developed and globally standardized LMS with CMS capability, created an automated global real-time L&D scorecard, globalized training room inventory systems and numerous other processes and systems to support the business of learning - with over $4MM in annualized (Six Sigma measured) savings in the one year alone.

I managed a global team of uniquely Dell training professionals with members in the US, England, Scotland, Ireland, France, Germany, Malaysia, and Japan. Being a manager of an extremely diverse team spread all over this planet was one of the most enjoyable and rewarding aspects of this role.
