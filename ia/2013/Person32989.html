David Badley's Summary

I work at a mobile ecommerce startup called Airbrite working on NodeJS, BackboneJS, MongoDB, HTML5, Postgres and mobile web development. We're working on disrupting the ecommerce space. We've worked on some great code that everyone and their mother need to have to make ecommerce work. That's given us lots of opportunities to code. A lot. I've always worked from front to back so I can only imagine writing for half the stack would be extremely limiting.

I've worked at several startups. At CrowdStar, I worked both in marketing and on game development and understand how to tie in virals, stats, analytical data, and monetization with games. I was also one of the few engineers who worked on the frontend and backend and was the serverside engineer for several projects.

After, I worked on the backend MyPad, Facebook for the iPad. While there I worked on several different Facebook related and app related projects. The backend used was Rails with Redis for performance on the DB end. Would have used NodeJS if I knew what I know now.

I got the itch to start my own company, and started working a number of different ideas and none of them really panned out. Spread myself a little thin with too many ideas and not enough time. Lesson learned: focus on one idea and pivot when it doesn't pan out. I had the pivoting part down pat.

Without a cofounder and without funding, I spun out after a number of months and ended up looking for something new. I worked at a 500 startups company for a couple months before finding the very new and interesting YC incubated startup "Airbrite" through a friend, Gene Tsai. I was impressed by the team and decided to join up.

I've been a programmer since I was 15. I started with C++ and Java and worked my way towards web, mobile and programming games.

Specialties: nodejs, backbone, ruby, rails, javascript, jquery, html5, css3, sql, php, redis, nosql, mongodb, mysql, scalable architecture, memcache, java, mobile web programming
