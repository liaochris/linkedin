Kristi Colvin's Summary

I am currently seeking a contract, full-time or part-time remote + travel position (from Kansas) doing user experience design, analysis and consulting. User interface design and usability improvement as part of an overall user experience plan for software products and sites, plus branding and marketing for product and service-based organizations is what I do. You can see my portfolio of example work at http://freshid.com/portfolio

Specialties: brand design, product line design and integration, visual standards and guidelines, user-friendly software & retail improvements, user experience design and testing for sites & software

Kristi Colvin's Experience

Principal

Fresh ID

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Design industry

April 2003 – Present (10 years 7 months) Kansas City Area

As Principal of Fresh ID, Kristi Colvin has created business communications since 1993 for leading software organizations and small-to-medium size start-up companies. From a foundation in print design and retail visual merchandising, she added web site design, user interface design (gui design + information architecture + branding + usability) and human factors research to her creative skill set and now specializes in user experience planning for software and sites.

Using an integrated, customer-focused approach that includes social media, digital marketing, brand identity, strategy and world-class design and development, she deliver valuable solutions suited for a mixed media landscape. Areas of emphasis include creative, user experience, web application design and social media marketing for clients and their customers or teams and their fans.

Founder

Women United FC

March 2012 – Present (1 year 8 months) Kansas City, Missouri Area

Women United FC is a community for women who love soccer, a concept created by Kristi and brought to life with 6 other Founders. The community is managed by Founders and Team Reps for all MLS teams and is a great example of what people will do when passion and similarity are brought together. Read more at http://goal.blogs.nytimes.com/2012/04/05/women-united-f-c-a-launch-of-its-own

User Experience Manager

SigmaFlow

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry

February 2006 – September 2007 (1 year 8 months)

As user experience manager for SigmaFlow, Kristi was responsible for defining both the corporate and the product line branding and creating brand standards for everything the prospective customer or user sees. She designed and maintained the corporate web site, designed a new enterprise web-based application, redesigned existing products and developed many unseen prototypes for product redesigns and new product ideas.

User Experience Manager

PentaSafe

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Computer Software industry

January 2001 – April 2003 (2 years 4 months) Houston, TX

While doing consulting for PentaSafe, Kristi was asked to establish the User Experience department within the R&D organization, to build a passion for user experience across the entire organization. She built a usability lab, managed staff and took responsibility for the product design, documentation, usability and packaging for the VigilEnt product line, a cross-platform, integrated security solution designed for large enterprise environments. This resulted in being a contributing factor of the acquisition by NetIQ after only two years.
