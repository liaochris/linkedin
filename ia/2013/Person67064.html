John Sleeper's Projects

  • SkipIt

    • January 2012 to May 2012
    Team Members: John Sleeper, Joel Dubien, Jack Chapa, Andrew Bleeda, Leslie Crandall, Peter Parker, Ben Sullins, , Mathias Gran, Rico Nagtalon, Christopher Rolfs

    The SkipIt 'invitation' (Flash) provides the option to skip an on-line video advertisement, and is presented to users more than 20 million times a day on many different content websites.

    It consumes JSON data for all displayed text and customer account details (like balance), and is i18n compatible for internationalization (supporting multiple languages).

  • Scottrade Media Center Player

    • June 2010 to August 2010
    Team Members: John Sleeper

    I created the Scottrade Media Player using the standard Flash Pro IDE. (The work to integrate with web page was done by Wall Street on Demand web developers.)

    All of the controls are custom built:
    * Scrub bar is client's purple, with gradient.
    * Pop out button, to put video in its own window.
    * Disclosures pop up.
    * Full screen button.
    * Custom Mute/Volume bar.
    * Tool Tips custom class.

    For authenticated users, there are additional screens for your favorite videos and ranking them with stars.

  • E*Trade MarketTrader Tutorial Player

    • September 2010 to October 2010
    Team Members: John Sleeper

    The player is a Flash based frame for viewing sets of tutorials. A few custom control buttons.

    Our team also created the initial content videos, aligning them to recorded voice overs.

  • Financial Times -- Lexicon Search Widget

    • April 2010 to June 2010
    Team Members: John Sleeper

    The FT Lexicon search widget can be added to your blog or website, enabling your readers to search for financial terms and definitions. The Lexicon is a glossary of more than 10,000 financial terms, with content being added by FT journalists on a daily basis.

    This widget was created using the FlashDevelop open source IDE.

    It consumes XML data in order to display Terms of Day, intellisense while typing term, and all definitions.

  • "Best Revenue-Assurance Project" -- World Billing Awards 2005

    • September 2003 to December 2004
    Team Members: John Sleeper, Brad Porter, Sunil Shah, Wendy Wollard, Danielle Harper, Trace Webster, Adam Jenkins, Wilds Ross, Craig Goudy, Greg Knaddison, Mandy Blumreich (Leapoldt)

    Azure Solutions, the revenue-assurance company, and KPN, the Dutch telecoms operators, have won the ‘Best Revenue-Assurance Project’ award at the World Billing Awards 2005, which took place in London on 6 April 2005.

    A panel of industry experts evaluated numerous company nominations and Azure and KPN were awarded ‘Best Revenue-Assurance Project’ based on the work the two companies did to implement an enterprise-wide revenue-assurance system across KPN’s fixed network in the Netherlands.
