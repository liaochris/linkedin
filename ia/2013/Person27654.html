Crystal Rast's Summary

So, here’s the deal: I spent the better part of a decade serving in sales and marketing roles (recruiting, telecom and software). I also owned/operated 2 companies (CandidatesforFree LLC and CWR Consulting LCC) and served on 2 boards (Atlanta Society for Business Intelligence—which I founded—and Atlanta Telecom Professionals) while writing in the wee hours of the morning. After having written a few novels, a novella, and a couple of graphic novels, I took a chance on writing full-time and pursued my Master of Arts in Professional Writing from KSU. In the meantime, I consulted with companies providing everything from whitepapers to web copy to recruiting services and taught English 1101 and 1102 to KSU freshman. Samples are available on my website: http://crystalrast.wix.com/portfolio

Specialities
Writing for web, content development; research and statistical analysis; market strategy; sales; targeted marketing; public relations; C-level networking; start-ups; lead generation; creation of RFIs/RFPs, sales proposals, whitepapers, blogs, and brochures; public speaking. Experience with AP, MLA, and APA styles. Additional creative experience writing fiction, nonfiction, plays and screenplays.

Technologies
Office software (MS Word, Powerpoint, Excel, Access, Publisher, QuickBooks, Quicken, Prezi); CRM (Salesforce.com, Siebel, Zoho); Social Media (Facebook, LinkedIn, Tumblr, Blogger, Twitter, WordPress, Pinterest, YouTube); Research (LexisNexis, Galileo, Diigo); Collaboration (GoogleDocs/Drive, Sharepoint, WebX, GoToMeeting); Website Creation and Content Management (Wix, Joomla, GoDaddy); Creative (Twine, Final Draft, Scrivener).

In the meantime, I’m still a workaholic, and I'm available for contract or perm employment in a communications or marketing role.

And no, I’m not willing to do sales anymore. Those days are long behind me.

In short, if you have something you’d like to run by me, then give me a call. I’m always open to new possibilities.

Crystal Rast's Projects

  • Get the Job You Want− Negotiate the $$ You’re Worth: Job Hunting Strategies from the Recruiting Industry.

    • April 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Crystal Rast

    Although unemployment of just under 8% is not as far-reaching a problem as it was in 2008-2009, economists predict that as a result of increased payroll taxes, increased healthcare expenditures, and sequester budget will result in increased unemployment by the end of 2013. First quarter 2013 hiring was largely a disappointment to leading economists which means that those looking to find a job or change jobs must be savvier than ever to land a position they want at a salary they deserve. But how many people actually know how to find a job?

    Although finding a job is one of the most critical skills a person will use in his lifetime, it is the one least exercised. After all, how many people look for a job every day of their career? The only people who do so are professional recruiters which is why _Get the Job You Want−
    Negotiate the $$ You’re Worth_ leverages my experience of having sold and recruited for hundreds of small, midsized and enterprise companies both in the U.S. and abroad.

    _Get the Job You Want−Negotiate the $$ You’re Worth_ teaches its readers how to not only market themselves but also how to navigate the often murky hiring strategies of various companies. Because there are great jobs out there. You just need to know how to find them.

  • HR Outsourcing for SMBs

    • March 2012 to May 2012
    Team Members: Crystal Rast

    Since the early 1990s, companies have implemented outsourcing strategies to leverage intellectual capital, decrease fixed costs, and employ a flexible labor source; however, over the past seven years, attention has shifted from outsourcing technology processes to outsourcing business processes. Initially offering transactional, task-oriented services such as payroll, human resources outsourcing companies are now providing services of greater business value in the form of training, employee selection and retention, and strategic planning. Human resources outsourcing is still a relatively new concept for many organizations, resulting in a number of perceived and actual risks; however, if these risks are understood and addressed early on, it can lead to a successful, strategic relationship between the service provider and client. From this Essentials Series by renowned business strategist Crystal Rast, readers will learn about the factors driving companies toward a human resources outsourcing model, the risks and advantages of outsourcing human resources functions and processes, and the approaches available for mitigating risk and ensuring a mutually successful partnership.
