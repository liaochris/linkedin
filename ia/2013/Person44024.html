Faisal Azam's Summary

With a degree in Rhetoric from UC Berkeley, a background as a writer and advertising analyst, and a passion for contemporary art and music of all kinds, Faisal Azam is more than just a film editor. Faisal’s dynamic creativity, technical expertise, keen eye for story, and vast knowledge of film—from experimental and avant-garde to obscure foreign films, documentaries and Hollywood blockbusters—allows him to enhance, and even create, films in the editing process. With a particular gift for drawing out subtle themes and strengthening narratives through thoughtfully juxtaposed imagery, Faisal applies his literary, musical, and artistic sensibilities to bring films to life.

Versatile and adept at his craft, Faisal has cut commercials, promos, fine art pieces, documentaries, television shows and narrative films. In the past year, Faisal has completed television documentaries for Dan Rather Reports, National Geographic and Current TV’s Peabody Award winning show, Vanguard. He has also co-produced and edited the feature length documentary, Without Shepherds, a look at Pakistan beyond the sound bites, which premiered at Slamdance and won the Grand Prize for Best Film at the Brooklyn Film Festival. Over the course of his career, Faisal’s work has screened at top tier film festivals like Sundance, Claremont-Ferrand, Palm Springs and BFI London. Salar, the last narrative short he edited, won the Best Dramatic Short Film award at the Austin Film Festival and was shortlisted for an Academy Award in 2013.

As the quality of his work illustrates, Faisal sees every project as a story to be told with style, skill and sensitivity to those subtle details whose mysterious harmony ensures that every narrative hits its intended emotional mark.

Specialties: Film editing; producing; story structure; script and voiceover writing; ideation and concepting; graphic, title and motion design; art direction; photography; color correction and sound design.

For the past nine years, I have worked as a freelance film editor and producer specializing in narrative storytelling within the context of fiction, documentary, commercial, fine art and corporate projects. I have worked with independent fimmakers, fine artists and high profile clients such as Nike, Rolling Stone, Illy, LVMH, National Geographic, TLC, CurrentTV, Consumer Reports, Food & Wine and Mayor Bloomberg’s office.

Editor

Dan Rather Reports

March 2013 – May 2013 (3 months)

Edited a groundbreaking episode of Dan Rather Reports which investigates "Operation Streamline," a controversial federal program that criminally charges undocumented immigrants picked up in the U.S. and sends them to jail. While advocates say that Operation Streamline is a deterrent, critics call it assembly line justice. I collaborated closely with senior producers at Dan Rather Reports to research various aspects of the immigration debate, structure the show, and write and record voiceover.

Editor / Co-Writer

Efran Films

December 2012 – January 2013 (2 months) New York

Edited and co-wrote a 48 minute TV documentary for Current TV's Peabody award winning show, Vanguard. Entitled "The Right to Kill: Stand Your Ground USA," the show investigates the controversial law that came to national attention in the shooting of Trayvon Martin in 2012. In addition to editing and structuring the entire show, I co-wrote the script with the producer, including the voiceover for Vanguard correspondent, Christof Putzel.

Editor

Pipeline 39

February 2012 – May 2012 (4 months) NYC

Edited a 48 minute TV documentary for National Geographic International called "Nazi Sunken Sub," which follows Finish underwater divers as they discover the wrecks of two missing German U-boats. As the finishing editor, I was brought on-board to address extensive network notes and take the show from rough cut to final delivery. My work involved restructuring the story; editing beautiful, underwater diving sequences and archival footage to accompanying music; selecting appropriate and compelling music and audio that hit required pacing and timing marks; adding high-resolution graphics and animated maps; recording and sound editing scratch voiceover tracks; and refining and polishing the entire cut. I also prepared the show for final delivery, including transcoding frame rates, creating a final master and putting together deliverables according to rigorous network specifications.

Colorist

Bray Entertainment

September 2011 – December 2011 (4 months) NYC

Color corrected a 12 episode season, including graphics and green screens, of Mission Menu for TLC International and NuvoTV. Mission Menu follows a self-described "United Nations of Chefs" as they help struggling New York restaurants reinvent themselves by creating authentic signature dishes.

Co-Producer / Editor

Without Shepherds Film, Inc.

January 2010 – November 2011 (1 year 11 months) NYC

Without Shepherds is a narrative-style documentary that follows six subjects across the landscape of Pakistan, and paints a humanistic portrait of the country. As a co-producer, I organized and supervised a team of translators; set up and managed a file transfer system for audio, video and text-based assets; helped shape a successful crowd-sourcing campaign that exceeded its $25,000 fundraising goal; independently raised an additional $25,000; designed high-resolution photographic wallpapers as gifts for donors; and managed relationships between members of the post production team, key donors and media contacts. As an editor, I viewed over 600 hours of raw footage to find, select and craft a compelling narrative on both the story structure and aesthetic levels. I collaborated closely with a co-editor and director to construct dramatic character arcs and plot points, and weave together important cultural, historical and political information. Additionally, I helped train assistant editors, translated footage from Urdu and Punjabi, and subtitled large portions of the film. Without Shepherds premiered at Slamdance in 2013 and won both Best Documentary and Best Film at the Brooklyn Film Festival.
