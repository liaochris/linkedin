Kelly Karius' Summary

I've worn quite a few hats, (see the specialty section below), and I'm thrilled to be pursuing my passion right now.

Bullying is schools is rampant, and many of the currently promoted solutions revolve around awareness. There have been so many suicides and tragedies caused by bullying that the focus is on the losses, and on finding and punishing the bullies - and even their parents.

WE ARE MISSING SOME STEPS!

Along with Ron Graham, I have written a program called "No Such Thing as a Bully". We want to change the language, the mindset and offer concrete solution focused tools that instruct what each one of us can do at both micro and macro levels to fix the problem.

No Such Thing as a Bully does not search out "bullies" and "victims". Very few people will self-identify as a bully...so instead, we focus on actions. Bully actions and victim responses. We all use them at different points of time and context in our lives. You do too, even if you wouldn't call yourself a bully.

It is skills that we need, more than we need punishment.

The world can't be changed by one or two people though, so if you support this idea, please get in touch with me. We need the help of everyone interested. For more information... http://nosuchthingasabully.com

I am passionate about equipping children and teens with foundational Life Skills essential for thriving and communing optimally with others. I have earned a position of Thought Leader and Ambassador for LinkedIn's top ranked, "Parenting 2.0" group.
Blessings!

Specialties

Bullying Prevention and Intervention Expert, Conflict management in the workplace, communication skills training, public seminars and in-house training, Myers Briggs Type Indicator testing and seminars, custody and access issues, adoption homestudies

December 2008 – Present (4 years 10 months) International

The "No Such Thing as a Bully" Program is a game changer in the cycle of bullying and bullying intervention. Stripping traditional labels, the program replaces "victims" and "bullies" with bully actions, victim responses and perhaps most importantly, bystander behaviour. The program provides the tools that adults need to teach children the important skills that create a positive self concept.
