Frank Hannigan's Summary

I got hooked on media in 1970 watching Pele in Mexico on my family's first TV set.

Media ever since has been a central part of my working life.

Fundraising for Goshido through Linkedin became a global media story in 2010.

BBC's Dragon's Den, however briefly my appearance, was my largest audience more importantly I have featured regularly on TV, radio and print on both sides of the Atlantic.

My day job has been in media for thirty years - starting off in Radio and working in Magazines and Daily and Sunday Newspapers.

When the online media took off I worked there.

Later when Social Media took off I worked there too.

In Ireland I have been lucky to work as a founder, and mentor and a director of great start-ups.

Through my work with EBRD I have advised Government Departments and ICT CEOs in Taiwan, Romania, Albania, Mongolia, Kyrgyzstan, Azerbaijan, Serbia and Montenegro.

I have been a visiting lecturer in UCD, DCU, DIT, CIT, LIT, UCC mostly focused on entrepreneurship.

In 2013 I started writing my first business book with Ronaldo Immink - 'Being Asymmetric"

My career is one of constant re-invention, constant improvement and constantly breaking new ground.

Specialties: Start-ups, Turn-around, Innovation, Media, Social Media, Innovation, General Manager, Entrepreneurship, Investment, Sales, Leadership, Business Development, Internet, International Development, Project Evaluation, Networking, Door Opener, Catalyst, Self Starter, Training, Non Executive Director, Mentor, partnership, MBO, Organisational Behaviour, Project Management, Funding, Partnership, Consultant, Newspapers, Magazines, Radio, TV, Augmented TV, Motivational Speaker, Public Speaker, Asymmetric Management, Humour.

  • Common Purpose

    • 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Frank Hannigan

    Common Purpose is an international, not-for-profit organisation that has been running leadership courses which mix people from the private, public and not-for-profit sectors since 1989. Our courses give people the inspiration, skills and connections to become better leaders both at work and in society.

    We help people, organisations, cities and regions to succeed, by broadening the horizons of their leaders and developing their ability to work together to lead complex change.

    Common Purpose deals with real life issues and insights, so our courses are neither classroom nor lecture based. We go beyond standard leadership models and learn instead from the successes and failures of leaders from all sectors. Participants are taken out of their familiar environment so that they examine why, when and how they lead.
