Arthur Balter's Summary

Arthur Balter transforms creative concepts into dynamic environments. Several projects that he has overseen have been featured in Architectural Digest, Chesapeake Home and LIving, Washington Spaces, Washington Home and Design, Capitol File, Inform, Landscape Architect and Specifier News, Washingtonian Magazine, Architecture DC, and Period Homes.

His solid experience in project management, communications, client relationship management, landscape architectural design, and business development simplify the layers of implementation complexity to bring creative concepts to life, efficiently and effectively.

From concept development through project delivery, client objectives remain in the forefront. His insights are the culmination of expertise garnered from a wide range of design and technology engagements, including:

• synthesizing and articulating client requirements;
• developing strategic plans;
• working with design and development teams (nationally and internationally); and
• managing projects and budgets across multi-disciplinary teams.

As a creative problem solver with the talent to anticipate the nuances of complex projects, he sets the stage to form logical and successful courses of action by assembling and choreographing a consortium of highly specialized talent including:

• design resources;
• artisans and craftspeople;
• technologists; and
• subject area experts.

Talent teams are created to address project-specific requirements, including design, research, fabrication, and installation.

Each project is orchestrated and managed from beginning to end, including: conceptual development and refinement, bidding and negotiations, contractor selection, permitting, schedule management, implementation oversight, financial management and reporting, quality assurance, and warranty and maintenance.

Clients and colleagues recognize that this enriched perspective and unwavering perseverance are crucial to making creative visions become rewarding results.
