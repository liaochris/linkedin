Kate Renee's Summary

I live and work in Minneapolis, Minnesota as a professional artist. I graduated from the University of Minnesota with a BA in fine arts, art history, and a minor in design, and have worked with various galleries in the Twin Cities. During my undergraduate studies, I began as an intern at the Katherine E. Nash Gallery and also worked as a gallery attendant at the Larson Art Gallery. I volunteered at the American Swedish Institute as a museum receptionist, and worked at The Minneapolis Institute of Arts.

I finished up her Assistant Gallery Director internship at Altered Esthetics, a non-profit art gallery in Northeast Minneapolis, where I had the chance to curate an exhibition and write grants for the organization. As of January of 2011, the board elected me as the first Director of Solo Exhibitions. In this position, I developed a program designed to offer emerging artists career development guidance and a solo exhibition.

My professional artistic career is building a national reputation with exhibitions throughout the United States. I received honorable mention at the 2010 Arrowhead Biennial at the Duluth Art Institute and was Featured Artist for the Altered Esthetics’ Level_13: Cheat Codes exhibition. Most recently, I was A-listed in City Pages for my exhibition at Gamut Gallery, Imaginarium, with local Minnesotan artist Brett Early. In January 2013, I was awarded a Minnesota State Arts Board Artist Initiative Grant and I am completing my Beauties Behaving Badly Series and implementing a solo exhibition at Gamut Gallery in 2014.

During 2013, I am a protégé in the Women’s Art Resources of Minnesota (WARM) Mentor Program. I am working alongside artist, mentor and life coach, Jill Waterhouse, for two years to build upon my professional arts career.

Kate Renee's Projects

  • Solo Exhibitions Program Development

    • January 2010 to Present
    Team Members: Kate Renee, Amber White, Summer Scharringhausen, Jamie Schumacher

    As an Assistant Gallery Director, Kate Renee and I spearheaded the development of the new Solo Exhibitions Program. This program provides crucial career development and exhibition opportunities to emerging artists to advance their careers. As a new program, we had to develop a schedule, policies, procedures, recruit business partners, recruit artists, and problem solve along the way.

    As I continued on to join the Board of Directors at Ae, I have co-chaired the Exhibitions Committee which continues to oversee the progress and execution of the Solo Exhibitions Program.

  • Artaversaries

    • January 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Kate Renee, Julia Helen Rice, Mark Eric Elton, Marnie Erpestad, Mary Foote, Kristy Childress

    The Artaversaries are a group of artists who work in a variety of media who are coming together to explore their artwork by using the theme of traditional anniversaries. This group includes: Julia Helen Rice, Sarah Theisen, Kristy Childress, Marnie Erpestad, Mark Elton, Mary Foot and Kate Renee.

    The artists first met through the 2012 program cycle of Altered Esthetics’ Solo Exhibition Program, facilitated by board member and program developer, Kate Renee. This group of artists decided to continue to work together and collaborate by fostering creative work and creating a supportive atmosphere for one another. The artists plan to continue to work together by exhibiting in consecutive annual exhibitions based on the anniversary theme.

    The Artaversaries marked their one year anniversary of working together by exploring paper as a media and tool to create, communicate and interact through art at Echo Arts in 2013 during the St. Paul Art Crawl.
