Erich Campbell's Summary

Embroidery Digitizer and Designer with several years of experience in a high-volume production environment and substantial skills in detailed and/or difficult design execution. Wide base of technical knowledge and skills in several related and unrelated fields makes for versatility in small to medium businesses. Interested in creating new methods for using machine embroidery and exploiting e-commerce channels to create and sell decorated apparel.

I sincerely believe in the power of embroidery as a community/team building product.

Specialties

Sculptural digitizing, rendering of difficult and detailed art, low stitch-count / large coverage designs, leveraging new technologies

Black Duck Embroidery and Screen Printing

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Apparel & Fashion industry

I have, and continue, to digitize production friendly, and yet artistic embroidery designs for a high-volume apparel decoration company, and I'm sough out for my ability to execute detailed and difficult designs. Embroidery Digitizing/Design

January 2000 – December 2004 (5 years)

While at Matrix, I not only digitized designs for high-volume embroidery production, but I helped to create and maintain several website properties used in the national marketing of several licensed brands, most notable those of FDNY and NYPD as well as USAF and the US Army in the years immediately following the tragedy of 9/11. In my position I not only created the art assets for the websites, but coded them. Erich Campbell's Projects

    • November 2011 to November 2012
    Team Members: Erich Campbell

Stitched on black twill, with polyester and metallic threads on a Brother BAS-415 single-head embroidery machine.

  • Golden Needle Award, Design of the Year - Artistic Corporate

    Stitches Magazine
    • November 2009

    I won this award for my Low-Stitch count jacket back created for the Albuquerque International Balloon Fiesta. I was lauded for being able to create a large and visually impressive piece while using only a very small number of stitches in order to control costs and running times.

  • Golden Needle Award, Best Multimedia Design - Fashion

    Stitches Magazine
    • November 2009

    I won this award for a jacket-back design featuring a print by Newbern Taylor that we applied to a jacket back and quilted. My main contribution was the addition of free-standing embroidered lace dragonfly wings on the several dragonflies in the print and elsewhere around the print. I also stitched the dragonflies bodies, all digitized to look like the original pieces, to create an interesting shift in texture, as well as a shift in dimension with the wings lifting entirely off of the piece. The piece was lauded by the judges as artistic boutique-styled work.

  • Golden Needle Award - First Place: Single-Color

    Stitches Magazine
    • 2008

    I won this award for my large redwork rendition of the Dion's Pizza logo character. This piece was an intricate engraving-styled image, which I rendered at a size to cover a large apron. The main portion of the stitches were placed one by one in my software in order to create the hand-tooled and craftsman-made look present in the original artwork.

  • Impressions Awards, Third Place: Digitizing Artistry

    Impressions Magazine
    • 2007

    I won this award for the Sunderi Imports design mentioned in my previous Stitches Stitch-Off win below. I had entered it in both contests.

  • Stitch-Off, First Place: Knit

    Stitches Magazine
    • 2006

    I won this award for my Sunderi Imports left chest design. Sunderi Imports brought us a beautiful oil-painted piece of traditional Indian art depicting a seated woman on a cushion. I rendered this design as a small, left chest design, managing to capture much of the essence of the painting, and through my 'carving' techniques, brought dimension to the piece that was widely lauded. This piece was created for and stitched on a highly-textured knit polo shirt, a difficult, albeit common, substrate for commercial embroidery.
