Peter Sherry's Summary

I am an experienced business leader and enabler of change with specific expertise in the learning, recruitment, workforce management, talent management and resourcing sectors. I possess a unique set of skills that runs from being a skilled business leader to training practitioner – giving me a real appreciation of managing organisations where skills acquisition (whether through learning or recruitment) is a core service. I have extensive experience not only of management but also working directly with customers in demanding roles.

I am a passionate advocate of lifelong learning and am driven by a strong desire for people to transform both their lives and career options through training.

My skills include devising and implementing new strategies where significant change is required, including for business development, business growth, business change, operations, service delivery, talent management, managed service provision, recruitment, workforce resourcing, recruitment process outsourcing, master vendor programmes and managed service programmes.

I also have in-depth knowledge of return to work/recruitment programmes, contract management for the Department of Work and Pensions and working with industry bodies such as Learning & Skills Council; European Social Fund and the Learning and Skills Council.

I have an impressive and consistent track record of achieving significant improvement in business growth, operational performance and commercial effectiveness. My experience is pan-European and often involves cross-border solutions and partnerships.

Peter Sherry's Experience

Chief Executive

National Skills Academy Logistics

Nonprofit; 11-50 employees; Logistics and Supply Chain industry

September 2012 – Present (1 year 3 months)

The National Skills Academy for Logistics directs, promotes and facilitates pre-employment training and skills development in the UK’s growing logistics sector. I am responsible for all aspects of business development, operations, growth, business strategy, strategic relationships within industry and suppliers/partners, marketing, product development and business performance.

My goal is to help drive employment within the logistics sector – especially with the implementation of pre-employment training programmes, delivered in partnership with training specialists and businesses, which provide real career opportunities for those currently unemployed. I also want to help raise the profile of the logistics sector as one which is not only vital to the UK economy but is also a real UK success story.

The logistics sector employs around 8% of the country’s workforce, but around 50% of the workforce are not qualified for their roles. This impacts on the sector’s performance and credibility – and it is my mission to change this. My role includes: increasing skills in the logistics sector; transforming the profile of the sector into one which attracts more people as a first-choice career; getting more young people into the sector; ensuring that the Academy delivers skills-development programmes which are relevant to the sector; working with employers to develop partnerships between the Academy and industry; developing long-term strategic plans; leading the Academy’s board in the development of strategy; acting as the ambassador and champion for the Academy with employers, trade associations and other stakeholders; developing the Academy’s brand and achieving wider recognition; building an effective executive team and workforce.

Sales/Commercial Director Omega Resource Group

Datum RPO

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Staffing and Recruiting industry

March 2012 – September 2012 (7 months) United Kingdom

Datum RPO is a leader in recruitment process and talent management. I was hired to create and implement a strategy of continuous improvement for both new and current customers, based around the concept of ‘listening to the voice of the customer’. The strategy covered a comprehensive range of talent management solutions, including technology, content and recruitment process outsourcing. A key part of this was to help establish, promote and drive highly successful pre-employment training programmes and apprenticeships.

A key goal was to recognise that substituting internal resources with vendor services requires the implementation of three essential metrics – compliance, supplier performance and contractor performance. This was combined with a discovery phase which supported two delivery frameworks – resulting in a business model which is designed to drive continual improvement and, at the same time, mitigate risk and make suppliers accountable.
