Romy Macasieb's Summary

Leveraging the years of experience I have in software development, my career path shifted to product management where I've launched several products, across multiple platforms, to millions of people.

The first product I helped software develop and product manage was AIM - one of the most popular instant messaging services available. Later, I led a team to build AV by AOL - dead simple, low barrier to entry, video chat. And now, I help people protect, organize, and share their photos & videos via ThisLife.

ThisLife was acquired by Shutterfly where I continue building and strengthening the user experience and expanding our product line.

Lead Product Manager

ThisLife

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Photography industry

May 2012 – Present (1 year 7 months) Palo Alto, CA

Working directly with the co-founder/head of product and technologies, I'm the sole PM managing ThisLife across all platforms. In addition to product managing, I help fill the void of not having project management, tech management, and recruiting in an effort to support a lean startup.

A few of the products I manage:
• Web - Built using HTML5, the web app is able to lay out thousands of photos & videos in a single view. Technologies used include Backbone.js, CoffeeScript and platform responsive CSS.
• Backend - Architected using much of Amazon Web Services to handle ThisLife's storage and infrastructure. Other features include image processing, facial recognition, dupe detection, and social importing.
• iPhone/iPad app - A full featured experience that makes it so customers never have to touch the web. Though the app is built using a single universal binary, the experience itself is much different based on whether you're on an iPhone or iPad, understanding that each platform comes with its own advantages.
• Desktop Uploader - The uploader is available on Mac and Windows, and includes enhanced functionality such as device auto-import and dupe detection.
