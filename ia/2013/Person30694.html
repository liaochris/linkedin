Darlene Artist's Summary

DARLENE is an artist and visionary, accomplished in a multitude of creative disciples (illustration, the book arts, computer art, photography, painting and copywriting).

Currently, she works as an inhouse marketing specialist for a multi-million dollar corporation, but she seeks more creative projects on a freelance basis which will utilize her advanced skills in digital illustration, graphics/logo design or the book arts.

DARLENE began her career over thirty years ago in Lake Geneva, Wisconsin as the first female fantasy artist for role-playing games. Darlene’s sensitivity for Gothic forms, medieval art and her skills in calligraphy lent a look of authenticity to the burgeoning RPG industry of the vintage RPG era of the 1980s.

At Indiana University, DARLENE earned an MFA in Design (1987). Her education included honing her intuitive ability for sculpting spacial relationships. Applying the principles of Sacred Geometry makes Darlene’s graphics work classic and harmonious--communicating subtly but powerfully on many levels at once. Meaning is amplified: memory is harvested.

By the early 1990s, DARLENE had moved from being a calligrapher with Gothic sensibilities to a type designer adopting the Renaissance ideals of space division and proportion.

DARLENE has sought to balance right and left brain functions–intuitive expression with the fruits of research and exacting detail. As an illustrator, Darlene works intelligently with archetypes and symbols, understands their significance and uses them powerfully and appropriately. Guided by the power and spirit of beauty, her work serves to bridge people’s understanding of important metaphysical concepts.

Specialties

logo design, publication design, book illustration, children's book illustration, greeting cards, fantasy illustration, game design

First Inventor

RPG CARD GAME DESIGN

1982 – 1985 (3 years)

The publication of "JASMINE:THE BATTLE FOR THE MID-REALM" is significant:
• Marks the first occurence of a role-playing card game. DARLENE developed the gaming system, did the art, design and production work for all 112 cards, the layout of the rules and the packaging.
•Also marks first time art as miniature paintings appears on RPG playing cards as 40 of the cards were full-color portraits of characters and locations.

In 1985, the card game was recognized by the Strategist Club as being "The Most Outstanding New Game in a New Category." (awarded Gen Con).

The card game also received favorable reviews:
• Merle Rasmussen, “Fun Is In The Cards With JASMINE,” THE DRAGON Magazine, TSR Periodicals (Lake Geneva, WI) January 1983.
• Patricia Lentz, “Cards and a Good Deal More,” GAMEPLAY Magazine, Avalon-Hill (Baltimore, MD) June 1983, pp. 60-61.
