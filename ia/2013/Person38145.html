Michael J. Miller, Ph.D.'s Summary

Dr. Miller has 25 years experience in the pharmaceutical and medical device industries. He is currently President at Microbiology Consultants, LLC. In this role, he is responsible for providing microbiology, rapid microbiological method, sterility assurance, biotechnology contamination control, isolator technology, Process Analytical Technology (PAT) and regulatory solutions for the pharmaceutical, biopharmaceutical and medical device industries. Prior to establishing Microbiology Consultants, LLC, Dr. Miller held numerous business development, manufacturing, quality and R&D leadership roles at Eli Lilly and Company, Bausch & Lomb, Johnson & Johnson, and Pharmaceutical Systems, Inc.

Dr. Miller has authored over 100 technical publications and presentations in the areas of rapid microbiological method validation, regulatory acceptance and implementation, pharmaceutical microbiology, PAT, ophthalmics, disinfection and sterilization. He is also the editor of the Parenteral Drug Association’s (PDA) Encyclopedia of Rapid Microbiological Methods. Dr. Miller currently serves on a number of PDA’s program and publication committees and advisory boards, was a co-chairperson for PDA’s Annual Global Conference on Pharmaceutical Microbiology and has chaired numerous global technology meetings on rapid microbiological methods.

Dr. Miller holds a Ph.D. in Microbiology and Biochemistry from Georgia State University (GSU), a B.A. in Anthropology and Sociology from Hobart College, and has served as an adjunct professor at GSU and the University of Waterloo, School of Optometry. Recently, he was awarded PDA’s Distinguished Service Award and was named Microbiologist of the Year by the Institute of Validation Technology (IVT).
