Ben Eubanks, PHR's Summary

During the day, I work as the HR Manager at Pinnacle Solutions, a defense contractor in Huntsville, AL, with approximately 80 employees.

We work with the DoD and private companies in several capacities-software development, aviation training, technical publications, and more. Check out our openings: http://www.pinnaclesolutionsinc.com/careers.html

We handle everything from building/designing flight simulators and software to providing instructor pilots for the military to designing technical publications. In my little corner of the Pinnacle world, I do recruiting, employee relations, benefits, manager coaching, and a little bit of everything else.

During the evenings, I write at upstartHR.com. I'm an HR blogger with a passion for leadership, straight talk, and zombies (okay, the last one's a joke).

I've been speaking for several years and enjoy talking about social media for HR and corporate culture, among other things.

I've written an eBooks and developed courses to help HR pros be better at what they do. More here: http://upstarthr.com/store

Specialties: Leadership, social media, communication, writing, zombies, blogging, speaking, human resources, recruiting

  • Employee Retention eBook

    • March 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Ben Eubanks, PHR, Heather Vogel, MHRD SPHR, Benjamin McCall, MBA, Tim Sackett, SPHR, Paul Hebert, Robin Schooling, SPHR, Chris Ferdinandi, Dave Ryan, SPHR, Laura Schroeder, Keith McIlvaine, Shauna Moerke, PHR

    That’s a lot of people.

    We all know the numbers on employee turnover and the impact it has on the bottom line. So what’s our alternative? Retention. Keep the good people around (not by force, hopefully) and keep them on our team. Check out this guide to learn more about how to retain your best and brightest.
