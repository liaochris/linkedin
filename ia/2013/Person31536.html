David Boundy's Summary

Led efforts that led the White House Office of Management and Budget to direct the Patent Office to withdraw the infamous and overreaching Continuations, 5/25 Claims, IDS, Markush and Appeal rules, saving over $20 billion of private sector burden per year. Authored key amicus briefs in Tafas v Dudas case in Eastern District of Virginia.

Author of several famous patents.

In transactional work, represented underwriter in Hewlett-Packard's spin-off of Agilent Technologies ($7 billion), acquiror of The Hoover Co., and represented parties in several other billion-dollar-range corporate transactions, patent portfolio transactions, securitizatons, and financings. Represented many small companies through their early stage to market success, and a number of financiers who helped startups succeed.

Specialties

Obtaining patents on computer software, hardware and architecture (10 years' industry experience as an engineer), and financial structures (structured finance, securitizations, etc.). Litigating patent disputes, including Digital v Intel (Alpha patents vs X86 patents), Reuters v Bloomberg (foreign currency trading), Refac v Lotus (the Pardo patent against Lotus 1-2-3).
