Daniel Charny's Summary

Daniel Charny is an independent curator. Co-founder and director of From Now On, a creative projects consultancy. Professor of Design at Kingston University, Faculty of Art Design and Architecture. And recently co-founder of Fixperts.org.

Charny was the founding curator of The Aram Gallery for experimental and new design. In recent years he has guest curated and advised internationally including to the Design Museum, British Council, Korean Design Foundation, Spanish LABoral Art Centre and Swedish Form museets vänner. In 2010 he guest curated Power of Making at the Victoria and Albert Museum, seen by over 320,000 visitors it is to date the V&A's most popular free exhibition ever staged. Since 2011 Charny consults the Design Museum on Content and Interpretation. Leading the work on future programming and curating of the permanent collection display planned for the New Design Museum building opening in 2015.

At From Now On Charny leads on projects with clients interested in commissioning and developing unique design projects. Working also on agency-side with clients including Estudio Mariscal and Heatherwick Studio.

Charny has been involved in design education for 20 years and was Senior Tutor at the Royal College of Art where he taught for 14 years. His extensive international teaching experience includes lecturing in China, Denmark, Ireland, Israel, Japan, Singapore, Spain and Sweden.

Specialties: Curating contemporary design, concept development, commissioning creative projects, strategic creative programming, brief writing, collection policy writing, teaching design process, design education, industrial design, judging design competitions.

Daniel Charny's Projects

  • Fixperts

    • September 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Daniel Charny, James Carrigan

    Fixperts is a creative social project for makers and thinkers to fix things or situations for people that need a little help in their lives. The story of each fix is captured in a mini documentary that is uploaded online for stimulating inspiration and sharing information.

  • Power of Making

    • June 2010 to January 2012
    Team Members: Daniel Charny, Damien Whitmore, Lauren Parker, Zoe Whitley, Claire West, Annabelle Campbell

    Celebrating the combination of imagination and skill. Also the V&A most visited free exhibition ever staged.

  • The Incidental

    • September 2009 to September 2010
    Team Members: Daniel Charny, matt jones, Antonia Halse, Evonne Mackenzie, Maki Suzuki, Catherine Ince, Dee Halligan

    Commissioned from Daniel Charny by the British Council's Catherine Ince and Alison Moloney. The Incidental was a community-generated digital physical news pamphlet and website for discussion, reviews and recommendations. Published during key design events, The Incidental first launched at the Milan Furniture Fair in April 2009, with a second instalment at the London Design Festival in September 2009, and reached an estimated 30,000 people from over 70 countries. The project was developed initially with Berg and run with Åb Ä Ke. Later joined by Electronest and Glodsmiths graduate group for London Design Fair.

  • Super Contemporary

    • December 2008 to October 2009
    Team Members: Daniel Charny, Ben Weaver, Josephine Chanter, Ashley Woodfield, Tim Beard, Martino Gamper, Melanie Spencer, Deyan Sudjic

    London thinks, designs and makes like no other city; it creates and the world follows. A magnet for mavericks and freethinkers, London has nurtured a creative community that continues to rival all other design capitals. So, Design Museum joined forces with Beefeater 24 to celebrate the fearlessly progressive spirit of London's greatest creative minds, past and present.

    These creative network spurred each other on and this exhibition illustrated how London's pursuit of new, better and braver, crosses architecture, industrial design, graphics, fashion and communications alike. Endlessly pushing at the forefront of design and constantly inventing for new worlds, London's design output is continually Super Contemporary.

    At the heart of the exhibition were fifteen commissions from some of London's most dynamic creatives, as a group they demonstrate the diverse approaches to design in London:

    Bus Shelter by David Adjaye. Gone with the Wind by Ron Arad. Listening Station by BarberOsgerby. Freedom Space by Neville Brody. Batterseum by Nigel Coates. Rain It In by Paul Cocksedge. London Transport by Tom Dixon. Horatio’s Garden by El Ultimo Grito with Urban Salon. Thames Pin by Kit Grover. Vision for the city of London by Zaha Hadid. K9 Post Office Kiosk by Industrial Facility. Lamp Post Chandelier by Thomas Heatherwick. KiosKiosk by Wayne Hemingway. Head to Toe by Ross Phillips. New London Rubbish Bin by Paul Smith.

Daniel Charny's Publications

  • Power of Making

    • V&A Museum, London
    • September 5, 2011
    Authors: Daniel Charny, Christopher Frayling, Daniel Miller, Bruce sterling, Martina Margetts, Patricia Rodriquez, Ele Carpenter

    The Power of Making is a joint publication between the V&A and the Crafts Council.

    This fascinating book features an introduction by curator and educator, Daniel Charny, alongside contributions from international authors that explore contemporary attitudes towards skill, and the potential that skilled making offer the arts and creative industries. Seemingly disparate objects are brought together in a cabinet of curiosities to unite and reinforce creative, cultural, social and educational points of view all offering different ways of understanding the potent power that comes with making. The book also poses incisive questions about the increasing distance people have from making, and the impact that deskilling and the deterioration of making knowledge may have on cultural production and society.
