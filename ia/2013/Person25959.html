CLPM - Certified Localization Project Manager, International Business

There are generally three terms which are often misunderstood referring to adapting products to foreign language markets. Internationalization refers to creating a basic mono-lingual product template that may be easily modified for target markets around the world. Internationalization requires removal of text strings from embedded code, ability to use different units of measure (English vs metric), change date and currency formatting and ability to easily modify a product for local laws and regulations. Globalization refers to a strategic policy to market and sell products in markets around the world. Localization is the most specific process starting with an internationalized product and adapting it to the needs of a specific target market. It most often requires translation of the user interface, help files and documentation and may require changes in features to accommodate local laws and customs.

Activities and Societies: On-line modules based on the Project Management Institute - PMBOK adapted by the Localization Institute to train and award certification in Localization Project Management. Localization is the process of developing products for specific countries and markets.
