Scott Stephens' Summary

Musician, singer / songwriter, skater and more, Scott Stephens is the driving force behind the musical group Liquid Blue. His group has performed in more countries than any other, bringing music and a message of unity to people across the globe. The band was one of the first ever to be certified "green".

As a teenager growing up in Los Angeles, Scott founded Raw Power Magazine, a mostly underground publication covering punk and hard rock music. During the magazines three year tenure, he interviewed the likes of Ozzy Osbourne, David Lee Roth, Iggy Pop, Ted Nugent and many others.

His first job as a singer was with L.A. based Electric Warrior, a band that featured current Liquid Blue guitariest Michael Vangerov (The Grovler). Michael and Scott were 18 when they started performing together.

Scott first put on a pair of roller skates at age 7 and at age 18 he became a pro roller derby skater with the Los Angeles Thunderbirds. He skated and traveled with Roller Games for four years. He continues to skate regularly and brings his skates on almost every Liquid Blue road trip. Of the more than 400 cities Scott has visited, Amsterdam and Paris are his favorites.

Scott lives a green lifestyle powering his home with Solar panels; investing in only socially screened mutual funds; using locally grown organic foods and much more. For more info, see www.Liquid-Blue.com/Green.

Vices include (but not limited to) Absinthe, Kava, Coca tea, Yerba Mate, Organic/free trade coffee, chocolate and other things that cannot be listed here. Most of these vices were formed as a result of Liquid Blue international performances and Scott was introduced to Absinthe in the Czech Republic; Kava in Fiji, Coca Tea in Peru and Mate in Argentina.

Specialties

Entertainment Management, Recording & Touring Vocalist, Insurance Agent, Financial Consultant, Nutrition.

Scott Stephens' Experience

Manager/Vocalist

Liquid Blue Band (Sole Proprietorship)

Sole Proprietorship; 11-50 employees; Entertainment industry

January 1996 – Present (17 years 8 months) Greater San Diego Area

Liquid Blue® is a multi award-winning international recording / touring act and cover band for high-end events. The San Diego based ensemble has performed in more than 100 countries and hold a Guinness World Record®. The band scored a Billboard Top-10 Hit in 2010 and have been featured on Seven Magazine Covers. In 2005 Liquid Blue became the first American act to be signed by a major Chinese record label and was chosen to represent the United States at the CCTV kick-off concert for the 2008 Summer Olympics in Beijing. The group has performed at sold-out, ticketed shows worldwide and has received more accolades, awards and recognition than any other cover band in the music industry. Liquid Blue is a certified green business.

Liquid Blue is the most accomplished and highly acclaimed party band in the event business. Music To Move You"® is the band's official slogan and the audience is sure to be pumped up and excited from the first number until the finale. Honored as "America's Best Dance Band" by the National Music Awards, Liquid Blue was also named "Entertainment Act of the Year" at the 2012 Biz Bash Readers Choice Awards as well as "Entertainer of the Year" at the 2011 Event Solutions Spotlight Awards. Locally they've been recognized "Best Cover Band" at the San Diego Music Awards. The band covers over 600 songs including a multitude of musical styles. This is not your typical cover band but an internationally acclaimed, cutting-edge ensemble that will intrigue your guests and leave them wanting more. Liquid Blue has proven both on-stage and in the studio to be one of the world's premiere dance bands!

Vocalist

The Pacesetters Band

1981 – 1984 (3 years) Anchorage, Alaska Area

The Pacesetters were the house band at the Pines Club in Anchorage from about 1972 to 1988. The Pines was the largest night club in the city and the Pacesetters were broadcast live over KBYR AM Radio nightly. The group performed five nights per week, 250 shows a year at the club.

Owner

Raw Power Magazine

1977 – 1980 (3 years) Greater Los Angeles Area

Raw Power was founded by three sixteen year old teens in Woodland Hills CA; Scott Stephens, Robert Olshever and Murray Schwartz. What started out as a fanzine covering mostly local Los Angeles talent turned into a glossy magazine with interviews and features on national rock acts. The short lived publication eventually morphed into the first "rock & roll travel incentives" company in the United States and today has offices in London, New York, Chicago and Los Angeles.

Interviewed the likes of Ozzy Osbourne, David Lee Roth, Iggy Pop, Ted Nugent and many others.
