      The Blood Donor Justice movement grew out of a continued frustration at the campus-level with FDA policies regarding gay and bisexual men giving blood. Any man who has had sex with another man at least once since 1971 receives a "permanent deferral" from giving blood even if he has tested negative for sexually transmitted diseases and infections. In essence, this effectively bans all gay and bisexual men from donating FDA blood despite a national shortage of blood in blood banks. More importantly, it bans these men based on a general classification by sexual orientation and not on specific evidence that these men carry pathogens that endanger the blood supply. The Sarah Lawrence College community has maintained a ban on campus blood drives until the FDA reverses its stance. Using the "We The People" petition site as a platform for interacting with the federal government, the Sarah Lawrence community attempted to gather the requisite number of petition signatures in under a month to prompt a response from the Obama administration.

      As the Marketing Lead, I coordinated the strategic meetings that defined the movement's branding and message while also attempting to network with other like-minded movements on a budget of $0. Though the petition gained 15,000 out of the 100,000 necessary, the traction it gained was at least educational to many people who had never been aware of the policy's existence.
