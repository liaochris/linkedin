  • How to bridge the gap in capability levels

    • December 13, 2007
    Authors: Martijn van der Cruijsen

    The embedded software is gaining more and more importance in the automotive industry. The number of applications where software is directly controlling the safety of the vehicle and its passengers is increasing rapidly.
    Usually, the responsibilities for these systems are distributed over the OEM and several TIER-1s and TIER-2s, each with specific domain knowledge, background and quality level. These multiple disciplines and different company cultures lead to different interpretations of topics like safety, development-process, quality assurance etc. Standards like automotive-spice and IEC61508 will help to clarify and quantify these topics, but different company cultures and engineering backgrounds still leave room for discussion.
    By using a ‘steer-by-wire’ example that currently goes into production, the typical pitfalls and challenges are explained. Rialtosoft developed most of the embedded software of this safety critical system.
