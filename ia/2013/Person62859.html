Jessa Chupik, MA's Summary

My motto is that relationships are the only currency that matter in recruitment and talent management.

I have spent the last ten years recruiting talented people in the higher education, not-for-profit and healthcare sectors. I have experience as an internal recruitment/talent management specialist as well as a number of years as an executive recruiter serving external clients.

To better understand the opportunities and challenges that executives and managers face, I decided to leave executive recruitment for a period of time to gain experience as a senior leader with the BC Public Service. During my career in the BC Public Service, I held progressively more senior roles focusing on talent management and building research partnerships between post-secondary institutions, industry and government. I also developed partnerships with applied researchers (faculty and students) from British Columbia’s post secondary institutions to increase decision support capacity and enhance expertise and objectivity of research.

I hold an Honours BA in Indigenous Studies and a Master of Arts degree in Indigenous Studies and Canadian Studies both from Trent University. Currently, I am a volunteer with Samara, an independent charitable organization that works to improve political and civic engagement in Canada.

I am very proud to be a staff member at Ryerson University.

Please note that I only accept invitations from individuals that I have had direct contact with.

BC Public Service

Government Agency; 10,001+ employees; Government Administration industry

June 2007 – October 2009 (2 years 5 months) Victoria, BC

I held progressively more senior roles with the BC Public Service. I started as a Corporate Recruiter and was a member of a small team that developed talent management and recruitment strategies and services for the entire BC Public Service (30,000 employees). I built talent relationships with internal and external applicants.

I moved into a Director role focusing on research partnerships and outcome evaluation with the Ministry of Citizens' Services. I encouraged government employees to become outcome champions. I led some fascinating government-wide projects and provided human resources management to the branch (45+people).

I was then sent off on a special assignment to help recruit senior management positions in the Ministry of Citizens' Services and the Ministry of Finance.

After my special assignment, I returned to Knowledge and Information Services (KIS) where we provided ministry clients with affordable and responsive internal research consulting services. KIS connected clients who shared common research interests, resulting in cost savings, reduced duplication and greater collaboration throughout government. KIS developed partnerships with applied researchers (faculty and students) from British Columbia’s post secondary institutions to increase decision support capacity and enhance expertise and objectivity of research.

In my role, I was in charge of various programs, mentored staff, and was part of a team that tried to get people to share information across government. My career with the BC Public Service was very exciting, but I returned to Ontario in 2009 for family reasons.
