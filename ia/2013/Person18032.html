Brianna E. Williams' Summary

Brianna Williams is an attorney, diversity management consultant, and visionary.

She is the founder of Mélange, a legal and consulting firm that specializes in diversity management, strategic visioning, corporate and personal brand management, organizational development, HR consulting, and entertainment law. Through Mélange, Brianna has helped several organizations manage their differences, similarities, and tensions in meaningful ways.She has presented at conferences across the country on the topic, and her work has been published nationally.

Brianna also serves as Of Counsel at StokesWagner, an employment law boutique.

Prior to entering law school, Brianna ran a consulting firm aimed at addressing the unique needs of musicians, artists, and photographers. Through Ethereal Image, Brianna brokered CD replication agreements with nationally recognized companies; regularly assisted her clients with the attainment of copyrights and mechanical licenses; designed websites, press kits, and marketing media, negotiated record deals, management, and booking agreements, and facilitated the distribution of her clients’ releases through established outlets.

Brianna is also an active member of the community. She has served as the Director of Communications for the National Bar Association, is a voting member of The Recording Academy; is an active volunteer for the Georgia Lawyers for the Arts; has served as a guest lecturer in Music Business at Loyola University, and is a board member of Genesis Shelter. In 2010, Brianna was recognized as one of the Nation’s Best Advocates: Top 40 Under 40 by The National Bar Association and IMPACT.

Brianna is admitted to practice in both Georgia and Louisiana. In her free time, Brianna is an instructor of the Law and Justice course at Morehouse College, an initiative she engineered through the Project Identity Institute to encourage young men to enter the legal pipeline. She also enjoys playing jazz piano professionally.

  • A numerical test of different integral conditioning approximations for a semiclassical initial value representation for wavepacket propagation

    • Chemical Physics Letters
    • December 9, 1997
    Authors: Brianna E. Williams, Herman, Michael

    Recent numerical evidence indicates that the rapid spreading of nearby classical trajectories in chaotic regions results in numerical difficulties and reduced accuracy for semiclassical initial value representation methods. These problems can be significantly reduced and the accuracy of the methods increased through the application of integral conditioning techniques, which have been developed for the numerical evaluation of integrals with highly oscillatory integrands. This work studies the effectiveness of these conditioning techniques, when applied to one semiclassical initial value representation wavepacket propagation procedure, the HK propagator method.
