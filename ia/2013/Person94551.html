Mike Sanders MA, PMP's Summary

If I were to summarize my passion, it would be as a change agent and student of organizational change. I have been through five significant organizational changes in my career with roles of both planner and participant. My experience and education in this arena has been invaluable in strategic planning and change implementation.

Professionally, I am an experienced program and project manager, technical manager, soft-skills instructor, technical writer, and public speaker. Very comfortable working in fast-paced, evolving environments, I am able to influence others using a positive, collaborative, servant-leader approach with a track record of meeting organizational goals.

I have taught at universities, colleges, and companies, as well as facilitated many workshops on Native American practices and philosophy, advancing and following the life practices of respect, honor, and accountability.

I am also a popular business and management public speaker, presenting on topics including Controlling Communication Channels, "Advanced" Multitasking, Organizational Psychology, Organizational Change, Learning Organizations, Knowledge Workers, Management Psychodynamics, SuperConnectivity, and Minimalist Business Writing. Speaking venues include the Project Management Institute (PMI) (Riverside, Los Angeles, Orange County, San Diego, and Central Coast); IIBA (Orange County); Naval Weapons Center (San Diego); SCQAA (Orange County and Riverside); the annual SoTeC Conference (Long Beach); annual PMI Conferences (San Diego and Orange County); annual PMI Symposiums (Silicon Valley and Las Vegas); the UCI Project Management Certification Program (Irwindale and Rosemead); IEEE (Orange County); ASQ (Irwindale and Temecula); STC (Los Angeles and San Diego); and APICS (San Fernando Valley and Orange County).

Educationally, I have earned BA and MA degrees in Organizational Management and certifications in project management (PMP) and writing instruction.

Presentation: Rise of the Knowledge Worker: Emergence of the Learning Organization

Project Management Institute

Nonprofit; 201-500 employees; Nonprofit Organization Management industry

January 2013 – Present (8 months) Orange County, California Area

Peter Drucker predicted in 1959 that Knowledge Workers would be the most influential work group in the modern organization. They are, and that organization is the "Learning Organization." Learning Organizations are driven by Knowledge Workers and fueled by teaming and systems thinking. The products of these dynamic organizations are innovation and Intrapreneurship, not to mention perpetual organizational change. This compelling presentation presents the concept of Knowledge Workers and Learning Organizations in simple terms, edified by four short films from the Harvard Business Review and a review of systems and systems thinking with Dr. Russel Ackoff, a contemporary of W. Presentation: SuperConnectivity - Advanced Management using Learning Organization Techniques

Project Management Institute - Los Angeles Chapter

Nonprofit; 1-10 employees; Nonprofit Organization Management industry

July 2012 – Present (1 year 2 months)

SuperConnectivity is a four-hour, PDU-credited workshop describing advanced management concepts and techniques. SuperConnectivity optimizes managerial performance to achieve individual and organizational goals. Rather than striving for typical improvement targets of 5, 10, or 20%, advanced-management techniques work together for order-of-magnitude, breakthrough improvements. The five advanced-management concepts are based on Dr. Ken Blanchard's developmental matching model; Dr. Childre's Heartmath-influenced perceptive introspection; Dr. Mark Waller's three brains; Dr. Kahili King's Native American self-mastery; PMI's PMP communication channel analysis and control; and Dr. Gary Chapman's connective-language model. The goal of SuperConnectivity is to arm managers with a complete set advanced-management tools and the knowledge to use them. The application of SuperConnectivity will produce lasting solutions, confidence, wisdom, peace, and order-of-magnitude results.

Specifically, developmental matching matches employee development levels to management styles. Perceptive introspection defines our four levels of consciousness and how to influence behavior and performance within them. Native American self-mastery describes the seven ancient principles at work within our lives and how their use will make us better managers. Communication channel analysis and control are vital to controlling processes, the business conversation, and ensuring desired outcomes. The connective language tool deploys the five means we all use to effectively communicate and the single best language each of us responds to.

The SuperConnectivity workshop was a great success and produced excellent participant evaulations including, "Mike kept the class engaged and interested. He has an effective teaching style", "I thought Mike did an excellent presentation. Really enjoyed it and the time went by fast", and "Great subject."

Mike Sanders MA, PMP's Projects

  • Book - Rise of the Knowledge Workers: The Explosive Transformation of Learning Organizations

    • January 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Mike Sanders MA, PMP

    This book describes the 20-year trek of the knowledge worker in business through the evolution of learning organizations. The knowledge worker model and environment required to nurture and use this advanced workforce effectively are described. In the discussion, the future of organizations, required leadership skills, equal importance of education and experience, and systems thinking are outlined. But the ultimate test of knowledge is in its application. This book prescribes methods to implement the environment and organizational architecture required to manage 22nd century organizations. The expectation: order of magnitude, not incremental improvements: to do more, to work less, and to be happy.
