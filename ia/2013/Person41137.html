Eric Grant's Experience

Creative Director, Emerging Experiences

Razorfish

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; pub; Marketing and Advertising industry

July 2012 – Present (1 year 3 months) New York

“Innovating tomorrow, today” isn’t just a tag line, it’s what the Razorfish Emerging Experiences group does every day. Comprised of designers, tinkerers, developers and dreamers, their passion is to create never before seen experiences that live at the intersection of physical and virtual worlds.

As the principal creative lead for the Razofish Emerging Experiences Group, Eric manages national teams working on projects that live at the intersection of physical and virtual worlds. He directs all phases of creative work—from concept development through production—and serves on the group’s leadership team where he manages social media efforts, assists in business development, project planning, and staffing.

Clients include Ford Motor Company, The Lincoln Motor Company, Capital One, L.L. Bean, Target, Bridgestone/Firestone, and American Express.

Director of Brand Experience

Hot Studio

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Design industry

April 2007 – July 2012 (5 years 4 months) New York City

Eric’s time at Hot Studio was a tale of two cities. Having started out in their San Francisco home, Eric got tired of the fresh air and outstanding wine and decided to move east to help grow Hot Studio’s New York office. As Director of Brand Experience, Eric created and managed design efforts for a variety of projects across multiple platforms—in addition to growing, managing and mentoring the design team. He was responsible for participating in all aspects of the business development and design lifecycle—from the initial pitch and collaborative workshops, to the user experience, creative and development stages.

Clients included Google, Time Inc., Gilt Groupe, Rodale Publishing, Liquidnet, Warner Music, Cisco, eBay, LexisNexis, Ancestry.com and the YMCA.

Note: Hot Studio was recently acquired by Facebook.

Art Director

a5 group

2003 – 2007 (4 years) Chicago

Sustainability and community involvement is in the DNA at a5 and gave Eric the opportunity to work on a variety of projects that fostered social change. Whether it was giving Chicago's premier political watchdog group a platform to more effectively expose corruption, or designing an experience that helped preserve the memories of those lost in the Holocaust, Eric's work at a5 helped raise awareness to the world around us.

Clients included the Better Government Association, the Environmental Protection Agency, The New York Stock Exchange, Timex, and the Illinois Holocaust Museum & Education Center.

Freelance Designer

Eric Grant Design

2000 – 2003 (3 years) Madison, Wisconsin

When the dot-com bubble burst in 2000, Eric was "let go" from his cushy job at one of the Midwest's rockstar companies. But instead of grieving, Eric packed up his desk, pushed in his Aeron chair and drank the night away at Madison's famed Cafe Montmartre (it closed after he left the Dairy State). It was there he decided to spend his days freelancing in sweats, found a local design co-op and politic. Eric designed websites and identity systems, and created print campaigns for clients across the country. They included Warner Classics & Jazz, Planned Parenthood, the AFL-CIO, the United States Congress and countless political campaigns and community groups.

Senior Designer

Sonic Foundry

Public Company; 51-200 employees; SOFO; Information Technology and Services industry

2000 – 2000 (less than a year) Madison, Wisconsin

Eric's stint at Sonic Foundry included rock stars, pizza Fridays, crazy parties and an organization that started from a few people, grew to nearly 500 in a few years, and shrank to less than a hundred overnight. This was pretty common in the late nineties, but was something to brag about in a state dominated by cheese heads. Working with some of the best talent in the industry, Eric's work at Sonic Foundry focused on creating dynamic packaging and ads for the music industry.

Designer

Wisconsin Legislature

Government Agency; 10,001+ employees; Government Administration industry

1995 – 2000 (5 years) Madison, Wisconsin

Once upon a time, before an i-anything existed, Eric designed a bunch of things today's kids would look at in confusion: ink on paper. Working for the leadership of the Wisconsin Legislature, Eric combined his passion for politics and design and created an insane number of innovative propaganda pieces. And even though he designed, built and maintained one of the first interactive experiences for the State of Wisconsin, Eric's work with pulp has given him a greater understanding of pixels.

Policy Analyst

Wisconsin Legislature

1993 – 1995 (2 years) Madison, Wisconsin

After running unsuccessfully for the Wisconsin State Assembly at the age of 22, Eric decided to take his passion for politics to the state capitol anyway. Eric worked with the majority in the WIsconsin Legislature to help draft legislation and write speeches and political talking points - while getting paid to argue.

Eric Grant's Projects

  • YMCA of San Francisco

    • 2009 to Present
    Team Members: Eric Grant, Jay Cosnett, Julian Pscheid, Roy Martin

    Together with Emerge's design partner Hot Studio, Emerge Interactive created a new website experience for the YMCA of San Francisco, integrating the website using the WebActive Content Management System (CMS), which allows the YMCA-SF staff to easily make changes and updates to the content of their site in minutes.
    WebActive provides the ability to the YMCA-SF to create and manage multiple micro-sites. This innovative feature allows individual branches to control and update the content of their own sites, while news, events and other content assets can be seamlessly shared between all the branches and the headquarters home site.

  • URI.org

    • 2009 to Present
    Team Members: Eric Grant, Margot Merrill Fernandez, Jay Cosnett, Julian Pscheid, Roy Martin, Erik Ruthven

    URI is a nonprofit peace-building and interfaith organization dedicated to promoting enduring, daily interfaith cooperation, and works around world to end religiously motivated violence and to create cultures of peace, justice and healing. Emerge was pleased to provide a substantial portion of their work to URI in the form of an in-kind donation as a part of Emerge's ongoing effort to support non-profit organizations which help others and make our world a better place.

    The solution that Emerge developed with Hot Studio was to empower URI members around the world to become content creators, decentralizing as much of the website as possible. Each of the 470+ Cooperation Circles around the world has a page on the new website, and can share their activities through blogging, online forums and events.
