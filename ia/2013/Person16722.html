Brenda Branson's Summary

Founder, Director of "Broken People," (division of ITMH not-for-profit org.)

"Broken People" is a community for people who are struggling and searching. In addition to providing free resources, online encouragement, and free counseling, we offer:

Training on-site for individuals and churches wanting to start domestic violence support groups.

Consultation and training on-site for pastors, churches, and organizations who want to know more about domestic violence and how to help families in crisis.

Presentations at retreats and conferences on topics:

"A Woman's Journey With God"
"Encountering God--Love Letters from Scripture"
"The Woman at the Well"
"The Women Jesus Loved"
"Pain and Joy--Do they really coexist?"
"God Among Us--Preparing Our Hearts for Christmas"
"Violence Among Us?"
"Why Does He Do That?"
"Are You in an Abusive Relationship?
"What Does a Healthy Relationship Look Like?"
"Why Doesn't She Just Leave?"
"Pastors' Wives and Violence Behind Closed Doors"
"Red Flags for Dating Relationships"
"Red Flags for Parents and Teens"
"Setting Healthy Boundaries"
(Many other topics not listed here.)

Co-Founder, President of FOCUS Ministries (1975-2008)

Public Speaker: Seminars, Conferences, Teaching, Radio/TV interviews (Focus On The Family, Moody Radio, AACC World Conference, PASCH Conference)

Author: "Overcoming Abuse God's Way" published by Westbow Press, "Violence Among Us" published by Judson Press, authored more than 1000 articles (http://brokenpeople.org), developed 4 training manuals on domestic violence and abuse

Trainer and Consultant (Workshops, Seminars) on Domestic Violence Awareness and Starting Support Groups (Moody Church in Chicago, IL, Coral Ridge Presbyterian Church in Ft. Lauderdale, FL, and more)

Leading Support Groups, Small Groups, Bible Studies

Women's Conferences: Speaker, workshop presenter

Women's Ministry: Director, Bible Study Leader

Specialties

Women's ministry, publishing, writing, public speaking, consultant, trainer and seminar presenter on domestic violence and relationship topics.
