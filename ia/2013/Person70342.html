Judith Leary Joyce's Summary

I've worked with people for 35 years – originally as a psychotherapist and trainer; now as a facilitator, executive coach and consultant. I am fascinated by personal development - my own and other peoples – so I'm always learning and stretching my thinking. My delight is to find ways of simplifying complex psychological concepts into simple models that anyone can use, because the more we understand, the easier it is to change.

I've written three books: Becoming an Employer of Choice, Inspirational Manager, The Psychology of Success. All are based in my own research and are full of stories about the wonderful people I met along the way. I am a chatty writer, so my work is easy to read and apply. It's great when I walk into an office and see my book beaten up, written on and well used!

Having developed a series of highly relevant, easy to understand models, I enjoy sharing them with business audiences. I believe presentations should be entertaining, interesting and engaging, so I keep it short, involve the audience in relevant activity and use plenty of stories to illustrate my point. I love hearing the buzz in a room full of people as they apply their learning to real time situations and explore how to take it back into the workplace.

I set up Great Companies Consulting in 2002 to help organisations become great places to work. Our recent focus has been on talent development - understanding how talented people stretch into their potential and how we can facilitate this in a business setting. Providing the right conditions for true development and seeing people blossom and shine is pure magic!

Specialties

International inspirational speaker
Working with leadership teams and senior managers to refine their people skills, so they can function more effectively in their business
Developing Inspirational Managers so they deliver exceptional results through their people, including managing their high performers.
Building talent is a stable and sustainable way.

  • Inspirational Presentations

  • Inspirational manager- emergent style

    • 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Judith Leary Joyce, Sarah Jones

    Working with a colleague, Sarah Jones, we recently developed a new and emergent way of running the Inspirational Manager one day workshops. The cultural shift taking place was extremely demanding for the participants and the dominant need was changing by the day. We needed to find a way of making the content of each workshop totally relevant, but any plan put in place could be redundant before we began. We started by gathering the prominent issues and clarifying which were most significant. Sending the group off for a coffee, the facilitators then formulated a process that enabled participants tp explore the issues in a creative way, including any relevant strategic and psychological constructs that would help. Its a very exciting way of working - demanding, but extremely gratifying - and very successful for the client.
