Marianne Carlson's Summary

As a speaker, trainer and Change Management expert, I work with organizations who want to manage change, so they get better buy-in, less resistance, and maximum benefit from the changes they need to implement. I combine humor, science, and genuine expertise to present programs that help people understand how to manage change and lead their organizations through the challenging changes ahead.

Change is difficult, but it's absolutely necessary in today's business environment. I work with business leaders and change agents to develop change methodologies that help minimize resistance and maximize the ROI from their change efforts. I show them how to effectively lead the transformations they envision.

Computer Support and Training Coordinator

Vermont State Judiciary

November 1999 – November 2011 (12 years 1 month) Vermont - statewide

I trained and managed a team of "Power Users" to provide the first-line support in each county courthouse, and provided IT training on a regular basis to new hires and IT Support personnel. I also provided one-on-one training with all judges and Supreme Court Justices, and I served on the Judiciary's Education Committee which planned and implemented annual training for the judges clerks, and other personnel. I wrote the Power Users' Manual - a 200-page manual that explained the procedures and troubleshooting techniques that helped them provide support to the users in their courthouses, and I wrote the first-ever Internet Use Policy for the Judiciary. In 2001, under my leadership, the Power User Team was honored as an "Outstanding Team for Public Service", the only team from the Judicial Branch to receive such an honor.

Website Designer/User help Desk

US Military Academy at West Point

Government Agency; 10,001+ employees; Military industry

October 2004 – May 2007 (2 years 8 months)

The Army's Chief of Staff requested we create an online Leadership Development tool, which came to be called the Army Benchworks. As the project's web designer, I was responsible for the content and layout of hundreds of inter-linked pages on the Benchworks site. I worked closely with the project's Network Chief and database programmer. The site was highly interactive, and serviced the entire Army worldwide. I was the sole customer support person for the project.

IT Specialist; IT Security Lead

US Army

Government Agency; 10,001+ employees; Military industry

November 2002 – June 2004 (1 year 8 months) Germany and Iraq

I ran a helpdesk and did general IT support for the Army in Germany, and when the Iraq War began, my unit (and I) were deployed to Iraq. There, I continued to serve in IT support and was put in charge of IT Security for the entire 3rd Corps Support Command - a until of over 20,000 soldiers across Iraq and Kuwait. I trained other support staff and non-IT personnel in the stringent IT procedures that were necessary to keep communications secure and reliable - a critical role under the circumstances.
