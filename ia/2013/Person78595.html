Lary Coppola's Summary

As Executive Officer of the North Peninsula Building Association (NPBA), I'm responsible for all day-to-day business operations, as well as leading efforts surrounding membership and revenue growth, Built Green, and reinvigorating the group’s well-respected Future Builders Program — an educational partnership introducing high school and college students, to all aspects of the building industry. It emphasizes key academic, trade and personal skills for career and life success.

My industry experience includes serving as a Board member of the Homebuilders Association of Kitsap County (HBA), and its Government Affairs committee for almost 20 years; having been a State Director of the Building Industry Association of Washington (BIAW), serving on its Legislative Policy Committee and PAC, as well as graduating from the IBEW/NECA Electrical apprenticeship.

I founded Wet Apple Media, serving as President and CEO. The company publishes the Kitsap Peninsula Business Journal, WestSound Home & Garden Magazine, and Remodel Kitsap Magazine.

I was Editor & Publisher of the Business Journal from its debut in April 1988, until April 2012, turning the reins over to Tim Kelly, to focus on leading the strategic effort to transform our company from a traditional print-based publisher to one with subscription-based online editions of existing print products, and recreating the firm into one that’s major focus is to now showcase online, social media, and smartphone-based publications with a printed component.

I have been writing about cars on a regular basis for over 20 years, and my automotive review column, "Behind The Wheel," is published in a number of City/Lifestyle magazines and websites — plus the publications owned by wet Apple. The Auto Reviewers Blog has been published since 2008.

Mayor & Chief Administrative Officer

City of Port Orchard

January 2008 – 2012 (4 years) Port Orchard, WA

Elected in November of 2007, to serve as the City’s Mayor and Chief Administrative Official, the Mayor is responsible for all financial, budgetary and personnel functions, including writing and administering the annual $30+ million budget, and overseeing all City departments, and Department Heads, including the Police Department and Courts, as well as 70 FTEs. The Mayor is also the public “face” of the City, and primary news media contact.

Major Accomplishments Include:
• Instituted private sector business practices, which changed the City’s approach to spending, as well as to providing services. This stabilized its long-term finances without layoffs or furloughs, and without cutting any services or capital projects. Net gain: $500,000+ in annual savings and higher levels of customer service.

• Three major annexations: An opportunity was recognized in the Puget Sound Regional Council's (PSRC) mandate on Urban Growth Area annexation. The City annexed three commercially-zoned areas with a significant amount of retail, and additional retail growth potential. Net Gain: Over $3 million in new revenue after the cost of delivering services to those areas, and over 250 retail jobs came into the City.

• Recruited New Employers: Net Gain: Over 100 new family wage ($20+ per hour) jobs in the City.

• Qualified the City as an AWC “Well City:” Net Gain: Two percent savings on City employee healthcare costs.

• Instituted a Citywide “Green Initiative:” Net Gain: $40,000 savings on paper costs, smaller carbon footprint.

• Overhauled the City’s Permitting Process: Net Gain: It is now the fastest in the Western Puget Sound Region.

• Crime was reduced by 60 percent and violent crime by 45 percent. Net Gain: A much safer community.
