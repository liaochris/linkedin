Co-founder, Vice President of Media Relations

Boston Women in Media & Entertainment

January 2012 – Present (1 year 9 months) Greater Boston Area

In the spring of 2012, radio personality Candy O'Terry and event architect & media relations strategist Dayla Arabella Santurri launched Boston Women in Media & Entertainment, a non-profit organization dedicated to creating a vibrant, interactive community of support for women within the broadcast and performing arts in the Boston area.

Originally formed in 2002 as the Boston Chapter of the American Women in Radio & Television, Boston Women in Media & Entertainment (BWME) was co-founded in 2012 by board President Candy O’Terry and Executive Vice President Dayla Arabella Santurri. Through the Live & Learn series of programs in partnership with the Arts & Business Council of Massachusetts, signature series 'The Story Behind Her Success', networking opportunities and need-based scholarships in broadcasting and the arts, BWME strives to nurture and mentor this generation of women and the next in all aspects of media and entertainment.

BWME's award winning event, Stars Over Boston reveals local, undiscovered talent with singers performing for their favorite charities and a guaranteed spot on the next season of the Emmy Award winning Boston based talent show Community Auditions.
