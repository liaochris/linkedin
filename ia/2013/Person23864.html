Christian Aubry's Summary

After studying for two years in History of Art, Christian Aubry worked in Marketing Research and Travel & Tourism, then became a professional journalist in French magazines in the mid-80s. In 1989, he moved to Montreal and pursued his career in the Tourism business, designing, planning and marketing inbound tours in Canada.

Fascinated by the Internet he "plundged" into this medium in 1997. He was first Managing Editor at the IT daily news Multimedium website and, in 1999, he launched an online travel agency. This startup eventually merged with the Vancouver-based inbound tour operator SNV International, where he was appointed as an Internet Content Manager. SNV went bankrupt after the 9/11 attack had ruined the high season (sigh!).

So Aubry turned to consultancy, managing small-size Tour & Travel computer systems, writing Web content for Via Rail (the Canadian railways), and advising the Web team at Centre de Recherches Informatiques de Montréal. In 2002, he produced the new website of the RISQ (Quebec Scientific Information Network) and managed it until his hiring as Webmaster in Sept. 2003. His responsibilities then grew to include corporate communications, project management related to Web and Open Source computing, RISQ's videoconferencing service management, and the organization of RISQ's annual conference.

Christian turned to consultancy again in February 2006, with RISQ as his first customer. He launched his own digital communication trademark, Economie Logique, organized two major technology conferences and defined new cutting-edge standards in independant digital video journalism. In November 2006, he launched YULBUZZ.TV, a leading French-Canadian videoblog, with his friend and fellow business partner Philippe Martin.

In 2007 he was joined by Laurent Maisonnave at Economie Logique Communications (ELC) inc. up to October 2008, when they decided to keep working in partnership but each one from his own business. (To be continued!)
