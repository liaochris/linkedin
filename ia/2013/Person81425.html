Lisa Boaz | Air Freight | Air Charter ✈'s Summary

Award-winning Air Freight | Air Charter leader elevating organizations to fulfill potential through advanced expertise in staff development, recruitment, and team-building. Demonstrated ability to motivate and galvanize teams across diverse entities, from corporate to philanthropic organizations. Solid human resources background complemented by comprehensive experience in all facets of C-level operations to develop rare perspective invaluable to building top-tier teams and optimizing personnel functions to significantly impact overall performance. More than 10 years of experience complemented by Certificate in Human Resource Management and a bachelor’s degree.

Lisa emphasizes close customer relations; stays in tune with customers' expectations about value and service and seeks input from internal and external sources. She cultivates contacts with people in a variety of strategic positions and keeps in touch with a wide network of key people to foster client relationships and expand business.

In 2006, Lisa earned her "WBE" Certification (Women's Business Enterprise), for a woman-owned business, from the WBENC (Women's Business Enterprise Nat'l Council).

Lisa was awarded the Leader of the Future Award by California Governor Gray Davis at the 2002 Inaugural Ball hosted by the Los Angeles Area Chamber of Commerce. The Leader of the Future Award is presented each year to an entrepreneur under the age of 40 who has demonstrated significant leadership and has had a positive impact within their community.

Prior to Vital Express, Lisa served in the training and development capacity for Apotex, Inc, a leading pharmaceutical manufacturer. Lisa was responsible for the operation and management of a $2 million training facility dedicated to professional and personal employee development. Before joining Apotex, Lisa served as a human resources generalist for a leading Men's Retailer, where she oversaw the training and development of all managers across 120 stores nationwide.
