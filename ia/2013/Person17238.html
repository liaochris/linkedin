Brian Castleberry's Projects

  • Pixel Fusion Brand Identity

    • January 2013 to March 2013
    Team Members: Brian Castleberry

    Logo for the gaming podcast 'Pixel Fusion'. Took the idea of atomic and retro science (the spinning oval shape) and fused it with the concept of video (denoted by the red, green, and blue or RGB).

  • The Gaming Historian Brand Identity

    • October 2011 to November 2011
    Team Members: Brian Castleberry, Norman Caruso

    Logo for the online show 'The Gaming Historian'. The client wanted to mix the concept of history and video games into one form. In finding iconic representations of history and video games, I came to a few conclusions.

    Books have been the classic vehicle in the delivery of history. In turn when I think of a representation of video games, no one example stands out greater than the NES cartridge. In the concept phase I decided to work towards melding these two distinct elements into one harmonious design.

    Hence the mixture of the book and the cartridge into the logo bug. Along with the logo bug is the serif text which represent scholarly endeavours.

  • Substance TV Brand Identity

    • June 2008 to January 2009
    Team Members: Brian Castleberry

    Logo bug for the brand 'Substance TV'. We needed a mark which allowed the brand to be instantly recognizable in small formats or from far away.

    It uses the three letters from the acronym of Substance TV (STV) to form a logotype. The 'S' is created from negative space, while the 'TV' is created from part of the 'S' shape and continued in white. This contrasting use of negative and positive space also separates the two words ("Substance" and "TV").

  • Knowware Brand Identity

    • February 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Brian Castleberry

    KnowWare provides products and services for companies that want to fire up their profits using the tools of Lean Six Sigma. Thier products consist of software, books and training videos for Lean Six Sigma and SPC. Our QI Macros SPC Software for Excel is an affordable, easy-to-use set of Excel add-ins for all of your data analysis needs.

    The logo implemented the company's monochromatic blue color scheme. The implied arrowhead formed from the negative space of the 'K' results in an upward right arrow line using the upper part of the 'W'. This was used to imply and upward motion.
