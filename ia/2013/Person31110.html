Dave Harper's Summary

User Experience Designer working remotely and living in beautiful Southern Indiana. Passionate about creating engaging and user-centered design solutions for web applications.

I have ten years of experience designing and developing interactive media for a variety of clients, and have a unique skill set in the higher education market. My experience includes working as an interactive art director for Indiana University, teaching web design as assistant professor of Visual Communications at Ivy Tech Community College, and currently designing for Ellucian, creating web applications and products for higher ed. I've received regional, national, and international top honors from Adobe Systems, Inc., the W3 Awards, and the American Advertising Federation.

Specialties: User experience designer in an agile development environment, lead designer within product teams, creator of wireframes and prototypes, responsive/adaptive design guru, skilled in front-end development, HTML5, CSS3, web-standards aficionado.

Dave Harper's Projects

  • GAGE Mobile Website

    • July 2011 to August 2011
    Team Members: Dave Harper

    I’ve been a design partner with the Growth Alliance for Greater Evansville since 2009. Initially, GAGE needed help combining three fragmented websites into one cohesive GAGE online experience. Immediately following the redesign, I placed Google Analytics onto the new website to track visitors and understand the audience behavior online. We learned that users were consistently visiting the website on mobile devices, and it became apparent that an optimized mobile design was needed to accommodate these users.

    Optimizing a desktop-viewing website for a mobile-viewing audience not only requires a visual redesign, but also a structural and navigational redesign. Asking, what will users need to have access to quickly, and on the go, are the type of questions that inform the decisions regarding the mobile website.

    Both the mobile website and the desktop website pull the same content that is generated from the content management system, therefore giving GAGE total control over the content for both audiences.

    I created the all of the visual components of the design, including the icons on the home screen. I produced the custom design using HTML, CSS and JavaScript.

  • NoteWagon Multi-Document Upload Wireframe

    • February 2012 to February 2012
    Team Members: Dave Harper

    NoteWagon is a student note marketplace originating from Canada with plans of expanding product offerings and services to the US student market. As a part of a UI/UX design challenge, I was given the task of sketching/wireframing a multi-document upload feature from specifications that were provided.
