Aaron Ansarov's Summary

Aaron creates images with impact and emotion. He has a masterful use of light, color and an ability to push the envelope. Using insight to the subjects' apprehension, he breaks down barriers and creates imagery to be remembered. His up close and personal style allows him to create captivating, beautiful and original images. Aaron has traveled the world as a military photojournalist for more than 14 years, and has experienced life through the interactions of many cultures in peacetime and in war. Aaron has been recognized for many years as one of the most award winning photographers in Department of Defense history. He has been published in magazines, newspapers and books globally.

Aaron Ansarov's Projects

  • I am Las Olas

    • August 2009 to October 2009
    Team Members: Aaron Ansarov, John Sturdy

    A personal project, conceived, developed, art directed, produced and executed by myself. With the help of the Las Olas association to gain access for the project, this resulted in a coffee table book as well as gallery opening on the release of the book.

  • The "I'm Staying" ad campaign

    • May 1999 to May 2001
    Team Members: Aaron Ansarov, Jim Watson, Bob Houlihan,

    Conceived, produced, art directed, copywrote and executed dozens of feature portraits with one common element of a red chair that traveled around the country and even to Japan. The chair symbolized the core values of the US Navy as 'Honor,' 'Courage' and 'Commitment.' The underlying theme was that each of us benefit greatly with every new tour of duty and by "Staying in the Navy" we are gaining more. Thus the slogan "I'm Staying."

    The campaign was such a success it ran consistently in the Navy's flagship publication, "All Hands" for over 2 years including other magazines and publications throughout the military.

  • The 1to1 project

    • January 2008 to Present
    Team Members: Aaron Ansarov, Anna Ansarov

    The 1 to 1 project is a self generated fine art project of the largest proportions ever. By photographing the human figure at a 1:1 ratio at about 3 inches from the skin, we are able to see details like never before. These individual high resolution images are then printed and placed back together to recreate the subject in a size that has never been imagined before. The project is being produced and proposed to #Art Buyers and #Interior Designers for use in building installations and and advertising projects. Gallery exhibits are in the works.

  • Zooids

    • December 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Aaron Ansarov, Anna Ansarov

    Long term fine art collection utilizing live Portuguese Man O War captured from the local beach, photographed on a light table and released back to the beach unharmed.
    The resulting patterns, shapes and colors create fantastic and surreal works of art that have been featured in publications around the world.
