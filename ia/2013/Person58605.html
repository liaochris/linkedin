Markus Jansson's Publications

  • Identification and treatment of acute myocardial infarction by nurse at emergency care

    • Turku University of Applied Sciences
    • December 2002
    Authors: Markus Jansson, Ari Laaksonen, Toni Liimatainen

    The purpose of this bachelor’s thesis is to study and collect information and skills that a nurse needs when working in emergency of a patient with myocardial infarction. Based on the theory of our bachelor’s thesis we also created teaching materials for nurses education. This was put into practise in the form of a Powerpointâ presentation.

    Our bachelor’s thesis is theoretical and based on results from earlier studies on the subject and literary material. Our research methods were analysis and synthesis. The main concepts myocardial infarction, emergency care of patient with myocardial infarction and nurse’s role in performing trombolytic treatment where divided into different parts during the analysis phase. Based on these concepts, at the synthesis phase we collected the material for our bachelor’s thesis about identification and treatment of acute myocardial infarction by nurse at emergency care.

    It is important for the nurse to recognize a threatening or acute myocardial infarction based on the symptoms and EKG findings. Recognizing the need of care makes it possible to consult a physician and, if needed, to start quickly the trombolytic treatment by nurse. The nurse must have the necessary know-how about the treatment of a patient with myocardial infarction, indications and contra-indications of trombolytic treatment and implementing the trombolytic treatment. A nurse must also recognize the complications that might arise from trombolytic treatment and perform the treatment which they need. Quick implementation of trombolytic treatment greatly improves the prognosis of a patient with myocardial infarction.
