Julianne M. Miranda's Summary

Julianne M. Miranda brings nearly 20 years’ experience as an educator, professionally certified life coach, facilitator and consultant to higher education and non-profit organizations. A longtime wellness advocate, she blends this passion into traditional approaches to bring a whole-life perspective to change management. She is frequently sought as a speaker and facilitator providing leadership development, organizational change and strategic planning facilitation through individualized workshops, coaching and consulting engagements. She presents regularly at national and international conferences on topics including education, technology, personal and corporate leadership and wellness. She is known for her collaborative approach, honesty and focus on relationship-building.

Specialties

Higher education, K-12 and non-profit consulting, particularly the integration of technology into curriculum; individual and group coaching for learning, growth and change; strategic planning and group facilitation for vision and leadership development; non-profit management

  • Indiana Music Teachers Association, Board of Directors

    Trustee, Past-president, Technology Development Chair
    • August 1995 to Present

    For more than 120 years, the Indiana Music Teachers Association (IMTA) has fostered the instruction, study and performance of music. More than 300 individuals throughout the state of Indiana join 24,000 colleagues nationwide to form a truly unique community of music professionals.

    Member programs provide a multitude of opportunity for professional growth. Certification programs recognize pedagogical competence and promote the highest standards of music teaching. State conferences offer master classes, informational sessions and an opportunity to share ideas with fellow teachers. Competitions encourage high standards of performance and composition among young musicians. Our print resources include the Achievement in Music Syllabus, IMT Newsletter, and the Directory of Lecturers and Clinicians.

    IMTA continues to support efforts to retain music as an essential element in a complete education. Through commissioning grants, we foster the creation of new music and through District and Local Association programs, we support independent music teachers and defend their traditional workplaces.

  • Blue Ash Montgomery Symphony Orchestra, Board of Directors

    Board Member
    • June 2007 to June 2008

    The Blue Ash/Montgomery Symphony Orchestra (BAMSO) had its beginning as the Blue Ash Symphony Orchestra in 1987. Since that time citizens in the Cincinnati Ohio's Northeast Corridor have enjoyed free concerts by professional musicians. Originally, it was partially funded by the City of Blue Ash and now is partially funded by both the City of Blue Ash and the City of Montgomery.

  • ﻿The Putnam County Coalition for Education and the Creative Arts, Inc. (The Castle)

    Board member
    • July 2010 to Present

    The mission of The Castle is to partner with teachers in Putnam County schools in grades K-12 to invigorate the curriculum by means of an integrated-arts approach. Through collaborative partnerships, teaching artists, and volunteer efforts, The Castle offers free programming that draws on visual arts, movement, music, theater and writing to nurture self-expression and encourage essential developmental skills for lifelong learning.
