Ashley Maria's Summary

Ashley Maria is a recent graduate from the University of Southern California studying Film & Television Production at the School of Cinematic Arts.

Her love affair with film began in high school and continued through her undergraduate career at UNC-Chapel Hill, where she produced and directed for the local television network and interned at various companies such as ESPN and Current TV.

Blending the horror/comedy genres in her film “Friday Night Fright,” Ashley received the prestigious Student Directing award for the film from the Director’s Guild of America. Her latest venture, "Volcano Girl," mixes action and comedy to build a VFX intensive super hero world within real world values. It's a movie about sisters...and then there's fire!

Currently she is in development on a children's animated webisode series called "Sully Wood: The Squirrel Who's Afraid of Heights."

Finally, she has screamed on the Great Wall of China, swam with dolphins and is both a Mac and PC person.
