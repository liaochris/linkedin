Greg Thomas' Experience

Voice Talent

DeepWarmVoice.com

August 2009 – Present (4 years 2 months)

I specialize in providing professional voice overs for corporate videos, medical instruction, television and radio commercials, eLearning, and political advertisements.

Co-Owner/General Manager

KELI-FM San Angelo

August 1986 – March 1999 (12 years 8 months) San Angelo, Texas

Just about every DJ dreams of owning their own radio station, and for 13 years, I got to live the dream and know all the "joys" of owning your own small business. Got the license and built this 100,000 watt radio station from the ground up (with much technical assistance from Jack Sellmeyer.) KELI started out as an automated elevator music station and evolved into a personality driven Lite A/C station that stayed in the top 3 for Adults 25-54 for 6 years.

Midday Talent and Research Director

KLOL-FM Houston

1979 – 1981 (2 years) Houston, Texas

KLOL was a legendary album rock radio station, and every rock DJ in Texas wanted to work there. I was there in the last of the free form days and the during the changeover to a Lee Abrams-influenced format. During that time, we drove direct competitor KILT-FM out of the format. KLOL has undergone several ownership changes since I left, and now broadcasts a Spanish format. :-(

Midday Talent and Public Service Director

KNOW Radio Austin

1977 – 1979 (2 years) Austin, Texas

KNOW was an AM Top 40 radio station, and I was blessed to be able to work there in the last of the glory days before FM killed almost every music format on the AM band. It was a tremendous learning experience, and I owe a huge debt of grattitude to Wendell Mayes, Jr. and Bill Mayne.

Afternoon Drive and Music Director

KLBJ-FM Austin

1973 – 1976 (3 years)

One of the original "Laid Back Jocks." Worked on the air 4pm-8pm. Selected all the albums that went into the control room on this free-form Album Rock station. Imagine a bunch of college kids let loose in a room full of record albums who could play pretty much anything they wanted. Today's corporate radio types freak-out at the concept, but we all had the same vision for how the station was supposed to sound. It worked so well that we drove our well-established competitor into another format.
