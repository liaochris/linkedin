Chris Hayes' Summary

You may wonder what led a student of Psychology to pursue a career in web development? A better question might be what led a web developer to undertake a degree in Psychology?

The answer is philosophical and beyond the scope of this summary, so I will instead characterize my humble beginnings as a web developer. I began building websites at the age of 15 and quickly fell in love. I spent the latter years of my high school career tucked away in my room, consumed by learning as much as I could and putting it into practice. In this context the latter question seems more appropriate.

My thirst for knowledge continued into University. In 2009 during my second year I began freelancing in an effort to subsidize my education and expand my skillset. In doing so I learned as much about business and building relationships as I did about web design and development.

Today I work as a web developer building various types of websites and applications. I build database driven web applications and custom Facebook applications. I design and develop simple and beautiful static web pages for small businesses. I work extensively with WordPress and various other open source software projects. I am also a social media guru and not only have a deep fundamental understanding of social networks and platforms, but I also integrate their APIs into products I build to create social web experiences.

I am also an eternal student. I may no longer be enrolled in a post secondary institution, but my passion for development, whether it be web, personal, or other, is as strong as ever, and so I continue to create opportunities for myself to learn and expand my horizons.

Specialties: Website design, web application development, social media, front-end development
