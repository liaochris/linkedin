Bob Williams' Summary

I am a student of marketing technology professionally, academically, and in my free time. My passion is to connect customers with business by matching a need with a product or service. To do this I consider myself an agent of change. That means I constantly examine results, challenge thinking, and initiate new work. This gives me opportunities to influence strategic direction because I stay connected with business goals and customer needs.

My professional profile shows a constant growth in responsibility with positions in information technology and marketing. While I've shared many successful projects and initiatives with my colleagues, I've also experienced failures. Nobody likes to fail, but these events are impactful to my maturity and decision making. I list some of my memorable failures here along with what I learned from each situation.

Selected career failures:

* Software release with expected annual return in excess of $1M and capital budget of $350K. Project was over budget and behind schedule before it was postponed. Learning: Stop a project when key metrics fail and create a recovery plan or abandon the project.

* Presentation of software projects and business case to executive steering committee that were not approved. Learning: Don't let failure of an idea keep me from generating more ideas.

* Justification that a software product could work with external clients. Project remained as an internal program used by the billing group to save on operating expenses. Memorable quote: "Are you willing to bet your paycheck on it?"

* Recommendations to numerous bank clients on how to improve their profitability through eCommerce changes. The clients would not implement many changes because of cost, risk, or return. Learning: Not all sales engagements succeed. Continue to offer solutions to clients to increase their profitability.
