Amy Boutwell's Experience

President

Collaborative Healthcare Strategies

Nonprofit; 10,001+ employees; Hospital & Health Care industry

2011 – Present (2 years) Lexington, MA

I founded Collaborative Healthcare Strategies to pursue work aligned with the objectives of the CMS Innovation Center and the Partnership for Patients, specifically the goal of engaging thousands of communities across the nation to work across settings and across sectors to improve healthcare delivery to achieve "three-part aim" outcomes.

Collaborative Healthcare Strategies is a research, advisory, and educational firm working with a wide variety of partners. Examples of our work include:
1. Advising over $75M in Innovation Challenge Grants in Round 1 and $70M in Round 2 proposals
2. Advising over 25 CCTP applications in 5 states
3. Physician Consultant, National Coordinating Center, Integrating Care for Populations and Communities Aim of the QIO 10th Statement of Work
4. Co-Principal Investigator, multi-state AHRQ funded project to test and adapt best practices to improve care transitions to ensure relevance to the Medicaid population
5. Advisor, information technology firm creating cross-setting communication platform
6. Faculty for several HENs, state conferences, educational events, speaking engagement, content expert, panelist, research advisor.
7. Advisor, Maryland's state-wide Transitions: Handle with Care Initiative
8. Subcontractor, CMS ACO Learning System contract with Mathematica Policy Research

STAAR Initiative

2008 – 2011 (3 years)

Designed, launched, and expanded a multi-state initiative to reduce readmissions, taking the state (as opposed to any one type of care setting) as the unit of interest. Championed the policy and systems change management components of the initiative, including issues around engaging stakeholders, creating the case for change, examining the financial impact of the initiative on providers, data availability and measurement issues at the geographic and setting levels. Early experience published in July 2011 Health Affairs theme issue on Innovations in Care Delivery.
