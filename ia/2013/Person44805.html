Fiona Lake's Summary

Fiona Lake (McKindlay) is a writer and photographer specialising in agriculture.

Self-employed since 1988, Fiona has lived and worked in numerous locations across Australia and has first-hand experience with many different types of agriculture.

Fiona has produced 2 best-selling coffee table style books featuring photographs of Australia's largest outback cattle stations (the largest in the world); plus outback greeting cards, stock photographs for commercial use and limited edition artwork, and magazine features.

Art exhibitions and books have been launched by a number of people including Governor-General Michael Jeffery, AACo CEO Peter Holmes a Court, NT Administrator Ted Egan and ABC Landline Producer Peter Lewis.

Fiona's primary goals are to write, photograph and travel more, plus new conversations with others interested in agriculture, all over the world. These interests plus past experience have come together in 2013, via employment with Quadrant (ag tours).

Refer to the website (400+ pages including the blog) for more information and testimonials: www.fionalake.com.au

Also busy on the modern Bush Grapevine, twitter: @FionaLakeAus

Specialties: Agriculture specialist with 25+ years of experience, providing personal, thoughtful and efficient service. Renowned for being able to provide a degree of in-depth, reliable, ag-related information and quality images unobtainable elsewhere.
Ag marketing skills include quality, search engine optimised writing created specifically for a rural audience, especially via social media such as the modern bush grapevine (twitter), as well as websites, blogs and forums.

Fiona Lake's Publications

  • A Million Acre Masterpiece

    • Fiona Lake
    Authors: Fiona Lake

    Reprinted twice, 'A Million Acre Masterpiece' is a coffee-table style book containing more than 200 fascinating images of many of Australia's largest & most famous cattle stations. These outback photos date back to 1984 when cattle still had full sets of horns & horseriders wore felt hats, never helmets; and it was still usual for stockyards to be made of timber & windmills to be fully functional.

  • Life as an Australian Horseman

    • Fiona Lake
    Authors: Fiona Lake

    'Life as an Australian Horseman' is the sequel to 'A Million Acre Masterpiece'. This best-selling book also contains photos taken on a selection of large & well known cattle stations spread across Nthn Australia (QLD, NT & WA). Unlike the first book it does not contain a glossary; instead it includes a larger number of photos & a special chapter on bronco branding. Both books are quality hardcovers with gloss dustjackets.
