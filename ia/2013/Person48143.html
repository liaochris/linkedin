Gerard F "Jerry" Becker, PhD's Summary

Experienced organizational leader, teacher, management consultant and author who consistently achieves successful results in building, leading and educating effective teams and individuals focused on professional services, information technology, financial services, supply chain and lean manufacturing. Has performed strategic advisor and professional services leadership roles for several organizations.

Tenure tracked associate professor at Nyack College in the School of Business and Leadership who also teaches as an adjunct or visiting professor at NYU, DeVry/Keller Graduate School of Management, Kaplan University, Central Michigan University, LIM College and European School of Economics; advises master thesis students on research projects associated with various aspects of organizational development and leadership.

Frequently published in peer reviewed journals, as well as authoring and contributing to multiple books. Co-authored a book entitled "Mastering a New Paradigm in Management Consulting-2nd ed." available at www.amazon.com.

Specialties: Management consulting and mentoring; Professional Services; Organizational development; leadership; organizational assessment and re-engineering; development of business cases for capital raises; and, teaching business courses at several universities.

Gerard F "Jerry" Becker, PhD's Publications

  • Mastering a New Paradigm in Management Consulting-2nd edition

    • Createspace
    • January 29, 2008
    Authors: Gerard F "Jerry" Becker, PhD, Marilyn Harris, PhD, Anne Saber Hallcom, PhD, Richard L Ponschock, PhD

    Can also be found at Amazon.com along with the provided link above. Mastering a New Paradigm in Management Consulting (2nd edition) is developed for use in practical learning settings, where today's business world is the classroom. This holistic approach advises the client's management to influence changes in the system - not in the employees - thus effecting lasting transformative change. The book addresses gaining understanding first, then mastery of critical learning processes. Understanding and mastery of the new paradigm are presented and developed as Learning to BE, Learning to LEARN, and Learning to DO. The Consulting Systems Worldview framework covers four distinct iterative and interactive phases in the consulting engagement. Two real cases provide an opportunity to use the key framework tasks and the explanation of what was actually done in these cases to reach a successful outcome. The book capitalizes on presencing - leading and learning from the future as it emerges.

  • Digitation of Society: The Continuum to a Speciation Event

    • Review of Business Research/International Academy of Business and Economics
    • July 22, 2012
    Authors: Gerard F "Jerry" Becker, PhD, Richard L. Ponschock, PhD

    To be published in October 2012; This research paper expands on the theoretical contexts of a speciation event related to the digitization interrelationship among the business, academic and political societal norms. Based on the prior research (Ponschock & Becker, 2011), it is evident that existent theoretical constructs among these distinct environments are increasingly inter-dependent as related to the velocity of change in social networking capabilities. The further research inherent within is formed from a meta-analysis of existent theory, and ultimately tied to the increasingly avant-garde technological advances that bring these global societal groups into a coherent model of interconnectedness. Further research is still required to more closely bind these elemental aspects and forge a holistic view for positive sustainability and leverage among these groups. It is the intent of this research to be continually transformed with the goal of establishing and substantiating that holistic model for future use.

  • Digitation of Society: The AVATAR Effect

    • Review of Startegic and International Studies
    Authors: Gerard F "Jerry" Becker, PhD, Dr. Richard Ponschock

    The research presented herein provides substantive insights and analysis as to the contributions to that speciation event based on the adoption of technological identities, different than the individual identities with which we operate on a daily basis. It is rapidly becoming apparent that additional psychological and sociological issues may become evident in such research, but the focus here remains the identification of the technological identities that have, and are evolving, and their contribution, termed “The Avatar Effect” to the posited speciation event. Additional research may be warranted from a psychological and sociological perspective.
