Martha Mooke's Summary

- Composer/electro-acoustic violist, pioneer in the field of electric five string viola
-Yamaha Artist and leading clinician on electric and alternative approaches to string playing.
-Music Producer for international & national events, specializing in solutions for productions featuring diverse elements.
-A composer with an innovative palette for creating evocative images in sound and defying stylistic boundaries,
-Has also created scores for theatre and dance as well soundtracks for film and multimedia events.
-Genre-defying recordings, the solo Enharmonic Vision and Bowing's Café Mars (duo with electric guitarist Randolph A. Hudson, III)

The Village Voice wrote, “If the sonic images were precise, the forms were agreeably loose, making each piece feel like a sonic landscape: Sometimes desert imperceptibly morphed into forest; other times, at the push of a foot pedal, we'd turn a corner and suddenly encounter a completely different vista.”

Mooke is founder and violist of the electro-acoustic Scorchio Quartet which has performed and recorded with David Bowie as well as championing new works. She has toured internationally as a member of Barbra Streisand’s acclaimed orchestra and performed with Bon Jovi, Philip Glass, Enya, Lou Reed, Tony Bennett, David Byrne, Moby, Luciano Pavorotti, Broadway and the Orchestra of St. Luke’s.

She has received awards from ASCAP, Meet the Composer, Arts International and the Irene C. Fromer Award for Creative and Performing Arts. Mooke was honored with an ASCAP Concert Music Award for conceiving and producing the new music showcase THRU THE WALLS featuring ASCAP composer/performers whose work defies categorization.

Specialties

-Music Production/Conception for diverse cultural events
-Live multi-dimensional performance and soundtracks
-Clinics and workshops on electric strings and electronics, improvisation, new music, innovative approach to creating and listening to music

Founder/Violist

Scorchio String Quartet (Sole Proprietorship)

Sole Proprietorship; 1-10 employees; Music industry

January 2001 – Present (12 years 10 months)

When rock superstar David Bowie asked electric violist Martha Mooke to assemble a string quartet for an upcoming performance at the 2001 Tibet House Benefit Concert at New York's Carnegie Hall, Scorchio was born. The quartet subsequently collaborated with Bowie (who bestowed the name Scorchio on the group) and producer Tony Visconti on Bowie’s 2003 release "Heathen".

As resident string quartet for the annual Tibet House Benefit Concerts at Carnegie Hall Scorchio has also performed with Philip Glass, Lou Reed, Patti Smith, Michael Stipe, Ben Harper, Trey Anastasio, David Byrne, Sujfan Stevens, Ziggy Marley, Rufus Wainwright, Chocolate Genius and Vampire Weekend.

The double quartet of Scorchio and Kronos rendering Visconti’s arrangement of “Space Oddity” was cited by Alec Downie as a “stunning interpretation” and ConcertLiveWire.Com called their performance on “Heathen” “quite beautiful”.

Recent news includes "Trey Anastasio & The Scorchio Quintet: Live from Princeton", performances with Lou Reed and feature in the award winning documentary "Inside the Perfect Circle: The Odyssey of Joel Thome".

Also known for their performances of new music, Scorchio has premiered works by Tony Visconti, Joel Thome, Anthony Cornicello and Martha Mooke. Scorchio is proud of their work with young composers and in-school demonstration concerts.
