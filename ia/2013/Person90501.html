Michael Christian McCaddon's Summary

Michael Christian McCaddon is a graphic designer living and working in New York City focusing on branding, identity, and interactive design. He was a founding partner and creative director at RANGER where he directed strategic planning, information architecture, and user experience for clients including Google, Hasbro, 2x4, and IDEO. Prior to RANGER, Chris worked at Project Projects, where he led design and development teams for clients including Warner Music Group, Steven Holl Architects, Bernard Tschumi Architects, Michael Van Valkenburgh Associates, Tablet, and SALT. Chris’s additional client experience includes strategic and identity work for Kiki de Montparnasse while at Base Design. In addition to his professional experience, Chris has taught as a guest critic at Parsons The New School for Design, the University of Connecticut, and the Art Institute of Seattle.
