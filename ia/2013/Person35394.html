2011 – Present (2 years) Miami/Fort Lauderdale Area

A Chief Listening Officer is a position that many companies should consider implementing. It is important for companies to use all of the social media and technology tools that are available to stay in contact with their fans and customers. A Chief Listening officer and his staff should be the central department for the customers to lodge their complaints or concerns. Through Social Media or otherwise.

The Chief Listening Officer would be responsible for the daily maintenance and updating of the companies Facebook, Twitter and other social media sites. In addition, it would be his responsibility to post surveys asking about customer needs and wants. These surveys will also be a great way of using social media to tap into what could be changed or updated with current products.

It is essential that the Chief Listening Officer also take these customer concerns and suggestions to the decision makers in the company. It is foolish to simply gather suggestions and not actively implement them. The Chief Listening officer should track these ideas and let customers know that they have been heard and that the suggestions are being implemented.

As our culture swings toward wanting instant gratification, it is important that corporations respond to this need. The Chief Listening Officer will need to have a staff that allows them to quickly reach out to customers through social media or other, more traditional means.

A Chief Listening Officer is a position that is just as valuable to a company as an Accountant , Attorney or Human Resources person because they will keep the company and the customers connected.

Twitter Author, Social Media Keynote Speaker, Inspirator, Tweeter(onix)

Dubbelzinnig

2010 – Present (3 years) Miami/Fort Lauderdale Area

This book was not written in the normal manner, but it was put together by a group of 16,000 people. These people, who we call Tweople (Twittering People), have composed the book, by voting for the best tweets from @Dubbelzinnig (‘Double Meaning’). The voting was done by ReTweeting the funny quotes with a double meaning that were published daily. For this book we collected a few hundred of the most retweeted quotes.

@Dubbelzinnig is Dick Raman, living in Miami, Florida, USA at the time. It started as a social media experiment… just testing the application Tweeteronix, to see if you can really get many followers if you tweet something interesting. It turned out to be an enormous success! Within 6 months @Dubbelzinnig had 16.000 followers with tweets only in Dutch!

The interesting thing was that the followers started to tweet their own linguistic quirks, which were retweeted by @Dubbelzinnig so all the followers could enjoy them. In this way a whole community of language-lovers grew, which prompted publisher Terra-Lannoo, to create a book.

The result is this booklet, which is a nice gift for anyone that appreciates the Dutch language. You can find the book in the bookstores in The Netherlands or you can order it online.

  • XML/EDI: Cyber Assisted Business In Practice

    • ISBN 978-144-0499-111
    • April 11, 1999
    Authors: Dick Raman ✔

    This book was written in 1998 and first published in 1999, before the dotcom boom and before ebXML. The book contains descriptions of the concepts of eCommerce, the business processes and the underlying technology. The book clearly describes the strengths and weaknesses of "traditional" EDI and those of the Internet as a medium for eCommerce. It positions XML/EDI is a solution for marrying the strengths of traditional EDI with the strengths of the Internet. It describes how UN-XML would take this a stage further. UN-XML combines the experience, stability and international robustness of the EDIFACT standard with the XML/EDI concept. This leverages investments made in "traditional" EDI and enables the rapid adoption of EDI across the spectrum of all companies and industries, while supporting international trade in the global environment. UN-XML was the founding concept for what was later called ebXML, the UN/CEFACT - OASIS standard for eCommerce over the Internet.
