Brendan Keefe's Summary

38-time Emmy award winner. Named Best Large Market TV Reporter by the Ohio Associated Press Broadcasters 2011, and AP Best Photographer with back-to-back wins in 2012 and 2013.

Brendan is an experienced broadcast journalist with a solid background and an eye toward the future. Selected as Senior Multi-Media Journalist for all Scripps television newsrooms.

It takes bona fide reporting credentials and broad knowledge of current affairs to competently deliver facts and analysis in a breaking news environment. Brendan brings those qualities to the anchor desk after 23 years "on the street" as a reporter in every corner of the nation, and in hot spots around the globe.

Represented by Peter Goldberg at N.S. Bienstock -- pgoldberg@nsbtalent.com

Specialties: Storytelling, anchoring, investigative reporting, writing, producing, photography, and editing

I-Team Anchor & Chief Investigator

WCPO-TV

Public Company; 51-200 employees; Broadcast Media industry

October 2007 – Present (5 years 11 months) Cincinnati, Ohio

Shoot, write, edit, produce and present investigative reports as a "multi-media journalist." Voted Best Large Market TV Reporter and Best Photographer by the Ohio Associated Press. Honored with eight Emmy Awards in 2013 -- 31 Emmys in the last five years with WCPO -- nearly all for solo work as an MMJ. Weeknight evening anchor 2007 - 2012.

Scripps Senior Multi Media Journalist (Corporate)

The E.W. Scripps Company

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; SSP; Media Production industry

October 2012 – Present (11 months)

Develop, design, and deploy cutting-edge storytelling techniques for all Scripps multi-media journalists (MMJs) in 14 television newsrooms nationwide. Selected to lead innovative program training and re-tooling the company's reporters, photographers, and storytellers. Major focus on technology.

Correspondent

WCBS-TV

February 2003 – October 2007 (4 years 9 months)

11:00 P.M. weeknight reporter in New York City. Weekend morning anchor for two six-month stretches. War correspondent with three combat reporting tours in Iraq and Kuwait. Covered the terrorist bombings of commuter trains in Madrid. Won NY Emmy awards for continuing coverage of Hurricane Katrina in New Orleans, and for breaking news coverage of the NYC Blackout. Won the coveted Silurian Award for outstanding coverage in Iraq from one of the oldest press clubs in the nation.

Reporter

KPRC-TV

Public Company; 51-200 employees; Broadcast Media industry

October 1997 – November 2002 (5 years 2 months)

Special Projects Reporter for five years. Also the "go to" choice for breaking news. Flew regularly in the news helicopter as the Chopper 2 reporter. Covered the collapse of Enron, the Andrea Yates child drownings, multiple hurricanes, three serial killers, Houston floods, the Clinton impeachment hearings, U.S. Capitol shootings, and the Space Shuttle program. Flew twice in zero gravity with NASA aboard the KC-135 "Vomit Comet." Won four Suncoast Emmy awards, including two for an underwater cave diving documentary he shot alone while on vacation in the Yucatan Peninsula.

Anchor/Reporter

Reporter
