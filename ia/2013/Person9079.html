Anup Sawant's Projects

  • VIVO

    • January 2010 to August 2011
    Team Members: Anup Sawant, Rebecca Younes, Brian Lowe, Jim Blake, Ellen J. Cramer, Jon Corson-Rikert, Deepak Konidena, Manolo Beviá, Nick Cappadona

    VIVO is an open source semantic web application originally developed and implemented at Cornell. When installed and populated with researcher interests, activities, and accomplishments, it enables the discovery of research and scholarship across disciplines at that institution and beyond.

  • VIVO Recommender

    • December 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Anup Sawant, Hugh Devlin, Ph. D., Willem Pieterson, Noshir Contractor, Jon Corson-Rikert, David Eichmann

    The C-IKNOW VIVO Recommender demonstrates use of heuristics for recommending collaborations between researchers. These heuristics are informed by empirical studies that test theories from the social sciences regarding the formation of effective collaborations and teams. The C-IKNOW VIVO Recommender is a "mash-up" of linked open data from multiple VIVO instances and supplemental bibliographic data (citation data) from the MEDLINE data server at the University of Iowa.

  • Team Assembly

    • January 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Anup Sawant, Willem Pieterson, Hugh Devlin, Ph. D., Noshir Contractor, Amy Wax, Dorothy Carter, Raquel Asencio

    The C-IKNOW DreamTeam Assembler is developed and built by the SONIC Research Group at Northwestern University in close collaboration with the DELTA Lab at Georgia Tech. The DreamTeam Assembler uses network heuristics to provide recommendations for team assembly and is based on ongoing research in the two labs.

  • VIVO Search

    • June 2011 to August 2011
    Team Members: Anup Sawant, Brian Lowe, Manolo Beviá, Nick Cappadona, Deepak Konidena

    The purpose of VIVO Search is to provide an example multi-institutional search across the VIVO data of the seven partner institutions on the NIH grant.
