Kevin Saunders' Summary

I'm the project lead for Torment: Tides of Numenera, being developed by inXile entertainment. I led our recent Kickstarter campaign -- it was inspiring how much support we received from our backers. (I'm happy to provide feedback on Kickstarter campaigns - feel free to send me an InMail.)

I've worked in the video game industry for about 15 years. I have designed and led a variety of efforts, ranging from Shattered Galaxy (a massively multiplayer real-time strategy game launched in 2001) to Mission to Senegal (an online language learning role-playing game in which players converse with non-player characters (NPCs) using speech recognition).

I have been credited on 11 shipped entertainment games and was a key contributor to seven, with an overall composite review score of 81%. I've managed up to five significant projects simultaneously, led as many as 18 direct reports and been responsible for products and teams with as many as 70 employees.

As a manager and leader, I strive to establish a collaborative team environment with individuals being empowered with appropriate decision-making authority and guided by clear high level direction. I am driven by the intrinsic rewards of aiding others in maximizing their potential. I prefer agile development techniques when practical.

As a designer, I emphasize the importance of the user experience, including understanding who the user is and what they seek from the product, using data-driven approaches when possible. I believe user interface design is fundamental to good game design - to that end, I authored an educational text on game interface design intended for aspiring game designers. The 2nd edition was published in March 2012.

Role-playing games are my favorite both to play and to create. The other genres I'm most experienced with are real-time strategy (RTS), massively multiplayer (RPG and RTS), and serious games. Platforms with which I've worked have included PC, Mac, Xbox, Xbox360, PS2, PS3, iOS.

Kevin Saunders' Publications

  • Game Development Essentials: Game Interface Design, 1st Edition

    • Cengage Learning (Delmar)
    • 2007
    Authors: Kevin Saunders, Jeannie Novak

    As an introduction to the complex topic of user interface design, this in-depth book discusses player interface design conventions and looks at the effects of platform, genre, and design goals related to electronic games. Readers will explore everything from the history of game interface design and basic design theories to practical strategies for creating a winning, interactive interface. A host of professional applications, revealing insights, and illuminating anecdotes from dozens of pioneering game designers will inspire readers to explore this fascinating aspect of the electronic games industry.

  • Game Development Essentials: Game Interface Design, 2nd Edition

    • Cengage Learning (Delmar)
    • 2012
    Authors: Kevin Saunders, Jeannie Novak

    This comprehensive introductory text immerses students in the foundation, theory, and practice of interface creation and user experience design, while including interviews with working professionals, examples from every gaming era and many genres, and hundreds of screenshots from contemporary games. Also featured are an expanded practice section with a wide variety of design examples, coverage of interface design for mobile and motion-sensing devices, social networking games, and much more. Students will explore everything from the history of game interface design and basic design theories to practical strategies for creating a winning, interactive interface.

Kevin Saunders' Projects

  • Torment: Tides of Numenera

    • November 2012 to Present

    A new character-driven CRPG set in Monte Cook's Numenera (www.numenera.com). Torment is a single player, isometric game being developed in Unity for PC (Windows), Mac, and Linux, and in English, French, German, Italian, Polish, Russian, Spanish. It's being developed at inXile, and our "publisher" is the 75+K backers who supported our crowdfunding campaign.

    The game's four pillars are:

    1. A Deep, Thematically Satisfying Story. The philosophical underpinnings of Torment drive the game, both mechanically and narratively. Your words, choices, and actions will be your primary
    weapons.

    2. A World Unlike Any Other. The game has a fantastic, original setting, with awe-inspiring painterly visuals, imaginative locations, truly offbeat items, and massive feats of magic. In Numenera, however, “magic” is something surprisingly different.

    3. A Rich, Personal Narrative. The story is thoughtful and character-driven—epic in feel but a
    deeply personal narrative, with nontraditional characters and companions who have their own
    motivations and desires that drive them throughout the game.

    4. Reactivity, Choice, and Real Consequences. The game emphasizes replayability and reactivity,
    and your choices will make a real difference. You can play the game with a different approach and
    discover entirely new pathways. Most important, we won’t tell you how to play. The best ending
    is the one you choose, flowing naturally from your actions throughout the game.
