Andrea Hansen's Experience

Assistant Curator (with Charles Waldheim)

Isabella Stewart Gardner Museum

Nonprofit; 51-200 employees; Museums and Institutions industry

October 2012 – Present (1 year 3 months) Boston, MA

Composite Landscapes examines one of the twentieth century’s dominant techniques for representing landscape architecture: the montage view. Composite Landscapes gathers seminal examples of photomontage and collage drawings by a dozen of the world’s leading landscape architects. The first exhibition of its kind in North America, Composite Landscapes revisits the origins, aspirations, and exhaustion of montage and collage as media of landscape representation. The drawings gathered for the exhibit shed light on the ubiquity and redundancy of many contemporary collage practices. With the global proliferation of photoshop views, contemporary practices of landscape representation verge on exhaustion, both critical and cultural. By reexamining the analog, cut and paste construction of post-modern landscape views, Composite Landscapes promises to critically reanimate contemporary discussions of digital media and landscape drawing.

Visualizing Systems

July 2012 – Present (1 year 6 months) Boston, MA

Visualizing Systems investigates how we map data to understand complex networks. The website serves as a collection of visualizations of global systems that range from watersheds and greenbelts to distribution chains and social networks. Visualizing Systems will also be published by Princeton Architectural Press in 2014.
