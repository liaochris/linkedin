Anne Kerns' Summary

As a designer, I focus on creative expressions of messaging, business goals, and client needs to deliver communication materials that clarify and engage. I believe the design activity is a process that benefits from the thoughtful participation of all stakeholders.

I have held graphic design and management positions in the Washington, DC, area for 20 years. Learning graphic design before the computer was such a ubiquitous tool taught me the production processes behind print design and gave me an eagle eye for detail.

As an avid promoter of good design and fine typography, I taught design at George Mason University (periodically 2002–2010), have given seminars at area high schools, and generally try to educate anyone who wants to know more about things like grid structures, dingbats, and kerning. Yes, isn’t it ironic that I actually have a typographic term for a last name? (Thanks, Dad!)

Anne Likes Red, Inc., is my venture as an independent designer and consultant. I work directly for businesses, organizations, and government agencies, as well as for other creative firms as an extension of their creative services and marketing capabilities. Consulting experience includes business recruiting of senior level design professionals in the Washington, D.C., market. I have a network of experts that are available for complex projects and I am a seasoned project manager.

And while red is my favorite color, I design across the spectrum.

Specialties

Graphic Design. Print Design. Design of logos, identity, collateral, publications, reports, brochures, book covers, direct mail, and more. Consulting. Recruiting.
