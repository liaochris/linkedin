Andrew Stenhouse's Summary

Andrew Stenhouse has been developing leaders in business and non-profit organizations for nearly thirty years.

As Dean of Graduate and Professional Studies at Vanguard University, Andrew drives the growth of high-quality, mission-focused, market-responsive programs in organizational management, ministry and leadership, human development, nursing, early childhood education, clinical psychology, education, theological studies, and leadership studies. Developing passionate leaders for nearly 100 years, Vanguard University is one of U.S. News & World Report's best baccalaureate colleges in the west, and named by The Princeton Review as a “Best in the West” College.

Prior to his return to Vanguard, Andrew founded and directed the Organizational Psychology program for Concordia University Irvine, overseeing Southern California’s only Bachelor of Science Degree in Organizational Psychology specifically designed for working professionals.

Before joining Concordia, Andrew was Acting Dean of Academic Affairs for the Southern California campuses of the Chicago School of Professional Psychology, the nation’s oldest and largest regionally accredited graduate school exclusively for psychology and behavioral health sciences.

During his first tenure at Vanguard University, Andrew was Managing Director of the Judkins Institute for Leadership Studies, Director of the Organizational Leadership graduate program, Associate Dean and Director of Graduate Programs for the School of Religion, and Dean of the School for Professional Studies.

He has taught courses in both undergraduate and graduate programs, including Foundations of Leadership, Team Development, Conflict Management, Strategic Management, Leadership Theory, and Organizational Behavior.
