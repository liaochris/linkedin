Christopher King's Summary

We tell stories.

• American Express wanted all levels of supervisors to celebrate their teams' success in order to keep morale up at their customer call centers; so we told their stories.

• Intel's Ultrabook is a versatile, portable, and powerful computer. We told its story through the eyes of a nature photographer, a traveler, a musician, and a stay at home dad.

• Clarion's safety signs save lives and protect manufacturers in liability lawsuits. We explained the benefits of strong signage from the perspective of an attorney, an insurance appraiser, an engineer, the ISO team in charge of developing symbols, and most powerfully, a mother.

• The Homestead School advocates that children learn best with their hands so we built mini-documentaries on the pottery and fiber arts classes.

• Woodloch Pines is a family resort so we told the founder's story and how he brought his family together.

• The YMCA of Newark wanted to engage donors so we told the story of Terrifying Tony, the ex-pro boxer who regained his health, and the story of Jeffrey and Alnissa, the couple who found their footing after flirting with homelessness.

Our talent is for capturing the essence of a brand and telling compelling stories around its themes.
