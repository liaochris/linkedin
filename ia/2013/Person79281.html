Lawanda Baker's Summary

Having a love for art, Baker chose Middle Tennessee State University as her career objective to pursue a career in art and design. She earned her Bachelor of Arts in graphic design from Middle Tennessee State University (MTSU) in 1994. After graduation, Baker began her graphic design career in Memphis, Tenn., where she served on the board of the Memphis Advertising Federation, worked for Towery Publishing, The Downtowner Magazine and St. Jude Children’s Research Hospital. During this time, Baker’s design work was well recognized earning her multiple awards including the Public Relations Society of America’s VOX award, among others.

After relocating to Atlanta, Ga., and working for The Atlanta Journal Constitution, Baker returned to her alma mater to work in the graphics department, earning multiple awards on behalf of the university. In 2004, Baker relocated to Birmingham where she is employed by STYLE Advertising and has continued exceeding expectations and earning numerous awards. Most recently, Baker was selected as the 2011 national winner of the American Marketing Association’s 75th Anniversary logo design contest.

Outside of the office, Baker volunteers her time and talent to her church, New Vision Christian Church, and has been instrumental in designing t-shirts, publications and a host of other marketing materials.

Baker's passion for art, creativity and culture, makes it no surprise that her career in the communications industry has been one full of endless successes.
