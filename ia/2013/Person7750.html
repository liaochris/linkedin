Angela Paul's Summary

Angela Paul is a writer, model, and a professional Life Coach who leads women’s groups and workshops on relationships, marriage, personal transformation and graceful aging. Her first book It's Hard Being Human: Thoughts on The Journey from Suffering to Joy was published in 2006 and featured a foreword by The Secret's teacher Michael Beckwith. Her most recent book, "The Beauty of Aging: A Woman's Guide to Joyful Living," was published in 2011 and available through online retailers such as Barnes and Noble, Amazon, iUniverse and Books-a-Million. The book will be available later this year in major bookstores nation wide. She was born and raised in Yorkshire, England. A former Miss Teen England and successful fashion model, Angela traveled extensively in Europe and Asia and lived in Japan for eight years. It was during this time that she began her 32 year meditation practice and study of Eastern philosophy and religion. She has also worked as a docent and trained facilitator at the Simon Weisenthal Museum of Tolerance in Los Angeles, and is a “Big Sister” with the Big Brother/ Big Sister program. Angela is also a long time sponsored sister with Women For Women International. In the 2005/06 contest, Angela was a winner of More Magazine’s Over 40 Model Search and is now a contracted model with Wilhelmina agency in NYC and Los Angeles. In 2011 Angela was a winner in Harper's Bazaar's Fabulous At Every Age Contest. She has been a featured guest on Good Morning America, CBS Early Show, Inside Edition and Fox News where she was a recurring guest and special commentator for the Royal Wedding/Diamond Jubilee. Her recent modeling clients include: Sheraton Hotel, Radiesse, WEN Hair Products and Terranea Resorts. Her husband of thirty-two years is Alan Paul, a founding member of the multi-Grammy winning vocal group, The Manhattan Transfer.

Specialties: Women's Health, Graceful Aging, Relationship and Marriage Coaching, Beauty, Fashion, Modeling, Sex, Meditation.
