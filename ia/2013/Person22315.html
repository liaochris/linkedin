Freelancer

Self Employed

May 1989 – Present (24 years 5 months) Sandpoint, ID

When I was 12-years old I pitched a 3-part cliffhanger to my hometown newspaper and the editor accepted. While most 6th-graders groaned over the dreaded "spelling words" story week after week, I wrote 5-10 pages of adventure and then read the stories out loud to my classmates. I was a writing nerd.

My actual freelance career began with a job as a reporter for a town paper in 1989. I wrote press releases and articles for groups I belonged to, but it wasn't until I thought I needed a college degree that I began to earn money for my craft. By the time I graduated in 1998 I had earned numerous paychecks.

As marketing manager for Valley Natural Foods, I freelanced frequently in both regional and national publications. For nearly four years I had a regular monthly column in Stressfree Living until the publication fell victim to the economy and decrease in ad revenue.

I continue to write in the areas of marketing, food and community relations. I am most interested in stories. Every subject and every person has a story. My blog, Bayfield Co. Chronicles is a collection of stories that I find daily as I research my novel in this beautiful area.

My pen is still for hire. And, you'll make my day if you ask me to read my story out loud.
