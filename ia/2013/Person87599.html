Core Org Team Member

Hack the Barbican

January 2013 – Present (9 months) London, United Kingdom

An interdisciplinary playground of art, technology, and entrepreneurship at the Barbican Centre throughout August 2013, community-organised and on a tiny budget. Founding member, key organiser, and community facilitator.

I like to think of it not as a centrally curated event, but as a slowly growing city that is gradually taking over the Barbican’s public spaces, with many imperfections, but also many moments of unexpected magic. The Barbican Centre proved to be a perfect venue with a fascinating structure and history, and many hidden corners we can occupy.

Core Org Team Member

Electromagnetic Field

October 2011 – Present (2 years)

A non-profit camping festival in the UK for people with an inquisitive mind or an interest in making things: hackers, geeks, scientists, engineers, artists, and crafters. For three days in early September 2012 we erected a tent city in Milton Keynes, complete with power and high-bandwidth wireless Internet. 500 participants and some high-profile coverage made this a very successful event. The next camp is planned for 2014, and a one-day event for 2013.

Trustee

London Hackspace Ltd

August 2011 – Present (2 years 2 months)

Member of the organisation since early 2009. Was elected director and trustee in 2011; this is a mostly symbolic function. The London Hackspace is governed by its community.

Among other things initiated the Young Hackspace, an infrequent event that takes the ingenuity, skill and experience of seasoned hackers to teach young children and teenagers skills in science, craft, technology, ... we have lasers.

Technical Advisor

One Click Orgs

2008 – Present (5 years)

Virtual governance for legal structures.

One Click Orgs is an open source project and website that makes it easy to set up an unincorporated association and provide a simple voting system for their members. We are now starting to implement support for incorporated organisations, with a particular focus on virtualising the governance of the London Hackspace.

The project provides interesting challenges of both a technical and legal nature. We have a great team of web application developers, and advisors specialising in UK and US company law.
