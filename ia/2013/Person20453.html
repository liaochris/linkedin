Carol McIntyre, Artist/Color Teacher/Speaker's Experience

Artist, Color Expert, Speaker, Art Tips Blogger, Art Instructor

McIntyre Fine Art Studio & Gallery

1995 – Present (18 years) Black Forest, Colorado

Professional painter bringing color harmony, depth and intrigue to your home and office environments via my original paintings. My passion is to create art that takes you another time and place.

My abstract paintings are often fused with realism, in that I create windows to another world. Commissions are welcome and I approach them as a collaboration. Working with the buyer to create a picture that will last a lifetime, is a pleasure.

Color Instructor for Artists

Art Centers & Art Societies Throughout the US

January 1996 – December 2004 (9 years)

From "Mud to Magic," I teach artists working in oils, acrylics, watercolor and water mixable oils, how to overcome the frustration of mixing colors.

Curator, Judge, Jurer

The Edina Art Center

January 2004 – August 2004 (8 months) Edina, Minnesota

Founded then curated the regional art show entitled "Celebrating 22 Miles!" The purpose of the exhibition was to call attention to Minnehaha Creek and Minnehaha Falls, that runs 22 miles through Minneapolis and into the Mississippi River.

I juried the 150 entries in to a stunning show of 45 paintings, sculptures, photographs, mixed media, etc. In conjunction, my 12 paintings commemorating the creek were featured. Awards were given to those I judged as exceptional.

This event was collaborated with the Watershed Counsel and the Edina Art Center. Several community events took place to heighten our need to be better stewards of the creek that is vital to the Minneapolis community.

Adjunct Art Professor

St. Catherine University

Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry

January 2003 – May 2003 (5 months) St. Paul, Minnesota

College level art instruction focusing on learning an art medium, color mixing, composition, design and presenting art work. Students were directed at the end of the semester to organize, publicize and hang a group exhibition. Students also attended local art exhibitions to learn how to observe, respond and write about art.

President

Minnesota Watercolor Society

January 1992 – January 1994 (2 years 1 month) Twin Cities, Minnesota

300+ member art society for the purpose of providing educational and exhibition opportunities for watercolor artists. As president, I facilitated the mission statement for the 10-year old organization, stream lined communication, accounting system and computerized the monthly newsletter.

Sales Training & Marketing Manager

National Computer Systems

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Information Technology and Services industry

1984 – 1987 (3 years) Greater Minneapolis-St. Paul Area

Managed the sales and product training for 100+ sales and customer service employees. Introduced, authored and designed the company's first sales and product training curriculum. Managed, created the agenda and produced the first national sales meeting.Delivered multiple selling skills training programs.

School Psychologist

Hutchinson Special Education Co-operative

May 1978 – August 1981 (3 years 4 months) Hutchinson, Minnesota

A school psychologist serving 4 different rural school districts. Testing and assessing children of all ages to assist in their individual educational programs. Special knowledge of the hearing impaired and the impact of alcoholism and physical violence on children. Developed and implemented an innovated program to assist parents in determining if their children should enter school early. Negotiated a 2-year contract with the School Board for 22 special education professionals.

    • self
    • 2006
    Authors: Carol McIntyre, Artist/Color Teacher/Speaker

    Have you ever wondered:
    What or how an artist thinks?
    What are the stages of the creativity cycle?
    How an artist creates a large body of artwork & why?

    A visual artist's voice is rarely heard in words. Inspired by history, I pursued my dream of creating a large body of artwork commemorating the women of the 1800's.
