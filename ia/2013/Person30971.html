Daun Thomas Frankland's Summary

Daun Thomas Frankland, Founder of Paris at My Feet, offers a “bespoke” service of tailored, discreet and personalized experiences of Paris. Clients share her passion for the decorative arts, antiques, auction houses, galleries and the history, culture and lore of France. A proud Virginian, her roots reach back to when Layfayette fought with George Washington for independence. Living in Paris, her adopted home for the last 15 years, her love for both countries is alive and vibrant as she delights in showcasing the treasures and corners of the “City of Light”.

Bi-lingual with a dual degree in Art Studio and Art History, she has woven a tapestry career in the U.S. and Europe. Early on Daun worked for the renowned antiquarian Gep Durenberger organizing his famous Decorative Arts Seminars. An entrepreneur since 1988, she opened Thomas Matthews Antiques in the D.C area where her antiques, carefully shipped and crated from Europe, are showcased.

Daun launched Paris at My Feet in 2001. Her “sejours” are unique as her clients are discerning … designers, art dealers, historians, artists, executives, diplomats, architects, celebrities and many returning to the “City of Light” seeking a different experience. This could be a trip to the flea market, an art gallery, a chateau, or a visit to the fashion houses of Chanel or Hermes. With her connections the term “behind the scenes” takes on true meaning with Daun as your “accompagniatrice”.

Paris at my Feet has been exclusively word of mouth or “bouche d’oreil”, with clients from her vast network of contacts in the design and art world. However, she came into the public eye when notoriety in the press (House Beautiful and Architectural Digest) shined the light on her taste and flair for the decorative arts. Inspired to share her passions virtually, her upcoming site opens with a warm welcome from her Parisian apartment with video tours of her favorite corners of Paris, as well as a showcase of her treasures. Stay tuned.
