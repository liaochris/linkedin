Michael Ivanovich's Summary

Because energy issues in commercial buildings and industrial facilities will be a dominant focus among engineers, owners, regulators, and equipment manufacturers, and with my passions aligning on energy and instructional communications, I've taken a position as AMCA International's first-ever Director of Strategic Energy Initiatives.

As such, I'll be focusing on building-energy issues in North America, Asia, and Europe, including codes, standards, regulations, and engineering practice.

This comes after a little over 12 months of running my own research, consulting, communications, and training company, The Ivanovich Group, LLC. The Ivanovich Group served outstanding clients, including Danfoss North America, Siemens Buildings Industry, and CFE Media. And, AMCA International... Under an agreement with AMCA International, The Ivanovich Group will remain open to serve key clients on an occasional basis, especially in the domains of research and training, where Dr. Amy Smith, VP Research and Training, will continue to lead projects.

Before having a 14-year career in publishing as a chief editor of Consulting-Specifying Engineer and HPAC Engineering magazines, I was a research scientist in the fields of building science and computer technology working for Pacific Northwest National Laboratory, Minnesota Department of Health, Camroden Associates, and NOAA Aeronomy Laboratory (through Univ. of Colorado at Boulder).

I am married to a warm and talented professor of Education, Dr. Amy Smith, and we have a dog, Bogart, who keeps the house from ever getting too serious.

Specialties

Building science and energy systems engineering practice; communications and business development strategies; context and content development; networking; event development; custom media; Business-to-Business media.

Michael Ivanovich's Experience

Director of Strategic Energy Initiatives

Air Movement and Control Association (AMCA) International

July 2011 – Present (2 years 6 months) Arlington Heights, Illinois

AMCA International is a not-for-profit international association representing over 300 member companies in North America, Europe, Asia, and the Middle East. AMCA members manufacture air-system products, including fans, louvers, dampers, air-monitoring stations, and, acoustic attenuators for industrial, commercial, and residential markets.

My role is as follows:

Lead the development and advocacy of AMCA positions (gained through member consensus) on energy efficiency and green construction codes, standards, and regulations pertaining to air systems in commercial and industrial applications.

Lead the development of air-system education, training, outreach programs, and publications for manufacturers, engineers, contractors, building owners, and commissioning providers. This includes technical articles in AMCA inmotion magazine, published annually as a supplement to ASHRAE Journal; developing website content within the Energy Initiatives webpages on www.amca.org; and technical articles, webinars, and electronic newsletters.

Current initiatives include:

Representing AMCA and its members in the U.S. Dept. of Energy's initiative to develop a federal fan efficiency standard for commercial and industrial fans.

Representing AMCA and its members as a consultant to ASHRAE SSPC 90.1 and its Mechanical Subcommittee, for the continuous maintenance of ASHRAE 90.1 Energy Standard for Buildings Except Low-Rise Residential Buildings.

Representing AMCA and its members on International Energy Conservation Code and International Green Construction Code change proposals and hearings.

Michael Ivanovich's Publications

  • Special Report: Fan Efficiency Guidelines

    • Consulting-Specifying Engineer Magazine; CFE Media
    • September 23, 2013
    Authors: Michael Ivanovich

    A summary of model codes and standards that are adopting fan-efficiency requirements for commercial buildings. Includes introduction to the new metric, Fan Efficiency Grade, based on AMCA Standard 205, Energy Efficiency Classification for Fans.

  • Fan Efficiency Requirements for ASHRAE 90.1-2013

    • ASHRAE Journal / ASHRAE
    • April 23, 2013
    Authors: Michael Ivanovich, John Cermak, PhD

    A description of the new fan-efficiency requirements in ANSI/ASHRAE/IES 90.1-2013 Energy Standard for Buildings Except Low-Rise Residential Buildings. ASHRAE 90.1 is the model energy standard for commercial buildings, representing baseline efficiency requirements for buildings and HVAC equipment in the U.S.

  • Fan Efficiency Regulations - Where Are They Going?

    • AMCA inmotion / Air Movement and Control Association
    • August 23, 2013
    Authors: Michael Ivanovich

    An update on U.S. model codes and standards that have adopted fan efficiency requirements, and a forecast of future codes/standards activity.

  • Fan Efficiency Grades: Answers to Frequently Asked Questions,

    • AMCA inmotion / Air Movement and Control Association
    • September 23, 2011
    Authors: Michael Ivanovich, Tim Mathson

    A description of fan efficiency grades based on AMCA 205 Energy Efficiency Classification for Fans, the metric Fan Efficiency Grade (FEG), and how to use FEGs (and not) when sizing and selecting fans for commercial-building applications.
