Wolfgang H. Mahr's Summary

I have gathered 20+ years of international experience in ICT consulting and project management and during the last 15+ years specialized in Business Continuity Management (BCM). My passion for enhancing an organization’s resilience and continuous improvement brought me into BCM. I first identified the need for having a “Plan B” back in the late ’90s, when Y2K threatened to shut down ICT systems.

As CEO and owner of governance & continuuuity, I am offering both world-class consultancy services and training courses based on ISO standards such as the ISO 22301, 22313, 27001, 28000.

Any enterprise might be affected by consequences of disruptive events:

► Does a proper preparation help saving human life, huge unforeseeable financial losses and preserving the image of a caring, professional organization? ♦ Definitely

► Does planning ahead and shielding against disruptions of business-critical resources and the supply chain improve your competitive position? ♦ Definitely

By managing BCM projects culminating in implementing a BCM System (BCMS), I guide customers to effectively protect their enterprises from these consequences.

My offering consists of a unique mix of up-to date knowledge and solid international experience acquired through a range of projects in various industries. Publications, a blog, and lectures at international conferences support the exchange of ideas and further development of current issues. Participation in global standards bodies (ISO TC223, CEN TC391) help to stay abreast of current trends.

My vision is that every enterprise should have a BCMS, simply because it becomes more competitive.

Various endorsements and recommendations confirm that I am reliable and competent business partner, having offered comprehensive business services on a proven track.

The three “u”s in continuuuity stand for ongoing, continuous services in case of a disruptive event, offering protection of the enterprise from serious consequences: three “u” – good for you.

    • governance & continuuuity gmbh
    • March 2011
    Authors: Wolfgang H. Mahr, Terry Dawes

    This guide provides an overview of what Business Continuity Management (BCM) means, why organizations need to do it and how an organization should approach the planning, implementing and maintaining of a Business Continuity Plan.

    Business Continuity Management (BCM) can be considered as a risk treatment method, complementary of a wider Risk Management method, explicitly focused on the management and containment of continuity risks, introduced by certain natural or man-made threats that, if realized, can cause material damage to people and assets and unavailability of services.
