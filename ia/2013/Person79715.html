Lee Harland's Summary

Over 15 years experience leading a team performing data mining, semantic integration and information/text analysis within the a global pharmaceutical company.
- Development of major company-wide information platforms. Designed, built and maintains over 10 distinct self-built information intelligence systems
- Data warehousing & federation: Design & delivery of major Oracle 11g systems (including oracle text, oracle XMLDb) along with an in-house RESTful web service framework we co-designed.
- Internal/External integration: Facilitating the integration of data on both sides of the firewall, using public standards (REST/SOAP/RDF) and internal web-services
- Lead in Text Mining. Constructed an extensive enterprise text mining capability, using a combination of open source (Lucene) and commercial NLP. Designed many novel algorithms.
- Lead in Semantics. Developed novel prototypes for enterprise information connectivity through semantics (triple stores, RDF, OWL). Manage multiple vocabularies/ontologies.
- Focus on the user: Our tools focus on non-computational experts, delivering complex data in a more business-relevant context
- Rapid R&D of information systems: We have delivered a number of systems for competitor intelligence, collaborative knowledge mapping, text-mining, target discovery, literature management and analysis, trend analysis and intelligent search
- Precompetitive: Our group is very active in cross-industry/academia initiatives to reduce infrastructure costs and contribute more to the public domain
- Technologies: Oracle, Java, perl, mysql, php, mediawiki, semantic mediawiki, RDF, REST/SOAP, text-mining (Lingumatics i2e, lucene, in-house designed systems), grid computing and AWS, Ajax

  • Building disease and target knowledge with Semantic MediaWiki

    • Woodhead Publishing
    • October 2012
    Authors: Lee Harland, Cathy Marshall, Ben Gardner, Phil Verdemato, Rich Head, Meiping Chan

    The efficient flow of both formal (stored/archived) and tacit knowledge (held in people’s heads) is critical in the new era of information-powered pharmaceutical discovery. Yet, one of the major inhibitors of this is the constant flux within the industry, driven by rapidly evolving business models, mergers and collaborations. A continued stream of new employees and external partners brings a need to not only manage the new information they generate, but to find and exploit existing company results and reports. The ability to synthesise this vast information “substrate” into actionable intelligence is crucial to industry productivity, and is therefore the subject of much activity within IT departments. In parallel, the new “digital biology” era, with immense DNA sequencing capabilities, ever-larger genome wide association studies and next generation disease/systems modelling provides yet more and more data to find, analyse and exploit. In this chapter we look at the contribution that the Semantic MediaWiki (SMW) technology has made to meeting the information challenges faced by Pfizer. We describe two use-cases that address different aspects of biomedical research, highlighting the flexibility of this software and the ultimate benefit to the user.
