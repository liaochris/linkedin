Co-Founder, Marketing

The Great Bowel Movement

Nonprofit; 1-10 employees; Health, Wellness and Fitness industry

August 2010 – Present (3 years 3 months)

My partner and I started the Great Bowel Movement as an initiative to empower those affected by Crohn's and Colitis to spread awareness. Our organization thrives on the notion that anyone has the power to make a difference and spread awareness just by inviting conversations, sharing their stories, and living their lives as IBD role models within their communities and networks.

My main responsibility within the organization is brand development- this includes largely graphic design, resource development, blogging, and social media marketing. We focus on providing an array of tools to meet the non-medical needs of the IBD community, with a focus on self-confidence/overcoming shame and stigma, acceptance of diagnosis, empowerment through living with a chronic disease, and encouraging and supporting people to become more open.

We support and promote many other organizations within the IBD community to point patients and others affected to where they may find the best help for themselves, including mental health practices, nutrition websites, philanthropic events, programs for kids, and ostomate-specific organizations. We frequently feature guest bloggers on our site and prioritize finding professional and reliable experts such as psychologists and dieticians, as well as featuring members of our community who have made an impact with their story.

We maintain a very strong brand identity, or voice, that finds the balance between serious and funny- we work to be relatable to those affected, and promote IBD as almost normal- that is, common, not something to be ashamed of. We focus strongly on providing quality resources and discussions, rather than gaining the most Facebook fans. We believe and operate that if we produce material that is relevant and helpful, people will join our community. This has proven true.

Please visit our website at www.thegreatbowelmovement.org, or our www.facebook.com/thegreatbm for examples of social media, graphic design, and blogging.
