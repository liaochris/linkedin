Craig Everett's Publications

  • Measuring the Social Responsibility Discount for the Cost of Equity Capital: Evidence from Benefit Corporations

    • The Journal of Behavioral Finance & Economics
    • September 1, 2013
    Authors: Craig Everett

    In 2010, Maryland became the first state to allow firms to incorporate as “benefit corporations,” which are for-profit entities with a social purpose. Since then, nineteen other states have followed. Using survey data from the population of 94 benefit corporations existent at the time of the survey, this paper directly measures the “social responsibility discount” – the degree to which investors in a benefit corporation have a lower required return on equity than they would have for traditional firms. This paper finds that the discount is approximately 35%. This paper also provides unique descriptive statistics about benefit corporations and their founders.

  • Toby Gold and the Secret Fortune

    • Fiscal Press
    • October 18, 2012
    Authors: Craig Everett

    Toby Gold and the Secret Fortune is a middle-grade urban fantasy that introduces kids to financial literacy principles in a fun way.

    The book has received critical acclaim. It was selected as a finalist for the 2012 Book of the Year Award by ForeWord Reviews, a prominent book review magazine.

  • Private Businesses Predict Limited Growth for 2013

    • Graziadio Business Review
    • January 31, 2013
    Authors: Craig Everett, John K. Paglia

    The quarterly private capital access (PCA) index and the private capital demand (PCD) index, economic indicators published by the Pepperdine Private Capital Markets Project, predict continued low levels of both demand and access in the near future. Thus the near-term prospects for robust economic growth are restricted. The implied policy recommendation for national and local governments is to enhance loan guarantee programs for private firms with revenues of less than five million USD ($5M).
