Cary Michael Robinson's Projects

  • Project MENTOUR: BBBS Marketing Campaign

    • October 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Cary Michael Robinson

    Attempting to implement a more active approach to attracting young, male professionals to Big Brothers Big Sisters of the Coastal Empire, Project MENTOUR will began with a “tour” throughout Savannah, GA in search of the city’s elite array of young professionals and inviting them to
    attend a mentoring workshop in which networking and collaboration will be encouraged. The marketing strategy consisted of visiting numerous “male-dominated” organizations and establishments in Savannah including area colleges and universities, churches, outreach ministries, philanthropic/Greek letter organizations, etc. In honor of the 1,000,000 MAN MARCH movement, individuals united to experience and apply their knowledge of mentoring through brief video cases, lectures and written exercises, and group problem-solving tasks including the M&M Challenge, using the candy M&M’s to facilitate creativity and understanding amongst participants, and to reduce any friction one may have had towards joining the organization. User profiles were generated from workshop activity data in order to gain a better understanding
    of the targeted propect mentor market. Project MENTOUR concluded with a marketing revision based on solutions derived directly from research results. Marketing creatives included recruitment messages with colloquial themes (job posting recruitment print material, spokesperson, BBBS paraphernalia), membership rewards program, and social intranet and mobile application proposals.

  • MVS 2013 Resident Appreciation Month Marketing

    • June 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Cary Michael Robinson

    In celebration of Resident Appreciation Month, the Resident Services department at Mount Vernon Square Apartments arranged and executed planning for a diverse assortment of events for community residents. During the entire month of June, residents were engaged in activities to include a movie night, military brunch, ice cream day, National flip flop day, annual pool party, paint and party night and professional organization seminar (to name a few) targeting all residents to encourage an enhanced community involvement. This video embodies the essence of our Resident Appreciation Month activities, and will be used as a utilization tool for marketing targeted prospects.

  • The Napkin Holder

    • February 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Cary Michael Robinson, Curvan, Billiejean, Hani Alharthi, Yuan-Te (Danny) Yang

    This project implemented contextual research and strategy in order to design solutions for the napkin holder that will increase its value as an everyday kitchen resource, provide Sears with a unique solution that will build and increase revenue within the “upscale” home goods market (of which they currently do not compete well) and offer product concepts that facilitates multiple functions within social settings and within everyday dining use. Solution included 4 concepts: 3 tangible concepts that accommodates traditional dining, spontaneous dining and social dining, and 1 tactical recommendation for retail positioning in Sears' HomeGoods department.

  • JCPenney 2012 Marketing Campaign

    • November 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Cary Michael Robinson, Stephanie Butler, Shenetha Chisholm

    This project involved performing a market analysis, conducting observations, and developing personas in order to develop a marketing strategy aimed towards enhancing the brand image of JCPenney, increasing profitability, and attracting and retaining more loyal customers. This project also focuses on the promotion of JCPenney's image as a family-oriented business, increasing customer awareness of their private labels, emphasizing on the additional services they offer, and expanding the targeted market to include younger consumers. Marketing creatives included the utilization of traditional media and new digital forms of marketing (television, radio, direct mail, public transit marketing, ambient media marketing, etc.), promotional models (derived from personas), media and budget plan inclusion, in-store and digital experience re-modifications, and new partnerships.

  • American Legion Ethnography

    • February 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Cary Michael Robinson

    This exploratory study involved the use of ethnographic research to understand the relationship between existing memberships and services of American Legion Post 135 and the conditions providing opportunities for holistic growth and organization development.
