  • Writers & Websites: Mechanics of Writing Web Page Content and Your Website

    • A Writer's Edge
    • October 1, 2011
    Authors: Georganna Hancock, M.S.

    Are you an author or writer? Do you offer writing or editing services that include working on web pages? Are you publishing a book? Are you planning to do any of these activities?

    All these are excellent reasons to have one or more websites. You can hire a professional web design company or build a site yourself. But wait! You also need to know how to write your material or content most effectively for web pages. Perhaps you are a freelancer - more jobs and better-paying ones now require writing that will appear online.

    Writing for web pages is different from all other types of writing.You need to know about certain special requirements to provide the viewers with the best possible experience of your content. There are pitfalls to avoid and practices that are much better to use than the ordinary ones for the kind of writing you've been doing for print media.

    This guide takes you through the complicated process of getting a website built and then doing the writing for web pages - on your site or for others. It also provides valuable tips on how to make the most effective use of your writer's or book's website. Three divisions are structured with Step by Step sections and checklists for the more complex parts.

    The author has written for the Internet since before Al Gore invented the World Wide Web back in the early 90s. In the previous millennium. She has also developed websites for herself and other writers as an occupation, and has seven certifications in site design and management.

  • BASICS FOR WRITERS: STARTING A WRITING CAREER

    • A Writer's Edge
    • August 4, 2010
    Authors: Georganna Hancock, M.S.

    This updated version is reformatted for a better user experience, especially with navigation that might be available on your reader. It is also jazzed up with spicy and impertinent illustrations by Bonnie Boots. BASICS FOR WRITERS offers everything you need to know to get going in the writing game--and to learn if it is the life for you. Whether you want to create stories, articles for print and digital periodicals, novels, essays, even poetry, simple but specific elements apply to every kind of writing.

    It isn't a matter of just sitting down and writing (unless you're only doing it for fun). The writing life consists of about 40% writing, and the rest is devoted to business matters, marketing your creations, submissions processes, and self-promotional activities.

    BASICS FOR WRITERS covers the terminology, techniques and tools you'll need
    to succeed:

    * fundamentals of copyright and plagiarism
    * getting an agent (or not)
    * formatting various types of manuscripts
    * different kinds of publishing in the digital age
    * dealing with defeat -- and reaching the goal
    * writers groups, community and communication
    * resources for finding who and what you need
    * performing research necessary for fiction and nonfiction
    * continuing your writing education

    BASICS FOR WRITERS is loaded with updated links to Internet websites that support every aspect of the writer's life. These references extend the book's information to higher levels of professionalism, databases of people related to the publishing industry, a multitude of resources to learn more about individual topics, and methods for integrating yourself into the larger community of writers world-wide.

    More volumes in the BASICS FOR WRITERS series will focus in depth on the topics covered in this one, with special attention to using digital resources, social and multimedia to develop a writer's career and writing skills.

Georganna Hancock, M.S.'s Summary

Editorial services, manuscript critiques and guidance for those seeking publishing opportunities for their writing.

First published as a poet in 1964, most of my print bylines are in my former name, Georganna Simmons. Also known as an active freelance writer and public relations consultant since 1970.

My writing, both creative and nonfiction, and my photography have won awards. Now I'm happy to help others find their ways to success with nit-picking, perfectionistic editing services and advice about publishing.

Manuscript evaluations for developing and finished writing determine if additional editorial work is needed. My critiques point out strengths, weaknesses, and ways to improve the piece as well as estimates of its saleability in current market conditions.

When a synopsis has a writer stumped or a query drives him crazy, an objective but interested consultation can solve the dilemmas and provide the final touches needed to spark the drive toward publication.

Other editorial services include proofreading, formatting, preparing files for all types of self-publishing,and rewriting, but not indexing or developmental editing for fiction. Referrals provided for those services I don't offer.

Specialties

* Proofreading and copy editing at all levels.
* Writing features and profiles.
* Developing website content.
* Manuscript critiques.
