Kimberly Stanford's Summary

From the company brochure:

Kimberly Stanford has been a nurse for over 22 years. She is currently a Registered Nurse with a degree in Business Administration and also maintains an Administrator’s license. Kimberly’s nursing experience includes a multitude of Health Care environments including Hospitals, Long Term Care, Assisted Living, Nutritional Centers and Home Health Care. Kimberly has started Home Care companies, served on Home Care Advisory Boards and had her own consulting business assisting Home Care Agencies with Survey Compliance. Her consulting business also provided services for Group Homes, providing Psychiatric Nursing Supervision for the mentally and physically handicapped. In some states she is recognized as a Qualified Mental Health Professional (QMHP). She is also qualified to train Alzheimer/Dementia Care and is a Community CPR and First Aide Instructor. Kimberly has over 10 years of Regional Management experience and has worked for 4 of the largest National Health Care Companies in the USA. As a Regional Manager, she had financial, sales and operational responsibility for a number of Home Care Offices and/or a number of Assisted Living Facilities.

“I have always wanted to start my own company. I am thrilled to start my first office in Sarasota, Florida. I promise to deliver excellence in service with a focus on customer service. My customers are clients, family members of clients, professionals assisting clients, employees and referral sources.”
Kimberly Stanford

Kimberly Stanford's Publications

  • Kimberly K. Stanford Named to Exclusive Membership Organization

    • Who's Who and Google
    Authors: Kimberly Stanford

    SARASOTA, FL, November 24, 2010 /Cambridge Who's Who/ -- Kimberly K. Stanford, Owner of A Bridge For Independence, has been recognized by Cambridge Who's Who for demonstrating dedication, leadership and excellence in health care.

    With 24 years of professional experience, Ms. Stanford has developed an expertise in home care, mental health and Alzheimer and Dementia care. On a daily basis, she oversees human resources activities, including interviewing potential employees, hiring and supervising 60 employees. Further, she handles financial, sales and marketing initiatives. Her inspiration to enter the field stemmed from caring for her mother, who was in a horrific motorcycle accident when she was a teenager. Starting as a home care pediatric nurse, Ms. Stanford worked as a registered nurse in long-term care for 19 years. After ten years in regional management, she decided to venture out and open her own business. Ms. Stanford's business is the only company that offers mental care services in her area.

    In 1992, she earned a Bachelor of Science in nursing from Minnesota State Community and Technical College at Fergus Falls. She also graduated with a Bachelor of Business Administration from Cardinal Stritch University. A licensed administrator, she became a licensed practical nurse through the Alexandra Technical Institute in 1987. Ms. Stanford is a member of the Sarasota County Aging Network and the Manatee County Aging Network, as well as the chambers of commerce of Siesta Key, Longboat Key, Lido Key and St. Armand's Circle. A supporter of the Alzheimer's Association, she hopes to continue providing quality service to her community and expand her company.

    For more information about A Bridge For Independence, visit http://abridgeforindependence.com.
