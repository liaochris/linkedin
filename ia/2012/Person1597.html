Sarah Hill's Summary

Sarah Hill is a 12 time Emmy award winning journalist who is now using her skills to tell stories about our nation's veterans. Sarah is a digital storyteller and Google Plus Hangout Host for Veterans United. She was the world's first journalist to use a Google Plus Hangout on TV and she continues to be fascinated by the power or Human Media: living, breathing, social media. Sarah started Veterans United's "Virtual Honor Flight" program for terminally ill and aging World War II vets. You can follow her stories by subscribing to the Veterans United YouTube Channel. www.youtube.com/VeteransUnited or Hangout with Sarah on the VU G+ page. www.google.com/+veteransunited . Check out her blog www.sarahhillstories.blogspot.com .

Sarah is a former news anchor at KOMU-TV 8, the broadcast lab for the University of Missouri School of Journalism. She also served as adjunct faculty for the J School.

Sarah started her career in radio in the early 90's, covering the city beat in Springfield, MO. She came to KOMU in 2000. There, she reported a weekly feature franchise called "Sarah's Stories", anchored U_News @ 4 and helped students hone their storytelling skills. She also started a weekly segment called "Your View", which sought to educate viewers about decision making in local news.

Sarah's reporting has taken her around the world. She's covered the aftermath of the 2004 tsunami in Sri Lanka and Indonesia and the aftermath of Hurricane Katrina in Louisiana. In 2007, Sarah traveled to Vietnam for a documentary, "Mercy in Motion." Most recently, she traveled to Central America for "The Culture that Crawls," a documentary in Guatemala about people who have to crawl on the ground because they have no other source of mobility. www.theculturethatcrawls.com
