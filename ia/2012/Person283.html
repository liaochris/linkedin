Christine Burns' Projects

  • The Health Equality Stakeholder Engagement (HESE) approach

    • October 2008 to Present
    Team Members: Christine Burns, shahnaz ali

    The Health Equality Stakeholder Engagement approach is a sustainable approach to long term engagement with expert stakeholders among key third sector equality organisations, representing a range of protected groups. The design grew from evidence in our regional study (see: "A Landscape of the Region") pointing to a lack of success with consultative approaches on a long term basis between the public and third sectors. The process (which begins with a thorough procurement exercise and leads to substantive Service Level Agreements) was commenced in the North West in the Autumn of 2008. Success is indicated by the number and quality of consultations undertaken with a stable cohort of experts and the fact that the relationships are still in place after more than four years.

  • The Health Equality Library Portal

    • August 2009 to Present
    Team Members: Christine Burns, shahnaz ali, Philip Donohue

    The Health Equality Library Portal addresses a major gap in accessibility to best practice examples and qualitative and quantitative evidence about inequalities across equality strands. The need for this was demonstrated by a preceding study of the health economy carried out in 2008 (see publication: "A Landscape of the Region). HELP was designed to be accessible to everyone, including other public and private sector bodies, and stakeholder organisations. It is continuously updated and includes, in addition, an events calendar, news, blogs and a bulletin board for collaboration. HELP remains a uniquely authoritative resource.

  • Equality Performance Improvement Toolkit

    • October 2009 to July 2011
    Team Members: Christine Burns, shahnaz ali

    The first ever outcome-focussed, objective, consistent, evidence-based performance measurement and improvement methodology for the UK health economy. Achieved a significant icrease in demonstrable outcomes for equality target groups across North West England (66 organisations) and became the basis for the national NHS Equality Delivery System

  • Just Plain Sense

    • March 2008 to Present
    Team Members: Christine Burns

    Just Plain Sense is a media project about Equality and Diversity. It has Podcast, Blog and Video components, using a variety of mainstream platforms. The Podcast was started in March 2008 and remains the only channel of its kind dedicated to Equality, Diversity and Human Rights topics, with 86 episodes released as at the start of 2012. The complementary blog was registered earlier, in 2004, but regular posts only began in 2008. The volume of posts increased significantly in 2011. The Podcast and Blog are also complemented with occasional video content, hosted on YouTube and Vimeo. As at February 2012 the Blog had achieved 40,000 page impressions a year and the Podcast had received 140,000 distinct visitors and over 60,000 downloads overall.

  • Pride in Practice

    • October 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Christine Burns, shahnaz ali, Loren Grant

    Pride in Practice is a benchmarking tool that identifies GP surgeries that are fully committed to assuring that their lesbian, gay and bisexual patients are treated fairly and able to discuss their issues openly with their GP or healthcare provider. The process involves surgeries undergoing self assessment which are then verified by the LGF, which will help to identify precisely the kind of gaps which can then be addressed to ensure that their surgery is fully accessible to their LGB patients and their needs. The pilot project to develop this was commissioned by us as the SHA's Equality, Inclusion and Human Rights team and carried out by a team at the Lesbian and Gay Foundation, who will continue to develop it as a sustainable system

  • LGBT History Challenges and Successes

    • September 2010 to February 2011
    Team Members: Christine Burns, Loren Grant, shahnaz ali

    The timeline celebrates the history and achievements of LGB&T people, particularly those that have contributed to healthcare over the years, and the development of a vibrant and active community in the North West. It was commissioned by us as the SHA's Equality, Inclusion and Human Rights Team, with detailed research carried out by staff from the Lesbian and Gay Foundation and the Trans Resource and Education Centre

  • Everything you always wanted to know about Sexual Orientation Monitoring ... but were afraid to ask

    • September 2010 to June 2011
    Team Members: Christine Burns, Loren Grant, shahnaz ali

    This project recognised the barriers which public sector managers perceive when faced with the need to collect monitoring information about sexual orientation in the work place and at points of delivery to service users. We commissioned and funded the development of this guide as the SHA's Equality, Inclusion and Human Rights team. The actual development was undertaken for us by a team at the Lesbian and Gay Foundation, assisted by an expert consultative panel drawn from a wider sphere

  • Improving diversity of NHS Workforce

    • June 2010 to March 2012
    Team Members: Christine Burns, Loren Grant, shahnaz ali, Nienke Wolters

    This project was set up in response to an equality impact analysis of the workforce strategy of the SHA. We recognised that the NHS needs to attract a wider range of people into the workforce to ensure that it better represents the communities we serve.
    This social marketing project encourages people to think about applying to work in the health service – and to consider doing jobs they may not think are for them.

    Just a couple of examples – many people think health visitors are, or should be, women. Or that if you are a male nurse, you must be gay.

    So we’ve started collecting real-life stories about NHS staff working in roles that might be considered unusual – or had concerns that because they are disabled or because of their sexual orientation or ethnicity, they would not be comfortable working in the service; but have been pleasantly surprised.
    We have begun publishing case study examples and are looking for more.....

  • Ali, S., Burns, C. and Grant, L. (2012), Equality and diversity in the health service: An evidence-led culture change. J of Psych Issues in Org Culture, 3: 41–60. doi: 10.1002/jpoc.20095

    • J of Psych Issues in Org Culture
    • April 26, 2012
    Authors: Christine Burns, shahnaz ali, Loren Grant

    Nine years after the first human rights legislation came into effect, many public sector organizations struggle to demonstrate meaningful and sustainable outcomes for protected groups. The workforce stubbornly fails to reflect the diversity of the communities it serves. Equality stakeholders report that services ignore the specific needs of their section of the communities, resulting in poor outcomes. To redress this, National Health Service (NHS) North West developed an evidence-based strategy, supported by the introduction of tools and best practice methods. It applied cultural change management, defined the leadership competencies required, and introduced a consistent and objective framework for measuring outcomes across a range of 13 key areas. Three years into the strategy, outcomes include the national adoption of a leadership competency framework and outcome-based performance measurement tool, as well as demonstrable improvements in the cultural competencies of NHS bodies in the region. Within 18 months, the number of primary care trusts able to evidence tangible “achieving” outcomes affecting at least three equality target groups has doubled.
