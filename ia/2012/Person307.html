Colin McGovern's Summary

I am a Software Architect and Developer with 16 years experience in a wide variety of companies in Ireland and The Netherlands.

In my career, I have managed the team responsible for UPC’s Pan-European monitoring systems where I halved the number of incidents related to those systems and integrated Cablecom’s Swiss network into the Pan European footprint. I have also delivered productised core frameworks for Logica, a telemetry system for the ESA Herschel-Planck project (for Terma), and developed ISDN protocol drivers for Linux. I have worked both in large organisations as a developer and manager and as a self-employed developer.

I have dealt with a wide variety of technologies and worked primarily in an international environment but my main strength is in product development. I have worked with a lot of clients to turn their vision into workable, marketable products.

Specialties

Technical management, operational management, strategic vendor relationships, software requirements analysis and architectural design, project definition and delivery, service oriented architectures, Java/C/C++ development.

UPC Broadband

October 2004 – June 2007 (2 years 9 months)

I joined UPC as a Java developer with a role to integrate legacy monitoring systems using an enterprise service bus. I was fully responsible for the architecture and strategic direction of the integration solution and worked with other departments to come up with a company-wide approach.

I lead the project to integrate Cablecom’s residential broadband network into UPC’s existing monitoring solution, I also lead projects to integrate Video-on-demand and Conditional-Access systems into UPC’s monitoring systems.

Integration/Software Development Engineer

Terma

Privately Held; 1001-5000 employees; Defense & Space industry

June 2002 – September 2004 (2 years 4 months) Leiden, The Netherlands

In Terma, I was involved in both software development and the integration of various applications and software components into a single, turn-key solutions.

I delivered a Telemetry viewer with realtime view, playback and history of telemetry. The system was a windows application developed in C++. I also delivered a checkout system (for telemetry analysis based on the SCOS 2000 system) for the Herschel-Planck Space Observatory programme.

Freelance Software Engineer

CMG

December 2000 – December 2001 (1 year 1 month) Utrecht Area, Netherlands

In CMG, I was primarily involved in designing and implementing customer driven improvements of CMG’s Short Message Service Centre product and writing all associated customer documentation and test definitions. The improvements were in all aspects of the product, requiring knowledge of SS7, TCP/IP, X.25, SMPP, UCP at an implementation level; all were implemented on a VMS platform using C.

During my time there, I delivered an internal protocol test tool for a major feature enhancement in the product. What was notable was that the design decisions that made the tool flexible meant that it was used and improved for a number of years afterwards.

Software Architect/Team Leader

Logica

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; Euronext: LOG; Information Technology and Services industry

November 1999 – December 2000 (1 year 2 months) Dublin, Ireland

I began in Logica in the architecture team designing and prototyping Logica’s Next Generation Telepath Short Message Service Centre.

During the productisation process that followed, I became a team leader with responsibility for delivering framework components used by all parts of the Telepath product.

The software delivered was implemented in both C and Java on Linux and Solaris platforms and addressed all areas of the protocol stack, along with a set of proprietary clustering modules dealing with persistence, inter-process peer-to-peer communication, monitoring, and so on.

The team I lead pioneered many of the processes used by other teams with respect to documentation and the manner in which the components we developed were delivered. We also took charge of training the other members of the development department in the best use of the components we designed.

Software Engineer

Eicon

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; EIC; Telecommunications industry

December 1996 – October 1999 (2 years 11 months) Dublin, Ireland

This was my first development role after graduating and it involved developing improvements to the existing Eicon X.25 and TCP/IP connectivity products (application level and driver level) on various Unix platforms.

In this role, I delivered ISDN PCI Card Protocol Stack driver software for SCO UNIX, UnixWare and SuSE Linux. I also worked on embedded code, delivering phone-hook detection and interpretation embedded software (on Motorola 68000/Siemens based hardware) for Diva ISDN Terminal Adapters.
