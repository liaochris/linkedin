Carol Alevroyianni's Summary

Experienced in developing & delivering large and small scale projects around partnerships, cultural change, community development, celebration and education.
I work with clients to develop their vision and deliver programmes to meet strategic objectives within agreed deadlines and budgets. Multiple experiences of launching high level pilot programmes.
Skilled in communications and advocacy, with experience of building media platforms.
Comfortable in both the private and public sector with high level experience across 3rd sector working with government departments linking with media, arts and entertainment. Extensive live networks across Media, Arts, Entertainment, Culture & Education.
Passionate believer in the potential of Arts & Culture to transform lives and build stronger communities. My goal is to make a difference by stimulating activity - raising aspiration, ambition and confidence.

Specialties

Ideas. Employability. Work Related Learning. Partnership development. Relationship building. Utilising social networks.
Working to tight deadlines. Troubleshooting. Profile raising. Bringing together creative teams. Communications and advocacy.
Making things happen.

Director SHINE festival

Creative Partnerships

Education Management industry

2008 – 2009 (1 year) London, United Kingdom

Responsible for the creation, delivery and profile of the first national festival of youth talent SHINE from concept to delivery in 6 months. Translating the high level vision of the chair of the Task Force at the Government Department into reality. Testing the brand and personality of the festival drawing on schools networks and harnessing the energy of teachers and students as well as cultural leaders. Championing the new brand and developing solutions to capture activity and profile the widest possible breadth of activity through the creation of a networked web site acceptable in schools - the virtual wall of talent. Launching the new campaign working closely with the task force team in developing an effective media platform. Activating 350 organisations and their schools networks, inspiring key individuals from the creative, sports and education sectors to advocate the new festival. Managing risk and expectation. Communicating success and positive messages.
Resulting in 2,500 schools and organisations and around a million young people taking part in some 10,000 events from June 30 - July 4th 2008. Active partners included the RSC, Channel 4 , BBC, The Sage Gateshead and Youth Music. Senior ministers including the PM and champions such as Peter Jones attended events across the country. Shine achieved three days of national broadcast media out of 5 with extensive regional coverage reaching over 40% of the population of England. All media coverage was positive with over 80% carrying the url and key messages.
