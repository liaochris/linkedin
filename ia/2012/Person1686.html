tim spencer's Summary

I am a specialist in creative insight, culture analysis and strategy, working on multi-country semiotic research and analysis projects for global clients across categories.

I am a senior partner at Truth. Previously I worked for Space-Doctors, a semiotics consultancy. I was priveleged to present a workshop on cultural investigation and triangulation at the New University of Bulgaria in 2010, and a paper on transhumanism at the innaugural Semiotics conference of Cyprus University in 2011 during my time there.

In 2005, I took two years out of my career to start a family. I decided to be a freelance illustrator to pay the bills. It was surprisingly successful and I ended up doing it for five years.

Prior to my sojourn in illustration, I worked with the Scissor Sisters, creating and maintaining their visual territory, right from creative pitch to UMG for an unknown band through to the culmination of their first album campaign (five singles, album, DVD... nearly three years, and tons of accolades) and Brit Award success. i art directed (and illustrated) their marketing, packaging and communications.

In the early noughties I steered Prudential to a new, consistent global identity which was built upon a fundamental re-think of their consumer approach and anticipated their digital future.

I worked for The Overland Group as the head of visual communications where I managed a team of multi-disciplinary creatives delivering creative and strategic direction to fashion and footwear brands.

I worked with British Airways in the mid 90's on various initiatives at the primitive end of digital. I was also a team leader on Barclays rebranding project responsible for rebranding Barclays global Premier Banking division.

In the distant past I worked with Sir Paul Smith's creative director, but we fell out because I refused to go and collect his Christmas turkey from the butcher during a production crisis. I was young and hot headed with much to learn about the world.

Specialties

Strategy for forward planning, curing brand stagnation, linking current perceptions to new visions, developing opportunity and potential for business needing change.

Studiospooky & Studiofury

2002 – 2005 (3 years)

Studiospooky was the name I was working under for freelance engagements. Studiofury was a small, two man creative studio established to service clients in fashion and the music industries exclusively. I set the studio up with a senior designer I had recruited to my team at Overland. I had a roster of clients from the fashion industry (Levis, WDT Group, Pepe Jeans, Scotch&Soda etc) and my business partner had music industry connections. For the three years that I was working under the name Studiofury I was the art director for Scissor Sisters, creating the look and feel for the band's launch campaign and creating and executing the theme for each release. I honed an interest in illustration during my engagement with Scissor Sisters. As well as art directing I was also creating the illustrations used for the sleeve artworks and press/promotional materials. After completing their debut campaign (which culminated in a name check during the band's award acceptance speech at the 2005 Brit Awards ceremony... http://www.brits.co.uk/britstv/2005-international-breakthrough-scissor-sisters) I moved to Brighton and set up as an illustrator, in order to start a family without long term project distractions.

Creative Strategy consultant

BamberForsyth

May 2001 – December 2001 (8 months)

A six month project-specific contract to run the rebranding of Prudential which I was introduced to by Major Players at the commencement of a period of time where I decided to freelance following the closure of Overland after their take-over. Prudential's rebranding was undertaken with a small, focussed, creative team which I headed up, and a single project manager. BamberForsyth was sold to Cordiant at the end of the six month contract, and I reluctantly decided against an offer of an ongoing position with them. It was a brilliant company, I just didn't want another merger experience.

Head of visual communications / Creative director, design

Overland Group

1998 – 2001 (3 years)

I created and ran the company's in-house visual communications team, who were responsible for all ongoing /seasonal communication and branding initiatives and maintaining consumer-cultural relevance of the company's six owned or licensed global footwear and apparel brands.

Responsibilities included:
- Creative direction of brands both long term and seasonal
- Creation of new footwear and apparel brands
- Development and nurturing of existing global brands such as Caterpillar Inc, Merrel, Stride, Harley Davidson, Paul Smith footwear, Travel Fox and British Industrial Giant
- Providing consultancy creative engagement with clients outside the portfolio of owned and licensed brands.
- Seasonal marketing communications
- All product graphics, branding, packaging, in-store, merchandising, catalogues and brochures, and peripherals were designed and produced by my team.

I personally authored and ran a series of four 'Blueprint' workshops for the directors of Caterpillar Inc, Wolverine and Overland Group which were hosted at Wolverine's Grand Rapids HQ. The purpose of the Blueprint workshops was to completely re-engineer the Caterpillar merchandise business globally, streamlining, cutting back on individual territory skus' and sub-brands, simplifying the brand vision by returning to the core brand attributes re-imagined for a contemporary consumer, and re-launching Caterpillar's apparel and footwear businesses globally.

The Overland Group merged with Wolverine and all operations were eventually relocated to the US.

senior designer

Newell&Sorrell (and later, Interbrand)

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry

1996 – 1998 (2 years)

Head-hunted from DMB&B due to my experience with financial brands, I joined Newell&Sorrell to head up one of four teams responsible for the global rebranding of Barclays.

I authored and developed two major projects for British Airways following the embarrasment caused by their re-brand. One of the projects went into production as an internal global intranet network (Talkingpoint) and the other (Videospace) went into a significant development phase before being effectively overtaken by the increased capability of the internet (and so incoporated into BA's web presence thereafter). It was at this point that I made the transition from design to strategy and fully engaged with situations and ideas rather than visual execution.

My final project at Newell&Sorrell(Interbrand) was for the Royal Museums Greenwich who required some inventive problem solving in the run up to the millennium. I provided a strategic solution to a long-standing problem at the site which provided the foundation for their marketing and identity development over the Millennium period, a crucial time for this heritage site as it includes the Prime Meridian.

Senior designer, then joint head of design for DMB&B Financial

DMB&B

1994 – 1996 (2 years)

I joined a small in-house design team at the prestigious DMB&B's London office and then took over as joint creative head alongside Susan Marshall (now of MarshallOvery) to build a much bigger design studio with aspirations to stretch beyond the internal DMB&B network. Clients included financial, automotive and FMCG brands.

Freelance creative consultant

AboudSodano (For Paul Smith)

1991 – 1994 (3 years)

An engagement that started during my college years and continued for two years afterwards. I worked on a variety of branding and marketing projects for Sir Paul Smith, whilst also undertaking art direction and design projects for Channel 4, Vauxhall Motors, Pepe Jeans, Oliver Peoples, DesignMuseum etc.

My work here featured heavily in design magazines such as Eye, Creative Review, Graphics International and a piece of hand drawn typography was purchased for the St Bride's Type Library. Work was also featured in the design books of the day and won D&AD Silver and an IPC advertising award in 1994.
