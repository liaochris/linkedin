Deltina Hay's Summary

At a glance:

* Author/Columnist
* Web Developer
* Speaker/Presenter
* Educator
* Small Business Owner/Entrepreneur
* Graduate Education: Applied Mathematics/Computer Science/Psychology

Deltina Hay is the author of the critically acclaimed books, The Social Media Survival Guide, and The Bootstrapper's Guide to the Mobile Web. An avid writer, presenter, educator, and blogger, Hay is a veteran Web developer and advocate of open-source technologies.

She is the founder of the Web 2.0 resource, Social Media Power; the social, search, and mobile optimized Website consulting service, PLUMB Web Solutions; and hosts The Internet Empowerment Series of video tutorials on YouTube.

Currently, she educates businesses on the strategies, technologies, and tools for creating a sustainable and optimized presence in the Social Web. She is also the facilitator of Drury University's Social Media Certificate program, online version.

In 2010, Hay became an adviser to the International Marketing Standards Board.

Her graduate education includes applied mathematics, computer science, and psychology.

Specialties

The Mobile Web, web 3.0, social media, web 2.0, web development, writing, speaking

Deltina Hay's Experience

Author

The Bootstrapper's Guide to the Mobile Web

May 2012 – Present (3 months)

Creating a successful mobile-web presence is achievable with the tools found in this guide—without needing to learn a programming language or become a Web designer. Such a presence is now a necessity, rather than a luxury, for all businesses, organizations, and independent professionals to stay competitive. This quick, practical, hands-on introduction to the nuts and bolts of using the mobile web to grow a brand, improve sales, and increase profits is written for lay people and avoids jargon and programming concepts. Time- and money-saving solutions are presented, teaching technical novices how to quickly adapt their existing websites to the mobile ones and how to easily create mobile applications without having to learn to program. Step-by-step instructions stand alongside real-world examples of successful mobile-web transitions, and advice on best practices is provided to help business owners, entrepreneurs, marketing professionals, and creative professionals create the presence they need to help their business flourish.
