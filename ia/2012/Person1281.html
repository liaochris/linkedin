Molly Cannon Stevenson,CFRE, CAHP's Summary

I engage with organizations to achieve better bottom lines, social and financial, and to develop leadership.

My professional mission is to facilitate generosity and leadership development.

I chose the field of philanthropy early in my career because it breathes new life into social enterprise. I help organizations to move forward when they encounter opportunities for renewal. I have worked for my entire career to lead, manage, raise and grant funds.

I choose philanthropy now because the nonprofit sector still has greater opportunity for innovation to create social change. I continue on my path because my skills enable me to make a contribution which is larger than my scope. I enjoy seeing how resources can grow and transform organizations and create greater impact. I am committed to helping people and organizations to get to the next destination. I enjoy helping organizations to achieve maximum opportunity.

One of my strengths is to bridge difficult places. I have been able to raise funds where there is not a history. Also, I can connect the dots to show relevance to the home base. And I can mobilize when people or organizations become blocked.

While doing these things, I try to build community and cultivate a generous spirit.

I believe that a rising tide lifts all boats.

I can be reached directly at mstevenspip@aol.com.

Molly has been certified by the Association of Fundraising Professionals, CFRE international and the Association of Healthcare Philanthropy. She holds certificates in succession planning and executive transition management from Compasspoint. She is on a member of the Advisory Board for the National Network of Consultants to Grantmakers and The Grace Children’s Foundation.

She has served as an advisor to two grant makers in Colorado and a humanitarian agency in New York serving children living in extreme poverty in China. She is an alumna partner/investor of Social Venture Partners-Denver.
