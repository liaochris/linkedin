Consultant and Project Manager for Corporate Creativity and Innovation Program

Artmosphere, Inc.

2000 – 2002 (2 years) Oakland and Pleasant Hill, California (headquarters)

Artmosphere was a venture-funded start-up focusing on experiential family entertainment, a program and retail center, and corporate creativity and innovation training. I was the consulting project manager and a co-designer / co-developer of the corporate creativity and innovation program. I also consulted in business development, marketing and public relations outreach for corporate events.

For the corporate creativity and innovation program, I researched, managed and implemented the training curriculum, along with an advisory and working Board, which I helped to select, following extensive interviews with top-ranked authors, consultants and thought leaders in business creativity and innovation. I reported directly to the CEO and advised him, as well as coordinating with other department managers (such as the marketing director). I then managed beta-testing of the program and analyzed results.

Although the company's programs were launched successfully, and were widely praised by customers and the media, its venture funders discontinued funding during the 2001-2002 Silicon Valley bubble era. More information is available upon request.
