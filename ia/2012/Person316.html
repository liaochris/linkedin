Stephanie Pitcher Fishman's Summary

I specialize in genealogical writing, editing, and research. A homeschool mom since 2000, I write extensively on the subject of integrating family history with a homeschooling lifestyle. I am also author of two regular columns: Columbus Genealogy Examiner on Examiner.com and Family History 2.0 on HomeschoolMosaics.com.

Through Corn and Cotton Genealogy I offer the following services: Genealogical Research, Planning, and Analysis; Freelance Writing and Editing; Heritage Photography; Document Retrieval.

Member of the Association of Professional Genealogists and the FGS Outreach Committee in addition to other local and national genealogical and historical societies.

Stephanie Pitcher Fishman's Publications

  • Family History 2.0

    • Homeschool Mosaics
    Authors: Stephanie Pitcher Fishman

    Family History 2.0 is a monthly column at Homeschool Mosaics that shares ideas and examples of how you can integrate family history projects into your homeschool subjects to bring your family’s story to life. Are you new to genealogy? No worries! Find simple steps that will make it easy to integrate it into your lesson plan. Whether you are homeschooling younger children or have moved into the high school years, family history can still be included in your day.

  • Learning Through Family History: A quick guide for using genealogy to encourage active learning

    • Corn and Cotton Genealogy
    • December 2012
    Authors: Stephanie Pitcher Fishman

    Learning Through Family History is your quick guide to integrating your family's history into your child's education. Discover how foundational skills such as historical context, research skills, and critical thinking are highlighted through genealogical research. Include your family’s history in your social studies lessons. By homeschooling your family history your children will have a new perspective on current events.

    All this and more!

    Learning Through Family History is perfect for homeschooling families, parents looking for ways to interest their kids in learning, and grandparents wanting fun activities for their grandkids.
