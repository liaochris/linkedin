Violet Blue's Summary

"Violet Blue is omnipresent on the Web." -Forbes

The London Times named Blue one of the 40 bloggers who really count.

Violet Blue is a self-made wunderkind who is now one of our chief pundits on sex education, porn for women - as well as technology, privacy and security culture. Courted by OWN and appearing in many major magazines and periodicals around the world, everyone wants to know what Violet Blue has to say about sexuality, gender, privacy and new tech today.

Author, journalist, educator, speaker, activist and editor - Blue curates one of the most respected and top selling erotica series for women Best Women's Erotica; her sharp editorial eye and skill as an author has won her many awards with her 50+ books that have been translated into many languages.

Blue is not just a pundit on sex and tech but also an agent of change and raises the bar for discussion on critical issues that affect at-risk populations in the realms of tech and sex ever higher.

Violet Blue is also a top 25 Forbes "Web Celeb" and one of Wired's "Faces of Innovation." She is regularly interviewed by outlets such as Newsweek, MSNBC, The Wall Street Journal, NPR, BBC, CNN, NYT, PBS, Oprah Winfrey Show, Esquire, Maxim, Wired's "Experts Predict The Future" feature to David Levy's "Love + Sex With Robots" and more.

Forbes called her podcast (Open Source Sex, named for the intersection of tech and sex, and the free-flowing information exchange of the open source software movement) "One of the Internet's most popular podcasts." Blue's SF Chronicle column of the same name connected with 4 million readers a month. After 4 years, Blue resigned in a dispute with management in which she believed unethical SEO tactics were being employed with her online columns.

Violet speaks to cyberlaw classes at UC Berkeley (Boalt; Samuelson Law Clinic), human sexuality programs, global tech conferences (ETech, SXSWi), and volunteers at SFSI, Without My Consent and UCSF's NGO Global Health Training Program.
