Keith Boone's Summary

I am a highly skilled soup-to-nuts architect with several years of project management and some product management experience as well. My technical specialty is the manipulation, storage, retrieval and use of information, especially that used for healthcare.

I am a prolific writer of specifications and code, having written over 2500 pages of technical documentation in the last five years, and over 35000 lines of code in the same time. I am a voracious reader, routinely consuming 50-100 page technical specifications in a matter of an hour or two. I am also a good public speaker and instructor.

I think that maintaining technical skills is important, especially when you aren't responsible for product code. I speak more programming languages than I can reasonably list, learn new technologies very quickly, and have an in-depth understanding of system architectures, design patterns and algorithms.

Specialties

Internationally recognized expert in Clinical Document Architecture (CDA).
Co-chair of HL7 Structured Documents Working group, Integrating the Healthcare Enterprise (IHE) Patient Care Coordination Committee, ANSI/HITSP Care Management and Health Records TC, and EHRA Quality and Clinical Decision Support workgroup.
Coeditor of more than 10 implementation guides on CDA, including the HL7 Continuity of Care Document (CCD).
Member of HL7, IHE, ANSI/HITSP, ASTM, ISO and Continua
