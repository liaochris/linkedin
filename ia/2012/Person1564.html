Ruairí McKiernan's Summary

ruairi@community.ie | Twitter @ruairimckiernan
Latest Irish Times article:
http://www.irishtimes.com/newspaper/opinion/2012/0813/1224322099877.html

34 year old award dreamer and schemer, moving and making social change happen. People powered projects in communications, online, advocacy, campaigns, youth, health, wellbeing, arts and culture.

From Cootehill, Co. Cavan, founded Dublin based SpunOut.ie National Youth Organization. Appointed by President Michael D Higgins to Council of State and been involved with 100s community initiatives including organising the Dalai Lama's 2011 visit to Ireland.

In 2004, started Community Creations & SpunOut.ie as a (Co. Donegal) bedroom based project with the aim of empowering people to create positive personal & social change. Later established offices Galway and Dublin.

Between 2004-2011 led SpunOut.ie to become a pioneering multi award-winning youth development initiative, fundraised millions from philanthropic, state and community sources & recruited national team of full-time, part-time & project staff.

SpunOut.ie work included developing one of world's leading youth websites, national health and social awareness campaigns, running a pioneering activism academy, a mini-grants scheme, a youth media magazine & organizing events including the 2011 Youth Rising Presidential debate.

Regularly speak in the media and at events on social issues (MacGill, RTE etc.). Profiled in the Irish Times, RTE TV & in a book on social entrepreneurs.

On boards of Volunteer Ireland and Gaisce - The President's Youth Achievement Award. Also involved in running President's 'Being Young and Irish' youth engagement initiative.

Member of Council of State

Office of the President of Ireland

January 2012 – Present (9 months) Ireland

Recently appointed to the Council of State (2012-2018) by President Michael D Higgins as one of his 7 appointees.

More info:
The Constitution of Ireland provides for a Council of State to aid and counsel the President on all matters on which the President may consult them. The circumstances when the President must consult the Council of State are specified in the Constitution.

Members include 7 appointed members as well as Taoiseach (Prime Minister), Tánaiste (Deputy Prime Minister), Chief Justice, President of the High Court, Ceann Comhairle (Chair of the lower house of parliament), Cathaoirleach of the Seanad (Chair of the upper house of parliament), Attorney General. Other members include former office holders of the role of President, Taoiseach or Chief Justice.
