Julian Dobson's Summary

My aim is to share ideas about improving towns and cities, and to see what grows. I do that by writing, facilitating conversations, speaking and listening, researching and explaining, connecting and encouraging.

I am a writer, editor, facilitator and consultant specialising in regeneration and sustainable communities - in other words, how to make the places we live in better and enable the people who live there to create better lives for themselves.

I was the founding editor of New Start magazine in 1999 and was editorial director until October 2010. I was editor of Inside Housing magazine from 1993 to 1998. I previously specialised in healthcare (the Health Service Journal), social housing and trade unions, having served an apprenticeship on local papers in Kent and South Wales.

Through Urban Pollinators Ltd I help people and organisations share learning and good practice, communicate in language people of all backgrounds can understand, manage editorial and communications projects - and much more!

I am a Fellow of the RSA and a voluntary board member at the Centre for Local Economic Strategies.

You can also find me at http://twitter.com/juliandobson

Specialties

Placemaking and urbanism; editing, writing and editorial project management; training and facilitation; bridging the gap between policy, research and practice.

Julian Dobson's Publications

  • Grey Places Need Green Spaces: the case for investing in our nation's natural assets

    • Groundwork UK
    • May 21, 2012
    Authors: Julian Dobson

    A decade after the UK's Urban Green Spaces Task Force, we need to think again about the future of Britain's green spaces. This report, commissioned to mark the 30th anniversary of environmental charity Groundwork, makes the case for rethinking traditional approaches to ownership and management, and calls for the principles of the historic commons to be extended to all publicly accessible green spaces.

  • Forces for Good: local benefits from surplus military land

    • Bill Sargent Trust, Portsmouth
    • May 2012
    Authors: Julian Dobson

    Forces for Good explains the implications for local communities of the reorganisation of the UK armed forces, with large tracts of military land becoming surplus to requirements. It shows how new community rights could be used to create positive uses for surplus military land and positive partnerships between local communities and the armed forces.

  • 21st Century Town Centres

    • Action for Market Towns
    • October 2011
    Authors: Julian Dobson

    A summary of a vision for 21st century town centres, based on a submission to the UK government's review of the high street. Describes the work Action for Market Towns is doing to help achieve this vision.

  • Seeing the Wood for the Trees

    • The Mersey Forest
    • June 2011
    Authors: Julian Dobson

    Why the UK government's review of forestry matters - and why we need forests that support community, commerce and climate.

  • A Future for Community Forests

    • The Mersey Forest
    • January 2011
    Authors: Julian Dobson

    A contribution to the debate on the future of England's woodlands, written for The Mersey Forest, the country's largest community forest. In the context of plans (subsequently abandoned) to sell Forestry Commission land, it examines the value of woodlands to local communities and the scope for community management and ownership.

  • We Can, You Can

    • Renew Northwest
    • February 2007
    Authors: Julian Dobson

    Lessons learned from the Renew Northwest exemplar learning programme 2007.
