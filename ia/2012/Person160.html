Benjamin Cichy's Summary

Benjamin Cichy is the Software Architect and lead Flight Software (FSW) engineer for NASA's next mission to Mars, the Mars Science Laboratory (MSL). Ben received Bachelor's and Master's degrees in Computer Science from Cornell University in Ithaca, NY, and a Master's degree in Aerospace Engineering from the University of Southern California in Los Angeles, CA. He joined JPL in 2002 and has made significant contributions to a number of key flight missions and technology demonstrations at the laboratory.

From 2002 to 2005 Ben helped to build the Autonomous Sciencecraft Experiment (ASE) as part of NASA's New Millennium Program. ASE was uploaded to the EO-1 spacecraft in 2004 and demonstrated the technologies required to autonomously plan and execute spacecraft operations. For this work, the ASE team shared NASA's prestigious Software of the Year award in 2005.

In 2005 Ben joined the FSW team for the Mars Phoenix Lander. In this role Ben developed FSW for Phoenix's Guidance and Control and Entry Descent and Landing (EDL) systems as part of a joint NASA-Lockheed Martin FSW team in Denver, CO. After returning to JPL in 2007, Ben joined the EDL Systems Engineering team, and led study teams investigating the robustness of the EDL system, culminating in the successful landing of Phoenix on the surface of Mars in 2008. For his work on Phoenix Ben was awarded NASA's Exceptional Achievement Medal.

On MSL Ben is responsible for the software that controls all onboard functions of the Rover. This includes the software for Launch/Cruise, EDL, and Surface operations.
