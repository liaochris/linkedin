Dr Mike Cadogan's Summary

Emergency physician with a passion for medical informatics and medical education.
I founded the medical education blog - Life in the FastLane (http://lifeinthefastlane.com) to promote emergency medicine and critical care education at both undergraduate and postgraduate training level. Latest development is the Global Medical Education Project (http://gmep.imeducate.com) providing free online interactive medical education resources

I work as a social media and medical informatics consultant for corporate groups such as Elsevier, BMJ, iMeducate, HealthEngine.com.au, Popfossa.com and FutureHealth.com.au to help create an educational eLearning tools which to facilitate discriminatory feedback and enhance and further develop logical learning tools.

Specialties

Concepts, ideas, vision and a drive and enthusiasm to see the dream to reality. Medical Informatics and using Information Technology to develop education and learning in medicine. Medical author.

Dr Mike Cadogan's Publications

  • Toxicology handbook 2e

    • Elsevier
    • December 15, 2010
    Authors: Dr Mike Cadogan, Lindsay Murray, Frank Daly, Mark Little, Jason Armstrong

    The Toxicology Handbook 2e is a practical, didactic guide to the approach, assessment and management of poisoned patients. It has been written for hospital-based doctors at all levels and describes the risk assessment-based approach pioneered by the principal authors. The concise layout enables the reader to quickly locate information in a poisoning emergency. The book also features locally relevant information on bites, stings and envenoming.

  • Emergency Medicine Diagnosis and Management 6e

    • Hodder
    • August 25, 2011
    Authors: Dr Mike Cadogan, Anthony F.T. Brown

    The sixth edition of this international bestselling emergency medicine handbook has been completely redesigned, revised and expanded to include the very latest evidence-based guidelines suitable for interns and junior doctors new to the emergency care setting, right up to specialist emergency medicine trainees.

  • On Call Principles and Protocols 2e

    • Saunders Australia
    • August 25, 2011
    Authors: Dr Mike Cadogan,

    This book provides a structured approach to the initial assessment, resuscitation, differential diagnosis and short-term management of common on-call problems. It provides an overall guide to hospital practice and acute clinical skills, and is designed to help junior doctors and senior medical students acquire the logical, practical and efficient approach which is essential for problem-based learning and acute management
