Richard Young's Summary

I'm a freelance writer and editor with 17 years experience as a business journalist. As deputy and acting editor of Financial Director, then editor and publishing director of Real Finance, I spent eight years as a leading commentator on, and observer of, board-level management – particularly financial executives. I've written features on a wide range of subjects including corporate IT, private equity, strategy, people management, marketing, branding, governance, banking, investor relations – pretty much everything related to business, in fact.
In 2011, that experience earned me the part-time role as strategic editor for the ICAEW's Faculty magazines, a portfolio of seven titles covering every field accountants work in, from IT and corporate finance to tax and audit. I offer advice on magazine structure, help shape coverage, develop new formats and support a large team of designers and subs producing around 60 editions each year.
So now I divide my time between this work and feature writing for a range of business publications; editing books and magazines for corporate and public-sector clients; and running training on Better Business Writing. I'm also an experienced conference chair and have hosted debates and round-table discussions for a number of business organisations including the Market Research Society, the ICAEW and the FDs' Forum.

Specialties

Private equity, business technology, management, financial management, board-level decision-making, market research, the 2012 Olympic Games and Paralympic Games, intellectual property rights, flexible working, the future of work, corporate responsibility - but plenty of other areas, too.

Business Computer World was aimed at IT decision makers in medium to large sized organisations, with an emphasis on connecting their technology decisions to business fundamentals. My role was to ensure copy had a distinct business flavour, but I also acted as news editor for the title.

Staff writer

BusinessAge (VNU Business Publications)

August 1995 – July 1996 (1 year)

VNU bought BusinessAge from maverick publisher Tom Rubython and under the stewardship of Peter Kirwan assembled an excellent team of writers and freelancers to turn it from a writ-ridden scandal sheet into a UK version of Forbes. The plan didn't work, sadly. But as the most junior member of the team, I learned a huge amount about solid journalism, good writing and tight subbing before the plug was pulled. (Any connection between VNU's re-engineering of the ad sales department - including the closure of the training function - and the failure of BAge is entirely co-incidental. We just spent too much money on "content".) I wrote my first articles about the then-emerging "world-wide web", including an analysis of the potential for internet commerce, for BAge.
