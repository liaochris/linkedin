Christopher McLean's Summary

An established and experienced researcher with Ipsos MORI, I have developed strong research skills across qualitative and quantitative methodologies. Having gained experience working on projects for a range of clients in a variety of sectors, I have particular expertise in social research, political polling and digital research methods. A diligent, hard-working and creative individual, I have the ability to work on my own or as part of a team. I have a passion for new and innovative ways of working, while maintaining a focus on producing high quality work.

I have contributed articles to the Times, Holyrood Magazine and the Total Politics blog, and appeared on Newsnight Scotland and the Politics Show Scotland.

I also established and manage a successful Twitter account for Ipsos MORI Scotland, assist in the development of the website and regularly contribute to our quarterly methods newsletter, Approach.

Specialties

Social and market research, public attitudes and opinion polling, political polling, social media monitoring, reputation research, employee research, qualitative and quantitative research methods.

  • On Track: Class of 2004: The Fourth and Final Wave

    • Scottish Funding Council
    • April 2010
    Authors: Christopher McLean, Amy Homes, Lorraine Murray, Jane Denholm

    Commissioned by the Scottish Funding Council (SFC), 'On Track Class of 2004' was a five-year longitudinal study of people qualifying from Scotland's colleges and higher education institutions. The aim of the study was to track the progress of the 'Class of 2004' and to explore the impact of students' learning experiences on their futures in order to help universities and colleges to respond to the needs of learners more effectively.

    By the fourth and final sweep in 2009, the study focused more on respondents as ‘people’, rather than ‘learners’. In addition, the final sweep of the survey coincided with a turbulent financial period culminating in global economic recession. For these reasons, and also because it was the final report, the questionnaire included a more holistic examination of the Class of 2004 as people and citizens, asking more about attitudes and general wellbeing. The reports considered these aspects, and how respondents compared with others in the general population, as well as looking back at the journey they have taken.
