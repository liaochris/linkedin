Paul Allen's Summary

I am a social entrepreneur who seeks to build profitable companies that have meaning and purpose beyond money.

In 1990 Dan Taggart and I founded Infobases, Inc. Our mission was to "digitize all the most important books in every field of human knowledge and make them available on CD ROM."

In 1996 we founded Ancestry.com. We launched our first genealogy site in June 1996. In December 1998 we launched a private network for families called MyFamily.com - the fastest growing social site up to that point. We started attracting millions of users. Revenue doubled every year for our first 6 years. We brought in an executive management team that raised $90.5 million in venture capital. I moved from Utah to Silicon Valley during the bubble, and we planned to take the company public.

We survived the dot com bubble burst (barely) but became profitable shortly thereafter. I left in 2002 after the company had decided to focus on Ancestry.com while I felt the MyFamily.com social concept was our biggest opportunity.

Ancestry went public in Nov. 2009 and is now worth more than $1 billion. Like many founders, I did okay with this business, but not as well as I had hoped. As Ray Noorda of Novell fame once quipped, "finders keepers, founders weepers."

From 2007-2011 I focused on FamilyLink.com, which built Facebook apps, mobile apps, and web sites to help people find and connect with relatives. Our most popular app on Facebook was We're Related, which attracted 80 million users and had 21MM active users at its peak, until Facebook changed its Platform.

In late 2011 we sold the genealogy portion of our business to MyHeritage and in early 2012 we sold our We're Related app to Ancestry.com.

In the fall of 2012 my family relocated to the Washington, DC Area where I joined The Gallup Organization full time.
