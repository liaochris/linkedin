Matt Werner's Summary

I've been a Technical Writer at Google since March, 2010. My technical writing for Google has been translated into 40 languages and quoted in dozens of publications, including the Wall Street Journal.

Outside of Google, I write books, including a collection of artist interviews titled "Oakland in Popular Memory" (2012) and a photobook titled "Bay Area Underground" (2013).

In my free time, I volunteer as VP of Marketing and Communications for Microclinic International, work as a webmaster for nonprofits, and DJ.

Specialties: Writing, editing, technical writing, grant writing, fundraising, branding, copy editing, proofreading, fact checking, transcribing, radio interviewing, reporting, playwrighting, and assembling oral narrative collections.

New media skills: professional audio recording for radio broadcast with Audio Hijack Pro and Garage Band; audio editing with Audacity; video editing with Final Cut Pro and iMovie; web designing with Dreamweaver. Publishing experience with Quark and Adobe InDesign.

  • Oakland in Popular Memory: Interviews with 12 cutting-edge artists from Oakland and beyond

    • Thought Publishing
    • May 15, 2012
    Authors: Matt Werner

    The image of Oakland, California has been tainted in the mainstream media with news reports focusing on violence in Oakland. Matt Werner explores a different narrative in Oakland in Popular Memory, interviewing young artists from Oakland, and established artists who’ve influenced Oakland musicians. ISBN: 9780982689837

    Matt Werner, in the spirit of Studs Terkel, conducted long-form interviews from 2008-2012 which cover the 2008 election of President Obama, the shooting of Oscar Grant, and the Occupy Oakland protests. Werner spoke with these artists at length, discussing topics like race relations in Oakland in the post-Oscar Grant era, postmodern literary theory, and the changing landscape of the music industry during the digital revolution.

    Through these interviews, Oakland is seen as an engine of cultural innovation, as a city bustling with lively avant-garde art and music scenes, spanning from indie rock to spoken word to hip-hop. Oakland in Popular Memory captures those artists putting a new “there” in Oakland.
