Robbie Banks' Summary

The Columbus Chamber helps businesses thrive, which in turn strengthens the Columbus Region. Through the Chamber's Attract & Retain Talent initiative, local employers are assured access to a workforce that meets their needs now and in the future.

The Attract & Retain Talent Initiative is comprised of three areas of focus:

ColumbusInternships.com helps link local employers to college students seeking internships in the Columbus Region.

The Chamber's suite of "Recruiter Solutions," including liveworkplayColumbus.com assists employers when they are recruiting candidates from outside the Columbus Region.

Young Professionals (YPs) are integral to the competitiveness of the Columbus Region. Because the population of the generation following the Baby Boomers is significantly smaller, it is imperative that the Columbus community actively attract and retain younger workers to meet the needs of local employers.

Specialties

Attraction and retention of young professionals, including presentations to various audiences; employees (getting involved as YPs), college students (how to effectively network) and organizations; volunteer management and recruitment; event planning and execution; meeting facilitation; member/customer relations; connecting people to people, businesses/organizations, information and resources; Columbus facts, people, places and things; social media (Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, blogs and videos).

Robbie Banks' Projects

  • What Makes Columbus "Cool" for the Young and Talented? 2011 State of the Young Professional Survey Results

    • September 2011 to October 2011
    Team Members: Robbie Banks, Jung Kim, Andrea Applegate, Michelle Bretscher, Hannah Ullom, Molly Osmon, Susan Merryman, APR

    Young professionals comprise a significant portion of the Columbus Region's workforce and entrepreneurial community, making them a vital part of the Columbus Chamber's mission of helping to retain and expand businesses.

    According to the Chamber's 2011 survey, "What Makes Columbus 'Cool' for the Young and Talented," young professionals choose to make the Columbus Region home because of its amenities, vitality and social engagement. More than 1,000 young professionals responded to the survey. The Chamber is encouraged to see positive feedback from this key stakeholder group, justifying the commitment made to attracting and retaining the young and talented.
