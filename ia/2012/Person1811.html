Wayne Schulz's Summary

I am a (non-practicing) CPA specializing in helping companies fix, improve, or upgrade their Sage 100 ERP software. I also do lots of work providing paid second opinions about third party solutions, integrations, major enhancements or integrations.

Find me on Google+ = http://goo.gl/6JShA = where I share more industry specific content.

Helped to found The 90 Minds group which offers some of the most experienced independent consultants from across North America who specialize in Sage 100 ERP as well as custom programming, scripting, advanced Crystal Reports, manufacturing, financial report writing, SQL migration, and more.

My services are usually offered for new (10%) or existing (90%) users of Sage 100 ERP with clients ranging in size from startups to $ 500 million and are located throughout North America (about 60% of my customer base is non-local and serviced remotely through online remote access).

75% of my time is spent performing systems analysis and looking at a company's existing procedures so I can make recommendations on improvement. My focus is on the long term as opposed to throwing a bandage on a current problem.

In my spare time I've authored technology articles for Journal of Accountancy, Accounting Today, Accounting Technology and the Connecticut Society of CPAS.

I spent two years as Senior Editor seven days a week for Geardiary.com which is an online ,Technorati 5K rated,technology blog.

I was awarded a Spirit Award by Sage for -- well, I guess being high spirited.

Much of my work these days is done in the cloud. I'm a big Google Apps user, and use Android as my smartphone to keep in touch while on the road. Within the next five years I expect most companies researching new systems to seriously review the cloud/SaaS environment.

All of my work is 100% money back guaranteed - and performed at a fixed price which you will know in advance.

    One of my top WOW tips is to never write a lengthy general purpose email to someone without also re-purposing it as a blog post. These items which if sent via email can go to waste or reach only one or two people. Whereas if you post the item on your blog you share with the world.

  • You'd Have To Be Crazy To Ignore Email Newsletters

    • ERP Life
    • September 9, 2011
    Authors: Wayne Schulz

    If you are not sending out a monthly email newsletter to your clients I recommend:

    1. Create 2 to 3 posts per week on your web site. So long as the site provides for RSS you can

    2. Feed that RSS into Mailchimp and have it automatically create an email newsletter on a daily/weekly basis.

    3. Don’t get hung up on creating the perfect content, perfect newsletter, perfect mailing list. They don’t exist.

    4. Be consistent. Measure what’s working. Measure what’s not. Change.
