Alex Mitchell's Summary

I am the Co-founder and Chairman of Young Brits (providing a global voice for young UK entrepreneurs), I am a Founding Member and the United Kingdom President of the G20 Young Entrepreneurs Alliance (a global network of young entrepreneurs and the organisations that support them), I am Vice President of the European Confederation of Young Entrepreneurs (the major association of young entrepreneurs throughout Europe) and I am Head of Influencer Relations at the Institute of Directors (Europe's largest professional body for directors).

In addition to these roles I am an Ambassador for Global Entrepreneurship Week (the world’s largest celebration of entrepreneurship), I am a member of the steering committee for the Commonwealth Secretariats Youth Programme (engaging and empowering young people from 54 countries to enhance their contribution to development) and I am a member of the Chairman’s Committee for Young Enterprise (the UK largest business and enterprise education charity).

Previous roles i have held include being a member of the External Advisory Board of vinspired (the UK national youth volunteering charity), a business representative on UK Trade and Invests Asia Task Force (the UK governments agency helping business to export), being a judge on the 2011 Business in the Community Awards, the 2010 National Shell LIVEwire awards and the 2008 and 09 National Business Awards. Prior to the Institute of Directors i worked at the Confederation of British Industry (one of the UK's leading independent employers' organisation. Representing over 250000 public and private sector), where I held a number of roles ranging from International Researcher to briefing the Director General on FTSE 350 member companies.

Specialties

International market experience, enterprise and entrepreneurism development at national and internationally level, liaison and briefing at senior business and government levels, start up support, small and medium sized business support, volunteering within a corporate sense, negotiation both at a business and political level, senior level government liaison, community engagement with business, social enterprise start up and support and governance setup within business and organisations.

President of the UK Delegation

G20 Entrepreneur Summit - Toronto 2010

May 2010 – July 2010 (3 months)

I have been asked to be President of the UK Delegation going to the G20 Entrepreneur Summit - Toronto 2010.

The two-day G20 Young Entrepreneur Summit provides forum for young entrepreneurs (ages 18-40) from G20 countries to meet, exchange ideas and to establish international networks and partnerships that will drive sustainable global economic growth.
The G20 Young Entrepreneur Summit will build on the success of the inaugural G8 Young Business Summit held in Stresa, Italy in 2009. Attendees will meet with highly successful entrepreneurs, prominent business leaders, socialpreneurs and government officials, and will participate in identifying key actions that governments can take to unleash the potential of youth entrepreneurship in the G20 countries.

The Summit has been recognized by the Canadian federal government as an official G20 event, and the main goals for the Summit are as follows:

- Prepare a concise one-page communiqué that will be shared with the G20 Leaders Summit

- The communiqué to be reflected in the final deliberations of the G20 leaders summit

- Ensure that a global agenda for youth entrepreneurship be included not only in the outcomes for 2010 but in all future G20 Summits
