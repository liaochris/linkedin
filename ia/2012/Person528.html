Felicia Day's Summary

Felicia Day is a professional actress who has appeared in many TV shows and movies. Check her IMDb page or Wikipedia page for a full list of credits. Felicia is most well-known for her acting in web video; in Joss Whedon's epic internet musical "Dr. Horrible's Sing-Along Blog" and in her own show that she writes, stars in and co-produces, "The Guild". She is also an avid social networking fan, most prominently on Twitter. For more press, contact information and her blog check out her website.

Felicia Day's Projects

  • The Guild

    • July 2007 to Present
    Team Members: Felicia Day, Kim Evey, Jane Selle Morgan, Greg Benson, Sean Becker, Sandeep Parikh, Jeff Lewis, Robin Thorsen, Amy Okuda,

    "The Guild" is an award-winning online sitcom about the lives of gamers who are members of an online guild called "The Knights of Good". It is written for gamers, about gamers, by a gamer. Episodes vary from 3-6 minutes in length, and follow the Guild members’ lives online and offline.

  • Dragon Age: Redemption

    • October 2011 to November 2011
    Team Members: Felicia Day, Kim Evey, Dan Kaplow, Greg Aronowitz, Peter Winther, William Mesa, Emily Wallin

    "Dragon Age: Redemption" is a six-part web series set in the world of the EA/Bioware RPG "Dragon Age". The series tells the story of Tallis, an Elvish assassin, who gets a last chance at redemption when she's sent to capture a rogue Qunari mage intent on wreaking havoc in the world.

  • Vaginal Fantasy Hangout

    • January 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Felicia Day, Veronica Belmont, Bonnie Burton, Kiala Kazebee

    Once a month four ladies discuss a Vaginal Fantasy book in a G+ Hangout. Interpret at will.

  • The FLOG

    • April 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Felicia Day, Kim Evey, Sheri Bryant, Sean Becker, Brit Weisman

    Hosted by Geek Goddess Felicia Day, "The FLOG" is a spirited, eccentric and quirky adventure through life. Join Felicia each week as she explores new hobbies and experiences, curates links from across the web, and connects with her favorite people: her fans!

  • Written By a Kid

    • 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Felicia Day, Kim Evey, Sheri Bryant

    Stories told by 5-9 year olds are put in the hands of some of today's coolest online directors including Rhett & Link, Dane Boedigheimer, Daniel Strange and more! All ages will love this visually stunning, often hilarious anthology series that brings the madness and magic of a child's imagination to life.

  • Sword & Laser

    • 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Felicia Day, Kim Evey, Sheri Bryant, Veronica Belmont, Tom Merritt

    The popular podcast "Sword & Laser" can now been seen as well as heard on the YouTube screen! Hosts Veronica Belmont and Tom Merrit bring you the latest in tech, geek and entertainment news and engage in often irreverent, always dynamic discourse in this community-driven science ﬁction and fantasy-themed book club.

  • Dark Horse Motion Comics

    • April 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Felicia Day, Kim Evey, Sheri Bryant, Mike Richardson, Eric Bruhwiler

    The nation's largest independent comic-book publisher brings a selection of their most popular titles to the video screen with Dark Horse Motion Comics. Using never-before-seen motion graphics techniques, watch some of the comic world's most iconic characters spring to life.

  • TableTop

    • April 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Felicia Day, Kim Evey, Sheri Bryant, Wil Wheaton

    Wil Wheaton hosts this fun, lively show where “Celebrity Poker” meets table top board games! In each episode, Wil plays a different game with celebrity guests from ﬁlm, TV, and YouTube. Filled with witty banter and cheeky camaraderie, "TableTop" introduces viewers to the best of tabletop gaming.

  • Learning Town

    • 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Felicia Day, Kim Evey, Sheri Bryant, Paul Sabourin, Greg "Storm" DiCostanzo, Josh Cagan

    Nerd music duo Paul and Storm bring their harmonies and smartly offbeat humor to this new comedy musical. The learning curve is steep when the two are tasked with reviving the ﬂagging educational show of their childhoods in the face of villainous hipsters, ghosts who give bad advice and ﬂammable puppets.
