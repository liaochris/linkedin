Jackie Hutchings' Summary

My working life has been varied, to say the least. I'm at my best when I'm working in a fast-moving start up; inspiring and supporting people. I love software and technology. I can sell and I've learned my online marketing skills by doing it for myself. I've got a great sense of humour which can sometimes be misinterpreted as flippancy! I help people, so please contact me.

I love gadgets, technology, innovation, Bruce Springsteen, dogs, Percy Pigs and scuba diving. My favourite book is The Little Prince which has a great quote "language is the source of misunderstandings" - how true is that?

My child-rearing years...... much easier to work like crazy during term time so I could be at home during the school holidays (although, as any self-employed person can empathise with, it doesn't always happen that way as it's so hard to turn work away). However, it worked most of the time and I did some interesting and varied contract work including delivering IT training courses through to the design of courseware, supervision of training needs analysis and management of development projects.

Marketing Director

WordNet UK Limited

June 1982 – June 1984 (2 years 1 month)

Prior to the uptake of personal computers, companies relied on word processing systems to manage every day tasks. WordNet marketed a device that captured data input from golfball typewriters and fed that data to the word processing system for editing. Raised venture capital and took the company to the Unlisted Securities Market before motherhood robbed me of my brain and turned my world upside down.

Founder

Pressure Points

July 1979 – June 1982 (3 years)

Identified a gap in the market which existed when companies changed from using one word processing manufacturer to another; that of incompatible data. Consequently, the partnership was one of the first in London to offer an electronic data conversion service initially using acoustic couplers. Does anyone remember them? And, if anyone has access to a Rank Xerox 800 magnetic tape word processor, I'd love to use one again!

Sale Executive

AES (UK) Limited

July 1977 – July 1979 (2 years 1 month)

A dream job ..... AES (UK) Limited was a forerunner in the UK market place for supplying screen based word processing systems.
Instigated and developed the BBC account which, initially, installed systems in 24 departments. This account developed into one of the largest users of AES products

Rank Xerox (UK) Limited
