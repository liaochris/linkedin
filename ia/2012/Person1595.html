Sarah E. Holland's Summary

After a two-year sabbatical from the academic and archaeological worlds to care for my terminally ill mother, I am looking for a new and challenging role in which my diverse work experience and academic background can excel. Having worked in textbook publishing at Garland Science (a division of Taylor & Francis Academic Textbook Publishers) in the UK for almost three years, I am now looking for the next step in my trans-Atlantic professional life. I would welcome an opportunity to work in any field related to science, archaeology/anthropology, environmental sciences, and/or education outreach and community engagement. I am eager to be given a chance to continue to develop my interest in education and publishing while also furthering my skills in diving, maritime archaeology, and project management. I am eager to be given a chance to continue to develop my life-long interest in archaeology, history, research, and academic endeavor in my home country. I’m confident that my enthusiasm, passion for learning and work commitment would be an asset to any organization. I have experience in both commercial and academic environments and am equally comfortable working in both.
