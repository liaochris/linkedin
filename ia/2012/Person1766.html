S Tony Kutlu, MBA, PMP's Experience

Manager, Project Management Office

Ohio Technology Consortium / OARnet / The Ohio State University

July 2011 – Present (1 year 7 months) Ohio

In collaboration with the Chancellor for the Ohio Board of Regents (BOR) and the University System of Ohio (Education Technology Division), and in accordance with The Ohio State University policies, goals, and objectives.

The Manager of the PMO provides leadership for all facets of the PMO Office including IT strategic planning, fiscal planning, prioritization and execution of projects using Information Technology Infrastructure Library (ITIL) principles. Coordinates collaboratively with all Ed Tech Division agencies. Supports a robust IT Governance process internally and across organizations.

OH-TECH functions as the umbrella organization for Ohio Supercomputer Center's cutting-edge research, OARnet's expansion of the broadband network to Ohio higher education, K-12 schools and state and local government. The consortium provides the IT infrastructure to the BOR, the state of Ohio, and offers cutting-edge and innovative student services for higher education customers in Ohio.

OARnet facilitates and encourages the development and dissemination of knowledge throughout Ohio by connections with other networks, locally, nationally and internationally, as well as through the implementation of effective shared IT services among member institutions. A prime goal of OARnet is to facilitate and to encourage both collaborative projects between institutions and the general sharing of resources

The Ohio Technology Consortium (OH-TECH) was established in 2011 to serve as the research and technology arm of the Ohio Board of Regents, offering innovative technology resources and services for Ohio higher education, K-12 schools and state and local government. OH-TECH, comprised of the Ohio Academic Resources Network (OARnet), the Ohio Supercomputer Center and eStudent Services, leverages the strengths and increases operational efficiency of each organization. For more, visit www.oh-tech.org.

Ohio Dominican University

Educational Institution; 501-1000 employees; Higher Education industry

February 2010 – Present (3 years)

I have the privilege and responsibility of teaching (Business 102 in the undergraduate and Business 545 in the graduate programs) at Ohio Dominican University on a part-time basis. My focus is on technology in business and strategic decision making.

Ohio Dominican's primary mission is to provide degree and continuing education programs for the intellectual development, the growth in truth, of each student.

As a Catholic liberal arts University within the Dominican tradition, ODU is guided in its educational mission by the motto: contemplare et contemplata aliis tradere (to contemplate truth and to share with others the fruits of this contemplation).

In keeping with the Dominican tradition, educational experiences are grounded in the liberal arts, foster the development of spiritual, aesthetic, and ethical values, promote physical and mental health, and provide for the development and responsible use of human skills and talents in professional and social service.
