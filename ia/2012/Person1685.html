Stuart Chalmers' Summary

I gained my PhD in Agent Technologies from the University of Aberdeen (UK) and since then my research has focused on agent based applications of constraint programming and more recently on the use of Semantic Web technologies for knowledge representation.

I have worked at the SP Technical Research Institute of Sweden using semantics in the context of driving simulator design and more recently at the University of Glasgow (UK) on the modelling and re-use of astronomy information as part of the International Virtual Observatory Alliance (IVOA) data representation initiative.

Stuart Chalmers' Projects

  • BIM Gateway

    • January 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Stuart Chalmers, Geoffrey Makstutis, Alex Tait, Alisdair Aldous, Anne Dye

    The BIM Gateway Project is a one year Technology Strategy Board funded initiative that aims to produce a reusable, open version of the Uniclass Construction Classification standard and the Common Arrangment of Works Sections (CAWS) using the Semantic Web and linked open data standards.

    Project Background

    In September 2009 Paul Morrell, The Government’s Chief Construction Advisor, presented a short paper to the Government Construction Clients board outlining a BIS-led Industry working group that would deliver a comprehensive report on the potential future use of Building Information Modelling (BIM), and its impact to the future of the construction industry.

    The final report delivered in March 2011 on Building Information Modelling and Management suggested a roadmap and strategy to enable the progressive use of BIM on Government building programmes as well as providing a framework for procurement and delivery standards.

    A full version of this report is available online at the BIS website.

    In this roadmap Uniclass and the Common Arrangement of Works Section were seen as progressing into a “new classification to assist lifecycle delivery”.

    This project aims to take Uniclass and CAWS and do just that, by creating Uniclass Online as a reusable, open, interroperable data resource.

Stuart Chalmers' Education

University of Aberdeen

Ph.D., Agents and Constraint Logic

1998 – 2002

Thesis Title: Agents and Constraint Logic

Abstract: Using Constraint Logic Programming and a data model approach, we provide an agent with a flexible way to plan and direct its actions and to manipulate and represent its knowledge. We use constraint solving techniques developed in the KRAFT project to provide the agent with a sophisticated reasoning and deliberation process. We explore the declarative use of constraints within a BDI Agent framework to represent knowledge as complex quantifed constraints and apply these techniques to a courier scenario where cooperating agents communicate, delegate and exchange desires and information using Generalised Partial Global Planning mechanisms to solve a given set of tasks.
