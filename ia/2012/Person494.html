Cara Health

September 2011 – Present (10 months) Greater Chicago Area & Trinity College Dublin

Cara Health delivers smart listening that facilitates early intervention. The system guides non clinical staff through adaptive outbound telephone conversations with frail elderly patients. During the conversation Cara Health accurately predicts pre-acute trends and generates alerts. This facilitates early intervention to reduce avoidable hospitilisations. The system does not require sensor, billing or Electronic Health Record data.

The predictions improve over time and follow the patient journey.

Researcher

National Digital Research Centre

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Investment Management industry

June 2010 – September 2011 (1 year 4 months)

The aim of PAJR is to reduce the number of avoidable hospital admissions for elderly patients and associated problems that occur with such.

The PaJR software is deployed within a Care Management Call Centre environment analysing the phone calls between the Care Guide/Clinical Nurse Specialist and the patient. The PaJR system can detect Linguistic, Non-Linguistic and Meta Linguistic features which are predictors of deterioration of the patients health and increased risk of an unplanned event such as a hospital admission.

The majority of Telemonitoring systems detect physiological pattern shifts and alert the medical team. In general these are too late to prevent the patient having to enter Secondary or Tertiary care. There is limited evidence to show that current Connected Health solutions reduce avoidable hospitilisations in order to be cost effective at their current price point.

PaJR is up to 10 times cheaper than current Connected Health + Care Management configurations.
