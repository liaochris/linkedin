Richard Catherall's Summary

Through my company and networks, including active joint ventures I have an international mission to energise and inspire people so that they can be better leaders, enlighten to build great enterprises and empower them to do good business which can deliver sustainable change.

These are three values which help design what I do and the way I disrupt the status quo, poking the ordinary whenever possible; energise, enlighten, empower.

Richard Catherall's Experience

Creative Executive Director

Katarsis Ventures

2011 – Present (1 year) United Kingdom & Cyprus

The 21st Century will be defined by a fresh approach to doing business, and doing good business together will achieve many positive things. The CEO of the global Manpower group said,

"The success of the turnaround depends on whether or not organisations have the right talent in place, and effective leadership is at the core of such decisions. Even first-mover advantages are erased with increasing speed as an organisation's products, services, technology and business models can be quickly replicated. The talent organisations employ has emerged as the single most important element of sustainable differentiation. Consequently a new era is upon us - the human age. It will be human potential that will be the catalyst for change and the global driving force behind economic, political and social developments. Talentism is the new Capitalism."

Couldn't put it better myself, so I didn't. Katarsis Ventures is a change company. We are interested in human difference as a source of creativity and strength. We believe in the boundary spanning language of business, one of mutual advantage. Enterprises can nurture the leadership skills so valuable in other aspects of life. We are inspired and motivated by the potential for sustainable growth. Katarsis Ventures has an international mission to energise, enlighten and empower people to become significantly better leaders, create great enterprises and do good business together.

Katarsis Ventures makes an impact in the areas of business modelling, growth and culture change strategy, entrepreneurial leadership, change leadership and management, joint-ventures and other types of collaboration. Katarsis Ventures is resourced to make an impact in the UK social business sector, Cyprus and ultimately the middle east through our joint ventures, networks and expert partners.

Senior Manager - Corporate Teams

Suffolk County Council

Government Agency; 10,001+ employees; Government Administration industry

2008 – 2012 (4 years)

Suffolk public services embarked on the biggest change to the design of and access to them in modern memory. The Council took a bold approach at first. Boldness is courageous and effective if the vision engages people in a way that enables them to move forward; to lead not just follow. Thinking the unthinkable and identifying universal changes proved a difficult vision to convey. People found it difficult to turn into practical proposals and different ways of working and accessing services; working together by necessity not just choice. In the context of budget cuts the vision was sometimes contradictory. The change in professional behaviour and public expectations in the UK and perhaps Europe will take much longer.

My outlook is creative, collaborative and growth orientated. I saw myself as an agent of improvement rather than Council employee. My networks and experience enabled me to offer a different way of solving problems, finding original enterprise opportunities. Where we could capture the imagination of professionals we designed change programmes based on learning by doing, including joint ventures. We led and influenced internal redesign, including new teams and spin out ventures. We engaged internal and external stakeholders in big opportunity issues to influence policy makers.

These achieved cashed savings of millions £s and new growth. We identified a million £ of dormant trusts, liberated them and invested in community priorities. We solved insolvency and competition problems in the supply chain. We applied this knowledge to develop a range of relationship and funding ideas to engage the potential of the social sector. Despite budget pressures and competing priorities this included the launch of a multi-million £ social investment fund. This contributed to the Council internally and externally becoming more of an enabler. People were energised, enlightened and empowered to change. I became more self-aware. I wanted more of this.
