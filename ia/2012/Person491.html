Emily Miggins' Summary

Miggins is a Sr. Sustainability Manager for Safeway Inc. and has been in the sustainability field for 20+ years. Previously a Sr. environmental scientist specializing in Corporate Zero Waste Strategies Miggins knows the ‘ins and outs’ intimately of reverse worldwide logistics with almost any material known to ecology, possessing skills to conduct intensive life-cycle audits while providing Environmental Performance Indicators, which translate neatly to organizational policy. Miggins has a long-term background in ecology this experience has made her a trusted advisor to companies, GO's and ENGOs. Miggins is "a top thought leader, among sustainability executives and sources of sustainable news according to @GuardianSustBiz"
Miggins creates solutions where there are none.

Earlier in Miggins’ career she worked for a variety of cutting edge nonprofit organizations and served on Board of Directors; including GrassRoots Recycling Network, Amigos de las Americas, Native Forest Network and executive committees for the Sierra Club. She was appointed to the Nonwoods Fibers Committee of the Technical Association of Pulp & Paper Industry from 1996-1999. Miggins was founder and former Executive Director of the award winning ReThink Paper of Earth Island Institute. In 1997 the Federal Environmental Executive, appointed Miggins to the White House Paper Task Force. In 1995 she was an NGO Delegate to the United Nations Women's Conference in Beijing, China. A cornerstone to the Wood-Use Reduction movement, in 1994 while working for Rain Forest Action Network she brought together a major conference of leading industry players & environmentalists to create opportunities to lessen pressures devouring ecosystems world wide. Miggins has lived and worked extensively in Costa Rica’s Dry Tropical Forest regions. Her undergraduate & graduate studies were accomplished at L.I.U./Brooklyn-Global College & United Nations Graduate Advanced Certificate Program.
