  • 10 LinkedIn Tips for Professional Services Business Development

    • Footnotes
    • March 2012
    Authors: ANNE PRYOR, M.A.

    10 LinkedIn Profile Tips for Business Development
    Over 70% of professionals services executives report that they use LinkedIn as their primary networking online tool.
    1) Get your profile to 100% - this ensures that your profile will come up higher in searches.
    2) Know your industry key words – create a list of key words that your clients use to find you when seeking to hire your services. Add these in the Specialties, Skills and Interests sections.
    3) Build a Company page – create a free company page on LinkedIn. Go to Company, Add Page. Add key words in Specialties.
    4) Get Recommendations – this is especially important for business development. (90% of my business comes from LinkedIn referrals.) Get them on your Profile and Company pages.
    5) Use the Applications – upload your company PPT presentation with Slideshare. It's located under the MORE tab.
    6) Start using Advanced Search.
    7) Leverage the Companies tab to find prospects – do you have a list of target companies. See People, Departures, New Hires, Skills, revenues, growth, and news.
    8) Invite the right Connections – connect with influencers and relevant contacts that you can help and that can help you.
    9) Join Groups – the action in happening in Groups. Type in the Group categories that you’re interested in, like CPA referrals or Franchisees. Join the Groups; watch the Discussions, then start a Discussion. This is where the business development is really happening.
    10) Start your marketing – using the Status Bar, ‘what are you working on’, sharing relevant tips that your connections would be interested in. My three strategies are: 1) Humble self-promotions, 2) Promotion of others and 3) information or education content. This information can be connected with your Twitter account, so keep the character count to 140 characters or less, about 15 words.
