Ben Piché's Summary

An internally motivated NLP engineer with a passion for computational linguistics and an inability to relax. Just as comfortable fifteen pages into a research paper as committing code to a weekly build. Experience in syntax/semantics, text analysis, named entity recognition, sentiment analysis, and parsing social media makes for a silver bullet on any problem with unstructured text. This stuff keeps me up at night.

Ben Piché's Experience

Computational Linguist

SAP Labs

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; SAP; Computer Software industry

May 2010 – June 2012 (2 years 2 months) Cambridge, Massachusetts

The SAP Labs Text Analysis team has its origins at PARC, which spun off Inxight and sold to Business Objects in 2007 (itself acquired by SAP AG months later). Services include text analysis, entity extraction, and sentiment analysis for the company's largest customers in 31 languages. I was with the team through two transitions, from the Data Services product in 2010 to the HANA program in 2012.

• Gathered, cleaned, and annotated gold corpora and training corpora for named entity recognition from a variety of online resources incl. blogs, Facebook, SMS
• Wrote scripts to improve remote build machine performance and local daily workflow
• Developed fast, industry-leading German sentiment analysis module for social media
• Used statistical finite-state parsers incl. part-of-speech taggers, stemmers, tokenizers
• Daily exposure to Agile development and software engineering best practices
• Led weekly Stanford Engineering Everywhere NLP/ML course viewings and discussions
