Glen Richardson's Summary

Glen Richardson is a British technology Entrepreneur with 14 years experience in the Internet industry particularly in social media, eCommerce and SaaS. Glen has founded various companies, some of which have received significant press attention. Amongst other achievements Glen was on the Jury panel for the Chancellor's 2011 Autumn Statement for the Telegraph.

Glen was born in Cumbria, orphaned at the age of 10 and grew-up in 13 different foster placements. This is the backdrop against Glen’s prolific career that drives him to encourage others regardless of their circumstance.

In addition to his entrepreneurial endeavours: Glen has served as Foster Care Panellist for Hampshire, Youth Worker at Emmanuel Community Church, Keynote Speaker for Furness College, Furness Enterprise and the University of Hertfordshire. Glen has also organised many charity events both Nationally and Internationally.

Glen lives near the Lake District with his Wife and Son and is a keen photographer, musician and dedicated Christian.

(Notable Linkedin recommendations from: Oxfam, HMV, The Hut Group, ByBox, Bluff Magazine, O2 and TNT Express)

Specialties

tech, start-up, investment, product delivery, management, sales, marketing, strategy, innovation

Chief Executive Office (CEO) and Co-Founder

SendSocial

March 2009 – June 2010 (1 year 4 months) London

In 2009/2010 I was the founding CEO of SendSocial.com which exploded onto the market seeing press attention from ITV, The Telegraph, The Sun, T3 Magazine and various others. During my time as CEO I managed the business from concept to reality by forming a Strategic Partnership with MyHermes and ByBox as well as managing a remote team of Entrepreneurs.

I was responsible for:

• Infrastructure, revenue and strategy
• Investment
• PR
• Product delivery
• Strategic partnerships

-- About SendSocial --

SendSocial was a very ambitious, before it's time, start-up that set out to disrupt the postal and delivery industries.

The vision was simple; do away with the postal address in favour for a social media ID. So instead of writing an address on a parcel or letter you simply wrote their twitter, Facebook name or e-mail address.

The first stage of the project we partnered with MyHermes to offer door-to-door parcel delivery using twitter costing £3.99. We then partnered with ByBox offering a box–to-box service - very innovative, in fact the Sun described it as like "being on The Crystal Maze and probably even more exciting than receiving the present itself".

Sadly, this is where the story ends. We decided to make the tough decision to close the doors in 2010 due to lack of consumer take-up.
