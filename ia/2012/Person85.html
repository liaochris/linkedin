Amy Dillman, MPA, SPHR's Summary

My professional intention, always, is to share my expert business, HR and communications experiences, ideas and skills with great people and terrific employers - and it wouldn't hurt to have a little fun in the process. I thrive on contributing to a productive and enjoyable work environment for employees and management, creating a strong business ethic and managing and contributing to a strategic mission in the process.

Personally, I love music, the beach, sports and high heels. I've never been accused of being a wallflower. I play a mean game of eurchre. I've walked beans, shucked corn and I know how to bait my own hook.

Feel free to contact: amyranae@gmail.com

Specialties

An HR generalist and business partner - see the mission, find where HR can contribute.
A strategic planner and analyst - explore the statistics, stake a claim for improvements.
A communicator, social media enthusiast and presenter - listen, learn and share the best.

President

I've volunteer. In return, I've gained unmeasurable HR competencies and built an amazing network of experts, colleagues and friends.

Resource and Policy Analyst

University of Illinois - Office of Planning & Budgeting

Educational Institution; 10,001+ employees; Higher Education industry

April 2010 – Present (2 years 3 months)

Good planning makes for great execution. The University Office for Planning and Budgeting (UOPB) supports university-wide decision-making at all three campuses. My niche is HR numbers (such as staffing, employee migration and turnover, pension liabilities, salary compensation studies), but gathering and presenting those numbers also means having a strong understanding of employment law, HR systems, organizational structures, hiring trends, management styles and business acumen. What do your staffing numbers say about you?

Administrator of Employment Services (Generalist, Recruiter and Trainer)

National Center for Supercomputing Applications

Educational Institution; 201-500 employees; Research industry

July 2004 – April 2010 (5 years 10 months)

As the HR leading generalist for a nationally-recognized (and government-contracts sponsored) research facility, I recruited tech heads and hired geniuses, strategized business personnel needs with managers and administrators, consulted with hiring managers and company leaders, and managed employee relations and HR processes. True to the Generalist role, I also developed and delivered onboarding and training programs, implemented recognition programs, created policy and procedure handbooks and performed and analyzed exit interviews. I tried to make all their lives (and the self-service benefits apps) a little easier to navigate. Fluent in interviewing, social media strategy, FMLA, EEOC, labor contracts, Civil Service systems, HRIS, FLSA, Word, Powerpoint, I-9 compliance, SCT Banner HR and data warehouses.

Human Resources Officer (HR Generalist and Trainer)

University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign

Educational Institution; 10,001+ employees; Higher Education industry

July 2001 – July 2004 (3 years 1 month)

The usual and not-so usual HR, I worked with the complexities of the University's State Civil Service system rules and regs, interviewing staff and consulting with management on promotion and succession planning. Foremost, during this time, I was one of few asked to serve as a trainer for the ERP implementation of SunGuardHR SCT Banner, a multi-year roll-out of new finance and HR systems.

Advisory Product Analyst

Allsup Inc.

Privately Held; 501-1000 employees; Insurance industry

1994 – 2001 (7 years)

A health care services company, located in Belleville, Illinois, this company offers social security disability, medicare, and insurance options services and consulting to individuals, organizations and companies. Client relations, client communications, process and workflow analysis and improvements and overall go-getter.

Communications Specialist

Wetterau Inc.

1992 – 1994 (2 years)

A Fortune 500 national food distribution company located in St. Louis, MO, Wetterau Inc. was a leader in food distribution and bakeries (acquired by SuperValu). Working at national headquarters in the corporate communications office, I was responsible for and assisted with production of annual reports, quarterly reports, shareholders' meetings, employee communications, news releases and press and shareholder relations. With amazing mentors, I was able to really get my hands on several aspects of the transportation and distribution industry.
