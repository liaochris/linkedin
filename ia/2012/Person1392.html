Peer Lawther's Summary

An executive summary:
1. You can contact me at peerlawther at gmail dot com
2. I've got a skilled knowledge of, and confidence in, the web that I've put to good use in getting various organisations' messages out into the wider world
3. I'm a strategic, tactical and operational social media thinker and doer who's worked across many diverse sectors

Let's go into detail:

I use and appreciate the rise of new web technologies to inspire, create, market and communicate, and how that fits into the world in the second decade of the 21st century.

Seeing the rise of the web from the inside, I've worked my way from an internet start-up during the first dotcom boom to head up the social media for a national charity.

I'm comfortable in formulating strategic positioning, employing tactical considerations of social media tools, or even just the operational concerns of day-to-day engagement with the online community and their conversations.

I'm making a name for myself as a strategic online marketing and social media consultant and advisor for businesses wanting to establish within the online arena. I enjoy helping them get results using networks, and trying to establish myself as a thought-leader on how the web is revolutionising the way we do business and present ourselves.

Specialties

On a theory level, an ethos of appreciating what marketing in the new media age means for organisations and how, where, when, and most importantly why we need to start the conversation.

On a practical level, successful online marketing on a small budget, vast knowledge of social media and its practical applications, knowledge of email legislation, online customer care, online promotion using evangelists, confidence in talking to an online audience of millions, SEO and PPC.

Senior Online Marketing Executive

NMSI

Nonprofit; 501-1000 employees; Museums and Institutions industry

December 2007 – July 2010 (2 years 8 months)

I oversaw the strategic, tactical and operational online marketing for three national (and globally-recognised) museums - the Science Museum in London, the National Railway Museum in York and the National Media Museum in Bradford.

Successes include:

- Strategic management of the National Media Museum's appearance on Flickr Commons, in the process becoming the first UK museum to do so, to great acclaim. More details: http://www.flickr.com/commons/institutions/

- The National Railway Museum winning 2 CIPR Awards for my Director of Fun idea, to install a six-year-old as a spokesperson, to attract younger visitors. More details: http://www.cipr.co.uk/pride-awards-2010/regions/yorkshire-lincolnshire-pride-awards-results-2010

- The Science Museum established Lates, a monthly late-night event for young urban professionals; around 1200 of a monthly audience of 3000 visited because of my tactical/operational work in social media and email marketing. My average marketing spend to attract this audience was less than £10 per month

- Managed every part of the museums' move into using professional systems and best practice in email marketing, building large email databases and successful email newsletters in the process. The resulting metrics (including conversion rates of 55%+) were regarded as the best in the sector and were widely compared to the best seen across any industry

- Built huge social media outreaches for all three museums from a standing start to industry-leading standards, on Twitter, Facebook, YouTube and Flickr, gaining over 100,000 followers and fans

- Managed many successful online advertising, SEO and PPC campaigns

- Presented/spoke at many industry conferences on the role of social media marketing in museums

- Produced a successful, overarching social media strategy for the three museums

- Me and my line report Marivanna Chessa performed minor miracles on very limited budgets
