Matthew Talbert’s Summary

Currently at Mathematica, I am conducting research on substance abuse counseling centers nationwide on behalf of the Federal Government. It is my responsibility to collect data on these substance abuse counseling centers and administer surveys to facility directors. The data collected is used to create a national directory for physician referral, as well as helping law makers develop policy and determine geographic areas in need of funding for prevention of drug and alcohol abuse.

I previously gained research and administrative experience while working for the City of Chicago. My work at the Department of Revenue in Chicago was a independent and multi-task oriented, with the main function being quantitative research. My research entailed the gathering of data on Chicago area business with outstanding debt, and determine whether the debt can be collected.

I also participated in an Innovative Revenue Project with the Chicago Budget Office. As part of this team, it was my role to discover benchmark Revenue sources being used by other cities, and use statistical algorithms to forecast Chicago's future budgets and expenditures.

I wish to continue to gain experience in field of public policy and gather data that will help lawmakers form the best policy for the social good.
