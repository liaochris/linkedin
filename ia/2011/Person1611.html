Alicia Hall's Summary

I make complex sites seem simple and dull content interesting. I think holistically and strategically. And I document the hell out of everything.

I'm a senior information architect and content strategist. I've worked with a range of big agencies and boutique firms, for clients like the American Medical Association, Allstate, USAA, the Chicago Board Options Exchange, Nestle Purina PetCare, Visa, Princeton University, Kohler, and others.

I'm well-versed in key related disciplines, such as prevailing web technologies, web writing and design best practices, branding concepts, and business strategy. Taken together, these skills make my voice valuable at every step of the process.

I'm a quick learner with an insatiable appetite for knowledge; I see every day as a new chance to learn and grow my skills. I have a reputation for being methodical, detail-oriented, patient, thorough, and hard-working. I don't care for political games or cutthroat egotism; I just want to do good work on good brands.

Alicia Hall's Education

Wheaton College

B.A., Ancient Languages

1998 – 2001

My degree laid an unlikely but solid foundation for a career in IA and content strategy. The first taxonomy I ever saw was of how all Indo-European languages relate to each other.

For my degree, I studied mostly Classical Greek and Latin with a sprinkling of linguistics and Classical Hebrew. Each dead language is taught as a system (with declensions, conjugations, morphemes, and all that). So studying them involves a lot of categorizing and differentiating between similar pieces of information. It's like that for all languages in certain ways, but there's a lot more analysis with the dead ones.

Lucky for me, those skills have translated (ahem) to a good career--since there isn't much call in the marketplace for folks who can conjugate irregular verbs.
