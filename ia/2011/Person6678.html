Gail Nickel-Kailing's Summary

Good food is everybody’s business. From the beginning of time, food has been the foundation of cultures throughout the world. How is it then that the goodness of food eludes us? The modern food industry has made food cheap and plentiful; disguising the true cost.

At GoodFood World, we address the food quality deficit by providing accurate information to consumers and to conscientious food practitioners involved with the production, processing, distributing, retailing, and serving of high quality natural and organic food products. By seeking out people who are at the grass roots – on the ground – we report on real issues and the people affected.

We do this by collecting and reporting the news about good food at the source; and by analyzing food systems to determine their merit on the basis of social responsibility, environmental resiliency, and economic vitality – our primary measures of sustainability.
