Bethany Goodrich's Summary

Having ~10 years of drug development industry experience, my career has progressed from research to regulatory affairs. I joined Rigel Pharmaceutical’s newly established pharmacology department and was quickly promoted to the first Vivarium Coordinator. This role included not only the operational management of the vivarium, but also the design and documentation of study protocols, establishing department SOPs. Over the years, I’ve developed numerous technical abilities including, but not limited to, in-vivo sample collection, various dosing routes of drug administration (IV, ID, IM, IP, SubQ), necropsy, and cannulation (femoral, jugular, carotid). I have aided in the growth of the pharmacology department by training and managing both intern and full time technical staff. Also, I have interacted with regulatory agencies such as DEA, USDA, IACUC, and California State ensuring compliance. I enjoy and respect the challenge of moving potential drug candidates forward in hopes for clinical success and future marketability. My interest in bringing valuable medical treatments to market has now led to the completion of the Clinical Research Conduct and Management program at UC Berkeley and the pursuit of a Master of Science in Regulatory Affairs (est graduation Q1 2012).

Specialties

data analysis, documentation, GCP, ICH, CFR, inventory management, materials management, protocol management and execution, research, safety, regulatory affairs, pharmacology
