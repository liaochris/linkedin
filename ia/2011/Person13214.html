Ernest Watson Hutton Jr.'s Summary

Ernest Hutton, founder and principal of Hutton Associates Inc., trained as an architect and city planner at Princeton and the University of Pennsylvania and has over thirty-five years' experience in large-scale development projects in both urban and developing areas throughout the US.

Mr. Hutton received the 2011 Associate Award from the national American Institute of Architects. He is a Fellow of AICP (the professional arm of the American Planning Association, of which he is a Charter Member), an Associate of the AIA (for which he has participated on three ‘Regional Urban Design Assistance Teams'--R/UDATs). He is a Fellow of the Institute for Urban Design and a resource participant of the Mayors’ Institute on City Design.

A member of the 2007-2010 AIANY Board, he is co-chair of the its Planning and Urban Design Committee and Co-Chair of New York New Visions, a 350-member volunteer group he helped organize following 9/11.

Specialties

Planning, Urban Design, Development Strategies, Cultural Planning, Community and Stakeholder Outreach
