Ran Golan's Summary

new enterprise base in ISRAEL, MarCell - Marine BioTechnology.

The major target of MarCell is to develop ubiquitous technologies for the establishment of continuously proliferating cell cultures from marine invertebrates that produce economically important bioactive compounds, enabling the use of high throughput production of these novel potent materials for the development of new drugs, cosmetics, agrochemicals and other products for variety of commercial purposes.
At this stage, MarCell main goal is to address the lack of sufficient supply for the development of new drugs from marine organisms, some of which indulgently wait on shelves for supply solution. Activities for the production of permanent cell cultures (cell factory) from these organisms are based on knowledge gathered for more than 15 years by our chief scientist (backed by scientific publications in peer-review journals).

Research results clearly attest for the development of unique expertise in this scientific discipline and the development of technologies for primary cultures from diverse marine invertebrates (sponges, corals, sea-squirts).
MarCell is therefore incorporated for the purpose to convert the knowledge (published and unpublished) into the applied route and is seeking the right amount of fund that will allow the development of novel patentable technology for cell cultures from marine organisms.

The market for novel bioactive compounds (new pharmaceuticals, agrochemicals, antifoulants, fine chemicals, cosmetics, etc) from marine invertebrates is evaluated at tens billion US$ per year.
