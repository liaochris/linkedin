Johan Andersson’s Summary

Johan is a senior software architect in the Frostbite Engine team at DICE. For the past 10 years he has been working with rendering, performance, and core engine systems for games in the Battlefield & Rallisport series and DICE's proprietary Frostbite engine. He drives the rendering and performance side of Frostbite on multiple platforms (PS3, Xbox 360, DX11) for future game titles across DICE and EA.

Johan is a member of multiple industry & hardware advisory boards and has frequently presented at GDC, Siggraph and graphics hardware conferences on topics such as parallelism, procedural shading, terrain rendering, game engine design and future CPU & GPU software & hardware architectures.

Worked on the following games:

- RalliSport Challenge 1 (Xbox)
- Battlefield 1942 (PC)
- RalliSport Challenge 2 (Xbox)
- Battlefield 2 (PC)
- Battlefield: Bad Company (Xbox 360 & PlayStation 3)

And since 2005 and forward worked on building the Frostbite engine.

Public talks:

- 2006 SIGRAD keynote. "Frostbite Rendering Architecture"
- 2007 GDC talk. "Frostbite Rendering Architecture and Real-time Procedural Shading & Texturing Techniques"
- 2007 SIGGRAPH course. "Terrain Rendering in Frostbite using Procedural Shader Splatting"
- 2008 Graphics Hardware 2008 keynote. "The Intersection of Game Engines & GPUs: Current & Future"
- 2009 GDC talk. "Shadows & Decals - D3D10 Techniques in Frostbite"
- 2009 GDC talk. "Your Game Needs Direct3D 11, So Get Started Now!"
- 2009 SIGGRAPH course. "Parallel Graphics in Frostbite - Current & Future". Beyond Programmable Shading course.
- 2009 Intel Dynamic Execution Environment Symposium keynote. "Parallel Futures of A Game Engine".
- 2010 Stockholm Game Developer Forum "Parallel Futures of A Game Engine (v2.0)".
- 2010 SIGGRAPH course. "5 Major Challenges in Interactive Rendering". Beyond Programmable Shading course.
- 2010 SIGGRAPH course. "Bending the Graphics Pipeline". Beyond Programmable Shading course.
