John Lund’s Summary

As SVP, Supply Chain Management, Dr. Lund is responsible for optimizing the performance of supply chains across Disney’s Parks and Resorts, an $11 billon business.

Lund began his career with Disney in 1992 as a manager for Euro Disney. His responsibilities continually expanded, and by 1995 he was named director of telecom, transportation, & multimedia, creating their first online services in that year. From 1996-98, he led a project that reduced back-of-house costs by over 20%. From 1998-2000, he led a centralized organization for planning the company's 8000 operational employees. He negotiated an innovative, government-certified on-the-job training program with the French government and unions. In Jan. 2001, he was named VP/Chief of Staff, with responsibility for productivity; revenue management, and the chairman's office. In 2002, he also assumed leadership of marketing & sales and strategic planning.

In 2003, Dr. Lund moved to California to serve as VP, Process Improvement at Walt Disney Imagineering. In 2004, he was promoted to SVP, Strategic Asset Management, providing global oversight and strategic direction for all aspects of Walt Disney Parks and Resorts’ physical assets, including sustainment and safety strategy and processes.

Prior to joining Disney, Dr. Lund worked at RAND, an internationally renowned non-profit research center for public policy. He advised the US government on national security strategy, political-economic affairs, military planning, and logistics. While at RAND, he authored or co-authored numerous books and reports.

Lund holds a doctorate in policy analysis from the RAND Graduate School, a MA in international affairs from Columbia University, and a BA in international relations and economics from St. Joseph’s University.

Dr. Lund has served on the baords of the International Association of Amusement Parks and Attractions, and the Advisory Board of the College of Arts & Sciences of St. Joseph’s University.

John Lund’s Specialties:

Strategic planning and program execution; change management; supply chain; international affairs and economics. Cognitive neuroscience, visual cognition, and business effectiveness.
