Elizabeth Johansen's Summary

I have a great passion for creating partnerships that can accelerate society toward a more sustainable world. In search of opportunities for improving the world around me, I have used my engineering and design background to create innovations in medical technology that improve human health, enabled the world's leading edge design firm, IDEO, to put design muscle toward making a positive social impact, and fostered a creative collaborative atmosphere for many groups who, in turn, generated joyful design solutions across many domains.

Specialties

Design thinking, mechanical engineering, facilitating productive and fulfilling team dynamics.

Elizabeth Johansen's Experience

Project Lead & Engineering Designer

IDEO

Privately Held; Design industry

July 2001 – January 2010 (8 years 7 months)

For eight years, Elizabeth Johansen worked first as a design engineer and then project lead based in IDEO's Boston office. Her experience spans client strategy to design for manufacturing. Past clients include BriteSmile, Reynolds, Becton Dickinson, Alcon, 3Com to several start-up companies. Her two most recent product launches are the Eli Lilly Kwikpen insulin injector and the Target Itso storage system.

Elizabeth also has a passion for design for positive social impact. Her work includes mentoring Design that Matters (a non-profit product design consultancy focusing on developing countries), facilitating several IDEO workshops for the MIT/Olin/CalTech International Development Design Summit and assisting in the MIT ELIAS conference linking businesses to NGOs for sustainable innovation.

Prior to IDEO, Elizabeth worked with Beckman Laser Institute and then Oregon Medical Laser Center to design and build two patented laboratory tools used in testing lasers for groundbreaking biomedical applications.
