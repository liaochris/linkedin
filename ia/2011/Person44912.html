Christopher Vetter's Summary

"Christopher Vetter is an innovative executive and entrepreneur," said Portland Mayor Sam Adams. "He is an asset to our city and enjoys my enthusiastic support." Vetter is an accomplished business professional with 18 years of experience in corporate development, public relations, publishing and politics. "He serves his clients with distinction," said former Oregon Governor Vic Atiyeh. "Christopher is creative, sincere and resourceful." In 1996 he founded the Vetter Group, a dynamic communications company offering Web development, marketing, copywriting, graphic design and technology consulting.

In 2007, Vetter shaped national marketing trends in a celebrated research study for Turner Media that persuaded Time Warner to invest millions in online advertising. "Christopher did a fantastic job for me on a project he worked on to help us prove the value of advertising on CNN.com,” said Keith Berkelhamer, Vice President of CNN Digital Research. “He was extremely organized, very reliable and paid attention to detail.”

In 2003, Vetter launched Inside Portland, a magazine exploring the Pacific Northwest. The Portland Tribune called Inside Portland "a lively new Internet newspaper." Portland Business Journal Editor Rob Smith called the publication "an influential must-read among fellow journalists and corporate executives." Vetter recently promoted The Peyton-Allan Files by Phil Stanford to the top of the regional bestseller list. Pulitzer Prize nominee Renee Mitchell branded Vetter a "brilliant communications professional who gets outstanding results."

Since 1992, Vetter managed more than a dozen political races and presented at over 30 conferences. "His work for the Clinton administration confirmed his prowess as a political professional," said former White House Press Lead Kelliann Amico. "His support for local schools, higher education, abuse prevention and environmental protection make him an exceptional citizen," said Oregon Labor Commissioner Brad Avakian.
