J.M. Woody van Horn, CS’s Summary

J.M. Woody van Horn grew up in the coastal community of Encinitas, California. His passion for hospitality began by spending time with his father, a yacht captain, seafaring up and down the coast of California and Baja, providing unforgettable and tailored service onboard luxury yachts. In 1995 he was selected as a Student Ambassador, representing the United States, fostering relationships with foreign diplomats while experiencing the culture of Australia and New Zealand. In 1996 he entered the hospitality industry, initially in the kitchen, working his way up at a local Italian restaurant.

Woody’s professional experience includes time spent working under chefs Bradley Ogden, Carl Schroeder, Dan Barber as well as Wine Directors Jesse Rodriguez, Brian Donegan and Ted Glennon. In 2003 Woody enlisted in the United States Navy serving a four-year tour of duty around the world as an Operations Specialist with a focus in Anti Submarine Warfare and Ballistic Missile Defense. This provided him with the opportunity to sample the diverse cuisines of Russia, Japan, Guam, Saipan, Mexico and British Columbia while refining his disciplined and professional work ethic. In 2006 Woody began his wine studies under the Court of Master Sommeliers, currently accredited as a Certified Sommelier and now a candidate for the Advanced Sommelier Certification. Woody has also participated in the 2009 Napa Valley Harvest and as a member of the James Beard Foundation, volunteering time at the James Beard House and Foundation Awards. In 2009 Woody was selected by the Confrérie de la Chaîne des Rôtisseurs and inducted as Maitre de Table Hotelier as well as the Société Mondiale du Vin as Professional du Vin.

These experiences led Woody to attend the Culinary Institute of America in Hyde Park, New York, where he was elected as group leader and since spring 2010, has held a seat as Justice on the school’s Judiciary Board.

J.M. Woody van Horn, CS’s Specialties:

Following the completion of his degree in Occupational Studies at The Culinary Institute of America, Hyde Park campus, Woody intends to relocate to the Napa Valley with his wife, Amanda, to complete the Accelerated Wine and Beverage Certificate Program (AWBP) at the Culinary Institute Of America, Greystone campus, furthering his experience and accreditations in food, beverage and hospitality
