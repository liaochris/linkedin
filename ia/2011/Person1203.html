Alberto Roca’s Summary

I publish the premier web portal on the minority postdoctoral experience. This resource on career development, job opportunities, fellowships, and diversity issues serves as a unique channel for reaching this targeted audience. Furthermore, my experience and network positions my consulting services for proactive recruiting of minority Ph.D. talent particularly in the STEM disciplines.

My scientific research involved determining the molecular mechanism by which recombination proteins interact with DNA. My approaches used the techniques of structural biology, biochemistry, biophysics, and bioinformatics.

    Directs strategic planning, development, and implementation of recruitment and graduate student preparation and retention programs at UC-Irvine. Also, Director of the NSF-funded Alliance for Graduate Education and the Professoriate (AGEP) program.

  • Assistant Project Scientist

    University of California, Irvine

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    September 2003 — December 2007 (4 years 4 months)

    RESEARCH: Structural analysis of recombination DNA repair proteins.
    Solved the crystal structure of the RecA protein showing a novel conformation of the carboxy terminus.
    Implementing open source crystallography software such as PyMOL, Coot, and the CCP4 suite.
    Visualized the RecA-DNA complex using Atomic Force Microscopy.

    RESEARCH: Bioinformatic analysis of recombination DNA repair proteins.
    Developed a unique Java program (JProfileGrid) for visualizing large multiple sequence alignments.
    Generated and curated the largest alignment of RecA bacterial homologs to date.
    Established a MySQL database of over 1400 RecA mutants for comparative analysis with alignments.
    Implementing open source bioinformatics software such as Clustal, muscle, and the EMBOSS suite.

    RESEARCH: Pre-steady-state kinetic analysis of recombinational DNA repair initiation
    Probing molecular interactions during RecA-DNA complex formation using stopped-flow spectrofluorometry of nonnatural DNA bases and fluorescent mutant proteins.
    Programming in DynaFit script language for global fitting analysis.
