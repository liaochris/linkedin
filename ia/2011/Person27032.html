Lucia Pomales’s Summary

Lucia began her career as a TV producer at the Washington DC bureau of MSNBC in 2000. Her training in the trenches continued at the cable news network's headquarters in Secaucus, NJ in 2002. Lucia wrote and segment produced for MSNBC Newsforce, NBC's Early Today, MSNBC's First Look and Weekend Dayside.

Upon her return to Orlando in 2006, Lucia joined the NBC team once again as a producer on the network's most ambitious interactive Television project to date: NBC's iVillage Live.

When the show relocated to Chicago, Lucia turned down NBC's generous offer to transfer in order to stay close to home near family and accepted a Show Producer position with PBS.
At WMFE Lucia developed, produced, wrote, managed and ran an award-winning weekly half-hour in-depth news program entitled "This Week."

Then Los Angeles called and Lucia answered. She travelled to tinsel town to Show Produce E! Entertainment Television's "Live From The Red Carpet" programs during the 2008 season.

Still, family remained a priority for Lucia so she returned home to Orlando and joined the traveling freelance world through her company Luna Media LLC.

Clients include Sesame Workshop, Univision, Telemundo, Argo Digital, Plain English Coaching & Consulting, Merisini Media, Buzzie Productions, Toc Marketing & Entertainment, and more.

Today Lucia continues to seek freelance work nationally and locally, while pursuing a full-time position with a company that will utilize her creative skills and experience in media and production.
