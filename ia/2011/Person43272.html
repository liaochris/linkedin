Tierra Ryan, PharmD's Summary

Tierra Ryan, Pharm.D, RPh is a highly motivated Post-Doctoral Fellow completing her second year of a two-year fellowship at Hoffmann-La Roche Inc via Rutgers Institute for Pharmaceutical Industry Fellowships. Her core fellowship is in the Regulatory Affairs functional area. In this role, she supported regulatory strategic decisions for several therapeutic areas. Tierra is currently on rotation in the Clinical Science - Virology area. In addition to her pharmaceutical industry experience, Tierra also has experience in hospital and retail pharmacy.

Tierra Ryan completed her Doctorate of Pharmacy degree at Temple University School of Pharmacy. Outside of her academic course work, Tierra was involved in several pharmacy organizations: American Pharmacist Association, American Society of Health-System Pharmacist, and Student National Pharmacist Association.

After completing the demanding fellowship program at Hoffmann-La Roche Inc - RPIF, Tierra is seeking a challenging position in the pharmaceutical industry that will allow her demonstrate the skills she developed during her fellowship program.

Tierra’s passion is to bridge the gap to success. She firmly believes that you can excel at anything you want, just as long as you stay focused on your goal.

Regulatory Affairs Post-Doctoral Fellow

Roche Pharmaceuticals

Public Company; rhhby; Pharmaceuticals industry

July 2009 – September 2010 (1 year 3 months)

Drug development within the pharmaceutical industry is
complex and requires compliance with U.S. and non-U.S.
government regulations and Health Authorities.
Pharmaceutical companies are required to adhere to
strict guidelines regarding preclinical and clinical drug
development, product manufacturing, labeling,
advertising and marketing.
Regulatory Affairs supports the interpretation of
worldwide Health Authority requirements (including
the Food and Drug Administration-FDA) and provides
the regulatory intelligence necessary to generate and
present information to meet the needs of Health
Authorities, patients, purchasers and prescribers. The
Regulatory Affairs department at Roche provides the
regulatory strategy required to optimally develop, license
and market Roche products globally.

The Regulatory Fellow acquires a better understanding of
the drug development process and the regulatory affairs
role within a pharmaceutical company through direct
experience and/or exposure to:
• Investigational New Drug Applications
• New Drug Applications
• Development products
• Marketed products activities
• Chemistry, manufacturing and controls
• Clinical documentation
• Labeling
• Regulatory records and information
• Regulatory intelligence
• Health Authority interactions
The Regulatory Fellow receives individual guidance from
Regulatory project managers and interacts with global
project teams and Health Authorities as appropriate.
The Regulatory Fellow will obtain an understanding of
the role of a Regulatory Affairs department in the drug
development process and current regulatory guidelines
from a global perspective.
