Patrick Seaman’s Summary

Patrick is a technology and media-savvy pioneer with deep experience managing and developing products and connecting them with their audience and marketplace. With 27 years of U.S. and international experience in online/digital media, entertainment and technology, he pioneered the intersection of technology media and communications.

In 1995 he helped launch the Internet media revolution as employee # 1 and Director of Technology at AudioNet / broadcast.com and wrote the first major book on Internet media. Since then he has designed and marketed B2B and B2C products and ventures in new media, social networking & social media, edutainment, live interactive, video on demand, digital interactive, and all-media storytelling.

At AudioNet / Broadcast.com (merged with Yahoo! in '99), Patrick built and managed what effectively became the first Content Delivery Network, negotiated issues with China for Intel and then produced and broadcast the first live webcasts from inside China. Patrick negotiated the BBC's first master Internet contract and produced the then largest-ever webcasts with audiences exceeding 1m (Victoria's Secret/Super Bowl, etc.)

Patrick has launched groundbreaking new media, broadband and iTV projects, including launching the first audiobook on the web, the first on MP3 CD, and later the first nature-wildlife broadband live video with animal mounted wireless cameras.

Most recently, Patrick helped launch whichboxmedia.com, the first fully integrated cloud-based online content publishing platform that combines next generation content creation and management, online publishing, social networking, and social media technology. WhichBox Media is a winner of the 2010 “OnMedia 100” Award for the top 100 most disruptive companies in online media.

Patrick consults on digital media strategy, product development and marketing services for products, media productions, New Media, content management, and advisory services for startups and emerging companies.

  • Chief Technologist

    Deer Channel (World Wildlife Network)

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    June 2006 — March 2008 (1 year 10 months)

    Deer Channel began as a wildlife research effort founded by Tom Brooks, inventor of the patented Deer Cam. A leading authority on Deer behavior, Tom wanted to go beyond old-style radio collars and the generational folk-knowledge of Deer habits, diet and behavior. Tom wanted to see and hear what the deer saw and heard — in real time — without physically intruding on their protected research habitat.

    Enter: The Deer Cam. A patented wireless camera and microphone system, designed for extended operation in a controlled, protected habitat. This was a 1st in the world system — it had never been done before. The operator can view, record and broadcast the live feed — putting the viewer in the deer’s perspective, interacting with the deer’s environment, other deer and wildlife.

    The project involved the wireless camera systems and infrastructure, design and rapid evolution of the broadcast center and broadcast operations and iteration of the website CMS.

    This was an exciting low power wireless video and Internet broadcast project that integrated lots of technologies and was in the "never done before" realm. Engineered wireless A/V cameras that we physically mounted on wildlife for extended behavioral observation on a protected wildlife habitat. Responsible for the overall technology direction of the company.

    This cross-discipline project involved designing and building a remote wildlife research center, with a fiber loop around the wildlife habitat, a series of tower mounted digital PTZ cameras, low power wireless camera mounted on the animals, server room, remote studio, editing suite, as well as high speed data connections to the remote lab area.

    The web component included the entire live and on-demand webcasting physical infrastructure, encoders, studio integration, integration with Akamai, e-commerce, content management system, pay-per-view, and subscription model, and a network of over 50 web portals and ad network.
