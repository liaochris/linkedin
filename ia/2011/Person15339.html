Gabe Petlin’s Summary

11+ year track record in leading regulatory policy, government affairs, and business development for renewable energy and environmental commodity trading and marketing organizations. Recognized national expert on renewable energy certificates (RECs). Founder and President of Renewable Energy Markets Association (REMA), a D.C.-based trade association promoting renewable energy market policies. Trained mediator with demonstrated success in building coalitions with diverse stakeholders to support renewable and climate initiatives, developing long-term strategic partnerships, and collaborating with internal, government, and C-level stakeholders.

Gabe Petlin’s Specialties:

Environmental commodity markets and regulatory policy, renewable energy certificates (RECs), carbon offets, REC and Carbon project due diligence, forestry carbon credits and forestry management, mediation and facilitation.

  • Director Regulatory Affairs and Carbon Markets

    3Degrees Group, Inc.

    (Privately Held; Renewables & Environment industry)

    January 2005 — June 2010 (5 years 6 months)

    Directed regulatory strategy for a leading REC and carbon offset trading and marketing company ranked #1 U.S. Renewable Energy Credit Dealer (Energy Risk Commodity, 2008), Best Trading Company in Greenhouse Gas Voluntary Markets (Environmental Finance, 2008), and recipient of U.S. DOE Renewable Energy Marketer of the Year award (2007, 2008, 2009). Part of management team. Key accomplishments:
    • Developed long-term strategic renewable energy marketing partnerships with large IOU, AmerenUE and Dominion.
    • Monitored and analyzed state, federal, and regional renewable portfolio standard and carbon pricing policies to identify company opportunities and mitigate risk in coordination with Origination and Trading.
    • Prepared regulatory and legislative positions including providing testimony, comments, briefs, and proposed orders at utility commissions, state legislatures, agencies, and Congress.
    • Directed a consulting engagement to develop commercial sector marketing collateral for the launch of PG&E’s groundbreaking ClimateSmartTM carbon offset marketing program.
    • Founder and President of Renewable Energy Markets Association (REMA), a D.C.-based trade association promoting renewable energy market policies.
    • Represented the company at industry trade associations, regulatory workshops and rulemakings, and collaborative organizations such as CA AB 32, Western Climate Initiative, Federal Energy and Climate Legislation, Green-e Energy and Climate, U.S. EPA Green Power Partnership, U.S. EPA Climate Leaders, The Climate Registry, and Climate Action Reserve.
    • Developed and oversaw execution of thought leadership activities, including webinars, white papers, and internal trainings, to educate clients and staff on environmental commodity markets.
    • Oversaw the GHG emission reduction quantification and documentation process for registration of carbon credits with the Gold Standard for Honduran rural efficient cook stove project.
    • Founding member of 3Degrees

  • Green-e Program Manager

    Center for Resource Solutions

    (Non-Profit; Renewables & Environment industry)

    2001 — 2004 (3 years )

    Managing a national certification and branding program for renewable energy products, Green-e, the preeminent third party certifier of voluntary RECs and green power products in the U.S. Key accomplishments:
    • Conducted a nation expert stakeholder consultation to complete a product standard and launched the first third party certification program for the renewable energy certificate market.
    • Hired, trained, and managed a national team of outreach, policy, and verification staff to implement the Green-e program.
    • Doubled the number of certified providers, resulting in a 250% increase in certified products and 200% increase in certified renewable energy supply.
    • Designed and implemented a volumetric fee structure that increased revenue 200%.

    RENEWABLE ENERGY CONSULTING PROJECTS AT CRS

    • Designed and implemented benchmark study and analysis of green marketing programs, and designed recommendations for the NJ Board of Public Utilities for development of the Clean Energy Program state-wide utility green power marketing program.
    • Recommended best practice designs for the CT Department of Public Utility Control Clean Options Program.
    • Co- author, Guide to Purchasing Green Power, CRS, U.S. DOE/EPA, and World Resources Institute (2004).

Designed and facilitated NWCC plenary meetings, forums, and workgroups on transmission, air emission trading, green power marketing, avian-wind power interaction, siting and permitting, and economic development.

    Wind Energy Credit Trading, NWCC, RESOLVE
    Founded and led Credit Trading Workgroup, directing the first collaborative investigation of the concept of trading the environmental attributes of renewable energy separately from the underlying energy (later known as RECs)). Designed and facilitated workshops and white papers that resulted in the first major paper on RECs and a set of market design best practice principles.
