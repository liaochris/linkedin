Deborah Dong’s Summary

With over twenty years experience in business, law, and technology, including over eight years of “large firm” experience, I opened my own legal practice in 2007.

I work primarily with high technology and emerging growth companies, concentrating on software licensing and technology transfer transactions and assisting clients in protecting, developing, acquiring and commercializing their intellectual property. This includes structuring, negotiating and drafting contracts such as distribution/channel, OEM, development, strategic alliance/joint venture, open source, SaaS, and end-user licenses. My clients' businesses have included software, hardware, telecomm, Internet, multimedia, data security, IT services, bioinformatics, semiconductor, and energy/cleantech.

For institutional clients such as banks and investment funds, I advise on in-licensing matters (obtaining services and licenses for technology). For investors/acquirers, I perform IP due diligence on companies seeking funding and on potential targets.

I also serve as outside general counsel for businesses of all types – managing day-to-day legal matters for companies without in-house lawyers, being their first point of contact for all legal matters, managing outside counsel, and handling contracts of all kinds such as partnership/LLC agreements, leases, and employment/consulting arrangements.

While I have represented companies of all sizes up to multinational, industry-leading public companies, I particularly enjoy representing privately-owned small- to medium-sized businesses, especially in negotiations with larger companies.

For executives and other individuals/families, I design and prepare estate plans with an emphasis on tax minimization and asset protection.

I am admitted to the Massachusetts and New York State Bars and the United States Tax Court.
