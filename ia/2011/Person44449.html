Tim Turner, Ed.D.’s Summary

Dr. Turner retired as a SSA after serving 22 years with the FBI. Dr. Turner was an Adjunct Professor for the Univ of Virginia and instructed at the FBI NA and the UV Northern Center. Dr. Turner worked with the LELI at the FLETC were he provided instruction to supervisors from 83 Federal Agencies as well as to the International Law Enforcement Academies. Dr. Turner currently works as a Leadership and EI Consultant for MHS an organization specializing in psychometric assessment.
Dr. Turner was assigned to the Mobile, New York and St. Louis Divisions were he was responsible for the oversight of a variety of training and operational programs including crises coordinator, hostage negotiator, aviation coordinator as well as serving as a primary relief supervisor. Dr. Turner has been a Training Director, Security Director, Investigative specialist and Curriculum Developer.
He was a presenter at the 2008 ICEI and provided a key note address at the IAWP Annual Training Conference.
Prior to his assignment at FBI Headquarters, Dr. Turner was the original case agent for Major Case 98, the primary terrorism case in the United States at the time. Dr. Turner was the administrative case agent in the first use of the federal hate crime statue in the United States. Dr. Turner was operationally involved with the John Gotti and Robert Hanssen cases, Talladega Prison Riots, Los Angles Riots and was a negotiator/pilot for the Waco Standoff.
Dr. Turner earned a BS degree in Education from Baylor Univ; an M. Ed. Degree’s from Miss State Univ in Administration and the Univ of VA in Social Foundations/Psychology. He also attended the Univ of Nebraska, School of Law. Dr. Turner completed his Ed.D. in Leadership from the Univ of VA. His research efforts have focused on the use of emotional intelligence for succession planning.
