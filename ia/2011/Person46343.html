Yngve Löfstrand's Summary

After my M.Sc. in Applied Physics and Electrical Engineering at Linköpings Tekniska Högskola 1992 I worked about four years at the Swedish Defence Research Agency. I gathered results from field trials, put together physical models, performed simulations and performed evaluations of military technology and equipment.

Since then I have worked as a C/C++ SW developer in the telecom industry for 15 years, both as an employee and as a consultant.

At Ericsson in Kista I implemented the enhanced full rate channel encoder/decoder algorithms for the D-AMPS base station transceiver TRX 884. It was assembler and C programming of the Texas Instrument DSP TMS320C50 and the software was running on Enea OSE.

At the Ericsson factory in Gävle I developed functional and performance delivery tests for PDC and WCDMA mobile base stations. I was implementing the test software in C++ and the tests, using instruments such as network and spectrum analyzers, were running on Windows NT. I was also responsible of a test software at a quality assurance test with DoCoMo.

At ST-Ericsson in Lund I developed a security library and flash loaders written in C. I also worked in customer projects with trouble reports and adaptations of the mobile platform. In this work I often used tools such as Lauterbach. The software was running on ARM processors.

At Sony Ericsson in Kista I have worked with fixing trouble reports regarding the ST-Ericsson mobile platform and with the CAIF module. CAIF is handling the protocol between the Communication CPU and the Application CPU and is written in C++ for the Symbian OS. I also implemented Symbian software for the firmware over-the-air protocol. The platform work was analysis of crash dumps and logs and also C programming of the ARM processors.

Specialties

Programming in C/C++, embedded systems, design, simulation, implementation, verification and test.
