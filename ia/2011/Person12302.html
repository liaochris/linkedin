Don Metzler's Summary

I am interested in theoretical and practical information retrieval problems, as well as machine learning applied to large scale textual applications.

My research has focused on retrieval models, query/document representations, term weighting, term proximity models, and learning to rank (machine learned ranking functions). I am always interested in expanding the breadth and the depth of my research into other related areas or fields of study.

I am also passionate about seeing my research applied to real world problems, especially those dealing with large, complex data sets. Along these lines, I have experience evaluating and designing novel search algorithms for news search, web search, summarization, and online advertising systems.

My research has been published at all of the major information retrieval and web search venues, including SIGIR, WWW, CIKM, TREC, ECIR, and Information Retrieval, I've also co-authored a book with W. Bruce Croft (UMass) and Trevor Strohman (Google) titled "Search Engines: Information Retrieval in Practice", which is now available.

I am also a very active member of the research community, having recently served on the PCs for SIGIR, WWW, WSDM, EMNLP, and EACL.

Specialties

Information retrieval, web search, computational advertising, relevance, ranking, machine learning, natural language processing

Research Scientist

Yahoo Research

Public Company; YAHOO; Internet industry

September 2007 – April 2010 (2 years 8 months)

As a member of the Search and Computational Advertising group at Yahoo! Research I worked closely with various product teams on a wide variety of real-world problems involving large-scale data sets.

My most significant accomplishment was the design and implementation of highly effective text matching features for Web search. The features were deployed in various markets worldwide and yielded significant relevance gains, especially for long queries.

Other notable projects included temporal query analysis, predicting when not to show ads, sponsored search ad matching for tail queries, enriched Web page representations, and ranking in match-making (e.g., dating) systems.

Research Assistant

University of Massachusetts Amherst

Educational Institution; Higher Education industry

September 2002 – August 2007 (5 years)

I worked on a variety of projects during my time at the CIIR, including:

Markov Random Field Model -- I developed a highly effective, formally motivated retrieval model for information retrieval based on Markov Random Fields. The model can not only model dependencies between terms, but also incorporate arbitrary features in a unified retrieval framework.

Indri -- Indri is an effective, efficient, flexible open source information retrieval toolkit developed primarily by Trevor Strohman. I helped design the Indri query language and underlying retrieval model.

TREC -- I took part in the 2004, 2005, and 2006 Terabyte Tracks (with Trevor Strohman) and the 2005 Robust Track (with Fernando Diaz). Our systems were either first or second best in terms of retrieval effectiveness in all of the tasks that we submitted runs to.

During my early years at the CIIR, I also worked on projects dealing with text reuse, image retrieval, and question classification.

Research Intern
