Marc Menninger's Summary

Marc’s career in information technology and data security started in 1992 when he began five years of service as a Computer and Communications Officer in the United States Air Force. Marc worked at Langley Air Force Base in Virginia and Barksdale Air Force Base in Shreveport, LA. He also served a short tour in the Persian Gulf during Operation Desert Shield.

After leaving the Air Force as a Captain, Marc entered the civilian world filling a variety of technical roles including help desk supervisor, network administrator, and network and information security consultant. Marc’s jobs lead him progressively west and north across the U.S., from Dallas to San Francisco and finally to Seattle.

Marc was a Vice President in the Corporate Information Security department of Washington Mutual Bank in Seattle. He is currently the Security Manager at PEMCO.

Marc has been a speaker at numerous technical conferences and seminars, has written several Cisco white papers and contributed to a published study guide for Cisco certification. He received his Bachelor's degree in Mathematics from the University of Tampa in 1991 and has been a Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP) since 2000.

Specialties

Security Policy Writing, Enterprise Risk Assessment, Data Security Program Management, Online Marketing
