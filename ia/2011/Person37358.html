Robert Servranckx, PMP's Summary

I am a PMP-certified customer-facing Technical Project Manager, working on medium to large scale e-commerce solutions. I am involved in all aspects of project management through a project's life cycle, from initial scoping, planning and analysis, through design and development, QA, and to the final delivery of the project to the customer.

I have very strong organizational, planning, problem solving and analytical skills, an innate ability to foresee potential risks before they become issues, excellent communication skills, all of which ensure successful delivery of high-quality projects on time and on budget.

I have a deep technical background, with almost 25 years of experience in Retail and e-Commerce software development, and have done extensive technical design and analysis, programming, as well as leading large teams. When combining this with the ability to understand business drivers and needs, I bring the rare capability of being able to talk "technology/design/analysis" with Dev Team members, and "business requirements" with the Business Analysts, QA and the Customer. I am able to bridge complex technology concepts with business-friendly ideas and language, and am able explain things in an easy-to-understand way for all team members and the Customer, regardless of their position or skill-set.

My previous positions include: System Analyst Lead, DevTeam Lead, and Functional Subject Matter Expert for large scale e-Commerce projects. Lead Technical Designer and Technical Team Lead of retail software projects.

On the personal front, I am an accomplished nature photographer, specializing in landscapes and macro work, and have been published many times in magazines such as Outdoor Photography Canada, Nature's Best, National Wildlife, Photo Life, Photo Selection, Yankee, Grit, and in many textbooks. I regularly co-teach nature photo workshops. You can see my work at VisionsInNature.com

Robert Servranckx, PMP's Experience

Technical Project Manager

Cactus Commerce

Privately Held; Computer Software industry

December 2008 – Present (2 years 4 months)

I am a customer-facing Technical Project Manager on medium to large scale B2C e commerce projects, with project teams of up to 25 people. In this role, I am involved in all aspects of project management throughout a project's life cycle, from envisioning to final delivery.

My tasks and responsibilities include: scoping, planning and estimating, scheduling and progress tracking, resource planning, task assignments, budgeting and monthly financial reporting, weekly project status reporting and chairing client meetings, scope control and change management, risk management, chairing the project team meetings, assisting on SOW and RFP. I am also involved in performance reviews for the member on my project team.
When there is need due to resourcing challenges, and having the ability to do so, I will actively help my project team member by performing numerous additional duties, including: business analysis to gather and document requirements and change requests, assisting the QA team to write test plans and test cases, working with Development Leads to help in feature design and to diagnose technical problems, contributing to the UX team for creative design and graphics, and working with the Deployment team on configuration issues.

For most projects, I’ve dealt with customers and vendors in remote locations, including Germany, Mexico, Switzerland, Belgium, the UK, and other locations, and I am therefore very comfortable working in virtual environments with remote clients and teams.

Manager, Business Analysis and Presentation Teams

Cactus Commerce

Privately Held; Computer Software industry

June 2009 – February 2010 (9 months)

For a period of 9 months, I acted as interim Functional Manager for the Business Analysts and Presentation teams, comprised of a total of 12 employees. During this time, I was also fulfilling role of Technical Project Manager.

As Presentation Manager, my role was to drive the team to efficiently develop the presentation layer of e-commerce projects, to meet the client’s expectation for the UI and associated areas (SEO, accessibility, skinning, UI maintainability), to help establish standards to be followed on all projects, and to help educate the other teams (developers, QA, PMs, Functional Managers) on the presentation concepts and developments, and to help create the Presentation Plan given to all customers to explain the presentation goals, best practices and guidelines.

As BA Manager, my role was to be an e-commerce subject matter expert in order to provide leadership and oversight to the BAs on the team, to support and participate in projects requiring in-depth functional or technical knowledge of Cactus’ solutions.
My duties included resolving personnel and personal issues within the teams, conducting performance evaluation and salary reviews, conducting weekly 1 on 1 meetings with the team members, helping with career management, working with other managers on project resource allocation, and terminating employment.
