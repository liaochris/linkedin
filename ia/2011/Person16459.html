Grant Moss's Publications

  • Patent Activity in Bankrupt Technology Firms: An Empirical and Case-Based Study

    • Turnaround Management Association
    • October 4, 2010
    Authors: Grant Moss

    Patents are considerably valuable assets for technology companies, but organizations often do not fully appreciate and extract value from those assets. In analyzing the patent portfolios of 100 technology firms that filed for bankruptcy between 1997 and 2006, no clear and effective method for managing IP emerged. However, a limited number of organizations did monetize their IP by ultimately transferring those assets to IP acquisition companies or other operating companies. This study tracks the patent management practices of many of these bankrupt technology companies.
