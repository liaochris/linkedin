Pedro Damian Kostelec’s Summary

Pedro D. Kostelec was born March 3rd, 1992 to parents of Slovenian roots in Argentina. Since his first years he was thought both Slovene and Spanish. At age nine he moved with his family to France where he attended a French primary school resulting in complete mastery of a third language. In 2004 he moved to Slovenia where he attended national Slovene schools, and finally ended up at an International Baccalaureate Diploma program which he completed in May 2010. Throughout his life he has developed his persuasion skills as well as the ability to think critically as well as mastered four languages.
Pedro has a creative mind and has always been willing to spread his knowledge and enthusiasm. For example, during the past two years he has volunteered as a tutor for elderly people, whom he thought about the basics of information technology. This experience helped him to become more patient in addition to further improving his communication skills.
For the past three years he spend a large part of his summer holidays volunteering in an English summer camp, where he mentored a group of about 14 children. In addition to being the youngest counselor in the history of the camp, he has also been named ‘best tutor’ by both the children and the supervisor.
In June 2008 and 2009 he was a mentor at a ‘week for children (called Oratorij)’organized by the local church. He was also responsible for editing and designing a record of daily occurrences which was then distributed to the parents.
Pedro Kostelec is an excellent swimmer, and enjoys mountain biking. His knowledge of computer software is also worth mentioning together with his passion for photography and film making.
During the past year he has learned to unicycle, began intensively training karate, and learned the Colemak keyboard layout (an alternative to the classic QWERTY layout).
