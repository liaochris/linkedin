C. Sara L. Minard’s Summary

I've spent my professional career which has included one year in Trento, Italy, two years in New York City, three years in Senegal, five years in Washington, DC. seven years in France, focused on working with governments (US Congress, OECD, ECOWAS), private sector companies and NGOs, research institutes, and community-based organizations to develop common positions on issues ranging from social entrepreneurship, aid efficiency, the environment, including sustainable use of natural resources like fisheries, women's rights, corporate social responsibility, economic equity and poverty reduction. My intellectual approach is multidisciplinary, although by training I tend to employ a socio-economic and political economy analysis to finding short-term or long-term solutions that harness market mechanisms and, where possible, propose multi-stakeholder strategies to reform the role of international development aid and private investment. My work in eighteen West Africa countries, Europe and North America has included both academic and policy research in economics and private sector development, Master-level university teaching and curriculum development, private sector consulting and community development, specifically in a small village in the North of Senegal where we've started a social enterprise called Yarum Jen, which means 'raising fish' in Wolof.
