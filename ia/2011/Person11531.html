  • VP, Advanced Technology & Technology Strategy Group Operations

    Walt Disney Company

    (Public Company; DIS; Entertainment industry)

    1995 — 2009 (14 years )

    Building Disney’s Technology Community & Culture
    Responsible for community management to build an enterprise technology culture at The Walt Disney Company that led to the creation of Disney’s Technology Strategy Board of CTOs which develops the annual technology strategic plan to enable new enterprise media opportunities (i.e. re-purposing media assets across business units), a shared media infrastructure (e.g. enterprise nomenclature for Disney’s media assets, enterprise mass storage resource, a worldwide media production and distribution network), collaborative sourcing opportunities and a reduction in redundant development.

    Targeted activities:
    • Produced Disney’s quarterly New Technology Forum for 13 years that harnessed the power of Disney’s Disney’s 5,000 innovators coupled with tech industry innovators to build an enterprise technology community to educate, share and collaborate on technology. For many years, the New Technology Forum was the only place where innovators could regularly gather outside their own business units.
    • Developed and deployed enterprise social network and collaboration tools: Clik!, one of Disney’s first intranet sites; “ID” (Intranet Disney), Disney's first intranet search engine; MikiWiki, one of Disney’s first internal wikis for online communities; and SourceForge, Disney's first collaborative software development platform for the enterprise.
    • Co-developed plan for a multi-business unit technology planning and management structure -- Disney’s Technology Strategy Board, TSB Office and Working Groups.
    • Co-created and co-produced Pooh Camp, the annual unmeeting of Disney’s innovators inspired by Foo Camp, to tackle The Company’s technology challenges and inspire creative uses for new technology.
    • Managed Corporate New Technology Strategy Group operations including budget, staffing and facility requirements.

  • Design Administrator/Business Analyst

    Walt Disney Imagineering

    (Public Company; DIS; Entertainment industry)

    1980 — 1995 (15 years )

    The Disney Channel
    Co-authored and “imagineered” the original Disney Channel Business Plan in 1981 which created Disney's first cable television network. Proposed Disney repurpose a huge existing library of films and TV shows which significantly reduced programming costs.  Initial investment was $60M in capital and $38M in first year expenses.  Cable Networks is now Disney’s most profitable segment (3X Theme Parks & Resorts, 27X Studio).  Disney Channel alone has 200M viewers worldwide. Disney XD (a spin-off) has 150M.  In 2009, Disney’s Cable Networks Group which now includes Disney Channel worldwide, ESPN and ABC Family generated $10.6B in revenues and $4.3B in operating profits.

    Euro Disneyland (now Disneyland Paris)
    Analyzed European consumer market research to make recommendations on theme park design for the original Euro Disneyland design team (i.e. how to adapt a Disney theme park for the European marketplace). Planned and conducted Euro Disneyland consumer polling in France, Great Britain and West Germany in 1987 to profile European leisure behavior and preferences and to analyze implications on the design of the theme park. In 2009, Disneyland Paris was again the number one tourist destination in Europe.

    Walt Disney Imagineering’s Creative Computing Center
    Led Disney Imagineering’s introduction to computer graphics technologies which transitioned the creative process from analog to digital. Co-directed creative computing strategy for Walt Disney Imagineering's Creative Division from 1990 to 1995. Co-creator of the Creative Computing Center, a computer graphics resource and training center to develop the design, animation, CAD and pre-visualization skills and talents of Imagineering’s three hundred designers, producers and writers.
