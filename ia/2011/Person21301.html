João Pedro da Costa's Summary

João Pedro da Costa is a Fundação para a Ciência e a Tecnologia fellow and a PhD student in Information and Communication on Digital Platforms at Porto and Aveiro Universities (Portugal) and a collaborator of Instituto de Literatura Comparada Margarida Losa with a research project about the digital propagation of music videos based on an interdisciplinary epistemic approach between Web and Literary Studies. He's an antviller and an acafan of music videos.

He was born in Mulhouse (France) and came to Portugal aged of ten and sold books and music records while he graduated in Portuguese and French Language and Literature in the Faculty of Arts of Porto University. Then, he made ends meet writing for television (MTV Portugal), radio (Antena 3), cinema (Radio Fazuma’s Radio Dreda music documentary) and the Web (blogs and other social networks). He’s a published and internationally awarded author in Portuguese and French literature and he’s also a diabetic that does not believe in the therapeutic proprieties of insulin. As Bob Dylan would put it, he’s been to Sugar Town and he shook the sugar down. Therefore, he runs everyday to keep those frightening shots at bay. And he loves it.

Specialties

Media, music, music videos, digital communication, audiovisual aesthetics communication on digital platforms, convergence and participatory culture, fandom, collective intelligence, user-generated content, transmedia communication, web and literary studies.
