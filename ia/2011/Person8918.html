Liz Daniels's Summary

Hi, my name is Liz Daniels and I have Cerebral Palsy. I have done many things in my life, from working my way up to obtaining my BSc degree in Psychology to working in hospitals and many different organizations. I have always tried to maintain a happy work/fun balance and with that always strived to have a healthy social life. But as a young woman with a disability I have had constant struggles when it comes to social occasions, whether it’s due to accessibility or being miss-informed in regards to facilities. During these times I’ve often felt let down and often socially isolated feeling like a change needed to be made somewhere, with this in mind I decided I shall be the one to make that change. I have realized over the years that many of my friends with different disabilities and impairments struggle when it comes to planning weekends away or evenings out, the research that has to be done to obtain accessibility information which quite frankly should be readily available. So here I am, 28 years old with all this experience and frustrations from my own social life that I am convinced I am not the only one!



So Clubability is for all those people that have had to spend hours on the internet trying to find the best place to go on a Saturday night, those who have had to read the phone book to ring hotels to see if they have a disabled room (that is actually functional in a wheelchair) for that weekend away you’ve been looking forward to for ages. Basically Clubability is here to fulfill a basic need that isn’t available at present, so your wants and needs are what drives this. you need to tell ME what you expect from Clubability.
