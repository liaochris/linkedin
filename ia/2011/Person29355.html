Michael Butash’s Summary

I have over 10 years professional experience in most every engineering aspect of the tele-communications and service provider markets in a technical capacity. Much of my experience has been working for large service providers and large enterprises developing and deploying highly scalable, redundant, and secure best of breed infrastructures with robust management/monitoring capabilities. From DWDM switching across data centers to dialup and everything in between, I've done it, and typically big.

As interests I tinker with various databases, Apache, BIND, PKI/crypto, IIS, AD to name a few, and use extensively various open-source management/monitoring solutions to always keep tabs on any network. Several VMware servers with a lot of linux-based VM's hosting mail, dns, domain, jabber, vpn services, and run a home Cisco network that routes a larger prefix than most corporations do, with better redundancy for my own amusement. Fiber channel for storage expansion is now online too. :)

Avid cell phone hack, flashing cooked roms and running homebrew software, especially with linux coming on strong now. I use only linux (ubuntu), aside from keeping windows desktop vm's around for visio and some other pesky windows apps. I keep fresh with Windows Server technologies for my enterprise clients, but meh, it's just Microsoft otherwise. Just about anything open-source, I tend have experience or precursory knowledge of at the very least, it's still my hobby.

Who else cuts apart a FC SAN shelf to fix a dropped corner, welds it back together with their mig, and paints it themselves all in the pursuit of fiber channel storage for their media and VM servers at home? Me, but only because Infiniband clustering components haven't dropped cheap enough yet on ebay.

Michael Butash’s Specialties:

Expert proficiency in Linux, Unix, BSD, and Windows environments supporting small to large scale clustered server deployments including systems optimization, security, directory services, PKI, and various application development frameworks.
