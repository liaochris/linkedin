Summary: This position started as systems administration for VMS systems on various DEC platforms for Melbourne Water, and then progressed on to systems administration for Unix servers of various flavours for various companies.

For over 4 years while at MITS, I was the system administrator exclusively responsible for a group of over 20 VMS servers and workstations at Preston Reservoir and the Brooklyn sewage pumping station. Monitoring of performance, installations, preventative maintenance, backups and other system functions were my main tasks.

I also provided 7x24 on-call support (one week in three). To assist in this function, I took it upon myself to interface the servers to a paging system that was previously in disuse. System metrics such as disk space, memory usage, CPU load, hardware errors and critical processes were now being monitored, and messages were sent to the on-call pager in real time when preset limits were exceeded. This had the effect of reducing system downtime considerably, as advance notice was given regarding failing system components and parameters before outages occurred.

I was the Systems Administrator and team leader for two other Unix System Administrators at United Energy (now TXU).

My responsibilities included evolving and enforcing standards, co-ordination and allocation of resources, ensuring that deadlines were met, customer liaison, and the facilitation of intra-team communications.

I was also the System Administrator for the Melbourne Port Corporation, a site with over 20 SUN Solaris servers.

While at MITS, I was solely responsible for the planning and implementation of the
project that transitioned Melbourne Water from a bridged network (using DECNET, TCPIP and X25) to a routed (DECNET & TCPIP) network in preparation for the split into separate water businesses.
