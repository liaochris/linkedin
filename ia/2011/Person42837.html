Tess Lawson's Experience

Founder, Entrepreneur, VP Marketing

Viridian Asset Fund Inc.

Investment Management industry

April 1997 – Present (14 years)

Tess is a seasoned, high energy entrepreneur who has owned and operated The Bailey Capital Fund, a full service equipment leasing brokerage and consulting firm since 1997 and began her first investment fund division in 1999. Tess currently manages the Lawson Philanthropic Trust, which has been aligning expertise in the debt financing industry for the development of sustainable, green funds for the Canadian exempt marketplace since 2005.
www.blog.viridianfund.ca

September 2010, Tess brought together fellow Vancouver Rotary Club colleagues to form the Viridian Asset Fund Inc., a 100% sustainable-content fund for the Canadian market. Viridian Asset Fund Inc. will launch spring of 2011 as an RRSP, RESP, RRIF and TFSA eligible green fund which will provide a quarterly income-producing investment product through the financing of clean energy technology equipment in Canada.

In 2006, Tess's dedication to investment and asset security won her a National Research Council (NRC) Grant to develop an expert knowledge system for Canadian Trust & Tax law distribution via the Internet. She was also recognized by the Odlum Brown and Price Waterhouse Cooper's sponsored 2010 “e-Series” Class of 12 Women Entrepreneurs for her work in high growth, leveraged business.

Tess has been a board member for the Membership Review Committee for The Better Business Bureau of the Lower Mainland of Vancouver since 2007 and is a past Woman's University Club member. Tess was recently elected on the Board of Directors for The Vancouver Rotary Club 2011-2013 fiscal years and is also Chair of The Vancouver Rotary Investment Club Education Committee.

Tess’s volunteer work includes: The Vancouver Board of Trade Task Force Boards (2005-2010) including:
Education & Skills Committee, Crime & Public Safety, Downtown Eastside, Community Affairs Committee and the 2010 Olympic Subcommittee, where she shares her passion and energy and does her part to improve the quality of life in our community.
