Roselynn Imbleau’s Summary

My creating surfaced with my belief in preserving the beauty and health of the environment- the land. This appreciation of nature was born in my youth when I admired the mists rising off of the Appalachians and Blue Ridge. I am drawn to paint landscapes, marshscapes, seascapes that includes water imagery. Subject matter began with delicate ecosystems:marsh and mangrove forests. I hope via my website to promote awareness of the dynamics that thrive in these fragile ecosystems.

BIO
I paint with my feelings and emotions.
I love art because it allows me personal expression and growth, and it is a vehicle in which to celebrate “mind-full living.”

Art provides a way to record my life. I enjoy looking at art, I am often inspired by the creativity in others. Creating pulls me into an “emotional” space where I run from the rational.

Subjects:
Landscapes,Special Places,Moments...Life.

Creating is a “solo recording artful living” experience. I bring to my work an appreciation of what I have experienced and the environment of each place I have been that inspires me. I create in order to share my own “personal translation.” I experiment in all media but I favor pastels and oils for their immediacy and blending characteristics.

Background: Life.
Art probably had me in kindergarten. In first grade I took to art and my teacher labeled me “an artist.” I won my first ribbon in 4th grade for a Pooh and Tigger rendition, but it was not until after college that I seriously studied under local and nationally recognized painters. It was then I was awarded the opportunity to illustrate for LTE, Inc. and publish my work into a book entitled “It’s Potty Time.” What followed was Julia Cameron’s inspiring book, “The Artist’s Way.”

I am influenced by people, time spent abroad, roots and the ocean.

I reside with my husband, Claude, two children and our dog and I care for my mother who has Parkinsons. Most of my extended family lives in Ottawa, Ontario.
