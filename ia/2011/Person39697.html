Sergio Maistrello's Summary

I'm a freelance journalist, self-employed and contributing writer for italian newspapers and magazines. I wrote three books in italian language about blogs, social networks and the new information ecosystem: "Giornalismo e nuovi media" (Apogeo, 2010), "La parte abitata della Rete" (Tecniche Nuove, 2007) and "Come si fa un blog" (Tecniche Nuove, 2004).

Since 2006 I'm editor-in-chief at Apogeonline (www.apogeonline.com), one of the oldest and most trusted italian webzine about technology and Internet issues. Besides the webzine, Apogeonline is the official site for Apogeo, one of the most important italian publishing houses in the technology shelve, part of Feltrinelli group.

Since 2009 I'm contract professor at University of Trieste. I teach Journalism and new media inside a Communication and Advertising Course. Since 2010 I'm also contract professor for the Master of Science Communication at Sissa-International School for Advanced Studies in Trieste.

I'm part of the organizing staff of State of the Net (www.stateofthenet.it), an aim-to-be international conference about the Internet and society, whose first edition was held in february 2008 in Udine, in the north-est of Italy.

Since 2000, I had been part for almost five years of the editorial staff of Internet News, a monthly magazine wich used to sell up to 100.000 copies nationwide in Italy.

I've been a contributing writer for newspapers and magazines since 1988. I'm a member of the italian Journalists Association (Ordine dei Giornalisti) since 1990.

Specialties

Writing, editing, public speaking, teaching, technical advisory (focused on Internet, Social software and Journalism)
