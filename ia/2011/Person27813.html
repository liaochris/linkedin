On the field of play, leaders emerge and become captains. Someone the team turns to for direction both on and off the field, doing whatever it takes to prepare and compete so that the team has a shot at achieving its goals. Just like on the playing field, captains emerge to lead their organizations, making wise business decisions, and guiding/inspiring those around them to meet the goals set out for them by management.

Managing projects and managing the game have many overlaps and similarities. Just like a defense prepares for the no huddle, a project manager needs to gain the commitment and trust of their team members to develop an effective risk-management plan that everyone knows will be there in the event things go wrong. The cost analysis done for the launch of a new web site can be just as complicated as juggling contracts to get under salary cap.

All my life I have enjoyed working with others from the playing field to the cubicle. I have sought to bring the two together, combining my passion for sports with the skills I have developed in the workplace.
