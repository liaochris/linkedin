Dr. Andre Lescot Williams’s Summary

A demonstrated instructional leader with fifteen years of educational experience in urban public schools. A proven track record as being a strong disciplinarian with a solid record of achieving school discipline, as well as a catalyst for improving students’ academic success. served as a Special Education teacher for five years at the middle and secondary levels in New York City Public Schools and served as an administrator in various capacities at the building and district levels.

Was Principal of Middle School 232 located in the South Bronx section of New York City. Implemented numerous systems that have allowed the school to see a marked increase in students’ test scores, improvement in attendance, parental involvement, and a decrease in campus violence. Implemented various literacy and mathematics blocks, and worked collectively with various organizations and community groups to ensure students’ success. Under my instructional leadership, the school experienced improved decorum, increased certificated staff; double digit gains in Mathematics and English Language Arts and improved student behavior.

A knowledgeable curriculum clinician who is hardworking and dedicated—a strong visionary with a no nonsense approach to children’s learning, a consensus builder. Presently serving as principal of Sammye E. Coan Middle School in Atlanta Public Schools. Under my leadership, Sammye E. Coan Middle School made annual yearly targets for the first time in seven years and experience a 23 percent increase in mathematics. Under my leadership Sammye E. Coan Middle School has made annual yearly progress consistently.

Served as a member of the graduate faculty of New Jersey City University and currently teaching graduate level courses as well as supervising dissertations at Argosy University, Atlanta and Sarasota.
