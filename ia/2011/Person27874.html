Maria Culp's Summary

Hi! I'm a recent graduate from the University of Oregon and the School of Journalism and Communication with a Bachelor of Arts in public relations and minors in German and communications.

Over the past four years, I've honed my skills as a writer, researcher, editor and speaker. Additionally, I've studied and become familiar with various social media including Twitter, Wordpress and Facebook.

My two study abroad experiences played instrumental roles in shaping my college experience. Through the journalism school, I visited Quito and the Galapagos Islands in Ecuador where I combined two of my greatest passions: writing and environmental conservation. My junior year, I spent three months living in the culturally diverse city of Vienna, Austria with a host family. While I was fully immersed in the Austrian culture, I focused on improving my German. I was also fortunate to spend several weekends exploring other parts of Europe.

Although I will miss my Ducks at Oregon, I'm looking forward to my new adventure in San Francisco.

Specialties

Writing, editing, researching, public speaking, social media (Twitter, Facebook, Wordpress, Blogger, etc.), media kits, news releases, German, conversational Spanish

Greenhill Humane Society

Public Relations and Communications industry

April 2010 – June 2010 (3 months)

Greenhill Humane Society is a non-profit animal shelter located in Eugene, OR. GHS is dedicated to ending animal overpopulation, finding homes for stray animals and educating the public about animal responsibility.

Every spring, GHS holds its most successful fundraiser, Bark in the Park, a run/walk where 100% of the proceeds go to the shelter. Although the event is popular within the Eugene community, there was a noticeable disconnect within the campus community.

On a team of four, it was our job to promote Bark in the Park and spread awareness within the University of Oregon community. We started by creating a public relations plan, and spent three months executing our strategies and tactics. Some of our tactics included: creating pre- and post-event surveys, having a presence at campus events (Street Fair, Rent-a-Pooch, etc.), creating and distributing promotional flyers, inviting students to the event's Facebook fan page and creating and sending a press release to the campus newspaper.

Public Relations Intern

Ophelia's Place

Public Relations and Communications industry

November 2009 – June 2010 (8 months)

Ophelia's Place is a non-profit organization dedicated to bettering the lives of girls. Ophelia's Place originally branched off of Womenspace and offers workshops, counseling and support groups for girls.

On a team of three, we worked to propel Ophelia's Place into the spotlight. We created an aggressive public relations plan in November and executed our strategies throughout the year. We successfully utilized Twitter and MySpace to maximize awareness among both girls and donors. GirlsRule!, an annual event presented by a coalition of non-profits that provides a free day of workshops for girls and their parents, was a huge success -- we increased attendance from last year by 100%. To publicize the event, we created flyers using InDesign and also wrote press releases. We collaborated with a local marketing agency, VERB Marketing + PR, to revamp and redesign the organization's website.

Marketing Intern

University of Oregon Recreation Center

Marketing and Advertising industry

May 2007 – June 2010 (3 years 2 months)

For three years, I was a a member of the Recreational Sports Marketing Team. On this team of five, I promoted the various sponsors of intramural sports on campus. Sponsors I worked with include Nike, CLIF Bar, Tinactin, Muscle Milk, Kodak and Schwinn.

A constant presence on campus in the Student Recreation Center and also at intramural games allowed me to continually put my communication skills to use. Some of my responsibilities included distributing product samples from our sponsors and photographing championship games with visible sponsor logos. From Spring 2009 to Spring 2010, I was chosen by my supervisor to launch the CLIF Bar (natural energy bar) campaign where I managed the team, spoke at intramural meetings and organized sponsorship events.

Each spring, the team and I interviewed potential candidates and selected new members for the team. Spring of 2010, I was selected by my supervisor to lead the interview process, which included contacting all candidates after the interviews and notifying them of our decision.

Ethos Magazine

Writing and Editing industry

January 2010 – March 2010 (3 months)

Ethos Magazine, formerly KD Magazine, is an enitrely student-run publication in the School of Journalism and Communication at the University of Oregon and has received national recognition and several awards. The magazine is a multicultural blend of both local and international stories.

On staff at Ethos Magazine, I was a member of the public relations team and also a writer. As a public relations associate, I promoted various events, including a "Battle of the Bands" and pancake fundraisers, through social media, press releases and flyer distribution. We generated a total of $3000, which was substantial considering that past terms the staff raised around only $300.

The editor in chief chose my story idea, and I contributed a 1000-word article for the magazine's spring 2010 issue. I collaborated with editors and designers throughout the term to ensure that my story was ready for print.
