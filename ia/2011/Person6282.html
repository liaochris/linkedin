Brian Moriarty’s Summary

I enjoy helping companies with inorganic growth - M&A, alliances, licensing, investments. Successful corporate development programs require superb strategic planning, execution and integration. My background is unique -- I've driven and managed all of these efforts.

As an investment banker and a corporate development executive I have:
* Advised and developed plans for business leaders, CEO's and boards on many strategic growth initiatives.
* Closed nearly 100 M&A transactions ranging in size from very small to several billion dollars.
* Closed virtually all types of business combinations including mergers, acquisitions, divestitures, joint ventures and minority stakes.
* Formed licensing and strategic alliance relationships.
* Led and participated in a dozen successful integrations.
* Co-chaired Sun's Investment Committee which was responsible for investing in venture-backed companies and managing the portfolio.

At Sun, under my leadership we cut the cycle-time of our transactions in half without sacrificing quality, making us more efficient, nimble and a more attractive counter-party. In addition, we increased integration plan customization and better aligned our integration work with what matters most to our internal customers. These efforts led to increased customer satisfaction and improved business results.

In April 2009, we contracted to sell Sun to Oracle Corporation in a transaction which closed in January 2010. After helping Oracle with integration planning, I transitioned out of the company in February 2010.

In October 2010 I started a new position with Hewlett-Packard, where I'm Vice President of Human Resources for Mergers, Acquisitions, Divestitures and Outsourcing (MADO). It's a great opportunity for me to grow in an entirely new function (HR) while contributing my transactional expertise.

Brian Moriarty’s Specialties:

Leading planning and transaction teams, strategic and business planning, financial analysis, structuring, valuation, negotiation, balancing risk / return issues through due diligence, post-merger integration, venture investing .
