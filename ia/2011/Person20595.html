Jesse Kramer’s Summary

In 1996 Jesse Kramer co-founded Finch Brands’ advertising agency discipline, formerly called fabGORGON. Over seven years, he grew the agency group as the company’s President from a freelance copywriting shop into a creative marketing powerhouse. Under Jesse’s leadership, Wharton’s Philly 100 ranked the agency as the 38th fastest growing small business in greater Philadelphia, and in 2002 the Greater Philadelphia Chamber of Commerce named the firm the Innovative Business of the Year. In 2003 Jesse led the agency to be acquired by Finch Brands (called Kanter International at the time), at which point he became the consolidated company’s Vice President of Sales & Marketing. In 2009 Jesse was brought into a client, MEDecision, Inc, a leader in collaborative health care technology, for an undertaking to catalyze the company’s brand development efforts.

Today, in addition to leading the marketing and brand charge at AWE Tuning, building Electronic Ink's marketing function, and being a Partner at Finch Brands, he chairs one of the mid-Atlantic’s most historic and renowned small business support programs, the Greater Philadelphia Chamber of Commerce Small Business Board, helping to identify and mentor the next generation of regional category dominators. He has sat on committees and panels for The Breast Health Institute, Temple University’s Fox School of Business and The ESPN X-Games. 2004 brought Jesse the coveted Philadelphia Business Journal’s “40 under 40” award, recognizing business leaders for their career and community accomplishments. He has also been named in Philadelphia Style's top ten to watch (clearly not for fashion).
