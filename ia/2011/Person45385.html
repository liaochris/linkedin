  • Research Assistant - Spintronics and Vacuum Electronics Lab

    University of Cincinnati

    (Educational Institution; 10,001+ employees; Higher Education industry)

    December 2007 — June 2009 (1 year 7 months)

    Hands on experience in fabrication, testing and characterizing top and bottom emitting OLED.
    Hands on experience in growing and characterizing rare earth monosulfide.
    Responsible for maintenance of the thermal evaporation system, glove box and high vacuum furnace.

    Projects:

    1. Fabrication of Quantum dot inverted OLEDs on a self assembled nanoporous alumina
    template: The aim of this project is to fabricate quantum dot OLEDS on a nanoporous alumina template.
    Nano domes and Nano pines are distinct structures which are present when materials are deposited on a
    nanoporous alumina template, these structures are excellent field emitters. The OLED so fabricated takes
    advantage of these structures which in turn should decrease the turn on voltage and improve the
    efficiency of the OLEDs.

    2. Growth and Characterization of NdS target: Rare earth monosulfides have a very low work function
    (~ 1ev). A 2 inch NdS target is being synthesized for PLD on nanoporous alumina template to form self
    assembled nanoscale arrays. The properties of arrays will then be characterized using AFM, FE- SEM
    and SAFEM techniques.

    3. MEMS Pressure Sensor: The project involved the design, fabrication, calibration and testing of a
    MEMS pressure sensor. The sensor was based on the concept of Wheatstone's Bridge and the minimum
    feature size was 30 µm. The change in resistance, when pressure is applied, was measured and values
    ranging between 0-30 psi were measured. The fabrication involved Si semiconductor processes including
    RCA clean, BOE, Dry & Wet Oxidation, spin coating PR, Soft Bake, Hard Bake and UV-proximity
    lithography for patterning, Wet etching, Anodic Bonding, device testing and integration.
