Dennis Findley’s Summary

The architecture and planning career of Dennis Findley, AIA (American Institute of Architects), is deep, diverse and spans locations across the U.S. and around the world. University projects from Cambridge, Massachusetts to Baghdad, school projects from Chicago to Baltimore, a natural history museum in Atlanta, a maritime museum in New England, a U.S. embassy in Algeria, a U.S. District Courthouse in Alexandria, VA, adaptive-reuse projects, mixed-use projects and high-rise projects from Texas to New England, master plans for numerous institutions including Massachusetts General Hospital in Boston, residential projects from Maine to Napa Valley and more.

He has served individual clients, small business owners, entrepreneurs, developers, institutional clients, non-profit organizations, public school districts, the U.S. Government, and others. Several projects have involved historically sensitive buildings and sites, and many projects incorporated the latest strategies and technologies for energy conservation and green building systems. He has also entered important architectural competitions such as the Martin Luther King, Jr. Memorial in Washington, D.C., and each of the three competitions for memorials to the victims of 9-11.

Dennis received his Bachelor of Architecture from Auburn University, graduating First in Class, and received his Master of Architecture from Harvard University, graduating With Commendation.

In addition to having his own architectural business in McLean, Virginia, he has worked for The Architects Collaborative and Graham Gund Architects in Cambridge, Massachusetts. He is currently an Associate and Senior Project Architect at Bowie Gridley Architects in Washington, D.C.

In 2009-10, Dennis was a candidate for the U.S House of Representatives in Virginia's 10th Congressional District and, in 2009, was appointed by then Governor Tim Kaine to the Virginia Board of People with Disabilities.

Dennis Findley’s Specialties:

Creative building, interiors, landscape and urban design, energy conservation, green building systems.
