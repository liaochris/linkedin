John Montorio's Summary

Former senior editor and manager at The New York Times, Los Angeles Times, Newsday and The Washington Star. Also former editor-at-large for Entrepreneur Media; and contributing writer for House Beautiful.

Specialties

Editing and writing for magazines and newspapers. Strong leadership, communications and recruiting skills. Excel at directing print and web teams, inspiring the best work from editorial and art departments, while maximizing the potential of the business side.

Associate Editor

Partnership; Newspapers industry

November 2005 – July 2007 (1 year 9 months)

Continued to be in charge of all arts, entertainment and life-style coverage for the paper, managing one of the largest portions of The Times' total reporting and editing staff (at the time about 150 people) and a budget of approximately $30 million. Responsible for the vision, direction and content of this coverage. Oversaw the conception of stories and covers, editing, writing, design and photography. Also responsible for the online presence of all features coverage and charged with generating approximately 50 Page 1 stories a year.

Deputy Managing Editor

Los Angeles Times

Partnership; Newspapers industry

September 2001 – November 2005 (4 years 3 months)

Hired to rethink the paper's coverage of all entertainment, arts, cultural and life-style subjects, including the Book Review and the Los Angeles Times Magazine. Led a staff of approximately 160 reporters, critics, editors and support staff. Among the key accomplishments: Redesigned the Calendar section transforming it from a tabloid and into a two-part broadsheet Sunday package (eventually supplemented with a special Thursday broadsheet edition as well), netting the Times some $40 million a year in incremental revenue. Also, led the Sunday magazine to a first-ever break-even point with profitability on the horizon. And revamped, Food, Home, Real Estate, Books, Health and Weekend, while at the same time launching a new Outdoors section.

Style Editor
