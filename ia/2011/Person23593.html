Kate Buska's Summary

Working with media outlets as diverse as The Los Angeles Times, Sunset and The Travel Channel, Kate Buska has spent over a decade practicing public relations on behalf of some of Southern California’s most renown brands. During more than seven years at the San Diego Convention & Visitors Bureau, she has worked with international and national media on behalf of California’s second largest city to promote the region's $18 billion tourism industry to the more than 32 million annual visitors.

Her expertise includes developing and implementing targeted public relations strategies, proactively pitching journalists, fostering media relationships and developing new markets, building alliances with key partner organizations, social media engagement and coordination of press trips and individual media visits.

From appearing on-camera for MTV to working behind-the-scenes to meet editors’ high-pressure deadlines, she tackles each new challenge with enthusiasm and an eye toward results.

Recent highlights include extensive outreach in anticipation of the 2008 US Open at Torrey Pines, that resulted in coverage clearly positioning the destination among the nation’s best for golf travelers, and early stage promotion of San Diego craft brewing as a tourism asset, that helped establish the region as “The Best City in America for Craft Beer,” according to the October 2009 issue of Men’s Journal.

Specialties

Strategic Planning, Traditional Media Relations, Social Media Outreach and Engagement, Event Planning and Promotion, Interactive Marketing and Copywriting. Tourism, travel, hospitality, culinary, dining, restaurants and craft beer.
