Blake Adams's Summary

Are you crazy? Are you really helping to start a Division 1 College Football Program from scratch in Downtown Atlanta? Are you really joining in on a program that in no way will develop a fan base?

Those are the questions that people asked as I began my career as Director of Video and Creative Services for Georgia State University Athletics after a successful four years at Georgia Tech. Those are the questions I at times looked back on as I entered an office with nothing more than an empty desk and chair. Those are the questions that drove me as I built a program through strategic hires, innovative equipment purchases and a broad range of creative marketing and production initiative. Those are the questions that were answered on September 2nd 2010 as more than 30,000 fans filled the Georgia Dome in downtown Atlanta creating one of the most electrifying game day experiences I have ever seen.

Getting to that point was a long and often times difficult challenge, a challenge that for many would have been too daunting to even have glanced at, a challenge that I eagerly tackled through hard work, dedication and a little creativity. This is my passion… this is the thing that gets the blood pumping through my system like nothing else can… this is where I belong. There is nothing in this world like Creative Production and Leadership. The hours may be long, the work hard and tedious, but the payoffs are incredible. It is my goal to tap that, to unleash a tidal wave of enthusiasm around a program that will reach out to create excitement that turns into customers.

Over the past year I have transformed the Video and Creative Services department from one that was non-existent to one with a full time staff producing over 20 videos a week, producing a weekly television show, planning creative marketing initiatives that reach through the city of Atlanta and introducing innovative production techniques at not only Football but all of our sporting events.
