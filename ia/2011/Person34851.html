Phil Farrar’s Summary

I have a passion for building/developing organizations to jumpstart or aggressively grow new technology revenues. In true Greenfield opportunities, this means hunting (i.e. cold-calling and networking) to find individuals who can benefit from a technology infusion or upgrade. In established markets, this means documenting the differentiated value proposition for a specific customer need and developing a plan to respond. Sometimes this means re-architecting an organization, such as in several cases where I proposed and won support for bringing technical resources out to the field to increase customer satisfaction and improve responsiveness.

Two significant professional influences for me are Geoffrey Moore author of “Crossing the Chasm”, and Clayton M. Christensen, author of “The Innovator’s Solution”. These two giants provide a fantastic framework for understanding how and where a technology will or won’t succeed.

I started Technology Marketing Consultants in 2001 to carry this out on a consulting basis to work with companies at the napkin/idea stage, but also work in a full-time capacity in medium sized and large organizations that need more concentrated or long-term assistance.
