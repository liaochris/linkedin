Robert Briggs’s Summary

During my twenty years of electric utility experience, I have been involved in just about every facet of electrical transmission and distribution systems, from the design and construction of gas insulated substations "GIS" to the maintenance practices employed to keeping the electric grid reliable. I have broad experience in all phases of electric system operations, including supervision and training of IBEW employees. In 2007, I obtained a Master’s degree in Public Administration and graduated from the University of La Verne with my General Manager.
My vision is to add public value. The duty of the utility extends well beyond the fiscal responsibility to provide safe, reliable, cost-effective electricity and water. In a shifting political environment, significant changes have occurred in legislation that fundamentally affects the way utilities are run. The electric system is operated closer to its limits, large capital expenditures are often deferred and reducing expenses are all part of a modern utility’s strategic plan.
