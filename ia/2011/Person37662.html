Rolf Butters’s Summary

Rolf Butters has served the United States of America since 1994 when he entered Federal Civil Service as a Department of Energy engineer with the title of Industrial Specialist. He continues service at the U.S. Department of Energy/Office of Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy, in the Industrial Technologies Program.

Rolf graduated from Texas A&M University in 1984 with a Bachelor of Science degree in Mechanical Engineering. Rolf’s cooperative education broadened his education in: manufacturing; oil industry service operations; and consulting engineering. He applied his engineering skills in mechanical system design and computer analysis in private practice organizations for about 10 years prior to federal service.

At the Department of Energy, Rolf strategically plans for the lasting good of U.S. citizens by optimizing program effectiveness and by mentoring entrepreneurial companies in technology and business areas as they commercialize energy technologies with large potential energy impacts. Rolf built upon his solid foundation of engineering skills with the development of business and technology transfer skills on-the-job through these sustained relationships supporting technology commercialization efforts of roughly a hundred small business grantees of the Small Business Innovation Research and the Inventions & Innovation programs. These companies face incredible challenges to commercialize technologies in new markets and showed their appreciation of Rolf’s mentoring by awarding him the Entrepreneurs for Energy Efficiency, Inc.’s 2005 Vision Award – “Champion of Entrepreneurs”. Mentoring categories included technology development, intellectual property protection, independent testing, strategic government and private collaboration, marketing, licensing, strategic partnerships, and technology continuance planning beyond the principle’s death.

    Rolf supports energy assessment and technology implementation activities. Activities include program planning / implementation of an effort that combines prior assessment experience with implementation of technologies such as smart wireless sensing/control/awareness for energy/emissions/cost management.
    For a reasonable cost, these technologies provide the granularity of data necessary to reveal timely opportunities to reduce energy consumption which is otherwise buried as noise in the overall plant energy consumption tracked at meters. These secure (applicable to many applications), reliable (in noisy RF environments), open standard, and convenient technologies (no data cabling) are available from many companies (GE, Eaton, Honeywell, Wi-Fi Sensors).
    These can be used with a variety of process-support-energy systems such as steam, process heating and cooling, pumps, fans, compressed air, motors, as well as non-process industrial buildings and grounds systems such as efficient HVAC, lighting, water, and electrical distribution.

    For instance, there are opportunities to combine human-occupancy sensors with lighting, fans, HVAC, hot-water systems to keep a minimum of that utility on for safety and applying instantaneous additional amounts for human "demand-based" activity in that location.
