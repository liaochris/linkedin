Tim McLain's Summary

An experienced online marketing, social media/community, and product development executive working with diverse teams of professionals to increase sales and increase product awareness in a variety of cutting-edge online markets with fast-paced environments.

I bring 16+ years of leadership planning, creating, and marketing a variety of educational products for both online startups and a traditional multinational firm.

Accustomed to wearing a variety of hats across an organization, working as a team within all internal departments, staying in close contact with customers to inform product enhancements and new offerings, and vetting and experimenting with the latest online marketing tools to impact sales.

Proven ability to successfully analyze an organization's critical online marketing and community requirements, develop innovative and cost-effective solutions for enhancing competitiveness, engineer fixes and extensions to existing campaigns, increase revenues, and improve customer service through print catalog, email, enewsletter, social media, smartphones, Web/Web 2.0, and more.

My strong work ethic, tech-savvy, creative writing skills, and user-centered approach result in superior deliverables, creative marketing, and measurable uplifts in product awareness and sales.

Specialties

Online marketing (OMS Online Marketing Professional Certificate), social media outreach, landing page and email campaigns, end-user video training, website development, content authoring, customer engagement with smart phones.

Tim McLain's Publications

  • Educator's Internet Companion: Classroom Connect's Complete Guide to Educational Resources on the Internet

    • Classroom Connect
    • January 1, 1995
    Authors: Tim McLain, Gregory Giagnocavo

    After starting Classroom Connect in 1994, and getting our monthly printed newsletter up and running, Greg and I worked together to create this guidebook that helped thousands of teachers, librarians, and school leaders get online and starting using the Internet for K-12 instructional purposes.

  • Educator's World Wide Web Tourguide

    • Classroom Connect
    • July 1, 1995
    Authors: Tim McLain, Vince DiStefano, Gregory Giagnocavo

    Long before Google and Yahoo! guided educators to the best websites and free online content, we created this slick printed guide to help speed 'Net surfers to the best of the best in K-12 content and learning resources.

  • Classroom Connect Monthly Newsletter

    • Classroom Connect
    • January 1, 1994
    Authors: Tim McLain, Vince DiStefano, Gregory Giagnocavo, Chris Noonan Sturm

    This is any teacher's resource for technology integration. First published in 1994, the Connected Newsletter remains a practical and popular resource that helps teachers easily incorporate technology and the Internet into their teaching. Published nine times a year, each issue of the Connected Newsletter includes articles, useful tips, and countless Web site resources that teachers can pick and choose from depending on what they're teaching in the classroom that day.

  • Educator's Guide to Using WebWhacker

    • Classroom Connect
    • August 1, 1997
    Authors: Tim McLain

    Early Internet access in most schools was spotty at best. We teamed up with the developer of WebWhacker to help ensure access to critical online content anytime, anywhere. The software gave teachers and librarians the ability to harvest webpages locally to their PCs. These files were then internally re-linked together and make local surfing possible. It was a real life-saver for cutting edge teachers in the late '90s.

  • Internet Issues Bookshelf: Creating Acceptable Use Policies

    • Classroom Connect
    • May 1, 1997
    Authors: Tim McLain

    As schools started coming online in 1994 and 1995, they quickly started writing, sharing, and adopting Acceptable Use Policies (AUPs). In my capacity as both the senior writer and developer of many of our products, I also had my ear the ground as our community manager. Our mailing lists were home to thousands of early adopters, and I had ready access to discussions about and early drafts of these policies. This book was written to help schools better understand and implement these policies to set the standards for daily Internet use in a learning setting.

  • Internet Driver’s License: Internet Guide and Workbook

    • Classroom Connect
    • August 10, 1997
    Authors: Tim McLain

    This binder product contained a classroom-ready series of lessons and activities to teach kids how to be safe online. At the end of the class, the kids were each given a full-color Internet driver's license, which we sold in sets of 20 to classroom teachers. The included video had me driving Tim Wentworth's custom racing Mustang up to the front of a local PA high school. (That's a story for another day!)

  • All in One Internet: Access Software & Tourguide

    • Wentworth Worldwide Media
    • March 1, 1994
    Authors: Tim McLain, Gregory Giagnocavo

    After founding Classroom Connect, Greg and I co-authored this book and diskette set used by several ISPs in PA to get new customers dialed into the Internet with their modems.

  • Internet Business Advantage Newsletter

    • Wentworth Worldwide Media
    • February 1, 1994
    Authors: Tim McLain, Gregory Giagnocavo

    Before we launched the monthly Classroom Connect Newsletter, Greg and I started producing this publication for businesses looking to make use of BBS software, Telnet, Gopher, and, yes, the original WWW for business purposes.

  • Net Seminar: The Comprehensive Guide to Teaching Internet Basics

    • Classroom Connect
    • May 1, 1997
    Authors: Tim McLain, Vince DiStefano, Chris Noonan Sturm

    A binder, video, and editable Powerpoint product, containing a four hour class for use by school tech staff to get teachers and librarians using the Internet for teaching and learning. By far the largest and most content-rich product we created for schools.

  • A+ Yellow Pages: K-6 & 9-12 Editions

    • Classroom Connect
    • April 1, 1998
    Authors: Tim McLain, Chris Hofer Borror

    Schools asked us to catalog the best websites for teaching and learning. This pair of yellow-paper tomes were the result, a collaborative effort between many writers for our monthly newsletter and our internal staff of editors.
