John Payne’s Summary

History of success in building or turning around early stage venture funded software and technology companies as a founder and in conjunction with the founders and investors of high opportunity companies.

Former CEO of two public Internet software companies; Stamps.com (NASDAQ: STMP) and Day Software (SWX: DAYN) Jumpstart/turnaround CEO for additional startups where he was "the sonofagun brought in by the VC's" at a troubled or opportunistic point in the company's life.

These jumpstart/turnaround exits include one successful NASDAQ IPO (NASDAQ: STMP), one turnaround of a public enterprise software company (SWX: DAYN) recently acquired by Adobe, one sale to a public company (NYSE: McAFEE) and one sale to a leading provider of information security and compliance solutions (www.trustwave.com ).

He is an experienced fundraiser who has raised over $550 million in public and private equity, including $150 million+ in early stage venture capital raised from well known venture firms and strategic investors.

John Payne’s Specialties:

Interim and permanent CEO level management and strategic redirection to jumpstart or turnaround software and technology companies.

Secondary specialty in markets where patent prosecution, litigation and strategic positioning are major factors in the landscape.

John Payne’s Experience

  • CEO & Board Member

    Zumbox, Inc.

    (Privately Held; Internet industry)

    2010 — Present (1 year )

    Zumbox is the the leading provider of digital postal mail services. The company has created a web-based platform for the delivery of paperless mail. For every U.S. street address, there is a corresponding digital mailbox – a Zumbox – enabling mail and other content to be sent as digital files and received online with no paper, printing or postage.

  • Chairman

    CircleUp, Inc

    (Privately Held; Internet industry)

    2006 — Present (5 years )

    CircleUp, Inc. is the leading group messaging platform for payments, wireless and VOIP services. It's primary offering, the SmartMessage Center, combines a structured Internet messaging service for groups with a messaging gateway that connects email, the instant messaging networks and social networks, including Facebook, proprietary providers such as Kintera in the non-profit markets and many others.

    CircleUp operates a consumer service and offers co-branded and white label communications services for partners including eteamz and The ActiveNetwork, Kintera (Nasdaq: KTA), Blackbaud and other software as service providers of vertical applications.

  • President and CEO

    Airmedia

    (Privately Held; Wireless industry)

    1994 — 1998 (4 years )

    AirMedia Live was awarded the prestigious "Editors Choice" by PC Magazine for its wireless "push technology." It was chosen as a finalist by the Software Publishers Association for "Best New Product of the Year" and was a winner of the Innovations '97 Award at that years Consumer Electronics Show.

    It also won New Media Magazine's 1996 Gold Medal gold medal for technical innovation and creative excellence in interactive multi-media, and was honored as a Premiere Product along with Sun's introduction of JAVA, the global announcement of the Palm Pilot and other leading technology products at InfoWorld's highly respected Demo '96 conference.

    AirMedia OEM licensees included Hewlet Packard, NEC, Global Village Communications, Philips, Compaq and other PC and mobile device manufacturers who manufactured and distributed AirMedia software and hardware based upon an AirMedia reference design to enable PC's and handhelds to receive broadcasts from the AirMedia Live Internet Broadcast Network.

    The AirMedia Live! Network delivered a national footprint of wireless broadcast, narrowcast and one-cast content and messaging with real time alerts and notifications.
