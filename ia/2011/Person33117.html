Mechanical Test Engineer

Maritime Applied Physics Corporation

Privately Held; Higher Education industry

December 2009 – August 2010 (9 months)

Mechanical Test Engineer responsible for supporting the Navy DDG 1000 program by aiding multiple vendors in satisfying military standard test requirements. I was the Test Lead for EMI, Power (1399) and E3 testing. I would travel to the test facilities with the shipboard components to monitor and troubleshoot during testing. My responsibilities also included, but were not limited to, writing technical documents to be submitted to the Navy and providing failure analysis reports and solutions.

Mechanical Engineering Intern

Germfree Mobile Laboratories, Inc.

Privately Held; Biotechnology industry

May 2009 – December 2009 (8 months)

Mechanical Engineering Intern assisting in the development, design and packaging of high containment mobile laboratories. My responsibilities included creating 2-D wiring diagrams, schematics and plans in AutoCAD Lt 2009 as well as the design of prints for sheet metal fabrication to create custom packaging. I assisted in the selection of onboard security and electrical equipment and the shop floor installation of the equipment. I also played a role in creating production scheduling using MS Project.

Controls Systems Team Lead

ERAU Formula Hybrid SAE Team

Mechanical or Industrial Engineering industry

August 2008 – May 2009 (10 months)

The Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University SAE Formula Hybrid team developed, manufactured, and tested a high performance formula racecar to compete in the 2009 Formula SAE race. As the control system team lead I was responsible for the successful generation and implementation of the vehicle control system in Labview. The team tested the system using Labview to create a Hardware-In-the-Loop (HIL) Simulation. The final control system was implemented using an onboard cRIO.

Systems Engineering Intern
