Résumé de Matthew DalSanto, Ph.D.

I have a Ph.D. in Applied Economics and am currently in my final year of pursuing a J.D. at the University of California, Berkeley, School of Law (Boalt Hall).

I will utilize my extensive background in economics in my legal practice by bridging the gap between lawyers and the economic experts that they employ. This will allow me to assist in an integral way with the development of litigation strategies.

My research interests center on issues such as antitrust, financial regulatory reform, interstate compacts, and joint powers agreements. I am currently working on several papers related to these last two interests - economics of intergovernmental cooperation. This research is supported in part by the Berkeley Center for Law, Business and the Economy.

Compétences de Matthew DalSanto, Ph.D.

Applying empirical and theoretical economic methods to legal matters.
