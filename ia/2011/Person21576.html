Johann Blake's Summary

Programming since the age of 15, my love for computers, software and the Internet has taken me on a journey into three countries over the past two decades. Having worked in or with diversified companies at all levels from programming and hardware design up to major web/mobile application architecture and development, I came to realize in the mid 1990s what an important role the Internet would play in virtually every business sector. So convinced that I even built Unitron's first website and registered the domain unitron.com to demonstrate to the company its potential, even though web development wasn't my job in the company at the time.

Having worked on many web, desktop and mobile applications over the years for companies in North America, Europe and Israel, I have had the rare privilege as a developer to see how buyers, sellers and information seekers from various countries collaborate and interact with each other across diverse cultures, languages, educational, financial, military and political environments.

Given the explosive market for mobile applications in 2009 and given my extensive background in developing international eCommerce solutions, I architected and developed the mobile app store, Aproov. It is the only app store currently on the Internet that was designed and built to become an ecosystem for a global multilingual market. Aproov was designed to support rich multilingual content, multimedia, hundreds of product categories, app/device compatibilities and the ability to browse or search for apps without having to wade through tons of useless apps. Aproov uses strict filtering and does not even allow adult material on its site. A proprietary algorithm called App Rank, that is analogous to Google's Page Rank, is used to determine an app's true value. Upcoming development will include paid apps in multiple currencies and payment systems.
