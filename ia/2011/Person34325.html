World Rainforest Fund

Non-Profit Organization Management industry

November 2009 – Present (1 year 5 months)

The World Rainforest Fund was founded in 1984 as a subsidiary of the Foundation for Biological Conservation and Research by the distinguished evolutionary biologist, David Seaborg. The purpose of the World Rainforest Fund is to preserve the rapidly diminishing biodiversity of the earth, protect its rainforests, and combat global warming. It accomplishes these goals by a number of means. The first is public education, which includes lectures, publications and writings, and its website. It also works with other environmental organizations to purchase endangered land habitats that are hot spots of biodiversity, focusing primarily on tropical rainforests, which have the highest species diversity of all terrestrial ecosystems, being home to one half of all terrestrial species on earth. The organization also works to empower indigenous and local tropical peoples, helping them protect their land, which is also prime habitat for a high diversity of animal and plant species, many of which are endangered.
