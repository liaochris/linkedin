Susan S. Bryant, CPA’s Summary

I began my career in 1999 as an internal auditor with Bank One Corporation, now JP Morgan Chase, where I performed operational, financial, regulatory compliance and information technology audits. As an auditor and audit supervisor, I performed in-depth risk analyses, developed strategies to evaluate procedural and systematic controls and recommended cost-effective and creative solutions to senior management. While with Bank One, I received client service and corporate synergy awards for forging collaborative relationships with internal audit clients and among audit staff.

In May 2002, I joined a local accounting firm in Denton, Texas where I provided assurance and tax services for small companies and high net-worth individuals. I also directed two fraud investigations which uncovered over $3.3 million in employee and management fraud and resulted in legal and criminal prosecution. Further, I revolutionized the firm’s internal processes by automating LIFO inventory valuation and financial statement preparation procedures.

I joined a Top-25 DFW public accounting firm in June 2004 and served as Financial Services Senior where I designed, supervised, and assisted in performing audit, review and compilation engagements. I also served as the point-person for researching and resolving complex accounting and auditing issues, provided internal control and general business consulting to clients and participated as a key team member on due diligence projects for manufacturing and technology companies.

In July 2005, I became a Manager for Tad Accounting, Inc. where I served in a Controller/CFO capacity for my small business clients. In this role, I performed monthly duties related to financial statement preparation as well as managed payroll conversions, provided audit assistance and served as a trusted business advisor to management across industry segments.
