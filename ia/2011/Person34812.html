Paul Friedman's Summary

Paul's driving career focus, in business development and sales, has been on bringing exceptional and measurable value to his employers by continually building relationships with, selling to, and helping solve the challenges of business leaders.

In 2011, Paul returned to Chicago Public Media to lead the Corporate Sponsorship department and build relationships with top companies and professional service firms around the region.

In 2010, Paul joined Weigel Broadcasting in Chicago, as an Account Executive. Paul collaborated with new and former clients to create dynamic, integrated campaigns across five television stations, including WCIU - The U, MeTV, MeToo, ThisTV and WCUU - The UToo.

In 2009, Paul joined GoPicnic in a Senior Sales Account Manager role, identifying new opportunities within a variety of sales channels..

In June of 2007, Paul was recruited through a board of directors relationship to spearhead a new model of business development for NVISION and its consultative approach, designed to create cost and process efficiencies within the marketing operations area of Fortune 1000 companies.

Paul topped his department in billing each year of his sales career at WBEZ in Chicago, leading to a 400% growth of the station’s sponsorship revenue during his 9+ years with the station. In his final year with WBEZ, he managed over 50% of the station’s annual sponsorship sales.

For 16 seasons, Paul was the Voice of Wrigley Field for the Chicago Cubs, providing his customers a unique link into one of the most famous professional sports franchises in the world.

Paul is also an accomplished voice-over actor, having recorded parts on internationally recognized programs like "This American Life" and "Wait Wait...Don't Tell Me" and recorded commercial voice overs and narrations.

For three years, Paul held executive committee leadership on the Board of Directors of Emerald City Theatre Company, Chicago's 4th largest not-for-profit theatre.
