Flavio Rodrigues's Summary

*Has started playing the guitar with his Father at the age of 5.

*In 1992, he entered in "Groove" (Free Music School), where he studied Popular Brazilian Music and jazz with Mr. Leyve Miranda for 3 years.

* In 1994, he started to study flamenco guitar in the "Centro Flamenco Pepe de Córdoba" (Sao Paulo-Brazil), with the guitarist Fernando de la Rua.

* Since 2000, whilst living in Madrid, he has worked with flamenco artists such as Antonio Canales, Juan Andrés Maya, Concha Jareño, La China, Domingo Ortega, Belén Fernández, Belén Maya, Rubén Olmo, Adela Campallo, Rocío Molina, Olga Pericet, Manuel Liñán, Marco Flores, Belén Lopez, Alfonso Losa, José Maya, Pitingo, Rafael Jiménez "Falo", Talegón de Córdoba, Guadiana, Agustin Carbonell "Bola", Lole Montoya, Pepe Habichuela, José Luís Montón, Domingo Patricio, Juan Parrilla, Rubém Dantas, Chicuelo, José Soto "Sorderita", and also with important brazilian artists such as Filó Machado, Nana Vasconcelos, Carlinhos Antunes, Yamandu Costa, Flavia-N, Sizao Machado, Renato Martins, Thiago Espirito Santo, Alex Buck, etc.

*He has also colaborate with artists of different styles from another countries: Hossam Ramzy (Egypt), Yoshida Brothers (Japan), Trilok Gurtu (India).

*He also was “Flamenco Guitar Supervisor” of the big production “Zorro, the Musical”, produced by Isabel Allende and directed by Chris Renshaw, with music by the Gipsy Kings and John Cameron.
