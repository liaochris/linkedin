Armando Oliver Suinaga's Summary

BIOGRAPHY
Born in Mexico City, 1969, worked for the National University in the Lakes Project (1998-1999). From 2002 to 2003 in FDU in projects concerning Texcoco Lake in Mexico City. From 2009 to 2010 he collaborated with RMIT University in Melbourne, Australia.

Office twentyfourseven obtained the first prize in the 2009 dawntown competition for a metromover station in the Bicentennial park in Miami. He obtained a Honorary Mention, in the international anonymous contest for the museum and memorial for the Tsunami victims in Khao Lak, Thailand in 2006 and a third place in the international competition for Prague’s Castle Pheasantry (Architectural Review 01-1998).

As an academic, has participated in UNAM, TEC-CCM and UIA in Mexico City, twice as a jury in Harvard and in RMIT. His academic work has been exposed in the Rotterdam, Sao Paulo and Venice biennials.His work has been published in: Arquitectura, Arquine, Trazos, Architekt (CzR), the Architectural Review and the book “Landscape Urbanism, A Manual for the Machinic Landscape”, published by the Architectural Association. While in Australia obtained a joint first prize in the Unlandscaped competition of the Landscape Australia Magazine.

His projects have been exposed in the Art&Idea gallery in Mexico City, in the AA in London and in the Architecture Institute in Thailand. The Olinca Kindergarten was exposed in 2005 in the gallery of the American Institute of Architects (AIA) in New York in the Mexico Dialogues Exhibition. In December 2009 he presented his work in Landmark, at the Bergen Kunsthall, Norway invited by the Bergen Architecture Institute.

In 2010 he achieved national professional certification through the CONARC and international APEC Architect registry in Mexico with recognition to his ten years of professional experience. In 2010 he obtained a grant to develop a landscape urbanism project in Mexico City and became a member of the National System of Art Creators SNCA 2011-2013.
