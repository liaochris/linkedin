Maartje Gorte's Summary

27-year old with a lust for learning, who is creative, communicative, collaborative, unconventional, broadly interested, open-minded, decisive, strategic and goofy, and who has a huge heart. I'm formally trained in Biology, with an emphasis on Genetics, but decided a career in science is not for me.

So, now I am looking for work! I'm open to all interesting possibilities, from project manager to copywriter to dog trainer.

I'm looking for a non-hierarchical, enthusiastic group of people that do multidisciplinary projects that improve the world. You're interested and interesting, and meet regularly for strategy sessions while also doing a lot of work independently. You are open and honest in your dealings, and communicate well on all levels.

Is that you and are you looking for a loyal, hard working teambuilder who excels at using creative methods to get things done? Please drop me a line!

Specialties

Writing, editing, communication, project management, public speaking, organising and leading events, graphic design and webdesign, various software skills, extensive knowledge on biological subjects, lab skills.

Maartje Gorte's Experience

PhD student

Utrecht University

Educational Institution; Research industry

October 2007 – July 2010 (2 years 10 months)

My main interest within the field of Biology is development, or how to get to a fully mature organism from a single cell. My PhD research was on the Arabidopsis root stem cell niche, trying to find the signals cells send to each other and themselves that determine which cells get stem cell identity.

Unfortunately I was not able to finish this, but my data looked extremely promising and I am sure my former colleagues will get a lot out of it!

Courses:
- Design of Experiments (Statistics)
- Gene Networks in Stem Cells
- Project Management for Scientists

Vocalist/Bass player

Alerion and Unspoken For/Justus

Music industry

July 2005 – March 2009 (3 years 9 months)

As a founding member of metal band Alerion, I had a great time composing and recording a first demo. We played several gigs, with me as lead vocalist and bassist. I left the band in 2007 because the oh-so-original 'musical differences' got too big.

Good thing, too, since Alerion went on to record a second demo including all the musical elements I was veto-ing and losing most of the elements I was fighting to preserve. And, although their music is nothing like what I wanted to make, they sound great doing it. I went on to play bass and sing background vocals for Utrecht-based rock band Unspoken for (now 'Justus'), with excellent music that included much of what I love.

We played several gigs, produced a demo and expanded the band's sound with harmony vocals. Due to personal circumstances I parted from Justus in 2009, and am currently between bands.

Internship with the Bos Group

UMC Utrecht

Non-Profit; Hospital & Health Care industry

October 2006 – June 2007 (9 months)

This internship was part of my Master's Education - I worked on signal transduction in the model organism Caenorhabditis elegans. Report title: "MIG-10 as RAP-1 effector"

This group was a very interesting to work in - many people working on the same subjects, but in different model systems and with different techniques. The group was very big and had three subdivisions, which made it an excellent environment to study leadership and management strategies.

Grade: 7.5/10

Internship at the Molecular Genetics Research Group

Utrecht University

Educational Institution; Research industry

September 2005 – October 2006 (1 year 2 months)

This was the major internship for my Master's Education - I looked for conserved elements in the promoter regions of PLETHORA2 orthologs, in order to find binding elements for upstream regulators. I liked this group so much (and they liked me) that I took a PhD position there. Report title: "A phylogenetic analysis of the PLT2 promoter"

Grade: 9.0/10
