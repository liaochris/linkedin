Michael McCann's Summary

I am a tenured Professor of Law, Director of the Sports Law Institute and Chair of the Faculty Hiring Committee at Vermont Law School, where I teach sports law, antitrust, sales and torts. I am also a Legal Analyst at Sports Illustrated, the "Sports and the Law" Columnist on SI.com, and Co-Founder of the Project on Law and Mind Sciences at Harvard Law School.

I also teach a sports law and analytics reading group at Yale Law School. It is the first sports law and analytics course to be offered at a law school.

I have placed scholarly articles in such publications as the Yale Law Journal, Boston College Law Review, Wisconsin Law Review, Connecticut Law Review and Harvard Journal of Sports and Entertainment Law. My most recent article is American Needle v. NFL: An Opportunity to Reshape Sports Law, 119 Yale Law Journal 726 (2010).

I am also the Distinguished Visiting Hall of Fame Professor of Law at Mississippi College School of Law, where I was an Assistant Professor of Law between 2005 and 2008 and where I now teach a sports law class every summer. During my three years of full-time teaching in Mississippi, I received the Professor of the Year Award in 2007 and 2008, the Professor of the Year for First-Year Courses Award in 2006, 2007, and 2008, and the Shirley Norwood Jones Faculty Award in 2008.

Prior to becoming a law professor, I served as counsel to college football star Maurice Clarett in his lawsuit against the National Football League and its age eligibility rule (Clarett v. NFL, 369 F.3d 124 (2nd Cir. 2004). I also served as a Visiting Researcher at Harvard Law School and Legal Counsel to U.S. Congressman Marty Meehan.

I have been interviewed on sports law and sports business issues on a number of national television programs, including HBO's Bob Costas Now, CNN's The Situation Room with Wolf Blitzer, American Morning, Headline News, and Glenn Beck Show, Fox News' Fox Live Desk, and CNBC's Morning Call and Power Lunch.

Specialties

Sports Law, Antitrust, Torts, Sales, Administrative Law, Consumer Law, and Food and Drug Law.
