Ellen St. George Godfrey’s Summary

Elli St.George Godfrey is passionate about entrepreneurial small business owners to think, plan, and stay sane while leading their organizations. Combining a background in psychology and her work with small business owners, she has developed a 3 keys process that integrates coaching with metrics that can be seen in the bottom line and in each person. By utilizing short-term coaching cycles, her clients feel confident in identifying and developing strategies to be more effective leaders, plan more creatively, innovate, and overcome the fears and obstacles that interfere with building thriving small businesses.

Speaking Topics for small business owners:

Using Living Business Plans: The Best Kept Secret For Focused Business Growth

Leading Your Teams Beyond Fear and Panic

How To Use the CEO MIndset for Small Business Success

Later Isn't Coming: 5 Strategies To Stop Procrastination



Elli has worked for both profit and nonprofit organizations including food, retail, and mental health industries. Her experience in the mental health field spans 17 years. Her work and volunteer history includes positions in program development as well as management and leadership and her varied experiences contribute to her creativity, her ability to connect with people, and a deep appreciation for learning and understanding of each client’s business style and culture.

Elli is a past 4-term board member for the Boston chapter of the National Association of Women Business Owners (NAWBO). Elli finds being a member of networking groups is a great way to avoid isolation and build relationships.

Elli trained at the Coaches Training Institute. She received her baccalaureate from Emmanuel College and a Master’s of Arts from Antioch University New England.
