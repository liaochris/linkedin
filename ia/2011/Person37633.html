Privately Held; Motion Pictures and Film industry

June 2006 – August 2009 (3 years 3 months)

As a Digital Compositor my job profile required me to shuffle between various tasks and taking different responsibilities depending upon the nature of work required in a particular project. After the initial start on this job with the movie Vivah as a roto artist trainee I eventually turned to Compositing as I found the medium to be very challenging and creative with ample scope for developing a strong skill-set.

My works ranged from compositing green screen/ live action footage with CGI elements or live background plates on movies like Kaminey, Jaggubhai, 1920, Laaga Chunari Mein Daag, The Japanese Wife & Dhamaal. These projects also rigorously trained me to handle huge amounts of work and deliver them on time. In addition to that, doing highly advanced to complicated Paint and Rotoscopy for Wire and Rig Removal was also a frequent task. It even sometimes involved Motion Tracking for CGI as well as Matte painted elements (Mumbai Salsa), creating background plates for cleanup in Adobe Photoshop, Logo conceptualization and Animation plus additional optical transitions and titles on movies lie Dhan Dhana Dhan Goal, Firaaq.

My job also enabled me to surf the web for references, and therefore I have a good experience of combing the Internet to find material in relation to the shot. Sometimes, stock photography downloaded off the Internet helped us create matte paintings for movies (God lives in the Himalayas - Nepalese). My superiors helped me gain the knowledge of different shot framing techniques, which has so far proved indispensible in some cases, especially its helped me a lot while video editing my project film 10 a.m.
