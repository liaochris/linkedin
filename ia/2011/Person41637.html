Steven King’s Summary

8 years of experience in the DHS Office of Infrastructure Protection at DHS headquarters. Rapidly promoted within the organization to include managing $30M budget and hundreds of senior technical/professional federal employees and contracts. Represented DHS at the White House and on Capitol Hill on chemical and biological security issues. Currently representing DHS at the US Department of Justice.

22 years of experience as a firefighter, designated fire officer, fire marshal, and EMT/paramedic in both career and volunteer departments. Former sworn local law enforcement officer and fire/explosion investigator.

Published author and frequent presenter at national and international conferences on issues related to infrastructure protection, terrorism, firefighting and emergency medical services (EMS), and emergency response planning and operations.

Doctoral candidate with an MBA and a Master of Science in Biodefense. Senior Executive Fellow at Harvard University's Kennedy School. Board Certified in Security Management as a Certified Protection Professional (CPP), certified PMI Project Management Professional (PMP), Certified in Homeland Security - Level V (CHS-V), and numerous firefighting, technical rescue, EMS, and law enforcement related certifications.

Steven King’s Specialties:

Homeland Security, critical infrastructure protection, suspicious activity reporting, biological and chemical security, CFATS, project management, fire/rescue, emergency medical services, explosives training, intelligence analysis, risk management, emergency preparedness, public speaking, program management, and emergency management

  • Division Director

    DHS Headquarters

    (Government Agency; Government Administration industry)

    July 2009 — October 2010 (1 year 4 months)

    Division Director for the Contingency Planning and Incident Management Division (CPIMD) in the Office of Infrastructure Protection at DHS headquarters. The division ensures that the Department's infrastructure protection mission is integrated into the nation's unified approach to domestic incident management. In response to potential threats or actual events affecting critical infrastructure, CPIMD's National Infrastructure Coordinating Center (NICC) shares timely, relevant, and accurate information with critical infrastructure private sector owners and operators, DHS senior leadership, other government agencies, and nongovernmental organizations.

  • Deputy Director

    Department of Homeland Security

    (Government Administration industry)

    November 2007 — August 2008 (10 months)

    Deputy Director of the U.S. Department of Homeland Security’s Sector-Specific Agency Executive Management Office (SSA EMO). I was the principal author of the congressionally mandated National Strategy for Securing the Chemical Sector, the DHS Chemical Sector-Specific Plan for the National Infrastructure Protection Plan (NIPP) and the DHS Office of Infrastructure Protection’s Best Practices for Infrastructure Protection guidebook. Also served as COTR for multiple contracts.
