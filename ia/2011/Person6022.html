    I generated primary game documentation.     I presented products to the press in high-profile venues and online interactions. I participated in "developer vs community" in-game matches.

    I provided feedback to other teams on their own products on what worked and where I felt the product could be improved - both in realistic timeframes, and in the long term (for future iterations of the product.)

    I generated a large number of product pitches. I led tech demo sprints - one-week efforts to put together a brief but noteworthy example of how a game would work after a full product cycle.

  • Designer / Scripter

    n-Space

    (Privately Held; Computer Games industry)

    March 2007 — December 2007 (10 months)

    I was responsible for adapting general level descriptions and instructions from my lead to a 3D level's layout. This included elements like AI placement, AI pathing, object and destructible placement, management of save points and power ups, and ensuring the overall fun factor and stability of the level.

    In addition, I playtested other designers' levels and provided feedback on what worked and what didn't, and what was fun and what wasn't.

    I also created extensive documentation on areas of the game that needed it. I helped design, in particular, the player's progression curve through the game, as well as the individual powers that the player could accumulate. I provided instruction to the animators and effects artists that put the powers together, as well as the engineers who were responsible for implimenting those powers.

    I was almost entirely in charge of story implementation for the game. I managed both dialogues and cinematic sequences. I made sure that the story (delivered to us by the client) was presented in a logical, entertaining fashion with appropriate camera work, animation support, and sound queues.

    I also provided scripting support to other products who needed additional hands-on-deck.
