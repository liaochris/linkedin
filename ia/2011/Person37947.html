Ryan Parman's Summary

Ryan Parman is an entrepreneur, open source evangelist, fierce user advocate, and a web multidisciplinarian who takes a wide view of the whole system. He is the founder and visionary behind SimplePie™ and CloudFusion™, co-founder of WarpShare™, member of the RSS Advisory Board, and creator of the AWS SDK for PHP.

Founding SimplePie in 2004, he grew the open-source project until it became the world's most popular RSS software for PHP developers. SimplePie has been bundled with popular open-source projects like WordPress, Joomla! and Drupal, and has been used by companies such as Yahoo!, Sony, and MTV Networks. SimplePie is currently in-use by hundreds of thousands of websites, all over the world.

In 2006, four digital natives wondered why it was so hard to watch, listen and share their favorite music, movies and TV shows with their friends. WarpShare was created to make it fun and easy to enjoy your favorite content with your friends, in a way that is fair to the people who create the content we all enjoy.

In 2008, Ryan and WarpShare open-sourced CloudFusion — a fast, easy-to-use toolkit for working with Amazon Web Services. Because of the popularity of CloudFusion with Amazon's customers, Amazon chose to adopt CloudFusion in early 2010 as the basis for the official AWS SDK for PHP.

Ryan inherently understands that the customer is the single most valuable asset an organization can have, and is driven by the unrelenting pursuit of customer-driven focus, ideals and user experience. His attention to quality and customers has garnered raving fans all over the world.

Specialties

Running startup & open-source project teams (e.g. WarpShare, SimplePie, CloudFusion). Front-end web engineering, performance and accessibility (e.g. HTML, CSS, JavaScript/JSON, Ajax, YUI, jQuery, WCAG, 508, cross-browser development). Designing web service APIs and developer-facing SDKs.

Ryan Parman's Patents

  • Hive-based peer-to-peer network

    • United States Patent WO/2008/033424
    • Issued December 9, 2007
    Inventors: Ryan Parman, Matthew Clower, Vada Dean, Joe Ross

    A collaborative data transferring process can combine segments from all known servers and peer-to-peer (P2P) sources simultaneously, regardless of their native protocols. The process uses variable data block size that can be dynamically selected according to sizes provided by sources, e.g., according to the protocol of the source, and can generate hash values or validation codes on the fly so that compliance with validation techniques (if any) of other protocols is not required. The process may be classified as a P2P protocol, although it also contains centralized elements. Machine language implementations and low syntax overhead allow file exchanges over a homogeneous network with high throughput and low bandwidth consumption.
