Sanchita Saha's Summary

Sanchita is a UCL Mathematics graduate who enjoyed a successful 7 year career at the BBC across the areas of Finance, Operations, Production and Marketing.

Her last role there was to help start up and launch the BBC's digital black music station 1Xtra before leaving to further whet her entrepreneurial appetite. She began testing and developing her idea for an events-based social network for young professionals based in and relocating to UK cities, to meet new local friends with shared interests and explore the city's social scene.

Sanchita's vision was to combine online tools with a real world social experience and, with a Prince's Trust loan, 18 months of R&D followed, during which she was nominated as a regional finalist in the Shell Livewire Entrepreneur Awards '05.

Raising friends & family investment to build her v1 site, she officially launched CitySocialising.com as a subscription-based service in Jan '07. It quickly became London's leading socialising network for professionals living in, working in and relocating to the Capital and within 8 months the business financed its national expansion. Today the model has been successfully replicated in 19 cities including Leeds, Manchester, Bristol, Edinburgh & Glasgow and rolled out to a further 30 cities in June 2010.

In March 2009 she successfully raised £260k early-stage investment from the London Business Angels network, and most recently, in Nov 2010, a £1m Series A round led by European VC fund PROfounders, launched by tech luminaries Brent Hoberman (Lastminute.com) and Michael Birch (Bebo.com).
