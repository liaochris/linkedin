John Harmston’s Summary

I have fifteen years experience in marketing. I’m proficient in a variety of disciplines, from product marketing to corporate communications. Also experienced in all aspects of online and buzz marketing. Let me share some principles I have learned over the years that guide my marketing strategy:

Gut Check Time
One of the most critical components to successful marketing is finding out what the customers actually want. While your organization might have expertise in a particular area, your marketing is destined to fail if the product benefits don’t match the customer’s needs. I believe people depend too much on their "gut instinct", and not enough on actual research. “Your opinion, although interesting is irrelevant.”

John’s USP
“The best way to have a good idea is to have a lot of ideas.” The single greatest strength I bring to the table is creativity; out-of-the-box problem solving is my forte. This is manifest in my portfolio, but it is my brainstorming ability that really sets me apart. During a recent brainstorming session I came up with over 2,000 potential name candidates for a new product brand. I would be happy to show you the list.

Portfolio Sample
I invite you to view a couple of Flash movies that I created for past employers. The “Meet Max” Flash tour introduces Max the dog, the highly-successful mascot I created for the Photomax brand, by walking you through his photo album as you learn more about photomax.com. The “BP Internet Security” Flash teaser provides a brief introduction to the product’s benefits while dramatically motivating potential customers to download a free trial. To view the samples, simply visit www.harmston.com.
