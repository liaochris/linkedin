Joseph Bernstein’s Education

  • University of Michigan

    Ph.D. , Astronomy & Astrophysics , 2000 — 2007

    Doctoral dissertation: "Probing the Depths: Hydrodynamic Simulations and X-ray Observations of Pulsar Wind Nebulae". Relativistic, hydrodynamic simulations showed that the interaction of relativistic pulsar winds with the ambient medium can give rise to asymmetry reminiscent of the Guitar nebula and a relativistic backflow harboring internal shocks waves. Chandra X-ray Observatory observations of the supernova remnant MSH 11-62 showed that pulsar wind nebula asymmetry strongly suggests that the remnant reverse shock crushed the nebula and that the ambient medium has dynamically affected the remnant/nebula morphology.
