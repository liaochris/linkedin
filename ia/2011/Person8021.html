Chris Austria’s Summary

Chris Austria is the Director of Music Licensing for Independent Distribution Collective (IDC), a music distribution and licensing company in San Francisco. Austria is responsible for managing the music catalogue, producing , and finding content for music supervisors in TV and Film. IDC has a diverse catalogue of original music from E-40, K'naan, Black Sheep, Hieroglyphics, Eartha Moore (Grammy award winner) Dr. Fink from Prince and the Revolution, Pharoah, (who won a Grammy for writing"Blame It On The Alcohol,") and a variety of artists. Our list of genres, including Latin, reggaeton, country, urban, rock, and doo wopp. . IDC has licensed music for Universal Pictures, DreamWorks Animation, The Oprah Winfrey Show, EA Sports, NBC Universal’s Oxygen network, E! Entertainment, MTV, and the USA network. Austria is also a co-owner of Peak Performance Music Publishing with veteran entertainment lawyer, Michael A. Aczon. They are currently licensing their catalogue to FOX Sports Music. Prior to working at IDC, Austria worked at the Right's Workshop, representing Carly Simon's catalogue for licensing.

Austria grew up in a musical family. His father was a violinist in the San Francisco Symphony for 35 years and his mom was a flamenco guitarist. Austria is a graduate of Pyramind, an audio production training center in San Francisco. Austria also spent 13 years as a tiger and lion trainer, working at Six Flags Marine World in Vallejo, CA and Siegfried and Roy in Las Vegas. While at Marine World, he composed and produced music for the tiger show. Austria has served as a tiger consultant for ABC, CBS, and has been featured on The Early Show and Nightline. You can read more about his musical tiger adventures on his blog at www.chrisaustriatigers.blogspot.com
