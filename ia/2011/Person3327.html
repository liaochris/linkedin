Anthony Russo’s Summary

I am a family man first and foremost and work by integrity over money.

Throughout my adult life I have had a number of jobs and careers. They have primarily been in regards to selling, managing, collecting, and a number of other fields here and there. Now looking back on them, I notice a common theme in them all.

No matter what the position or field I am working with, I almost always gravitated to a position of teaching others. In many instances, this was an expected component of the position. In many others though, it just came naturally for me and was recognized by my superiors that I had an excellent grasp on the job at hand, and an uncanny knack for being able to have others learn it as well.

As I look further into it now, I notice that even in positions where I was not "training" someone specifically. I was still teaching. In sales you are teaching the customer why they need your company's product or service and a good salesperson answers more questions than makes general statements or claims about their product. In my past roles in collections, I would be explaining to a person the best way to accomplish their goal of paying off their debt, and the steps to take to do so. It seems that my strength in many positions I have had in my life is the ability to relay information to others in a way that they can understand and agree with.

I look presently for a company that approaches their leader positions in the same aspect. A good leader does not "whip people into shape" but rather gives them the tools and knowledge for them to do the very best job they can accomplish. A great leader brings them to a point they did not even know they were going to.
