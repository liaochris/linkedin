  • President

    TLS Associates

    (Human Resources industry)

    July 2009 — Present (1 year 7 months)

    Executive Search and Human Resources Consulting for Management and Boards of Directors of for-profit companies and not-for-profit organizations.

    At the end of August, I completed a month-long contract assignment for the Town Manager in Barnstable, the largest town on Cape Cod. The assignment was to evaluate the operations and management of a new, $25 million town facility. A beautiful building of 105,000 square feet with a gymnasium, walking track, and two skating rinks, the Hyannis Youth & Community Center opened one year ago. My assessment of the strengths and areas for improvement at the Center highlighted a variety of issues, both immediate and longer-term, that need to be addressed in order to improve operations, boost revenues and control energy costs, and maintain the facility properly. The Town Manager, John Klimm, was pleased with my work, indicating: “You nailed it!” and asking me to conduct a follow-up assessment a year from now.

    In late August, I embarked on a more extended contract assignment, working with the President, staff, faculty, and students at Bunker Hill Community College on issues pertaining to diversity and inclusion. Bunker Hill is the largest community college in the Commonwealth, enrolling more than 11,700 students on two campuses and at four satellite locations. BHCC is one of the most diverse institutions of higher education in the state. Six in ten students are people of color, and more than half are women. The College, which provides a gateway to the “American Dream” to those who need it most, also enrolls more than 800 international students who come from more than 95 countries and speak 75 different languages. Its faculty and staff are more diverse than the workforce at any other institution of higher learning in Massachusetts.

    This assignment will give me an introduction to work in the world of higher education. It will also mean that networking in Greater Boston will be much easier.
