Catastrophe Risk analysis is a backbone for pricing a Reinsurance program. It gives support and confidence to the underwriter for writing a Optimal business.

Catastrophe Risk modeling is especially applicable to analysing risks in the nonlife-insurance industry and is at the confluence of Financial understanding, actuarial science, engineering, meteorology, and seismology.

My work primarily focused on modeling Catastrophe risks using vendor models and VBA (MS Excel). As a risk analyst I have been involved in analytical as well as organizational activities and work closely with actuarial department and underwriting. Interactions with underwriters have blended this intense analytical work into more involved reinsurance underwriting process. I have been responsible for doing risk estimation and preparing reports for supporting underwriters.

- Analyzed and interpreted catastrophe risks associated with portfolios of major Non-life insurance companies across the world and understand the financial structure of the program.

- Proficiently used state of the art catastrophe risk models (RiskLink, CLASIC/2, CATRADER and EQECAT) along with SQL and VBA .

- Risk analysis of Japanese insurance companies: involving intricate insurance policy structures, and working on In-house aggregate vendor model for aggregate analyses.

- Responsible for modeling untraditional perils like Workers Compensation, terrorism and offshore modeling using standard Vendor models.

- Also have modeled Aggregate for several countries, involves interpreting and analyzing the complex raw exposure and premium data of Insurance Companies.
