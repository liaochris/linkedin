Research Assistant

Aalto University

Educational Institution; Higher Education industry

September 2009 – June 2010 (10 months)

Master's Thesis worker at SoberIT's MaPIT research project. The aim of MaPIT research project was to address the challenges of distributed software development projects and to find solutions and useful practices for managing and working in globally distributed projects. The project concentrated on six essential areas: software development processes, communication and collaboration practices, IT-tool support, practices for building and maintaining trust, effects of culture, and managing distributed projects.

My task in the research project was to analyze four industrial global software development projects, concentrating mainly on the initiation and preparation phases of these global projects. The findings from the four indstrial projects and a literature study can be found from my Master's Thesis, "Preparing and Initiating a Globally Distributed Software Development Project". During my time at the MaPIT research project, I also participated in writing a chapter for the MaPIT workbook, "Practical Guide to Managing Distributed Software Development Projects". After the completion of my Master's Thesis, I did a short literature study on measurement and metrics in agile software development projects.

Social Media Developer

Kaisla Innovations

Internet industry

May 2009 – December 2009 (8 months)

During my time at a start-up company, Kaisla Innovations, I was working together with a team of eight people on a social media service called Illtags. The idea of Illtags was to prevent people from catching and spreading common cold, influenza and other contagious diseases. My main responsibility was to develop Facebook and Twitter applications for the service. Part of the project's funding came from Slush Helsinki, a business plan competition that Illtags won.

Web Developer

Activeark

Public Company; Marketing and Advertising industry

May 2007 – April 2009 (2 years)

Being part of the technical team at Activeark, my main responsibility was to develop websites and applications based on the requirements set by the customers. My work consisted also of building user interfaces based on the graphical designs and guidelines set by members from the design team. (X)HTML, CSS and JavaScript were used for building interactive, and both semantically rich and valid user interfaces. Back-end development was done using PHP and MySQL in order to integrate an existing content management system or to build a custom back-end solution. Used content management systems included frameworks such as Wordpress, Joomla and ExpressionEngine. During my time at ActiveArk, I worked on over 20 projects, out of which several were for high profile clients.
