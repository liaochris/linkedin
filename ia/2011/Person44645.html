Rob Fay's Summary

Rob Fay is Blackboard's User Experience Architect. In his role of being the user advocate, he leads the company's user research efforts, curates their design pattern library, and designs products that transform the experience of education.

He has a passion for user experience topics including information architecture, interaction design, social media, information management, and usability.

Rob holds graduate degrees in Information Management and Marriage and Family Therapy from the University of Maryland, College Park. He lives in the outskirts of DC in suburban Maryland with his wife, twin 5 yo daughters, and his dog named Mattingly.

Responsible for being the user advocate and effectively communicating product design needs. Maintains knowledge base of current usability research, theories and methodologies (especially user-centered design processes) and industry best-practices. Senior Information Architect

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; E-Learning industry

Additionally, played a critical role in crafting broader interaction models between K12's digital products, K12's internal staff, teachers and principals of its schools, and-most importantly, the students.

Employed a user-centered methodology to create functional vision and documented artifacts to meet the needs of both product users and developers. In addition, designed product functionality, navigation flows, site architecture plans, wireframes, user interface design, and functional specifications for the presentation layer.
