Mark Lee’s Summary

I have two primary roles within my portfolio career (a status I chose to adopt in 2006).

1) Head of Network at Winmark where I run the Tax Director Network and the FD Chamber. Members of these networks are, respectively, Tax Directors or FDs.

2) Chairman of the Tax Advice Network: Our members are typically independent tax advisers and we promote their services specifically to UK accountants in general practice.

I am also a frequent speaker, commentator and blogger on UK tax matters and the accountancy profession. I've been interviewed for BBC Newsnight, Working Lunch, News and Breakfast. And I've been published by most of the top Accountancy and Tax press.

By way of background I qualified as a Chartered Accountant (not the boring type) in 1982 and then worked my way up to become a partner in two top ten firms of accountants in London. I was also elected Chairman of the ICAEW Tax Faculty and remain active on a number of Institute committees as well as the LSCA Tax Committee and the LCCI Tax Committee.

Having spent 25 years advising on tax and achieving a great deal in my tax career I realised that I had stopped getting a buzz from actually giving tax advice. I also realised that I had much preferred those aspects of my roles that involved different aspect of business development activities.

Running three quite different but high quality professional networks allows me to remain active in the profession and plays to my strengths. Best of all none of my roles involves me in providing tax advice to anyone. Perfect!

Away from work I've been deputy Treasurer of a children and family services charity (Norwood) since the late 1990s. And I try to make time for my long established hobby of 'magic'. I'm still a fully paid up member of The Magic Circle even though I perform far less frequently than once was the case.

I am flattered by many of the kind words contained in the recommendations that have been attached to my LinkedIn profile.
