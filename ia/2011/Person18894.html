James Rhodes's Summary

Biography

James Rhodes is Managing Partner, Executive Director and co-founder of Magnuss, LLC, a leading renewable energy ship propulsion technology firm. Magnuss develops onboard systems that reduce fuel consumption and emissions for today’s global shipping fleet. Mr. Rhodes brings more than 20 years of experience in renewable energy project finance and development, investment banking and strategic consulting. He is a frequent speaker at industry events on sustainable energy, shipping technology and the global carbon markets.

Mr. Rhodes was CEO of Eco Capital LLC, an energy advisory firm. Among assignments in renewable energy and carbon finance, Mr. Rhodes advised General Electric and AES Corporation on its US carbon business, which made investments in renewable energy and emissions reduction projects. In 2008, he was appointed Chief Operating Officer of GE/AES GHG Services, LLC, a venture between GE and AES, and served as a member of its Executive Management and Investment Committees. He led strategy, investments, M&A, business development, project development, and carbon asset management.

Before founding Eco Capital, Mr. Rhodes was Managing Director of Morgan Stanley and Head of US Power and Energy Fixed Income Capital Markets and Derivatives. Within the power and energy sectors, he served organizations in twelve years with the firm ranging from global Fortune 100 to emerging growth companies and completed over 150 advisory assignments. Prior to joining Morgan Stanley, Mr. Rhodes spent ten years in investment banking at Goldman Sachs and in management consulting at Booz Allen & Hamilton. He holds an M.B.A. with distinction from Columbia Business School and a B.S. in Electrical Engineering from Brown University.

Specialties

Expertise in investment management, venture capital, private equity, investment banking, strategic consulting, business strategy, finance, mergers and acquisitions, risk management, derivatives and capital markets in the renewable energy, clean technology, carbon finance, power, utilities, oil and gas sectors.
