Emily-Anne Rigal’s Summary

Emily-Anne Rigal is an enthusiastic, hard-working sixteen year old from Virginia. She enjoys making YouTube videos on her channel, “Schmiddlebopper,” where she has received the support of over 3,500 subscribers and well-over 125,000 video views. Emily-Anne is the founder and director of WeStopHate, a program whose mission is to empower teens through the power of social media.

Emily-Anne has a passion for radio and television and began hosting her own weekly segment, Teens Going Green, at a local radio station at the age of fourteen. Emily-Anne has served as president of the daily school news broadcast, Trojan TV, since she was a freshman. She is a natural entertainer who loves performing in her school productions and is rarely seen without a smile on her face. In her spare time, Emily-Anne loves to read inspirational quotes, autobiographies, watch Bravo reality shows, and tweet – a lot!

"Emily-Anne has that rare combination of talent, intelligence, and personality. I was continually impressed by her abilities in class and by her determination to make something of herself. As an eighth grader in our high school she soon worked her way to being the President of our TV news and the lead in a play, as well as volunteering for important leadership roles in school organizations. She is a tireless worker and very focused. And she is a joy to work with – her presence in a classroom brings a palpable energy and welcome humor." Steven DeLaney, Walsingham Academy

"Walsingham Academy benefits greatly from Emily-Anne’s creative energy. Her efficiency keeps the Trojan TV morning news personnel on track and her on-air personality adds a positive boost to every broadcast. I enthusiastically provide her with opportunities in public speaking and use her frequently as a master-of-ceremonies because she has talent and gives 100% to every project." Ree Stone, Drama Club and Trojan TV Moderator
