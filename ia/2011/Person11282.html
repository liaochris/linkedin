Debbie Reinhardt's Summary

In the nearly 20 years as managing editor of AAA Midwest Traveler, I still get a kick out of opening a carton of magazines and seeing what I helped to create. I had the privilege to launch AAA Southern Traveler 12 years ago. I love making magazines for our 700,000 readers.

As a member of the travel/tourism industry, I believe I've developed a respected reputation not only as an editor of a well-known regional magazine, but also as a travel writer. I love to tell good stories and help writers to polish their prose.

Last year (2009), I launched a Web site and blog that speaks to families in transition. A friend of mine shared this idea, "make your mess your message," and as a 11-year divorce survivor, I can say that mess has been an important component to my life journey. Stop by my site (www.hopefultransitions.com) or blog (hopefultransitions.wordpress.com) to join the conversation or say hello!

In June 2010, I became a certified professional life coach so that I can help people move through life transitions with greater ease into a life of peace and abundance. Remember that the phoenix is reborn from a small pile of ashes! If you are struggling with change in your life, contact me for a free 30-minute consultation.

Interests:

Interests of mine include writing, editing, traveling. I also volunteer as a marketing mentor for the Union Avenue Opera Company in St. Louis (www.unionavenueopera.org). I volunteer for the Adoptive & Foster Care Coalition in St. Louis to help with annual projects and the coalition's clothing store, which provides new or gently used clothing to foster families. I also volunteer for Stray Rescue of St. Louis (www.strayrescue.org), a wonderful non-profit that takes stray dogs and cats off the streets, nurses them back to health, and rehabs them for adoption through an amazing network of 200+ fosters and volunteers. I'm proud to be associated with these community organizations.
