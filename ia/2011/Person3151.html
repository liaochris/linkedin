Anne Marie Graham's Summary

My professional experience in Occupational Health spans 9 years.

I graduated as a Registered General Nurse from Beaumont Hospital, Dublin. I graduated with BSc (Hons) in Health Studies from the Royal College of Nursing, London. From this I progressed in to and specialised in Occupational Health and Safety which led to further, namely Post Graduate Diploma and Master of Arts and in Occupational Health and Safety. Her specialist area in her thesis was Ergonomics with the focus being on “Prevention of Work Related Upper Limb Disorders in Office and Sedentary Workers”

I have worked in both the service provider and the industrial sector of occupational health and safety for the past 9 years and have a wide range of experience within industry including health and wellness, financial services, manufacturing and production, food and hygiene, hospitality and transport and engineering sectors of industry.

I have also been involved in the teaching and training aspects of Occupational Health. I lectured in Dublin City University on the Bachelor of Nursing Studies Program and in Marino CDVEC on the Fetac Module, Safety and Health at Work, Level 5 on a part time basis for 4 years.

I am a member of a number of professional bodies including An Bord Altrainis, The Irish Nurses Organisation, and The Occupational Health Nurses Association of Ireland, the Irish Institute of Training and Development and BNI (Business Networking International).

I have also launched a new program called Weigh Less. This is a motivational and behavioural change weight loss program where I work with individuals for 12 weeks. The program is safe, uses healthy principals. It guarantees a weight loss of at least 12 pounds!
