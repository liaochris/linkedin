Nicholas Schwartz’s Summary

Nicholas Schwartz is a new employee (as of August 2010) in the Threat & Vulnerability Assessment: Threat Forensics and Surveillance department of Information Security at BNY Mellon. He is a recent graduate of the Bard College Class of 2010, where he majored in Political Science and Global and International Affairs with minors in East Asian Studies and Social Psychology.

While attending Bard College’s Globalization and International Affairs program for two years, he attended night classes while working days as a social media intern for the National Committee on United States-China Relations as well as consulting in Chinese Language Initiatives at Asia Society. These experiences culminated in his bachelor’s thesis ‘Chinese Internet Nationalism: The Resurgence of the Public Sphere in Mainland China and its Consequences’.

He is an unrelenting technologist, bringing both his experiences with and his love of computer architecture and networking to otherwise technologically devoid problems or issues. He looks forward to expanding his technological skill sets through his work at BNY Mellon.
