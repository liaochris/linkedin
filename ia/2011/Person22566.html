Joshua Li's Summary

Gmail ID: joshxli

I have done all kinds of quantitative tradings after moved to finance from software industry.

I started from trading fundamental based models using accounting and analyst forecast data. These models explore various equity asset pricing anomalies. This was a perfect combination of my newly learned skills in financial statement analysis, asset pricing theories from Wharton School and my previous programming skills as a principal software engineer from Oracle Corporation.

Then, I received two offers from Lehman and one from Goldman. As 2 is larger than 1, I moved to Lehman. What a simple decision! The Lehman job was very technical oriented. We were coding ultra high frequency co-lo system to be an automatic market maker. I got myself familiar with market micro-structure, FIX, Tibco, KDB, ITCH, OUCH and so on.

Dig myself out of Lehman dust, I moved to my current hedge fund building systematical futures trading models. After mining hundreds gigabytes of data and running hundreds of thousands simulations, I found a few interesting models trading futures in global markets.
One of my intraday model generated Sharpe ratio above 4 in the simulation with previous 25 years data. One of my daily models had been alive in last year. With some luck, the real time trading generated compounded annual return around 40% with Sharpe ratio 3.

Specialties

Futures, trend-following, systematical trading, high frequency trading, quantitative trading, Bloomberg, Reuters, Series 7, Series 63, stat arb, ITCH, OUCH, FIX, SDE, Brownian Motion, KDB, SQL, Q, PL/SQL, Tibco, C/C++, OOA/OOD/OOP, design pattern, Solaris/Linux/Windows, financial modeling, market anomalies, asset pricing, market micro-structure
