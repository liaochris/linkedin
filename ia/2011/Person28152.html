Mark Blayney Stuart's Summary

Mark was awarded the Somerset Maugham Prize for 'Two Kinds of Silence'.

His second book Conversations with Magic Stones was described by JOHN BAYLEY as ‘remarkable stories… as good as some of the best of Elizabeth Bowen’s, and praise cannot go higher than that.’

Mark blogs for The Guardian, has been profiled on BBC4, appears regularly on BBC Radio Wales and has published stories in London Magazine.

Mark is Head of Research for the Chartered Institute of Marketing where he publishes regularly, speaks across the UK, has written for The Independent and appears on BBC Radio 4.

His third book 'Five Nights in Seville' is forthcoming. Mark was born in 1973 and lives in Cardiff.

Mark Blayney Stuart's Publications

  • What Hasn't Happened Yet: the shape of digital to come?

    • The Chartered Institute of Marketing
    • March 23, 2010
    Authors: Mark Blayney Stuart

    We now routinely use technologies that a century ago would have seemed like magic. Cars that can guide us to our destination, somehow knowing exactly where we are. Animations that we can part of as they are happening. Messages that can be read on the other side of the world, a moment after being sent.

    If technology has become magic, then marketers are the alchemists because marketers often know what customers want before the customers do themselves. You liked this DVD? Here are three more DVDs you’ll like. We noticed you went to Cyprus on holiday; well you’ll love Dubrovnik in Croatia; and by the way, here are some hotel and hire car options that will appeal to you. It’s your mother’s birthday next week; do you want to send a card?

    Sometimes we worry we’re buying things on the recommendations of a robot, but we’re not: the robot is merely enabling dialogues that weren’t previously possible. There’s far more potential for companies to respond to customers’ needs and wants in ways they like, and that won’t be seen as intrusive, than are currently being taken advantage of.

    This paper taps into some of the magic behind leading companies’ digital successes, with examples from companies as diverse as 3M, Cadbury’s, Yahoo! and Dell.

    Free download at www.cim.co.uk.
