Rachel Kinney's Summary

I have a variety of experience and interests that have led me on an interesting career path so far. I have always been committed to making the world a better place by volunteering my time and working with disadvantaged groups and at risk youth. After graduating from CSU, Chico, I went to work as a technical sourcer/recruiter for Google. That experience taught me the ins and outs of the staffing world, created in me a passion for new technology, and showed me how progressive a company can be with regards to the way they treat their workers and product, while caring about their impact on the environment. After several short-term sourcing contracts with nVIDIA and Microsoft, I moved into public service by becoming a part of the inaugural San Francisco City Hall Fellows program. This one year public policy fellowship showed me what it takes to run a city from the inside out. I was placed in SF City Hall's Office of Labor Standards Enforcement, working as a Compliance Officer for the Employer Spending Requirement of the Health Care Security Ordinance (HCSO), dealing with enforcement and compliance issues. The Employer Spending Requirement requires covered employers to provide minimum health care benefits for their employees working in SF. This is the groundbreaking law which created the Healthy San Francisco universal health access program, which has been examined as a model for universal healthcare at the national level, and is commonly referred to as Healthy San Francisco.

Now I work for SEIU-UHW, United Healthcare Workers West, as a research analyst for the homecare team. I provide the necessary information to help workers organize and fight for their rights. I am learning much more technical work, such as budget/financial analysis and costing, and I look forward to continuing to be a part of the national healthcare debate and fighting for workers!

Specialties

In-Home Supportive Services (IHSS), SF's Health Care Security Ordinance (HCSO), employer/employee labor laws and compliance issues including compliance audits and investigations; public policy, civic leadership, technical sourcing and recruiting, boolean search, education/teaching, leadership development.
