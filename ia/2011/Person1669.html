Alison Clancy's Summary

In 2004, I was a freshman at Umass Amherst, which happened to be one of the first 10 schools in the world to gain access to Facebook, a social networking site for college students which would soon grow to become one of the biggest online communities in the world. I think of that year as my introduction course to the world of social media, and from there it's only been a natural progression to become the Social Media Manager for the internationally-known food non-profit Oldways. The years I spent there taught me more than I could have ever prepared for, and I feel very confident in my new role in Seattle as the Online Marketing Manager at LiquidPlanner.

When I transferred to Northeastern University, I spent most of my college career working on campus at Snell Library as a Marketing and Events Assistant. Aside from doing office administration and traditional marketing, this is where I started to really learn about online marketing for a business or institution. In these pre-Twitter days, I ran the library's blogging efforts, YouTube channels, Facebook Group (not Page yet), and started to build a real community. It was a wonderful experience, and I'm so fortunate to have found a place that was so open to new forms of communication.

In the summer of 2008, I discovered Tumblr, a blogging/networking platform that I have continued to pledge my loyalty to, which introduced me to a large Boston-based community of social media professionals. In February 2009, I became the first Social Media Manager at Oldways, and I ran our various social media efforts: managing multiple Twitter accounts, writing content for The Oldways Table Blog, and producing and editing videos for our YouTube channel, the OldwaysTube.

My future goals basically include consistently keeping up with new technologies and acting as a confident and personable voice for great companies like LiquidPlanner.

Specialties

Social media, online community managing, online marketing, blogging, communications, writing, networking, new technologies, brand definition, the interwebz in general
