Mechanical Design Engineer

WJ Communications

Public Company; WJCI; Semiconductors industry

December 1997 – November 2005 (8 years)

I performed the mechanical design of high performance RF equipment, including cell phone infrastructure equipment, high power data radios, RF filters and duplexors, RFID readers, and thin film modules. I designed test fixtures for both manual and automatic testing of RFIC's. I performed thermal analysis of electronics packages from IC's through large, high power equipment using Cosmos/Design Star finite element modeling software. I set up CNC machining capability in our model shop and learned CNC programming using MasterCAM. Design and drafting was performed using AutoCAD and Solidworks. My equipment design involved injection molded plastic, machined aluminum, die cast aluminum, and bent sheet metal. I worked closely with our machinist to create prototypes, samples, and test fixtures, and to improve reliability and tolerance control.

Performance Engineer

Foster Wheeler

Public Company; FWLT; Oil & Energy industry

July 1990 – November 1997 (7 years 5 months)

I performed design and analysis of the heat transfer surface of fossil fuel fired steam generators using classical techniques and proprietary performance software. I worked in an aftermarket department that designed replacement equipment for our own and other OEM's boilers to improve performance or to allow different fuels to be fired. I authored engineering studies detailing the results of our analysis and our recommendations to the Customer. I wrote proposals for the supply of heat transfer equipment for boiler retrofits. I co-authored a paper that detailed the conversion of a steam generator to allow it to fire Powder River Basin coal. I presented this paper to the Joint Power Generation Conference in Orlando Florida in 1996.
