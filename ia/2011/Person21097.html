J. Keith Dunbar’s Summary

Professional Experience:

Today the volatility, uncertainty, complexity, and ambiguity of the global environment create organizational challenges impacting their ability to execute strategy. For organizations to succeed in this environment, they need leaders that think strategically and execute tactically. Leaders that can bring clarity from chaos, but are skilled at creating timely chaos to evolve their organization and team to meet customer needs. Organizations need a leader that can create strategic human capital capabilities that transform challenges into competitive and decision advantage.

A proven global learning leader in the public sector with broad-based experiences and skills , I am recognized for understanding customer needs, integrating human capital and talent management solutions and providing results that have a sustainable impact on organizational culture and the mission. My 25 years of military and federal government experience has prepared me to thrive and excel in organizations facing global challenges. These cumulative experiences provide me a very powerful capability in short supply in today's global talent...the capability of transformational leadership. When coupled with my other strengths in strategic visioning, innovation, risk management, teambuilding and individual motivation, I enable organizational, team and individual success on a global scale.

Based upon this body of my work, I was recently selected for the prestigious Wharton/UPenn Graduate School of Education Executive Program in Work-Based Learning Leadership to create multi-disciplinary Chief Learning Officers. Additionally, I was invited by IBM as one of four global Human Resource leaders to provide video case studies supporting the 2010 Global Chief Human Resource Officer (CHRO) Study.

Goal: Fortune 500 Talent Management, Leadership Development, and/or Learning Executive.
