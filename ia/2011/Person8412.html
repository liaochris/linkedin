Christine Capota’s Summary

Christine Capota is an educational technology enthusiast who specializes in educational media and research projects. Christine holds an Ed. M. from the Harvard Graduate School of Education, where she focused on Technology, Innovation & Education. Her interests lie in educational technology, international development, and psychology.

Prior to her graduate studies, Christine worked as a Digital Media Analyst at Sesame Workshop, the non-profit organization most known for its production of Sesame Street. At Sesame, she analyzed the usage of new digital media initiatives such as podcasts, websites, and mobile video clips. She was also involved in the production of several Latin America projects, including the introduction of Plaza Sesamo to mobile phones in the U.S. and Mexico.

Christine holds a Bachelor of Arts from McGill University in Montreal, where she focused on Psychology and Music. At McGill, she worked as a Music Cognition Researcher for 3 years.

Christine Capota’s Specialties:

educational media, instructional design, new and social media, project management, evaluation and assessment, web analytics, experimental design, formative and summative evaluation, music education, Spanish-language media, outreach
