Clyde Sanadi’s Summary

A developer of ERP and PC software early on, software marketing manager at Wang Labs for eight years, on faculty at USF's Business School for five years, sell-side technology M&A broker for three years, product marketing manager at Treev/Ceyoniq (now Metavante) for three years, consultant to technology firms and entrepreneurs, part-time promoter of Latin music and adventure travel.

Clyde Sanadi’s Specialties:

Product marketing, industry research, business development, branding, presentations, marcom, financial analysis, strategic alliances, M&A, software distribution, patent licensing, music promotion.

  • Product Marketing

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; Computer Software industry)

    1999 — 2002 (3 years )

  • Technology M&A Broker

    Windward Group

    (Investment Banking industry)

    1996 — 1999 (3 years )

    Sold private technology firms to public acquirers and private equity firms, advised in $200M in M&A transactions.

    Created a highly competitive environment at the CEO/SVP level between Beta Systems, Sterling, EMC, CA, Platinum and Legato that resulted in a $20M cash offer for HARBOR, a $3.5M storage management vendor.

    Other M&A clients included newspaper circulation, real-time data-driven Java graphics, Oracle Automotive EDI, process control middleware, network management, mobile data terminal, oil-well pump optimization, warehouse logistics, digital stock image, order management, production scheduling, and fiber optics vendors.

    Engaged by Lotus to coach their partners in marketing, led development and delivery of a 17-city workshop program to marketing directors of 750+ Lotus business partners.

    Founded Dolphin Labware with a biochemist, branded the technology, and negotiated patent licensing terms with a $6.3B life sciences vendor in an agreement valued at $3.2M.

    On board of directors of MediaMap, a marketing automation and technology media and analyst research firm serving PR agencies, from start-up to Inc. 500 recognition.

  • ISV Software Marketing

    Wang Laboratories

    (Public Company; WANG; Computer Software industry)

    1982 — 1990 (8 years )

    Marketing manager for Peachtree Software as Wang led in office automation against IBM, as the PC industry emerged.

    Negotiated and managed strategic alliances with leading software vendors including Microsoft, Informix, Software Arts, Borland, Attachmate, SPSS, Oracle, SAS, Symantec, Caelus, Computron and MCBA.

    Marketed vendor software to direct and partner channels, identified a $100M annual market, authored the business plan, secured $2.3M in budget, led Wang’s entry into this space against Unisys.

    As a product marketing manager, presented desktop strategy to Wang’s national accounts, drove product requirements for major bids, rolled out graphics, imaging, and business intelligence products.

    Recruited over 100 PC software vendors as part of a small team, sourced 25+ software products from ISVs, built a $15M revenue stream, and managed marketing relationships with data base, manufacturing, sales force automation, system tools and accounting software vendors.

  • Programmer

    Mann Data | CU | Brown Shoe

    (Computer Software industry)

    1974 — 1982 (8 years )

    Sold custom software, MAPICS modifications and ERP applications from Software International and Berkus Systems to manufacturers, wholesale distributors and hotels running on IBM, Pick, DEC, Basic Four, Wang, Prime and Data General platforms.

    As a programmer at Mann Data, developed and implemented accounting, manufacturing, and wholesale distribution systems at Polaroid, Timberland, Rockport, Esselte, Griffin Greenhouse and F.W. Webb.

    During this time, also held operations and software development positions at Brown Shoe Company and Commercial Union Insurance Company.
