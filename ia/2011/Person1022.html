Akihisa Fujita's Summary

Fujita has a worthy experience in which two create an important advertising market.

1st is an Internet ad market in Japan.
Fujita who was doing work of the Internet from 1994 was engaged in establishment of the first Internet ad special company "cyber communications" (cci) in Japan in 1996, and led to the success as a COO till 1999, towed the annual turnover of cci to 40 million dollars.
The activity at this time was evaluated and he is inaugurated as the youngest vice-chairman of the board of directors in "J IAA" (Japanese version of "IAB").

2nd is the world's first mobile ad market.
When Fujita was engaged in establishment of the world's first mobile ad special company "D2 Communications" (D2C) in 2000, he was inaugurated as CEO over ten years after that, and D2C grew up the sales of number of 200 million dollars and employees into 300 persons.
The activity at this time was evaluated and he has won the "contribution prize" from "Web Ad Bureau of JAA" (Japanese version of "AAA").

The challenge volition which is Fujita's biggest feature does not finish it as this.In 2010, he began a new challenge.
It is that he makes successful the ad agency conglomerate "Dentsu Digital Holdings"(DDH) that does not distinguish the digital technique and the analog technique.
Although DDH are sales of 1,500 million dollars, and 2,000 employees, he leads the further growth as a position of No.3 of this company.
Moreover, he who is also the fund manager for whom the fund of 100 million dollars was prepared is investing positively to the company with new tech or new market creativity.

He was invited to many countries until now, and has performed 200 lectures or more.
Also he is one of honorary members in ad:tech tokyo.
Furthermore, he is also one of the first speaker as a Japanese in ad:tech held except Japan.
The writing with which he dealt counts ten works until now, and is published not only in Japan but in South Korea.

  • Mobile Marketing in Japan

    • Journal of Integrated Marketing Communications
    • January 1, 2008
    Authors: Akihisa Fujita

    In Japan, broad penetration of information technology is
    helping to change consumer opinion and accelerate the speed
    of research and development and distribution, all of which
    are crucial components of marketing. Yet in this environment,
    traditional marketing communication does not allow companies
    to keep pace with these changes, creating a bottleneck in their
    competitive strategies. In order to cope, Japanese marketers
    have begun using Internet-enabled mobile phones to reach
    consumers anytime and anywhere, thereby overcoming
    traditional limitations of time, space and cost. This trend is
    having a significant impact on corporate marketing strategies in
    Japan and can be expected to emerge in global markets in the
    near future.
