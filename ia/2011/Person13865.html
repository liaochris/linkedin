Eric Dose’s Summary

Effective and concise liaison between scientists and the businesses that depend on their results--most recently for advanced flavor, beverage, food, and related products. Expert in aligning scientists' enthusiasm with business needs, and in clearly explaining technical results and strategic meaning to other company leaders, no matter what their technical fluency.

Consistently identifies and hires first-rate corporate scientists at technician through Ph.D. and manager levels; integrates their talents into exemplary teams.

Extensive experience in measurements, especially chromatography, color technology, product stability, and estimating confidence in results. Expert in: facilitating Development and Introduction of challenging new products and upgrades; anticipating future measurement needs; coordinating projects spanning R&D and QA; high-resolution chromatography (gas and liquid); chromatographic isolation of trace components.

Eric Dose’s Specialties:

Design and apply effective performance metrics for product candidates. Set strategy for measurement laboratories including analytical chemistry. Assemble tasks and projects into understandable, efficient, and realistic portfolios.

Identifies, hires, and mentors superior technical talent. Ensure that they (1) get and stay on the right projects, (2) have what they need to progress rapidly, (3) work extremely well together, (4) enjoy doing it.

Eric Dose’s Experience

  • Mgr, Flavor Innovation

    Hill's Pet Nutrition

    (Public Company; CL; Consumer Goods industry)

    August 2010 — Present (6 months)

    I LOVE my new position, even if I'm still learning (and helping invent) how to do it. Each day I work with a lot of smart scientists to figure out which healthy ingredients, flavors, and textures dogs and cats prefer and why (through elaborate and sometimes very subtle Design of Experiments and statistics--our consumers can't just tell us). Then we make sure those flavors, textures, etc. are in fact presented, just as we planned, in our company's vast variety of pet diets.

    The technology we bring to bear on these problems exceeds anything I had imagined. My own group often has to invent relevant technical measurements with little history to rely on--which of course is right up my alley. And I've absolutely never worked with such enthusiastic consumers! (> 450 happy, healthy, rambunctious dogs).
