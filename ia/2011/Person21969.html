John Walker FBCS CISM CRISC CITP ITPC MFSoc's Summary

John is a World Class Security Professional who has worked within the Covert Worlds of CESG, GCHQ, ‘TK’ Sky Technology, with the CIA, and Security Services. He seamlessly migrates between disciplines of Technical & Procedural Security, Process & Procedure, and Academia, meeting at the Interstate of Sociological Economics, aligned to joined-up, and pragmatic thinking. He has delivered over 60 Global Presentations, and has originated over 100 Papers, and Articles on Cyber-Security. In the operational role, he has delivered successful Security Projects to Global Organisations. He believes that ‘effective’ security is 'not' achieved by over stated Security, or dictates from the Ivory Tower, but believes that 'effective', security 'must' be Pragmatic & Visionary – simply looking to the present or past, can be dangerous to any effective Security Mission. John is a 'proven' Visionary in the field of Cyber-Security. Back in the late 1980's, based on research, focusing on the early works of Fred Cohen and Ralph Burger (Author of Viruses - A High Tech Disease) John predicted that cyber-viral risks would escalate to present a real-time threat. 1993 he addressed the Virus Bulletin Conference, Amsterdam where, to a doubting audience of ICARA members, and Anti-Virus Developers, he raised the threat of virus authors using encapsulation methods to hide malicious code - from that time, to the current day, such techniques have continued to grow, and are now a major contributions to successful undetected communication of Malware Attacks. In the early days of SPAM John alerted the potential dangers to Government Technologies Communities. However, again, at that time the proposed threat was not considered realistic, and as with viruses, it was again considered to be simply a passing trend & nuisance! Today SPAM in the form of unsolicited email is one of the 'major' contributors responsible supporting the successful compromise of millions, if not billions of Global Computer Systems!
