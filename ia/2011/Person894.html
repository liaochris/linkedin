Ahmed Kamel’s Summary

1995 – 2003 Chief Scientist - Space Systems/Loral
Provided technical leadership for all weather and multifunctional satellites at the company. Provided key contribution to the winning MTSAT-1 and MTSAT-1R proposals. Led an R&D study on satellite precise pointing using star trackers and near-zero momentum control system.
1991 - 1995 GOES I-M System Engineering Manager - Space Systems/Loral
Managed both the space and ground segments to meet NASA/NOAA requirements. Coordinated design and pre-shipment reviews, integration, test plan, interface control documents, flight and operational software, and mission operations. Received NASA launch and post launch test team achievement award, NASA GOES project award, and GSFC individual achievement award.
1985 - 1991 GOES I-M INR System Manager - Space Systems/Loral
Invented and led the design of the GOES I-M Image Navigation and Registration (INR) system for Imager and Sounder, a key feature of the proposal for GOES I-M, resulting in winning a one billion dollar contract from NASA/NOAA. This system was flight proven and became the basis for the next generation GOES N-Q and GOES-R INR requirements. Received GSFC INR team achievement award in recognition of dedication, ingenuity, and sustained technical excellence in the design, development, test, and implementation of the GOES INR system.
1982 - 1985 Advanced Systems Engineer - Space Systems/Loral:
Technical leadership for advanced system design of high performance propulsion modules.

Ahmed Kamel’s Specialties:

Over 35 years experience in meteorological and control systems (space, ground, and operations). Invented GOES I-M Image Navigation and Registration (INR) system that became the foundation for subsequent GOES systems. Developed GOES-R ABI INR algorithms for ITT. Received four NASA/GSFC awards and nine patents on GOES and MTSAT INR, precise satellite pointing and stationkeeping. Published over 25 technical papers.
