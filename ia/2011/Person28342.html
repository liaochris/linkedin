Mark Pasetsky's Summary

Mark Pasetsky is a seasoned media professional consultant with a unique combination of senior-level editorial and communications experience.

Highlights include serving as managing editor of OK! Magazine, founder and editorial director of CoverAwards.com, blogger for the Huffington Post, editor in chief/general manager of Life & Style Weekly, VP communications for MTV Networks’ online division, and founder of independent PR firm Mark Allen & Company.

As the managing editor of OK! Magazine from 2009 to 2010, Pasetsky led the magazine to consistent profitability, expanded the magazine’s readership and lowered the magazine’s median age to 28.

Pasetsky launched CoverAwards in 2008 and continues to serve as the site’s editorial director/publisher. Readers from around the world visit CoverAwards for the hottest celebrity and media topics of the day.

Pasetsky served as the the editor-in-chief and general manager of Bauer Publishing’s Life & Style Weekly from 2004 to 2007. The title became the fastest growing magazine on the newsstand and was named the 2004 Magazine Launch of the Year by magazine guru Samir Husni.

From 2001 to 2004, Pasetsky oversaw his independent communications firm, Mark Allen & Company. Clients included Bauer Publishing’s In Touch Weekly, MTV Networks Logo TV, and Infinity Broadcasting (now CBS Radio). Pasetsky also piloted a program called Executive Branding, which is designed to raise the profile of CEOs and senior-level executives.

As vice president for MTV Networks online division from 1999 to 2001, Pasetsky oversaw all corporate and consumer communications activities for the company’s web sites including MTV, VH1, and Nickelodeon.

Pasetsky became entrenched in the Internet space in the late 90s, when he held a variety of senior level positions at the nation’s leading Internet public relations firm, Middleberg & Associates. There he counseled clients, including MTV Networks, TheStreet.com, BMG Music and others.
