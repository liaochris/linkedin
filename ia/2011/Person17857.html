Andrew Ibbotson's Summary

Andrew Ibbotson currently serves as President and CEO of Digital Assent LLC, a fast growing healthcare technology company based in Atlanta, GA. The company’s wireless touch-screen PatientPad™ solution streamlines the patient check-in process and delivers targeted health information and advertising to patients while they sit in the waiting room or exam room.

The PatientPad™ recently won the 2010 "Cool Technology of the Year" award presented by TechAmerica and the Technology Association of Georgia. For more information about Digital Assent’s rapidly growing point-of-care network, please visit www.patient-pad.com.

Prior to Digital Assent, Andrew served as President and CEO of Synthis Corporation from 1999 until 2009. During his tenure at Synthis, Andrew oversaw the development and launch of an interactive advertising and selling solution which grew to over $500 million per month in sales transactions before being acquired by a multi-billion dollar, market-leading software company.

Prior to Synthis, Andrew worked for Manhattan Associates, a worldwide leader in supply chain execution systems, and for Accenture in their Global Supply Chain Strategy Practice. Andrew has lived in seven countries, speaks four languages, and served as a United States Envoy and Attaché to the Cuban Olympic Delegation during the 1996 Summer Olympic Games.

Andrew holds a degree in Industrial and Systems Engineering from Georgia Tech where he graduated with high honors and was elected to the Tau Beta Pi Engineering Honor Society. Andrew is a founding member of the Georgia Tech Business Network and a past member of its Board of Directors.

Away from the office, Andrew has a passion for adventure sports. He is an accomplished distance runner, mountain biker, Ironman triathlete, and scratch golfer. In 2005, Andrew had the honor of racing with the number one ranked adventure racing team in the country and is a past winner of the Nissan Xterra Southeast Region Off-Road Triathlon Series.
