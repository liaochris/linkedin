  • President and Chairman

    Checking For Charity Corporation

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    March 2009 — Present (2 years )

    Checking For Charity Corporation - "Our Goal Is To Assist"

    Checking For Charity is a non-profit corporation that spawned from a group of men's league hockey players seeking to make a difference though the game they love. The company went from locker room idea to 501(c)3 corporation in a matter of months and raised over $9,000 in the inaugural event. The format has participating teams declare a 501(c)3 charity which they will be representing. Each team is guaranteed a percentage of the proceeds from the tournament with the opportunity to earn more towards their charity depending on how they place in the competition. By donating 100% of all proceeds to charity, Checking For Charity combines a passion for the great game of hockey with the passion to give something back to the community. The second year saw the company grow more than 100% by raising more than $22,000 for 18 unique charitable organizations. Checking For Charity is looking to replicate its early successes and become a vehicle for others to put on succesful charity hockey events nationwide.

  • Contracting Officer - CENTCOM Contracting Command RCC Central, Baghdad Iraq

    United States Air Force

    (Government Agency; Defense & Space industry)

    May 2010 — November 2010 (7 months)

    CENTCOM Contracting Command supports operational war fighter requirements and host nation reconstruction efforts by awarding contingency contracts for supplies, services, and construction to commercial sources generally within Iraq and Afghanistan. This cultivates local and national economic development and fosters conditions for host nation sovereignty. RCC Central is the largest regional contracting center in Iraq and handles the majority of contracting dollars spent in Iraq.

    As the Deputy Branch Chief in the Operational Support Branch, Regional Contracting Center Central in Baghdad, Iraq I led a team of 8 that managed more than 800 base support contracts valued at $484M critical to the quality of life and mission of 65,000 troops at over 20 bases throughout Iraq. I also served as the Program Manager for the largest Contracting Officer Representative (COR) program in Iraq and was responsible for the training and oversight of more than 350 CORs serving as the critical quality assurance component for joint forces in Iraq.
