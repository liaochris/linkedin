  • Research Assistant

    University of Michigan

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    January 2008 — September 2008 (9 months)

    I worked with a soluble splice variant of Corticotropin Releasing Hormone Receptor 2 Alpha (CRHR2a), which could potentially modulate the stress response mediated by Corticotropin Releasing Hormone (CRH). CRH mediates the stress response through control of the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis. In the HPA axis, CRH is released upon stress and stimulates the release of adrenocorticotropic hormone (ACTH) from the anterior pituitary. ACTH causes glucocorticoids release, which illicits physiological responses to stress. CRH signals through two G-protein coupled receptors, CRHR1 and CRHR2, which have widespread expression throughout the brain. Recently, a splice variant of the CRHR2a receptor (sCRHR2a) has been isolated that was predicted to encode a soluble decoy receptor and inhibit CRH activity. The role of this soluble transcript in vivo was one of the main focuses for our research. At one point I did isolate a soluble variant of CRHR2b, but the laboratory had decided to go in other directions.
