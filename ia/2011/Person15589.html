Gary Ziegler’s Summary

Explorer, archaeologist, mountaineer, sailor, cowboy, and sometime philosopher...

Gary Ziegler grew up on skis in Colorado. As a teenager, he ran the first Pikes Peak Marathon, completing it again on the marathon's 50th anniversary. He staffed for the Peace Corps en Washington DC, Puerto Rico and briefly in Ecuador/Colombia. Graduating from Colorado College, he led an American Alpine Club expedition to Peru in, later climbing 14 peaks above 20,000 feet included first ascents of seven over 18,000'. He completed graduate studies in archaeology at San Marcos, Peru’s national university. He served as an intelligence specialist with Army Special Forces and the Australian SAS in Vietnam leaving as a Captain. He founded and currently operates 4000 acre Bear Basin Ranch near Westcliffe, Colorado, raising horses and long horn cows. Gary has served as County Sheriff, volunteer fireman and trained the local Search/Rescue unit, recently volunteering as commander of the Sheriff's Mounted Horse Patrol. He is an experienced ocean sailor, having crewed on a square rigger, worked as mate on sailing charters, and sailed his own chartered boats around South/Central America, the Caribbean and the Aegean Sea.

Gary's explorations in Peru include discoveries of the Inca ruins of Lisascayhuana, Cota Coca, and Llactapata. He led part of the National Geographic team that located and excavated Corihuyrachina. He is a recognized expert on Machu Picchu. He has co-directed and featured in films for the Discovery Channel, History Channel, BBC, National Geographic, Reader's Digest, Lonely Planet and has been a guest expert on NPR's Science Friday program. He is published in profession journals, numerous publications and has written a book, "Beyond Machu Picchu"
He is a fellow of the Royal Geographical Society of London, the Explorers Club and a sometime lecturer at Colorado College.
