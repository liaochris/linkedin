Johnathan Peterson's Summary

Johnathan is a JD/MBA Candidate at the Wharton School and the University of Pennsylvania Law School. A former professional hockey referee, he now focuses on the business and legal issues in professional sports. He has experience in legal and financial services, and is currently pursuing a career in management consulting.

Johnathan Peterson's Experience

JD/MBA Candidate

University of Pennsylvania

Educational Institution; Higher Education industry

September 2007 – Present (3 years 7 months)

As Co-Chairman of the non-profit Fighting for Philly Kids, Johnathan lead a 37.5% profit increase in the annual charity boxing event, Philly Fight Night, and has raised over $95,000 for the Boys & Girls Clubs of Philadelphia to date. In 2008, he boxed in the event and won an unanimous decision. Johnathan was named an Associate Editor of the University of Pennsylvania Journal of Business Law, has worked with Prof. Kenneth Shropshire arbitrating decisions for the NFLPA CARD, and has assisted Prof. Scott Rosner in publishing an article on the efficacy of Olympic Bans and Boycotts for the Texas Review of Entertainment and Sports Law. As President of the Penn Entertainment and Sports Law Society and Trek Director for the Wharton Sports Business Club, he increased academic and professional opportunities for students at both schools by organizing cross-disciplinary events and speaker series. He was the Co-Founder of the Penn Law Hockey team, helping lead the team to a 19-1 championship season, and plays for the Wharton Hockey Team. His leadership contributions to the school were recognized when he was named a Founding Advisory Board Member of the Penn Law Leadership Initiative, and his academic accomplishments have been recognized through Teaching Assistant positions in LGST 228 (Sports Law) and OPIM 621 (Decision Models & Uncertainty).
