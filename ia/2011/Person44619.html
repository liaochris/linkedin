Yanyan Zhang's Experience

Research scientist

RTI International

January 2011 – Present (3 months)

Investigate the pharmacophore of GPCRs and develop agonists for the receptors with state-of-the-art design of peptidyl mimetics.

Postdoctoral Research Scientist

The University of Akron

Educational Institution; Higher Education industry

March 2010 – January 2011 (11 months)

-Established a solid phase synthesis platform and synthesized peptide nucleic acid (PNA) probes to search for non-coding RNA interacting proteins to elucidate the mechanism of sex chromosome related gene inactivation events. Techniques used are: design of peptide nucleic acid (PNA) probes, solid phase synthesis of PNAs, development of cell nucleus penetrating probes, human cell line culture, proteomics study, 1D PAGE, 2D PAGE, DIGE, photo cross-linking, pull down

-Developed a high throughput method to study the interaction between peptidyl ligands and HIV TAR RNA. The new method saves experimental time by at least a hundred fold than traditional EMSA assays, eliminates the need for radioactive compounds and facilitates the identification of hits. Techniques used are: novel cyclic peptide library linker design, photo labile linker design, cyclization strategy design, linearization strategy design, sequence identification strategy design

Graduate Research Associate

The Ohio State University

Educational Institution; Higher Education industry

March 2005 – December 2009 (4 years 10 months)

-Studied the functions of 17 human disease related proteins by a combinatorial chemistry approach, generating a table of binding specificity data, elucidating the similarities and subtle differences among these proteins, expanding current knowledge of protein-protein interaction network, and facilitating database searches to predict physiological binding partners. Techniques used include: PCR cloning, protein expression in prokaryotic system, chromatographic purification, solid phase peptide synthesis following Fmoc/HBTU chemistry, HPLC, MALDI, ELISA, enzymatic labeling, enzyme activity assays, SPR, FP characterization, biostatistics, data analysis

-Investigated ligand binding differences of SHIP1 and SHIP2 proteins by comparing the binding specificities and binding kinetics, concluding that cis-trans isomerization of the peptidyl-prolyl bonds could be an additional factor that contributes to their in vivo functional differences. Techniques utilized are: high-throughput screening, SPR, global fitting, site-directed mutagenesis (STD), protein purification, 1D PAGE analysis, ITC, HPLC, UV spectrometry

-Identified the first potent cyclic peptidyl inhibitor for Grb2 protein by screening 300,000 cyclic peptides and demonstrated its potency to inhibit cell growth and disrupt actin filament formation in human breast cancer cells. Some techniques involve: bead segregation, individual peptide synthesis, cell penetrating peptide design, mammalian cell line maintenance, MALDI spectra analysis

-Discovered a novel binding mode for SH2-ligand binding by growing crystals with SH2-ligand complex. Some techniques are: X-ray crystallography, NMR, HSQC, 31P NMR, isotope labeling

-Developed a new protein refolding procedure for protein purification from inclusion bodies, leading to the functional determinations of 4 challenging proteins
