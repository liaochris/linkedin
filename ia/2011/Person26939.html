Robert Unterberger's Summary

I have a particularly strong and compelling background in LPO; one that may be unique in the legal outsourcing industry.

I practiced complex litigation, including large scale document review and outsourcing, for 20 years before pursuing LPO. Among the many prominent nation-wide cases I litigated involved drugs, tobacco, toxic waste, radiation, securities, antitrust, insurance, employment & bankruptcy.

I was introduced to LPO at its earliest stages, and have worked in it as CEO, Manager, Head of Legal Solutions & Consultant. I know what works in LPO, and what doesn't; how to get the job delivered; and how to maintain quality control and communication throughout the process to grow client relationships.

LPO has brought together four attributes of my legal career: (i) involvement in complex legal matters; (ii) document support and management; (iii) training; and (iv) sales and marketing. These skills are crucial to LPO success.

I have contributed to LPO publications and LPO training programs. I am a regular contributor on LPO to Rees Morrison's preeminent Law Department Management Blog.

I am now writing the first reference guide to LPO. I hope this will be the definitive text on the wide range of issues (substantive, procedural, ethical) to be considered by general counsel and law firms, whether outsourcing to India or other countries.

In addition, my legal support skills have been profiled in the National Law Journal, New York Law Journal, American Lawyer, Lawyers Weekly, Los Angeles & San Franciso Daily Recirds, Philadelphia Legal Intelligencer,Metropolitan Corporate Counsel, National Jourist, LexisOne, Boston Herald,European Lawyer, National Jurist, AmLaw.com, LexisOne, and Entrepreneur, among other publications.

I am an active member of several outsourcing organizations, as well as legal marketing, legal peer-to-peer and bar associations.

Specialties

My objectives are to maximize LPO sales, market share and brand; to be part of and advise senior management on LPO initiatives, strategy, progress and performance; and contribute to my company's success by building a strong and profitable LPO. Now is LPO's "tipping point”. LPO is a means for general counsel and law firms to thrive in an uncertain economy.
