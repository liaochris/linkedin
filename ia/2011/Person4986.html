Bernie Kent, P.E.’s Summary

Selling machinery protection (vibration, shut-down, performance monitoring, turbine supervisory instrumentation, machinery diagnostics/surveillance) systems to provide monitoring, trending and protection of critical rotating and reciprocating machinery in various industries (Power Generation, Refining, Chemical, Pipeline, Waste Water, Pulp and Paper, Manufacturing, etc.) across south Texas. These systems can be standalone, as well as interfaced to interact with DCS/PLC, SCADA and CMMS in local and/or wide area networking. Selling Reliability Services and Consulting Services to assist our customers in optimizing the value associated with any industrial maintenance program which integrates traditional recommended maintenance practices, condition based asset health programs, predictive maintenance technologies, and lean process execution.

Bernie Kent, P.E.’s Specialties:

Selling enterprise and plant level projects that include Machinery Diagnostics, proper application of appropriate sensors for supervisory monitoring and protection, performance (thermodynamic and mechanical) monitoring of rotating machinery that include critical machinery such as steam turbines, gas turbines, pumps, compressors, fans, etc. Selling skilled services necessary to commission the appropriate system that meets a specific business need.

  • Sr Field Test Engineer

    NL MWD (aka Halliburton)

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; NL; Oil & Energy industry)

    January 1983 — September 1986 (3 years 9 months)

    Among the first of several Field Test Engineers responsible for the original deployment and testing of early NL MWD (Measurement While Drilling) negative pressure, pulsed telemetry logging tools with Battery Backup, high resolution recorded downhole memory in petroleum well drilling operations. Formation measurement sensors included 2MHz Electromagnetic Wave Resistivity (EWR), Natural Gamma Ray (Scintillation Geiger Mueller type), as well as Neutron (Am Be) Porosity and Gamma (Cs) Density. Directional inclinometers and accelerometers deployed in non-magnetic collars also provided real-time directional drilling azimuth and trajectory of wellbore. Field tests were conducted in South Texas, South Louisiana, off-shore Gulf of Mexico, North Slope of AK, and the North Sea. Assisted with the initial commercial deployment of these MWD services and training of new Field Engineers.
