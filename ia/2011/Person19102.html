Janine Flaccavento's Summary

Nine years ago my partner Scott Reid and I started Philter Communications with the belief that no one wants to be sold anything, but everyone loves a great story. And with that, a story:

A few years ago at an unfinished business series event at OCAD I saw a presentation about innovation. I suppose you would call this the simplest form of innovation. The speaker told a story about his college roommate who would send a letter to his girlfriend each night. And each night he would take a #10 envelope from his shelf and find a different way to address, stamp and seal it. This all happened within the constraints of the USPS: return address top left, stamp top right, address centered. But each night he did it differently. The story ended with the marriage ceremony of the letter writer to the recipient (who can resist a happy ending) and her unrolling a 20-foot long mural of each one of these envelopes in different colours, styles, graphics, type treatments, etc. Whether the story was true or not, the idea behind it changed the way we look at our clients' businesses.

Whether we're designing POP signage for our Philter Retail clients, or a corporate brochure for our Philter Communications clients, there are always constraints and rules. But what this story taught me was that working within these rules can be liberating and can lead to a entirely new realm of creative possibilities.

Got a story to tell? I'd love to hear it.

Specialties

But in the end, we're all storytellers.
