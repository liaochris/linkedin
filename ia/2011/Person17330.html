Heloisa Moura, PhD (moura@id.iit.edu)'s Summary

● Long term experience with design thinking, innovation strategy and user centered research (mapping innovation culture; developing corporate innovation strategy and metrics; structuring innovation process; giving trainning in design thinking; leading P&D).

● Multidisciplinary academic background, and cross cultural experience.

● Post-doc in Computer Science (project 'Hybrids: Environments for Hybrid Multimodal Interaction, Collaboration and Learning', PUC-Rio, Brazil).

● Post-doc in Design (project 'ThinkeringSpaces', Institute of Design, Illinois Institute of Technology, USA).

● Ph.D. in Design (thesis 'A Methodological Framework for Bringing Multimodality and Affordances to Design of Technology-Enhanced Learning Environments', Institute of Design, Illinois Institute of Technology, USA).

● M.S. in Education, expertise diplomas (graduate level) in: Computer Graphics; Internet, Interface & Multimedia; Distance Education; and Psycho-pedagogy. B.S. in Mathematics.

● Research coordinator for the ThinkeringSpaces project (www.ThinkeringSpaces.org), a new genre of exploratory environments that seeks: to bring school-age children together to tinker with things, both physical and virtual, reflect upon what they do and discover, and elaborate their ideas in ways they can share with others; and to create new opportunities for face-to-face collaboration through large scale and multi-input technologies.

● Work experience in designing services, teaching (primary to graduate level), managing projects, and developing games and websites.

● Former coordinator of the educational technology department of the Rio de Janeiro state government, Brazil.

● Interest in sustainable social innovation, collaborative environments, multimodal interaction, affordances, learning, technology and mixed realities.

● Fluency in Portuguese and English. Language skills in Spanish, French and Dutch.

Specialties

design thinking, innovation strategy, design planning, user-centered research, service design, collaboration, multimodality, distance and mobile learning
