Jason Metz’s Summary

I recently had an epiphany. I’m great at sales – a natural “talker” with relentless pursuit of the chase, capacity to engage with Janitor and CMO alike, yearning for achievement, fear of failure and appreciation for relationships. I work hard; I’m passionate, think strategically and stop at nothing to excel.

And thanks in large part to this “natural ability”, I’ve accounted for over $50MM in revenues over the past 6 years, winning new clients including Porsche, Procter & Gamble, Adidas, Novartis, Forever21, Kraft Foods and the LVCVA. I’ve created, sold and helped manage marketing campaigns in all sectors, targeting consumers, B2B, trade, PR and investors.

But I had it all wrong – regardless of what my success may have suggested. Professional services are bought, not sold, and natural ability without the appropriate strategic approach is 100% counterproductive. To sell a service you can’t simply pitch the product. It’s almost always too similar to the competition, and if your timing is off and they’re not in the market to buy, your message will ALWAYS fall on deaf ears.

No – the CORRECT approach to business development is to provide prospects with greater insight into their target audience and allow them to do their job more effectively.

I call it the “Expertise Lure”, and through an integrated system of expertise-based email marketing, PR, web & social media optimization, agency branding, speaking engagements and face to face meetings with WARM prospects, my success has increased 10-fold... no more one-offs; now it’s about AOR status.

I’ve sold marketing programs in sports, fashion, CPG, auto, pharma, electronics, film and TV, F&B and travel, with targets including boomers, athletes, rich men, LGBT, housewives, Hispanics, Tween girls and Mommy Bloggers. My expertise spans experiential, digital, social media, TV and content. I love selling. But I’ve found an even greater passion for strategy.
