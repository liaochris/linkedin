Jim Brazell’s Summary

Jim Brazell is a technology forecaster, public speaker and strategist focusing on innovation and learning systems. He is a member of the Schriever Institute, Radical Platypus group and the Thornburg Center for Professional Development.

Since October 1 of 2010, Jim has delivered speeches to DoD Reliance 21 STEM Workshop, National Career Pathway Network, National School Board Association, Armed Forces Communications Electronics Association (Cyber), Citadel, Old Crows (Billy Mitchell Chapter) and the Huether Lasallian Conference.

Jim is currently volunteering to the Greater San Antonio Chamber of Commerce Technology Committee, "Cyber Action Team," San Antonio Public Library Foundation Next Generation Libraries and San Antonio College Human Innovation Library.

Between 2005 and 2010, Jim delivered over 300 speeches to audiences ranging from the Defense Learning Strategies Consortium to the International Conference on Technology Policy and Innovation on energy policy in Norway in 2008 and on solutions to the financial crisis in 2009 in Portugal.

Jim Brazell is a 1995 graduate of Bradley University, Bachelors of Science in Sociology, Summa Cum Laude. He is a 1995 George Gilder Fellow in High Technology, Entrepreneurship and Public Policy. At Bradley, Jim was the co-principal investigator of Interlabs and social informatics minor. Jim resides in San Antonio, Texas, with his wife, Lisa Cervantes and daughter Ava Brazell.
