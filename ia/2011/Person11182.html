Daniel Comarmond's Experience

Partner Systems Engineer

Cisco Systems

Public Company; CSCO; Computer Networking industry

January 2006 – February 2011 (5 years 2 months)

North Sydney, New South Wales, AUSTRALIA

As of 27 September 2010 I became the national Partner SE responsible for UXC Connect, Fujitsu, and IBM. My role is to be a Cisco architectural and technical leader to sales, pre-sales, design, architecture, engineering, and implementation teams within my partners. This includes understanding the business and go-to-market models of my partners and articulating how Cisco architectures and technologies support these models, and how my partners can better leverage Cisco to support and enhance their sales and post-sales processes. Cisco resources I connect my partners range from Customer Briefing Centre (CBC) to Technical Assistance Centre (TAC), and online resources such as partner tools on cisco.com.

As a Channel SE, I also 'own' certain tools and initiatives for all Cisco partners in Australia, such as partner certifications and specialisations; deliver sales and architecture training to multi-partner audiences such as the 646-205 Cisco Sales Expert curriculum; and the planning, design, and implementaton tool Netformx DesignXpert - which has seen some partners cut down sales and implementation cycles by up to 60%.

For the past two years I was the Channel SE looking after Cisco Gold, Silver, and Premier partners across Australia, conducting architecture, sales, technical, and partner tools training for systems integrators. I also helped co-ordinate marketing and technical training activities that Cisco distributors would hold for their customer systems integrators. Prior to that I was a customer-facing SE.

My technical specialisation focuses on information security, especially trends in risk and user behaviour and how that affects perceptions on information security. I also present regularly on Cisco's security portfolio, including at AusCERT 2010. My other technical focus areas are Cisco data centre technologies such as Unified Fabric and Unified Computing, as well as wireless LAN.
