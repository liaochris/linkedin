Elizabeth Nelson's Summary

Elizabeth leads outreach and documentation for LabKey Software. The LabKey team builds and customizes LabKey Server, an open-source platform for integrating, analyzing and sharing biomedical research data, including the results of high-throughput experiments. LabKey Server is used by top research institutes across the globe to study HIV/AIDS and other diseases.

Elizabeth completed her Ph.D. in Biological Sciences at Stanford University's Hopkins Marine Station, where she used statistical models for turbulent mixing to address questions in marine conservation biology. After completing her Ph.D., Elizabeth joined Microsoft as a Program Manager and worked on the first release of Windows Presentation Foundation (WPF), part of the .NET Framework. Elizabeth also holds a B.S. in Civil & Environmental Engineering from Stanford University.

In her free time, she leads Project Seattle Pools, a grassroots community organization working to enhance and expand Seattle's public pool system.
