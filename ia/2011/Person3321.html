Anthony Kane's Summary

I am a Law & Politics BA(Hons) graduate who has recently completed the GDL at BPP Law School, Waterloo. I currently study the BPTC (Bar Professional Training Course) at City Law School, with the intention of being called to the bar in 2011.

Previously, I have worked as a paralegal in Leicester where I regularly attended both the Magistrates and Crown Court. I experienced a wide variety of legal matters during my time as a paralegal, both family and criminal. This experience has stood me in good stead and convinced me to pursue a career at the criminal bar. The mini pupillages I subsequently undertook allowed me to gain a greater understanding of the rigours of a career at the criminal bar.

Specialties

I have played rugby to a very high standard, having spent 2 seasons with Leicester Tigers whilst at university. I have also represented Ireland Under 19s and been part of the Ireland Under 21 squad. It is my hope that I can incorporate my love for the sport into my professional life by developing a sports law practice alongside my criminal law aspirations.

Anthony Kane's Experience

Volunteer

London Innocence Project

Law Practice industry

November 2010 – Present (4 months)

The London Innocence Project is an organisation founded by 1 Pump Court Chambers and a team of law students. It is a non-profit legal resource clinic and criminal justice centre that works to examine and prevent potential miscarriages of justice. We work to exonerate the wrongfully convicted by examining their cases and subjecting both the evidence and the trial process itself to close scrutiny. We distribute research reports on criminal justice and strive to ensure that the rule of law is maintained on the basis of equality before the law and procedural fairness. The London Innocence Project has a dedicated team of barristers committed to providing high-quality legal representation to those who maintain their innocence.
