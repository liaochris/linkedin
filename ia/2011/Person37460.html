Robin Warshaw’s Summary

I create and develop editorial concepts, conduct interviews, research, report, write, and edit all types of projects, including co-authoring and ghostwriting. I'm very good at connecting with people at all levels of expertise, listening to them and melding their insights with other reporting to produce material that engages and informs audiences.

You'll see my byline in many print and electronic publications. I also write for non-profit organizations, foundations, educational and healthcare institutions, public agencies, professional groups, businesses and individuals.

I'm accustomed to coordinating editorial projects and working with in-house staff or outside consultants. My experience includes media contact, publicity and communications for the local affiliate of a national charitable organization.

Robin Warshaw’s Specialties:

I work on articles, books, Web content, email and print newsletters, reports, educational guides and booklets, speeches, public relations communications, marketing collateral and other projects. I write under my name or as a co-author or ghostwriter for experts and clients.

Robin Warshaw’s Experience

  • Freelance Writer and Editor

    Self-employed

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    January 1981 — Present (30 years 1 month)

    I write and edit on many subjects, including health, medicine, social issues, education and business for print and online publications, non-profit organizations, healthcare and educational institutions, foundations, websites, corporations and others.

    My projects include articles, books, reports, web content, newsletters, speeches, press releases, columns, ghostwriting, publicity and development materials, social networking content and more.

    You may have read my articles in The New York Times, healthywomen.org, The Nation, Rutgers Today, BrainWork, Woman's Day, MyBusiness, New Choices, financialmuse.com, Philadelphia Inquirer, Medscape, Working Woman, Better Health & Living and others. I'm also the author of a HarperCollins nonfiction book (see below) that was a Literary Guild Alternate and named to several "best books" lists.

    I work on editorial projects for a wide range of clients. These have included: Living Beyond Breast Cancer, National Women's Health Resource Center, Thomas Jefferson University Hospital, The Dana Foundation, P4 Healthcare Communications, The Robert Wood Johnson Foundation, The Pew Charitable Trusts, Abington Memorial Hospital and Main Line Health System and more.

    In addition, I'm the media representative for the Pancreatic Cancer Action Network, Philadelphia Affiliate. I handle publicity, media contact, social networking and communications for the Delaware Valley organization's year-round efforts to increase awareness about pancreatic cancer, support patients and boost research funding. My work includes promoting local activities and events year-round, including the major annual fundraising event, PurpleStride Philadelphia, a 5K walk/run. In 2010, this event drew 3,100 registrants to Fairmount Park and raised more than $450,000 for pancreatic cancer research, patient programs and advocacy. I've been in this position for four years, supporting organizational efforts since the Philadelphia affiliate's inception.
