Saeed Ghassemzadeh's Experience

Principal Member of Technical Staff

AT&T Labs - Research

Public Company; T; Telecommunications industry

July 1995 – Present (15 years 9 months)

In 1995, I joined AT&T Wireless communication center of excellence at AT&T Bell-Labs as a member of technical staff involved in design and development of fixed wireless base station. I also conducted research in areas of CDMA access technologies, propagation channel measurement and modeling, satellite communications, wireless local area networks and coding in wireless systems. Currently, I am a principal member of technical staff in Communication Technology Research department at AT&T Labs-Research, Florham Park, NJ. My current research interest includes wireless channel measurement and modeling, remote Tele-health monitoring, wireless LANs, cognitive and collaborative communication.

Principal Engineer

InterDigital Communications

Public Company; IDCC; Wireless industry

1991 – 1995 (4 years)

– Developed Anti-jam and low probability of intercept techniques for BCDMA systems in PCS environment.
– Performed Link budget measurement and analysis at 300, 800, 900 and 1800 MHz in heavy forest and swamp environment.
– Represented InterDigital in standardization of the Wideband CDMA (IS-95) in the TIA. Supported product development and BCDMA standardization activities.
– Performed BCDMA system capacity analysis / simulation based on random traffic in overlay and none-overlay scenarios.
– Significant contributor to standardization of the BCDMA−Overlay in the TIA.
– Planned, coordinated and performed field-tests for BCDMA-Overlay demonstration.
– Responsible for measurements and analysis of BCDMA channel characterization and signal propagation at 800-900 MHz, 1.85-2.2 GHz and 3.7-4.2 GHz.
– Characterized AMPS traffic and signal strength within the overlay cell. These tasks involved design and development of an automated channel sounder.

Teaching/Research Assistant

The City University of New York

Higher Education industry

September 1989 – November 1992 (3 years 3 months)

– Taught graduate and undergraduate courses. These courses included, Digital Signal Processing, Probability, Linear Systems, Digital and analog communication systems and undergraduate engineering laboratories.
– Designed and simulated circuit switching, packet switching and various local area networks for the undergraduate laboratory courses.
– Created a new undergraduate local area network laboratory. These activities involved writing manuals for the students to follow the experiments.

Research Analyst/ Engineer

SCS Mobilecom

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Wireless industry

1989 – 1991 (2 years)

Propagation channel measurement and modeling:

Designed and built a Channel Sounder device to measure the characteristics of the wireless mobile channel at 2 GHz. Performed propagation experiments in Microcellular and indoor environments to characterize the path loss and fade margin of spread spectrum signals with various chip rates. Modeled the propagation path loss of the microcellular channel.
