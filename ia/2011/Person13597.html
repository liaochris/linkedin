President of The Stalwart Group, student-run advertising agency

St. Bonaventure University

Educational Institution; Higher Education industry

2007 – 2009 (2 years)

American Advertising Federation is the premiere college advertising competition. It is a national organization designed to give colleges and universities the opportunity to create a working advertising agency on campus that will compete in the National Student Advertising Competition (NSAC). AAF's college-chapter program has 226 affiliated chapters throughout the U.S. and abroad. The program includes nearly 8,000 undergraduate student members and more than 350 faculty advisers.

St. Bonaventure’s chapter runs under the name, The Stalwart Group, which consists of 30 students, half journalism and mass communication majors and half marketing majors. In the fall semester, AAF runs as a club, but in the spring semester, AAF acts as a three-credit class. Students undergo a rigorous application process and are selected by the The Stalwart Group officers based on their work experiences and genuine interest in working with the team.

The Stalwart Group works year-round to create a plans book complete with marketing research and advertising initiatives. In May, the team travels to New York City for the regional competition to make a 20-minute presentation that is judged by a panel of nationally recognized advertising executives.

Past clients include The Century Council, AOL AIM and Coca Cola.
