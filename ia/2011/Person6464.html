Bruce Lazarus’s Summary

Former Director of Business and Legal Affairs for Walt Disney Theatrical Productions is a transactional attorney with over 18 years experience in entertainment law, producing, and representing artists and entrepreneurs.

Bruce Lazarus’s Specialties:

Known for representing the legal, business and financing concerns of over 20 Broadway, off-Broadway, touring and licensed theatre productions. Also Independent film, television, music, publishing and new media.

Bruce Lazarus’s Experience

  • Entertainment Attorney

    Law Office of Bruce Lazarus (Sole Proprietorship)

    (Sole Proprietorship; Law Practice industry)

    1997 — Present (14 years )

    Visit: www.BroadwayAttorney.com.
    Known for representing the legal, business and financing concerns of over 20 Broadway, off-Broadway, touring and licensed theatre productions. Services include:
    - Acquisition of underlying rights for musical and dramatic plays both on and off Broadway
    - Collaboration agreements among bookwriters, composers and lyricists
    - Option and production agreements for Broadway, Off-Broadway, touring and international productions
    - Regional theatre, workshop and other developmental production agreements
    - Preparation of offering circulars, limited partnership and limited liability company agreements and other investment documentation and all necessary securities filings
    - Drafting and negotiation of all other agreements for theatrical productions, including the agreements with directors, stars, choreographers, designers, orchestrators and general managers
    - Joint venture agreements among producers/financing parties
    - Negotiating grand rights and synch rights licenses with music publishers

  • Theatrical Producer

    Lazarus Theatre Productions

    (Law Practice industry)

    1991 — Present (20 years )

    Bruce Lazarus received a Tony Award for Best Play nomination for the Broadway production of SAY GOODNIGHT GRACIE by Rupert Holmes which starred Frank Gorshen and won the National Broadway Theatre Award for the national tour. His other Broadway producing credits include THE GATHERING by Arje Shaw which starred Hal Linden.

    Bruce Lazarus also produced several notable off-Broadway productions including SHAKESPEARE'S R&J adapted by Joe Calarco for which he won the Lucile Lortel Award for Outstanding Production; ONLY KIDDING by Jim Geoghan which was nominated for the Drama Desk Award for Outstanding New Play and for which two of its stars Paul Provenza and Howard Speigel won Theatre World Awards.

    His producing partners include Roger Gindi, Dana Matthow, Allan Sandler, Steve Leber, David Krebs, Patrick Hogan, William Franzblau, Martha R. Gasparian, Lawrence S. Toppall, Martin Markinson, Elsa Daspin Haft and the late Frederic B. Vogel,.

  • Lecturer

    Producing Theatre

    (Law Practice industry)

    1990 — Present (21 years )

    Guest lecturer at UCLA (“International Dynamics of Producing Theatre”), Carnegie Mellon University Master of Arts Management Program (“Producing Theatre”), Commercial Theatre Institute and Theatre Resources Unlimited (“Legal Aspects of Producing Commercial Theatre”).

  • Director of Business and Legal Affairs

    Walt Disney Theatrical Productions

    (Entertainment industry)

    February 1996 — August 1997 (1 year 7 months)

    Having represented the off-Broadway hit “Blueman Group Tubes” in negotiations with Disney for a possible motion picture and after the success of Disney’s “Beauty and the Beast” on Broadway, I was invited to come in house to help develop the soon to be formed Disney Theatrical Productions.

    My responsibility was to negotiate and draft the artistic and business agreements for the Broadway productions of “The Lion King”, “and Aida” and the international companies of “Beauty and the Beast”. This included creating the template agreements to be used in future Disney productions with composers, authors, directors, choreographers, designers, arrangers, orchestrators, music publishers and actors.
    Provided legal counsel to Division of Labor Relations.

    Provided legal counsel for the reopening of New Amsterdam Theater, 42nd Street, New York City.

  • Associate

    Law Offices of DaSliva and DaSilva

    (Legal Services industry)

    1990 — 1996 (6 years )

    It was my great honor to be hired and mentored by the late Albert DaSilva, one of the leading theatrical attorneys and deal closers of his day, representing many theatrical luminaries including Neil Simon, Cy Coleman, Larry Gelbart, David Zippel and Lloyd Richards. My responsibilities included drafting agreements for producers, writers and composers. I also drafted and filed Regulation D private placement documents and state Blue Sky filings for Broadway and off-Broadway productions.
    I was encouraged to bring in my own legal projects and most notably represented the long running hit “Blueman Group Tubes”.

  • Artist Representative-Personal Manager

    Bruce Lazarus Personal Management

    (Entertainment industry)

    1984 — 1990 (6 years )

    Working my way through law school I became a Hollywood talent agent and learned the art of negotiating and closing deals. I later opened my own business as personal manager for actors, guiding their career decisions.

    As a manager I represented RAY LIOTTA in deals for Martin Scorsese’s “Goodfellas”, the films “Field of Dreams”, “Dominic and Eugene” and “Something Wild” and the television series “Our Family Honor”. I persuaded producers of the super-hit television series “Dynasty” to create a series regular role for then unknown TROY BEYER. I represented SCATMAN CROTHERS on Seven Spielberg’s “Twilight Zone”, and Stanley Kubrick’s “The Shining” and negotiated MARIAN MERCER’s deal on the syndicated television series “It’s a Living”. PHILIP BAKER HALL's career was launched with Robert Altman’s film adaptation of the stage play “Secret Honor” and followed with several starring film roles. I also closed 4 film deals for DAVID CARUSO prior to his rise to fame on television in “NYPD Blue”.
