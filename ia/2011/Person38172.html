Ruud Rensink's Summary

Ruud Rensink, a passionate trainer, was born in 1960 in The Netherlands, where he still lives. He comes from a nest of instructors. His father, just like his grandfather, was a teacher and school director. Many of his uncles and aunts occupied positions in educational institutions and some of his brothers and sisters still do.

In 1990, after a successful carrier, Ruud started giving training sessions. At first as an employee and, since December 1992, for his own company called DigiTrain
OpleidingsCentrum. The first courses were related to computer training.
To guarantee the maximum effectiveness of the training, Ruud deepened himself in the studies of people’s stimulants with emphasis in Neuro Linguistic Programming (NLP). As a result, he uses the knowledge of human brain to develop Brain Training since 2005. These 1 or 2 days courses discuss the learning stimulants, memory techniques, speed reading, time management and the effective selection of objectives, mind mapping and a little something about
NLP. In other words, a complete training.

In the last years, the demand for a specific mind mapping course has increased considerably. Because of that, Ruud has specialized since 2007 in this brilliant technique. Both hand-drawn and computer created MindMaps have become Ruud’s passion. Today, he is the only trainer
in The Netherlands holding the title of both Buzan Licensed Instructor (BLI) and iMindMap Master Trainer. iMindMap the software that allows the easy construction of mind maps on the computer.

With these titles, Ruud is able to give training sessions in both mind mapping and iMindMap, and also to certificate other people to become trainers.

My Mission:
To provide the benefits of Mind Maps to the largest number of people possible.

Specialties

My knowledge and ability in Mind Mapping & my enthousiastic way of teaching.

Mind Mapping:
Mind Mapping is the visual expression of thoughts in a way that uses both sides of the brain, maximizing the flow of creativity and improving memory. Words form a network of ideas that connect with each other generating meaningful relations, similar to the way the human brain works. With mind mapping it is possible to handle information in a fast, smart and flexible way. It is an ideal tool for learning, developing and presenting new ideas.
