Bonnie Natko's Summary

Cultivate. Evolve. Progress.
As a career creative, my career has taken me in many directions. From my days in marketing when I was pulling all-nighters working on decks, to working as an Account Exec in the burgeoning world of stock photography in the dot-com era. From transitioning to the creative recruiting field to working with graphic and interactive design students making sure they put their best foot forward as they transition to the professional world, and back to creative recruitment, I've come full circle.

I consider myself to be a "creative bloodhound" - I love to find, harvest, and cultivate talent. My specialty lies in developing junior talent, as I love assisting fledgling creatives with their career search, self-branding and marketing, portfolio/resume development, and tips on how to build and maintain their social and live network. I am always open to consulting or teaching opportunities. Another area of interest to me is helping companies with identifying some of the best and brightest upstarts in the creative world. Developing internship programs, employee retention, and creating company alumni programs is also something that intrigues me.

When I'm not recruiting, I am also a photographer and I have worked with experiential agencies, PR firms, associations, and publishers covering events and commissioned location shoots. My images have been published in: Gothamist, The New York Observer, The Village Voice, Curbed, WNYC.org, The Wall Street Journal (blog), O'Reilly Media, Dwell Magazine Online, NYC.gov, The Brooklyn Paper, and Grubstreet among others. I was also commissioned to shoot one of the girls profiled in the Penguin/Puffin book, "Girls Gone Green" published earlier this year.
