Michael Carniol's Summary

I am a statistical arbitrage trader, but my passion is value investing. I take the investment principles that others employ on an ad hoc basis, and I systematize them. That lets portfolio managers manage more money more profitably without doing more work.

Over the last three years, I developed stock selection and portfolio construction models around value investing principles that returned 55% from October 2008 to October 2010.

My experience encompasses greater breadth than the typical quantitative analyst. Specifically, I have:
• researched and traded event driven situations for a statistical arbitrage fund,
• earned my MBA at night,
• developed a GARP equity strategy to invest my own money,
• researched and traded equity options volatility for a bank's proprietary desk and a hedge fund,
• produced client-focused equity options research for the sell-side,
• clerked on the CBOE floor,
• formed value investing study group with friends, and
• participated in a giving circle to contribute seed-stage funding to new Jewish and Israeli causes.
