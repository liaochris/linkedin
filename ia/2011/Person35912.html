Ralph Page’s Summary

I'm a physicist who does laboratory experiments, data analysis, computer modeling, library work, presents results verbally and in writing, manages small-team efforts, and prepares funding proposals.

My main areas of expertise include condensed-matter physics and chemical physics; optics, optical materials, and lasers; many kinds of spectroscopy.

I utilize good physical intuition to get laboratory experiments working well, to devise "sanity checks," to make sense of observations in real time, to optimize signal-to-noise ratios, and to debug computer codes.

I seek technical work in the areas of vision research, chemical physics, laser development, alternative energy, telecommunications, and perhaps other fields. And I am willing to acquire further training (e.g. in project management or in technical areas.)

Ralph Page’s Specialties:

Fiber optics and waveguides, solid-state lasers, nonlinear optics, luminescence, thin films and coatings, large laser systems, adaptive optics, ultrashort pulses, molecular beams, RF sputtering, optical plasma diagnostics, electronics, isotope separation, wide-gap semiconductors, analog electronics, signal processing, vacuum systems, materials science, laser-driven shocks, and shock science.

Also good at writing journal articles, mentoring students, and providing technical leadership.
