Franklin Servan-Schreiber's Summary

My professional experience is varied and international.

Beginning in 1989 at ELLE and ELLE Decor magazines in New York I entirely revamped the editorial production process with early desktop publishing technology. I then moved to Japan for four years (1990-1993), working as international coordinator for the Cultural Philanthropy division of the Shiseido Corporation.

I returned to the USA in 1995 to establish the Digital User Interface Laboratory for SONY Corp where I led a team of designers and programmers to explore cross-devices user interfaces.

In 1999 I joined the International Olympic Committee in Lausanne, Switzerland, as Director for New Media, with broad responsibilities over strategy and management of Internet development for the Olympic Movement. I was assigned responsibilities for crisis management in the face of the Salt Lake City bid allegations of corruption. Soon after he was promoted Director for Communications and New Media, with overall responsibilities for all public relations, publications, Internet, and Olympic Games media logistics.

After leaving in 2001 I became a consultant in Internet strategy for diverse clients including USAToday.com, The Denver Research Group, Soundwalk Inc., Datops S.A. (Paris), Human Rights Watch, FullSix USA Inc., and Isodis Natura S.A.(Brussels).

In 2005 I joined the Paris Olympic Games Candidacy, building an innovative Zoomable User Interface to navigate all the venues on the satelitte view of the city. I then created the first stiched aerial views of France by IGN into a zoomable rendering. Presented the project at the Mayors' conference of France in November 2005.

From 2006 until June 2010 I ran Zoomorama SA, a zoomable user interface startup. Raised more than €2M, and build a team of more than 10 engineers and designers. Successes with Darty and Club-Privé who saw their sales dramatically increased.
