What is a Muse? According to Greek mythology a muse is any of the nine daughters of Mnemosyne and Zeus each of whom presided over a different art or science. A muse is also a guiding spirit, a source of inspiration, or a poet. As a verb it means to consider carefully or to meditate.

After careful consideration we have meditated and launched a new venture that reflects our passion: publishing, writing, and illustrating books and developing products to support them.

Muse River is the English name for the River Maas which flows through the city of Maastricht, in the Netherlands.

MuseRiverProductions will flow as mightily as the Maas, which has been a source of inspiration for Schaefer Communications' work over the last dozen years.

As a first volley we are proud to present The Adventures of Yin & Yang: Snoepje and The Pizza Box which will be available this year in the autumn of 2010, just in time for holiday giving! A collaboration between Susan Hermse Schaefer and Michael Bower Putman, of Putman Illustration & Design, this illustrated tale will delight and captivate cat lovers and simply lovers of all kinds. It is the story of two people who meet and fall in love later in life and the furry family they create. Oh yes, did we mention that they find a new member of this family in a most unusual place? This first edition is in English and Dutch. We hope that subsequent editions can appear in English and other languages. Therefore the book will be a language learning tool as well as a lovely story.

We hope to create a 12-month wall calendar as a companion piece, and will be using proceeds to support a national animal rescue organization in the US and the Netherlands.


Please expect Yin and Yang to capture your heart, mind and spirit.

Honors and Awards:

I am accredited in public relations (APR), signifying that I have passed the rigorous oral and written examinations administered by the Public Relations Society of America (PRSA). I was a member of its national professional development committee, was the 1995 President of the Minnesota Chapter of PRSA, have served on its National Honors and Awards Committee, chaired its International Film and Video competition. I am certified by the MIT-Harvard Public Disputes program and have completed two courses in Hamline University’s Law School in Environmental and Public Interest Dispute Resolution. I earned my Bachelor and first Master of Arts degree from Temple University, where I also was an adjunct professor of English for 10 years. In 1992, I was the chair of the Greater Minneapolis Chamber of Commerce’s Leadership Minneapolis Program™ and sat on GMCC’s Public Policy Committee.
