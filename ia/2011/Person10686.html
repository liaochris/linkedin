David Wamsley’s Summary

Wamsley and his wife Cynthia relocated back to the San Francisco Bay Area in January of 2010 to begin work on GravityEight, an explosive new start-up specifically designed to help users create balance and wellbeing for life.

From 2003 until 2009 Wamsley served as founder and CEO of K2 Urbancorp, LLC, an eco-friendly real estate development company most noted for its signature project Evening Rose. This $80MM, 36 acre mixed-use development has received statewide recognition for its work in sustainable development and construction of US Green Building Council LEED certified homes and commercial buildings.

Prior to K2, Wamsley served as founder, Chairman and CEO of Campsix, a 65 person technology incubator located in San Francisco. The company secured $25MM in venture funding and successfully built and graduated multiple startup companies, including a joint venture with the Dow Chemical Company and Accenture. Campsix gained national media attention in the The Wall Street Journal, The Economist, The New York Times, Fortune Magazine, and dozens of other industry publications.

Wamsley entered the “startup” world of Silicon Valley in 1995, when soon after he founded Adauction.com (later OneMediaPlace) and served as its Chief Executive Officer. Adauction.com was the first online media marketplace to connect buyers and sellers of print, radio, television, and outdoor advertising in a dynamic real-time environment. The company secured over $88MM in venture capital and was later acquired by Seattle based Media Passage.
