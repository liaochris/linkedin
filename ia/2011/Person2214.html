Amy Lyndon’s Experience

  • CEO

    The Lyndon Technique

    (Entertainment industry)

    January 1992 — Present (19 years 2 months)

    The Lyndon Technique is a revolutionary "15 Guideline Map To Booking" Technique that provides a practical approach to auditioning and booking the job. This way of working is for any actor at any level in their career who wishes to learn the art of competition. By learning this technique you will know how to break down a script properly and always know what you are doing in your audition. This technique can take you from the bottom 99% to the top 1% of actors booking jobs.

    "The Lyndon Technique" has helped thousands of students learn how to book acting jobs, run successful businesses and consistently earn their living as actors. Amy Lyndon just launched http://tltaccess.com to help actors build their acting business from online educational information.

    Purchase lectures and The Lyndon Technique "The 15 Guideline Map To Booking" Handbook at http://thelyndontechnique.com

  • Actress

    Amy Lyndon

    (Entertainment industry)

    January 1983 — Present (28 years 2 months)

    Lyndon has 40+ films to her credit. She can be seen in 5 LionsGate Feature Films: "B.T.K," "Bram Stoker's Dracula's Guest", "Chicago Massacre: Richard Speck", "Ed Gein: The Butcher Of Plainfield" and "Are You Scared?" She also has 7 more Feature Films coming out in ‘11: “And They’re Off…” opposite Sean Astin, “Grooming Giselle,” “40 Sundays,” "The House That Jack Built” opposite Joe Mantegna, "The Poughkeepsie Tapes" for MGM, "Trinity: Gehenna,” “The Loneliest Road In America” and “The Vanquisher." She also Starred with Chris Pratt in "Cursed Part 3," by Rae Dawn Chong.

    Lyndon Guest Starred in over 25+ television shows including: “Law and Order: Los Angeles,” “Eagleheart” opposite Chris Elliott, “NCIS,” "Ugly Betty," "Entourage," "NYPD Blue," "JAG," Recurring Roles on “Days Of Our Lives,” “The Bold and the Beautiful," "General Hospital," "Young and The Restless" and a Recurring Guest Lead in "Freddy's Nightmares." Lyndon also just shot the “Untitled Lon Diamond Comedy Pilot” through Smart Entertainment.

    On Stage, Lyndon's wacky performance as Bobbi Michelle in "Last Of The Red Hot Lovers" at The Tiffany Theatre garnered critical notoriety and earned her a Marquee Comedy Award. Lyndon worked her comedy skills at The Groundlings, The Comedy Store, CBS On The Lot and The Int’l Blend Comedy Troupe with Rick Overton.
