Partnership; Marketing and Advertising industry

September 2008 – May 2010 (1 year 9 months)

I was headhunted by DFR whilst working at EOC Group.

As Specialist Event & Marketing Coordinator at DFR my role is diverse and my skill set is quite broad. My role objective is to identify marketing opportunities and develop relationships with tertiary education institutions and industry partners to increase recruitment into the Australian Defence Force (ADF) in the areas of Engineering, Health and Technical Trades.

In order to achieve this objective I coordinate and manage a variety of branding events and recruiting activities including large careers expos across Victoria, Tasmania and South Australia, information sessions, Military site visits and road shows and support the ADF as required.

I assist our National Marketing team with National campaigns as well as look after local marketing within the areas of Health, Engineering and Technical Trades.

My relationship management, negotiation, communication and business acumen are areas in which I am often identified as surpassing expectations.

This role requires me to be proactive and show imitative and identify areas and opportunities for new or improved business, both internally and externally.

I have received excellent performance reviews confirming my abilities. “Elyse demonstrates a proactive approach [and] has worked hard to develop sponsorship partnerships with numerous universities that are tailored specifically to ensure maximum return on investment and maximum exposure for Defence Force Recruiting”. Also noted is a superior ability to network, negotiate and organise client requirements as well as “a high degree of professionalism and enthusiasm”.

Junior Horse Riding Instructor / Animal Maintenance

Melton Equestrian Academy

Professional Training & Coaching industry

January 2000 – March 2009 (9 years 3 months)

I became a student of Melton Equestrian Academy (MEA) when I was 6 years old and have since had a strong interest and involvement with horses, and with the Academy.

When I was 15 I started a casual position at MEA assisting in any area that I was required including general farm hand work and general caring of horses. Our objective at the Academy is to not only instruct on the practical riding side but also to create well educated and above average future horse owners and equine industry employees.
