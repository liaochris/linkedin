Betty Cohen’s Summary

Betty Cohen, a senior media executive and cable network CEO, advises both traditional and digital media companies via both consulting and board positions.

She is best known for leading the Cartoon Network as its President from its US start-up in October, 1992 to becoming a global phenomenon with asset value of nearly $3 billion by June 2001. Most recently, she was CEO of Lifetime Entertainment, the leader in women’s television.

Companies that are building brands at the intersection of content, community, commerce and experiential learning benefit from Cohen’s innovation track record, strategic sense, creative taste, leading edge talent relationships, senior media industry contacts and particularly powerful knack for building and “phenomenizing” content brands across platforms by understanding and connecting to specific target audiences within the current Zeitgeist.

Through her consulting and board positions, Cohen helps both “early stage” digital media tech clients and more established brands seeking to move into additional media arenas with:
• Cross- platform content creation/sourcing/distribution and marketing strategy;
• Introductions to/negotiations with game-changing talent, partners or customers;
• Guidance on pitch presentations from the traditional media executive’s perspective, all in order to grow her clients’ presence and adoption in the marketplace, whether B2B or B2C.

Cohen’s helps traditional media companies and content creators by exposing them to new technologies or applications that are relevant to their interests and critical to their future.

She is particularly interested in:
• “Values conscious” cross-platform media ventures designed to entertain while empowering online and offline community participation
• Projects at the leading edge of using narrative content + interactive aps in service of experiential e-learning offerings to engage students of all ages

Betty Cohen’s Specialties:

• Building and running diversified media companies generating revenues in the $ hundreds of millions
• Content and marketing strategy to launch existing properties across new platforms
• Identifying new hit programming and repackaging library content for new audiences
• Business development for branded entertainment ventures and digital media tech companies
• Kids/teens/young adult audience/user insight
• Women 18-49 audience/user insight
• Identifying all-star teams
