Joseph Holmes's Summary

As founder of Acuity Edge in '00, Joseph (Joey) enjoys the technology-business interface as found in university tech transfer, corporate IP management, entrepreneurial ventures, and private equity deals. He helps clients to commercialize inventions through strategy dev't, opportunity evaluation, tech marketing, and deal negotiations. He has evaluated well over 1000 inventions leading to hundreds of license deals, tens of startups, and millions in client revenues. Through his career, Joey has conducted projects aiding Arkema, Avery Dennison, BASF, DOD, Duke, EPRI, Ford, GE, Kobe Steel, Matsushita, Mayfield, Michigan State U., Motorola, MVC Capital, NASA, Nomura, RTI, Rohm & Haas, Sherwin Williams, Siemens, Textron, The Tokarz Group Advisers, U. of Illinois, Uni-Systems, and Xerox. Through his broad contact base, his team has explored diverse domains including aerospace, biomedical devices, biotech, electronics, energy, materials, medical, pharma, software, and telecom. He also has coached entrepreneurs, reviewed SBIR proposals for gov't agencies, judged business plan competitions, and conducted due diligence for private-equity investors. Joey identifies with inventors, can speak their language, and enjoys helping clients bridge the gap between science and market success.

Synergistically, Joey has also served as Adjunct Prof. & Strategy Consultant for Duke's Master of Engineering Management (MEM) program on a part-time basis since '04. He co-developed growth plans with MEM leaders where annual enrollment has since risen by over 300%. He has taught "Commercializing Technology Innovations" (CTI) leveraging 48 industry projects to date, and founded the "Consulting Practicum Program" (CPP) securing 35 projects from sponsors including Alliance One, Cisco, Deutsche Bank, Hill Rom, IBM, Lenovo, Microsoft, Nokia, Parsons, and SES Americom. Helping students interface with industry, Joey helps Duke achieve their goal of developing "business-savvy" technology managers.

Specialties

Strategy development, business development, licensing deals, intellectual property management, technology assessment, educational assessment, market intelligence, startup coaching, and project management.
