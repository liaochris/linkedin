Steve Hearsum’s Summary

I work with clients who are struggling to make sense of the patterns that emerge from the chaos and uncertainty when people come together in organisations, departments, groups, teams and indeed many other settings. I help clients to see the big picture, and have a knack for drawing out underlying issues, empowering clients to find sustainable solutions to the challenges they face.

I’m a coffee fan and, curious as to why I could never find a decent cappuccino, I began searching for the perfect blend of bean, grinder, milk and steam, and the best way to combine them. The interconnectedness of ingredients and process has become a governing philosophy for me. Applying this to organisations, and concerned with cultural and interpersonal ‘fit’, or how to integrate people with technology or processes, I’ve noticed that clients often seem preoccupied with putting things in boxes.

They overlook the relationship between the ingredients and how they are combined, or the resultant impact on authenticity and, ultimately, performance. I help clients to debox themselves and their organisation, bringing about real change and innovation.

I have experience across a variety of industries/sectors, including newspaper & magazine publishing, TV, telecommunications, not-for-profit, inter-governmental, NHS and local government.

I am a qualified Human Systems Dynamics Practitioner, collaborating on an innovative approach to improving performance through understanding and cultivating workplace relationships and researching a book on interpersonal 'chemistry'.

  • Co-founder

    The Fit Forum

    (Research industry)

    July 2009 — Present (1 year 8 months)

    The purpose of the Fit Forum is simple. It exists to provide a space a for conversations and dialogue about Human Fit in all its varied forms.

    As a starting point, we see the following as categories of fit, and we are open to the list being added to.

    * Cultural
    * Organisational
    * Interpersonal
    * Management style
    * Person & environment

    The key factor is that all of these emerge from the individual lived experience of one person with one or more other people and/or the environment. In other words, Human Fit is relational.

    Who is it for?

    Practitioners, researchers and anyone in between. Professionals in the world of business who have an interest in the territory, whether from a management, HR, change management or other perspective are all welcome.

    Steve co-founded The Fit Forum with Bruce Lewin of FourGroups.
