Joshua McCoy’s Summary

Joshua E. McCoy is co-Founder and Chief Executive Officer of The J. Lewis Group, a burgeoning integrated communications firm that focuses on building brand awareness in a way that facilitates purchase decisions. In this capacity, Joshua helps new and established brands develop a succinct message and communicate that message through a plethora of media and touch points, while cutting through the noise of the marketplace.

Joshua’s first experience in strategic consulting came at a very young age. While only 12 years old, Joshua was a tapped as a key voice for Dell Computer’s “Earn-n-Learn” community outreach program. The insight he gave to decision-makers proved to be an integral part of the improvement and expansion of the program.

Through various leadership positions in organizations in his school and community, Joshua was able to enhance his reputation for actualizing lofty visions and goals. At the age of 16, he led efforts to raise over $25,000 so that 20 students could attend a national youth empowerment conference. He was also instrumental in the planning and execution of the “Jail is No Place to be Somebody” seminar aimed at keeping Denver youth out of the criminal justice system.

On the collegiate level, Joshua led many organizations and campus initiatives that improved student life and the perception of the institution in the community. As a sophomore, he launched Project B.E.A.U.T.Y. (Beautifying Every Aspect of the University Through You), which is still active on campus today. This campaign was setup as student-run campus beautification which focused on building first the inner beauty of students.

As a motivational speaker and life coach, he addresses students, entrepreneurs and business teams on many topics including the following: brand identity (personal or business), vision and leadership. His speech “Blood of a Slave, Heart of a King” was recognized with the Gold Medal of Achievement by the William Hooper Councill Oratorical Society.
