Tess Andersen's Summary

SUMMARY
A Senior Designer and Art Director with a passion for avant-garde typography, gripping photography and cutting edge design. A conceptual thinker, she has the ability to see the world in new ways. Possesses the willingness to explore solutions that integrate issues and demands into innovative design solutions. Over ten years experience creating compelling, effective design and/or directing brand positioning for corporate, non-profit and media companies, including Colliers International, Drugstore.com, EMAP, Flaunt, Microsoft, Nautica, Reebok and Seattle Pacific University.

Tess Andersen's Experience

Manager, Global Brand Design | Global Marketing

Colliers International

Privately Held; FSRV; Commercial Real Estate industry

August 2009 – Present (1 year 8 months)

Led, visualized, developed and launched comprehensive new global brand for Colliers International to 480 offices in 61 countries worldwide—a company with almost US $2 billion in annual revenue. This formerly dated brand was recreated in a modern and innovative look—cutting edge for a conservative industry. The complete redesign contributed to repositioning Colliers as the leader in global real estate services, propelling it into the number two brand position for its category from a number five position just a few years earlier. The overall global re-brand was a key driver in positively influencing mergers and acquisitions, reflected in an impressive 2010 49% global growth. The successful launch included:

• Art directing a creative team in the creation of over 160 marketing communications, templates and assets for localization.
• Collaborating with strategic executive team and creative vendor in the initial visual direction of the rebrand.
• Creating, in just four months, a highly effective global intranet to distribute design materials to markets.
• Writing and designing multiple branding subsidiary guides and leading market compliance.
• Developing a compelling new brand road show including speaking at the Colliers Canadian marketing conference and presenting education courses at the Colliers University.
• Providing daily creative oversight and brand counsel to global marketing directors to evaluate which position and design directions would drive greatest impact in supporting the company’s vision.
• Designing global and US CEO presentations for use in critical corporate mergers and key communications to over 15,000 employees.

Creative Director & Owner

Inlumino

Graphic Design industry

August 2005 – August 2009 (4 years 1 month)

Founded and developed Inlumino as a boutique creative firm focused on developing client brands. The studio held over twenty core clients and hundreds of successful brand and marketing communications projects. Key projects included:

• Producing brand system development, identity creation and marketing communications for clients such as: Drugstore.com, Beauty.com, West Sound Utility District, Seattle Pacific University, Harrison Medical Center, The City of Bremerton and Olympic College.
• Design of 2009 Beauty.com lookbook for skin, spa, value sets and color products, as a feature gift interest hook for both fashion industry editors and customers. This pulled in additional merchandise interest and boosted holiday sales.
• Creation of effective interior ad and communication campaign to launch the new Harrison Medical Center $35.5 million electronic medical records system. Expanded and defined Harrison Medical Center brand by developing much needed branding standards and design for advertising, exterior and interior publications including: posters; signage; brochures; music CDs plus a human resources recruitment campaign featuring: ads, collateral and exhibits.
• Additional work such as: business development; maximizing budgets to client needs; estimating, sourcing and buying print; presenting concepts and branding proposals; writing style-guides; and hiring and art directing various support, such as photographers and writers.

Designer

Inviso

Privately Held; Marketing and Advertising industry

October 2008 – February 2009 (5 months)

Microsoft Corporation channel marketing collateral was designed to strengthen each sub-brand or product and complement corporate branding requirements. This included projects such as:

• Creating Windows Server 2003 to 2008 transition campaign including designing large distributer to reseller sheets for worldwide localization, online advertising, and channel email campaigns for multi-national accounts.
• Developing a well-received retail newsletter template for utilization by internal Microsoft designers. This was targeted to educate big-box retailers on the latest Microsoft Office and Microsoft Hardware products and technology.
• Conceptualizing and designing a Microsoft Hardware marketing kit, comprised of: a catalog showcasing the newest mouse products, keyboard products, desktop sets, gaming products and Internet communications. The book featured a DVD with a user interface and dozens of customizable sell sheets promoting each product. This was used by large distributors as an effective tool to promote these innovative products to resellers.
• Constructing a facelift for Microsoft.com/piracy/”knowthefacts’ multi-page website focused on customer education. This touched upon the risks of counterfeit software, proper licensing practices, intellectual property rights, and legalization. This effective site featured clever visual analogies and language. While designed in 2008, it is still in use as of 2011.
