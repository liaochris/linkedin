Bernie S. Ocampo’s Summary

Seasoned real estate equity & debt asset manager with over 20 years experience.

Experienced in maximizing collateral value during market downturns.

Multidisciplinary skill set: asset & portfolio management (equity, debt, and REO); transaction management (acquisitions, dispositions and leasing); distressed loan workout & foreclosure; equity/debt underwriting & valuation; credit risk & financial analysis; lending; joint venture partnership governance; rehab, conversion & major construction/development monitoring; predevelopment entitlement process; market research; plus, property management & operations.

Nationwide experience in a wide variety of real estate types, sizes & complexity.

Experienced in Argus, Excel & Powerpoint.

MBA – THE UNIVERSITY OF CHICAGO.

BA (Focus: Urban Development) – UNIVERSITY OF WASHINGTON.
-----------------
Previously with TriMont, Commercial Capital Bank (JP Morgan Chase), Prudential Real Estate Investors, GE, Trammell Crow Company and Grubb & Ellis.

Oversaw up to $4B in asset values of diverse property types: high rise office, multifamily, retail, mixed use, hospitality/resort, predevelopment land, apartment condominium conversion, low to high-rise residential condominium development, & office/industrial condominium development. Previous portfolio involvement totaled over 50 projects, 9,000 residential units & 2.5M sf of commercial space.

Experienced in both institutional investment strategies (core, value-added and opportunistic) & small balance deals.

Provided highly client-oriented services for institutional investors (pension funds, life insurance companies, investment banks, private equity funds, REITs and commercial banks), foreign capital & high net worth individuals.

Capital markets: preferred equity, joint ventures, loans (senior, mezzanine, bridge, corporate, as well as whole, securitized [CMBS], syndication and participation), and also combination of equity & debt in the capital stack.

Bernie S. Ocampo’s Specialties:

Value added real estate asset repositioning.
