  • Community Outreach & Educational Development

    U.S. Peace Corps

    (Government Agency; International Affairs industry)

    June 2004 — July 2006 (2 years 2 months)

    EDUCATIONAL PROGRAM DEVELOPMENT & CURRICULUM PLANNING
    Sarah taught English to youth and teachers in a K-12 school. Her curriculum included personal development skills, literacy, empowerment and self esteem development, Sexually Transmitted Disease (STD) prevention practices and public health. She was responsible for the development of all lesson plans, student projects and determining a culturally appropriate grading system. These accommodated classrooms of varying scholastic and emotional development levels. Sarah organized and taught library appreciation classes on record keeping and organization. Additional literacy and responsibility initiatives were enforced when she set the standard of an open library every day. Above all, Sarah was a teacher and friend who listened, advised and encouraged.

    COMMUNITY OUTREACH & NUTRITION PROGRAMS | FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT
    Sarah's operational responsibilities included managing donor communications, relations and conflict resolution between community beneficiaries, local donors and regional donors. Sarah was responsible for tracking all financial expenditures, managing project budgets, leading requests for additional project funding and dispersing payment to local vendors. She audited food stores and daily in-take to measure and evaluate program effectiveness in relation to donor goals and community gains. Sarah mentored and trained community members to assume her responsibilities upon her completion of service.

    HIV/AIDS PREVENTION TRAINING
    She worked with community members to identify local health issues, isolate the most critical and develop an action plan. Sarah and her local team created programming to educate at-risk male youth in the mining industry regarding risk factors for contracting HIV/AIDS. She secured funding from local and international donor agencies, secured facilitators and coordinated all logistics including; meals, lodging, transportation, and materials given the available local resources.

  • Educator

    Southwestern University of Economics & Finance (Chengdu, China)

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    June 2002 — June 2003 (1 year 1 month)

    ENGLISH CURRICULUM DEVELOPMENT & STUDENT EDUCATION PROGRAMS
    Immediately after earning her undergraduate degree in accounting from Marietta College in Ohio, Sarah was selected to travel to Chengdu, China to spend a year teaching graduate and post graduate course work in Business English Studies, Conversational English and English Through Films. In this role, Sarah developed and implemented the annual curriculum, created projects and specialized grading scales, trained and organized the collegiate speaking team for the national English competition and navigated the cultural gap to ensure each of her programs was successful. Sarah organized and ran a tutor program for students struggling with advanced course material. In addition, Sarah created the Debate & Conversation Club. This group provided an outlet for students to practice their English skills by exploring new ideas in a safe environment where they were encouraged to discuss issues which affected them including politics, equality, freedom, family obligations, adult relationships, STDs and similar life topics.
