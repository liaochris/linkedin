Ashok Abbott Ph.D. MBA’s Summary

Ashok Abbott is a tenured associate professor of finance at West Virginia University in Morgantown, West Virginia. Ashok received his M.B.A. in Finance from Virginia Tech, Blacksburg, VA, in 1984. He received his Ph.D. in finance, also from Virginia Tech, in 1987. His Ph.D. dissertation title was "The valuation effects of tax legislation in corporate sell-offs".

He has over 20 years of experience in the conceptual and practical aspects of business valuation. In addition to publishing extensively in scholarly finance research journals, Ashok has presented at national and international finance and valuation conferences. His research focuses on corporate valuation in mergers and acquisitions, with an emphasis on discounts for lack of liquidity and marketability.

Ashok provides analytical and research support to members of valuation community. His recent assignments include working with S&P, Duff & Phelps, and Willamette Management Associates. He has served as an expert witness in business valuation for 10 years.

Representative assignments include:

-Event study for identifying impact of death of a CEO on market value of a publicly traded software company.
-Event study to identify market over-reaction to merger of gas and electric utility companies- resulted in a major capital gains tax savings to selling stock holders.
-Seasonality analysis to identify excessive pre-season discounting generating post-contract, pre-takeover soft drink container sales in Latin America, resulting in a favorable settlement in the lost revenues dispute.
-Probability of IPO success and post-IPO performance evaluation for a valuation dispute, resulting in a favorable settlement
-Analysis of active and passive components of appreciation in the value of a closely held software company during marriage, resulting in a substantial beneficial settlement.
-Discounts for lack of liquidity for blocks of stock in estate and gift tax matters, resulting in a favorable settlement.

Primary areas: Premiums and discounts in business valuation assignments, Active and Passive appreciation in business value during marriage, Event studies: Economic Impact, Discounts for lack of liquidity, lack of marketability, lack of control, Merger premia.
