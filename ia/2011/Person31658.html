Marcia Treiger-Goldner's Summary

I hold a MFA from Tyler School of Art, where I studied with Will Larson, Michael Beacotte, and Ken Josephson. I started teaching at the college level upon graduation in 1976, and have been teaching since then to the present.
My website: www.marciatreiger.com displays many of the diverse projects I have pursued over the past 33 yrs, and my artwork can also be viewed and purchased through www.discoveredartists.com. I am also a working advocate for Jamuse.com, an international company tying artists to designers, etc.

Specialties

I specialize in the use of the camera obscura, or commonly known as a pinhole camera, the entire basis of the physics of photography, where I can take photographs without a lens, just a small pinhole pierced in a small piece of tin. There are two galleries on my website devoted entirely to pinhole photography.

Las Damas De Arte

Fine Art industry

July 2009 – Present (1 year 8 months)

Las Damas De Arte is the oldest group of working women artists in Tampa, and surrounding areas, devoted exclusively to raising scholarship money for non for profit art programs and scholarship money for art students. Once a year they hold their annual fundraiser called Sunday in the Arts, where all art is auctioned off, in order to achieve and replenish their scholarship funds.

Lead Faculty developing the newly founded BFA in digital photography

The Art Institute of Tampa

Photography industry

July 2008 – Present (2 years 8 months)

I was hired and moved from Philadelphia, to take this opportunity to direct the founding of a new BFA in digital photography. My responsibilities include all course development and materials, purchasing and overseeing new digital leaf backs, studio equipment, and ordering books on prominent and historical photographers, and processes. I also give the open house lectures to attract new students to the program. My student work is hanging in the hallways of the Art Institute, and is a testimony to the hard work and strides they have achieved in just one short year since it's inception.
