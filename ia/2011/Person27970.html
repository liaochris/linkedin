Mari D. Gonzalez's Summary

Mari is an interpreter and a graduate student in Intercultural Relations at the University of the Pacific, School of International Studies. Her research interest is in Latinos/Hispanics as consumers of social media; language and culture in social-network sites, ethnic identity, acculturation and integration. In May 2010, Mari received the MAIR, Masters of Arts Intercultural Relations, Pacific Fund Grant to research cross-cultural indicators in social-network sites.

She earned a B.A. in Education and Social Sciences from the Universidad Pedagógica Nacional in Jalisco, México, and graduated with honors from Holy Names University in Oakland, California, where she majored in Business Management and Communications.

In 2004, she founded Ixmati Communications, a consulting firm that conducted cross-cultural market research. Ixmati provided marketing and outreach strategies and translation services. Ixmati’s clients included John Muir Medical Center, The Contra Costa Times, The City of Concord, Chevron, and U.C. Berkeley, Labor and Occupational Health Program.

She is a member of the International Medical Interpreters Association (IMIA). In 2005, Mari was a featured consultant with the National Center for Cultural Competence (NCCC) at Georgetown University.
