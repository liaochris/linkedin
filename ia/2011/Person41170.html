Julie Starr's Summary

I run allaboutthestory.com, a smart place to buy and sell news stories, features, profiles, cartoons and illustrations. We make it easy for writers and editors to do business with each other.

It's free to join and upload stories and images for sale, and it's free for editors to browse or search for content in their areas of interest. Registered publishers can buy what they want, when they want it, and download it for immediate use.

I also do consultancy work on newsroom design and workflows.

Currently based in New Zealand, my background is in reporting, sub-editing and producing daily newspapers in New Zealand and the UK. I was a key member of the team who designed and launched the web-and-print integration project at the Telegraph in London. I fell in love with process mapping while I was there and continue to consult on newsroom integration and worklow projects.

In more than 20 years in journalism I have been a reporter, writer, sub-editor, page layout sub, chief sub-editor, radio presenter, workflow specialist, editorial manager, teacher and now owner of a media startup.

I recently developed an online journalism training programme on Moodle for distance learners. This role, at the Waikato Institute of Technology, ranged from developing the curriculum to engaging with our first cohort of students.

I have also done some face to face teaching, mostly drawing on my experience in the transition newspapers are making into the digital world. My goal in teaching is to highlight the profound changes in media and give students tools to break new ground in storytelling and make sense of new newsrooms.

Specialties

Startups 101, workflow design, web-and-print newsroom integration, newspaper production, EPC process mapping, presenting, writing, sub-editing, teaching journalism, curriculum development, Moodle course management.

February 2009 – Present (2 years 1 month)

allaboutthestory.com is an online marketplace where you can buy and sell news features, profiles, reviews, cartoons, caricatures, illustrations and more.

Writers and artists upload and price their own work, and approved buyers can browse the site and buy what they want, when they want for immediate download and use.

We handle commissioning for stories and illustrations, and we manage the syndication of a number of regular columns, including current New Zealand politics and business, the great outdoors, movies and parenting.

Our goal is to create efficient and affordable ways of finding, sharing, buying and selling content, and to help connect great content creators with quality publishers.
