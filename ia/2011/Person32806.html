Nigel Dalton's Summary

Ever since joining the New Zealand government as a graduate economist, I have lived in the twilight zone between business people and technology people. I bring a story-telling ethic to the binary precision of IT, and a practical understanding of machines to the world of business.

I have worked in public service, multi-nationals, start-ups, small business and consulting, and enjoyed them all. America, UK, Europe, Australasia - I appreciate the differences. Coal-mining, energy production, electronics, computer manufacture, financial services, industrial psychology, marketing and advertising, and now publishing - I learn fast.

Agile methods of software and product development have been central to my success this century, coupled with a growing understanding of the philosophy of Lean and the application to business and organisations.

And having cleaned up a lot of shit in my time, there is little that daunts me.

Specialties

Change management, technology, agile development, branding, marketing, economics, the human capital thing, leadership, internet, web 2.0, merger chaos, planning, and most of all - listening.

Deputy Director, Digital

Lonely Planet

September 2010 – Present (7 months)

General Manager 1's and 0's

Lonely Planet

Privately Held; Publishing industry

April 2007 – September 2010 (3 years 6 months)

As General Manager of IT since April 2007, my job's objective has always been clear enough - build a world class IT Operations and Software Development team, to enable a business emerging from 30+ years as a book and map publisher, to succeed in future as a multi-format media company. The context for that change program has always been the complication, and the exciting bit!

Off the back of an epic failure disguised as a waterfall-governanced IT program in 2005-6, we have transformed as an IT group over the last 2 years to a world-class agile (Scrum/XP) and ITIL based team, half its original size.

With the support of BBCW as a shareholder, we also managed to deliver the world's largest travel website, winning the Australian iAward for the simultaneous transformation+delivery outcomes, and the People's Choice Webby for best travel website.

Foundation now in place - let's build!

Consultant

R+R Robertson Communications NZ

Marketing and Advertising industry

July 2004 – March 2007 (2 years 9 months)

Working with globally experienced brand and advertising team supporting NZ and multinational clients with their marketing, strategy and technology requirements. Also did the monthly invoicing and made coffee. As you do.

CXO (Chief Everything Else)

ePredix

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Internet industry

February 2000 – April 2004 (4 years 3 months)

Managing ‘human capital’ became a new way of providing competitive business advantage (as detailed in McKinsey's War for Talent) across Banking, Telcos, Food and Beverages, Health, Automotive and Airline sectors. ePredix transformed the 50 year old science of I/O Psychology to a new business model supporting both online recruitment assessment and employee development.

My various roles at ePredix included managing Technology, Marketing, HR, general operations, patents, Sales, Company Secretary and CMO (Chief Moving Officer).

The products and services that ePredix offered were packaged as ‘solutions’ for clients – this was something of a missionary sale in a market used to buying simple psychometric tests.

My time in the USA developed my comfort and fluency at Board and Executive levels, as well as utilising the basic antipodean ethic of doing whatever it takes to ‘get the job done’. This helped to build a profitable company with $22m revenue and $4.5m EBITDA in just 4 years.

Application Development Manager

AXA Australia

Financial Services industry

February 1996 – March 2000 (4 years 2 months)

After working on a contract basis for the NZ branch of National Mutual as it underwent acquisition by French based multi-national AXA, I took a role in the merger and restructuring project based in Melbourne at the end of 1997. The project was run by McKinsey and Co with ongoing execution by Accenture.

In NZ my work had been primarily focused on customer service, marketing and IT functions to bring them into line with best practice within the AXA group. In the process I project managed a new telecom communications infrastructure into the head office, and worked on a variety of rationalisation and re-engineering projects.

In Australia the M&A work led me back to the world of Information Technology, in charge of 450 staff initially in Production Systems then Applications Development (after moving to a break-fix v development model), as well as Project Leader for Organisational Change. I was one of only two non-Accenture employees on the initially established IT Executive.

Sales and Marketing Guy

Silicon Systems NZ

Public Company; 501-1000 employees; Semiconductors industry

June 1991 – January 1996 (4 years 8 months)

I was Sales and Marketing Manager in this successful startup that offered a unique slant on the ‘PC box building’ business in NZ. By leveraging a quality assured, New Zealand assembled positioning backed by ISO9001 certification (the first for a computer company in Australasia) considerable business was gained from a maturing group of government and corporate buyers who recognised ‘clone PC’s’ as being capable of being assembled to order from premium components.

As winners of the NZ Baldridge Quality Awards, the company developed considerable business with clients including the NZ TAB and the Open Polytechnic based on its ability to deliver consistent high quality products and services.

Sales Manager - Computer Products Division

Melco Sales (Mitsubish Electric) NZ

Consumer Electronics industry

1989 – May 1991 (2 years)

As Sales and Marketing Manager in a startup division delivering a range of computer components, I absorbed the lessons provided by New Zealand’s most successful marketers of video players, televisions and other homewares, growing the division to over $10m in sales over 2 years.

This was jumping in at the deep end of a sales education - building a team, motivating salespeople, managing fast moving IT products, learning everything from the importance of stock turnover to how to collect debts.

Manager

NZ Government

Oil & Energy industry

May 1985 – 1989 (4 years)

Using my degree-based skills in economic analysis and social impact assessment for both coal mine and energy project development, and subsequently across mine closures and industry rationalisation, was a strong grounding for future career paths.

As technology was introduced to link customers to the coal-face (including the first 0800 customer number installed in 1987), I took the opportunity to transfer to the new SOE environment where I worked in HR and Marketing, establishing the Coal Shop brand and an award winning advertising campaign for domestic coal usage.

I worked in all the regional offices of SCM, including Ohai in Southland where I project managed the closure of the Morley Mine; and Huntly where I received the PRINZ Cherrington Award for the State Sector for work communicating the impacts of mining on the houses and communities above the Huntly East underground mine.

Investigating Officer

NZ Treasury

Government Administration industry

December 1984 – May 1985 (6 months)

I joined the NZ Treasury as a graduate from Waikato University. Assigned to the legendary Government Stores Board for the first year, I quickly realised my ambitions to take up a role in Energy Planning that had eluded me the year before.

The incumbent union member had not taken up the role and it appeared only 2 months later in the Public Service newsletter, enabling a transfer from the drudgery of re-writing the TY101 Government Stores Order Form to exclude sexist language, and stock taking the Masterton Government Printing operations.
