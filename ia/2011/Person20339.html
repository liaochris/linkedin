Burns & McDonnell

May 2006 – Present (4 years 11 months)

Founded in 1898, Burns & McDonnell (www.burnsmcd.com) is an award-winning engineering, architecture, construction, environmental and consulting services firm. We are a 100-percent employee-owned firm with our World Headquarters located in Kansas City, Missouri and offices nationwide. Burns & McDonnell plans, designs, permits, constructs and manages projects all over the world with one mission in mind -- to make our clients successful. From the water you drink, the air you breathe, the roads and airports you travel, to the power that illuminates your home, you don't have to look far to see ways that Burns & McDonnell touches peoples lives.

My role is instrumental in supporting Kansas City with recruiting and hiring needs which includes all levels of engineering (entry level through senior level management). Headcount doubled from 1500 to 3000 employee-owners in the past decade. Most of the hiring occurred in 2007, 2008 and 2009.

We are still actively hiring and I encourage you to check out jobs at www.burnsmcd.com/careers or connect with me to learn more!

Human Resources Manager

Truman Medical Center

Hospital & Health Care industry

January 2000 – May 2006 (6 years 5 months)

Truman Medical Centers is a non-for profit, academic health center providing accessible, state-of-the-art quality healthcare to the Kansas City community.

I was promoted twice during my tenure at Truman Medical Centers. I held roles specifically in recruiting and retention (Recruiter and Nurse Recruiter titles) and handled a generalist role of employee relations and training (Human Resources Manager).

Truman Medical Centers allowed me to develop best practices in searching for hard to fill nursing, radiology, respiratory and pharmacy jobs. I recruited top talent to an organization that deals with a challenging patient population.

As a certified DDI (Development Dimensions International) Instructor and Facilitator, I gathered, maintained and developed the behavioral job competencies and interview guides for over 900 different jobs in the organization. Additionally, I was one of a few individuals in the company who trained all the managers on behavioral interviewing techniques. The success rate at Truman Medical Centers of selecting the right candidates for the job after the training was used and implemented in a 3 year hiring timeframe was used as a case study nation-wide for DDI.

March 1998 – December 1999 (1 year 10 months)

Previously named Aliant Communications, it was bought out by Alltel Corporation. Alltel is a wireless telecommunications services provider, primarily based in the Southern United States. Before the merger with Verizon Wireless, Alltel was the fifth largest wireless telecommunications company in the United States, with 14.7 million customers. At its peak, Alltel operated a network in 34 states, with a wireless coverage footprint comprising the largest network in the United States by area.

I started at Aliant Communications as a Human Resources intern in college which quickly led to working over 30 hours a week in my junior and senior year of college. Due to the merger with Alltel, the HR department was downsized and they did not have a full time position once I graduated.

I assisted in the recruitment of entry level positions as well as technicians and I.T. openings. I learned to work with union and non-unionized environments. Other duties consisted of handling a variety of front office assignments as needed.
