Nick Farina’s Summary

I am currently President of Voltage Digital Media. We are a full-service Advertising & Marketing Agency with an emphasis on digital and interactive media. We work with established companies and high-growth potential early stage tech companies.

As for my background...

Entrepreneurship is a lifestyle for me; I don't think anything is more exciting than building a company or finding fresh ideas to solve a problem (I've never skydived though). In 2003, I founded one of the first eBay drop-off companies, and later expanded that into a specialized inventory liquidation service.

After exiting One Step Auction I moved into messaging at Brand Equity Advisors, specializing in digitial media strategy and crisis communications. I learned a ton there, and had the privilege to work alongside my father, who taught me everything I know about the core principles of media and marketing.

I struck out on my own again as a founding partner of e-Conversation, a digital marketing agency specializing in internet video. We worked with clients such as American Express, U.S. Department of State, and the City of Chicago, among others. Here, I combined my experience in marketing and messaging with digital media tools and developed deep expertise in online video.

I also love screenwriting. I have written four feature-length scripts, and making a movie has always been one of my dreams.

  • Board Member

    FMI and CAN TV

    (Financial Services industry)

    December 2009 — Present (1 year 3 months)

    I serve on the Board of Directors of the Financial Management Institute and CAN TV (Chicago Public Access Television)

    At FMI, we feel that Financial illiteracy is a crisis in America, made worse by the fact that community groups and nonprofits don't have the resources available to help educate their communities. Most financial curriculums are outdated, sponsored by financial companies, or simply ineffective at reaching their audiences. FMI has solved this problem by providing a completely unbiased financial curriculum that is designed around the way people actually learn.

    CAN TV offers something that grows more important by the hour: a voice for people who are not always heard. Some say that the internet gives everyone a voice, but in my digital media career I've seen that even for huge companies, cutting through the tremendous amount of noise online is a huge challenge. So what about for people with limited resources? It can be impossible. That's why CAN TV, which is one of the most widely used cable access programs in the country and operates five cable channels in Chicago (19,21,27,26,42) is so important, and why I am honored to serve on the board to promote its mission.

    I also serve on the Corporate Affairs Committee of NAWBO Chicago.
