Blaine Schnare's Summary

My journalism professor in college - whom I practically saw daily for four years straight - is likely sticking pins in a me-shaped VooDoo doll upon learning I've moved from print news to public relations. Some call it the 'dark side' - I'd like to refer to it as the 'awakening.'

My two and half years in post-college newspapers was indeed an experience I'll never forget. Without it, I would have never recognized or been given a chance to pursue my real passion.

I'm currently immersing myself in the world of corporate marketing - and, as the famous quote reads, no project was ever completed on time and within budget. I've learned more about "the way things are" the last (nearly) three years working in health care public relations than I likely had 18 years in public school. But the end result is always the same: a forward-thinking project, dynamic group effort and the tangible end result of which to be proud.

My mantra about "adult work life" has always been the same, even when the ink on my college diploma hadn't dried: It's not as much about what you do as it is about who you're working with. It brings me mental and emotional happiness that with each job I've been lucky enough to hold, I've been taught by some of the most generous individuals around - and not just businessmen and women - the kind of Average Joe and Josephines you'd meet walking your dog. A real life work environment = real life results.

My dream is to one day acquire the funding, time and mental preparation to expand my knowledge of graphic design. My self-taught Photoshop skills are intermediate, but in dealing with a program as complex as the world's biggest Rubix cube, I'd love to sink my teeth into the real meat and potatoes of candy cane letters and fire breathing shadows.

Of course, in my spare time, I enjoy sharing couch cushions with any domesticated four legged and furry creature, a really good Sushi dinner paired with a Pino Grigiot and a motivating Spin class.
