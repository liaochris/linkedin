Lori Sarageno, Ph.D. J.D.’s Summary

I am a patent attorney with significant previous laboratory research experience. I have a combined 15 years of polymer and organic chemistry research experience at General Electric Corporate Research and Development and the 3M Company. I am a coinventor on 20 U.S. Patents. I became a patent agent in 2002, then left research in 2003 to attend William Mitchell College of Law full time, graduating in 2006. I am licensed to practice before the U.S. Patent and Trademark Office as well as in the State of Minnesota and the United States District Court for the District of Minnesota.

To find some of my patents, go to the USPTO patent search website - advanced search - and type in "IN/Engle-L$".

Lori Sarageno, Ph.D. J.D.’s Specialties:

Green chemistry, filtration materials, microreplication, mechanical fasteners, inkjet printing, flexographic printing, extrusion and coextrusion techniques, electronic paper, adhesive technology, protective chemicals, fluorochemicals, anti-soiling polymers and colloids, anti-staining polymers, inorganic colloids and grafting of organic polymer thereto, waterborne polymers, rheological additives, silicone RTVs, thermoreversible crosslinking, and general organic and polymer synthesis.

  • Director of Intellectual Property

    Segetis, Inc.

    (Privately Held; Biotechnology industry)

    November 2007 — March 2010 (2 years 5 months)

    As Director of Intellectual Property at this green chemistry startup company, I managed a substantial patent and trademark portfolio. My responsibilities included patent drafting and prosecution, foreign filing strategy development, drafting of non-disclosure agreements, and joint development agreements. I also provided education of technical staff on topics such as notebook recordkeeping, inventorship, and trade secret management. I vetted and managed outside counsel on various matters.

  • Associate

    Merchant & Gould

    (Partnership; Law Practice industry)

    2005 — 2007 (2 years )

    I was a Law Clerk at Merchant & Gould while in law school and stayed on as an Associate after graduation. I did patent prosecution primarily in the chemical and pharmaceutical arts. Areas I addressed were inorganic and organic chemical inventions including new polymer structures and polymer blends, medical devices, microfluidic devices, coatings and films, fine fibers, filtration materials and devices, drug synthesis, and polymer synthetic methods. I also assisted senior counsel in freedom-to-operate and patentability opinions.

    January 1991 — August 2003 (12 years 8 months)

    Coinventor on 18 US patents. Extensive experience in technical team leadership, supervision.     June 1985 — August 1987 (2 years 3 months)

    Conducted research in silicone polymers, polymerization, and crosslinking mechanisms. I was named a coinventor on two patents, and coauthored two journal articles.
