Sameer Halepete’s Summary

Thirteen years of experience in IC design with the last nine managing complex high performance ASIC and CPU design projects from concept to production, building high caliber teams, developing design methodologies and interfacing with foundries (TSMC, UMC, IBM).

Sameer Halepete’s Specialties:

High performance ASIC and CPU design management, semiconductor foundry interface, ASIC design methodology, lower power IC design.

  • Director of HW Engineering

  • Director of CPU Design

    Transmeta Corporation

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; Semiconductors industry)

    February 1996 — October 2002 (6 years 9 months)

    Managed the architectural specification, logic design, verification and circuit design on a second generation high performance VLIW x86 microprocessor with a target frequency > 1GHz in 90nm. Managed day-to-day operation of microprocessor design team with 70 engineers.

    Managed circuit design, physical integration and tape-out of a high performance VLIW x86 microprocessor and drove it to production.

    Did circuit and logic design on a high speed microprocessor. Designed Shifters, adders, register files, datapaths for the integer and floating-point unit, the branch prediction array and fifos. Designed a serial-ROM controller, developed a standard cell characterization flow, RC extraction flow, datapath buffer-block flow, standard-cell P&R methodology. Was responsible for integrating two complete microprocessors and getting it LVS, DRC and ERC clean. Silicon ran successfully at speed the first time and both chips were shipped for revenue.

    April 1995 — February 1996 (11 months)

    Worked on low threshold voltage (Vt) CMOS technology. Participated in tape-outs of three test-chips and worked on a fourth one. Filed three patents and four disclosures of invention.
