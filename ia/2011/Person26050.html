Len Wright’s Summary

I am a Research Associate in the Climate and Civil Systems (CliCS) Research Group, in the Department of Civil, Environmental and Architectural Engineering at the University of Colorado at Boulder. I specialize in sustainable water resources infrastructure planning and management under changing climatic conditions. I am currently serving as project manager and lead hydrologic modeler for a study in Africa on the economics of adaptation to climate change. The team of local and international experts assembled for this project are charged with estimating the cost of adapting to climate change in the countries of Mozambique, Ghana, and Ethiopia. We are using results from Global Circulation Models (GCMs), biophysical models of the impacts of future climate scenarios on agriculture, infrastructure and energy, and CGE economic models to make these estimates.

I also teach graduate and undergraduate courses in water resources (systems analysis, hydrologic modeling, and hydraulics) for the Department of Civil, Environmental and Architectural Engineering at the University of Colorado at Boulder.

I am a licensed Professional Engineer in Colorado and New York and a Diplomate of Water Resources Engineering for the American Academy of Water Resources Engineers. Past consulting projects have included climate change analysis and policy work, peer review tasks for integrated water resources models, urban wet-weather pollution modeling, and sustainable wet weather infrastructure management. Recent clients include the World Bank, Stratus Consulting, US EPA, the Metropolitan District in Hartford CT with CDM and Malcolm Pirnie, the Hopi Tribe in AZ., Integrated Hydro Systems, and Broward County FL.

Len Wright’s Specialties:

Impacts of climate change on multi-scale infrastructure systems,
Feasibility and performance of Low Impact Development (LID) Stormwater systems,
Sustainable wet-weather control,
Risk analysis of water resources systems using Monte Carlo Analysis,
Long-term, continuous simulation models,
Whole-life economic optimization.
