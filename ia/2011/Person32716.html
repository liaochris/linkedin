Nicolas Sopel's Summary

I have worked for over two years in the Hedge Fund industry for SGAM Alternative Investments on the front office side of the business. I was part of the  Global Volatility Arbitrage Group. I was trading volatility as an asset class across Equities, Fixed Income, FX and Commodities with a main focus on Asian equities (Japan, Korea, Hong Kong, China and also Australia). Given the concerns over liquidity in the various markets most of our trading was all first generation, non exotic options with the majority being exchange traded so there was complete transparency and reduced counterparty risk. The only major exception was in the case of FX options where we traded primarily OTC options via our prime broker but even there we focused on keeping the maturity of options short dated i.e. less than 2-3 months. Regarding interest rates, we traded government bonds like Japanese JGB and US T-Notes.

I was co-managing the portfolio with a senior manager for the Short Bias Pan Asia Fund. The fund was setup with the objective of extracting alpha from equity markets in Asia using a combination of quantitative and systematic options strategies that allow for performance when stocks markets are stable or go lower, while preserving capital during bull markets.

Besides the trading, I was also in charge of some research themes. I developed my own management tools such as a real-time monitoring spreadsheet of the positions, which included pricing and exposure (delta, theta and vega) with daily automatic recording.

I have through my experiences and particularly in trading through last year’s extreme moves, developed a very good understanding and knowledge of the markets from a macroeconomic point of view. Thanks to my senior manager, I have been able to express my own analysis and to develop my quantitative skills with research themes. I started as a middle officer in Paris for the same team before moving to Singapore in early 2008.
