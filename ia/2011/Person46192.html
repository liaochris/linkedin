Simon X.Y. KONG's Summary

I am a graduate student pursuing a dual Master’s degree from Peking University and the University of Hong Kong in Management and Finance, respectively. During my undergraduate career, I majored in Economics, which provided a solid foundation in economic theories and increased my interest in pursuing advanced degrees and research. I did relevant research on Industrial Organization and Environmental Economics.

Seeking a more practical academic route after my Bachelor’s degree, I decided to study Management and Finance in consideration of pursuing managerial career in financial industry. Currently, I am engaged in a number of case studies, especially those related to Enterprise Strategy and Internationalization. My experience has provided me many opportunities to study the business strategies and models of different companies and make comparisons within and among industries. I learn many financial courses at HKU, which equip me with the tools to discern the real situation of enterprises.

As a 75th generation descendant of Confucius, I am highly interested in Confucianism and comparing Confucius’ managerial theories with those of contemporary managerial theories from the world and putting them into practice under the background of China. Besides, social sciences, philosophy, the arts and Internet are all my interests. I run an independent blog named 子曰： translated as “Confucius Said” to record my perspective and opinions on things I am interested in. You are welcome to visit it.

My internship experience spans several industries including commercial banking and consulting. I have participated in a summer programme held by National University of Singapore on Entrepreneurship and Economics. I was awarded the Scholarship for Excellent Freshman from HSBC and supported by the Dean’s Professional Development Fund from Peking University. I think these are some of strengths and advantages as a graduate student in business school. I would greatly appreciate your contacts.
