CEO

Interactive Marketing, Inc.

Internet industry

January 1992 – August 2001 (9 years 8 months)

Mr. Batkin founded Interactive Marketing, Inc. (IMI) in 1992. and became the leading interactive strategic consulting and promotion marketing company. IMI offered its clients interactive marketing, electronic commerce, and business planning and development services with a focus on creating new revenues.

In addition IMI owned a leading interactive publishing and conference company named Digitrends, which facilitated the growth of interactive marketing, Online, In Print, and In Person. Digitrends inspired interactive marketing strategies through case studies, peer-to-peer exchange, industry news and insightful analysis. IMI produced a series of senior-level conferences, (Camp Interactive, The Media Buyers Summit, Brand Leadership Forum, and Variety’s Interactive Marketing Summit) as well as the Digitrends.net Web site.

In 1996, Mr. Batkin founded SOFTBANK Interactive Marketing Inc. (SIM) after his company was bought by SOFTBANK CORPORATION in Japan. Under his guidance as Chairman and CEO the company sold 50 million dollars of interactive media in its first twelve months and grew to be the world's largest interactive media rep firm and largest single source of Web impressions reaching nearly half – a full 48.8 percent – of all active Web users through a portfolio of eight of its select, branded sites.

In early 1995, IMI, became the Interactive Marketing Agency of Record, for Yahoo! and developed the branding and media strategy to create Yahoo! into a powerhouse Internet media company with a multi-billion dollar valuation. IMI was also the exclusive interactive advertising sales organization for Yahoo! for the first two years of Yahoo’s young life and handed over 1700 sales relationships to the new sales team that was developed by Mr. Batkin. IMI performed similar work for Netscape, ZDNet, Playboy, the National Football League, where Mr. Batkin created SuperBowl.com; NBC, MapQuest, Hollywood.com and many others.
