Pavel Pinkava's Summary

Since 1990, I’ve worked in international sales, trading, marketing, IT and educational roles. My hands on experience in the industry over that time coupled with an inquisitive and analytical approach have made me a subject matter expert on a broad range of financial products and their risk/return management.

With a PhD in Theoretical Physics some would consider me highly numerate. However colleagues find me as much a people person as a technical specialist. Indeed, many share my view that the markets react as much to soft factors at play as they do to more readily quantifiable variables. The "subprime" crisis and the broad liquidity reaction to it bear this out and have led me to take an even greater interest in market history and economics. I have a particular ongoing interest in the financial IT business as it relates to the changing relationship between exchange tradable and OTC derivatives.

In graduate training and CPE I enjoy bringing the best out of people whatever their technical knowledge and background. Likewise I delight in extending myself through positive interactions with others.

Specialties

KEY WORDS: Bonds, Broking, Commodities, CDS and other Credit Derivatives, Czech, Deposit, Distribution, Emerging Markets, Excel/VBA, Exchange, Exotic & Vanilla Options, Financial Markets Training, Foreign Exchange Spot, FX Swaps, HOTCE, Inventor, IRS, NDF, OIS, Product Management, Repo, Risk Management, STIR and Bond Futures, Structured Products, Trading Tools, Trading, Visual Basic, x-Swaps.

Financial Innovator/Pioneer

any time

Capital Markets industry

1994 – Present (17 years)

I consider myself to be an authority on, and an innovator within, the field of exchange tradable derivatives. Indeed I have single handedly extended the range of derivatives tradable on exchange (beyond futures and options). Likewise I have described a new type of exchange that redefines the market model such that it behaves more like an OTC market. I have other ideas... Insights in the public domain and my publications are:
• 2010: “How to move OTC Derivatives Markets on to Exchange: A Detailed Guide For Leaders & Specialists”, January.
• 2009: I developed a new market paradigm which I call "HOTCE" i.e. Hybrid OTC/Exchange margin system and market microstructure.
• 2008-9: “Microstructure evolution in the credit default swaps market”, co-author, Review of Futures Markets, Volume 17 Number 3, Pages 235-246.
• 2006: Four US patent applications which had been filed in 2005 became public and hence were given patent numbers 20060224491, 20060224492, 20060224493, and 20060224494.
• 2004: I discovered how the product distribution model of a classic electronic futures exchange can be transformed using mapping algorithms so as to allow products like interest rate swaps and credit default swaps to be listed. In fact I developed a new category of product called "x swaps" and designed individual "xCDS", "xOIS" and "xIRS" products.
• 1994: My model for extracting implied probability distributions from exchange traded options series was published in the LIFFE quarterly journal. I was invited to visit the Deutsche Bundesbank to explain my system the following year.
