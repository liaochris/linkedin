Maher Attar's Summary

Maher Attar is a highly renowned photographer with over 28 years of experience with international photo agencies including AFP, Sygma and Corbis in Paris before he created his own agency in 2002. He has also published several high quality photography books.

A retrospective of his work - "A Twenty Year Journey" -was held in Paris in 2003, accompanied by a brochure with a limited print run of 1,200 copies. On May 18, 2006, his native country of Lebanon honoured him with the exhibition "Born in Lebanon" during National Heritage Day. At the same time, to mark the International Year of Deserts and Desertification under the aegis of the United Nations Environment Programme, an exhibition of his work was held at the Espace Pierre Cardin in Paris entitled "Auprès de Mon Arbre" ("Beside My Tree") featuring nine large format photographs celebrating nature, water and life.

Maher's "Bonheur" project blossomed in the summer of 2006 in the form of a series of exhibitions and a book. Following that, he published "Once Upon A Time...Souk Waqif" capturing the mystery and spirit of the old market in Doha, Qatar. Images from this book were exhibited on large format canvas boards in Doha during the 2006 Asian Games and later at UNESCO headquarters in Paris. Lastly, "Portraits...Lebanon" focused on personality portraits of leading figures from contemporary Lebanon's economic, artistic and literary sectors.

A resident of France since 1990, Maher has been living in Qatar in recent years. He serves as the the Director of the Photo Library and official photographer of Her Highness Sheikha Mozah bint Nasser Al-Missned, first lady of Qatar, and concentrates on special projects including a program to teach the children of third-world Asia the techniques and joys of photography.

Specialties

Professional photographer, specialized in portraits, grands reportages and corporate photography.

Sygma Photo Agency

Photography industry

May 1986 – August 2002 (16 years 4 months)

Sygma was launched in 1973. It quickly became one of the leading photo agencies in the world, representing best photographers around the world. The Sygma archive also includes images from the 1950s and 60s obtained from Apis, Universal Photo, Interpress, Spitzer, Reporter Associes and other agencies.
In the 1990s it changed hands several times, but in 1999 Bill Gates acquired it through Corbis. It was not a painless acquisition. In 2001, 90 employees, including 42 photographers, were made redundant after weeks of protests and strikes. Since then, the agency has been inactive, with no new content submitted in the archives, and former Sygma photographers now known as Corbis contributors.
