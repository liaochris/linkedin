Dean Stanberry, CFM, LEED AP's Summary

As a seasoned corporate real estate and facilities management professional, I continually analyze trends influencing the fundamental nature of work. Over the past decade, these trends have increasingly affected how, when and where work occurs. Other factors such as technological advancements, environmental concerns and corporate social responsibility have further influenced the way we think about the workplace.

As business cycles shrink, the need for increased flexibility and innovation in workplace design and operations becomes imperative. The desire for effective and efficient use of space has grown in stature, while diminishing the archaic entitlement mentality. In this new reality, best-in-class organizations promote an environment that leverages the triple bottom-line of people, planet & profit.

To be successful in this environment, contemporary corporate real estate and facilities management leaders need to possess a composite of business, financial, technology, and sustainability expertise. I strive to maintain a balanced blend of these skills and talents.

Specialties

A visionary real estate and facilities leader offering over 15 years of experience, from start-ups to multi-national corporations. Prominent skills include workplace strategy, real estate, sustainable facilities, critical infrastructure, program & project management, quality & process improvement, procurement, and finance.

An innovator possessing exceptional organizational abilities, recognized expertise in expense control and known for consistently delivering results on time and on budget.
