Kate Hoppe's Summary

As I go through life, I hold the vision of a world dedicated to sustainable community values. By this I mean a way of life that is holistic on every level - one that works on our business practices and aligning these with social needs, on our health and the lifestyle choices we make, how we perceive and interact with our environment, and on how we treat one another as human beings.

In the individual and collective search for creative solutions to our current challenges, we can see a strong trend toward greater community cohesion, with corporations taking on greater social responsibility, the preference for localized food choices, and a widespread acceptance of the "green" movement in businesses and homes nationwide.

As with any vision, tools are needed to build it. Below are some of the tools I possess for helping to bring this vision forward.

- I have extensive experience in the communications and managerial fields where I've dealt directly with the public and media, and where I've been responsible for the successful completion of large-scale projects, such as attaining media coverage for national tour, managing the production of a biannual newsletter and appeal, and executing an ethnographic study abroad.

- I am a powerful writer with experience editing the content of scholarly articles for a women's academic journal.

- I have a passion for sustainability and have lived and worked on three organic farms in the U.S. and abroad.

- I'm tech savvy with an insatiable curiosity and desire to learn more. (Macs, Microsoft Office, Adobe CS4, some HTML/CSS, 83 wpm, PeopleSoft, Convio, Fundraiser).

- I’m an adept public speaker with the ability to instill passion in other people toward some idea or cause.

- I’m a quick learner and creative thinker with the ability to work outside the box, being inventive and resourceful when it comes to promoting something I believe in.

Specialties

Public relations, communications, sustainability, food and agriculture, writing, editing, public speaking, creative and fresh ideas.

Educational Institution; Higher Education industry

The ethnography came together in 75 pages and focused on the Western idealization of Eastern "enlightenment". But more than that, it dug into the effects of this popularization on the Thai people and their culture. I explored the rejection of consumer culture by "spiritual" Americans and showed their continued participation in consumerism through tourism.
