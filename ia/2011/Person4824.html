Benjamin Koehler bjk35@georgetown.edu’s Summary

I recently graduated from Georgetown University's School of Foreign Service and joined BNP Paribas Project Finance team.

Until 2009, I was an Infrastructure Finance Analyst at Castalia, where I advised businesses and governments on the operation, valuation and structuring of transactions for power and water utilities.

Prior to joining Castalia, I worked in the Portfolio Management Division at the Overseas Private Investment Corporation (OPIC). At OPIC, I was responsible for determining the creditworthiness of infrastructure projects in Argentina, Venezuela, Nigeria and South East Asia.

    January 2008 — August 2008 (8 months)

    • Performed credit analysis on five large energy projects, two hotels, and a telecommunications company with a combined NPV of over US$800 million. Analyses included reviewing covenants, projecting pro forma financial statements, sensitizing cash flows, and projecting key profitability and cash flow ratios, such as the debt service coverage ratio. Based on these analyses, recommended reserve ratios for OPIC loans
    • Wrote eight five-page memos summarizing the results of the creditworthiness analyses and detailing the rationale behind my recommended reserve ratios
