Ed Prince’s Summary

Professionally:
In 1985 I began my career as a valuation actuary with Pyramid Life Insurance Company in Mission Kansas. I moved to Blue Cross Blue Shield of Kansas City where I developed the actuarial work on seven new products. In 1995 I went to Employer’s Reinsurance Corporation to build a managed care department. In 2000 I was recruited to Sprint to build a Vendor Management Program.

In 2003, with the support of my wife, I quit my corporate career to become a financial professional. I felt it a great risk to stay in Corporate America. No one was going to control my career, but me.

Before trekking out, I seriously thought how I would operate my practice. Because I had been around the block, I knew what I liked and what I didn’t. I also knew the results that I had accomplished on my own. From that, I developed “The Tenants” which guide me, everyday.

Seven years have come and gone. I used to say that I was in a PhD program on steroids and the learning curve was steep and long. Now I say it’s a lesson in people and it’s a learning cliff, straight up. I am not the person I was, thankfully so. I have accomplished a lot with a lot more to go. I have made a beneficial impact on many people. I have met outstanding people who I would not have been able to meet. I am happy. I have begun to understand why the Lord has put me on this earth.

Networking:
For most folks, networking is a learned skill. Because human beings are social animals, networking is crucial in order to be successful. I believe networking requires the following: a) a desire to do it; b) the desire to help others in their efforts; e) persistence; f) extraordinary professional courtesy; g) follow-up and follow through; and, h) goals.

LinkedIn is an excellent tool. Tools are used to do great things, when used effectively.
