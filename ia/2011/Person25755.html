Lauri Bayless's Summary

Elsewhere, LLC is a virtual administrative assistant service committed to managing the minutia of your business tasks. We're committed to pulling it all together for you and lightening your load!

While working with entrepreneurs and business executives, we specialize in working with writers, authors, aspiring authors, published or unpublished. Peter DeVries says it so clearly, "I love being a writer. What I can't stand is the paperwork." We manage the paper work for you.

Specialties

Manuscript preparation, Kindle and other electronic book formats, website maintenance, permissions and fact research, marketing functions, social media, webinars, powerpoint presentations, audio, bookkeeping your book, and much, much more.

Competencies in Adobe including Dreamweaver, Photoshop, InDesign, Illustrator, Contribute; MS Office including Outlook, Excel, Word; and QuickBooks. Competencies in various virtual tools such as webmail, audio recording and editing, video editing, and more.

Lauri Bayless's Experience

Owner

Elsewhere, LLC

Executive Office industry

January 2006 – Present (5 years 3 months)

In January, 2006, Lauri launched Elsewhere, a company devoted to remote Administrative Assistant services. Elsewhere quickly developed a reputation for innovative web services including webinar hosting, virtual meetings, and internet product sales. In short order, Lauri's relationship-building, secretarial and office administration, and leading edge technology skills resulted in her introduction to the internet self-publishing industry. A symbiosis quickly developed as she began to assist her clients in their publishing endeavors, resulting in a fine-tuned focus as a Virtual Administrative Author's Assistant, providing a seamless array of self-publishing services and recommendations for authors.

Business Manager/Owner

Prime Focus Solutions, Inc.

Hospital & Health Care industry

January 1992 – January 2006 (14 years 1 month)

In January, 1992, Lauri embarked upon a 15-year-long business venture with husband and Pharmacist, Ken Bayless as together they purchased an independent pharmacy in Greenville, South Carolina. Collaboratively weaving Lauri's keen business intuition, sensitive relational discernment, and seasoned strategic endurance with Ken's competencies in health care administration, disease state management, and optimal patient care, they initially found a niche in providing superior pharmacy care services to a small but appreciative circle of clients and friends. One of those client-friends, an attorney, suggested expanding the business offerings into Medical Transcription for physicians, a logical step, considering Lauri's already significant organizational and documentation skills developed through working as a legal assistant. Having survived the initial start-up difficulties of most businesses, the transcription/documentation company ultimately flourished as a result of a superior product, timely delivery, and personal daily interaction with physicians and management staffs. Divesting the pharmacy in 1995, Prime Focus became known for state-of-the-art digital technology, unshakable commitment to quality, and flexibility in tailoring its product to individual physician office needs. As 2005 progressed, Lauri and Ken realized the rise of the electronic medical record industry would soon eclipse the need for transcription services, leading them to suspend their venture in early 2006.

Business Secretarial Instructor

Northland Baptist Bible College

Educational Institution; Higher Education industry

August 1986 – May 1988 (1 year 10 months)

In the midst of a developing, satisfying and rewarding career at Builder Marts of America, a lumber brokerage firm, having risen to the Billing Department Manager in a short period of time, Les Ollila, then-President of Northland Baptist Bible College, requested Lauri to come to Northland to help the school develop a Secretarial Sciences curriculum. Initially resisting because of financial obligations, Les simply requested that they both pray about the matter. Within the week Lauri was informed that her department at BMA was moving to Colorado, and she had the option to move there to keep her position. Sensing God's hand in this development, she chose rather to pursue the position at Northland, where Providentially God reinforced in her life the meaning of being His Servant in helping other young ladies prepare for life's challenges.
