Tammy Castro’s Summary

I have been consulting for over 15 years supporting JD Edwards' clients. Prior to consulting, I was an HR Coordinator for a large farming company. This history provides me with a much needed understanding of how an HR Department functions, and the need for clear processes and data reporting. I earned my PHR back in 1993 and retained it up until about 3 years ago, when due to overseas assignments, it expired. I point this out, because it demonstrates that I have a strong HR industry background.

My consulting approach for the ever-changing business climate is to work from a ‘big picture’ perspective. Implementation tunnels can be dangerous and cave in, and I’ve been called in for rescue and repair on such cases too often. Clients with admirable intentions of saving money have ended up paying more in the long run due to tunneled vision implementation plans not considering the big picture for proper system configurations.

The JD Edwards HCM system is only part of the overall solution. The entire business process needs to be evaluated and taken into consideration when configuring JD Edwards. For current processes: How many touch points are there? Is it efficient? Are there bottlenecks existing? Does the HCM system offer functionality that can benefit a given process relieving touch points and bottlenecks? I specialize in working with the users in the department to clearly identify all the business processes dissecting the steps of each process which I diagram on a functional band flowchart.

I am then able to analyze and re-engineer the diagrams for performance excellence based on JD Edwards HCM system functionality coupled with my industry experience and JD Edwards expertise. The critical component to this success comes from understanding the specifics of your organization, the culture, and business requirements including government regulations.
