John Byrne’s Summary

Producer of print publications for resort and golf course operators in the U.S.

John Byrne’s Specialties:

Print production, art and creative direction, custom publishing, digital and large format photography, golf and travel writing.

John Byrne’s Experience

  • President & CEO

    Review Publications

    (Publishing industry)

    1995 — Present (16 years )

    Review Publications is the parent company for all publications produced by John Byrne. These include golf publications in Hawaii, and the mainland U.S.

  • Editor & Publisher

    Maui Golf Review

    (Publishing industry)

    1995 — Present (16 years )

    The Maui Golf Review is a guide to Maui's rich golfing resources. It has won every significant regional award for photography, editorial content, and design, and is widely regarded as one of Hawaii's leading periodicals.

  • Editor & Publisher

    Palm Beach Golf Review

    (Publishing industry)

    2001 — 2002 (1 year )

    Robert Rippee, then director of sales and marketing at The Ritz-Carlton, Palm Beach, has been a client of mine when he held the same position at the company's Kapalua, Maui property. He contracted me to produce the Palm Beach Golf Review as a marketing tool for the resort, over a two-year stretch. The end-result was a 9"x12" film-laminated golf guide for Palm Beach with the hotel as its centerpiece.

  • Group Publisher

    ParValu/Golf Southern California

    (Publishing industry)

    1998 — 1999 (1 year )

    ParValu, Inc., purchased two regional golf publications (Island Golf Review/Hawaii and Golf Southern California) with intent of building a network of regionals across the U.S. As group publisher, it was my mission to manage this expansion by creating alliances (partnerships) with existing publishers, or establishing a competitive title in markets where this was more practical. The company's core business of golf equipment sales was a barrier to growth in its publishing business which ultimately closed.

  • Editor & Publisher

    Island Golf Review

    (Publishing industry)

    1997 — 1999 (2 years )

    The Island Golf Review was a Hawaii guide to golf that covered the full gamut of golf editorial in the islands. This included amateur and professional golf events and personalities, golf course reviews, lifestyle stories, and all other related topics. Island Golf was purchased, in 1998, by ParValu, Inc., a Carlsbad-based company.

  • President & CEO

    Fleet Maintenance Corporation

    (Automotive industry)

    1987 — 1991 (4 years )

    This beta company was created as a test to establish the feasibility of a fleet service company/franchise. It was a highly successful concept that took hold in four NE/Mid-Atlantic states: NY, NJ, PA, DE. The company was spun off into a consumer-service enterprise that thrived in this market for over 10 years, before being sold.

  • Advertising Sales

    Institutional Investor Magazine

    (Publishing industry)

    1986 — 1987 (1 year )

    I was hired by Arnie Obler to sell advertising space to NYC banks, insurance and investment banking companies in CT, as well as companies based in LA, TN, and AR targeting institutional investors. It was a very short stint, and also my last, selling ad space for a NYC-based publisher.

  • Associate Publisher

    Grosvernor Press International

    (Banking industry)

    1985 — 1987 (2 years )

    My job was to sell advertising space, over the phone, to manufacturers of products that interior designers and architects would buy on behalf of their clients. Space sold was in annual table-top books produced by this British publisher, e.g., International Collection of Interior Design, Architecture International, Commericial Interiors International. Worked out of their 245 Park Avenue South, NY, NY office, under Douglas Chouteau.

  • SVP/Associate Publisher

    Madison Square Press

    (Publishing industry)

    1985 — 1986 (1 year )

    I was hired to fill the void left by Ari Koppelman, who was a partner to Tad Crawford and Gerry McConnell. My job was to train and man a sale staff to replace Koppelman's role as chief salesman and operator of the American Society of Magazine Photographer's annual resource book, which was produced by Madison Square Press, under the auspices of Annuals Publishing. This was a first-time management role for me.

  • Business Administrator

    Leaders Magazine

    (Publishing industry)

    1983 — 1985 (2 years )

    Started as assistant bookkeeper doing payroll, a/p, a/r, and keeping barter records. Worked as assistant to president and editor-in-chief, and served in variety of other capacities related to small business operations. It was here that I decided to move into sales, and left the company to train in a smaller operation called: NY Directory Advertising.
