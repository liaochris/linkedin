Marcia Zidle's Summary

In my work as an executive coach, leadership builder and career strategist, I’ve seen first hand the problems that rapid non stop change causes.Yet some leaders seem to thrive on change and use it as a positive, driving force in their lives and organizations. Are these peak performers extraordinary? No not really. Rather, they know how to keep changing to keep ahead of the changes around them.

Marcia Zidle, The Actualizer, with over 25 years of leadership and business consulting experience, is known for:
- Being street smart with her insights and expertise developed in the real world
- Solving problems by seeing with fresh eyes, connecting the dots and getting results.
- Creating smart moves for smart change to impact people’s lives, careers and leadership.

Marcia is the author of What Really Works Handbooks for Leaders and Career Savvy: Life Preservers for Today's Rough Seas and Tomorrow's Raging Waters. As a dynamic speaker on organization and personal change, she is available for media interviews, conference presentations and panel discussion on how to “Take the Lead and Not Play Catch Up In Change!”

She has taught leadership, organization behavior, business communication and human resource development at universities in Los Angeles and Dallas, TX. She is a National Certified Career Coach and a professional member of the International Coaching Federation.

Passionate about advocacy and community outreach for the National Multiple Sclerosis Society, she has attended public policy conferences in Austin, Texas and Washington, D.C.

Marcia's "claim to fame" is living, as an expatriate, with her family in Scandinavia and Australia. In fact, one of her children is an "Aussie". As a veteran explorer, she has traveled in over 30 countries throughout Europe, the Middle East, Far East, and the South Pacific.
