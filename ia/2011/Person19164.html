Japheth Dillman's Summary

It was the Atari 2600 I received for my sixth birthday that sparked and ignited the fire, and I was hooked. My passion for games came to fruition in college as I studied programming in order to code my own games. My video game career began in ’02 with MGB Inc. as a Producer. Since then, I have worked in various roles as a Producer, Game Designer, Product Manager, Advisor, Consultant, CCO, CSO, and Evangelist for Social Games. In these roles I am responsible for leading the creative vision, evaluating the market, meeting milestones, generating new game proposals for executives, and managing the development production cycles.

Currently, aside from my full time role, I also run two Bay Area events in the game industry, work as an advisor to a handful of startups, and run my own consulting firm, specializing in monetization through virtual goods, virality, and game design mechanics.

My analytics background was influenced while working at Offermatica (now Omniture). I have carried this over into the video game space using analytics to determine how to keep our players engaged and evaluate what features need to be rolled out to maintain higher daily active use.

Beginning with founding the PC Club in college, I continued to serve as an active leader in my local community in many ways. Mentoring children at an elementary school’s chess club, serving on the Executive Board of Directors for the Champaign Urbana Jewish Federation, and as a volunteer docent giving tours at our local art museum are just a few of the ways I have provided leadership in my community. Finally, I have authored a published White Paper in the game industry (on the topic of creating platform agnostic viral channels using features).
