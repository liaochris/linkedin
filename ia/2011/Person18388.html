Ivan Chambers's Summary

Ivan has decades of hands-on experience in manufacturing, product design, corporate training, program management, benchmarking, and organizational performance improvement processes. Ivan also has extensive experience in formal development processes for integrated product design and manufacturing. Ivan has conducted hundreds of planning and implementation efforts using Six Sigma, Lean Design® and the Quality Report Card®, to achieve “world class” Quality and customer delight with the least amount of cost and time. Ivan has performed dozens of physical benchmarking and redesign exercises resulting in detailed customer proprietary reports with comparisons of competitive products and identification of many millions in product cost savings.

Specialties

Lean Design/ Manufacturing, Design Prophet, Six Sigma Master Black Belt, Manufacturing Process Development, New Technology Investigation, Total Accounted Cost, ROI, Product Benchmarking and Design Improvement

Ivan Chambers's Experience

Senior Associate, Design Prophet

Munro & Associates, Inc.

Privately Held; Management Consulting industry

October 1995 – Present (15 years 5 months)

Ivan has significant experience in competitive product benchmarking & offering redesign ideas for clients' products, including HVAC systems, refrigerators, automotive parts, body systems & powertrain (engines & transmissions). Ivan has extensive knowledge of emerging technologies through investigations in support of his consulting & benchmarking services – including polymer composites & non-conventional metals processes. Ivan invented the Quality Report Card® to price & prioritize Quality improvement opportunities. Facilitated Lean Design® & QRC® sessions & provided very successful support for dozens of companies including Club Car, Raytheon, United Components, Trane, & Cardinal Health. Supported Cirrus Design in doubling small aircraft production in a six month period. Developed & documented a corporate Quality Improvement Process, trained key personnel, & facilitated initial QIP projects with dramatic cost savings. Provided Lean Design® & QRC® analyses for defense programs including ATK WCMD and Raytheon Excalibur.

Program Manager

Texas Instruments

Public Company; TXN; Semiconductors industry

January 1982 – October 1995 (13 years 10 months)

Ivan was one of the first Six Sigma Master Black Belts and has extensive experience in Six Sigma deployment and Black Belt training. Ivan was on a strategic team that led the group in winning the Malcolm Baldrige National Quality Award in 1992. Ivan was the Six Sigma program manager tasked with training all 15,000 group employees in one year. Over 2,000 customer and supplier personnel were also trained in that year. Ivan helped develop and implement the first Six Sigma Black Belt curriculum to certify internal experts. Ivan was elected by his peers to Member of Group Technical Staff. Ivan earned a corporate Quality Award for Excellence. Ivan was a member of TI’s Six Sigma Design Coordination Board directing the implementation of Design For Quality for the division. Ivan was registered as a Texas professional engineer. Prior to the corporate thrust activity, Ivan was a course developer and an instructor in the TI Learning Institute.
At TI’s Industrial Controls Division, Ivan was Manager of Factory Automation and Mechanization resulting in numerous productivity and Quality gains and the implementation of an SMD (surface mounted device) facility for electronic assemblies which became the basis for a very successful custom electronics business unit in TI. Also, in the Industrial Controls Division, Ivan held positions in product design, manufacturing, management, and marketing.

Chief Engineer

Kippcast

Mechanical or Industrial Engineering industry

February 1980 – January 1982 (2 years)

Ivan led the transformation of an old time-worn facility into an automated showplace of customer acknowledged excellence for zinc and aluminum die castings. Equipment rebuilding included complete disassembly, stripping of all electrical controls, wiring, and hydraulics. Frames were inspected for cracks and weld repaired. Platens and toggle links were machined and new bushings were installed. Machines were fitted with new auto lube systems, new TI programmable controls, switches, sensors and wiring. New hydraulic pumps, valves, accumulators, cylinders, and distribution tubing were installed. Also, new mechanized ladlers and Unimate industrial robots were fitted and connected to the automated work cell. A large furnace was built on site to receive and hold 30,000 pounds of molten aluminum. Ivan directed all of this activity and personally programmed all controls and robots for the work cells.

Advanced Manufacturing Engineering

General Electric

Public Company; GE; Financial Services industry

December 1969 – February 1980 (10 years 3 months)

Ivan planned and implemented manufacturing facilities, aluminum die casting processes, and machining & deburring processes. Ivan developed equipment specifications, supervised installations and directed equipment rebuilding. Ivan designed special equipment & assembly fixtures and trained operators in proper use. Ivan purchased the first industrial robot for the division and was certified in programming and preventative maintenance. The products were residential and commercial heating and air conditioning systems and consumer products.
