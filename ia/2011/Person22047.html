Jonathan Miller’s Summary

Seasoned International Entrepreneur, Investor and Investment Banker.

Jonathan has over twenty years of experience developing & bringing new products to market, raising capital and growing businesses through multiple successful exits.

He has help raise $100's of millions of dollars for companies that have returned billions of dollars to their investors.

Experience included, but not limited to; The Limited Brands, Federated Department Stores, Flycast Communications, The Fortunecity Network, PeopleLink, Open Wireless, TRUEreq (now Green Array) and IBC | International bag Corporation.

Jonathan presently serves as the executive director at Miller Gold working out of the Hong Kong and San Francisco offices.

Jonathan Miller’s Specialties:

Jonathan has started companies and served as an officer of corporations. He has held the positions of CEO, COO and VP having led companies, product management, engineering, marketing and sales teams.

Jonathan provides advisory and capital acquisition services to early-stage and growth stage companies. His experience spans the United States, Europe and Asia. Currently he based in San Francisco, California, USA & Shanghai, China.

    TRUEreq by ProductSoft is an On-demand Product Lifecycle Management (PLM) solution. Realizing the enormous opportunity of delivering ASP process management solutions across the enterprise ProductSoft has expanded its focus and is now called Green Array.

  • CCO / VP Product Management

    PeopleLink

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Computer Software industry)

    April 1999 — June 2000 (1 year 3 months)

    PeopleLink is the first IdeaLab! company and at one time was the number three instant messaging company in the world. Through its evolution PeopleLink developed online collaborative tools (such as instant messaging, chat rooms, message boards, email discussions, groups and calendars) then later become what is today a provider of collaborative Customer Relationship Management (CRM) solutions.

    During my tenure as VP of Product Management I led the product management and engineering teams that delivered new iterations of six different collaboration tools in nine-month cycles. During this time we also successfully patented and launched the online presence indicator PLINK. As well, I was part of the team that successfully raised over $30M in venture capital funding. Later, I was promoted to Chief Community Officer overseeing the strategic direction of the company and large customer implementations.

  • Director of Operations (COO)

    The Fortunecity Network

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Internet industry)

    November 1997 — June 2000 (2 years 8 months)

    Fortunecity.com is a leading web hosting, tools and online community network. Founded in the United Kingdom in 1997, The Fortunecity Network grew to being one of the worlds largest web hosting providers and the 11th most visited site on the Web by 2000. Fortunecity had a successful IPO in March 1999.

    When I joined Fortunecity in 1997 it had already become the 200th most visited site on the Web by leveraging the network of its citizens. The appeal was the opportunity to harness that growth and retention engine into a profitable business. As director of operations I oversaw product development, engineering, member acquisition and retention as well as facilities and asset management. During my time as director of operations we opened operations in seven different countries (including; Sweden, Italy, Spain, Germany and Japan) while growing to being the 26th largest property on the Internet.

  • Product and Marketing Manager

    Flycast Communications

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Online Media industry)

    April 1996 — November 1997 (1 year 8 months)

    Flycast Communications was a pioneer in Internet advertising. Flycast eventually grew to being a network on over 1,000 websites auctioning advertising space. Flycast Communications had a successful IPO in June 1999. Subsequently, Flycast was purchased in October of 1999 by CMGI and merged with Engage.

    Internet advertising was in its infancy when I joined Flycast in 1996. There were no standard banner sizes and there was no market for web advertising. The challenge was to compliment a real-time ad serving technology with a set of tools that would enable buyers and sellers of advertising to utilize this technology. As a part of the product management team we launched five web advertising buying and selling tools. In my role as marketing manager I was a part of the initial market launch and two subsequent corporate identity and customer acquisition campaigns. I am proud to have been a significant part of this important time and segment of the Internet’s growth.
