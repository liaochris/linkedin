Michael Bell’s Summary

Michael Bell is an American contemporary artist best known for his large thematic figurative series paintings and as a "Celebrity Artist" to the stars, with an infamous portrait painting clientele that includes numerous actors from The Sopranos, Goodfellas and more.

In 2010 Michael Bell and "Sopranos" star Joseph R. Gannascoli partnered up for Bell to create the artwork for Gannascoli's new CUGINE Cigar Line. This idea was born at one of Bell's star-studded 2008 Red Carpet Portrait unveiling with actors Frank Vincent, Dominic Chinese and Joe Gannascoli at the Stars & Cigars Gala at the Vanderbilt on Staten Island, NY. In 2008 Bell launched a new TICKET TO RIDE thematic painting series, debuted for the public at a New York City celebrity hotspot, The Plumm.

Bell has also played a vital role in pioneering the development of the Visual Journaling movement, giving workshops throughout the country, and donating his time and art to numerous charity benefits that have helped raise awareness and thousands of dollars from his painting sales.

Mr. Bell has also become accomplished in both the field of art and education. In the world of education, being awarded Southern High’s first ever “Teacher of the Year” award in 2001. In 2002, the Maryland Art Education Association awarded Bell the “Most Outstanding Art Educator of the Year Award.” In 2004, Bell was Anne Arundel County Public School’s 2004 “Teacher of the Year,” was one of 24 2005 Maryland State Teachers of the Year and in 2010 Bell was the OYEA Teacher of the Year Finalist. Bell is currently one of 3% of all Teachers in the nation that are National Board Certified and has been commissioned to paint the official portrait of the Maryland State Superintendent of Schools in Baltimore, Maryland. Mr. Bell is also the Art Department Chair at Southern High, serves as a MD Teachers of Promise Mentor and last year he netted students scholarships in art totaling over $344,000 and two National Award Winners in Painting.
