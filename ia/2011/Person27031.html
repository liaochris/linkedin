Lucian Tarnowski's Summary

Lucian Lucian is the Founder of BraveNewTalent.com, the social recruiting platform. He is Europe’s youngest Young Global Leader (YGL) by the World Economic Forum and the winner of multiple awards including Global Enterprising Young Brit 2009. He speaks to groups around the world on the change that Gen Y, Social Recruiting and the social web are having on the international workplace.

He is also winner of the Global Enterprising Young Brit of 2009, Finalist for Young Entrepreneur of the Year and in the UK’s ‘Top 10 Young Entrepreneurs to Watch in 2010’. He was named as one of Britain’s rising business stars by The Spectator Business and is on the UKTI (UK Trade and Investment’s) Global Entrepreneur Program with BraveNewTalent being described by the UK Government as a “Technology Company of Exceptional Potential”.

Aside from BraveNewTalent, Lucian is passionate about social media, youth leadership and entrepreneurship. He has a long association to India and is a youth advisor for Next Generation India on the Board of the UK India Business Council. In his spare time he runs Take Heart India, a no overheads charity that provides blind students in rural India with the IT skills that guarantee them a job for life.

Lucian Tarnowski's Experience

Owner

www.BraveNewTalent.com

Privately Held; Internet industry

June 2008 – Present (2 years 10 months)

BraveNewTalent is Europe’s first Social Recruiting platform. The site acts as the bridge between social networks and employers – enabling employers to hire from a community of individuals that connect to them in a professional way.

Clients include a number of blue chip employers including McAfee, Tesco, L’Oreal, Allen & Overy. Clients are getting better results using BraveNewTalent than they are with Facebook and Twitter to build Talent Communities. The mission is to map the global talent market, be best at connecting people to employers and create a new channel for employers to engage, recruit and train their future candidates pre-application.
