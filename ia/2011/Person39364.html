Steven Foster’s Summary

Steven Foster is a respected name in the field of medicinal and aromatic plants and herbs. I began my career in 1974 at the Herb Department of the Sabbathday Lake, Maine, Shaker Community, America’s oldest herb business dating to 1799. For thirty-five years, I have photographed and researched herbs from the Amazon rainforest to the highlands of Vietnam. Always looking for new opportunities to tell the story (in words and photos) of companies, ingredients, countries, or your story. We have the largest collection of stock photography of medicinal and aromatic plants with over 250,000 images available for editorial and commercial licensing.
I'm the author of seventeen books, including co-author and photographer of the new National Geographic Guide to Medicinal Herbs, National Geographic’s A Desk Reference to Nature’s Medicine (2006, with Rebecca Johnson),named a 2007 New York Public Library “Best of Reference.” Senior author of three Peterson Field Guides, including A Field Guide to Medicinal Plants and Herbs (with Dr. James A. Duke), 2nd edition, 2000, A Field Guide to Western Medicinal Plants and Herbs with Christopher Hobbs, (2002), and A Field Guide to Venomous Animals and Poisonous Plants of North America (with Roger Caras, 1995). More information at my website: www.stevenfoster.com

Steven Foster’s Specialties:

Consulting generalist in medicinal and aromatic plants. Specialize in spinning the story in words and images of companies, individuals and ingredients.

Steven Foster’s Experience

    American Botanical Council

    (Information Services industry)

    October 1998 — Present (12 years 4 months)

    Founded in 1988 the American Botanical Council is a leading nonprofit organization addressing research and education regarding herbs and medicinal plants. ABC’s members include academic researchers and educators, universities and libraries, health professionals and medical institutions, botanical gardens, government agencies, members of the herb, dietary supplement, cosmetic, and pharmaceutical industries, journalists, consumers, among others from over 70 countries. ABC publishes the quarterly journal HerbalGram, the monthly e-publication HerbalEGram, HerbClips (summaries of scientific and clinical publications), reference books, and other educational materials. ABC also hosts HerbMedPro, a powerful herbal database, covering scientific and clinical publications on 220 herbs. ABC is tax-exempt under section 501(c)(3) of the IRS Code.
