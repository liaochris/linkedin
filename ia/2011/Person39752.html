Metin Seven | Sevensheaven.nl's Summary

Born in Brussels, Belgium. Raised in the Netherlands during the early 8-bit days of the digital revolution, spoon-fed with computer audiovisuals, roaming the internet since early 1993.

Designer and graphic artist of commercial games for a range of computers and operating systems, starting with the Commodore Amiga. Game titles include Hoi, Clockwiser and Moon Child, published worldwide in the 1990s.

Co-creator of two VPRO television animation series: Mannetje & Mannetje and Tattletoons.

Cartoonist for the Dutch Nu.nl news website.

Illustrator for various Dutch national magazines and newspapers, including Esquire, Elsevier weekblad, NRC.next, De Volkskrant and Emerce.

Creator of the Seamour Sheep characters, comics and toy designs.

Designer of characters and logos for companies, campaigns and products.

Visualizer of photorealistic 3D product impressions.

Author of articles and reviews for iPhoneclub.nl and iPadclub.nl.

3ds Max, V-Ray, Maxwell and Photoshop trainer.

You're invited for a visit to www.sevensheaven.nl for an impression and more information.

Computer Games industry

During this period I ran a semi-autonomous computer games creation division, together with a partner. We created a commercial Microsoft Windows platform action game named Moon Child, published by Valkieser Publishing. The game became nominated for an EMMA multimedia award in Cannes.

After the completion of Moon Child I left Valkieser New Media because of sombre prospects for the company at that time, mainly due to a failed investment in Philips CD-I authoring technology. CD-I was the predecessor to DVD.

Owner and computer graphics creator

Metin Seven Digital Art Productions

Design industry

July 1989 – October 1995 (6 years 4 months)

My initial company as a freelance computer graphics creator. Projects included co-creating 10 episodes of the animated television series 'Mannetje & Mannetje' for VPRO television at the Dutch Comic House studio.

During this period I also co-created a number of internationally published computer games and two music editors. The titles, in chronological order:

1: Sidmon (music editor, 1989),
2: Venomwing (shoot-'em-up game, 1990),
3: Digital Mugician (sequel to Sidmon, 1990),
4: Hoi (platform game, 1992),
5: Cognition (puzzle game, 1993),
6: Clockwiser (puzzle game, 1994).
7: Moon Child (platform game, initial Amiga version in 1995. The game was later redeveloped for Valkieser New Media, and released in 1997).

In 1992 I co-created the first custom-made computer game to appear in a (Dutch) television commercial, for Calvé peanut-butter. It was also the first Dutch commercial with a length of one minute, and the live-action part featured the debut of the Dutch actress Lieke van Lexmond. After the commercial aired on television, the Calvé Peanut-Butter Power Game was played by kids in the popular RTL 4 children's matinee Telekids, where they could win their length in stacked peanut-butter jars.

In early 1993 I co-created the first and second demonstration in the world for the new generation of Amiga computers, before Commodore's official hardware guides were published. The demos were titled Planet Groove and Mindwarp, forming part 1 and 2 of the Hoi sAGA demo trilogy, and were used in computer stores around the world to show off the capabilities of the new Amiga generation.

The Amiga system was superior to Windows PCs and Macs since its introduction in 1985, until the Commodore company went bankrupt in 1994 because of bad management, to much regret of many dedicated Commodore users, including myself.

Divo Publishing

August 1989 – September 1989 (2 months)

Worked at Divo Publishing as an editor and author for Amiga Magazine. Resigned soon after an offer from the Comic House agency I couldn't refuse.
