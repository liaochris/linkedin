Lauren Gilger’s Summary

I grew up in newsrooms, thanks to my mother, whose journalism career has spanned 30 years and five states. And so I am the only 22-year-old I know who gets up early every morning to make sure no one steals her New York Times.

I like to think my journalism career began before I began high school. From "Take Your Daughter to Work Days" at The Arizona Republic and its "Teen Scene" section in high school, and summer jobs learning the local beat at The East Valley Tribune and the editorial track at Phoenix Magazine.

As a senior at Fordham University, I am on the way to finishing two majors, neither of which is Journalism and both of which I love: French and Visual Arts. And I am now ready to tackle the changing landscape of the journalism industry today.

I believe there is room for real reporting in the 24 hours and 1,000 forms of media emerging daily. It must be the standard, not the exception. There are stories – and then there are stories that matter.

A journalist has the chance – and the responsibility – to tackle that immense challenge. In my future, I hope to do no less.
