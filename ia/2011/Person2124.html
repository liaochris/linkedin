Amnuai Beckenham's Summary

Thai Silk and Beyond

After a very limited formal education and a range of menial job roles, I am proud to say that as a rural Thai lady I now have a good command of the English language and understand the basics of business success.

I will be constantly working on my leadership and management skills with our team of Thai silk weavers as well as working on developing my social media marketing strategies.

We create handmade Thai silk using only natural dye colour materials

All our Thai silk fabrics, scarves and accessories are organic, eco-friendly and enable people to look and feel great at bargain prices.

Why? Because our customers purchase DIRECTLY from our weavers and 100% of the profits stay right here to help our village and our children.

The success of our village project, Thai Silk Magic, is something I am really passionate about and I am lucky to have around me a team of dedicated highly skilled Thai silk weavers, technical experts and an understanding and supportive Aussie husband.

Our motto says it perfectly:

"Woven by Hand With Love, For You"

My goals are simple yet challenging:

To fully apply the wonderful silk weaving and natural dye techniques passed down to me by my grandparents for the benefit of our entire village.

To provide the best educational opportunities for our 2 daughters and every other child in our village.

To always strive to be the best mother and wife I can be.

To make a sustainable success of our community project, Thai silk Magic, so we can help to improve the lifestyles of our village and give people a financial choice for the first time in their lives.

To be a motivation to other Thai villages like ours, so they can see that there really is a chance to improve lifestyles and break the viscous "cycle of debt" that plagues so many poor Thai villages.

To build trusting business relationships with colleagues on Linkedin for mutual benefit.

Amnuai Beckenham's Experience

Founder

Thai Silk Magic

Non-Profit; Apparel & Fashion industry

November 2009 – Present (1 year 5 months)

After re-organising my rice farm labour demands we started in earnest to create a business that would help our village. Thai Silk Magic was born towards the end of 2009.

This was a dream come true for me as finally I could use the wonderful weaving and natural dye skills that my grandmother and great-grandmother had passed down to me to really help my family and my entire village.

We create handmade Thai silk using only natural dye colour materials.

All our Thai silk fabrics, scarves and accessories are organic, eco-friendly and enable people to look and feel great at bargain prices.

Why? Because our customers purchase DIRECTLY from our weavers and 100% of the profits stay right here to help our village and our children.

Our motto says it perfectly:

"Woven by Hand With Love, For You"

In my role as founder of Thai Silk Magic I have a wide variety of duties and challenges. These include organising our own silk worm supplies plus purchasing supplementary silk threads; training, supervising and inspecting our hand weavers, experimenting and creating new natural dye colours, training in the art of natural dye creations, educating and assisting women in our village to start a savings program for their family future, working with my husband to build our brand and our social media presence for Thai Silk Magic, constantly upgrading our website with help from our technical guru Justin Wheeler, consistently implementing and developing our article and video marketing strategies to drive more targeted traffic to our website.

As we are a community project in a remote Thai village, we face many challenges but we are determined and passionate to succeed as we share 100% of our profits to help improve village lifestyles and the education opportunities for the children of our village.

I don't want our village children to have to leave school early as I had to as a good education is the key to breaking our Thai village "cycle of debt and poverty"

Rice Farmer

North Eastern Thailand Rice Farmer

Farming industry

August 2005 – February 2009 (3 years 7 months)

We returned from living in Bangkok to be near my parents in the remote Thai village of Ban Dong Yang, up near the Cambodian border

I expanded the family rice land holdings and after 1 year split the land into 4 parcels, one for my parents and one for each of their 3 children.

My role was to organise and supervise all the farm activities associated with rice farming with a "hands-on" approach.

My focus was on an organic approach to rice farming with minimal use of any type of fertilizer. This belief in the advantages organic materials would carry over to our Thai Silk Magic business.

During my rice farming years (we still have the farm but now pay others to help run it for us) I could see that this was not going to help me achieve something that had been driving me for so long - to ensure the children of our village would have the best education possible.

Housekeeping - Supervisor

International Hotels in Bangkok

Hospitality industry

September 2002 – July 2005 (2 years 11 months)

With little formal education I was lucky to have a wealthy Thai lady who befriended me. She was a major shareholder in a smaller Bangkok hotel and got me a position on the housekeeping staff.

From there I was given more responsibility by being promoted to supervisor of the 18 housekeeping staff.

Then I was approached by an international hotel to come and work for them in the same role, but with 120 housekeeping staff and higher pay.

I spend 3 years at this hotel and during that period they also sponsored me to study basic people management and development skills.

I left this position to return home with my family to establish our Thai Silk Magic community project to help our village

Manual Labour

Various Canning Factories

Consumer Goods industry

January 1998 – August 2002 (4 years 8 months)

Hard physical labour working long hours in a range of fish and fruit canning factories. Needed to work 2 shifts each day to save enough money to send my brother to university and to help my very poor family.
Finally promoted to shift supervisor but left to join the hospitality industry
