Lisa Wildman’s Summary

Currently, I sing as alto and soloist at Princeton Pro Musica, alto soloist and section leader at St John's Presbyterian in Devon, flutist and vocalist with Cantatica, flutist/piccoloist with Camerata Ama Deus, and alto with the Philadelphia Singers Chorale.
I'm in 2 bands: Group W (folk) and Peanut Butter Jellyfish (kids' music)--both of which have received airplay on local public radio. Group W has played live on WXPN's folk show; Peanut Butter Jellyfish's self-titled CD won a Parents' Choice award.
I teach private lessons on flute, piano and guitar.
I co-conduct the Doylestown Comfort Choir (hospice).

Previously,
-led week-long sacred chanting workshop at Friends General Conference (a gathering of Quakers from all of No. America)
-led group and master classes in Suzuki flute as a guest clinician at the music school of Delaware
-sang at Sabbath service at Brothers of Israel congregation
-sang the alto solos of Mozart's Requiem at Swarthmore College
-sang Handel's Messiah with Vox Ama Deus
-played flute for Camerata Ama Deus
-sang principal roles with Amici and Delaware Valley Opera Companies
-acted lead roles in plays at the Mermaid Inn Playwrights' Theater
-played principal flute and piccolo and solo concertos with the Abington Symphony
-substitute taught music history at Delaware Valley College
-practiced music therapy as a freelance contractor and at Northwestern Institute
