Kevin Sheehan’s Summary

With over 9 years of IT experience, I have worked in the capacity of a business analyst for 7 years. In this time I have demonstrated an exceptional ability to communicate with and enable both system users and system builders. This ability can be attributed to my studies, professional experience and personal interest in organizational behaviour, structure, business process and the integrity of the information required by an organization to be efficient and successful in all of the above areas.

While working in the capacity of a business analyst, I have been in a leadership role for the design, integration and release of many Federal Government information system solutions needed to meet many different requirements. While meeting the requirements of each organization, I have demonstrated an exceptional ability to adapt to unique people, ideas and formal System Development Life Cycles (SDLCs). Furthermore, I am equally committed to representing the ideas of a system user and a system builder and believe that conceptual, logical and physical representation of ideas and requirements must be developed, documented and managed to ensure that a system user perspective can be mapped to a system builder perspective.

As a business analyst, my primary focus has been business process and the information required to support business process. The high-level responsibilities that have been assigned to me have included:

- research and analysis of new and existing internal and external systems, related architecture and standards;
- development and documentation of statements of requirements (business requirements) with business and technical resources;
- development of business narratives and data, process and use case models to represent business requirements; and
- translation of business requirements into a technical specification for network, programming and database resources.

Kevin Sheehan’s Specialties:

I specialize in working with subject matter experts (SMEs) to discover, model, document and integrate information required to support process or activity which must be integrated into one or more information systems. Furthermore, ensuring that the information can be shared by connected systems, and users can trust the integrity of the information.
