Andrew Boxall's Summary

Here at Synaptica Electrical Solutions, we cover all domestic work in Hertfordshire, Bedfordshire, Essex, Cambridgeshire, North London, and the M11 corridor.

We are a NICEIC domestic installer and Trustmark registered. We cover a wide variety of electrical services and solutions that are guaranteed to meet your requirements.

From the small job to the large, look no further than Synaptica Electrical Solutions. We are the benchmark electrical subcontractor for LiftAble stairlifts, and have a 5 star rating on Rated People. We have developed a large client base of satisfied customers as well as obtaining a desirable reputation for our reliable and professional service.

Unlike our competitors, we offer complementary visual inspection and testing for each job we quote for, so you know the safety status of your electrics before we start. It's the only way to be sure that your electrics are safe enough to be worked on and modified, and it's the only real way of complying with the Wiring Regulations. Don't let any other electrician tell you differently.

So why wait? Whether you need a full rewire or an extra socket in your home, no project is too small! Get in contact with Synaptica Electrical Solutions today.

Synaptica Electrical Solutions are NICEIC-registered and Trustmark-endorsed domestic electrical installers. We offer our services to all Hertfordshire, Essex, Bedfordshire and Cambridgeshire residents. We are seen by our customers to set the standard for the electrical trade: just look at our testimonials!

We have an excellent reputation as electricians in East Herts and the surrounding areas. Synaptica Electrical Solutions undertake all manner of electrical work from the small (adding a socket, for example) to the large (rewires, refurbs, and new builds). Andrew is also a CITB-qualified central heating and boiler controls installer.

Andrew used to be a research scientist but left science in 2006, retraining as an electrician. He gained his City & Guilds and Part-P registration with the NICEIC, and acquired the necessary on-site skills by working for free for a number of local electricians.
