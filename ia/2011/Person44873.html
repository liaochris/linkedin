Verena Mei's Summary

I started in motorsports in 2002, when I transitioned from modeling to racing, and became the fastest Asian-American woman in the US, running a 9.46 sec quarter-mile. In 2004-2007 I competed in the Formula Drift series, where I became the top female drifter in the US. In 2008, I went onto run the Redline Time Attack Series, and went "green" running on E85. I had a podium finish at every event, and won a class championship, and became the the first and only alternative fuel car in the series, as well as the first woman to win a championship in the series.

I've always dreamed of getting into Rally Racing, and running the X-Games. . . and now I'm attacking that dream head-on!

I'm partnering with the gurus of rally racing at the Team O'Neil Rally School, where I'll be training as much as possible. My goal is to become a championship driver in Rally America and compete in the X-Games.

When I'm not racing I'm a precision/stunt driver for commercials, and work for various auto manufacturers as a Pro Driver, Facilitator, or Product Specialist. I'm also a BFGoodrich Brand Ambassador where I test tires, and am a speaker at national sales meetings, and dealer trainings.

I've been host for the shows "Sexy Road Test", and "Racing Greener" on Octane TV, for VOD on Comcast/Time Warner Digital Cable, and had a cameo role in the movie "Fast & Furious Tokyo Drift"

My personal goals through my accomplishments are to inspire, and become a positive role model for young women, to educate the community about the importance of using alternative fuels, and to impact the lives of others by teaching car control and safe driving techniques.

Specialties

Driving, Providing companies with unique marketing/media opportunities, Being a spokesperson, Connecting with the fans, Green messaging by example, Being a positive role model, and Kicking ass on the track
