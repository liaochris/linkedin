Dale J. Stephens's Summary

Dale is the founder of UnCollege, a new model for self-directed higher education. UnCollege provides resources — ranging from a project-based learning curriculum to mentors to peer support to personal guidance — to help individuals design their own higher education in the real world. At only 19-years-old he has been covered by the Chronicle of Higher Education, The Huffington Post, ABC, and more.

As an unschooler, Dale lived in France for five months and learned to speak French fluently. He started his first businesses, a photography venture, at age eleven. In 2009, he served as the photographer for Gavin Newsom’s Gubernatorial Campaign.

While still in high school Dale created a position for himself at Zinch, the college admission social network which connects more than 2 million students with 850+ colleges and graduate schools worldwide. He currently does business development for the site.

Dale speaks about youth, education, and entrepreneurship to audiences worldwide. Speaking engagements (past and future) include the Homeschool Association of California Conference, the UCLA Academy of Business Leadership, Rethink Everything, and TEDxKidsBC.
