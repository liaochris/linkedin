Defy Gravity - the Book

Management Consulting industry

2010 – Present (1 year)

Defy Gravity is a guide to finding and capturing your best opportunities for sustainable, profitable growth. The book defies status quo corporate speak with practical advice to help corporate leaders and entrepreneurs alike avoid crash-and-burn strategies born of corporate legends.

Defy Gravity moves beyond those legends, focusing on how to identify your best strategy for sustainable, profitable business growth.

Think Differently to Defy Gravity
Defy Gravity teaches all of us to think differently, about our business and its value, our markets and the opportunities they provide - and about the way we've always done it.

Following are examples of the strategic thinking found in Defy Gravity.

The way we’ve always done it may be limiting our success. Releasing stale corporate legends is the first step toward high-velocity growth.

The best strategy depends on your situation. Paint by numbers approaches don’t work. Why? Because today’s market isn’t the predictable, slow-moving market we took for granted. Everything changes, continuously. That means you have to create your strategy based on today’s reality – then evolve to soar the dynamic winds of market changes to sustain growth for tomorrow.

Don’t bet your business on those numbers. All those trends and patterns are about your past. That past no longer depicts your future. Not in today’s fast paced world. You have to vet those numbers with your business intuition, born of market and customer knowledge.

Value is in the eye of the beholder. What your customers, partners and other audiences believe is your guide to success.

Opportunity is all around us. That’s why you need to focus.

Match distinct value with a market that is willing to pay for that value and you’ll grow. It really is that simple.

Defy Gravity blends conceptual fundamentals, real-world applications of those concepts, case studies, questions and exercises to help you think differently.

    Brown, a management consultant, presents a smart and readable guide for executives seeking to improve the effectiveness of their corporate strategies. Even though one-third of all business strategies fail, executives tend to stick with the plan, maintaining that a bad plan is safer than no plan at all. Brown shows how to break free from this mentality and makes her message stick with energetic prose and cheeky humor. She questions prevalent corporate beliefs and shows how following these misguided approaches can ruin an organization. She highlights change as the key factor; it's the "anti-gravity" that will propel a company forward. Citing such successful examples as Nu Skin and Southwest Airlines, Brown illustrates key points on what works and what doesn't, and concludes each chapter with a helpful summary. A new voice in a very crowded field, Brown provides an original perspective on corporate growth. ~ Publisher's Weekly
