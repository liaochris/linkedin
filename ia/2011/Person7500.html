Celal Sami Tufekci's Summary

I gained my mechatronics skills throughout my professional education. All my degrees (BS, MS, PhD) are in the field of mechanical engineering with a focus on mechatronics.

I am currently working as a Chief Researcher at TUBITAK UZAY - Space Technologies Research Institute. Specific duties include design and software implementation of various estimators, propagators, and controllers for scientific LEO satellites.

In the past I worked as a Manager and as a Senior Satellite Design Specialist at R&D and Satellite Design Department for Turksat AS, Ankara, Turkey. I also worked in R&D Center of Pitney Bowes, Shelton, CT, USA, -the maker of fast postage and mail processing systems. In addition, I worked in various universities at different capacities:
- Rensselaer Polytechnics Institute, Troy, NY, USA (Research/Teaching Assistant)
- University of Connecticut (UConn), Storrs, CT, USA (Assistant Dept. Head)
- University of Ontario Institute of Technology, Oshawa, ON, Canada (Instructor)

Celal Sami Tufekci's Patents

  • Transport Method and System for Controlling Timing of Mail Pieces Being Processed by a Mailing System

    • United States Patent 6685184
    • Issued February 3, 2004
    Inventors: Celal Sami Tufekci, Gary S. Jacobson

    A transport method and system that operates to feed mixed
    size mail pieces in singular fashion and adaptively controls
    the velocity of the mail pieces such that overall system
    performance is optimized is provided. The length of a mail
    piece is measured and a desired gap time between the mail
    piece and a subsequent mail piece is calculated. The gap
    time between the mail piece and the subsequent mail piece
    is measured, and a difference between the desired gap time
    and measured gap time is calculated. Based on the calculated
    gap time difference, the velocity of the subsequent mail
    piece is adaptively controlled to decrease the difference
    between the desired gap time and the measured gap time
    such that the measured gap time is adjusted to be approxi-
    mately equal to the desired gap time, thereby optimizing
    throughput of the mailing system.
