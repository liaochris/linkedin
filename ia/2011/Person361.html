Andrew Bowerbank's Summary

Mr. Bowerbank is a respected authority on green buildings, sustainable development, industrial design, and low-carbon economics. Most recently, Mr. Bowerbank has been supporting developments in smart-grid infrastructure, renewable energy and electric vehicles.

His diverse background enables him to work effectively with public and private sector leaders. He has extensive entrepreneurial experience in business management, marketing, and promotions through a number of successful initiatives, and has recieved awards around the world for his public speaking and industry leadership.

As a published author, Andrew is proud to have had two of his books on the Canada "best sellers" list. He has also published a number of leading market reports on green building design, electric vehicles, low-carbon economics, and more.

His training in industrial design allows him to communicate effectively across a number of fields including manufacturing, computer graphics, building science, ergonomics, systems protocals and engineering.

Andrew's professional accreditations in the fields of education, and coaching support his ability to negotiate new partnerships, lead staff and volunteers, communicate well with peers, and speak confidently to professional audiences.

Andrew's main efforts today include:
-Initiating high-profile market transformation projects
-Cross sector collaboration
-Global diplomatic relations
-Public speaking
-new technology integration.

Added Note:
Prior to 1990 Andrew also spent a number of years in the manufacturing sector as an engineering supervisor. He feels fortunate to have worked on various projects including the redesign of the monorail carrier system for the Ford assembly plant in Oshawa, Ontario, a computer system for Toshiba Canada, and specialized packaging / transportation systems in the automotive, and electronics sector.

Chairman

EC3 Initiative

Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry

December 2009 – Present (1 year 4 months)

EC3 Objective:
Energy, Climate, Communication, and Collaboration make up the elements of the EC3 initiative. Each element represents core areas of opportunity in support of a low-carbon economy for Canada and across North America. Building on the strategic efforts of our European counterparts, EC3 will strive to establish a Canadian response to the evolving concept of the “Third Industrial Revolution”. Sector leaders will be engaged to transcend common barriers and short-term targets, and offer their insight in support of Canada's evolving economy.

Of equal importance, EC3 will present opportunities for cross-sector collaboration and market awareness. For many years, leaders in the USA have clearly demonstrated the effectiveness of aggressive promotion strategies for engaging interest groups and transforming market perception. EC3 will embrace these strategies in efforts to promote projects that foster the development of low-carbon economy.

To truly mobilize as a nation, we need to gain confidence around what projects are showing success and who is providing the leadership, then set an aggressive agenda focused on a common goal. No small task – but neither are today’s challenges of environmental security, economic prosperity and social stability.

Mr. Bowerbank was recently honoured by an invitation to participate in “Birmingham Charter”, an international protocol designed to bring together the “100 greatest minds” in the world and create a new model for defining sustainability.

World Green Building Council

Non-Profit Organization Management industry

May 2007 – December 2009 (2 years 8 months)

The World Green Building Council is a union of national Councils whose mission is to accelerate the transformation of the built environment towards global sustainability. The current member nations of the WorldGBC represent over 50 percent of global construction activity, and touch more than 10,000
companies and organizations worldwide. World Green
Building Council members are leading the movement that is globalizing environmentally and socially responsible building practices.

The WorldGBC provides an international forum, and champions proven tools that significantly accelerate market transformation from traditional, inefficient building practices to new generation high-performance buildings, provide “branding”, and transform the skills and knowledge of the industry as a whole. This is a critical response strategy for cities and countries worldwide to their national and international commitments to reduce carbon emissions and redress other environmental impacts.
