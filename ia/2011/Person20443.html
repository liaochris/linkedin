Jeremy Spiller's Summary

I've worked with the internet for nineteen years (yes, pre-web) and sixteen years with the web, all at Director level and as a business owner. I hugely enjoy what I do and find the ever changing internet landscape challenging and interesting.

I have expert knowedge of SEO, PPC and Social Media Marketing and regularly run workshops and seminars and write on these subjects. I believe I have a very good understanding of web and search marketing, web technology, software development, business strategy and business processes.

At my current business White Hat Media, which I set up with my good friend and business partner Kevin Elliott, I work with the best team I've ever worked with. Everyone is hugely talented, committed to excellence in what they do and are a pleasure to work with. I have the honour and pleasure of working with clients such as Microsoft, Toshiba, The Royal Opera House, The NHS, The Gym Group and more than 40 other companies to build their web presence and help them increase their sales.

My personal business goal is to continue to grow the business within the sector, provide excellent service to our clients and enjoy the journey. We are blessed with superb clients many of whom have become personal friends.

I regularly speak at events and run workshops globally on digital, search and social media marketing. Recently I've spoken at events and run workshops in Europe and The Middle East and for E-consultancy, The Chartered Institute of Marketing, McCann Erickson, The British Standards Institute, Shure and The Direct Marketing Show to name a few. I have numerous articles published in the trade, business and national media.

When not working I live in Arundel with my wife Karen and two daughters. I like to windsurf, walk on the Downs with the dog, read and relax.

Specialties

Considerable experience in managing website design, development and marketing backed up by many years in business development.

I founded Clockwork Web in 1995 and within four years it was one of the top ten agencies in the UK with an impressive turnover and an amazing client list.

We rode the wave of the dot com boom and worked for many household names such as Dun & Bradstreet, Cable and Wireless, The Rank Group and others.

We built the first website for Red Dwarf, the first website for Butlins and a website that won many awards for Pete Townshend.

It was a time of utter madness but also hugely enjoyable.

Head of Business Development

Ashmount Research

Internet industry

September 1992 – December 1994 (2 years 4 months)

Ashmount Research was a company that developed internet software before the web.

This software was called an off-line reader and allowed for the automatic downloading of threaded messages from message boards. Back in the early nineties we were all using narrowband so to stay on line while reading messages was extremely expensive. Hence the need for a very clever software application that pulled all those messages onto your desktop and then organised them for easy reading.

Ashmount Research had some impressive clients including The Open University, and Nat West Bank and was way ahead of the curve with regards to technology.

It was a tremendous period and a time of pioneers and a feeling that we were on the verge of something very, very big.
