Carl Prindle’s Summary

Carl is President and CEO of Blueport Commerce, the trusted provider of e-commerce technology and services to retail chains representing billions in retail sales.

As e-commerce evolves from a commodity marketplace to one where consumers make considered, "big ticket" purchases online, Blueport Commerce enables retail chains to catch this next wave of online growth.

Blueport provides retailers in big ticket markets with outsourced e-commerce solutions customized to meet their localized, multi-channel needs.

Fundamental to the technology we build and the services we provide is the understanding that enabling big ticket purchases online is different than traditional e-commerce, long typified by consumers purchasing inexpensive, simple products online and receiving shipment via parcel service.

Big ticket e-commerce presents unique challenges. It involves more expensive, less well-understood products - furniture, appliances, TVs, flooring, construction materials. Prices are higher and consumer confidence is lower. Inventory is bulky, expensive to move around the country and more expensive to return.

For these reasons and more, Big ticket commerce is fundamentally local. Stores play a critical role. E-commerce becomes a powerful tool to help stores compete in their local markets rather than a national channel that bypasses them. Online efforts serve to drive store traffic, generate leads and consummate online transactions, cost effectively and measurably.

Our platform is the only e-commerce platform architected around this need for multi-channel localization. Our services drive results through a deep understanding of how consumers research and complete big ticket purchases online and in stores.

Blueport Commerce is a one-stop, outsourced e-commerce provider, combining a decade of experience, innovative technology and customized services that meet the unique, localized needs of retail chains selling big-ticket items.

Carl Prindle’s Experience

  • President and CEO

    Blueport Commerce

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    February 2001 — Present (10 years 1 month)

    As President and Chief Executive Officer, Carl provides Blueport Commerce with strategic and product leadership as the company helps retailers build profitable e-commerce channels for products once thought “impossible” to sell online.

    Before the company became Blueport Commerce, Carl was President and Chief Executive Officer of Furniture.com, growing the company to become a trusted e-commerce provider to $4 Billion in retailers.

  • SVP Product Development

    Furniture.com, Inc.

    (Internet industry)

    December 1998 — November 2000 (2 years )

    In 1998, Carl left McKinsey & Company to serve as SVP of Product Development for the original Furniture.com. Responsible for the company’s website design, merchandising, and direct marketing efforts, Carl drove online furniture sales to an annualized $84M in less than a year.

    When restricted capital markets closed the company in November of 2000, Carl led the effort to purchase and repurpose the company’s name and technology assets. The idea? To use what was built and learned during the dot-com boom to serve top furniture retailers seeking to enter e-commerce.

  • Engagement Manager

    McKinsey and Company

    (Partnership; Management Consulting industry)

    September 1995 — December 1998 (3 years 4 months)

    After graduating with an MBA from the MIT Sloan School of Management, Carl joined McKinsey and Company where he served as consultant and engagement manager for retail, technology, and growth strategy projects.

  • Industrial Designer

    Design West

    (Design industry)

    September 1990 — August 1993 (3 years )

    While at Stanford and after graduation, Carl led product design efforts for Fortune 100 firms, involved in such projects as the design the original Reebok Pump, the 1995 Ford Explorer, and a Dust Buster for Black and Decker. Carl holds 9 US Patents.
