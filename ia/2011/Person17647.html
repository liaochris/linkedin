Howard James's Summary

My professional experience has always been in the people sector with a degree in Education and my early career based in the Early Childhood Education area where I was a teacher/educator, Children's Centre Director, regulatory advisor to children’s services and I also ran a small professional development company. As part of that professional development work, I was published in this field and the main book was accepted as a text in multiple learning institutions.

I moved from there into youth employment projects where I re-met some of my pre-schoolers as they came out of the school system. Part of these programs involved close liaison, working with employers across a broad spectrum of industries which was the integral skill in my subsequent roles, including my current role and the role I held with the Australian Chamber of Commerce and Industry promoting apprenticeships and traineeships and developing employment options with Business groups, and individual companies.

My current professional goals are to continue to innovatively develop business solutions and options for my Candidates and Client companies in a global downturned economy yet still with a skill based candidate poor market.

I will be implementing this under my philosophy of Developmental recruting, aimed at meshing the aspirations of the client company with the plans and ideals of the client candidate.

I will continue to develop my interest and skills in Career change, transition and planning for a cross section of the employment market, including school leavers and mature men who often struggle mid career to find new relevance and direction when their career hits a crisis of some sort.

This interest is partly around Developmental Recruiting as a an addition to existing recruiting methadologies that look at the past to predict the future, but do so, out of the context of the candidates career passion, is a fundamental mistake often made in the consulting process of recruitment.
