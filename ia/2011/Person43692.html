Todd Jordan (@toddrjordan)’s Summary

US Navy - operated and supported nuclear propulsion plants on submarines. Worked in 3M management, Supplies, and Quality Assurance as well. Last duty station I worked hand in hand with civilians, government and Navy personnel to coordinate shipbuilding oversight, ship christenings, promotions, and other events. Programmed message handling system for classified messages, set up system users, and established the command's first website.

Consolidated Communications - Lead operator in the St. Louis facility. Ran jobs, managed the tape library, created training materials, and created backup programs and other tools.

MEMC - System Administrator for AS/400 systems, where I provided programming and support for KRONOS, PRISM, and Software2000, covering timekeeping and manufacturing systems. Worked on transition off of AS/400 backend systems to SAP.

COVIDIEN, formerly Tyco Healthcare, aka Mallinckrodt - System Analyst, originally in EDI systems and support involved AS/400, PC based, and networked EDI software and integration. Worked with hundreds of B2B partners establishing electronic communication, including the use of X12, HTML, XML, and technologies such as AS1, AS2, and FTP.

Transitioned to JDEdwares ERP team. Became a lead programmer and problem solver. Installed, supported, and connected JDE both at local plants and remote, including transitions from legacy ERP systems to JDE.

Recently on the Manufacturing support team as the lead analyst. Programming, problem investigation and resolution, new design, and managing support activities for the team. Working across IS teams to resolve issues, install new software, as well as helping integrate internal ERP systems.

Outside the cube world, I'm a blogger, writer, photographer, and general creative. An early adopter, I'm involved with new technologies as participant, sharer, and mentor. Blogging and social media has lead to relationships both local to St. Louis and around the world.
