Elizabeth Gormley's Summary

I am working to improve horse health and welfare by promoting collaboration between equine health care and training professionals.

My current activities: Assisting others with writing articles about equine veterinary science research, and also writing articles to encourage communication between trainers, breeders, scientists, and equine veterinary medical practitioners. Association for Veterinary Informatics associate member.

I, Elizabeth Gormley, am not a veterinarian, and do not provide veterinary advice. Specifically, I cover new advancements in veterinary medicine, veterinary science, equine genomics, and horse management. All evidence I translate is fact checked by a licensed veterinarian.

I recently (2009) initiated the request for creation of the PeroxisomeDB horse peroxisomal proteome data resulting in identification of 35 predicted Peroxisomal Targeting Sequences, which included the identification of a PTS2 in equine catalase.

My other activities include equine health science web site evaluation of evidence-based content, and web page designer collaboration. My ongoing goal is to assist veterinary clients to participate in and understand evidence-based diagnosis and treatment.

Working to make equestrian sport more popular in the USA, thus reducing numbers of unwanted horses.

My interests include equine behavior, veterinary science of jumper and eventing horses, science of music, history of equitation, and equine functional genomics.

Eventing, Show Jumping, Dressage, gardening.
