Ron Berk's Summary

1. "TEACHER, WRITER, and RESEARCHER" for 42 years: Elementary school teacher (4 yrs.) to assessment specialist (3 yrs.) to university professor (30 yrs.) and assistant dean (5 yrs.) to professional speaker and writer.

2. "PROFESSIONAL SPEAKER" (Broadway-Style Productions):
Keynotes and workshops for FACULTY to improve
(a) teaching skills with humor and technology strategies;
(b) evaluation skills to develop and use rating scales for teaching improvement and summative decisions;
(c) assessment skills to custom-tailor their measures for their students;
(d) stress management skills to change their classroom and work environments so they are "FUN"; and
(e) time and meeting management skills to increase their effectiveness and efficiency.

CORPORATE MANAGERS, STAFF, and STUDENTS on
(a) stress management skills to cope and improve their work environment;
(b) top 10 tips to create smash-the-mold PowerPoint presentations;
(c) career skills to determine their unique gifts and passions; and
(d) time and meeting management skills to increase their professional and personal productivity.

I've presented at institutions and conferences in 40 U.S. STATES and 13 COUNTRIES in EUROPE, EASTERN EUROPE, ASIA, AND THE PERSIAN GULF.

See "TOP 10 REASONS TO HIRE RON," TESTIMONIALS on Website, and RECOMMENDATIONS on this profile.

CEO & President

I'm a professional speaker to university faculty and students and corporate HR directors, managers, and employees on the topics of humor and multimedia, faculty evaluation, time management, stress management, career direction, and test-taking skills. Professor Emeritus

The Johns Hopkins University

Educational Institution; Higher Education industry

July 2006 – Present (4 years 9 months)

Professor, Biostatistics and Measurement, & former Assistant Dean for Teaching

The Johns Hopkins University

Educational Institution; Higher Education industry

August 1976 – June 2006 (29 years 11 months)

As Professor, I taught 30 years at JHU, 11 years in the School of Education and the last 19 in the School of Nursing. Here are the statistics (Aren't you excited?): 170 biostatistics, measurement, and research courses to 6000 students at the undergraduate, master's, and doctoral levels; chair or member of 96 doctoral dissertation committees in the areas of education, psychology, nursing, public health, and religion. I served as Assistant Dean for Teaching for 5 years and was a co-investigator and statistician for more than 30 research grants in medicine, nursing, public health, and education. Since retiring in 2006, I am Professor Emeritus.

Visiting Professor

University of Umea, Sweden

Educational Institution; Higher Education industry

September 1985 – December 1985 (4 months)

Taught several courses in psychometrics for doctoral students, seminars for faculty on a variety of topics, and advised on 6 dissertation committees. I returned to Umea in Jan. 1995 and September 2007 to conduct seminars on teaching and measurement and consult with faculty and students.
During my 1st stint, I also served as a guest speaker and consultant to the Swedish National Board of Education, Stockholm.

Assessment Specialist

Montgomery County (MD) Public Schools

Primary/Secondary Education industry

July 1973 – July 1976 (3 years 1 month)

As a staff member of the Department of Appraisal and Evaluation, I designed, wrote, analyzed, and reported results for nearly 50 criterion-referenced tests in a variety of subject areas plus numerous scales and questionnaires on student attitudes and behaviors. I also conducted surveys and research on those instruments as per requests by the Board of Education and audited projects by the Department of Research.

Teacher; Chairman, English Textbook Evaluation Committee

Washington DC Public Schools

Primary/Secondary Education industry

August 1968 – June 1972 (3 years 11 months)

Although I was prepared to teach history and related courses at the secondary level, I was placed in an elementary school in one of the project areas of DC. I taught 3rd and 5th grades. During those 4 years, I received a financial award for developing a Black Studies program in my school. The research I conducted using those materials became my master's thesis and, later, my 1st professional journal article. I greatly miss recess to this day.
