David Dao's Summary

I am an open mind and curious spirit. My belief is that brand communication could use a little modernization. The more I listen the more I see persuasion at play, which has been the rule of the game since advertising was invented and 99% of the time is still used today, even in digital communication. My view is this, if you have to persuade someone to hang out with you, they're not really going to stick around for very long. I believe brand communication should provide value, over and above the message. Without the method of communication being of value, nobody is going to take much notice of the message.

Specialties

Multimedia conceptual thinking. Strategy. Design. Art direction, writing and directing.

Head of Art

TBWA Chiat Day

Public Company; 501-1000 employees; OMC; Marketing and Advertising industry

July 2007 – March 2010 (2 years 9 months)

In the summer of 2007 I joined TBWA\Chiat Day Los Angeles as Head of Art on Nissan North America but then was soon asked be available to work across all of TBWA\Chiat Day’s brands.

After working closely with Lee Clow, TBWA WORLDWIDE’s Global Chief Creative Officer to developed a Pioneer campaign, I developed several new microbrewery beer products for Anheuser-Busch complete with a back-story and brand strategy. When Chiat Day pitched and won Visa global, I was appointed as head of art on VISA FIFA world cup , seen by the client as fundamental to winning the business. I was then made global creative director on Infiniti to help redefine and reposition the brand around the world with a focus on Japanese culture. I later worked on Gatorade with industry legend, Jimmy Smith and helped launch Gatorade’s first major product innovation for 40 years: The G Series and Gatorade Natural.

Creative Director

Cunning Communications

Privately Held; Marketing and Advertising industry

April 2006 – July 2007 (1 year 4 months)

When I joined Cunning, the brilliant founders John & Anna, asked me to help them refocus their agency. I obliged by re-positioning the brand and creating a new company culture with a thought leadership strategy, which used a mix of trend analysis and creative intelligence for new business. The aim was to make this famous guerilla marketing company more strategic without moving away from it’s roots. I created a new creative department combining traditional creatives with interactive designers and planners. The results were, winning the launch of Ultimate Fight Championship in the UK, the re-launch of Avon and Esprit in the UK as well as sustaining accounts such as Bloomberg and Nissan GB.

When I joined TBWA London, it was the most creatively awarded agency in London. I was lucky enough to learn from some of the most creatively gifted people in the business.
