Philip Pitman's Publications

  • MSc Thesis: Utilisation of Municipal Solid Waste Fly Ash as a Binder in Sustainable Mortars

    • October 15, 2010
    Authors: Philip Pitman

    There is a current pressure from governing bodies for the whole community to reduce carbon dioxide production, landfill, resource use and waste production. All of these can be achieved by substitution of cement constituents to make a composite cement. This report investigates the use of Municipal Fly Ash (MFA) combined with Portland cement (PC), blastfurnace slag (GGBS) and pulverised fuel ash (PFA).

    A literature review was conducted to examine existing research, it was identified that although there is a vast amount of research concerning MFA used with PC, there is no research available for MFA combined with other binders, hence this was the scope for the research.

    A selection of binder mix ratios were examined and can be summarised as follows;

    PC:MFA - 100:0 - 90:10 - 70:30 - 50:50 - 0:100
    GGBS:MFA - 70:30 – 50:50 – 30:70
    PFA:MFA - 70:30 – 50:50 – 30:70

    The results showed that the ratio identified as most promising for PC:MFA use in literature, 90:10 was accurate. However, in order to obtain the strengths obtained by comparative literature which was above 100% PC mixes, there should be some pre-treatment of the ash.

    GGBS reacted very well with the MFA to produce strengths up to 50% of the 100% PC control mix, considering all the binder products were waste this was exceptional. Investigations into an optimal mix ratio for GGBS:MFA should be examined.

    PFA had very little reaction with the MFA, however it was concluded that due to PFA’s extended reaction time, further study with longer curing times would be beneficial to rule it out completely.

    Further research on the properties of the 100% waste binder mixes regarding leachates should also be examined to identify if the binding effect is similar to that of PC:MFA mixes, i.e. leaching is controlled at an environmentally safe level.
