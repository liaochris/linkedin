Todd Herman's Summary

Todd Herman is a digital media executive, social mobilizer and rainmaker. Most recently he served as Chief Digital Strategist for the Republican National Committee, where he oversaw the Party's technical resurgence. Prior to joining the RNC, he was Chairman & Chief Creative Officer of SpinSpotter, the winner of a DEMO god award. Todd has been at the forefront of some of the most fundamental developments in digital: video, radio & social media mobilization.

Until September ‘07, he was General Manager, Media Strategy & Monetization for MSN, a member of a team responsible for increasing yield on $1 Billion in revenues, and crafting company-wide strategies for engagement with major studios, networks & content creators. He held a number of senior management positions with MSN Entertainment, and MSNBC Interactive. Under his leadership, MSN Video inaugurated over 100 traditional TV brands into Broadband Video; revenues grew four fold over four consecutive years. In recognition, AdAge Magazine named him “the media guy” at Microsoft.

A pioneer in broadband media since 1996, he served as Co-Founder/CEO of theDial from '97-'01 a VC-backed Internet radio network. theDial’s syndication platform was the first to welcome Fortune 500 companies to Internet radio advertising. In 2008, Streaming Media Magazine named him one of 25 initial inductees into the Streaming Media All Stars for his work in establishing the broadband video and Internet Radio segments.

Todd is a provocative speaker on technology as a driver of cultural shifts, social mobilization, government 2.0 & digital story telling. He has been featured in Business Week, The New York Times & Ad Age, and has spoken at Ogilvy’s Verge Summit, Ad:tech, iMedia, Johnson & Johnson world-wide Media Days, AMP: Activism, Media & Politics & The Personal Democracy Forum. He is a frequent guest lecturer on college campuses including UCLA’s Anderson School for Media for MBA candidates, and the executive education series.
