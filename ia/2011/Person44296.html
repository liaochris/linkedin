Troy D. White’s Experience

  • Senior Director, Sales & Marketing

    Council for Economic Education

    (Non-Profit; Education Management industry)

    March 2003 — April 2010 (7 years 2 months)

    The Council for Economic Education faced a common non-profit challenge: they had great programs, but few people knew about them.

    To get their programs into classrooms around the world, I built a direct-to-teacher marketing program that increased revenues from $800,000 per year to over $1.85 million within 4 years, mainly by expanding into elementary and middle school markets. The number of teachers using their programs jumped from 7,000 to over 30,000 per year.

    Lead generation was the lifeblood of my marketing strategy: I used all of the tried-and-true direct-response marketing strategies -- catalog, print, email, online, search engine and trade shows -- to build a strong pipeline of prospects. I also grew the in-house database from 11,000 to over 150,000 customers and prospects.

    Technology played a big role: I implemented Salesforce.com to manage leads and enhance customer follow-up; and Google Apps for Education, to help us collaborate more effectively.

    I also introduced and launched new product lines, such as online training for K-12 teachers, online educational gaming and other technology-focused learning products for both teachers and students.

    White Swan Sales & Marketing Strategies

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    October 2001 — January 2003 (1 year 4 months)

    After September 11, I provided strategic sales & marketing advice to World Trade Center businesses that were impacted by 9/11 through a volunteer program coordinated by the NY American Marketing Association. I worked closely with a computer consulting firm owner and a restauranteur to help them rebuild their businesses.

  • Web Marketing Strategist

    American Management Association

    (Non-Profit; Education Management industry)

    July 2000 — October 2001 (1 year 4 months)

    As the first Web Marketing Strategist at the American Management Association, I developed and implemented a digital marketing and content delivery strategy for the $125 million public seminars division. I established stand-alone web and e-mail marketing programs, as well as integrated digital marketing into offline initiatives.

    I also launched the first "blended" e-learning program, which provided pre- and post-learning resources to attendees of in-person, public seminars. This blended approach gave attendees a richer, more infusive learning experience, and is now a mainstay of American Management Association seminars.

  • Business-to-Business Marketing Manager
