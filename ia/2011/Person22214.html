Jonny Shipp’s Summary

VISION AND STRATEGY
- Developed O2’s rich, practical yet tightly defined vision of ‘five ways we’ll help keep children safe online’. Based in extensive stakeholder engagement and detailed customer research, it set the standard in industry and inspired other sectors.
- Led international networks of experts to respond to EU and UK consultations on content regulation.
- Chaired advisory and enabling forum at the heart of O2 UK’s best practice process for managing the content standards risk to its £5 billion brand. Created and agreed policies for internet filtering, moderation, location services, age verification, mobile advertising, media literacy, marketing and children and social networking.

LEADERSHIP AND CHANGE
- Telefónica is ahead in tackling images of child abuse. My leadership, foresight and strong external relationships enabled deployments to happen quickly across 17 countries.
- Architect of 4 year, £5m internet filtering and content access control investment, driving end-to-end delivery across platforms, phased to protect revenue throughout delivery. Parents see O2 as a leader because it differentiates by offering safe mobile browsing for younger children.
- Recruited and developed the high performance team that created O2 UK’s first authoritative online design, usability and accessibility guidelines and deployed manual and automated processes to audit compliance.

INNOVATION AND COMMUNICATION
- Established a knowledge network of 100+ ‘Children’s Champions’ with a goal ‘to become recognised as trusted experts in our field’. Jim Gamble, chief executive of CEOP, the UK police’s internet safety unit, wrote how impressed he is by O2’s organisational commitment to safeguarding young people.
- Created ‘Who Wnts 2 No?’, an illustrated children’s book. Published by O2 on World Book Day 2009, now in 8% of UK schools and becoming a staple e-safety learning resource in UK primary schools.
