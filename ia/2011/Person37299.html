Robert Minton-Taylor's Summary

Robert Minton-Taylor’s career spans 43 years in journalism and public relations (PR).

He began as a national consumer journalist in the late 1960s. In 1973, he joined ferry operators, Townsend Thoresen as a press officer. In 1977 he became head of promotions for the shipping, harbours & property firm, European Ferries. In 1979 Robert joined the global PR agency, Burson-Marsteller. He was elected to the agency’s UK board in 1987 and was subsequently made director of media relations and head of leisure, travel and transport.

In 1994 he moved to Yorkshire for a better quality of life and became managing director of Charles Walls PR. It didn’t work out and following redundancy he became an independent consultant working for No.6 Barristers’ Chambers, Wallenius Lines and Yorkshire Water.

Until September 2010 he was the global media relations manager for Wallenius Wilhelmsen Logistics, a global Scandinavian shipping & logistics company.

He now combines his main role as an associate senior lecturer at Leeds Business School, part of Leeds Metropolitan University while working as an independent PR consultant, under the Minton-Taylor Consultancy brand specialising in global media relations.

Working with an amalgam of great clients and truly talented people, Robert has been fortunate enough to have gained: 2010 "The John Richman Lifetime Achievement Award", Seahorse Club,Gold winner, Xchangeteam Freelancer of the Year, Yorkshire & Lincolnshire (2010); Sole Practitioner of the Year” (2006); finalist, “Team Working in Education & Training” (2004); finalist, “Outstanding Achievement by an Independent Consultant" (2003); winner, “Outstanding Achievement by an Independent Consultant” (2002); finalist, "Sole PR Practitioner of the Year Award" (2002); winner, "Special Projects" (1993); winner, “Community Relations" (1990); winner, "Best Non-Commercial Campaign" (1989); winner, "Best International Campaign" (1988); and nomination, “Best Design for PR” (1987).
