Art Schlussel's Summary

My goal is to help organizations succeed. How? By helping them develop the atmosphere, attitudes, and processes needed to help their employees thrive. By focusing on outcomes, organizations can create a culture where employees are empowered, trusted, and expected to meet and exceed their goals. Organizations that create such experiences will attract talented and passionate people who will seek out ways to work smarter, innovate faster, create value, and enjoy their jobs. All that increases productivity, profits, and well being.

Over 25 years of experience helping organizations, teams, and individuals deliver value, create positive impact, and achieve desired outcomes through the use of practical training, evaluation, and knowledge management practices and approaches. Experience in the commercial, public and defense sectors as both an operator and consultant. A proven leader and manager, his accomplishments range in scope from ensuring communications systems operators aboard Air Force One are fully trained and competent, to leading the development and deployment of the first Intranet for a multi-national consulting firm, to developing knowledge management maturity and competency models for the U. S. Army. He is a strategic thinker who has the ability to create initiatives and projects that deliver products and services that are adopted, used and useful.

Specialties

Driving organizational adoption and use of Knowledge Management, collaborative learning, knowledge transfer, business transformation, performance management, leadership, team-building, program management. Developed and taught KM courses.

Art Schlussel's Publications

  • Measuring the State of KM

    • Inside Knowledge
    • November 2009
    Authors: Art Schlussel, Rob Hoss

    The degree to which an organisation effectively applies the art of knowledge management (KM) – creating, organising, applying and transferring knowledge to facilitate understanding and decision making – is an indicator of its KM maturity. Measuring the state of KM maturity provides a baseline from which to build KM. Developing metrics that assess KM impact and value is both essential and difficult. It is a topic that has often been studied, written about and debated. However, we have found little in the way of practical methods that are easy to understand and apply. This series will provide practical ways to measure the state of an organisation’s KM strategy, its initiatives and their impact.

  • Embedding KM Strategy

    • Inside Knowledge
    • July 2009
    Authors: Art Schlussel

    Art Schlussel takes a look at the essentials of embedding an effective KM strategy to achieve organizational success.

  • Preparedness Assessment: A Prerequisite for Enterprise Content Management Solution Acquisition and Deployment

    • Infonomics
    • May 2009
    Authors: Art Schlussel, Wayne Brooks

    Enterprise Content Management (ECM) Preparedness Assessment is an innovative approach that focuses a stakeholder’s attention on baselining and improving content lifecycle processes before the acquisition and deployment of an ECM solution, to assure an organization not only acquires the right capability and solution, but is ready to adopt and use it.
