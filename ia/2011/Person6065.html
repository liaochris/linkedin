Brent Lehmkuhl’s Summary

An accomplished electrical engineer and department manager with significant experience in commercial, educational and industrial building electrical systems. Background includes more than 14 years of electrical building systems design and management. Engineering experience includes power distribution, lighting and lighting control systems, grounding, fire alarm, communications (voice/data), A/V and mission critical systems. Experienced at interfacing with architects, clients, and other engineers. Registered Professional Engineer in 13 states, as well as a BICSI Registered Communications Distribution Designer (RCDD). Exceptional technical, organizational and interpersonal skills. Joined RTKL's Baltimore office in October 2004. Currently a Principal managing the engineering department in our Washington, DC office.

  • Project Manager - Principal

    RTKL

    (Privately Held; Architecture & Planning industry)

    October 2004 — October 2009 (5 years 1 month)

    Job change is never easy, but sometimes a new challenged is warranted. RTKL gave me that opportunity to do both the architectural/engineering projects, as well as the mission critical projects. A key focus of our group has been mission critical work for the federal government. However, we also stretch into the corporate world. Mission critical, data center and high performance workplaces make up a large portion of the portfolio.

    Specifically at RTKL, I managed a studio of electrical engineers in our Baltimore office. While most projects are architectural prime, some are MEP prime. This requires managing a multi-discipline team of both engineers and architects. My main focus is managing key clients, project pursuit, and working with a team of engineers to design critical electrical systems for facilities. Types of systems designed include:

    1. Electrical distribution including service entrance, UPS and generator systems. Redundancy and concurrent maintainability are key drivers in the electrical design.

    2. Mission critical electrical systems, including redundancy and concurrent maintainability (n+1, n+2 and 2n electrical systems).

    3. Grounding and bonding systems for electrical and telecommunications systems, including signal reference grids in computer spaces.

    4. Lighting design and complex control systems to conform with energy codes.

    5. Low-voltage systems such as voice/data, security, CCTV and fire alarm.

    Types of projects include design-bid-build, design-build, RFP preparation, construction administration, and peer reviews.

  • Engineer / Project Manager

    MDA Engineering, Inc.

    (Construction industry)

    July 1996 — October 2004 (8 years 4 months)

    It's all about opportunity. My first boss gave me the opportunity to get into the construction design profession. From that point on, I became a sponge, absorbing all the technical information that I could. After I had advanced considerably within the company, I recall one of the owners telling me at my performance review that "you are an excellent engineer, but in order to succeed in this field, you also need to be an excellent consultant." I took that statement to heart and really put for the effort to grow on all levels. The three P's: People, project and profit. Focusing in on all three of those elements (the three-legged stool if you will) allowed me to grow as a person, consultant and engineer.

    My time at MDA Engineering, Inc (previously known as Mechanical Design Associates, Inc) was challenging, exciting and rewarding. Highlights and responsibilities include:

    1. Engineered various schools under the umbrella of the Ohio Schools Facilities Commission.

    2. Interfaced with architects, owners, mechanical, structural and civil engineers. Unlike RTKL, key clients were outside architects.

    3. Manage multiple clients and key electrical accounts for the company.

    4. Manage studio team of electrical and mechanical designers and production technicians.

    Specific engineering responsibilities included:

    Electrical service entrance and power distribution system design
    UPS and generator system design
    Grounding and bonding systems, including signal reference grids
    Architectural lighting systems
    Low-voltage systems including fire alarm, voice/data/video, security, CCTV, and sound systems
    Short-circuit studies and load analysis
    Energy calculations
    Construction administration

    I have no regrets about the time spent at MDA. In fact, I often think back with fond memories of the relationships that were developed. The decision to move on was one related to challenge and opportunity that I felt another company had a better ability to offer.
