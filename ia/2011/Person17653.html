Howard Tong’s Summary

I went to Penn State where I graduated in Architectural Engineering, played varsity tennis and was President of the Penn State Tai Kwon Do club; got my first degree Black Belt. I practiced AE (specializing in Mech HVAC) in LA at the firms of Levine Seegal and JC Chang Associates before starting my own computer company www.laptopcomputers.com in 1996-1997. I got acquired and soon afterwards I joined ABS as the GM of Internet with the mission to develop the company's online platform. This led to being a co-founder of Newegg.com. In year 1999 we were selling gaming PCs but realized customers needed parts/upgrades. Customers were asking "can i buy some memory or a graphics card?" and we were like "no?!", we could only sell them a PC unit! So in 2000 I wrote the business plan for Newegg.com. We built a website for the Tech Geek IT (which i am one of) to find all the parts you need at the best prices, fastest shipping and the best cust svc. I designed the website functionality and requirements, the cust svc philosophy - after the "dot com bubble" burst we had to let customers know we can be trusted. BANG! It exploded! finally a place where you can go to get the latest cutting edge technology at the best prices with no worries. We grew Newegg from zero to $1B rev in 4 years. It's near $2.5B today still selling IT/CE stuff. I am still a shareholder of Newegg but left in 2006 to be the COO of US Autoparts, participating in its IPO on NASDAQ in Feb 07. Damn, that road show was hard! but still fun, stayed at USAP until Dec 2007.

Most of 2007 I have been consulting for Dr. Leonard Kleinrock, PHD; one of the "Founders" of the Internet and Professor at UCLA.

I am now CEO of AutoShepherd.com. Think of it as the "Newegg of Autoparts".

Just like the IT days when you had no choice? tired of being ripped off at the dealership/mechanic? I spent 2 years building the architecture/database so now you can get parts for your car, cheap, fast, no worries =) Time to PLAY!
