Melissa Jordan Grey's Summary

Melissa Jordan Grey brings more than 24 years of experience as a seasoned creative technologist, entrepreneur and digital media artist. As CEO/Chief Creative Officer and co-founder of digital video startup NewBlue®, Inc., Grey assures that the company remains the fastest growing in the integrated video effects market. Her longtime vision of "video for everyone" led to the development and upcoming release of Vibop™, consumer-oriented software as a service that puts video editing in the hands of everyone.

After earning an undergraduate degree in media studies at U. Rochester/Indiana University (Phi Beta Kappa, summa cum laude), she was award an International Radio and Television Society Summer Fellowship and later attended the University of Southern California's master's program in cinema-television.

Her interests in music & television led her to co-found The Blue Ribbon SoundWorks, Ltd. at age 23. Blue Ribbon pioneered music software technology, doubling in revenue and profit 5 years in a row. During its 7 year life, Grey also composed the multimedia score for the City of Atlanta's 1996 Olympic Bid. Her debut software effort Bars&Pipes MIDI sequencer broke ground in object-oriented user interface design and was followed by the profitable release of 23 additional titles. Blue Ribbon's AudioActive interactive music technology won Best of Comdex in 1995 and soon after, the company was acquired by Microsoft Corporation.

At Microsoft, Grey served in a variety of leadership roles, including busdev, marketing and digital media product design. Her group's Interactive Music Architecture found its way into PowerPoint, MSN, Internet Explorer and ultimately Windows as part of the DirectX platform. Pushing the limits, she created Rifff, an online interactive music experience with musicians Paula Cole, BB King, Cyndi Lauper and others. Before leaving in 2000, Grey worked in the digital media division on prototypes for personalized, convergence-centric digital media experiences.
