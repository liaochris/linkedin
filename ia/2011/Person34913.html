Project Manager for Lithography

Intel

Public Company; INTC; Semiconductors industry

February 2005 – September 2006 (1 year 8 months)

Accountable for all safety, scope, schedule and budget deliverables for the installation of prestigious Lithographic Nikon and TEL semiconductor equipment, performing management of multi disciplined architectural, mechanical, process and electrical teams to high value contracts.

Using key milestone dates from Primavera I developed and proactively maintained multiple detailed MS Project microschedules with up to 5,000 tasks. This provided comprehensive visibility of the actual and near critical paths, allowing prioritisation of the short and long range task interdependancies to the team, ensuring consistent performance against schedule. This became the bkm standard for Tool Install Qual projects.

Working closely with Finance and Procurement groups to consistently track multiple project budgets from concept to completion. This position was a natural progression from previous design manager roles, turning the design and contract scope into reality in a timely manner while maintaining overall financial responsibility.

Design Manager

Intel

Public Company; INTC; Semiconductors industry

January 2002 – February 2005 (3 years 2 months)

Responsible for the preparation of a request for proposal for the installation design of 500 semiconductor toolsets. This included preparation of the detailed scope for design, working with Procurement to ensure the most appropriate forms of contract were selected and utilised. This became the bkm standard for Tool Install Design projects. Responsible for detailed bid analysis and presentations to senior management regarding award recommendations.

Developed and implemented detailed risk assessments based on hazard, probability and severity. Also developed associated risk mitigation method statements, to meet and exceed health and safety legislation (best in class v's minimum compliance).

Clients’ Representative of the appointed third party architectural and engineering firm, managing the project deliverables of scope, schedule, quality and safety. Reporting the project performance ensuring any corrective actions were agreed and implemented, and that any contractual disputes were minimised and swiftly resolved.

Preparation of a request for proposal for the installation design of 300 semiconductor toolsets, working with fellow stakeholders and implementing previous lessons learned. Since this was a new strategy for the client, this also involved secondment to USA to train over 300 engineers on the new design and contract strategy.

Joint venture with main contractor to develop a design model whereby the trade contractors would develop their own IFC design, to determine the main contractors Design Build capability.

Design Lead

Intel

Public Company; INTC; Semiconductors industry

January 2000 – January 2002 (2 years 1 month)

Lead Design Engineer for various semiconductor toolset Functional Areas, responsible for the close coordination and management of multi discipline teams to deliver detailed design drawing packages Issued for Construction (IFC) and supporting any Requests for Information (RFI).
A demanding client schedule, balanced with unionised trade contractor constructability input and client sensitive process requirements. All against a backdrop of a facility conversion and incident and injury free environment.

Project Manager

Siemens Power Generation

Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; si; Electrical/Electronic Manufacturing industry

1997 – 2000 (3 years)

This international position provided the opportunity to work on both Open Cycle (OC) and Combined Cycle (CC) arranged power station projects in Brazil (Cuiaba), Israel (Reading), India (Paguthan), New Zealand (Otahuhu), England (Seabank) and Germany (Erlangan), ranging in size from 365 to 750 MW electrical output.

Responsible for ensuring the successful management of mechanical sub contracts in accordance with schedule requirements and quality standards. A large degree of client and contractor interface was required, acting in accordance with predefined Siemens contract terms and conditions, liason with back office Project Management and on site Commercial Management.

The role required detailed coordination between design issues, client expectations, and contractual requirements. ISO 9000 Auditor certified and NDT (Non Destructive Testing) certified wrt process piping and any QA/QC issues arising.

Each project had a structure where the respective mechanical contractors’ site management reported directly to me on all technical and schedule issues. A typical contractor workforce of over 300 people would be working directly on my projects at any given time.

Projects comprised a wide range of mechanical systems supporting gas (GT) and steam (ST) turbines, heat recovery steam generator (HRSG) systems, and de-ionised water plant. These included gas, steam and process pipework, salt water intake pumping station, crane erection and testing, acoustic enclosures, storage tanks, GT air handling equipment and cooling towers.

Task prioritization skills, strong and effective management, and the ability to work with local contractors to international standards were essential.
