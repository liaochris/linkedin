Andy Blacksmith’s Summary

Andy Blacksmith is an award winning major market personality, who began his career in morning radio at Baltimore's legendary 98 ROCK-WIYY. In January 2000 he signed a deal to join Clear Channel's high profile syndicated morning show "The Morning Buzz". With knowledge of how to gather large numbers with men in the key demographics of 18-34 and 25-54, coupled with his appeal to the female listening audience, Andy has become a very valuable commodity in all formats of radio from Talk to Rock.

Prior to getting into radio, Andy spent 8 years finding, signing and developing new talent for legendary music manager Dee Anthony. Anthony was responsible for creating the careers of such iconic performers as Peter Frampton, Joe Cocker, Gary Wright, J. Geils, Sammy Hagar, Mott/Hoople, Humble Pie, Tony Bennett, Peter Allen, Devo, Peter Allen and King Crimson. During his tenure at Dee Anthony Entertainment, Andy was involved with the multi platinum songs, "Time Of My Life" and "Hungry Eyes" from the hit movie soundtrack "Dirty Dancing" which has sold over 10 million copies worldwide.

Along with co-hosting a morning radio show, Andy performs his own brand of stand up comedy at the world famous Comedy Connection in Boston. Also, as a member of the Boston Casting Company he's been cast in numerous television commericals, most recently in a nationwide ad campaign for the National Hockey League starring with Boston Bruins captain Zdeno Chara.
