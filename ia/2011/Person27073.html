Ludon Lee’s Summary

Ludon Lee is a social entrepreneur and industry veteran in the entertainment industry with over 12 years of senior management experience at independent motion picture and interactive companies.Ludon taught courses for Video Game Design and Art, a Bachelor of Arts program at the Art Institute of California in 2004.

Prior to working in Corporate America, Ludon was a volunteer at a Civil Rights organization Asian Law Caucus (ALC) and contributed to community advocacy for Employment Discrimination, Housing, Immigration; and an Anti-Asian Hate Violence survey, a joint venture with National Asian Pacific Islander Legal Consortium. He organized his first fundraising event in Golden Gate Park for ALC at age 21. The following year, he worked with Eric Mar at Northern California Coalition for Immigration Rights and organized a community rally for Welfare Reform at Contra Costa County prior to joining Electronic Arts in 1997. Since his initial departure, he has continued to volunteer and served as a donor to other nonprofit San Francisco communities, including Asian Pacific Islander Legal Outreach and Kearny Street Workshop. Ludon was also a part-time instructor at James Denman Middle School Beacon afterschool program in 2009 where he taught a business and technology course to urban youths in Mission & Excelsior district that are at-risk from gang violence and drug abuse.

Ludon left D2C Games in 2008, one year after the company had received $6,000,000 (USD) Series A equity funding from Silicon Valley venture capital firms and founded Grapple Entertainment. He conceptualized Hybrid Distribution for converging motion pictures with interactive content on Google Android Internet-enabled devices such as Google TV and Video-On-Demand application on Facebook. He is currently developing plans for a pre-initial public offering startup with a Stereoscopic 3D multi-platform studio and distributor in San Francisco and Singapore with a pre-money valuation of $8,000,000,000 (USD).
