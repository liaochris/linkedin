Scott Bellware’s Summary

I am a software product designer, developer, manager, and agile coach living in Austin, TX. I work with web startups on rapid new product development as well as with IT shops to improve the quality of their products, processes, and performance.

I am a teacher, a student, an organizer, and an activist who strives to communicate simple and powerful software development topics that become obscured by complex, esoteric language. I relentlessly pursue effective solutions by seeking out root causes. One of my customers has suggested that I change my title to Assumption Destroyer.

My customers have included VersionOne, McLane Advanced Technologies, Microsoft, Baker-Hughes, Zep Manufacturing, Chevron Canada, Environmental Resource Management, The Mexican Trade Delegation to Canada, Harte-Hanks, Texas Higher Education Coordinating Board, Dovetail Software, Minggl, Pluck, Quick Scripts, and Triangle Technology.

I am the founder of the Lean Software Austin group, and have founded and helped organize numerous professional groups and events regionally, nationally, and internationally

I speak at software industry conferences and teach agile development practices and software production methodologies in workshops in the US, Canada, and Europe.

I am the organizer of the Monospace, ALT.NET Open Space and Continuous Improvement conferences in Austin, and the founder of the AgileATX community of agile software practitioners in Central Texas.

I served as the track chairman for agile development at the DevTeach conferences, the software track chairman for the InnoTech conferences, and the chairman of the INETA Speaker Committee.

I have been a champion of progressive software development in the Microsoft community, and a five-time recipient of Microsoft's Most Valuable Professional award for efforts in organizing, leading, and mentoring in software development community.
