Katie Walmsley’s Summary

Achieved four promotions over three and a half years in finance and operations management at one of the country’s largest industrial distribution companies. Quickly learned the business and earned management responsibility for 25 employees. Demonstrated relentless inquisitiveness and a drive to maximize efficiency and accuracy in the operation. Sought deep connections with individuals and communities through a year of volunteerism and international travel. Gained a broadened global perspective and a focused commitment to addressing challenges of sustainable development, education and equal opportunity.

My experience as a volunteer in New Orleans was so compelling that I decided to relocate here permanently. This city is serving as an innovation laboratory for sustainability, technology, urban planning and economic growth. I intend to use my career to have a lasting, positive impact on the world, starting in New Orleans. Our challenge is to create economic growth with environmentally sustainable, 21st century technology at its core to provide a solid foundation for pathways out of poverty, good-paying jobs, neighborhood revitalization, and an environmental future. My focus is on the private sector because I feel that sustainability isn't real until you make it inextricable from private business.

In March 2009 I began leading business development and marketing efforts in New Orleans for an internet start-up called KODA. For the first time in many decades, New Orleans is becoming a magnet for innovation and entrepreneurship. The business community is emphasizing the development of new industries and attracting young talent. The city is becoming a new hub for technology start-ups and creative media. By helping to foster this innovation-revolution, I hope to spur economic growth and social change.
