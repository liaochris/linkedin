Mark Slater's Summary

Oxford educated, second generation professional musician and composer. Mark was recording records, appearing on radio and TV broadcasts from the age of 8, making music with the world renowned Christ Church Cathedral Choir.

Mark's work includes high profile TV documentaries, full dome films, feature films, shorts and commercials. Winning awards for his work with the London Symphony Orchestra at Abbey Road for the PBS science special 400 Years of the Telescope and the Philips Aurea microsite. He has scored several films for the full dome large format digital theaters (aka planetariums). Natural Selection in 2010 won an award for best soundtrack at the Imiloa Full Dome Film Festival (Hawaii) from over 50 films. Another such film Two Small Pieces of Glass was the most shown film in 2009 and can still be seen in theaters across the world.

In the concert world, Mark has written numerous works for orchestra, choir, organ and chamber ensembles. "Awakenings" in 2009 was performed by members of the LSO.

In addition to Music, his acquisition of skills and experience has included Law, Engineering and Management, a practical polymath, business minded, with a strong track record of getting things done under pressure, showing innovation and leadership in customer focused industries, making Mark ideally suited to applying his musical skills in the world of film and entertainment.

For the last 2 years, Mark has been living and working in Los Angeles, but he has a studio base in London, connections in Germany and Hungary and developing connections in Japan and the east coast of the USA.

Specialties

Film Music Composition and Scoring, Music Supervision, Consultant on Copyright and Royalties, Budget & Cost Setting and Management, Spotting, Orchestration, Composition, Studio recording and working with orchestral musicians, Score & Parts Preparation and Printing, Conducting and Session Management, Mixing, Music Editing
