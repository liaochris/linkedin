Ron Hebshie's Summary

I have used my passion for film, television and new technology as a guidepost throughout my career. I have also had the good fortune of working with amazing brands of all shapes and sizes while building a base of knowledge and experience where versatility, brand integrity, innovation, flawless execution and quantifiable results are constants. I am a free thinker but I maintain a perspective where the "old" and the "new" freely mix to form the "now." I firmly believe that marketing is a contract between brand and consumer and, as such, it must honor the needs and interests of both. I am a proud member of the television industry with a unique and highly successful track record in the traditional consumer marketing space as well as viral/social media, live events, branded retail/premium programs and contests/sweepstakes.

Specialties

Marketing (consumer, affiliate and social media), branding, special events, advertising, sponsorships, publicity, promotions, contests and sweepstakes, broadband content partnerships, client/partner synergy and support, project and account management.

Ron Hebshie's Experience

Director of Marketing

G4 Media

Public Company; Broadcast Media industry

January 2009 – Present (2 years 2 months)

The G4 brand forms a cross-platform bond and serves as a destination for gamers, geeks who matter, gadget hounds, movie and TV junkies and those responsible for the "quiet revolution" that made the internet indispensable and transformed social media from curiosity to vibrant lifestyle. The G4 demo target is young and male with a core audience that is unusually passionate, articulate and plugged in to the brand as a whole.

In addition to all of the traditional responsibilities that come with managing a TV/online consumer marketing team, my position at G4 affords me many unique opportunities that, collectively, have broadened and enhanced a strong background in live events, retail, online/viral/social media marketing and strategic brand initiatives. I am especially proud of leading prototypical projects that have resulted in strategic, fully branded and content driven tactics designed to deepen the relationship between G4 and our audience, affiliates and agency partners.

Major project assignments include full concept and brand strategy creation for the marketing and interactive overlays to our event space at both E3 and ComicCon. Also responsible for all related logistics, staffing, budget tracking and project planning.

Created four new branded entities out of my event planning process for 2010: The G4 Girls (an upscale, on-brand refresh of the "booth babe" concept complete with national talent search), G4 Brand Masters (real G4 audience members and fans selected from a pool of application essays to fill paid "brand ambassador" staff roles at E3 and ComicCon), G4 Live@ (a versatile, umbrella brand collective that ties together the key consumer elements on any live event at which G4 has significant presence) and G4 Ltd. (a new line of limited edition clothing and collectibles).

Director of Marketing & Network Branding

No Good TV

Entertainment industry

March 2006 – December 2008 (2 years 10 months)

NO GOOD TV (NGTV), an internet start-up, produced uncensored celebrity news and entertainment programming. With virtually no marketing budget or paid placement, I initiated a viral and social media strategy that, among other things, helped NGTV amass more than 300 million clip views in 18 months. By the time I left in 2008, the network had become the #3 most viewed partner of all time on YouTube. Position reported directly to the network president and covered all aspects of consumer and trade marketing, special events, branding and promotions. Also responsible for managing all aspects of content partnerships, sponsor integration, talent relations and publicity (with publicists at BNC and Endeavor).
