Hans Laros's Summary

Well skilled in a wide range of ICT specialities. Experience in managing teams, coordination of testing and development, requirements management, quality improvement and maintenance.

Somewhat contrary to the daily routine of IT services, I strongly believe IT should be an optimizing enabler of primary processes and have a keen interest in the human component in the chain. Boxes and wires are cheap, but provide you with essentially 'very little'. However, creative thinking, collaboration, clear goals, planning, effective processes and... people will bring you closer to your targets.

Specialties

ITIL, ASL, Test coordination and management (TMAP), Prince2, HRM, infrastructure, information analysis, software development process and optimalization of IT maintenance.

Acting Manager Servicedesk, Incident Coordinator

CBS

Government Agency; Government Administration industry

May 2009 – July 2010 (1 year 3 months)

When I began last year, the servicedesk and surrounding support roles were in a dire state after personell cutbacks, slightly under 50%. The backlog in support tickets was 360+ and growing, plus an unknown number of missing requests. There had been a long, global preparation of the reorganisation, but the prerequisites handn't fully been put into place. Moral of the employees was at an all time low.
My responsibility was to assist the manager of the servicedesk with identifying and implementing ways to improve the efficiency and effectiveness in order to regain the level of service prior to the personnel cutbacks, all while reducing the ticket backlog asap to manageable numbers. The improvements included technical changes in the infrastructure as well as better management tooling, documentation and streamlining of work processes.
Together, we were able to reduce the huge backlog within a few months to reasonable levels, after which we focussed more on structural changes. Today, I'm pleased to report that the support groups are back in control of the workload, with a fast response, a notably high first contact resolution rate and overall low resolution time.

Application Coordinator

July 2008 – January 2009 (7 months)

In July I started at the CBS in the role of Application Coordinator during a transition of the work process of the application development and maintenance division in alignment with ASL principles (closely related to ITIL).
My position involved the assessment and planning of software changes, release management, routing of incidents, planning and leading a special taskforce with a variety of experts aimed at solving particularly persistent bugs in the statistical programs of the CBS. I was successful in leading the group towards a fix of a long-term problem in a rather elaborate set of intertwining programs which, among others, provided the essential backend for large-scale online surveys.

Test Manager Data center

CBS

Government Agency; Government Administration industry

March 2007 – February 2008 (1 year)

Although my jobtitle was Test Coordinator, my responsibilities and deliverables included those of the Test Manager as defined in the TMAP method. The test object was a new data center for the CBS, a virtual infrastructure based on VMWare, 75+ ESX servers, 2500+ workstations and a high availability LAN/WAN spanning three major locations in the Netherlands. Deadlines had been delayed and testing was obviously still necessary. This included determining a *manageable* list of requirements. Together with key workers on the CBS staff and project managers of 'project PUIK', I managed to clarify these requirements, ensuring the agreement of the customer and program management. I continued to determine the test strategy, draw up the Master test plan, detailed test plans, logical test cases, scripts and planning for the acceptance test of the various project groups and the project as a whole.
