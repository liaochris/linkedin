Ron Larson's Summary

What's in it for You?
Most real estate salespeople must spend a large part of their workday searching for new business by placing ads, telephone canvassing, generating mailing campaigns to the general public and holding weekend open houses. My focus is different. When helping people, my goal is to do such a fantastic job for them that they will become raving fans, whether we do business together or not. As a result, many eagerly introduce me to their neighbors, friends and family. These essential connections form the foundation for my practice and free me to serve at a higher, more personalized level. I strive to place their interests ahead of my own, which enables me to become a trusted advisor, rather than just another salesperson. My clients and those they introduce me to benefit. It’s an enjoyable way for me to work. In the end, we all win.

Getting Personal
In the year 2000, my wife, Jeanette, and I made a life-changing move. We sold our Seattle area home and headed to Santa Rosa, leaving family, friends, and hundreds of past clients behind (in good hands). It was risky, as we knew not a soul in Sonoma County. I'm happy to say that we have been blessed with great friends and clients. Since then, I've had the pleasure of helping many others relocate to this wonderful area. After years as an at-home mom and volunteer, Jeanette is now an Operations Coordinator at our church. We cheer on our two kids, now teenagers, in soccer, baseball, music and drama. Other interests include singing, hiking, photography, watching movies and playing with Riley, our King Charles Cavalier Spaniel. Life is good. We love it here.
