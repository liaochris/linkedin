Adam Cobb’s Summary

Dr. Adam C Cobb, a US citizen, is Professor of International Relations, CSC, USMC. Dr Cobb has written and taught courses on Energy Security, Culture and Interagency Operations, Strategy, Cyberwarfare, Asia Pacific Security, and Irregular Warfare. He advises the Pak-Af Coordination Cell on the JCS and provides strategic advice during weekly strategy sessions with GEN McChrystal. He won a Visiting Fellowship at Wolfson College, Cambridge. In 2008, Dr Cobb briefed the National Intelligence Council on China’s military modernization. In 2007, he was a panelist in the Tswalu Process in South Africa. He has testified before the US China Economic and Security Review Commission. He has held a number of other positions. He was director of a think tank in Sydney, Australia; Special-Director Strategic Policy, RAAF HQ; Senior Defense Adviser, Australian Parliament; on the Congressional Liaison staff of the Australian Embassy in Washington DC; and on the staff of a US Representative. Dr Cobb studied at the Britannia Royal Naval College (Dartmouth, UK), and received a B.A. at Sydney University (including exchanges at UC Berkeley and UT Austin), an MA at ANU and a PhD at St John’s College, Cambridge University, where he read History with Professor Sir Harry (FH) Hinsley (Official Historian of British Intelligence).

Dr Cobb's publications include:
“IEDs, Energy Security and Enemy Strategy” in Marine Corps Gazette, (2010); “Some Global Strategic Implications of the US Election”, Wolfson College Journal, Jan 2009, No 32, Cambridge University Press, pp.70-78; “National Security Dilemmas”, RUSI Journal, Dec 2009; “Balancing Act: Australia's Strategic Relations with China and the United States”, Georgetown Journal of International Affairs, (Summer/Fall 2007); “Iraq: A False Choice” in Small Wars Journal Blog, (November 5, 2007); “Iraq: A Strategic Assessment” in Civil Wars, Vol 9:1 (2007); and “Australia and the RMA” in The Information RMA in Asia. (Palgrave Press, 2004).
