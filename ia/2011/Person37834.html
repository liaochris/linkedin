Rose-Anne Raies, Pres. A+ Virtual Assistant, Inc.’s Summary

After 25 years experience in office management, human resources management, employee benefits and legal support, I decided to put my extensive computer skills to work as an online personal assistant. A virtual assistant can add to your staff or substitute for a conventional employee. You pay only for the work that is done, on an as-needed basis.

With today's increased technology, there is no reason why your personal assistant must be sitting by your side in the same physical location. Virtual assistants provide the same services for less money. You do not have to pay for employment related taxes, insurance, and other benefits. Virtual assistants use their own equipment, supplies. You can save on space rent and many other administrative overhead items.

The average entrepreneur spends 40-60% of their time on non-revenue producing activities. Doesn't it make sense to outsource to a pro?
