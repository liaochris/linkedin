  • Director of Security, Administration, Personnel, and Publishing

Supervised Defense Nuclear Agency Printing Plant that produced over 6 million documents annually. Earned Defense Meritorious Service Medal for performance.   • Officer in Charge, Pacific Command Liaison Office

    Defense Nuclear Agency

    (Government Agency; 1001-5000 employees; Military industry)

Involved fully in fast-response and short-warning military objectives, as well as geopolitical, foreign affairs, and public relations impact involving Pacific Rim nations. Impacted U.S. Pacific Command’s Non-Strategic Nuclear Forces Study Program and U.S. Pacific Command Independent Review Group, leading to improved organization-wide understanding of nuclear matters. Leveraged operational experience, Washington-based programmatic awareness, and weapons engineering background to highlight key nuclear issues for senior DoD and joint principles in the area.

Served as Security Officer, Nuclear Weapons Personnel Reliability Program Certifying Officer, and Maintenance Manager. Led unit to achieve Golden Anchor Award for highest employee retention in the U.S. Navy and Ney Award for #1 food service. Engineered 180-degree turnaround for operation with prior performance issues. Guided unit in winning 7 of 9 departmental awards, as well as nominations by executive-level personnel to win Best Ship in the Fleet award. Implemented continual improvements following submission to and approval from organization leader. Awarded Navy Meritorious Service Medal. Qualified for Command at Sea. Mentored by ship's commanding officer, then-Commander Michael G. Mullen, now Chairman, Joint Chiefs of Staff.

  • Assistant for Surface Anti-Air Warfare Systems

    Office of the Chief of Naval Operations (Pentagon)

    (Military industry)

    1983 — 1985 (2 years )

    Significantly participated in research, analysis, and development of the Anti-Air Warfare Threat Appraisal which established procurement and funding priorities for the Navy based on current capabilities, threat, and projected technological advances. Provided technical and operational expertise in the following Navy studies and working groups: High Energy Laser Weapon System, Surface Ship Survivability and Signature Reduction, Battleship Battle Group, Navy Anti-Terrorist Strategy, Airship Surveillance, Outer Air Battle, Non Nuclear Ordnance Requirements, Nuclear Standard Missile, and Special Access Program involving advanced technologies. Served as Program Coordinator for the Advanced Surface-to-Air Missile System, a $1 billion program over the Five Year Defense Plan. Provided day-to-day technical, financial, and programmatic analysis and briefings to Navy, DOD, and Congressional principals and their staffs.

    (Military industry)

    1981 — 1983 (2 years )

    Served as Chief Engineer in an $800 million guided missile equipped cruiser. Led, managed, organized, trained, and supervised 10 officers and 110 enlisted personnel in the performance of their duties in an extremely complex, high pressure, and demanding operational environment. Managed the second half of a 15-month $100 million shipyard overhaul of the engineering plant. Significantly planned, trained, and executed the successful two Light Off Examinations and Operational Propulsion Plant Inspections. Ship was the first cruiser in the Pacific Fleet in 18 months to get underway on the first day of the three day Operational Inspection.
