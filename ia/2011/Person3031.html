Anke Bangma's Experience

Member of the Board of Advisors

DasArts, Amsterdam

Fine Art industry

2010 – Present (1 year)

curator and editor

(independent)

Fine Art industry

2008 – Present (3 years)

Since 2008 I have been working as an independent curator, and realized the exhibition Performing Evidence at SMART Project Space, Amsterdam (2009), and a project with Rod Dickinson and Steve Rushton at the Hannah Arendt reading room at the Bard College library, NY (2009). These projects were realized with generous support from the Netherlands Foundation for Visual Arts, Design and Architecture and the Center for Curatorial Studies at Bard College.

I am currently collaborating with artist Marjolijn Dijkman on a book project forthcoming in 2011. Previous book projects include "Katarina Zdjelar: But if you take my voice, what will be left to me" (Museum for Contemporary Art Belgrade/Serbian Pavilion of the 53rd Venice Biennial, 2009); "Resonant Bodies, Voices, Memories" (with Deirdre M. Donoghue, Lina Issa and Katarina Zdjelar, Piet Zwart Institute, Rotterdam & Revolver Publishing, 2008); and "Experience, Memory, Reenactment" (with Steve Rushton and Florian Wüst, PZI & Revolver, 2005).

As from January 2011, I will take up a new role as curator of contemporary arts at the Tropenmuseum in Amsterdam, one of Europe's leading ethnographic museums.

Supervisor

Norwegian Artistic Research Fellowship Programme

Fine Art industry

2008 – Present (3 years)

In the framework of this artistic research programme, I work closely with two artists, Pedro Gomez Egana (Bergen National Academy of the Arts/KHIB), and Siri Hermansen (Oslo National Academy of the Arts/KHIO).

Huis & Festival aan de Werf is an annual theatre festival in the city of Utrecht and a residency programme that produces new work by young theatre makers and artists in related disciplines.

Board Member

Uqbar

Fine Art industry

2004 – Present (7 years)

Uqbar is a foundation initiated by artists Mariana Castillo Deball and Irene Kopelman, which realizes projects on the intersection of art and science.

Associate Professor

National Academy of the Arts, Bergen, Norway

Fine Art industry

2004 – 2010 (6 years)

Between 2004-2010 I was Associate Professor in Theory and Curating at the Bergen National Academy of the Arts in Norway. The Academy has also generously supported my curatorial research, and contributed to the realisation of the exhibition "Performing Evidence", SMART Project Space, Amsterdam (2009).

guest curator

SMART Project Space, Amsterdam

Fine Art industry

2009 – 2009 (less than a year)

At SMART Project Space I guest-curated the exhibition "Performing Evidence" (July 11-August 25, 2009), for which I brought together works by contemporary artists and documents from the history of psychology. The exhibition explores how the very making of pictures provides the conditions for the enactment of certain
social scenarios.

With (new) works by Guy Ben-Ner, Anneke A. de Boer, Ian Charlesworth, Rod Dickinson and Harun Farocki, and historical documents from the collections of the Filmmuseum, Amsterdam; Tropenmuseum, Amsterdam; Museum Dr. Guislain, Ghent; and the Wellcome Trust, London.

guest editor

the Museum of Contemporary Art Belgrade / 53rd Venice Biennial

Fine Art industry

2009 – 2009 (less than a year)

For the Serbian Pavilion at the 53rd Venice Biennial I edited the publication "Katarina Zdjelar: But if you take my voice, what will be left to me?" (2009). With essays by Mladen Dolar, Frans-Willem Korsten, Branimir Stojanovic, Jan Verwoert, and artist Katarina Zdjelar.

Course Director MA Fine Art

the Piet Zwart Institute, WdKA, Rotterdam

Fine Art industry

1999 – 2007 (8 years)

At the Piet Zwart Institute for postgraduate studies and research I developed a curriculum for an international community of young artists. Students would combining independent work and individual tutorial support with an annually changing programme of thematic projects. This curriculum continues at PZI until today.

In this context, I commissioned thematic projects by amongst others artists Lara Almarcegui, Bik Van der Pol, Babak Afrassiabi & Nasrin Tabatabai, De Geuzen, Emil Hrvatin & Peter Senk, Sarah Pierce, Hinrich Sachs, Milica Tomic and Uqbar Foundation; publisher Christoph Keller; critics Georg Schöllhammer and Jan Verwoert; cultural theorists Maaike Bleeker, Rene Boomkens, and Lieven de Cauter; curators Frederique Bergholtz, Hilde de Bruijn, Annie Fletcher, Eva Gonzalez-Sancho, Maria Hlavajova, Maria Lind, Lisette Smits, and Tracey Warr; curators/theorists Dorothea von Hantelmann and Nina Möntmann.

As part of the PZI programme, I also initiated a series of public lectures and exhibitions realized in collaboration with TENT centre for visual art in Rotterdam, and a series of publications disseminated in collaboration with Revolver publishers.

Assistant Curator

Witte de With, centre for contemporary art, Rotterdam

Fine Art industry

1993 – 1998 (5 years)

At Witte de With, I worked with Chris Dercon and Bartomeu Mari, and collaborated on exhibitions and publications including "Craigie Horsfield: The Rotterdam Project" (1998); "Still-A Novel" (1996); "Jeanne van Heeswijk & Susan Kozel: Room with a View" (1996); and "Jef Geys: What are we having for dinner tonight?" (1993).
