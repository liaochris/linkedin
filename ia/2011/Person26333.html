Linda Cheung’s Summary

A results-orientated, dynamic leader with in-depth strategic and operational experience and an exceptional talent for simplifying complex business issues.

Connectegrity CEO, Linda Cheung has an impressive track record in devising innovative software and business solutions that routinely deliver benefits worth millions of pounds in the toughest of markets. Her reputation as an entrepreneurial yet pragmatic business leader, trouble-shooter and fixer, is built upon an infectious passion for excellence and proven commercial acumen – all underpinned by a strong sense of personal integrity.

A former Executive Director at Morgan Stanley, Linda was promoted to a Chief Operating Officer role at the age of just 32 after making her mark as a key player in the launch of the company’s first ever credit card. This ability to enter new markets through the creation and delivery of new products is one of Linda’s greatest strengths, which together with her in-depth understanding of how technology can drive business success, provides a powerful combination of skills for achieving competitive advantage. By bringing affordable board-level expertise to mid-sized businesses wanting to achieve growth, manage risk or implement organisational change, Linda enables them to compete effectively with major market players.

Linda was awarded a CIMA Fellowship in 2003, was a judge for the CIMA Annual Awards 2010, and is a committee member at the Institute of Directors Young Directors Forum. Linda also has extensive experience in mentoring students and professionals and is author of Cash, Parties and Kudos: 7 Rules of Have a Life, Pass Your Exams and Reap the Rewards.

Why not contact Linda to find out how her motivational, enthusiastic and, above all, approachable leadership style could help your business to make the most of today’s technology?

Linda Cheung’s Specialties:

Organisational change; Innovative software and business solutions; Project management (multi-geographical and cross functional); Strategic initiatives; New business launches; Product and market development; System design and implementation; Process re-engineering; Operations management (including book, capital and personnel management and development; risk reduction and control; and business continuity planning).
