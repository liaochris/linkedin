  • Velocity and Pressure Fluctuations on Inclined Tube Banks Submitted to Turbulent Flow

    • International Conference on Structural Mechanics in Technology Reactor. Transactions. Washington: 16th SMiRT.
    • August 2001
    Authors: Cristiano Rodrigues Barcellos, ENDRES, L. A. M. ; MÖLLER, S. V. .

    This paper presents the experimental study of pressure and velocity fluctuations and their interdependence, in the turbulent flow impinging on arrangements of yawed circular cylinders simulating inclined tube banks with square arrangement and a pitch to diameter ratio of 1.26. Measurements were performed with hot wires and a pressure transducer.   • Dynamic characteristics of the turbulent flow trough inclined tube banks

    • M. Eng. Dissertation, PROMEC, Federal University Rio Grande do Sul
    • 2001
    Authors: Cristiano Rodrigues Barcellos

    The purpose of this work is to present the experimental study of pressure and velocity fluctuations and their interdependence in the turbulent flow impinging on arrangements of inclined circular cylinders, with different incidence angles (30o, 45o, 60o and 90o), simulating inclined tube banks with square arrangement and pitch to diameter ratio P/D= 1,26.
    The mean pressure distribution on one channel side wall, with the presence of the tube bank, shows that isobaric lines are not parallel to tube axes, indicating that the pressure gradient is neither perpendicular to the tubes, nor parallel to main flow direction. RMS values of pressure fluctuations indicate a more uniform distribution of dynamic loads around the tubes of the banks with inclinations of 30º and 90º. Analysis of the results of pressure fluctuations spectra shows that as the flow incidence angle increases, peaks appear for smaller and smaller dimensionless frequencies. Behavior of velocity fluctuations spectra shows a soft decay of the curves when the flow incidence angle increases. Cross-correlations between velocity and pressure fluctuations indicate that when the flow incidence angle decreases, important three-dimensional effects appear. These effects characterized by the oscillations in cross correlations for the incidence angle of 45º seem to vanish as the incidence angle is continued to be reduced.

  • Particle Model for Fluidized Beds

    • V Salon of Scientific Initiation at UFRGS
    • 1993
    Authors: Cristiano Rodrigues Barcellos, V. Borges

    This paper presents a study of the hydrodynamic behavior of fluidized beds, based on models proposed by Foscolo and Gibilaro and Kunii and Levenspiel. From the parameters of the particles and fluid can predict the behavior of the bed as a function of porosity in which it finds itself. Also presented are several correlations for the fluidized bed under the condition of particles. These correlations were applied to the case of RNLF, allowing basic preliminary information about the hydrodynamics of the reactor core.

  • Analysis of Mixing Correlations for the Flow Through Rod Bundles of a Pressurized Water Reactor

    • PROMEC, Federal University Rio Grande do Sul
    • 1997
    Authors: Cristiano Rodrigues Barcellos

    This work presents a study of the mixing parameters of two adjacent subchannels, simulating the fuel element of a PWR reactor. With this purpose, a computacional program was developed, to evaluate the temperature distribution along the subchannels, considering the mixing through correlations proposed by several authors and comparing the results with the hipothetical case where no mixing occurs. The results, in form of tables and graphics, show the reduction temperature gradient between subchannels due to the mixing process.
