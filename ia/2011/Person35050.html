Pieter Danhieux's Summary

Pieter Danhieux is a Certified Instructor for the SANS Institute teaching military, government and private organizations offensive techniques on how to target and assess organizations and individuals for security weaknesses. He is also one of the founders of the security and hacking conference BRUCON in Belgium, where he designed and ran cyber-intrusion exercises (The Hex Factor) across Europe since 2009 together with a group of talented people.

Pieter started his information security career early in life. In 2004 he obtained the Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP) certification and was one of the youngest persons ever in Belgium to do so. He then obtained the Certified Information Systems Auditor (CISA), the GIAC Certified Forensics Analyst program (GCFA) and is currently one of the select few people worldwide to hold the GIAC Security Expert (GSE) certification.

Pieter is one of Ernst & Young’s top information security experts and he is currently working out of their Sydney office in Australia. He is fluent in three languages. Until January 2011, Pieter was based in Belgium where he was one of the senior managers in the European attack and penetration team operating in the financial industry space. As a security professional, Pieter is perceived as one of the leading technical experts in Belgium. As a business developer, Pieter has experience communicating with executives, negotiating contracts and presenting to large audiences. As a manager, Pieter is perceived as a hard but fair role model for his team members applying situational leadership. He is selective in nature and tries to motivate everyone he works with to deliver the highest quality.
