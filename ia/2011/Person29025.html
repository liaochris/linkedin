Matthew P. Schwartz's Summary

From being the lead writer on FOX's evening news to speaking in front of PR and marketing executives on the challenges facing their industries, I have had an extremely versatile media career.
Most recently, I have focused on myriad changes gripping the media industry as this sector adapts in a digital age. During my tenure at ScribeMedia, I hosted the WebTV series, From Print to Digital, in which I interviewed several key media figures such as Gordon McLeod, president of The Wall Street Journal Digital Network, and Jim Roberts, The New York Times editor of digital news.
I also gained solid experience shooting video while covering events ranging from digital-media conferences to the Museum of Modern Art's exhibit on George Lois’ Esquire covers. I produced several podcasts exploring how the Web is changing business and culture, and also contributed several items each day to Scribe’s News Briefs section.
As a full-time reporter at Crain’s BtoB and BtoB’s Media Business, I wrote a monthly column titled "Business Media," penned many cover stories and was the lead writer and producer of several special reports. As Editor of PR News, I modernized the publication, both graphically and editorially, and participated on several media panels that analyzed trends in public relations and corporate communications.

Specialties

Managing, writing, reporting, editing voice, online video programming (both in back and in front of the camera) podcasting, voice-over work, strong background covering b-to-b and b-to-c media/consumer magazines/trade publishing/marketing and advertising and the dramatic changes engulfing the (interrelated) industries.
