Larissa Glubb's Summary

Larissa is an internationally experienced (over 15 years PQE) commercial lawyer with particular expertise in Information Technology Law.

Larissa has advised on, negotiated and drafted a wide variety of technology and outsourcing agreements, both as in-house legal counsel for leading ICT and energy companies including Perot Systems Corporation, Vodafone Pacific and Mobil Oil, and as a private practice lawyer for leading law firms in the UK, Australia and New Zealand including Gilbert + Tobin and Bell Gully.

Between 2006 and 2009 Larissa provided remote legal consulting services to Simmons & Simmons, a global law firm headquartered in London. From her (then) New Zealand base, Larissa created a suite of best practice ICT standard form documents for roll out across Simmons' international network of law offices.

In 2009 Larissa joined forces with Andrew Hamilton, a former Director of Project Management at Deutsche Bank, to establish Latitude South with the goal of providing high quality remote legal services to a global audience including technology-focused law firms and in-house legal teams working on technology transactions.

Larissa's years of experience negotiating and documenting international, business-critical ICT transactions, her industry knowledge and expert contract drafting skills means she can assist her clients to create deal specific documents that meet their business objectives, or work with her clients to develop, streamline and update their organisational standard form agreements and templates to ensure a more efficient, risk mitigated contract management system.

Specialties

Information, Communication and Technology Law specialist. Expert contract drafting skills.

Legal Consultant

Simmons & Simmons

Partnership; Law Practice industry

November 2006 – April 2009 (2 years 6 months)

The provision of remote legal consulting services to Simmons & Simmons' Information, Communications & Technology (ICT) International Practice Group.

Responsibilities included:

1. The development of a suite of best-practise, client and industry-specific standard form agreements and model documents including business process and telecommunication services outsourcing agreements, software development, licence and support agreements and various other technology-related agreements.

The purpose of the standard form documents was to enable the ICT International Practice Group to leverage these documents (with regional variations) as the foundation, or starting point, for the majority of transactions undertaken. As a result, each standard form contained guidance notes, relevant alternate clauses and full schedule content (or guidance for completion of schedules) and was drafted to allow maximum flexibility to interchange provisions between the standard forms, where appropriate.

2. The development, co-ordination and implementation of an internal training schedule for the ICT International Practice Group.

3. Drafting business development documents, marketing materials and capability statements promoting the expertise and experience of the ICT International Practice Group.
