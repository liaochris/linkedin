Bill Bennett's Summary

I'm an experienced editor and journalist specialising in technology and business. I work part time as the New Zealand editor for Communications Day – the rest of the time I freelance.

Over the years I've worked for Australia and New Zealand’s best known newspapers including; The Dominion, The Evening Post (now Dominion-Post), The Sydney Morning Herald, The Melbourne Age and The Australian Financial Review. He was also a technology commentator on ABC radio and Channel 7.

My first paid job was as a writer on Practical Computing in London in the early 1980s. After that I edited a music industry trade magazine and worked on an in-house staff newspaper for British Telecom. From I moved to become the press officer for the Science and Engineering Research Council in 1985. It was here I first encountered what later became the Internet while writing about UK projects at CERN.

In 1987 I left the UK to edit the computer section of The Dominion in Wellington. I left to run a freelance business, publish a telecommunications trade magazine and work as a columnist for the National Business Review. ACP recruited me and move me to Australia where I launched PC Magazine Australia and PC Magazine New Zealand. I also edited the Australian Net Guide.

My degree is in Physics from Manchester University in the UK. In the early 1980s I co-wrote the best-selling Usbourne Guide to the Micro. I'm arguably the inventor of the computer magazine cover disc, being responsible for creating a 45rpm flexidisc for the British Your Computer magazine in 1982.
