Currently providing specialized consulting services for the decontamination, decommissioning, and demolition of radiologically and chemically contaminated facilities associated with the nation's nuclear weapons manufacturing program. Also provide expertise in the conduct of operations of nuclear facilities and in all facets of nuclear facility safety bases - from development, through operation, and ultimately downgrading for D&D.

Project Manager

Bechtel Jacobs

Government Agency; Environmental Services industry

October 2004 – June 2009 (4 years 9 months)

Deputy Project Manager for the High Risk Equipment (HRE) removal portion of the $802 million K-25/K-27 D&D project. These two buildings are old gaseous diffusion facilities built to enrich uranium for the Manhattan Project and for weapons used to win the Cold War. At one mile long and 44 acres under one roof, the K-25 D&D effort is one of the largest cleanup projects within the Dept. of Energy. The HRE scope consisted of removing and mining specifically identified equipment of national security interest; venting, purging, and draining of all process pipe and equipment; foaming of process pipe and equipment; and installation of nets and barriers to protect workers from falling debris. The HRE project staff consisted of 25 salaried professionals, 3 field safety professionals, 6 criticality safety officers, a direct-hire labor force of 146, and functional support from HR, admin, engineering, procurement, quality assurance, and radiation protection.
