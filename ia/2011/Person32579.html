Nick Gray's Summary

After 25 years in resource management, sales management and communications roles for a major consultancy business, I am an expert in what makes a people-based SME work.

I have developed and operated resource allocation processes to match the demands of clients with staff availability, skills and career objectives, in order to provide robust project solutions. I have also designed and implemented measurement systems giving timely insight into the the sales pipeline, highlighting bottlenecks and risks, and informing decisions on market focus, project pricing and investment. The combination of these two roles was crucial in Analysys Consulting's transformation from academic think-tank to top strategy consultancy in its field.

Following the acquisition of Analysys by international IT services company Datatec to form the Analysys Mason Group, I remained in place to bring my expertise to the wider group, and to help guide its investment in professional services automation systems. Now that this work is close to completion, I am leaving my full-time role with the company in order to explore opportunities in other enterprises with similar attributes and challenges.

Specialties

Resource management and pipeline management for people-based businesses; definition and implementation of key performance indicators; business-to-business communication

Chief Operating Officer

Privately Held; Telecommunications industry

April 1995 – July 2009 (14 years 4 months)

Designed and developed processes for resource management of consulting business. Operated and refined those processes through quadrupling of resource-base to 100 professionals, and through merger with another consultancy with similar resource base.

Responsible for effective operation of all stages of project lifecycle (lead, proposal, contract, execution and billing) and for availability of key metrics (utilisation, profitability, orders, hit-rate, backlog, sales risk, etc).

Managed growth in consultancy project turnover from £3m to £18m per year. Developed revenue tracking and forecasting processes to keep pace with this growth, providing sector managers and the Board with the means to understand the state of the sales pipeline at any time, informing strategic and tactical decisions on the basis of accurate assessments of revenue impact and opportunity cost.

Adapted resource and pipeline management tools to address challenges of growth, short-term fluctuation (market vs resource constraints), and also the recession in the telecoms consulting sector following the dotcom crash.

Operational management of joint venture with leading investment bank to develop equity research expertise for the telecoms and digital media sector.
