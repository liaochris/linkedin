Ivan Zuzak's Summary

I'm a computer science Ph.D. student, teaching assistant at the University of Zagreb and open-source Web engineer.

My Ph.D. research is focused on software architectures, the WWW, the REST architectural style and end-user programming. I have been involved in several research and development projects in the field of distributed systems and end-user programming. I am currently working on the Geppeto project (http://geppeto.fer.hr/). I have been on internships and specializations at Ericsson and Google, I have published several academic papers and have given several technical talks (including a Tech Talk at Google - http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fbUvCdADz_A). As a teaching assistant, I participate in several courses: Introduction to theory of computation, Programming language translation, Software design, Service-oriented computing and Middleware systems. I have directly worked with and mentored more than 20 students, usually over periods of 2-3 years. Many of my students have been on internships to world-class companies, such as Google and Tuenti or are Ph.D. students at world-class universities, such as University of Southern California.

I contribute to many open-source projects and have started several projects of my own: pmrpc (http://code.google.com/p/pmrpc/ - a JavaScript library for cross-context communication in Web browsers), feed-buster (http://code.google.com/p/feed-buster/ - a Python AppEngine service which transforms RSS/ATOM feeds to include image links from blog posts) and node-revhttpws (https://github.com/izuzak/node-revhttpws/ - a NodeJS proxy server which enables each Web page executing in a browser to receive and process HTTP requests and WebSockets connections). I maintain the This Week in REST blog (http://thisweekinrest.wordpress.com), a bi-weekly roundup of news related to the REST architectural style. I also have a personal Web site at http://ivanzuzak.info where I write a blog of my ideas and keep track of talks, papers and projects.
