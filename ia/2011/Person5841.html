Bradley Merrill Thompson’s Summary

Advocate. Consensus builder. Counselor. Investigator.

In my law and consulting practices, I focus on government regulation of the medical device and pharmaceutical industries, including FDA and Medicare. Within that space, I further focus on:

1. Within the range of regulatory requirements, mostly:

a. FDA sales & marketing requirements and issues associated with off label promotion
b. FDA clearance and approval strategies
c. Clinical trials
d. Medicare coverage
e. Company compliance investigations, assessments and auditing
f. Government in the sunshine and Due Process in guidance and rule development

2. From a technology perspective, mostly:

a. In vitro diagnostics, or IVDs
b. Combination products and drug delivery
c. The hardware and software components of connected health, otherwise known as mHealth, eCare, health information technology or HIT, and remote monitoring

3. Policy work for associations such as AdvaMed, the Combination Products Coalition, the mHealth Regulatory Coalition, and Continua Health Alliance.

Beyond my law practice, I advocate for:

1. Christian causes, highlighting the work of volunteers who care for those in need

2. Encouraging the genre of Christian Documentary Photography

As an advocate, I use four tools:

1. Writing, often in a journalistic, plain English style. Books, chapters and articles. Those related to law are listed on my firm’s website. For an example, see my 50-page eBook on "FDA Regulation of mHealth," published by www.mobilhealthnews.com.

2. Public speaking, with a heavy reliance on visual media.

3. Photography in a documentary style.

4. Interviews captured in audio recordings.

While due to the nature of my work my office is in Washington D.C., I live in a small Indiana town and on airplanes.

I have taught Food & Drug Law at Indiana University School of Law and Columbia Law School.

Bradley Merrill Thompson’s Interests:
