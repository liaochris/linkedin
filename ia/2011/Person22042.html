Principal contributor in the design, development and implementation of the Operational Systems Support (Oasis) system for USinternetworking, Inc. (USi). Oasis includes network, server and website configuration management, monitoring and alerting system. Oasis is based upon open source tools (Perl, Python, Apache, CVS and MySQL) and certain commercial elements that met the our business needs sufficiently well at a low enough price point (NetCool, SystemEDGE and Topaz). Oasis replaced the previous commerical software systems centered on Tivoli.

Expanded Oasis to include on-demand, automated testing of services and applications with detailed results and clickable links to specific failure remediation steps contained in our Confluence wiki.

Designed, developed and implemented USinternworking's bandwidth monitoring system based upon Cisco network spans, libpcap, and RRDtool. The C language based code has run fault free for over seven years (as of June 2009). The collector examines every packet that traverses USinternetworking's core switches. Byte counts and packets counts are written to disk every five minutes. The classifier assigns each packet to a particular client and creates 95th percentile billing information. Graphical and tabular representations of the data are available via Oasis.

Developed a secure instant messaging system (SecureIM) based upon the jabberd implementation of XMPP for communication between employees of USinternetworking. Service has proved indispensable for collaboration with employees located in India and other remote offices. All connections are SSL encrypted and use company servers exclusively, thereby allowing users to communicate sensitive data between sites without disclosure to unauthorized parties. Subsequently, several clients have purchased SecureIM as a service for their own secured instant messaging needs.
