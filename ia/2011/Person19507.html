Javier Sanchez Cano's Summary

For over ten ten years, I have been working and reflecting on the field of how local and regional governments and administrations position themselves and seek their growth and prosperity in the international context. I have occupied different positions and served as an advisor, a consultant, and a team and organisation leader.

At the same time, I have tried to develop and share the expertise and knowledge deriving from this activity, engaging in collaborative research projects and taking the time to produce written outputs which can help others understand and get further insight into this new, enthralling field.

Specialties

International and European local and regional associations and networking
Building institutional and administrative capacity.
Acquisition and management of international/European projects and programmes
Lobbying, decentralized cooperation, strategic planning

Generalitat de Catalunya

Public Company; Government Administration industry

Director, European Centre for the Regions

European Institute of Public Administration

Educational Institution; Professional Training & Coaching industry

2008 – 2010 (2 years)

Seconded by the Government of Catalunya, the director of EIPA-ECR manages a group of experts who deliver training, research and consultancy on different issues in the regional-European interface: regional participation in EU policy-making, legal transposition, compliance, projecte management and acquisition.

I was the Supervisor of three Euromed programmes: Euromed Justice II, Euromed Training of Public Administrations, and Euromed Market. I also developed expertise in some research and training activities I leaded myself: EU lobbying, administrative adaptation to the EU system.

Director of EU policies, Secretariat for the European Union

Generalitat de Catalunya

Public Policy industry

September 2005 – September 2008 (3 years 1 month)

Leader of a team of policy advisors, in charge of monitoring the legislative processes and political outlook relevant to EU policies, and providing information, assistance and training to the different regional ministries for the EU activity of the Catalan autonomous government.

Advisor / Chief of Staff

Ajuntament de Barcelona

Non-Profit; Government Administration industry

2001 – 2005 (4 years)

Serving at two City Councillors in the municipality of Barcelona: Civic Participation, Solidarity and Cooperation (2003-2005); and Presidency and Finance (2001-2003).

The functions in these two positions were similar: guidance, coordination and monitoring the work of the executive areas assigned to the two Councillors (among others, civil service and international relations in the latter; international relations, development cooperation, and citizen participation in the former).

In both cases, the European and international dimensions were essential: relations with multilateral agencies, coordination of European projects; coordination for the Master Plan for International Cooperation of Barcelona; relations with local European and international associations, etc...

Secretary-General

Centro Iberoamericano de Desarrollo Estratégico Urbano, CIDEU

Partnership; International Affairs industry

2001 – 2004 (3 years)

CIDEU is an association of Cities with headquarters in Barcelona and dedicated to supporting local development processes to through counselling, training and education of administrations and local managers, with a participatory and strategic approach.

This role allowed by to build solid knowledge on city development strategies and local governance. I also built important expertise in project acqusition and management, especially in the field of institutional strengthening of the local administration.

I became familiar with the procedures for obtaining resources, both multilateral organizations (World Bank, IDB) and the European Union, in its various dimensions: lobbying, national funds, provision services, international tenders, partnerships, ...

Consultant

ergo sum

Research industry

1999 – 2001 (2 years)

In 1999 I created my own company: the mission of ergo sum was to supply public policies with innovative thinking based on sound social research. Noteworthy assigments were:

- The external evaluation for a project on labour re-integration of women prisoners (http://www.surt.org/mip), funded by the 4th EU R+D Framework Programme.

- The European Papers series for the Diputació (County Council) of Barcelona, featuring different case studies on EU issues with high impact at the territorial level (enlargement, Committee of the Regions, IGC...). The studies assessed such impacts for the municipalities in the greater Barcelona area, and identified strategies for influence at the EU level.

- One major assignment was the definition and drafting of a comprehensive study on the state of economic, social and cultural rights for the DESC foundation, where I was the leader of a multidisciplinary team with members of the faculty of the Autonomous University of Barcelona, and other expert staff.
