Andrew Hoffman’s Summary

I am a litigation associate at the Boca Raton office of Proskauer Rose LLP. I have worked on matters involving a broad range of practice areas, including fiduciary litigation, commercial litigation, and privacy and data security law. Prior to joining Proskauer, I served as an interim law clerk to Judge Mark E. Polen of the Florida Fourth District Court of Appeal.

While in law school, I competed and won best brief awards in three appellate advocacy competitions as a member of the Moot Court Board. I served as a research assistant to Professor and Dean Emeritus Jon L. Mills, who wrote on matters of privacy law and policy. I was also heavily involved in student activities, serving as the Executive Vice President and Treasurer of the Jewish Law Student Association, as an Ambassador for incoming law students, and as a member of the Student Recruitment Team. After my first year of law school, I served as a judicial intern to U.S. District Judge Steven D. Merryday of the Middle District of Florida.

Before law school, I was a U.S. Department of Homeland Security Scholar and completed internships with the government at the Department of Homeland Security Privacy Office, the White House, and the Los Alamos National Laboratory.
