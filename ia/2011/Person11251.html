Dean Russell's Summary

Dean Russell is a Director at Fleishman-Hillard, and leads the UK Digital Practice. Outside of work Dean is also a St Albans District Councillor.

With over 10 years experience in digital he was recently appointed as a special advisor to the British Interactive Media Association and is a Volunteer Champion for the UK's leading Communications charity, Media Trust.

With a passion for brand, social media & communications in all forms, Dean writes a regular column for the Haymarket Charity publication, Third Sector, along with features articles for Cue Entertainment magazine.

Previously Dean has also contributed to many articles for publications such as the Health Service Journal & the Times Higher Education Supplement. He has also spoken at many conferences on topics ranging from Web3.0, Social Business and Mobile marketing.

Formerly the Vice Chair of the NHS HealthSpace Reference Panel, and member of the National Clinical Reference Panel, Dean also co-led a strategic review into the NHS Online Landscape for the Department of Health (which featured in the Health Service Journal and the Guardian newspaper).

Back in 2006 Dean also conceived the strategy behind the popular entertainment site MrPaparazzi.com, which was one of the first consumer journalist sites, and won the Yahoo! Finds of the Year award for Entertainment.

Dean's involvement in accessibility also led to him authoring the UK's first professional accessibility course, sitting on the PAS 78 review panel & conceiving the World's first accessible Flash application for the RNIB back in 2002.

Dean has been a judge for several awards including the British Video Association and Third Sector Excellence awards.

Prior to working in the digital industry, Dean studied Physics up to research level and has an MPhil from his thesis on the Preparation and Characterisation of Gallium Nitride and other Group III Nitrides.
