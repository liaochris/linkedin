Omkar Sapre's Summary

I am a storyteller by passion and have been in journalism for the past 10 years to satiate my passion. I have worked across three languages English, Hindi and Marathi and in my latest assignment, I am employed as a senior correspondent with The Economic Times, India, which is one of the largest business dailies in the world.
Over the course of my career till now, I have written (told stories) across various fields like media and entertainment, non-profits, entrepreneurial ventures, technology, animation, gaming, agriculture, brands, sports, civic issues, crime etc. I love to travel, meet new people, maintain contacts, develop friendships, explore new subjects, deepen my knowledge across various fields and I am always open for new challenges. I am a technoholic, I love machines and I am interested in knowing in detail how they work.
I want to become one of the greatest storytellers who ever lived on this planet. I am working out plans to build the world's best team of young journalists, over the next eight years. Any suggestions would be always welcomed with an open mind!

Specialties

Interviewing, writing, editing, photography, trying to learn videography, conceptualising, out-of-the-box and unconventional thinking.

Omkar Sapre's Education

University of Pune

PG Diploma in Journalism

2004 – 2005

By the time I had completed my graduation in Economics, I had already been working in journalism for almost five years, but in vernacular publications. I was good at English and vernacular dailies had no scope for growth, so I wanted to shift to English newspapers. I tried approaching some, but in our country people do not believe you are born unless you have a birth certificate. So I decided to pursue masters in journalism from the University of Pune. I passed the entrance exam, but the interviewers rejected me in the personal interview, for reasons best known to them. The diploma program, which was new then, wasn't filled to capacity, so I was rehabilitated there. I had a practical experience beforehand and this program helped me get a certificate and internship. I was floored by a girl I met at this program, who later became my life partner.

Activities and Societies: I edited the experimental newspaper on one of the groups, I was put in. As a part of my course, I interned at The Maharashtra Herald, a part of the Sakaal group then. The head of news liked my work in the internship and gave me an offer to work with on the business bureau. MH was an independent newspaper earlier, when I had also written some articles in, but was later sold to Sakaal group. It got subsequently renamed as Sakaal Times.

Sir Parashurambhau College

Bachelors In Arts, Economics

2002 – 2004

By the time I had applied for my final attempt in Class 12, I had already started working in journalism, with a recommendation from my father, at a newspaper owned by his friend. In fact, then one did not need a qualification (neither does one now). Three months into my first job, I realised that I loved storytelling, stories about various fields, and journalism is the best career I can make justice to. When I told this to my dad, he said ok, but said that if I am sure about journalism, I should become a best journalist and aim to work for The Economic Times, the newspaper he was in awe for and enjoyed reading. So I opted for Economics for my graduation. Incidentally, I developed a keen interest in Economics, passed with a first class, loved and still love the subject very much.
