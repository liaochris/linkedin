Nicola Marinelli's Summary

I am working with a concept of Prop Trading more similar to that of a Distressed Fund rather than the "classical" banking desk.
The current and future environment requires a desk not only to be profitable, but also capital efficient.
It is not just a question of cash vs synthetic: it means that the focus should be on taking advantage of the excesses of the markets. The "dislocations", the extremes or the distressed valuations are the places where the value is hidden and where the upside is higher than the downside.
On the other hand this means that there might be periods in which the opportunities are scarce; but a desk that is able to widen its scope can probably almost always find a market, a product, a company, a rate or a price that is either too low or too high given the assumptions.

Specialties

Credit in general, current focus on Financials, Energy, Oil and Asset Backed Securities / CDO, with a particular interest on first loss / equity tranches. Distressed funds.
