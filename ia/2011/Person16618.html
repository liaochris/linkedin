Gregory D. Greenman, II's Summary

Gregory Greenman recently left the Institute for Metropolitan Affairs at Roosevelt University to return to his home state of Michigan. He completed the coursework for his Master of Public Administration in Non-profit Management and will receive the degree in August 2011. He studied economics, English, and political science as an undergraduate. His research interests include: education policy, policy development analysis, and strategic planning for government and non-profit agencies, particularly tying unit missions to the overall mission of an institution.

As an undergraduate, Mr. Greenman played an instrumental role in the development of the undergraduate curriculum at Salem State University. He also served on four university committees and was Vice President of the Student Government Association. In 2008, Mr. Greenman helped author the Illinois General Assembly's Legislative Task Force Report on the Employment of Persons with Past Criminal Convictions. While completing his studies, he also served as the treasurer of the PMA Sinfonia Chicago Area Alumni Association. In 2001, he assisted the White House in selecting members for the White House Office of Faith-Based and Community Initiatives (OFBCI).

Specialties

Strategic planning, policy development and analysis, grant writing, non-profit administration, project management, community group organizing, program evaluation and needs assessment.
