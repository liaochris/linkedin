Mark Hart’s Summary

I am involved in new product development (NPD) activities from the commitment to a product concept to the launch/commercialization portion of development.

I have contributed to the development of technology products (such as measurement systems for scientists and engineers, software, computers, and training applications) that have become global leaders in their categories. My projects have included work with NASA, Hewlett-Packard, Agilent, Finnigan (now Thermo Scientific), and IEEE.

I solve intractable problems that emerge in geographically-dispersed, open innovation networks.

Consistent with the Manifesto for Agile Software Development, I value "Individuals and interactions over processes and tools." I collaborate with other NPD contributors such as designers, engineers, programmers, scientists, support specialists, market specialists, and documentation specialists. Because of my science (chemistry, electronics, and software), training development, and business experience, I can communicate and collaborate effectively within an interdisciplinary NPD environment.

In 2010, I served as the Chair of the webcast committee for the Product Development and Management Association (pdma.org). In that role, I have produced about 20 premium (pay to attend) webcasts. These events have enabled thought leaders in new product development to share their insights with an international audience.

I am a founding board member of the Pittsburgh Chapter of PDMA.
