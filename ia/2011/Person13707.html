Michelle Roberts, CSI's Summary

Michelle A. Roberts, a Professional Affiliate of the Boston Society of Architects, is the Business Development Leader: Healthy and Energy Efficient Homes and Buildings for Owens Corning. Michelle is charged to bring new commercial and residential innovations/specifications to the market place and also teach colleagues, architects, specifiers, general contractors, developers, building code officials, government agencies, and real estate professionals a deep and practical understanding of safe and healthy building science and systems.

Michelle was a Building Systems Designer/Specifier, educator and consultant, and the Principal and CEO of Chatham Hill Residential Design and Build, LLC; a partner of the National Center for Healthy Housing. In Spring 2009, she was contracted by the NCHH to author a full day Energy Efficient-Healthy Modular Housing Course and partnered with the NCHH created the Massachusetts Healthy Housing Summit held at Fitchburg State College in March 2009. She was also appointed by HUD’s Office for Healthy Homes as track Co-Chair for the 2011 National Healthy Homes Conference. This Conference is hosted by the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development’s Office of Healthy Homes and Lead Hazard Control, in partnership with the U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, U.S. Department of Energy, U.S. Department of Agriculture, and U.S. EPA.

She is also the Founder and Creator of Ecohealth Homes Inspired by Caroline McKenna.

Affiliations: Construction Specification Institute, Northeast Sustainable Energy Association (NESEA), Professional Affiliate of the Boston Society of Architects, Energy Star Design Partner, Society of Building Science Educators, The National Association of Home Builders(NAHB), The National Housing Center Board of Governors, NAHB Building System Council Board of Trustee, The NAHB Professional Women in Building Council, Licensed Massachusetts Home Improvement Contractor and MA Licensed REALTOR.
