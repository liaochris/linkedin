Bill Davies's Summary

BREIF BIOGRAPHY
I currently work for Chevron as Lead Structural Engineer on the Wheatstone LnG project, accountable for the FEED development of the jacket, topsides and associated transport and installation activites.
Previousy with Woodside I was a leading structural and lifting technical authority in both production and project development departments. I also held project and engineering management roles for major brownfield and greenfield projects.
I was Offshore Structures Manager, responsible for the structural engineering across the project and development function portfolio; including Sunrise, Browse, North Rankin 2 and Cossack Pioneer Projects. Previously, I was the Integrity Process Owner developing and implementing the integrity processes across the company from exploration through to Production.
My career started with Wimpey in the United Kingdom, working in jacket reassessment, and the design of subsea strengthening systems.
Following this period I worked on major execution projects in the North Sea and Australia, on FPSO and major fixed facilities, with Brown and Root, Kvaerner and Worley (then WGP).
In the late 80s, working extensively in the fire and blast fields in the Post Cullen Environment, I developed the Fire and Blast philosophy for the Miller Development in the North Sea. As Head of BOMEL’s design office I was responsible for the delivery of analysis, and subsea strengthening projects for clients worldwide.
I came to Australia in the early 90s, as a Lead Engineer, working on projects both for Australia and across the region, and latterly became Head of Worley’s Advanced Engineering Group, before moving to Woodside.
Over my career I was for a period Technical Secretary for the BS5950 committee, an observer on the ISO SC7 committee, and sit on the Australian Standards Lifting Equipment Committee.
