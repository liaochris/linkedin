Andrew Waltho's Summary

I am a geologist with more than 25 years experience covering a broad spectrum of exploration and mining geology. Particular experience in resource evaluation, feasibility studies, technical systems development for new mining projects, operational improvement studies and due diligence studies
spanning a range of commodities with a particular focus on coal and base metals. This experience was gained
through roles in industry and with several consulting companies and self employment.

Career highlights have included:

* My current role with Rio Tinto - working on energy project generation in a very competitive and challenging technical and commercial environment.

* Manager Mining Services with Century Zinc Limited which involved managing geological and resource
aspects of the feasibility study for the project and building the geology and survey teams.

* Manager Geology for Pasminco Limited's Mining Division, responsible for mine geology practices, resource
evaluation, improvement projects and feasibility studies at four Australian and two US base metal mines.

* Managing resource estimation studies, resource risk assessments and geotechnical studies for a series of open
cut and underground thermal coal projects in Australia, New Zealand and Brazil in consulting roles.

I am a Fellow of both the Australasian Institute of Mining and Metallurgy and the Australian Institute of Geoscientists.

I have a special interest in professional ethics and standards and promotion of the role of geosciences in the general community.

Specialties

I have specialised in resource evaluation and mining geology for a range of commodities including coal and base metals. Keen interest in professional issues and ethics and the public perception of geosciences and geoscientists.
