Chelsea Anderson’s Summary

I am fortunate to have discovered my interest in geology very early, to have a supportive family, to have friends and relatives who live in geologically interesting places, and to have the opportunity to study under one of the world's finest Earth Sciences faculties.

Academic Geological Field Study:

2010: Stonehaven, Scotland – Mapped the stratigraphy of the old red sandstone beach on the North Sea coast of Scotland near Aberdeen.

2010: Portencross, Scotland – Mapped the stratigraphy and structure of the outcroppings along a beach on the west coast of Scotland.

2010: Roseneath, Scotland – Studied the Dalradian structure and stratigraphic anomalies along the shore of Gare Loch (a branch of the Firth of Clyde on Scotland’s west coast).

2009: Loch Lomond, Scotland – Studied the metamorphic and sedimentary structures in Balmaha, along the shore of Loch Lomond.

2009: Isle of Arran, Scotland – 10-day University of Glasgow field trip to this microcosm of Scottish geology for mapping exercises and logging of sedimentary outcroppings and dike swarms.

2008: St. Monas, Scotland – 1-day University of Glasgow (Paleobiology) field to observe the stratigraphy and fossil compositions of the local limestone, sandstone and drift coal.

2005: Hawaiian Islands – 18-day exploration of three Hawaiian Islands as follow-up to Hastings High School’s Advanced Geology class; we hiked the Pololu Valley, Mauna Loa, Mauna Kea, olivine beaches, the Pu’u’o’o lava tubes, and several other destinations.

Additional Geological Field Study:

2006: Cascade Mountains – 3-week trip as a follow-up to an extensive research paper; observed Crater Lake’s developing dome of Wizard Island and various other locations in the Cascade Range; also visited the Oregon coastline.

2004: Utah – 2-week trip included visits to Zion, Arches and Goblin Valley National Parks, Antelope Island, Timpanogos Cave National Monument; also went geode digging near the Bonneville Salt Flats.

Hydrogeology, Paleobiology, Tectonic Geomorphology, Metamorphic Petrology, Igneous Petrology, Geochemistry, Stratigraphy, Paleoclimatology, Sedimentary Geology, Structural Geology, Isotopic Geology, Volcanology, Geological Mapping and Environmental Earth Science
