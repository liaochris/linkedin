Ricia Taylor's Summary

Ricia Taylor has 14 years of clinical, public health, and management experience in the field of nutrition. Currently, she serves as Nutrition Educator for Kaiser Permanente. Her responsibilities entail providing nutrition and health education to members and staff. This position affords her opportunity to review articles and reference evidence-based guidelines in order to remain abreast of trends and policies related to nutrition and health. Her skills in public health and research were sharpened while serving as Project Director of Go Girls, a public health research initiative funded by the National Institutes of Health and administered through the Rollins School of Public Health at Emory University. As Project Director, she provided project management and guided a team of researchers in implementing innovative strategies to improve the diet and increase the physical activity of overweight African American adolescent girls.

Ricia's experience in the nutrition and health fields incorporated with her critical and analytical skills, written and oral communications, and desire to contribute to the scientific community are some of the assets she brings to any position.

Her areas of interest include health promotion programs for special populations, particularly diabetes prevention and childhood obesity and prevention among African-American populations.
