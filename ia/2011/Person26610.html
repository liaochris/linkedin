Liz Craig’s Summary

My dad was an advertising man, a copy/contact guy and a writer/producer, when I was a little girl. And because I admired my dad, I wanted to be just like him. When I told him I wanted to go into advertising, he tried to discourage me, saying it was a dog-eat-dog business. But I was undeterred.

Life intervened before I got there, though, and I had a marriage and a son, Jon, before finishing my college degree. A few years later, I got that degree, and soon after, I got the job and the education of a lifetime with SCG Advertising in Overland Park, Kansas.

For several years, I worked for SCG and its sister company, New Product Insights (NPI), creating new product concepts for Fortune 500 companies, from Chesebrough-Ponds to Taco Bell. If the product succeeded in focus group testing, then we were responsible for creating the roll-out and continuing advertising for it. Sort of a post-graduate education from the best marketers in the U.S.

I worked at major agencies in Omaha and Kansas City, where I helped win creative awards and great results for clients, including a 1400% ROI for a Colorado manufacturing company. (For more info, please see my online portfolio.)

In the past couple of years, I've been learning about the world of social media marketing through live events and Webinars, and talking to Web gurus. Hearing Guy Kawasaki speak was an inspiration to me.

Whatever the media, I've found, the communication to the target audience must be clear, concise and relevant to their interests and desires. Social media adds an extra twist: it must begin to create a relationship. I love the whole idea. And I believe that social media marketing, combined with traditional marketing, can do a client worlds of good without spending piles of money.

I enjoy positioning, branding, and creating communications that get great results for clients, in whatever medium best suits the product, service or target audience. Need ideas? Let's chat.
