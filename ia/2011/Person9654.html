Christian Warden’s Summary

I have been using Linux since 1995, and started working as a systems administrator in 1997. I prefer to work with open source software, including Apache and Lighttpd, MySQL, Subversion, Exim, and Dovecot. I have also worked with Solaris, AIX, and QNX in the past, but find GNU/Linux systems provide the most pleasant and productive working environment.

On the networking side, I've done routing and load balancing with Cisco, Enterasys, and Alteon hardware using HSRP and VRRP. I've also designed high-availability, load-balanced solutions using Linux Virtual Server and related tools such as heartbeat and ldirectord.

In 1999, I added web developer to my title, and have written a lot of PHP and SQL especially for content management. I have also continued to wear my sysadmin hat, building development and deployment systems. The neologism, devops, describes much of my career well.

In 2007, I took a small step beyond back-end development, and finally started learning JavaScript. The availability of jQuery and Firebug made this transition rather pleasant. The same year, I also began doing development on the Force.com (Salesforce.com) platform. Since January 2009, I have led the development of CRC's CRM, used by about 400 employees in marketing and intake roles.

In 2006, I returned to school part-time to study economics and completed my BA in 2009.

Most recently, I have been using open source tools, Palo OLAP Server and Pentaho Kettle ETL tool, to combine and analyze data culled from Salesforce, Google AdWords, and web logs.

Other current interests include node.js, R, the use of collaborative software within a firm (Enterprise 2.0), and git/github.
