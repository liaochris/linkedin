Senior Process Engineer

Ausenco

Public Company; aax; Mining & Metals industry

July 2007 – December 2008 (1 year 6 months)

Conducted constraint (bottleneck) analysis of large scale Cu/Au concentrators, and completed a Feasibility Study for a new 8 Mtpa Cu/Au concentrator. Completed HAZOP Facilitator training and was a member of the company Safety Committee.

Studies include flowsheet development, design criteria, PFD’s, P&ID’s, mass balance, equipment sizing and selection, control operating philosophy, CAPEX, OPEX, etc.

Senior Plant Metallurgist

Compañia Minera Antamina

Mining & Metals industry

January 2001 – January 2004 (3 years 1 month)

The Antamina concentrator was designed to treat 70 kt/d of various complex ore types containing copper, zinc, molybdenum and bismuth. Production is in the order of 1 million tonnes of copper concentrate and 700,000 tonnes of zinc concentrate annually. Process equipment includes state of the art vari-speed SAG and ball mill grinding, three flotation stages, concentrate thickeners, and 300 km pipeline.

I was on the Commissioning Team that successfully started the concentrator in May 2001 – ahead of target and under budget. My role included directing operations staff to meet production targets and reduce costs, developing and managing a customised production accounting system, controlling all plant consumables, and collaborating with maintenance and mine operations. Also recruited and managed the metallurgical team and acted as Chief Metallurgist when required.

Senior Plant Metallurgist

Ok Tedi Mining Limited

Public Company; Mining & Metals industry

June 1994 – December 2000 (6 years 7 months)

The Ok Tedi concentrator treats 83 kt/d of blended porphyry, skarn, and siltstone ores to produce 600,000 tonnes of copper and gold concentrate annually. Process equipment includes SAG and ball mill grinding, flotation, and thickeners for concentrate and tailings.

My responsibilities included technical input towards ongoing concentrator operation, management of all plant consumables, networking with suppliers, short and long term strategic planning of circuit development and improvements, networking with mine operations staff for short and long term ore supply, management of all concentrator improvement projects.

The other side of my role was to manage the Metallurgical Team including development and training of the Papua New Guinean National staff. A key achievment was to introduce a program to rotate Metallurgists through the Operations area.

A major achievement was to initiate and manage a US$4million project to expand and reconfigure the flotation circuit, which enabled higher grade skarn ores to be processed with higher copper and gold recoveries.

Also initiated and designed an upgraded reagent handling area that improved safety, secured inventory, and decreased logistics costs.

Tested and evaluated OK tank and Jameson flotation cells, as well as gravity gold recovery process equipment.
