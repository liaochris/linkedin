Sue Rowe's Summary

Women in Business WA is a West Australian owned website designed to provide an online networking environment for WA business women, and enable them to showcase their products or services through directory listings, enewsletters and advertising.

Women in Business WA members have a common interest in offering a support network to other women sharing the same business goals. Women in Business WA creates an online platform and opportunity for other women to share, learn and be inspired by others.

As women are excellent communicators and networkers the www.womeninbusinesswa.com.au website is an excellent tool for business women.

Sue Rowe is the driving force behind Women in Business WA. Sue feels women ‘do business differently'. They draw inspiration from other women, and willingly share and learn from each other.

Because women are supportive of each other, Women in Business WA has evolved from a few connected women in 2004 to an online network of more than 800 business women in 2010.

Membership is open to all West Australian women who own or are employed in a legitimate business. Take advantage of our online membership special and Join Now

I'm Sue Rowe, the owner and editor of Women in Business WA.
Women In Business WA specialises in promoting and connecting women in business to other women in business through fortnightly newsletters, Coffee Club networking mornings and Cocktail functions.

The membership enables women to promote themselves, their business, events and workshops through the newsletters and online on the Women in Business WA website.

During my dealings with small businesses I realised a need for like minded women to be able to communicate and network with each other through a common forum. I invite you to share the articles and activities on Women in Business WA.

About Sue:

A career woman all her married life, Sue was born in Yuna, a farming community north east of Geraldton. She worked on a Morawa farm, a North Queensland cotton property and administrative roles in Kalgoorlie before settling in 1995. With husband Greg operated a transport business from 1995 to 2006. Greg is Sue's rock. He is a huge supporter of her business drive and an excellent person to deliver reality checks to the ever optimistic Sue.

Living life to the full is a given for this farmer's daughter, truckie's wife and mother of 3. Growing up on a farm, she is an old fashioned girl at heart who has learnt the lessons of self reliance and inner strength.

As a hockey, basketball and sprinter herself, she is really proud of Mitchell's sporting achievements. Daughter Natalie is travelling and living in the UK, Sue knows that she will achieve her goals and is very proud, 28 year old Shaun a qualified tradesman living in the north of the state and achieving excellent results.

Sue feels women ‘do business differently'. They draw inspiration from other women, and willingly share and learn from each other. Because women are supportive of each other, Women in Business WA has evolved from a few connected women in 2004 to more than 800 business women in 2010.
