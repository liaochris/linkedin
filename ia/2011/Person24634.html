Kim de Groot's Summary

Kim de Groot is a new media designer, teacher at the Willem de Kooning academie for Crosslab and researcher as part of the Imaginary property agency at the Jan van Eyck academie. Kim's research project deals with the inverted relation between image and reality. Moving from representation to the performative, from the visual to the infrastructural, images are no longer created to represent a reality but to manage it. Does the organizational role of images take over the significance of their visuality?

Lectoraat Communication in a Digital Age

Design industry

2009 – Present (2 years)

"Communication in a Digital Age" is the new research project ('lectoraat') on design and media of the Willem de Kooning Academy's Piet Zwart Institute. With a team consisting of lector Florian Cramer, visiting international research fellows, Ph.D. candidates, staffers and students of the Willem de Kooning Academy, it organizes lectures, conferences and creates publications on the future of communication design in relation to most current technological and social media developments - such as the challenge of print through online media ("print/pixel" conference in 2009), the shift from mass communication to user communities ("Viral Communication" conference in April 2010), and digital media cultures outside the Western mainstream.
