Brent Marcus's Summary

Brent Marcus has over 20 years of technology management experience. As a co-founder of Cyberspace Promotions, Inc. (CPI), he launched one of the earliest internet music ventures with Virtual Radio, a Music and Advertising Forum on the World-Wide-Web in 1994.

Since then, CPI has expanded into a Business Process Management Firm with partnerships with AT&T, IBM and Microsoft. Brent’s main responsibilities are in the areas of strategic planning, finance, operations and project management. Brent has overseen projects for a wide array of well know brand name companies including: CFC Capital Partners (Allianz), AutoByTel, Kawasaki, American Honda, O’Reilly Auto Parts, Sears, Pioneer Electronics, Cendant Software, Vans Shoes, Home Town Buffet, Pearl Izumi, and Columbia Sportswear.

Prior to CPI, Mr. Marcus served as Software Developer at Information Associates, leading their production of Accounting Software solutions in the Restaurant and Apparel Industries. He also served as Sr. Software Engineer at McDonnell Douglas – Guidance, Navigation and Control, Delta Program Division where he provided process improvement for their Delta Redundant Inertial Measurement System (DRIMS), converting to PC-based data collection and analysis.

Brent received a B.A. in Computer Science and Economics from the University of California, Berkeley in 1989 and is currently earning his MBA from the UCLA Anderson School of Management with an emphasis in Finance and Entrepreneurship.
