Research Scientist (Ecology)

Bookmark Biosphere Reserve (MAB program - UNESCO)

Nonprofit; 11-50 employees; Research industry

June 2002 – December 2002 (7 months)

The Bookmark Biosphere Reserve covers more than 900.000 hectares and preserves the natural native biodiversity of the floodplains of the Murray River in South Australia.
I was in charge of independently leading my research project. The precise tasks were the implementation of an infestation diagnosis of the vegetation of the park by an aerial vegetal parasite (Loranthaceae); the study of the biology of the identified mistletoe species; and the study and evaluation of the range of wildlife dependent on the mistletoes.
The final output of it was a dissertation redefining the actual role that is played by the mistletoes in the considered ecosystem. Following my dissertation defense in front of a MAB representative, I was invited to give a conference at the UNESCO in Paris for some of the MAB executives to highlight how ecological considerations such as mistletoe infestation can be used within economical and social issues.
