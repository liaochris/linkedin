Richard Brubaker's Summary

For the last 8 years, I have been living in China (Beijing and Shanghai). It has definitely been the most dynamic time of my life, and it is a place where I have found you really can do and be anything. I currently work on a number of projects that are for profit and non-profit ...

Like many who came to China, I originally came to China to seek out career opportunities as China’s economy gathered steam, and in pursuing those goals I developed market entry and distribution strategies for firms who were also looking for China based opportunities.

Soon after arriving in Shanghai, I developed my first community enterprise HandsOn Shanghai (now HandsOn China) as a means to promote, develop, and manage volunteerism in China. An organization that would open me up to the human side to the economic engine that was fueling China’s growth, and would ultimately lead to his development of further platforms in CSR and sustainability.

Now as Managing Director of Collective Responsibility and as a visiting Professor of Sustainability at the China Europe International Business School, I am focused full time on building platforms that promote long term organizational capacity to address the economic, environmental, and social hurdles that are faced.

It is a huge task, and I am fortunate to be surrounded by like minded people who are amazingly talented and motivated by going great things.

To learn more about my work in these areas, you can visit my other blog Cleaner Greener China (www.cleanergreenerchina.com) or the website of Collective Responsibility (www.collectiveresponsibility.org).
