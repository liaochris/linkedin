Eric Ives's Summary

Sgt. Eric Ives served over 4 years in the United States Marine Corps, conducting amphibious assault and security operations. Qualified by the USMC’s Special Operations Training Group, he was forward deployed with the 24th Marine Expeditionary Unit (Special Operations Capable) in 2001. During this time he trained with military units from Spain, England, France, Italy, and Greece, and also provided overseas security operations during 9/11/2001. In March 2003, he participated in Operation Iraqi Freedom with the 1st Marine Expeditionary Force involved in combat operations. Eric was in charge of the security of his units advance to Baghdad during the initial invasion. After receiving an honorable discharge, he spent another 7 years working in critical infrastructure protection for the private security industry. Eric has provided high risk security in over a dozen countries and throughout the United States, and continues to do so today. As a private security contractor he has provided anti-terrorism and security force protection operations at US nuclear facilities, and US military air stations and weapons storage instillations. He is highly experienced in the real world tactical application of small, medium and heavy weapons. He has been qualified to conduct operations involving the Tactical Rescue of Aircraft Personnel; Non-Combatant Evacuation Operations, Military Operations in Urban Terrain, Enemy Prisoner of War Handling, Low- Light Tactical Operations, Active Shooter Response, Immediate Action Rapid Deployment, Ship Board Active Shooter Response and Defense, and High Risk PSD operations. He is a certified OC Aerosol Instructor, Less-Lethal Munitions Instructor, Chemical Munitions Instructor, NFDD Instructor, Active Shooter Response Instructor, Firearms Instructor, NRA Firearms Instructor, Home Firearm Safety Instructor, Range Safety Officer, Defensive Folding Knife Instructor, Firearms Retention/Disarming/Recovery Instructor, and certified Beretta & GLOCK Armorer.
