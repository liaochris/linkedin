Tim Staney’s Summary

Tim Staney is a web applications developer living in St. Petersburg, Florida. Staney has worked for large corporations under the parent of Fidelity National Financial most notably Chicago Title, MicroGeneral Corporation, RealEC, LSI and Fidelity National Information Services' Office of the Enterprise—building database-driven web and rich-media applications on a variety of platforms.

Staney has also completed many independent projects for Aeronex, GSP Marketing Technologies, the University of South Florida and highly trafficked web sites like Crains Communications' AutoWeek and AutoNews, and Canada's largest online daily newspaper, Cyberpresse and the LaPresse Affaires Business e-Zine.

In addition to his corporate work, Tim has volunteered his web development expertise to the Worcester Pride Committee, AIDS Project Worcester, The Freedom to Marry Coalition, Equality Florida and the Barack Obama campaign's Florida Data Team in 2008.

Tim has been featured in 12-Volt News and “Who's Who” in the Tampa Bay Business Journal as Person-of-the-Week in May of 2006. Tim has attended many conferences on web futures at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology and is a frequent attendee of Microsoft-sponsored “Code Camps” and other training events in the Tampa Bay area.

In 2009 and 2010, Tim helped launch the Internet strategy and websites for Wonderland the Musical which opens on Broadway soon.

“As media converge – Internet , TV and mobile device – there has never been a more exciting time to be involved in new media design and development. I’m thrilled to be a part of that,” said Staney in 12-Volt News (Tuesday, 10 July 2007).

Tim Staney’s Specialties:

ASP, PHP, ColdFusion, ASP.NET (C#), .NET Web Services, Flash/ActionScript, SQL Server, Crystal Reports Enterprise, Adobe Flex, Omniture SiteCatalyst, Tessitura Web API, a Kentico CMS Partner and member of the Microsoft Website Spark programs.
