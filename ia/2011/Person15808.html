    2005 — 2008 (3 years )

Oversaw sale of Computer Shopper Magazine to SX2 and numerous acquisitions of domestic and international businesses.
    Replaced finance leadership team, including Chief Accounting Officer. Restructured finance group to create a central FP&A team, and optimize IT and Tax organizations to yield increased responsiveness and effectiveness. Replaced Auditors KPMG with PWC.
    Established financial and operating control processes. Co-sourced internal audit function to remediate deficiencies contributing to prior 2004 material weakness; closed 2005 fiscal year and subsequent periods without material weakness. Oversaw all SEC reporting, international working capital management, SOX compliance and remediation, and transactional accounting for acquisitions and dispositions.

  • COO International Division

    Gap Inc.

    (Public Company; GPS; Retail industry)

    1997 — 2005 (8 years )

    I worked with Gap Inc. from 1997 to 2005 in a variety of leadership positions. I served as CFO of the Banana Republic division, and as the SVP of Corporate Finance. My last position with Gap Inc. was COO of the International division.

    COO Responsibilities:
    Lead a 267-member team, responsible for planning and operational management of $1.6B international business in France, UK and Japan. Primary focus was to transform international business from a centralized organization to an in-market operation.
    Created the infrastructure and operating plan to launch Banana Republic brand in Japan.
    Established a production group dedicated to sourcing of international product; integrated production function with product design group for comprehensive product life cycle management; developed strategic alliances with European vendors to provide rapid-response capabilities.
    Repurposed international trademark protection group to explore joint-venture opportunities; managed disposition of stores in the German market.
    Revised processes and established new planning practices to enable localized inventory investment with centralized governance; established in-country merchandising and planning teams.
    Oversaw distribution and logistics functions; built centralized organization with capacity to manage expansion. Established demand-based logical distribution to minimize unproductive inventory.
    Replaced Divisional CFO and rebalanced European and Japanese Finance teams to optimize performance; reorganized centralized San Francisco group to work more effectively with in-country teams, provide better planning expertise to senior management, and support Sarbanes Oxley requirements.
