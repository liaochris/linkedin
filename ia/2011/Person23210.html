Jasper Van der Bruggen’s Experience

  • Sr GIS Analyst

    Calera Corporation

    (Privately Held; Renewables & Environment industry)

    April 2010 — Present (11 months)

    As the lead GIS analyst at this fast paced Silicon Valley start up I use ArcGIS10 as a powerful tool for site screening and site evaluation activities. Analyses include natural and industrial feedstock (liquid and solid) relevant to Calera’s processes, e.g. groundwater, ores and gangue, and industrial waste streams.

    I performed a study on the global, regional and local potential ("prospectivity") of traditional CCS versus an innovative Calera-developed carbon geo-sequestration scheme. I gathered data on (complexity of) sedimentary basins, porosity, permeability, cap rock; sources included DOE/NETL, IPCC, Natcarb and oil & gas industry. I generated total potential sequestration capacity (Gt), pore volumes, depths, thickness, etc, and maps.

    I implemented MetaCarta – a geosearch and geotagging engine – to quickly analyze the relevancy of thousands of (scanned) documents; this also included integration with ArcGIS and Google Earth.

    Additionally, I double as the lead hydrogeologist, and I perform groundwater flow and transport modeling (MODFLOW) for site selection and well field design. I collect and analyze the hydrogeologic data required (from USGS, state GS, oil and gas industry, scientific papers etc). I use ArcGIS and Hydro Geobuilder for conceptual model building and preprocessing, and Visual MODFLOW as the user interface for numerical modeling.

    More key works: QA/QC spatial data; ArcGIS; KML and kmz: import/export ArcGIS-Google Earth and Google Maps; focus on natural resources and energy industry; data mining; data analysis and data presentation. Projects in US, China, Australia and EU.
