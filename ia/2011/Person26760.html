Lon Welsh's Summary

Background: Eight years in strategy consulting at Deloitte and Touche, then Accenture (Arthur Andersen). This taught me how to lead teams to develop breakthrough strategic approaches to gain market share. This experience underpins our current efforts at YCRE.

Objective: Our objective is not to be the largest real estate firm, but rather to be the real estate thought leader in our marketplace. We are the third largest independent brokerage in the Denver metro. In 2009, the overall market was down about 16% in transaction volume. We were UP 29%. As of July 2010, we are up +127% vs. same period 2009. It's only a bad market if you are focusing on the wrong segments.

What we're up to: We've developed a real estate valuation engine - give it an address, and it will tell you what the property is worth. Unlike Zillow, it's based on sold comps from the MLS, which is what a licensed appraiser uses to value a home. We can use this technology to develop price opinions for every active listing on the market, and determine which are overpriced and which represent excellent values for our clients. The 250+ Realtors who work for YCRE use this as a significant competitive advantage for attracting new prospects and helping clients capture value in the marketplace.

We publish retrospective trends analyses in many Colorado Real Estate Journals to increase the firm's profile in the industry. Prospective forecasts are used primarily for clients interested in properties that will outperform the market in appreciation.

Finally, my second book "Thrive - How to Succeed in a Down Market" has been instrumental in helping me help Realtors re-design their business plans and re-start their careers. Helping people succeed is the best part of my job. I just released my third book, "Unlocked - Revealing the Eight Secrets of Highly Efficient Sales Professionals." We have eight workshops - one for each secret. Let me know if you have interest.
