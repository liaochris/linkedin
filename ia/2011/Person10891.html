David Lytel's Summary

I am an experienced digital media professional with outstanding creative, analytic and management skills. I have the intellectual agility to manage people and projects successfully.

As Vice President of Campaign Strategies at public affairs agency E-Advocates I provided digital strategies to PBS, NPR, XM/SIRIUS, AARP and others. This involved prospecting, presenting, proposal writing and contracting. I supervised the execution of integrated marketing initiatives using Web sites, e-mail marketing, online communities and paid advertising.

As Vice President and Chief Internet Strategist for Mental Health America I was responsible for the organization's public presence and messaging on the Internet.

Much of my experience has been in the political sphere. Redefeat Bush engaged volunteers in voter contact activities. I produced animations, cartoons, videos and avatars for our very successful viral marketing and social networking outreach. While with Democrats.com I provided Internet campaign strategies and services for Senators Wellstone, Edwards, Corzine, the DCCC and others.

I served in the Office of Science and Technology Policy in the Clinton White House. I wrote for Vice President Gore and worked on Internet policy. I was co-developer of the award-winning White House Web site.

I hold an M.S. and Ph.D. in political communication from Cornell. I also have a B.A. with honors from Brown and an M.S. in corporate communications from Ithaca College. I had a post-doctoral appointment at Harvard and taught public policy at Georgetown.

Most recently I worked as a consultant at MITRE Corporation leading a Web site rebranding and reconstruction project, including a refocusing of the organization’s message and audience.

I am an active listener, adept at understanding clients and potential clients. My quantitative skills are outstanding. I am a well-organized, focused and conscientious manager and leader.

Specialties

Online political organizing and fund raising, Internet-enabled business development including marketing, e-mail list development, customer acquisition and digital product development.

Information Infrastructure Specialist

White House Office of Science and Technology Policy

Government Agency; 1001-5000 employees; Government Administration industry

1993 – 1996 (3 years)

After a post-doctoral appointment at Harvard’s Kennedy School of Government I joined the Clinton Administration in the White House Office of Science and Technology Policy. At the start I managed the inter-agency Information Infrastructure Task Force, ghost wrote for Vice President Gore, prepared Congressional testimony and worked generally on the commercialization and privatization of the Internet. In 1994 I argued within the White House that the best way to legitimize and validate the Internet as a place for commerce and democracy was to build a large scale Web site. Out of this was born the first White House Web site (1994) and its version 2.0 (1996). Called “easily one of the best sites on the Net” by Hotwired, the site was innovative for its use of image mapping and the first guide to government services.
