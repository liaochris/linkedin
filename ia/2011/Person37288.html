Robert Matelski’s Summary

I am currently a 1st-year MBA student at The Johnson School at Cornell University, where I am pursuing the Sustainable Global Enterprise Immersion (a unique semester-long practicum that integrates economic and environmental factors into strategic planning concepts within the context of a real project for an established business). Prior to business school, I spent over five years rising through the ranks in the architectural field, where I was managing clients as well as junior staff and consultants. My architectural experience and expertise includes historic preservation, green building, higher education, healthcare, and retail design. I am a licensed California Architect and a LEED Accredited Professional. Most of my responsibilities as an Architect were highly project-based, and required me to analyze problems, develop strategies, and see the implementation of solutions through to completion. As an Architect, my work has consistently required both a high-level understanding of the overall project and a detail-oriented focus on many individual aspects of the project.

Strategy, Marketing, Sustainability, Real Estate, Architecture, and Historic Preservation.
