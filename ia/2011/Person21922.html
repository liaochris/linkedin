John Ferreira is a Registered Shiatsu Therapist and the President of the Shiatsu Therapy Association of British Columbia.

Shiatsu is a traditional hands-on Japanese therapy. It is known as acupuncture without needles. As the manual form of acupuncture, Shiatsu employs manual static pressure by using thumbs, palms, fingers, and sometimes elbows and knees to prescribed points and zones to both assess and treat those acupuncture points and the meridian networks that connect them to engage the body’s own healing powers through a reflexive feedback loop between the musculo-skeletal system and the internal organs.
