Dr. Will Courtenay's Summary

Dr. Courtenay is a consultant, psychotherapist, distinguished author, researcher, speaker, and radio host. He is considered one of the world's leading experts - and innovators - in the field of men's health. He has a documented history of success in shaping and promoting this new field, as well as new perspectives on fatherhood, boyhood, and masculinity.

♦ Author of the book, "Dying To Be Men: Psychosocial, Environmental and Biobehavioral Directions in Promoting the Health of Men and Boys,” and many journal articles and book chapters.

♦ Served as clinical faculty member in the Department of Psychiatry at Harvard Medical School, and University of California, San Francisco, Medical School.

♦ Researcher for 20 years, producing ground-breaking findings about men, masculinity and men’s health.

♦ Founding Editor of the first professional journal of men’s health.

♦ Developer of evidence-based interventions for improving the health of men and boys, and for reducing their disproportionate health care costs.

♦ Consultant to medical and public health professionals, social scientists, and government agencies, providing psychosocial, environmental, and biobehavioral approaches to understanding men’s health.

♦ Trainer of hundreds of employee wellness staff in effectively reducing male employees' preventable health care costs.

The American Psychological Association calls Dr. Courtenay, “a leading psychologist in the field of masculinity” and “one of the leading scholars, researchers, and public policy shapers in the psychology of men.”

Dr. Courtenay is a much sought after speaker and media resource. He’s a powerful, effective voice about men's health and the changing roles of fathers, boys, and men, heard nationally on radio and television – including CNN, Good Morning America, World News, ABC, NBC – and seen in print – including NY Times, LA Times, Wall Street Journal, U.S. News & World Report, NPR, Newsweek, USA Today, and Chicago Tribune.

Dr. Will Courtenay's Experience

Founder & Director

Men's Health Consulting

Health, Wellness and Fitness industry

March 1993 – Present (18 years 1 month)

Founded and serve as Director of the oldest educational and consulting firm promoting better health in men and boys. Provide continuing education training to health professionals nationwide, consult with colleges and universities, government agencies, public health departments, family planning clinics, industry, corporations, and medical centers, and conduct employee wellness workshops. Develop evidence-based materials and services that address how psychological, behavioral, and social factors – including beliefs about masculinity – influencing the health risks of various populations of men and boys. Foster multidisciplinary dialogue and interdisciplinary and collaborative work and research among health professionals.

Researcher & Author

Independent Social Scientist

Health, Wellness and Fitness industry

February 1993 – Present (18 years 2 months)

Conduct research on the subjects of men and boys, masculinity, men’s health, fathers, and fatherhood. Published work includes over 40 journal articles and book chapters, which are referenced by authors of nearly 1,400 other publications. Dr. Courtenay’s current research is being conducted in collaboration with the Center for Men and Young Men at McLean Hospital, Harvard Medical School, and is examining the experiences of over 3,000 fathers with babies. He is particularly interested in postpartum depression in new fathers and understanding its potential underlying causes.

Dr. Courtenay was recipient of the “Researcher of The Year” award from the American Psychological Association for published work and research on masculinity and men’s health. Many of his original works have proven to be influential and foundational, as noted by one leading scholar: “Hegemonic masculinity is often identified as the reason why men tend to die at younger ages than women in western societies. Will Courtenay’s 2000 article discussing constructions of masculinity and their implications for men’s health was seminal in developing this line of argument.” — From Editorial by Ellen Annandale, in the Social Science & Medicine (Journal) 2007 special issue, “Gender & Health.”

Founder

Several Web Sites for Men, Including SadDaddy.com and Mens-Depression.com

Internet industry

January 1993 – Present (18 years 3 months)

Founded several cutting-edge web sites for men, including MensHealth.org, Mens-Depression.com, and most recently SadDaddy.com - which is web site for men with concerns about depression, anxiety or other problems with mood after the birth of a child. SadDaddy.com, or PostpartumMen, promotes self-help, provides important information for fathers – including a self-assessment for postpartum depression, hosts an online forum for men to talk to each other, offers resources, gathers new information about men’s experiences postpartum, and most importantly, helps fathers to beat the baby blues.
