Karin Straus's Summary

Royal DSM N.V. creates innovative solutions in Life Sciences and Materials Sciences that contribute to the quality of life. DSM’s products are used globally in a wide range of markets and applications, supporting a healthier, more sustainable and more enjoyable way of life. End markets include human and animal nutrition and health, personal care, pharmaceuticals, automotive, coatings , electrical and electronics, life protection and housing. DSM has annual net sales of EUR 9.3 billion and employs some 23,500 people worldwide. The company is headquartered in the Netherlands, with locations on five continents.

In my current role I am responsible for company wide organisational development projects and consultancy assignments:
• Design, launch, project management and content expert for the worldwide DSM employee engagement survey (40 countries, 17 languages, 25 project team members). The DSM Employee engagement survey is positioned as a ‘GPS for change’. Assisting Management worldwide in translating survey results aligning them to business strategy and into local action plans.
• Organisational diagnosis projects related to the ‘revamping’ of strategy, identification of focus areas, organisational gap analysis, offshoring, divestments, mergers and/or aquisitions.
• HR Functional Excellence activities aimed at career management and development of HR potentials
• Projects and activities related to the People aspects in DSM’s Sustainability policy (human rights, business ethics, diversity, employee engagement/social responsibility programs, HR sustainability reporting (GRI/A+), sustainability benchmarking (Dow Jones Sustainability Index, FTSE4Good).

Next to this, experienced in local and regional politics as a elected local council representative and board positions in (semi)government institutions.
If you are interested in my activities in local politics, please check my Dutch LinkedIn profile for more information.

Specialties

HR Business partner, Talent development, Strategic HRM, Resourcing, Change Management, Mergers & Acquisitions, Organisational development, Employee Engagement, local and regional politics.
