Dave Rockenbaugh’s Summary

It's all jazz.

When I was a struggling 7th grade geek of a bassoon player, my private teacher, Mr. Don Van Doren, warned that the only way I was going to get good was to practice. "rightsureuhuh." He told me the great Charlie Parker (whom we both admired) became brilliant only after he had every scale—Dorian, Phrygian, whatever—under his fingers. He practiced for hours and hours. Just scales. I said, "big deal. It's because he could make stuff up; that's why he was creative." Mr. Van Doren said Charlie needed to know what notes were expected to be played with each key and chord in a piece of music, so he could play the unexpected. Know the rules and then break, or bend, them.

Well, that’s my lesson for today. You can’t be creative without practice. So go. Read about Rosser Reeves and Lester Wunderman. Dig through old CAs and reconstruct the strategies for each campaign. Take a course in improv. Listen to people who buy your products. Listen to them again. Scribble things down on a pad of paper. Live and remember it.

With that, you can then turn advertising into jazz: fresh, unpredictable, lyrical and yet, logical. (A flat six chord should be followed by the subdominant. You gotta close with a call to action.) Working with your art director and strategist and client is then like being in a jazz combo. You give, you take, you build off each others’ riffs. If you listen and share, it leads to something your audience will want to respond to. With more than just applause.
