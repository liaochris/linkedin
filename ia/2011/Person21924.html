John J. "SKI" Sygielski’s Summary

John J. “Ski” Sygielski serves as the sixth president of Mt. Hood Community College (MHCC), Oregon's fourth largest community college, and the chairman of the American Association of Community Colleges Board of Directors.

Dr. Ski is an author, speaker, university lecturer and community college adjunct credit and non-credit business faculty member. A native of Cleveland, Ohio, Dr. Ski is the only member of his working-class family to graduate from college. A biking enthusiast, he currently lives in Gresham, Ore.

John J. "SKI" Sygielski’s Experience

    Mt. Hood Community College

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    July 2008 — Present (2 years 7 months)

    Mt. Hood Community College is a multi-campus two-year public comprehensive community college serving a three county-region east of the City of Portland with a population of more than 350,000. The service region’s character ranges from inner city to commercial, retail and industrial parks to diversified agriculture and horticulture. The two campuses and one center serve over 31,000 credit and non-credit students annually through more than 1,000 full- and part-time employees. I report to a seven member elected board.

  • President

    Lord Fairfax Community College

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    July 2003 — July 2008 (5 years 1 month)

    Lord Fairfax Community College is a multi-campus two-year public comprehensive community college serving eight localities in the Shenandoah Valley and Piedmont region of northwest Virginia with a population of more than 450,000. The service region’s character ranges from diversified agriculture and horticulture to federal government installations, commercial, retail and industrial parks. The two campuses and one center serve more than 7,500 credit and 8,000 non-credit students annually through more than 450 full-time and part-time employees with an annual operating budget of $22 million. I reported to a fourteen member board.

  • Vice Chancellor, Workforce Education

    Virginia Community College System

    (Educational Institution; 5001-10,000 employees; Education Management industry)

    July 1999 — August 2003 (4 years 2 months)

    Virginia Community College System (VCCS) includes 23 Community Colleges on 40 campuses located throughout the Commonwealth of Virginia, serving the equivalent of 90,000 full-time students representing 250,000 individuals enrolling in one or more credit courses. Another 150,000 Virginians and over 4,000 businesses are served annually through workforce training programs. I was the first vice chancellor in this role and reported directly to the Chancellor.

    I also taught as an adjunct business instructor at John Tyler Community College (Richmond).

  • Director, Economic and Workforce Development

    College of DuPage

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    February 1990 — June 1999 (9 years 5 months)

    College of DuPage (C.O.D.) is a multi-campus two-year public comprehensive community college west of Chicago serving more than 30,000 credit students and 60,000 non-credit students in a two-county suburban region with more than 1,000,000 residents and 30,000 businesses. The Business and Professional Institute (BPI) at College of DuPage provides businesses and organizations with assistance and training support through customized contract training, open enrollment programs and business assistance programs. Throughout my career at College of DuPage, I reported to a director, dean, vice president and president.

    I also taught as a credit and non-credit adjunct business and software instructor at the College.

  • Director, BPI

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

He and his team developed the Suburban Law Enforcement Academy, significantly increased community and business outreach training activities, developed and implemented new classes and programs, taught credit and non-credit business courses, received numerous federal and state grant awards, participated in the exploration of developing a community college in Bangalore, India and served on a variety of state, regional and local committees.

  • Director, Corporate Training

    (Computer Hardware industry)

    1986 — 1990 (4 years )

    Wang Laboratories, Inc. was a global network and desktop integrating and services company with nearly $1.3 billion in annual revenues. Once at the vanguard of the minicomputer boom, Wang now concentrates its efforts on maintenance and software support, service and help desk functions and installation and management of global computing and telecommunication networks for large, multinational companies and military and civilian government agencies.
