Christopher Paradies’s Summary

Christopher Paradies, Ph.D. focuses his entire legal practice in the areas of intellectual property law, biotechnology, nanotechnology, the life sciences and healthcare. His practice team includes attorneys and paralegals trained to assist clients in intellectual property, federal regulatory approvals and resolving disputes in state and federal courts and before administrative agencies. He has represented clients in complex copyright, trademark, patent and federal regulatory matters and disputes, and works with clients to increase the value of their intellectual capital and to reduce their litigation risks.

Representative Legal Experience

Sams v. Ultimate Video Showcase, represented defendant in settlement of copyright infringement litigation in the Western District of Pennsylvania.

Verilux v. OttLite Technologies, represented defendant in successful transfer of patent litigation from the District of Connecticut to the Middle District of Florida.

GF v. Cepex, et al. Middle District of Florida, represented defendant in settlement of patent infringement litigation. Lead litigator and negotiated settlement.

Pump & Brush Finland Oy v. Philips Oral Healthcare Inc., et al., Middle District of Florida, represented plaintiff in settlement of patent infringement claims. Lead litigator and negotiated settlement.

Represented nanotechnology company in development and patenting of intellectual property portfolio and technology transfer and licensing agreement with global building materials manufacturer.

Lead patent counsel and lead negotiator for development, technology transfer and commercialization of technology during a five-year research, development and commercialization program.
