Monica Fineis's Summary

Monica joined Martin Waymire Advocacy Communications in May 2008 as an intern, but quickly earned respect for her understanding of public relations tactics and work ethic. She joined the staff full-time after receiving her Bachelor of Arts in advertising with a specialization in public relations from Michigan State University in 2009.

Notably, Monica played an instrumental role in leading a large-scale community support effort for MSU's bid for a science research laboratory called the Facility for Rare Isotope Beams (FRIB) in 2008. She coined the slogan "Bring FRIB to our Crib!" and organized a "FRIB Frenzy" event that brought national exposure to the project. Her grassroots efforts were further recognized when the campaign became a national PRSA Silver Anvil finalist in 2009 along with winning several regional awards.

Monica’s fluency in social media and creative analytical thinking helps engage key audiences for a variety of clients which include associations, educational institutions, non-profits, health care organizations and casinos. By keeping on top of the latest social media marketing trends and being an influencer on many of the platforms herself, she earned her role as a facilitator for the company’s social media trainings and leads program execution to help clients breakthrough in the digital space.

After work hours, Monica builds relationships as the New Professionals chair for the Central Michigan Public Relations Society of America chapter and as the Communications chair for Grand River Connection — the state's largest young professionals networking group.

As an undergraduate, she was appointed director of a national public relations student campaign competition where she led MSU's team to a second place finish out of nearly 80 teams across the U.S.

Specialties

social media community management, copywriting, grassroots advocacy, messaging, strategy development, event planning, media writing, media relations
