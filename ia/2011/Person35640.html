  • PhD student (OIO, Onderzoeker in Opleiding)

    Netherlands Cancer Institute / Antoni van Leeuwenhoek hospital (NKI/AvL)

    (Non-Profit; Research industry)

    May 1996 — December 1997 (1 year 8 months)

    Performing research aimed at identifying the requirements for T-cell activation, specifically the development of cytotoxic T cells directed against tumor associated antigens. Immunogenic (lymphoma) as well as non-immunogenic (melanoma) tumor models were adopted to test the role of adhesion and costimulation molecules in T-cell activation in vitro as well as in vivo. A new costimulation molecule was identified which, upon introduction into tumor cells as a vaccin, boosted the immune response and confered long lasting protective immunity in mice. The magnitude of possible auto-immune reactions (when tolerance to tumor associated antigens is broken) was analyzed using a specific lymphoma model together with a panel of transgenic mice.

    Following extended courses on immunology and carcinogenesis in the "Onderzoeksschool Oncologie Amsterdam'.
