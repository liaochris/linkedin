Ken Yagelski’s Summary

Kenneth W. Yagelski provides operations, strategic, and regulatory support to the energy industry. He has over twenty-five years of direct experience in natural gas and electric utility management in the areas of system and resource planning, contracting, sourcing, asset optimization, engineering, and local and federal regulatory matters. Ken's career has spanned three decades critical to the evolution of the natural gas industry. He has established policy for adopting interstate pipeline deregulation (1980s), created competitive retail service programs (1990s), and changed the utility’s capacity cost management function into a revenue center through asset optimization (2000s). Mr. Yagelski has been an expert witness in several regulatory proceedings and government hearings, a popular guest instructor for industry associations, and a speaker at energy industry events.

Throughout his career, Ken has held various leadership and committee positions with professional and community organizations, such as the American Gas Association (AGA), the North American Energy Standards Board (NAESB), the Institute of Electrical and Electronic Engineers (IEEE) and the Metropolitan Washington Council of Governments (COG).
