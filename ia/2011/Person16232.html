Gregory Richey’s Summary

I'm a veteran C-level digital media entrepreneur with the scars and graying hair to prove it. Before joining Brushfire as its CEO, I was COO of Chrysalis Ventures, a $400MM capital venture fund. I've worked for and with venture-funded, interactive media companies for over 17 years--hence the graying hair.

Before going into venture capital, I was COO and partner of Butler, Shine, Stern & Partners, one of the leading independent ad agencies on the West Coast. At BSSP, I helped manage the company through several years of growth at an average rate of over 30%. During that time, we added major accounts, including Mini, Converse and Priceline. I got into the agency business when the venture fund William E. Simon & Sons brought me in as COO/CFO to manage their $10MM investment in SF Interactive, one of the pioneers of interactive advertising. After guiding SFI's finances through the carnage of the dot.com crash, I then helped engineer a successful merger with BSSP.

In the pre dot.com crash days, I played a key role in building and financing several other early digital media companies, including Paycom, an ecommerce payment processing and Web development business; and RNet Health, a television and interactive media company that partnered with cable giant TCI to bring health programming to a multimedia market. I started my professional career as a Wall Street corporate tax attorney with O'Melveny & Myers.

Gregory Richey’s Specialties:

C-level entrepreneur. Extensive experience with building early stage digital media companies, including operations, fund-raising, finance, HR, production, marketing and PR, IT and, of course, legal. I am accustomed to working with venture capitalists, bankers, and lawyers as well as creatives, engineers, and sales and marketing professionals. Not to mention dealing with landlords, contractors and the guy that fixes the copier--all the stuff that needs to get done in early stage companies.
