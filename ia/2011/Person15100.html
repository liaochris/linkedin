Frank Corridori's Summary

I've worked as a creative professional in some capacity for as long as I can remember. My more prominent roles have been that of Senior Art Director and Lead Designer in both Agency and In-House environments. My responsibilities have included generating strategic marketing materials and advertising campaigns; as well as cohesive brand strategy and creative campaigns across a wide range of media for a diverse set of prominent local and national clientele. I have often supplemented my career working as a Creative Consultant (design, marketing, identity, and web), Photographer, Illustrator, Writer, Director, and sometimes Web Designer. These skills have often been handy and complimentary to my main professional pursuits. I am a conceptual thinker that has often been called upon to visualize large, cohesive campaigns; generate strategic marketing solutions; and write, storyboard, and direct motion and video concepts. I started in the trenches, and have strong technical knowledge and ability in both print and web. I always have my eyes and mind set on producing staggering creative work that will not only stand out on a national stage, but get results as well.

Specialties

Advertising + Design, Marketing, Branding + Identity, Photography, Illustration, Print, Web + New Media (Flash). A student of, and expert in pop-culture and youth culture. I am an unquenchable student of media and communication in an ever-evolving landscape.

Adjunct Faculty / Instructor

University of Missouri

Educational Institution; Higher Education industry

January 2010 – Present (1 year 3 months)

Strategic Design & Visuals II

Taught and developed syllabus and coursework for the second-level Strategic Design class as part of the Advertising program and curriculum at the prestigious MU School of Journalism. I worked closely with graduating Seniors to push and improve their work, and prepare them for the next steps in their creative careers including the interviewing process. Coursework that I developed for the syllabus centered around creating three integrated creative campaigns across the media spectrum over the course of the semester. Students were able to choose from three progressive, innovative companies and products. They developed creative work for their chosen company in print, interactive, video, new media, and guerilla components. Students honed their creative process through immersion in a close-to-life experience complete with creative briefs. The end goal was to not only have students walk away with a better understanding of the process of working with a real-life client, but to also have three complete integrated campaigns which they could include in their portfolios.

Interests:
