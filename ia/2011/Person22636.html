Jonathan Armstrong's Summary

Jonathan Armstrong is a lawyer who specialises in counselling multinational clients on their risk and compliance across Europe. Recent projects include advising a number of retailers in moving sales online or entering new markets, advising on the launch of innovative new products and services, ethics and compliance code implementation, internal investigations, security breach, emerging technology, consumer marketing, branding, corporate governance, online reputation and on pan-European privacy policies. Clients include Fortune 250 organisations as well as household names in manufacturing and financial services in Europe.

A regular broadcaster, Jonathan has also written articles on technology and compliance related topics. Jonathan is a Fellow of The Chartered Institute of Marketing; Vice-Chair of the New York State Bar Association International Section and was formerly a member of the Advisory Board of the Georgetown University Advanced eCommerce Institute, Washington DC.

Jonathan has spoken at conferences in the US, China and across Europe. His presentations have included sessions with the American Bar Association, New York State Bar Association and PLI. In 2004 he was a guest speaker at the International Information Integrity Institute conference in The Hague. In 2005 he was a keynote speaker at the EC funded ‘Personal Data Protection: Policy and Practice in the EU Accession and New Member States’ conference in Sofia. In 2006 he was a guest speaker at the W3C conference - the so-called 'Olympics of the Internet'.

In addition Jonathan has been involved in the development of a number of technology applications and has twice been a Regional Finalist in the UK Goverment dti/ISI Awards for Innovation in e-commerce.

Specialties

Qualified as a solicitor in the UK in 1991; specialised in technology, risk & governance since 1996. The 2010 Legal Experts Report again featured Jonathan as one of the UK’s leading experts in the field. Jonathan’s practice is international. One of the 3 co-authors of the LexisNexis definitive work on technology law ‘Managing Risk: Technology & Communications’.

  • German Privacy Regulators Issue Decision on Data Protection and Safe-Harbor Self-Certification of US Companies

    • Martindale
    • June 7, 2010
    Authors: Jonathan Armstrong

    A recent decision of German regulators may make compliance with privacy protections more challenging for US businesses doing business in Germany. Germany operates a regional system of data privacy regulation where each Land (or state) appoints its own regulator for the private sector. Those regulators try to adopt a common stance on issues affecting Germany through an informal organization known as the Düsseldorfer Kreis.

  • Information - the new trade war?

    • Managing Partner
    • June 3, 2009
    Authors: Jonathan Armstrong

    Spring is always a time to take stock of the year just gone and make predictions for the year ahead. For most of us we were happier than usual to say farewell to 2008 - the tougher task was to predict the big compliance issues of 2009. We looked at issues caused by the global financial plight and some of the side effects, including the increasing extra-territorial reach of the US authorities, the perceived sprawl of e-discovery to European soil and greater opposition to both of these developments by European data-protection authorities. In general in all of these areas Europeans see the US as wanting too much information - litigation techniques like wide discovery and litigation holds are simply too rich for the European palate. At the same time the US authorities have been ever-anxious to regulate US corporations (and others doing business with the US) in all of their activities around the world. Companies both here and abroad - and their lawyers - are likely to get caught in this perfect storm.
