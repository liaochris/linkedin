Event Manager, Coordinator

Hat Fair Festival of Street Theatre

Performing Arts industry

September 1990 – July 1997 (6 years 11 months)

"One of the great pleasures of street arts festivals is that they make you slow down and put the wonder back into everyday life. Wherever you were in Winchester at this years' Hat Fair, you could hear the distant sound of laughter – as if the whole town was smiling together."

Hat Fair is Britain's longest-running festival of Street Theatre, founded in 1974 by Jonathan Kay. Always highly-creative, original and ground-breaking, Hat Fair continues to delight and inspire tens of thousands of people every year with top quality Street Theatre and outdoor entertainment.

As one of the members of Attic Theatre I took part in, and helped to run Hat Fair for decades. From 1991 - 1997 I was its Coordinator; responsible for all aspects of the event including the artistic programme, marketing and branding, budget management and liaison with funding bodies and sponsors.

In the late eighties, Hat Fair had suffered a crisis of funding and reputation. Under my coordination, the festival began to consolidate, and benefit properly from, its unique brand identity: building strong, professional relationships with the community, businesses, local authorities and Arts funding bodies.

Over 6 years, through improved branding, relationship-building and marketing, I re-established the Hat Fair’s identity as an International Arts event and local cultural treasure, and doubled its turnover in sponsorship and funding.
