Cynthia DuVal’s Summary

My professional experience includes working as an artist, curator and teacher/mentor and as an applied design ethnographer in think tank, advanced technology and product design and manufacturing organizations.

I've worked for or with the following organizations: Xerox Palo Alto Research Center, Interval Research, Apple Advanced Technology, Microsoft Mobile Electronics and Entertainment, Verizon Laboratories, IBM Software Group, Radius Product Design, Transitioning to a Viable Society DesignShop, e426 Innovation Corps for America, Strategy and Design Solutions among others.

I study people and how people work, think and interact in cultural historical context as new ideas and technologies emerge and change the way we think and behave. I use a process that enables me to envision and create alighnment between the programs, processes and products we design and the people and organizations that use them.

I'm a change agent and DuVal Ethnographic is a change agency.

My goals are to apply ethnographic research and ethnographic films to innovation discovery and social change goals and to achieve entrepreneurial, educational and social change outcomes that are at once good for people, good for business and good for the world.

I help my clients identify and communicate change goals and lead and mentor others in the design and implementation of strategic ethnographic research initiatives. I also run research programs I've identified as having high potential to improve sustainable living outcomes through entrepreneurial, educational and social change interventions.

I invite you to phone me at 360-366-0327 and see http://www.duval-ethno-graphic.com to learn more about DuVal Ethnographic research, educational, design and entrepreneurial projects, strategic planning facilitation and mentoring consulting services and visual media productions created by the DuVal Ethnographic Research Center and Change Agency network of creative professionals.

Cynthia DuVal’s Specialties:

change facilitation, applied ethnography, understanding cultural work groups, program, process and product design, emerging technologies, sustainable living, local food economies, collective thinking facilitation, on-line identity, entrepreneurship, cultural and cognitive change, aesthetics

    Rock Hollow Studios

    (Internet industry)

    July 2005 — January 2009 (3 years 7 months)

    ---Rock Hollow Studios is currently transitioning to a new business model and entity: Pacific Ethnographic Research Center. Rock Hollow Studios provided independent ethnographic research consulting. PERC will have a different scope of work and include research, education, advocacy and entrepreneurial activities. Too often applied ethnographic work fails to have the impact that it should for many reasons. One of these is that compelling research findings are not applicable to the direct goals of corporate project sponsors. Complicating this is the fact that when innovation opportunities are discovered the development of those opportunities requires cross-company, cross industry and/or multi-national collective action and the economic and social networks aren't in place to drive these opportunities forward. PERC will work on these difficult problems by housing a think tank dedicated to the development of economically viable ethnographic research collectives with social entrepreneurship and cultural change and preservation goals.
