Ann Brauer’s Summary

Nationally recognized contemporary art quiltmaker that uses color, fabric and the traditions of quilt making to create distinctive abstract landscapes of my imagination.

My quilts will be in upcoming shows at the National Quilt Museum in Paducah, KY and the OSilas Gallery in Bronxville, NY. My work is in numerous public and private collections including includes a series of quilts for the new Federal District Courthouse in Springfield, MA through the GSA'a Art in Architecture program, the Lodge at Turning Stone in Verona, NY and the Surdna Foundation in New York, NY. In 2006 I received an Award of Excellence from the American Craft Council at the Baltimore Fine Craft Show.

My studio is in Shelburne Falls, MA. I am available for gallery shows and custom opportunities. Having been in business for 29 years, I enjoy the challenges of being a small business owner.

Ann Brauer’s Specialties:

Contemporary art quilts that use color, fabric and the traditions of quilt making to create abstract landscapes. Custom quilts for private and public collections. Gallery shows. Lectures. Articles.

    Distinctive contemporary art quilts that use color and the traditions of quilt making to convey abstract landscapes of the imagination.

    My quilts are in the new Federal District Court House in Springfield, MA, the Lodge at Turning Stone in Verona, NY and the Museum of Art + Design in New York, NY. I have received and Award of Excellence from the American Craft Council at the Baltimore Fine Craft Show in 2006. In 2007 I had a show at the Springfield Museum of Fine Arts. The United States State Department has purchased my work as a gift for a state visit. My quilts have also hung in the United States Embassy in Seoul, South Korea and Dili, Tamil-Leste.

    I love working with decorators, consultants and individuals to create the perfect piece for a given location. I also do have a number of quilts currently available.

    I normally show my work at a number of the fine craft shows each year. Upcoming shows include the PA Guild of Craftspeople Show in Wilmington, DE, the Paradise City Arts Festival in Northampton, MA, the Washington Craft Show and CraftBoston.
