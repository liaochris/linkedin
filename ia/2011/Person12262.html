Donato Falotico's Summary

I am a social researcher both by training and by nature.
I have always been extremely curious about social, psychological, and cultural phenomena, willing to make sense of their mechanisms and dynamics.

My idea is that in society there is always something to observe, record, research and explain, and that there is always a story to tell about it - a story whose lines can be shared and productively used.

I have experience in the social research field (qualitative research: ethnography, media & market research, audience research, consumer behaviour) and in the creative industries (music, performing arts, media; coordination, management and research)

My goals are simple: I'd like to be able to research, analyse and report; in short, tell stories about what I observe. I particularly love field work and visual methods and I am very interested in popular culture, folklore and media. I love stories, and every social or cultural phenomenon is to me a story that needs to be told.

Donato Falotico's Education

London Metropolitan University

MA, Mass Communications

2009 – 2010

Researching Communications, Principles of Mass Communications, Qualitative Research, Advertising and Mass Communications, Media Audiences, Globalisation Internationalisation and Communications. Under the supervision of Dr. Paul Cobley (reader in Communication), for my MA dissertation I conducted an ethnographic comparative study on media, folklore and popular culture. My role consisted of recruiting the sample, conducting in depth qualitative interviews (filmed), transcription, systematic literature review and data analysis. In the sampling I selected respondents originating from different cultures in order to gain a cross-cultural validity of the study. I pursued a cross-sectional analysis making comparisons across my whole data set on specified themes constructing interpretive categories to test my hypotheses

Università degli Studi di Roma 'La Sapienza'

BSc, Psychology

2002 – 2006

Cognitive psychology, Research Methods and Statistics, Psychopathology, Sociology of Communication, Cultural Anthropology, Theories and Techniques of Test Administration and Scoring (with practicum of evaluation techniques).
Under the supervision of Professor Antonio Riccio (professor in Cultural Anthropology) for my BSc dissertation I conducted an ethnographic research in the field in three different villages in Southern Italy. My role was to conduct literature review, recruit respondents, conduct unstructured interviews (audio-taped), transcription, and perform data collection and analysis. The research was aimed to explore the dimension of popular religiosity and its possible links with superstition.
