Francois Bazin's Summary

François Bazin is a Mining Engineer with a BSc Eng from Queen’s University in Canada. He gained operational and technical experience at the Quinsam Coal mine in Western Canada and at the Nanisivik underground lead-zinc mine in the Arctic before moving to Australia in 1999. François spent 4 years at Rio Tinto’s Hamersley Iron mines in Western Australia before spending 2 years at Comalco’s Weipa operations in Cape York. At Hamersley, François worked in various roles of increasing responsibility including; drill and blast engineer, short term planning engineer and long term planning engineer for the Marandoo, Brockman, Tom Price and Yandi operations. At Comalco Weipa, he was employed as a short term planning engineer and was promoted to specialist mining engineer to aid the company with their long term planning and strategic focus.

Since joining IMC in 2005, François has managed several field programmes and has been involved in numerous projects covering both coal and metals in Australia, Mongolia, Indonesia, New Caledonia, West Africa and the Solomon Islands. More specifically, François has worked on a feasibility study for a large multi-seam open pit coal mine in NSW and has completed several mine planning studies for gold mine expansions in West Africa. François managed the Sulawesi nickel field program and subsequent the mine planning and resource estimation study, equipment procurement and start-up mining operations. Additionally, Francois managed the mine planning and reserves estimation for a low rank coal project in Sumatra.

François is bilingual in French and English and a member of the AusIMM. He has a high aptitude in data manipulation, mine optimisation, planning, design and scheduling using various software packages including Vulcan, Whittle, MS Access, Minemax, MineSched, Talpac, XPAC and Datamine.
