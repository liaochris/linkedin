Stephanie Miller’s Summary

Highly driven, competent and accomplished environmental professional with 11 plus years of experience that includes managing environmental compliance for one of the largest rural telephone carriers with over 3 million access lines in 25 states with over $3.2 billion in annual revenues, along with brokering waste for the largest single site hazardous fuel blender in the U.S. Vast environmental experience that encompasses most aspects of environmental due diligence, site investigation, waste management, remediation, regulatory compliance, project management as well as cost estimations and project tracking. The application of these skills has resulted in completed scopes of work, strong client satisfaction and the appropriate contribution to the bottom line.

Stephanie Miller’s Experience

  • Sr. Environmental Consultant

    Windstream Communications

    (Public Company; WIN; Telecommunications industry)

    May 2006 — Present (4 years 9 months)

    Currently, I manage environmental compliance for over 2,500 facilities across 25 states to ensure over 460 site filings for hazardous chemical inventory reporting (EPCRA), over 100 NEPA sites & more than 75 sites with air, water, storage tank & other miscellaneous permits. Since being at Windstream, I have established a solid corporate environmental management system as part of the final submittal to the U.S. EPA in November 2006 for successful closure of a Self-Disclosure Consent Decree. Also, I have spearheaded and developed the company’s “Go Green” program which includes company wide recycling, cost saving protocols, conservation & sustainability by conducting company wide podcasts, webcasts & newsletters. This has also led to Windstream being one of Newsweek magazine “greenest” big companies in America (Sept 29, 2009).

    My job entails managing contractors on environmental projects that include Phase I/II site work for environmental due diligence, stack/opacity testing for air modeling/permitting, NEPA, removal & closure of leaking storage tanks, ground water monitoring & indoor air quality issues such as abatement of asbestos, lead-based paints as well as remediation of mold. I also maintain many working relationships with federal, state & local regulators for all environmental issues affecting the telecom industry.

    * Serve as the environmental liaison to internal department such as Network Engineering, Corporate Legal, Corporate Regulatory, Corporate Procurement, Corporate HR & Corporate Property Management/Facilities as well to contractors & the public with respect to interpretation & application of environmental regulations;

    * Coordinate & conduct on-going training on Corporate Environmental Management polices & practices to internal employees as well as to external contractors;

    * Integral member for all environmental due diligence for the Corporate Integration Team for acquisitions & transitions.

  • Non-Fuels Supervisor/Lead/Chemist

    Rineco Chemical

    (Privately Held; Chemicals industry)

    January 2003 — May 2006 (3 years 5 months)

    At Rineco, I supervised a staff of 3 that brokered hazardous and non-hazardous waste for the largest single site fuels blender in the United States. During my stint at Rineco, my department increased the Non-Fuels revenue from $5 million to over $25 million annually in 3 years. I provided professional guidance, training and leadership by ensuring that company objectives were achieved along with being a technical expert for the handling of material & technology at each non-fuels outlet. I managed facility personnel & assisted customers in the processing of waste profiles for the network of non-fuels outlets by evaluating, approving or rejecting waste profiles based on the facility’s ability to process the material in a safe & environmentally complaint manner. Also, I designed and facilitated developmental training for site personnel related to waste profile composition & safety factors along with outlet procedures; and wrote monthly newsletters as a training document for sales & customer service.

    I was the single point of contact for the company in regards to the corporate accounts department and provided support on major waste and fuel bids. My other duties included auditing and evaluating ultimate treatment/disposal facilities to secure approvals for general waste streams & facility pricing for the sales department based on disposal and transportation.

    - Awards: 2003 Outstanding Service Provider, 2004 Outstanding Team Player

  • Staff Scientist

I also performed the field sampling for many of these events.
