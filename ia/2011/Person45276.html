Vivek Mutalik's Summary

Synthetic and System Biologist with extensive experience in quantitative experimental and computational biology research. Currently working on Generalized Expression Operating System that will be useful to engineer protein expression at genome scale systems. Experienced in developing synthetic biology tools for predictable protein expression by engineering transcriptional and translational processes. Extensive experience in developing mathematical modeling approaches to analyze biological regulatory and signaling systems; Has three years of fermentation, bulk drug manufacturing and resource management experience in Biotech Industry.

Specialties

Synthetic and Systems Biology, Predictable protein expression, expression operating systems, Transcriptional and translation insulators, Promoters, transcript stabilizers, terminators, Promoter prediction methodologies, codon optimization, mathematical modeling of biological regulatory systems, Statistical Multivariate data analysis, strong analytical, managerial and organizing skills

BioFAB [http://www.biofab.org]

Biotechnology industry

August 2010 – Present (8 months)

The BIOFAB: International Open Facility Advancing Biotechnology (BIOFAB) was founded in December 2009 as the world's first biological design-build facility under the leadership of Profs. Adam Arkin, Drew Endy and Jay Keasling. BioFab projects are designed to produce broadly useful collections of standard biological parts that can be made freely available to both academic and commercial users, while also enabling the rapid design and prototyping of genetic constructs. Currently BioFAB is located in JBEI premises.

I am directly responsible for designing, building, and testing a collection of engineered genetic components that control DNA replication, constitutive RNA production, RNA processing and degradation, translation initiation, and protein degradation. For each class of functional genetic element, we will engineer and validate a full suite of specific elements, and make them freely available. For more details visit BioFAB website.
