Jeff Schwartz's Summary

J. Schwartz Design (JSD) is a boutique full service design firm working with clients on interiors, architecture and construction project management.

JSD founder, Jeff Schwartz, has lived in greater Boston for over 25 years. A graduate of Brown and Harvard Universities, Jeff holds the Certificate of Decorative Arts from the Boston Architectural Center. He has served as Vice President, IFDA New England and as a Director of the Newton Historical Society. His home has been featured on the Newton House Tour. He is a former Governor of the Copley Society of Art, America’s oldest artist owned and operated Gallery. Jeff and his work have been featured in the local and national press, including: NECN's "New England Dream House,” The Boston Globe, and Women’s Day Kitchens and Baths.

JSD seamlessly manages all design, construction and decorating professionals. We offer our interior design and architecture clients a single source for creative, project and cost management.

Whatever style appeals to you -- traditional, contemporary, transitional or modern - J. Schwartz Design will create beautiful, comfortable environments for family and friends.

Jeff Schwartz's Publications

  • NECN's New England Dream House-Seaside Bungalow

    • NECN
    • February 22, 2010
    Authors: Jeff Schwartz

    Transformation: Bringing an Old Seaside Bungalow Back to Life. In the spring of 2007, Jeff Schwartz, who has had a home in southern Maine for 24 years, was approached about a neglected old bungalow on a very special piece of land. The house had served as a coast guard barracks in the Second World War and in no way took advantage of the beautiful site and surroundings. It was overgrown, dark and cramped on the inside and had fallen into general disrepair. All of the improvements made over the years were piecemeal and not in harmony in any way. Jeff and his wife, Nancy Moss, a public relations executive, bought the house with a vision of what it could be. They would honor the modest bungalow genes of the house and its relationship to its neighboring dwellings, while making it truly wonderful and every bit their own. Transforming it without tearing it down. Jeff and Nancy had four key goals for the modest house (approximately 2800 SF with the addition of a rear roof dormer and some strategic bump-outs) and the site: - It should look from the outside like it had always been there, sit well on the site and complement its neighbors. - It should meet their lifestyle needs. They are empty nesters whom enjoy the seaside year round while hosting guests throughout the summer. Every part of the house should be used. All of the spaces should seduce you into entering and enjoying them. - It should be surprising and wonderful with beautiful natural light within, with nooks and built-ins and materials indigenous to the Maine coast. - It should bring the seacoast and the neighboring architecture indoors in unexpected ways and be enjoyed four seasons of the year. Design and planning work commenced in 2007 and continued through the fall of 2008, when final design plans were submitted to and approved by the local Coastal Commission. The house was gut renovated and now is on a new chapter of a new life, completed May of 2009.
