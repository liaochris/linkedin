Sean Hazlett, CFA’s Experience

  • Principal

    (Financial Services industry)

    June 2009 — October 2010 (1 year 5 months)

  • Clean Energy Equity Research Senior Associate

    Morgan Stanley

    (Public Company; ms; Financial Services industry)

    November 2007 — June 2009 (1 year 8 months)

    Helped build Morgan Stanley’s new clean energy franchise. Conducted competitive analyses and market research, assessed feasibility of emerging clean energy technologies, developed financial models, and prepared 113 equity research reports for clean energy companies in industries ranging from energy storage to wind and solar energy. Operated as an internal consultant and subject matter expert to vet potential IPOs. Attended industry conferences and networked with venture capitalists, industry experts and academics to identify future leaders in the clean energy space. Primary focus on transportation, energy storage and smart grid technologies.

    - Team achieved 97% StarMine rating in 2008 (#2 in Morgan Stanley North American Equity Research).
    - Ethanol stock call resulted in return of 1,418% relative to S&P500.
    - Achieved top tier ranking by general and specialist sales force (top 18% and 27% of senior associates).
    - Published Wall Street’s first smart grid market sizing and industry report, which was cited in The Economist.
    - Led firm’s highly differentiated clean energy security research effort, which assesses the effect that geopolitical and policy drivers have on the clean energy industry and energy markets in general.
    - Created comprehensive industry supply and demand model that predicted ethanol industry rationalization.
    - Conducted analysis of potential market effects of proposed federal legislation on greenhouse gas reduction.
    - Developed solar demand model to assess potential of residential solar PV market in California.
    - Produced proprietary levelized cost of energy analysis for thin-film and polysilicon solar PV to assess when both technologies would reach grid parity in the United States.
    - Developed proprietary industry models for advanced battery and fuel cell companies in the transportation, portable power and grid stabilization spaces.

  • Investment Banking Associate

    products, aerospace and defense, rail, automotive, solar energy, biofuels and clean coal companies.

    - Advised US biodiesel company on $345MM IPO.
    - Advised $1.1Bn resources company on IPO of clean coal subsidiary.
    - Represented $1.0Bn regional railroad on potential acquisition by infrastructure fund.
    - Performed analysis on valuation drivers in the waste management industry for financial sponsor.
    - Developed complex tax structure to maximize value for $10Bn consumer products company acquisition and simultaneous split-off of non-core assets.

  • Research Associate

    Stanford Harvard Preventive Defense Project

    (Defense & Space industry)

    September 2005 — June 2006 (10 months)

    Provided geopolitical and energy security analysis for senior defense policy-makers.
    - Wrote 33 policy memoranda ranging from India’s nuclear program to the Afghan security situation.

    - Provided thought leadership for a private conference on Iran’s nuclear program with participants including former Defense Secretary Perry, former Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff General Shalikashvili, former Senator Sam Nunn and The Weekly Standard editor, William Kristol.
    - Provided policy recommendations on options to halt Iran’s uranium enrichment program absent a negotiated agreement for former Undersecretary of State for Political Affairs, R. Nicholas Burns. Final product was awarded top policy analysis recommendation at Harvard’s Kennedy School of Government in 2006.
