Jim Farrell's Summary

In his most recent position at Motorola since 1994, he led the public relations campaigns for several microcontrollers, including Nitron, digital television chips, set-top boxes and digital audio chips (56300 DSP audio series). Jim’s activities included the successful trademarking and promotion of Streamaster™ set-top box technology, and Symphony® digital audio technology.

Prior to rejoining Motorola in Austin in 1994, Jim spent nine years with VLSI Technology, Inc. in Tempe, Arizona and San Jose, California. During this period, he successfully managed PR and marketing communications for PC chip sets and ARM products. These campaigns included trademarking and promoting TOPCAT™, EcoChip™ and Polar™ PC Chip Set brand names. He also managed the publication of the first ARM processor Users’ Manual.

Jim started his technical career at EAI, solving applications problems for computers in simulation projects. In 1977, Farrell joined Motorola semiconductor in Austin. During his first nine years at Motorola he managed the successful introduction of several 8-bit, 16-bit, and 32-bit microprocessor and microcontroller families, including the industry-standard 6805 and 68000.

He has written over 100 technical articles and papers on microprocessors, microcontrollers and related topics.

Jim served as a member or Chair of the WESCON Technical Program Committee, and has served on other conferences’ program committees, both in the U.S. and abroad.
