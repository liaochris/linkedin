  • Health Claims Manager

    ELCA BOP

    (Professional Training & Coaching industry)

    1990 — 2001 (11 years )

    Same job title, different work each year.

    When I was hired, 3 groups had recently merged into one. We had a severe lack of customer confidence and low satisfaction levels. Through process improvement and employee development, we became a respected, reliable administrator of benefits with consistently high customer satisfaction levels. (Measured by surveys, frequent personal contact with customers by the VP’s and President, audit results, phone reports, turnaround time reports, and written customer communications.)

    My VP boss told me, "You brought order to chaos."

    In 2000 - 2001, I lead an 18-month implementation of outsourcing of the remaining indemnity, dental and Medicare claims administration to two outside vendors and closed my department.

    Retained 97% of staff & temps throughout the process. (Basically, only 1 person jumped ship before the need for that job ended.)

    Customer service and individual employee performance remained high (measured by established methods of monitoring performance and increased audits) and our claims turnaround time actually improved.

    Received the company’s Values Leader Award for my work and leadership during the 18-month outsourcing process. (Only one employee received this award annually. Values were: integrity, excellence, respect, collaboration and stewardship.)

    It's a fine company with wonderful people and a meaningful purpose.
