Jim Plaxco’s Summary

Jim Plaxco is a digital artist specializing in astronomical and planetary art, as well as abstract and algorithmic art. His digital art tools include photography, 2D and 3D graphics software as well as graphics software of his own design.

A portion of Jim's work can be seen at http://www.artsnova.com with information on commissioned art available at http://www.artsnova.com/art_commissions.html

As a Solar System Ambassador for the NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory, Jim has used his artistic and image processing skills to create astronomical and planetary images using data from a variety of space missions to illustrate his educational presentations.

In addition to creating art, Jim has judged space art contests for both NASA and the National Space Society.

Jim is also a programmer with expertise in web design, data mining, database design, SAS, REXX, and ISPF, HTML, CSS, C.

Jim is a frequent lecturer on the subjects of algorithmic art, space art, space exploration, planetary science, astronomy, and image processing as it relates to planetary science missions and astronomical science. Jim has taught astronomical image processing as a Guest Lecturer at the Adler Planetarium in Chicago
