Walt Shiel's Summary

At Slipdown Mountain Publications, we publish books for the unfettered mind with primary emphasis on military history, alternative views of science, the paranormal, science fiction, and historical fiction.

Our subsidiary, Five Rainbows Services for Authors & Publishers, provides a wide range of services to help authors and independent publishers achieve their publishing goals -- these services include book and cover design and typesetting, indexing, book illustrations, cataloging-in-publication data, news releases, and custom-tailored publishing mentoring programs for self-publishers.

I have been a published author since 1992, with dozens of magazine articles published in the US, UK, and Australia and five published books. I am also an experienced public speaker, most recently conducting seminars on topics of interest to authors and self-publishers.

Specialties

Writing, book and cover design, self-publishing mentoring, military aviation history.

Walt Shiel's Publications

  • Once a Knight: A Novel of Aerial Combat & Romance in World War I

    • Slipdown Mountain Publications LLC
    • June 1, 2010
    Authors: Walt Shiel

    In 1916, Everett Ross quit the Texas Rangers and traveled to England to join the Royal Flying Corps, trading his horse for a Nieuport pursuit biplane. Between dogfights, Ross loses his heart to a young French beauty whose domineering mother fights her own battle to protect Geneviéve from this American cowboy wearing a British uniform. Ross soon must decide between love and duty, between orders and necessity. This fast-moving story combines romance and combat action in a land knocked out of kilter by a deadly war often seemingly without objectives.

    "A superbly crafted historical novel." -Midwest Book Review

  • Devil in the North Woods: A Novel Based on the Tragic 1908 Metz, Michigan, Wildfire

    • Slipdown Mountain Publications LLC
    • May 1, 2005
    Authors: Walt Shiel

    Based on contemporary reports and recorded oral histories, this book accurately depicts the fire’s genesis, growth, and aftermath and contrasts the terror of nature’s destruction with a chronicle of family, love, sorrow, and recovery. The story leaves the reader buoyed by faith in the resilience of the human spirit and belief in love’s ability to germinate amid the ashes of ruin.

    "Shiel’s compassionate writing creates a community of bright, supportive, and determined people, whose characters develop and deepen as the fire grows." -Historical Novel Society
