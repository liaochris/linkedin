Fleur Prinsen’s Summary

I hold a PhD in Educational Psychology (Thesis on Computer Supported Collaborative Learning). Currently I'm conducting research on the opportunities for identity formation in online social networks. In my last research project I conducted educational research in Singapore, studying student resource use during inquiry (science) learning.
I also worked for the Institute for Knowledge Innovation and Technology (University of Toronto). There I was researching ways to improve students understanding of science and scientific progress, getting students to work creatively with knowledge.
Previously, I've developed courses on motivation in education, worked on integrating competency-training into education, studied learner differences and the integration of ICT in education.

Fleur Prinsen’s Education

  • Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam

    PhD , Computer Supported Collaborative Learning , 2003 — 2007

    For my PhD research I worked with teachers and students from 12 different schools. In the classrooms I took the role of teacher/researcher, implementing a controlled experiment aimed at improving the quality of student participation in CSCL, thus aiming to improve their learning outcomes. The students were of diverse backgrounds and the experimental intervention proved to be especially beneficial (in terms of learning outcomes) to minority students.
