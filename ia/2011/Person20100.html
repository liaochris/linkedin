Jena Ardell’s Summary

I am an experienced freelance photographer, production assistant, photo retoucher and assistant producer.

Artist statement:

I was born in the wrong decade. My affinity for 1960s and 1970s textiles, interior design and retro atmosphere runs rampant throughout my work. I recreate pieces of summer’s past in order to experience those decades in the present. In doing so, I create an endless vacation for myself and others, where the sun is always shining, the water is always warm, the waves are always calm and vintage bikinis are always in style.

All of the photographs in my “Summer Love” collection were shot over a period of four years, using vintage Polaroid cameras and expired film. I use Polaroid film in order to produce nostalgic soft edges and dreamy sun-bleached tones. The outcome is much like a faded summer memory. I adore the fact that--unlike digital images--Polaroids are tangible, similar to a gift shop memento or postcard.

My work has been published in the July 2010 and Aug 2009 issues of Elle Girl Korea Magazine. In December, a few of my images will be published in Raynal Pellicer's book Photobooth: The Art of the Automatic Portrait. And in January, one of my Polaroids will be the cover art of Drinking Closer to Home: A Novel, by Jessica Anya Blau, published by HarperCollins.

In addition to print, my work has been featured on numerous blogs and online magazines, including: {Frolic!}, Decor8, Modish and The New York Times Blog; as well as 10x15 Magazine’s “I Love Pools” issue, Posi+tive Magazine and Share Mag.

I was one of the 160 featured photographers in The Month of Photography Los Angeles (MOPLA) 2010.

Past exhibitions include the following group shows:
