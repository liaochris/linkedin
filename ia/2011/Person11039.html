David Sloly's Summary

David enjoys advising organizations on how to engage their publics – especially organizations in transition. Most recently, as Seneca College’s Webmaster, David chaired the team that managed Seneca’s move from impersonal, static websites to personalized and dynamic web portals. Concurrently, as member of its Centre for New Technology in Teaching and Learning, he helped facilitate the shift from traditional classroom to mix-mode and on-line instruction.

As a senior staff member of the International Development and Relief Foundation and a number of other international development organizations, David helped lead transitions from relief work to capacity building and even from dissolution to revival. At CCH Canadian, he was part of the Research and Development team that enabled its move from print to electronic publishing. As Manager of the Mail-Phone Division of Osgoode Hall Law School's Centennial Campaign, David was part of the team that organized the school's first professional capital campaign.

David’s volunteer service is extensive. One of his earliest commitments was lobbying local politicians and rallying Greater Toronto Area youth in support of the Save the Rouge River Campaign. This successful campaign led to the creation of Canada’s largest urban park. David has also served as a senior member of a number of political campaign teams including the leadership campaign team of one of Canada’s longest serving first ministers. He currently sits on the Board of Transforming Faces Worldwide, a charity funding capacity building for organizations in developing countries that provide surgery and rehabilitation services for children with cleft lip and pallet.

David has a B.A. in Political Science, a Certificate in Web Design, and is working on his Masters in Environmental Studies at York University. The focus of his Masters research is the use of information technology to engage youth and marginalized citizens in environmental and social justice issues.
