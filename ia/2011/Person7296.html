Cassie Lancellotti-Young’s Summary

I am an Internet professional with robust fiscal, analytical and operational experience. I began my career at Citigroup, where I worked as a corporate finance analyst on the technology, media, and telecommunications coverage team. Fascinated by the digital landscape and eager to gain operational experience in the industry, I left Citigroup in 2006 to join an online start-up called TheLadders.com. At TheLadders I held a variety of marketing and business development roles, ultimately growing the company's customer base to 3 million members. Seeking to bridge the gap between my tactical background and the strategic mindset necessary for an effective managerial career, I decided to leave TheLadders in 2009 to pursue my MBA full-time at Tuck.

I have remained extremely active in the digital arena while at Tuck. In addition to serving as an MBA Fellow to the Glassmeyer/McNamee Center for Digital Strategies, I held a consulting engagement with Sapient Interactive during my first year of studies, through which I advised on revenue management and analytics for Hawaiian Airlines. Further, in the summer between my first and second years, I relocated to the Silicon Valley to intern for the SVP of Business Operations at AOL; in that capacity I worked on a variety of key business initiatives related to AOL's consumer applications suite and corporate turnaround.

Most recently, I have been working for a NYC-based start-up called VillageVines, a yield management solution for restaurants that provides consumers with 30% discounts off of their entire bills (food and alcohol) at premier restaurants in major cities nationwide; in this role I am managing consumer acquisition and retention strategies as well as user analytics and insights.
