Natesh Vaidyanathan's Summary

Graduate of the Lockheed Martin Finance Leadership Development Program (FLDP).

Graduate of the University of Pittsburgh (BSBA 2005, Magna Cum Laude), and Georgetown University McDonough School of Business (MBA 2010).

I have extensive experience working competitive proposals for commercial entities, the Department of Defense, and Foreign Military Sales (FMS) in the fields of pricing, material cost engineering, proposal development/analysis, and cost volume development. I have worked three separate $1B+ competitive proposals, and several more worth >$100M. I have participated in Black Hat reviews, Independent Non-Avocate Reviews (INARs), Red Team assessments, and have interfaced consistently with Program Management, Finance/Business Management, and Business Development professionals.

Spent four summers as an intern for Lockheed Martin (2001-2004), working within the Independent Cost Evaluation (ICE) Group.

Joined ICE full-time for my first FLDP rotation, then moved onto International Program Finance for Surface Systems Programs and Ballistic Missile Defense. Moved to Manassas, VA in July 2007 to pursue my MBA at Georgetown University and conclude my tenure within the FLDP by 2008, my final rotation being in the Pricing / Cost Engineering Group for the Undersea Systems Line of Business.

Completed a three-month project in Middle River, Maryland, working on the Lead Pricing Team for the FY08-FY09 Littoral Combat Ship Proposal (March-June, 2008). Increased competitive proposal experience on the ISIS-II and TCS Proposals (August-September, 2008) as Lead Pricer and part of the Cost Volume Team. Returned to LCS in 2009 and 2010 in increasing capacities, ensuring Cost Exhibit accuracy and auditability.

Spent another two-month term with the LCS FY10-14 Proposal Team (February - March 2010), again in Middle River, MD -- this time, as the Cost Exhibit lead; bridging the Pricing, F&BM, and Cost Volume teams on a ten-ship "winner-take-all" multi-billion dollar proposal.

Specialties

Statistics/metric development, financial modeling, competitive proposal experience, cost estimation, pricing, business management, material cost engineering, program/proposal finance, cost volume development, overhead analysis, risk assessment, earned value management, cash flow (NPV/IRR), management policy.
