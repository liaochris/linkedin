Volker Kleinhenz's Summary

During my professional career, I covered agricultural research, commercial horticultural production, agribusiness and development work.

As a postdoctoral Research Officer at CQU, I managed two projects associated with developing two new primary industries targeting export vegetable production in Australia. While subsequently holding the position of Associate Scientist at AVRDC in Taiwan, I focused on coordinating a number of collaborative projects around Asia. At AIT in Thailand, I developed agronomic practices for protected cultivation of tomatoes. These scientific assignments have resulted in 47 publications.

My primary responsibility as Chief Agronomist for FieldFresh Foods in India was executing business plans by technically developing, implementing agronomic practices for multiple vegetable crops. During this assignment, the total production area increased to 2691 ha. A prerequisite for successfully exporting fresh produce was meeting produce specifications and farm certification standards.

As Director Quality Management for a German wholesale company, I implemented customer product specifications including those for pesticide residues by auditing legal and B2B pre- and post-farmgate standards incl. GlobalGAP, QS, BRC, and IFS, and ethical/social standards at conventional and organic farms and farmers’ groups worldwide.

As Manager Agriculture Division for Jenaan Investment, I managed agriculture operations for large-scale (5685 ha) field crops including Alfalfa, barley and millet in the Sahara desert of Egypt.

I am currently employed with Deutsche Gesellschaft für Internationale Zusammenarbeit (GIZ) in Cambodia, developing on-farm and off-farm agricultural and non-agricultural income-generating activities in Kampong Chhnang province.

Specialties

Besides my principal occupation, I am working as an agribusiness consultant for Gerson Lehrman Group and Guidepoint Global, and as a referee for several international agriculture journals.
