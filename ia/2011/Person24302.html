Kerry McGinley’s Summary

I'm a storyteller. My words connect people, issues, crises and victories. Whether they're spoken, written, tweeted, IMd or emailed, those words keep me plugged in to the world around me.

Here's my story:

I started my career as a PR assistant at the Red Cross in San Antonio. I thought I'd found a way to kill time until I became a world-famous writer. But the job taught me my world was too small, that I was a part of something bigger.

It also renewed my commitment journalism. I went to Northwestern for a master's in broadcasting. Now I could add pictures to words to make a difference.

And I could enjoy the scenic sights and smells of Lubbock, TX. (No offense, Lubbock, but those feed lots...YIKES.) For two years at KAMC/KLBK I covered crime, cut my hair shorter and quoted "Dukes of Hazard" and "Star Wars" over the 2-way radio.

Then I applied to the local paper, the Lubbock Avalanche-Journal. I had to convince the editor I was smarter than I looked. It was a hard sell.

I started as a regional reporter, then moved back to courts and crime. I might be there still if a sexy airport manager hadn't convinced me to give up the single life. My brand-spankin' new husband got a job in Colorado the week we got back from our honeymoon.

I cried the day I left the AJ. I cried again six months later when I read my pregnancy test. I'd just started a job at The Gazette in Colorado Springs. I had nine months to find the world's greatest and cheapest nanny or start freelancing.

Freelancing won. It led me to Operation Homefront, where I eventually ended up managing editor and program manager.

Now I'm back on my own, helping clients tell their stories in 140 characters or less. Or more. It all depends on who you are, what you offer and what you need to say.
