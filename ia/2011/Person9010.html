Dan Cohen’s Summary

Dan Cohen's experience ranges from founding and serving as CEO of startups to developing and leading technology businesses in major corporations. His roles have encompassed a full spectrum of operational areas including general management, business formation, product management, strategy, marketing and business development.

Cohen is currently CEO of mySpoonful, a startup that delivers free new independent music to busy fans. He recently served as consulting GM of Zoomerang, a leader in online surveys. Cohen was also consulting CEO of Keibi, a provider of SaaS solutions for moderating social media. His engagement culminated in Keibi’s acquisition by Lithium.

Previously, Cohen was CEO of Pageflakes, the social personalized start page (funded by Balderton Capital). Under his leadership, the site grew rapidly from a European beta site with fewer than 5,000 visitors into an award-winning Web 2.0 product with over one million unique visitors. Cohen also built the company's management team and US operations, formulated product and go-to-market strategy, created media buzz to drive user growth, raised additional venture funding and improved engineering output and product quality. He also established key media and distribution partnerships, and later directed the company’s acquisition by LiveUniverse.

Prior to Pageflakes, Cohen was Head of My Yahoo!, the most popular personalized start page in the world, with over 60 million unique visitors. Before Yahoo!, Cohen was Group Product Manager of Personalized Products at Google, including iGoogle and Google Reader.

Cohen was previously the CEO and cofounder of Personity, a provider of presence server infrastructure and consumer applications for mobile operators, later acquired by Openwave. As the CEO and founder of USConnect, Cohen bootstrapped and built a successful enterprise applications development business. He later led the company’s acquisition by IKON, then served in several senior management roles.
