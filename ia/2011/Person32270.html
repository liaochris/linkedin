Neal Hallford's Summary

For over twenty years, I've been in the business of making best-selling games. My work has not only helped secure the financial success of my employers, but also garnered all of those titles top industry awards and critical acclaim. My talents have been called upon by respected development houses like Gas Powered Games, Westwood Studios, Cavedog Entertainment, and Dynamix. The story I crafted for "Betrayal at Krondor" became a New York Times best-selling hardcover novel, and my work on "Dungeon Siege" was adapted as a major motion picture. In 2001, Prima Publishing released my how-to technical book, “Swords & Circuitry: A Designer’s Guide to Computer Role-Playing Games,” and subsequently S&C has now become the textbook for teaching game design at several universities across the country.

I’ve done everything from designing broad scale game systems down to placing objects, monsters, and quests in customized level-editors. I’ve written in-game dialog, created monster A.I., and managed subordinate designers.

In addition to my work as a game designer, I'm also an independent film producer and am currently at work on my first feature, a supernatural horror movie called "Witch Creek" (witchcreekmovie.com).
