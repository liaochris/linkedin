Sharon Ruchman | Classical Musician | Composer's Summary

As a classical musician and classical music composer almost all of my adult life has been spent following my passion for music. I would like to share my love for music with you with my release of two CD's that I hope you enjoy.My first release, a classical contemporary CD featuring "Sea Glass" and my latest release " Arrival of Spring". Both available for purchase at my website. www.sharonruchman.com

In addition, my piece for Cello and Piano" was chosen to be played in San Francisco by NACUSA, a composer's organization. also my piece " Day at Play and Day's End" was performed at the Women Composer's Festival in Hartford last spring.

My compositions have also aired on WMNR and WSHU, classical stations in CT. I am also a member of NACUSA, American Composers Forum, American Music Center and IAWM (Alliance for Women Composers). She has been fortunate to meet Mary Costanza, cellist and a Washington resident, whom she has performing with the last few years.

Sharon Ruchman | Classical Musician | Composer's Education

Yale University

Masters of Music Education, Music

1971 – 1973

While attending Yale I was an alto soloist with the Yale Glee Club and New Haven chorale. I was invited to participate in the opera program at the Norfolk Music Festival in Norfolk, CT. I studied theory with Allen Forte (harmony, counterpoint, analysis of classical and atonal music) and Willie Ruff (Jazz).

Activities and Societies: Yale Glee Club, New Haven Chorale

New England Conservatory of Music

Bachelor's of Music Education, Music

1967 – 1971

While attending the New England Conservatory of Music, Sharon accompanied the Conservatory chorus under the direction of Lorna Cooke DeVaron and guest conductor Seiji Ozawa. During the summers, Sharon attended the Ambler Music Festival and the Blossom Music Festival, where she was chosen to sing in Robert Shaw's chamber choir.

Activities and Societies: Conservatory Chorus
