Nikiah Nudell’s Summary

Nick is nationally recognized as an accomplished Emergency Medical Services systems designer working in all forms of administration, research, development, analysis, and planning. With nearly 20 years of experience in emerging technologies, Nick is also a rising star in the field of health information systems design and analytics.

Nick has authored and championed strategies, and administered numerous programs, grants, and budgets for the provision of EMS under public health models. These include predictive modeling, electronic patient tracking, disaster registries for vulnerable populations, uncompensated care reimbursement, centralized statewide EMS deployment models, strong physician oversight contemporary scope of practice and medical oversight rules, and the first peer reviewed research and consensus based field triage guidelines.

Using sharp analytical and problem solving skills, big picture thinking, and an encyclopedic knowledge of technical and clinical issues, Nick has demonstrated success by rolling up his sleeves to lead large complex projects and business units across multiple operating locations. These successes have been enabled by bringing together divergent stakeholder groups to achieve common organizational objectives. In more than 10 years of working in EMS he has worked at all levels in both rural and large urban systems and has developed a great appreciation for the diverse needs and issues of EMS across the country.
