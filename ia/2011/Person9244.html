Corinne Erni’s Summary

Accomplished curator and cultural manager with 11-year record of realizing high-quality, international art projects. Creative leader with proven success in producing complex programs touching on the cultural zeitgeist, attracting large audiences and media attention. Published art writer and editor.

Corinne Erni’s Specialties:

Visionary project development, planning, and execution.
Strong managerial and team-building capability, including non-profit.
Key partnership development and negotiating skills.
Specialized in nation-branding art and culture projects.
Expertise and special interest in art and sustainability.
Skilled at art PR and marketing.
Fluent in French, German, Italian, Spanish; basic knowledge of Portuguese.

Corinne Erni’s Experience

  • Project Manager, New Arts & Design Festival

    New Museum

    (Non-Profit; Museums and Institutions industry)

    June 2010 — Present (8 months)

    Manage new arts & design festival with focus on sustainability, which demonstrates the power of the creative community to imagine the future city (spring 2011). Coordinate exhibitions, lectures, and other events to show how culture and creative thinking can animate the city, and how a collective force of partnerships can exert influence on public awareness.

  • Founder and Director

    ARTPORT_making wave. An international art and sustainability project

    (Fine Art industry)

    January 2006 — Present (5 years 1 month)

    Curate and produce international art and sustainability projects that raise awareness about climate change linking art, politics, and science; including exhibitions, panel discussions, and artist exchanges. Raised $80,000 for climate change exhibition, (Re-) Cycles of Paradise. Established partnerships with major international arts and environmental organizations.
    Projects:

    (Re-) Cycles of Paradise: exhibition on gender and climate change at DGI-byen, Copenhagen, December 7-18, 2009, in conjunction with UN Climate Conference COP15; and in partnership with Global Gender and Climate Change Alliance (GGCA), International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN), and United Nations Development Program (UNDP). Plans to travel to Mexico, U.S., and Switzerland.

    Cool Stories For When the Planet Gets Hot: series of compilations of short videos and animations on global warming, presented worldwide at art fairs (focus 09 Basel, Scope 07 Basel; PooL 07 Miami), cultural institutions (Lincoln Center New York, MuVIM Valencia); festivals (International Film and Photography Festival on the Environment, Paris 07; Hopenhagen 09). Partnerships with the New York Foundation for the Arts, UNESCO, the City of Arts And Science Valencia, and others.

    Key achievements:
    - Raised awareness about global warming among art audience; introduced art as a catalyst for change in political environments.
    - Generated positive press reviews.
    - Built networks involving art institutions, environmental organizations, and artists worldwide.
