Gary L. Cole AIA, ALA, Esq.'s Summary

Gary L. Cole AIA, ALA, Esq. is a Chicago-based Illinois and Florida-licensed attorney and an Illinois-licensed architect – one of the few individuals in the country possessing licenses in both law and architecture.

Mr. Cole’s training and experience as an architect in building design, combined with his contractor’s experience in construction, allows him to understand first-hand the legal and business priorities of developers, architects, engineers and contractors, and to understand the physical aspects of projects, transactions and disputes in ways not always apparent to attorneys without this training and experience - thereby providing value-driven, cost-effective legal services.

He places a strong emphasis in discussing and explaining legal issues to his clients not simply in the language of the law, but in the language of the real estate, design and construction industries so that clients may confidently make well-informed decisions affecting their businesses.

As an architect, prior to becoming an attorney, he worked for several architectural firms, operated his own design and construction firm, was a Historical Architect with the Illinois Historic Preservation Agency, and was a Visiting Associate Professor of Architecture at the University of Illinois for more than a decade.

As an attorney, he worked for two of Chicago’s largest law firms - Winston & Strawn LLP and Seyfarth Shaw LLP - and was in-house counsel for one of the largest retail developers in the southeast – The Sembler Company.

Mr. Cole earned his law degree at Loyola University Chicago School of Law (1998), and holds both Bachelor of Architecture (1988) and Master of Architecture (1992) degrees from the University of Illinois.

He is a construction arbitrator for the American Arbitration Association (AAA) and Certified Mediator for the Center for Conflict Resolution.

Further details about Mr. Cole, his experience and services can be found at www.lawarkbuilding.com

Specialties

Design and Construction: Preparation of design/engineering and construction contracts; litigation, mediation and arbitration.

Real Estate Development: Property acquisition/disposition, leasing, management, land use/zoning issues, finance and development economic incentives.

Historic Preservation: Historic rehabilitation tax incentives, federal/state regulatory compliance, landmark applications/disputes.

Accessibility: Local, state and federal accessibility disputes and compliance.
