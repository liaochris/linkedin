BIOGRAPHY

Jill has over seven years of experience working within the mental health field. Her extensive experience with Latino clients and parent-child conflict resolution and relationship therapy in precise treatment modalities. Her additional areas of professional practice include: female adolescents, anger management, recovery for victims of trauma (abuse and other).

Jill graduated from Azusa Pacific University with a degree in Psychology; and with a Master’s degree in Marriage and Family Therapy from Fuller Graduate School of Psychology. With her second Masters degree in Cross-Cultural Studies, Jill exhibits sensitivity to and enjoys working with clients of all cultural, religious and socioeconomic backgrounds.

Jill strives to provide her clients of diverse backgrounds with the highest quality of care, delivered through means of communication that are individualized to their particular culture(s).

Jill completed her traineeship with “Friends of the Family” (FOF) in Van Nuys, CA. At FOF she gained experience with marital, family, and individual counseling; she also facilitated two mental health groups: one for children experiencing trauma, and one with sexual offenders striving towards rehabilitation.

Jill’s experience continued with an internship with Star View Community Services of Highland Park, CA. There she gained experience with agency/community work, focused mainly on children with complex, multifaceted problematic areas including: poverty, gang affiliation, trauma, abuse, and behavioral problems.

Jill currently works with an agency contracted with the California Department of Mental Health, and as such is proficient in all DMH paperwork and procedures. She is also expanding her mental health services via her own private practice located in Monrovia, CA.
