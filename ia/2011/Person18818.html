Jim Janis's Summary

Jim Janis is a strategic management consultant and advisor to private, public, and non-profit organizations and companies, primarily in the energy, environmental, and security fields. You can reach Jim at Jim@JanisGroup.com, and find out more about him at www.JanisGroup.com. The objective of his practice is to help his clients achieve sustained competitive advantage in their chosen markets. He has focused most recently on helping his clients understand and benefit from the stimulus program for green energy projects, developing positive branding strategies, finding new executives, and winning major new contracts for his clients in the United States and in the United Kingdom.Follow Jim on Twitter: @JimmyJ96, and on Facebook: @Jim Janis.

Specialties

Jim works with firms offering scientific and technical services, engineering, construction, and anti-terrorist security systems. Clients include non-profits, small and large, international defense, engineering, construction, and energy firms in the US and the UK. Jim also helps small, minority, service-disabled, Alaskan-native, Native-American and woman-owned firms. If you need help with grants, loan guarantees, and contracts eligible for stimulus funds, visit our website at www.JanisGroup.com.

Jim Janis's Experience

President

The Janis Group, Inc.

Environmental Services industry

May 1996 – Present (14 years 11 months)

The Janis Group, Inc. works with firms that offer engineering, construction, technology, scientific and technical services, protective force services, and anti-terrorist security systems. Mr. Janis began his consulting practice in June 1996, with Margaret Tifft Janis, and serves as President of their firm, The Janis Group, Inc. Their clients include several non-profit organizations and about twenty companies, covering a wide range from small businesses to large, international engineering, construction, and energy firms. Most of the Janis Group’s clients work in the energy, defense, homeland security, and environmental marketplace in the US and in the United Kingdom. Most of Jim’s clients are CEO’s, Presidents, Boards of Directors, or Business Development managers. In recent years, Jim has helped his clients obtain more than $100 billion in new projects.

Principal

Janis-Bradburne Executive Recruiting LLC

Environmental Services industry

2006 – 2009 (3 years)

Janis-Bradburne Executive Recruiting LLC was founded by Jim Janis and John Bradburne, former President of Fluor Fernald, to assist clients in recruiting senior managers and business development executives in the energy, environmental, defense and security businesses. We have special expertise in the nuclear, health and safety, and environmental remediation markets, and work extensively in the United States and United Kingdom.

Executive Vice President

BNFL

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Environmental Services industry

October 1992 – May 1996 (3 years 8 months)

As Executive Vice President of BNFL Inc. (now part of Energy Solutions, Inc.), the American subsidiary of British Nuclear Fuels, plc., Jim was responsible for the operations of BNFL in the United States in environmental restoration, waste management, decontamination and decommissioning, facility management, and government site operations.
• Jim provided strategic guidance to overcome barriers of foreign government ownership, geographic location, cultural differences, and resistance to new technologies. He led the company into new markets including the first operating contract at a U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) site to be managed by a company owned by a foreign government – the Rocky Flats site near Denver, CO.
• BNFL Inc.’s market share grew from an annual rate of $2 million to more than $200 million (unconsolidated) during Jim's three years as Executive Vice President.

Chairman and Chief Executive Officer

ICF Incorporated

Environmental Services industry

February 1980 – October 1992 (12 years 9 months)

Before joining BNFL in 1992, Mr. Janis held a variety of senior management positions at ICF International, beginning in 1980. Among other assignments, he was Chairman and Director of Kaiser Engineers Hanford Company, Chief Operating Officer of ICF International, and Chairman and Chief Executive Officer of ICF Incorporated (today it is known as ICF International, Inc.), the founding company of the ICF family of companies.

Along with his colleagues, he devised and carried out the strategy that helped ICF grow from a small policy and economics consulting firm of less than 100 people in 1980 when he joined ICF, to a large, international engineering and construction company of more than 4,000 when he left in 1992 to help establish BNFL Inc. in the United States.

Deputy Assistant Secretary

US Department of Energy

Government Agency; 10,001+ employees; Government Administration industry

November 1977 – January 1980 (2 years 3 months)

Before joining ICF, Mr. Janis was a senior manager in the Federal Government in Washington, DC. He was an employee of the U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) when it was created and an employee of the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) when it was created. Mr. Janis was also a Commissioned Officer of the United States Public Health Service in Washington, DC.

He served DOE as Deputy Assistant Secretary for Planning and Evaluation, responsible for strategic planning for energy crises. Mr. Janis was also Executive Director of the Energy Policy Coordinating Committee, a Cabinet-level group created by the President to coordinate energy policy across thirty-six Federal agencies and Departments.

Director, Program Evaluation Division

US Environmental Protection Agency

Government Agency; 10,001+ employees; Government Administration industry

December 1970 – August 1977 (6 years 9 months)

Mr. Janis held a variety of senior management positions at the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency, and helped organize the agency's Office of Radiation Programs when EPA was created. As Director of EPA's Program Evaluation Division, he was responsible for evaluating the effectiveness of all of the agency's programs. He managed EPA’s Regulations Steering Committee, responsible for reviewing the economic and environmental impacts of all regulations issued by the Agency for air pollution, water pollution, hazardous waste, noise, solid waste, toxic substances, radiation, and drinking water.
