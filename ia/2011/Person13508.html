E. L. (Betsy) Smith, PhD's Summary

I am a “Leadership Development Expert” who works with leaders and their teams to move from average to extraordinary - with strategic boldness. Other services include Strategic Planning, Group Facilitation, Team Building, and Succession Planning. I work with large and small business, non-profits and governmental agencies.

I provide executive and leadership coaching to people and organizations who want to go, with strategic boldness, from average to excellent.

Leadership is not for the faint hearted and I has served in leadership roles at the local, state and national levels. My clients are organizations who realize the benefits of investing in themselves and their employees.Since I have been in many leadership roles, I recognize the delicate balances a Leader experiences.

Not only I have studied leadership, I have served in leadership roles at the local, state and national level.

One of my specialties is working with the nursing community offering classes and coaching to take nurses from surviving to thriving.

I co-authored "Second Blooming for Women: Growing a Life that Matters after Fifty" with Kathleen V. Logan. Many of the concepts in the book - attitude, strengths, vision, values, purpose, and action plans are the same concepts that I integrate into my leadership and team building consulting.

    Second Blooming for Women: Growing a Life That Matters After Fifty authors Kathleen Vestal Logan and E. L. (Betsy) Smith coach women step by step to consciously and purposefully plan their future through guided reflection, personal assessments, and action plans. “While some women navigate this stage of life well, other women need encouragement and guidance. The uniqueness of our book is that it is prescriptive, with specific strategies for creating a ‘second life’ that provides women personal fulfillment while making valuable contributions to their community,” notes Smith. “It engages every aspect of a woman’s being, setting her on her own path to an abundant stage of life. We want her to be able to say, ‘My life matters,’” adds Logan.
