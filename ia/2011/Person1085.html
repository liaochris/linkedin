  • Past Chairman and Director

    CompTIA (Computing Technology Industry Association)

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    1984 — 2000 (16 years )

    Served as chairman for three terms, first as Vice Chairman and completing the term of the resigning Chairman and then elected for a full term; recruiting/hiring John Venator as Executive Director who, with quality staff and board direction, executed a turnaround from no cash in 1984 to a $50 million annual budget (with surpluses) by the late-nineties. Elected to serve a third term as Chairman during this period, helping to expand the scope of the organization to embrace the entire computer industry value chain. Also served as a Director, as chairman of CompTIA's Electronic Commerce Standards Board succeeding in adopting an industry wide barcode labeling standard; helped form and chaired CompTIA's Public Policy Committee until 2000 working with industry coalitions gaining Congressional support for tax free Internet, IT training initiatives, telecom reform and updating labor laws for the IT industry.

  • Director

    CoreData, Inc.

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    1997 — 1999 (2 years )

    CoreData, Inc., a privately held company, founded in 1995 in Phoenix, AZ, was a leading software developer of high speed backup software for remote and mobile personal computers.   • Co-Founder, Chairman, Vice Chairman, President, Director

    MicroAge, Inc.

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    1976 — 1999 (23 years )

    Alan Hald and Jeff McKeever co-founded MicroAge, Inc in 1976 with the trademarked motto, "Where Vision Becomes Reality" as its banner for the computer revolution yet to happen; a vision of building a billion dollar business in seven years and a business plan focused on the reality of surviving through December. Served in various executive positions including Chairman, Vice Chairman, Director and President directing the growth of the company with Jeff, from a franchised Byte Shop start-up retailing microcomputer “build it yourself” hobby kit computers to a leading global multi-billion dollar Fortune 500 Company in the booming personal computer and networking industry of the 1990's. MicroAge had a unique quality, like several other companies of its era, to continually reinvent itself and its business with an entrepreneurial spirit that drove change, anticipating and shaping emergent markets.

  • Chairman

    Image Choice Inc.

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    1993 — 1997 (4 years )

    Image Choice, Inc., in 1993 started as a wholly owned MicroAge, Inc subsidiary anticipating the availability of standardized workflow application platforms (within that decade) that would “Change the Way the World Works”. The company quickly became the first and leading imaging integrator to successfully leverage the reseller channel in the planning, implementation and support of FileNet and IBM lower cost industrial-strength, midrange document imaging and workflow system solutions integrated into the distributed computing environments of mid-to-large organizations. Standardized workflow platforms did not develop as anticipated limiting the scope of the company which was sold in 1999.
