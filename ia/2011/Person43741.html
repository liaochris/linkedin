Tom Antos's Summary

Tom is a filmmaker with a background in special effects/animation.

Upon graduating from high school, Tom secured a position as an animator. In the years that followed, Tom gained valuable experience in animation and various aspects of special effects production.

Although Tom has been experimenting with the camera for many years, since 2001 his efforts as a filmmaker have become decisively more focused. That year he directed the shorts "The Assistants" (presented at 2001 World Film Festival in Montreal) and "The Screwdriver Plan".

In 2002, he followed up with "Dead Quiet". More importantly, that same year he shot "Under Black Skies" - his first feature film. As any independent project, this film offered plenty of challenges, especially since this was a World War II piece. Fortunately, his experience in the field of special effects made it possible to provide high production value with limited resources. After that Tom Directed/Produced five more short films, and several music videos.

Tom's hope is to offer films & videos that the audience finds both entertaining and insightful.
