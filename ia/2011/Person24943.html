Founder and President of MSOE SAE SuperMileage Vehicle Program

SAE International

Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry

September 2008 – June 2010 (1 year 10 months)

From my prior involvement in similar projects, I introduced the SAE SuperMileage program to MSOE in the fall of 2008. Prior to this program, all SAE projects/competition builds were only open to students of senior status to actively take part in. After spending nearly 12 months organizing space, forming a team, and fundraising, the fabrication phase of the project began in the fall of 2009.

The project was ultimately completed May 2010 in preparation for the 2010 SAE SuperMileage Vehicle Challenge hosted by the Eaton Corporation in Marshall, Michigan. Here we competed against a total of 32 competitors from across the world including India, China, Canada, France, and Dubai.

A list of the more notable accomplishments include:

• First project led solely by underclassmen
• Raised over $60,000 in total product/service/monetary support
• Presented project at 2009 Global Energy Summit in Milwaukee, Wisconsin
• Only team to pass rigorous technical inspection first try
• Only team to fully complete all 8/8 allotted test runs without failures or break-downs
• Scored perfect 100/100 on verbal report at competition
• Organized and held 'SuperMileage Days' at Discovery World Museum July 2010 with over 3000 visitors
• Earned 11th place (out of total 33 teams) at competition
• Built sustainable program which is being progressively continued at MSOE
• Changed structure of MSOE competition design teams (all projects now actively encourage and include underclassmen)
