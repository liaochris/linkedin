Elizabeth Alvarado’s Summary

Elizabeth Alvarado is a professional in the field of non-profit and print communications. In the past five years, she has gained research, writing, and editing skills working for a local newspaper and national magazine; outreach experience helping organize campaigns and events for the United Nations Foundation (UNF); and project management skills coordinating a communications department—from writing fact sheets, brochures and web content, to developing message platforms and communications strategies—at the Grassroots Business Fund (GBF).

Parallel to her communications work, Elizabeth is a mentor and recruiter for the North Jersey Media Group’s Journalism Workshop. She mentors high school juniors and seniors to pursue careers in journalism by hosting a week of classes and workshops at the S.I. Newhouse School of Communications, where students meet undergraduates and professors and report on events and activities.

Elizabeth holds an M.A. in Latin American and Caribbean studies from New York University and a B.A. in print journalism from Syracuse University. In her free time, Elizabeth volunteers with after-school programs such as Horton's Kids and the Latino Student Fund, and enjoys running, visiting NYC, and traveling to new places. So far, Elizabeth has traveled throughout Europe, India and the Caribbean.
