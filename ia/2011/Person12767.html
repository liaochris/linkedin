Duncan Byers’s Summary

LAW PRACTICE:

My practice focuses on intellectual property protection and complex litigation. This includes Trademark and Patent prosecution, as well as handling related matters in the Federal Administrative Courts of the United States Patent and Trademark Office.

My firm handles litigation matters in both state and federal court, as well as appellate courts at the state and federal level.

BACKGROUND/EDUCATION:

I attended Old Dominion University (BS Chemistry, 1997, MS Chemistry, 1999). I received my JD degree from Washington and Lee University School of Law in Lexington, Virginia.

Prior to entering law practice, I spent nine years in the United States Navy, including tours of duty on two guided missile destroyers homeported in Charleston, South Carolina and with a helicopter squadron based at the Norfolk Naval Air Station. I spent several years working as a commercial and industrial electrician, and nineteen years of experience as a volunteer firefighter and emergency medical technician.

Duncan Byers’s Specialties:

Intellectual Property (Patents, Trademarks, Copyrights)
Civil Litigation / Complex Litigation/Appellate Matters

Intellectual Property prosecution/protection nationally and internationally;

Complex litigation services in the Commonwealth of Virginia as well as Federal Litigation both in Virginia and outside the state and Appellate work.

We have additional admissions in DC and Maryland.
