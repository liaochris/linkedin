Kai D. Wright’s Summary

As an award-winning freelance strategist, Kai uses his 5+ years of experience to help build bridges between consumers & Fortune 100 companies. He's all about building sustainable brand relationships that span online & offline channels.

Kai has worked in strategy & account planning departments for Organic & Ogilvy serving clients such Bank of America, P&G, IBM, SAP and Johnson & Johnson.

He thinks like a behavioral planner but acts like a business consultant - combining a deep understanding of consumer behavior with a watchful eye on emerging business opportunities. He has advised clients about digital strategy, social media management, and integrated marketing. In the process, he facilitates custom/secondary research, briefs creative teams, and manages live channels. In 2010, he won a Gold Ogilvy award as well as a Grand Ogilvy award for strategy & research innovation - both from the Advertising Research Foundation (ARF).

Before advertising, he spent three years in nonprofit at the Collegiate Scholars Program as a project manager. In his role as Assistant Program Manager / Program Advisor, he was responsible for managing an entrepreneurship program sponsored by the Goldman Sachs Foundation and the University of Chicago Booth School of Business. Duties included day-to-day program management, grant reporting, and program evaluation.

As a former military brat, Kai considers himself a global citizen. To date, he has lived in the US, Germany & France. Italy is his favorite country. In his spare time, he maintains a marketing blog with a Gen Y perspective and teaches marketing at a charter high school.

He holds a master's degree in Strategic Communications from Columbia University and a bachelor's degree in Economics from the University of Chicago.
