Barry Jenkins’s Summary

Think of me as an Organizational Physician and Architect, someone leaders call when there are problems—big problems—that have to be solved where people, process, know-how, and liability converge. It’s not easy to orchestrate change at the scale of large business, but that’s what I do best. And, there is lots of change happening!

What motivates me?
I thrive on solving problems and building healthier organizations.

What does the work I do look like?
When I’m playing the role of Organizational Physician, I help diagnose the most important priorities to address in the organization, suggest a change-management plan, and then guide the organization through the necessary change. Once the problem is understood and it’s time to solve it, I switch roles to Organizational Architect. Here, I create organizational design, process design and improvement, and large-scale change management programs—so that what gets changed stays changed.

What makes me different--and better?
Both my educational and professional background is full of interesting experiences which have given me a very broad perspective. My ability to see things from different points of view (for example, my undergraduate degree is in Accounting but my graduate degree is in Social Work) lets me create approaches to change that let people from different departments converge on a common goal. Having worked with for-profit and non-profit organizations, in both large and small organizations, gives me an insider’s view of team and organizational dynamics that I rely on regularly. I share traditional values, but I am not afraid to challenge the status quo when that’s the right thing to do. I appreciate the value of process for organizations and to people and I’m also capable of creating out-of-the-box solutions when called upon. I am known as being relaxed amidst chaos and for being a long-fuse in a short-fuse world.
