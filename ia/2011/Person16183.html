Glenn Head's Summary

I began working with Tartoosh in November of 09. Our objective is the rebuilding of sustainable futures on Indian lands. The company is led by Robert Martin, former National Ombudsman for the EPA in Washington from 1992 - 2002. (continues under experience)

I have been engaged in the profession of Network Marketing since 1995. In all of my years of business consulting (since 1979), this collaborative, win-win business model offers the greatest potential for personal growth and financial independence.

In 2002, I co-founded Networking University (NetworkingTimes.com/university) to offer much-needed, easily accessible training so that anyone could learn how to be successful in this profession, regardless of which company they represent.

My wife Marian and I also serve the network marketing profession as featured contributors to “The Ultimate Guide to Network Marketing” with our chapter, “VITAL Signs of a Healthy Business.” Additionally we offer an educational PowerPoint presentation for those considering a part-time business offer or career change called “Network Marketing: The New Paradigm in Business.” We really enjoyed doing this for my friend Phil at What's Next Career Transition Services in Denver.

Since Feb. 08, I have been collaborating on a new business model for the networking profession based on the work of MIT’s Peter Senge and Robert Fritz. (creatingourfutures.net/10033). The outcome will be a new way to leverage the multi-level compensation strategy used by many companies to further one's own personal business brand.

Because of the residual income network marketing provides for us, I am able to follow my entrepreneurial drive to engage in new business start-ups.

Specialties

Leverage! I have leaned to leverage myself to create multi-generational family income and I show others how to do the same.

Environmental Services industry

January 2009 – Present (2 years 3 months)

In September 09, Marian and I co-founded a non-profit called The National Environmental Public Advocates (The NEPA). This organization (also led by Robert Martin) is developing a web-based diagnostic tool to allow communities across the United States to determine their level of exposure to toxic pollution. You may not know that one in four of Americans lives within five miles of a federally recognized toxic waste site. We intend to provide a tool that will enable citizen advocates in communities across America to stand up for themselves and face this issue head-on.

And then there’s The Big Green Rabbit! This children’s show on public television is all about kids’ relationships to their health and nature. As of January 09 it’s in over 40 million homes. If you have children ages 3-7, you have to see this show. BigGreenRabbit.com.

Let me know if you would like to collaborate on any of these great projects!
