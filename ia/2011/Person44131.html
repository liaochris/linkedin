Trafton Esler's Summary

I was born in Amarillo, TX, but my family and I moved frequently so my formative years were spent in Massachusetts and Illinois. I love technology - probably a result of my curiosity about space exploration and related interest in science fiction. I always say to people, “I will walk on the Moon – even if I have to build my own rocket to get there.”

I graduated with a degree in Computer Engineering from the University of Illinois where I also attained certification in Technology Commercialization. My whole life – academic, personal and professional has been all about technology and business. My first foray into the world of computers began when I decided at age 13 that I needed my own computer. Without funds to buy one, I built my own. I went on to sell my services repairing computers for my neighbors. Ever since, I’ve been looking for ways to turn creative technical ideas into a paycheck.

To date, I’ve worn a variety of hats. I worked with electronic medical charting as a software engineer at Cerner Corporation. I worked on chip design for an enterprise processor as an engineer at Intel. I’ve even had the opportunity to merge my interests in technology, business and health care as a member of the product strategy team at Strata Decision Technology, a provider of financial analytics software to health care organizations. In my last position, I fine tuned business processes for clients as a consultant for CONTAX, an SAP Channel Partner in the United States and Canada.

I believe that technology has fundamentally changed how people interact and how business should operate. After completion of my MBA in May 2011, I plan to join a company that is engaging the need for "social business" processes and the software that will support them.

My hobbies are varied, ranging from mountain biking and downhill skiing to boating and SCUBA diving. I also enjoy running and in July 2010 I completed my first marathon in Missoula, Montana.
