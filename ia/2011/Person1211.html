Albert Trexler’s Summary

Worked with the Pennsylvania General Assembly to pass numerous revisions to the Pennsylvania CPA Statute included a dying class accountancy provision, mandating a continuing education and peer review requirements for CPA relicensure, a 150 – semester-hour requirement of entry level education to sit for the CPA examination, and mobility of the CPA certificate and license across state boundaries.

Albert Trexler’s Specialties:

All aspects on non-profit organizational structure including governance, structure, budgeting, ethics enforcement, and continuing professional education.

    (Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    March 1969 — Present (42 years )

    Chief Executive Officer and secretary of a 19,000 individual member professional association with offices in three locations (Harrisburg, Philadelphia & Pittsburgh) and a staff of 52.

    Major Duties
    • Developing, shaping and implementing policies established by the Council, PICPA Foundation Board, Executive Committee, Scholarship Fund Trustees, Insurance Fund Trustees, and the CPA-PAC (PICPA’s Political Action Committee).

    • Direct staff activities and responsible for an annual budget of $8,000,000.
    • Ensure a current and up-to-date knowledge and understanding of developments in the accounting profession by effectively maintaining ongoing liaison with other associations, recognized leaders in the field, and federal and state regulatory bodies.
