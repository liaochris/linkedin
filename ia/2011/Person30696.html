  • Mechanical/Reliability Engineer

    Kidde Aerospace and Defense

    (Airlines/Aviation industry)

    January 2006 — November 2009 (3 years 11 months)

    Contributed value-added engineering expertise for product development and manufacture of fire protection systems for commercial and military aviation, and for military ground vehicle applications.
    • DFMEA leader established risks for every potential failure and implemented key characteristics to mitigate critical risks of new products. Boeing recognized Kidde as a leader implementing the new key characteristic requirements.
    • Identified new 3M polyester label and specific ink jet printing that passed legibility requirements for military vehicles, military aircraft and commercial aircraft. Created one test report that included test requirements from military vehicle, military aircraft and commercial aircraft with additional robustness testing for sunshine and rain exposure, thus replacing the metal embossed label for annual cost savings of $75,000.
    • Helped production ramp from 60 to 240 military extinguishers per shift. Sorted casting per visual acceptance criteria, created Go/No-go fixtures to quickly accept or reject parts and helped one supplier eliminate casting porosity.
    • Defined safety considerations for each component of the P-8A Poseidon aircraft fire protection system. Engaged military to conduct additional tests to ensure maintainer safety during transport of extinguishers.
    • Wrote Gulfstream G250 maintenance steering group analysis, MSG-3, of fire protection system to identify required maintenance tasks by addressing hidden functional failures along with eliminating redundant and ineffective tasks.
    • Reviewed field data yielding two components that required design and/or processing improvements to achieve defined aircraft reliability requirements.
    • Obtained field data from a new pressure switch, PS, supplier to determine the PS failure rate showing PS reliability being better than the reliability prediction contained in NPRD-95.

  • Senior Engineer (air and fuel)

    Magneti Marelli

    (Privately Held; Automotive industry)

    1998 — 2005 (7 years )

    Lead Design & Development Engineer for an international company of components for the automotive sector responsible for 4 automotive airflow product lines and 1 marine fuel product line.
    • Spearheaded production solutions and design changes for low-to-high production lines.
    • Lead Design Engineer of first USA plastic automotive throttle body. Used statistical results, purchased test equipment, defined repeatable processes and modified one core pin to increase production throughput from 82% to 92% first time throughput with 886 assemblies minimum per shift.
    • Reduced the idle airflow of plastic throttle body by 75% to achieve Chrysler desired CAFE standards. Transferred a technology from Marelli, Italy, and incorporated an environmentally friendly latex coating. Optimized the application of the coating through design of experiments.
    • Collaborated with various departments to install the first paint spraying booth into the existing production line within 3 weeks to paint black plastic parts with black paint, and to cure the paint prior to the emission’s critical idle airflow adjustment station at 886 parts per shift with 92% first time throughput.
    • As Lead Engineer, included customer in collaborative design and failure analysis meetings, designing an innovative water cooled marine fuel pump module that lifts fuel from a 300 gallon tank, filters the fuel to 5 micron, and pumps fuel at 48 gallons per hour with a 46 psi fuel rail pressure. Made customer responsible for seawater corrosion and efficiently implemented their solutions.
    • Selected fuel pumps based upon cost, statistical performance and reliability data. Established several designs of experiments to resolve and optimize issues resulting in customer’s most reliable marine fuel pumping system.
    • Reduced costs by 15% on three throttle bodies. Designed safety critical multifunction plastic part to eliminate three metal parts.

    • Revitalized existing printer by creating a patented magnetic ink character recognition reader.
    • Conducted theoretical mechanical analysis for the entire IBM RTP site using FEA that solved complex problems and successfully resolved lawsuits.
