Robert C. Haight, MPA's Experience

Assistant Director of Residence Life

Colorado State University-Pueblo

Educational Institution; Higher Education industry

July 2008 – Present (2 years 9 months)

Duties include creating a holistic living, learning environment that enhances a sense of
community, through providing co-curricular and extra-curricular programs and activities.
Directly supervises a student staff of thirteen resident assistants, fifteen student front
desk assistants, two residence hall directors, and an administrative assistant. Manages
educational and social events that are provided by the hall staff, ensuring the programs
are in the required learning domains. Maintains fiscal responsibility over all programs
conducted in the residence halls. Ensures the health and safety of over 900 residents in
four residence halls. Assists in maintaining standards of conduct appropriate to residential
living and adjudicates students in matters of student code of conduct violations and/or
university housing policies. Assists in the development of policies and procedures in a

growing residence life program. Promotes the personal development and education of
student and professional staff. Advises residents and hall staff in matters concerning
academic work, social needs, and assists in crisis intervention. Responsible for the billing
of room and board charges from all residential students.

Significant accomplishments:
• Conducted qualitative and quantitative research to determine the factors that
predict if a student will return to university housing.
• Developed a programming model, based upon the Learning Reconsidered
framework and CAS, which allow resident assistants to provide programs to
residents that aid in the development of the whole student and provides a
mechanism for formative assessment.
• Successfully implemented an online version of a housing management system to
allow staff to view current assignments and report incidents online.
• Assisted in the successful opening and operation of three new residence halls in
2009/2010 and 2010/2011, increasing the on-campus residential capacity to 1290
students.
