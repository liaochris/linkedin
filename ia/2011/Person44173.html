After college I spent half a decade living in Japan and returned to Iowa on July 13, 1993, the day of the great flood of Des Moines. I began the practice of law in 1996. In 2002 I was called to the bar of England and Wales, becoming one of a handful of practicing lawyers who are also English Barristers. Yes, we still wear the wig. Most recently I have obtained my Certified Fraud Examiner's designation. My volunteer time is spent on my duties as the current Chair of the Iowa Supreme Court's Attorney Disciplinary Board and as Secretary of the Des Moines Radio Amateur Association.

As an attorney-at-law, I pride myself on providing four functions for my clients regardless of the type of legal matter they come to me with or the amount at issue in their case. These four functions are advisor, advocate, negotiator and evaluator:

* As their advisor, I provide them with an informed understanding of their legal rights and obligations and explain their practical implications.

* As their advocate, I zealously assert their positions under the rules of the adversary system.

* As their negotiator, I seek a result advantageous to them but consistent with requirements of honest dealings with others; and

* As their evaluator, I examine their legal affairs and report about them to my clients or to others.

My education, training and experience as set forth on this profile all combine to help me fulfill these four functions for my clients. If you have any questions or want to know more, please don't hesitate to contact me nor offer an invite.
