Marc Thomas’s Summary

Marc Thomas is the founding partner of Thorium Capital Ventures & Consulting, LLC (TCVC). Thorium specializes in deal sourcing, structured financing, and operational effectiveness. TCVC is designed to acquire, invest and/or advise medium size companies primarily located in the U.S. and Canada.

While our partners have been trained at some of the world's leading organizations, each member of the team has applied his/her experience to improving middle market enterprises, and possesses first-hand knowledge of the unique challenges faced by companies of this size. We embrace these challenges, as well as the opportunities they create, and look forward to enhancing the growth and prosperity of the companies in which we acquire, invest and or advise.

Marc is a former President & CEO of GE Aviation Materials, LP and has held numerous senior leadership and board positions within GE. Prior to joining GE, Marc was a consultant with McKinsey & Company’s Houston office concentrating in the energy, consumer products, and telecommunication industries. Additionally, Marc spent over 10 years in the U.S. Army serving first as a Special Forces Operational Detachment (“A” Team) Commander in Africa and the Middle East, and later as an Assistant Professor of Engineering Management at the United States Military Academy, West Point, NY.

In 1994, Marc was appointed by the President as a White House Fellow and subsequently assigned to the Office of the Vice President as Special Advisor for National Security Affairs. While at the White House, Marc’s portfolio included: U.N. Peace Keeping; Counter-Terrorism; Refugee Affairs; Global Environment; and Africa. After the White House, Marc became a Legislative Assistant to the United States Senate focusing on the Armed Services; Environment; and Public Works matters.

Marc's goal is direct and simplistic - help others whenever possible. He currently resides in the Dallas-Ft. Worth area and is working on his first novel.

Marc Thomas’s Specialties:

Mergers and Acquisitions; Contract Negotiations; Supply Chain Management (Sourcing Operations); Quality (Six Sigma); Lean Manufacturing; and Government Liaison.
