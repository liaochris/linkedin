Bernard Martin's Summary

Mr. Martin has a unique background. Starting his career in finance and management & sales consulting he has first hand management experience in manufacturing, channels programming, distribution and manufacturer's agency as well as branding, business intelligence, & social media marketing, which gives him a very unique skill set.

Mr. Martin has an undergraduate degree from the University of Pittsburgh in Political Science and Economics and worked on his MBA in San Francisco at the University of Phoenix with a 4.0 GPA

Mr. Martin was the VP of Sales & Marketing for Toolex and managed all aspects of their global workholding product rollout. He later moved to California and worked for Western Tool & Supply, one of the largest, privately held, cutting tool distributors in the US.

In 2000, Mr. Martin started his own "rep" agency and quickly became the #1 sales agency and/or the #1 growth agency for many of his principals managing channels programs for approximately 65% of of the North American market.

In late 2008, Mr. Martin became President of Highlander Tool, an start-up industrial distributor based in Johnstown, PA. Starting from scratch he negotiated with over 180 manufacturers to develop a unique brand & product offering.

In July 2010 Mr. Martin formed Rapid Production Marketing, a marketing and consulting agency working within the industrial and automotive sectors.

Please contact Mr Martin about Social Media training & implementation, speaking engagements, marketing projects or questions at www.rpmconsultants.us

Managing Directorre, Cortile

"The Cortile" @ Pittsburgh Vintage Grand Prix

Automotive industry

October 2007 – Present (3 years 6 months)

In 2007 proposed the idea of an All-Italian auto show as the featured “Marque of the Year” for the 26th Annual event. Due to the overwhelming success of the "Marque of the Year" celebration, developed and planned the entire Cortile Car show event including team recruitment, event location, sponsors, event planning and the creation of the website.

2008 saw the introduction of the "Cortile", and an international branding effort, featuring stunning examples of Italian macchina from the likes of Ferrari, Maserati, Lamborghini Alfa Romeo, Lancia, Abarth, and even Vespa. Created a very unique event within the Pittsburgh Vintage Grand Prix that features opera singers, an art show, and museum level displays of racing memorabelia from private collections.

The Pittsburgh Vintage Grand Prix Association is a 501(c)(3) non-profit organization that holds the only vintage race in North America on city streets. It is the region's premier 10 day summer event for over 250,000 people that combines charitable fundraising with car shows and vintage sports car racing on city streets. Since 1983 this volunteer-driven event has raised over $2.5 million for the Autism Society of Pittsburgh & Allegheny Valley School.
