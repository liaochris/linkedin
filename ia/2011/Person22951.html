Julian Richardson's Summary

I am working on both symbolic and machine learning applications of semantics and natural language processing in Bing, based at Powerset.

From June to December 2009 I led the Frontend and User Experience Group at Powerset, and from July 2008 to June 2009 I led the Natural Language Tools group here.

As a developer I believe in rapid prototyping for exploring problems and problem solving approaches, in careful and documented architecture and design for substantial new components, and extreme care when developing or changing production code.

As a UX lead I believe in the importance of early user studies coupled with data derived from instrumented prototypes/production systems.

As a researcher and manager I believe strongly in the importance of data and its documented analysis for decision making.

I believe in management which is based on clear communication, evidence-based goals and targets, lightweight standards and best practices, matching developer skills to problems, and respect for the individual.

Before coming to Powerset I was a researcher at NASA Ames Research Center, where amongst other things I was principal investigator of a $3.5M NASA grant, leading a team of researchers from half a dozen different institutions.

I have an undergraduate degree in mathematics from Cambridge University, and a masters and PhD in artificial intelligence from the University of Zurich. I worked as a researcher at the Ecole Normale Superieure in Paris, the University of Zurich, and in the Division of Informatics at the University of Edinburgh, then as a lecturer at Heriot-Watt University in Edinburgh. In 2002 I was general chair of the Automated Software Engineering conference. I have published several magazine articles, and over 30 refereed journal, conference or workshop publications.

I am fluent in English, Spanish and French and speak some German.

In my spare time I build things using Arduino and AVR processors, and cut things up using a laser.
