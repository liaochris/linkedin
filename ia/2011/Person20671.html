Jessica Holt's Summary

Jessica is close to attaining her Masters degree in Human Factors in Information Design from Bentley University. She is currently gathering research for her thesis's literature review and finalizing the experimental design to support her hypothesis.

In her agency roles, Jessica has served clients using both interaction design skills, usability best practices, social media and brand strategy to optimize the experience. She has experience performing usability testing sessions, conducting and analyzing eye tracking studies, creating wireframes and prototypes. She also participated in usability study for Sun Microsystems and co-authored a paper from Proceedings of the third ACM conference on Recommender systems.

Specialties

Usability, User & Customer Experience, Prototyping, Usability Testing, Interaction Design, Design Thinking, User-Centered Design, Human Factors

Jessica Holt's Publications

  • Generating Transparent, Steerable Recommendations from Textual Descriptions of Items

    • Proceeding RecSys '09 Proceedings of the third ACM conference on Recommender systems
    • 2009
    Authors: Jessica Holt, Stephen J. Green, Paul Lamere, Jeffrey Alexander, François Maillet, Susanna Kirk, Jackie Bourque, Xiao-Wen Mak

    Article Summary: We propose a recommendation technique that works by collecting text descriptions of items and using this textual aura to compute the similarity between items using techniques drawn from information retrieval. We show how this representation can be used to explain the similarities between items using terms from the textual aura and further how it can be used to steer the recommender. We describe a system that demonstrates these techniques and we'll detail some preliminary experiments aimed at evaluating the quality of the recommendations and the effectiveness of the explanations of item similarity.


    My involvement: Participated in usability study for Sun Microsystems using the Bentley DUC testing facilities to conduct these tests. Co-author of paper from Proceedings of the third ACM conference on Recommender systems.
