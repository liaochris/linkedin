Ritu Verma's Summary

International development scholar and researcher with trans-disciplinary experience in Anthropology, International Development/Affairs, Civil Engineering and Project Management in East and Southern Africa and the Himalayan region. Interested in critical in-depth research, action and scholarship on issues of vulnerability, marginalization and dispossession of land and natural resources as experienced by rural farmers, indigenous peoples and pastoralists, and in the context of increasing commercial pressures, elite capture, climate change and neo-liberal development policies. Special areas of interest: Kenya, Madagascar and Nepal.

Specialties

Natural Resource Management & Environment, Indigenous Knowledge & Rights, Land Rights and Relations in Tenure, Rural Development (Agriculture, Pastoralism, Food Security, Natural Resource Conflicts), Climate Change, Anthropology of Development, Anthropology of Science, International Affairs, Development Practitioner/Farmer Interface, Gender Analysis & Equity

Ritu Verma's Education

Ph.D., Anthropology/Anthropology of Development

2000 – 2009

Original and multi-sited fieldwork in Madagascar exploring the dynamics and effects of development projects, as well as examining the various types of disconnects that arise becuase of differences in social and working lives of development practitioners and rural farmers. The work is part of an emering field of study in the field of the anthropology of development, the anthropology of science and the anthropology of development practice and practitioners. Ph.D. thesis: Culture, Power and Development Disconnect in the Central Highlands of Madagascar. Ph.D. Supervisors (SOAS): Dr. Johan Pottier, Dr. Christopher Davis. Ph.D. Advisor (University of Sussex): Dr. James Fairhead.

Activities and Societies: Ph.D. Student Representative, Department of Anthropology and Anthropology Student Society Facilitator, Ethnography in the 3rd Millennium Seminars on Fieldwork Ethics and Interdisciplinarity
