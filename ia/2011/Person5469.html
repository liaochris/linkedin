Carlos Garchitorena's Summary

Language means everything to me.

Born with a love for words and blessed with an activist streak, I have been interested in the art of expressing all my life. My life's thesis is driving group interest in law and policy, current events, creativity, and economic culture. I wrote my first full-page tabloid at 10, and failed at a second one at 11.

My experience in speaking competitions and writing contests have molded my hearing, and listening. For better or worse, sincerity and urgency are key, followed by analysis of content and artfulness.

In what I consider now to be the last years of youthful rebellion, I resisted formal education, and pursued a writing career instead, entering newsprint, TV, marketing, and CSR investigation.

Today, the mainstream thrives in and is threatened by gigantic complexity. Today, I invest my energies in politics and technology, society and happiness, ending poverty and defending art.

You could say that I look for contradiction in a way a pig looks for truffles. You could say I crave it like a drug.

Because it is in conflict that we are busied the most in self-expression (and not to mention self-preservation) it is important that we find it, if not seek it--and seek it if not create it.

And while quality can be strained under pressure, and in wild, muddy waters, hackneyed writers elegance can locate, we need literary and moral guides—at the very least our own devices—for decoding the universe's reflective modes of expression.

George Bernard Shaw just recently told me, "The reasonable man adapts himself to the world. The unreasonable man adapts the world to himself. All progress depends upon the unreasonable man."

Thus, my adult rebellion begins.
