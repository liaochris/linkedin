Stephanie Smith's Summary

Stephanie Smith is a Harvard-trained architectural designer and social entrepreneur. Her ideas have made her “one to watch” in the worlds of architecture, technology, art and culture. Stephanie has been featured in many publications, including The New York Times, Dwell Magazine and GOOD; and on NPR (National Public Radio). In 2008 the Whitney Museum identified her as the designer/entrepreneur most actively taking the ideas of Buckminster Fuller into the 21st century.

Smith has a varied background as an entrepreneur, manager, systems and product designer, brand strategist and project manager. She’s also a published author.

She has very strong communication and organizational skills, and she excels at designing, implementing and managing infrastructures, processes, systems and products. She’s known as an accurate forecaster with a knack for branding; and an intuitive strategist who is extremely passionate about acting on what’s next in the worlds of technology, design and mainstream culture.

Smith has managed corporate teams as well as founded and run two startups.

Stephanie Smith's Experience

Founder/Platform Designer

WeCommune

Privately Held; Computer Software industry

March 2009 – February 2010 (1 year)

WeCommune, Inc. provided aggregated micro-communities the online tools and apps they needed to share resources (i.e. product and service sharing and swapping, bartering and group bartering, group buying). Stephanie raised $100k seed financing and launched the site in beta at the heart of the recession in 2009. WeCommune.com had about 1000 users when it was shut down for retooling. The company is currently on hold awaiting more financing and a technology partner.

Founder/Product Designer

Ecoshack

Architecture & Planning industry

September 2003 – April 2009 (5 years 8 months)

Ecoshack began as an experimental green design lab in the California desert and later became an LA-based design studio and product manufacturer. Ecoshack’s first shelter product, the Nomad Yurt, was featured in numerous blogs and publications, was named “the country’s best yurt” by Dwell magazine in 2007, and was nominated for a Cooper-Hewitt Peoples’ Design Award. The product was sold to the commercial hospitality market, which tanked in 2008 due to the recession. Stephanie closed the product line, but recently initiated a strategic partnership with a former vendor to bring the product back under a royalty arrangement. It’s set to re-launch spring 2011.

Design Instructor

Southern California Institute of Architecture

Architecture & Planning industry

1999 – 2008 (9 years)

Stephanie conducted brand and design seminars for graduate students, and was a critic at design schools including Harvard University Graduate School of Design, SCI-Arc, UCLA, and Art Center College of Design. She was also a design instructor at SCI-Arc. Course titles included Instant City: A Manufactured Kit for a Temporary Community, and Unplugged: An Off-the-grid Ecovillage for 20 Surfers.

Brand Strategist/Project Manager/Management Consultant

Stephanie Smith (Consultant)

Design industry

2000 – 2007 (7 years)

Stephanie worked directly for companies, and also for advertising agencies, offering strategic advice, brand and project management, and directional design concepts. Working for Ogilvy she spent a year as lead strategist and project manager on a global retail strategy for Motorola, and a directional store installation for Reebok. She also did consulting projects for IKEA, The Coca-Cola Company and Steelcase, as well as numerous smaller companies over a 7-year period, including social and environmental entrepreneurs and nonprofits like Los Ninos, a 25-year-old nonprofit organization that promotes nutrition and wellness in the San Diego/Tijuana border region.

Brand Manager/Project Manager

The Jerde Partnership

Partnership; Architecture & Planning industry

1997 – 2000 (3 years)

Stephanie spent three years with the influential experience designer Jon Jerde (famous for CityWalk, Bellagio and Canal City Hakata), working directly with Jon as a strategist, trend forecaster and brand manager; and also as a project manager on the firm’s large-scale retail and entertainment projects.

Stephanie Smith's Education

Harvard Graduate School of Design

M Arch, Architecture

1993 – 1997

Stephanie received her BA degree at Bennington College, spent two years living in London while attending the famed Architecture Association, and then was trained as an architect at Harvard. Her thesis explored the transformation of local communities in China as they adapted to a global economy (published as To Get Rich is Glorious by Taschen Press in Great Leap Forward, 2002). She lived in China to conduct research. After graduation she worked for her thesis advisor, Rotterdam-based, Pritzker-prize winning architect and theorist Rem Koolhass. As project leader of Koolhaas’ Harvard Project on the City (which later became AMO, the strategic consulting arm of his firm OMA), Stephanie oversaw design and research projects in China, Hong Kong and Germany, including print and exhibition designs for the international art fair documenta X.
