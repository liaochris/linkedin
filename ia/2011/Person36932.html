Rich Ullman's Summary

Rich Ullman is a digital media and marketing executive with deep experience at the forefront of emerging Internet media, dating to the first advertising on the Web. Most recently, he was Senior Vice-President, Marketing for Ripple6, Inc., the social marketing platform that he helped launch in 2006, and which was acquired in 2008 by Gannett Co. Inc., one of the world’s leading media companies. He has been a frequent speaker/presenter at social media and ad-marketing conferences, including OMMA, iMedia Connection, Digidays, and the ARF.

Mr. Ullman led marketing for About.com from start-up in 1997 as The Mining Company, through its IPO, re-branding to About, and it becoming the fifth most-visited property on the Web. He helped create and launch one of the first interactive ad agency groups at MVBMS Euro RSCG, where they built some of the first ever corporate Web sites and placed a number of the first ads ever on the Internet for Volvo, MCI, Philips and Club Med. He began his career in the agency business with McCaffrey and McCall as a media planner for Mercedes-Benz and Tiffany & Co., and he worried that his brand experience might be all downhill from there.

He is a graduate of The Ohio State University, and lives with his family in Montclair, NJ.
