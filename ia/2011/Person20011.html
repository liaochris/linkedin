Jeffrey Hall’s Experience

  • CEO; Publisher & Editor

    WESTSIDE TODAY, LLC

    (Publishing industry)

    January 1991 — Present (20 years 1 month)

    Founder of this community newsmagazine publishing company that services the upscale neighborhoods of Beverly Hills, Bel-Air, Brentwood, Santa Monica, Pacific Palisades and Malibu. See www.WestsideToday.com. Company co-produces a series of weekly e-mail blasts (see www.ThisWeekInBrentwood.com); we also host a popular speaker series (business mixers, political debates, "New Media Made Easy," etc.).

  • President -- Valley & Ventura County Editions

    LOS ANGELES TIMES

    (Newspapers industry)

    October 1988 — September 1991 (3 years )

    As first president of the Valley Edition, led all business operations for this regional edition and showed first market-share gains in ten years. Launched the Ventura County Edition. 250,000 circulation combined; 1,100 employees; over $100 million in annual revenues.

  • Vice President, Marketing Services

    LOS ANGELES TIMES

    (Newspapers industry)

    October 1988 — August 1991 (2 years 11 months)

    Recruited by the Los Angeles Times to help the company reinvent itself. Oversaw marketing research and strategic planning and served as the company's liaison to the advertising agency community. Ran the creative services departments (promotion, graphic arts, copywriting, audiovisual, special sections, etc.), producing the company's TV and radio spots, marketing materials, billboards, rack cards and movie trailers.

  • White House Fellow

    THE WHITE HOUSE

    (Government Administration industry)

    September 1987 — August 1988 (1 year )

    Selected as one of 12 from over 850 applicants nationwide to work with cabinet-level and agency officials for a year. Program is considered something of a "Rhodes Scholarship" for future leaders. Met almost all top leaders from all branches of government. Served in the Office of Policy Development in the White House, specializing in welfare reform. Legislation we worked on "ending welfare as we know it" eventually passed. Traveled extensively both in US and abroad.

  • Vice President, Marketing

    THE KANSAS CITY STAR

    (Newspapers industry)

    January 1979 — August 1987 (8 years 8 months)

    Began as a general assignment reporter and eventually headed up all marketing and revenue-producing areas for this Capital Cities/ABC newspaper. Credited with leading a "golden era" of growth and innovation in the eighties. 285,000 daily circulation; 400,000 Sunday. $140 million in annual revenues, $40 million annual expense budget, 650 employees. Turned around a highly troubled circulation department and was named Vice President, Circulation, in 1981. Promoted to Vice President of Marketing in 1982, overseeing all advertising sales, circulation, promotion, public relations and marketing research.

  • President & Founder

    May 1978 — January 1979 (9 months)

Later licensed the product for a subsequent run. A second product, "Skylab Protective Helmets," followed in 1979.
