Charlotte Fleming’s Summary

I graduated summa cum laude with the Masters in Library Science (M.L.S.) degree from the University of Kentucky in December 2008, and graduated magna cum laude with a B.A. in Psychology from Berea College in May 2005. I passed my M.L.S. comps exam in November 2008 “with Distinction”, and have maintained a 4.0 GPA in the Library and Information Science program since I began in May 2007. I am a member of ALA, KLA, ASIS&T, and the UK Library and Information Science Student Organization (LISSO), where I served as blog editor from August 2007 to May 2008. I currently volunteer part-time at the Madison County Public Library in Berea, Kentucky, and spent a year as a Graduate Assistant under the Head of Acquisitions at the University of Kentucky’s libraries. As a graduate student I received three scholarship awards, the Williena Burdine Broyles Scholarship, which I received twice (2007 and 2008) and the Hallie Day Blackburn Scholarship (2007). My interests in library science lie primarily in reference and instruction, especially information literacy instruction in academic libraries.
