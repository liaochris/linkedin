Ken Forrester's Summary

The profession of a Headhunter is a very mysterious one indeed.

A highly skilled industry expert, who is engaged by a major organization (the client company) to seek out a ‘difficult to find’ individual.

These talented individuals are in ‘high demand’. Most likely they are presently employed by the client company’s competitors; and are therefore much sought after by the client company.

In effect, a Headhunter poaches the ‘top talent’ away from a competitor and places that talent within the client company’s organization.

‘Top talent’ can often generate millions of dollars in revenue for a company – if these individuals are recruited by a Professional Headhunter and placed within a competitor, the ‘losing’ company will consequently lose these millions of dollars in revenue.

Utilizing the services of a Headhunter is a business investment.
Client companies will happily pay a placement fee of $30,000 in return for recruiting an individual into their firm who will generate $200,000 of profit every year. After 12 months, the client company has made a profit of $170,000; so it is simply ‘good business’.
