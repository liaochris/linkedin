Bonner Bellew’s Summary

Bonner Bellew began his career as a DJ on a syndicated “digital music” radio show for Clear Channel gaining notoriety as a pioneer in techno music. Since then he has worked in every form of media and now brings over 15 years of senior level creative experience to his role as the Executive Creative Director of Rocket XL.

Having recreated the image of multiple television networks, he produced countless award winning on-air/online national advertising campaigns for NBC, CBS, and The WB Television Networks while creating an online integrated promotions model to drive a new stream of significant revenue. Bonner moved into reality television and helped produce the number one rated television show in 2003, NBC’s “For Love or Money”. He then went on to create multiple music related show pilots for Warner Brothers and Universal Television including the first show to engage viewers to participate online.

Along with his television and online experience, Bonner has produced and edited award winning independent films for Artisan and New Line including the, critically acclaimed, festival hit “Scratch” and the gripping urban drama “Redemption”.

Since co-founding Rocket XL in 2004, Bonner has created successful creative campaigns for clients such as Toyota/Lexus, Samsung, HBO, Activision, Sony, P&G, Unilever, and The United Nations. Bonner is also responsible for helping conceptualize and create the architecture for “My Good Deed” and the United Nation's "Say No to Violence" campaign. He has also created the first social brand monitoring dashboard, enabling his clients to see dozens of key performance indicators including real-time brand health throughout each digital / social creative campaign.
