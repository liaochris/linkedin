  • Owner/Production Manager

    J & A Hinges

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Machinery industry)

    August 2002 — Present (6 years 3 months)

    J & A modifies commercial hinges to make the pin non-removable, a process called NRP or Non-Removable Pin. When a door is installed on a public building, for safety reasons, it needs to swing to the outside of the building. When the door swings outward (one pushes the door to exit) the butt (knuckles) of the hinge is exposed to the outside of the building. If the hinges are not NRPed then the pins can be removed with a hammer and punch, which separates the two sides of the hinge and permits the door to be removed from the building. Once the hinges are NRPed a stainless steel allen-head cup point set screw is locked against a groove in the pin and it cannot be removed from the hinge. So essentially the NRPed hinge is used as an anti-burglary measure.
