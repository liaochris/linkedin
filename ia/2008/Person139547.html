Neil Levine’s Summary

BMG Label Group, the Bertelsmann-owned half of music giant Sony BMG, is launching a new incubator label for its fledgling rap and hip-hop artists.
The imprint, to be known as Battery Records, will act as a feeder for BMG's RCA and Zomba divisions, signing and growing promising talent not ready to cross over to larger mainstream audiences.
It will also oversee the early-stage development of some acts signed by labels including RCA's J Records or Zomba's Jive Records.
Neil Levine, the former head of urban music for EMI's Capitol Music Group, will lead the effort in the newly created role of Battery's senior vice president and general manager.
Label execs familiar with the situation said the new structure will give BMG, headed by Barry Weiss, greater flexibility when it comes to signing and promoting rap and hip-hop acts.
Urban-focused labels will often sign 10 to 15 new acts a year on top of a slate of returning artists - forcing hard choices on how to prioritize marketing efforts.
Battery will carry a roster of around a dozen acts, with releases distributed and marketed through Sony BMG's independent distribution arm, RED.
"This will provide a broader platform and offer a greater margin of success for our evolving urban artists and new artists in general," said Tom Carrabba, Zomba Label Group's executive vice president and general manager.
The formation of Battery also is designed to help further solidify the presence of RED, which has been historically heavy on rock and pop acts.
