    What is The Third Chamber?
    The Third Chamber is a shadow parliament in the Netherlands for 120 Dutch nationals and 30 representatives from developing countries. It deals with international development. The members work on proposals to improve international cooperation and they discuss with Dutch politicians. In addition, members of The Third Chamber work to inform the Dutch public about all aspects of development cooperation.

    What does a Third Chamber member do?
    All members of The Third Chamber work on at least one proposal. Those proposals deal with Dutch development cooperation and other internationale issues. Are globalisation and free trade good or is there another answer to the economic troubles of Africa? Should the EU end its agricultural subsidies and what is the best way to do that? Does business have a role to play in international development? The millennium development goals of the United Nations are the framework for The Third Chamber.
