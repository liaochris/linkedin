Jason Eden’s Summary

Perhaps my most significant recent accomplishment is the development of VMware's PSO eLearning department from scratch. In just over six months I researched tools, techniques, and design methodologies and developed a unique rapid eLearning development process. We define eLearning as being driven by "guided demonstrations" followed by interactive simulations, and where appropriate, scored assessment simulations and quizzes.

In addition to full-time content developers, I have also developed a methodology to leverage off-platform instructors as content developers to significantly increase our content production capacity by Q1 2008.

Part of our methodology separates content development and technical editing. This allows us to leverage relatively expensive content developers to get more content produced and use relatively inexpensive editors to perform tasks such as audio/video synchronization and callout generation, and low-to-mid cost editors for actual video manipulation and product finalization.

I also recently pioneered an interview mentoring process to leverage off-platform instructors as interviewers for instructor candidates. Using a 3-point system that I developed (one-part ideology, 2-parts methodology) we have dramatically increased the effectiveness of many of the instructors that have gone through the program, resulting in fewer bad recommendations and more thorough input about candidates before a decision is made to bring them in for a test teach.

I possess a strong professional services background that includes experience in technical training and instructor-led course development, technical certification item writing and review, and consulting.

I will earn my Executive MBA in December 2008 from the Olin Business School, Washington University in St. Louis, MO.
