Dan Mahoney’s Summary

Dan Mahoney provides a range of strategic communications services to clients in CSG's Financial Services, Environmental Sustainability and Public Affairs practices.

While at CSG|PR Dan has worked with some of the nation’s leading renewable energy and financial services companies including those in investment banking, asset management, real estate and private equity. He has also played a significant role in media relations programs for several public policy and political initiatives in the state of Colorado. For his clients, Dan regularly secures significant press coverage from coveted media outlets, including Wall Street Journal, The New York Times, CNN, NBC, ABC, FOX News, NPR, CNBC, C-SPAN, BBC, Guardian, Bloomberg, Reuters, AP and US News & World Report, as well as key industry trade media.

As an active member in the community, Dan currently serves on the communication/public relations task force for the 2008 Democratic National Convention Host Committee and on the Communications Committee for the American Diabetes Association, as well as volunteers with other non-profit organizations and political causes.

Dan received his bachelor's degree in Political Science from the University of Northern Colorado and has been a regular columnist for Colorado Biz Magazine.

    2008 Rocky Mountain Round Table

    (Public Relations and Communications industry)

    August 2008 — August 2008 (1 month)

    Coincident with the convention, from August 25 through 28, the Denver 2008 Convention Executive Committee has launched a series of ten issue-oriented, nonpartisan roundtables to be held in the Denver Performing Arts Complex, collectively entitled the 2008 Rocky Mountain Roundtable. Participants in the Roundtables will be drawn from the thousands of dignitaries scheduled to be in Denver during the convention week, from individuals across the country and the world by invitation, and from citizens in the local Denver-area community. The Roundtables are intended to take advantage of a unique opportunity to create profound dialogue on many of the most critical issues of the day, including health, emergency preparedness, education, philanthropy, technology, retirement security, transportation infrastructure, women in leadership, energy and climate change, and international relations.
