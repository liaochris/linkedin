Julie Fanselow’s Summary

I am a widely published writer. My byline has appeared in magazines including Sunset, American Heritage, United Airlines Hemispheres, Parade, Entertainment Weekly, Via, and dozens more. I am the author of numerous books including Traveling the Lewis & Clark Trail and Idaho Off the Beaten Path. I've also completed writing projects for organizations including Lonely Planet, Smithsonian, the Wyoming Division of Tourism (via Miles Media), Mobil Travel Guides, and many more.

Since 2002, I have worked extensively for the Study Circles Resource Center, where I now serve as a writer and online organizer. SCRC, soon to be renamed Everyday Democracy, works nationwide to promote creative community dialogue and change. You can read many of my articles for SCRC at the website linked below. I am currently helping SCRC launch a blog, DemocracySpace.org, and working with community organizers to use the Internet to advance their work and collaborate with others.

I also have worked in electoral politics, serving as blog manager and netroots coordinator for the Larry Grant for Congress campaign (ID-01) 2005-2006. Larry was named a national netroots candidate and the Grassroots for Grant blog was named the region's best campaign blog by the Northwest Progressive Institute. I also helped 2008 U.S. Senate candidate Larry LaRocco kick start his campaign during the spring and summer of 2007.

Please contact me for more information about Write the Change. I am always eager to put my experience and my entrepreneurial spirit to work for organizations that promote justice, creativity, innovation, and work-life balance.
