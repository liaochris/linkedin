Alan Khalfin’s Summary

Alan Daniel Khalfin is currently the CFO and Director of A&R of MixMatchMusic, Ltd., a social music service he founded with his roommates from UCLA. He received his Juris Doctor from Santa Clara University School of Law in 2007, as well as a certificate from the Cornell Law School Institute of International and Comparative Law in 2006. His studies focused on intellectual property, entertainment, and internet law and in his third year, he wrote a research note entitled “Digitalization and its discontents: Safeguarding artists’ rights in the digital music age.” Alan was president of the Entertainment and Sports Law Association at Santa Clara.

Alan received his Bachelors of Arts in French and Francophone Studies in 2004 from UCLA, and an International Diploma from the Paris Institute of Political Studies (Sciences-Po) in 2003. Alan is a musician and a producer, currently recording under the name Gavroche. He has played music since childhood, and plays drums, percussion, guitar, keyboards, as well as sings. He has served as a business consultant to The Bayliens, Throw Me the Statue, and Cait La Dee. Alan was also a radio dj for KSCU (103.3 FM, San Jose) from 2005-2006, spinning several shows per week, and a production assistant for Noa Noa Music in 2004 (Los Angeles). Alan has traveled extensively throughout the world and speaks fluent Russian and French.

Alan Khalfin’s Specialties:

Entertainment/music contracts and copyright law. Consulting regarding music industry business, strategy and A&R, including digital monetization and fan engagement in the web 2.0 and social music space. Consulting for artists as to how to commercially optimize their art in an era where the traditional value chain seems to be broken.
