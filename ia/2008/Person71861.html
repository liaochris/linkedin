  • External Consultant Project Manager

    Neath Port Talbot County Borough Council

    (Government Agency; 5001-10,000 employees; Government Administration industry)

    2003 — April 2004 (1 year)

    Neath Port Talbot asked me to manage design and procurement of a corporate Document Management System. Previous attempts to introduce departmental Document Management Systems had failed due to poor usability.
    I led the Requirements Gathering and produced an Invitation to Tender, and managed the Tender process with supplier conference, responses to supplier queries etc. From previous work I was conscious of the need to exclude early the least credible suppliers but also make the process palatable to credible suppliers so that the customer had a range of genuine, well-developed proposals to choose from.
    It became clear during the Requirements gathering & Business Analysis phase that the system would need to conform also to emerging government Records Management requirements. I had to halt the process to understand and introduce these new requirements.

  • External Consultant Programme Manager

    City and County of Swansea

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; Government Administration industry)

    July 2003 — March 2004 (9 months)

    Swansea asked me to define a Standard Operating Environment and develop & implement a programme to deliver it to all departments on ~70 sites. We replaced file, print and email servers, implemented an Active Directory and built an infrastructure to deploy applications and manage licenses throughout the authority. We replaced ~4500 desktop systems with new hardware, prioritising by end of lease dates and minimising personnel, equipment and software license costs.
    I was not responsible for most detailed technical design, but did argue through many important points which would define the IT cost structure going forward.
    My firm was also consulting on possible outsourcing of IT, so the people I relied upon were worried about their futures. I had to stay independent of that other project and work around its effects on my team.
    I led the first third of the desktop replacement programme while training the staff project manager to lead the remaining two thirds of sites.

  • External Consultant

    British Museum

    (Government Agency; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry)

    January 2001 — December 2003 (3 years)

    Led procurement of a long-term Network supplier for the British Museum, defining standards for networking, and led the supplier through cabling a refurbished wing and installing new core switches and fibre trunking around the Museum site.
    The new vendor had to meet the Museum's unique aesthetic & legal requirements and I also had to set up structures to monitor compliance and correct non-compliance.
    European Union and the United Nations procurement rules both apply while UK Government rules require a Gateway scheme whereby at several stages before committments take place the business case for the expenditure is re-assessed. This project also worked through some choppy commercial waters where bidders were being investigated for possible wrong-doing in other markets. I was required to be brave and forthright on occasion and diplomatic on other occasions, and to meet the Museum's practical needs, the public expectation of probity and accountability and the commercial need of contract.
