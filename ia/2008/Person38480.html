Clemens Utschig - Utschig’s Summary

Before leaving Europe and moving to sunny California in October 2005, I worked for Oracle Consulting Services in Austria (Europe) as an architect and crisis manager. During these days I spent considerable time on planes and airports travelling around the globe, to support and fix critical projects (e.g in Switzerland, Turkey, Dubai, UK and Cyprus) - J2EE and SOA based.

My (international) experience is not just built on a strong technical background from years of architecting, developing and troubleshooting JEE and SOA applications, but also on social skills from working in all sizes of international teams and leading them through crisis to live projects.

These days sponsoring critical SOA implementations, helping on enabling the field with SOA knowledge (technical, methodology), managing the OTN (Oracle Technology Network, otn.oracle.com) SOA developer community as well as evangelism at conferences/prospect customers.

Aside from the typical PM work, architecting SOAs with customers - writing best practices, publishing, and still writing code that makes its way into the product.

In a nutshell some sort of an engineering product manager - right now in the midst of Oracle Fusion Applications development helping designing the SOA bits and pieces and mentoring engineers.

In 2006 I was awarded as the - to date - youngest Stanford Certified Project Manager, as well as the youngest Oracle ACE, for contributions to the SOA developer community on the Oracle Technology Network.
