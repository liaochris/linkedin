Louise Devlin’s Summary

As a sub-editor for Rave Music Magazine, I got the position from my hard work, reliability and inquisitiveness into news, whilst I was an intern for 2 months.

I had no training in journalism beforehand, although with a strong theatre background, text and the power of the written word was second nature to me.

Communication is pure beauty. It delves into the unknown and releases information that was hidden beforehand.

My goal is to keep writing and to become the best journalist I can be. My influences are John Pilger and Anna Blundy.

I am doing my NCTJ (National Council of the Training of Journalists) course and exams in September 2009.

Louise Devlin’s Specialties:

I specialise in the conviction that journalism is a story told journey of the human and worldy conditition- whether it is the prime minister reading the newspaper/magazine, or an uneducated working class person reading the newspaper/magazine, both and all should be able to undertstand it.

More poignantly, freedom of speech is a huge part of democracy and journalists are needed to help stay in attune of this.
