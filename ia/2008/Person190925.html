Tom Gorman’s Summary

I really enjoy technology. Since the first computer I remember having seen (TRS80 Model III) I was fascinated. That later lead to my gathering paper route money to buy my first computer, a Commodore Vic 20. Later still, I got hooked by the thinking and philosophy of the Free Software movement. From there it was natural to start loving Linux, Mozilla, Apache, Wiki, WordPress. etc.. Those two passions in me: the passionate interest in technology and my passionate interest in liberty have really molded and shaped my decisions and it is only this clear to me as I look back on all the things I have moved toward, and away from, throughout my life.

I enjoy doing interesting work! I also enjoy both time and geographic freedom which means flexibility in my schedule and the ability to work remotely: my basement, coffee shop, customer site, wherever. Don't get me wrong, I like coming to the office, spending time with peers, etc.. My social skills are excellent, I love collaboration and I enjoy people as the greatest things of value in my life! Spending 9-5 Mon-Fri in an office cube, however... not so much fun. If you need someone you can rely on to get the job done and done well, I am your man.

Lately, due to interest and opportunity, I have added some new dimensions to my skill set: site building and middleware. RapidWeaver, WordPress, Drupal, Google Apps, Tibco, Websphere and JMS, for example, are technologies that I am demonstrating, troubleshooting and helping my customers to work with. Learning new technologies is a big part of what makes my career fun. Outside of work I have two AIX boxes, one Sun Ultra 20, a Windows server, a Linux server, and a MacBook Pro in my computer room at home. I own seven web domain names that I pay to have hosted 24x7 on Linux where I run my own websites, blogs, wiki, and bulletin board; technology is not just what I spend time on when someone is paying me to do it.

Tom Gorman’s Specialties:

Pre & Post Technical Sales Support, Customer Service, Linux, Unix, opensource software configuration, Site Building, Middleware (Tibco, Websphere, JMS), using the aforementioned tools to solve interesting problems. Excellent written and spoken communication.
