Waheed Bajwa’s Summary

Waheed Bajwa received the BE (with Honors) degree in electrical engineering from the National University of Sciences and Technology (NUST), Islamabad, Pakistan in 2001 and the MS degree in electrical engineering from the University of Wisconsin-Madison, Madison, WI in 2005. He is currently pursuing a PhD degree in electrical engineering (mathematics minor) from the University of Wisconsin-Madison under the supervision of Prof. Robert Nowak and Prof. Akbar Sayeed.

Mr. Bajwa's graduate research is mainly focused in the emerging field of information processing, and has appeared in a number of prestigious peer-reviewed journals and conference proceedings. In addition, he has 3+ years of experience working in the industry, including a summer position at GE Global Research, Niskayuna, NY in 2006. He has also received a number of awards and recognitions for his scholarly achievements and community service.

Waheed Bajwa’s Specialties:

Theory of sparse signal representation (à la compressed sensing), statistical signal processing, learning theory, wireless communications, and applications in biological sciences, image and video processing, finance, and cognitive radio & wireless sensor/ad-hoc networks.
