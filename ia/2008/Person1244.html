Abbas Sumar’s Education

  • Carleton University

    MA, Law & Economics, 2004 — 2007

    I examined the connection between international bank supervision and Islamic banking, specifically, how to supervise & regulate an Islamic bank in a conventional environment. I argue that because Islamic banking differs from conventional banking, & because a hard law supervisory approach would frustrate attempts to level the playing field, the way forward lies with the Basel Committee on Bank Supervision. The BCBS is premised on a decentralized approach to cross border supervision. Managed effectively, the BCBS serves as a model for transnational cooperation, making regulation of Islamic banks an obvious candidate. However, the BCBS does not regulate Islamic banks. The recommendation involves the BCBS as facilitator of supervisory guidelines for Islamic banking. This requires the creation of a semi autonomous body (Islamic Banking Supervisory Committee) within the BCBS developing a supervisory programme that recognizes the differences and fosters competition & fair market practices.
