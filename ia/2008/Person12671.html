Angela Hedges (Lucas)’s Experience

  • Senior Product Manager

    Kaboodle, Inc

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry)

    November 2007 — Present (4 months)

    As Senior Product Manager at Kaboodle, a leading social shopping community, I define and deliver features that improve the user experience. While electronics and media are well established online shopping markets, lifestyle goods like apparel and home furnishings are more firmly rooted in real-world shopping. In lieu of trying on that cute sweater or feeling the quality of a duvet cover, Kaboodle.com offers online shoppers a vibrant user community where people share new products, exchange advice and uncover trends. In my role, I help to define product strategy to offer exceptional features to online shoppers.

  • Product Manager

    TheFind.com

    (Internet industry)

    November 2006 — October 2007 (1 year)

    While at TheFind, I drove syndication and partnership programs to increase the reach of this comprehensive shopping search engine. As Distribution Product Manager I was responsible for end-to-end product development including functional requirements, implementation management and success metrics for turnkey partner programs and customized cobranded sites like PayPal, About.com, Outdoor Life and PointClickHome.

  • Senior Online Programs Manager

    Synopsys, Inc.

    (Public Company; SNPS; Semiconductors industry)

    July 1999 — November 2006 (7 years 5 months)

    In my 7 years at Synopsys I worked on SolvNet, the company’s customer support website, helping to achieve and maintain its position as the industry leader in EDA support. From an original focus of support marketing I expanded my role to Senior Online Programs Manager, which encompassed content management for the extensive knowledgebase, project definition using the Rational Unified Process (RUP), and use case and user interface development for a variety of innovate features. I enjoyed a role where I was able to leverage limited resources to provide solutions to users in need. The consistently positive feedback was a great motivation and inspired me to seek out a more consumer-focused position.

  • Marketing Manager

    The Benjamin Group Inc.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Public Relations and Communications industry)

    February 1998 — July 1999 (1 year 6 months)

    When I think about my days at TBGI, I’m still inspired by terrific energy and incredible achievement of this small firm that was consistently recognized as the top grossing PR firm in Silicon Valley. I came on board as they were looking to transition away from their staid corporate-style branding and show off their innovative, passionate, leading-edge take on PR in the dot com world. As Marketing Manager I drove projects to create bold mixed-media marketing pieces, online showcases of award-wining campaigns, and branding guidelines that ensured all the amazing creativity remained focused on the core messaging. TBGI was eventually acquired by the global firm Weber Shandwick.

  • Marketing Communications Specialist

    Amdahl Corporation

    (Computer Hardware industry)

    September 1994 — January 1998 (3 years 5 months)

    My professional career began at Amdahl, which for a big-iron giant was surprisingly early to embrace the internet as a critical tool for conducting business. As early as 1994, I was developing intranet resource sites that promoted consistent marketing practices across business units. As Marcom Specialist, I also developed collateral pieces, managed a technical email bulletin, organized closed-circuit satellite broadcasts and revamped the corporate press release process. It was a unique opportunity to try out a variety of roles, and from that early start dabbling with the possibilities of the internet I focused my career on web program management.
