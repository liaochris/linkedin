Ben Malter’s Summary

Feature film credits include Moulin Rouge, In The Cut and Mr Magorium's Wonder Emporium.

I was born in 1978 and finished my Higher School Certificate (final year of high school) in 1996 and I have been working in the animation and computer graphics industry as a 3D artist since 1998.

I am Australian with UK ancestry visa.

My strongest skills are lighting and rendering using 3D Studio Max and Vray, which I developed after spending 3 years on the "Cartoon Network" project with Animal Logic Film, for which the team won the Australian Effects and Animation Festival (AEAF) Award.

Prior to this, our team also won another AEAF Award for our work on the US Discovery Channel, "Extreme Martial Arts" documentary.

I also have strong skills using Eyeon Digital Fusion, after compositing my shots on the Sci-Fi TV series "Farscape" for 3 years with Animal Logic Film.

For 6 months I was working for Frantic Films in their Sydney office, during which time I have been using the Nvidia Gelato renderer, working closely with the Frantic Films developers and VFX for feature film "Mr Magorium's Wonder Emporium.”

After the Frantic Films Sydney office closing in July 2007. I have been working as a freelance artist. During this time I have completed a 60 second TVC for the Max Music channel. On this job I created all assets and all lighting rendering receiving a Phoenix Film Award and an AEAF Award.

Software of choice include:
*Adobe PhotoShop CS
*Autodesk 3D Studio Max, Vray and Brazil
*Eyeon Digital Fusion
