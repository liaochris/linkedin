Arne Hulstein’s Summary

I have a passion for people and technology. A passion which comes out both in my work as well as other activities. Work wise I have been involved in various forms of business and concept development often including new technology or new ways of working. Privately I have been involved in a lot of cultural activities amongst which were youth work and international exchanges. Feel free to ask me about it when you want to know more.

After years of playing with computers, my main trigger was when I first came on-line in 1994. Through IRC and forums I started to enjoy the fruits of social networks worldwide. This lead to an attempt to start and internet company in 1996, but I lost my potential project to IBM. My next big project in 1999 was the creation of an e-learning project for a local college. I then joined VHIC where I tried to incorporate new technology in document management. This included forecasting to government agencies the mobile internet and documents on demand at any time. At the time I also taught on the uses of the internet.

In 2004 I joined an internet company where I would be sitting down with customers to look at their thoughts on the internet and distilling it into an internet strategy and a design to work with. A time with great challenges. Sales Challenge was to be my way to assist companies in their sales strategy. However, I was requested by Cost Engineering to join their staff to work on their sales strategy.

Right now I am looking at revitalising Sales Challenge and Digital Dreamers and turning them into a company helping organisations to get to grips with new technology and social networks. And to show that 2.0 is not just a difficult term. It is everyday reality if you want to serve your customers to the maximum of your abilities.

I am also very interested to network internationally with other Christians. I believe there are many opportunities for sharing experiences, encouragement, sharing faith and sharing business opportunities.
