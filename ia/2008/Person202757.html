  • Corporate Communication Manager

    Virgin Express

    (Privately Held; 501-1000 employees; Airlines/Aviation industry)

    February 2000 — March 2006 (6 years 2 months)

    When Yves decided to join Virgin Express, many considered this career move as a bad one. In the period 1999-2000 the low cost carrier "enjoyed" the reputation of being the synonym of delays, technical problems, bad media and government relations. The challenge was enormous, and the financial difficulties the company was in, did not make the task easier. The corporate communication activities, which included press relations in various EC countries, were a single man's operation. The media relations improved drastically and after a few years Virgin Express was again amongst the well-respected airlines in Europe. At the occasion of the anouncement of the common strategic plan of Virgin Express and SN Brussels Airlines, Yves decided to focus on the activities of Panneels & Partners, the communication and reputation management consultanty that he had set up a year before. He left Virgin Express with the feeling "mission accomplished".

  • Chief of Staff

    the Office of the Governor, Antwerp

    (Executive Office industry)

    April 1997 — January 2000 (2 years 10 months)

    Working at the office of the governor of Antwerp allowed Yves to establish many valuable business contacts in the Antwerp region and in federal and Flemish government circles. In the period 1997-2000, the province was confronted with various crises (floods, incident at chemical plant, legionella epidemic,...). Yves managed media relations. In addition, he coordinated the weekly deployment of local police forces and gendarmerie with regard to the alcohol and speeding and the first drug controls which were conducted to reduce the number of casualties on the roads during weekend nights.

  • PR & Communication Manager

    Distrigas

    (Public Company; 501-1000 employees; DIST; Oil & Energy industry)

    May 1996 — April 1997 (1 year)

    During Distrigas's first year on the Brussels Stock Exchange, Yves managed the press relations and was focusing on financial communication issues.

  • Press Officer

    Sabena

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Airlines/Aviation industry)

    February 1993 — April 1996 (3 years 3 months)

    During the Pierre Godfroid's era, Yves managed the external communication at the occasion of many social conflicts and other crises (bomb alerts, crash of the Tarom A310,...).

  • Sergeant

    UN Operation Winter Lodge

    (Military industry)

    October 1992 — December 1992 (3 months)

    Yves joined the medical service of the Belgian Armed Forces as a candidate non-commisioned troop officer. After his training at the Royal School of the Medical Service, he was sent to the Military Hospital of Brussels. In the Summer of 1992 he was detached to the Red Cross of the French Community in Belgium to help to take care of Croatian refugee children. At the end of his military service, Yves participated to Operation Winter Lodge, a humanitarian action lead by the Belgian Army under the auspices of the UNHCR in favour of Bosnian refugees in Croatia.
