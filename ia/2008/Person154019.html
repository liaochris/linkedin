  • Art Director/Editor

    SNDS Magazine

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Newspapers industry)

    2006 — Present (2 years)

    SNDS Magazine is published as a membership publication four times a year. The organization SNDS - Society for News Design - holds about 600 members from the Scandinavian countries, and every year organizes a conference and a competition, Best of Scandinavian News Design.

  • Owner

    Tolstrup Pryds Grafisk Tegnestue / Tolstrup Pryds Graphics

    (Graphic Design industry)

    June 1987 — Present (20 years 8 months)

    A husbond and wife company, originally established under the company name "Grafik&Text", where I was the "Grafik" and my wife Lisbeth Tolstrup was the "Text". We have done thousands of different things through the years - including books, catalogues, magazines, exhibition design, editing, publishing, web design, graphic design, type design, textile design etc. etc.

  • Art and Design Teacher

    Freelance (Self-employed)

    (Self-Employed; Myself Only; Graphic Design industry)

    1984 — Present (24 years)

    Freelance teaching at folk high schools, teachers' colleges, Teachers' After Education, Danish School of Journalism, Danmarks Pædagogiske Universitet etc. - in disciplines like graphic design, calligraphy, type design, news design, photo-illustration etc. One-time lectures are still a possibility.

    Morgenavisen Jyllands-Posten, JP/Politikens Hus

  • Art and Design Teacher

    Seminarium for Håndværk & Design, Nørresundby

    (Educational Institution; 51-200 employees; Education Management industry)

    1993 — 1994 (1 year)

    After a period of freelance periods of teaching calligraphy and graphic design basics, I was the primary teacher when building up a brand new department for graphic design and graphic art. The combination of free expression (here: drawing, sketching, woodcut, drypoint and etching techniques) and more commercial disciplines (layout, photography, typography, graphic design - and introduction to the computer applications of graphic design: Photoshop, QuarkXPress, Illustrator) was always a key point for me during these few hectic years, and the education we offered was quite unique for this kind of teachers' college. I still think that it is very important for a designer or an artist to have a feeling for both "sides" of the graphic language.
