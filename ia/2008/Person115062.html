Lucia Milena Finguerra’s Summary

My latest qualification degree is a Master in Economics with Business Administration specialization and, currently, I’m attending the final year of a PhD course in “Mathematical methods for Economics, Industry, Finance and Insurances” at LUISS “G. Carli” University in Rome. The course will end in January 2008.
I’m a student member of a National Council for Research (CNR) team, working in an Italian Treasury Ministry project on public debt control. The same CNR granted me a fellowship covering the entire course.
My tasks in the research group are two:
- performing the European Harmonized Index of Consumer Prices (HICP) seasonal adjustments, using the US Census Bureau’s X-12-ARIMA software;
- modeling the break-even inflation (commonly known simply as BEI) rate.
In my course’s first year I travelled and studied abroad for more than half time: I attended the AARMS (Atlantic Association for Research in Applied Mathematics) Summer School, held at Dalhousie University in Halifax in Canada between July and August 2005. Next, since September of the same year to February 2006 I have been a visiting student at “Institut Galilée” of Engineering Faculty at “Universitée Paris XIII” in Paris (France). There I attended some courses and wrote two works in Time series analysis and Game theory (that can be found on my web site in the University section).
