Gideon Strauss’s Summary

My personal mission is: Asking big questions - Enabling wholehearted work - Equipping worldchanging organizations.


I do this by choreographing and directing learning experiences, introducing people with compatible projects to one another, bringing good ideas to the attention of people who can act on them, connecting writers and teachers with readers and learners, reading and writing, recommending imaginative and educational media (mostly books and shorter writing), and managing teams of researchers and media producers.

Gideon Strauss’s Specialties:

Philosophically informed zeitgeist hunches, a love of stories, and a fascination with elegant techniques in every area of life. I am a skillful and voracious reader, an imaginative designer of learning experiences, and I have a passion for making connections between people who can help one another do good work. In my editorial work I have good intuition about content and authors. I am a skillful project leader and pay close attention to both motivation and metrics.

  • Research and Education Director

Gideon Strauss’s Education

  • Regent College

    Christian spirituality 1998 — 1999

    Regent College was a time of sabbatical refreshing and personal development. I focused on learning to pray and read the Bible, tried to learn as much as I could from JI Packer and Eugene Peterson, and worked as a teaching assistant to Craig Gay. Our family loved Vancouver.

  • University of the Free State/Universiteit van die Vrystaat

    PhD, Philosophy and ethics, 1985 — 1995

    I studied philosophy with good teachers. My mentor, Kobus Smit, introduced me to aesthetics, trained me in ethics, and supported my partner and I as we tried to come to grips with our political responsibilities against apartheid. There was much wrong with the university during the time I studied at it, most significantly the fact that it was an apartheid institution that excluded students on the basis of their race.

  • Jim Fouche Secondary School

    Matriculated, 1980 — 1984

    Jim Fouche is an academically oriented public school in my birth town of Bloemfontein, South Africa. During the apartheid years it was complicit in the policies of racial segregation enforced by the then government.
