Paul McConnell’s Summary

Paul experienced the power of the Internet in the 1980’s – first at University and then in several of his early jobs. Always a strong advocate of the technology, he was well positioned in 1993 to take advantage of Internet commercialisation as the WWW rapidly evolved. Then again in 2004 he was positioned to enjoy the telecommunication shake-up with the emergence of IP Telephony. As a business owner, Paul is proud of the role that he has played in helping establishing the Internet in Australia.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    As the owner and MD of OfficeLink+ Paul leads a team of extraordinary talent to provide business grade VoIP and private networks to selected clients within Australia. See http://www.officelink.net.au

  • Chairman

  • Electronic Service Delivery Manager

    (Government Agency; 501-1000 employees; International Trade and Development industry)

    In 2002 ACIS was awarded the public tender for the outsourcing of Electronic Service Delivery for the federal government agency, AusAID. Paul was responsible for managing a team of ACIS Internet specialists to deliver all aspects of the agencies electronic services delivery including IT security, web application development, content management and network services.

  • CEO

    Third Arm Pty Ltd

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Computer Software industry)

    November 1999 — June 2002 (2 years 8 months)

    Paul headed up this DotCom start-up and successfully commercialised GeneralManager bringing it to market in 2000. The GeneralManager product is a hosted consulting services application. Third Arm and GeneralManager were acquired by ACIS Pty Ltd in 2002.

  • ACT Regional Coordinator

    Australian Public Access Network Association (APANA)

    (Non-Profit; 201-500 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    November 1993 — March 2000 (6 years 5 months)

    As the Regional Coordinator, Mr McConnell established the ACT's first permanent internet connection. This saw APANA ACT grow to several hundred members and continue strongly for over 7 years.

  • Senior Technical Consultant

    Dept of the Health and Family Services

    (Government Agency; 1001-5000 employees; Public Policy industry)

    March 1998 — October 1999 (1 year 8 months)

    Because of his earlier Internet/Intranet involvement (1997), Mr McConnell was invited to return to the department as the most senior technical consultant on their HealthInsite project. The HealthInsite web portal (www.healthinsite.gov.au) was publicly launched in March 2000 and was a $6M project.

  • Senior Technical Consultant

    CentreLink

    (Government Agency; 10,001 or more employees; Government Administration industry)

    July 1997 — February 1998 (8 months)

    As the most senior technical consultant for implementing Centrelink's national Intranet, Mr McConnell was responsible for establishing one of Australia’s biggest Intranet solutions. This was successfully completed in early 1998, and Mr McConnell then left the ongoing maintenance in the hands of other ACIS consultants so that he could undertake the HealthInsite project.
