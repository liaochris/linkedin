Vinette Brown’s Summary

-Vinette Brown is Associate Director of Resource Development for the NGO Family Health International (FHI) where she manages the team responsible for developing and funding FHI’s healthcare programs in 70 countries around the world.

-Vinette was formerly the Associate Director of Development for Ashoka: Innovators for the Public, where she spent four years working to increase the number of investors and partners, and the fundraising capability of Ashoka’s staff across the globe.

-Vinette has worked for the Academy for Educational Development (AED) where she wrote and edited project publications, reports and workplans for the USAID-funded Food and Nutrition Technical Assistance (FANTA) Project, conducted process evaluations of HIV/AIDS youth education projects for the Health Communication Partnership Project, and researched best practices in reproductive health and HIV/AIDS programs for the Advance Africa Project.

-Vinette helped launch and is on the Board of Directors of the Educated Consumer Project (ECP)- a non-profit organization that provides youth in low income areas in Washington, DC with financial literacy education.

-Vinette is a member of the Board of Directors of YouthNetwork.org- a website that is a one-stop tool for accessing youth-related resources.

-Vinette is a Returned Peace Corps Volunteer (RPCV) and spent two years in Haiti as an Agricultural Extension Volunteer.

-Vinette has a B.A. in International Relations from Boston University, a M.A. in International Development Studies, with concentrations in Development Management and International Education, from the George Washington University, and speaks English, Haitian-French-Creole and Spanish.
