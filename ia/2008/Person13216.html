  • Senior Systems Engineer

    NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory

    (Government Agency; 1001-5000 employees; Defense & Space industry)

    Systems Engineer for the Entry Descent and Landing Team on the Mars Science Laboratory Mission (Mars 09). Responsible for systems engineering on the parachute decelerator system supersonic qualification program.   • Senior Engineer - Contract Technical Manager

    NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory

    (Government Agency; 1001-5000 employees; Defense & Space industry)

    September 2005 — June 2007 (1 year 10 months)

    Contract Technical Manager for the Parachute Decelerator System on the Mars Science Laboratory Mission (Mars 09). Responsible for requirements definition, technical oversight of fabrication and testing, contract technical management, and leading the JPL-NASA-industry-academia team.

  • Senior Engineer - Task Manager

    NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory

    (Government Agency; 1001-5000 employees; Defense & Space industry)

    June 2003 — September 2005 (2 years 4 months)

    Task Manager for the Prometheus 1 Ion Thruster Technology program. Project management responsibilities during this Phase A activity included management of a $5.2M annual budget, developing work assignments and management of 12 engineers/technicians, and contract technical management of industry subcontracts. Provided technical leadership over the fabrication of EM/DM thruster hardware, integration and performance testing in the 10ft space simulator, and environmental testing in the JPL ETL vibration test facility.

    Task Manager and Co-Investigator for VHITAL, a $4M NRA funded propulsion technology program. Responsibilities included contract technical management of industry subcontracts, technical supervision of thruster component development and testing, coordinating the multi-disciplinary team activities and program reviews, and reporting to management on technical / financial progress.

  • Staff Engineer

    NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory

    (Government Agency; 1001-5000 employees; Defense & Space industry)

    June 2001 — June 2003 (2 years 1 month)

    Task Manager for the Extended Life Test (ELT) of the DS1 Flight Spare Ion Thruster. Test program led to new qualification of NSTAR thruster technology enabling the Dawn mission and future discovery class missions. Responsibilities included conduct of the qualification test, leading the multi-NASA Center post test analysis team, coordinating program reviews, documentation of results in a NASA TR and AIAA proceedings, and reporting to management on technical / financial progress. Project resulted in AIAA best paper award and JPL Test engineer for JIMO Life Modeling and test activity specializing in ion engine testing, data acquisition system setup, high voltage electrical system design, plasma diagnostics, magnetic field modeling, and next generation of ion thruster design. Developed new NSTAR thruster magnetic field design that improves life and efficiency by 20% as part of USC dissertation.

  • Cognizant Engineer

    Boeing Space and Communications

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Defense & Space industry)

    January 2000 — June 2001 (1 year 6 months)

    Cognizant Engineer for the Xenon Ion Engines of the Hughes 702 spacecraft program. Responsible for review of unit level qual testing, hardware inspection, process and test anomaly resolution, thruster requirements definition, and product sell off to the customer for Galaxy 3 spacecraft. Participated in spacecraft level testing, integration and on-orbit propulsion system support for the XM2 Satellite Radio Spacecraft.

  • Engineer/Scientist

    Boeing Space and Communications

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Defense & Space industry)

    June 1998 — June 2000 (2 years 1 month)

    Test engineer for the X37 Vapor Ingestion Experiments. Designed instrumentation system, data acquisition system, and digital video system for the test rig. Participated in ground testing, variable-gravity flight testing, and test data reduction.

    Computational fluid dynamicist for propellant tank design and performance analysis for programs including Space Shuttle, SOTV, X37, and company funded IAD.

    Mechanical Systems engineer on the Delta IV Launch Vehicle Common Booster Core Team. Interfaced with vendors for the selection of feed system cryogenic valves. Served as propulsion group’s interface with the engine section design team. Participated in the program from PDR to CDR.
