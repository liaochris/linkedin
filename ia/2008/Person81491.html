Gopal Iyer’s Summary

Gopal Iyer is a Design Engineer in the Network and Storage Peripherals Group at LSI, India. In this role, his primary responsibilities consist of complete layout ownership of several custom analog blocks, global internal-customer support for Mixed Signal IPs, development of Testchips and mentoring of young engineers on mixed signal layouts. He has 5 years of experience in the Electronics industry.
Gopal brings a depth of technical proficiency to his work. He was a valedictorian at his University scoring an aggregate of 78% marks. Gopal won several awards like ‘Siemens Outstanding Student Scholarship’ for all-round performance, Gold medal for topping the University in the final year, Prof. P R Bastikar (IISc Bangalore) award for topping across all branches and many more.
Gopal began his career as an Embedded Developer with Honeywell Automation India Limited. He re-engineered Analog circuits that were part of Honeywell’s flagship product: TDC3000, an automation solution targeted at the Oil Refineries. He worked on some signal condition cards such as Hiway Coupler Module, Redundancy module, and Low Level Analog Input card on HC900. The Hiway-coupler-module team, won critical acclaim for their outstanding efforts as one homogenous Global team.
After a successful stint at Honeywell, Gopal moved to LSI as a Physical Design Engineer in their Network and Storage Products Group. His primary responsibilities included building Testchips for validating LSI’s IPs on leading technology nodes, full custom layouts of LSI’s Tape Channel and Read Channel Mixed Signal IPs and building digital macros. Gopal was recognized as the “Most Significant Impact” employee for his work on the 90nm Analog Testchip. Consequently, he was deputed to Fort Collins, Colorado to work on NSPG’s most esteemed product – 65nm Analog Front End for the Read Channel. The 20 member team’s efforts were recognized at the highest level within LSI.
