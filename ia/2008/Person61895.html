  • Managing Director

    Impact Marcom Ltd

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Telecommunications industry)

    November 2004 — May 2007 (2 years 7 months)

    Impact Marcom is one of the UK's leading audio visual systems integrators. It supplies, installs, maintains & supports audio visual equipment in the corporate & public sector marketplaces.

    In 2005, a few months after taking on the role of MD, the UK operation was returned to profitability after continuing focus on rationalisation. I established AdJuice in 2007, shortly after leaving Impact Marcom, assured of the most profitable year in its 33 year history.

  • Finance Director

    Impact Marcom Ltd

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Telecommunications industry)

    August 1997 — November 2004 (7 years 4 months)

    Re-built the finance team which was transformed from 'overhead expense' to competitive advantage. Simplified the group company structure, reducing the number of banks, accounting systems and the number of trading companies from 6 down to 1 and which led to an enhanced unified brand and much reduced cost base.

    In August 2004, after several years of trading losses, the UK group could no longer pay its debts and the UK management requested a formal audit by BDO Stoy Hayward which confirmed the company was technically insolvent. I initiated and successfully completed negotiations with banks, government authorities and creditors to agree a financial rescue plan after the group's shareholders confirmed their unwavering commitment with an injection of new share capital, all of which were pre-requisites to continuing to trade. I was promoted to Managing Director shortly afterwards.

I headed up the finance function and handled everything from the integration of several acquisitions, financial reporting, audit & tax, change of accounting system and introduction of profit-related tax-efficient employee bonus schemes.
