Michael Lubofsky’s Summary

The practice of law is very demanding; probably far more so than non-attorneys can appreciate. Combining this reality with the dizzying pace of technological advent can precipitate debilitating brain cramp.

But for you attorneys with any modicum of bandwith remaining this morning, afternoon, or evening, can I get you to consider one thing?

“The Internet is creating unprecedented channels for connecting with consumers and businesses seeking legal counsel, and tapping into these new media need not cripple your practice.”

Consider the following:

• Potential new clients, both consumer and business, can now find professional information on qualified lawyers with the mere sending of a text message from their mobile device, e.g., www.LAWYERTXT.com;

• Accomplished attorneys can have their services recommended by esteemed colleagues, clients , and friends and pets and have these recommendations factor meaningfully into his or her online visibility at no charge, e.g., LinkedIn;

• You, or your friendly Internet marketing professional, can instantly disseminate information on significant positive practice developments and professional accomplishments directly to only those who want to hear from you, e.g., Twitter.

OK, so if you began reading this article without a brain cramp, I’ll pass the Ben-Gay.

So why am I telling you this? To create an awareness of the unprecedented opportunities out there. Why would I do this on my own time? Because I would like to help connect qualified lawyers with people and businesses seeking legal help by leveraging these new media. I would welcome your call to discuss how - just wait until the cramp subsides prior to dialing.
