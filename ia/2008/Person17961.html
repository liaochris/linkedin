Andrew Tomlinson’s Summary

• Andrew Tomlinson recently joined the Quaker UN office in NY as Director and Representative, seeing an opportunity to use his extensive private sector experience in a humanitarian role. Quakers have maintained a presence at the UN since its inception, and won the Nobel Peace Prize for their work with postwar refugees in 1947.
• He founded the Dilmun One Fund, a socially responsible investment fund, in 2005. Before founding Dilmun, he was a Director with the Salomon Smith Barney division of Citigroup, where he served as a senior member of the derivatives capital markets team, structuring and originating hedging and capital raising transactions for Fortune 500 companies in the equity, fixed income, foreign exchange, commodity and credit markets.
• Prior to his association with Citibank in 1993, Mr. Tomlinson worked in the London and later the New York offices of S.G. Warburg & Co. as an investment banker, where, among other things, he participated in the design and execution of the first generation of interest rate and currency swap transactions.
• Mr. Tomlinson was born in October, 1958 and has a Bachelor of Arts in Archeology and Anthropology from St. John’s College at Cambridge University (1980, First Class Honors) and a Master of Arts in Oriental Studies from the University of Pennsylvania (1983, Thouron Scholar). While at Citigroup he held Series 7 and 24 securities licenses and he holds the Chartered Market Technician (CMT) designation from the Market Technicians’ Association.
• He has contributed articles on peace-making, technical analysis and ethical investing to a variety of periodicals. Mr. Tomlinson is a United Kingdom citizen and a permanent resident of the United States
