Frédéric Lhoest’s Summary

- in 1998 : I started working on a Nortel/Telrad DMS-300 International Gateway as Switch Operator.

- in 1999 : I was working on the AIN Platform (Advanced Intelligent Network) to administer Telecommunication Services such as Follow Me number, Prepaid, Calling Card and Voice Mail services.

- in 2000 : Phone PrePaid System deployed in Congo RDC, 200k Prepaid cards sold out over the country. Development of an Intranet for the Corporate to report call Statistics and Customer Support. We started development from scratch with Linux and PHP.

- in 2002 : Celtel Group bought AfriLink/Afrisat, IT Integration using Windows 2000 Active Directory Technology and Exchange 2000 Mail System. Deployment of a SAN System with HP MSA-1000. Deployment of VPN with Headquarters in The Netherlands with Cisco PIX Technology

- in 2003 : Expansion of the SAN with a redundant SAN in two sites : Veritas Storage Foundation to replicate the data across Optical Fibre. Implementation of SAN Backup withTape Libraries.

- in 2005 : Gateway Communications bought CCA (Celtel Affilitate). IT Integration in the entire group : 4 countries connected with VPN and Microsoft Windows 2003 Active Directory + Exchange 2003 Mail System.

- in 2006 : Implementation of BlackBerry Services around the Company. Mobistar is the primary Partner in Belgium but Orange CH, Vodacom ZA and Vadafone UK are also in the game.

- in 2007 : Gateway Communications to acquire GS Telecom. IT Integration started accross Africa and Europe : 14 countries to interconnect via VPNs massive usage of Cisco ASA 55xx.

- In 2008 : New projects needed Citrix to be implemented. Marval project to raise Gateway ITIL compliant and on the way to the ISO 20000 certification.
