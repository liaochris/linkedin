    ICANN is responsible for the global coordination of the Internet's system of unique identifiers. These include domain names (like .org, .museum and country codes like .UK), as well as the addresses used in a variety of Internet protocols. Computers use these identifiers to reach each other over the Internet. Careful management of these resources is vital to the Internet's operation, so ICANN's global stakeholders meet regularly to develop policies that ensure the Internet's ongoing security and stability. My main duty is to ensure the daily server and network operations of ICANN Infrastructure. I am also technical lead to the IT-OPs team that consist of System Administrators and Network Engineers.
