Mark Chassy’s Summary

I have 12 years of IT experience in Europe and in Africa. During the first half of my career I served as a database expert and architect in large scale information systems such as the TARGET project, which established a real time money transfer system between banks across Europe. I have spent the last 6 years developing QA and integration strategy, both as a consultant and as a project manager and/or coordinator. For example, I was responsible for all QA procedures of a $7 million project at EuroNext in Paris.

My solid technical base is complimented by a wide knowledge of business practices. My focus on system conception, validating functionality and testing, makes me uniquely versatile and able to work in just about any sector. My involvement in large internationally distributed development programmes has given me first-hand experience of the ‘checks and balances’ needed to achieve successful results from sub-contractors and/or partners.

I have extensive project management and coordination experience with a clear vision of how client preferences can be reconciled with the realities of implementation. At Axios International, I first conducted an analysis of existing functionality to determine the possible paths forward. I then coordinated end-users and managers ranging several countries to broker a compromise among all stakeholders.

Finally, I understand that the correct management of user requirements is the basis of any IT success story. During my career I have translated requirements into valid technical solutions, weighed them against the constraints of time, money, and existing systems, contributed to improving them through model abstraction and analysis, and of course validated them during the testing phase of each project.

I am currently helping banks across Europe to coordinate their self assessment processes with SWIFT so as to reach SEPA compliance by the end of the year.
