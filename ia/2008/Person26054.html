Brad Lichtenstein’s Summary

I've been working in documentary production since 1992, as a producer on many PBS films including FRONTLINE’s Peabody award-winning presidential election year special, Choice ’96, and Lumiere Production’s PBS series, With God on Our Side: The History of the Religious Right. With Lumiere, I produced and directed André’s Lives, a portrait of the “Jewish Schindler;” Safe, about 3 women who seek refuge from domestic violence; Caught in the Crossfire, chronicling the lives of 3 Arab New Yorkers in the wake of 9/11; and Ghosts of Attica, about the infamous 1971 prison uprising and aftermath, for which he was awarded a Dupont-Columbia Award for Excellence in Journalism. I've also produced for Now With Bill Moyers. My most recent project was for PBS’s INDEPENDENT LENS, Almost Home, about people who live and work in a elder-care community. With Vernon Reid, I'm producing What We Got: DJ Spooky’s Quest for the Commons, a hip hop documentary animation mashup about the over-privatization of public goods. I teach in the film department of the University of Wisconsin, Milwaukee, where I founded and direct docUWM, a program that produces student-faculty collaborative documentary projects and presents documentary work to the community.
