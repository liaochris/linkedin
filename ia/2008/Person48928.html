Deborah L. Berman, LMSW’s Summary

Deborah L. Berman, LMSW holds a masters degree from the Columbia University School of Social Work (CUSSW). She has an undergraduate degree in Jewish Social Studies with an academic focus on Jewish-led American social justice movements of the 20th Century. She is a practicing mental health clinician in an acute care psychiatric hosptial setting where she provides psychotherapy and concrete services to adolescent boys and their families, many of whom come from the most impoverished and disempowered communities of New York City. Ms. Berman worked at the Brooklyn Center for Psychotherapy and trained as a clinician at the Jewish Board of Family and Children’s Services and at Orthox Union's Yachad/National Jewish Council for Disabilities. She was Program Manager for the Beth Elohim Human Rights Campaigns Board, developing and supervising short- and long-range community-based social justice campaigns for women’s, immigrant, and worker rights. She was also Director of the 150-member Brownstone Brooklyn Synagogues and Friends Interfaith Coalition for Women’s Healthcare, where she supervised grassroots volunteer staff, led a 98-person lobbying delegation to Capitol Hill, and produced a workshop series for teenaged girls on visual art and social justice, facilitated by noted female artists and activists.

Deborah L. Berman, LMSW’s Specialties:

Individual, Family, Group, and Couples Therapy; Inpatient, Outpatient, MICA, & Day Treatment; Program Management and Community Organizing; Proficient in immigration stress & refugee mental health issues; Expertise in diverse Jewish cultural considerations in health, mental health, and disabilities; Experience with LGBT population; Proficient in applications & resources of OMH, OMRDD, DDSO, ACS, APS, CPS, CSPOA, ICM/Waiver, Medicaid/Medicare, Board of Ed/CBST, OMH, State Hospital, & NAMI.
