Mark Neely [mark.neely at gmail.com]’s Summary

I'm a speaker, writer and consultant on the subject of strategy, discontinuous innovation and leading change. I'm a firm believer in adopting a systems-thinking approach to strategic planning and change management, coupled with an emphasis on creativity, problem-solving, and the pursuit of 'why?'.

I spend my time showing organisations how to anticipate and respond to emerging market shaping phenomena, through the provision of ‘third horizon’ strategic direction and hands-on implementation guidance.

I have recently spent a great deal of time working with organisations and individuals looking to make sense of the causes, ramifications and opportunities inherent in the digitisation of content and the media value chain, and the broader shifts in media consumption, advertising expenditure and the emerging 'digital culture'.

My blog (www.infolution.com.au) is an open invitation to you to discuss, share and debate ideas related to strategy, discontinuous innovation and leading change as it applies to your industry.

Mark Neely [mark.neely at gmail.com]’s Experience

  • Co-Founder

    Rife Media Pty Ltd

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Online Media industry)

    December 2007 — Present (9 months)

    Rife Media was launched in 2007 with a single goal – to create content that audiences love.

    Our business model is simple: identify audience segments currently under-served by ‘traditional’ media, and create content properties that anchor profitable, audience-oriented content ‘value networks’.

    We achieve this by creating interactive content experiences around a common theme, interest, or objective that unites an audience with advertisers and other complementary actors (such as media partners or content co-developers).

    Because Rife Media is independent of (and thus not beholden to) a specific media distribution channel, it is able to develop content using formats most suited to satisfying audience needs, and deliver it to them using the platforms they prefer.
