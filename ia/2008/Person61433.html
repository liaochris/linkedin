Eric Tjoeng’s Summary

As a leading practitioner of business transformation and improvement, Eric has a proven track record in bringing businesses to the next level. Eric has over 25 years experience and understanding of business strategies, business management, financial control, accounting and business systems.

Eric has demonstrated successes as an on tap Business Advisor and Chief Financial Officer for Small and Medium Businesses (SME), he has been appointed to several SME advisory boards;
as well as in various senior Management, Accounting, and IT roles with blue chip companies including Cycle & Carriage Australia, Hyundai Automotive Distributors Australia, Audi Australia, Avis Australia, and Jaguar Rover Australia.

Eric is a leading Partner of CAD Partners, a team of over 80 Business Advisors/Chief Financial Officers helping businesses throughout Australia and New Zealand. Eric’s mission is to create a better profit, better cash flow, better life style, sustainable and competitive business for Business Owners. He has recently been awarded “2006 Australia and New Zealand Partner of the Year” by CAD Partners.

He is a believer in life long learning, and he has obtained his first academic qualifications in Bachelor of Economics from the Sydney University, to be followed by Grad. Dip. EDP (Nepean), Grad. Dip. Management (Deakin), MBA (Deakin), MA (MGSM), Executive Coaching (MGSM).

Eric is a Certified Practising Accountant, a member of the Australian Institute of Management, and a member of the Australian Computer Society. He is in the judging panel of Small Business Awards: Champion of Champions, as well as a recognised conference and business speaker.

Eric Tjoeng’s Specialties:

Create better profit, better cash flow, better life style and a competitive business for Business Owners through:

1. Strategic Management to provide clear business direction and a competitive edge.
2. Financial Control to improve cash flow and profit.
3. Business Systemisation to facilitate consistent, effective, efficient business operation and to increase business value.
4. Business Exit Management to maximise return and to manage transition.
