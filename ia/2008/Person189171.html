Timothy Musgrove’s Summary

I have worked on natural language processing for over ten years, resulting in a number of product launches, publications and patents. Tools developed by my teams have been generating content (and/or filtering content) in high volume for several popular websites.

My current goal is to launch a ground-breaking semantic search engine for the Web.

Timothy Musgrove’s Specialties:

My focus is on synthesizing and productizing research results from diverse sub-branches of cognitive science, including not only the mainstream artificial intelligence research but also the relevant sub-disciplines within anthropology, philosophy and pyschology.
Meanwhile I have successfully led a startup through growth, acquisition, and integration with a new parent company.

Timothy Musgrove’s Experience

  • Founder & CEO

    TextDigger, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Internet industry)

    December 2004 — Present (3 years 10 months)

    Developing semantic search engine for the Web, currently in stealth-mode. Backed by CNET Networks and private investors. Product started private beta at DEMO 07 in Palm Springs.

  • Senior Research Fellow

    CNET Networks

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; CNET; Internet industry)

    May 2002 — January 2006 (3 years 9 months)

    Came to CNET upon its acquisition of Smartshop. At CNET, led the artificial intelligence R&D activity. One major deliverable was the product brief engine, which auto-generates tens of thousands of natural language product reviews, published today on CNET's websites.

  • CTO

    Smartshop.com

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Internet industry)

    August 1999 — May 2002 (2 years 10 months)

    Developed a feature-rich comparison shopping engine which was distinguished in its ability to merge catalog data (product specs and features) from different sources, normalizing their divergent syntax and semantics, and presenting one unified catalog. Acted as Smartshop's CEO during its final 6 months of operations, selling the Comany to CNET Networks in 2002. Most of the Smartshop technology is now incorporated into CNET's various related websites.

  • VP, Artificial Intelligence R&D

    Mindmaker, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Internet industry)

    July 1996 — August 1999 (3 years 2 months)

    Developed several natural languge processing (NLP) products, the foremost being WordHound, a smart spell-checker/OCR-checker. This was utilized by Yahoo! for over two years as the very first "Did you mean...?" spell correction on a major search engine. It was also used on other sites and in high-end OCR packages such as Prime Recognition.

  • Sr. Speech Research Engineer

    Creative Labs

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; CREAF; Computer Software industry)

    August 1994 — February 1996 (1 year 7 months)

    Worked on computer telephony, speech recognition and speech synthesis software applications, including the specification of 3rd party products and some in-house R&D projects. A successful prototype that added emotion to synthetic speech (e.g. happy, angry, sad) using Creative's sound card, was demoed at COMDEX and the Game Developer's Conference.

  • Lecturer

    University of La Verne

    (Educational Institution; 201-500 employees; Education Management industry)

    September 1990 — August 1994 (4 years)

    Lectured in logic, introduction to philosophy, and comparative religions while working on my Ph.D. at the nearby Claremont Graduate University.
