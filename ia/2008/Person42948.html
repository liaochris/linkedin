Dan Coates’s Summary

Dan's current entrepreneurial endeavors are twofold:

1. Presiding over GlobalPark's expansion into North America

2. The formation of SurveyU, a niche-panel that is focused on bringing young adults into the survey research process using web 2.0 instrumentation and mobile web capabilities. Powered entirely upon Globalpark tools, SurveyU is a living, breathing showcase of the Globalpark platform.

Dan's previous roles include:

- Vice President of Sales & Marketing at Polimetrix
- Vice President of Surveys & Samples at publicly traded SPSS
- Co-founder of PlanetFeedback.com

Both Polimetrix and PlanetFeedback successfully transitioned from early stage ventures to high-value acquisitions.

A pioneer of interactive technologies within the marketing community, Dan spent the first decade of his career developing cutting edge practices on behalf of Burke Marketing, Millward Brown Interactive and the Angus Reid Group, enabling companies such as IBM, Intel, Microsoft, Sun Microsystems, Viacom and Turner Entertainment Networks to develop online marketing insight.

Dan Coates’s Specialties:

Startups, marketing, business development, ASP platform development, marketing research, product management, services management, sales management, partnership management, panel building, community building.

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Market Research industry)

    In German speaking markets, Globalpark is the market leader.   • Co-Founder

    SurveyU - The Voice of the American College Student

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Market Research industry)

    February 2007 — Present (1 year 2 months)

    SurveyU is an online community and data collection platform, targeting North American students.

    There are over 20 million college students within North America, spending more than $120 Billion annually and influencing the consumption of millions more. Buzz spreads rapidly within the 18-24 year old age group, leading marketers to seek to understand this category at a higher level of resolution than the rest of the population.

    While demand for research on the youth segment is increasing, research undertaken among the general population chronically under-represents young adults, creating demand for young participants within mainstream research studies.

    We have developed a definitive sampling frame that enables us to draw and recruit highly accurate samples at college, state, regional and national levels. This increased accuracy is critical to marketers who make major decisions based on the attitudes and behaviors of this important and influential segment.
