Dieter Meinekat’s Summary

I worked as a building worker until 1970 until my military training in 1972. In the army, my main occupation was a drill machinist. I carried out my military service with the mountain division for 15 months.

After my military service, I worked as a machinist at a building firm, my duties included long distance lorry driver, lorry loader and machine worker.

In October 1974 I married and one year later my daughter Ramona was born. From 1975 I returned to study, completed a sheet metal worker qualification, and graduated successfully.

When I was initially unable to find work in this field, I tried working as a merchant sailor for three months, but did not enjoy this and subsequently left

From 1978-1979 I was became self employed and conducted business in the gas industry. From 1979 I worked with MBB Manching and during 4 weeks trained as a Aircraft technician and made the fuel tanks on the Phanton Hunters. During this employment period I was often sent away into the field service, where I also had to repair various other aircraft parts.

I worked until 1985 as a motorist, sheet metal fitter and as a form caster. I re-married and we had two children, a son, born 23.9.84 and a daughter, born 23.8.85. I accepted a well paid placement job with Libya Air Force and lived with my family in Tripoli, Libya until 1992. My main work for the Air Force was as a Sheet Metal Worker.

I returned in 1992 to Germany although was unable to find suitable work, mainly as I was told that I was now too old. After a period of searching unsuccessfully, we went to South Africa where I supported my acquaintance at his company’s sawmill, building wooden pallets.

I unfortunately contracted malaria in Africa, and returned to Germany. From 23.11.1998 I have been employed at DIS AG as a skilled metal worker.

I am searching, ideally, for military aircraft construction contracts throughout the middle east region. Available within 30 days.

Dieter Meinekat’s Specialties:

Main areas of expertise 1) Preparation (drilling, sewing, senken, sanding) of and building of aircraft parts. 2) Lamination of parts from CFK and GFK (Prepeg and Vacuum technique. 3) Support in design and mouting of metal supports from CFK.
