Greg Davies’s Summary

Academic work on the interface between psychology and economics - particularly decision making and behavioural finance. Applied work consists in commercialising cutting edge academic insights into decision making, behavioural economics, and consumer behaviour. Previous experience in strategy consulting for the financial services industry with a focus on risk management.

Greg Davies’s Specialties:

Behavioural Finance, Behavioural Economics, Risk Management, Consumer Behaviour, Decision Science, Project Management, Price Perception, Fund Management

  • Director

    Decision Technology

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Management Consulting industry)

    July 2002 — November 2006 (4 years 5 months)

    Commercialising academic insights from cognitive psychology, behavioural economics, behavioural finance, and decision science. Managing client projects on customer behaviour, marketing, product design, and decision processes. Co-ordinating research into aspects of investor psychology and its relation to stock market behaviour at both an individual level and an aggregate level. Of particular interest are ways of using cutting-edge knowledge of the behavioural psychology of risk and return perceptions, and models of investor interaction, herd behaviour, and social networks to understand the aggregate pricing behaviour of investments. Further work is based around commercial applications of understanding consumer financial decision making, and the way in which people mentally structure their finances.
