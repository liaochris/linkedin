Meghan Fitzgerald’s Summary

I am currently a math and science teacher at Ethical Culture Fieldston School in Riverdale, NY. I have six years of experience teaching math and science in grades 4 through 8 as well as two years of teaching language arts in the middle grades. I am also a student at Bank Street College of Education, where I have nearly completed the Math Leadership program. Early in my teaching career, I attended the Teachers’ Institute at the Exploratorium Museum in San Francisco, where my passion for teaching math and science through investigation blossomed.

I believe that mathematical reasoning and scientific inquiry are essential tools needed to be a successful citizen of our world and to face the immense challenges in the present and future. I also believe that although traditionally many students have struggled to relate to the discourse in math and science classes, the content could not be more relevant and compelling if and when students can see how to connect it to their lives. For this reason, I am continually seeking to bring relevant issues and applications of math and science into the curriculum I develop, both as a teacher and freelance writer.

I am actively searching for a new role in Fall of 2007. I seek an opportunity to apply my years of experience teaching math with my leadership work at Bank Street within the Leadership in Mathematics Education program in the role of Math Coordinator or Math Staff Developer in a New York public school district. I am committed to finding a role in which I can train and coach math teachers in grades K-8 as they develop and improve their own teaching, while I also help build a cohesive, coordinated, and effective district-wide math program.

Curriculum Development, Technology Knowledge, As of Fall, 2007, I will be certified in NY State as a General Teacher, grades 1-6 as well as School Building Leader.
