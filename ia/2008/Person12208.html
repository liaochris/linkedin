Andy Black’s Summary

Extensive experience in sales, marketing and business development, particularly for new information, content and "search" based products or services, in the media, publishing and entertainment sectors.

Andy Black’s Specialties:

Trained in solution selling, online marketing, online advertising and public relations, website, blogs, articles, video production, organising events and presentations, email marketing, SEO, market research, market analysis, competitive intelligence, press releases, sales support, surveys.

Andy Black’s Experience

  • Marketing Director

    Convera Corporation

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; CNVR; Computer Software industry)

    2005 — Present (3 years)

    Convera provide vertical search as a service, enabling publishers to create customized vertical search applications for specialist audiences.

    Vertical search sites, toolbars and widgets, aggregating and presenting sub-sets of the most authoritative unstructured Web content, as well as offering access to proprietary structured databases, enables publishers to extend their brands through viral distribution through their affiliate channel partners and also by getting embedded in the workflow (browser,iGoogle,mobile,pda) of their online communities.

    I am responsible for identifying target publishers and the professional communities they serve, online marketing, online advertising and public relations, website, blogs, articles, events, email marketing, SEO, market research, market analysis, competitive intelligence, press releases, sales support, surveys and ongoing relationships with relevant publishing and media trade associations in New York, London and Washington DC.

  • Account Director - Media

    Business Objects

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; BOBJ; Computer Software industry)

    2002 — 2004 (2 years)

    Sold business intelligence software licences and services to a range of media companies and worked with SI's or the in-house IT teams to build applications. Major projects included a management BI dashboard for Jaguar cars, an advertising reporting system to Channel 4 Television, a circulation analysis system to the Daily Mail and a reporting tool for BBC TV Licensing.

  • Director of Business Development

    Chyron

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; CGS; Broadcast Media industry)

    2000 — 2001 (1 year)

    Chyron were the global market leaders in character and text generating systems for broadcasters - they created a new division called Chyron Interactive Services to provide both live and interactive video and audio content hosting and streaming services.

    As a new start up division, my role in Business Development was to identify new flagship applications to launch the service - this involved getting reference accounts in the music (Nimbus), gambling (William Hill) and fashion industries (London Fashion Week) - we unfortunately the dot-com implosion and 9/11 meant that the parent company refocused on its core business and closed down this new division.

  • Senior Sales - Media and Publishing

    Excalibur Technologies

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Networking industry)

    1997 — 2000 (3 years)

    Sold and marketed enterprise search software licences and services to media and publishing companies. Major deals included a large scale web crawler licence for Reuters (Factiva), QXL.com. global site search perpetual licence for Bell and Howell (ProQuest), image search licence at The Independent, Taylor and Francis, Clifford Chance, Watson Wyatt.

  • Marketing Director

    Perfect Information

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Financial Services industry)

    1990 — 1997 (7 years)

    I was one of the founding management team of Perfect Information and helped build it from "bleeding edge" start-up to profitability and eventual acquisition by Centaur Media in 1997.

    Perfect Information created a hosted library of digitised images - indexed and searchable regulatory documents from companies listed on the London Stock Exchange and private company filings from Companies House. Access to this online library of scanned original image documents was via dedicated broadband or ISDN connections. As well as offering a 10 year archive of original images of report and accounts, company shareholder circulars and RNS announcements, the library was updated in real-time as companies released compliance documents via the London Stock Exchange or through Companies House - immediately scanned, indexed and made available. Clients included Goldman Sachs, SG Warburg, NM Rothschild, Reuters, Freshfields, JP Morgan, Kroll Associates and Cazenove.

  • Sales and Marketing Manager

    TeleTape Video Ltd

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    1986 — 1989 (3 years)

    Teletape Video (TTV) was a subsidiary of Granada Group PLC. TTV sold broadcast equipment - edit suites, cameras etc as well as offering equipment hire and crew services for programme creation and production.

    I was the sales and marketing manager for the Videowall Division and was involved in selling the first analogue to digital broadcasting services involving programme creation, visual and messaging services and turn key solutions for clients in advertising, entertainment, defence, politics and TV. My major work projects included the launch of Sky TV, Conservative Party conference, Farnborough and Paris Air Shows for British Aerospace and a video roadshow for a tobacco company.

    BBC / HTV / Columbia / Theatre

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    1984 — 1986 (2 years)

    Worked across a range of radio, TV, theatre, corporate video and film projects
