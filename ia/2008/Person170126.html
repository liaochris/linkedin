Sau Sheong Chang’s Summary

I manage a software development team for a global loyalty and payment software company (http://www.welcome-rt.com). We deliver retail payment-related software for banks and do fun stuff like J2EE, smart cards and point-of-sales terminals. I've been doing something like this for more than 12 years (it's a long time, I'm old).

Sometime back I started up a software company (http://www.elipva.com) with Thomas, and plugged at it for 4 years. We developed all sorts of web-based e-business applications for enterprises like banks, insurance companies and tertiary educational institutions. I was both the VP for Product as well as Chief Architect because I designed most of the software we sold.

I used to write technical articles for Javaworld, Dr. Dobb’s Journal, Java Report, Computer Times but nowadays I stick mostly to my own blog (http://blog.saush.com). I'm currently writing a Ruby on Rails book tentatively titled 'Ruby on Rails Web Mashups' with Packt Publishing.

I run some open source projects like the Ruby-PayPal library (http://ruby-paypal.rubyforge.org), Jaccal (Java Card Communications Access Layer) (http://jaccal.sourceforge.net), Screensvr (http://screensvr.rubyforge.org) and Chinese-English Dictionary (CED) (http://ced.dev.java.net).

I have a Bachelors in Computer Engineering, a Masters in Commercial Law and a Graduate Certificate in International Arbitration. I'm a Fellow in the Singapore Institute of Arbitrators and a fully qualified arbitrator in Singapore. More importantly, I have a passion for creating useful software and is interested in a variety of subjects including mathematics (esp statistical analysis), economics, artificial intelligence, electronic payment, linguistics and mobile networks.
