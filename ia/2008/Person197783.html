Fredrik Vogel’s Experience

  • Programme coordinator

    The Danish Academy for Interactive Digital Entertainment (DADIU)

    (Educational Institution; 51-200 employees; Computer Games industry)

    February 2007 — Present (1 year)

    Computer games are here to stay. Therefore, we need to educate people to develop and produce games. The National Academy for Digital, Interactive Entertainment is a collaboration between five universities and three art schools in Aalborg, Viborg, Aarhus and Copenhagen, Denmark. A joint language should be deleloped, and through this a joint understanding of what computer games are, and the students from the various educations should meet and together produce a game in a simulated reality.

    The Academy sees is as its task to promote research and development within the area, to be a core contact for the interactive entertainment industry, to support a national contact, as well as creating a foundation for the establishment of scientific as well as industrial projects. The Academy will also arrange workshops and seminars about digital, interactive entertainment.

  • Associate lecturer

    The National Film School of Denmark

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; Higher Education industry)

    September 2004 — Present (3 years 5 months)

    The National Film School of Denmark, has a four-year programme with specialization in the fields of direction, sound design, photography, editing, production, television production, script writing and animation direction.

    I teach the students from the producer department, essential skills regarding budgetting, legal issues regarding co-production and distribution deals, project presentation and managing animation production.
    I also act as a supervisor for the animation directors, assisting them with issues of idea development, and production method.

  • Owner

    Vogel Film & Media (Sole Proprietorship)

    (Sole Proprietorship; 1-10 employees; Media Production industry)

    June 2001 — Present (6 years 8 months)

    VOGEL FILM & MEDIA is a production company in the field of animated films for the Nordic Public Service broadcasters. Fredrik Vogel is also an associate producer teacher at the National Film School of Denmark, as well as consultant for the European MEDIA programme.

  • Managing Director

    Angel Distribution ApS

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Motion Pictures and Film industry)

    August 1998 — June 2001 (2 years 11 months)

    In charge of establishing an organisation capable of 20 annual releases of european and domestic art-house films for the theatrical, video/DVD, and institutional markets in Denmark.
    In charge of co-ordinating the logistics of international deliveries of film materials to and from Denmark, as well as negotiating delivery and financial terms with film and video laboratories all over the world.
    Management and administration of ApS (limited company) with 5 full time employees.
    Participating in Cannes, Göteborg, Berlin, Haugesund festivals. Active part of sales and acquisition network, activities included market screenings and presentations, contacts to daily trade press.
    Developing distribution strategies, calculating and implementing advertising campaigns for more than 20 theatrical releases, among these; DANCER IN THE DARK, LOLA RENNT, VÄGEN UT, EN KORT EN LANG, ROMANCE, KIRIKOU, HANUMAN, KARLA KANIN BIO 3, DEN LILLE DØD.

Fredrik Vogel’s Education

  • Malmö högskola

    Master, Communication for development, 2006 — 2008 (expected)

    The media play a crucial role at all levels in the globalization process and, potentially, for global development. The demand for media and communication skills is rapidly increasing in the developing countries and as a result in the donor agencies and organizations. Media and communication efforts will be increasingly important for international development cooperation as a whole

    The master course in Communication for Development at Malmö University was started in the year 2000. It is aimed at journalists, information officers and other professionals in the media and culture sector and the objective is to provide an in-depth understanding of the interplay between media, communication and social change through a unique combination of theoretical studies and practical field work.
