Marlon Van Der Linde’s Summary

Spending most of my youth accompanying my father (technical/management posts at ESKOM), I have grown fond of electrical concepts quite early in life.

Strange happenings, bits of bad planning and other personal issues led me to start my studies at Welkom College directly after school. Here I gained a thorough interest in what is, electrical, electronic and other physics related subjects.

However, having moved with parents to the Western Cape, led me again on to a new path. Opensource, programming and linux in general became a hobby, and later a career when Clue Technologies offered me an oppertunity to grow with them.

I spent one year at Clue, working with Linux in depth, under one of the most skilled unix persons I have met. This was an important part of my career, but I soon felt that I had more to offer.

I joined DevSource (Under Dewet Diener) as a junior PHP programmer, and with his help, became stable enough with the language and opensource, that I am now playing a leadership role(currently).

Currently working at AOSIS (African Online Scientific Information Systems), a company taking pride in being the sole Moodle Partner in South Africa among other things. Here I have worked for over two years, as a leader for the last year. I am the direct contact between clients and the technical aspect of our company, and involved in software customization/development myself, while also overseeing work flow in the tech department.

I enjoy what I do, and can do it forever. However, ultimately my goal is to not work inside 4 walls and on a chair for 8+ hours/day. My goal is to expand and move into one of the areas in my lists of interests. For this, I might consider electronics or the electrical field (no pun intended). I like being on the move and always busy.

I really feel fascinated by the electrical/electronic fields, long distance transport and nature related careers, and will eventually move into one of these areas.
