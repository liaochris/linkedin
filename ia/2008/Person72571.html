Gregory L. Smith’s Summary

Gregory L. Smith is a Technical Fellow with the Boeing Technology organization of The Boeing Company in Seattle, Washington. Mr. Smith has over 30 years of experience in computing and information technology arenas. He is an internationally recognized expert in the areas of information extraction, translation and analysis. Mr. Smith applies his expertise to the design, development and deployment of award winning, cost saving, information systems solutions supporting the engineering, manufacturing and business sectors. He is a consultant to numerous Boeing, industry and U.S. Government programs.

An expert in web-based, object-oriented and knowledge-based software, Mr. Smith served as software director and architect for the NIST sponsored Pre-Competitive Advanced Manufacturing Processes (PreAmp) program, the IDA sponsored ARIES program and the DARPA sponsored Team Integrated Electronics Response (TIGER) program. The PreAmp and TIGER programs focused on developing, integrating and deploying data exchange protocols (ISO 10303, STEP) into the engineering environment. The ARIES program focused on capturing and reusing design knowledge. His work in executing the PreAMP and TIGER programs led to award-winning, innovative software used by the Boeing, the U.S. Government, universities and numerous Fortune 500 companies.

Mr. Smith is the innovator and developer behind the CIPHER software application. CIPHER programmatically classifies, identifies and supports the resolution of hidden information and steganography in line of business application data files. CIPHER directly supports Export Compliance, Subcontracts Management, Proposal Development, Correspondence Control and classified organizations throughout The Boeing Company. Two patents have been issued for the concepts and algorithms behind CIPHER. The application is being sold outside of Boeing and is being used by the DoD, the Army, the Air Force, the Navy and numerous fortune 500 companies.
