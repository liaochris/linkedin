Jeff Goldberg’s Summary

Jeff Goldberg is currently working with his partner, Chaim Ackerman, on creating a new consumer electronics product and service.

Jeff had been with the IDT and Net2Phone family of companies since from 1995 until mid 2007. Jeff was most recently the Chief Scientist of the Net2Phone Cable Telephony Division, leading the evolution of proven, scalable operations technology that was originally created in Net2Phone and Adir Technologies, to support DOCSIS-based voice over cable outsourced solutions. He defined and created the Net2Phone technology and served as Net2Phone’s Chief Technology Officer. He also served as the Chief Technology Officer of ADIR Technologies, a subsidiary of Net2Phone. Prior to joining IDT, Jeff was as an industry consultant for 10 years to telecom, process-control and data processing companies using a real-time version of Unix (called UNOS) that he created as the VP Software at Charles River Data Systems, Natick, MA. Jeff began his career consulting on various real-time systems and later worked at Bell Labs on Multi-Media Office of the Future concepts.

Jeff earned degrees in Computer Science (MIT, 1977) and Electrical Engineering (Cooper Union, 1974), was a founding member of the UNIX Standards Committee later known for producing the POSIX Standard, and holds two patents: one (#6,389,038) on Voice Over IP Bandwidth Utilization and the second (#6,981,035) on Managing a Flow of Network Status Messages, and has several other patents pending.
