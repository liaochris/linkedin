  • Assistant Professor of Computer Engineering

    (Educational Institution; 5001-10,000 employees; Higher Education industry)

    1996 — 1998 (2 years)

    Published in the areas of I/O-efficient algorithms, microarchitecture, and system performance analysis.

    Designed, implemented, and supervised student implementations in the SimKit environment. SimKit provided high-level simulator generation tools that enabled instruction set descriptions to be compiled into efficient simulators.

    Applied theoretical concepts from automata theory and statistics to create highly efficient branch predictor simulators.

    Consulted on simulator design for Motorola, IBM, and Ross Technology.

    Received School of Engineering Outstanding Teaching Award.

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; RTEC; Semiconductors industry)

    1995 — 1996 (1 year)

    Designed and built the first microarchitectural simulator capable of booting and running full system loads under SunOS.

    Supported micro-architects using the system to study caching, branch prediction, memory bandwidth, and I/O performance of proposed designs under full-system loads.

  • Ph.D. Student, Research Assistant, and Teaching Assistant

    Brown University

    (Educational Institution; 10,001 or more employees; Higher Education industry)
