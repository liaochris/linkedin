John Haley’s Summary

john_e_haley@yahoo.com

John Haley has 20 years of professional experience, the majority of which he spent providing management consulting services to Fortune 500 Financial Services companies. His industry experience is deepest in Banking with a focus on Consumer Finance and Credit Card sectors. His functional experiences have been focused on Customer Experience Management. John operates across the breadth of the Customer Lifecycle: acquiring, converting, servicing, and cross/up-selling customers, and is highly experienced in improving the effectiveness and efficiency of customer facing operations.

John is currently a Managing Director at PricewaterhouseCoopers serving in the Banking consulting practice. John is a lead in the Customer Centricity Practice whose focus is on customer experience management including sales and marketing operations, customer lifecycle development, customer relationship management, loyalty, and channel management. John is also a lead in the Credit Card & Payments Practice helping to lead ~130 mid and senior level resources skilled in these sectors.

Prior to PwC John spent 7 years at aQuantive Inc., holding company of Razorfish Inc, one of the world’s largest independent interactive technology and marketing management consultancies. John was a co-founder of the strategy practice and P&L lead for the Financial Services strategy practice. Later as Client Partner John led teams that helped F500 Financial Services companies build and manage their Web channels including strategy, marketing, and creative/technology implementation. A number of John’s clients have been cited by independent third parties such as Forrester, Gartner, and Gomez as ‘best practice’ and ‘best of breed’.

John is a 1988 graduate of the United States Military Academy: West Point where he was recognized for excellence in academics and leadership. John is also a 1995 graduate of the University of Chicago: Graduate School of Business

John Haley’s Specialties:

- Customer Experience Management across all phases of the Customer Lifecycle
- Customer Segmentation (needs, behavioral, attitudinal, demographic, etc)
- Growth strategy and implementation: market share (acquisition), share of wallet (cross/up-sell, pricing), retention
- Loyalty program development
- Channel management including channel analytics
- Customer operations efficiency including sales force effectiveness, contact center consolidation, self service, and channel migration
