Shannon Mullen’s Summary

I'm interested in building long-term relationships with creative business leaders in marketing/communications, product design and innovation.

I have always been interested in how brands help us express our own identities. I left an academic track 15 years ago as a would-be cultural anthropologist to work as an applied anthropologist in an ad agency and have pursued this same interest in brands through advertising, new product development, interactive communication between brands and people and most recently by developing brand experience stores.

For the last year I have been combining this interest in brands and identity-creation/development by working as an independent brand strategist and as a headhunter who helps people define the brand that is themselves and match-making between these individual personal brands an branding companies, consultancies and integrated ad agencies.

I know that this dual role may sound a bit odd but I'm really enjoying it and have a sense that I'm really helping people -- helping them define what they want and present themselves to the best of their ability to companies that would love to have them. I have deep relationships in the NY market with a range of brand-related companies and as for the talent I place in them, I'm always interested in meeting creative business thinkers from account people to account planners/strategists to interactive producers.

As for continuing to do brand strategy myself, I now get to pick projects and co-workers that I love. My hope is to find more companies who want help with their consumer insights and brand concepts earlier on in the process than the moment of communication. I want to help brands walk the walk and would love to find more new product design and brand design -- baking brand values into products and experiences.

Shannon Mullen’s Specialties:

Truly integrated marketing concepts including integrated brand programs (integrating PR, CRM, all advertising media channels, retail brand experience), new product ideas, naming, packing strategies, and most recently concept store development.
