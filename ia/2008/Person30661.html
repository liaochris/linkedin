Carl Jacobson’s Summary

My core focus is the empowerment of musicians.

As Cakewalk's Marketing Director I am responsible for messaging, branding, and creative vision for technology-leading music applications worldwide. In my role at Cakewalk, I have been blessed to work with pop icons including Ray Charles, George Clinton, Jon Anderson, and leading technology companies, including Intel and Microsoft in bringing their solutions to the music industry.

I regularly speak on creativity, music, technology, and marketing. My work with Cakewalk was recently spotlighted in Kevin Roberts' book "The Lovemarks Effect" (2006), and I recently moderated a panel of the some of the music industry’s most successful producers at the ASCAP I Create Music Expo. I write the Integral Music Blog at Microsoft’s Crossfader.com and I have also written for or been interviewed by Computer Music, Electronic Musician, Future Music, Keyboard, Post, Sound on Sound, and Wired.

My creative direction has resulted in the Judges Selection for Logo, Identity, and Packaging Design from Alternative Pick Magazine, an AD.Q Award for Outstanding Advertising from EQ Magazine, along with mentions of superior packaging design in the New York Times, On Computers, Computer Music, Future Music, and Remix.

As a musician I studied composition with film composer Shawn Clement, and I have achieved a few notable credits including original music in the Artisan Entertainment film Greenmail starring Stephen Baldwin, and the engineering for Igor Khoroshev's(Yes) solo album PianoWorks. I recently completed music for a benefit album for Kiva, an group who provides zero interest micro-loans to individuals in developing countries.

My previous work includes launching the Soundsbig.com Internet broadcasting network, and positions with New World Records and A&M. I currently have a TV treatment in development with a Hollywood producer and my long term goal is to start a school for creative individuals of all types.
