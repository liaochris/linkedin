  • Inspections Technician

    US Steel Corp

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; X; Mining & Metals industry)

    February 1974 — September 1974 (8 months)

    Inspection of continuous cast steel slab.

    Imagine standing 4ft from a 7ft wide x 8inch thick slab of red hot steel in Baytown (near Houston) Texas on a hot summer day (39degC, 96% humidity). The objective is to identify imperfections in the surface of the slab. Besides the heat load and persperation the main undesireable effect was an artificial sunburn on your face.

    To break the monotony there was an occasional "break out" wherein molten steel drips out of the casting machine comes into contact with cooling water thereby generating an explosive conversion of water to steam. If the "break out" occurs high in the casting machine near the mold, the explosion can blast molten steel out the top of the mold and injure various workers and damage the casting floor equipment.

    Continuous casting, a facinating process. See :
    http://www.kempro.com/products.htm
    http://www.123eng.com/projects/steel_melting.html
    http://www.tms.org/pubs/journals/JOM/0201/Thevoz/Thevoz-0201.html
