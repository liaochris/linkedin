Herb Wexler’s Summary

I concentrate on software usability in the broadest sense. This means working with all the product related departments (development, sales, training and support) to ensure that the right features are being delivered to the customers. The goal is to reduce the total cost of ownership. For example by working with the technical support people and the developers I can find ways to prevent technical support questions. Working with the trainers and users I can find areas of the software that are confusing or irritating and then work with the developers to simplify the product. The result is increased client retention and lower costs to maintain the existing clients.

    November 1987 — December 2005 (18 years 2 months)

    In 1987, I co-founded Micro Edge Incorporated. I designed and wrote the Medical Edge software which was used in over 300 offices in 11 different states.

    The software automated common tasks in an independent physicians' office. The design philosophy was driven by the fact that the users really have other jobs. The software had to be easy to learn and assist the office staff rather than the staff assisting the software. By watching how people use the software and monitoring the support calls, I simplified the software and reduced support costs.

    As the VP of Micro Edge, I frequently lent a hand when an area did not have the necessary staff or time. So on occasion, I did sales, purchasing, installations, training, support and customer relations. This enabled me to simplify these processes making sure that each area was functioning smoothly.
