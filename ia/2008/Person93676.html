Joelle Brink’s Experience

  • Research Specialist, Director, BioWeb

    University of Tennessee, Biofuels Program

    (Educational Institution; 10,001 or more employees; Research industry)

    January 2005 — Present (4 years)

    I presently work on the Sungrant BioWeb, bioweb.sungrant.org, a peer-reviewed biofuels education website sponsored by the The National Sungrant Association, a network of major research universities and government laboratories conducting research in biomass and biofuels development. BioWeb is at present the sole source of current, unbiased information on biofuels policy, feedstocks, technologies, and environmental impacts. The site had been developed at three levels, including a short, jargon-free level for laypeople, a second general level for journalists and others interested in more detail, and a third technical level for biofuels researchers.

  • Manager, Corporate

    NIIT

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; NIIT; Information Technology and Services industry)

    December 2001 — December 2004 (3 years 1 month)

    I was manager of a new JV company, The Hole in the Wall, formed for the purpose of determining whether all children are able to self-instruct when provided with computer and internet access in an environment that fosters group learning, and how best to assist their self-instruction. Our work focused on cognitive research and interactive design, technology development, and field research with slum and village children at 22 sites in India and overseas.

Joelle Brink’s Education

  • Harvard University Graduate School of Education

    Ed.M, Education and Media, 1968 — 1970

    I attended HGSE during the Harvard student strike and thus had more opportunities to participate in institutional governance and experimental projects than would otherwise have been the case. As a former teacher in the Boston Public Schools I had participated in an early METCO project that lent still and movie cameras to inner city children so that they could document their lives. Their work, broadcast on WGBH, Boston's Public Television station, resulted in a revolution within the Boston public school system, the introduction of school bussing, and exchange programs between inner city and suburban schools. This experience of the power of children to make positive social change for themselves and others, if provided with appropriate technology, led to my later work in The Hole in the Wall and other influential pilot project.

    Activities and Societies:
    HGSE Curriculum Committee, Harvard/Wilmington Project, Harvard Bureau of Study Counsel
