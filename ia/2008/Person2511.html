Adam Lynch’s Experience

  • President / CEO

    Coast Plating, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Aviation & Aerospace industry)

    August 2007 — Present (8 months)

    Coast Plating, Inc. provides metal finishing services to the aerospace industry, including non-destructive testing, cleaning and applying anodize finishes, chemical conversion, and paint coatings to a aluminum, titanium, stainless steel alloys.

    Coast was purchased by a private equity group in 2007, having previously been a family owned enterprise since 1964. Coast employs more than 80 professionals at facilities in Gardena and Carson, CA.

    Coast has been involved in every USA space exploration program since its inception, and continues to invest in new technologies to provide the highest quality service, while acting as custodians of the natural environment. Coast possesses numerous Prime Contractor approvals and is certified to AS9100 and NADCAP. As a result, Coast has been awarded multi-year contracts with both the Boeing and Northrop Grumman. Coast is committed to excellent quality, timely delivery, superior customer service with unmatched world-class resources and expertise.

  • Consultant/Program Manager, Boeing 5553 Titanium Forgings

    VSMPO-Tirus, US

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Airlines/Aviation industry)

    May 2007 — July 2007 (3 months)

    I served as the Program Manager for 20 Titanium 5-5-5-3 forgings to support Boeing's 787 platform. I purchased raw material from a mill in Russia and maintained material certifications, ensuring complying chemistries and Beta Transus temperature ranges. I imported and cleared customs in the USA, including the generation of Commercial Invoices. Additionally, I coordinated the qualification of suppliers for vacuum heat treating, destructive testing, dimensional inspection via CMM using solid model CATIA files. Moreover, I developed daily status reporting methods and completed job and certification recording keeping schemes using Lean Enterprise techniques and information technology tools.

  • General Manager

    Aerocraft Heat Treating

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Aviation & Aerospace industry)

    October 2004 — April 2007 (2 years 7 months)

    Aerocraft Heat Treating Company, Inc. is an independent commercial heat treater of steel, titanium & high temperature materials. Founded in 1959, Aerocraft processes forgings, castings, bar, plate and rough-machined parts.

    Systems are Nadcap and AS9100/ISO9001:2000 certified. Materials are processed for most of the aerospace prime contractors. Aerocraft operates to AMSH6875, MILH6875, AMSH81200, MILH81200, ISO10012, ANSI/NCSL Z540, MILI45208, MILSTD45662, and numerous other aerospace, commercial and federal specifications.

    Aerocraft operates 19 custom built, batch type, gas fired, air atmosphere furnaces with temperature ranges from 450F to 2250F, tolerances of +/-9F to +/-25F, and working zones as large as 18 ft x 20 ft x 7 ft. Material can be water or oil quenched, fan or air-cooled. Sub-ambient and sub-zero capabilities to -110F are also available.

    Straightening and forming are additional processes performed at Aerocraft.
