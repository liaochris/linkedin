Bonnie Lowe Jones’s Summary

UPDATE:

June 2007: Bailey, et.al v. Georgia Department of Transportation, Superior Court of Dawson County, GA. Jury verdict award in the amount of almost $2 million for the wrongful death of 67-year old woman caused by DOT's failure to properly maintain a bush located on its right-of-way. Largest jury verdict award in the history of Dawson County, GA. Lead counsel: Steven Leibel; Co-counsel: Bonnie Jones.

OVERVIEW:

Ms. Jones is an active member of the State Bar of Georgia and part of the Bar's Trial Practice Section. She is currently admitted to practice before all State and Superior courts throughout Georgia as well as the United States District Court for both the Northern and Southern Districts of Georgia. She is certified by the American Arbitration Association.

BACKGROUND:

Ms. Jones began her career in 2002 in Savannah, GA with Sage Brown & Associates, PC where she concentrated in civil litigation. In 2006, she relocated to Cumming, GA as an Assistant County Attorney with Jarrard & Davis, LLP, a boutique firm representing various county governments. She later moved to Casey Gilson Leibel, PC and began working with Steven Leibel (who currently holds the record for the highest jury verdict award in the history of Georgia- $776 million). When Mr. Leibel parted ways with Casey Gilson in May of 2007, Ms. Jones moved with him to his new office in Dahlonega, GA. In August of 2007, she left to open her own practice where she specializes in litigation.

To date, her career accomplishments include: a jury verdict on behalf of a customer assaulted by store owners; a favorable settlement on behalf of a hot-air balloon passenger for injuries sustained as the result of a crash landing; a six-figure settlement against a private utility company for personal injuries to a minor child arising from his being run over by a trailer; and favorable settlement against a chemical company for injury to a security guard caused by inhalation of toxic fumes.

Bonnie Lowe Jones’s Specialties:

Civil litigation, primarily plaintiffs' representation: personal injury; medical malpractice; toxic torts; transactional fraud; probate litigation.
