Kyle Machulis’s Summary

Throughout college, I worked for Microsoft as a Technical Evangelist. In this position, I worked with faculty to integrate MS technlogies into cirriculum, and started a user group to get students interested in MS software. I did two internships at the company, as a Software Tester and later as a Test Development Engineer.

During college, I was VP (1 year) and President (2 years) of the Association of Computing Machinery. I graduated from the University of Oklahoma with a Bachelors in Computer Science (Minor in Mathematics) in December 2002.

After school, I started work at the KISS Institute for Practical Robotics as a Software and Robotics Engineer, where I am still employed today. While working there, I developed software for space-related platforms as well as an array of embedded robotics and control devices. I engineered the internal web applications system for the company, as well as creating and maintaining many high-visibility applications. Much of this work relates directly to the Botball Robotics Competition (http://www.botball.org), fostering education in engineering and mathematics for middle school and high school students around the globe. Other work includes versions of Interactive C, a full featured, cross platform compiler and GUI for a version of the C language, targeted at small scale robotics.

In 2005, I started Nonpolynomial Labs, a research firm dedicated to producing small scale, low cost immersive environment hardware and UI research tools, as well as hosting an extensive blog network on varying technological subjects. Projects by NP Labs have seen press from many online gaming magazines, as well as articles in Wired.

From July 2006 to May 2008, I worked at Linden Lab, in San Francisco, California, as a Software Engineer on the Second Life (http://www.secondlife.com) virtual world.
