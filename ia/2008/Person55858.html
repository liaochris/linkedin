Henry Eason’s Summary

In 1989, I founded a small marketing and communications agency in San Francisco that has effectively served companies in many industries: financial, technology, hospitality, real estate, professional services, transportation logistics, basic manufacturing, life sciences, health care, government agencies and nonprofit associations.

We provide an integrated blend of marketing communications services (PR, media training, sales materials, advertising, direct mail, relationship marketing and online content) tailored to each assignment. We have launched entire companies, as well as many hundreds of products and services.

We have a small core staff and an extensive network of seasoned associates with whom we work in a collegial fashion--giving our clients both a quality and cost advantage over large and expensive bureaucratic marketing agencies.

Henry Eason’s Specialties:

Writing, public relations, media training and coaching, graphic design, direct marketing, marketing strategy, publication design and editorial, online marketing and Web site design and research.

Henry Eason’s Experience

  • Media Relations Director

    U. S. Chamber of Commerce

    (Non-Profit; 1001-5000 employees; Government Relations industry)

    April 1987 — April 1989 (2 years 1 month)

    I was head of the department that developed and implemented media strategies and tactics for the world's largest business federation, based in Washington, D.C. As director of what was an internal "PR agency," I managed a staff of five associate directors covering more than 90 business and economic issues. We communicated the business community's position to the Washington and international press corps. We provided them with information and resources they needed to understand the American business community's posture on trade, labor, financial, regulatory, environmental, legal and many other issues areas. We held frequent press conferences and theme discussions with the media.

    We also created innovative communications training and coaching programs for the staff attorneys and economists. I and my staff also provided counsel to several dozen ad hoc issue coalitions and hired and directed outside PR agencies.

  • Senior Editor

    Nation's Business magazine

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Publishing industry)

    February 1982 — March 1987 (5 years 2 months)

    I was senior editor for this 1 million+ circulation publication based in Washington DC, whose editorial mission was to cover business and economics from the public policy perspective. During my four years there I covered international trade and travel, energy, envrionment and defense beats.

  • Washington Correspondent

    Atlanta Journal-Constitution (Cox News)

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; Newspapers industry)

    September 1976 — February 1982 (5 years 6 months)

    I was political and business reporter based in Atlanta, then a Washington-based newspaper correspondent for Cox Newspapers, a newspaper chain of more than 30 dailies. I covered Congress, the White House, business and economic issues and the Pentagon.

  • State Editor/Columnist

    The Greenville News

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Newspapers industry)

    December 1973 — September 1976 (2 years 10 months)

    As a desk editor, I supervised our state capitol bureau, four regional news bureaus, many stringers, a staff writer and two obituary writers. In addition I wrote a political column.

  • Editor

    American Chronicle

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Publishing industry)

    March 1971 — April 1972 (1 year 2 months)

    Editor of American Chronicle, a monthly that published articles and documents of American history.

Henry Eason’s Education

  • The George Washington University - School of Business

    Adjunct Professor, Graduate School of Communications, 1987 — 1988

    Activities and Societies:
    Conducted seminars and lectures for students already employed in communications positions who were seeking a graduate education. Focused on promotional writing and media relations.

  • The University of Georgia

    M.A., Mass Communications, 1972 — 1974

    Before attending the Henry Grady School of Journalism to get my M.A. in mass communications, I spent a year in the Department of History graduate school studying for a Ph.D in political history. I dropped out--most reluctantly--when it was apparent there were very few teaching positions open in that field.

    Activities and Societies:
    I was a part-time editor at the University of Georgia Press.

  • Armstrong Atlantic State University

    B.A., Political science & history, 1966 — 1970

    Activities and Societies:
    I worked my way through college, so I had little time for social or fraternal activities.

  • Georgia Southern University

    Freshman year, Political science, 1965 — 1966

    I was a member of the completely nonexclusive but private Knights of Georgia. It was essentially a cinder-block building on the edge of Statesboro, where rowdy students and townies gathered to drink beer, eat pickled sausages and dirty dance in a county that banned alcohol. For $20 you'd get a card that you showed to this old guy with a big red nose, and he let you in. Very fond memories of that place. That's where I first learned to network.
