Maurie Dobbin’s Summary

Maurie Dobbin is a highly experienced telecommunications engineer and manager gained in over thirty year's involvement with government, operators, manufacturers and major users. During this period Maurie has been responsible for the founding of a number of service-orientated businesses each of which has established a reputation for innovation and professionalism in its own right

Principally, however, Maurie has established a reputation as one of Australia's leading experts in the field of wireless communications leading a number of landmark studies related to the development of national, regional and global markets. He has proven experience as a manager leading multi-disciplinary teams in marketing and engineering assignments.

Maurie possesses superior presentation skills with the ability to address the wide range of complex issues that are influencing the growth and development of the telecommunications industry.

Maurie Dobbin’s Specialties:

market analysis, negotiation, technology evaluation, report writing, radio communications standards, virtual operators, radio propagation studies and network optimisation, project management and new venture development..

Maurie Dobbin’s Experience

  • Managing Director

    LegalResources Pty Ltd

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Legal Services industry)

    March 2006 — Present (2 years 5 months)

    LegalResources has been formed to address the need for cheaper and more flexible para-legal services sourced from India's large pool of law graduates. Business Process Outsourcing (BPO) is a well understood way in which corporations can drastically reduce the cost of basic office functions. Legal Process Outsourcing (LPO) is an out-growth of this practice applied to the legal profession and one of the fastest growing segments within the industry. LegalResources applies consultancy and project management skills to contract para-legal support sourced from LexSphere International of India.

  • Managing Director

    TeleResources Engineering (Aust) Pty Ltd

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Telecommunications industry)

    January 1983 — Present (25 years 7 months)

    For the past twenty years Maurie has managed the operation of private companies engaged in the telecommunications industry. He was Managing Director of Laurie Systems Engineers (now known as LSE Technology) a project management and implementation company that he founded in the late 1970's. Subsequently he formed TeleResources Pty Ltd a telecommunications consultancy company. In these positions he had full financial accountability including the recruitment and management of specialist staff.

    In 2001 TeleResources Pty Ltd was sold to TeleResources Engineering (Aust) Pty Ltd with the company expanding its interests from consultancy into engineering services and software products for the wireless industry.

  • Co-Founder and Director

    VirTel Intelligence

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Telecommunications industry)

    February 2001 — December 2004 (3 years 11 months)

    VirTel Intelligence was Australia's first Mobile Virtual Network Enabler (MVNE) and a wholesale partner of Vodafone Australia. The company enabled the entry of new brands to the Vodafone network by providing access to white-labeled wholesale voice and data services. VirTel works closely with billing and customer service partners that charge on a 'pay-as-you-go' basis making it easy and economic for brands to launch their services.

    In December 2004 the company purchased the customer base of Vodafone ASP Mobile Innovations to form VirTel group and its associated MVNO, Reward Mobile.

    In June 2005 VirTel Group was acquired by Sonnet Corporation (ASX: SNN)

  • Managing Director

    Laurie Systems Engineers

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Telecommunications industry)

    1979 — 1983 (4 years)

    Laurie Systems Engineers was formed to undertake turnkey telecommunications projects building on the knowledge and contacts of past employees of Page Communications Engineers. I founded the company with the assistance of the directors of a boutique economic consultancy, The Implementation and Management Group and civil engineers Valentine Laurie and Davies.

    Following my departure in 1984, the company was divided into two entities, LSE Technology and LSE Manufacturing.
