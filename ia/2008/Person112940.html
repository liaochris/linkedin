Leri M. Thomas, Ph. D.’s Summary

Living in a rural community for the past 27 years has necessitated professional diversification. I started in newspaper and broadcast media in 1969 (graphics, illustration, typesetting, composition, litho, writing, photo, copywriting) at U. S. News & World Report in Washington, D. C. and went from there to Eagle River Alaska, where I worked for the Army and at the Eagle River Star.

Since then I have worked as a free lance entrepreneur in Virginia as a political activist, researcher, writer, PR consultant, an educator, contractor and a farmer. I have designed and painted theatrical scenery professionally since 1993.

My doctorate is in Social Foundations of Education; American Studies focused on education.

However, being a survivor of misbegotten matrimony and a single mother has proven to be some of my most valuable experience.
I have a firm grasp of the meanings of creativity and freedom because I have earned them, and I'm very good at showing others how to get there.



I'm open to all sorts of challenges, large or small.
