David Tschanz’s Summary

Convinced that "specialization is for insects", Dave Tschanz has advanced degrees in history, public health and arachaeology, as well as 9 computer certifications including an MCSE, MCDBA, CIW, A+ and Inet+.

From 1982-2001 He held a variety of jobs in epidemiology, including a VD investigator in South Carolina, head of infecyious disease epidemiology for the state of Arizona and was hired in 1989 by Saudi Aramco as head of the Field Epidemiology Program for the oil company giant.

In 2001 he was named Communications Officer and special consultant to the vice president of the 4000 member medical department, a position he currently holds. In addition to general writing and message tuning tasks, he also oversees the operation of the 80,000 page Saudi Aramco Medical Services Organization (SAMSO) website

He has written over 750 published articles on a wide variety of topics including infectious disease control, military affairs, demography, patch management, Kerberos secuirty, product review, history, Middle East affairs and travel.

Tschanz has authored or co-authored seven books, including a highly regarded 1997 work on the Nabataean capital of Petra.

His latest work, Designing an Exchnage Server 2007 Infrastrcuture: A Business Oriented Approach is due out in May 2008

He is co-author of Mastering SQL Server 2005 with Mike Gunderloy and Joe Jorden.

He also serves as editor of several internal publications as well as the military history journal "Cry Havoc!" and "Journal of Islamic Medical History" He is also author of many IT industry white papers.

A well regarded speaker he has been the Anne Nicholson Guest Lecturer at Pinewood College; a speaker at the Croft Institute for International Studies at the University of Mississippi as well as other venues.
