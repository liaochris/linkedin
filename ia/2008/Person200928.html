Jamie Workman’s Summary

Yale and Oxford graduate; award-winning journalist; recruited to U.S. Cabinet-level conservation speechwriter; essayist on water scarcity & global warming; strategist who focused teams at national and international level of conservation, communications and policymaking; in Africa and Asia tested durability of water and energy resources use, impacts and development in a changing climate; contract with Walker Press/Bloomsbury publishers to write forthcoming Heart of Dryness: A true story about the end of water.

Jamie Workman’s Specialties:

Synthesizing complex concepts, dry ideas, and jargon filled information into compelling, eloquent and persuasive essays and arguments for various audiences, ages and nationalities.

Jamie Workman’s Experience

  • Principal

    Confluence

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Environmental Services industry)

    January 2000 — Present (8 years 3 months)

    Confluence has developed innovative strategies on controversial water, dams and natural resources issues for: heads of state like Nelson Mandela; businesses like Bechtel; and NGOs like The World Economic Forum and The World Conservation Union. It drafted a cap-and-trade groundwater pumping policy for India’s government and a hydropower-revenue reinvesting strategy for China’s. An award-winning reporter, recruited by US Interior Secretary Bruce Babbitt, Workman pioneered dam removals in America. For the World Commission on Dams, Confluence synthesized the words and experiences of a global spectrum of competing interests. After two years living in a Land Rover in southern Africa writing about the causes and consequences of water scarcity, tracking the desert siege between Botswana and the last Kalahari Bushmen; Bloomsbury/Walker will publish Confluence's forthcoming book: Heart of Dryness.
