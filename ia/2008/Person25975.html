Brad J. Ryder’s Summary

Brad has worked in public policy and public affairs roles in a number of organizations at various levels around the world.

In Canada, Brad has worked at both the Provincial and Federal Levels of Governement as a Legislative Assistant to at Queen's Park in Toronto and as Research Assistant for a Senator in Ottawa.

In Geneva, Brad worked at the World Economic Forum (WEF) in Geneva as researcher for The Global Competitiveness Report, and Manager of the Healthcare Industries sector during the Annual Meeting in Davos and Regional Summits and was also instrumental in the creation of an effort to build public private partnerships in global health and worked with UNAIDS to assist with the coordination of private sector outreach on HIV/AIDS.

In 2002, Brad moved to Gaborone, Botswana to manage the external relations and communications of the African Comprehensive HIV/AIDS Partnership – a PPP between Merck and the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation to create a comprehensive model in the fight against HIV/AIDS.

In 2006, Brad joined Merck Frosst in their Toronto Office at the MaRS Centre as Industrial Policy Manager and worked on a number of issues related to Merck's Global Health and Corporate Social Responsibility Inititiatives as well as the development of Ontario's Research and Innovation Strategy.

In 2008, Brad joined Vale Inco - the nickel division of Vale - as Director of Corporate Affairs where he is responsible for the strategic planning and management of key issues, government relations and developing and maintaining relations with stakeholders.

Brad holds a BA and MA from the University of Ottawa, a Certificat d'etudes Politiques from l'Institut d'Etudes Politiques de Paris and and MSc in International Relations from the Graduate Institute of International Relations in Geneva.
