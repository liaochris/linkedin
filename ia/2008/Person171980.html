Sean Nelson’s Summary

2008 is going to be a great year. In September of 2007 I merged my company, Atlanta Health & Life (AHL) with Acuity Benefits Solutions (Acuity) and took over as President of the combined company.

I wasn't looking to merge at the time, but once I was introduced to Acuity and their innovative ideas on how to serve the small business client, it was an opportunity that I could not pass up. Acuity simply put, provides the services, that normally only large businesses receive, to their small business client's.

What are these services?

1. We brand the companies benefits to "the business", not the insurance company

2. We create unique, branded benefits training presentations and educate the employees.

3. We provide a "Benefits at a Glance" document to summarize your benefits in laymen's terms for your existing and potential employees.

4. We help you create a compensation statement so that your employees understand the financial commitment your company is making to provide these benefits.

5. We have a paid "Client Advocate" that handles all of your billing, claims, and benefits questions...you no longer have to call the insurance companies.

and so much more.

The emphasis is on helping the employer get a better return on their benefit investment. If your employees
1.) Don't understand their benefits; or
2.) Don't understand the cost of their benefits

you are not getting the full credit you deserve for providing employee benefits.

If you have a benefit plan in place you can keep the same plan, pay the same price and get these additional services. You're already paying for them in the form of commissions to your broker. The only question is "are you getting what you are paying for?"

If you don't have a benefit plan, we help you develop a benefits objective and strategy. Then find the best priced carriers to put your plan in place.

We're always your advocate.
