John Mennell’s Summary

"Feed a person, so they have the strength and the dignity to learn how to fish… next, you need to teach them to read!"

I founded MagazineLiteracy.org, a 501(c)(3) charity, as the first-ever, only global, magazine industry-wide family literacy campaign.

My professional career spans Capitol Hill, Wall Street, Main Street, and the Information Superhighway. I am a social entrepreneur recognized for innovation, leadership, and public service, and have extensive experience as an ITconsultant in the financial services, telecom, and human service industries.

During a summer at Cornell, I walked/hitched 20 miles each week to help teens start their own community newspaper. After graduation and working as a congressional campaigner, and a press secretary and legislative aide on Capitol Hill, I took a spirited run for the Maryland legislature. During the campaign I discovered empty food pantry shelves that led to organizing community and national hunger relief efforts.

A few years ago, to raise awareness about hunger in America, I walked from Washington, D.C. to Boston - over 1 million steps - 20 miles a day, for 30 days. I left with 63 cents in my pocket. There were many memorable moments. Somewhere in New England, I left a 63 cent tip on a free cup of coffee.

I've spent many hours contemplating the road ahead, and have encountered many forks in the road. Regardless of what path I choose and what attractions I see, my destination remains the same. My travels in this life are about joining with others to share and leverage our talents and resources, to improve the opportunities for children and families, and to create social and community wealth.

Some say, "when you give a person a fish, you feed them for a day." The essence of my journey is to "give a person a fish so they can have the strength to learn to fish...to teach a person hungry to read so they may learn all about the fish, and the ocean below, the world around, and the limitless sky above."

John Mennell’s Experience

  • Founding Director and Chairman

    MagazineLiteracy.org

    (Philanthropy industry)

    September 2004 — Present (4 years 5 months)

    Founded the The Magazine Publishers Family Literacy Project (MagazineLiteracy.org) - the first and only global, magazine industry-wide campaign to help children and families learn to read and to build their self-esteem. MagazineLiteracy.org devises innovative programs that foster community and business partnerships to get magazines into the hands, homes, and hearts of children and families so they can learn and love to read. Learning how to read is vital to a child's ability to succeed in every school subject and in life. Adults unable to read were once children who did not learn how. MagazineLiteracy.org unleashes the awesome potential of magazines as a powerful literacy resource for teachers, early-learning programs, after-school programs, boys and girls clubs, homeless and domestic violence shelters, child advocacy and foster care programs, and other literacy agents who are passionate about helping kids and families learn to read.

  • Founder

    National Emergency Hunger LifeLINE

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Philanthropy industry)

    1994 — 1999 (5 years)

    Engineered a national telephone hotline that used advanced telecommunications routing to connect over 50,000 people directly to local organizations that could use or provide help. The project was recognized by the ComputerWorld / Smithsonian Award program and the Benton Foundation for leveraging innovative technology in public service, and is archived in the permanent collection of the Smithsonian Institution.

  • Co-Founder and Manager

    NoVA CORE Resource Center for Non-Profits

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    1994 — 1999 (5 years)

    Partnered with the local volunteer bureau director to create a model three-story collaborative office facility for community non-profit organizations that was chronicled on the front page of the Wall Street Journal. The resource center received a Eureka! Award from the United Way.

  • Owner

    New Century Retailing (Sole Proprietorship)

    (Sole Proprietorship; Retail industry)

    1992 — 1994 (2 years)

    Founded start-up that was among first in U.S. to retail bottled water via vending machines; performed extensive beverage industry research; wrote detailed business plan, including financial projections; negotiated business agreements; executed market test in fitness center and high school.
