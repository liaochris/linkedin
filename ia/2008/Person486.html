Chris Saila’s Summary

Chris Saila -- principal of 416design.com, has been active in the web design community since 1997. Always looking for ways to innovate, and gain the favor of clients + colleagues, Chris has worn many hats in his profession. At age 21, Chris originally entered the professional realm of web design working for BLUEZONE ENTERTAINMENT with a passion and natural flair for Flash Motion Graphics + user interface design. The release of Version 4.0 of Macromedia Flash in late ‘99 gave way to the birth of ACTIONSCRIPT which brought his focus more towards the programmatic aspects of design forcing him to use the logical side of his mind in tandem with his artistic abilities. This opened up a whole new world of reference texts + specifications. Hired full-time to work on a 6 million dollar project, Chris was responsible for the video interface of CTVNEWS gemini-award winning news website. Being introduced to terms such as 'VIDEO ON DEMAND' and mentored by some of Canada's best skilled programmers+ designers, Chris used this opportunity to develop + enhance his already well-rounded skill set. Experience with Macromedia GENERATOR in early 2000 gave Chris the opportunity to learn, and put emphasis on dynamic + database driven content.

With this opportunity Chris also had his first exposure to emerging technologies (at the time) such as DHTML, CSS, SMIL (streaming media integration language), and using them to style REALVIDEO + QUICKTIME players for media delivery.

After leaving BLUEZONE, Chris was hired by FUEL ADVERTISING (later acquired by DRAFT WORLDWIDE). In the role of Senior Web Developer, He was responsible for designs for companies such as Hudsons Bay Co, Canadian Tire, FedEx, Grand + Toy, Hewlett Packard, Ontario Lottery + Gaming Corp, Sunquest Vacations, and Canada Post – experience was gained using as well as building Content Management Systems.
