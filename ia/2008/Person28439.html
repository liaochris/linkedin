Bruce McLellan’s Summary

Although I am involved in a number of projects, my main focus is in coordinating Disaster Recovery (DR) drills for Network Services at Xerox. This project involves acquiring, coordinating, and managing resources, scheduling drills, preparing drill documentation, attending and auditing of actual drills, reviewing final drill documents, and notifying management.

By nature DR drills are intended to be boring. We expect the best but, plan for the worst. So, they are executed after-hours so that normal business operations are not impacted. I'll take you through a drill from an eagle's perspective.

First, we simulate a network disaster by “breaking” (disabling) a network connection. At that point a redundant connection is supposed kick in and takes over. If everything works the way it is supposed to, then someone using that network to read email might notice a few seconds of disconnect as the backup network kicks in. If things go bad (and they do) we immediately reconnect the broken connection. And, then we celebrate. That’s right! We celebrate because the outage did not happen during business hours and because if a real disaster happens communication would have been lost. Potentially, business could be lost as well.
