Matthew Borten’s Summary

Matt Borten is a seasoned production pro, with more than 20 years experience as a writer, producer, director and editor of television series, specials, and films. Matt is also among the pioneers of interactive TV development, serving as a Producer with Discovery Channel InteractiveTV in the mid-1990s, and as Director of Content Development - Advanced Television with Discovery Digital Networks at the end of the 1990s. Matt has also served briefly as VP of Business Development & Professional Services for NetBlender, Inc. where he managed the business development and content creation of a small, start-up interactive multimedia firm that perfected the WebDVD platform (content partners included Sony Pictures Home Entertainment). Matt left NetBlender to start Retirement Living Television. He was the network's first Executive Producer / Content Developer (the 2nd overall employee at RLTV), where he oversaw production of RLTV's premiere program, the Telly Award winning show "The Daily Apple." During 2007-2008, Matt developed and EP-ed RTLV's twice Emmy-nominated investigative, news magazine program, known as "Viewpoint," anchored by Lea Thompson (formerly of NBC News and "Dateline"), managing a production staff of up to 30 people, including story developers, producers (from APs to Supervising Prods), editors, and graphic designers.
