Greg Corson’s Summary

I am very interested in exploring new technologies in entertainment, particularly high-end themed computer simulation based attractions and games both smaller scale (for retail locations) and large ones (theme park). I've been in the industry since 1975 and was one of the key developers of the Virtual World Entertainment multiplayer simulator game system. I would be very interested in becoming involved in similar projects in the future.

I believe we MUST make high-tech products easier for the average person to setup and use. I've had a lot of success doing this in the past so if you want to find out how to do it WITHOUT having to "dumb down" your product, I'm the guy to talk to.

I have also done a lot of research into business methods and products for digital music and video distribution.

Greg Corson’s Specialties:

Troubleshooting, Complex Systems Design/Analysis, Multiplayer Networked Games, Embedded Systems Programming, Location Based Entertainment (LBE), Social Environments For Games, PC and Console Games, User Interfaces, Product Design, Factory Automation Systems, High Reliability Systems, High-End Entertainment Simulators, Human Factors, User Interface Design, Radio and Television Production

Greg Corson’s Experience

  • Sr. R&D Software Engineer

    Sony Computer Entertainment America

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Computer Games industry)

    July 1999 — Present (8 years 8 months)

    My research (and the lab's in general) is dedicated to developing new technologies, tools and techniques related to computer entertainment on the current and next-generation Sony Playstation hardware.

    My work here has included digital content distribution, virtual worlds through distributed processing, PS3 library development, user interfaces, debugging/quality assurance of various libraries, experimental/demonstration software for PS2/PS3 and most recently working to develop, promote and assist others in using the COLLADA 3D content exchange standard which has become widely accepted in the game and 3D tools industry.

    Currently I have 7 patents pending, 2 for user interface devices, 1 for a content delivery platform and 4 for general information management.

  • Sr. Multimedia Applications Engineer

    NEC Electronics

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Computer Games industry)

    April 1997 — July 1999 (2 years 4 months)

    Did field applications support for the PowerVR line of 3D graphics hardware. This involved helping game developers to troubleshoot and improve performance of their games on PowerVR hardware on both PC and the Sega Dreamcast platform. I assisted teams representing over a hundred different game titles at major software development companies. Also provided design oversight and project tracking/management for the next generation of PowerVR chips.

  • Chief Software Engineer

    Virtual World Entertainment

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Computer Games industry)

    January 1990 — April 1997 (7 years 4 months)

    Developed high end simulators and multiplayer games for location based entertainment including giant robot combat in FASA's BattleTech and the 600mph "Red Planet" scifi race game. Sites included computers to create video broadcasts of games, printed scoresheets, do player training and had up to 32 simulator cockpits. The site design coordinated over 300 networked computers, microcontrollers and multimedia devices.

    I invented their "mission review" system that automatically printed a play-by-play commentary on the game for players to take home. It also featured a virtual cinematography system that made a video of the game with the production values of a TV sportscast. This could be live or stored for later playback.

    I also participated in the "Toaster Project", prototyping Virtual Reality games for a system Hasbro was considering marketing and had some involvement in early prototypes for the DisneyQuest project.

  • President/Owner

    Kangaroo Koncepts, Inc.

    (Privately Held; Myself Only; Computer Games industry)

    January 1980 — January 1990 (10 years 1 month)

    Kangaroo Koncepts was a company I operated to offer on-line services and do development of multiplayer games. Under this name I provided consulting services to Control Data Corporation, McGraw Hill, General Electric, Compuserve and Byte Magazine. I created one of the first public "computer bulletin board" services called "The Connection" that could handle multiple users and the first one that was successful at attracting non-technical users (tough in the early '80s). This was cited by a number of magazines as being the easiest to use piece of software they had ever seen.

    In the late 80's I also developed the on-line multiplayer first-person-shooter (FPS) game "A-Maze-ing" for the GEnie online service. This has been credited as the first ever commercial on-line multiplayer FPS game. (Credit for the first ever non-commercial game of this kind goes to XEROX PARC who developed "Mazewars" in the mid '70s)

  • Engineering Specialist

    Digital Interface Systems

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Electrical/Electronic Manufacturing industry)

    January 1988 — August 1989 (1 year 8 months)

    Developed factory automation computer systems for assembly lines, steel mills and other industries. High reliability of these systems was a must as the software and hardware had to operate in hostile/hazardous industrial environments but still be easy for factory workers to operate.

    Projects included steel-mill blast furnace control, the man machine interface system for a steel rolling mill, an oil depot truck loading system, assembly line monitoring in an appliance factory, a pull-through warehouse and several other industrial systems.

    I was also in charge of starting up a reusable software/hardware engineering initiative and was responsible for specifying configuration of computer systems with total values of up to two million dollars.

  • External Game Developer

    General Electric (GEnie)

    (Public Company; Computer Games industry)

    1984 — 1989 (5 years)

    Through Kangaroo Koncepts, Inc. (see above) I developed multiplayer games for the GEnie on-line service. This included multiplayer casual games such as checkers, chess and GO. I also developed A-Maze-ing, a first person shooter game (see above) and a prototype "text browser" system which cached the GEnie menu pages on the user's local computer, greatly speeding up access to the service when most users were connected on modems slower than 2400 bits/second.
