Pat Dane’s Summary

Pat Dane is an Entrepreneurial driven Executive who has had a successful 30 year career in business. Dane spent his first 15 years at Xerox departing as a Senior Executive. In July 1995, Dane created, and was CEO of, Dane Interactive, Inc. (www.PatDane.com ) a Boutique Consulting Company that helped newly funded Start-ups get traction in the marketplace. In the Spring of 1996, Dane Founded Tuneup.com ( www.tuneup.com ) an online PC service center which was acquired by Symantec Corporation.Dane was the first outside Business Development “Catalyst” for four of the current top ten e-commerce subscription sites. eFax, Gator, McAfee and NetFlix worked with Dane to gain their initial market momentum back in the late 1990’s. These sites today thrive on taking care of key e-commerce needs for the consumer marketplace. Dane is the Chief Revenue Officer for MyPublicInfo and is responsible for Sales & Business Development

  • Chairman & Chief Marketing Officer

    Tuneup.com, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Consumer Services industry)

    November 1995 — June 1997 (1 year 8 months)

    Executive Summary

    TuneUp.com solves the problem of personal computer maintenance for non-technical computer users through an innovative combination of “subscriberware”, content, and support services delivered via the Internet, introducing the concept of maintaining your computer the same way you maintain your automobile. The subscriberware model is based on generating an ongoing annuity stream from monthly subscribers that can easily exceed the cost of purchasing software with similar capability. Moreover, the cost of delivering subscriberware is significantly lower than the cost of existing retail software channels. We believe that TuneUp.com has a significant first-mover advantage.

    Tuneup.com was subsequently acquired by Symantec. The Anti Virus delivery vehicle that symantec uses today called Live Update actually originated from the architecture designed and developed by Tuneup.com.

  • President

    Pipeline Communications, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Consumer Services industry)

    June 1993 — July 1995 (2 years 2 months)

    Pat changed the registration process of all software and PC's sold to consumers in the Spring of 1993. The idea was that most people never filled out the registration card in the software box they purchased and even fewer people filled out the card in the PC they bought. The actual numbers back then were about 15-18% for software and less than 10% for PC's. From the period of August 1993 through June of 1995 Pat led the adoption and integration of Electronic Registration into the heart of the 10 largest PC players ( Compaq, IBM, HP, Acer, NEC, etc.) and 50 of the largest software companies in the world ( Symantec, Lotus, Adobe etc.). The results of the Pipeline Electronic Registration process increased registration rates from 15% in software and less than 10% on PC's to over 75% which had a direct impact on the bottom line net profits of over 77 of the largest companies in the industry. Pipeline Communications was sold to a public company, Intelliquest Communications, Inc.

  • President & CEO

    SofNet

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    June 1991 — May 1993 (2 years)

    Pat took over this struggling Windows based Fax software company (SofNet, Inc.) in July of 1991. The company was losing $50,000 per month and couldn't get its product to market. Pat took over as President and CEO and immediately raised $2 million dollars in financing, hired a new management team, and acquired new technology and a group of World-Class software developers. With the underlining technology that was the best in the business and a new name FaxWorks for Windows, Pat and his team opened up Retail Distribution to over 12,000 Retail outlets and went on a frontal assault to the PC OEM market. After a period of 5 months, FaxWorks for Windows was the #1 player in the PC OEM marketplace with its software being shipped on PC's ranging from IBM, HP to Nec and over 15 others. In the Retail marketplace FaxWorks was #2 behind market leader WinFax. SofNet was saved from extinction and bankruptcy and later sold to a public company, Global Village Communications, Inc.
