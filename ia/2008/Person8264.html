Alpay Ulku’s Summary

Alpay Ulku is a Senior Technical Writer specializing in IT, ERP, and business process documentation, with a background in regulated industries. Alpay’s most recent projects include creating end-user documentation for Bank of America’s anti-money laundering software, for which he also gathered functional requirements and performed analysis. Other experience includes creating a PeopleSoft HCM manual for the Chicago Mercantile Exchange and Unix SOPs for a PepsiCo SAP implementation. Alpay has frequently performed dual roles as a senior technical writer/documentation specialist and business analyst.

Alpay has driven documentation projects that involved working with Subject Matter Experts to inventory existing documentation, identify gaps, institute a project plan, create document templates and change control systems, win buy-in for the created documentation, and route and publish documentation. Alpay has designed documentation systems and templates that allow stakeholders to take ownership over the process, and can customize these templates and systems for clients or can create new ones for them. Alpay has also gathered functional requirements and performed analysis as a Business Analyst for non-documentation-related projects.

Throughout his 10+ years as consultant, Alpay has worked with Subject Matter Experts across the organizational spectrum to document procedures, info-map process flows, and define and articulate department-level and division-level policies. He has gathered information by utilizing internal and external first-source documentation (such as run books and design documents) as well as via group meetings and one-on-one interviews.

Before becoming a technical writer, Alpay taught writing and communications in colleges and universities, including Carnegie Mellon University. Alpay Ulku holds a Master’s degree from the University of Iowa Writers’ Workshop, the number-one writing program in the country.
