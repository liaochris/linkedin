V Ivana Foster, M.Ed., IPMA-HR’s Summary

V.Ivana Foster, M.Ed., IPMA-HR is an Human Resource Consultant within the renowned State Personal Administrative (formerly the Georgia Merit System) Georgia Leadership Institute and Enterprise Learning Division, one of the many initiatives sponsored by Governed Purdue’s Commission for a New Georgia (CNG). She serves as a member of Council for State Personnel Administration (CSPA), and International Facilitators Association (IFA). Ivana is also a certified instructor of the Franklin Covey’s 4 Roles of Leadership®; The 4 Disciplines of Execution® and The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People® and International Public Management Association for Human Resources (IPMA-HR).
Ivana’s instructional and curriculum design experience and knowledge was exhibited when she served as one of the two-instructional/program designers for the Georgia Department of Juvenile Justice, Management, and Leadership Development Program (MLDP), implemented in 2003. Developed with a focus on management and leadership competencies, this program is currently producing successful executive graduates for its agency.
Dedicated to her passion involving the enhancing and building of “Success-Driven Individuals” within the community, Ivana serves as Division Director of the Leadership Program for The Jewell Jackson-McCabe Emerging Leaders Institute (ELI).
Ivana is currently publisher and Editor-in –Chief of The SpeakHers’ Notes®.
Ivana’s academic achievements include a Bachelor of Arts in Psychology/Sociology and a Master of Education in Instructional Technology/Curriculum Design. Embracing the notion of continuing to “sharpen her saw”; she is currently a Doctoral student pursuing her Doctorate of Education in Instructional Leadership.

V Ivana Foster, M.Ed., IPMA-HR’s Specialties:

Life Coach: assisting "Success-Driven" individuals, teams, and organizations. Specializing in assisting women 18 and over as well as women in the life.
Creating, delivering, and implementing topics ranging from curriculum and skill development to diversity and culture competency training.

  • Pressure Point Control Tactic (PPCT) Instructor

    Certification, Defensive Tactics: Law Enforcement/Juvenile Justice, 1999 — 2003

    PPCT Defensive Tactics course is the first subject control system developed through tactical, legal, and medical research. Tactically, the system addresses the most common types of resistance officers encounter, allowing the instructor to maximize training on job related techniques. Legally, the PPCT System teaches a simple use of force continuum which clarifies the appropriate force level for every level of resistance. Medical research was conducted on every PPCT technique to refine technique efficiency and to ensure the medical implications were proportional to the level of resistance.
