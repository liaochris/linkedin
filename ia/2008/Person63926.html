Martin Filko’s Summary

Martin Filko is an Adviser to the Minister at the Ministry of Finance of the Slovak republic. He is responsible for research, analysis and policy advisory in issues related to education, health care, social security, science & research and knowledge economy. He is also a Junior Researcher and PhD student in
Economics/Behavioral Decision Science at Erasmus University Rotterdam. At the moment, his academic work focuses on case-based decision theory, incentive-compatible assessment of human visual attractiveness, regret theory and equity in health.

He was a Chief Analyst and a Project Manager at Sanigest Solutions, health and human services consulting company. As a part of Sanigest team, he participated in several projects in Slovakia and Central and Eastern Europe. His duties included consulting and advisory work in both policy and business areas. Above all, he was involved in assessing investment feasibility and general economic climate for the investments in health care for various clients in the CEE region, including a health care private equity investment fund. Internally, he was responsible for the development of the Sanigest Analysis team. Prior to joining Sanigest, Mr. Filko worked as an analyst for the Ministry of Finance of the Slovak Republic.

Mr. Filko received his MA in Clinical and Organizational Psychology from Comenius University, Bratislava. He also took two semesters in Philosophy. He holds Master's degree equivalent in International Economic Relations from the University of Economics in Bratislava. He supplemented his academic experience by studying at the University of Nijmegen (International Economics) and Erasmus University Rotterdam (Economics), where he also completed MSc studies in Health Economics and is working towards a PhD in Economics/Behavioral Decision Science.

His areas of expertise include health economics, behavioral decision science, empirical economics, economics of human services and public economics.
