Marcia Call’s Summary

Currently an investor in the firm of McKinley Marketing Partners, Inc., Marcia Call has worked in the staffing arena for more than a decade. A well-rounded professional whose expertise has been utilized by companies in many different industries, Ms. Call was named President of McKinley in 2005 and served in this capacity until December 2007.

After a successful career in marketing and consulting, Ms. Call was given the opportunity to contribute her expertise to the growth and development of McKinley in 1999. She eventually became responsible for the company's entire sales, recruiting, and marketing efforts. Her experience has shaped many of the company's key operating principles and best practices.

Prior to her work with McKinley, Ms. Call served both trade and consumer nonprofit organizations. With the American Trucking Associations, she managed the association’s extensive acquisition and retention program. In this role, she created and managed an award-winning membership campaign using corporate volunteers from some of the nation's largest transportation companies. Earlier with the Cystic Fibrosis Foundation, she oversaw the start-up of a national telemarketing facility, producing a net return on investment in the first six months of operation. She also had profit and loss responsibility for 14 chapter operations. Previously, she managed public relations/information for one of the March of Dimes largest chapters.

Ms. Call is a cum laude graduate of Bates College where she earned a B.A. in English, graduating Phi Beta Kappa and with honors in her major. After college, she served in the Democratic Republic of the Congo (Zaire) with the Peace Corps, providing health information in Swahili and French to a rural population in the eastern region of Kivu. A native of New Castle, New Hampshire, Ms. Call now resides in Alexandria, Virginia with her husband and four children.
