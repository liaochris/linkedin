Brianna Williams’s Summary

Brianna Williams is a federal law clerk for The Honorable Ralph E. Tyson, Chief Judge, in the United States District Court in the Middle District of Louisiana. In her role as a judicial law clerk, Ms. Williams analyzes pretrial motions, drafts rulings, and attends trial proceedings.

Ms. Williams is also the Director of Communications for the National Bar Association, the oldest and largest minority bar association in the nation, with a network of over 44,000 African-American attorneys and judges. Her duties encompass the management of presidential communications which include speeches, press releases, and internet operations.

During law school, Ms. Williams clerked for a large, international Virginia-based law firm in Atlanta, a smaller New Orleans law firm, a private equity firm, and two federal judges. Also during law school, Ms. Williams served as an active member of the Trial Advocacy Board and the Frederick Douglass Moot Court team -- which, in 2007, placed second in the region and fourth in the nation.

Prior to law school, Ms. Williams ran a website and graphic design company aimed at addressing the unique needs of creative professionals. As part of her duties, Ms. Williams brokered CD replication agreements with nationally recognized companies; regularly assisted her clients with the attainment of copyrights and mechanical licenses for the recording and manufacture of cover songs; and facilitated the digital distribution of her clients’ releases through established outlets.

Ms. Williams is also an active member of the community. She has served as a guest lecturer in Music Business and Promotion at Loyola University, she has participated as a panelist and speaker in the areas of networking and development at Louisiana State University, and she is an active motivational speaker at elementary and middle schools.

Ms. Williams is a member of the State Bar of Georgia. In her free time, she enjoys playing jazz piano professionally.
