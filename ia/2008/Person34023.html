Chauncey H. Robinson’s Summary

As an emerging professional in the oil & energy industry, Chauncey was a 2007 Houston Summer Intern for ConocoPhillips; the third largest oil company in the world.

He holds a Bachelor of Arts degree in Psychology from Alabama A&M University. Currently, he is completing his Master of Business Administration degree at Clark Atlanta University.

Chauncey H. Robinson is the chief executive officer and founder of the Earthtone Foundation, Inc.( formerly the Chauncey H. Robinson Youth Foundation); a philanthropic and program oriented organization that funds, supports, and evolve global environmental awareness to inner city communities.

His affiliations include Omega Psi Phi Fraternity, Inc; the Atlanta Urban League Young Professionals; the National Black MBA Association Atlanta Chapter; Atlanta Inner Circle; American Marketing Association Atlanta Chapter; Young Professionals in Energy; and the Alabama A&M University Alumni Association.

His personal statement is from the famous quote, “Carpe Diem-Seize the Day.”

He has broad experience in sales, marketing, operations, and consulting. His interest involves career opportunities in the oil & energy industry.

Chauncey is seeking a technical or business consulting opportunity where skills in collaboration, strategic planning, client management, and customer satisfaction are necessary and will be enhanced; Role should heighten his business strategy development, technology implementation, and operational improvement knowledge, experience, and accomplishments; Comfortable working both independently and as an integral member of a consulting team.

Chauncey H. Robinson’s Specialties:

Excellent analytical and problem solving skills, marketing segmentation, branding, strong quantitative and qualitative skills. Unique and diverse background in energy system analysis, downstream oil & gas operations, digital oilfield solutions, production optimization, corporate/organizational energy efficiency planning, energy policy and social entrepreneurship.

    • Developed alternative supply plan to avoid double taxation.
    • Recommended terminal re-classification as an element of the new supply plan, which would result in a savings of $29MM for ConocoPhillips.
    • Evaluated the potential risks associated with the recommended changes and provided solutions to mitigate the risks.
