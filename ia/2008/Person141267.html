Giandomenico Sica’s Summary

I graduated in Philosophy (University of Milano, 2003) with a thesis in Philosophy of Science. After the bachelor (2004) I co-founded Polimetrica, an international scientific publisher. In four years Polimetrica published more than 80 books and was one of the first worldwide company to publish open access academic books.
Open-access (OA) literature is digital, online, free of charge, and free of most copyright and licensing restrictions. Polimetrica has developed a business strategy to make this publishing model sustainable.
We have cooperated with many researchers coming from some of the most important universities of the world: e.g. Harvard, MIT, Stanford, Oxford, Chinese Academy of Sciences, Columbia University.
In 2005 I was visiting researcher in Logic and Philosophy at the University of Neuchâtel where I have co-organized the "1st World Congress and School on Universal Logic". Universal logic is the field of logic that is concerned with giving an account of what features are common to all logical structures. The second edition took place in 2007, in China. The event was sponsored by several scientific institutions, such as Association for Symbolic Logic, Swiss National Science Foundation, Swiss Academy of Sciences, National Nature Science Foundation Committee - China, Chinese Association for Artificial Intelligence, Chinese Mathematical Society. In 2008 I started working as director of communication and innovation at Itsme, a spin off company of the University of Milano - Bicocca that is designing and developing a new personal computer for the knowledge worker. This computer has an embedded operating system based on a metaphor that is different from the desktop/folders one: it is the metaphor of "stories and venues".

My main interests pertain innovation in the publishing area, namely in the design of products and services aimed at knowledge sharing.
