Trevor Cornwell’s Summary

Trevor Cornwell
Founder
Embarkons, Inc.

Trevor Cornwell, Founder and President, Embarkons, Inc., a Palo Alto
based Social Networking Utility that connects people through their
ideas and projects. Cornwell's career has been spent developing and
growing new enterprises in media and technology-based businesses
deploying Internet technologies to provide content and streamline
customer offerings. Most recently Cornwell developed video personalization software which produces customized video streams based on viewer behavior.

In 1997 he founded the business jet reservation network Skyjet,
after determining that technology solutions for aggregating
charter aircraft inventory would create market efficiencies. He
secured private financing and developed and launched the first
real-time booking engine for the charter jet industry in 1998. In
July 2000, Montreal-based Bombardier acquired Skyjet.

Prior to forming Skyjet, Cornwell started several media companies in
emerging markets, including CET Online, an early pioneer in Internet-distributed news.

Earlier in his career, Cornwell founded a not-for-profit
organization, the National Service League, Inc., which brought
businesses and young people together to aid in the transition to
emerging democracies in Eastern Europe.

Cornwell has also served in positions of responsibility for political
organizations, notably as Deputy Political Director for the U.S.
Democratic Senatorial Campaign Committee. He also has held leadership
positions with several U.S. political campaigns.

Born in New York City in 1964, Cornwell is a native of
Hastings-on-Hudson, NY. A popular speaker at industry
conferences and national business forums, he has
been quoted extensively in the national business press. Cornwell
received a Bachelor of Arts degree from Johns Hopkins University in
1987. He is married and has a daughter.

Trevor Cornwell’s Specialties:

Embarkons is a new social utility network that people will use to build momentum for ideas and projects. Users post ideas from newly hatched projects to business proposals to up-and-running start-ups, which creates an idea profile, called an embarkon. These user-owned profiles become the center of sociality for Embarkons. The collection of ideas, users and information creates an idea-centric social network that builds and measures momentum.
