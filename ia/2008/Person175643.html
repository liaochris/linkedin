John Shulansky’s Summary

Mr. Shulansky is an experienced adviser, consultant, and member of Board and C-level teams. His consulting practice was founded in 1993 and specializes in organizational design and strategy, governance and board development, institutional advancement and public policy, management, and capital formation. Engagements include corporate, private, nonprofit, voluntary, and government organizations.

Mr. Shulansky has a long history of leadership in community service, guiding a number of organizations through significant strategic issues, with a particular passion for policy formulation, health care and external affairs. A member of the Executive Committee at Hebrew Health Care - where he was Chairman of the Board from 1989-1992 - he presently chairs the human resource, executive compensation and officer nominating committees, and serves on the finance and governance committees. He was two-term member and Minority Leader of the West Hartford (pop. 63,000) Town Council and an active member of the Audit, Budget and Finance, Community Planning and Physical Services Committees. He also served two terms on the West Hartford Ethics Commission. He is founder and President of the West Hartford Land Trust; trustee and founding past President of the Hebrew Health Care Foundation; Corporator of Hartford Hospital; member of a Board of Visitors of the University of Hartford; officer and director of Tumble Brook Country Club; board member of the Anti-Defamation League of Greater Hartford; as well as past Chairman and President of the Capitol Region Forum for the Future; and, past Chairman of the PGA Tour Canon Sammy Davis, Jr. Greater Hartford Open.

Mr. Shulansky actively seeks consulting engagements and certain instances, funding for entrepreneurial ventures. He is open to explore strategic alliances that could further mutual interests, on behalf of clients, employment, and various other entrepreneurial opportunities.
