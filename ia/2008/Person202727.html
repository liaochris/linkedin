Yoram Vardi’s Summary

I started my conversation with computers when I got my first computer at age 13. It was an Atari and I quickly moved from BASIC programming into machine code in order to write some application and games.
As I grew up I continued my journey with computers and technology and as a teen I had written code and applications for various peoples and companies such as an application to calibrate an automated landing system for Israel’s Airport Authority and as system administrator for a company that had several VAX minicomputers.
Later in life I have opened my own startup company, left it and landed a job at Geo interactive (later known as Emblaze Systems) as a C++ developer (where I earned the nickname "the eraser" for my knack for finding and fixing bugs). I've quickly moved through the ranks (team leader, department manager) and finally became the company’s software architect. I've continued my career in Shunra software and later at Random Logic as their System / Software Architect for their online gaming platform.
Currently I am well versed in all current development, network, database and network technologies. I especially enjoy taking a new problem, breaking it into its various components and to look for the best solution: software, system, network and resource wise.
