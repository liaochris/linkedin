Emily Dugan’s Summary

Emily is an interdisciplinary artist with a focus on collaborative and ensemble work. Through her work with educational and outreach programming Emily strives to instill creative thinking skills and a sense of artistic community in youth and teens in a variety of economic, social, and educational settings.
As a performer and in her development of new works Emily often asks her audience to change their perception of or question their existing views on a particular subject or issue. She tries, however, to accomplish this goal while maintaining a sense of humor and high production value in all her work.

Emily Dugan’s Experience

  • Artistic Director

    Teen Comedy Fest (Chicago Improv Festival Productions)

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Performing Arts industry)

    December 2006 — Present (1 year 11 months)

    As the Artistic Director of the Teen Comedy Fest, Emily works with Chicago Improv Festival Productions to create, organize, and produce a yearly two-day festival for teen improvisors. This year's festival (on October 19th an d20th) will take place at the South Shore Cultural Center and will feature a full day of intensive workshops for each participant followed by an evening of performances by teen troupes from across the country.
    Emily also teaches numerous improvisation workshops throughout the year, specializing in Ensemble Development, Character Development, Hosting and Emcee skills, and Freestyle Rap.

  • Artistic Director, School of the Arts and Outreach

    Beverly Arts Center

    (Non-Profit; 51-200 employees; Performing Arts industry)

    April 2005 — Present (3 years 7 months)

    As the Artistic Director of the School of the Arts Emily guides the artistic mission and oversees the daily operations of the Music, Visual Arts, Dance, Drama, Literary Arts, and Fitness departments at the BAC. The school offers 10-week sessions in Fall, Winter, and Spring as well as additional classes and camps during the summer and December's special four-week "Sneak Peek" session.
    As the Director of Outreach Emily works with about 30 schools and organizations in the greater Chicagoland area to create and implement arts programming both at their campuses and at the Beverly Arts Center itself. While most outreach programs are situated in the neighborhoods closest to the Beverly Arts Center, the center has formed programming partnerships with schools as far north as Lincolnshire.
    Both programs share a focus on community and ensemble, arts integration, and interdisciplinary projects.

  • Freelance Director

    Self-employed

    (Self-Employed; Myself Only; Performing Arts industry)

    January 2004 — Present (4 years 10 months)

    As a director of student, community, and professional productions Emily specializes in colorful, large-scale productions with a strong visual impact and a dry sense of humor. Currently, Emily regularly directs for Immaculate Conception Grade School, Elmhurst Children's Theater, The Beverly Arts Center, and the Chicago Womens' Charity Players.
    Currently, Emily is in the process of directing a re-mount of her original musical, "The Night Before Christmas", at Beverly Arts Center.
