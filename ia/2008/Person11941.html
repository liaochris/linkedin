Andrew Simone’s Summary

My goals and experience are, as they should be, very much related. My most fundamental goal is being able to express and experience, idea, or perspective as effectively as possible. In short, to answer questions that are important or fascinate me. There are three broad, but interwoven, tracks that followed:

My first question was, "How can I best express being human and what does it mean?" This brought through my unique discussion based education studying Western thought and, finally to my theological studies. When I felt I had a sufficient answer, I moved on.

The second question stemmed from the observation that Americans in particular seem inarticulate about taste: "How can I describe this food or beverage intelligably?". The obvious place to start, at least for me, was in the Wine industry. And, while I am not a connoisseur, I am much more facile with descriptors than I have ever been. My hope at this point is to increase my breadth of knowledge, not just with alcohol, but also with proper food parings.

My third and final question (for the moment) is more meta: what is the best way of expressing a particular concept? This has brought me to a fascination with design, both visual and sound (two areas very much outside my expertise), and the aggregation of data. Hence, my proddings on the internet (clusterflock.org) and my current employment at a small start-up that produces gadgets. Our most recent line is a variety of kitchen measurement products (kitrics.com).

Andrew Simone’s Specialties:

Communicating complex ideas simply, negotiation, connecting people, listening, not presupposing, and knowing the right questions and who the best people are to answer them.
