Maria Angeles Capellades’s Summary

My expertise lies in research, development, prototyping, analysis and implementation of novel algorithms in the areas of digital image processing and computer vision. I have also lead teams of 4-5 people responsible for the development of software applications for satellite data analysis, related product generation, and data visualisation.

In particular, I have been heavily involved in both the research and the systems engineering aspect of software development in the context of Earth Observation and space-borne optical systems applications, i.e. gathering information from scientists, customers and/or end-users, defining requirements, prototyping, designing product and systems, as well as benchmarking scientific algorithms and solutions. I focus on designing software and systems that can be easily adapted to fast-changing requirements, and managing the development of the corresponding test tools, test data and configuration control procedures for efficient fast development of quality software.

I enjoy working in multi-disciplinarian scientific environments the most. I have a Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering (in Computer Vision) from Penn State and a Masters in Physics & Electronics Engineering from Simon Bolivar University.

    RapidEye AG

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Aviation & Aerospace industry)

    September 2007 — Present (1 year 2 months)

    In charge of designing satellite-image-based (multi-sensor) products and integrating them to weather services for the crop insurance industry in the US and Europe. Based on knowledge I gather from the customer and the end-users, I am responsible for the conception, prototyping, benchmarking, and development of required algorithms, conception of final products, design of the service and system, as well as leading a team of 4 people for the implementation of pilot and operational services. The service is based on Landsat, Spot and DMC data, but it will be implemented and customized for RapidEye sensor data.
