Don Blohowiak’s Summary

At Lead Well, I provide leadership development and coaching services to “leaders of teams and teams of leaders.”™

Using a highly effective "whole system" method, executives and professionals achieve both results and fulfillment while successfully adapting to complexity under pressure.

This systems-oriented approach to leadership coaching is drawn from behavioral science. You'll experience a coaching process that is highly responsive to your needs and based on sound, developmental principles.

My training as a coach includes an outstanding graduate certificate program in Leadership Coaching at Georgetown University, and on-going work toward a Ph.D. in Human and Organizational Development. My insights into effective leadership were honed over more than 20 years of executive experience in Fortune-500 companies, high-tech firms, and not-for-profits, and as the author of many management books and articles.

When engaged as my coaching client, you'll find that you are working toward meaningful objectives with some rigor. Action Learning is a central component in the process, and that means that your coaching relationship will be much more than chatting. You’ll learn by doing.

That said, I consider myself a developmentalist not a behaviorist in my approach. What you *do* will change because how you *think* will change.

Moreover, you'll find that I favor relatively short-term coaching rather than a dependency relationship. My objective is to help clients develop skills they can draw on for a lifetime.

Most of my clients are accomplished and smart, holding significant positions of responsibility with advanced degrees. (About a third hold doctorates, and more than half are women.)

While my work has spanned virtually every sector and industry, I have a special preference for working with "Leaders who are working to make the world a better place" in non-profits. Ask about our "Better Leaders, Better World" project.

Don Blohowiak’s Specialties:

• Building (rebuilding) trust within an executive group;
• Professionals recently promoted to executive positions or who have a new boss or new responsibilities
