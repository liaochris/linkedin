Bayu Slamet’s Summary

A young, skilled IT professional with leadership ambitions.

Currently I am highly interested in how business process improvement paradigms may benefit from a Service-Oriented Architecture (SOA). I strongly believe in the profound agility gains that can be the result for companies that heavily rely on IT if SOA and process improvement approaches are combined with agile software engineering practices. Last year I read everything about the Theory of Constraints (ToC) by Eliyahu Goldratt and recently I learned about Lean Manufactoring which was conceived by Toyota. At KLM I will find myself in an environment where I can acquire in-depth experience with an enterprise-scale SOA. There I want to deepen my understanding of the possibilities and limits for IT agility and I am rather keen on combining all these text-book theories with real-world experience.


As of 2000 I have been studying the amazing science of artificial intelligence and in particular the vast complexities faced by fully autonomous teams of rescue robots. In my research I addressed the highly challenging task where a robot-team needs to explore and map a disaster site and locate as many victims as possible without human assistance or tele-operation. In 2007 I graduated with honors for my master of science at the University of Amsterdam.

Besides my studies I have also always worked for 3 days per week as IT professional ever since 2000. In addition, I have been responsible for several software engineering projects on a freelance basis as of 2003. Therefore I currently also have 7 years of working experience as IT professional. For my current employer I hold the position of Software Architect.

In January 2008 I will start as J2EE Project Architect at KLM Royal Dutch Airlines in the Information Services - eCommerce department.
