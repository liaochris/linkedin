Kristian Darigan’s Summary

Kristian Darigan is a marketing strategy, corporate citizenship, business management and fundraising specialist. She has led the creation of many of the nation’s most critically-acclaimed programs and campaigns for some of the world’s leading organizations, including: Alticor, American Heart Association, American Red Cross, Dell, Gillette, Make-A-Wish Foundation, Microsoft, PNC Bank, Sprint PCS, Thrivent, Wal-Mart, Washington Mutual, Wells Fargo and Western Union, among others. Darigan has also provided general strategic marketing counsel for accounts such as Timberland, RadioShack, ConAgra Foods and Whole Foods Markets.

As an expert in cause, she serves as a spokesperson and futurist for Cone on social marketing trends, and she has been a featured keynote and panel speaker at many conferences and seminars for organizations including: American Marketing Association, Cause Marketing Forum, Harvard Business School, Duke’s Fuqua School of Management and MIT Sloan School of Management. She has been featured in Jocelyn Daw’s Marketing for Nonprofits, PR News’ Top 100 Case Studies in PR, and Philip Kotler’s Marketing: Improving the Quality of Life, Third Edition. Recently, her work with the Alliance for a Healthier Generation was included in Bill Clinton’s Giving and her most successful cause campaign, Go Red For Women, was turned into a Harvard Business School case study.

She served as an advisor in the creation of the PR News Nonprofit Awards and for many years, a judge for awards programs administered by PR News, The Publicity Club of New England and Net Impact. Her client work has been honored with more than 45 industry awards for unprecedented shared business and social impacts.

She blogs at www.doyoustandforsomething.com and is working on a new book on cause marketing for nonprofits.
