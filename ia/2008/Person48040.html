Dawn Bradley Cooper’s Summary

Dawn Cooper is the Owner of Soma Educational Services, whose workshops are geared towards providing students, parents and the organizations that serve them with the opportunity to reach their full potential through education. It has been Dawn's lifelong goal to provide those who lack the information, motivation and the resources to obtain a better quality of life through education, the assistance needed for them to succeed. As a former college basketball player, Dawn has an additional interest in helping student-athletes achieve a positive balance and utilize their unique opportunities to succeed. Dawn is also the Director of the Early College Initiative in the Board of Regents P-16 Office. Early Colleges are small schools where traditionally underrepresented students earn both a high school diploma and up to 60 hours of college credit. She served as the Assistant Director of Education GO Get It-a statewide initiative in Georgia seeking to improve high school graduation and college enrollment rates. A native of Orangeburg, SC, Dawn received her Bachelors in African American Studies and a Speech Communications Minor from University of North Carolina-Chapel Hill and a Masters in Education from Emory University.

Dawn has also served as former Vice President and current member of the Black Child Development Institute (BCDI) and Co-Chair of their Entering the College Zone Program, member of the Atlanta Falcons Youth Sports and Education Conference Advisory Committee, SCALES Speakers Bureau, Georgia Educational Research Association, Board Member-Gems and Jewels Family Resource Center, Board Member-Ivy Preparatory Academy for Girls, Manga African Dance, Inc. and General and Black Alumni Associations for UNC-Chapel Hill and Emory University. In addition, she served as co-captain and former starter for the University of North Carolina-Chapel Hill Women's Basketball team from 1988-1992.

Dawn Bradley Cooper’s Specialties:

Working with students and parents of all ages and demographic backgrounds as well as the organizations, educational institutions and communities that serve them, to promote and provide resources for students to reach their goals and full potential through education.
