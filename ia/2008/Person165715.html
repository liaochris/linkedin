  • Chairman

    The President's Advisory Commission on Asian American and Pacific Islanders

    (Government Agency; 10,001 or more employees; Government Administration industry)

    May 2004 — June 2007 (3 years 2 months)

    On May 2004, President George W. Bush appointed 14 individuals to the President's Advisory Commission on Asian American and Pacific Islanders. The purpose of the Commission was to address the issues affecting the over 14 million AAPIs currently living throughout the United States and its territories. Rudy Pamintuan was designated Chairman late 2005.

    The Commission worked directly with the President and with the established IWG (Interagency Working Group), composed of representatives from the various Federal Departments and Agencies at the Undersecretary level, to offer recommendations affecting the AAPI community.

    Members of the Commission were actively engaged in outreach activities on behalf of the President and the Administration.

    In May 2007, the Commission submitted a report entitled, "Enhancing the Economic Potential of the AAPI Community" to the President.
