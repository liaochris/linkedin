  • Researcher

    Systems Control & Automation Dept. University Federico II of Naples

    (Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Research industry)

    January 2004 — February 2006 (2 years 2 months)

    My research is focused on the electronic control of the oxygen partial pressure in an underwater breathing apparatus with gas recycle. Closed-circuit underwater breathing apparatus, commonly known as rebreather, essentially permits completely autonomous diver operations at very deep depth without an umbilical. Rebreather needs a feed-back electronic controller that, based on the measure of the oxygen level in the counterlung, injects oxygen by operating a solenoid supply valve, so as to regulate oxygen partial pressure to a given set-point value during the entire dive. The purpose of my work is the design of on-board controls, diagnostic strategies and faul tollerance algorithms improving both performances and safety in the device. The final goal of the research project is the design and the development of an embedded microcontroller for the optimization and the supervision of the whole closed-loop underwater system.
