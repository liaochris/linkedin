Peter Trivelas’s Summary

Chances are good that you've seen my work. Where?, you ask.

Do you ever watch Martha Stewart's television show? No? How about NBC News? How about the Discovery Channel or PBS?

I am a "video" editor with broad television experience on Network, Local and Independent levels, with significant responsibility at various times in production, management and technical capacities. I have extensive hands-on background as an editor, producer, writer and project manager.

I'm also a member of the Writer's Guild of America.

I am now looking to edit feature films among other projects.

Freelance credits include:

• THE MEN WHO BROUGHT THE DAWN, 60-min doc., Hiroshima, editor. Program is now part of the Smithsonian Institution's permanent archive library.
• THE JUSTICE FILES, editor, first six seasons of top-rated Discovery Channel series.
• CHERNOBYL TODAY: LIVING UNDER THE CLOUD, 60-min doc., editor. Winner 1994 San Francisco International Film Festival Golden Gate Award. Special Recognition, NAAEE Film & Video Festival, Cancun, Mexico.
• NICK NEWS (Nickelodeon) with Linda Ellerbee, segment editor.
• RAP IT UP, 30-min. AIDS education drama, editor.
• MARILYN MONROE: IN HER OWN WORDS, 30-min. doc., asst editor.
• THE WALKER CUP, competition history, handout to golfers, editor.
• NOT IN A THOUSAND YEARS, 60-min. Zimbabwe Special, editor.
• AMERICAN PLAYHOUSE, Ass't Editor for "Great American 4th of July".
• THE ROYAL WEDDING, 3-hour BBC/PBS package, ass't director.
• EVENING AT POPS: Itzak Perlman, Andy Williams, Leontyne Price, editor.
• DECISION AT DIABLO, "Seabrook" seg for 60-min. PBS special, editor.
• DATELINE: COPENHAGEN, 1980 Women's U.N. Conference, 60-min., editor.
• INDEPENDENT ANIMATION SPECIAL, 60-min. PBS package, editor.
• OPEN TO THE PUBLIC, New Boston Fine Arts Museum wing, 30-min., editor.

Peter Trivelas’s Specialties:

Offline editing - taking hours of footage and crafting it into the perfect product. I have the personality to be an independent decision maker in the editing process and/or to produce a very specific result under close supervision.

Peter Trivelas’s Experience

  • Editor, Producer, Writer

    Freelance

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    June 1972 — Present (36 years 8 months)

    Freelance "video" editor. Producer. Member of Writer's Guild of America, East. Have worked in various capacities at all the Networks, many Cable production companies and independent production houses. As a member of a production team, I can take detailed direction or I can help make the critical decisions that make the difference in the finished product.

  • Segment Editor

    Martha Stewart Living Omnimedia

    (Public Company; 501-1000 employees; MSO; Publishing industry)

    April 1998 — May 2004 (6 years 2 months)

    The show editors make Martha Stewart look polished and coherent. Very often a finished 6 minute segment (like roasting a turkey or making a wine rack) will have taken 30 minutes or more to videotape. When the viewer sees the finished 6 minute piece, they feel that it all happened in 6 minutes, smoothly, easy to follow and fun to watch. The editor probably made a thousand decisions along the way to make that happen.

    The show was nominated for an Emmy for best editing every year during my tenure there, and the team won the Emmy for best show for many of those years.

  • Newswriter

    ABC News

    (Public Company; Broadcast Media industry)

    January 1990 — January 1995 (5 years 1 month)

    Wrote copy, directed videotape editors and directed national feeds for ABC's NewsOne Affiliate News Service. This was a Writer's Guild of America position.

  • Videotape Editor and Operations Manager

    NBC News

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Broadcast Media industry)

    1982 — 1988 (6 years)

    Edited news and feature segments on a deadline for NBC Network News in New York City. Segments were broadcast on The Today Show, NBC Nightly News and other News Shows and Specials.

    After my stint as an editor, I was drafted for the position of Operations Manager for the Network News Editing department. This was a 24-hour operation with a staff of 70 union editors and archival librarians, an inventory of 25 editing rooms and extensive videotape library, on a yearly budget of $10 million.

  • Videotape editor

    WGBH

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    1978 — 1982 (4 years)

    Staff member on The Ten O'clock News, a Boston area daily news show anchored by Chris Lydon. Assistant Director position entailed editing news segments with the reporters and filling in for the Director for the live daily broadcast.

    Directed hourly National PBS News headlines.

    Edited various shows originating at WGBH such as American Playhouse, Evening at Pops, and National PBS Specials.
