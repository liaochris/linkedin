Frances Yllana’s Summary

The job I have is not just a job. It's the combination of the two things I love to do – both for work and play: I fell in love with art after my sister taught me that a head was shaped like an oval (rather than the average 4-year-old's circle). I fell in love with writing at 3, when my sister taught me that the words "no" and "on" meant two different things (maybe also growing from my love of "storytelling" to get out of trouble).

Then, my high school sweetheart's mom was a graphic designer, saw what I liked to do separately, and showed me the magical job created after combining them. My course was officially set. I went into college, aimed at becoming a "communication artist."

In September 2001 – I started at Eisenberg And Associates and advanced from junior art director to creative director within 5 years. I worked on projects from competitive concept to completion/launch ranging from print to web to radio and gained a great amount of design, business, marketing and management experience. The majority of Eisenberg's work is annual reports, business to business and marcom, with about 8% business to consumer projects.

After a year of creative directing, I decided to leave Eisenberg, accepting the associate creative director position at Witherspoon in Fort Worth.This new position has allowed me to pursue many of the other goals I have - all of course, associated with this lovely, lucky world of design. As of Spring 08, I am also an adjunct professor at the University of Texas at Arlington's school of Art + Art History, helping to shape their growing and improving visual communications program.

On the superside, I have lofty ideas of buying an old building, opening a restaurant and small design store on the first floor, a studio upstairs with old school printing, furniture building and textile making tools. All the while, starting a non-profit organization like make-a-wish, but for adults.
