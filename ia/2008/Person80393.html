Abdul M. Ismail, abdul_m.ismail@interplanetary.ae’s Summary

Abdul M. Ismail worked as a propulsion engineer on two short-term NASA contracts with a NASA sub-contractor based in Casper, Wyoming, USA in 1995 and 1998 where he gained experience on developing cryogenic rocket engines, composite solid rocket motors and in-situ resource utilisation (ISRU) engines for use on Mars. He also provided advice to the UK Civil Aviation Authority about the launching of high power rockets into UK airspace as well as feedback to the Health & Safety Executive (Explosives Directorate) regarding composite rocket propellant. In 2001, he worked as an adviser on a project for a potential telecommunication satellite for Bangladesh. Under the banner of IPEX, he contributed to Aurora, the European Space Agency’s infrastructure development program pertaining to future Moon and Mars missions, as a Principal Investigator by completing a Phase 0 study contract on “Technology Requirements for Mars Sample Return using CO2/Metal Powder Propellants”. IPEX has authored several PDPs for a number of potential clients, some of which are presently being considered. In 2003, IPEX teamed up with Actimi Ltd. and successfully conducted a PDP on satellite engineering for a space insurance broker based in London. Nowadays, IPEX focuses its attention on assisting developing nations obtain space technology and has several mutual agreements with a number of satellite manufacturers and every attempt is made to secure the most appropriate service from suppliers of satellite services. Most importantly, IPEX assists developing nations, who express interest in procuring space technology, by authoring marketing documents in order to convince their governments to authorise funding for multi-million Euro projects.

The company also retains interest in the research, design and development of advanced space propulsion systems.
