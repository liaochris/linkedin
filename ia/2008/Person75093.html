Harry Chemko’s Summary

After co-founding Elastic Path in 2000, Harry has grown the company to become a leader in enterprise ecommerce software, competing with giants such as IBM and Microsoft. As an extension to Elastic Path's core software business, Harry negotiated the exclusive rights to ecommerce operations for the Vancouver 2010 Olympic and Paralympic Winter Games. This business unit includes a world-class online store, fulfillment, merchandising, and customer service. In another notable transaction, Harry negotiated a partnership with leading infrastructure software vender BEA Systems, in which the $7 Billion company re-sells Elastic Path as their own branded AquaLogic Commerce Services to their customer base of 18,000. BEA has recently been acquired by Oracle Corporation.

Harry has grown Elastic Path organically at an average annual growth rate of over 70% without any outside equity. The company has attracted over 180 customers, including global brands such as Google, Aeroplan, Nike, Time In., Avis, Samsonite, United Health Group, Telus, and Sony. Harry has led the company to receive the Canadian Youth Business Foundation’s National 2005 Best Business Award, as well as to earning a spot at #14 on Profit Magazine’s Hot 50. He also received the 2005 Young Entrepreneur Award for being the youngest CEO on the Profit Hot 50. More recently, Elastic Path Software was listed at #23 on the Profit 100, the definitive guide to Canada's fastest growing companies. Harry sits on the Board and the Audit and Finance Committee of the Canadian Youth Business Foundation, a $14 million non-profit organization dedicated to empowering aspiring young Canadian entrepreneurs to start their own businesses. In February 2007, Harry was featured in a full page article as an Emerging Entrepreneur by the National Post.

Harry attended Royal Roads University, receiving a B.Com in Entrepreneurial Management, and also the Eric C. Douglass Award for excellence in entrepreneurship.
