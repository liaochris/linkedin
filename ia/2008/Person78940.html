Ian Stevenson’s Summary

I am a Chartered Engineer with a broad background in technology mangement, software development and engineering. I have a particular interest in start-up companies built around new technologies. I am a member of the IET Management Network Executive Team, the organising committee of the IET "Business Essentials for Engineers" course, an Associate of the Institute of Directors, and a Business Advisor for Young Enterprise Scotland. I write regularly for the Visual Systems Journal, mostly on .NET technology, and I have also written for a number of IET publications.

Ian Stevenson’s Specialties:

Management, technology strategy, consulting, software architecture, software development, development methodology, WDM Device drivers, C, C++, C#, Microsoft .NET, electronic imaging, metadata management, bulk data processing, telecommunications.

    Scientific Generics

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    September 2000 — September 2004 (4 years 1 month)

    My work at Generics included a wide range of projects, from helping a major global mobile telecommunications company to develop their terminal platform strategy to researching the use of electronic imaging technology in endoscopy. I gained project management skills, particularly in drawing together groups of consultants from different disciplines (electronics, software, optics, mechanics, design etc), and experience of working commercially at a very high level (board/director) within client organisations. I made significant contributions to a number of due dilligence projects carried out on behalf of VCs, and in helping to select candidates for incubation schemes in large companies. I maintained my interest in software however, both as a hands-on developer and by launching an initiative to assist our clients in understanding - and where appropriate adopting - .NET technology.

  • Senior Engineer

    VLSI Vision Ltd

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; Computer Hardware industry)

    1996 — 2000 (4 years)

    I lead the Device Driver Development team at VVL, a company which produced CMOS Image sensors. I was the architect of the "G2" second generation Windows device driver (WDM) which became the basis of all PC device drivers for USB connected webcams and digital cameras, and I introduced many new tools and practices including source control, static and runtime code analysis and the policy of building debugging code into all products. I was closely involved in the design and specification ASIC blocks for microcontrollers and USB connectivity and the development of firmware for those ASICs. I also designed the instruction set and architecture of a custom microcontroller for camera chips, and developed the assembler and linker to support the instruction set. This controller has shipped as an integral part of millions of units in products from webcams to camera phones.

  • Senior Engineer

    1999 — 2000 (1 year)

    Formerly VLSI Vision Ltd - before becoming the "Imaging Division" of STMicroelectronics after a buy-out.
