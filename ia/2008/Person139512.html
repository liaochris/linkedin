Neil Greco’s Summary

Neil has over 20 years of experience in the technology industry holding a variety of roles with a concerted focus in sales and marketing of technology products and services.

Neil’s experiences offers considerable breadth – from early phase and start-up organizations to running a business unit within Compaq then Hewlett-Packard. His knowledge set spans multiple facets of starting and building successful business teams: from business development to comprehensive business and product launch to sale of physical and intellectual properties.

Neil has built successful brands and associated channels within the software industry at both a national and international levels. Most recently, Neil was instrumental in the carve out a software business unit from within Hewlett-Packard and successful sale of the business to what is now a division of Symantec.

Neil’s experience in hardware, software and services in a variety of sales and marketing roles underscore his industry expertise and functional specialization.

Neil is currently Vice President of The Onsott Group, one of Boston's premiere retained executive search firms. The Onstott Group has over 20 years of recruiting experience building some of the areas most respected executive management teams.

Neil also serves on the Board of Advisors for a start-up print and on-line publication dedicated to the arts in New England. Neil’s personal interests lie in the historic value of fine art where he is involved in a family run business in the antiques and fine art industry. He is an avid sailor and is married with one child.

Neil Greco’s Experience

  • Vice President

    The Onstott Group

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Staffing and Recruiting industry)

    January 2007 — Present (1 year 9 months)

    The Onstott Group has served the New England region for 20 years as one of areas most successful retained executive recruitment firms. As Vice President, Neil is responsible for new business development as well as candidate recruitment and placement. In this capacity, Neil lends nearly 25 years in the technology market place bringing a solid business acumen to the development of executive management teams in technology, bio-tech and bio science fields.

  • Business Development

    Altiris, Inc.

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; ATRS; Computer Software industry)

    April 2001 — June 2003 (2 years 3 months)

    Altiris represented the culmination of Altiris' acquisition of the Carbon Copy Remote Control technology from Hewlett-Packard where Neil was an instrumental player in the development and delivery of the Business Prospectus and ultimate "carve-out" of the Carbon Copy business unit. Post-acquisition, Neil led marketing efforts to announce the acquisition. He worked with senior executives in the positioning and messaging of the acquisition to Gartner and IDC analysts. He was also instrumental in the development and execution of Altiris' Channel Partner Program where he led the execution of Altiris' first Channel Partner Summit. Neil worked along side the Strategic Partner Sales team in the development of accounts such as: CompuCom, EDS, IBM North American Services and Unisys where he built account specific marketing strategy, go to market plans and execution of progams to drive sales revenue.

  • Senior Marketing Manager

    Hewlett-Packard

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; HPQ; Computer Hardware industry)

    July 1995 — April 2001 (5 years 10 months)

    While at Hewlett-Packard (previously Compaq), Neil worked along side the Business Unit Manager to reposition and revive Windows-based remote control software brand - Carbon Copy. During this transition, Neil worked to strategically re-position the software technology to remote support applications. Neil led development and execution of national advertising campaigns, re-design packaging and supporting collateral for worldwide markets, waged concerted public and analyst relations campaigns resulting in Editors Choice Awards and leadership positions with leading industry analysts such as International Data Corporation (IDC).
    As a relatively small revenue stream for now Hewlett-Packard, Carbon Copy is challenged in the new environment. Sale of the intellecutal property and related development team is suggested to senior management. After 15 months, the Carbon Copy management team successfully executed a "carve-out" of the business unit to Hewlett-Packard strategic partner Altiris, Inc.

  • Product Marketing Manager

    Interleaf

    (Public Company; Staffing and Recruiting industry)

    1990 — 1996 (6 years)

    Neil joined Interleaf as the product manager responsible for the "porting" of Interleaf 5 to the Macintosh platform. Interleaf's flagship product was Unix-based with a complex menu structure and user interface. The project required an in-depth review with Bruce Tognazzini, Apple Computers foremost UI authority in addition to a considerable development effort required to re-engineer the product. Although Neil was sucsessful in early stage market evangelism for the product, inclusive of a key participant in the worldwide launch of Apple System 7, Interleaf 5 for the Macintosh never came to market. At this stage, Neil's efforts were focused on sales and marketing of Interleaf 5 for DOS on a worldwide basis. Neil went on to join the WorldView Product team responsible for one of the industries earliest viewing technoloigies.
