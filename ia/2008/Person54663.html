David Roman’s Summary

I began my IT Career fresh out of college as a Systems Integration Specialist. All I was tasked to do was to export Event Viewer Logs in NT 4.0. I quickly gained my certification and began to troubleshoot the errors myself (secretly).

Someone took notice and I was transfered (within IBM) to perform some work with NT 4.0 Terminal Services on the IBM-Kodak account, which required some script fabrications. I did very well with the task and I was moved to work with an internal Thin Computing team that specialized in Wyse thin clients and Metaframe 1.8.

I then migrated to a backup team lead role on the IBM-Ace Insurance account while working with Metaframe XP implementations for ACE alongside Price Waterhouse Cooper employees.

My backup team lead responsibilities were numerous, but I handled a team of 17 without a problem when our team lead was on vacation or serving elsewhere within IBM.

I was promoted to the full-time team lead role on the IBM-Campbell's account where I managed a team of 6 people. I did that job with excellence once more and after a little over a year I moved on to bigger and better things.

I ended my carrer at IBM as an IT Architect on the Pearson commercial engagement specializing in Infrastructure hardware such as IBM's xSeries and pSeries hardware, IBM's DS4000 series of Fiber-Disk Controllers, Brocade Fabric Designs, Server Consolidation with VMWare ESX 2.5.2 Server and IBM Blades.

I am currently employed at The Stanley Works as a Service Delivery Analyst. My current project include implementation of the ITIL model for Stanley's Technical Services division and an outsourcing agreement for Level 1/2 Support and server monitoring.

Throughout my entire career I have been known to succeed even with the odds stacked against me. I will fill any grey area and serve with excellence. What I don't know, I will master. What I do know, I share by mentoring others that share the same passion for technology as I do.
