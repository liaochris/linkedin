Jens Staal’s Summary

I have studied the innate immune system both in plants and animals using two different approaches - genetics and cell biological methods. I have been writing a review discussing the possibility of synergisms between the two research fields, which is also a field of research I am eager to follow up on as I establish as an independent scientist.
Until then, however - I am acquiring experience from the human innate immunity to get a deeper and wider experience from research.

Jens Staal’s Specialties:

Bioinformatics, "thinking outside the box", genetics, QTL analysis, Co-IP

Jens Staal’s Experience

  • post doc

    VIB / Ugent

    (Educational Institution; Research industry)

    January 2007 — Present (2 years)

    I study the innate and adaptive immune responses of humans. Primarily, I focus on a protein called MALT1 which is involved in several pathways leading to NF-kB. One of the major models to study the function of this protein is the T-cell receptor (TCR) response, but also to find other contexts where this protein is important is part of the study. In contrast to my PhD project, this project focus on protein-based methods to study signal transduction.
