John LaCroix’s Summary

A wide range of entrepreneurial and agency experience over the past 16 years has helped me develop the ability to adapt to any environment, handle any situation and innovate in any medium. I think fast, make smart, educated decisions and solve complex problems while keeping the team creative and well organized. I'm continuously inspired and driven by accomplishing seemingly impossible missions.

John LaCroix’s Specialties:

Live action and 3D animation direction, motion design and visual effects, NLE editing, corporate identity and brand management, print and publication design, user-interface and software design, DVD compression and authoring, broadcast video technologies.

John LaCroix’s Experience

  • Creative Director

    Sportvision, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Broadcast Media industry)

    December 2004 — Present (3 years 4 months)

    Built a complete team of motion graphic designers, 3D modelers and asset creators. Manage all creative staff and direct all creative projects.

    Designed graphics for multiple Emmy Award-Winning broadcast products including the 1st and Ten line and K-Zone for network clients such as; ESPN, FOX, ABC, TBS, TNT, NBC, USA, CBS on programs including; the World Series, the Superbowl, PGA Champions Tour, Indy 500, Daytona 500 and the Olympics.

    Developed proof of concept pieces and mock-up prototypes for interactive applications, mobile phones, data intensive broadcast graphics, gaming, ITV / IPTV set-top boxes.

    Designed user-interface and features for real-time interactive applications synchronized to national TV broadcasts and producer consoles for custom broadcast graphics software.

    Invented the patent-pending live broadcast effect, FreezeCam, used first by NBC during the 2006 Winter Olympics and by ESPN during the '06 and '07 NBA Playoffs broadcasts.
