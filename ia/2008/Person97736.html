Joseph Yeh’s Summary

Joseph has counseled and provided political expertise for government agencies, political campaigns and nonprofit organizations in Washington D.C., Philadelphia, Sacramento, Los Angeles and the San Francisco Bay Area.

In 2004, his efforts were an integral part of the successful No Child Left Behind educational outreach to the Asian American community nationwide, which included then-U.S. Secretary of Education, Rod Paige and reported back to the White House.

As an unwavering leader and literary contributor in over 15 nonprofit organizations, he is compelled to help address veteran issues, professional mentorship and ethnic awareness. As an auxiliary member for the oldest Chinese American veterans post in the nation, American Legion Cathay Post #384, he has executed advance work for then-U.S. Representative to the U.N. Economic and Social Council, Ambassador Sichan Siv, as well as then-U.S. Secretary of Veteran Affairs, Anthony Principi. He is a member of the Commonwealth Club of CA and Churchill Club.

He has been involved in fundraising, event planning, newsletter writing, and community relations for the National Association of Asian American Professionals of San Francisco, Asian Business League of San Francisco, Asian Pacific Islander American Public Affairs Association and Asian Americans for Good Government PAC. He helped start the Judge Lillian K. Sing Scholarship and Bok Pon JROTC/ROTC Award Trust Fund. His first attempt at creating a nonprofit web company, Taiphoon.org, never materialized, and it developed into TaiwanseAmerican.org.

Critical to his success has been a constant awareness of confirming support from over 140 features in both national and local television, radio and print outlets including ABC’s WBRZ, CBS’s 5 & 11 Evening News, San Jose Mercury News, The Seattle Times, World Journal, and Sing Tao Daily.

He is a small shareholder in Pluto, a luxury home automation company, targeting the Middle East/Asia markets.
