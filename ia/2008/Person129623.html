Michael Duff’s Summary

Massachusetts-born Michael Duff is the former lead singer and songwriter of Columbia Records' critically-acclaimed alternative rock act, Chalk FarM. Selling over 70,000 copies of their debut CD, his success with the band includes a Top 10 and a Top 40 single on Billboard's Rock and Modern Rock Radio charts ("Lie on Lie" & "Live Tomorrow"), MTV airplay and years of North American tours performing for tens of thousands with Matchbox Twenty, The Goo Goo Dolls, Better than Ezra and others, plus appearing on the cover of Music Connection and other magazines. You may have also seen him and the rest of the band in Jerry Bruckheimer's smash hit "Coyote Ugly."

Since disbanding Chalk FarM, Duff has had songs recorded by Meatloaf, Australian sensation Kate Ceberano, has been heard singing on CBS's "The Young and The Restless" and in the Disney/Warner Bros. film "A Cinderella Story."

His debut solo CD entitled "Unbreakable" has many all-star guest songwriters including Marti Frederiksen (Aerosmith, Sheryl Crow), Reed Vertelney (Clay Aiken, Destiny's Child), Lindy Robbins (Nick Lachey, Lisa Loeb), Grammy-nominated Shelly Peiken (Christina Aguilera, Meredith Brooks) and Academy-Award and Grammy nominated Jud Friedman (Whitney Houston, Ray Charles).

The LA-based Duff is currently planning a touring schedule to take advantage of the still-loyal Chalk FarM fan base.
