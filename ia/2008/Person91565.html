  • University of California, Berkeley

    No, Music and Math, 1961 — 1961

    My first University experience. My first time away from home. The First time I had to take care of myselft. We searched hard for an apartment in Berkeley during the Summer but could not find one, so, I rushed fraternities. I was not fraternity material, until I met Henry Sloss, one day when the ZBT house was practically empty. He was kind and funny and we had an immediate friendship. I believe that Henry got me a bid to ZBT. I thought it would be good, but I was wrong.
    I was facing some very frustrating studies. During the Summer, I had begun to study music. Almost everyone who studies music in the University had years of study. I was behind but determined to be a music major. I loved to sing, which I had done by ear since I was a child and which I debuted publicly my final year in High School.
    I was a math wiz but I had had only one teacher in High School that taught English well.

    NO, Music and Math, 1961 — 1961

    . I had taken English 1A at the Junior College during the summer to avoid taking "dumbell English" at Berkeley. English 1B was full of Shakespeare and I could not understand anything.
    On top of the difficulties that I had with studies, there was a jock in the ZBT house named Don Bell who harassed me throughout my stay in the fraternity. I met Bell at a party that ZBT gave to meet the potential brothers. Bell asked me, "Where are you from?" I told him "North Hollywood" for the High School to which I went. His response was, "Oh, I don't like guys from North Hollywood." Bell was a real charmer. He'd make me clean his room and sleep on his floor and was just an all-around horror to me.
    There was another guy who would make us pledges laugh a lot, Dick something. He would sport a beret, line us up with open mouths, insert a large black bushy paintbrush in our mouths and yell "Woobee, Woobee, Woobee, Whobee, Woo."

    NO, Music and Math, 1961 — 1961

    . If we laughed, we had to clean up the entire house. Of course, we all broke up laughing and then had to clean up the whole house at 1 or 2 AM.
    I had a pledge brother, Roger something but he could not help me adjust to the "festivities" and loss of sleep.
    Before Berkeley, I detested coffee. But, I was so sleepy, I had to do something to keep awake in class and elsewhere. I began drinking copious amounts of coffee, while I studied, often at LaVal's on the North Side near the ZBT house. in the evenings, the pledges studied together without coffee until about 10 PM, then I would drag myself to LaVal's and try to catch up. I was falling more and more behind.
    After Thanksgiving, I quit ZBT. I had no choice and an opening in an apartment of a friend made it possible for me to move out. It was a great move but it did not work out for my college career. I was already too far behind when our new little family unit got together.

  • University of California, Berkeley

    NO, Music and Math, 1961 — 1961

    We were 3 boys in an apartment in Berkeley. Next door to us, there were 3 girls, I believe. My recollection is that we had dinner together almost every night. I don't recall being romantically attached to any of the girls but they were all kind, pleasant and good to be around.

    Every weekend there were parties and drinking (liquor). I really detested liquor, even more than coffee. I had tasted hard liquor and felt sick everry time. But, one weekend, I got drunk and walked in an apartment of one of girls in the building, not the next door "family" girls but another. I went to her bed, crawled in and went to sleep. Some time later, when she found me there, she called the police, who were very understanding. I don't think that they booked me but did report me to the school.

    It didn't matter, I decided to quit. I wanted to build my body and sing, nothing else mattered.
