Mike Collins’s Summary

My goal since 1987 has been to inform and educate people about music and media technologies while working on projects using these technologies.

I am currently writing and producing music in my project studio and working on a new book about Pro Tools.

My greatest expertise is with Music Technology and Music Production, and I am also knowledgable about Video and Multimedia/Internet Technologies.

I am always looking for interesting new opportunites in any of these fields and am willing to travel or relocate as necessary.

Mike Collins’s Specialties:

Music Technology Consultant and Author: Acknowledged Pro Tools expert with several books published by Focal Press covering Pro Tools and related music and audio software.

Recording Engineer, Music Producer and Studio Musician: Many professional credits on commercial music recordings, film soundtracks, TV and Radio broadcasts.

    Ralph's Records

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Music industry)

  • Club DJ

    Magic Village/Blue Note

    (Privately Held; Music industry)

    October 1969 — October 1970 (1 year 1 month)

    I played as a DJ at two legendary clubs in Manchester. The Magic Village, run by Roger Eagle (who subsequently established Eric's in Liverpool) ran all night sessions on Saturdays where I played Progressive Rock (Jefferson Airplane, Cream), Blues, R & B and Deep Soul music (Mitty Collier, Lorraine Ellison) for the audience until 7AM. From 9 AM until 12 Noon on Sunday mornings, and from 7PM till 11PM on Sunday evenings, I played at the Blue Note club on Gore Street, close to the legendary Twisted Wheel club. Here, the soul music fans loved the mix I played of Stax, Motown (and what later became known as 'Northern Soul') that this audience demanded with obscure 45 rpm vinyl discs from Bobby Bland, James Brown, Little Milton and similar artistes changing hands among fans for large sums of money as they were so difficult to buy from shops.

  • Guitar Player

    Self-employed

    (Self-Employed; Myself Only; Music industry)

    July 1965 — September 1969 (4 years 3 months)

    While still at school, I started playing guitar professionally in 'covers' bands, mostly at weekends. Material was mostly covers of Stax and Motown songs, with some pop, folk, blues and rock.

    The band supported many top artists of that era, such as Stevie Wonder and his Motown Orchestra, Eddie Floyd, Ben E. King, The Drifters and James & Bobby Purify. This culminated in a 3-month tour, playing mid-sized venues throughout England and Scotland in the summer of 1969.
