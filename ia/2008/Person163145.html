Rob Toker’s Summary

Representing the British Government - Rob leads trade and investment teams in Texas, New Mexico, Oklahoma, Arkansas, and Louisiana as well as the Houston based International Energy Team. Formerly Rob led the Miami based Trade and Investment team including the Latin American and Caribbean Market Intelligence Unit. These teams focus on finance and deep, convergent technologies in growth industries including but not limited to clean technology, renewable energy technologies, materials technology, carbon finance, advanced bio-fuels, optimisation, efficiency, automation and control systems (ACS), security technology and aerospace.

Rob's trade and investment teams confidentially consult with a portfolio of UK client companies ranging from MNE to SME and technology start-ups. Further Rob and his teams engage in private consultation with a portfolio of US client companies, entrepreneurs and investors seeking to establish European operations via UK subsidiaries.

Rob has diverse experience and broad capabilities in a number of technology sectors including ICT, Life Sciences, Bio-nano-technologies, Systems Dynamics and Financial Services. Rob was a technology entrepreneur; he was the Founding CEO of Evolved Nanomaterial Sciences, Inc (ENS) in Medford, MA. Rob led multidisciplinary engineering, operations and business development teams at several Boston area technology start-ups. He helped co-ordinate the integration of two large healthcare IT organisations at the Harvard teaching hospitals Massachusetts General Hospital and the Brigham and Women’s Hospital under the Partners Healthcare network.

In his early career Rob worked at the United Nations in New York. He spent nearly four years conducting schizophrenia research at Tufts New England Medical Centre and the Lemuel Shattuck Hospital in Boston. Rob was an assistant economist at the Confederation of British Industry (CBI) and an intern at Timberhill Trading and GLP Investment Research. .
