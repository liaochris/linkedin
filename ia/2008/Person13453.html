Anna Clark’s Summary

I am a journalist and a fiction writer; an editor and an activist; a progressive person interested in creative approaches to art and to justice work.

Anna Clark’s Experience

  • Communications Coordinator

    Michigan Suburbs Alliance

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Environmental Services industry)

    September 2007 — Present (8 months)

    I do the media work, edit three publications, and develop the online presence of a nonprofit that is:

    Building collaborations among historically divided communities in SE Michigan, and with the city of Detroit.

    Growing environmentally sustainable redevelopment projects, and challenging urban sprawl.

    Building social equity and regional governance in the most segregated region in the U.S.

  • Freelance Journalist

    Self-employed

    (Self-Employed; Myself Only; Writing and Editing industry)

    1999 — Present (9 years)

    My writing has appeared or is forthcoming in Utne Reader, The Nation, Bitch Magazine, The Women's International Perspective, Women's eNews, ColorLines, Clamor Magazine, Kitchen Sink, The Ann Arbor News, The Herald-Palladium, and The Current. I also have guest blogged at WIMN's Voices and Critical Mass, the blog for the National Book Critics Circle.

    My journalism has been cited by The New York Times, The New York Times Book Review, Salon, ESPN, BBC Radio, Air America, Pakistani News Service, Fox News, Lenta, The Drudge Report, Editor & Publisher, and many others.

  • Community Member/Volunteer Coordinator

    Haley House

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    February 2005 — April 2007 (2 years 3 months)

    I was a live-in community member at Haley House, a place that constantly challenged my ideas of justice, poverty, community, spirituality, and generally what it means to live a good life.

  • Assistant

    Center for New Words

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Writing and Editing industry)

    2007 — 2007 (less than a year)

    I do organization, fundraising, and editing work with the Center for New Words, a small nonprofit that "builds spaces and places where women's words matter."

  • Arts Editor

    What's Up Magazine

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Media Production industry)

    July 2005 — September 2006 (1 year 3 months)

    --Recruit, assign, and edit arts features and columns
    --Plan layout, art, issue themes, and features
    --Mentor and supervise writers and volunteers

  • Workshop Facilitator; Speakers Bureau Coordinator; Executive Committee Member

    Prison Creative Arts Project

    (Non-Profit; 51-200 employees; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    September 1999 — May 2004 (4 years 9 months)

    I spent five years facilitating creative writing and monologue theater weekly workshops in prisons and detention centers via The Prison Creative Arts Project. Through the same, I partnered in developing and served as leader in The Portfolio Project, matching mentors to incarcerated artists and writers to create a professional portfolio of their best work. I spearheaded PCAP's Speakers Bureau for three years, mentored and trained new members, and was elected to its Executive Committee.
