Robert Horne’s Summary

My practice is concentrated on wills, trusts, estate & trust administration and controversy, probate, and representing entreprenuers and their businesses in business transactions.

Although each client's needs are different, a majority of my clients (as part of his or her estate plan) are interested in how (s)he can transfer assets to future generations in a tax efficient manner. By using Wills and other transfer vehicles (such as Trusts and planned giving), I advise my clients how to transfer assets to their beneficiaries in a manner that limits estate, gift, inheritance and other taxes to the extent possible.

Equally as important to providing for future generations is ensuring the long term viability of the business and protecting the business owner's personal assets. To this end, I assist individuals who currently own or are interested in starting their own business with forming, operating, buying and/or selling a business.

My representation of a business client can be comprehensive or limited to a particular engagement, such as: choosing a business structure (corporation, LLC or partnership); drafting / negotiating employment agreements; leases; sales / purchase / service agreements; and constructing a business succession plan.

In the event a client becomes involved in a lawsuit, I work closely with my firm's litigation department to ensure that my client's goals are accomplished and his/her/its rights are protected.

If you or someone you know has a legal problem or would like advice on planning to avoid one, please feel free to contact me.
