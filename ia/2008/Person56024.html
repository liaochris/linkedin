Enrique Carrillo’s Summary

Mr. Carrillo has been actively involved in the branding and strategy involved in establishing the Hispanic Banking Program across the bank's network of branches and customer contact center. Most recently Mr. Carrillo was interviewed by Channel 7 and Univision (Spanish) on topics related Hispanic banking. He is the creator and host of Pachanga Cubana, a customer appreciation/networking event held at his home. Print media including the Washington Post, Baltimore Sun, Washington Business Journal, Daily Record, DC Examiner, Business Monthly Newspaper among others covered this event.

At Comerica Bank, he was responsible for establishing the Hispanic Initiative in Michigan, a special bank community outreach effort directed at the state’s growing Hispanic population.

From 1994 to 2001, Mr. Carrillo served as regional director of the Liaison Outreach and Service Program (LOSP), a program of the U.S. Department of Transportation (USDOT), the Office of Small and Disadvantaged Business Utilization (OSDBU). This program provided information regarding transportation-related contracting, financing and business opportunities to small and minority businesses. Mr. Carrillo was responsible for all facets of the program’s operation in the District of Columbia and 13 states in the Mid-Atlantic and Southeast U.S. His duties included serving as liaison to USDOT, the State Departments of Transportation, and other small business service providers.

Mr. Carrillo taught as an adjunct lecturer at Florida International University (FIU), Miami-Dade Community College and Miami Senior High Adult Education Center from January 1994 to April 1997.

In 1993, Mr. Carrillo was conferred with a Master of Public Administration from FIU’s School of Public Affairs and Services. In 1988 he graduated with a Bachelor's in Arts degree from the University of Miami with General Honors in English and History.
