Matt Barnes’s Summary

I've recently joined the design team at Philips Design in Atlanta as the Sr. Structural Packaging Designer.

I am a versatile designer with 6+ years experience in designing solutions in many diverse areas of the packaging industry. I enjoy blending my skills as a creative/innovative designer with my ability to understand the mechanical workings of my creations via materials and manufacturing processes. It's a hybrid role of being a designer and an engineer that allows me to bring solutions to the table effectively no matter what industry I am working with. As a structural packaging designer, my role is to bring together my expertise of design, materials and manufacturing to compliment the graphics developed by the rest of our team in order to produce packaging that garners the attention of consumers.

Past experiences in packaging design have included returnable packaging, brown box corrugated, commercial-grade packaging, temp & semi-temp POP/Displays. My goal is to build on my experiences of the past in order to grow further as a designer with my versatility giving me the opportunity to work in whichever industry requires my expertise.

Matt Barnes’s Specialties:

ideation, 3D Design, packaging materials, manufacturing machinery & processes, project management, sketching, merging graphics with structure, packaging sustainability, market research, creative solutions, structural integrity, packaging testing procedures, communication with international manufacturing, supplier/vendor relations

  • Structural Packaging Designer

    Griffin Technology

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Consumer Electronics industry)

    June 2007 — October 2008 (1 year 5 months)

    The title basically explained my core job duty, which was designing the structural workings of Griffin's packaging to compliment the graphics and style. This included product box dielines and interior product protection such as molded foams or plastic thermoformed blisters. I worked almost exclusively with SolidWorks in developing every structural aspect of the packaging. Having the ability to pull fresh 3D files directly from our mechanical engineering or industrial design team allowed most of my designs to be done entirely in 3D versus needing actual components. I really enjoyed my time spent at Griffin and the rewards of seeing my work in retailers such as Best Buy, Target and the Apple Store.
    **Gained Experience: Chinese manufacturing relations, 3D design, manufacturing knowledge, knowledge of various packaging materials, importance of blending graphics with structure for customer sales, sustainability of packaging

    November 2004 — May 2007 (2 years 7 months)

    As the Design Manager for the Birmingham Division of TimBar, I oversaw the packaging design/development for our Alabama & regional sales staff. I played an important role in delivering successful packaging solutions to a wide variety of customers including produce, furniture, automotive and other various industrial parts. Most of the design work consisted of structural design with ArtiosCAD, creating both corrugated/brown box packaging, as well as semi-temporary to temporary product displays. I also utilized my graphic design skills to develop graphic arts for customers from time to time. This position allowed me to broaden my range of experience in the industry while managing a department.
    **Gained Experience: Design management, graphic design/layouts, structural design for temp/semi-temp displays, corr pallet design, manufacturing machinery knowledge, sales

    (Privately Held; 501-1000 employees; Packaging and Containers industry)

    October 2003 — November 2004 (1 year 2 months)

Automotive customers included Honda and Mercedes via Delphi, Johnson Controls, Mid-South Electronics, ISE & Brose. By taking on these roles, my leadership skills were tested and utilized more every day.
    **Gained Experience: project development/management, heavy-duty corr design, organizational skills, teamwork, supplier relations, sales/design calls to customers
