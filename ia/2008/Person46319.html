Dave Craigmile’s Experience

  • Wireless Specialist - Network Planning

    cincinnati bell

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Telecommunications industry)

    July 2006 — January 2007 (7 months)

    This was a fun and interesting job.
    This job was a technical project planning position which investigated and implemented new technology and features into the GSM switching infrastructure.
    Projects included implementing high speed links for communication between the HLR and MSC to the STPs. This was using the Nortel Universal Signaling Point.
    Investigation of different class of services between prepaid and post paid subscribers. Using GPRS data connectivity for personalized bus shuttling service for one of the local colleges.

  • Systems Test Engineer

    Nortel Networks

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; NT; Telecommunications industry)

    June 2004 — June 2006 (2 years 1 month)

    This was interesting to say the least. In this position we were responsible for validating the interoperability of third party infrastructure products with the Nortel MSC. Much of this testing was based across an SS7 link between the two systems whether in the same lab or across the country.
    We did quite a bit of functionality testing using the Nortel MSC, other vendors HLR and somebody else's SCP. If problems occurred, we would get down to the bits and bytes of the protocols used to determine where the problem occurred.
    I also did testing and validation across wireless IP based applications such as video telephony and push to talk (PTT) which used SIP and RTP protocols for signaling and media transfer.
    Every time we had a new vendor in the lab we were expected to come up to speed on the new technologies they were using.
    I helped to mentor new people when they joined the organization.

  • Handset Test Engineer

    Siemens

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; Telecommunications industry)

    February 2003 — June 2004 (1 year 5 months)

    This contract position was initially intended for testing newly developed GSM/GPRS handsets in multiple service providers’ networks. We would often go to the various service providers networks with the new handsets and do full functionality checks of the handset verifying the compatibility in the different network elements available.
    If we came across issues, we would capture detailed information via multiple tools to present to the design engineers.
    Siemens then entered into an agreement with Ericsson for testing a new Push To Talk (PTT) phone application at the Ericsson facility in Richardson. I was fully involved with that effort from October 2003 until June 2004.
    This testing was using the Ericsson GPRS wireless network with the Siemens handsets. We were validating phone functionality, stability and SIP/RTP protocols.
    These were some of the earliest PTT trials using strictly IP based functionality.

  • Systems / Sales Engineer

    Logica Mobile Networks

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; Telecommunications industry)

    August 2000 — June 2002 (1 year 11 months)

    This position used my knowledge of SMS, OTA and other messaging systems and protocols.
    I traveled with our sales executives as their technical resource for Logica’s products.
    My duties at Logica were to help architect messaging systems for various telecommunications providers. Along with these duties, I also demonstrated and presented Logica's messaging applications to current and possibly future customers. These presentations were given to anyone from the engineering staff, marketing staff to the executives of many service providers.
    As new products were introduced, I would help train our sales executives to help them come up to speed.
    I've generated multiple presentations, RFPs, RFQ and any other documentation necessary to satisfy the customer requirement for information.
    I was the subject matter expert at Logica for their CDMA Over The Air (OTA) product and visited with many interested parties teaching them what the system was and how it worked.

  • Systems Test Engineer

    Nortel Networks

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; NT; Telecommunications industry)

    November 1994 — August 2000 (5 years 10 months)

    I started as a test engineer for their TDMA wireless product. My duties were to work with the software development groups and define requirements necessary to fully test the new wireless features. I would participate in code reviews to help determine if the approach taken was the correct one. I would generate test plans and then test cases for each new feature assigned to me and lead my test team to the completion of the effort.
    I also worked on many special projects that required detailed knowledge of how the MSC operated. These special projects were quite interesting at times and usually had prototype hardware and/or software which needed complete analysis and detailed testing.
    I was then invited to enter into a new program called "Open Solutions" which tested customer hardware functionality with the Nortel infrastructure. This new group was fee based and was funded by the vendors which came into the lab. I've worked with most of the messaging vendors while working in this group.
