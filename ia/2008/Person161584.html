Reuben Musgrave’s Summary

I am a communications generalist with more than 25 years of experience in publishing, public affairs, and teaching, having worked as a successful project manager and team mentor for employers ranging from nonprofits and private corporations to the White House. I am currently interested in exploring freelance or consulting projects that require creative problem-solving with a strategic, "big-picture" approach.

Reuben Musgrave’s Experience

  • Manager, Executive Correspondence

    Washington Metropolitan Area Transit Authority

    (Government Agency; 5001-10,000 employees; Public Relations and Communications industry)

    May 2001 — Present (6 years 10 months)

    As Manager, Executive Correspondence Team, oversee a staff of writers and editors who draft correspondence for executives’ and Board Members’ signatures. Work closely with senior staff to coordinate and align messaging while maintaining highest levels of customer service and writing quality. Previously served as speechwriter for Metro’s CEO, Board of Directors, and senior staff. Wrote speeches, op-ed articles and guest columns, and managed marketing and communication projects ranging from internal and corporate communications to public campaigns. Worked closely with Media Relations and Intergovernmental Relations offices to plan and develop white papers, Congressional testimony, and Board presentations. Represented Marketing Office in strategic communications planning throughout WMATA.

  • Director of Communications

    American Architectural Foundation

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Public Relations and Communications industry)

    January 2000 — May 2001 (1 year 5 months)

    As the first Communications Director of this small nonprofit arm of the American Institute of Architects, oversaw planning and execution of all publications, media relations, and public outreach activities. Worked directly with AAF Board of Regents and Executive Committee on special initiatives, including development of a comprehensive strategic plan. Oversaw complete redesign and launch of AAF Web site.

  • Speechwriter (Pub. Affairs Specialist)

    White House Office of Science & Technology Policy

    (Public Relations and Communications industry)

    February 1999 — January 2000 (1 year)

    Wrote speeches for delivery by Dr. Neal Lane, Assistant to the President and Director of the Office of Science and Technology Policy (OSTP). Helped plan and implement long-range communications strategy for Dr. Lane. Wrote and edited Wellspring of Prosperity, OSTP's biannual report to Congress on science and technology efforts (available online at: http://clinton4.nara.gov/media/pdf/wellspring.pdf.) Researched, wrote, and edited a broad range of other science policy documents, including Congressional testimony, op-eds, press releases, and brochures.

    The White House

    (Public Relations and Communications industry)

    Directed drafting and editing of letters, congratulatory messages, and proclamations for President Clinton's signature. Supervised staff of writers and editors who worked closely with senior Administration officials to help the President respond effectively to high-level policy correspondence. Drafted remarks for the President for delivery at Correspondence Department-related events. Authorized use of the President's official signature on outgoing correspondence.

  • Managing Editor of Educational Media

    National Geographic Society

    (Public Relations and Communications industry)

    September 1988 — March 1996 (7 years 7 months)

Served as Editorial Director for National Geographic Kids Network, an international online curriculum in environmental science. Wrote and edited curriculum supplements, teacher's guides, user's manuals, and marketing and promotional materials for Division products. Worked directly with focus groups of classroom teachers to plan and evaluate new products before and after publication. Participated in Divisional planning, budgeting, personnel, and marketing; hired and supervised staff and freelance writers, editors, and designers.
