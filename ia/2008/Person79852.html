Imran Habib Ahmad’s Summary

Mr. Ahmad has over fourteen years of diverse professional experience in the area of environment and sustainable development. Mr. Ahmad holds an undergraduate degree in electrical engineering from Pakistan and a Masters degree in Sustainable International Development from Brandeis University, Heller School of Social Policy (USA). He is an alumnus of the UC Berkeley Environmental Leadership Program, Kennedy School (Harvard) Environmental Economics and Policy Analysis Program Bucerius Summer School on Global Governance (Germany) and a participant of the European Union Visitors Program.

He has been actively engaged in the areas of environmental issues, energy development, policy planning, development/aid coordination and effectiveness, project management and project cycle management in the government, non-profit, development agency and private sector in Pakistan, Denmark and USA since 1992 and is an international resource person in the area of climate change and multilateral environmental agreements.

Mr. Ahmad has remained an elected member of the UNFCCC expert group on technology transfer (EGTT) and Pakistan’s lead negotiator at UNFCCC COP7, SB 18 and COP9 meetings in Marrakech, Bonn and Milan respectively. He has represented Pakistan at major international meetings and has been invited to speak and participate in various international conferences and seminars including Chatham House, Wilton Park, East-West Center, IIED, IISD, IDS and the Pew Center.

Mr. Ahmad has traveled extensively round the world and has lived in Europe, Africa, Asia and North America. He is currently a recepient of AusAid Scholarship to pursue doctoral studies at the Australian National University in the area of climate change and sustainable development.

International/Multilateral Negotiation, Intergovernmental Negotiation and process, policy/aid coordination and effectiveness, monitoring and evaluation, climate change policy
