Rowan Stanfield’s Summary

I have worked in the book industry since graduating in 1999 - initially as a bookseller at two branches of Waterstone's, then subsequently moving into publishing. Whilst at Waterstone's I was a buyer for various sections, including: Children's, Biography, Audio Books and Fiction. I also held responsibility for in-store marketing, including window displays and events.

Since moving into the publishing side of the industry, I have been employed by four very different publishing houses. Starting out at Frances Lincoln, a small independent publisher of high-quality illustrated adult and children's books, I was quickly promoted from Publicity & Marketing Assitant to Publicity & Marketing Manager, with overall responsibility for the promotion of the entire list.

At Orion, one of the major mainstream UK publishers, I worked solely on the children's list, and launched several new authors with successful high-profile campaigns, including Michelle Paver's Chronicles of Ancient Darkness and Caroline Lawrence's The Roman Mysteries series. During this period I also gained significant experience in event management, organising author tours, festival and school events.

I have also had experience of the academic sector, at John Wiley - one of the world's oldest publishers - at which I worked on the Business, Finance and Architecture lists.

Currently working for the Guild of Master Craftsmen's publishing and books distribution arm in Lewes, I am responsible for the promotion of its eclectic lists, which includes arts, crafts, hobbies and lifestyle.

Outside of office hours I write freelance for several publications, including children's book reviews for Achuka.co.uk and Carousel magazine. I also keep a regular blog - www.rowstar.blogspot.com - which I see as a practice run for when I take over from Jon Ronson in the Guardian Weekend magazine...
