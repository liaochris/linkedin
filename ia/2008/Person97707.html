Joseph Phillips’s Summary

I earned my B.S. in History and Policy (with University Honors) from Carnegie Mellon University in Pittsburgh, PA and have recently earned my M.A. at the Center for Middle Eastern Studies at the University of Chicago. My thesis, "The Value of Dual Identity: Lebanese Christians Under the French Mandate," deals with Lebanese Christians and their role in domestic political processes considering their unique position as simultaneously Arab and Christian.
I now plan on entering the work force for a public policy research company, non-profit/NGO, or government agency where I am able to use the skills which I have acquired over the course of my education and through my internships.

Joseph Phillips’s Specialties:

I am well versed in all aspects of the Modern Middle East. I have the most thorough knowledge regarding the Levant from the fall of the Ottoman Empire to the present. I have also engaged with the Arab-Israeli conflict in many capacities. I have well honed research skills and have developed conflict resolution skills from my experience with Soliya. I am also intermediately fluent in Arabic and have beginner capabilities in French and Hebrew.
