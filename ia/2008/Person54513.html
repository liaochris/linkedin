  • Primary Therapist

    Appalachian Regional Healthcare Psychiatric Center, Hazard, KY

    (Privately Held; 1001-5000 employees; Mental Health Care industry)

    September 2003 — October 2005 (2 years 2 months)

    Psychological services provider for 24-bed dual-diagnosis locked unit in frontier Appalachia. Our catchment area stretched from NE TN through E NC, SW VA, E KY all the way down to the Bluegrass region. This vast, rugged turf, combined with an epidemic of Rx drug, ETOH, & "streetdrug" (especially Methamphetamine) abuse that infested the region to synergistically keep many people very sick and our unit very busy. Starting with detox and moving as efficiently as possible into motivational-interviewing-based therapy, the challenge was to assist these hardcore addicts in keeping their commitment to invest in their stepdown Tx in group-homes, outpatient clinics, and the like. Addiction has a very high relapse rate and devastates the entire family. Therefore, I had to constantly adjust the interventions I provided the pts who darkly referred to themselves as "frequent fliers" while I engaged in management of family strife associated with the identified patient's predicament.

Dr. Hillel N. Goldstein’s Education

  • Adler School of Professional Psychology

    Psy.D., Clinical Psychology, 1996 — 2006

    Successfully juggled APA-accredited clinical psychology training supplemented with ample clinical practica alongside my FT job on the North Side of Chicago. Overcame hurdles too many and too personal to list here, but as Nietzsche told us before it became a Great American Cliche, rather than killing me, this made me stronger than ever and thus a more resilient and resourceful practitioner than I ever imagined possible.

    Activities and Societies:

  • University of Wisconsin-Madison

    M.S., Experimental Psychopathology, 1992 — 1995

    After deciding that the practice of psychology was of much greater interest to me than the pursuit of grants and publications, I made the best of the situation and obtained excellent Master's-level training in experimental psychopathology with an emphasis on the study of the cognitive-behavioural-affective mechanisms underlying the various subtypes of impulsivity and psychopathy. From there I proceeded to a program best-suited to training me to be the best practitioner I could be: Adler School of Professional Psychology (Chicago), an APA-accredited Psy.D. program with the distinction of also being the oldest professional school of clinical psychology in the USA. It was established by Rudolph Dreikurs, M.D., Alfred Adler, M.D.'s top student, who successfully imported this optimistic variety of psychodynamic (yet pragmatically cognitive-behavioural) clinical psychology to the USA.

    Activities and Societies:
    Full Academic Fellowship in Research and Teaching.

  • Princeton University

    M.A., Social Psychology, 1990 — 1992

    Here at Princeton I had a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity to work with world leaders in the social-cognitive experimental study of stereotyping and prejudice, a topic that I began studying in depth as an Honors-Program undergraduate at Maryland. This superb, Master's level education in social psychology will always play a role in keeping me, a clinical psychology practitioner, forever attentive to the impact of social units big and small on the behaviour of the individual.

    Activities and Societies:
    Full Academic Fellowship in Research and Teaching. Academic Excellence Grant.

  • University of Maryland College Park

    B.S., Psychology Honors, 1986 — 1990

    This enormous university was the ideal launching pad for my academic and clinical career. Starting off a zoology major, I quickly reached the conclusion that my fascination in animal behaviour would have to take a back seat to my rapidly growing fascination with the so much more unpredictable and generally complex behaviour humans display in health and in illness.

    Activities and Societies:
    Phi Beta Kappa, Phi Kappa Phi, Golden Key, Phi Eta Sigma, Behavioural and Social Sciences Dean's Scholar, Summa Cum Laude Graduate.
