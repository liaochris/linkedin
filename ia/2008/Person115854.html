Louis R. Bucalo’s Summary

Dr. Louis R. Bucalo is a physician, medical scientist, and entrepreneur involved in numerous programs for innovation in medicine, education and sports.

Dr. Bucalo received his B.A. in biochemistry from Harvard College magna cum laude in 1980, and his M.D. from Stanford University with Honors in Clinical Research in 1985. After working at Genentech in clinical research, Dr. Bucalo founded Titan Pharmaceuticals, and was the company’s President and CEO for 15 years until his retirement as CEO in 2007. Under Dr. Bucalo's leadership, Titan established several novel pharmaceutical development programs and was chosen as one of Entreprenuer Magazine's top Hot 100 young companies in the U.S. for 2001.

Dr. Louis Bucalo established the company’s ProNeura programs for treatment of drug addiction, chronic pain, Parkinson’s Disease and other conditions. He is a lead inventor of the company’s Probuphine product for treatment of opiate addiction, and established the initial Phase 3 study demonstrating safety and effectiveness of Probuphine. Dr. Bucalo also directed the development of Probuphine to the treatment of chronic pain. It is his hope and vision that Probuphine will help prevent the addiction that afflicts millions of people who receive prescription drugs to treat chronic pain.

Dr. Bucalo retired from the company as President and CEO in 2007 and Chairman in 2008 to pursue other activities, at which time he remained the largest individual shareholder of record of the company.

Dr. Bucalo is also a member of the Board of Trustees of Carnegie Mellon University and serves on the Research and Technology Committee. He has also served on university committees for music and the arts.

Louis R. Bucalo is also involved in innovative development of sporting activities in the United States, including motoring, soccer and sports broadcasting. These programs and companies are working to enhance the popularity, safety and enjoyment of these sports on a national level.
