Scott Hamlin’s Summary

1. I support academic technology projects in the Arts and Humanities with a focus on electronic textual editing, digital asset management, podcasts, blogs, wikis, Blackboard, and academic web site development.
2. I provide technical training and support for faculty and students in several standard academic applications including Photoshop, PowerPoint, FileMaker, and Wheaton's web content management system.
3. From April 2006 – March 2007, I stood in for the Visual Resource Curator. The duties included overseeing the creation of digital images in the slide library; hiring, training, and supervising 12-15 student workers, and consulting with faculty about digitizing slides and images from books.

My primary goal is to take on more of a leadership role in this area in the future.
