Kevin Briscoe’s Summary

Kevin is the communications officer for Amtrak, where his primary focus is internal communications, managing the production of Amtrak Ink and Amtrak This Week.

Prior to joining Amtrak, Kevin was vice president of communications for The Public Affairs Group/Working Mother Media, which included Diversity Best Practices, Best Practices in Corporate Communications and the Business Women's Network. While there, he maintained senior responsibility for the major communications efforts of the entire company. His publications included:

• Kevin M. Briscoe, Communications Primer 2007 © Best Practices in Corporate Communications (Washington, D.C.)

• Kevin M. Briscoe, Industry Leaders in Diversity © 2006 Diversity Best Practices (Washington, D.C.)

• Kevin M. Briscoe, Winning the Race for Diverse Talent © 2006 Diversity Best Practices (Washington, D.C.)

• Mary-Frances Winters; Kevin M. Briscoe (Ed.), CEOs Who Get It: Diversity from the Heart and Soul © 2006 Diversity Best Practices (Washington, D.C.)

Prior to joining The Public Affairs Group, Kevin was the communications manager for the Defense Mission Systems division of Northrop Grumman in McLean, Va., where he led the division's employee communications functions and served as editor of the Inside DMS newsletter. He also managed the division's diversity initiatives, which led to the creation of the Ambassador Plan, a partnership program with the country's minority engineering organizations to build a stronger career pipeline for their respective memberships.

A seasoned 20-year veteran in the areas of journalism, corporate communications and public relations, Kevin was the managing editor of The National Society of Black Engineers Magazine and the senior editor for TelOSSource Magazine, a telecommunications industry publication.

Kevin is also the editor of PowerNomics: The National Plan to Empower Black America by Claud Anderson Ed.D.
