Marius Vassiliou’s Experience

  • Analyst

  • Executive Director

    Rockwell Scientific

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; Defense & Space industry)

    June 1999 — February 2003 (3 years 9 months)

    Ran (with P&L responsibility) the Information Sciences Division at Rockwell Scientific, a highly successful $17M R&D division employing up to 65 advanced technical personnel in 4 geographic locations, working on wireless systems, wireless sensors, multimedia, human-computer interaction, intelligent systems, and diagnostics&control. Also led the $30M ARL Federated Laboratory Consortium on Advanced and Interactive Displays, a six-year, $30M consortium comprising industry, Government, and universities which achieved world-class results in collaborative planning, human factors, multimodal human-computer interaction [speech, gaze, 3D audio, & other], augmented reality, display stabilization, and other areas. Editor of the Computer Science Handbook for Advanced Displays.

  • Program Director

    Rockwell Science Center

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; Telecommunications industry)

    January 1996 — June 1999 (3 years 6 months)

    · Developed new business and directed large programs leading to products. Developed strategic business/technology plans with Rockwell commercial business units and their subsequent spinoffs. Placed particular emphasis on new growth opportunities and technology discontinuities. Managed associated development programs (total budget about $10M) involving multiple technical divisions and ensured proper business focus. One project developed into a new customer business area (CMOS imaging). Other projects led to two customer product introductions (commercial call-center software packages that were named product of the year in 1996 and 1997 by Call Center magazine). Two other projects resulted in product introductions by a customer (auto-imposition and magnetic cutoff). Organized and presented briefings to top management at customer sites.

  • Manager

    Rockwell Science Center

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; Defense & Space industry)

    January 1995 — June 1999 (4 years 6 months)

    Manager, Multimedia & Human-Computer Interaction Dept. 1995-1999
    [NOTE: partially concurrent with Program Director Position above]
    Built up a world-class multimedia and design-automation research group from an embryonic state in less than 3 years. Organized and led the group in two interacting teams, one focusing on video technology, and the other focusing on hardware/software codesign with a multimedia emphasis. Products now form part of the MPEG-4 standard, in Synthetic and Natural Hybrid Coding (standardization of facial parameters for talking agents, view-dependent scalability), media integration of text and graphics, and video (n-bit video). Other products include a design environment allowing for plug-and-play design and virtual prototyping of multimedia applications by mixing and matching between software libraries in C and hardware libraries in parameterized VHDL. The environment has led to significant design cost savings at customer sites.

  • MTS, Senior Scientist, and Project Mgr

    Rockwell Science Center

    (Defense & Space industry)

    June 1985 — January 1995 (9 years 8 months)

    · Performed a major technology and market analysis of new-generation printing processes for a commercial customer. Interviewed 27 individuals at 16 of the customer’s ultimate customers. Worked with marketing, sales, and engineering staff to establish the technological needs, economic foundations, and strategic direction for advanced-technology projects in printing. Led to a successful demonstration of a photoelectrochemical direct-to-cylinder printing press at Print 97 and other trade shows. Was awarded Rockwell Chairman's Team Award in 1998, in recognition of this early work.
    · Wrote proposals, secured funding, managed, and performed published research in computational electromagnetics, materials science, and imaging science. Made several advances in the fast multipole method and other computational methods. Performed experimental and theoretical studies in the dynamic consolidation of powders to maintain metastable microstructure.
