Kathleen Fitzgerald’s Summary

As a senior executive with 15 years of business-to-business and business-to-consumer marketing experience, my strength is in knitting strategic planning, marketing, operations, and public relations, to bring measurable value and contribute to business sustainability and success. I use a variety of lenses to identify and capitalize on core competencies in order to build agility and optimize operational efficiencies.

Utilizing a unique breadth and depth of experience in public, private, for-profit and not-for-profits, I work as a consultant helping businesses capitalize on core competencies and reposition themselves as nimble and responsive in the global marketplace.

Current projects include: publicity for a Connecticut-based industrial designer; a market opportunity assessment for an international software firm anxious to open in the US market; focus group planning for an economic development think tank; and a book launch for an international organizational development firm.

Through a nationally recognized consulting firm specializing in higher education, I was recently recognized in the top 5% company-wide for exceeding goals during a 15-month contract.

As vice president for marketing and enrollment, I managed a team of 60+ employees and was responsible for generating $37 million in revenue, requiring a delicate balance between cost of sale, delivery of services, and net revenue.

Prior experience as a marketing and public relations professional required an ability to come up to speed quickly on various business models including characteristics and attributes of resin production at GE Plastics, principles and practices of fine paper production at Mohawk Paper, and the study of ergonomics in hand held appliances for Anderson Design, a nationally ranked industrial design firm.

Kathleen Fitzgerald’s Specialties:

Completed three process re-engineering initiatives. These cross-functional process mapping initiatives led to a complete re-engineering and technology integration of online registration system, billing, purchasing, and accounts receivable processes.

Using Motorola’s Six Sigma approach to error reduction, the initiatives began with an evaluation of the cost, effectiveness and efficiency from the initial point of contact through their entire customer experience.
