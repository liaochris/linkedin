Steve Edwards MBE’s Summary

Open Source Intelligence Training Ltd (UK)

Open Source Intelligence Training is a company run by a 15 year OSINT intelligence operations veteran with a background of 30 years in law enforcement. The owner of the company, has won numerous awards for his work in developing OSINT as a recognized intelligence discipline within law enforcement in the UK including recognition for his work by her majesty Queen Elizabeth who awarded him the status of a Member of the Order of the British Empire (MBE) for his work.

In addition to the training role the company also conducts high-level OSINT research and consultancy for both government and commercial customers including acting in an advisory and consultancy role.

  • Liaison Officer

    Serious Organised Crime Agency (SOCA) e-Crime sub-directorate

    (Government Agency; Law Enforcement industry)

    January 2006 — August 2007 (1 year 8 months)

    Since January 2006 I have been working in the role of Liaison Officer within the e-Crime sub-directorate of the Serious Organised Crime Agency (SOCA) which, until April this year, was the National High-Tech Crime Unit (NHTCU).

    Part of my responsibility within SOCA was to act in an intelligence sharing role and deliver security advice with regard to e-Crime threats through confidential relationships. I did this by providing a combination of sound advice at tactical level with relevant experiential guidance at strategic level for which I maintained a high level of awareness of trends and threats.

    I participated in a number of committees and groups such as the Cabinet Office Cybercrime Outreach Committee and The Fraud Advisory Panel Cybercrime Working Group.

  • Tactical and Technical Consultant

    National High-Tech Crime Unit (part of the National Crime Squad (NCS))

    (Government Agency; Law Enforcement industry)

    August 2003 — January 2006 (2 years 6 months)

    From August 2003 to January 2006 I worked as a Tactical and Technical Consultant in the National High-Tech Crime Unit. This role required me to act as a point of contact for enquires and requests from around the world at many different levels.

  • Government Affairs

    Private Staff of the Commissioner of the Metropolitan Police Service (New Scotland Yard)

    (Government Agency; Law Enforcement industry)

    September 2000 — August 2003 (3 years)

    Between September 2000 and August 2003 I had the unique opportunity to serve on the private staff of the Commissioner of the Metropolitan Police Service (MPS), Sir John Stevens where I was responsible for providing him with political intelligence and liasing with external central and local governing agencies.

    Whilst there I also ran the Police Parlimentary Scheme which required me to organise attachments to the Metropolitan Police for members of parliament and I was responsible for the hospitality and itineray for VIP visiting officers who were guests of the Commissioner.

  • Project Leader (New Scotland Yard)

    Intelligence Directorate SIB (Service Intelligence Bureau) responsible for OSINT.

    (Government Agency; Law Enforcement industry)

    January 1998 — September 2000 (2 years 9 months)

    Between January 1998 and September 2000 I set up and managed a small team within the Metropolitan Police Intelligence Directorate SIB (Service Intelligence Bureau). This project involved me proving new concepts that resulted in important attitude changes with regard to the police use of external intelligence.

    I was tasked with the management of a team of intelligence officers and given the job of finding new intelligence gathering sources and techniques to support the fight against terrorism and organised crime.

    Largely as a result of this work I was awarded the Order of the British Empire (MBE) for services to the Police and a number of international awards.

  • Team Member

    MPS Directorate of Intelligence SID Project (New Scotland Yard)

    (Government Agency; Law Enforcement industry)

    July 1994 — September 2000 (6 years 3 months)

    In July 1994 I left uniform policing and took up a detective position at the MPS Directorate of Intelligence (SO11). Whilst there I worked as a project team member responsible for the planning and implementation phases of a project converting the Metropolitan Police intelligence system from a paper-based one to an integrated IT solution and helping the MPS to become an intelligence-led organisation.

    This role involved me acting as an advisor to the technical team as well as developing new apporaches towards the use of intelligence. The Systems for Intelligence and Detection team was responsible for a transformation in the Met Police use of intelligence.
