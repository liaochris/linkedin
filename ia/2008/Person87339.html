Jeff Hardee’s Summary

After serving in the military and completing my B.A., I have worked in the IT industry for over a decade in help desk, systems administration, and network engineering positions. In addition to these positions, I hosted an IT radio show on a local AM station for a few months, taught a variety of IT subjects in the classroom for close to a year, and I mentored students and taught courses at one of the premier on-line learning companies. Currently, I work as a Senior Network Administrator at Orbital Sciences where I provide a wide range of network administration services from managing hardware to implementing security.

I consider myself to be at the intermediate stage of my career in IT, with a solid base of skills and a plan for future development. I'm currently digging deeper into networking and security and I continue to expand my writing credits with various projects. I'm also completing a Master of Science in Technology degree, specializing in Global Technology and Development. My thesis examines the cultural aspects of digital movie piracy. I strive to be active in various IEEE societies as well, allowing me to examine my field from multiple angles.

I enjoy the changing nature of IT and am glad to be a part of its evolution. Although I don't expect to master every aspect of technology, I do strive to be highly competent in my chosen areas of expertise, as well as to communicate those areas to the average user in informative and enjoyable ways.
