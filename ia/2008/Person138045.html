Liz Brinkley’s Summary

In my former life I was a Genie but decided that a career change was necessary when the excitement of living in a lamp wore off. Aladdin was sad to see me leave but promised to keep in touch. So armed with a bunch of great references, my head full of ideas, I headed off to the West Country.

My career as a Genie took many twists and turns. My first wish came true when I landed in Sydney at Pete Sherman’s place - 42 Wallaby Way, where my creative talents were put to great use at advertising agencies Leo Burnett’s and BMF, where I worked on multinational accounts from Kelloggs to Heineken.

A year later after a slight cock up with immigration, I found herself washed up on the banks of the river Thames, with a only a dollar to my name and a slight Australian twang.

After pounding the streets of London (not easy when you’re inside a lamp) I was rubbed up the right way by BurkittDDB who recognized my talents and immediately set me to work on John Lewis, Peroni, Schwarzkopf and Hasbro. Narrowly avoiding working with animals and children, I've rubbed shoulders with some of the most talented creatives in the business, I sure know my ABC1’s from my FMCG’s whether above or below the line.

I've persuaded Eskimos to use environmentally friendly, sustainable ice cubes and focused on unearthing new and exciting projects for Nameless, making all their wishes come true.
