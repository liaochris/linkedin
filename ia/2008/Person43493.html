Daniel Friel’s Summary

Daniel Friel founded the Strategic Alliances and Investments group at Bank of America. As President of Bank of America Technology Investments and Bank of America Ecommerce Holdings, Daniel managed the investment of over $125 million in more than 40 portfolio companies, resulting in realizations exceeding $250 million and additional returns and value-add to the bank of more than $1 billion. Daniel’s team focused on commerce, security, real time reporting, intelligent agent technologies and related platforms. Notable investments included Signio, an electronic payments gateway which was acquired by VeriSign for more than $1 billion, Shopping.com, which pioneered online comparison shopping and is now owned by eBay, and Archipelago, the nation's first totally open, all-electronic stock exchange which recently merged with the New York Stock Exchange. Daniel and his team also structured and negotiated Bank of America’s electronic billing and payment strategic agreement with CheckFree Corporation valued at roughly $500 million in cash, equity, and warrants. Daniel has served as a director and advisor to a number of companies including WebGen Systems, Inc., CapitalStream, and InQuira.

Daniel began his 18-year career with Bank of America and its predecessors (NCNB and NationsBank) as Senior Vice President and Director of Financial and Economic Analysis. Daniel is a graduate of Louisiana State University and attended the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill’s graduate school in Economics. He also taught economics at North Carolina State University.
