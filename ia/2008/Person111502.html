Laurent Chauvet’s Summary

What is a CIO ?

Somebody who knows perfectly well the job of operational teams; a head of project who needs to follow a method, whatever the circumstances are (sometimes critical); a manager who put himself and his choices in question every week and is transparent towards his team; technical competencies that need to be updated regularly; a quasi-permanent resistance to stress; an necessary listener; a clear communication of any instant in order to explain, prove, convince, optimise; and at last an efficient and strong buyer.
Information systems manager, it is a perfect knowledge of what is needed, of the answers to these needs, and all this within record time and at record price, whilst answering to the necessity to satisfy a future need / an optimization that only the information systems manager and his team are aware of in the company. The information systems manager is clearly autonomous; indeed if he needs to be able to understand the operational technician and the DAF (and understand means that he must be able to communicate, therefore assimilate the organisational fundamentals of these two profiles), they do not necessarily do.

What is the job of the CIO in the company?

Despite false beliefs, the CIO is the spine of the organisation. As a computer scientist of today, the CIO is not just a software engineer. A computer scientist is lucid, logical, organised, methodological, efficient, since without these characteristics, his system will never hold together. Actually, he knows perfectly how is organised the company, as much in its operational activities as in its transversal ones.
