  • Volunteer

    Phoenix Children's Hospital

    (Non-Profit; Civic & Social Organization industry)

    1996 — Present (12 years)

    I manage and fundraise for the Ty Troidl Party Fund. The Troidl family established this fund in order to keep alive the memory of Ty Troidl (1985-1997). Monies go toward throwing parties and have been utilized to serve milk and cookies at Christmas, throw a high school graduation party for a student unable to leave the hospital, and to purchase wagons to transport patients.

    Previously, I served as a founding member of the Children's Advisory Council, a group of eight to eighteen-year old hospital patients and siblings of patients. I was the first sibling to serve on the board and I expanded the group to include two sibling positions in an effort to fullfill the hospital's mission of family-centered care. We expanded the Teen Room, making it age appropriate. We also successfully lobbied hospital staff and administration to implement changes including bedside accessibility EMLA cream.
