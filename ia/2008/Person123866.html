Martin (Marty) Smith’s Summary

You don’t know what you don’t know may seem like a strange summary for a career. Finding jobs where I can learn new stuff is the single common thread over the last thirty years. Does anyone else feel like it is crazy to type "thirty years" since college and have it be accurate? I spent five years at Vassar. My first job was selling Vassar to students around the country. Left Vassar to work for P&G because they had a great sales training program. Was smart enough to know just how much I didn't know as a freshly minted college graduate. Left P&G trading the training and those great P&G systems to M&M/Mars. Created the first crude sales information system in the land of Snickers and Milky Way with a PC, VisiCalc and Dbase (remember those?). That got me noticed. I got to play with a great team in the M&M national office developing the Sales, Management, Analysis, Reporting and Telecommunications (SMART) system (had to love those acronyms). Last job at M&M was as a Region Marketing Manager in Chicago. Got a call from Doug Wheeler, a former M&M VP, to come sell sweetener (NutraSweet) in Deerfield. Selling NutraSweet sounded different and I liked the symmetry (candy then sweetener). Worked for Mary Kay O'Connor, the BEST boss, and we got NutraSweet through patent expiration without losing Nabsico, Dannon or Nestle. Decided it was time to do my own thing. Started Found Objects with Janet McKean (my ex) to sell funky gifts to specialty gifts stores. Ever play with words on magnets on your fridge or look at Rorshock Ink Blots? Found Objects sold David Kapel’s first couple of million Magnetic Poetry Kits making it the “Pet Rock” of our time. We also sold the first Rorshock games, Zen Boards and many other gifts you may know and love. I created FoundObjects.com, a web site featured in O, Redbook and Elle Décor. Divorce meant the business had to be split, so I headed out to find the next thing I didn’t know.

Martin (Marty) Smith’s Specialties:

I come from a long line of southern storytellers. That mixture of Faulkner and Margaret Mitchell helped sell bar soap to Wegman's Store Managers in upstate New York, sweetener to H. J. Heinz and strange gifts to millions. All marketers are storytellers. Now my stories spin out on web pages, get pulled into search engines and reach places I would never have imagined in ways I couldn't contemplate.

  • Unit Manager

    M&M/Mars

    (Privately Held; 10,001 or more employees; Consumer Goods industry)

    August 1982 — January 1989 (6 years 6 months)

    Started selling candy in Buffalo sales unit for Stu Brownstein. Purchased a personal computer in 1982 with personal funds to set up the first primitive sales information system at the company. Promoted to the National Office in Hackettstown, NJ to work on Sales, Management, Analysis and Telecommunications (SMART) system, the first laptop based sales management system in major consumer products. That record stood for about a week as other sales units took up the challenge. People came from all over Kansas to see the display and local radio set up a remote broadcast for over a week. At the time, Wal-Mart was more than a store in rural Kansas.
