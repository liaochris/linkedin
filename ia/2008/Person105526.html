Kevin Buckwalter’s Summary

Kevin R. Buckwalter is a certified Diplomate on the American Board of Family Medicine (ABFM), and he uses a combination of academic knowledge and years of real world experience to provide the best health care possible for his patients. Buckwalter’s medical practice, Kevin Buckwalter, M.D. Ltd, has achieved success in its location in Henderson, Nevada, thanks to Buckwalter’s commitment to proper health care.

Dr. Buckwalter earned his Diplomate status from the ABFM in July 1998. Formerly known as the American Board of Family Practice, the ABFM is a non-profit organization that provides certification for physicians working in the field of family medicine. Founded in 1969 in an attempt to promote doctors to enter the general practice field of medicine, the ABFM is one of the biggest medical specialty boards in the United States. To achieve Diplomate status from the ABFM, Doctors must meet a strict regimen of eligibility requirements that ensure only incredibly qualified doctors like Buckwalter can take on the heavy responsibility inherent with family medical practice.

Buckwalter gained his Diplomate status the same year he finished his residency in the Department of Family Medicine at the University of Pittsburgh Medical Center at the McKeesport Hospital in Pennsylvania. Buckwalter served his entire post graduate, pre-certified medical years in Pennsylvania, receiving his first taste of real world medical procedures in 1993, when he interned with the Department of Surgery at Pennsylvania State University Hospital, working in the prestigious Hershey Medical Center.

Buckwalter studied for his medical degree at the Ross University School of Medicine. He made the schools Dean’s List from 1998 to 1990 and graduated in the top 10 percent of his class with honors in 1993.
