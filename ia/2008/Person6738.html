Alexandre Gonthier’s Summary

I am an entrepreneur with international experience in general management, business and corporate strategy, m&a, fundraising, business development, and technology development in the internet, wireless, financial services and software industries.

Alexandre Gonthier’s Experience

  • Managing Director

    Gonthier Venture Partners

    (Management Consulting industry)

    2004 — Present (4 years)

    I offer advisory services to European and North American venture capital-backed companies with funding, m&a, growth strategies and operations management.

    Operating closely with major venture capitalists, I am particularly focused on telecom and media (broadband internet and mobile) as well as payment companies (B2C and B2B) delivering software or software as a service.

  • Chairman, President and CEO

    iPIN (now Valista)

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Computer Software industry)

    1997 — 2004 (7 years)

    I co-founded iPIN, a global leader in Internet and mobile payments, in San Francisco in 1997. Backed by Accel Partners, Sutter Hill Ventures and Wells Fargo Bank, iPIN’s key customers and partners were AT&T, AOL, France Telecom, Orange, Wanadoo, SFR, Vodafone, BT, T-Online, Tiscali (now Alice), Wells Fargo, HSBC, Visa, General Motors, Gemplus (now Gemalto), Convergys, Portal Software (now Oracle), Accenture, Capgemini.

    I started out as CTO, running product management and development, took responsibility for our European operations after securing France Telecom, the company's largest deal to date. I was eventually named Chairman and CEO, and personally ran the m&a process that led to the merger of iPIN with Irish-based Network365 in early 2004 to form Valista.

    Today, Valista’s products are deployed across blue-chip customers in N. America, Europe, Asia and Japan, and process over 30m payments every month for over 150m users and 3,000 merchants.

  • Co-Founder and Partner

    terreActive Consulting

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry)

    1996 — 1997 (1 year)

    I led business development and acted in a technical program management capacity for the development of large scale middleware-based transactional websites. Key customer engagments included Club Med and Sony.

  • Research Scientist

    ArrayComm

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Wireless industry)

    1994 — 1996 (2 years)

    As part of the R&D team, I designed and implemented algorithms in digital signal processing and operations research for cellular planning and capacity analysis. I also designed a smart antenna technology-enhanced digital receiver for the GSM mobile telephony standard.
