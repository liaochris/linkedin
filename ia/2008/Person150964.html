Phil Howson’s Summary

I graduated from Imperial College London in 2004, where I did a BEng in Computing. During my time at Imperial I won a number of academic awards for achieving the highest mark in my year, my group project and my individual project. Additionally the Institute of Electrical Engineers awarded me the title of Best IT Student 2004. See the below links for more information:

http://www.iee.org/OnComms/Circuit/benefits/Editorials/Announcements/SET_2004.cfm

http://www.doc.ic.ac.uk/teaching/prizes/2004/
http://www.doc.ic.ac.uk/teaching/prizes/2003/
http://www.doc.ic.ac.uk/teaching/prizes/2002/


I then worked at Morgan Stanley in the technology division on systems for Algorithmic trading, specifically automated proprietary trading strategies in Fixed Income securities. During this time I was heavily working with a quantative analyst on systems for simulating the performance of new algorithms, executing the algorithms and delivering the correct services to be able to run the algorithms. The algorithms were essentially predictive models for various fixed income securities either investigating relative value or predicting the direction of price movements in certain securities. I recently left Morgan Stanley and joined UBS on their Equity Smart execution team, working on the system which allows clients to use automated trading strategies to execute very large equity orders at a specified price (i.e. VWAP, TWAP, arrival price) or reduce their market impact through the smart scheduling of orders on various equity exchanges.



During my time working in these jobs I became more interested in the business side, and the "black box" algorithms behind the strategies. I also became interested in the mathematics behind these algorithms i.e. the field of nonlinear time series analysis. I would like to learn more about the business and these areas.
