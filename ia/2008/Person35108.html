Chris Crawley’s Summary

Chris Crawley currently works as Executive Director for the Milwaukee University Graduate School Cooperative (MUGS Co-op), the local affiliate for the National Work-Based Schools Initiative (NWBSI). He formerly served as Director of Employment for MUGS Co-op since 2004. He is also Director of Employment for the NWBSI. NWBSI & MUGS Co-op has helped thousands of high school students qualify for, prepare for, and find employment since their inception.

Chris Crawley mentored over two hundred high school, community college, and college internships for Milwaukee students while founder and President of Think Innovative Multi-Media and Think Global Technologies, one of Milwaukee’s largest multi-media software training and development companies (1994-2006).

Chris served as Executive Director for Congress For a Working America where he helped launch the New Hope Project, the Milwaukee Community Service Corps, expanded Job Ride, EITC and many other initiatives.

Chris also served as Regional Director for Wisconsin’s Environmental Decade and other organizations, before becoming Associate Director and Sales Manager for the Milwaukee Careers Cooperative, where he helped generated employment for over seven thousand disadvantaged inner-city Milwaukee residents.

Crawley earned his BA in Communications at Marquette and University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee, an MBA at Cardinal Stritch University where he focused on curriculum development and business modeling of the National Work-Based Schools Initiative. He is now pursuing an MSM with a focus on Human Resources which will be completed next summer. Chris plans to pursue a Ph.D. in either Educational Leadership or Educational Policy in the fall of 2008.

Chris' personal big-hairy-audacious-goal is to help secure employment for over 1 million high school students in his lifetime.
