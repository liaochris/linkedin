Arjan Haring’s Summary

Former Neuromarketing Consultant who dedicates his life to change organisations in a positive way through the Sciene of Happiness.

You have read the work of Daniel Gilbert, Mihály Csíkszentmihályi, Martin Seligman, Daniel Kahneman and Alan Krueger. Just to name a few of the big names in the Science of Happiness. Does it taste like more?

Wether you're interested in the model of Gross National Happiness or what positive psychology can do for your business. Maybe you're even looking for ways to implement Key Performance Indicators that are meaningful rather then indicators that show how much money gets pumped around in your organisation. Wondering if you could motivate employees to work more based on their personal passion?

The Science of Happiness is ready to be implemented! And I am glad to organise (round table) events that will feature today's most prominent thinkers and doers on the subject. Feel free to contact me at arjan at instanthappiness dot nl.

Arjan Haring’s Specialties:

I made it my job to understand organisations, their language and their behaviour. From IT to marketing and management to finance. I know what goes around in organisations. Luckily I also know what goes around in people. Funny how things start making sense when you really know the latter.

  • Volunteer

    Homeless Shelter "Catharijnehuis"

    (Non-Profit; 51-200 employees; Philanthropy industry)

    July 2007 — January 2008 (7 months)

    One day a week I help out in a daytime-only homeless shelter for non-drug addicts. I help out in the soup kitchen, talk/listen to clients and make them feel at home.

    The shelter's philosophy is based on the Theory of Presence. This theory (by Dutch Professor Baart) provides a new perspective on care. Presence is based on involvement and proximity; it is carefully slow and general instead of specialised; attention is a keyword. By criticising detachment, the Theory of Presence strips managers of many tried and tested methods. Authority, firmness and speed should be replaced by attention. Management and market jargon, just like performance contracts, is regarded as taboo.
