Benjamin H. Bratton’s Summary

I am an Academic/ Executive. Still a bit "hybridy" in 2008, but an inevitably more common (and interesting) position and perspective from which to work.

At SCI_Arc (the Southern California Institute of Architecture) and UCLA (where I co-direct the Brand Lab with Rebeca Mendez) my practice draws upon the experimental laboratory model, translating it into scalable strategies for clients.

As a strategist, my role is to connect investments in networked media, architecture and brand: organizational programming and architectural strategy, brand integration and product mutation, channel-centric creative strategy, case modeling + scenario planning, proving that divergence beats convergence most days.

My current role with Yahoo! is to lead the redefinition of the network as a flexible, socially-focused brand platform. I collaborate directly with clients, agencies and B.U.'s to define successful next-gen brand platforms. Ergo, I conceive, drive and hopefully launch the things that will characterize the next phase of deeply social digitial media and channels: more global + more local (less USA-centric), more flexible (platforms not products), more dialogic (interfaces not touchpoints).

My writing focuses on the development of a comprehensive theory of the "interface" as a point of contact between complex systems (digital, human, spatial, logistical, etc.) and its implications for communications, architecture, and organizational strategy. As co-chair of 'ambient:interface' the 54th International Design Conference in Aspen, this work forms an agenda for strategic design in a network society and economy.

My publications range from brand theory to interactive architecture, from bio-media art to software studies. Current editions of AD (Architectural Design) and Volume feature my work, and my introduction to the 2006 edition of Paul Virilio's Speed and Politics from Semtiotext(e)/MIT Press is now available.

See www.bratton.info for more info on all this

Benjamin H. Bratton’s Specialties:

experience design, brand strategy, creative development, product planning/ surgery/ evangelisim, divergence/convergence, pushing the social in social media, deep culture, deep research, deep planning, deep program, doing the groundwork to get things right the first time, big trends and little trends and knowing the difference: presuming the emergence of new disciplinary, organizational and institutional isomorphisms, and working in the image of their potentials.

  • Co-Director of Brand Lab

    UCLA

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    September 2003 — Present (4 years 10 months)

    With Rebeca Mendez, I teach a two quarter sequence on Brand Theory. We begin with the notion that all complex organizations --no matter how rational-- are organized around foundational fictions. When those organizational fictions are so informed and infused by capital that "they become images," we call them brands. The Lab takes a single project for the year, and by using and misusing bleeding edge brand research and design methodologies, we -in essence- recode the ficitons of our virtual clients. In successive years, the Lab has 'rebranded' the UCLA D|MA itself, the materiality of "money," and the MVNO for the next two billion web users.
