Harsh Luthar’s Summary

I am a Professor in the College of Business in the Management Department at Bryant University. Currently, I am also the President of Bryant Faculty Federation which is affiliated with the American Federation of Teachers.

My teaching and research is in the areas of Human Resource Management. I have published in a variety of journals in the areas of Outsourcing, Business Ethics, Employee Rights, Sexual Harassment, and Gender and Race issues. In order to make management knowledge accessible to a broader audience, I have appeared on PBS affiliate Channel 36 for 6 separate half-hour interviews with Paul Zangari. Topics covered during these interviews have included, Internet as a Collaborative Tool, Business Ethics, Employee Loyalty, Sexual Harassment in the Workplace, and Stress Management. I have also given a number of interviews in the print media.

In recent years, I have been integrating my interest in Eastern Philosophies with my professional interests. I have presented twice at the Academy of Management (2005 and 2008) on how Eastern Philosophy and its orientation towards the nature of decision making, actions, and leadership presents useful lessons for modern leaders and managers. One of the papers presented at the Academy of Management on Mahabharata and Krishna's leadership is on my personal website.

One of my goals over the next 5 years is to compile some of my writings in a book form.

Harsh Luthar’s Specialties:

My expertise is in Human Resource Management and Educational Research. My research papers and cases have been on Diversity issues, Business Ethics Education, Cross-Cultural Sexual Harassment, EEO and its impact on the workplace.

Besides teaching and doing research in the HR area, I have also been involved in Labor-Management Relations, Negotiations and Collective Bargaining, and Contract Administration for many years.
