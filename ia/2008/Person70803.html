Glenn McGee’s Summary

Founder of the two year-old Alden March Bioethics Institute ( AMBI ), a major research organization in the capital city of New York devoted to research, education, policy consultation and outreach concerning ethical, legal and social issues in biomedicine and biomedical science, and comprised of 25 faculty from 15 institutions from around the New York Capital District.

McGee is also Editor in Chief of The American Journal of Bioethics, with an ISI Impact Factor of 3.37, and of MIT Bioethics books, and serves on many editorial boards of journals of bioethics, science and philosophy.

He has held the John A. Balint MD Endowed Chair in Medical Ethics in the Albany Medical College and has been a tenured full professor of medicine. He is or has been Adjunct professor at: SUNY Albany (Public Health, philosophy), Albany Law School, Sage Graduate College; Fellow, Rockefeller Institute of Government; and Penn Leonard Davis Institute. He was appointed Chief, Office of Bioethics, Wadsworth Center of NY State Department of Health in 2005. He was assistant professor of bioethics at University of Pennsylvania from 1995-2005, before he was given his endowed chair in Albany and appointed directly to Full Professor, skipping associate entirely.

Author of more than 100 essays in, e.g., Science, Nature, JAMA and others. Authored The Perfect Baby, and Beyond Genetics, edited > 20 books, including the MIT Press book series Basic Bioethics. From 2005-2007 he authored a monthly column for The Scientist, the leading magazine for scientists, and a monthly column for the Albany Times-Union.
