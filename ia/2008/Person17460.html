James Chan’s Summary

James Chan of Asia Marketing and Management (AMM) has advised more than 100 U.S. companies in developing business relationships in China and other Asian countries.

He began his business career in 1981 at Academic Press, Inc. (a subsidiary of Harcourt Brace Jovanovich, a Fortune 500 company) as the company's China Area Manager and International Promotion Manager. He founded his Philadelphia-based independent consultancy in 1983 to advise U.S. firms expand their international operations in China and Asia.

In 1996, the American Management Association (AMA) commissioned James Chan to create its first, three-day, management seminar on China titled "Business Skills for the China Market." James Chan now offers the course at client locations across the country.

Many leading U.S. trade associations have engaged James Chan as their keynote speaker on doing business in China and Asia at their annual meetings or international conferences. They include American Hardware Manufacturers Association (AHMA), Lake States Lumber Association (LSLA), Metal Powder Industries Federation (MPIF), Motor and Motion Association (SMMA), National Federation of Advanced Information Service (NFAIS), Robotic Industries Association (RIA), Unified Abrasives Manufacturers Association (UAMA), Wood Components Manufacturers Association (WCMA) and others.

James Chan is the author of the book, Spare Room Tycoon: Succeeding Independently, the 70 Lessons of Sane Self-Employment. He is a keynote speaker on entrepreneurship.

James Chan’s Specialties:

Doing business in China, keynote speeches at association annual meetings, the art of exporting, management consulting on China strategies, personal entrepreneurship
