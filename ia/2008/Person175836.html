adil abrar’s Summary

I chose advertising because it was the only job I could think of that paid well, where I could wear trainers.

Was an account man. Lasted three months.

Went off to work with a new media company. Got out before the first dotcom bubble burst, and it went pear shaped.

Worked on the Labour Party Election campaign in 2001. Got out before we invaded Iraq and that went pear shaped too.

Ended up hanging around an ad agency working on brands like Playstation, Holsten, Nissan, Strongbow, and a few others I can't remember. It was fun but I was never quite sure what I did.

Unlike when I went off to work at Amnesty International, where I did lots of stuff - podcasts, virals, seeding, poster campaigns, magazines, mugs, badges, TV programming, events, festivals - and along the way, did my little bit for humanity.

At Amnesty, I developed / stumbled upon a new way of working, which is half thinking, half doing and half trying new things. I'm calling it planning-producing.

Now, I'm looking to do more planning-producing with other nice people. Clients or agencies, I don't mind. As long as it's nice work with nice people.
