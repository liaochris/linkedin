Bjorn Roche’s Summary

I am an experienced programmer and systems administrator. I enjoy Mac OS X, Unix (especially FreeBSD and Debian Linux), Java, C and C++. My specialty is audio programming, including audio processing/DSP, and audio GUI design. I am also experienced as a scientific programmer, having worked on several bio-medical imaging projects, and projects involving advanced statistics. Most of my time right now is taken up with XO Audio, but I occasionally consult and work part time as a scientific programmer and systems administrator.

  • Research Programmer

    New York University School of Medicine, Department of Radiology

    (Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Research industry)

    March 2002 — September 2003 (1 year 7 months)

    Designed, implemented and tested command-line image analysis application in C++. Application performed texture analysis on images from lung CT scans using linear scale-space techniques (aka ``Gaussian Derivatives''). Analyzed output from application in SPSS to diagnose severity of a class of diseases known as small airway disease. Published and presented results at SPIE's Physics of Medical Imaging conference in 2003.
    Contributed code to other research projects based on lung CT.

    (Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Research industry)

    August 2002 — August 2003 (1 year 1 month)

    Designed, developed and deployed semi-automated image processing software.
    - Researchers used GUI portion of software to fine tune settings for image processing.
    - Back-end performed a variety of image processing tasks such as rotation, cropping, removal of debris and standardization of brightness and contrast on a high volume of large (>200 MB) microscope images.
    - Maintained and administered Sun and Linux servers and Windows workstations. Managed Samba, NFS, Legato NetWorker (Solstice Backup), Apache/Tomcat, MySQL and custom software.
    - Performed regular patches and updates as needed to maintain security and performance.
    - Specified and installed new systems and software when needed.
    - Created and delivered data tapes as specified by NIH using tar and custom software.
