Toby Harris’s Summary

Lord Toby Harris has been a member of the House of Lords since 1998 and Chairs the All-Party Parliamentary Group on Policing and is Treasurer of the Parliamentary Information Technology Committee (PITCOM). Member of the House of Lords Select Committee on Personal Internet Security (2006-7).

Graduated from Cambridge in Natural Sciences and Economics. Worked in the Economics Division of the Bank of England and then at the Electricity Consumers’ Council (Deputy Director from 1983). In 1987, became Director of the Association of Community Health Councils for England and Wales (the national statutory body representing patients’ interests). In 1998 established his own public affairs consultancy, Toby Harris Associates.

Member of the London Assembly 2000-4 (led the Labour Group) and was first Chair of the Metropolitan Police Authority (MPA). Continues to sit on the MPA as the representative of the Home Secretary with a remit to oversee the national and international functions of the Metropolitan Police - primarily its role in counter-terrorism and security.

Member of Haringey Council 1978-2002 (Leader 1987-99; Chair of Social Services 1982-87). Chair of the Association of London Government, representing the 33 local authorities in London, from its formation in 1995 until 2000, having previously chaired the Association of London Authorities.

Chair of the Association of Metropolitan Authorities’ Social Services Committee (1986-93) and led for local government in negotiations about the introduction of Community Care and the Children Act. Vice-President of and was formerly a member of the Executive of the Local Government Association.

Currently, first Chair of the Institute of Commissioning Professionals. Formerly, a non-executive director of the London Ambulance Service, a Senior Associate of the Kings Fund, and a member of the Committee on the Medical Effects of Air Pollutants.

Former member of the Committee of the Regions of the European Union.

Toby Harris’s Specialties:

Knowledge of security, counter-terrorism and public safety issues. Public affairs; development and implementation of public policy; government liaison; stake-holder management; public consultation and representation. Expertise in London Government and politics. Knowledge of IT security issues.
