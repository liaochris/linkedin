Luís Miguel Sequeira’s Summary

I have started my professional life as a junior researcher at the national lab where the Internet "started" in Portugal. Not unsurprisingly, after a team work that launched Portugal's first web site in 1993 (and probably the first web-based intranet), I co-founded Portugal's first commercial Internet Service Provider.

The next step was launching an Application Service Provider — another buzzword at the time, but that nowadays is almost common business practice: the deployment of web-based applications running on a central server, accessed through browsers on low-end desktop computers. During that time, and in a period of about one year, I co-designed three different data centres to support tens of thousands of users, participated in the process of designing software and overseeing teams of developers (in-house and outsourced), as well as managing all related issues of a company in my new role as member of the Board.

Being accustomed to a "pure" service industry, I lacked the skills for dealing with stocks-based accounting and managing, which are quite different. I thus participated in two further projects, launched consecutively, one related to a novel way to bring to market handicrafted items, the other by launching a small-scale franchising operation for custom-built kitchens and house remodelations.

In both cases, I developed (and deployed) business plans, organised the marketing, provided overseeing on the F&A, and helped with sales, as well as deployed the necessary IT infrastructure (quite limited in those cases).

Sadly, the recession hit Portugal quite strongly, so I returned to my first love — Internet-based applications, by working with Portugal's only software house that has launched an open source application, and built a very successful business model around it. This is still my "primary" job these days.

In my "secondary" job I develop 3D presences on the Internet for companies and organisations, using virtual realities.
