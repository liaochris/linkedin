  • Design Engineer

    Edge Innovations

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Motion Pictures and Film industry)

    September 1994 — October 1996 (2 years 2 months)

    This job involved the design of full-scale robotic creatures for major motion pictures. I worked on hardware and software for the robotic whales in "Free Willy III", as well as dolphins for "White Squall" and "Flipper", dolphins and sharks for "Zeus and Roxanne", and snakes for "Anaconda".

    These full-scale, waterproof robotic Anacondas included one 25' long and one 40'. The 40' snake weighed around 6,500 pounds, had 120 axes of coordinated motion (!), was controlled by 60 Texas Instruments floating point DSPs in concert with seven Pentium PCs, and powered by a 200 horsepower, 5,000 psi hydraulic pump. I seem to recall we used over 70 miles of wire in each snake. Both had more axes of coordinated/simultaneous motion than any robot ever built.

    Now, I worked on the robotic snakes ONLY. Regardless of what you think of the story or the actors, our scary beasts were amazing!
