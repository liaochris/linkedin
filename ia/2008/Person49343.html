Deirdre O'Connor’s Summary

I am a criminal defense trial lawyer, exclusively representing the indigent. Over the years, I have represented 1,000’s of clients and tried 75+ jury trials with a fair bit of success. I was privileged to work with extraordinary public defenders in Los Angeles, San Jose, CA, Anchorage, AK, and Boston, MA.

In 2003, I was blessed with the most wonderful child ever created! I shifted to appellate work because I did not want to miss one magical moment watching my daughter as she discovered the world.

In July, 2007, while serving as Director for the Indigent Criminal Defense Clinic at Emory University, I learned that Georgia was planning to execute an innocent man. Despite compelling evidence of his innocence, Troy Davis was scheduled to die on July 18, 2007! The news both captivated and tormented me. Naturally, I understood that innocence means little to those who cannot afford a dedicated lawyer with the resources to properly defend them. But to watch as these extreme consequences of a failed system played out was intolerable. I was compelled to act, as were countless other concerned citizens. Troy was scheduled for execution four times – 7/18/07, 9/23/08, 9/29/08, and 10/27/08. Each time, Troy and his family said their goodbyes. Fortunately, fate intervened with a last minute stay - one came within 90 minutes of execution. For now Troy lives. His fate is in the hands of the 11th Circuit Court of Appeals. To learn more about his case, please visit http://www.freetroydavis.com.

Troy and his case have forever impacted my professional aspirations. I am setting up a non-profit “Innocence Matters” where I will assist in non-DNA innocence cases. My goal is to develop an Innocence Matters clinical program in collaboration with a law school.

Lest you think I’m too single-minded, I also want to start a small bike touring/photography business in Ireland so that I can spend 4-6 weeks in Ireland with my daughter each year.
