Mark Maxey’s Summary

As Vice President of York Media, a division of York Telecom, Mark Maxey manages an award-winning, multi-million dollar television and video production operation. Mark’s work has appeared on ABC, CBS, CNBC, CNN, FOX, MSNBC, MTV, NBC, and PBS, as well as in movie theaters nationwide.

Mark has a new feature film project in development, and recently helped produce the horror film “Crazy Eights” (2007) starring Traci Lords, Frank Whaley, Gabrielle Anwar, and Dina Meyers.

Look for Mark’s PSA spots featuring Troy Aikman and CSI:NY star Hill Harper. Shot in HD and Super-16mm film, the PSA’s can be seen nationally on FOX and CBS.

Mark produced the “Sending You A Little Christmas” music video for award-winning pianist Jim Brickman. The song reached #1 on Billboard’s charts. Mark was Associate Producer of the primetime PBS special featuring legendary composer Marvin Hamlisch conducting the National Symphony Orchestra in concert at the Kennedy Center.

Other musicians with whom Mark has worked include: Alanis Morissette, Blues Traveler, Michelle Branch, Sheryl Crow, Beach Boys, Jimmy Buffett, Hootie & the Blowfish, Five for Fighting, Sister Hazel, Pat McGee, J. Geils. Mark has also worked with actors Robert Duvall, Brian Dennehy, Gary Sinise, Bob Hope, Connie Stevens, Ken Howard, and Mister T.

Prior to joining the York Telecom team, Mark helped build Intervu, the world’s first distributed global streaming media network and event production service later acquired by Akamai Technologies for $2.8 Billion. In the early 1990’s, Mark was part of the team that built one of the first major interactive television companies, TV Answer, and later helped found the Network News Corporation, a real-time, satellite-based news and data delivery service acquired by Wave Systems, Inc. in the mid-1990’s.
