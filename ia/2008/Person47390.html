David Phillips’s Experience

    CEO of Internet start-up, naturalpath.com and greenadworks.com. Naturalpath is a online resource for natural health and green living and GreenAdworks is an advertising network for the green living vertical.

  • EVP, Corporate Strategy & General Counsel

    IGN Entertainment

    (Internet industry)

    2004 — 2006 (2 years)

  • Owner/General Partner

    Lark Ventures

    (Partnership; 1-10 employees; Online Media industry)

    May 2000 — June 2004 (4 years 2 months)

    Phillips served as General Partner of Lark Media, which provides advisory and management services to media and technology companies in the areas of strategy, finance, partnership development, and operations management.

    In this capacity, Phillips served as a strategic and management consultant to several companies in the technology and media sectors including ZG Ventures, In2Books (www.in2Books.org), Epoch Innovations (www.epochinv.com), DVD Station, Inc. (www.dvdstation.com), CoreFix (www.corefix.com), Digital Innovations’ Neuros Audio division (www. Neurosaudio.com), and Napster Inc.(www.napster.com).

  • VP, Music Services

    Napster

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Internet industry)

    January 2002 — September 2002 (9 months)

    In 2002, Phillips served as Vice President for Music Services and a strategic consultant to Napster, Inc. where he advised the CEO and Bertelsmann executives on business development and content strategies. Phillips also managed the business and product development efforts to re-launch Napster as a paid subscription music and media service (the Service was launched in Beta with 35,000 registered users).

  • CEO

    Crunch Music Ltd

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry)

    January 2000 — February 2002 (2 years 2 months)

  • Various

    (Internet industry)

    1994 — 2000 (6 years)

  • Senior Vice President (MD, UK; GC Europe)

    AOL Europe

    (Partnership; 1001-5000 employees; Internet industry)

    May 1997 — December 1999 (2 years 8 months)

    At AOL Europe, he served as Senior Vice President, Business and Legal Affairs where he advised the CEO on corporate development and strategic partnerships and negotiated key content, technology and distribution deals. As acting Managing Director of the UK, Phillips led the merger and integration of AOL and CompuServe UK, and had responsibility for strategy, organizational structuring, budgeting and new services. He served as the principal negotiator and chief counsel of AOL Europe's $350 million acquisition of CompuServe Europe in 1998.
