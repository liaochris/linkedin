Ken Herts’s Experience

  • VP Finance, Consumer Media Group

    Dow Jones

    (Public Company; Publishing industry)

    2007 — Present (2 years)

    Ken Herts is vice president, finance of the Consumer Media Group of Dow Jones. He is responsible for financial support of The Wall Street Journal’s print, online, and international editions, Marketwatch, Barron’s and other ventures of the Group. Mr. Herts leads the budgeting, forecasting and business planning processes, as well as financial analysis of capital investments, acquisitions, and other strategic initiatives. He and his team generate actionable recurring financial reports and ad hoc analyses to help managers in the Consumer Media Group achieve their business goals.

  • VP & General Manager, Newswires

    Dow Jones

    (Public Company; Online Media industry)

    January 2002 — December 2006 (5 years)

    Prior to assuming his current responsibilities in January 2007, Mr. Herts spent five years as vice president and general manager of Dow Jones Newswires. He was responsible for strategy, product development, business planning, acquisitions & joint ventures, financial management and the business operations of Dow Jones Newswires. Mr. Herts led the development of new products and services, such as NewsPlus, Wealth Manager, and products for algorithmic trading, which have boosted Newswires revenue and customer satisfaction. He helped negotiate acquisitions and joint ventures in Europe, enabling Newswires to expand to ten languages, helping to drive revenue and profits internationally.

  • Publisher

    The Wall Street Journal Europe

    (Public Company; Publishing industry)

    1998 — 2001 (3 years)

    From April 1998 through June 2001, Mr. Herts served as publisher of The Wall Street Journal Europe. He was based in Brussels and had responsibility for all business operations of the paper. During his tenure, the European Journal became the first edition of the Journal to be redesigned using editorial color. Marketing, printing and distribution operations were expanded, helping audited paid circulation increase from 68,000 to more than 100,000, and the newspaper to increase its advertising market share.

  • Senior VP, Technical Development

    Telerate

    (Online Media industry)

    1993 — 1997 (4 years)

    In March 1993, Mr. Herts was appointed director of technical development for Dow Jones Telerate, and in October 1993, he became senior vice president of technical development. In May 1994, he was given added responsibilities for the unit's global network operations.

  • Director of Financial Analysis

    Dow Jones

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; DJ; Publishing industry)

    June 1987 — May 1990 (3 years)

    Mr. Herts joined Dow Jones in July 1983 as staff assistant for special projects in the comptroller's department and became manager of financial analysis in October 1985 and director in June 1987. In those roles he was responsible for business modelling and analysis of acquisitions and other major expenditures. During this period, he led financial analysis efforts on a number of acquistions and divestitures, with value totalling more than $2 billion.
