Ludovic Blain’s Summary

Ludovic Blain recently was the National Campaign Coordinator for Equal Voice for America‘s Families. He developed the campaign plan, led coalition-building, supervised 9 off-site field staff, support staff, and 6 off-site consultants, and managed the $5 million national campaign. His successes include leading the campaign team to hold 60 town halls over 5 months in more than 12 languages across 12 states with over 10,000 attendees.

Previously Mr. Blain was Organization Services Director of the New Progressive Coalition. where he encouraged synergy and collaboration among 200 organizational members. Previous to that he was Associate Director of the Democracy Program at Demos, leading campaigns for Election Day Registration, felon voting and other voting rights reforms. He was also Associate Director of We Interrupt This Message, an anti-racist strategic communications organization, and held several leadership positions on environmental justice, voting rights, and consumer protection issues over a decade at NYPIRG.

Ludovic’s international work includes engaging Denmark's ruling party officials regarding how right-wing promotion of racism and xenophobia was a strategy to erode public support for progressive public policy. As a member of PeaceWatch Ireland he led discussions about sectarianism and other forms of racism with community leaders in Northern Ireland and the Republic of Ireland. He has led NGO capacity building work in Haiti, Canada and The Gambia, where he received honorary citizenship from the President.

Ludovic currently serves on several boards, including Rockwood Leadership Program, the NYPIRG Fund, Communications Leadership Institute, and the Curriculum Project.

Mr. Blain holds a bachelor's degree from the City College of New York, where he received awards for his activism. He’s a graduate of leadership and organizing programs, including the Industrial Areas Foundation and the Rockwood Leadership Institute.

Ludovic Blain’s Specialties:

Issue advocacy, community organizing, strategic communications, coalition and collaboration development, organizational development, leadership development, anti-racist analysis.
