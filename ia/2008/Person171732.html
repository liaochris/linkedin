  • Research Assistant

    Stony Brook University

    (Educational Institution; Research industry)

    2001 — 2006 (5 years)

    My research focused on the regulation of the Ras oncogene. Ras is a key regulator of numerous cellular processes including cell division, cell death, and cellular differentiation. Its aberrant regulation has been implicated in numorous cancer types. My work demonstrated the physiologic implication of a novel positive feedback loop between Ras and its activator, Son-of-Sevenless (Sos). Ras activation results in an increase in Sos activity, and consequently, further Ras activation. This mechanism has an affect on virtually all levels of the Ras signaling cascade and can be a future target for cancer therapeutics.
