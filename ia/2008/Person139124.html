Nelson Brito’s Summary

Nelson Brito has over nine years of experience in high-tech, most of which have been spent in computer security. He has worked in several different areas including: research and development, consulting, writing, and training. He is the author of several “Proof-Of-Concept” tools and texts, such as: 1) Public Tools: Sucker-0.1.tar.gz, brute-fw1-agent.pl, rcpt-analisys.tgz, nadas, sdi-brutus.pl, ath0-2.sh, nb-isakmp.pl, nb-isakmp.c, farpce.pl, and ftpe-crypt.c; 2) Private Tools: NBaddress.pl, NBlookup.pl, NBfever.pl and NBbrute.pl.

More recently Nelson has completed work on the Intrusion Detection / Prevention Accuracy Methodology, which results in some comprehensive tools to test IDS/IPS: NUMB Next Generation False-positive Tool (nng-4.12.tgz), ENCORE False-negative Tool (encore_project.pm), Pattern Matching IDS/IPS Shellcode detection and bypass technique matrix (New Signatures Analysis (20060818).pdf), and Two private versions of public exploits for MS02-039. Those are a completely toolset for testing IDS/IPS detection capabilities.

Nelson has led consulting engagements for lead companies in Latin America in many vertical markets and sectors including: Financial, Automotive, Transportation, Government, Manufacturing and Oil. As a sought after speaker, he has presented to industry professionals at conferences such as: Security Week, CNASI, H2HC, Gartner, ITA, and IME’s Security Conference.

Currently, Nelson holds a security engineer role at IBM Brazil; prior to that Nelson has held senior roles at Secunet AG and Internet Security Systems.

Nelson Brito’s Specialties:

Network security; research and development; protocol analysis and tools development; methodology development; IDS/IPS accuracy detection toolset development; competitive analysis; protocol, binary, and malwares reverse engineering; security solutions deployment; and security services deployment and offer development.
