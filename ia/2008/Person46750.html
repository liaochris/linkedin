David Hauser’s Summary

As co-founder and Chief Technology Officer of GotVMail, David Hauser has engineered the company's overall technology roadmap and continues to promote GotVMail’s innovation by managing the company's engineering teams, network operations, and QA testing. This hands-on approach has resulted in the most reliable and state-of-the-art hosted phone system for entrepreneurs and small businesses.

Hauser was recently featured on the inaugural installment of the new CNN business program, “Young People Who Rock,” and was selected among the country’s “Top 5 Entrepreneurs Under 25” by BusinessWeek. Additionally, he was included among the “40 Under 40” talented young leaders by American Venture Magazine; was named a small business “Power Player” by Entrepreneur Magazine; and recognized as the “Massachusetts Young Entrepreneur of the Year” by the U.S. Small Business Administration.

Hauser has also been featured in various business publications, including BusinessWeek and the Wall Street Journal, as well as authoring a chapter about his experience launching GotVMail in “Student Entrepreneurs Giving Back: 10 Student Entrepreneurs Tell Their Stories of Social Impact.”

A technology start-up veteran, Hauser co-founded Return Path, an e-mail performance management company with many Fortune 500 clients on its roster. In addition, Hauser has consulted for various technology companies as well as mentoring budding entrepreneurs at his alma mater, Babson College.

Hauser received a Bachelor of Science in Business Administration from Babson College. While at Babson, he garnered several awards, including the prestigious "Small Business of the Year Award," the 2004 "Coastal Region Global Student Entrepreneur Award," and the 2004 Global Student Entrepreneur Awards' “Innovation Award.” Hauser is a member and serves on the board of Entrepreneurs' Organization (EO), and is a Global Advisory Board member of the Students for the Advancement of Global Entrepreneurship (SAGE).
