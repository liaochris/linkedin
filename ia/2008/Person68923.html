George King’s Summary

George A. King (48) is the Vice Chairman of the publicly listed company Global Capacity (www.globalcapacity.com CGSY.OB) as well as the President. Prior to assuming these positions, Mr. King was co-founder, Vice Chairman and President of 20/20 Technologies, Inc. (since December 2002) which merged with Global Capacity in September 2006. Global Capacity is a global network integrator and information services company doing business in more than 120 countries, and co-headquartered in the US and UK. Mr. King has more than twenty years of experience in law, capital markets finance and telecommunications, including serving as a Director, Chief Development Officer and in several President operating roles at Universal Access from 1997 to 2002 as the company moved from start-up through its Goldman Sachs – led IPO and operated as a multi-billion NASDAQ – listed public company. He began his career from 1984 to 1995 with the Wall Street law firm of Mudge Rose Guthrie Alexander & Ferdon and as a Vice President in the New York office of the global investment banking firm of Credit Suisse First Boston. He was a co-founder of an investment banking spin-off from CSFB, Cambridge Partners, LLC, in 1995 and operated his own corporate finance advisory firm from December 1996 until August 1999 when he joined Universal Access on a full time basis. Mr. King’s board experience includes serving as Chairman of Norsat International, Inc. (TSX: NII), Vice Chairman of Global Capacity, and as a Section 16 Officer of Universal Access, a US public company, as well as Director or Trustee of several private companies and universities in the United States and the United Kingdom. Mr. King holds an A.B. (cum laude) from Colgate University and a J.D. from Fordham University School of Law, where he was Editor-in-Chief of the Fordham Urban Law Journal. Mr. King lives in New York and since 1999 has divided his time between the United States and European markets.
