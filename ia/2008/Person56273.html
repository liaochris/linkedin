Eddy Kindermans’s Summary

" Ne spekkewinkel" , " A candy store", that's how a West-Flemish entrepreneur reacted when he saw the different offerings on the back of my business card.

Yes, I write convincing texts AND professional screenplays AND direct videos or tv programs AND design (not in the graphical way) usable websites AND teach all those things to others...

Everything I do is about COMMUNICATION between people: getting the message across from A to B. It can be a story, a product, an attitude, a message,a good feeling...

It means that at the end of the day I do a lot of listening. I listen and try to understand what the client/student really wants and needs.

I believe in separating the "architect" from the "builder". When my architect-work is done, we select the right medium: print, moving image, web,etc. Over the years I created a network of the finest "builders" in the country:graphical designers, cameramen, developers, editors, actors, etc.

Most of the time, an enormeous added value is liberated when open-minded specialists in their field are working together!

I'm not an ad agency (some of them are my best customers ;). If you compare an ad agency to a supermarket, my company is a small delicatessen store.

So you see, the "candy store remark" wasn't too far from reality, but hey, I don't know about you, but I spent some of the best times of my life in candy stores, as a matter of fact I was BORN in the food store of my parents...

For the past 6 years I've been running my own shop working with a network of the finest freelancers and agencies in Belgium. I'm 47 but I'm still going strong and I'm full of new ideas !

I speak 5 languages quite fluently: Dutch (mothertongue), French, German, English and Spanish. Professionally I write in Dutch and English.

MBTI personality type: ESTP (look it up!)

  • General Manager

    Celebrations Entertainment, Brugge, Belgium

    (Public Company; 11-50 employees; Entertainment industry)

    November 1995 — December 1997 (2 years 2 months)

    This company runs medieval dinner shows (fighting knights, jester, firespitters, etc) in a former church in the center of Bruges. Average of two shows a week, with +- 200 people per show. As general manager I was responsible for an industrial kitchen (feeding up to 500 people at once), the show (now I know how a circus director feels;) and the marketing and sales, mainly to Belgian companies and tourists from all over the world (directly or via touroperators).

    Belgium is a country where theme dinners are relatively unknown by the public. In Holland, for example, several major companies exploit a range of different sites and themes, sometimes catering for 1500 people at once in the appropriate setting. Themes go from pirates, Romans, the roaring 20's, medieval to Arabian nights, etc, etc...

    It's the most satisfying job you can think of. Imagine 500 people giving you and your co-workers a standing ovation (in a church!) at the end of every show...unforgettable!
