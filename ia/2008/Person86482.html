  • Free Communications Tip

    JCW

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    January 1995 — 1996 (1 year)

    “Okay, we need a communications plan.”

    Famous last words, literally. Unfortunately, one of the key reasons companies do poorly at internal communications is that often it’s an afterthought. Those words—“we need a communications plan”—too frequently are uttered after a team or group has met for months. By the time they enlist a writer, the project they’ve been slaving over is ready for launch. Imagine how much time could have been saved—and angst avoided—had the writer been invited to learn about the project months before its launch?

    So here’s the tip—keep the writer in the loop from the get go.

    Caveat: good writers can make sense out of jig-saw-puzzle stories and they can respond to tight deadlines. That isn’t the issue. The issue is communicating with employees proactively. The grapevine is always working, so you may as well be a proactive participant in it. Doing so garners good will, minimizes rumor (and its attendant energy-sapping forces) and builds credibility.
