Ayssar Arida’s Summary

I am an urban designer (MA with Distinction, Oxford Brookes University, 1998), architect (BArch, American University of Beirut, 1994), writer, and entrepreneur. Currently I run London-based Q-DAR development | architecture | research (please see our website http://q-dar.com).

I have over 13 years of international experience in leading multi-disciplinary teams on medium and large scale projects. I am also extensively involved in various projects and productions that overlap and interact with the world of architecture, from public relations campaigns, corporate identity and environment branding, to software and web development, multimedia, art and film.

As a confirmed researcher and innovator, my design work has received major accolades and top industry awards (including Cityscape Dubai 2005 and MIPIM Cannes 2006).

I have taught architecture and urban design at AUB, lectured internationally and published numerous books and articles on urbanism. My writing including work on 21st century cities and culture is now influencing design education and practice across Europe and the USA.

I have consulted for different organisations, including New York based Parsons-The New School for Design, London based Zenith Foundation for Contemporary Culture, Brussels based CEMEDE (Centre Euro-Méditerranéen pour le Développement), and Beirut based PRS Public Relations and Services. I have co-founded the Centre for the Spatial Realm, a creative research group particularly focused on the interaction between art, culture, science and technology in public space.

I am interested in fixed-term projects, design or brainstorming workshops, collaborations, talks, articles, corporate or public presentations (e.g. on cities, science, design, beirut, etc).
