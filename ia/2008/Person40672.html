Craig Gander’s Summary

AAPG Certified Petroleum Geologist with 11 years experience generating and evaluating oil and gas prospects in the Rocky Mountain and Midcontinent regions, and in field operations. Also has substantial experience as a consulting geologist and field operations manager, and proficient with Geoplus PETRA.

Craig Gander’s Specialties:

Extensive major oil company training in structural geology, stratigraphy, geophysics, petrophysics, log analysis and reservoir engineering.

Craig Gander’s Experience

    Schedule personnel and equipment, and manage wellsite geologists who describe cuttings and perform mudgas analyses for vertical and horizontal wells being drilled in Colorado, Utah, New Mexico, Wyoming, Montana and North Dakota.

  • Senior Project Manager

    Carter & Burgess, Harding Lawson Assoc. & others

    (Privately Held; 1001-5000 employees; Environmental Services industry)

  • Senior Petroleum Geologist

    Chevron

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; CVX; Oil & Energy industry)

    1981 — 1992 (11 years)

    Served as Chevron’s development geologist for the Powder River and Wind River Basins of Wyoming and the Williston Basin of North Dakota and Montana. Duties included performing field studies, subsurface mapping including seismic interpretation, prospect generation, log analysis, monitoring competitor activity, evaluating trades and partner proposals, recommending workovers, wellsite supervision, and providing expert testimony to regulatory agencies. Worked closely with reservoir and production engineers to maximize field profitabilities.
