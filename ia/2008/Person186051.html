Thomas Caruso, PhD’s Summary

With expertise spanning research, corporate, and government arenas, I have a broad range of proven capabilities that can help your client capture, manage, and grow emerging opportunities in biomedical informatics. Specifically, I offer: (1) Strategic business perspective derived from years of experience guiding numerous stages of healthcare/technology commercialization – including concept evaluation, business planning, financing, joint ventures and partnerships, product development, quality assurance, and project management. (2) NIH and caBIG™ contacts developed through five years’ participation in caBIG™ Work Groups (Strategic Planning and Data Sharing & Intellectual Capital) building connections with key people at the NCI Center for Biomedical Informatics and Information Technology and the 60 NCI Cancer Center informatics professionals, and two years developing a CTSA partnership between Virginia Tech and Wake Forest University and participating in Clinical Research Informatics SIG of the AMIA; (3) Industry-academic relationships developed and managed over twelve years at Virginia Tech and through active involvement in professional associations for medical informatics, pharmaceutical, and human genetics professionals; and (4) Project management skills obtained executing simultaneous software projects and managing internal and external relationships during complex, large-scale, long-term initiatives. The emerging field of biomedical informatics offers exciting opportunities for organizations that can translate research findings into marketable products – and that is precisely the value that I offer.

Thomas Caruso, PhD’s Specialties:

Program management and software project management, grant writing, strategic planning, budget management, cancer Biomedical Informatics Grid (caBIG), data sharing, intellectual property, academic-industry relationships, financial modeling, and biomedical research.
