Charles Rainey’s Summary

In May, 2007, I graduated cum laude, earning a juris doctorate and masters in business administration from the University of Nevada, Las Vegas.

While in my last year of law school, I worked with Kurth Law Office, specializing in litigation and government affairs. However, while there, I also worked on issues related to corporate formation, real estate, family law, criminal law, and a host of other practice areas.

Previously, as a summer associate with Greenberg Traurig, I worked principally in the areas of litigation and intellectual property, drafting motions, appellate briefs, and a wide variety of legal documentation.

Additionally, in 2005, while working for Senate Minority Leader Harry Reid in his Washington, DC office, I was afforded the opportunity to draft detailed legislative and policy analysis on behalf of the Democratic Party.

As a law school research assistant, I provided detailed analysis on a myriad of government policy and legal issues.

Also, I competed and regionally placed in the Jessup Moot Court Competition and the National Moot Court Competition, meanwhile serving as the Editor in Chief of Jus Gentium, an on-line magazine devoted to issues of international law.

While earning my MBA, I developed UNLV's first student-run consulting firm, the University Consulting Group, offering business-related research and analysis to small and midsized companies in the Las Vegas valley.

Before earning my bachelor’s degree, I worked in the entertainment industry, operating my own production company in Los Angeles, where I produced short-form works for television and the Internet. At that time, I also worked as a union crew member on numerous feature films, music videos, commercials, and television programs.

Charles Rainey’s Specialties:

My principal interests include: Corporate Finance, Project Finance, International Law, and Government Affairs.
