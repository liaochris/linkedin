Jan Creemers’s Summary

--- Sjamayee, a real composer ---

More than 20 years ago I started to develop a small software product, based on the experience I had with a model-driven generator.
It was long ago (no graphical, OO, XML, WWW).
With success, I had made a first product (in Progress a 4GL) and than I really saw the possibilities (like model on model on model ...)
I have made a big mistake in trying to rewrite the product in Windows (VB, Delphi) and later in Java/Swing and trying to bring it to the web.
I have stopped this development also years ago, but now, when I see the evolution of the software development (OO, XML, MDA, SAP), than I am convinced that there is still an enormous need for such a small product.

SJAMAYEE is about:

1. Small product, capable of working in different environments (model-driven).
2. Simple/dynamic/fast user interface (MS Excel alike, grid based, compact)
A few screens (mainly one with 3 sub-screens), no pop-up's or dialogs, simple user-interaction.
3. Small db-model (Entities, Relations, Attributes, Texts).
4. Model-driven generator written in C.
5. Model(s) and meta-data are kept in double linked lists and so almost everything (depending on model) is by default available with where- and what-used views.
6. Building simple applications in different ways:
Defining a simple model with only entities, relations and attributes on these.
Defining applications like a PIM, a Helpdesk-system, a Catalog and lots of similar applications like processing standard letters with easy integration with editors.
Defining more complex applications when extending the model with model-texts (XML), combining and/or stacking different models (security, presentation).
7. Now only model associations, later much better with inheritance, integrated versioning and much more
8. Integration, re-use, OO, context, SAP, Documentum, Legacy apps, AND integration ...
9. ORBAC - Object Role Based Access Control
OpenID - For single sign-on.
10. ... 15 etc.
