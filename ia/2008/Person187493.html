Brian Galvin’s Summary

I left Genesys to become a partner in a patent firm. In retrospect, I see my six month legal career as a sabbatical from my tech career to which I have returned. Fortunately, it was a great sabbatical that left me fresh and with a new perspective and new directions.

I find solutions to complex problems whose solutions have eluded others. My main focus has been contact centers. The need for change in this industry continues to grow. Among other things, a fresh way of looking at the massive amounts of data that contact centers generate is needed, a way not driven by "what product category can I create and dominate" thinking, but by the demands of the problem and the available data. Applying this data thoughtfully in the real world is also needed.

The same is true wherever processes (as opposed to merely transactions) are occurring that involve multiple actors with distinct goals. Typical Operations Research/Industrial Engineering thinking isn't so helpful here (these are by their nature not clean, designed, "locked down" processes), and transaction-oriented analytics (the kind the "BI" industry and classical economics were built on) offer only partial glimpses of what needs to be done.

Bio:
Following 13 years as a nuclear engineer in the US Navy, I joined AMD for a few months in microprocessor marketing. Shortly afterward I joined Genesys as Director of Operations, one of the early employees during the company's initial growth. Following our successful IPO and growth during the late 1990's, I left Genesys to join SAIC as VP, Enterprise Architecture. Shortly afterward, I founded a startup (CommPlexus Corporation) which focused on Internet data mining.

Later I developed a consulting practice serving large companies first as a systems integrator and later as a business consultant focusing on Customer Service Chain Management. My consulting practice ended in 2004 when I rejoined Genesys, first as VP of Product Management and then as CTO.

Brian Galvin’s Specialties:

Unconstrained problem solving, path analytics, simulation, technology strategy and innovation management, invention (author of five issued/allowed and about 25 pending US patents), speed and "small unit tactics" in business problem-solving situations, "redesigning the box".
