  • Producer, Actor

    Prague Playhouse

    (Design industry)

    2004 — 2004 (less than a year)

    In May 2004, I inadvertently "co-founded" The Prague Playhouse when I produced and co-directed the successful production "American Evening" at Prague's Divadlo Na Pradle. My good friend Brian Caspe, who in fact founded the theater company, requested that I raise the curtain under our new group's nameplate. The run of "American Evening" temporarily capped a rewarding acting career that included straight plays, musical theater, television commercials, acting classes, short films and two roles in feature-length films. I regularly return to the stage and screen when my availability permits.

  • Art Director

    Ogilvy & Mather Praha

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    February 2001 — December 2002 (1 year 11 months)

    Sole American expatriate employee at the Prague offices for O&M. An enriching and trying design experience with enduring cross-cultural friendships and a few life lessons. This position rewarded my hunger for experience in television campaign advertising and storyboarding. A wonderful and wise mentor, Peter Chan, oversaw my development here and I am grateful for his tutelage. Enduring creative processes included print and television campaign advertising, corporate identity, direct marketing, copy writing and food styling.

  • Art Director

    Globopolis.com

    (Graphic Design industry)

    2000 — 2001 (1 year)

    A brief yet intense and rewarding splash down in the heart of Europe. Globopolis.com is the enterprise that brought me halfway around the world from California. An unfortunate (albeit late) victim of the dot-com bubble burst at the dawn of the decade, Globopolis capped my Web 1.0 design career and introduced me to the idiosyncratic and lovable people that are the Czechs. Design experience included advertising design and production, corporate identity, user interface development, marketing materials and light copy writing.

  • Art Director

    Falcon Ventures / Take 2 Interactive Software

    (Graphic Design industry)

    August 1999 — August 2000 (1 year 1 month)

    This position encapsulates the lunacy of the Web 1.0 startup world. A dizzying journey from startup to acquisition (twice) to cushy redundancy in less than one year. Founded by three driven Berkeley undergrads, DVDWave (Falcon Ventures) was a real-life thrill ride and eye-opener to the perils of the internet business. As Art Director, I design and oversaw all front-end development for DVDWave.com. It being a startup and all, my duties expanded to corporate identity, User Interface and content development, market analysis, naming, copy writing, production, new business development, and database integration.

  • Graphic Designer

    Wallerich Berard Strategic Design

    (Graphic Design industry)

    January 1998 — August 1999 (1 year 8 months)

    Tom Wallerich (and his partner Michel Berard) provided me with a design apprenticeship (a job, really) and a terrific design mentor in himself upon my arrival to California in the rainy winter of 1998. Drawing from his extensive experience as a Bay-area designer and educator, Tom brought me under his wing and taught me much of what he knew. I am eternally grateful for his kind hand in guiding my early career. I repaid him in kind by running the business for a few months in his absence following a heart attack and bypass surgery. He remains a close friend. The ample experience I gained under Tom includes collateral marketing, web development, package design, exhibit design, alliance campaigns, naming and corporate identity.

  • Proprietor / Designer

    Velocity Graphic

    (Graphic Design industry)

    August 1996 — January 1998 (1 year 6 months)

    Velocity Graphic provided me with an appreciation for successful business owners everywhere. I worked hard, made some money and discovered the true nature of competition in the marketplace. Business really can hard on the little (and in my case, inexperienced) guy. Still, persistence and my reputation as an honest businessman kept me afloat long enough to escape the desert to the fertile job market of San Francisco. My key learnings and design experience at Velocity Graphic included silkscreen and embroidery design, identity and poster design, tax preparation, marketing, and sweeping and mopping.

  • Art Director

    B.M.O.C. Sportwear

    (Textiles industry)

    May 1996 — August 1996 (4 months)

    My first job following graduation was special only in the way that it provided me with an important lesson in character. As I discovered soon after starting full time, I was employed by a morally and financially bankrupt man who was deeply in debt and could not afford to pay me. I gathered myself (and our client list) and founded my own enterprise before his had even been shuttered. Exactly ten years later I would work for a very similar individual. I suppose sometimes lessons bear repeating. Design duties included silkscreen and embroidery design, light print advertising, and managing the office.
