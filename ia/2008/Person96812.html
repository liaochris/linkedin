Jon Kibler’s Summary

I am a Systems Architect for Advanced Systems Engineering Technology in Charleston, SC, where I also serves as the Chief Technical Officer. I have over 36 years experience in information technology and has worked in a variety of industries including: aerospace, defense, systems engineering, manufacturing, communications, utilities, transportation, information services, general business, banking, financial services, wholesale and distribution, retail and point of sales, health care, security, technical training, and consulting. My current emphasis is systems and network security consulting, and security research.

Since 2000, the majority of my work has been in information security. However, my background in information security extends back to the mid-1970s when I first learned penetration testing skills while working on a defense department contract. I am a Certified Ethical Hacker and teach several different ethical hacking and penetration testing courses. I developed and I am primary instructor for a 120 class room hour, hands-on, penetration testing course based on the EC-Council's CEH courseware. I also teach a variety of other security courses, and presents seminars on security to professional groups and the general public.

My security consulting emphasizes systems and network hardening to reduce security exposure, and the deployment of tools to rapidly identify security issues. My expertise includes a diverse range of areas, including: Unix and Linux systems hardening; web application and server hardening; name server hardening; secure system architectures; Cisco router, switch and firewall hardening; secure remote access; intrusion detection; security policy development and deployment; secure programming; and user awareness training. I regularly performs a variety of security services for clients,from basic reviews and assessments to extensive penetration tests and ethical hacks.
