Charlotte Banks’s Experience

  • Lead Marketing Principal

    MMC Group, L.P.

    (Privately Held; 501-1000 employees; Staffing and Recruiting industry)

    January 2005 — Present (3 years 3 months)

    Develop, implement, and manage various marketing, business development, and corporate initiatives for the limited partnership and its affiliates. Duties include: generating, investigating, and responding to new business opportunities; proposal development and management; developing marketing, collateral material, and other corporate communications documents; representing the partnership at various client/small business/networking events; serving on the Board of Directors; and participating in management/business/strategic planning meetings.

  • HUB Program Director

    Texas Facilities Commission (formerly Texas Building and Procurement Commission)

    (Government Agency; 501-1000 employees; Government Administration industry)

    December 2001 — January 2005 (3 years 2 months)

    As Director of the State of Texas Historically Underutilized Business (HUB) Program, my primary duties included managing the State's certification and compliance program for minority and women-owned businesses. My duties also included drafting and implementing Government Code in accordance with state law for all aspects of the HUB Program, including the State's Mentor Protégé Program, the HUB Subcontracting Plan, HUB Reporting and HUB Forums. In addition, I was responsible for preparing a $9.8 billion dollar expenditure report to the Texas Legislature on a biennial basis. My position included the oversight and management of more than $1 billion in HUB expenditures and 12 full-time professional employees.

    In two years, I facilitated changes to the program’s administration that contributed to a 37% increase in the number of HUBs available for state procurement and facilitated the highest percentage of state agency spending with HUBs since 1998.

  • Regional Economic Development Specialist

    U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development

    (Government Agency; Government Administration industry)

    August 2000 — November 2001 (1 year 4 months)

    Primary objectives were to increase the impact of the Section 108 Loan Guarantee Program by marketing its availability, streamlining the application process, and providing ongoing technical assistance. Responsibilities included, developing and implementing a marketing plan within Texas, Arkansas, New Mexico, Louisiana, and Oklahoma. I developed and organized public information campaigns for the billion dollar Section 108 Loan Guarantee Program, as well as several competitive grant programs.

    While serving at HUD, I reviewed, scored, and made funding recommendations for competitive proposals and grant applications in excess of $75 million.

  • Governor's Small Business Advocate / Small Business Director

    Texas Department of Economic Development

    (Government Agency; 201-500 employees; Government Administration industry)

    April 1996 — August 2000 (4 years 5 months)

    Primary responsibilities included the development and implementation of a statewide marketing plan for small business development. Duties included, facilitating the development of new businesses in Texas by marketing financial and administrative resources via the Internet, presentations, trade shows, and a customer information call center. I routinely marketed financial packages and resources, including loans, grants, and venture capital, via presentations and trade shows. Responsibilities included forging new partnerships and managing existing relationships with affluent clients, elected officials, and small business service providers.

    In September of 1999, I was designated as the Governor’s Small Business Advocate for the State of Texas. In June of 2000, I had the honor of serving as a delegate to the Congressional Small Business Conference.
