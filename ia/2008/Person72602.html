Greg Russell’s Summary

Successfully led the cross-functional development and deployment of over 12 major releases of both web and enterprise software products and services

Designed and led the implementations of customized agile based software product development lifecycle methodologies that have increased the amount/quality of cross-functional collaboration and decreased time to market while also improving product quality by dramatically reducing the number of new and existing bugs

Created and implemented innovative requirements processes that allow for broad participation and cooperation using cross-functional “requirements teams” and provide for the capture of all commercial, functional and technical requirements in standardized and reusable templates

Created and tracked metrics to provide vital software development and quality trending data and analysis needed to make critical real-time adjustments.

Led the design and development of a sophisticated SOA based ecommerce engine and ASP based content delivery service as well as a full set of supporting web and enterprise services including digital content ingestion and management, digital downloads to PCs and mobile phones, carrier billing integration, and an elegant rich internet application (RIA) or web 2.0 driven interface.

Created products using a diverse set of web and enterprise based technologies including Java, J2EE, XML, XSLT, Xpath, Xquery, Flash, Flex 2.0, AJAX, Lazlo, LAMP, servlets, ActiveX, C++, HTML, XHTML, Fedora, Apache, Tomcat, Windows IIs Server, Oracle DB, Berkeley DB XML, Ipedo XML DB, MYSQL, and Crystal Reports XI

Utilized a diverse set of tools, environments, and digital content including Linux, Windows XP, Accept 360, MS Project, internal wikis and forums, Bugzilla, CVS, BaseCamp, Eclipse, Test Complete, VMWare ESX Server, Apple Fairplay, and Windows Media
