Alan Whitton’s Summary

A technical innovator with business acumen and over 20 years of telecommunications and high tech experience, and a toolbox full of technical skills, has worked in many diverse areas from System Architect to Product Management bringing innovative ideas and maturity to whichever team worked with. A published author in the area of network control protocols, and still technically relevant in both Wireless and Wired networking concepts, especially in the area of TCP/IP, while learning about the business of High Tech.

Alan Whitton’s Specialties:

SIP, IMS, TCP and networking, 802.11 radio networking, UNIX, LINUX, Windows, Network Protocols, CDMA, Voice over IP, Video over IP, Video Services, Video Payload delivery technologies, Transcoding (audio and video), Audio encoding technologies, OAM, Vendor Liason,

    • How these opportunities might fit into the new wireless protocols being sold to the home such as 802.11{a,b,g,i,n} and 802.16 , and how these technologies fit in the home of the future.

    Nortel

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; NT; Telecommunications industry)

Joined the project at it’s inception, helped choose which SS7 signaling server package to use and wrote the signaling interface specification for communication between the Signaling Server and the RAS (using a subset of DSM-CC). A steep learning curve was incurred on this project, as I had no “telephony” experience before this project, and am now the “expert” when it comes to SS7 signaling in the group. Skills honed in this project:

    • Signaling from the Server to the RAS (DSM-CC for now, with studies done on the possible implementations using either Diameter or Q.931).
    • Customer presentation skills (have done many as a technical resource to our sales team).
    • Group management, when manager is either on vacation or too busy to attend to the groups’ needs.
