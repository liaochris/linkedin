Paul Stuckle’s Summary

A partner at Stuckle and Ferguson, Paul Stuckle graduated Magna Cum Laude from St. Edwards University in Austin, Texas with a B.A. in Criminal Justice in May 1980 and went on to study Law at the Texas Tech School of Law in Lubbock, Texas, where he received his Doctor of Jurisprudence in May 1983. In the same year, Paul Stuckle became a licensed attorney in the State of Texas and graduated from the Dallas Police Academy. Today, Paul Stuckle has over 25 years of experience working in the criminal justice system.

After graduation, Paul Stuckle first worked for the Dallas Police Department as a police officer, patrolling the city streets and protecting citizens on the front lines. Paul Stuckle’s resume also includes serving as a Legal Advisor for the Ft. Worth Police Department, where he was in charge of all of the department’s legal training. Stuckle is also a former Assistant District Attorney for the Collin County District Attorney’s Office and Law Professor at Southeastern Paralegal Institute. In the midst of performing his public duty, Stuckle wrote a textbook on search and seizure laws and arrest procedures.

In addition to working as a Texas Criminal Defense Attorney for 20 years and fighting false allegations as a Stuckle and Ferguson Law Partner, Paul Stuckle worked part-time as a Municipal Court Judge, presiding over a variety of cases. Paul Stuckle was the Chief Municipal Court Judge for the City of The Colony, Texas for 13 years and as such heard an average of 2,500 cases every year.

Paul Stuckle is a member of the National Criminal Defense Lawyers Association and the National Child Abuse Defense Resource Center, and also served as the former president of the Collin County Criminal Defense Lawyers Association. Stuckle holds a certificate from the Texas Municipal Courts Training Center, which he has renewed annually since 1993, and is a former training assistant for the Dale Carnegie Course in Effective Speaking and Human Relations.
