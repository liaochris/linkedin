Mark Schannon’s Summary

Mark Schannon is a 25+ year veteran of public relations, having managed over 75 issue, crisis, and litigation communication challenges. He also counsels clients on corporate reputation, community relations, change management, and effective engagement.

A skilled strategist and writer, he counsels organizations to understand how high-stress situations can result in self-defeating behavior. He has conducted extensive research into new scientific theories which challenge traditional assumptions about how people take in and process information, and how they form opinions, values, emotions, and behaviors.

Primarily a problem solver, able to understand and help organizations make management and communications changes that improve their external and internal operations. Has conducted extensive research into new scientific theories which challenge traditional assumptions about how people take in and process information, and how they form opinions, values, emotions, and behaviors—all with the goal of not only explaining why most management and communications programs are haphazardly successful but also how to improve the odds of success.

He has written and spoken extensively about issue and crisis management, risk communication, environmental challenges, litigation communications, survey research, industry restructuring, and other issues.
