Louise Schaper’s Summary

Louise Schaper is a researcher and consultant with a passion for health informatics. Her research and consulting concern people’s responses to new technologies and leveraging technology to support and improve the ‘business’ of healthcare. She understands the value of technology to improving the efficiency, effectiveness, quality and safety of healthcare; and her research and consultancy focuses on these themes.

Louise is a self-proclaimed ‘lobbyist’ for the promotion and use of ICT within healthcare. She is also a registered occupational therapist. She has been invited to form and chair the E-Health International Advisory Group for the World Federation of Occupational Therapy and has been invited by the American Medical Informatics Association to help steer their 20x20 initiative to educate health professionals in America and throughout the world on the use of health informatics. She also writes a regular column called ‘Health-e-OT’ in OT Australia’s publication ‘Connections’.

The use of ‘smart home’ technology, home telehealth and telecare is another of Louise’s interests and she is currently working with a technology vendor and aged care facility to conduct research on the use of this technology. It is hoped that this research will become part of a regional Smart Home Technology Research Centre.

Louise works across industry and academia, always ensuring her work is independent, of a high standard, and applies strong academic rigour to real world issues and challenges.

Louise is Director of Comperio Pty Ltd. Comperio is Latin for ‘in the pursuit of knowledge’ which defines well her intention and passion. She is also the Research Director of CHIK Services, a health informatics research and consultancy company based in New South Wales; is working on NEHTA’s Benefits Realisation Study and is the Chair of the WA branch of the Health Informatics Society of Australia.

Louise Schaper’s Interests:

Louise’s primary area of interest is in the people, organisational and social issues of health informatics. Other areas of interest include:  Leveraging new technologies to support and improve evidence-based practice and the effectiveness, efficiency, quality and safety of healthcare.  IS acceptance, adoption and diffusion and people's responses to new technologies.  The design, uses and consequences of ICT in healthcare, looking at the individual, implementation and organisational contexts in which technology is used.  Evaluation and assessment of healthcare technologies.  ‘Smart’ home technology / home telehealth / telecare (using technology to aid independent living & the provision of healthcare in the home).  Incorporating organisational change management, workflow analysis, task analysis and other human concerns into information technology projects.  Health informatics education and the use of technology in education.
