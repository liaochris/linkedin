Evert Verhagen’s Summary

Mr. Evert Verhagen is a senior project manager working for his company REUSE.
Following the successful development of the Westergasfabriek project he first started his own business, Creative Cities in January 2006 and after that in January 2007 his company REUSE. He now works in different regeneration projects throughout Europe.

He started working for the district of Westerpark as the manager for public works in 1990. He was the project manager responsible for the Westergasfabriek project for more than ten years. This derelict gas factory in the centre of Amsterdam was decontaminated; the grounds were turned into a park, following a plan by the award winning American landscape architect Kathryn Gustafson. The fifteen listed historical buildings are now the home of the Dutch cultural avant-garde. He won the state price for best manager in an urban project in 2004.

He has worked as an assistant to the manager in Amsterdam's notorious high rise area, Bijlmermeer. In 1986 his book on Bijlmermeer was published.

Evert Verhagen today works in different projects, among them Noorderpark in Amsterdam with West 8 landscape architects. He worked on the redevelopment of a former Shell headquarters site (Tolhuistuin) on the city’s northern waterfront.

Evert Verhagen’s Specialties:

REUSE is specialized in complex regeneration projects of former industrial or military sites.
Creative Cities is specialized in the development of the creative economy and the effects of the spectacular growth of this new economy on cities and regions. See NAI publication: Creativity and the City.
