Mark Harrington’s Summary

An accomplished marketer with success in a wide range of disciplines, Mark's unique experience spans industries from banking to ecommerce and companies from start-ups to Fortune 500s. He's also played instrumental roles in landmark acquisitions worth over a half billion dollars.

Mark managed key aspects of the due diligence and integration processes for Ecount’s (Inc. 500) Citi acquisition. As SVP of Marketing he sits on Citi's Global Marketing Council and runs marketing for a portfolio that includes 40+ of the Fortune 100, like Verizon, Disney and GE. He also built and runs Client Quality, called “one of our most important initiatives ever” by the CEO, and spearheaded Ecount’s global rebranding to Citi Prepaid Services.

A Half.com co-founder, he built Retention and Direct Marketing and was the youngest management team member. Acquired by eBay for over $350MM, Half catapulted to profitability due in large part to his vision and execution of the textbook business; now one of eBay's largest categories, which earned him eBay's Out of this World Award. He also helped rename America’s first 'dot com city,’ Half.com, OR.

At Infonautics he co-founded Company Sleuth (PC Magazine Top 100) and Sports Sleuth (Yahoo Top 50), earning him their Innovation Award.

At Addison Wesley he promoted a book list that featured four national top ten titles, earning him their Summit Award.

As a hobby, Mark co-founded FightOnState.com (formerly PSU Playbook), the largest Penn State fan site online, which is now part of Fox Interactive Media.

He's appeared in publications like BtoB, Sales & Marketing Management, Promo and PhillyTech, has guest lectured at Vanderbilt, Cornell, Boston College and Drexel, and advised groups like the BEST Leadership Council, Lift For Life and Penn's Civilians.

A Melbourne Wesley Cummings Scholar, he holds a Marketing BS, Honors with Distinction, from The Pennsylvania State University and was honored with the Dorothy M. Kelly Award.
