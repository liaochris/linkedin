Robert (Bob) Brightwell’s Experience

  • Head of Corporate Communication

    United Biscuits

    (Privately Held; 5001-10,000 employees; Food Production industry)

    December 2004 — Present (4 years 1 month)

    Joined to head up internal communications for the business but in 2007 was promoted to manage all corporate communications for the group

  • Area Internal Communications Manager/Corporate Issues Internal Communications Manager

    Royal Mail

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Logistics and Supply Chain industry)

    March 2003 — December 2004 (1 year 10 months)

    Joined on a contract basis managing internal communications for south east England area and later offered the job permanently. Member of the area senior team and managed communications around national and regional industrial action as well as driving improvement on a range of performance issues.

    Later asked to join the communications central head office and manage internal communications for corporate issues such as pay, pensions, and social responsibility. Overall a hugely enjoyable job in a completely different industry with lots of challenges I had not faced before, but pay very low, hence the subsequent move

  • Senior Internal Communications Manager

    Charles Schwab Europe

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Financial Services industry)

    January 2001 — December 2002 (2 years)

    Managed internal communications through two of the worst years in broking history for this online stockbroker. Plenty of redundancy exercises, restructures and new business plans. Left with sale of the business to Barclays Stockbrokers at the end of 2002.

  • Internal Communications Manager

    Royal Bank of Scotland

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; RBS; Banking industry)

    June 1997 — December 2000 (3 years 7 months)

    Internal Communications Manager initially for the Personal Banking Manager channel but later expanded role to include a wide range of different projects including the launch of the Individual Savings Account (ISA) scheme for the bank, development of interactive sales aids and the establishment of an in house design function.

    The team I was in was the original NatWest National Sales Campaigns internal communication team but after the takeover by Royal Bank of Scotland, much of the responsibility previously enjoyed by the team went North and after a career in the bank spanning 18 years I decided to move on.

    If he ever reads this I would like to thank Peter O'Brien, former head of the National Sales Campaigns team, for giving me my first role in communications. While I enjoyed success earlier in my career in a variety of roles, this was the one in which I found my specialism and went from strength to strength from here.

  • Various culminating in Personal Banking Manager

    NatWest Bank

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Banking industry)

    July 1982 — June 1997 (15 years)

    Joined and worked my way through a variety of roles and completed the professional banking qualification, (degree equivalent.) Spent 6 years in several roles in the corporate banking department and then went on to a junior bank manager role. Spent three years as a succcessful personal banking manager, managing a portfolio of customers in the days when we could use professional judgement and over ride computer recommendations. However changes in the pipeline meant that there would be less scope to use professional judgement and an increasing sales focus. I did not want to become a financial salesperson and so changed direction to move into the communications field.
