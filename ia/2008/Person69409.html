Rich Geruson’s Summary

-----------------------------------------------------Bio----------------------------------------------------

Rich Geruson has held senior executive/general management positions at Nokia, IBM, Toshiba and McKinsey & Company and is currently on the Boards of several technology companies.

Most recently he was CEO of VoiceSignal, the world leader in voice-enabled mobile search and other mobile voice solutions. In less than 4 years after arriving at VoiceSignal, Rich increased shareholder value over 10-fold culminating in the sale of the business at a multiple of more than 12 times revenue. Within a year and a half of Rich’s arrival, VoiceSignal turned profitable. Now with a volume of over 150 million units, VoiceSignal has become the world leader in embedded voice interface solutions for mobile devices.

Prior to VoiceSignal, Rich was the Senior Vice President at Nokia Mobile Phones in the Americas. In 5 years Rich grew the business 10-fold to $6.5 billion and tripled its market share. This growth not only catapulted Nokia to # 1 market share in the US but also, for the first time, the #1 share in the world. Nokia’s brand became the 5th most valuable brand in the world and the # 1 non-US brand in the world.

At IBM, Rich was one of the youngest Vice President/General Managers. He stimulated rapid growth in an otherwise slower growing business by designing, developing and launching IBM’s first distribution channels for networking products worldwide.

As Vice President at Toshiba Americas, Rich doubled market share in mobile PCs from 11% to 22%, taking the business from market share rank of #5 to #1 in less than 4 years.

Rich has been awarded three graduate degrees from Oxford University including a Doctorate. He was also a member of the research faculty at Harvard Business School, where he completed the highly regarded book: A Theory of Market Strategy, published by Oxford University Press.
