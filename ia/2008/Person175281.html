Shiri Dori’s Summary

I'm a young, highly motivated Computer Science developer. I've been participating in excellence programs since I was nine years old, and have always tapped my curiosity and comprehension skills to learn, understand and go beyond, to ask further questions and make suggestions. I'm a quick learner and a sharp observer, and my long-retained memory ensures that I rarely forget something I've learned. I'm often told that I have a very bubbly personality.

I am always happy to help others, and am a serial volunteer, with past experiences in a hospital children's ward, with a blind student, and with a Junior Academic Staff Organization. I am always on the lookout for ways to give back to the community, and one way I do so is teaching.

Here's my vision of a perfect work-environment: first and foremost, in a supportive team. When you work with people that you get along with, your results are immensely better, by bouncing ideas off others, and getting feedback for your work. In the long run, I would also like to have a job which involves me teaching or guiding in some way. It's important for me to find a job that I will be passionate about and interested in. When I feel excited about a project, I tend to be extremely motivated and really do my absolute best. Which, if I may say so myself, is not too shabby.

Right now, my expertise lies with String Matching algorithms, with a year's experience at Answers.com. I'm excited now to consider my options for the future.

And for fun? I'm an all-out bookworm, love puzzles and music, especially Tori Amos - whom I met in person in summer 2007! Sluggy (www.sluggy.com) makes my day. I used to be a huge LEGO fan and am slowly reclaiming my bricks from my parents' house. I'm also crazy about my niece, Carmel, born Feb. 2005.

I speak both Hebrew and English as native tongues.

To receive recommendations from my direct and senior managers in any position, please contact me and I will make the connection between you.
