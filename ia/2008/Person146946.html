Patrick Lynch’s Summary

Patrick Lynch is the Director of Special Technology Projects, Office of the ITS Director, Information Technology Services, at Yale University. In his 35 years with Yale University he has been a medical illustrator, biomedical photographer, audiovisual producer, and a designer of interactive multimedia teaching programs for medicine and biology.

Lynch has won over 30 major professional awards for his medical illustrations, graphic design, software design, and Web design, including the two top Pirelli INTERNETional 2005 Awards (2005 Grand Prize and First Prize in Higher Education 2005), Best-in-Show Award from the Health Sciences Communications Association and a Gold Medal, Silver Medal and Award of Excellence in the international INVISION Multimedia Awards. He has authored over 100 professional papers, magazine articles, and book chapters, and is the co-author and illustrator of four books.

Yale University Press recently published Lynch's fourth book "A Field Guide to North Atlantic Wildlife: Marine Mammals, Seabirds, Fish, and Other Sealife," co-authored with the world-renowned naturalist and birding expert Noble S. Proctor. His award-winning book "Manual of Ornithology," also co-authored with Noble S. Proctor of Southern Connecticut State University, was published by Yale University Press in 1993 and is widely used in college and graduate school ornithology courses. In 2002 Yale University Press published the second edition of his book "Web Style Guide," which he co-authored with Sarah Horton of Dartmouth College. The "Web Style Guide" has sold over 150,000 copies, and has been translated and published in nine international editions.
