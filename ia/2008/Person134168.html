Mikkel Kristiansen’s Summary

I have been riding the .COM rollercoaster and learned from it. Take the time and effort to thoroughly describe what you are going to deliver, and more importantly what you're NOT. Then make sure that you can PROVE that you have delivered what you promised. In IT projects, the variable that is easiest to control (easier than time and costs) is often the "stakeholder expectations".
Remember: one definition of quality is "to meet or exceed expectations".

In leadership: Build trust by TAKING the blame and GIVING the credit.
Most managers practice the opposite.
