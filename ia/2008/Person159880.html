Richard Fine’s Summary

Experience has shown that, in most situations, problems can be resolved and difficulties overcome by identifying and satisfying each participant's common, underlying needs and goals, while recognizing and preserving their ultimate "self interests". If this can be done, the "deal" or "agreement" will be made, the "goal" will be achieved and the "lawsuit" will be avoided.

When this process fails, litigation and other costly misadventures, ensue.

My goal is to assist and guide you and your organization to find that solution. In the process, we use legal precedents, rules and procedures in conjunction with the demands of the "real world" and your "case", while also preparing for, and pursuing litigation in the event that the other party elects to be "unreasonable".

Richard Fine’s Specialties:

The "expertise" of the law practice has developed in numerous areas including, but not limited to, antitrust, appeals, business problems, civil and criminal litigation, environmental problems, governmental abuses and misuse of funds, interntional law, and writs of mandate amongst others. Please see the website at richardfinelaw.com or contact me, to determine if we may be of assistance to you.

Richard Fine’s Experience

    Law Offices of Richard I. Fine & Associates; Consul General-Kingdom of Norway 1995-

    (Law Practice industry)

    October 1974 — Present (33 years 5 months)

    The Law Offices of Richard I. FIne & Associates is unique.It has been retained by governments, multinational and domestic corporations, and individuals to solve a diverse range of public and private problems. It litigates cases in both Federal and State courts. Yet, it has remained small and supremely client-oriented while addressing interests on a local, national and global scale.

    Sovereign nations retained the firm to protect their diplomats. Corporations use the firm to resolve a multitude of legal concerns. Individuals seek the firm's help to protect their rights. Taxpayers, public interest groups and others retain the firm to stop the abuse of governmental power and safeguard constitutional rights.

    The firm has effectively stopped 26 years of budget crises in California, changed the way that public donations are processed in America, forced California to return approximately $300 million to taxpayers, and won millions of dollars for its clients.

  • Attorney

    United States Department of Justice, Antitrust Division

    (Law Practice industry)

    1968 — 1972 (4 years)

    I was in the Foreign Commerce Section of the Antitrust Division in Washington, D.C. I developed, and was involved in the Grand Jury investigation of the international Pulp, Paper and Newsprint industry, which occurred in New York City. I was involved in the investigation of international mergers and joint ventures, and I led the first Justice Department participation in a Tariff Commission Hearing (now International Trade Commission). From 1970-72, I led the team which developed the Grand Jury indictments and civil cases against GM and Ford for price fixing in the sale of fleet cars.

  • London School of Economics and Political Science, U. of London

    Ph.D, Law-International Law, 1964 — 1967

    During my tenure at the LSE, I studied both Public International Law and Private International Law (also known as comparative law), the Law of International and Regional Organizations, International Economic Law, and various specialized seminars, prior to concentrating on my thesis.
    My thesis was entitled International and Comparative Antitrust Law. It reviewed all of the then existing international, regional and domestic antitrust laws and concluded with a draft international antitrust treaty.
    To write the thesis, I was required to travel to Brussels, the headquarters of the EEC Commission, and work directly with the EEC antitrust laws and decisions for two months as no formal translation had been made.
    I also made a trip to the UN in New York to discuss an aritcle I had written about Article 19 of the UN Charter and peacekeeping with the UN General Counsel.
