Bob Aber’s Summary

An accomplished entrepreneur, I have built and successfully exited several start-ups and am currently focused on numerous projects within the young professional and governmental communities. Specific projects/clients include; Identi-fyi, NEO|SO, and several political campaigns in and around greater Cleveland.

My first business was started in Cleveland when I was ten years old and led me to become the first student at The Ohio State University’s Fisher College of Business to intern with his own business. My second venture was the Vesta Group, a mergers and acquisition advisory firm where I was responsible for business development, developing and executing client campaigns, and identifying Vesta’s future growth avenues. This entrepreneurial experience at such a young age has afforded me with a great deal of experience in organizational/resource management, growth strategies, and in developing vision plans for businesses.

I am active within the civic arena in Cleveland and am a strong proponent of growing and cultivating the young professional demographic. I chaired COSE’s 2006 Issues Conference and am a member of The City Club of Cleveland. I am the founder and president of The Young Buckeyes of Greater Cleveland, and am the former director of recruitment for The Cleveland Professional 20/30 Club. I have served as president of the Central Ohio chapter of the Ohio Business Brokers Association and was the youngest International Business Broker Association member in the world to receive the Certified Business Intermediary designation.

I hold a degree in Political Science from The Ohio State University and reside in the City of Cleveland.

Bob Aber’s Specialties:

Young Professional recruitment/retention, marketing to government entities, government relations, and political (candidate and issue) campaign managment.
