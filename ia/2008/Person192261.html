Tracy Goold’s Summary

I have shown leadership in individual accomplishments, in working with teams, and in working with other individuals. I was intimately involved in the research, selection and installation of our current EDI software, Gentran Integration Suite (GIS). For the launch, I had the sole responsibility of creating all maps. I was part of a small team to design the company’s new EDI infrastructure and processes in a manner that would meet current needs and still be scalable and flexible to be able to address future needs. I and one other person were solely responsible for researching and configuring Oracle’s eCommerce Gateway, including Oracle’s EDI-related processes, tables and files, despite the lack of documentation or support available. I have been heavily involved in the initial creation of all GIS business processes, in revamping them to better serve the company’s needs, and now I am rewriting them address the company’s changing and growing requirements. I have documented every aspect of the company’s EDI program, from flowcharts illustrating how data flows in and out of the company to databases tracking the number of trading partners and which EDI transactions we do with each. I proposed the current EDI support model of an intermediary tier of support in order to best serve our internal and external customers. I continually reevaluate our processes, research different or better ways of doing things, recommend solutions, and implement the improvements. I am an active member of several EDI, GIS and eCommerce groups and support communities where I can keep abreast of new and emerging technologies, best practices and other methods of ensuring our processes run at peak performance. I have worked very diligently to build collaborative relationships with internal and external customers as well as fellow teammates, and to that end I have become a trusted primary contact when they have questions, concerns or problems.
