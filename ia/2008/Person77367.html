Ho Law’s Summary

I am an international practitioner in psychology, coaching, mentoring and psychotherapy. I have had over 20 years experience in providing advice and consultancy for both public and private sectors. Highly energetic, creative and analytical, I have strategic vision to see complex issues as a whole and pays attention to detail to ensure successful implementation of business processes. I was one of the first equality advisors to the Assistant Permanent Under Secretary of State in the Home Office, and the Deputy chair of the BPS Standing Committee for Promotion of Equal Opportunities. I am the founding Editor of The Cutting Edge journal and a Consulting Editor of The Coaching Psychologist. I have published over 40 papers and delivered over 100 workshops and conference seminars in the UK and abroad including Australia, Barcelona, Brussels, Christmas Island, Hong Kong, Paris, Stockholm, and Zurich. I was the former Head of the Professional Forum for the Association for Coaching, and was selected as one of the top 20 European coaches to participate in the 2005 international coaching programme at Rice University, in Houston.

Ho Law’s Interests:

I provide coaching, mentoring and supervision for diverse communities. I am better known for my interest in arts and culture. For example, the 0,1 Space & Identity project successfully attracted £35K funding from the Arts Council England, Momentum arts in 2003. It explored the historical process of migration in Chinese and Jamaican communities. I did workshops for local schools and was the artist in residency for Peterborough Digital Art in 2004. The workshop at John Mansfield’s School was featured on TV (the BBC 6 o’clock News Look East). I delivered a coaching programme for the diverse communities in Peterborough (e.g. the Positive Image project). My comments/writings have been featured in ITV News, magazines and newspapers including BBC Magazine, Bliss, Daily Telegraph, Evening Telegraph, New Woman, Readers Digest & The Sunday Magazine. I've written a book: ‘Psychology of Coaching, Mentoring & Learning’, published by Wiley: http://eu.wiley.com/remtitle.cgi?isbn=0470028440
