Tim Walsh’s Summary

GAMES: In the early 1990s I co-invented TriBond and Blurt!, board games that have collectively sold over 4 million copies in thirteen countries. I actively invent new games and consult for a select group inventors, some of whom I've represented as their agent to toy companies.

BOOKS: I have written two books, Timeless Toys (Andrews-McMeel) and WHAM-O Super-Book (Chronicle Books).

FILMS: I am Executive Producer on several documentary films from director, Ken Sons.

NETWORKS: I am a partner in Think Networks which builds social online networks for non-profit organizations.

PEANUT BUTTER: I like peanut butter.

Tim Walsh’s Specialties:

Never be bored by imagining creative, fun ideas and molding them into sources of entertainment which enrich the lives of the people who use them.

  • Owner

    Keys Publishing Company Inc.

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    1994 — Present (14 years)

    In 2004 I self-published a book entitled The Playmakers, a pop-culture history of toys. Shortly thereafter, I licensed the book to publishing giant Andrews McMeel-Universal, who released it as Timeless Toys. I just finished writing a new, retrospective book on WHAM-O in celebration of their 60th anniversary. Chronicle Books will publish the tentatively titled WHAM-O Super Book in 2008.

    Also in 2008 comes a feature-length documentary film on toy inventors for which I am a creative consultant.

    I'm a frequent speaker on toys, and have appeared on The Early Show on CBS, NPR’s All Things Considered, Fox’s After Breakfast, The History Channel’s Our Generation, HGTV’s Games We Grew Up With, and CNBC’s The Big Idea with Donny Deutsch among others. I have moderated industry panels for the Chicago Toy and Game Fair and lectured at Toy Fair as a play entrepreneur. Also I bowl.

  • Vice President

    TriBond Enterprises

    (Entertainment industry)

    1990 — Present (18 years)

    I am one of the co-inventors of the TriBond game. I have successfully promoted the game by personally garnering media placement and publicity for it on Good Morning America, on NPR's All Things Considered, and in the New York Times among other media outlets.

    I have written a vast majority of the game's content over the 18 years it has been on the market and I have been a key negotiator in the execution of licensing deals for the game with Mattel, Sony Worldwide Networks, Game Show Network, Great American Puzzle, Time Factory, Aristoplay/Talicor, Brio and others.
