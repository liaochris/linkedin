Amit Gadhia’s Summary

As Shakespeare says...

"All the world's a stage,
And all the men and women merely players:
They have their exits and their entrances;
And one man in his time plays many parts"

While circling life on this stage,
Below is a summary of my parts, my exits and entrances:

Started my career at Merrill Lynch, Atlanta, GA. I was selling term loans and lines of credit to farmers, home builders & food processing plants. Within 2 weeks on the job, I sold more than what my boss sold in 2 months. Soon I find myself wrapped around an irresistible offer from one of Merrill's clients, an investment banking M&A boutique. I was now working in the Mergers & Acquisition industry, consulting with SMEs on exit strategies.

Summer of 2003, California was calling...: I pack my bags and started driving west coast. Shortly, I picked up a project with Inventors Publishing and Research powering their sales team with all thats Market Research. Soon, I was selling pens, pencils n paperclips for Staples: a $13.2 billion dollar company, fortune 165 and also the largest office supply distributor in the world.

In late 2004, the entrepreneurial bug bit me & I started my first company at 27 - export & distribution of plant-based pharmaceutical products. After 2 short years, I sold it to my partner in 2006. Shortly, I find myself working on a project in the web2.0 software development and technology consulting industry. Fell in love with this industry, never looked back and decided to start my own company.

Thus, www.Artyllect.com is born.
Even today, there is an acute shortage of great software developers. Why is great/genius software engineering so rare? Why is it so difficult to find great engineers, engineers who can just communicate? I decided to stop asking why and start answering how. The DNA of Artyllect speaks for itself i.e. a mind of the artistic and intellect. We're a bunch of geeky & passionate software engineers.
