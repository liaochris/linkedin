Jonathan Denholtz’s Experience

  • President & CEO

    MakingThings

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Computer Software industry)

    January 2004 — February 2005 (1 year 2 months)

    Transformed this leading-edge design and technology firm from an engineering-driven startup with minimal infrastructure and strategy into a profitable and self-sustaining operation specializing in embedded applications. Developed a business plan, redefined target markets, re-priced and repositioned product lines, reduced cost of goods by more than 20%, and prioritized new product development efforts. Increased revenue streams by developing a consulting service that leveraged existing assets and engineering capacity.

  • VP, Strategy & Business Development

    Teachscape

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; E-Learning industry)

    March 2003 — December 2004 (1 year 10 months)

    Managed strategy and business development for Teachscape, a professional development company that specializes in the creation and delivery of interactive, web-based courses for K-12 teachers. Partnered with West Coast VP of Sales to generate 50% of company revenue in 2003. Negotiated multi-year service agreements with numerous superintendents and school boards. Initiated and led the effort to open up the post-secondary market by developing a new product offering and by securing the company’s first university customer.

  • Founder & President

    Real Time Learning

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; E-Learning industry)

    January 1998 — June 2001 (3 years 6 months)

    Pioneered online tutoring with this educational services company that delivered high-quality academic assistance over the Internet to K-12 students. Built revenue-producing organization based in three cities with an internal staff of 20, a crew of consultants and an active database of more than 1000 accredited tutors. Designed and developed a critically acclaimed “virtual classroom” technology. Secured contracts with major educational institutions, including public libraries, foundations and government agencies. Negotiated sale of company to Tutor.com in 2001.

  • Executive Producer

    Founding member of this leading consumer and educational software company, best known for producing and publishing Sesame Street, Madeline, and School House Rock titles, along with a variety of ABC News-branded reference titles. Creative Wonders was acquired by The Learning Company in 1998.
