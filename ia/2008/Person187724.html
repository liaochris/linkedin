Dylan Mullins’s Summary

American-born Flash® developer, graphic designer and type design habitué,

During my education, I engaged in practical world theories on design strategy for both modern and traditionally-minded clients. The program enhanced the skills I already possessed and challenged me to develop my own conceptual philosophy on design, and to understand how I am responsible for it.

Dylan Mullins’s Specialties:

Adobe Creative Suite, including Illustrator, Photoshop, and InDesign, as well as developed knowledge in Flash, and After Effects. Other software knowledge: Fontographer, Cinema 4D, HTML / DHTML / XHTML, basic Javascript, ActionScript 1.0, 2.0 and some 3.0, entry-level AJAX integrations, CSS.

Dylan Mullins’s Experience

  • Technical Designer and Media Producer

    VerticalResponse, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    March 2007 — Present (1 year 6 months)

    At VerticalResponse, I work in the Marketing Communications division to assist in producing the visual flavor of most of our public-facing brand communications.

I also assist in creating print deliverables to serve our co-brands and partner product needs, as well as fulfilling internal design needs on our Self-Service Direct Marketing product (the #1 downloaded partner product application on Salesforce.com!).

I am surrounded by a team of insanely-talented people, and because of that, I learn new things every day -- be that about AJAX, CSS, web standardizations, JavaScript, ActionScripting or marketing in general.

  • Art Director

    Girard Creative / Ted's Creative Co.

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Graphic Design industry)

    August 2006 — January 2007 (6 months)

    I was the secondary Art and Creative Director at Girard Creative in the Hayes Valley district of San Francisco. A three-person creative house, Girard Creative was where I first got my taste of the workplace in San Francisco.

    I worked extensively on print campaigns for the Santa Ynez Band of Chumash Indians, including daily operations on their two monthly publications -- Chumash! Magazine, and Haku Magazine, distributely directly for their partner operation, the Chumash Casino, in Santa Ynez, California. Further, I worked on marketing deliverables for Girard Creative's sister company, Ted's Creative Co., a creative marketing solutions firm geared towards branding marketing research and consulting deliverables. I also completely re-branded the company under the new moniker, and developed a fresh satellite site for the company, including a fully-interactive Flash® website to launch the new brand's release.

  • Proprietor and Creative Director

    Slow Education Magazine

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Publishing industry)

    August 2004 — September 2005 (1 year 2 months)

    I ventured into self-funded publishing with Nick Martin of MachineCurl.com, in order to more serve the failing publishing industry in Richmond, Virginia. After the collapse of Punchline! Magazine, a four-year running journal on southern Virginia street-level culture, there was a loud cry cast from people of Richmond for a fresh, new, cultural journal to emerge. Nick and I stood up and answered the call in an attempt to re-enliven the then-defunct niche.

    Slow Education Magazine eventually became a single-issue full-color release as a monthly arts/ cultural / music / local digest for the middle East Coast. We carried the brand from December 2004 until September 2005 as a monthly arts show at Alley Katz Nightclub, which helped to shine the light on many previously unheard of artists and musical acts. From the support and interest given by the target circles in Richmond, Slow Education developed into a welcomed centrifuge of energy during a time of unsureness.

  • Video Editor / Motion Designer

    Channel 3 / Adelphia

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Media Production industry)

    May 1998 — August 2000 (2 years 4 months)

    At Adelphia Communications, I started off as a Junior Video Editor, working on turn-key editing systems like the Avid Media One, the Casablanca, and discreet* / ReelTime® non-linear editors. I eventually grew into an entry-level position as a motion compositor for on-screen graphics produced in LightWave 3D, and Alladin Systems' SpeedRazor, and which aired live on our local cable networks, Channel 2 + Channel 3 / LoudounTV.

    My other roles at CableVision / Adelphia included synching and editing voice-overs for commercials produced in-house, screenwriting for commercials concepts, and pitching those concepts to our team of writers and producers. Working for Adelphia got me seriously involved and professionally-minded about creating rich media for the corporate world.
