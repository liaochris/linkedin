Sam Diaz’s Summary

After almost 20 years in the newspaper industry, I've moved over to "the dark side" of PR. But this isn't PR where I pitch my old colleagues about the latest up-and-coming company. This is PR in a Web 2.0 world, where content creation oin the blogosphere, on YouTube and inside the social media world of Facebook are just as important as the news story in traditional media. Now, I am bringing my newspaper experience to the world of tech startups in Silicon Valley.

Before that, I spent more than two years as an editor/reporter/blogger/podcaster at The Washington Post. Prior to that, I worked as a technology reporter at the San Jose Mercury News. Joining the team in early 2000, I covered trends, companies and products out of Silicon Valley's tech industry. I started my career at The Fresno Bee in 1990.

I am also the past Financial Officer of the National Association of Hispanic Journalists, unable to finish my second term because I left journalism for PR (it's in the bylaws!) I previously served as San Jose Chapter President for the California Chicano News Media Association. I was also the director of Mosaic, the San Jose Urban Journalism Workshop for Minority High School Student, from 2002-2004.

Sam Diaz’s Specialties:

I specialize in consumer technology - from the products to the trends to the business strategies of the companies in this space.

    The Fresno Bee

    (Public Company; Newspapers industry)

    January 1990 — April 1997 (7 years 4 months)
