Douglas Li, CSCS’s Summary

I began my career in health and fitness in college as a physical therapist's aid in 2001. Later that year, I was accepted into the Athletic Training Program at San Diego State University. Serving as a student athletic trainer for almost two years, I was able to acquire the basic skill sets that are necessary when assessing mechanisms of injury, injury prevention, rehabilitation, and charting. After working in the medical aspect of health and fitness, I decided to delve into the personal training side.

After earning my first certification in 2003, I worked at Gold's Gym in San Diego for 6 months. I then decided to focus on academia and achieved the Dean's List in 2004. I earned the title Certified Strength and Conditioning Specialist (NSCA-CSCS) in 2005 and graduated from SDSU with a degree in Kinesiology that June.

I then relocated to Chicago, where I worked in commercial fitness as a personal trainer at Gold Coast Multiplex. After eight months and experiencing a Chicago winter, I moved back to the Bay Area.

After a month of being back in the Bay Area, unemployed, I took a position at Lockheed Martin with Health Fitness Corporation. Shortly thereafter, I was informed that there was an opening available with Club One. I joined Pacific Shores Club in May of 2006 and have been there since.

Club Management Concepts took over in November of '06 and I decided to stay at Pacific Shores Club. Since then, I have learned many of the day to day operational tasks, exceled at generating revenue via personal training and specialty series courses, taught group exercise classes, and have continued to further my passion in linking community with fitness by joining Team in Training's IronTeam.

In the future, I look to establish my experience and expertise as a value added asset in the corporate fitness field in the Bay Area. Furthermore, I wish to be able to connect with those in the community; utilizing fitness as a catalyst for wellness.
