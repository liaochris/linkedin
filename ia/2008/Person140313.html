Nicholas Gledhill’s Summary

Nicholas Gledhill has worked extensively as an actor in Australia and the UK for more than 25 years. His career began at the age of 6 when he played the central role (PS) in "Careful He Might Hear You", for which he was nominated Best Actor in 1984. Since then his television and film roles have included apearences in "Bodyline", "A Country Practice", "GP", "Dirtwater Dynasty" (See CV) - amongst others. Nicholas studied at the Birmingham School of Speech and Drama for three years (See Details) and, after working in the UK for some time, returned home to Australia to play John Darling in the Jim Henson's Creature Shop production of "PAN". He most recently played Edgar and Sabastian in the Shakespeare Globe Centre Australia's production of King Lear and The Tempest and Rosencrantz in Hamlet. Nicholas has also worked as a Stage Combat Master and Choreographer, an acting teacher and is a prize winning writer (ICI/STC Young Writers Competition).
