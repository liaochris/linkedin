Brian Cavanaugh’s Summary

Brian Cavanaugh was born in Portland, Oregon in 1969. He holds a Bachelor of Architecture degree from the University of Oregon where he received a one-year fellowship to study architecture and urban design at the Mackintosh School of Architecture in Glasgow, Scotland. He received a Master of Architecture degree from Harvard University’s Graduate School of Design. He is a licensed architect in the California and Oregon, a member of the American Institute of Architects, and is NCARB certified.

Upon graduation from the University of Oregon, Mr. Cavanaugh joined the Seattle firm of Arai/Jackson Architects & Planners where he served as a Project Designer and Manager on a number of civic and transportation projects. Before moving to Los Angeles, he worked with the Boston firm of Machado & Silvetti Associates as a Project Designer on the north campus of the J. Paul Getty Villa and Museum in Malibu, California.

Prior to returning to Arai/Jackson as a Principal in 2007, Mr. Cavanaugh was a Senior Associate at Michael Maltzan Architecture in Los Angeles, California for five years. There he oversaw the design and development of some of the firm’s most significant cultural projects, including MoMA QNS in Long Island City, New York; the Sonoma County Museum in Santa Rosa, California; the programming and master planning for the major expansion of the Vancouver Art Gallery in Vancouver, Canada.

The work Mr. Cavanaugh has been associated with has won numerous design awards – including local and state design awards from the American Institute of Architects - and has been published throughout the world and has been exhibited at such places as the National Building Museum in Washington, D.C.

In addition to his professional activities, he has taught at Victoria University of Wellington, the University of Southern California, the Boston Architectural Center, Woodbury University, and the Otis College of Art & Design.
