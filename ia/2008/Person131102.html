Michelle Harper’s Summary

Proven skills in turning around large scale publishing projects and in developing award-winning print and digital products. Exceptional customer focus.

Passion for the process of search and discovery has been a constant theme in my life--as a journalist, as an academic, and finally, as an information industry professional.

I began my professional career as a journalist for a regional newspaper in Virginia, focusing on investigative reporting and feature writing. A love for the study and teaching of history and literature led to the pursuit of an academic career at the University of Michigan, where I taught in the Great Books Program for 4 years. My interests eventually shifted to academic publishing, and I became the editor of The Journal of the International Institute. I have also written several historical pieces for K-12 reference publishers, including Grolier (now Scholastic) and Marshall Cavendish.

Since graduate school, I have been interested in the marriage of technology and history, and particularly in how technology can help people rediscover the past in new ways. This interest has allowed for several opportunities to speak and serve on panels relating to digital scholarship in the humanities.

I have equal passion for working with development teams to build new or improve existing products that facilitate discovery and learning. More than anything I have to be challenged by learning something new--with the opportunity to make a real difference to the world of research and scholarship through technical innovation. For the last several years, I have pursued opportunities in library publishing, most recently with OCLC NetLibrary, in the land of sunshine and mountains.
