Josh Sklar’s Summary

I've been a user, designer and developer of applications/systems that require modems since 1981* -- over 25 years on the Internet. Phew. So, as it turns out, my passion for the digital medium extends far beyond a simple desire for a career in the "new media" field. It's something I've always believed in as the next great frontier, and used to go on about to people perhaps a bit too much back then. Especially since they rarely knew what I was talking about. Imagine how pleasantly surprised I was when things started taking off in the mid-90s, a little ahead of schedule. It meant my work as a commercial graphic designer, copywriter and creative director could be leveraged and merged with my skills as a usability specialist, information architect and code monkey.

I ended up as one of the founding employees/leaders of a small group within the Cordiant Communications network that built up the very first and consistently #1 ranked online/digital ad agency in Asia (revenue, blue-chip clients, consistent wins of pitches, awards, reputation, etc). I believe that my experiences there as a business leader (Executive Creative Director, Regional CEO, global Chief Creative Officer), award-winning innovator, manager, and the like along with working in a number of other operations in Israel, Indonesia, Singapore, New York City and Austin, Texas over the past 22 years make me someone who knows what it takes to succeed in a field that unfortunately not many really "get" even yet. It was a good hobby back then and it's a fantastically exciting one now.

Coolest moment:
Neil French saying to me, as he awarded me the gold medal for the Nokia Asia Pacific site, "That's the best f@#%!ing Web site I've ever seen."

*(I was one of the featured subjects in "The BBS Documentary" -- a multi-disc DVD series about the creators and operators of the pre-Web Bulletin Board Systems of the late 70s-early 90s by filmmaker Jason Scott.)
