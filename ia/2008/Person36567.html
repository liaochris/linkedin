Christine Nelson’s Summary

If I were to summarize my experience thus far, I would have to say it's been "eclectic," and only in the best sense of the word. I was on the 10-year plan for finishing my college degree, alternately raising children full-time and bringing home the bacon (depending upon who or what needed the attention more at the time!). I've worked full-time and part-time, made lots of money and worked for peanuts, loved some jobs and disliked others, but all were approached with a sense of appreciation for what I could learn and become through the experience.

These experiences have helped to create in me a passion for helping other adult learners realize their potential and to realize just how important the process of learning is, not just the learning itself. I have experience working with adult learning in different settings - higher education, community education, and corporate training - and here's one thing I know for sure . . . our communities, families, and companies are enriched by investing in educating adults and encouraging learning through the "third age."

My goal is to stay involved in adult education in some capacity, advocate the use of technology in educating adults, and helping others achieve their educational (or training) goals. Regardless of the field in which I'm working, my commitment to improving an organization's service culture will stay constant.
