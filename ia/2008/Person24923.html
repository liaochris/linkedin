Bob Lenthart’s Summary

Bob's email address is derekhart59 [at] juno {dot} com

If you think we should be connected, then you're probably right.

Bob (Derek Hart) is an accomplished author and publisher with over twenty-seven (27) written and seventeen (17) published books and has been writing/publishing since 1979. As founder and CEO, Bob is an active employee pursuing his passion for writing, while leading this company from the developmental stage through to its revenue production.

Bob has more than 20 years experience in corporate training program design, training manuals, deployment and management leadership training, succession planning, corporate university development, technical and soft-skills training, employee development and communication, cross-functional buy-in, project management, and working within all levels of management. He is quick to locate and develop quality team members, fully aware he can't do it all himself. Bob credits many people for his success and is adding them to his team.

Derek Hart Ventures is looking for at least two more partners, those unique individuals who can recognize a lucrative and exciting opportunity when she or he sees it. However, this isn’t about writing a check and sitting back to collect your earnings. No, the people we’re seeking must be active players.

Anyone seriously considering evaluating the potential of Derek Hart Ventures, LLC should contact me and we’ll start the discussions. Our CFO will be happy to share our business plan, successful projects completed so far, and our creative assets. Financials are strictly confidential, but if we find the right potential candidate, then we will share that information as well, before you join our incredible team of writers, graphic novel and comic book artists, cover artists and illustrators, computer/video game designers, musicians, screenwriters, action figure modelers and a long list of other creative power.

Bob Lenthart’s Education

  • Roosevelt University

    1978 — 1980

    When I finally settled down to get an education, Roosevelt in Chicago was an excellent choice. In the heart of the city, it was fun just going to class. The teachers were superb and I learned more there, than any other formal education up to that point. My fellow students were as diverse as the city itself and as tolerant of differences as humanly possible. I strongly recommend this school for anyone who wants both a quality education and classmates who are real people.

  • University of Utah

    English - Creative Writing 1976 — 1978

    The University of Utah was a fantastic experience, but more from the people I met, than the formal education I was supposed to get. After all, it was Salt Lake City and so close to great skiing, incredible backpacking, and awesome pizza. I saw David Bowie live, met one of my best friends - Dan Hubbs, and wrote my first novel -Tracks of the Predator. It was my first taste of environmental activism, a Cheyenne warrior with AIM and other wildly unsocial behavior. It was grand!

  • Oakton Community College

    1973 — 1974

    Oakton was a great place to go to college, while I was trying to decide if I wanted to go to college. Rinda West, my Freshman English professor, was a fantastic teacher and she deserves the lion's share of credit for setting me on the journey of an author. I went to Oakton for one year, did very well, but then went off in search of other adventures. It would be a few years before I went back to college, but my memories of Oakton will always be very positive!

  • New Trier East High School

    1970 — 1973
