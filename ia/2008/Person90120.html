Jessica Castillo’s Summary

Jessica is a graduate from California State University, Sacramento with a BA in English Literature. Shortly after college, Jessica quickly started a career in the legal industry as receptionist at Lieff, Cabraser, Heimann, & Bernstein and within weeks was rapidly promoted to Case Clerk, Paralegal, then eventually became the Lead Intake Paralegal on the Vioxx case. After three years, Jessica decided to change gears and consider law school. While preparing for the LSAT, she was approached by a local recruiter with the opportunity to work as a Recruit Coordinator. This combined experience coupled with her established and extensive network, opened up doors to endless possibilities within the legal industry including, exposure to the non-profit legal sector, litigation support technology, and legal sales.

Jessica was later offered a position at Hudson, an international agency, within their Legal Contingent Recruitment Division as Director of Professional Legal Placement, San Francsico. With Hudson’s resources and specialized training Jessica refined her skills, expanded her network and broadened her knowledgebase. The invaluable experience gained at Hudson enabled her to maintain a proven track record of successful placements, with an emphasis on Paralegals and Litigation Support Technology.

Today, Jessica runs City Legal Recruiting, a San Francisco based permanent placement agency. She is an active member of the legal community serving on the National Board of Women in eDiscovery (WiE) as the National Charity Coordinator, team captain of the WiE-Walks Breast Cancer team participating in the Susan G. Komen’s 3 day walkathon, is a supported recruiter and panelist of San Francisco State University’s Paralegal Program, a member of the Bay Area Litigation Support Professionals (BALSP), as well as the San Francisco Paralegal Association (SFPA). Jessica also volunteers time to local organzitions as an English Literature tutor.
