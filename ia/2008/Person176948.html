Steve Kirchoff’s Summary

Steve has emerged as a technology market innovator, after building three successful software businesses in the emerging email, office automation and web-base enterprise management markets. As a business unit VP, Steve led the email and office automation businesses to become #1 market share leaders for 10 years running. Under his leadership, the business grew from $180k to almost $1B in annual revenue. Also, he was Managing Director of a $50M emerging technology venture fund which produced several successful new software and services ventures. As VP of Alliances, he ran a $100M highly successful business development program which rolled out NT and Exchange with Microsoft.

Prior to becoming VP Strategic Marketing for Compaq, Steve ran the software business unit that built the innovative Compaq Insight Management. As VP of enterprise computing group, Steve championed growth through both organic and strategic initiatives. While in this role, two major companies were acquired, and he led the post acquisition alignment and integration. In addition, he helped launch the “Services for Profit” offering, which resulting in over $7B a year in new profitable revenue. During Steve’s time as VP the Enterprise business grew from $2B to $16B. After the dot.com demise, he was called upon to clean up a $16B portfolio of technology investments. This resulted in substantial recover of cash for Compaq and investments that were better positioned to succeed.

Recently, Steve has been the President of Fujitsu Consulting. He has led three major initiatives. One as head of strategy and corporate development, where 11 companies were bought and integrated. Additionally, he headed up FC US Sales where sales went from $161M to a $1B run rate. Last, he was the President - GM of the US Territory and CEO Indian Offshore delivery unit which grew from $125M to $400M. All together, Steve has been involved in 11 successful acquisitions accounting for $13B in M&A value.
