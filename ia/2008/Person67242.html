Brian Galvin’s Summary

After a second, thirteen-year career as a technology executive, I have hung out my shingle as a patent law practitioner (I am a registered US Patent Agent), and I enjoy helping my clients develop and protect strong families of patents. I love technology, I love the law (and focus on keeping close to the rapid changes in patent law, which is crucial for ensuring sound patent prosecution), and I love helping businesses grow in multiple technology areas.

Bio:
Following 13 years as a nuclear engineer in the US Navy, I joined AMD for a few months in microprocessor marketing. Shortly afterward I joined Genesys as Director of Operations, one of the early employees during the company's initial growth. Following our successful IPO and growth during the late 1990's, I left Genesys to join SAIC as VP, Enterprise Architecture. Shortly afterward, I founded a startup (CommPlexus Corporation) which focused on Internet data mining.

Later I developed a consulting practice serving large companies first as a systems integrator and later as a business consultant focusing on Customer Service Chain Management. My consulting practice ended in 2004 when I rejoined Genesys, first as VP of Product Management and then as CTO.

I left Genesys to join a small patent firm, but returned to high tech as a CTO in Nuance Communications, Inc. (NASDAQ: NUAN). However, I subsequently found that the entrepreneurial urge is too persistent for me to stay in a large company. So, after one career in the Navy and a second in the software business, I have settled on the law, specifically patent law, as my long-term third act.

Brian Galvin’s Specialties:

Patent prosecution and portfolio management, strategic consulting on innovation, invention and the law, and helping tech companies with technology strategy.

Inventor of six issued and about 25 pending US patents.
