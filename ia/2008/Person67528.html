Garrin Hirschhorn’s Summary

With a background of over twelve years in marketing communications, Garrin would describe himself as experienced, customer-focused, resourceful and a problem-solver.

He has held marketing positions at companies and organizations such as Nextel Communications, Atlantic Records, Cadmus Interactive and IMAGE Film and Video. He is currently employed as Internet Marketing Manager with Logility, a NASDAQ-listed B2B technology company that specializes in supply chain management software.

Garrin launched his marketing career as a College Marketing/A&R Representative with Atlantic Records. He was one of 18 college marketing representatives selected out of 450 applicants across the USA. As a college representative he promoted emerging recording artists such as Stone Temple Pilots, Tori Amos, Collective Soul and Juliana Hatfield. While still in college, Garrin received a finder's fee for discovering the unsigned recording artist Mary Karlzen, who was later signed to Atlantic Records. Karlzen received airplay on Vh1, CMT and coverage in People and Billboard magazine.

Following his internship with Atlantic, Garrin moved to New York City and was hired by Atlantic Records as a Promotions Manager. In this role, he developed and executed grassroots publicity stunts, and utilized low-cost street marketing techniques to promote recording artists such as Everything But The Girl, Edwin McCain and Collective Soul.

As Marketing Manager at Nextel, he was the winner of Nextel's highest non-sales honor, the Circle of Excellence Award, which was presented by Nextel CEO, Tim Donahue. Garrin received this award following a co-worker's nomination, an endorsement by his supervisor and a committee review by Nextel's Southeast Area management.

More recently, Garrin handled marketing for IMAGE Film and Video Center, an Atlanta-based 501c3 not-for-profit organization that produces the Atlanta Film Festival, Out On Film and supports Atlanta's filmmaking community.
