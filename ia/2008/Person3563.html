Richard Laurence Baron’s Summary

“Industrial-strength marketer.” “Writer in a drum.” B2B and technical marketing communications have been specialties of mine since computers were the size of moving vans…and have kept it up into the era of websites, blogging and e-mail marketing. I position clients for better marketing, conceive and implement national and international communications programs and invent brand names.

I continue to receive creative awards from the American Advertising Federation, the Business Marketing Association, the One Show, and the American Business Press (to name a few).

I am an advertising man with a long and successful record in marketing communications. I execute concepts and copywriting for clients in virtually every business segment (as varied as healthcare, airborne electronic warfare systems, plant automation, SaaS, coal gasification infrastructure, oilfield technologies, and computer chip substrates).

My focus is on amiable working relationships.

Richard Laurence Baron’s Specialties:

Copywriting and creative direction for a wide variety of media (websites, ads, brochures, direct mail, e-marketing); brand-name development and branding strategies; positioning and message development. My Web site: www.signalwrite.com

Richard Laurence Baron’s Experience

  • Principal

    Signalwrite Marketing Communications (Sole Proprietorship)

    (Sole Proprietorship; Myself Only; RLB; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    August 2004 — Present (3 years 8 months)

    After 30+ years with advertising agencies, and 20 as Senior Vice President and Creative Director of a national business-to-business marketing communications firm, I resigned at the end of July, 2004, and went freelance. It is a "new-model life" for me. I work extensively in the energy, software, healthcare and engineering sectors.

    For business and technical corporations outside markets like New York or Chicago, I feel the ad-agency paradigm has become uneconomic as well as unwieldy. Business moves too fast today.

    Direct relationships work faster, and focus more intensely on solving the client's problems (rather than solving the ad agency's problems).

    I make my clients' working lives easier, often satisfying the needs of their internal clients as well as their own.

    In my market sectors (after 30 years in b-to-b), my learning curves aren't curves, they're flat lines.
