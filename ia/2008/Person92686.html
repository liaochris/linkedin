Jean-Marc Jot’s Summary

Experienced research scientist and R&D group manager in digital audio signal processing, with a proven track record in technology innovation and technology transfer, IP and product development leadership, hiring and coaching highly skilled engineers and scientists. Expert in digital audio effects, spatial audio and audio processing architecture for consumer electronics, computer music and gaming.

Jean-Marc Jot’s Specialties:

Digital audio signal processing. Spatial audio, room acoustics, psychoacoustics. Interactive audio technology and standards. Digital audio effects, audio playback enhancement. Artificial reverberation. 3-D audio. Multi-channel audio coding. Audio system architecture.
Group management, project management. Product quality assessment, critical listening. Technology scouting. IP protection and assessment.

Jean-Marc Jot’s Experience

  • Audio Research Fellow

    Creative Labs

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; CREAF; Computer Hardware industry)

    2006 — Present (2 years)

    Supervised a team of 7 PhD- and MSc-level scientists and engineers at Creative Advanced Technology Center to develop the next generation of Creative's audio playback enhancement technology, from invention to delivery of reference specification and implementation in C/C++. Supervised X-Fi technology transfer and certification activities for both PC sound cards and embedded/mobile audio products, including product engineering support and development of test specifications, sales demonstrations and technology evangelism. Developed synergistic relationships with marketing, engineering and quality assessment groups in the US, Europe and Asia. Invented new methods for multi-channel audio analysis and coding, upmix and format conversion, headphone and loudspeaker 3D audio.

  • Lead Audio Processing Architect

    Creative Labs

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; CREAF; Computer Hardware industry)

    2001 — Present (7 years)

    Responsible for the specification and development of audio signal processing architectures and digital audio effects for Creative's SoundBlaster Audigy and X-Fi sound card product lines under Windows XP/Vista (PCI, USB, HD Audio). Specified digital audio signal processing capabilities of proprietary audio DSP chips. Defined signal processing features of sound cards and embedded/mobile audio platforms. Advised in user interface specification.

  • 3-D Audio R&D Manager

    Creative Labs

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; CREAF; Computer Hardware industry)

    1998 — 2005 (7 years)

    Built and managed a team of 5 highly skilled audio DSP engineers at Creative Advanced Technology Center to design and implement the digital audio effect technology incorporated in Creative's industry-leading SoundBlaster Live!, Audigy and X-Fi sound card product lines. Specified and led the implementation of Creative's EAX API and interactive 3-D audio rendering engine from EAX 2.0 to EAX 5.0. Contributed to interactive 3D audio standard specifications: MPEG-4 Advanced Audio BIFS; OpenAL; 3-D MIDI. Primary author of the IA-SIG 3-D Audio Rendering Level 2 guideline (I3DL2). Invented new methods for computationally efficient 3D positional audio spatialization of multiple sound sources and spatial reverberation for gaming, virtual reality or computer music applications.

  • Audio Research Scientist

    IRCAM

    (Government Agency; 51-200 employees; Music industry)

    1992 — 1998 (6 years)

    Member of IRCAM Room Acoustics team. Developed in Max/MSP the IRCAM Spatialisateur library (Spat~), a software suite for the spatialization of sound signals in musical creation and performance, post-production, and virtual reality / telepresence (currently distributed as a Max/MSP library and in the form of audio software plugins). Carried out research in 3-D positional audio and room acoustics. Co-supervised doctoral students. Participated in music creation and post-production projects.

  • Audio Research Engineer

    Studer Digitec

    (Electrical/Electronic Manufacturing industry)

    1988 — 1992 (4 years)

    Designed and implemented on Motorola DSP56K a digital audio dynamics processor for a professional digital audio mixing console. Developed a revision of the mixing console’s real-time operating system. Researched and implemented a spatial reverberation system for the same platform, as part of my doctoral research project (more details below).

Jean-Marc Jot’s Education

  • Ecole nationale supérieure des Télécommunications

    Doctorate, 1989 — 1992

    Design and Implementation of a Spatial Sound Processor Based on Physical and Perceptual Models. Thesis advisor: Prof. Antoine Chaigne. Researched and developed a real-time digital audio reverberation and spatialization algorithm. Developed a general theoretical framework and rational method for the design of computationally efficient, natural sounding artificial reverberators using multi-channel Feedback Delay Networks (FDN). Proposed a time-frequency representation for the analysis of room impulse responses, the Energy Decay Relief (EDR). Supervised engineering student projects on room impulse response measurement and digital modeling of head-related transfer functions.

  • Ecole nationale supérieure de Techniques avancées

    Engineer, 1985 — 1987

    Studied Mathematics, Physics, Electronics, Computers.
