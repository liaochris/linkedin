Josh Walonoski’s Education

  • Columbia University College of Physicians and Surgeons

    PhD, MPhil, MA, Genetics and Development, 1999 — 2007

    Research assistant (aka doctoral student) at Columbia University for 8 years. Worked extensively on two projects: 1) The mechanism of action of growth factors in vertebrate limb development, and 2) The nature of requisite signals for spinal neuron development.

    My thesis detailed the molecular and genetic events that specify fate in autonomic motor neurons of the spinal cord during embryogenesis. Our lab recently discovered that these neurons receive an extracellular signal during development. I identified this signal as necessary for proper fate specification. Additionally, I characterized the signal as target-derived in nature, i.e., signaling via retrograde transduction from the axons to the neuronal bodies. This work added significantly to the understanding of the molecular and genetic mechanisms of autonomic motor neuron development, as well as the nature of signals that regulate the development of spinal cord neurons in general.
