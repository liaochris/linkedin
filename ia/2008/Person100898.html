Justin Korthof’s Summary

I have participated in every aspect of the online community experience. Sixteen years ago, I created my first community website. Since then, I have been a passionate and purposeful fan at the forefront of the online community movement. I have created independent portals and communities for several of my own passions, served as a third-party contracted Community Manager for Warner Brothers Pictures on an historic franchise, and worked at Electronic Arts to breathe new life into a community that had been too long stagnant. In addition to Community Management, I have operated in an Assistant Producer role on a triple-A first-person shooter franchise.


Game Industry Community Management Highlights include:

● Planned and executed an intensive, three-day Medal of Honor Airborne Community Summit where key community leaders - in addition to receiving in-depth game design presentations, hands-on gameplay, and exclusive developer interviews - were given the opportunity to fire the real WWII weapons that were featured in the game (an EA first!)

● Planned and executed a groundbreaking Community Summit at Fort Bragg, North Carolina to support Medal of Honor Airborne. Attendees lived on base for 3 days with the modern 82nd Airborne Division, played Airborne against the real troops, attended a "welcome home" dinner for troops who recently returned from service in Iraq, toured the 82nd Airborne Division museum, and even got to experience actual Airborne training by jumping off of Fort Bragg's famous 34' training tower, earning their honorary Airborne wings (an industry first!)

● Transformed a cynical group of four Community Leaders into an optimistic team of over 130 community-leading evangelists for Medal of Honor Airborne

● Orchestrated community efforts for five titles in two years, spanning six platforms and two major genres
