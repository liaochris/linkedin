Zavarin Gagov’s Summary

1. ASIC Designer at ON Semiconductor (Former AMIS)
2. ASIC Designer and Application engineer at Melexis
3. Ph.D. student and researcher at Seoul National University, Korea

1. ASIC Designer at ON Semiconductor - from March 2008 to present

In the Automotive division of the company I am involved in top level modeling, proving a concept by implementation in FPGA and foreseen HDL implementation in ASIC.

2. Melexis employee from Jun. 2003 until Feb. 2008 (4 years and 9 months)

Being hired as an Applicaton Engineer in the first 7 months I was involved in preparation of ASIC-based applications, preparation of technical documentation and keeping connection with customers. My major devices of concern were Hall sensor based applications such as cooling fan IC drivers and Hall sensors.

In 2004 I became an ASIC designer of CPU-based automotive chips. I have been using Verilog (VHDL), RTL design description, embedded C, assembler, CAD tools and tools for automated design such as HDL Designer of Mentor Graphics and Cadence tools. I also took part in measurement and verification of the designed production by programming in LabView and Matlab.

3. Ph.D. student and researcher at Seoul National University, Korea (graduated in February 2003)

As a Ph.D. researcher at Seoul National University I have attended the laboratory of Prof. Wook Hyun Kwon (http://cisl.snu.ac.kr). My major research was in the field of discrete event dynamic system (DEDS), dynamic optimization, Petri nets, logical modeling, graph theory, traffic control and similar. My interest in these areas was inspired by my participation in a two-year project of Elevator Group Control sponsored by LG Industrial Systems (renamed to OTIS-LG Elevator Company). The involvement in solving transport optimization issues established the base for my Ph.D. graduation thesis titled “Modeling and Analysis of Elevator Group Control Systems for High Rise Buildings”.
