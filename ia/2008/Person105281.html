Keri Billen’s Summary

Program manager, senior project manager and customer design-in manager with more than ten years experience within the consumer electronics industry. Have managed integrated circuit projects, software&hardware system creation projects, customer design-in projects and now complete programs. Deal directly with customers on a daily basis.

Keri Billen’s Specialties:

Project management, team motivation, building organisations, internet protocols, digital media formats, customer relationship management

Keri Billen’s Experience

  • Program manager and European design-in manager

    NXP Semiconductors

    (Privately Held; 10,001 or more employees; NXP; Semiconductors industry)

    November 2007 — Present (4 months)

    Fully responsible for the development of all NXP's internet-protocol set-top box (IPSTB) platforms, with a hands-on approach to the latest. At the same time am closely managing the engineers assisting our European customers with developing derivative products. Am also supporting the design-in teams in other continents. This multi-faceted role requires many different management skills from one moment to the next.

  • System project manager

    NXP

    (Privately Held; 10,001 or more employees; Electrical/Electronic Manufacturing industry)

    February 2005 — November 2007 (2 years 10 months)

    Managed the creation of the first IPSTB platform that could be based on either Linux or WinCE. The speed of development is seen as a landmark. The platform is feature-rich, and is being used by many customers, most notably Slingmedia (in their 'Slingcatcher' product) and Winbox (in many of their products). The team was constrained to only twenty-two engineers at its peak, so their motivation to perform consistently at a high level was crucial to our success.

  • System design-in project manager for Sony Asia

    Philips Semiconductors

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Electrical/Electronic Manufacturing industry)

    January 2004 — February 2005 (1 year 2 months)

    Was brought in to lead the communication with senior customer management during very difficult times caused mainly by quality problems with Philips' integrated circuits (ICs). Main achievement was bringing the quality problems to a halt exactly as we planned; thereby saving the business.

  • Integrated circuit (IC) development project manager

    Philips Semiconductors

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Electrical/Electronic Manufacturing industry)

    March 2002 — January 2003 (11 months)

    Managed the design, validation, market introduction and production ramp-up of a large and complex IC for the latest generation of digital televisions and multimedia applications (used most notably in Sony's flagship TVs in Europe). At the start of the project I was also the ad interim functional validation team leader.

  • Product engineer and industrial project leader

    Philips Semiconductors

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Electrical/Electronic Manufacturing industry)

    August 1997 — March 2002 (4 years 8 months)

    Was the industrial manager for an international IC creation project based in Nijmegen, The Netherlands. My 30-strong team developed the production line, optimised the design for manufacturing and testing, and led the customer quality program. Before this I saved the company (and its customers) many millions of pounds by managing production yield improvements, design shrinks and quality improvements to other ICs.
