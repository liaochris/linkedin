Rick Myers AIA’s Summary

Originally from Omaha, Nebraska, Myers graduated from Kansas State University in 1996 (B. Architecture and Minor in Urban Planning). He then joined Good Fulton & Farrell (GFF), and is currently a principal, with 12 years of experience. He has been responsible for 15 of the 25 stores GFF has completed for the national home furnishings retailer, Crate&Barrel, representing over $100 million in construction costs across 9 states.

He is a registered architect in Kansas, Nebraska and Texas, and a member of several professional societies and organizations, most notably the AIA, ULI, the Construction Specifications Institute (CSI) and the Real Estate Council (TREC). Rick is an alumni of TREC’s "Associate Leadership Council" (ALC) Program (2003-2004), and currently serves on the ALC Alumni Association Steering Committee. He is an alumni of the Greater Dallas Chamber of Commerce's "Leadership Dallas" Program (2006-2007) and has previously served on and chaired the Board of Trustees at Our Redeemer Lutheran Church in Dallas, where he presently a member of the Building Committee.

Rick lives in Dallas with his wife, Bernita Beikmann. They have two daughters, Ava Katharine and Emma Elizabeth.
