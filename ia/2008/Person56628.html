Eduard Emde, CPP, CISSP’s Summary

Every single company, whatever its nature or size, meets with risks at one point or another. However, there is a large difference here between ‘taking a risk’ and ‘being at risk’. Taking a risk means consciously taking a decision and implies a calculated risk that is inextricably bound to achieving success. Being at risk implies a fear of the unknown and uncontrollable.

Crime risks
Many companies are at risk of encountering crime. There is often a lack of profound knowledge of crime risks, so there is often not a conscious policy to gain command of such risks. In the opinion of Interseco, this is irresponsible and, moreover, it is often not necessary. The risk a company runs of becoming the victim of, for example, extortion, theft, threats or fraud is too big to ignore in our time and age. More importantly: it is not necessary. With a normal, healthy business-like attitude, these kinds of risks can easily be controlled.

Trust
Interseco helps and advises profit and non-profit-organizations to detect and gain control of crime risks. In every case, our service is founded on a mutual basis of trust. Our company’s policy is to investigate our clients’ problems and queries prior to offering our services. We only accept a project if we are convinced we will come up with a result.

Independence, discretion and secrecy
Our clients can fully trust on independence, discretion and secrecy in all aspects of our service. That’s simply how we work at Interseco.
