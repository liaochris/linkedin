Hans-Martin Adorf’s Summary

Senior Software Engineer / Research Scientist with more than two decades of combined experience in scientific research and advanced technology system development in different academic and business settings. Self-motivated, visionary generalist with attention to detail. PhD from Univ. of Strasbourg (France).

Volunteer flautist in regional ensembles and orchestras.

Always interested in innovative projects. Current goals: continue working with bright people in technically-scientifically oriented organizations, and bringing the breadth and depth of my experience to bear fruit.

Hans-Martin Adorf’s Specialties:

GRID computing: scientific workflows, high-performance computing. Software engineering: continuous integration, code deployment. Scientific research: advanced classical and quantum statistics. Technology assessments. Project development: fund raising, professional networking.

  • Software Systems Engineer

    Interval Logic Corp.

    (Public Company; 11-50 employees; BRKS; Computer Software industry)

    March 1998 — October 2002 (4 years 8 months)

    Worked in Mountain View, USA, at Interval Logic Corp. (ILC), a subsidiary of PRI Automation (PRIA), later acquired by Brooks Automation. Developed highly efficient optimization engines for ILC's planner products Leverage for Planning and Leverage for Operations Planning (applied to operations planning and supply chain optimization in the semiconductor industry). Constructed mathematical models of supply chains for specific customers. Led ILC's Linear Programming team. Mentored integration engineers. Consulted face-to-face with end-users in the fabless business sector.

  • Hubble Space Telescope Data Analysis Scientist

    European Southern Observatory

    (Government Agency; 201-500 employees; Research industry)

    February 1985 — April 1997 (12 years 3 months)

    Worked at the Space Telescope - European Coordinating Facility (ST-ECF), a joint venture of ESO and the European Space Agency (ESA). Developed a novel “dithering” technique for overcoming insufficient signal sampling (used e.g. in the Hubble Deep Field project and in the HST Advanced Camera for Surveys). Developed advanced algorithms e.g. for statistical pattern recognition and fault detection. Introduced the World Wide Web to the ST-ECF, from where it spread to ESO and ESA. Developed the first Web-based decision support system for the NASA/ESA Hubble Space Telescope (HST). Implemented the first system for restoring distorted HST images (applied e.g. to HST images of SN1987a). Led the ST-ECF Artificial Intelligence Pilot Project. Co-developed an efficient stochastic, neural network-based optimization algorithm (used by the STScI, Baltimore, USA, for HST observation scheduling). Contributed to the ST-ECF Newsletter. Presented accomplishments at numerous international conferences.
