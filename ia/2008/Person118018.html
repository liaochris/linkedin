Maggie Moon’s Summary

Currently working as a nutrition communications specialist, Maggie Moon combines her background in language arts with training in nutrition science to convey accurate evidence-based information to a range of audiences from researchers to consumers.

Maggie Moon earned her master of science degree in Nutrition and Education from Columbia University Teachers College, where research and theory is always linked to application and practice. She completed her clinical training at New York Presbyterian Hospital of Columbia and Cornell; and has community setting work experience from United Way of NYC, Columbia Head Start, and the Healthy Schools, Healthy Families program of the Morgan Stanley Children's Hospital of New York-Presbyterian.

She graduated early with a bachelor of arts degree in English Literature from U.C. Berkeley. From 2005-2007, Ms. Moon was editor of a publication focusing on nutrition, physiology and public health. Maggie Moon is based in New York, NY.

Maggie Moon’s Specialties:

Science-based communications for consumers; Low-literacy education materials; Metropolitan-area worksite wellness programs; Theory-based, behaviorally-focused program planning; Urban elementary school settings.
