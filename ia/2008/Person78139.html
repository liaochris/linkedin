Hugh Dobbie’s Summary

Hugh Dobbie, is the President of Interactive Netcasting Systems Inc (INSINC), a pioneer in the delivery of audio and video over Internet protocols. Prior to founding INSINC in 1998, Mr. Dobbie was the President of Dowco Computer Systems Ltd., one of British Columbia’s largest privately held ISPs. A technology veteran with over 23 year’s experience, he has a proven track record in creating business models with new technologies.

Mr. Dobbie has overseen a variety of contractual engagements with major sports, entertainment and communications companies, including relationships with CTV, Woodbine Entertainment Group, AT&T (Canada), Telus, Akamai Technologies Inc., the Banff Television Festival, Orca Bay Sports and Entertainment, Canadian Idol, TVG Netowrks, Maple Leaf Sports and Entertainment, the Vancouver Film Festival and Report on Business Television. He is widely quoted in media publications and has appeared on both radio and television as a streaming media expert.

Mr. Dobbie currently serves on the boards of Dowco Computer Systems, INSINC Lightyear Digital Theatre (Canada) Ltd. and Tri-M Wireless Inc.

Looking for strategic partnerships, equity financing, joint ventures, etc. to assist in scaling our business to the next level.

Hugh Dobbie’s Experience

    Director, key founder, and President of INSINC, a leading Internet streaming media provider. Raised $2.8M in private placement funding to launch company.

    Negotiated $3.6M service agreement and co-operative marketing agreement with AT&T Canada and has structured numerous strategic relationships.

  • Founder and President

    Dowco Computer Systems Ltd.

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    1994 — 2001 (7 years)

    Sensing a growing demand for computer products in the graphics engineering market, the Computer Systems Division of Dowco, headed by Hugh, began offering hardware and software solutions to customers in 1987. In 1989 and 1990 he won the Accugraph Corporation’s “Fastest Draw in the World” graphics competition and was awarded over $50,000 in software and prizes. The division's success lead to the formation of a Dowco subsidiary in 1994 - Dowco Computer Systems Limited with Hugh, Jr. as president and primary shareholder and Hugh, Sr. as business partner. The company focussed on systems integration establishing relationships with Hewlett Packard, Oracle, Microsoft, and Cisco.

    Shortly after the start of the company, Hugh noticed another opportunity - the World Wide Web. In September 1995, dowco.com Internet was born. This division of Dowco gives homes and businesses in the Lower Mainland and Fraser Valley access to the Internet as well as offering a full range of Internet services.
