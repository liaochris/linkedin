Ian Valentine’s Summary

I have a serial compulsion to start businesses which change the status quo, and add new ways of thinking and behaviour into industries, becoming flagship leaders in their field.

Industries that I have influenced have spanned CRM/Call Centers (Graham Technology), Interactive TV (waptv/BSkyB), General Aviation (FreeFlight Aviation) and now broadband delivered entertainment (Miniweb).

Building complex solutions is never hard, when end result can be visualised as a whole before the first brick is placed in the wall.

Exceptional communicator and public speaker, I have been a regular conference speaker and panelist for the last 14 years.

I thrive on problems that require strategic thinking, risk evaluation, and innovative never-been-done-before solutions.

Technology is not always the solution to industry problems, sometimes an industry needs to change the way it behaves.

Aviation and Technology have always been passions, as well as reaching out and helping individuals with generosity and genuine care.

Ian Valentine’s Specialties:

TV Standards work, Aviation Law, Developing New Technology, Interactive TV, Broadcast and Broadband Media, Internet Technology and Architectures, Media Business Models, Internet Advertising, TV Advertising, Web and TV Site development, Business Development, Business Start Ups, Business Plans, Technology Marketing, User Interface Design, Air Charter, Aircraft Operations.

Ian Valentine’s Experience

  • CEO

    Miniweb Technologies Ltd

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Broadcast Media industry)

    August 2006 — Present (2 years)

    Miniweb Interactive operates an aggregated interactive services portal for narrowband and broadband interactive TV devices, and Miniweb Research develops standards and technology to enable broadcasters and content owners to deploy services across multiple TV distribution networks unchanged.

    The Miniweb Group was established in August 2006, as a "spin-off" from BSkyB.

    It addresses the need for CE device manufacturers, traditional cable TV networks and emerging IPTV networks to share interactive TV content.

    By delivering interoperability of the interactive experience across multiple device types, Miniweb enables media rich web sites to deploy internet video to non-PC devices uniformly.

    Miniweb is now actively working with network operators, CE device manufacturers, internet site owners and broadcasters to enable new revenue streams derived from connecting TV audiences to interactive services on non-PC devices.

  • Owner

    Free Flight Aviation Ltd.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Airlines/Aviation industry)

    March 2002 — Present (6 years 5 months)

    FreeFlight Aviation is the the only "Fractional" or Shared Ownership scheme for Technically Advanced Aircraft in Europe. It specialises in the operation of Cirrus aircraft, for personal transportation purposes, both business and pleasure. It currently operates 5 aircraft from various bases around London UK. see www.cirrus147.com

  • Technical Alliances Director

    BSkyB

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; BSY; Broadcast Media industry)

    January 2002 — October 2006 (4 years 10 months)

    Ian Valentine joined Sky in June 2000 charged with the task of bringing Internet Compatibility to the entire installed based of Sky DigiBoxes. He was the driving force behind the development of the new WTVML Microbrowser for the Sky Interactive platform, and has championed Sky's partnerships with the Internet Industry both technically and commercially. Ian has also worked closely with the e-Envoy’s office, and was instrumental in providing mechanisms for the deployment of e-government services via interactive TV in the UK.

    He is now Technical Alliances Director for Sky Interactive, and is responsible for the ongoing acceptance and convergence of interactive Digital TV with e-business standards, and also represents the relationships necessary to create the tooling, content management systems, skills and design expertise in the market to take advantage of iDTV.

  • R&D Board Member

    BSkyB

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; BSY; Broadcast Media industry)

    August 2003 — October 2006 (3 years 3 months)

    Served as Interactive representative on the R&D steering board at BSkyB. The Board considered all new ideas and developments from products to new technologies, and evaluated their use to the business at a pre-ROI stage.

    Developed the "Sky Net" concept (mass market Internet content for TVs) from inception to launch, including the first evaluation of automated test mechanisms for Tv Sites, the first TV Search engine, and the benefits of localisation and personalisation in an online TV environment.

    Also involved in many aspects of set-top box device development, mobile projects and home networking.

  • Founder and Operations Director

    waptv Ltd

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Internet industry)

    April 2000 — December 2001 (1 year 9 months)

    Started waptv to develop a Micro-browser for the OpenTV platform, capable of displaying Internet sites designed for TV (TV Sites) on Sky Digital set-top boxes.

    Company was 95% acquired by BSkyB in June 2000. The last 5% was released on a share swap with BSkyB in May 2001. Became the basis of the Sky Interactive platform, which enabled "red button" interactivity and interactive advertising.

  • Product Manager

    Graham Technology

    (Internet industry)

    1992 — 2000 (8 years)

    Ian Valentine had the original idea for a "Business Process Server" - a software infrastructure designed to manage realtime business processes and telephone calls in a call center environment.
    Based on a layered architecture it allowed a thin desktop client to prompt a call center agent through a telephone based business process easily and quickly. Hidden were all the "back end" database updates and legacy systems calls, simplifying training and business process change.
    Business processes and even screen designs were all data driven, and manages by a spohisticated set of busienss process management tools.
    Being the first in class it rocketed Graham Technology from a 22 man Scottish Systems integrator, to a global employer of over 200 people, with clients that included some of the largest call center projects in the world (for example 5000 agents across 6 centers at BT)
    Ian championed the product becoming "multi channel" which lead him into the web and interactive TV industries.
