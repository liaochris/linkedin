Christopher Dart’s Summary

I am an experienced museum employee/historical interpreter, having served at the Autry National Center’s Museum of the American West as a Visitor Services and Membership Lead for over a year, and as a Museum Guide for El Pueblo de Los Angeles Historic Monument for almost two years. My work experience at the Autry touched on every aspect of visitor services from sales to answering a myriad of questions from visitors to department scheduling to handling VIPs, all with an emphasis on providing exceptional customer service.

In 2005 I completed a Resource Education internship for the National Park Service through the Student Conservation Association and AmeriCorps. I’ve done volunteer work (including first person living history character interpretation) for Heritage Square Museum, as well as Civil War reenacting. In 2007 I earned a M.A. in American Studies from Cal State Fullerton. My education has focused on American regionalism, cultural theory, race and class issues, masculinity, religion, and visual and popular culture. My passions revolve around music and the arts, youth work, building community, and faith. My personality is outgoing, friendly, helpful, creative, intellectual, and infectious.
