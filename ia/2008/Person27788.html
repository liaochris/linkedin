Brian Keller’s Summary

I am a graduate of Florida State University and received my BS in Business Management (’98) and a MS in Sports Administration (’00). While at FSU, I got my first experience in athletics as a manager for the men’s basketball team from 1996-1998. During this stint as manager, the team went to the 1997 NIT Championship and advanced to the second round of the 1998 NCAA Tournament. During college, I also got my first taste of ticket by being in charge of the dorm’s student ticket distribution for home football games.

After college, I worked as an intern in the ticket office of the Richmond Braves Triple-A minor league baseball club. Once the internship ended, I moved to Huntington, WV, for three years as the Box Office Manager for Marshall University. While in there, I used the Paciolan (TK2) system and assisted in computerizing all ticket sales. I also got to experience processing tickets for two bowl games, three Capital City Classic games (the annual West Virginia vs. Marshall basketball game in Charleston, WV), two Mid-American basketball tournaments and was able to work closely with the university’s donor group, the Big Green. I was also in charge of staffing all Graduate Assistants and student workers for the box office and balancing out the daily deposits in the ticket office.

In 2004 I joined the Potomac Nationals as the Director of Ticket Operations. While there I was exposed to a new ticketing system, Etix. While in my first year at Potomac, I played a key role in the automation and implementation of online ticket purchases and print-at-home ticketing for the first time at the P-Nats stadium. I later went on to work for the Nissan Pavilion and the Washington Nationals where I got to experience using the Ticketmaster and Archtics systems along with their Access Manager program. I later returned to Potomac for one final year before joining the XOS team in November of 2006.
