Mel Ochoa’s Summary

Mel is a student at the Stern School of Business at New York University and a fellow in the Catherine B. Reynolds Program in Social Entrepreneurship (http://www.nyu.edu/reynolds). Prior to graduate school, he was the acting director of marketing at the Bay Area Discovery Museum in Sausalito, Calif. -- a family destination dedicated to art, science and hands-on fun for children up to age 8.

Previously, Mel was vice president of corporate affairs and a co-founder at BridgePath, an enterprise software provider to the staffing industry. While at BridgePath, he was responsible for the company’s public relations strategy, recruiting program and internal operations. Under his direction, BridgePath was featured in The Wall Street Journal, Time, Red Herring, NPR, BusinessWeek, Business 2.0 and over 130 publications. BullHorn, Inc. acquired BridgePath in 2002. Past experience also includes work with Kraft Foods, a U.S. congressman in Washington, D.C., and Schwartz Communications, a leading high-tech PR agency.

From a civic perspective, Mel has been involved with several nonprofit organizations, including Boys & Girls Clubs of San Francisco, lovefutbol.org, Building Readiness in Tomorrow’s Entrepreneurs, California Alumni Association, and Global Degree (an e-journal on globalization). He has also instructed archery for Cub Scouts, led entrepreneurship classes for third-graders with BizWorld and taught English at a Korean summer camp.

Mel holds a bachelor’s degree with majors in History and Rhetoric from the University of California, Berkeley, and is a member of the National Eagle Scout Association.
