Michael "Roby" Robinson’s Summary

Michael E. "Roby" Robinson is licensed to practice law in the State of Tennessee and before the United States Patent and Trademark Office. Roby is currently an associate with the intellectual property firm Luedeka, Neely & Graham, P.C. in Knoxville, Tennessee. His work exclusively centers around patent, trademark, copyright, and trade secret law. Roby has over three years of experience in patent, trademark, and copyright litigation as well as the prosecution of these types of intellectual properties.
Prior to attending law school, Roby worked for Nalco Chemical Company and consulted with various facilities regarding their heat exchange equipment including chillers, boilers, and chill/steam loops. Some of Roby's clients while with Nalco included the McKinley Climatic Laboratory at Eglin Air Force Base; Sacred Heart Hospital in Pensacola, Florida; Keesler Air Force Base in Biloxi, Mississippi; the United States Naval Air Station in Pensacola, Florida; and the Honeywell Friction Materials plant in Lynn Haven, Florida. Roby also taught high school chemistry and physics at Tate High School for one year prior to entering law school.
