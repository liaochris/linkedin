Alex Horvath’s Summary

I have written for newspapers and magazines since my teenage years starting with a weekly newspaper in my home town. I have written about crime and politics (two subjects that are not mutally exclusive of each other), government, education, and financial services. My favorite positions have been the three years that I penned the weekly "Overheard" column for the Pacific Sun, as well as the six years that I wrote feature articles for the San Francisco Chronicle's "Friday" section. Both are jobs that paid little but offered great exposure and professional satisfaction.

Presently, I write for a business trade publication where I cover business news and trends involving Structured Settlements and related issues. My reporting often provides an opportunity to utilize my interviewing and research skills which ultimately give me the ability to offer expert analysis of what is happening for our readers.

Since July 2004, I have also been the webmaster and main contributor of articles and essays to Bolinas 2 Miles, a web site that is dedicated to the extended community of Bolinas, Calif.

Alex Horvath’s Specialties:

Story development, writing and editing. Pitching stories and delivering content on or before deadline. Working with editors to assign photographers or submitting photos/art of my own.
