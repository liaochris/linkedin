  • Technical Artist

    Electronic Arts

    (Public Company; ERTS; Computer Games industry)

    July 2002 — February 2005 (2 years 8 months)

    Technical Artist on "Medal of Honor: Rising Sun" which shipped November 2003. Responsible primarily for 3DSMax tools written in MaxScript. The key highlights were the Compartment Manager which allowed multiple artists and designers to work on the same levels simultaneously and the EZ Bake Oven, a vertex lighting tool.

    Technical Artist on "GoldenEye:Rogue Agent" which shipped in 2004. Updated the CManager with new functionality. EZ Bake Oven was also extended to render light maps and generate spherical harmonics. This project included extending MaxScript in C++.

    Technical Artist on "Medal of Honor:European Assault" which shipped in 2005.

    I also had the unfortunate experience as a member of working on RenderWare Studio after EA's acquisition of Criterion. This is where I first began coding in C# to write new modules for RW Studio. RW was eventually dropped by EA when it began abundantly clear that it was garbage.

  • Artist

    Treyarch/Activision

    (Public Company; ATVI; Computer Games industry)

    July 2000 — July 2002 (2 years 1 month)

    Production Artist on "Triple Play Baseball" for the PS2 which shipped in March 2001. Developed for Electronic Arts before Treyarch was acquired by Activision. This was the first version of "Triple Play" on the PS2.

    Production Artist on Xbox launch title "Tony Hawk's Pro Skater 2x" which shiiped in November 2001. As well as updating the original PSX and PS2 assets for the Xbox, I also created an original skate park: the construction site. On this project I was as tasked with reverse engineering the Tony Hawk scripting language. Neversoft was too busy working on "Tony Hawk Pro Skater 3" to return any of our phone calls.

  • Senior Artist

    New World Computing/3DO

    (Computer Games industry)

    February 1999 — July 2000 (1 year 6 months)

    Production artist on "Might and Magic VII: For Blood and Honor [PC]" which shipped in 1999. Primarily responsible for pre-rendered cut-scenes and a few of the sprites in this ray-cast CRPG.

    Production artist on "Might and Magic VIII: Day of the Destroyer [PC]" which shipped March 2000. Again, mostly cut-scenes and many of the sprites from MMVII were resurrected for this title.

    Senior Artist on "Legends of Might and Magic [PC]" which shipped in March 2001. I worked on the first four iterations of this title, before it finally shipped in its fifth incarnation as a Counter Strike clone. Modelling and animation. This is also the project on which I wrote my first tool: a Visual Basic tool to calculate motion vectors from Character Studio animations in 3DSMax.

    Also credited on "Might and Magic:IX." Apparently many of the assets I created for "Legends" found their way into MMIX, the Might and Magic franchise's first foray into real 3D using the LithTech engine.

    Lead artist on isometric action title "Betrayer." This title was sadly cancelled when pur friendly overseers in Tokyo decided to shutter the studio. It didn't help the "Iron John" was a terrible game with sales in the dozens.
