Charles Haynes’s Summary

I like to lead groups that build infrastructure tools, tools to help other people build things, or keep things running. In the past that's been compilers and debuggers, more recently it's tools to help manage massive collections of widely distributed machines, as well as manage supply chain and MRP (either Manufacturing Resource Planning, or Material Requirements Planning - choose your favorite buzzword.)

At this point I've achieved most of my personal professional goals, so I'm more interested in mentoring engineers and finding ways to make other people happier and more productive.

Charles Haynes’s Specialties:

The techniques I prefer are object oriented analysis and design, rapid prototyping, fast development cycles, and user driven design (more buzzwords, but user driven design is a religion with me.) I like large scale loosely coupled distributed systems, but I am comfortable with peer-to-peer, client-server, three-tier, you name it. I'm language agnostic.

My specialty, if I had to choose one, is building small high-morale teams with a passion for their users.
