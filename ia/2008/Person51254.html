I am a scholar of Hong Kong and Japanese movies, and have written hundreds of reviews. Also, I specialize in reading Chinese and Japanese names.

I spent over a decade in the Aus govt as a clerical, also a couple of years of IT Support and training. There was also a fair amount of public contact work, mainly by phone.
During this time, I spent 6 yrs as a volunteer English tutor for migrants. I had very keen Uni-educated adult students from a dozen countries. Also, I had also spent about 5 yrs tutoring Uni students in computer programming. I made regular trips to SE Asia, and learned Thai and Cantonese.

After leaving the service in the late 1990s, I took a variety of private industry jobs, mostly IT-related. These included helpdesk/callcentre positions, an IT product management position for a commercial photographics lab, and a 2 years as a ColdFusion programmer for a home loan finance company.

I moved to Japan with my wife in 2004. Unfortunately, the huge change in the English Teaching situation there over the last 2 years, and for the worse. So I'm back in Sydney.

Over the next 10 yrs, I intend to take on as many projects as is possible to fit in, including :

* ColdFusion / web design / databases
* Language, linguistics and cultural comparison
* Psychology, therapies and HR
* Research and analysis
* Cinema, particularly HK and Japanese

I am an extremely focussed, well-organized and clever individual, and believe that I can fit all of this and more into the next decade or so.
