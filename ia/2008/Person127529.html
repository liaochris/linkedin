Michele O'Donnell’s Summary

I have been a special educator for 14 years. I started out as a psychology major and had the opportunity to volunteer at a school for autistic elementary students. This is when I figured out my career path, I switched my major to special education. I currently resigned from teaching. My work load was taking valuable time away from my students. I was responsible for jobs that three teachers once had, yes I do understand……it’s all money. I couldn’t watch my students not receive the services they deserved. I still want to continue with employment in special education, especially with autism and behavior. I would like to get board certified in applied behavioral analysis.

  • SED Severely Emotional Disturbed PreSchool Teacher

    BACC Bertha Abess Children's Center/Miami-Dade Public School System

    (Education Management industry)

    August 1997 — June 1998 (11 months)

    I team taught 3-5 year old students identified with severe emotional troubles. We used the High Scope Curriculum, highly structured daily routines to educate students and to reduce problem behaviors. Everyday we followed the same schedule so students were prepared for what activity came next to eliminate melt-downs. We always arranged specific tasks for each student during transitions. We were trained in Safe Physical Management in case we had to restrain students in out of control situations. Most students were ADHD, ODD, Autistic,CD, physically or sexually abused, Bi-polar and/or had other mental health related difficulties. We used the token economy to reinforce positive behaviors. Points were not taken away, just not received if the behavior expected was not demonstrated by the child. Team teaching was the best. Having another teacher to work with was key in educating SED students. I loved the job, but I did go to the gym after work.
