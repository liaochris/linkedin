Daniel Tunkelang’s Summary

I head up Research at Endeca, a leader and pioneer in enterprise information access.

When we started Endeca in 1999, we saw the weaknesses of prevalent approaches (search engines, databases) and set out to build a better solution, one that better leveraged human intelligence through a transparent dialog between the user and the system. An approach that emphasized summarization of sets of documents over just ranking them by a best guess at their relevance.

Endeca's concrete success speaks for itself. Endeca has grown at over 100% annually for the last 5 years, and is recognized by industry analysts (Gartner, Forrester, etc.) as a market and technology leader. Our hundreds of clients include household names in retail (Wal-Mart, Home Depot); manufacturing and distribution (Boeing, IBM); media and publishing (LexisNexis, World Book), financial services (ABN AMRO, Bank of America), and government (Defense Intelligence Agency, National Cancer Institute).

Endeca is also an active participant in the scholarly research community, particularly in information retrieval. By participating in such conferences as SIGIR and CIKM, we hope to further the exchange of ideas between academia and industry, and particularly to encourage scholarly efforts around a more user-centric approach to information retrieval.

Endeca is always looking for top talent, both at our Cambridge, Massachusetts headquarters and in our regional offices around the world. If you have what it takes and are up to the challenge, I urge you to apply.

A short summary of my pre-Endeca career:

MIT class of '92, Computer Science (6-3) and Math (18). CMU PhD in Computer Science (SCS), completed in 1998. Worked at IBM T J Watson Research in Hawthorne in Information Retrieval and Analysis Group; also spent a summer in the Information Visualization group at Bell Labs (now AT&T).

Personal interests include social networks, decision theory, and behavioral finance.

Daniel Tunkelang’s Specialties:

Solving information retrieval problems by questioning the conventional assumptions of the field and developing clever algorithms and heuristics.
