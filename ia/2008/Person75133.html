Harry McCracken’s Summary

I'm an extremely lucky guy who gets to use the world's most exciting medium (the Web) and a wonderful tried-and-true one (print) to help millions of people get more out of the technology in their lives.

I've been editor in chief of PC World (the site and the magazine) since 2004, and have worked in the media biz since 1991.

And yes, I'm the guy who resigned in May 2007 and then, after an unlikely and remarkable series of events, happily accepted my old job back.

PCW is the planet's highest-circulation computing magazine, and we're international, with about 60 editions. We have a large and growing site, and our content also appears on Yahoo, MSN, and WashingtonPost.com, among other places.

I've been fortunate enough to have worked on many award-winning projects, including stories that won four Neal Awards for "Best Subject-Related Series," and a 2000 Grand Neal Award from American Business Media. Our magazine won Folio's Eddie award as best consumer computing publication in 2002, 2003, and 2004; PCWorld.com won the Neal award as best site in 2004 and 2005. In 2006, my blog won MIN's Best of the Web award; in 2007, we were named ASBPE's Web publication of the year. And in 2007 I was named one of MIN's 21 Most Intriguing People in the Media. I've also judged the National Magazine Awards and other editorial competitions.

Over the years, I've often guested on TV and radio (ABC, CBS, CNBC, CNN, MSNBC, NPR, the BBC, the History Channel, etc.), and I collaborated with Dateline NBC on a memorable PC repair investigation in 2000.

I spearheaded our site redesign in 2006 and a cool 2007 magazine redesign which further strengthens the ties between PCW's print and online incarnations.

Outside of PCW, I've written for Slate, USA Today, Family Circle, and other publications.

I graduated from Boston University and have lived in Boston, Portland (Oregon), London, and--since 2002--San Francisco.
