Raghid Kawash’s Summary

I'm currently working on my masters thesis which deals with designing a dedicated framework for distributed real-time systems based entirely on tweaked object-oriented design patterns. The results of my thesis are expected to illustrate how design patterns can be tweaked and specialized for "time critical" systems. Furthermore it will illustrate how design patterns can help provide simple abstractions for real-time middleware and completely conceal the notion of real-time away from developers, permitting them to focus entirely on their applications and letting the framework handle real-time constraints. (depricated)

I have just currently changed the focus on my masters thesis from designing a low-level middleware, as mention above, to evaluating group-oriented middleware focusing on the OMG DDS - Data Centric Distributed System - specification and concrete comercial and open source implementations. The DDS specification will mainly be evaluated in relation to industrial healthcare applications.

(Expected duration 1 year while working part-time at Cryos International)

Along side my thesis work, I put in a minimum of 20 hours a week developing inhouse software at Cryos International. Cryos International is a world wide recognized scandinavian Cryo Bank situated in Aarhus Denmark. Throughout the summer vacation 2007 I expect to put in full time at Cryos International and work part-time, evenings and weekends, on my masters thesis.
