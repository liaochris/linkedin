Jason Llorenz’s Summary

Jason is an attorney and strategic communications consultant serving entrepreneurs, small businesses, nonprofits and professionals.

Previously, Jason served as counsel and chief of staff to the Caraway Group, Inc., a two-decade old minority-woman owned strategic communications agency based in Washington, DC. As a part of the firm’s public affairs practice, Jason managed communications teams, provided counsel and developed advocacy, education and positioning strategies for corporate, national nonprofit and government clients, which have included Microsoft Corporation, MGM MIRAGE, the American Federation of Teachers, and Robert L Johnson’s RLJ Companies, which includes Urban Trust Bank, the RLJ Asset Management Group, and the 3 billion dollar hospitality and lodging fund, RLJ Development.

Prior to joining the Caraway Group, Jason was a Congressional Hispanic Caucus Institute Law Fellow, through which he clerked for Judge Richard W. Roberts, United States District Court for the District of Columbia.

Originally from Brooklyn, New York City, Jason received his B.A. in Social Sciences from Cazenovia College and J.D. from the State University of New York at Buffalo Law School. He is admitted to the New York State and Washington, DC bars and is active in the Hispanic National Bar Association as well as the Hispanic Bars of New York and DC. He serves on the boards of the Washington Internship Institute and La Cooperativa of Washington, D.C., an organization dedicated to financial empowerment of Latino professionals.

Jason Llorenz’s Specialties:

Strategic Communications and corporate reputation management, strategic partnerships, crisis communications, legal services for entrepreneurs and families.
