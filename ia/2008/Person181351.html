Steve Gehlen’s Summary

Over the past 12 years, Steve Gehlen has been the top Internet strategist at companies such as Nike, Digimarc, Hollywood Entertainment Corp. (dba the Hollywood Video retail chain) and Oregon Health & Science University (OHSU). He was responsible for producing the first public Web sites at the first three of those companies.

Steve started his career in Information Technology, with almost 10 years of experience integrating desktop computing into business processes at companies such as PacifiCorp and Nike.

Having studied both mass media and computer science at the University of Oregon, when the two started to converge in the mid-90's Steve shifted his career from IT to digital marketing and business development.

He made this career switch at Nike and was mentored by two Marketing VPs during his time there, including Andy Mooney, currently Chairman of Disney Consumer Products. The Nike.com site he produced and launched just prior to the 1996 Summer Olympics was technically innovative at the time, with every element on the page coming from a relational database.

In 2000, while working at Hollywood Entertainment Corp., Steve took over management of Reel.com, a leading movie information and e-commerce Web site and up until that time a subsidiary of the company. He was the architect of a deal with 800.com, adding a consumer electronics store to Reel.com. While under Steve's management, Reel.com's annualized sales volume was $12 million.

In 2002 Steve wrote the business plan for Nutcase Helmets (available at Target, Wal-Mart, Toys-R-Us etc.) as a consultant to Morrow Creative Group.

In 2004 Steve founded the Internet Strategy Forum, a nonprofit professional association for corporate Internet strategists. Prior to that he held volunteer Board positions with several other nonprofit organizations.

Steve was a judge for the 2006 Yahoo! Big Idea Chair Awards and currently sits on the Advisory Council for the InnoTech Conference & Expo.
