Arindam Mallik’s Summary

I am a computer architect specializing in system architectures that utilize the characteristics of applications, users, and materials in a holistic manner.
Computer Architecture serves as an interface between technology trends and marketplace demands. Traditionally, a computer system is usually represented as consisting of five abstraction levels: hardware, firmware, assembler, operating system and applications. My research questions this fundamental definition. Instead of viewing computer architecture as an interface, I aim at introducing a holistic view on computing that involves users, applications, as well as materials. Specifically, during my PhD, I have worked on architectural optimizations that consider new layers lying at two extreme ends of the current set of abstraction levels: systems, users, and materials.
My research work has been published in published in numerous international journals and conferences. A part of my research has been also filed as a patent.

  • PhD Candidate

    (Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry)

    September 2002 — Present (5 years 7 months)

    The PhD dissertation proposes and analyzes system architectures that utilize the characteristics of applications, users, and materials in a holistic manner. It supports the hypothesis that including individual user’s preferences to optimize system hardware utilization would lead to better performance and power. The results presented in the dissertation are obtained from a prototype implemented in a notebook processor and involves experiments involving actual users. A part of the research is focused on inherent system robustness for networking applications and utilization of such application characteristics to perform reliability-performance trade-off analysis.
