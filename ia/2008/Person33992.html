Chase Weir’s Summary

Serial entrepreneur, part-time visionary, full-time deal-closer and very occasional adventurer. Over seven years of experience creating and developing mobility and wireless software applications and utilities and, commercializing them in the market research and education and training industries. Eight years in senior management roles across business functions in small to medium size entertainment and technology companies, and another five years in senior research, policy, strategy and international business development roles. Talents include strong leadership, communication and team building/management skills, identifying and closing trusted partnerships and lighthouse sales opportunities, and moving or climbing a mountain and trekking a glacier here and there. Primarily interested in good citizenship while taking on big challenges that make a big impact.

Chase Weir’s Specialties:

Has managed capital funding, sales and marketing, business development, strategic planning, and operations, and led product development teams through five commercial software releases and multiple patent filings with the USPTO. Played key roles in one private company acquisition and reverse-merger, and led two software companies through early round financing as president, and one as chairman of the board. Currently, Chase is leading a new media venture which launched services in NOV '07.

Chase Weir’s Experience

  • Chairman & CEO

    TinselVision

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Broadcast Media industry)

    July 2006 — Present (1 year 7 months)

    Co-founded TinselVision with partners in the US and India in mid-2006 to license and distribute premium Bollywood films and Indian television content online via broadband to South Asian households globally. Described as a niche, video-on-demand and convergence media venture, TinselVision is an aspirational brand and 360 degree, online entertainment destination. Tinsel launches its invitation-only services during October, 2007.

    Chase is principally responsible for the company's capital finance and business development strategies, financial modeling, key partnerships, and S&M execution while recruiting staff, advisors and directors.

    The company has closed Series A and B financing prior to services launch and, secured licensing deals with Zee TV, Star TV, B4U Networks, Yash Raj Films, Shemaroo Entertainment and Zoom TV, with many more content and distribution annoucements to follow in the coming weeks.

  • Principal

    ASI Entertainment

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Market Research industry)

    February 2001 — November 2005 (4 years 10 months)

    Chase was a principal owner in ASI Entertainment. As president of Consumer Dynamics in 2000, he led the acquisition of ASI prior to founding eLearning Dynamics in Washington, DC, in 2001, expanding their IP from entertainment and politics into the education and training markets.

    ASI pioneered the use of one-to-one, live response technology in televisual research during the 1960s. Then part of Columbia Pictures and now privately held by Chase and his partners, ASI brings over four decades of leadership in television program, advertising and ad-hoc market research. ASI's clients include ABC, CBS, NBC, Discovery, MTV, UPN, WB, Fox, CNN, HBO, BET, Paramount, Showtime and many more.

    ASI's ViewTrac technologies are cross-licensed with TRC, extending these innovations through LearnTrac and PocketClassroom, delivering data collection and analysis applications to smartphones and mobility devices via all leading wireless protocols.

  • Chairman, Founder & EVP

    The Readiness Company (dba - eLearning Dynamics)

    (Privately Held; Computer Software industry)

    April 2001 — October 2005 (4 years 7 months)

    Founded and led company through first $3M in investment and sales. Conceived, designed and commercially released company's core software offerings. Recruited seasoned board of directors and veteran CEO. Established strategic investment and co-marketing partnerships with senior executives at HP, Palm, Microsoft and Symbol Technologies.

    In 2004, palmOne and other channel parters began distributing Readiness Company's LearnTrac™ solutions nationwide. In 2005, the company expanded into colleges of pharmacy, medicine and nursing, while adapting its IP to a broader range of smartphone applications with global reach. Readiness set the pace for wireless, one-to-one software and utilities for handhelds, laptops, and smartphones.
