  • Design Engineer

    Calex

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Electrical/Electronic Manufacturing industry)

    July 1996 — November 2000 (4 years 5 months)

    With project engineering responsibility, designed cost effective high frequency DC-DC switch mode converter power supplies. Designs included power MOSFETs, ultrafast and schottky rectifiers, voltage and current mode PWM controllers, opto-isolators, and voltage references. Designed transformers and inductors using various ferrite and powder cores for use within the converters. Supervised critical PCB layout for minimizing parasitic inductances and capacitances. Designs included wide input voltage range forward mode and push/pull converters with single/multiple regulated outputs including:
    • Standard product family of converters with wide input/temperature range, single output, 15 watt, in a 1"x2" package.
    • Sophisticated 5-output converter with wide input/temperature range, 70 watt output in a 3"x4" footprint. Compliance with European Telecommunications Standards Institute (ETSI) standard for electromagnetic compatibility. Production rate over 10,000 units/year.

  • Design Engineer

    ETM

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Electrical/Electronic Manufacturing industry)

    October 1989 — June 1996 (6 years 9 months)

    As project engineer, designed, supervised construction, and tested high power/high voltage linear power supply systems, test systems, and microwave equipment for use with radar and communication electron tube applications. Performed printed circuit board level to systems level design incorporating both digital and analog circuitry. Designed critical fault detection circuits employing high speed operational amplifiers, comparators, and HCMOS family digital logic circuitry. Specified EPROM embedded software requirements for interfacing with hardware input/output circuits for sophisticated system control and then tested implementation. Designed products to meet customer/military specifications including electromagnetic susceptibility requirements.
    Projects included:
    • 1.3 Megawatt peak, 16 kilowatt average, microwave high power amplifier to be used in the Air Force Range Standardization and Automation program.
