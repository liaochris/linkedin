Brandon Merkl’s Summary

Biography:
Though my stomping grounds include Denver, Colorado and locations in Eastern Europe, I now bid my time here at the University of Tennessee. Despite having a typical childhood and an adolescence riddled with pyromania, I now spend my time channeling my efforts into more beneficial societal roles. An avid traveler, I have seen action in Southeast Asia, bits of the Middle East (including a brief foray into the Sinai Peninsula), and most of middle Europe. Back on his continent, I have road tripped through 47 of the lower 48 (North Dakota has never had a lot of draw for me), as well as several Canadian provinces. I enjoy skiing, mountain biking, and late-night trysts with 14k-foot mountains in the winter.

Dissertation:
My dissertation will relate developing the signal processing and communications requisite of a Local Positioning System which is being built under the supervision of Dr. Mahfouz along with fellow students Michael Kuhn and Alex Zhang. By developing and augmenting the IEEE UWB channel model, this work aims to numerically simulate the presence of multipath interference in the operating room. The multipath interference can subsequently be mitigated using a number of subspace-based methods such as Independent Component Analysis or Hankel Total Least Squares. Another goal of this work is finding the optimal placement of receiving base-stations to maximize the performance of the system. The design specification of the system is to localize 30-40 tools in real-time while maintaining sub-millimeter accuracy. A large body of the work will involve information processing of an inherently stochastic process.
