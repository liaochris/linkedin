Robert Land’s Summary

Business Visionaire

Along with my work focused on changing the face of consumer marketing in the healthcare, entertainment and fashion segments, I'm investing my energy, experience and leadership ability to help emerging Entrepreneurs evolve to be "Business Rock Stars" in their respective field(s).

The storyline of my career centers on having a global perspective in implementing marketing and technology enabled business strategies that generate brand value and sustainable revenue. I’ve operated across several B2B market segments as well as on the B2C side of retail, healthcare and the entertainment sectors. My success has always been predicated on taking warranted risks backed by market insight research and hands-on business experience. More importantly, my approach in executing a plan has always come down to taking personal accountability for melding the business strategy, human capital, technology and marketing discipline as one.   

The value proposition that I bring to the table is one of an experienced innovator that has the vision of a Strategist, savvy of a Marketing Executive, and dynamic of a CIO. And I’m best associated with a series of key attributes that include having the ability to:  
  

• Listen, analyze and visualize solutions that create value for the business and customers I serve.
• Manage global clients from a revenue growth and P&L basis. 
• Develop Internet enabled business strategies that drive new revenue routes and sales process optimization. 
• Integrate e-commerce, on-demand, social media and mobile enabled technology to power new business opportunities.
• Manage strategic partnerships and relationships. 
• Take direction from a client with limited information other than a roadmap written down on a napkin.
• Serve as a mentor and coach in developing human capital and future business leaders. 
• Guide Entrepreneurs and Corporate Climbers to envision what is possible and working with them to make it happen.

Robert Land’s Specialties:

Directing service rich entrepreneurial businesses in developing sustainable revenue routes through a process of melding the right strategy, people, marketing, interactive technology and customer experience as one.

Robert Land’s Experience

  • Managing Partner

    Vision2Venture, LLC

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    February 2007 — Present (1 year 11 months)

    I drive the vision, business development and overall management of the firm.

    In late 2006, I initiated and funded a research project called Social Nomads. The focus of the project was to explore the impact of emotional and impulse buying behavior of consumers at points of heightened interest during unique customer and social experiences. In March 2008, I assembled the technology, business model and team to commercialize Social Nomads into a global service.

    Social Nomads is a member only marketing service that connects consumers with “things of interest” that get purchased, activated and redeemed from their mobile telephone. Through a proprietary Web-to-Wallet service delivery model, Social Nomads combines a Customer Experience Marketing discipline with a proven interactive, mobile and persuasive messaging technology platform that is designed to enhance brand value and drive incremental revenue opportunities for consumer marketers.

  • Chief Information Officer

    i-Trax, Inc.

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Health, Wellness and Fitness industry)

    June 2005 — February 2007 (1 year 9 months)

    In May 2005, I had moved back to the US after living between Dubai and London as a result of divesting from a successful venture in the wireless market. A trusted colleague from my past called to inquire if I was interested in participating in a exciting healthcare venture he was part of called i-Trax.

    I joined i-Trax June 28, 2005 as a member of the leadership team. My mission was to drive process improvement, establish profitable service offerings, delight customers and deliver shareholder value. An integral part of my role was managing the information technology side of Sarbanes-Oxley, driving lean Sigma and Six Sigma adoption and transforming the business to operate in an end-to-end Internet enabled information access model. In addition to my role as CIO, I had executive responsibility for numerous clients in support of the company’s sales and customer experience disciplines. Walgreen Co. ultimately acquired i-Trax in early 2008.

  • Executive VP-Marketing & Technology

    Outercurve, Inc.

    (Wireless industry)

    March 2003 — May 2005 (2 years 3 months)

    In January 2003, I was approached by a previous business acquaintance about joining Outercurve. The company had been acquired out of bankruptcy and was in desperate need of a complete marketing and technology makeover. My role included the strategy, technology, product development and marketing functions of the business.   

    The company launched InfoEdge, a technology driven service that extended the Bloomberg and Reuter’s financial desktop along with the Wall Street Journal and Financial Times web news service to a Blackberry. The service freed brokers, analysts and investment bankers from their desks and provided anywhere, anytime access to critical alert driven market-changing information.

    A significant part of my job involved traveling and living in various parts of the world to expand the business including London, Amsterdam, Bahrain, Saudi Arabia and Dubai. As part of a planned exit strategy, the company was sold to a private concern based in the UAE in 2006.

  • Founder & CEO

    OnlyBrands, Inc.

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    December 1998 — February 2002 (3 years 3 months)

    In 1998, I launched OnlyBrands.com that included a portfolio of over one hundred plus vertical “OnlyBrands” web sites. The site that gained the most attraction was OnlyMusicians.com.

    The best way to describe OnlyMusicians.com is to compare it to MySpace for unsigned music Artists, but with a carefully architected technology framework where an Artist could have their own site within a site along with the ability to participate in virtual local music scenes, sell their digital music offering and promoting the band. OnlyMusicians.com competed predominately with MP3.com but our focus was showcasing and feeding talent to A&R executives.

    At it’s peak, OnlyMusicians.com had approximately 8,000 independent Artists from twelve countries. The company was sold along with the associated e-commerce technology assets in 2002.

  • Vice President

    InteliHealth, an Aetna Company

    (Health, Wellness and Fitness industry)

    2001 — 2002 (1 year)

    InteliHealth is an industry leading Internet health portal and wholly owned subsidiary of Aetna in association with the Harvard Medical School.

    I joined InteliHealth responsible for the strategy, marketing and business development functions. The impact of the dotcom bust impaired the company’s existing strategy of selling web-based health content. Searching for new revenue producing options, I identified an opportunity to leverage InteliHealth’s information and technology assets to retain existing Aetna customers and attract new corporate accounts. The idea spawned the development of an Internet based, on-demand health and wellness service marketed as “Simple Steps to a Healthier Life”.

    My role transformed into supporting the selling efforts of regional directors in retaining and closing new business for Aetna. Today, Simple Steps continues to serve as a key component to Aetna’s marketing strategy. I left the company after Intelihealth was formally merged into Aetna.

  • Founder & CEO

    Adaptive Strategies, Inc.

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    November 1983 — November 1998 (15 years 1 month)

    In 1983, I saw an opportunity that was spawned by the introduction of the Personal Computer (PC) that focused on developing “interoperability” solutions that used the PC as an access point to information across the enterprise. I was smart enough to realize that the company's success would be predicated on surrounding myself with the best technical talent available.

    By 1990, Adaptive Strategies had developed a solid reputation with the top Fortune 1000 companies in the New York, Philadelphia and Washington, DC corridor. It was at this point of the Company’s evolution that we transitioned into Client Server Computing. Then came the call from Microsoft that changed everything.

    In 1991, Adaptive Strategies was selected as one of the original nineteen Microsoft Solution Provider Partners. We operated offices in New York, Chicago and San Francisco.

    Needless to say I was part of computer industry history, right up to the start of the dotcom era.

  • Regional and Commercial OEM Marketing

    Digital Equipment Corporation

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; DEC; Computer Hardware industry)

    November 1980 — December 1982 (2 years 2 months)

    My role involved managing commercial OEM accounts in the Mid-Atlantic region as well as supporting the launch and marketing of the DEC Rainbow (IBM PC clone) and MicroVax Computing line.

  • IT Analyst/Buyer

    Campbell Soup Company

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; CPB; Consumer Goods industry)

    June 1977 — October 1980 (3 years 5 months)

    I was initially hired as a summer intern in the position of Information Center analyst at Campbell Soup's World Headquarters in Camden, NJ. I accepted an offer and opportunity to stay on with the company full time and completed my under graduate degree at Villanova University in the evening. After two years in IT, I was promoted to work in corporate purchasing as a buyer- responsible for IT, office systems and managing the Wharton Econometric Forecasting Associates (WEFA) forecasting modeling system for commodity buyers. My time at Campbell Soup served as a valuable foundation for my career and understanding of business as a whole.
