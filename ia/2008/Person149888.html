Peter Hoang’s Summary

I have a background in both network administration in the field of Information Technology and analytical chemistry. My past work experience ranges from young startups to Fortune 500 companies and federal agencies such as the Department of the Interior. Over the last three years, my vocational focus has shifted to improving end-of-life solutions for electronic waste.

At the age of 30, I co-founded Techno+Rescue with the mission of emphasizing client data security, creating public awareness on sustainability, and advocating more stringent standards and business practices in the electronics recycling industry.

I currently manage internal operations and am integral in business development and marketing, while also serving as liason to community outreach programs.

Peter Hoang’s Specialties:

Branding and corporate identity, internet marketing strategies, search engine optimization, business development, environmentally-responsible and sustainable practices, and operational logistics improvement, information technology, computer recycling, e-waste disposal, environmental science

Peter Hoang’s Experience

  • Owner

    Techno+Rescue

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    September 2006 — Present (1 year 7 months)

    I co-founded Techno+Rescue in 2006 after being disillusioned with past working experiences within the electronics recycling industry. I felt that the overall industry was reckless with client data, did not deliver the proper disposal practices they promised to their customers, and often cut massive corners for a quick buck.

    I believe the mission of my company is rooted in emphasizing client data security, creating public awareness on sustainability, and advocating more stringent standards and business practices in the electronics recycling industry.

    I created the company branding and corporate identity and have an ultimate vision of creating a all-IT-solution portal for small to medium-sized businesses--to rescue them from any technologically-related headaches.

  • Business Manager

    Computers House (Sole Proprietorship)

    (Sole Proprietorship; 1-10 employees; Computer Hardware industry)

    April 2006 — August 2006 (5 months)

    Computers House was a retail computer repair business that is currently owned by a family relative. Without proper management, it was a fledgling part-time operation and mostly untended to by the proprietor. Immediately after taking over management of the shop, I revamped the branding, revitalized the marketing strategy, and improved operational procedures.

    Within a few months, it became a sustainable and modestly successful business. I left the business shortly thereafter to launch my own company. As of this day, Computers House is a fully operational shop and achieves greater success each fiscal quarter.
