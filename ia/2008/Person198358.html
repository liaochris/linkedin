Jim Walker’s Summary

Analyzing, creating, and developing new business opportunities in energy markets.

Entrepreneur who started one energy services business unit, one marketing company for energy and high technology clients, and two non-profit clean energy organizations.

Received the 2006 Presidential Citation from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) and the 2005 All Star Volunteer Award from the MIT Enterprise Forum for creating and co-founding the Ignite Clean Energy Business Presentation Competition.

Jim Walker’s Experience

  • Managing Director, Energy

    Global Insight, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 501-1000 employees; Information Services industry)

    October 2006 — Present (1 year 8 months)

    Responsible for North American energy business line leadership, including business development and thought leadership. Client engagements include a clean energy technology business census, an off-shore wind energy economic study, cyber-security IT marketing message to utilities, LNG terminal economic benefit study, a North American natural gas supply study, and a study of North Atlantic LNG pricing and contracting practices at United States terminals.

    Travel extensively across North America and Europe to meet with energy companies.

  • Co-founder and First Chair Energy SIG and Ignite Clean Energy Competiton

    MIT Enterprise Forum

    (Educational Institution; Renewables & Environment industry)

    2004 — 2007 (3 years)

    Volunteer Service.

    Co-founded the MIT Enterprise Forum's Energy Special Interest Group to spur the creation of the clean energy technology cluster in the Boston, MA area.

    Created and co-founded the MIT Enterprise Forum's Ignite Clean Energy Business Competition to help grow clean energy technology start-up companies. Raised the sponsorship funds, led the organizing committee, sold the idea to the MIT EF Board of Directors and donors, and helped recruit and organize 75 volunteers.

  • Director, Global Utilities & Energy Business Development

    Sun Microsystems, Inc.

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Sunw; Computer Hardware industry)

    February 2005 — October 2006 (1 year 9 months)

    Responsible for business development and thought leadership to grow Sun’s global utility sales. This strategy was focused on winning new proposal opportunities for Sun’s sales reps through presentations to CIOs, industry analysts, independent software vendors (ISVs), and system integrators (SIs). Also, wrote blogs and online articles on technology solutions that would be of high interest to CIOs.

    The result was the largest sales growth to utilities, a customer segment Sun had previously under served. Traveled extensively across North America, Europe, India, and Australia to meet with senior utility executives.

  • President

    Techadoption, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    January 2002 — February 2005 (3 years 2 months)

    Founded TechAdoption, Inc., a marketing communications firm that helped technology and energy companies attract and close new business with utilities and commercial & industrial customers. TechAdoption analyzed the right marketing message, tested the message with focus groups of business leaders, framed the compelling big idea, and developed the presentations and white papers to influence executive decision-makers.

  • Senior Analyst, Energy and Industrial eBusiness Strategies

    Forrester Research, Inc.

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; Market Research industry)

    April 2000 — January 2003 (2 years 10 months)

    Analyzed business-to-business (B2B) strategies for energy trading and industrial corporations. Evaluated technology solutions for improving the success and liquidity of these online markets. Wrote more than 30 analysis reports, interviewed by the press, and spoke at major industry conferences.

  • Senior Associate

    CERA

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; IHS; Oil & Energy industry)

    August 1999 — April 2000 (9 months)

    Following my graduation from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), I consulted to Cambridge Energy Research Associates to write a scenario analysis report on electric utility deregulation.

  • Senior Vice President

    XENERGY, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Utilities industry)

    April 1986 — June 1999 (13 years 3 months)

    Founded and built XENERGY’s energy services business. The business bought and sold natural gas and electricity on behalf of large commercial and industrial clients, as well as providing engineering services to improve energy efficiency at our clients' commercial & industrial facilities.

    Hired, trained and managed more than 40 engineers, energy purchasing managers, and sales staff. Developed a customer relationship management system to track energy purchases and sales, daily energy balances with utilities, and manage billing and accounting functions. Launched three new services, which garnered national press attention and publicity -- helping the business attract venture capital investment, top industry managers, and fortune 100 alliance partners.

  • Corporate Energy Manager

    GTE

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; GTE; Telecommunications industry)

    1982 — 1986 (4 years)

    Responsible for increasing GTE's energy efficiency and lowering annual energy costs at 100 manufacturing and chemical plants across North America. Programs included negotiating and arranging well-head natural gas purchases to each plant and energy audits of industrial processes and commercial buildings.

  • New Product Development Engineer, Heat Pumps

    Carrier Air Conditioning Company

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Electrical/Electronic Manufacturing industry)

    1976 — 1979 (3 years)

    Lead product development engineer for a new line of energy-efficient heat pumps. Patent received on the heat pump design.
