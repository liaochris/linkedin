Trina DasGupta’s Summary

In an increasingly fragmented media landscape, Trina DasGupta provides strategic clarity and break-through marketing concepts for consumer brands, non-profits, and political organizations, achieving deep audience engagement for each client.

Recently, Trina developed Planned Parenthood’s national Online Services division, creating the division’s strategic plan and future priorities for interactive service, education, and advocacy in the evolving Web 2.0 world. She has also worked with new media innovators, such as the rapidly growing events-based social networking site Going.com, to define their long-term brand strategy and cause-related marketing plan. Currently, she is in Johannesburg developing the national mobile marketing strategy for South Africa's largest youth HIV prevention NGO, loveLife.

Prior to consulting, Trina was the Manager of Integrated Marketing & New Business Development at MTV Networks. In this role, she created multi-platform marketing campaigns for MTV’s advertising partners, including Cingular/AT&T, Dunkin’ Donuts, Intel, JCPenney, Kohl’s, Sharpie, and Starburst, among many other top consumer brands. In less than two years, Trina’s ideas generated over $31 million in new revenue for the company.

In addition to her marketing roles, Trina has worked on the Emmy award-winning Know HIV/AIDS and Staying Alive campaigns for Viacom and MTV International respectively. Trina has also worked in the political space for various groups, including Rock the Vote, John Kerry for President, the American Democracy Institute, and the Democratic National Committee’s Trailblazers Young Trustee Program.

Trina is a graduate of the University of Pennsylvania with a Bachelor of Science in Business & Public Policy from the Wharton School and a Bachelor of Arts in Communications from Annenberg.
