Mike Kaplan’s Summary

I am responsible for NASA science mission business development within Boeing. I develop and refine marketing plans, initiate and lead pursuit/bid/proposal decision process, conduct competitive assessments, and frame teaming agreements. I develop and lead the implementation of capture plans in NASA science market sector at Boeing. I am responsible for:

• Developing and refining new business strategies in the pursuit of new opportunities in the NASA's Science market - a totally new market to Boeing that represents significant growth potential for the company;
• Leading new business teams comprised of all relevant Boeing functional organizations (e.g., business management & finance, contracts, legal, export, people. engineering, supplier management & procurement, production, and site managers) as well other Boeing business units and sites, academic institutions, other companies, Government laboratories, NASA field centers, as well as international organizations in the pursuit of new NASA robotic exploration and science business opportunities;
• Manage a business develop budget that has ranged from $1 to $3 million/year in the pursuit of new business opportunities;
• Develop near- and long-term business plans and define IR&D plans in support of these plans;
• Maintain liaison with NASA in the identification of new program requirements to enhance our new business development initiatives and increase revenue contribution. In addition I conduct periodic customer satisfaction surveys to measure NASA's perception of Boeing,
• Develop and deliver messaging derived from our marketing plans to our customers as well coordinate these messages with Boeing's communications office in support of press releases, and
• Led Boeing's team that won a TPF architecture study contract: Boeing's first science mission contract in over 20 years. This led to the creation of my current position.
