alison horton’s Summary

File Clerk

I am looking for a permanent records clerk position. I am between jobs because of an unrelated injury. I am completely healed now and eager to begin filing again. I have used my time wisely to take classes in Excel and Access.

I have extensive experience organizing files for different types of law. I have entirely reworked one law firm’s files, significantly improved another firm’s entire filing department, and created an organizational structure for a third. I have a strong affinity for records management. It’s a role I both enjoy and excell at.

When I worked for Ruiz and Sperow, I was the filing department. I had a great deal of control over the records. I reworked a filing system that had long standing errors. I straightened out the shelves and reworked the indices so that every single subfile had it’s own name and it’s own set of numbers and could be easily found. I made a number of small changes that have had long lasting, major effects.

At McKenna my work life changed substantially. The filing staff was diverse and the amount of filing overwhelming. I organized an efficient sorting system so that documents could be put away properly and quickly. I created a spreadsheet to identify documents by group number. I rewrote the filing protocol at McKenna’s request. I created the pleadings and discovery indicies and trained the other file clerks. I created an Excel workbook to keep track of it. I took steps to correct scanning system.

At Bell Rosenberg and Hughes, I created from scratch the organizational structure for a very large file. Because I have a paralegal certificate, they were able to bill for my time. This was a temporary position.

I’m organized, efficient, and hardworking. I prioritize my duties. I’m a self-starter and fill in before being asked if I anticipate a need. My work area is clean at the end of the day.

I look forward to learning news ways to organize and new records management systems.
