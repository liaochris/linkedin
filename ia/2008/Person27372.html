  • Co-founder

    New England Energy Innovation Collaborative (NEEIC)

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Venture Capital & Private Equity industry)

    July 2006 — March 2007 (9 months)

    The New England Energy Innovation Collaborative (NEEIC) aims to be the globally recognized, best of breed business cluster for energy innovation in New England. I played a significant part in NEEIC's inception by helping to identify the opportunity, define NEEIC's mission and establish the early, critical relationships. I also led the creation of NEEIC's flagship program, the NEEIC $150,000 Energy Business Creation Competition, and successfully executed the proposed plan by attracting significant energy-related deal flow well above our quantity and quality goals. NEEIC now has a permanent Managing Director, many program sponsors and the support of the east coast venture capitalst and entrepreneurial communities.

  • Summer Associate / Cleantech Consultant

    Advanced Technology Ventures (ATV)

    (Partnership; 11-50 employees; Venture Capital & Private Equity industry)

    June 2005 — October 2006 (1 year 5 months)

    Advanved Technology Ventures (ATV) is a bi-coastal venture capital firm managing over $1.4 billion in capital. I spent my summer MBA internship at ATV as a Summer Associate / Cleantech Consultant. My primary project was to formulated ATV’s cleantech / enertech early stage equity investment strategy. This project included comprehensive landscape, trends, deal flow, financial performance & projection analysis for solar, biofuels, water, energy efficiency, energy storage, hydrogen, fuel cells and other segments. This project concluded when I presented the results to the ATV partnership. In addition to this project, I consulted at ATV on several due diligence efforts related to their existing cleantech deal flow.

  • Investment Associate

    MA Green Energy Fund

    (Partnership; 1-10 employees; Venture Capital & Private Equity industry)

    January 2006 — February 2006 (2 months)

    MA Green Energy Fund (MGEF) is a private venture capital firm investing in a broad range of early stage energy technologies. While an extern there, I conducted due diligence on several potential deals related to fuel cells, photovoltaics and fuel processing. I utilized my personal network and expertise to perform analytical analysis on several potential deals and played a significant role in due diligence for MGEF's investment in CTP Hydrogen.

  • Director of Member Services

    US Fuel Cell Council

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Renewables & Environment industry)

    2003 — 2005 (2 years)

    The USFCC is the premier international industry association for the emerging fuel cell industry and works to commercialize hydrogen and fuel cell technologies within the United States.

    At the USFCC, I expanded the organization's program portfolio and helped to position it as the global leader for several issues including the micro fuel cell transportation regulatory effort. While at the USFCC, I had the pleasure of working closely with 120+ USFCC industry members (Motorola, UTC Power, Delphi, Siemens, DuPont, Gore, BIC, Intel, Toshiba, Casio, etc) facilitating group interactions and leading meetings that progressed this emerging industry towards commecialization milestones.

  • Technical Director

    Breakthrough Technologies Institute

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Renewables & Environment industry)

    2001 — 2003 (2 years)

    Breakthrough Technologies Institute (BTI) is an environmental energy technology think tank in Washington DC.

    At BTI, I managed the technical aspects of all BTI projects, including Fuel Cells 2000 and Bus Rapid Transit (BRT). I helped to significantly expand the BTI program portfolio and relationships. Speaking credits: Keynote at International Fuel Cell Forum in Korea, 2004 FedFleet in NYC, International PowerGen 2004 in FL, Wharton’s Mack Center for Technological Innovation, top Chinese government official meeting in Beijing, NASA's Langley Research Center Colloquium Lecture Series, Chairman of Hanover Fair's Excellence in Fuel Cells conference.
