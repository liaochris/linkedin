Germain Saval’s Summary

At the end of my studies in Physics and Computer Science, I specialised in safety critical software design and development. I had the opportunity to join the Faculty of Computer Science of the University of Namur (FUNDP). I am pursuing a doctoral research in the domain of software engineering, specifically: requirements engineering (elicitation and description of user/customer needs) and formal development methods (automatic reasoning, proof and verification of software). I am currently working on selection methods for “off-the-shelf” software components and their integration in the general software development process. I am also responsible for teaching activities in object-oriented design and development (Java), and information systems modelling (UML).

Germain Saval’s Specialties:

computer science, software engineering, requirements engineering, COTS selection, formal methods, conceptual modelling, model driven engineering, model driven architecture, Java, UML
