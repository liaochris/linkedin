Marian Nodine’s Experience

  • Senior Research Scientist

    Telcordia Technologies

    (Public Company; Computer Software industry)

    September 2000 — Present (7 years 7 months)

  • Member Technical Staff

    Microelectronics and Computer Technology Corporation

    (Computer Software industry)

    1996 — 2000 (4 years)

    Marian was a researcher and one of the technical leads on the InfoSleuth consortial project at MCC in Austin, TX. She worked on the agent infrastructure, developing the prototype for the agent communication layer and the generic agent functionality. She also worked on query processing over multiple (legacy) heterogeneous data sources in InfoSleuth, and oversaw work on infrastructure support for agents as well as core agent functionality such as brokering and ontology management. During the course of this project, she had to deal with many issues of interoperability, including middleware support, communication paradigms, data integration (including structural mapping and value mapping issues), and incorporation of legacy and COTS applications. InfoSleuth was used in several government and commercial contracts.
    In addition, Marian was a key member of the Agent Engineering project at MCC, which examined methodologies for developing and evolving agents and agent-based systems.

  • Postdoctoral Research Fellow / Adjunct Assistant Professor

    Brown University

    (Higher Education industry)

    1993 — 1996 (3 years)

    Marian's research was in the areas of object-oriented query optimization and advanced database transaction models. She also supervised industry-track Master’s students in collaborative programming projects. In 1995-1996, she taught sections of an introductory class in object-oriented programming.

  • Senior Member of the Technical Staff

    BBN Laboratories

    (Computer Networking industry)

    1985 — 1988 (3 years)

    As part of the Automated Network Management Project, Marian worked on early tools and user interfaces for Internet monitoring and management. She also did consulting work on the Packet Radio Network project.

  • Staff Scientist

    Sequoia Systems

    (Computer Software industry)

    1984 — 1985 (1 year)

    Sequoia Systems was a startup company building fault-tolerant multiprocessors. Marian participated in teams to develop fault-tolerant database systems. She also implemented the X.25 network protocols on their proprietary fault-tolerant computer system.

  • Member, Technical Staff

    Bolt, Beranek and Newman

    (Computer Networking industry)

    1981 — 1984 (3 years)

    As part of the Wideband Satellite Network Project, Marian helped implement the satellite channel scheduler and switch, and integrated the switch with other components of the system including the voice funnel, the modems and decoders, and the satellite link.
