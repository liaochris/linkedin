Dirk Lester’s Summary

FADE IN:

Thunder, a sporadic bursting gale and the blood-burnt twilight, washed a young man now known as Dirk Lester ashore, at Rio de Janeiro, nearly a decade ago—two days into a particularly debauched Carnival, with two 7.62 millimeter slugs and a curare-tipped dart in his back, ten-thousand francs and a flask of single malt scotch in his hip pocket and of course little or no memory of a past life. His love of both comic books and films began right then and there—when, as fate would have it, he was forced to dispatch one of several would-be assassins by garroting him with an illusive copy of Deadly Hands of Kung Fu # 57, then hide out for several long months in a bedraggled movie palace run by a formally black-listed ex-stuntman—who carefully coaxed the younger man back to full health while holding him hostage to screenings of nothing but obscure Euro-spy flicks, B-grade monster movies and Sam Peckinpah inspired Japanese samurai films twenty-four hours a day . . .

TO:

Following his daring escape from that mad, though well-intentioned, stuntman’s clutches, Dirk gradually wandered northward, reeducating himself at Georgetown University’s Georgetown College and Columbia University's School of the Arts, and at an eclectic assortment of occupations ranging form the sublime …Greenpeace Organizer, Child Welfare Advocate, Newspaper Reporter … to the patently ridiculous … Personnel Bodyguard, International “Currier,” a brief stint as a “Corporate Spy” inside Ken Starr’s infamous Kirkland and Ellis to the pretty darn enjoyable ... Comic Book Writer, Entertainment Industry Futurist, founding host of the MovieWeb.com column "The Daily Spin," and co-author of the 2006 book Science Fiction America. Currently, he walks the Earth sharing the Zen of SEO, SEM, SMM, SMO, PPC, VMM, OPR and New Media Marketing & Communications with Ad Men and entrepreneurs from Chicago to Shanghai.
