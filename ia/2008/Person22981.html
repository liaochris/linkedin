Andrew Evans’s Experience

  • President

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    2005 — Present (3 years)

    Building on his experience at CareerBuilder, Market10/JobFox.com and Presidio Studios, Andrew now provides user interface design and usability consulting on a contract basis to clients throughout the US.

  • User Interface Lead Developer

    market10.com (now JobFox.com)

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Staffing and Recruiting industry)

    2004 — 2005 (1 year)

    Andrew was tapped once again by online career guru Rob McGovern to join the startup team at industry game-changer Market10 (now called JobFox.com). Andrew was on the founding product specification team and provided all of the initial product information architecture at both the site and page levels. Andrew left Market10 after beta to provide IT assistance to the Red Cross during the Hurricane Katrina disaster.

  • Senior Technical Partner

    Presidio Studios Inc.

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    2002 — 2004 (2 years)

    Andrew co-founded Presidio Studios in his adopted hometown of Lewisburg, WV. Presidio provides interactive media consulting and advertising design to small-to-medium-sized businesses throughout the region.

  • Director of Application Development

    CareerBuilder.com

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; Staffing and Recruiting industry)

    1996 — 2002 (6 years)

    Andrew was Employee #5 at CareerBuilder, tapped by CareerBuilder founders Rob McGovern and Jim Winchester to be part of the startup "tiger team." He built and led the Application Development team within CareerBuilder's Engineering department. Andrew's team built all of the public-facing user interace for CareerBuilder's award-winning and industry-defining online career site, from launch through CareerBuilder's IPO and beyond. Andrew was personally responsible for the information architecture and usability engineering of each version of CareerBuilder's suite of sites during his tenure. Andrew's team also handled all affiliate customization for CareerBuilder's partner career sites, including MSN, ESPN, and Black Entertainment Television.
