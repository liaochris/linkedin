Jára Cimrman’s Summary

I proposed the Panama Canal to the U.S. government, including a libretto for an opera of the same name. I reformed the school system in Galicia. With Count Zeppelin I constructed the first rigid airship using Swedish steel and Czech wicker (the wicker being for the cabin). I was deported from Germany as an anarchist, and my personal documents carried a note that I was "a source of unrest." This led the Swiss company Omega to offer me a job to improve the balance wheel for their Piccolo line of ladies' watches. While in Switzerland, I introduced (and practised for some time) the profession of obstetrician, under the difficult Alpine conditions. I conducted investigations about the life of Arctic tribes who eat their fellows; and once, while running away from a furious tribe, I missed the North Pole by a mere seven meters.

In Paraguay I created the first puppet-show. In Vienna I established a school of criminology, music and ballet. I corresponded with G.B. Shaw for many years, but unfortunately the dogged Irishman never replied. I invented yoghurt. I generously helped many great scientists: on his own back I carried forty five tubs of pitchblende to the basement of Mr. and Mrs. Curie, I assisted Prof. Burian with his first plastic surgery, I reworked the electrical contact on Edison's first lightbulb, and I found an underlease for Mr. Eiffel. I am the creator of the philosophy of Externism. Because of my enthusiasm for natural sciences, I discovered the monopole (as opposed to the then well known dipole), but this discovery fell into oblivion until it was confusedly revived by 20th century economists.
