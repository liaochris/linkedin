Jeff Sanders’s Summary

Business executive and strategic advisor with extensive financial and corporate development experience, including eight years as a CFO, and eight as a banker, and six as a strategic consultant. Executive financial management experience in telecom, media and technology, including cable TV, Internet and information services. Banking and strategic consulting experience in telecom, media, programming, wireless, software, information systems and related B2B services and technology.

Jeff Sanders’s Specialties:

Financial executive - accounting, financial reporting, internal controls, budgeting and cash management; debt, mezzanine and equity financings; acquisitions, divestitures and other strategic negotiations; and Board membership and reporting.
Strategic consulting and advisory - strategic planning and business development; mergers, acquisitions and divestitures; debt, mezzanine and equity placements.

Jeff Sanders’s Experience

  • Executive Vice President

    Liberty Transnational

    (Management Consulting industry)

    January 2002 — Present (7 years)

Advise clients on strategic planning, business development, mergers, acquisitions, divestitures, buyouts, restructurings, capital formation and other strategic initiatives. Clients include entrepreneurs, business owners, corporations and private equity groups.

  • Founder and Chief Financial Officer

    Millennium Digital Media

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Telecommunications industry)

    May 1997 — December 2001 (4 years 8 months)

    Now part of Broadstripe, Millennium was among the nation's 20 largest cable MSOs. Co-founded Millennium and served on its seven-member board of directors. Built and led team of 12 employees covering all finance, treasury, accounting, financial reporting and corporate development activities. Completed 18 acquisitions and arranged $550 million in financing from 20 senior and mezzanine lenders and private equity investors. Built Millennium into a leading provider of competitive broadband and Internet services, generating over $120 million in annual revenue.

  • Executive Vice President and CFO

    Charter Communications

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; CHTR; Telecommunications industry)

    January 1994 — September 1996 (2 years 9 months)

    Charter is the nation's third largest cable MSO, serving over 5.7 million customers. Joined Charter as its seventh employee and served on Charter's five-member management committee (its senior policy making body). Built and led team of 40 employees covering all finance, treasury, accounting, financial reporting and corporate development activities. Completed 22 acquisitions, representing $1.6 billion and one million customers. Arranged over $1.8 billion in financing from a group of more than 100 senior lenders, high-yield investors, strategic partners and private equity investors.

  • Director and Unit Head, Mergers & Acquisitions

    Toronto Dominion Securities

    (Investment Banking industry)

    September 1991 — January 1994 (2 years 5 months)

    Toronto Dominion Securities is the New York-based investment unit of Toronto Dominion, one of North America's largest banks. As Director and Unit Head, founded and managed the US M&A practice, offering advisory and private equity placement services to firms in the media and communications industries. Directed $150 million US private equity portfolio, which was deployed both through private equity funds and directly in portfolio companies.

  • Director, Communications Finance

    Toronto Dominion Securities

    (Investment Banking industry)

    July 1989 — September 1991 (2 years 3 months)

    Led team providing leveraged lending and other corporate finance services to US cable television, cellular telephone, cable programming, and other media and communications firms.

  • Commercial Banking Officer

    Centerre Bank, NA (now Bank of America)

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Banking industry)

    July 1984 — August 1987 (3 years 2 months)

    Now part of Bank of America, Centerre was a St. Louis-based regional bank that provided an array of corporate, commercial, retail, and private banking and wealth management services. As a Commercial Banking Officer, managed relationships with St. Louis-based Fortune 500 and middle-market companies in a broad range of industries, from heavy steel fabrication to commercial real estate and prestige travel services. Agented a $30 million syndicated credit facilty, completed a $10 million construction loan for a 150-passenger cruise ship, and placed $8 million in US Maritime Administration guaranteed bonds.
