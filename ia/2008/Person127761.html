Mehmet Akten’s Summary

I entered this world of digital music and visuals in my early teens when the demo scene was quite big, messing about on the BBC Model B, Apple IIe, Amiga500 and various early PCs (8088, 80236, 80386 etc.).

After spending a few years (1995 - 1997) developing database driven apps for the corporate world (banks, telecommunications companies etc.) using VC++ / VB / SQL Server, I started a job making video games (1998 - 2003) and spent the next 6 years slaving for the industry: building / texturing 3D models and writing modules in C/C++ and x86 assembly (physics, particles, visual effects, 3D plugins). After 4 titles (7 SKUs) released on PC, PSX, PS2, XBox, and GameCube [and a DreamCast port that never got released :( ] I had enough of the monopolization of the video games industry and decided to go freelance in 2003. Since then I have worked on a wide variety of projects ranging from commercial / corporate promos to music videos to PHP / MySQL sites and full Flash apps- details of which can be seen on www.memo.tv.

While a lot of my commercial work is generally pre-rendered; my biggest passion is programmatic visuals (combined with music/audio). I do prefer written languages over node based but sometimes do delve into the latter - mainly Quartz Composer, because of its beautiful integration with VDMX (see vdmx.memo.tv for more info).
