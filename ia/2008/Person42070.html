    Two men, a simple toy, and a hundred years of ups and downs.

    Visit us at www.worldonastringyoyo.com for updates.

  • Member, Board of Directors

    Coalition of Asian Pacifics in Entertainment

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Entertainment industry)

    2002 — Present (6 years)

    The Coalition of Asian Pacifics in Entertainment (CAPE) prepares and positions Asian Pacific Americans for leadership in the entertainment and media arts industries.

    It does this through workshops, professional networking events, a contest to launch film and tv writers, and intensive weekend-long "boot camps" (connecting potential tv showrunners and writers with television executives, for example).

    See www.capeusa.org for a full list of Board of Advisors and Directors, plus programs.

  • Writer/Director/Producer, A. EYE FOR THE WHITE GUY

    Tears in Rain Productions

    (Motion Pictures and Film industry)

    July 2003 — August 2003 (2 months)

    An homage to a popular cable makeover show...what if five Asian style mavens helped a hapless white guy reach a higher state of fashion/culinary/design zen?

    Commissioned by Visual Communications for its Digital Slam series of micro-films, featured at a fundraiser for VC.

  • Writer/Director/Producer, RED THREAD

    Tears in Rain Productions

    (Privately Held; Motion Pictures and Film industry)

    February 2002 — August 2002 (7 months)

    Wrote, directed, and produced the independent short romantic comedy RED THREAD, starring Keiko Agena and Eddie Mui. RED THREAD is about the emerging relationship between a playwright and an actor--dating is like auditioning.

    It screened at upwards of 15 film festivals and won a prize. It was later released on dvd by Pathfinder Pictures.

  • Writer-Researcher

    ANCESTORS IN AMERICA (A Center for Educational Telecommunications Production)

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Broadcast Media industry)

    1995 — 1997 (2 years)

    Wrote and researched the script for a 3-part PBS documentary series on the history of South Asian, East, and Southeast Asian Americans in the Americas.

    This project was directed by San Francisco documentary filmmaker Loni Ding, and was supported with grants by the CPB, the NEH, the Ford and Rockefeller Foundations. Part I aired nationally in May, 2001, with Parts II and III airing thereafter.
