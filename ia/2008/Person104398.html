Kelley Holsinger’s Summary

My current position as a Clinical Research Assistant has given me the opportunity to learn the basics and motivated me to enroll in the online Clinical Research Administration Master's Program at The George Washington University. The GWU program will provide me with the background I need to attain my future goal of a leadership position in the health sciences community.

My current clinical research project includes testing a new medication for adults with ADHD at the Duke University ADHD Program. Within the first two months of working at Duke, I quickly learned the specifics of my designated trial and took on the responsibility of guiding participants through study visits without supervision. After only six months, I took on the responsibility of managing various project duties including data collection, documentation and communication with the sponsor, CRO, IRB and sub-sites.

Prior to working at Duke, I held a position at RTI International as a survey methodologist. I gained an in-depth understanding of survey research by conducting large federally funded national research projects. I was responsible for the questionnaire development and testing of one of the largest drug and alcohol field surveys conducted in the United States. I also completed program evaluations, and cognitive interviews contributing to data collection teams with outstanding response rates, surpassing client expectation. Other project responsibilities gave me hands on experience with managing respondent participation, incentives and coordinating field supplies.
