Dr. Rene M. Jansen’s Summary

I just started a new company: http://www.winkwaves.com
Winkwaves is a high tech startup company with the objective: creating a better work environment for busy people like you and me, offering better support for knowledge management, time management, project coordination and (distance) collaboration.

What I did before:
René studied "applied computer science" at the University of Amsterdam, focusing on the business impacts and marketing aspects of computer systems (1987-1992). After his study he worked as a multimedia specialist for CEITO, a small consultancy firm related to the University of Amsterdam, where he advised several companies (Rabobank, KVGO) on the subject of multimedia communication, marketing and e-learning.

In 1997 he was invited to be employed by Bortiboll Communications, one of the most innovative players in the upcoming e-business field, where he worked as a project manager and usability designer for retailers (a.o. Kijkshop and Bijenkorf), industrial companies (a.o. Unilever and Toshiba) and business services companies (a.o. Transavia and UUnet) to help them develop interactive communication solutions.

In 1999 René joined Bit-IC, a specialized e-business practice of Capgemini where he is working as lead consultant on the definition and roll out of the extranet and internet strategy of well known (international) companies; like Robeco, ASICS Europe, DHL and SFB.

Because René is always eager to combine practical solutions with smart thinking, he works as a research fellow one day a week at the University of Amsterdam. After 10 years of professional experience he decided to write a PhD thesis "E-formulas - strategy - design - success" on e-strategy, which he defended for a prominent international committee (i.e. prof. Peter Keen, prof. Joseph Pine) June 6th, 2002.
