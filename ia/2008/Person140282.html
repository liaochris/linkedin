Nicholas Benes’s Summary

I am trained in both law and business (JD-MBA), bilingual in Japanese and English, and specialize in advice on M&A and complex financial transactions/situations in Japan, including corporate governance improvement and the exercise of shareholder rights. I gained broad experience in investment banking during 11 years at JP Morgan, in New York, London and Tokyo. Since 1992 I have headed up my own firm, a independent investment banking boutique in Tokyo. Like Sherlock Holmes in my own way, I would like to work on the assignments that I find particularly interesting and challenging, and/or which contribute to improving the M&A market and corporate governance in Japan.

Nicholas Benes’s Experience

  • Chair, Foreign Direct Investment Committee

    American Chamber of Commerce in Japan (ACCJ)

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Public Policy industry)

    January 2001 — Present (7 years 10 months)

    For the past seven years I have served as the Chair of the Foreign Direct Investment )FDi) Committee at the ACCJ, the leading policy advocacy organization for foreign companies in Japan. In the face of many challenges, the activities of my Committee have had a significant impact on public policy in Japan with respect to FDI, the M&A market, corporate law, and corporate governance. In 2003 I proposed and led the ACCJ's highly successful "FDI Task Force", which made a number of detailed recommendations and commissioned Dr. Kyoji Fukao of Hitotsubashi University to undertake a detailed economic analysis of FDI flows into Japan and their impact on productivity, a report which later became an award-winning book. In 2004-2006, we successfully led a drive to change the Company Law so as to allow cross-border triangular mergers by foreign firms as an acquisition structure, in the face of stiff opposition by the Keidanren.

  • President and Founder

    JTP Corporation

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Investment Banking industry)

    January 1998 — Present (10 years 10 months)

    Advisors on M&A transactions and corporate governance practice in Japan. Also serves as external director appointed to Japanese companies.

    JTP Corporation specializes in mergers and acquisitions, spin-offs, strategic alliances, and joint ventures in Japan. It has proved it can create outstanding deals time and time again. The JTP team has advised Amazon.com, AIG, Johnson Controls, Itochu, Mercian, Mediatti, Cadence Design Systems, Advantest, MapInfo, Clariant, and a variety of other successful firms on transactions in Japan.

    I have served as an “outside director” on a number of corporate boards in Japan, including that of Alps Mapping, Livedoor Holdings, and the TSE-listed company, Cecile. I find that my diverse experience, my training in law and business, my bilingual capability, and my understanding of governance practice and finance, enables me to add significant value to Japanese companies.

  • Independent Director

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; 9937; Retail industry)

This job added significantly to my experience base related to direct marketing and retailing industries in Japan, which I had previously gained in advising Amazon.com, and two other (Japanese) clients.

  • Independent Director

    Livedoor Holdings Co., Ltd.

    (Internet industry)

    December 2006 — December 2007 (1 year 1 month)

    In January 2006, Livedoor was involved in a financial scandal that caused the company to be delisted from the Tokyo Stock Exchange in April 2006. Shortly before the delisting, a number of foreign hedge funds purchased a controlling stake in the company. Most of these funds had no experience in Japan, so they approached me and asked me to join the Board of Directors in order to turn the company around, sell off non-core and underperforming subsidiaries and assets, and develop a strategy for value enhancement and defending the numerous shareholder lawsuits. As a director, I proposed and led a major project to defend on the legal issues of loss causation and damages computation, which were crucial to the lawsuits.

  • Member, Japan Investment Council (JIC)

    Japanese Cabinet

    (Government Agency; 501-1000 employees; Public Policy industry)

    December 2000 — January 2007 (6 years 2 months)

    I served as a member of the Experts Committee"of the Japan Investment Council, one of only three councils that advises the Japanese Cabinet, in this case on policies related to foreign direct investment (FDI). Since M&A transactions are the primary driver of increases to Japan' s cumulative stock of FDI, my experience as an investment banker and M&A advisor was highly relevant and useful.

  • External Director

    Alps Mapping Co., Ltd.

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Publishing industry)

    March 2000 — March 2006 (6 years 1 month)

MapInfo thereafter appointed me as an outside director to serve on the Alps Mapping board of directors. I served until March of 2006, when Alps was acquired by Yahoo Japan.

  • Senior Managing Director

    Kamakura Corporation

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    March 1994 — December 1997 (3 years 10 months)

    Senior Managing Director in charge of investment banking for The Kamakura Corporation from 1994 to 1997. While at Kamakura, my team advised on various transactions including the sale of two leading Japanese accounting software companies, MilkyWay and Nihon Micom, to Intuit; and the sale of Pacifitech, the leading independent software localization firm in Japan. By sheer coincidence in a “small world”, the company formed by the merger of MilkyWay and Nihon Micom (Yayoi Kaikei) was sold for 71 Billion Yen by Livedoor Holdings in 2007 when I was serving as an independent outside director on Livedoor Holdings’ board. This was particularly gratifying, as was the fact that the value of the combined companies had grown by roughly 10 times during the intervening 10 years.

  • Vice President

    JP Morgan

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; JPM; Investment Banking industry)

    September 1983 — March 1994 (10 years 7 months)

    After completing JP Morgan's famous "Commercial Bank Management Program" (CBMP #49), in due course I was promoted to Vice President and worked in New York, Tokyo and London in a wide variety of different product areas, including corporate finance/M&A, capital markets, derivatives and swaps, equity research/sales, structured finance, and real estate. During the last few four years at JP Morgan, I served as head of the European Real Estate Division, Equity and Credit Research Section in Tokyo, and the Financial Institutions Group in Tokyo. While at JP Morgan, among many other transactions, I invented the "inverse floater" bond, advised PaineWebber on an equity tieup with Yasuda Life, designed and closed one of the largest packages of currency swaps ever engineered in the Tokyo market at that time, sold Morgan's European headquarter building to Sumitomo Life, and advised Mitsubishi Estate on the restructuring of Rockefeller Center.
