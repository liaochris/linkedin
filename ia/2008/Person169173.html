Michael Santullo’s Summary

Michael Santullo is a cleantech activist working to accelerate the creation and adoption of clean technologies. He is a Partner at Cleantech Circle, LLC and a co-founder of the California Clean Tech Open.

Cleantech Circle's partners are serial entrepreneurs and angel investors with a passion for Cleantech (renewable energy, air and water, energy efficiency, carbon sequestration). We create and invest in startup and early-stage companies, and we advise VCs and their portfolio companies on technology and strategy.

The California Clean Tech Open is the nation's leading competition for clean technology start-ups. In 2006 and 2007, the competition awarded over $1.2M in cash and services to early stage clean tech companies, and provided training and mentoring services to over 100 entrepreneurs. Michael was a competition Co-Chair in 2006 and 2007, and continues to actively support the competition as a board member.

Starting in 1994, Michael was the CEO and co-founder of the early Internet start-up Four11. He led the company from two person start-up until the successful acquisition by Yahoo in 1997. Prior to Four11, Michael spent 10 years in a variety of engineering, project management, and product marketing roles working on products ranging from consumer electronics to high speed fiber optic transmission systems. Michael holds a BSEE from M.I.T.

Michael and his wife Amy are involved with a number of not for profit activities. In addition to the California Clean Tech Open, they support Acterra, The Art Of Yoga Project, Downtown College Prep, Environmental Entrepreneurs, NRDC, and the venture philanthropy group SV2.
