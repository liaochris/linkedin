Steve Buerkle’s Summary

Focused on the renewable energy field. Current startup is in stealth mode.

Previous experience: Internet pioneer with substantial involvement in software and Internet-delivered content development. Five prior software and Internet startups since 1981. Skilled at conceiving and fostering new ideas, raising capital, and managing the development, marketing and sales of groundbreaking products in business and consumer-directed software/Internet markets.

Renewable energy technology (Photovoltaic, solar thermal, geothermal); Internet content development (conception & creation), finance, M&A, consumer-directed web-based products, intellectual property licensing, competitive analysis, branding, distribution channels, strategic positioning, deep due diligence

    Founder and COO at LipstickWisdom.com, a Web 2.0 media company blending the best of professionally produced video and user generated content on issues of on-going importance to women aged 30 and above.

  • CEO

    Benefits Data Trust

    (Internet industry)

    2004 — 2006 (2 years)

    BDT is a not-for-profit enterprise with support from the National Council on Aging (NCOA - Washington, DC). BDT’s mission is to enroll limited income senior citizens in Medicare Part D (LIS) benefits. Methodologies include data mining and data aggregation designed to locate seniors who would have “slipped through the cracks” of government outreach programs relating to Medicare Part D. Mr. Buerkle created the infrastructure including operations, technology, personnel, creating legal entity, all legal documents and negotiating strategic alliances. Successes included winning the right from CMS (Centers for Medicaid and Medicare) to remove names of the “already enrolled” from a national list of elderly potentially eligible for Medicare Part D – Limited Income Subsidy. This legal right is important as it is the first time this has been granted to any non-governmental entity and dramatically reduces the long term cost of finding eligible beneficiaries.

  • Managing Director

    Brooks Capital Group, LLC

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Venture Capital & Private Equity industry)

    September 2000 — September 2002 (2 years 1 month)

    BCG was founded in 2000 as a venture capital firm, a public equity investment arm and a family office for Brook J. Lenfest. Specific tasks included due diligence, structuring equity investments and representing BCG on board seats and in management reviews. Mr. Buerkle was also responsible for establishing BCG’s relationship with well known venture firms such as Kleiner Perkins, Sierra, Sequoia, Lightspeed, Austin Ventures, NEA, Redpoint and other institutions such as Princeton’s Sarnoff Laboratories.

  • Director - Internet Services

    Suburban Cable

    (Privately Held; 501-1000 employees; Internet industry)

    January 1997 — March 1998 (1 year 3 months)

    Responsible for starting up and managing a new, broadband Internet service across Suburban Cable's 3 state infrastructure. Operated as a “startup” business unit within one of the country’s largest cable companies. Wrote business plan and content plan. Substantial budget. Significant freedom to design business unit. Unique technology solutions and business model. Position was eliminated after TCI (run by John Malone: 50% owner of Suburban Cable) requested that @Home (ATHM – NASDAQ) manage all of Suburban’s broadband Internet services.

  • Vice President - Product Management

    RealNetworks, Inc.

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; RNWK; Computer Software industry)

    September 1994 — July 1995 (11 months)

    Member of founding management team (third executive employee), RealNetworks (previously named Progressive Networks), developer of RealAudio and RealVideo technology offering real-time audio & video over the Internet, Seattle, WA. Participated in writing of original business plan. Developed all plans for significant content database (2,000 hours of licensed audio across 10 subject categories, code named Rainier). Developed significant marketing and intellectual property licensing relationships and completed content licensing agreements with content partners such as Capital Cities/ABC, National Public Radio and others. Initiated and completed agreements with marketing partners such as Spry and Spyglass. Participated at a significant level in finalizing marketing agreements with Netscape and Microsoft. Progressive Networks changed its name to RealNetworks, went public through Goldman Sachs in November, 1997 and is now traded on NASDAQ under the ticker RNWK.

  • Founder/President/CEO

    Axxis Software, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    August 1991 — July 1993 (2 years)

    Publisher of content titles. Axxis Software first product TAXI, received in excess of 100 press accolades and awards, including the coveted PC Computing MVP award (Comdex Fall, 1992) and the Windows Watcher (Jesse Berst) International Innovation Award. This software product combines sophisticated mapping functions based on mapping data from Etak with travel text licensed exclusively from the Zagat Survey Restaurant and Hotel publications. Axxis Software was acquired in June, 1993 by Rupert Murdoch’s News Corp. TAXI was the first "personal navigation" (digital mapping) application. Mr. Buerkle conceived the product, raised the startup funding and hired and managed all personnel. Guided the software development process and negotiated all intellectual property licenses.

  • VP Content Development & Marketing

    Applied Optical Media Corporation

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    December 1988 — August 1991 (2 years 9 months)

    Based in West Chester, PA, AOM was one of the early leaders in development of digital interactive multimedia applications, including pioneer work with DVI (Digital Video Interactive) technology (precursor to MPEG, developed by RCA’s Sarnoff Labs). Mr. Buerkle was responsible for intellectual property acquisition/licensing, new product conceptualization, development team management, product marketing and product design. He conceived the product and licensed all content for World Vista, the first multimedia world atlas. World Vista received an award as "one of the most innovative software products" of the 1991 Consumer Electronics Show. Information content providers to the title include Rand McNally, the Smithsonian Institu¬tion, Photo Research and Inlingua (Berlitz competitor).

  • Founder/ President/CEO

    American Financial Systems, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    February 1981 — September 1987 (6 years 8 months)

    Computer software and services development and marketing company with two major products serving the investment advisory and retail brokerage markets. Two articles published, numerous speaking engagements. AFS software products were considered to be the "Rolls Royce" of the portfolio management/performance measurement software field and are still actively used by a broad group of investment professionals. Lead product (Investment Counsellor) was sold to Shaw Data Services (New York). Secondary product (Broker’s Notebook) was sold to Quotron (CitiCorp).
