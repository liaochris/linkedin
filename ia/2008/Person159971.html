Richard Mooney’s Summary

My law practice focuses on intellectual property and antitrust litigation, arbitration, and counseling. I have been lead counsel in numerous matters in both the state and federal courts of California and in various arbitral forums and have extensive trial experience in each. Recent clients have included individuals, fledgeling companies, and industry leaders such as Visa, Sun Microsystems, Zabasearch.com, Symantec, Tyco Electronics, and Businesswire. My pro bono work has included a major victory in the Ninth Circuit Court of Appeals, halting efforts to water down the famous “dolphin-safe” standard for canned tuna.

I gained big firm experience at Latham & Watkins from 1997 until 2004 and then was a partner at Holme Roberts & Owen before moving my practice to Liner. Before practicing law I was a car washer in Eugene, Oregon, a computer consulant at Harvard Business School, a dishwasher in Paris, and a law clerk for the Honorable Almalya L. Kearse of the United States Court of Appeals for the Second Circuit.

I received my law degree with high honors from the University of Michigan (1993) and my undergraduate degree with honors from Harvard College (1989).
