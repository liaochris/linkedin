Marc Fonteyne’s Summary

Nuclear Engineer from education, with experience in global sales of Aerospace Electronics, to System Integrators and Government.

Lobbyer, Networker and Consultant, in short : an Infopreneur, with good knowledge of the Aerospace-, Science- and Energy markets.

Strong in correct Government Relations. Offering the right information, to allow Policy Makers to decide with good, complete and ethical knowledge about a project.

SLC is providing networking - and consultancy-services to move forward towards the Space Based Economy. The Space Based Economy, is the economy which will emerge, as soon as the access to space becomes affordable. Currently, it costs approx. about 10.000 to 20.000 dollar to bring 1 kg to space (LEO : Low Earth Orbit). Main reason, is the business model, which is nearly 50 years unchanged : that of the "expandable artillery rocket.". If another business model could be applied, called the "airline business model", based initially on a TSTO (Two Stage To Orbit : first stage, a hypersonic carrier, second stage, a reusable space rocketplane with an all metal TPS (Thermal Protection System) then there are good indications that the price-per-kg-to-orbit will drop significantly. Current suborbital projects, as initiated through the success of the X-Prize (Virgin Galactic, Rocketplane, EADS), give a good indication of the prospect of a technology which is pushed by Space Tourism. Initially this will be suborbital, next step will be Orbital. Low Cost Orbital flights open the door to Space Hotels ( Bigelow Aerospace already has 2 prototypes in space). The nice thing about an Economy, and consequently also a Space Based Economy, is that all aspects of society will emerge.
