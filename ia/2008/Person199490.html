Wesley Grandmont III’s Summary

I have been a professional artist for over 13 years. I attended Syracuse University where I was named VPA Scholar and graduated with Honors in Computer Graphics. I initially worked in commercial post-production at Curious Pictures in NYC on projects for HBO, Oscar Mayer, Quaker Oats, Kraft, and Martini & Rossi, then moved into the game industry and spent 9 years at EA-Tiburon. There I had key roles on many of the studio franchises. At EA, I pioneered new procedural modeling techniques for constructing race tracks and the sprawling city of Metropolis, as well as robust solutions for improving in-game lighting for stadiums.

In 2006 I co-founded The Dreamhive (www.thedreamhive.com), an animation studio in Florida. The Dreamhive is dedicated to producing stunning visuals and assets for its clients, and bringing original creative properties to market. I have been responsible for designing and implementing the studio's core production technologies, including a custom real-time shading system, production tools and hybrid real-time/software rendering pipeline.

I have been a guest lecturer at BGSU and UCF-FIEA graduate program, lectured at the '05 Game Developer's Conference in San Francisco, served as an adjunct professor for UCF's undergraduate School of Film and Digital Media and was a Maya Masterclass presenter at SIGGRAPH '08.
