Steve Buccellato’s Summary

  • Publisher / Editor-in-Chief

    Comiculture

    (Publishing industry)

    August 2001 — August 2007 (6 years 1 month)

    "Comiculture" is the imprint of our publishing wing at the Mad Science Media studio. In the summer of 2002 our first publication, Comicultute Magazine, was released. The magazine was a full-color anthology of serialized comic book stories and articles pertaining to comics and pop culture. As Publisher / Editor-in-Chief, I oversaw the entire production of the magazine from concept through publication. Because of the small size of our studio, that meant I was involved at almost every level of production, including the pre-press, printing and distribution stages. Comiculture Magazine ran for only two issues that sold through the comic book "direct market," as well as through the newstand and bookstore market. In 2004, we produced a follow-up to the magazine in the "graphic novel" format, titled "Comiculture Anthology."

  • Associate Editor

    Marvel Comics

    (Entertainment industry)

    1985 — 1989 (4 years)

    Rose though ranks at Marvel's "Epic Comics" division, ultimately editing a line of books. Epic, under publisher Archie Goodwin, was especially interesting due to the diversity of the published work. Artistic methods varied from fully-painted stories to the first digitally-enhanced comic art. Book formats varied from the familiar "floppy" comic book to hardcover graphic novels. Epic also produced translated editions of foreign work, as well as developed properties from scratch.

    Each of these disparate projects came with specific challenges that made my experience at Epic an invaluable training ground for a career specializing in print media.

    • Hired & managed writers & artists
    • Helped creators to develop storylines, kept continuity
    • Trafficked projects (and pages sent to different artists, through internal production and manufacturing departments)
    • Communicated with sales & marketing
    • Checked film & printers proofs
    • Simple production work (paste-up / mechanicals)
