Chris Granata’s Summary

WIRELESS TECHNIQUES, LLC -

MULTI-DISCIPLINARY SYSTEMS DESIGN FOR WIRELESS COMMUNICATIONS, SENSING AND SIGNAL PROCESSING

Company Overview:
Wireless Techniques specializes in the design and implementation of custom multidisciplinary instrumentation. We have expertise in wireless communications, signal processing, sensing, embedded control and analog circuits.

Areas of Specialization – systems, hw & sw engineering for:
• WPAN & WLAN Wireless Communications
• Signal Processing for Sensors and Audio / Video
• Analog Circuits for Sensing & Power Conversion

Services:
• engineering design & application assistance
• technology evaluation / analysis
• architecture selection & systems integration
• design, construction & test of hw / fw / sw
• prototyping & low-volume implementation

Chris Granata’s Specialties:

Core Expertise:
• WPAN & WLAN networking with chipsets and modules
• Design of RF blocks (amps, filters, PLLs, etc.) up to 3 GHz

• Analog circuitry for high-precision, low-power or high-frequency
• Embedded system design with MSP430 and ARM7 MCUs
• DSP & Image Processing with the ADI BlackFin Processor
• Power electronics for power conversion and control

Chris Granata’s Experience

  • President

    Wireless Techniques, LLC

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Electrical/Electronic Manufacturing industry)

    December 2005 — Present (2 years 2 months)

    Responsible for systems engineering, hw&sw development and team mgmt. Presently working on the following consulting projects:

    1) Embedded, real-time video processing system for a biomedical application (based on the ADI BlackFin BF533 DSP).
    - Developing algorithms first on the PC platform using pre-captured images. Algorithms will be modified for efficient execution in the DSP.
    - Performing system-level design of the camera, optics and illumination platform. Selected optics and camera suitable for volume mfg.

The RX test path generates a calibrated tx signal at specific power levels and monitors the DUT (device-under-test) receiver output. The TX test path triggers the DUT transmitter and monitors the output RF signal characteristics.
