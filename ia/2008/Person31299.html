Carol Worthey’s Experience

  • Professional Composer, Painter and Writer

    Independent Contractor

    (Music industry)

    August 1978 — Present (29 years 7 months)

    Internationally recognized Composer Carol Worthey began her music career on the first day of life: Family friend Leonard Bernstein was in her house. Carol began composing at age 3 1/2. She wrote an Etude age 10 that premiered in Carnegie Hall by pianist Vivian Rivkin. At 13 Carol began composition lessons with Grant Beglarian at Merrywood School of Music (John Harbison, Music Director). She studied with Darius Milhaud at Aspen Music Festival, and Vincent Persichetti (of Juilliard) at Hopkins Center for the Performing Arts (Dartmouth). She was also mentored by Walter Piston and Elliott Carter. She studied at Columbia University under Otto Luening, Vladimir Ussachevsky and Henry Cowell.

    See her full biography at: http://www.carolworthey.com/bioc.php

    Currently, Carol is working on a composition for orchestra to be performed in Bejing in 2008 and paintings to be exhibited in Florence, Italy later this year.

  • Partner

    Worthgold Music (BMI)

    (Music industry)

    June 1978 — Present (29 years 9 months)

    Worthgold Music is the agent for and publisher of the works of Composer, Visual Artist and Writer Carol Worthey. Worthgold Music is registered with performing rights organization Broadcast Music Inc. (BMI). Carol has been associated with BMI since 1978.

    Ms. Worthey accepts commissions for new works on a selective basis balancing the worthwhileness of a project with her current composition calendar. If you have a project which you believe Carol would be interested, don't hesitate to communicate with us regarding same. If you would like more information on the process involved in commissioning a new work see url http://www.carolworthey.com/commission.php
