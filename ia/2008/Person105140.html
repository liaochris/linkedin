Kenneth Spassione Jr.’s Summary

My legal experience and expertise is complex litigation in the construction, product liability and government regulation in the areas of casino gaming and professional licensee regulation.

From 1999-2001 I was a regulatory prosecutions attorney for the Division of Gaming Enforcement in the areas of casino regulation, specifically in enforcing internal controls and operational procedures. From 2001-2002, I prosecuted violations of New Jersey Professional Boards Regulations, specifically those pertaining to physicians, nurses, optometrists and other licensed entities

Since 2002, I have practiced privately as both a defense and plaintiffs litigator in complex construction, product liability, consumer fraud and mass tort cases. I am presently working on two large scale matters involving synthetic stucco or EIFS with claims of Consumer Fraud in each case. One matter is up on appeal and the other is scheduled for an April 2008 trial date. In these matters, I have drafted winning briefs and motion papers, appeared for depositions and motion hearings and most recently crafted trial strategy and trial documents dealing with the large scale EIFS/Consumer Fraud case in my office.

With this, I have also begun to fold intellectual property and entertainment law into my practice and teaching endeavors.

On a personal scale, I am committed to moving toward integrating entertainment and IP into my professional practice to complement my work in complex litigation.

On a broader scale, I am also committed to creating a new perception of the role and abilities of attorneys in the new legal marketplace and in the business world in a way that highlights their versatility, intellectual ability and essential humanity, affording them opportunities to step beyond their traditional roles.

Kenneth Spassione Jr.’s Specialties:

Civil Litigation, consumer fraud law, construction law, product liability law, residential real estate, entertainment law, media law, intellectual property law.
