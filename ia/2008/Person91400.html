Jim Hirshfield’s Summary

After graduating from Columbia University (School of Engineering & Applied Science), I spent many years in business on Wall Street and in Silicon Valley. I have over 20 years of experience as a sales and marketing leader as well as starting and running my own companies.

I've been "playing" with computers since the late-1970's and while I'm no hardcore programmer, I do have a strong appreciation and understanding of technology (4 years of engineering school - not for nothing).

I've always loved to build things - and this extends to coming up with cool ideas, nuturing & developing them, and then implementing them. Over the years this has included the business development of Internet directories & search engines, web messaging applications (Email, IM, etc.), online communities, news services, and auction marketplaces.

So after getting into the first wave of dot com growth in 1995 by working at WhoWhere.com (by 1998 one of the top 12 most visited web sites on the net), Wired Digital, and Lycos, I went out on my own. Flush with ideas, seed capital, initiative, and enthusiasm, I started PledgeBay.com with the Chief Scientist of Lycos.

The two of us set out to change the world of charity auctions on eBay in 2000. We raised money from some great people and helped define the space. Our alliances with major search engines and exploratory meetings with eBay were very encouraging. Unfortunately, just like many of our friends, we ran out of runway by the end of 2000. Our timing was unfortunate due to the dry spell that hit the dot com world in early 2000. Perhaps we were ahead of our time; charity auctions flourished post-September 11th.
