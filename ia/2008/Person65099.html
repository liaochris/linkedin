Francine McKenna’s Summary

Ms. McKenna has managed business development, client relationships and project delivery in a range of industries, such as financial services, automotive and manufacturing.

She is working on independent projects and a writing project. Her book, tentatively entitled, "Keeping Watch - Can the Big 4 Still Protect Shareholders?", is planned for 2008. Her blog, http://ReTheAuditors.blogspot.com, comments on the Big 4 audit firms in the age of regulation and globalization.

She recently spoke at the Illinois Information Technology Association's in June 2007 on, "The Globalization of Sarbanes-Oxley" and at the IIA's Chicago Chapter Conference, April 2007 on, "Stock Options Backdating and Executive Compensation Issues - An Update for Auditors."

She is interested in project opportunities such as leading a PMO related to finance/accounting transformation, IT governance or SOX remediation on either the finance or IT side, especially as for operations in Mexico and South America.

She most recently served PwC Internal Audit Services clients and was a member of PwC's internal audit team, auditing PwC operations globally.

McKenna was Midwest RVP for Jefferson Wells, Inc., a subsidiary of Manpower, responsible for managing sales, operations, client relationships and overall P&L for ten offices, including Toronto.

She was the frst female MD in Latin America for BearingPoint, leading the Industrial, Automotive and Transportation practice in the region and serving clients such as Cemex and the Big 3 auto makers.

As Vice President at JP Morgan in Latin America, she led their Y2K PMO and is fluent in Spanish.

McKenna has completed the Harvard course, "Leading Professional Services Firms" and "The Women's Leadership Program" at the Center For Creative Leadership.

A CPA, she is a member of the IIA and ISACA (Chicago Chapter Vice President) and speaks frequently for both. She earned her B.S. in Accountancy at Purdue University.

Francine McKenna’s Specialties:

Experienced in creating, improving and expanding international service delivery to clients of professional services firms and in leading the Project Management Office for systems upgrade/integration, finance transformation, IT governance and SOX remediation in finance or IT.

She has established new offices and new practices globally, identified and sourced personnel globally, and developed/managed of models for successful international engagements.
