Reed R. Heustis, Jr.’s Summary

While serving as a workers' compensation claims representative for nearly three years in Los Angeles County, Reed earned the Claims Adjuster Designation certificate in 2006 through California's State Compensation Insurance Fund, the largest provider of workers' compensation insurance in the Golden State.

Today Reed practices as a Workers' Compensation Defense Attorney, utilizing his claims experience for an aggressive private professional corporation in Orange County, California, that defends employers, homeowners through homeowner policies, insurance companies and third party administrators throughout the State of California.

Reed earned his Juris Doctor from California Western School of Law (accredited by the American Bar Association in 1962), the oldest law school in San Diego; and his Bachelor of Arts in Political Science from the University of California, Irvine.

In 2001 Reed graduated from the National Litigation Academy with a scholarship endowed by the Alliance Defense Fund, a servant organization that provides resources that empower the spread of the Gospel of Jesus Christ through legal defense and advocacy of religious freedom, the sanctity of human life, and biblical family values.

Reed and his wife, who together raise two daughters, are members of Trinity Reformed Baptist Church of La Mirada, an affiliate of the Association of Reformed Baptist Churches of America; and are members of the American Heritage Party.

His hobbies include baseball, fantasy sports, golf, politics, classical music and internet marketing. In his spare time, Reed publishes a Christian Constitutionalist newsletter.

Reed R. Heustis, Jr.’s Specialties:

Defending against excessive and unreasonable claims; negotiating fair settlements with legitimately injured workers; deposing applicants and witnesses; litigating in all venues in Northern and Southern California; drafting monthly diary letters to client claims administrators; advocating at Status Conferences, Expedited Hearings, Mandatory Settlement Conferences, Lien Hearings and Trials at Workers' Compensation Appeals Board and other tribunals; disputing medical provider liens; and much more.
