Jayne Cravens’s Summary

Jayne Cravens has more than 20 years of experience providing management and capacity-building expertise for civil society and government organizations regarding media & public communications, community/volunteer involvement, donor relations, reporting, and governance. She has worked with groups on the local, regional, national and international levels, and extensively with multi-cultural, diverse audiences, corporate audiences, international aid workers, low-income communities, conservative socieities, and those who are traditionally socially excluded. She has a demonstrated commitment to mainstreaming women's issues into her work, including during her support to a government ministry in Afghanistan, and a background in working with people with widely differing belief systems and personal circumstances. She is frequently cited in various news and academic articles. If you type her full name, "Jayne Cravens", in quotes into google.com, you can learn in detail about her international reputation, the initiatives with which she has worked, and the many organizations that refer to her work and materials.

I am a citizen of the USA, with legal residency in Germany (I would consider moving elsewhere for the perfect opportunity).

See www.coyotecommunications.com for complete details about me, as well as links to my blog and monthly email newsletter.

Jayne Cravens’s Specialties:

- introducing controversial ideas to audiences in practical, less-confrontational terms

    AidWorkers Network (AWN)

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    January 2004 — Present (4 years 2 months)

    Aid Workers Network (AWN) is an online community network of people working in aid and development. It allows aid workers -- consultants, full-time staff, volunteers, researchers, local and international -- to share questions, advice and resources with each other. As a volunteer (an online volunteer, in fact), I monitor and regularly-contribute to the branches of its forum relating to volunteers, finding a job/careers, and gender, and help re-categorize posts that have been put in the wrong places. I also co-wrote a guide with other AWN volunteers to help small NGOs in the developing world engage in fund-raising, and a step-by-step guide to help AWN recruit a volunteer manager, to better support its volunteers, and to better involve the AWN community in supporting the organization. I also contributed a strategy to help the board better define and administer its duties, with greater volunteer assistance.
