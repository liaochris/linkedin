David White’s Summary

Started off working in missile tracking radar, which is why I refer to myself as a rocket scientist sometimes. I have always worked in innovation and was one of the very first to program microprocessors - Intel 4004... Hand coded using machine code in binary - before programming languages! Took days and could not include errors - pocket calculators are more powerful nowadays, but they were exciting at the time.

Left to join Rank Xerox in sales, I wanted to WORK with people rather than KILL them (previous job!). Won Honours Club in first quarter.

Set up own agency Alterindex in 1993 became Weboptimiser in 1996, still at it!

Today at Weboptimiser I am really interested in client business growth, business development, joint ventures, deal making and generally exploring and learning more about communicating and living life via the web.

I am proud to chair the Search Council at the Internet Advertising Bureau UK and Europe which puts me in close contact with major search engines, my peer group and major clients. In particular I have been able to produce a best practice guideline for the search engine marketing industry in early 2007. You will see a guide to advanced search engine marketing any day now, with a benchmarking project to reveal next.

Watch this space, or better still, join the IAB!

search engine marketing, sem, serach engine optimisation, seo, pay per click, ppc, Video optimisation, keynote speaker

For the search engine marketing industry I have just guided Search Engine Marketing Charter through the IAB in the UK and hoping to take this forward across Europe. I also keen to promote advanced SEM:

  • Managing Director

    Saga Systems

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Computer Hardware industry)

    June 1984 — September 1987 (3 years 4 months)

    I commissioned keyboards to be manufactured in Germany I managed to sell over £1m which at a unit price of £55 was great fun.

    The keyboard was an upgrade for the ZX Spectrum Sinclair home computer.

    Managed to continue in the peripherals market as I had by then established supply chains with other hardware suppliers, although I had no real cash - proves it can be done.

    I started with ambition based in my parents stairwell, made international calls without their knowledge. We we were surprised when the first Juggernaut container of keyboards arrived. There were many more...

    I just discovered the front page of Popular Computing Weekly - only 50p 6th August 1986... headline: "Saga to launch cheap Z80 micro" - a week before IBM launched the PC - I had managed to put a PC together without realising it, just did not have quite the marketing muscle... Loads of fun - I sold about 20 systems on the back of the IBM PC.
