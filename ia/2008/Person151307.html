Phil McMillan’s Summary

Technical Consultant specialising in Broadcast Video Networking and Solutions. Broad experience covering all aspects of analogue and digital video and audio, compression techniques and monitoring / service management.

I was part of the design team for the UK DTTV network roll-out during 1998, later working on international fibre-based contribution and MPLS-IP networks.

Phil McMillan’s Specialties:

Transport of SDI, HD-SDI, ASI and Analogue video signals, AES and analogue audio signals. MPEG2, MPEG4, JPEG2000, layer II Compression Techniques.

  • Head of European Design - Terrestrial Broadcast Networks

    BT Broadcast Services

    (Public Company; Telecommunications industry)

    November 2002 — December 2004 (2 years 2 months)

    This role expanded on previous roles and provided the opportunity to work with BT Divisions and Joint Venture Companies across Europe to develop innovative network designs to meet the demands of the broadcast industry. The process of design included working with European fibre providers to develop high availability (>99.99%) solutions.
    The role additionally required a significant amount of strategy planning and financial management to migrate legacy technology networks to ATM. Also part of the role was line management of a small team of Design Engineers and developing virtual teams across international boundaries.
    Key technologies: Network; ATM, SDH, DWDM, CWDM Video/Audio; MPEG2, PAL, SDI, AES, ASI

  • Senior Systems Engineer – Terrestrial & International Fibre Broadcast Networks and Video Streaming

    BT Broadcast Services

    (Public Company; Telecommunications industry)

    October 2000 — November 2002 (2 years 2 months)

    During this period, BT Broadcast Services started to develop fibre based international networks to meet broadcaster’s requirements for low latency, high availability contribution networks. I developed the design of the first BT BS Trans-Atlantic fibre based network for Broadcast Video, which has a modest capacity. The system proved so successful that the capacity was tripled after 6 months of operation and increased to over 12 times its original capacity after just 18 months. Expansion across Europe with switching capabilities at nodes in London, Paris, New York and Washington enabled a highly flexible alternative to Satellite based networks.
    I had line management responsibility with both a team of Pre-sales Systems Engineers and Post-sales Design Engineers.
    My role was expanded during the period to include video streaming design and development with my team developing BT’s MediaStream product range.
