Glenn Waldron’s Summary

Glenn is a software engineer and an entrepreneur. He has a wide range of experience in the defense, aerospace, intelligence, and commercial markets. Glenn's professional interests include geospatial computing, visualization, and enterprise architecture.

Glenn Waldron’s Specialties:

Geospatial technologies, visualization, modeling and simulation, GIS, mapping, location-based systems, web applications

Glenn Waldron’s Experience

  • President and CEO

    Pelican Mapping

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Computer Software industry)

    2003 — Present (5 years)

    Glenn co-founded this custom software development and data production services business. Pelican specializes in advanced mapping, 3D/2D visualization and GIS applications for customers in the defense, homeland security, intelligence, and commercial markets.

  • Product Manager

    IT Spatial (now Overwatch)

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    2002 — 2003 (1 year)

    Glenn designed and built the company's flagship decision support and visualization product suite, InterSCOPE. He also participated heavily in business development, marketing, and fundraising activites. (ITspatial was acquired by Overwatch in 2005; Overwatch is now part of Textron Systems.)

  • Founder and Chief Technology Officer

    WareOnEarth Communications

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Defense & Space industry)

    1997 — 2002 (5 years)

    WCI provides Information Assurance products and services to US Navy SPAWAR and other government customers. Glenn founded the company in 1997 and built it to over 100 employees through product development, research, and fundraising initiatives.

  • Lead Software Engineer

    USC/ISI (DARPA contractor)

    (Educational Institution; 51-200 employees; Defense & Space industry)

    1996 — 1997 (1 year)

    Glenn maintained and developed software for the CAVE visualization system for the DARPA Technology Integration Environment (a.k.a. "Enterprise"), the DOD's premier emerging technology showcase facility.

  • Software Engineer

    Camber Corporation

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Defense & Space industry)

    1995 — 1996 (1 year)

    Glenn engineered modeling, simulation, and visualization systems for multi-spectral camera deployment on a US Army contract involving mine detection.

  • Member of the Technical Staff

    MITRE Corporation

    (Non-Profit; 1001-5000 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    1993 — 1995 (2 years)

    Glenn developed modeling, simulation, and visualization systems for next-generation air traffic control (ATC) systems on FAA contracts. He started as a Virginia Tech co-op student and went on to full-time position.
