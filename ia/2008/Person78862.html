Ian McNally’s Summary

Wide knowledge of landline based telecomunications, including fibre to copper multiplexing and demultiplexing, and the use of software to map this in various forms (ICOMS NIM, Cramer, Cortex etc.)

As a result I have been involved in 4 data migrations of this part of the CMS structure for Telewest and have recently worked as part of the team to integrate NTL's plant and network into ICOMS.

I have used SQL in its various forms (Unix, AS/400, Oracle, Ingres and other variants of the language) for more than 10 years, and am proficient in its use.

In my current role I have had to learn the Cortex TDM system and have managed this to be able to coordinate the testing between this and Cramer.

As part of the Bytel to ICOMS migration I used ErWin as a data modelling tool to provide Convergys with their ICOMS relational data model, which is still in use at present.

In a past life I was an Ingres DBA and know a lot about relational database design and implementation, from the analyst cycle onwards to implementation of a database design, which I am very familiar with.

I also spent 8 years as a Unix sysadmin so I am also familiar with the tools required for working in such an environment.

As part of my original degree I also looked carefully at the social aspects of computing, and I hope to enlist on a Ph.D course on forensic computing when I get sufficient time off (!).

Ian McNally’s Specialties:

Solving problems. Experience to find technical solutions that have previously been though of as uneconomical. Data migrations from diverse sources and destinations (including cross-platform OS migrations). Security issues. Team leadership with a small team. Relational database design and implementation. Wide knowledge of landline telecommunication infrastructure and the support software used.General IT troubleshooting, on a number of different platforms.
