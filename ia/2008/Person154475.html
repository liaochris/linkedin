Jason Putorti’s Summary

Accomplished three-time entrepreneur with experience in business, design, advertising, and technology– specifically web related. Very passionate about taking business ideas from napkin scratch to working product with capabilities spanning these disciplines.

Currently lead product and visual designer for Mint.com in Silicon Valley, and a consultant to a variety of early-stage companies on visual design and user experience.

Jason Putorti’s Specialties:

Web applications; brand management; design and typography; user interface and experience.

Jason Putorti’s Experience

  • Lead Designer

    Mint.com

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry)

    March 2007 — Present (1 year 3 months)

    Currently responsible for all aspects of visual design for web 2.0 personal finance app Mint.com, from the critically acclaimed product user interface and experience to the look and feel of the brand deliverables.

    Since public launch in September '07, Mint.com won TechCrunch 40, two People's Choice Webby Awards, was named one of PC World's Top 25 Most Innovative Products of the Year; and praised by the NY Times, BusinessWeek, Fast Company, Wall Street Journal, and the Associated Press.

  • CEO

    Novaurora

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    January 2003 — Present (5 years 5 months)

    Drove multifunctional efforts as CEO that included design, programming, strategy, finance, and operations. Grew the print and interactive creative agency from a one-man shop, to one of Pittsburgh Magazine’s “Six Hot Ad Agencies” boasting clients such as Coldwell Banker, Sewickley Spa, Whirl Magazine, and Berner International. Contracted the Novaurora brand to Fitting Group on 1 September 2006, reacquired on 1 September 2007 as a consultancy.

  • Consultant

    Fitting Group

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    September 2006 — March 2007 (7 months)

    Created the interactive division of Fitting Group, Novaurora.fg, by leveraging personnel assets at both companies. Served as lead idea guy and technology evangelist for the challenger brand agency, advising how interactivity can empower client brands.

  • CTO

    College Prowler

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Publishing industry)

    May 2002 — January 2004 (1 year 9 months)

    Third co-founder of College Prowler– a company publishing individual guidebooks for over 230 top colleges across the country. Created and implemented all facets of web strategy and technology including e-commerce and fulfillment platforms. Also responsible for the visual branding from identity to interactive.

  • CTO

    3N2

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Sporting Goods industry)

Relationship continued with Novaurora until the sale of 3N2 in 2006 to outside investors.
