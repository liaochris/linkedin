Kendall Colman’s Summary

Kendall Colman is the founder and President of Colman & Company, a highly recognized leadership development firm based in Denver, Colorado. She strives to fulfill her mission by working diligently with executive leadership and their teams to create solutions that achieve an optimal state of organizational effectiveness, which is grounded in tangible results and accountability, and which generates direction, focus, momentum and value.

Her real-world expertise and results in the areas of assessment, leadership and team development, coaching, facilitation, tailored programming, and training provide her with the powerful tools to help her clients achieve this state of success.

She is a member of the Colorado Human Resource Association, Colorado Association of Commerce and Industry, International Coaching Federation and the National Speaker's Association (Colorado chapter).

Kendall Colman’s Specialties:

Kendall has worked in senior positions with Charter Medical Corporation, National Medical Enterprises, and Mental Health Management. She has worked with major companies in a wide range of industries including healthcare, biotechnology, energy, banking and finance, and government.

Kendall Colman’s Interests:

From a leadership perspective, who has most inspired you and why? The greatest example of leadership was constantly displayed by my Great Grandfather, Senator Burton Kendall Wheeler. This US Senator from Montana was known for his uncompromising integrity and an unrelenting commitment to questioning the status quo. Growing up with him and being in his presence left quite an impression on me...and on Congress. As President Harry Truman said about him, "one of the greatest periods of my life has been my association with Burton Wheeler." There was a movie about him, a classic Jimmy Stewart entitled, Mr. Smith Goes to Washington. In this Academy Award-winning classic from director Frank Capra, we get to see him having his hopes dashed by the unstoppable, repressive Washington scene. He persevered and the rest is history. Burton Kendall Wheeler, enjoyed a 22 year tenure in the Senate, and his 1924 run for Vice President.
