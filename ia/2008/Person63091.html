Fred Danowski’s Summary

During the time the I've been working in libraries, I have seen some tremendous changes occur in the profession regarding both technology, and how we as librarians approach serving our individual communities.

My professional goals are to find new and better ways to market libraries, creating better awareness about what we do, and to successfully implement new technologies in practical and innovative ways that enrich user experience.

Fred Danowski’s Specialties:

I'm the Library's all around computer guru, and miracle worker.

Fred Danowski’s Experience

  • Information Systems Librarian

    Milford Public Library

    (Government Agency; 11-50 employees; Research industry)

    April 2005 — Present (3 years)

    Responsible for the maintenance and operation of the Library's Horizon Integrated Library System (ILS), 3M Self-Check System, EnvisionWare PC Reservation System, and WiFi. Generates statistics providing metrics via MicroStrategy's Web Reporter, maintains backups and upgrades, troubleshoots technology issues and provides general technical support and training for both hardware and software, researches various reference questions at the Library's Information Desk, and provides general instruction on the proper use of the Library's computers, databases, and Microsoft Office applications.

  • Adult Services Librarian

    Monroe Public Library

    (Government Agency; 11-50 employees; Research industry)

    October 2000 — April 2005 (4 years 7 months)

    Responsible for the day-to-day operation of the Adult-Services Department including all aspects of circulation, technical (CARL/Horizon ILS's), and reference services. Primary duties included, designing and maintaining the library's web site, purchasing reference and non-fiction books, organizing and publicizing programs, fulfilling ILLs, scheduling support staff, creating displays, constructing bibliographies, conducting surveys, analyzing statistics, and assisting patrons at the reference desk through both research and instruction.

  • Senior Photographer

    Brooks Community Newspapers

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Newspapers industry)

    June 1994 — February 1999 (4 years 9 months)

    Photographed news, sports, self-generated features, advertisements, and illustrations. Responsibilities included digitizing and toning images in Adobe Photoshop, preparing images for the Internet, maintaining a C-41 film processor, writing cutlines, researching and distributing archived negatives. Maintain a digital / traditional photo library of 20,000 plus images.
