Edward Adams’s Summary

I manage the editorial and business operations of the flagship magazine of the American Bar Association. We cover the trends, people and finances of the legal profession from Wall Street to Main Street to Pennsylvania Avenue. More than half of the nation's 1.1 million lawyers read us every month.

Edward Adams’s Experience

  • Editor & Publisher

    ABA Journal

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Publishing industry)

    September 2006 — Present (2 years 1 month)

  • Public Information Officer

    U.S. District Court for the Eastern District of Virginia

    (Government Agency; 51-200 employees; Judiciary industry)

    June 2002 — August 2006 (4 years 3 months)

    As the only public information officer working in the federal trial courts, I created media relations plans for dozens of high-profile cases nationwide, including those of al Qaeda member Zacarias Moussaoui, "American Taliban" John Walker Lindh, enemy combatant Yaser Hamdi, al Qaeda scout Iyman Faris, Atlanta Olympics bomber Eric Rudolph and former HealthSouth CEO Richard Scrushy. Collaborated with judges, court administrators, and representatives of local law enforcement agencies, the U.S. Marshals Service, the U.S. Department of State, the National Security Agency and more than 150 media organizations.

  • Editor

    New York Law Journal

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Newspapers industry)

    May 1989 — May 2002 (13 years 1 month)

    Reported about the business of major law firms and created and ran websites for this daily newspaper for lawyers, which is part of American Lawyer Media.
