Michael Orr’s Summary

I am an ex. Unix "hacker" - System Programmer, Compiler writer, etc. With extensive experience in customer-facing contact.

Starting 1991 I transitioned from pure R&D to marketing/Product-management and customer-facing roles, and shifted to data-communications (LAN and WAN)

Starting 1999 I served as CTO of the obligatory Internet-era 10-person startup (that survived !) and since 2000 I have been with Radlan, which has gone from 80-person Startup when I joined to be acquired by Marvell Semiconductors for 250M in 2003/2004. I have now relocated to the USA, to a system-architect job at corp HQ (Hey. someone has to know what that bunch of Israeli Engineers actually DO .. ;-)

Hobbies: Reading, and Martial Arts (Mainly Japanese Karate and Aikido)

Note: Slight Dyslexia (so Apologies in advance for Typos - spell checker or not, I still manage to have some in almost everything I write ...)

Michael Orr’s Specialties:

Data-comm protocols, Network usage scenario's, TCP/Datacomm Optimization, Data-over-satellite and Data-over-cellular, Customer-facing communications, very strong communication/presentation skills, white paper/article writing
Very good at self-learning complex technologies (and then explaining them to near-techies, or non-techies), multi-level communications with Executives to Admin staff

  • CTO

    Flash Networks

    (Privately Held; Internet industry)

    February 1998 — September 2000 (2 years 8 months)

    Flash Network is an Israeli startup dealing in communication optimization over problem links (e.g. Data-over-Satellite, data-over-Cellular), providing both acceleration of communication, as well as improved efficiency in terms of BW usage, especially on shared-return-channel situation (e.g the above, Data-over-Cable modems, etc.)

    I joined when Flash was 8 people strong, and served as CTO for 2 years, through 2 rounds of financing and a complete management overhaul by the new VC owners.
    During that time I oversaw a complete rewrite of the product from scratch, as well as helping to define it, and define new areas of application for the basic technology,
    one of which is still the company's main target application today.

    The company still exists, and has about 100 Employees now, focusing on data-over-cellular applications.

  • Product Manager
