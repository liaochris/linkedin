Steve Fushelberger’s Summary

Steve Fushelberger’s career spans the spectrum of public and media relations, corporate / marketing / crisis communications, advertising, branding, web site development and sales. Clients and employers have ranged from entrepreneurial ventures and PR / advertising agencies to Fortune 100 organizations.

His experience encompasses the aerospace, defense, capital goods, technical / regulated products, consumer durables, retail, real estate, insurance / financial / professional services, healthcare / pharmaceuticals, publishing, and trade association arenas.

Previous highly matrixed and global employers include Rolls-Royce, General Mills, Cessna Aircraft Company and United Airlines.

His record of accomplishment ranges from Eagle Scout to multiple first place and best-of-show awards in national professional marketing and communications competitions.

He is versed at interaction with local, state, and federal elected officials and regulators, and ISO 9001 and 14001 standards and compliance, and TQM and Six Sigma processes.

Possessing a Commercial Pilot License with instrument, multi-engine, and seaplane ratings, Steve flies humanitarian medical missions for LifeLine Pilots, a volunteer organization of aviators who donate their aircraft and time to transport patients with serious medical conditions and financial hardships to distant medical facilities.

He also serves on the National Aviation Exploring Council (Boy Scouts of America) and with the EAA Young Eagles program to provide children with their first flight in an airplane.

Steve holds a Bachelors degree with a marketing concentration from the hihgly rated Kelley School of Business at Indiana University, and is a US Army veteran.

He is a life member of Sigma Chi Fraternity, the Indiana University Alumni Association, and the American Bonanza Society, and is a member of the Aircraft Owners and Pilots Association and Experimental Aircraft Association.
