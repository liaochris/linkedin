Sarath Samarasekera’s Summary

I have a wide and varied working experience from large Fortune 5 companies to small single man operations. I believe that to be successful you have to focus on creating value and selling that value at the same time.

Currently I am focused on growing Shopster.com into global retailing platform

I am also an active investor and am looking to work with and invest in very early stage companies. Creating the proper structures and business foundations early is key to long term success.

Sarath Samarasekera’s Experience

  • CEO

    Shopster E-Commerce Inc

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    September 2004 — Present (3 years 7 months)

    Shopster E-Commerce Inc. is the most innovative company to enter the consumer retail space in the past 50 years. Shopster provides a single platform which brings together suppliers and retailers. By focusing on creating a network based on specific transactions and providing seemless integration of the supply chain with with the marketing outlets, Shopster greatly reduces the transaction costs associated with retailing. This allows products to get to market faster and cheaper than ever before. By lowering the cost of becoming a retailer Shopster has enabled thousands of new entrants to begin retail operations.

    By focusing on the transaction and the needs of the network participants rather than on relationships between people, Shopster has vastly reduced the costs and increased the value of the retail channel.

  • Managing Director

    Samaradeen Group

    (Privately Held; Venture Capital & Private Equity industry)

    1998 — Present (10 years)

    A privately held venture capital firm. Samaradeen provides both capital and business insight to investee firms and entrepreneurs. Investment industry ranges from oil & gas to real estate, food & beverage to technology industries. We prefer to deal with very early stage companies in seed or first round financing.

  • Project Manager

    Trilogy International

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Automotive industry)

    1998 — 2001 (3 years)

    Trilogy International is a Michigan based automotive and technology consulting firm. As a project manager Sarath managed teams of consultants and internal employees (1 - 25) providing technology solutions to manage the extended supply chain for Ford Motor Company and other companies supporting the auto industry. Some companies that Sarath worked with or consulted for include; Delphi, Texas Instruments, IBM, SDRC, Johnson Controls and many other Fortune 500 companies. Some projects managed by Sarath included managing teams of computer engineers and business analysts in support of Ford Motor Company's divesture of Visteon, and the Ford/Firestone Tire Recall project in 2000/2001. Sarath was also responsible for business development within his client companies.
