Dr. Anthony Casas, Jr.’s Summary

“We are going to take a vacation to Mexico” my mother told the five of us as we listened, most only half interested. It was 1972 and she said that it was going to be our last family trip because my sister was starting college in September.

Before we knew it all 7 of us were piling into our new Chevy Impala. Soon, we were on a ferry, the Benito Juarez, bound for Mulaje in Baja. After a couple of days of fun, somehow, we missed the return ferry but learned that we could take the newly completed Baja highway and be back home, in southern California, in 2 days.

When we arrived at the place where we were to catch the highway, all we could see was a dirt road. My father checked with some locals and found out that the highway was not yet completed, but that the asphalt was only about 30 miles away. My father had to be back to work, so we decided to go for it. That information turned out to be incorrect and we ended up driving over 300 miles on unpaved roads in the Baja desert, during the middle of summer, in a Chevy Impala, with five children and few supplies. After three days of moving rocks, sleeping in, and on top of, the car, and bumming water from people in dune buggies, who looked at us like we were crazy, we arrived at the border.

My mother literally kissed the pavement! Later, we learned that there were 7 species of poisonous snakes in Baja and the car forever after had a layer of red clay that permeated the interior no matter how well it was detailed. Still, we survived and had great stories to tell that always entertain!

Though I was just 7 then, I learned the importance of teamwork, determination, and optimism from the experience. I also learned the importance of planning, maintaining a sense of humor, and double checking facts, regardless of the endeavor! I have since incorporated these lessons into my career, always with very positive results.
