Alan Cooper’s Summary

A specialist in Business Process Analysis / Improvement / Re-engineering and Transformation. Working with Local Government improvement and Partnership projects.

Career history as a Business / IT hybrid with a broad holistic approach to developing new business opportunities with IT as an enabler. Involved in designing and implementing leading edge systems, new business development and BPR. Key industries are government, insurance, and health.

Hands-on approach using structure methodologies leading to quality outcomes.

Research interest into customer led, market driving, process enabling, multi-channel distribution, providing new innovative services and dynamic pricing opportunities. Web site of _research_ work at http://www.managingchange.com

- Member of ValueAdding.com, specialists in BPR and ABC http://www.ValueAdding.com

  • Strategic Business Analyst

    Managing Change Ltd

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Management Consulting industry)

    June 1993 — March 2007 (13 years 10 months)

    Strategic Consulting including strategy development, business planning & development, business process reengineering (BPR), multi-channel distribution, one-to-one marketing and ebusiness.

    Example projects are:

    * Analysing nearly 300 waste management processes for 6 councils to assist them in creating the business case for a proposed partnership. Assisting them in the design of the new organisation.

    * Impact analysis for licensing processes for a district council so they were able to take a proactive stance to the new licensing regulations.

    * BPR for a London council to move tasks from back-office to a "one-stop" front-office.

    * Defining the ICT & infrastucture requirements and knowledge processes for a call centre for an innovative environmental transport organisation.

    * Designing a new independent emergency health organisation for doctors in north Hampshire. This project won an award from Glaxo/Wellcome/BMA.

  • Co-Founder and Secretary

    Henley Management College International Alumni Assoc. - eBusiness Group

    (Non-Profit; 201-500 employees; Education Management industry)

    May 1996 — February 2006 (9 years 10 months)

    Alan Cooper and Adé Bamigboye founded the The Electronic Commerce Focus Group in May 1996 having completed their MBA at the college. The main goals were to promote awareness of the state of electronic commerce and its future direction within the Henley Alumni and to provide a forum for networking.

    At the end of 1999, we renamed the group, the eBusiness Group, to reflect the broader nature of eCommerce and the increased range of business opportunities that were available to organisations taking a strategic view of the subject.

    We arrange networking meetings with speakers as well as joint initiatives with the College and other professional groups. We're on our 3rd web site and are looking at further enhancements for online administration and event management. There is now an enlarged committee taking the group forward. Membership is over 200 and is open to other professionals - do join us. See http://www.ebusinessgroup.org

  • Senior Software Specialist

    Den Danske Bank (The Danish Bank), Denmark

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; Banking industry)

    June 1975 — July 1978 (3 years 2 months)

    Database designer and administrator responsible for the databases in the online banking system and the personnel system.

    Key aspect of the banking system was designing and running for very high performance. With the Personnel system the emphasis was on high functionality. In both cases, data integrity and high availability was key. Systems were based on the IBM IMS DB/DC software.

    Established a set of automated operational processes for managing, monitoring and maintaining these systems and thereby also minimising risk.

  • Senior Software Specialist

    London Online Local Authorities (LOLA)

    (Government Agency; 201-500 employees; Government Administration industry)

    September 1970 — June 1975 (4 years 10 months)

    In 1972 LOLA implemented an extremely advanced real-time CRM system long before the term was invented! It was probably the first local council in the world to do so. LOLA acted for the 4 London Boroughs (local authorities) in the consortium.

    Responsible for the design and maintenance of the highly complex databases linking people and properties to associated council services giving a 360 degree view of all citizen interactions. All managed by the users in real time via VDUs in their offices. Built using a System Architecture approach, the design proved robust and versatile for over 30 years.

    Responsible for the design and build of sophisticated middle-ware so that programmers could concentrate on business functionality whilst all technical aspects were isolated and used in a standardised way.

    System was built using the then new IBM IMS DB/DC software.
    So advanced, we had a "hot line" to the developers in the USA!
