Leon Lee’s Summary

The multiple careers of Leon Lee redefines “generalist” as a term of praise.
From being a Managing Editor of That's Beijing to creating grassroots NPOs in China, the common denominator in his expansive achievements is a confluence of intangible qualities such as effective leadership and a creative mind.
Leon was formerly a Lunch Sous Chef at Greens, the premier vegetarian restaurant in the United States. In Beijing China, Leon has consulted for Alba Caffe on recipes and kitchen training. Currently, He is contracted with the Oriental Taipan group to reconceptualizing a gutted bakery into one of Beijing's best live jazz bars.
In the United States, he served in all departments within an NPO arts organizations from Director to Grant Writer, Publicist to Tour Manager in Community Arts, Fine Art, and Film & Media. In China, his Pentatonic Workshop and SambAsia Beijing have received both the attention of the Shanghai World Exposition 2010 and the Beijing organizing committee for the Olympic games.
As curator-producer Leon has partnered with Asia-Europe Foundation, Midi Music Festival, 798 Dashanzi International Art Festival, and Dangdai International Art Festival. He and his art projects have been profiled in the media on three continents including: The Wire, Rolling Stone China, China Daily, Vogue China, L'Officiel, San Francisco Weekly, and San Jose Mercury News to name just a few.
Leon’s leadership accomplishments include: directing an editorial staff of 30 to meet deadlines; supervising a 5 line cook fine dining kitchen to serve 400 diners per day; and organizing 50 plus volunteers to support 20 sponsored vendors to cater Gala receptions for over 3,000 attendees at an international film festival to name a few.
As a musician, Leon has received awards from Asian American Arts Foundation and Meet The Composer Fund. Currently, he performs as part of SambAsia Beijing, the first Rio de Janeiro-style Brazilian samba school in China, of which he is also the founder.
