Ed Chester’s Summary

Spacecraft systems engineer with microelectronic, data systems, and operations specialisation. Experience of working in all phases of mission life-cycle, from concept through design/build, AIV, and operations.

Extensive IT skills including network and facility management of Linux, Solaris, Windows environments (including mixed), of software and internet application development (PHP, Perl, MySQL, XML) with strong interest in distributed collaborative systems, concurrent engineering, and distributed team development.

Goals? More of the same, anywhere in Europe, and ideally another Mars mission!

    European Space Agency

    (Government Agency; 1001-5000 employees; Defense & Space industry)

    June 2004 — July 2006 (2 years 2 months)

    As a systems engineer within the OPS-ONV section of the European Space Agency, I am responsible for the mission ground segment integration and test programmes, including end-to-end specification and execution of tests involving all ground segment elements and the Herschel and Planck spacecraft. The systems approach to ground segment development and integration is a novel approach for these missions, requiring introduction of new ideas and methods into existing infrastructure. I introduced functional and resource analysis using custom tools based upon relational databases, as well as the formal IDEF/0 methodology, to enhance test coverage and test-phase operational realism.

    Within the H/P Flight Control Team, I am a Payload SOE, covering all payloads for both missions; their interfaces, operations, data return and end-end processing by instrument teams on the ground. How much fun is it possible to have?

  • Research Assistant

    University of Newcastle

    (Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry)

    May 1997 — April 1998 (1 year)

I interpreted a very coarse software emulation written in PASCAL of the proposed chip operation, and designed the actual hardware using Cadence IC design software. Significant parts of the chip were developed from schematic capture, with other areas built from cell macros (memory) and Verilog HDL (controllers and state machines). Gained self-taught familiarity with Cadence design toolset (including maintenance and training of other users) and principles of IC design. The role also required international collaboration, meetings and leadership in design decisions.
