Neri Oxman’s Summary

Neri Oxman is an architect and researcher currently based at MIT where she is a Presidential Fellow working towards her PhD in Design and Computation. She graduated from the Architectural Association School of Architecture in London. Her work has been recently exhibited in the “Emerging Talent Emerging Technologies” Exhibition at the Beijing Biennial 2006, World Art Museum. Neri has taught computation workshops at the Emergent Technologies and Design Master's Program at the AA, the IT-Master's Programme at the Oslo School of Architecture, Rice and Columbia University. She has collaborated with the Smart Geometry Group and has given workshops on parametric design at TU Delft, TU Vienna, Cambridge U.K, MIT, Columbia University, Harvard GSD, and KPF. Her work has been published in journals and books including AD (Emergence), AD (Techniques and Technologies in Morphogenetic Design), Icon, AA Files, Building Design (BD Magazine), Demonstrating Digital Architecture (Birkhäuser Publishers), Archiprix International 2005 (010 Publishers), Thresholds, and l’ARCA. In 2005, she was the recipient of the FEIDAD Merit Award, the Archiprix Award, and the 2005-2007 AICF Award of Excellence.

Neri Oxman’s Experience

  • Principal

    Material Ecology

    (Non-Profit; Myself Only; Architecture & Planning industry)

    April 2006 — Present (2 years 6 months)

    Material Ecology addresses the area of material and environmental guided fabrication. As an interdisciplinary research initiative it undertakes design research in the intersection between digital design fabrication, material engineering and ecology. It is concerned with the way in which material organization may be informed by structural and environmental performance across all scales of design practice. Material Ecology seeks to establish an integrated approach to form-finding associating material properties and behavior with digital fabrication tools, techniques and technologies. Combined with the aspiration to efficiently and effectively relate material organization to environmental resources and conditions by embracing technological novelty, this approach aims at establishing a new sensibility to ecological design production.
