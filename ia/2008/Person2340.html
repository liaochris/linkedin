Adam Fowler’s Education

  • Worcester Polytechnic Institute

    M.S., Biotechnology, 1998 — 1999

    Master's Thesis - Developed and optimized a Polymer-Enhanced Diafiltration (PEDF) System for the removal of heavy metals from aqueous streams. Performed studies that investigated the fluid dynamics of metal loading and the effects on membrane interactions in the TFF-based system in order to identify and optimize critical process parameters. Examined effects of polymer characteristics (molecular weight, polydispersity) on process dynamics. Resulting system capable of reducing aqueous metal effluent concentrations to levels well below guidelines set by the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency. Work referenced as part of United States Patent No. US 6,533,938 B1 (issued 3/18/03).

  • Worcester Polytechnic Institute

    B.S., Biotechnology, 1994 — 1998

    Graduated with Distinction.

    Major Qualifying Project - Developed and optimized a fed-batch fermentation process for poly-g-glutamic acid (g-PGA), a biopolymer product of Bacillus licheniformis. Examined the effects of medium composition, feed regimens, and operating conditions on product yield. Identified process parameters capable of differentiating biomass increases vs. protein production phases, as well as factors affecting polymer characteristics (molecular weight, polydispersity). Improved product yield 5-10 fold over previous batch process. Gained experience in polymer characterization; Visco-elastic analysis via rheological methods, and molecular weight/polydispersity characterization by size exclusion-multiple angle laser light scattering (SEC-MALLS).
