Stephen Burge’s Summary

Experienced components engineer who has created and managed a components engineer group. Having worked for small companies, I have a wide knowledge of piece parts – from mechanical pieces to the most sophisticated integrated circuit. Result of my efforts have been to make effective components engineers, standardize part use, prevent use of sunset parts, and insure correct parts are used on a program (quality, manufacturer centric, non-counterfeit, and correctly address pure tin issue).

Stephen Burge’s Experience

  • Research Engineering Staff

    Lockheed Martin

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    November 1991 — March 2008 (16 years 5 months)

    Worked with design engineering team, which allowed a 10% reduction in similar parts being selected by team. Additional benefits of effort were the prevention of sunset parts being selected for new design and prevention of pure tin parts being used.
    Led clean-up effort of existing piece part data that resulted in 20% reduction in parts in the database, standardization in nomenclature, and development of part description code.
    Led team of i2 User Group’s volunteers through enhancement voting process to drive i2 Technologies to develop and implement enhancements that were desired by the user group community.
    Mentored new components engineers in various aspects of component engineering that resulted in personnel being able to know who to contact, what questions to ask, and how to resolve piece part issues.
    Developed obsolescence tracking database for missile program that over a two-year period helped identify 330 obsolescence issues and avoided $2.2M worth of redesigns.
