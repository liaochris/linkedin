Anthony Dyer’s Summary

Bilingual (English & Spanish), successful commercial executive and entrepreneur with a strong background in corporate and European management covering commercial strategy, business administration, legal, product strategy, direct and indirect sales channel strategy (P&L level) and “go to market” marketing strategy (both sales channel implementation and product launch).

In addition to Anthony’s senior management experience for corporate organisations, Anthony’s experience also includes instigating and managing "start-up" businesses in foreign countries, pan-European business integration (acquisition integration), investment negotiations and M&A negotiations.

Anthony has lived in UK, France and Spain and has travelled extensively across EMEA and USA.

Anthony Dyer’s Specialties:

European Start-Up, Acquisition Integration, Investment (Seed Capital), Voice Over IP, Internet and Wireless Technologies.

Anthony Dyer’s Experience

  • Commercial Director, Founder

    The Connectivity Warehouse S.L.

    (Telecommunications industry)

    December 2004 — Present (4 years)

    To overcome the lack of telecommunication infrastructures and to provide telephone and internet services to the many unconnected communities in southern Spain, Anthony founded TCW in 2004.

    TCW is a licensed wireless network operator, an Internet Service Provider and VoIP telephone operator, owning its own network infrastructures and delivering its own services direct to end-user consumer and business customers. TCW’s 5GHz wireless network covers approximately 140km and gives connectivity possibilities to approximately 60% of the densely populated areas.

    TCW is a privately owned and funded company. Anthony is responsible for all commercial aspects of the business, which includes Investor Relations, Business Administration (Finance, Legal, and HR), Business Development, Sales and Product Strategy.

    Anthony has raised €2.2million in seed capital from private investors and banks to cover TCW’s initial R&D, operational and capital costs.
