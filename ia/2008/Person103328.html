Katherine Mancuso’s Summary

In my academic life, I have seven years of research experience on the anthropology of technology and internet communications, and three in disability studies. I am an experienced researcher, writer, and have presented at multiple conferences. I have also helped to organize multiple academic conferences.

In my life as an artist, I have worked in video, photography, dance, music, theatre, and new media, and have an extensive portfolio based around themes of dis/ability, the body, ephemeral/recycled media, and technology. On the arts management side, I have extensive technical experience with credits in video production and theatre lighting. I have worked in a variety of customer-facing and event/education roles, as well as a research job, within a museum environment. Currently, I am the technology committee chair for a major arts and activism organization.

As a technology professional, I have worked in a number of non-profits and educational settings. I have managed and written web content, managed Sharepoint sites, worked with databases, done technical support, and done graphic design. Most recently, I volunteered for LiveJournal.com doing direct user facing support and troubleshooting, using a bug tracker system.

I wish to combine my experience in these various sectors by becoming an online community manager. I believe that this is the type of job that can best combine my extensive experience in customer facing roles, my technical expertise, and my passion for creating community through activism and technology.

online community management, web content management, technical support, grassroots activism, media production, qualitative methods, anthropology
