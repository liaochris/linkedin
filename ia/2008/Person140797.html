Nick Zart’s Summary

Currently studying with CoachU for my ICF accreditation, I am living the best time of my life as a Business & Life Coach.

The biggest part of my career life was spent training, languages at first, then computer which led to setting up networks. My consulting career focused on figuring what my clients really wanted and matching them with the right professionals.

I've worked closely with small and mid-size companies, entrepreneurs and freelance consultants, understanding their needs and budgets, their structures and motivations. I owned a few small businesses, the last one in New York City where I associated with other consultants to build a highly successful team. I understand what both small companies and consultants are looking for, having been in both visions and aligning them with most of my business life. I felt most comfortable figuring out company's visions and aligning them with what they needed to make it happen. The most rewarding part of my career was listening to my clients, understanding their needs, getting the right people for the job, and determining a plan of action.

Working in real estate gives me a greater understanding of individual's needs and how they ask for them. It has helped me feel people's dreams and visions. It takes a lot of listening, asking the right questions and getting to the source. Working in real estate has cemented my faith in high ethics. I am lucky to work with an amazing team of highly dedicated professionals.

I thought I had been coaching people throughout my work experience until I took my first level of coaching at CoachU. Now I understand what coaching is (and isn't) and I am excited to start my coaching practice. Coaching is so much more subtle than any of the consulting or advising work I have done. I look forward to building a healthy practice this year. I finally found my calling.

Nick Zart’s Specialties:

An unquenchable curiosity for life and people, listening, asking questions, finding out about people, their lives, their history, their experiences, understanding them and company's dreams, visions and needs. Sensing core values, motivations and what works best. Computer skills, networks, hardware and software. Getting the right professionals for the right jobs. I have an innate sense of social networking and love building teams of professionals working toward the same goal.
