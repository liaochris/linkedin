Rony E. Amaya’s Summary

Researcher interested in Advanced Technologies for RFIC, MMIC and Sensor applications. Semiconductor Device modeling and Device fabrication experience with emphasis on Circuit and System applications. Interested in evaluating and pushing the boundaries of integrated semiconductor and package technologies: frequency, power, level of integration, low-cost, etc.

Experience working in a product-driven industrial environment but with emphasis on research and added academic flavour.

Rony E. Amaya’s Specialties:

Advanced Semiconductor Technologies for RFIC/MMIC/Sensor Applications: Silicon, GaAs, GaN and Package Technologies: LTCC and IPD.
On-wafer probing measurement and techniques: SOLT, TRL
RFIC and MMIC Circuit Design and Characterization.
EM Passive Modeling using 2.5D and 3D commercial simulator packages: Agilent Momentum, Sonnet, EMPOWER, IE3D, ASITIC, HFSS, CST Microwave Studio, Empire XCel.

Rony E. Amaya’s Education

  • Carleton University

    PHD, Electrical Engineering, 2001 — 2005

    Thesis Title: "Overcoming the limitation of Silicon MMIC's"

    Active and passive device modeling and fabrication which combined with circuit techniques, makes an attempt to push the limits of operation of silicon technologies at millimeter-wave frequencies. The emphasis of this thesis was to show that the SOC capabilities of silicon can potentially place it in direct competition with III-V technologies (GaAs, GaN, InP) for the high performance microwave market.

    Over 25 academic publications in refereed Journal and Conference proceedings as a direct result of PHD academic work. For an updated list of publications, visit my DOE website link below.

  • Carleton University

    M.Eng., Electrical Engineering, 1999 — 2001

    Thesis Title: "Contact resistance reduction in AlGaN/GaN heterostructure field effect transistors" presented at the 2003 Nitride Semiconductor Conference in Aachen, Germany and published at the Journal of Physica Status Solidi.

    R. Amaya, H. Tang, J.A. Bardwell, J.B. Webb, T.W. MacElwee and N.G. Tarr "Contact resistance reduction in AlGaN/GaN heterostructure field effect transistors," Physica Status Solidi c0, pp. 236-239, Jun. 2002.
