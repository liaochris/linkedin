Zayera Khan’s Summary

Zayera is an expert in usability, interface design, design strategy, and customer experience. She has helped organizations in the telecommunications, CPG, and utilities sectors transform their Web sites by creating a future vision for their online presence and providing cross-industry views of online best practices.
At Forrester, Zayera held the position of customer experience analyst, published the following reports:
> Senior European Visitors Have Specific Web Needs. How Web Sites Can Address Their Needs.
> European Mobile Operators' Cross-Channel Experience, 2007. We Apply Our Cross-Channel Review Methodology To Four Mobile Operators.
> Best And Worst Of European Cross-Channel Experience, 2007. Forrester Applies Its Cross-Channel Review Methodology To 12 European Firms.
> European Travel Agencies' Cross-Channel Experience, 2007. We Apply Our Cross-Channel Review Methodology To Four Travel Agencies.
> The VoIP Customer Experience: Work In Progress. VoIP Customer Experience Is Not Ready For Mainstream Users.
> Young Consumers Don't Just Wanna Have Fun Online. What European Young Consumers Want From Web Sites.
> European Young Consumers Are Concerned About Online Security. How Web Sites Should Address Young Consumers' Privacy Concerns.
> Do You "Remember Me"? Improving Automatic Sign-In Enhances Convenience.
> European Car Insurance Cross-Channel Experience, 2007 (co-author). We Apply Our Cross-Channel Review Methodology To Four Car Insurance Firms

Her previous work experience includes usability consultant and interaction designer for Swisscom Fixnet, Cambridge Technology Partners, and Unamite.
Zayera has interaction design and usability experience in the areas of the Web, IPTV, and mobile interfaces.
She has conducted research in the fields of eLearning and human-robot interaction. Zayera has a master's in interaction design from Malmö University, Sweden, and a bachelor's degree in cognitive science from the University of Skövde, Sweden.
