Catie McIntyre Walker’s Summary

From 1988 - 1997, I owned an event planning business that developed and managed large gift shows featuring cottage industry businesses that designed hand crafted gift items and specialty gourmet packaged foods. These shows were held in large hotel ballrooms and coliseums. The business also included developing mini-conventions for crafters and artisans with specialty workshops.

Over the past 35 years, as the number of wineries and vineyards in Washington grew from near zero to more than 500, my interest in vitis vinifera got piqued. I went to wine festivals and toured wineries across the state, mostly sipping Merlots and Rieslings. In the 1980s I visited Napa Valley and developed a taste for Cabernet Sauvignon. In the ‘90s I visited the Hunter Valley region in Australia and became very fond of Shiraz/Syrah. Later, I made more trips to the Bay Area in California and learned to love the ripe, spicy Zinfandels.

I have participated in many aspects of winemaking, from grafting Merlot clones on rootstock, planting a row of Merlot in a vineyard, working the autumnal crush, and onto the finished product bottled by hand, as well as working the automated bottling line. I toured vineyards in Washington State to understand the terroir that is only unique to those various appellations. I had the great opportunity to study under Dr. Ann Noble, Professor and Sensory Scientist, Emeritus at UC Davis, and creator of the Wine Aroma Wheel. I have a certification in Viticulture Science, but wine retail is in my heart.

I have taken courses in web design and retail, especially e-commerce with an emphasis in wine.

It was important to me to participate in every aspect of wine - - from the vine takes to the liquid in the glass. I have over 8 years of working retail and promotions at various wineries. Educating potential customers about wine is important to me and this cannot be done well unless I can bring my knowledge and experiences with me.
