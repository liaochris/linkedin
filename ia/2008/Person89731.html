Jerome Osselaer’s Summary

1983 : when my passion started. First season as a football player. One year later, France won the Euro and Canal + was born.

2008 : as an MBA ESSEC graduate, a few years of experience allowed me to enrich my course of study with an operational and international dimension. After three years running a sports management company I founded, I spent another three years in a consulting company, where I developed competencies such as analytical skills, priority management, day-to-day CRM, team-working experience, market research or computer skills.

As I just finished my MBA in Football Industry, I strongly desire to join the Strategic Marketing Department of a company in the sports industry, or a Sports Consulting Company, as a perfect professional achievement mixing my consulting and marketing background with my passion for sports, in a challenging environment. Fluent in French and English, and thanks to my maturity, my ambition and my persistency, I would like to actively contribute to the development of that company.

I’m interested in strategic marketing, including brand strategy, CRM strategy and sponsorship strategy, in order to generate new revenue streams or increase existing ones. The football industry, and clubs in particular, is financially TV-dependent and has been growing tremendously in the last 15 years, along with satellite and cable TV, especially in Europe. Consequently, there are still huge opportunities to develop other financial drivers through marketing and commercial levers, together with the redevelopment / construction boom of new stadia considered as profit centres.

I am open to relocate wherever, if needed for the right job.

According to me, 3 of the best Marketing Managers in Football : Edward Freeman, Peter Kenyon, and Lars-Christer Olsson.

Outside football, 3 of the best Marketing Strategies : Nespresso, iPod, Wii.

My motto : “Give a man a fish he’ll eat for a day. Teach a man how to fish, he’ll eat forever”.
