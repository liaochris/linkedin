Ian Jindal’s Summary

Ian Jindal is an entrepreneur, corporate executive and advisor, working in eCommerce for over a decade - in integrated multichannel retail, publishing and broadcast. Ian's expertise is in business transformation - whether at startup and growth stages, turnaround activities or leading step-changes in large organisations.

eCommerce is an area where marketing, merchandsing and technology collide: Ian's skills in sales, business development and corporate growth - supplemented by deep technical knowledge, analytical skills and business process engineering - form the basis of his advisory and training work.

Ian is active in senior, strategic consulting on etail change to major high street retailers (House of Fraser, Austin Reed) as well as direct and niche operators (Otto UK, CoutureLab.com) and works both as a retained advisor and non-executive director.

Recognising the skills gap in eCommerce, Ian is a frequent conference keynote and chair, speaking on online marketing, e-Commerce, business change and casting a cynical-yet-enthusiastic eye over Web2.0.

Ian undertakes senior, strategic training and development programmes - in conjunction with e-Consultancy.com - for clients including IPC Media, Turner Broadcast, Trinity Mirror and Barclaycard.

Ian was the founder and now Editor in Chief of Internet Retailing - the magazine, portal and conference for the UK's multichannel retailers.

Ian Jindal’s Specialties:

Large scale, enterprise eCommerce activities in integrated multichannel retail, online publishing and digital/interactive TV; digital marketing, including direct communications, customer acquisition and retention, web2.0 and complex behavioural segmentation; Journalism - commentary and analysis on retail, technology and marketing; Creating and building business - Startup, transformation and growth.
