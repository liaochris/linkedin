Kevin C. Tofel’s Summary

Involved with podcasting since it's humble beginnings, I have embraced this new content delivery method several ways. I have written tutorials on how to create podcasts, including what hardware and software is useful. Beta testing various podcatchers and reporting on their usage has helped provide me an end-user focus. I coined the term "podcatcher" on 10/9/2004; a term used extensively today. Through the bi-weekly technology podcast (MobileTechRoundup) and daily weblogging and video reviews of the latest in mobile technology, I've continued to develop an on-show presence and have even branched out to video podcasts and video reviews of mobile technology devices. My published writings have appeared outside of my own weblog as well: I've authored articles in Pocket PC Magazine and most recently PC Magazine, a Ziff-Davis property. Additionally, I helped start the HDBeat weblog on HDTV technologies and built the site & team up from scratch. In 8 short months traffic rose from a few hundred views per day to almost half a million per month. HDBeat eventually morphed into EngadgetHD and is one of the most prominent HDTV blogs today. I've also covered the Consumer Electronics Show with the Engadget team at their request, further exemplifying my areas of expertise.

Kevin C. Tofel’s Specialties:

Microsoft MVP - Windows Tablet PC. Subject matter expert on mobile technologies, particularly UltraMobile Personal Computers (UMPCs).
