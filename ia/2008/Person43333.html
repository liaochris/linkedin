I am an EMEA and Asia-Pacific Markets Expert in Corporate Actions at The Bank of New York Mellon. Woaaw!!! That sounds very professional.

Let me explain you what is a Corporate Action:
A C.A. is an event that can influence the value of a security and can affect the shareholder. Some examples are stock splits, tender offers, dividends, mergers, rights issues and spin offs.

My team is specialized in Global Securities Lending. Basically, we lend securities to institutional brokers and we follow up all Corporate Actions events on the entitled loan trades. The main reason to borrow securities is to cover a short position. Many complications can arise when the borrower (the broker) defaults on its obligations (like to return the loaned securities).

Securities Lending is a very lucrative business (not for me, but for the bank).

Let me now explain you the challenge of globalisation in Corporate Actions.
C.A. is plagued by the risk associated with:
- manual processing
- non-standardized processing
- languages
- market practices
- regional differences
- a multiplicity of intermediaries
- inconsistent data quality
- disparate systems

The big challenge is then to set up a straight through processing system and try to be cost-neutral. Custodian banks are spending millions of Euros every year to implement an automated solution.

I hope you have learned something new and I wish you a very good day.
