David Wroblewski’s Summary

As a writer: debut novel "The Story of Edgar Sawtelle", published June 10, 2008. See www.edgarsawtelle.com for details.

As a software professional: over twenty-five years of experience designing and building distributed object-oriented systems in commercial and research settings. Pivotal technical leadership roles spanning five corporations, with a track record of integrating novel technologies into new products.

Deep Java experience applying J2EE and related technologies, including Spring, Hibernate, Lucene, Servlets, SQL, RMI, Jini, JUnit, Swing, and Prefuse.

Exceptional writing and presentation skills. Effective in client/partner communication. Adept at making complex technology understandable, accessible and useful.

Knowledgeable in diverse domains, including tiered information services, workflow management, human-computer interface design, telecom operations/workflow, natural language processing, latent semantic analysis, heuristic graph search, planning and resource reservation systems, autonomous vehicle control, and embedded control software for ring laser gyroscopes.

Language mastery includes Java, Perl, C++, C, Tcl, and Lisp.
OS mastery includes Windows, Linux, Macintosh.

David Wroblewski’s Specialties:

GUI design, heuristic graph search, planning and resource reservations algorithms, distributed systems architecture.
