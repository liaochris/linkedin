James Barrett’s Summary

James 'Jim' Barrett
b. 1969

Education:

Journalism and Asian Studies

Masters of Arts
English Literature, University of Umeå (Sweden 2002-04)

PhD commenced 2004 University of Umeå
Expected completion date early 2009

Awards and Scholarships:
Knut and Alice Wallenberg Foundation Scholarship for Research in Modern Languages for 2004-05

I have professional experience in media (film audio and print), literature and cultural studies. During the 1990's I worked in community arts in Sydney Australia, publishing a fanzine, working in performance art, organising community cultural events and social justice projects. During this time I become involved with Aboriginal artists from Western Cape York and worked on three exhibitions that were bought to Sydney of work from the region. Between 1996 an 2000 I lived traveled in Asia, Europe and Australia. From 2000 I live in the north east of Sweden. I have studied web design, Swedish language (proficient speaker), pedagogy, English linguistics, Cultural studies, English literature, experience and interactive design, digital humanities and computer programing. I have been working with online virtual worlds since 2003, mainly with ActiveWorlds, Adobe Atmosphere and Second Life.
