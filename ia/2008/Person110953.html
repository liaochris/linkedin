Dr. Lauchlan A. K. Mackinnon’s Summary

I deliver business value to organisations by reviewing current situations and objectives, framing options, aligning business value to be delivered with corporate strategic objectives, and jointly implementing organisational initiatives - supported by effective change management - to realise the value.

Key areas of activity include:

* Innovation systems (capture, develop, select and implement ideas and turn them into tangible business outcomes)
* Creativity tools, techniques and culture
* Change Management
* Organisational Culture

Other foci of interest include:

* Balanced Scorecard and Strategy Mapping (define and implement metrics to realise and monitor the effectiveness of your strategy)
* Blue Ocean Strategy ('value innovation' to shape markets you own and where your competition is irrelevant)
* Organisational Learning, Knowledge Management, and Wisdom Management
* Scenario Planning (identify your primary business drivers, work with your management and staff to develop flexible mental frameworks to respond proactively to changes in your business operating environment)

Dr. Lauchlan A. K. Mackinnon’s Specialties:

change management, organisational culture, creativity, innovation, knowledge management, organisational learning, idea management systems, idea management software, scenario planning, balanced scorecard, strategy maps, performance management, PRINCE2

  • Doctoral Researcher

    The University of Queensland

    (Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Research industry)

    February 1998 — March 2006 (8 years 2 months)

    My doctoral thesis was concerned with creativity and the spread of ideas. I:

    * drew on the psychology of creativity literature to examine the genesis of economic ideas in the creative work processes of economists, and
    * drew on sociological theory to help understand generally how economic ideas become institutionalised into economic institutions.

    I argued that once economists have ideas about the economy, and those ideas are then disseminated within the economy, those ideas help change the nature and dynamics of the economic system and economists are not 'objectively' modelling economic systems being studied like classical 19th century physicists, but are instead active participants in shaping the economic system they are studying - more like quantum physicists in the 20th century.

    My thesis (http://eprint.uq.edu.au/archive/00004455/) was very interdisciplinary, drawing on literature in economics, psychology, sociology and philosophy.
