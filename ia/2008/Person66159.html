Frederico Duarte’s Summary

At some point in my life I failed to get excited about the generation of form. As a designer, this is almost suicidal – it's like being a pole vault athlete and growing tired of jumping. But at the same time I found out I actually got excited about something most designers aren't really concerned with: being critical, observing and comparing "designed things", see how they have an impact on (regular) people's lives. And ultimately, making all these "regular people" aware of the importance of all these forms being generated by designers everyday, all over the world.

  • Freelance design writer

    Self-employed

    (Self-Employed; Myself Only; Design industry)

    December 2006 — Present (1 year 4 months)

    I am currently writing regularly on design mainly for Público – Portugal's most respected newspaper – for its weekly culture supplement, Ípsilon, and also for P2, its daily culture and leisure supplement.
    As such, Público has been, since March, Portugal's sole newspaper (and one of the few in the world) to have a designer writing about design in its team of collaborators – an area largely overlooked in the general press, especially regarding dedicated criticism.
    I am currently writing a large series of profiles on Portuguese designers (from a 65-year old 'old school' poster designer to recently graduated mavericks), but am also writing other pieces, such as a design review on Jacques Tati's "Mon Oncle", conference and exhibition reviews and interviews with designers.

    Apart from Público, I am a contributor to MUDE's (Lisbon's Design and Fashion Museum) website and have contributed to Icon, Colors and Sugo magazines
