Paul K. Freeman’s Summary

I've recently joined Siemens as a Unix Support Team manager supporting the BBC websites.

Since 1997 I've worked in PruTech (Prudential IT) originally as an Incident Manager and then as an Application Support Team manager.

During my time as an Incident Manager I became recognised as "guru" on the supported systems and was usually called in to manage the safe & timely recovery "high profile" incidents. I was sent to India to provide training and support to the new Service Management team there. I was instrumental in the introduction of “Key Service Line” documents that aided the Incident Management team to manage incidents systems that they knew little about.

I've also attended the LESLP Crisis Management course to become a "bronze Crisis Manager".

As an Application Support Team Manager I was involved in the day to day running of a team of seventeen staff (including 2 contractors). Aspects of the role included:
• Technical, Man & Stakeholder Management including many third-parties
• Effective interpersonal, communication and time management skills
• Training, Staff appraisal, Recruitment & Performance Reviews.
• Presenting informative and enjoyable presentations
• Running effective business meetings, project meetings and attending Incident Review meetings
