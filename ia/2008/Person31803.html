Cathelijne Lania’s Experience

  • Managing Director (owner)

    A Beautiful Story

    (Privately Held; Myself Only; Apparel & Fashion industry)

    September 2006 — Present (1 year 6 months)

    I founded A Beautiful Story in september 2006 (at that time the name was LINK, but because of too much confusion with other organisations we changed). A Beautiful Story wants to connect the power in people by beautiful products. We link the products of talented entrepreneurs in development to the market they deserve. Every product has, how suprising... a beautiful story!

    A Beautiful Story focusses on fashion accessories in the first place. At the moment the biggest priority is setting up the sales channels: a store in Amsterdam, a webshop and a direct selling channel (party's at people's homes and companies). The plan won the first price in the social entrepreneurs contest Zin in Zaken (www.zininzaken.nl). Very proud!

  • Marketing Coordinator Utz Kapeh

    Utz Kapeh Foundation

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    April 2005 — August 2006 (1 year 5 months)

    Utz Kapeh, meaning 'good coffee' in a Mayan language, sets the worldwide standard for socially and environmentolly responsible coffee production and sourcing (see www.utzkapeh.org).

    As a marketing manager I was responsible for developing the trademark/brand and tools & concepts with a focus on the buying market. Customers included big multinational coffeeroasters and traders, as f.e. Ahold/ICA and Saralee, together with many local roasters in Europe, Japan, USA.

  • Brand manager Saralee/ Caffiato & Caffitesse

    Saralee Douwe Egberts

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Consumer Goods industry)

    September 2003 — March 2005 (1 year 7 months)

    As Brandmanager in the Douwe Egberts Out of Home marketing team (B2B segment) I worked on the launch of the new product Caffiato Iced Cappuccino and on several new concepts (coffee machines) for the segment small/medium busines, health & care, horeca.

    Activities included new product development, market tests, communication, salesconcept development and launch implementation.

  • Brandmanager Saralee/ Lassie & Natrena

    Saralee Douwe Egberts

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Consumer Goods industry)

    November 2000 — August 2003 (2 years 10 months)

    How wonderful can a start of a career be? I enormously enjoyed my first job as brandmanager of the products Lassie (rice) and Natrena (sweeteners). I was responsible for communications, salespromotions, e-strategy, new product development and co-developing brand strategies.

  • Volunteer Projectmanager Student Project Nepal

    Student Project Nepal

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Civic & Social Organization industry)

    September 1999 — March 2000 (7 months)

    Student Project Nepal (www.studentenproject-Nepal.nl) provides the means to education for underpriviliged students in Nepal. Students can get a sponsor to cover the educational costs (books, administration etc) and there is a computer institute for cheap computer lessons.

    As volunteer project manager I went to Nepal in the start up phase to set up the computer institute. Together with Marcel Peeters, another Dutch volunteers, we arranged the location for the school, furniture, teachers and other personell, the first concepts for the educational program, etc.

    My stay in Nepal during the time lay the base for my current company. Here I saw all those people with great talents, but their situation mostly not allows their development because of the struggle for life... Something I want to tackle with LINKmyworld!
