Michael Chernick’s Summary

I have over 30 years of experience in the fields of mathematics and statistics including time in the military (as a civilian), in the aerospace industry, in the insurance industry.and in medical devices and pharmaceuticals. I have published over 30 journal articles and two books and am completing the second edition of my first book. I have done book reviews for journals and reviews of articles for Mathematical Reviews. I was once a top 100 reviewer on Amazon for my statistical book reviews. I am a Fellow of the American Statistical Association and a former President of the Southern California Chapter of the ASA. I have been listed in Marquee's Who's Who in the West, 1984, Marquee's Who's Who in Frontier Science and Technology 1984-1985, Who's Who in Society 1987, International Who’s Who of Professionals 1999, Lexington's Who’s Who of Executives and Professionals 1999-2000 and Madison's Who’s Who 2006, 2007-2008. I have also taught undergraduate and graduate level statistical courses and several short courses on bootstrap methods. I also won the Jacob Wolfowitz Prize in 1983 for the best paper in the American Journal of Mathematical and Management Sciences in the 1982 volume of that journal.

I am currently working full time as the head of biostatistical services in the Clinical Research Center at the Lankenau Institute for Medical Research. I also do
private consulting and am always looking for additional clients that I can help.

Michael Chernick’s Specialties:

Expert in time series analysis, bootstrap methods, stochastic processes, extreme value theory, survival analysis, group sequential methods and adaptive designs. I also have some familiarity with mixed linear models, generalized linear models, general estimating equations, nonlinear models. simultaneous statistical inference, repeated measures analysis and pk studies.
