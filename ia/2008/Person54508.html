David Hampton’s Summary

David R Hampton, PhD is Senior Principal Scientist for Medtronic ERS over the past six years, directing global clinical and technical partnerships and creating innovative products for cardiac arrest, myocardial infarction, and stroke. He works closely with academic and clinical researchers, creating a portfolio of solutions for early detection and rapid treatment of resuscitative events, which he then manages through a technology transfer process to create businesses around marketed products. In recognition of his leadership, he was awarded a Bakken Fellowship in 2004, Medtronic’s highest scientific honor. He holds a management certificate from Stanford University and is a fellow of the AIMBE, with many issued patents and peer-reviewed publications.

Dr Hampton developed the first commercial quantitative EEG and brainmapping systems for neurology and psychiatry, and later led teams developing interpretive ECG mapping systems. He created the first FDA-approved AMI detection algorithms, formed a profitable Medical Informatics division within his company, and led development of lifesaving “shock predictive” technologies for cardiac arrest in collaboration with resuscitation researchers in Europe and the United States.

Dr. Hampton is recently completed an MPhil degree (with distinction) at the Institute for Biotechnology at the University of Cambridge (UK). His thesis work defines business models for creation of spin-out companies from corporate projects, and he received a prestigious business startup grant from the Cambridge Angels venture group to develop unique coronary stent technology. Currently working in Holland, he focuses on creating innovative businesses at the interface of biotechnology and medical devices, emphasizing cross-border opportunities for collaborative development.
