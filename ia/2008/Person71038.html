Giampiero Gallo’s Summary

Born in Verona on December 8, 1959. I graduated in Economics and Business Administration from the University of Florence in 1982, and I earned a Ph.D. in Economics from the University of Pennsylvania in 1989. I am Professor of Econometrics at the School of Economics of the University of Firenze where I teach Econometrics and Econometrics of Financial Markets. I have been a Maître Assistant Suppléant at the Département d’économétrie of the Université de Genève (1984), consultant with the World Bank (1986, 1990), Assistant Professor of Economics at UNC-Chapel Hill (1989-1992), and visitor at the Department of Economics of the European University Institute between 1994 and 1998. More recently (1999, 2000, 2001 and 2003), I have enjoyed the hospitality of UC San Diego, New York University and University of Pennsylvania. Current research interests relate to nonlinear econometric modelling, financial econometrics and econometric forecasting.
