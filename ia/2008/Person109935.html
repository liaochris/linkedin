Kyle Pasciutti’s Summary

I recently graduated cum laude from The University of Massachusetts Amherst where I majored in theater with a technical focus, and minored in psychology. I have been involved with technical theater in some capacity or another since the age of ten, and live staged performance will always be a calling near and dear to my heart. It has been my main area of study in the recent past, and the skill set I gained during that time tends to be a starting point for most of my endeavors. I fully expect however, that in addition to staged plays, I will as well contribute work to other fields such as film, television, other live events including concerts, and whatever else comes my way. I'm quite looking forward to it in fact. I like to challenge myself, and rarely approach a new task with any form of apprehension. Just ask the last Director I worked with who couldn't understand why I was smiling during tech load-in while moving 100 pound or more equipment and running cable all day in 90° weather with full sun. I like to keep things new and interesting and moving. I am currently situated in Cheshire, Connecticut though I eagerly anticipate traveling to wherever I am sought after. Travel seems to be one of the perks of an un-rooted recent college graduate, and thus far both New York and Boston have been main destinations. I hope to use this site as a means of branching out and seeing what the world has to offer.

Kyle Pasciutti’s Experience

  • Fright Haven Haunted House (Charitable Organization)

    (Entertainment industry)

    September 2008 — November 2008 (3 months)

    Initially hired on as an actor, I quickly became known as the scariest portion of the haunted house, going so far as to surpass previous records of “Scarring people out the door”. I was eagerly added on to the construction team after informing management of my design background. I worked on construction, painting, prop setup, prop restoration, animatronics setup, ride setup, ride troubleshooting, and breakdown. After only 3 days, I was quickly offered a managing position due to recognition of responsibility and talent in personnel management during construction. I further assisted in the areas of publicity, security, crowd management, and makeup.

  • Cheshire Performing and Fine Arts

    (Entertainment industry)

    1996 — July 2008 (12 years)

    Cheshire Performing and Fine Arts holds two annual summer show programs, both of which I can usually be found working. The first program is CYT (short for Cheshire Youth Theater), and focuses on youthful shows and a younger crowd. The second program, which I helped found, is Comedy l0l (LOL or 101 depending on how you look at it), and focuses on funny shows for High School graduates and above. I began working with the program in 1996 and gradually worked my way up from Run Crew to Set Designer, Sound Designer, and Tech Director. I have held just about every tech position that they have, and have even taught a bit here and there. I have helped cut costs for the program, streamline the overall process, and introduced new ways of doing things. They have always been good to me, and I like to return the favor.
