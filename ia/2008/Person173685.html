Shahzad Arif’s Summary

I've been working in General Practice for the past four years, working as a Principal in a Partnership of three General Practitioners (including myself) for the past three. I'm involved with practice-based commissioning, clinical governance and teaching medical students in my practice, in addition to the day to day clinical and non-clinical work I have to undertake which involves responsible delegation. I am heavily into time management & audit as a standard for monitoring and improving both clinical and non-clinical outcomes. I am also a member of the Local Medical Committe and sit on PCT (Primary Care Trust) committees. I am a skilled negotiator as I work as an independant contractor with profit motives which helps me take on expertise in my field of healthcare provision, to suit the needs of my patients. As a GP, I have extensive training in communication skills which is the foundation on which my expertise is based. In 2006-07 I personally generated additional income of £27K for the Practice, in addition to what our Practice as a whole achieved. I work with a budget of between £500K-£600K per annum. My goals are to further perfect telemedicine and medical databases to supplement the quality of care that can be provided to my patients, and to extend my teaching roles to lecturership at the University of Sheffield. Moreover, to work on ideas and incentives for achieving higher standards of quality and efficiency within my Practice and to work with similar people globally to learn from, strengthen and improve such skills and knowledge further. My intent is to put into practice such skills and to set into motion cycles of audit which will further increase quality and efficiency.
