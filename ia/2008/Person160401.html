Richard Landry’s Summary

Richard Landry is the founder and CEO of richmedium, a strategic consulting firm serving emerging organizations in media, information technology, and social enterprise. richmedium helps its clients to embed the values, technologies, and business practices of the social web strategically into their business models and operations, accelerating the growth and impact of their enterprises and conferring lasting competitive advantage.

Landry works with senior executives, investors, and funders who recognize the emerging role of social values in reshaping global markets, and who seek to develop and implement business strategies that produce a new blend of financial and social value. His strategy engagements have enabled clients to successfully launch, gain scale, enter new lines of business, and develop deep organizational understanding of the forces influencing their markets and enterprises.

A senior executive with more than twenty years of experience leading emerging organizations through periods of transformative change, Landry is an innovator and thought leader on the convergence of media, information technology, and social enterprise, and has been at the forefront of new ways to fund and grow media startups and social enterprises.

Richard Landry’s Experience

  • CEO

    richmedium

    (Management Consulting industry)

    2000 — Present (8 years)

    Richard Landry is CEO of richmedium, a strategic consulting practice serving clients in media and marketing-driven organizations. He has worked with clients in the areas of strategic planning, business development, marketing strategy, new product development, and financing.

  • Executive Director

    Independent Press Association

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Publishing industry)

    June 2003 — December 2006 (3 years 7 months)

    In 2003, Landry joined the Independent Press Association (IPA), and in less than three years developed and launched several cutting-edge initiatives designed to strengthen and grow the independent media sector through innovative partnerships between private enterprise and public philanthropy. Under Landry’s guidance, the IPA became a thought leader in applying the emerging disciplines of social enterprise and social venture capital to the problem of sustaining and growing independent media, within the context of an increasingly consolidated and difficult corporate media environment.

  • Chairman & CEO

    HyperMedia Communications, Inc.

    (Public Company; 11-50 employees; Publishing industry)

    January 1992 — November 2000 (8 years 11 months)

    In 1992, Landry joined HyperMedia Communications, a Silicon Valley venture-capital startup. In little more than a year, HyperMedia’s flagship property, NewMedia magazine, became one of the hottest titles in the technology publishing market, and HyperMedia launched a successful IPO on the NASDAQ, making it a pioneer in the public financing of media startups, an achievement later recognized by Folio magazine.

    Landry turned HyperMedia into one of the first examples of a true cross-platform media company, and in the process anticipated several major media business trends. At the start of the Internet boom, HyperMedia launched one of the first examples of an advertising-supported publishing venture on the World Wide Web. As an industry leader, HyperMedia helped legitimize the digital content market through its annual NewMedia Invision Awards Festival, which opened doors for independent digital producers and set standards of excellence in a fast-changing market.

  • Editor-in-Chief and Publishing Director

    PC World

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Publishing industry)

    1986 — 1991 (5 years)

    In 1986 Landry joined PC World, the first high-tech publication targeted solely at the business professional market. Landry directed the magazine to appeal to a burgeoning new crossover group of professionals who used personal computers both at work and at home, for business, education, and personal entertainment. Landry later became publishing director at PCW Communications, and was responsible for conceiving new media specifically targeting the emerging consumer digital lifestyle.
