Todd R. Lockwood’s Summary

I've had a number of seemingly unrelated careers. What they all have in common is a desire to combine art, technology and business. Portrait Photography was my first career, beginning in 1966 at age 15. I majored in photography in college and later opened a studio in Denver. In 1980, I segued into the music recording business, building what became one of the premier recording studios on the east coast. I finally returned to portrait photography in 2007, this time approaching it as a fine art.

Todd R. Lockwood’s Experience

  • Owner

    Todd R. Lockwood Photography

    (Fine Art industry)

    August 2007 — Present (1 year 3 months)

    In 2007, I returned to my first love: studio portrait photography. I am currently working on a series of non-commissioned portraits of interesting people I've known. The series, titled One Degree of Separation, will go on public tour beginning in August 2008.

  • Owner

    Scudi USA

    (Arts and Crafts industry)

    January 1999 — December 2007 (9 years)

    Scudi was a dealer in automotive art, representing Terzo Dalia and Maurizio Conti, both scale model builders from Italy. Dalia is known for his beautiful 1/3-scale replicas of Ferrari engines, and Conti for his 1/10-scale car models. Conti is the son of the late Michele Conti, whose hand-built car models have been featured at the Museum of Modern Art.

    Scudi also produced exclusive automotive road tours for Ferrari enthusiasts. Our annual events included tours to the Canadian Grand Prix and a fall Gourmet Tour to luxury inns throughout Vermont and the Adirondacks.

  • Founder

    The Brautigan Library

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    January 1990 — December 1996 (7 years)

    In 1990, I founded The Brautigan Library in Burlington, Vermont. Inspired by a fictional library in a 1970 Richard Brautigan novel, the Brautigan Library only accepted unpublished works on its shelves. I established the non-profit infrastructure and creative team to bring this idea into reality. The Brautigan received considerable national media attention for it's groundbreaking approach. The library has been closed for a number of years, but efforts are under way to revive it in a different city.

  • Owner

    White Crow Audio

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    January 1980 — December 1995 (16 years)

    White Crow Audio began in my carriage house living room in 1980 and eventually grew into one of the premier music recording studios in the Northeast. The studio served major record labels from New York, Los Angeles and London, and artists ranging from Alice Cooper to Phish.

    White Crow segued into the computer software business and developed two software products for the Macintosh computer.

  • Owner

    Lockwood Studios, Ltd.

    (Photography industry)

    June 1973 — June 1977 (4 years 1 month)

    Lockwood Studios produced multimedia and industrial film projects for businesses in the Denver area. Also during this period, I began to refine my approach to fine art portrait photography.

Todd R. Lockwood’s Education

  • Rochester Institute of Technology

    Photography 1969 — 1973

    My RIT experience was unconventional. Early in my freshman year, my photography professor took me aside and told me I'd be wasting my time in his class. My work was already somewhat advanced when I left high school. I spent most of my four years at RIT working on an independent study basis, exploring a path I had already found for myself. While there, I co-wrote and directed a short subject film that took Best of Show in a Kodak-sponsored film festival.

    This web site does not acknowledge high school activity, but I believe my high school experience had a greater influence on my future than did college. I attended Northwood School in Lake Placid, New York (1965-1969). By the tenth grade I was the school's official photographer. I won a school prize for the portrait studies I did of my classmates for the 1969 school yearbook. One of those portraits is in my current touring exhibition, One Degree of Separation.
