Scott McCuine’s Experience

  • Laboratory Manager/Sr. Research Associate

    Joule Biotechnologies

    (Privately Held; Biotechnology industry)

    June 2008 — Present (2 months)

  • Visiting Scientist

    Whitehead Institute

    (Educational Institution; 201-500 employees; Biotechnology industry)

    August 2006 — Present (2 years)

    Current duties involve coordinating the experimental aspects of a cross-functional team that includes expertise in neurobiology, computational analysis and visualization, and genome-wide characterization of DNA interactions. Primary goals of the project are to decode how key transcriptional and chromatin regulators control the gene expression programs of embryonic stem (ES) cells, leading to new insights into the means by which ES cells can be programmed for therapeutic purposes. Position is also responsible for planning budgetary needs for each grant year, submission of yearly progress reports as required by funding agency, coordinating work across labs at three universities, and preparation of manuscripts and abstracts. When required, other duties include ensuring compliance with federal, state, and local safety regulations, oversight of collaborative projects, training of students and research staff in various experimental methods, and the development of new experimental methods.

  • Principal Consultant

    Albion BioSciences

    (Privately Held; Biotechnology industry)

    October 2006 — June 2008 (1 year 9 months)

    As principal consultant at Albion Bio, I provided business and scientific consulting services to biotechnology and life science start-up companies and to existing companies who wish to expand their operations or enter a new product area. Previous clients of mine include academic laboratories, microarray service providers, start-up biotechnology companies, and established life sciences firms of all sizes. I've leveraged my experience in operational management, research program development, and expansion of intellectual property along with the relationships gained working globally in North America, Europe, and Asia to establish new facilities and new programs in genomics, informatics, medical devices, and other emerging biotechnology applications.

  • Laboratory Manager/R&D Scientist

    Agilent Technologies

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; A; Biotechnology industry)

    April 2005 — August 2006 (1 year 5 months)

    Established a new research facility focused on emerging microarray applications such as genome-wide location analysis (ChIP-on-chip) and on the integration of these applications with other array-based genomic technologies. Duties included coordination and management of the day-to-day operation of the laboratory including standard operating protocol (SOP) optimizations, development of new product designs and validations, generation of intellectual property, creation of life-cycle documentation, and the evaluation of data analysis methodologies. Also guided implementation of on-site procurement systems and guidelines, coordinated operation of three business units within the facility, and while acting as primary safety officer worked with federal, state, and local regulatory agencies to secure and maintain operational and safety permits. Certified as Six Sigma White Belt.

  • Laboratory Manager/Staff Research Associate

    University of California, San Diego

    (Educational Institution; 10,001 or more employees; Higher Education industry)

    January 2002 — March 2005 (3 years 3 months)

    (Position began at Whitehead Institute and was relocated to UCSD.) Participated in project to assemble a model of the cellular circuitry of DNA damage response during the primary step in the onset of cancer by evaluating in the yeast S. cerevisiae the effects of the DNA alkylating agent methyl-methanesulfonate (MMS), a causative agent of eukaryotic DNA damage. This model employed a novel approach that correlated global gene expression, protein-DNA binding, and protein-protein interaction measurements to elucidate the effects of general cellular stress. As lab manager, directed high-throughput microarray studies utilizing gene expression and ChIP-on-chip assays, further optimized microarray procedures, and performed subsequent data analysis. Responsible for purchasing all key supplies, equipment, and reagents, tracking budget levels of grants from various funding agencies, serving as primary safety coordinator, and contributing to manuscripts and grant applications as needed.

  • Laboratory Manager/Research Assistant

    Texas A&M University

    (Educational Institution; 10,001 or more employees; Higher Education industry)

    February 2000 — January 2002 (2 years)

    Primary responsibility of position was to manage the day-to-day operation of a genomics service facility that sold scientific reagents to researchers world-wide. While manager, gross sales were increased by approximately 300% during term of employment in this capacity. Other accomplishments include the implementation of new systems for quality control, accounting, and invoicing, as well as the establishment of new inventory storage facilities to house bacterial artificial chromosome (BAC) libraries created for physical mapping and marker development. Also transitioned laboratory to a new, larger operational space, integrated robotic systems to increase high-throughput aspects of library production and distribution, and initiated programs for microarray production. Further duties included characterization of cloning sites and insert fragments for work on rice indica (Teqing) BAC libraries, and the construction and assembly of BAC libraries of plant, animal and insect species.
