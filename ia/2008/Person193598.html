Tyan Hynes’s Education

  • University of Washington

    MS, Library and Information Science, 2005 — 2007

    My interests include the use of electronic and web media to facilitate community formation, information exchange, and knowledge management. I have studied how Communities of Practice and Social Networks such as Facebook provide a means to accomplish community formation. Currently I am researching how Boundary Objects facilitate this process.

    In addition I am also interested in issues concerning taxonomy, controlled vocabularies, and user centered design to facilitate the user experience. Currently I am working on applying metadata to digital objects using a data dictionaries and will soon help evaluate the usability of a digital media website and user interface.

    Activities and Societies:
    Independent Research with Prof. Jeffrey Kim on Boundary Objects, studied Communities of Practice with Prof. Hala Annabi, member of the Special Libraries Association

  • University of Washington

    MS, Organic Chemistry, 2000 — 2005

    Primarily I studied the use of structure based drug design to develop new treatments for parasitic diseases such as Sleeping Sickness, Chagas' Disease, and Leishmaniasis. I used parallel and high-throughput synthetic techniques to create targeted molecule libraries. I tested these libraries using a variety of screening methods such as fluorescence polarization.

    I also have extensive experience with searching scientific and technical databases such as PubMed, SciFinder, and various U.S. and world patent sites. As part of my research I created and maintained a databases of treatments for my target diseases.

    With the help of several other Chemistry Graduate Students I helped co-found the Catalyst Committee, an organization dedicated to promoting minorities in the Chemical Sciences. Through this organization we organized and promoted lectures of interest across campus and organized a panel discussion on the use of Title IX to support women in science.

    Activities and Societies:
    co-founder of the Catalyst Committee to promote minorities in the Chemical Sciences
