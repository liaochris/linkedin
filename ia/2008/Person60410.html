Erik Ahroon’s Experience

  • Business Development Manager

    Acacia Technologies Group

    (Public Company; 11-50 employees; ACTG; Venture Capital & Private Equity industry)

    July 2007 — Present (9 months)

  • Principal

    ZEBRAtour Inc.- zebratour.com, mousiki.net

    (Entertainment industry)

    June 2006 — December 2007 (1 year 7 months)

    ZEBRAtour (www.zebratour.com) was founded on the belief that technological creation is valuable and new innovations can be found to make up for the traditional revenue streams which have been lost because of the recent digitization of music. As an advocate for musicians' rights and an innovator in Internet technology I worked on a business model to move the discussion away from digital rights management, piracy, copyright infringement discussions etc. that still dominate the general media, toward practical solutions leveraging the strengths of integrating live performances and digital technology. ZEBRAtour is a patented innovative solution using the internet to set up and execute a global infrastructure, linking underground artists to smaller venues world wide: The Future of the Music Industry.

  • Co-Founder

    Balans Health

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Sporting Goods industry)

    August 2006 — May 2007 (10 months)

    I co-founded Balanshealth with another serial entrepreneur where we conceived and prototyped innovative athletic products geared for the baby boomer demographic. The majority of the project was dedicated to treadmill designs aiding in balance and gait disorder treatment and prevention. The designs were never commercialized but are patented and protected for future licensing.

  • President

    Cadis Design Group

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Design industry)

    2003 — 2007 (4 years)

    I ran a small design/consulting shop in parallel to my early career and MBA. I managed a handful of projects for local Orange County entrepreneurs and smaller companies in need of prototyping and CAD implementation.

  • Product Development

    Star Trac Fitness

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Mechanical or Industrial Engineering industry)

    2005 — 2006 (1 year)

    I was recruited to Star Trac Fitness, a global leader in commercial grade fitness products (mainly treadmills, spinners and ellipticals). My main duties included reverse engineering current product that had no documentation from a recent acquisition sister company, Flex Fitness (which was acquired to offer turn key solutions for gyms) and reverse engineer ideation conceived by Designworks BMW. In addition I assisted upper level management with due diligence efforts of products brought to Star Trac by entrepreneurs.

  • Project Engineer

    Quantum Technologies

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; QTWW; Automotive industry)

    2004 — 2005 (1 year)

    As the Integrated Product Team Leader for the V4 General Motors Hydrogen Fuel Cell Program I facilitated the verification and validation of certain components through European Integrated Hydrogen Project (EIHP). I was in charge mainly of the pressure regulation downstream of Hydrogen storage and prior to the fuel cell. In addition I managed the outsourcing of components to key vendors and monitored the strict compliance needed for the automotive industry.

  • Program Manager

    Carter Cryogenics

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Mechanical or Industrial Engineering industry)

    2001 — 2004 (3 years)

    I managed the design, manufacturing, assembly and testing of high pressure cryogenic centrifugal pumps for liquid natural gas terminals around the world. I aided in the standardization of a hydraulic product line using modern CFD-CAD analysis which innovated new revolutionary components such as spiral inducers, continuous cross over diffusers and new geometries of impellers which significantly maintained NPSH eliminating cavitation and increasing efficiency dramatically. I traveled extensively throughout the world monitoring the implementation and testing of the pumps for such industry giants as Chicago Bridge and Iron and Flour Daniel.
