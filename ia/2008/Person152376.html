Trevor Macenski, REA’s Summary

Trevor has six years of experience as a Project Manager for MBA. He is an experienced California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA) practitioner who has experience working with institutions and jurisdictions across California to achieve their objectives while adhering to CEQA. Trevor began his career as a Policy Analyst for the UC Davis Center for Environmental Conflicts Analysis, researching and developing polices evaluations for watershed collaboration partnerships. As an Environmental Planner, and Project Manager he has been an author of many CEQA and environmental documents such as: environmental impact reports (programmatic and project specific), environmental impact statements, mitigated negative declarations, initial studies, and EIR addendums. Trevor has experience with transportation planning and environmental compliance documents for roadway realignments, bridge replacement projects, roadway improvements, Regional Transportation Plans, Walkability Improvement Plans and other transportation and policy related documents. Trevor has experience in preparing HPSR's, NES's, NESMI's, Noise Impact Analyses (FHWA Model), Air Quality Conformity Documents, CMAQ Funding Documents and other transportation related compliance documents. Having worked with the Federal Highway Administration, multiple Caltrans districts and regional transportation agencies, Trevor has a solid understanding of the FTIP, STIP, and RTIP processes. Trevor has experience in preparing housing studies, State-mandated housing elements, and federally mandated consolidated plans. In addition, he is technical proficient in wetland delineations and assisted in designing and evaluating constructed wetland water treatment systems. Trevor has a wide range of environmental impact assessment and planning working in both the public and private sectors on environmental, socio-economic, and land use projects.
