Joachim Scholz’s Summary

I have been with Alcoa (AFL) since 1995. It all started out in Germany in 1992 where I was hired as a Systems and Wiring Harness Design Engineer to Michels Kabel GmbH.

In 1994 AFL Automotive, a joint venture between Alcoa and Fujikura, bought the small company Michels Kabel GmbH and my wife and I relocated for 2.5 years to Dearborn, MI. The job was a team leader position for Advanced Engineering where we studied European B-Car line wiring harness architectures for the American market. That was integrated in the overall vision of Ford Global 2000. After successful completing this project, we returned to Germany where I was promoted to Supervisor and later Manager for Core Engineering.

After three years, AFL needed my support again and asked if we were willing to relocate to help AFL's C3P transition in the Ford business. In November 2000 we relocated again to Michigan and I started as Manager CAD systems for AFL. Later my responsibilities increased and I was getting additionally the resposnibilities of the local IT department.

I stayed with Alcoa (AEES) for over 16 years. In March 2007 I was asked by SEWS-DTC (Sumitomo Electrical Wiring Systems - Detroit Technical Center) to join Sumitomo, a global leader of many industries.

My current position is a C3P/Systems Engineering Manager. With my expertise Sumitomo will be able to successfully implement Ford Motor Company's CAD tools and methodologies.
