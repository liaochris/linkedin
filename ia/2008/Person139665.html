Michael Nelsen’s Summary

Having spent 25 years in counseling individuals and families, I am passionate about programs and policies that treat the diseases of mental illness and addictions. I have designed clinical programs for adolescent substance abusers, built individual treatment plans and led parenting classes for those parents/grandparents trying to negotiate an overburdened health care delivery system for juvenile behavioral disorders.
Beginning my career by bringing senior citizens and community services together, I optimized networking for older Americans in need of housekeepers, personal attendants, financial experts, or just in need of company and conversation.
As I moved into community mental health, I began studying various therapies and counseling theories. I quickly connected behavioral medicine with troubled behavior and worked with nurses and psychiatrist to bring relief and management to incurable disorders. I expanded my expertise by moving to inpatient and residential modalities. While in this phase of my career I noticed that some patients just were not getting well and were repeaters of programs and hospitalizations. What those clients shared was not just mental illness, but also an addiction to alcohol and other drugs.
At this point having treated both adults and adolescents, I chose to work in depth with addictions and teenagers at a regional substance abuse treatment center. Here I could address family issues as well as active addiction and also community issues.
Now I find myself at the crossroads having moved from the Midwest to Northern Virginia. I still on the front lines of treating addiction, I now want to move into writing policies and program consulting as well as supervision and teaching. Always the idealist I seek to focus on personal journeys and human potentials. I am open to the spiritual path as the next direction I'll take in confronting any blockages to human growth and happiness.
