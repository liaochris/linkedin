Jason Frank’s Summary

Computers are used to simulate all kinds of processes in science and industry. An everyday example is the software used to predict the weather; but also everthing from molecular dynamics to cellestial mechanics, airplane design, circuit simulations, chemical reactions, and other industrial processes.

My research is in the field of numerical analysis, the mathematics behind the algorithms used to simulate such dynamical systems.

My team tries to use knowledge of the application to design specialized algorithms with superior properties.

Jason Frank’s Specialties:

Numerical analysis of dynamical systems including time-dependent partial differential equaitons, geometric integration (Hamiltonian and variational methods) for wave equations and fluid dynamics, especially those those of atmospheric and ocean science.
