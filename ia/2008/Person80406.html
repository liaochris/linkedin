Jennifer Toone’s Summary

I began at Emerson College as a musical theatre major and completed with a degree in communications -a change based on the prospect of combining my mind for business with my passion for creativity. I graduated from Emerson in 1995 with honors.

In 1995, I landed a position at a 1200 kHz radio station in Massachusetts. I tripled as on-air voice talent and copywriter, an FCC-licensed board operator for a nightly sports talk show, and by day, receptionist and assistant to the advertising staff. The station began airing The Gene Burns Program and hired me as executive producer.

My experiences with radio advertising led to my next opportunity. I was recruited by a Boston area advertising agency, Berkley Advertising. Within six months, I advanced to account executive in charge of my own client base. Within a year, I was promoted to director of client services.

In 1999, I moved to Providence, Rhode Island where I earned a position as vice president at Chaffee/Partners. I joined the board of directors of the Rhode Island Chapter of the American Marketing Association (RIAMA). We grew membership by 40% within a year. In 2002, I became president elect of the chapter, and served as president in 2003.

I was recommended for an instructor’s position in marketing at the Rhode Island School of Design and taught the continuing education class each Spring for three years.

In 2003, I accepted an offer to become client services director at Trion Communications. The firm had an exciting mix of clients, a new blend of services and a spirited philosophy that I could embrace.

In March of 2004, I made the decision to start my own firm. I founded In Toone Communication with the goals of effective communication; diplomacy; an ideal combination of heart and mind, and an overwhelming drive to achieve results. Still dedicated to these guiding principles, In Toone continues to successfully expand into new market segments and client industries.
