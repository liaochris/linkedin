Hogan Carter’s Summary

• Content Management: At GGL, I designed, implemented, managed, and taught fellow employees how to use a tracking system for GGL’s catalog of over 3500 web videos.
• Content Creation: Developed optimal standards for creating web-videos utilizing a wide-variety of programs like Apple Final Cut Pro & Compressor, Adobe Flash Media Encoder & Flash Video Encoder, On2 Flix Live Encoder & Flix Pro, and Newtek VT-4, VT-5, & Tricaster systems.
• Video Production: Extensive ENG camera operator experience, tape logging & digitizing on Final Cut Pro, lighting & sound setup for studio & location shoots, all-in-all, I was involved in the creation of over 1000 video clips, segments, and series.
• Live Web-video Production: I traveled around the USA & the World producing & directing professional videogame events streamed live on the internet and archived in real-time as videos-on-demand, including World Cyber Games US & World Finals, Digital-Life Pro/Am tournaments in New York & Chengdu, China, Championship Gaming Series, QuakeCon, Eurocup, and many more.
• Increased traffic: I contributed to the expansion of the web video department from an archaic & clunky content delivery system to a modern, user-friendly system with content syndicated across multiple web platforms (Youtube, iTunes, Brightcove, Metacafe, and many more), at first garnering under 1000 views per month (2006) to nearly 1,000,000 views per month (2008).
• Industry Pioneer: I was part of the core-team that re-created Epileptic Gaming into video format from an audio-only show as co-host & technical director and eventually grew to become one of the first weekly and then daily internet TV show to stream live, with over 40 episodes airing in 2007 alone, and currently streaming over Stickam, uStream, & Mogulus.
• On-Camera Experience: I was a co-host on over 200 live episodes of Epileptic Gaming, and performed on-camera for the Up All Night videogame review show.
