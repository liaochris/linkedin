Jonathan Blaine’s Summary

I've always called myself a "Marketing Guy." If I had a brand and logo, perhaps that would be my slogan. Measuring ROI is huge. Just because you're now using "new media" does not mean marketing fundamentals should be discarded. Customers' desires do not change. As Denny Hatch wrote: "Customers don't have needs; they have wants. And marketing people are in the business of creating wants, not serving needs."

I'm a "right-brained analytical" guy (if there is such a thing) who looks at a project several different ways. My first instinct is usually the correct one. I understand the P&L and budgeting, and come in under budget on projects --- most often with excellent cost-effective results.

I'm a "doer", and often a "fixer;" someone who gets things done and still gets a thrill out of customers actually buying something because of something I mailed to them, or an ad I placed. I let the graphic artists "do their thing," but most of my success has come from strategy, writing, how ideas are presented to the potential customer and the actual thoughts that somehow originate within the ether between my ears. I'll even fire up Quark XPress to show basically what I want, to write the letter for the DM package, or just to explore where I need to go with something.

I am a huge believer in that the brand should never outweigh the message, and that art should never win over copy. The mix has to be “just right.” And continually tested.

I have solid ryttan, err, written and verbal communication skills, and a reputation for consistently producing cost-effective quality work.

20 years of comprehensive expertise in Advertising, targeted Direct Marketing, retention & loyalty, internal communications and training, consumer, business and agency relations, creation of marketing campaigns, competitive analysis and press relations. Budgeting expenditure responsibilities of over $2 Million to drive and retain $100 Million of annual revenues from core and new product.
