Zoey Anderson’s Summary

Honestly? The majority of my experience falls under the heading "Administrative Assistant" with a bit of subtle, "don't let anyone think you are managing them" management thrown in. I list my primary industry as Architecture & Planning because, completely by chance, I have had two jobs on th outskirts of that industry. Besides, I spent some of my early childhood around it- oh the smell of the blue print machine...

I am not really an administrative assistant though. I make things change. I see problems and find ways to fix them. I usually do this in spite of the decision makers' utter fear of change. I let them get used to it gradually and I don't judge them. This takes patience and understanding and an ability to explain complex, sometimes technical things in very a simple way. I have all of those abilities.

I am a great employee because I am flexible and I thrive on change. I have no fear of stepping out of my job title or comfort zone if the need arises. I love to solve problems. I am calm in a crisis.

I am handy with all of the Microsoft tools including Access. I recently had to work for almost two weeks at a computer that didn't have Excel installed. It felt like I was missing a limb. I eventually got the software and necessary ram and installed it myself. I am very comfortable with Photoshop and Dreamweaver, and have used Illustrator, Flash and Maya.
