Jonathan Javitt’s Summary

Jonathan C. Javitt, MD, MPH is a physician with a background in information technology, health economics, and public health. Dr. Javitt is a Senior Fellow of the Potomac Institute for Policy Studies. He has been a founder of health information technology companies that have become part of Siemens, Inc., United Health Group, Inc., and Aetna, Inc.
Dr. Javitt was appointed by President Bush on May 8, 2003 to the President's Information Technology Advisory Committee (PITAC), where he chaired the Health Subcommittee. Their report, Revolutionizing Health Care through Information Technology has served as a blueprint for concerted Executive and Legislative Branch focus on computerizing the nation’s healthcare infrastructure. Dr. Javitt was subsequently appointed as an at-large delegate to the 2006 White House Conference on Aging.
He is the author of more than 200 publications related to health care delivery and holds two U.S. patents. He authored the first book on computers in medicine and has published more than 200 scientific works in peer-reviewed journals. His first novel, Capitol Reflections, has just been published by Sterling and Ross, to critical acclaim.
Dr. Javitt served as a member of the White House Health Reform Task Force. He has been an expert consultant to the World Bank, the National Institutes of Health, the Health Care Financing Administration, Department of Veterans Affairs, and the ministries of health of United Kingdom, Sweden, Australia, Japan, and the Netherlands. He has been an active consultant in pharmacoeconomics and pharmaceutical development and is admitted as an expert consultant in both Federal and State jurisdictions.

Jonathan Javitt’s Specialties:

Health Policy, Pharmacoeconomics, Pharmaceutical regulatory and reimbursement policy, Epidemiology, Software design and development.

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; AET; Hospital & Health Care industry)

    Active Health Management was acquired by Aetna, Inc., in 2004 and remains a wholly-owned subsidiary. Active Health pioneered the use of health information technology to identify common medical errors and deviations from best medical practices in the care of Americans with major illnesses. We developed the Care Engine, a proprietary product that assembles a virtual medical record from the insurance claims and online laboratory data of an individual and uses that information to alert the patient's own caregivers to life-threatening and also less acute opportunities to improve care. In a prospective trial of 40,000 people, we proved that this approach substantially reduced acute hospital admission and potentially saved significant health-related cost as well.
