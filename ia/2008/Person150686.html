Pietro Giordano’s Summary

I am a dynamic telecommunication engineer and I am attending a second level specializing master course in Navigation and Related Applications at “Politecnico di Torino”.
My main fields of interest are GNSS, such as Galileo and GPS, and all the other correlated technologies (i.e. WLAN and communication satellite systems).

My knowledge covers all the main aspects of GNSS systems:
• GPS and Galileo systems;
• GPS and Galileo receiver design (Acquisition strategies, Tracking strategies, Multipath rejection...);
• support systems for GPS and Galileo;
• terrestrial and satellite-based (e.g., EGNOS);
• GNSS regulatory aspects;
• receiver technologies Localization in cellular systems (and WLAN);
• navigation and communication hybridization (GNSS + wireless networks);
• applications of GNSS in surveys and mapping;
• added-value navigation-related services and applications;
• timing and time scale generation;
• synchronization.

I would like to work on navigation topics (GNSS systems, in particular Galileo) and/or everything correlated, such as communication hybridization (GNSS + wireless networks), added-value navigation-related services, WLAN, satellites communications, wireless/wired networks.

I have a huge background in signal processing thanks to my Telecommunication Master Degree, the Second Level Specialising Master in Navigation and my experience as a research engineer. I am skilled in GPS and Galileo SIS, BOC and MBOC modulation and also AltBOC modulation and multiplexing scheme.

Moreover I have a deep knowledge of DLL, PLL and FLL schemes, Galileo integrity and RAIM, PVT computation using LMS or WMS approaches.
