Stephane Padovani’s Experience

  • Head of research

    VirtuOz

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry)

    February 2006 — Present (2 years 10 months)

    VirtuOz is a company specialized in artificial intelligence. Our products are based on conversational agents also called "chatter bots" or more concisely "bots". The development of such as products requires the state of the art in natural language processing, task scheduling and machine learning.
    I currently hold down the head of research position. I manage projects requiring a high level of technological innovation for our private customers (eBay, Voyage Sncf,...) or our governmental partners (currently Paris VII University). My area of competence is mainly in machine learning ( neural networks, bayesian modelisation) and data mining techniques. My principal projects are a data mining software allowing to categorize and analyze the dialogs generated by the conversational agents and two CRM applications now integrated respectively with voyage.sncf.com and the french eBay web sites.
    I mainly develop in the .NET framework. I enjoy especially the C# and ASP.Net languages.

  • Lead software developer

    Fircosoft

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    September 2002 — February 2006 (3 years 6 months)

    FircoSoft is a Sword group business unit which applies linguistic technologies to payment/securities messages repair and filtering for international financial institutions.
    I have developed several products and tools for this company as for instance a banking messages routing system, a work flow dedicated to the collaborative routing decision-making process or an OCR for bank checks automatic analysis. I developed in C, C++ for different kinds of environments such as Windows, Hp and Sun workstations or AS400 and MVS mainframes. Graphical applications was performed in MFC and was enabled to interact with a large set of databases (MySql, DB2, ORACLE, SQL SERVER).

  • Consultant

    (Privately Held; 5001-10,000 employees; Automotive industry)

    June 2000 — September 2002 (2 years 4 months)

    As consultant for the Assytem Brime company, I have worked for two years in the R&D EDF Labs at Clamart. I have been involved in several projects requiring natural language processing and machine learning skills :
    -Development of classification algorithms based on kohonen maps and other statistical methods such as K-means or CPA techniques.
    -Enhancement of a classification algorithm of Reuters press dispatches.
    -Enhancement of a collaborative tool allowing experts to represent their knowledges through semantic networks.

    Applications was developed in C++ and Tcl/Tk on Windows and Unix OS.

  • Ph.D

    Strasbourg University

    (Nanotechnology industry)

    September 1996 — September 1999 (3 years 1 month)

    Holistic systems involve a large number of degrees of freedom and often do not present any analytical solution. Classical analysis techniques consist in mean field approximations and consequently limit the calculation predictions to simple mean values. The object of my research was to adopt a pragmatic point of view by using machine learning and data mining algorithms. The main idea was to forecast the future behavior of a system by using the knowledge of its past behavior. I have principally developed algorithms based on neural networks to analyze magnetic domains geometry at the nanometric scale and solid surface growth at the atomic scale.
