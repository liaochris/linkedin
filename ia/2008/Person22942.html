Bianor *’s Summary

Bianor is a full-service software product engineering company with extensive expertise in the domains of Mobile and wireless, Telecommunications, Computer appliances, Content management and E-business.

The company has significant experience in developing custom-made and own mobile and telecom solutions. Bianor’s solutions based on the self-developed platform MobiSDP, have been twice nominated for the most distinguished European award for IT innovations ICT Prize by Euro-CASE with the sponsorship and support of the European Commission.

  • WorldMate travel assistant extension

    Bianor

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; 5BI; Computer Software industry)

    2006 — 2008 (2 years)

    The aim of the project was to further extend the functionality of WorldMate application for Symbian Series 60 based on the current mobile product and development framework.

  • WorldMate Live itinerary assistant

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; 5BI; Computer Software industry)

    2007 — 2008 (1 year)

    Best Life Management Application winner 2007 of Handango Champion Awards

    Andrew Seybold Choice Award for Most Innovative Consumer Application - 2008 winner

  • Roam'n'Roll personal billing optimizer

    Bianor

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; 5BI; Computer Software industry)

    2005 — 2006 (1 year)

    European ICT Prize 2007 Nominee

    Roam’n’Roll is a solution that optimizes individual roaming costs. It consists of server-side service and Symbian client application installed on the mobile phones of people who travel a lot. The solution is based on the innovative concept to enable smart-phones to automatically choose in roaming the visited operator that offers best price for the particular service required. The decision for changing the operator is made in real-time and takes into account things like interoperator tariffs, available alternatives, service required (call, SMS, GPRS), call/SMS destination, time zone (peak/off-peak), average talk times, etc.

    Developed in collaboration with our technology partner WebGate.

  • IP network management system

    Bianor

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; 5BI; Computer Software industry)

    2005 — 2006 (1 year)

    The aim of the project was to build and introduce telecom-grade IP network management system that allows for automated centralized configuration, administration and monitoring of the existing high-bandwidth network infrastructure. It is a bespoke system that matches the work processes thus allowing for savings of the financial and human resources dedicated to the maintenance of the infrastructure.

    The system operates in seven supporting centers of the existing communication infrastructure of the client and is entirely based on Cisco Catalyst series network switches. The system allowed the customer to streamline the administration of the network infrastructure. The customer was enabled to quickly and easily setup new client profiles and configure the network to the specific individual needs of their clients.

  • MoGa mobile CMS

    Bianor

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; 5BI; Computer Software industry)

    2001 — 2003 (2 years)

    Certified “Powered by WebSphere” product by IBM
    European ICT Prize 2005 Nominee

    MoGa is a multi-channel (i-mode, Foma, J-Sky) content management system. MoGa detects end-users’ handset and delivers the image content with the highest quality supported by the specific handset.

    CREATE content - Provides tools to import and create: multilingual text, images and media content from various sources, both manually and automatically.

    PUBLISH content - delivers content tailored to each 3G device and service that connects to the application. API and tag libraries make modifying, presentation and business logic a breeze. Includes feature-rich customizable end-user default application.

    OPERATE a content delivery business - Role-based operator management; extensive logging and reporting; per operator customization support.

    MoGa CMS has been used with a great success to empower the AFP WSN site in Japan during the World Soccer Championship 2002 in Korea and Japan.
