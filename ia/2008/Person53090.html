Donald Ham’s Summary

Since 2001, I have been at the 50,000-foot level seeking the big trends in IT, especially the ways they will impact corporate and enterprise IT operations

Other than the CIO, I've been one of the few individuals tasked with knowing something about ALL parts of enterprises, not any one specialty.

My value proposition is this: identifying the few truly important trends out of the hundreds of possibilities, weighing their likely impact, and providing an interpretation that can be used to generate value-added actions.

Since 2001, the big stories I've predicted correctly include:

--Service Oriented Architecture (evangelizing since 2002)
--a revolution in mobile computing (since 2001)
--the struggles of the big players like SAP and Microsoft to retain their value proposition in a new "plug and play" world (2003)
--the impact of generational change on the workplace and the demands of knowledge workers (2005)
--tech populism and the way end users will take power whether corporate IT gives it to them or not (2005)
--Social networking and Web 2.0 technologies as unforeseen value-adds for corporations (2005)

I love talking with professional colleagues about the possibilities and the excitement the future holds; and I get frustrated with the corporate "we can't DO that!" mentality. The future belongs to those who are in the business of saying "Yes!"
