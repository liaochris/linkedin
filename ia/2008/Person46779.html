David Herzog’s Summary

Educator specializing in teaching university students and professional journalists how to use data and mapping analysis. Formerly worked in newspapers as an investigative reporter and editor. Author of "Mapping the News: Case Studies in GIS and Journalism."

David Herzog’s Specialties:

Investigative reporting, data mapping, computer-assisted reporting, freedom of information and information graphics.

David Herzog’s Experience

  • Academic Adviser

    National Institute for Computer-Assisted Reporting

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Professional Training & Coaching industry)

    January 2002 — Present (6 years 1 month)

    Serve as academic adviser to NICAR, a joint program of Investigative Editors and Reporters and the Missouri School of Journalism. I am managing editor of Uplink, NICAR's online newsletter, and a lead trainer at our computer-assisted reporting boot camps. Also, I help graduate students perform data analysis work for news organizations and speak about CAR and data mapping at professional conferences.

  • Associate Professor

    University of Missouri

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    January 2002 — Present (6 years 1 month)

    Teach Computer-Assisted Reporting at Missouri School of Journalism and serve as academic adviser to the National Institute for Computer-Assisted Reporting. My interests include investigative reporting, freedom of information, data mapping and information graphics.

  • Investigative Reporter

    The Providence Journal

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; Newspapers industry)
