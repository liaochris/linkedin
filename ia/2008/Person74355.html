Hammad Hussain’s Summary

With a consistent record of success in conceptualizing, strategizing and managing multi-disciplined utilities, water resources and energy management projects in North America, Asia, and Latin America, Hammad Hussain is currently pursuing his MBA degree at the Columbia Business School (CBS) to complement his technical background with finance and strategy. His post-MBA goals involve either working for an investment institution that invests in capital-intensive energy and water industries (clean technologies, renewable energy, water processing etc.) or becoming part of a global strategic consulting firm where he can advise major institutional and Fortune 500 industry clients to help them remain competitive while dealing with complex clean energy, climate change, and environmental issues.

Prior to joining the CBS, Hammad had 8 years of working experience in the private sector of energy and natural resources management fields in North America, Middle East and Asia. He specializes in providing technical advisory on global water and energy challenges to international development agencies (World Bank, Asian Development Bank, IFC, USAID), governments, municipalities and U.S. federal clients (FEMA, Army Corps of Engineers, EPA), with additional industry expertise spanning the environmental and transportation sectors.

In addition, Hammad has extensive experience in the design, construction, and management of utilities that help to protect public health and the environment. He has led several 10-15 member task forces to develop new asset management strategies, leverage existing asset management practices (asset inventory, condition assessment, performance measure, criticality analysis, funding forecast) and implement tactical plans and schedules to improve both financial and organizational productivity for over 15 water/wastewater utilities in Midwest and Southern California.
