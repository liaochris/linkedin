Francisco Astorga-Paliza’s Summary

Degree in Computer Science from Instituto Tecnológico de Culiacán (Mexico), he also has a Master degree in e-Commerce, Business and Information Technologies as well as a Master degree in Computer Science and Technology from Universidad Carlos III de Madrid [Escuela Politécnica Superior], Spain where he currently is a Ph.D student of its Computer Science Programme.

He coordinates SIDAR's (Seminario Iberoamericano sobre Discapacidad y Accesibilidad en la Red) main discussion list: AccesoWeb and is also responsible for its log.

His research interests mainly focus on topics such as accessibility, social integration by means of computer science technology, information systems for emergency situations, and Human Computer Interaction.

Francisco Astorga-Paliza’s Specialties:

Accessibility, Human-Computer Interaction, Universal Design, Emergency Notification Systems.
