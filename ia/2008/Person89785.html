JERRY ABRAMS’s Summary

Mr. Abrams is Management Consultant at The Center for Creative Leadership in Greensboro, NC. He has significant expertise in information technology, quantitative decision methodologies, and facilitation of group processes. He has a wide range of experience in these areas with particular emphasis on applied management science information technologies including, group decision support software, analytic hierarchy processes, paraeto optimized resource allocation, and monte carlo simulation, as well as campus Local and Wide Area Network design and implementation.

Mr. Abrams has over 17 years of progressive experience in providing customized information technology solutions to project management issues for both government and commercial clients as a consultant. He has 11 years of both operation and project management experience in the delivery of consulting and internal corporate services.

He is well versed in system engineering principles, object oriented analysis, and the application of collaborative decision support tools to the development and evaluation of user requirements, engineering trade-off studies, strategic planning, as well as economic and financial cost/benefit analysis. He has had special focus upon the use of collaborative and decision support methods to the Department of Defense’s integrated approach to system engineering and financial controls (DOD 5000).

He has software development experience in fields including sales and contract management, task and project management, and financial and statistical analysis.

He has experience in applied mathematics and statistical modeling. He has hands-on scientific research experience in physics and biology. In addition, he has 30 years experience in the use of ‘Socratic’ methods for collaborative inquiry.
