Michael Riggs’s Summary

As FAO’s information and knowledge management officer for the Asia-Pacific region, I am responsible for initiating and managing collaborations with partners around the region to improve sustainable development through the application of modern information and communication technology (ICT). I excel in fostering partnerships and new communities of practice, conducting information assessments, formulating knowledge strategies, increasing the impact of good practices, etc. I have extensive experience in the ICT4D field, and share this with others and as well as expand my own knowledge by speaking at major events and leading capacity building activities. I champion the regional e-Agriculture community (www.e-agriculture.org), and I also manage the regional implementation of FAO's internal knowledge and information exchange strategies, particularly preserving institutional memory and adopting standards for digital information exchange.

Prior to joining FAO, I worked in the private sector with responsibility for new project development and coordination of information flows between international partners.

I have advanced degrees in both economics and organizational management, and extensive on the job experience with cross-cultural management and communication.

Michael Riggs’s Specialties:

knowledge sharing methods and tools, catalyzing communities of practice and international collaboration, conceptualizing and communicating new ideas, information surveys, building individual and organizational capacity in ICT4D
