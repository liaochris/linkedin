Candida Blaker’s Summary

Candida Blaker has 25 years experience in philanthropic organisations, specialising in inter-cultural understanding, social inclusion, & arts for development.

She has worked for range of bodies including leading international NGOs, policy-makers, research institutions and voluntary sector organisations.

. Deputy Country Representative in Brazil, Oxfam GB.
Programme Manager, with £2m budget and Brazilian team.

• Southern Advocacy Policy Advisor, Oxfam GB
Responsible for building capacity to advocate for improvements in socio economic conditions; encouraging appropriate policies and practices; and pursuing related campaigns.

• Latin America/Caribbean Regional Co-ordinator for ActionAid
Transitional manager.

• Co-founder and Executive Director of Creating Routes, using the arts to promote integration of refugees and people seeking asylum, through strategic direction and implementation of practical projects.

• Evaluator of various refugee projects, including for Information Centre on Asylum and Refugees, and Arts Council London’s Senses of the City.

• Consultant to British Council Performing Arts & Development, European refugee integration, and the interface between arts and governance in Vietnam.

Candida is a Graduate of Bristol University, and of Common Purpose’s Matrix leadership programme. She has been on several Advisory Groups, and a Longplayer Trustee.

Selected publications

- Reflecting Upon Experience – focUS Connecting Futures (ICAR)
- Journey Home – Schools Resource Pack on migration (Pegasus Theatre) (forthcoming)
- Senses of the City and London Arts’ support for refugees 1999-02. (ACE)
- Guide to Southern Advocacy Planning. (Oxfam GB)
- Making connections between the arts & development sectors in Vietnam. (BC)
- Artists in Schools. A Guide to Funding and Planning an Arts Project. (LBHF)
- Hamlet and the Baker’s Son: My Life in Theatre and Politics. Augusto Boal. Translation. Routledge

Candida Blaker’s Specialties:

Candida’s work focuses on policy analysis, project management, strategic planning and evaluation.
Candida has in-depth knowledge of several countries as well as of refugees and asylum seekers in Britain. She engages with people from grassroots activists and practitioners to policy makers, at a national and international level.
