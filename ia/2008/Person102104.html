Karel Joos’s Summary

Karel JOOS was born in 1969 in Soest (Germany). As of 1984 he received a military education in the Royal School of Cadets, the Royal Military Academy and the Engineers Corps Academy. He holds a Masters' degree in Construction Engineering and Applied Economics.

He started his active military career in 1993 as a platoon leader with the 11th Battalion of Engineers. At that time he took part in the Belgian war mission in Somalia. When he returned, he served as a project leader with the Directorate of Military Constructions.

After leaving the army in 1997, he was a strategy consultant with Antwerp-based Policy Research Corporation, mainly active in transport policy. He advised public and private sector clients in Curaçao, London and Budapest but also in The Netherlands and Flanders. He is a co-author of four books on various topics in transportation economics.

As of 2001 he was a financial analyst with real estate merchant bank Leasinvest, a communications consultant with PR agency Anthonissen & Associates and finally economic advisor to the Director-General of Agoria Vlaanderen, Belgium's largest employers' organisation and trade association.

From the summer of 2004 on he was advisor to the Minister-president of the Flemish Government. His main competences were in the field of public works, logistics and transport policy, public-private partnerships, corporate finance, industrial economics and defense.

During the Belgian interim government of 2008, he was a director in the cabinet of the Belgian minister of public sector enterprises ("companies of public law").
