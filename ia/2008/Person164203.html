Ronald H Miskie’s Summary

I’ve been in the Knowledge Management and Knowledge Transfer consulting field for over 30 Years. Of course, it wasn’t called Knowledge Management 30 years ago. Basically, disciplines such as; change management, organizational development, process improvement, business analysis, training, performance improvement, et.al., are all forms of transferring knowledge. All were intended to increase corporate and individual performance inside of specific methodologies, processes and practices.

My design principle is simple…People, Process and Technology. Looking into the future, create the results People wish to produce by maintaining a Customer-Centric future focus, develop Processes that support and enable the realization of these results and finally, create, design and discover Technologies that enable the fulfillment of this future.

In 1994, as a foundation for the Knowledge Management conversation in the marketplace; created, developed and published the first knowledge management “metazine’ called KM Briefs; invented and created the executive position of Chief Knowledge Officer, developed job descriptions, responsibilities and accountabilities; created knowledge management value propositions and strategies for a variety of industries; founded the Knowledge Management Industry Association
