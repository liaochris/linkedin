Michael Schron’s Summary

Michael Schron has been involved in business event planning (MICE and business tourism) for over a decade, and has several additional years of experience as a business event participant. In his prior career as an investment analyst, Michael attended industry and investor conferences around the world and visited factories and other such facilities, often with corporate senior management teams.

His interest in business tourism as it relates to globalization and a desire to apply Wall Street-style analysis and efficiency to the MICE sector inspired him to join his family's business in 1998 after seven years at investment organizations such as Dean Witter Reynolds (now part of Morgan Stanley) and the Abu Dhabi Investment Authority (ADIA), the world's largest sovereign wealth fund. He spent over two years in the capital of the United Arab Emirates, a country quickly becoming the major business and tourism hub between Europe, Africa and Asia.

Michael's Wall Street and international experience gives him an extensive knowledge of global venues for business events, an understanding of the strategic goals of and required return on investment in such events, and a personal familiarity with the needs of demanding business travelers. This combination of backgrounds is rather unique in the MICE industry. Michael has worked with a diverse clientele including well-known global corporations like Volkswagen/Audi and National Australia Bank as well as prominent smaller or regional businesses such as Oklahoma Home Centers and Arcadia Solutions.

A member of the class of 1991, Michael is a graduate of New York University's Stern School of Business with a degree in Finance and Marketing, and is active in Stern School global alumni events. He also represents Schron Associates as a member of NYC & Company, the Big Apple's official tourism marketing organization. Michael has successfully completed Level I of the Chartered Financial Analyst (CFA) program.

Michael Schron’s Specialties:

Investment and general business research and analytical skills (e.g. competitive intelligence), investment management, business event and travel planning, industry knowledge (Wall Street, tourism, hospitality, aviation), globalization, adaptability to expatriate situations, disaster recovery & crisis management, small business marketing/cost-effective marketing, sovereign wealth funds/government investment in public companies, results orientation, etc.
