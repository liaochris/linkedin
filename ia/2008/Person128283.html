Stephen Meltzer’s Summary

I concentrate on real estate development, affordable housing, land use, and the corporate, partnership and other business and litigation issues inherent in such a practice and in general real estate and business matters. I have broad experience in all aspects of housing development, including permitting, deal structure, site assessment and acquisition and litigation. I have represented both proponents and opponents of development projects and permits and Planning Boards and Zoning Boards in more than 15 cities or towns.

I am an Adjunct Professor of Law at the New England School of Law where I teach Legal Research and Writing.

I also have extensive trial and appellate experience and has represented clients in the District Courts, Superior Courts, Land Court, the Appeals Court and Supreme Judicial Court of Massachusetts.

I am also active in my community, serving as the director of the Under 7 Program for the Framingham United Soccer Club and as a coach for a U7 team and a U14 boys team, when not injured, I play in the Over the Hill Soccer League and am a bicyclist (when not recovering from soccer injuries). I have served as President of the Board of the Dismas House of Massachusetts, as a Mock Trial Coach in the Worcester Middle Schools and as a Human Rights Committee Member for Charles River Health Management.
