Fine artist (watercolor, pen and ink, acrylic, montage)

Alicia Bay Laurel’s Experience

  • Owner/Operator

    Indigo With Stars, Inc.

    (Privately Held; Myself Only; Music industry)

    June 2002 — Present (5 years 10 months)

    Indigo With Stars, Inc. produces and sells Alicia Bay Laurel art, music and writing services and merchandise, including books, CDs, performances of music and storytelling, fine art, art for reproduction, and workshops. Indigo With Stars, Inc. also includes a DBA, Puna Garden Weddings, which offers wedding consultation, coordination, music, and other related services.

  • Freelance Artist, Author, Musician

    Alicia Bay Laurel (Self-employed)

    (Self-Employed; Myself Only; Publishing industry)

    September 1966 — Present (41 years 7 months)

    Wrote, illustrated and designed books, created fine art for sale, created illustrations for publications and advertising, painted murals, wrote articles for publication in print journals and online, performed as vocalist/guitarist and storyteller at private and public venues, and taught these skills to adults and children. Internationally reknowned as an icon of natural living, non-religious spirituality, environmentalism, and bohemian style. My first book, Living on the Earth, is studied by historians as an insider's account of the utopian commune movement of the late 1960's. My over-sized handlettered books with naive line drawings created a graphic art trend that is still evident today. All three of my recordings of original songs have garnered rave reviews.

  • Owner/Operator

    A Wedding Made In Paradise-Maui, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Events Services industry)

    September 1988 — July 1999 (10 years 11 months)

    Owner of independent (non-hotel) event coordination company. Responsible for event design and on-site coordination on the islands of Maui, Molokai and Lanai, including hiring of caterers, entertainment, clergy, photographers, videographers, limousine, etc. Researched and provided sites, party rentals, formalwear rentals, decorations, floral items, favors, souvenir t-shirts, and hand calligraphed wedding certificates. In charge of personnel (up to six full time employees, plus casual labor), inventory, purchase and maintainance of company van, insurance, legal matters, negotiations with hotels and other sites, web site, print advertising and brochure copy, design and updating. Additionally, worked intermittantly in a subcontractor role as wedding floral designer, wedding officiant, and wedding musician.

  • Owner/Operator

    Wedding Music Specialists (Sole Proprietorship)

    (Sole Proprietorship; Myself Only; Music industry)

    May 1986 — November 1988 (2 years 7 months)

    Entertainment bureau in Maui, Hawaii, specializing in music for wedding ceremonies and receptions, although we also provided music for other events, including conventions, art gallery openings, Chamber of Commerce Mixers, festivals, and private parties. I played a great deal of the music myself, often in duets, trios and quartets, for which I did the arranging and provided sheet music for the other players.

  • Teacher's Substitute

    Haleakala Waldorf School

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Primary/Secondary Education industry)

    September 1987 — June 1988 (10 months)

    Worked one or more weeks each month of the school year substituting for Ms. Diana Dahl, who had received a grant to teach drama in the public schools that year. I taught eight music classes (kindergarten through seventh grade), and creative writing to the sixth and seventh grade classes.

  • Underwater Photographer and Camera Rental Agent

    Mercer Richards Photography (Sole Proprietorship)

    (Sole Proprietorship; 1-10 employees; Photography industry)

    March 1985 — November 1986 (1 year 9 months)

    Operated a camera rental and underwater portrait concession on Seabird Cruise's day trip tour boat from Lahaina, Maui to Hulopoe Bay, Lana'i. Rented underwater cameras to guests on the boat, taught them how to use them, replaced their film as needed, and cleaned the cameras after they were used. Additionally, I would book underwater portrait sessions, secure a raft by rope to an underwater mooring in Hulopoe Bay Marine Reserve, take photos with a Nikonos 2 underwater camera, get the photos processed, and mailed them to the clients. I collected payment, accounted it, and gave half to the owner of the business, Mercer Richards. I saw whales and dolphins up close many times, learned the names of numerous reef fish, became a skillful underwater swimmer, and had more fun than I could possibly express here. I also played guitar and sang for guests on the return trips after I finished cleaning the cameras.

  • Teacher

    Heartlight School

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Primary/Secondary Education industry)

    September 1982 — June 1983 (10 months)

    Small private school with thirty students, aged four through eighteen, based at the old Stan Laurel estate in the San Fernando Valley, near Los Angeles. Taught art to first through third graders, first year Spanish language to high school students, music to kindergarten through twelfth graders, and modern dance to girls aged ten to sixteen. My music class made a recording of a parody I wrote of the song Kenny Loggins wrote about our school, which we sent to Dr. Demento's radio show, and we also sang backup for Kenny Loggins when he performed the song (with his own lyrics) at our school fundraiser in Hollywood.
