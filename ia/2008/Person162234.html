  • Founder and CEO

    My Digital Life, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Computer Software industry)

    April 2006 — July 2007 (1 year 4 months)

    My Digital Life is a software startup uniting the worlds of smartphones, consumer electronics, and online services. MDL is revolutionizing the way people chronicle, explore, and share their digital lives.

    The MDL Metalife platform observes and correlates the elements of a consumer's digital life as they live it through their mobile devices and personal computers. The resulting rich digital history provides the foundation for context-enriched services that offer a new level of user experience. This context information also enables the platform to provide highly-relevant advertisements.

    MDL's Memoir is the first context-enriched application powered by the Metalife platform. It allows consumers to explore, search, and share their digital lives through a Web 2.0 browser-based interface.

    As founder and CEO, I raised seed venture capital, helped define the product and architecture, hired consultants and employees, led the product QA effort, and worked with customers in Japan.

  • Senior Director Platform Engineering

    Juniper Networks

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; JNPR; Computer & Network Security industry)

    May 2002 — April 2006 (4 years)

    I was responsible for the development of all current and future generation platforms for the Security Products Group (SPG).
    These platforms include firewall and VPN systems, SSL appliances and IDP appliances. SPG is the business unit that was formed by the acquisition of NetScreen and generates annual revenues over $400M.

    Before transferring to SPG, I was responsible for all the Interfaces and Services software on Juniper’s industry leading M and T series product lines. These product lines leverage Juniper’s Internet Processor ASIC technology and the JUNOS operating system.

    My team also developed the Adaptive Services interface for the M, T and J product lines. This interface supports link services with QoS, compression for RTP, stateful firewall, NAT, tunnel services, IPsec encryption and J-Flow accounting. It was launched in 2003 and has generated over $100M revenue to date. This product line was based on the Nexsi intellectual property that was purchased by Juniper Networks.

  • Acting VP, Software Engineering

    Nexsi Systems

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Computer & Network Security industry)

    February 2000 — April 2002 (2 years 3 months)

    I was responsible for all software and development test activities and led the engineering group through first silicon bring up, beta, and FCS in February of 2002. Nexsi developed products providing multi-gigabit Layer4-Layer7 content services such as stateful firewalls, IPSec VPNs, server load balancing, and SSL acceleration.

    The products achieved their performance and scalability through a powerful custom-designed 16-way cache coherent packet processor and a flexible network OS and application environment. The Nexsi content services switch was a 16 gigabit blade based chassis architecture with over 1000 packet processors working in concert to provide wire rate L4-L7 services.
