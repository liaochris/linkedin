James Morando’s Summary

James Morando is a senior trial partner who has a diverse litigation practice with a particular emphasis in technology, intellectual property and other complex litigation. Founder and former chair of the firm’s Intellectual Property Litigation Practice Group, Mr. Morando’s varied experience includes patent, antitrust, copyright, trademark, trade secret, internet, software, computer performance, and other technology and licensing-related litigation.

Trusted by clients for 30 years, James Morando is well known for his special talents in translating the complicated, technical, and/or the scientific into a compelling language that is understood by juries and judges. He is chosen to serve as primary counsel repeatedly for his wise judgment, strategic skills, and his ability to manage the financial aspects of a case that result in winning solutions. To his clients he is a trusted advisor, a financially perceptive case evaluator, and a highly effective litigation strategist.

Mr. Morando has tried numerous cases in both federal and state courts, as well as in complex arbitration proceedings. He also has extensive experience in handling numerous multi-party mediations and related ADR proceedings. He has a nationwide practice and is admitted to practice before the Federal and Ninth Circuit Court of Appeals, and the United States Supreme Court.

Mr. Morando received his J.D. from Boalt Hall School of Law in 1979, where he was an Associate Editor of the California Law Review. He earned his A.B. in Economics from the University of California at Berkeley in 1976. Mr. Morando has been appointed as Judge Pro Tem for the San Francisco Superior Court. Mr. Morando is AV rated by Martindale-Hubbell and has been named as a Northern California Super Lawyer in both 2007 and 2007.

James Morando’s Specialties:

Some of the clients Mr. Morando has represented include: AverMedia Technologies, Bass PLC and Holiday Inns, Dainippon Screen, Digeo Inc., Dolby Laboratories, Fujikura Ltd., Genentech, Golden State Vintners, Macromedia, Micro Power Systems, Inc., Music Net, Napster Inc., Nestle Waters North America, Nokia, Opus One, Paramount Pictures, Pfizer, Roger Wagner Publishing, Sigaba, Smith Barney, Sun Microsystems, Topometrix, Universal Studios, Visa and Wells Fargo Bank.
