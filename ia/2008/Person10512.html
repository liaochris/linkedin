Ander Wensberg’s Summary

Agency-bred executive with depth in advertising, site development, brand activation, branded entertainment and content development. Strong track record in business development and strategic relationship management. Past and current clients include: Nestle, Burger King, Miller Brewing, Bell Atlantic, Lucent Technologies, Gateway Computer, Volvo North America, Hertz, GM Corporate, The GM Card, Buick, Delphi Automotive, Motorola, Philips, GE Money, American Express, ExxonMobil, CPP North America, AT Cross, Foglight Entertainment, AOL, Digital Broadcasting Group, The Above Network, and Eons.com. Company history includes JWT, Scali McCabe and Sloves, DMB&B, McCann-Erickson, Modem Media, Ignition, and Momentum.

Ander Wensberg’s Specialties:

Original web content development, production, monetization, internet distribution and syndication. Redeployment of DVD and independent film releases for the internet. Consulting in business planning, content development, licensing and royalty models, distribution network development, streaming solutions, advertising and sponsorship sales, and brand activation. Writing for show treatments and web content. Non-linear edit, HD videography and video player design for web publishing.

Ander Wensberg’s Experience

  • CEO and Founder

    Vidlife.tv

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Internet industry)

    May 2007 — Present (1 year)

    I've come to think more like an Executive Producer after many years creating strategic content through the lens of advertising, marketing sites and live events. My company, Vidlife.tv, aims to create video just for web sites interested in deepening their editorial while adding lucrative advertising inventory. We create content generation opportuities that bring the efficiency of serve serve with a custom-published delivery. Our opportunity is to become a leader in a new segment of video programming that competes just above pure user-generated. That is where I believe you need to play if you hope to offer content and distribution that is economically viable for both web publishers and advertisers.

  • Branded Entertainment Development

    Foglight Entertainment

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Entertainment industry)

    September 2005 — March 2007 (1 year 7 months)

    I represented TV show runner Greg Backer at Foglight Entertainment marketing TV show concepts directly to advertisers for branded entertainment development capital. This came to be after Greg embraced one of my TV treatments and we partnered to produce a trailer and add it to his development slate. We certainly raised Foglight's brand profile together. It was through this wonderful collaboration with Greg that I began testing the waters for ad supported content development on line.

  • EVP NYO

    Momentum

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; IPG; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    2002 — 2004 (2 years)

    Running the New York Office of Momentum had me focus on brand activation. Worked some very cool campaigns and big live events for American Express. Our scope was sports and entertainment sponsorships and activation. American Express has always had very good taste in sponsorship properties, like an endorsement deal with Tiger Woods, The World Golf Championships, US Open Tennis, and Sting's 2003 Worldwide Tour. We raised a bunch of money for VH1's Save the Music. Negotiated countless ticket deals for card members, including large ones with Radio City, Madison Square Garden and Ticketmaster. I worked with Mark Burnett Productions in managing Amex's branded entertainment investment in NBC's 2004 Summer Reality show The Restaurant. For all the clients and campaigns I had done, I was blown away by the impact that relatively small project had their small business card division. My first real look at the emerging economics and impact of branded entertainment.

  • VP Group Director

    Modem Media

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; MMPT; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    2000 — 2002 (2 years)

    So I decided one day, with almost two decades and a billion dollars worth of ad campaigns under my belt, that I should earn some chops at a smart internet agency. I certainly found one. Jumped in with both feet to lead relationships with Philips and GE. I turned up a $40 million global ecommerce build with Philips in my first six months. Became familiar with the concept of internet time, and worked with the perhaps the most talented and diverse group of people yet. Spent a fair amount of time in biz dev, particularly in Europe working with our Paris and London Offices. Virtual teamwork - can't beat it. I ended up needing to move on when Modem hit the bubble in 2002. I left feeling like I had gotten the immersion I was looking for, and my friends and colleagues from this time are still among my most active network co-conspirators to this day.

  • SVP WW Group Director

    McCann Erickson

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; IPG; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    1990 — 2000 (10 years)

    10 Years at IPG's mothership took me around the world and deep deep deep into General Motors. I lead the launch of the GM Card, to this day one of the most successful new credit card launches ever. From there, I was given the reigns of the Buick account. We got real close and personal with the Boomer generation, and spliced together integrated campaigns for five new car lines and a restage of the Division brand. I ran a huge and devoted team, and we put Buick on a path that they remain on today. I later came back to New York and spanned the globe as WW Account Director solving problems for Motorola and Gateway, while leading Lucent and Gateway domestically. I helped make an NYO pitch to win the Lowe's Home Improvement business, which I then ran for over a year.

  • VP Account Director

    J Walter Thompson

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; WPP; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    1983 — 1990 (7 years)

    JWT was a great place to land out of college. They actually had real training programs back then. I learned brand positioning and the tenants of advertising from some legendary talents, like Linda Kaplan Thaler and Jim Patterson, accumulating great account management experience on Nestle, Burger King, Miller Brewing, and Bell Atlantic.
