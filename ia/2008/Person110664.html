Larry Flick’s Summary

It's with a bit of initial reluctance that I immerse myself into this site's culture. Even though I have continually worked jobs that require a fair amount of public attention, I'm naturally shy and uncomfortable with the notion of "networking."

Yet, as I ponder my desire to widen my career scope, here I am, diving in. I'm ridiculously happy in my current gig as an on-air personality at Sirius Satellite Radio, where I've been gradually transforming myself from reclusive writer into loud and sometimes naughty chatterbox for the past four years. It's a dream job that should fulfill my every wish, but it doesn't... because I'm discovering my hunger to re-factor writing into my daily work regimen.

As this page will atest, I've been around the block a few times. I have loads of experience, most notably as an editor/writer at Billboard. But there's more to be told, and more to be tapped into. That's why I'm here. To "network," goodness help me, but to also explore how I can enhance and expand my work as a radio personality, while reconnecting with the side of me that will always be fascinated by the power of the written word.

Larry Flick’s Experience

  • Host

    Sirius Radio

    (Public Company; Broadcast Media industry)

    July 2003 — Present (4 years 10 months)

    You can hear me every weekday on my show, OutQ In The Morning (7-11am EST), which offers a mix of high-profile interviews with celebs and political figures, as well as caller-driven conversation on everything from the day's headlines and lifestyle issues to gossip. Although the show is queer-centric and airs on Sirius' LGBT, our topics are wide-ranging (after all, gay folks care about things like the economy, too!) and we have a nicely diverse audience that reaches beyond the community to also include hetero-male truckers, business-folks, and soccer moms.

    You can also listen to me on the weekends, hosting the music-driven Feel The Spin, Saturday and Sundays, 12noon-4pm. Content spans current pop, rock, R&B, and country, as well as classics and imports from the U.K. and Continental Europe. I'm proud to say that I've given the first-ever American spins to a lot of up-and-comers, including Leona Lewis. It's a little show with a lot of my heart and soul poured into it.

  • Dance and Senior Talent Editor

    Billboard

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Music industry)

    1989 — 2003 (14 years)

    For so many years, my life was defined by being part of the Billboard family. I'm proud to say that I was part of the clan during its music-and-artist-driven heyday, as well as under the leadership of the late (and controversial) Tim White.

    I worked my way up from being a part-time assistant to full-time assistant to dance music editor to talent (and finally senior) talent editor. During my 14 years there, I launched the Billboard Dance Music Summit, and I broke ground with coverage of life-issues affecting the industry like the HIV/AIDS pandemic. Along the way, there were lots of scoops, including world exclusives with Madonna, U2, Bowie, etc. It's a period of my life that I regard with immense pride.

  • Consultant

    Paisley Park Records

    (Music industry)

    January 1990 — June 1993 (3 years 6 months)

    Assisted with the creation of label releases by introducing artists with viable songwriters and producers. Working with Prince was a strange, but satisfying experience that taught me a LOT about negotiation... and how spirituality can play an enormous role business.

Larry Flick’s Education

  • City University of New York-Queens College

    1981 — 1986

    Being a student at Queens College was a love-hate thing for me. I was meant to attend UCLA, but had to decline admission after not getting enough financial aid. I didn't get the best classroom education at QC, but I learned a LOT about life as a young man who'd spent his adolescence tucked in shyness. Like most, I view my college days as some of the happiest in my life.
