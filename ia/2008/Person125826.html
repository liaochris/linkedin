Matt Tarrant’s Summary

Do you think that being able to sell – projects, services, ideas – is a key skill? Over the last two years I have improved my selling skills by teaching myself complex sales strategies and sales tactics and successfully applied these to my work. I recently attended the Huthwaithe SPIN® Major Sales course. I have exceeded my targets. Yet by how much?

Last financial year I achieved 418% of my target for business development – that is the revenue generated from the seven accounts that I control. The region’s profit was 292% of our target so this extraordinary revenue generation was matched by above average profit. This continues a trend.

In previous years I turned a £10,000 order into revenue worth 3.5 times my salary. At another customer, in three years I generated 24 repeat orders worth 19.5 times my salary.

I want to use these complex selling and marketing skills more.

Running seven customers at once, together with pre-sales work to fill the pipeline, demonstrate the ability to handle pressure. Taking the initiative to teach myself selling and marketing skills shows that I have energy and drive. You can see too that I took the initiative to change the planning strategy at CERN.

In my day-to-day work I communicate with a range of people, non-technical to technical and programmers to vice presidents. I work with customers, contractors, partners and colleagues, over whom I have only such authority that I create for myself. Success depends on being able to establish common objectives and communicating what must be done to reach them.

I create business, love making profitable deals and building teams that deliver customer satisfaction. The evidence for this is in exceeding my targets. I exceed my targets by attracting new customers and building strong relationships with them and then by building teams that deliver customer satisfaction, which in turn leads to repeat orders.
