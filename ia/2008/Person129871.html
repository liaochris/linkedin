Michael Hutchison’s Summary

Since the age of twenty-two, Michael has lived in the
trenches and knows what it takes to succeed. He
possesses a rare combination of leadership and speaking
talent.

He began his career “hunting eagles” as a corporate recruiter for Ross Perot’s Electronic Data Systems (EDS), where he was the top recruiter in the country. He was “hand-picked” to lead the Central European recruiting division and became the youngest manager in all of EDS/GM international operations.

He began his training career when he joined Robbins Research International
(RRI), Tony Robbins’s company, as a regional sales manager and trainer.
Because of his success, he was promoted to the position of vice president of sales
and marketing. Michael led the company to record growth for 7 consecutive
years. While at RRI, he cocreated Mastery University and conducted
hundreds of trainings in North America, Europe, and Australia.

Over the past decade, Michael has helped build several respected enterprises while continuing to model and study the lives of achievers who thrive on and off the job. In 1998, Hutch joined Interactive Telesis (OTC: TSIS) as its vice
president of sales. In less than 2 years, sales multiplied sixfold, and he was
recognized for helping the stock price increase from 50¢ to over $5 per share. In
2000, Michael became the director of channel sales for SpeechWorks International
(NASDAQ: SPWX), where he was named to the President’s Club. In 2002, Hutch
joined software start-up Vocalocity as vice president of sales, grew the
company's revenues from nothing, and helped the company raise $14.5 million
in venture capital.

Michael received his BA degree from Georgetown University, where he was also a varsity letterman in football and lacrosse. He is an alumni board member at Georgetown. He has four children: a daughter and three boys.

He has coached all of his children’s soccer, baseball, basketball, tennis, and football teams.
