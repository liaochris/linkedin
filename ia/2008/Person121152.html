Mario Arana’s Summary

I am an outstanding Upper Elementary teacher. Upon request I can forward you my resume, teaching certificate, recommendation letters, and pictures of my highly successful international teaching experience in Guatemala, Honduras, Costa Rica the USA, Bolivia and currently in Egypt.
In 2003 my 6th grade students, despite the limited resources available in school, received the highest score in my district in the South Carolina English Language Arts Test.
In 1993 I was awarded the Walton International Scholarship. This scholarship is awarded based on academics, purpose and character. I graduated (Cum Laude) from Harding University with a B.A. in Elementary Education and an English Minor in 1997. I also have a Masters in Educational Leadership from Framingham State College, MA.
I was born and raised in an old barrio of Guatemala City. I am an explorer. By the age of 20 I was guiding tourists through Mayan ruins, rain forests, colonial cities and busy indigenous markets in Guatemala. Since 1985 I've traveled in 18 countries and 20 states in the US. Having been born in Guatemala and educated in the US has enhanced my rapport with my international students. I appreciate the importance and the beauty of cultural diversity.
I am a competent, creative, and committed international educator with 10 years of outstanding teaching experience in 5 countries. I have taught successfully students from diverse backgrounds in different settings, from rural South Carolina in the US to exotic San Jose in Costa Rica. In June 2007 completed a two-year contract with Calvert International School in Cochabamba, Bolivia.
My personal, academic and professional experiences make me an excellent candidate to teach at your school. I am looking forward to hearing from you to discuss how I can contribute to the mission of your International School.
