Sharon Vosmek’s Summary

Background Information:
Astia – formerly the Women's Technology Cluster - was founded by Cate Muther, former CMO of Cisco Systems, Inc., in 1999 as part of her foundation, the Three Guineas Fund. Astia became an independent not-for-profit, 501(c)(3) organization, in 2003.

ABOUT ASTIA:
Astia is a community of experts committed to building women leaders and accelerating the funding and growth of high-potential, high-growth women-led start-ups.

ASTIA EXECUTIVE SERVICES FOR WOMEN-LED STARTUPS
Astia offers customized executive services for women-led companies that accelerate access to capital, address core business challenges to ensure the companies achieve high growth, and increase the financial literacy and leadership of the founders.

OUR CLIENTS
At Astia, we focus our attention on a small number of high-potential companies. Each company is carefully screened by industry experts and investors. Companies that are accepted into our programs benefit from significant subsidies provided by the generosity of our sponsors.

ASTIA CONFERENCE PROGRAM™
The Astia Conference is the premier opportunity for women-led companies seeking capital. Boasting a success rate exceeding 60%, the annual program pairs selected companies with expert advisors as the entrepreneurs work to raise capital and increase their business acumen.

OUR EXPERT ADVISOR NETWORK
Astia’s services are delivered by a community of over 350 experts that comprise the Astia Advisor Network. The network currently includes more than 70 former and current CEOs and 80 investors from across a broad range of disciplines.

Sharon Vosmek’s Interests:

Ms. Vosmek is active in the Bay Area nonprofit community, working closely with organizations that promote social, economic, racial, and gender equity and enfranchisement. She serves on the Board of Advisors of Astia (formerly the Women's Technology Cluster) and the Board of Directors of the YMCA of San Francisco. In the past she has served on the Boards of Global Exchange, the Institute for Food & Development Policy, and the Level Playing Field Institute, where she played key roles in strategic planning, project planning, and fundraising.
