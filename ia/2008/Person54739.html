David Terry’s Summary

I was fortunate to enter my career with NCNB's ( now Bank of America) credit training program as an undergraduate with a Biology degree. I was selected from my credit training class to join the Funds Management Division - now Capital Markets - and became a trader. I traded money markets, futures and US Treasuries.

I was recruited to head a foreign bank's trading group in NYC where my group and I successfully profited from the stock market crash in 1987. From this position I joined Citibank as a bond trader.

I left NYC when my dad died unexpectedly in 1990 and became a Managing Director at First Union (now Wachovia) founding and heading the Private Finance Group.

I spent the next 10 years as a private debt and equity specialist. In 1998, I formed Colby Capital which was closed in 2001 due to the downdraft in the NASDAQ and 9/11. In 2001, I joined the Library of Congress as Director of Development where I managed the James Madison Council and directed the Library's private funding efforts.

I formed Gorham in 2003 and have served as CEO of a biotech company in the RTP, VP of an internet/entertainment company in the Bay Area of California and much in between.
I hope to successfully integrate all my experience into a world class management consulting/business development firm.
