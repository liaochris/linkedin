  • Online Public Access Catalogue (OPAC)

    Leiden University Library (UL)

    (Non-Profit; 51-200 employees; Libraries industry)

    April 2004 — January 2005 (10 months)

    UL makes a major switch in library ict system: from Oclc Pica to Ex Libris Aleph. Main advantage: a standard way of unveiling the collection, which makes the collection more accessible by external systems. Important part in the project is the design and implementation of the OPAC, the search and ordering interface, the UL’s virtual showpiece.

    The Aleph OPAC comes ‘out of its box’ with roughly organized interface, partly with redundant, confusing functionalities. Interviews and usability tests with students and university staff helped creating persona’s and finding which functionality to leave out and which to adjust.

    The most important scenario’s were transformed in functional, interaction and graphical designs. Implementation took place at the same time. The biggest part of the application had been delivered working; the rest was delivered in design.

    The new OPAC both deliver the new possibilities of Aleph as well and keeps the strongest features of the old Pica OPAC.
