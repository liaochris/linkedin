Tabitha Borchardt’s Summary

Until moving to Big Fish Games in May 2005, I have worked at smaller graphic design firms and have done some design freelance in Seattle, WA. Through working on highly illustrated and interactive website for Nintendo games, I found that I really enjoyed working on UI and illustrative design more than on corporate marketing sites. I have always loved working on icons, symbols, and brands. At Big Fish Studios, I work with the Online Community games team to create ideas, artwork, and even some copywriting for the games.

Tabitha Borchardt’s Education

  • University of Washington

    Certificate, User Centered Design / Technical Communications, 2008 — 2009 (expected)

    Currently enrolled in the evening certificate program in User Centered Design at the University of Washington Technical Communications Dept. I started in the winter of 2008 and plan to finish in a year. The curriculum is fascinating so far and directly related to design for game systems. User centered design is a major differentiator between mediocre products and stellar products, and I am excited to be an active participant in making technology work well for people.

  • Art Institute of Seattle

    AA, Visual Communications, 1997 — 1999

    The Art Institute was a great place to add a business perspective to my art skills. I begin my education at AIS in the Computer Animation program, but quickly fell in love with design and the intimate details of layout and typography, then moved to the Visual Communications department. Annette Bauman and Fred Griffin taught me so much about design, and really helped me hone my attention to detail.

    Activities and Societies:
    Student Studio for senior honors students, working on pro-bono design projects for community organizations.
