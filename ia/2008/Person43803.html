Daniel Minick’s Experience

  • President & CEO

    International Referral Network, Inc.

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    February 1992 — Present (16 years 11 months)

    IRN is a business networking company operating chapters which provide our members with business referrals.

    IRN operates a program that organizes these chapters of entrepreneurs, sales representatives and professionals for the primary purpose of helping each other succeed by creating a structured environment where they receive referrals from a network of similar, yet non-competing business professionals.

    IRN members close nearly 70% of the referrals they receive from fellow members. If you're a new business, or an established business looking to increase your bottom line, IRN has a proven method that works!

    IRN is not just another networking group, emphasis on chapter “core groups”, “cross-chapter networking” between chapters and Forums, in-chapter and online networking training, sets IRN far apart from other typical networking groups in it’s structure and effectiveness.

  • President

    Meadville Chamber of Commerce

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    November 2003 — October 2005 (2 years)

    Board member 1996-2006.
    This is the nation's 3rd oldest Chamber, just over 200 years, preceded only by Charleston, SC and New Haven, Conn.

    As Board President, spearheaded the complete 2-year re-design of the Chamber's entire corporate model and board and committee agenda process, with the overall goal of building a stronger board management team.

    Guided the identification of the Chamber's four strategic priorities of Economic Development, Political Advocacy, Membership Development and Revenue Enhancement and implementation of the necessary action steps, the development of a board member profile that identifies the background and characteristics necessary to be an effective board member and the design of a totally new interactive web site.

  • President

    West Mead Recycling Corporation, Inc.

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    January 2000 — July 2005 (5 years 7 months)

    As West Mead Township Board Chairman, spearheaded the development of the County-wide Consumer Recycling Program, one of the most succcessful volunteer drop-off programs in the commonwealth of Pennsylvania, with a very high rate of resident participation and receiving over $3 million in PA DEP recycling grants.

    As president of WMRC, Inc., negotiated deals the with PA DEP and surrounding counties to facilitate the building of a large, high tech, regional recycling collection and processing center in Meadville to service most of the counties of N/W PA.

  • Chairman

    West Mead Township

    (Government Administration industry)

    January 1988 — December 1999 (12 years)

    Elected local municipal official.

    Served as chairman of the Joint Municipal Committee that developed the GREMLAN, which was recognized by several national publications as the first municipal computer bulletin board system of its kind in the World. Related link: http://www.govtech.com/gt/96082?id=96082&full=1&story_pg=1

    Also served as Member of the Meadville Area Industrial commission, President of the County Association of Township Officials and Chairman of the Publications-Public Relations Committee of the Pennsylvania State Association of Township Supervisors in Camp Hill, PA.

    Guided adoption of the Township's first building codes, televised meetings, newsletter and resident-driven committees.

    After working to establish the regional "Council of Governments” and speaking state-wide on the subject of regionalization, he was sometimes referred to as 'Mr. Regionalization' for his proven track record of getting governmental groups to work together for the good of the area.
