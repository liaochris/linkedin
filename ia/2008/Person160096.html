Richard Pait’s Summary

Music offers many excellent grammar, verbal, and writing skills. It offers excellent communication skills and computer knowledge. It develops a high energy level and teaches comfort performing multifaceted projects in conjunction with day-to-day activities. It teaches excellent interpersonal abilities; ability to get along with diverse personalities; tactfulness, maturity, and flexibility. It teaches good reasoning abilities and sound judgment. It teaches resourcefulness, organization, a high degree of dependability, efficiency and attention to minute details. It teaches an ability to establish credibility and be decisive, but able to recognize and support the organization’s preferences and priorities. It creates a mentality that is service oriented, but assertive and persuasive. It, finally, gives you the ability to speak (perform) effectively before groups of employees and senior management.
Music study develops skills that are necessary in the workplace. It focuses on "doing," as opposed to observing, and teaches students how to perform, literally, anywhere in the world. Employers are looking for multi-dimensional workers with the sort of flexible and supple intellects that music education helps to create as described above. In the music classroom, students can also learn to better communicate and cooperate with one another.
Through music study, students learn the value of sustained effort to achieve excellence and the concrete rewards of hard work. Music performance teaches people to conquer fear and to take risks. A little anxiety is a good thing, and something that will occur often in life. Dealing with it early and often makes it less of a problem later. Students of the arts learn to think creatively and to solve problems by imagining various solutions, rejecting outdated rules and assumptions. Questions about the arts do not have only one right answer.
