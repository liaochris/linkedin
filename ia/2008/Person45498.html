David Greenwald’s Summary

Ph.D Candidate in Genetics at Tufts University Sackler School of Graduate Biomedical Sciences in Boston, MA. Thesis concentration on gene therapy strategies for retinal degeneration diseases in the lab of Rajendra Kumar-Singh, Ph.D.

Co-founder & Director of Technology at Relay Technology Management, Inc. Relay is a patent commodity brokerage firm focusing on marketing intellectual property from academia to industry - specifically within the biomedical space.

Chairman and co-founder, Tufts Biomedical Business Club (TBBC), a student organization composed of Ph.Ds, MDs and MD/MBA students at Tufts Medical School in Boston, MA.

Business development, SBIR writing, co-development strategies, and licensure experience working with RQ Biosciences, Inc.

Two years employed at the Naval Medical Research Center as an Associate Investigator, investigating diagnostic devices for viruses, particularly Dengue virus. Managed corporate collaborations with Panbio, LLC to develop field deployable ELISA kits and hand-held assays, and with Tetracore, Inc. to develop real-time RT PCR diagnostic assays. Worked with FDA and NIH towards this goal.

Two years as a Howard Hughes Medical Institute Research Fellow investigating bacterial chemotaxis in the lab of Richard Stewart, Ph.D. at the University of Maryland, College Park.

Interested in working in biotechnology focusing on vertical integration strategies and business development.

David Greenwald’s Specialties:

Government liaison, microbial genetics, viral diagnostics, viral inactivation, business development, marketing, Intellectual property, PCR, gene therapy, associated-adenoviruses, adenoviruses, retinal degeneration, age-related macular degneration, microRNAs, zinc-finger nucleases, patent evaluation
