Alex Goldberg’s Experience

  • Chief Executive

    London Jewish Forum

    (Civic & Social Organization industry)

    June 2008 — Present (4 months)

    The London Jewish Forum has the remit to promote Jewish life in the UK capital in the areas of welfare, education, housing, community relations and culture. The Forum makes representation to the Mayor's Office, GLA, 32 Boroughs and London MPs. It has broad representation on it from across the Jewish community.

  • Chaplain

    University of Surrey

    (Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry)

    November 2003 — Present (4 years 11 months)

    I am the Jewish Chaplain of the University of Surrey on a part-time basis. I have been involved in developing a new Multifaith Centre which is due to open in 2008 at a cost of £3.5m. I have worked with other Chaplaincy staff to develop an interfaith study tour to Auschwitz in Poland. I have taken the lead in projects involving Guildford Borough Council and the University.

  • Director, Community Issues

    Board of Deputies of British Jews

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    July 2006 — June 2008 (2 years)

    The Community Issues Division of the Board has a very broad remit which includes many of the issues of the most immediate concern to the Jewish community, including education, communal relations, research, policy development, , regional and small communities, community development and monitoring demographic changes.

    The Division has launched the CPRG (Community Policy Research Group) in conjunction with Jewish Care, Jewish Leadership Council and the UJIA. This is the community's horizon-scanning think-tank.

    The Board of Deputies spans the complete life of the Jewish Community in Britain - its development, its needs and aspirations and its security. For over 240 years our purpose has been to protect, support and defend the interests, religious rights and customs of Jews in the United Kingdom and to promote the development of the Jewish community in Britain.

  • Senior Policy Officer

    Commission for Racial Equality

    (Government Agency; 201-500 employees; Public Policy industry)

    April 2004 — July 2006 (2 years 4 months)

    My main responsibilities lie in delivering the Safe Communities Initiative, developing best practice models for conflict prevention / resolution and good community relations. The project works with four UK Government Ministries, Scottish and Welsh authorities, faith communities, mediators, local and police authorities to develop models across five themes: Faith Communities; Romany Gypsies; refugees; youth cohesion; and racial hatred.

  • Executive Co-ordinator, Bar Policy and Research Group

    Bar Council of England and Wales

    (Non-Profit; 51-200 employees; Public Policy industry)

    December 2000 — April 2004 (3 years 5 months)

    The Bar Council of England and Wales is the regulatory body for barristers. In this position I set up and co-ordinated the Bar Council's think tank. As part of my job at the Bar Council I negotiated fees with the Government, conducted research into the legal progession, edited Future Focus and worked alongside senior chambers-based barristers and Government lawyers.

  • European Network Development Officer

    European Council of Jewish Communities

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Civic & Social Organization industry)

    July 1997 — September 1998 (1 year 3 months)

    In this position I developed European networks for 53 social welfare organisations, 500 Schools, 200 Elderly Care Homes and Museums. I assisted the UK Government in developing the Nazi Gold Conference. I edited Edulink and e-link. I was responsible for fundraising. As part of my job I liaised with different European Governments, EU and Council of Europe. I set up various internet networking projects.
