Kevin Nadal’s Summary

Kevin Nadal will receive his Ph.D. in counseling psychology in 2008. Currently, he is an adjunct college lecturer at the John Jay College of Criminal Justice (City University of New York). He has earned BA's in Psychology and Political Science from the University of California at Irvine and an MA in Counseling from Michigan State University, and is in the final stages of attaining his Ph.D. in Counseling Psychology at Columbia University.

His research interests focus on the Pilipino American experience, particularly Pilipino American identity and community development. His "Pilipino American Identity Development Model" (2004) has paved the way for Pilipino American culturally competent services in education, psychology, and health. His dissertation is titled "Ethnic Group Membership and Perceptions of Racial Discrimination for Filipino and Chinese Americans: Implications for Public Health." He published on a number of topics including Filipino American identity, experiences of gay/lesbian Filipino Americans, Asian American youth, substance abuse, racial microaggressions, combatting the model minority myth, and colonialism and colonial mentality.

He is experienced in multicultural consulting, primarily leading trainings on multicultural competence in corporate and non-profit organizations. He has delivered many keynote speeches and lectures round the country focusing on Pilipino American identity and experience, mental health, activism, and issues facing LGBTQ and communities of color.

He has taught the following courses: general psychology, abnormal psychology, drug use & abuse in society, gateway to the workplace, fundamentals of career advancement, and Pilipino American studies.
