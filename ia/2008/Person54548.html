Dr. Janice Presser’s Summary

Dr. Janice Presser, acknowledged Thought Leader in Qualitative Assessment, has a broad range of expertise including individual improvement, behavioral assessment, interpersonal competencies, leadership and organizational development, and related forensic applications. Trained in systems-oriented psychotherapy, as well as research and development, she is the author of five books and a regular contributor to Leadership Excellence, Society for Human Resource Management and Human Capital Institute. A founding board member of Human Capital Industry Group, she is a frequent speaker on human capital and personal growth topics.

She serves on the Society for Human Resource Management's (SHRM) Human Capital Assessment/HR Metrics Special Expertise Panel and is the author of numerous articles on assessment and metrics issues such as the development of measures for job fit and promotability. She also serves on the National Advisory Board on Talent Development and the Quality of Hire Expert Panel for Human Capital Institute and the Philadelphia Board of The Entrepreneurship Institute. She is on the adjunct faculty of Temple University and on the editorial board of HR Metrics Review.

Prior to co-founding The Gabriel Institute in 2001, Dr. Presser was a consultant, managed two human resource departments, was president of a family-owned sheet metal manufacturing company, served as adjunct faculty and dissertation consultant for several institutions and as a forensic consultant and expert witness in cases involving civil rights, sexual harassment, wrongful termination, ethical professional practice, and related matters. The Gabriel Institute is the originator of Role-Based Assessment, with applications for hiring, teambuilding, entrepreneurship, marriage, parenting, organizational assessment and development, career development and spiritual growth.
