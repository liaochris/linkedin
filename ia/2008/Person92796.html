John Buckley’s Summary

My professional experience starts with my college graduation and quickly jumps ahead 6 months to where I realized I wasn't going to find a full-time position as an Illustrator in Pittsburgh. This is where I came to the conclusion that I needed to be more than an Illustrator, which quickly gave way to becoming a Graphic Designer with "strong freehand drawing skills". From here I started to try and bone up on my design program knowledge which wasn't easy since I didn't own a computer. But I pressed on eventually landing my current job at Lamar which whisked me away back to the easy-to-navigate streets of Columbus OH.

At Lamar I learned many things: The end process of large format printing, quality control, how to check size and color before the final flight, and how to maintain NUR Blueboard printers. But even with the agreeable hours and a relaxed atmosphere, I also learned that if you're not doing what you enjoy you're not going to be happy. With that in mind I started looking for work outside of work, and experiences that would get me the experience I need to do what I really want to do: create. It was a slow climb, but if anything was worth the trouble...that was certainly it.

Then it seemed everything started to click. The last three freelance jobs have been interesting projects to work on. I've also seemed to be getting greater response thanks to reformatting my portfolio to a digital platform. I was lucky enough to obtain steady freelance work at Uweekly, as well as an internship doing layout work for Estates Media. More recently I've been working with the BestTransport Marketing team, doing my best to make the look of the company match it's slick solution to match shippers and carriers. All of these things are helping me achieve my goal: to excel in the position of an artist and having a good time doing it.

My online portfolio cound be found here: http://jnbuckley.carbonmade.com/
