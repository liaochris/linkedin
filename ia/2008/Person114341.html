Lisa Finkelstein’s Summary

Lisa is a passionate leader and an innovative organizer who has worked with philanthropic and community organizations in a variety of roles. She has been an enthusiastic organizer in the Jewish and LGBT communities for over a decade, including positions as program president of the University of Oregon's Hillel, a founder of the Flatiron Stonewall Democrats and has worked closely with the founding members of the Adventure Rabbi organization in Colorado.

Lisa moved to San Francisco in April 2006 to join Full Circle Fund as Director of Programs, where she helped cultivate community leadership and social change through the engaged philanthropy organization.

Previously, Lisa worked in Colorado with Jared Polis, co-founder of bluemountainarts.com and candidate for US Congress. Lisa founded and served as the Executive Director of Polis' philanthropic foundation, and helped start several non-profit organizations including Community Computer Connection. Lisa also helped start multiple charter high schools in Colorado focused on disenfranchised students, and served on the founding boards of the New America School and Academy of Urban Learning.

Lisa received a Masters in Nonprofit Management at Regis University and a B.A. from the University of Oregon.

    (Non-Profit; 51-200 employees; Philanthropy industry)

    December 2007 — Present (5 months)

    Primary responsibility for building relationships, programs and partnerships that engage and empower LGBT Jews to participate in Jewish communal activities.

    The Alliance commenced in 1996 as the Gay and Lesbian Task Force of the Jewish Community Federation. In 2001, it received a grant from the Jewish Community Endowment Fund to begin its efforts as a full-fledged division of the Jewish Community Federation providing a gateway for involvement in Jewish life for LGBT Jews and their friends and family. It is the first (and currently the only) dedicated LGBT division of a U.S. federation.
