Jeanne Milliken Bonds’s Summary

Proven ability to establish strategic direction and deliver successful results in a changing environment, including government, non-profit and the private sector.

More than twenty years of successful strategic relationship building ability for successful community and government relations, communications and marketing, and economic development. Since 1997, served in senior level positions, managing staff and developing and implementing key strategic initiatives.

Beginning in 1985, registered as a federal lobbyist. Featured on the front page of USA Today in 1986 for work on the Tax Reform Act of 1986.

Significant leadership in financial, crisis and reputation issue management and has created successful marketing, strategic and business continuity plans. Successfully acquired additional appropriations for the NC Courts for technology and personnel as a state legislative liaison for the Judicial Branch of Government.

In 1997, chosen to represent North Carolina as a Henry Toll Fellow with the Council of State Governments. Served as Mayor Pro Tem of Knightdale, NC and the Deputy Director of the NC Courts at the time selected. Advocacy for the courts at the state and federal levels resulting in more than $30 million in federal appropriations for technology and more than $30 million in state appropriations for personnel and technology.

Certified in emergency communications; fourteen years experience in media relations, developing strategic messaging and pitching features.

Elected to local office and served for nine years, including service as Mayor Pro Tem and Mayor of a fast-growing town. Elected to the Board of the NC League of Municipalities statewide by peers.

Successfully branded and rebranded organizations and cities, including the development of community visioning processes.
