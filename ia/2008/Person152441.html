Paul Lipson’s Summary

For over a decade, Paul Lipson has refined his skills in music, education, consulting, and management. His career has taken him all over the world, and his passion for education, technology, games, and the performing arts has helped him advocate for advancement in the interactive entertainment industry.

Paul has studied music for twenty-three years, and has enjoyed a longstanding career as a performer, leading his ensembles at venues across the United States, Southeast Asia, Europe, and Brazil. He has never ignored his life-long love of videogames, and founded Hit Point Music, an audio company specializing in music for games, film, and television to converge his love of music and interactive entertainment. His team at HPM has worked with a dazzling array of “A-list” clients, including projects for America Online, Hasbro Interactive, Harmonix/Sonic Implants, Associated Production Music, Hyundai Automotive, The Environmental Protection Agency, Urbane-Dog Creative, G4tv, and Legends-Tribal War, just to name a few.

Paul is extremely passionate about arts integration in education and the advancement of interactive audio, and currently serves as the President of the Game Audio Network Guild. He has worked closely with the officers of G.A.N.G. to originate the industry-first Program Partnership Initiative, and was the mastermind behind the Guild Scholarship Award for Game Audio. Paul was awarded the G.A.N.G. “Distinguished Service Award” at the 2006 Game Developer’s Conference for his work in education and advocacy for the interactive audio arts.

Paul holds a BM and MM with highest distinction from the world-renowned New England Conservatory of Music in Boston. He is a member of the International Game Developers Association, The Music Educators National Conference, BMI, and the Game Audio Network Guild.
