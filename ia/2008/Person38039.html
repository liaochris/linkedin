Claire M. Tallarico’s Summary

Claire's area of expertise is strategic public relations counsel and high profile media placement. She creates and implements well-timed strategies resulting in extensive media coverage for topics including diversity, women in the work force, health care, and education. Claire works closely with clients to use social trends and issues coupled with credible research to make news with lasting impact.

In Canada, Claire has directed communications strategies for a
diverse client base including the Royal Bank (RBC), the RBC Foundation, Toronto Hydro, the YMCA of Greater Toronto, the City of Barrie, Catalyst Canada, the Canadian Health Network (CHN), the University Health Network (UHN), Sickkids Foundation, the Red Door Shelter, Ryerson University Faculty of Business, Safe Kids Canada, and the Toronto Stock Exchange (TSX). In the U.S., her clients have included New Visions for Public Schools, the NY State Coalition Against Family Violence, the NYC Chamber of Commerce, the New York Commodities Exchange, and City Harvest Food Bank.

Prior to her return to Toronto in 2001 to resume her consulting practice after a two-year stint in California, Claire was a Vice President for The Weber Group’s (TWG) Silicon Valley office,responsible for an annual client portfolio of more than $1.5 million in billings.

Claire served as Director of Public Affairs at Women’s College Hospital, Canada's first hospital dedicated to women's health. As a senior communications’ advisor and director for a decade in the administration of New York City Mayor Edward I. Koch, her work included communication strategies and crisis management on a wide variety of finance, and policy issues.

A Concordia University graduate, Claire was awarded a Mayoral Scholarship for graduate work at New York University, and has taught media relations at Toronto's Humber College. In a prior life, she happily completed her chef's training at George Brown College in Toronto and still has a good set of knives.

Claire M. Tallarico’s Specialties:

media relations, strategic planning, project management, public affairs, writing, turning health, business and social policy research into stories, products and tools with lasting impact
