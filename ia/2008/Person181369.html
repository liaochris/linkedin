Steve Glenn’s Summary

Glenn is currently the founder and CEO of LivingHomes, www.livinghomes.net, a developer and builder of modern, prefabricated homes, designed by world-class architects, that feature heathly/green materials and energy systems and an affordable to moderate price point. The company’s first product, designed by Ray Kappe, is in development for a site in Santa Monica, California.

Previously, Glenn worked with the Clinton HIV/AIDS Initiative (CHAI), www.clintonpresidentialcenter.com, and managed the development of a $330 million program in Mozambique which will provide care and treatment to over 350,000 HIV+ individuals over the next five years. Glenn is founder and former CEO of PeopleLink, www.peoplelink.com, which raised $35 million from firms including GE, AT&T and Goldman Sachs and became a leading provider of online community software to clients including Oracle, GE, MTV, Paramount, Reuters and CBS. Glenn was part of the founding team and is a Partner of idealab, www.idealab.com, a business incubation firm which raised over $1 billion in equity and that founded or invested in a number of successful companies including GoTo/Overture (OVER), NetZero/United Online (UNTD), CitySearch (TMCS), Tickets.com (TKTS), eToys and CarsDirect. Glenn was co-Director of the VR Studio at Walt Disney Imagineering, which developed various virtual reality and online products. Glenn was the Vice President of at SimGraphics where he led the development the first real-time character animation system. Glenn co-founded Clearview Software, which was sold to Apple Computer. He graduated from Brown University, studied Urban Planning at the Harvard School of Design, and was a Coro Fellow. Glenn is a co-founder and serves on the boards of the Sustainable Business Council, sustainablebc.org, Kaia Parker Dance Fund, www.kaiaparker.org, and the Hope Street Group, www.hopestreetgroup.org, and is on the boards of LA Works, www.laworks.com, and the Brown Entrepreneur Forum, www.brownep.edu
