  • Naval Construction Training Center

    Class A-School with Honors, Structural Engineering, 1993 — 1993

    As with all military training schools this is an excellent school that trains and cross-trains all of it's candidates in a variety of aspects of engineering. Upon completion of this school SeaBee's go on to specialize in their chosen field: electrician, utilities, surveyor, mechanic, equipment operator, carpenter and mason and steelworkers.

    The Seabees have been in Gulfport since 1942 when the military construction arm of the U.S. Navy was established. The first Seabees were civilian construction workers who enlisted in World War II to build for the Navy in the Pacific Theater and in Europe. Their official name is the Naval Construction Battalion. They were called CBs for short and the C became Sea and the B became bees. From that nickname, their logo of a fierce bee wearing a sailor cap holding tools and a weapon, was designed.
