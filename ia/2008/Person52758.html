Dave Neary’s Summary

I help change the world.

As a consultant in Free Software, I help organisations understand understand the dynamics of free software communities, and help them navigate the minefield of license compliance and community development.

As a director of the GNOME Foundation, I have worked with a group of people who are making technology available to everyone. From schools in Spain and Macedonia to community projects in Brazil, India and Austria, GNOME is helping people, and changing the way the IT business works at the same time.

As manager of the Openwengo project, I helped create free software which allows everyone to communicate - with voice, video and text - regardless of their platform or preferred IM protocol.

Dave Neary’s Specialties:

I'm passionate about technology, and free software in particular. Inquisitive to a fault. Constantly learning and evolving. Surprisingly good at bringing things to completion.

Dave Neary’s Experience

    Neary Consulting

    (Privately Held; Myself Only; Computer Software industry)

    January 2008 — Present (4 months)

    I help companies who have questions about free software, such as: How can I interact more effectively with the community? What do I have to do to release a product under a free software license? Which license is the right one for me? How do I go about building a community?

    I bring 12 years experience in a variety of development and management roles with free software communities, including the GIMP, GNOME and OpenWengo, to bear on your particular situation, providing personalised advice and practical support to bear on your doubts and problems.

    (Non-Profit; 201-500 employees; Computer Software industry)

    September 2004 — Present (3 years 8 months)

    The GNOME Foundation is an organisation dedicated to supporting the GNOME project. Over 500 people, including many employees of companies such as Sun Microsystems, Novell, RedHat, Canonical and more, are working together to build free software computer user interfaces, both for te desktop and for embedded devices. The GNOME project is redefining the way software is produced, and through its leadership in the free software community with things such as our time-based release cycle and the GNOME Mobile initiative, we are also redefining how free software gets built.

I am also actively seeking ways to generate revenue, to enable me to keep investing in the development of the WengoPhone long term.
