Garry Bennett’s Summary

Using my industry experience and personal determination, I have started MYTOWN WEB LIMITED operating as www.mytown.ie.

Mytown.ie covers 90 towns in Ireland; viewing the Mitchelstown version of the site will certainly enable you to understand in detail, what the site offers. It provides visitors with news, weather, business listings, chat forums, events calendars, picture galleries and a map which are all specific to the selected town. The site will be used to promote local business to locals, and tourists planning to visit the town.

As we are all aware, the internet is now where consumers predominantly carry out their research and make their decisions on what hotel or B&B they stay at, through to which plumber they call. I have found that although some towns have websites with business listings, many of these websites are poorly designed (not attractive to tourists), difficult to navigate, badly maintained and lack direction. Mytown.ie has been carefully planned and designed over 5 months of production and is owned by “MYTOWN WEB LIMITED” with revenue being generated through advertising and paid business listings.

Garry Bennett’s Experience

    November 2007 — Present (7 months)

    Since November 2007, I have been working on the development of www.mytown.ie. The site was launched in January while data entry work is still continuing.

    Mytown.ie is a community website for every town in Ireland.
    The site offers locals and tourists information such as local news, weather, upcoming events and a town map. Mytown.ie also has interactive features such as chat forums, picture galleries and classified ads. Each town has an events calendar, allowing businesses and clubs to list their upcoming events such as bands appearing in a pub or a football match.

    Revenue is generated through paid business listings and advertising on the website. Businesses are encourage to take out a paid listing which will bring them to the top of their category and allow them to edit their own page on the site.

    Initially efforts are focused in Munster (South of Ireland), with plans to have content ready for all of Ireland by September 2008.
