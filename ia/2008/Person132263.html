Mike Kallas’s Summary

Major Accomplishments:

Product Development: I developed 3Dfy (http://www.3Dfy.com), from conception through design, development, release, sales, support, and maintenance. 3Dfy is a 100,000 lines commercial visualization application deployed and used by major clients including Raytheon and the US Army, for LADAR visualization.

Management: I have managed engineering teams ranging from 8 to 25 people at 3 different companies over 9 years (VocalData, Ramp Networks, and Nortel). My teams’ tasks included: software development, architecture, product management, systems engineering, interoperability lab, standards, and testing. I managed multi-location teams. I have been involved in the definition and management of the development process through all its stages.

Technology Pioneering: I have several formal contributions to standards organizations including: IETF, ITU-T, MSF, IEEE. I was the architecture workgroup chair at MSF and I won the MSF circle of excellence award. I have 2 granted patents and 2 patent applications in progress. Protocols and groups that I have contributed to include SIP, Megaco/H.248, MGCP, CPL, SIMPLE, AVT, SIGTRAN, IPTEL, MMUSIC, SIPPING.

Entrepreneurial Experience: Being responsible for my own product based, start up business taught me how to handle flurries of diversified problems in all aspects of business. I had to be an engineer, a tester, a marketer, a seller, a strategist, an accountant, a tax expert, a web site designer, a CFO, a product manager, a project manager. I had to meet my budgets since I had no external funding, and I was only funded by sales. Being a small business selling to large defense contractors and the U.S. Army, taught me how to look bigger, act bigger, deal with complex processes. Not only did I have to create a good product, but I also had to market it, sell it, and maintain it in very structured process heavy customers. And I had repeat business from my customers
