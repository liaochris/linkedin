Patty Purpur’s Summary

Patty was born in Grand Forks, North Dakota. In 1985 she was the top rated and most recruited high school female shot and discus thrower in the nation. She accepted a full athletic scholarship to Stanford University where she was a 9-time All-American and the Track and Field Team Captain for two consecutive years.

Patty Purpur's accomplishments include:

- Ranked 7th in the USA in Shot Put in 1988
- Competed in the discus at the 1988 Olympic Trials
- National Collegiate Olympic Weightlifting Champion in 1989
- Inducted into the North Dakota Sports Hall of Fame and the North Dakota High School Track and Field Hall of Fame
- Currently ranked 1st in the U.S. Masters Track and Field for Shot Put and Discus

Patty graduated with a BA in Psychology from Stanford University in 1989. In 1992, she founded TimeOut Services to offer personal fitness services and has since expanded to Corporate Wellness Program design and management. Her company has grown to 100 employees and several million in annual sales. TimeOut currently provides a full range of services to develop and manage corporate fitness centers and wellness programming, including facility design, equipment specification and procurement and staffing. Clients include some of the largest companies in the Bay Area, as well as smaller companies. Additionally, the Company manages community-based group exercise programs under the Community BootCamp® name, and has helped over 16,000 men and women kickstart and actual enjoy their daily dose of exercise since 1996.
