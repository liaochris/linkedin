Denis Royer’s Summary

Denis Royer completed his master in business informatics in 2003 at the University of Technology at Braunschweig (Germany). From 2000 to 2001 he studied information systems and business administration at the University of Nebraska @ Omaha, Nebraska (USA). Since 2004 he has been a researcher and project coordinator of the FIDIS NoE (Future of Identity in the Information Society Network of Excellence) at Johann Wolfgang Goethe University in Frankfurt, Germany. At the Chair for Mobile Business and Multilateral Security, he works on the economic evaluation of enterprise identity management systems (EIMS), decision support systems for the introduction of EIMS in organisations, and enterprise identity management (EIdM) process models, in the context of the European research project FIDIS. Furthermore, he is active in the GenericIAM Group of NIFIS, working on the creation of generic process models for identity and access management (IAM) systems.
