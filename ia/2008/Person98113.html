Joshua Davis’s Summary

Musician, producer, composer, and event planner. In 2006, successfully planned and excecuted a 20-date, 35-day world tour. Co-organizer of the four-day, 40-artist Blip Festival in downtown Manhattan showcasing cutting-edge electronic music and visuals, with attendance exceeding 1300 people. Have produced and released several independent records, and have been fortunate enough to enjoy numerous compilation appearances, the most recent being the 8-Bit Operators compilation released on Astralwerks. I take a conservative approach to blending commerce and art, but am always on the lookout for any genuinely interesting, unconventional projects that don't insult an audience's intelligence or further a money-first agenda.

Joshua Davis’s Specialties:

Electronic music production, event planning & promotion, composition, sound design, sound production, recording & sound engineering.

    Bit Shifter (Self-employed)

    (Self-Employed; Myself Only; Music industry)

    2001 — Present (7 years)

    Exploring composition, synthesis, & performance under the name Bit Shifter using a Nintendo Game Boy as the sole sound source. Made possible by independently-developed software, J. Kotlinski's Little Sound DJ and O. Wittchow's Nanoloop, the project has focused on the aesthetics of economy & pushing minimal music to a maximum. The project has enjoyed a surprising degree of success & visibility, with over 100 performances worldwide. Selected venues: Museum of Contemporary Art & Chicago Cultural Center (Chicago), CEPSUM (Montreal), Paradiso (Amsterdam), Museum of the Moving Image, Pace Wildenstein, & Lincoln Center (New York). Co-organized and executed a 20-date world tour & the 40-artist Blip Festival (over 1300 attending) in 2006. Featured in the documentary 8 Bit, premiering at MoMA 10/06. Releases on: 555 Recordings, 8bitpeoples, Mirex, Astralwerks, & Sonic Acts. Coverage: New York Times, XLR8R, frieze, Wired, La Presse, Rolling Stone, Los Angeles CityBeat, Village Voice, & NPR.
