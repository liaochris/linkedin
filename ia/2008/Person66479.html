Raul Espinosa’s Summary

Raul's projects range from managing bidding efforts for small businesses; consulting with resort developers like the Punta Cana Group; drafting small business initiatives and counseling sports organizations and sports sponsors, on ways to get involved in combating childhood obesity. He has been credited with conceiving 'The Umbrella Initiative,' to create a context of partnership between bureocrats with entrepreneurs; The ‘Fight Against Obesity’ (FAO) project whose mission is to ‘eliminate children’s obesity through sports participation;’ the Contracting Abuse Resolution Board (CARB) to bring fairness to government protests and the Free Universal Access (FUA) to open up access to public procurements. The Jacksonville Business Journal has recognized his leadership by naming him among the Most Notable Diversity Advocates in Northern Florida in 2007 and 2006 after his success at founding the Fairness in Procurement Alliance (FPA) and successfully challenging the legality of the 'FAR Exemptions' which resulted in the SBA declaring them illegal. In September 2003, Raul was honored as ‘Businessman of the Year’ by the Business Council for “his efforts to positively affect and influence the procurement landscape at the local state and federal level.” Raul is a Member of the Minority Business Round Table (www.mbrt.net) and is an Advisor - in procurement issues - to the American Small Business League (www.asbl.com) and MBELDEF (www.mbeldef.org).

Raul Espinosa’s Specialties:

Multi-lingual business development; communications; negotiation; representation; public affairs and entrepreneurship training. Skilled at bringing people together, motivating and directing them towards a common goal. A passionate advocate on issues affecting small and minority businesses.

Raul Espinosa’s Experience

  • Founder and Spokesperson

    Fairness in Procurement Alliance (FPA)

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Government Relations industry)

    April 2005 — Present (3 years 7 months)

    FPA, formerly the Fairness in Set-Aside Procurement Coalition (FSAPC), represents the procurement interests of all of the groups for whom Congress created the 'set-aside program, a constituency of 10 million businesses.' The mission of FPA is to bring fairness to public procurements so that small and minority businesses can both compete and prosper at the federal, state and local levels. FPA activities are aimed at protecting and enhancing ‘set-aside programs’ so they can achieve their intended results. FPA relies on 'teams' comprised of volunteers with expertise on various affinity areas. The FPA's history and priorities can be seen through thislink (http://www.prweb.com/releases/2007/05/prweb525536.htm). FPA current teams operate through the University of North Florida (UNF) and they comprise a 'think tank'; a 'legal and advocacy center' and a 'training center.' FPA is actively recruiting volunteers to participate in their 'teams.'

  • President and CEO

    FitNet International Corporation (FitNet)

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Government Relations industry)

    April 1995 — Present (13 years 7 months)

    FitNet is a purchasing and consulting group which specializes in public procurement management and niche marketing consulting. FitNet also offers advocacy representation to major trade groups FitNet operates three divisions: The Purchasing Alliance, The Fairness in Procurement Alliance and The Consulting Alliance.
