Jason Hayes’s Summary

I have been a professional in the entertainment industry for over 10 years. From 1995 to 1997, I attended the Art Institute of Pittsburgh where I also worked as a traditional animator for a local animation house.

After graduation, where I recieved an Associates Degree in Computer Animation, I began my career in games at Electronic Arts Tiburon. While at EA, I was a content artist on Madden NFL '99 and quickly moved up into a Cinematic Artist position on Madden NFL 2000.

Shortly after shipping Madden NFL 2000, I helped co-found a small production company called The Ballistic Pixel Lab. I played key roles in many projects, including cinematics for James Bond Racing, Army Men: Sarge's War and Lords of Everquest. Additionally, I was the Lead Artist for our contract with Turbine Entertainment on The Lord of the Rings Online.

In 2004, I took a Cinematic Artist position at Volition, Inc. where I worked on cutscenes for The Punisher and was awarded Volition's 2004 Artistic Excellence. Shortly after, I moved into the position of Senior Technical Artist on Saints Row where I developed tools and pipelines for the game.

Last year, in 2006, I spoke at Autodesk University on how some of our tools and pipelines work for Saints Row. Most recently, at the 2007 GDC, I gave a talk about how we created the city of Saints Row.

I currently hold the position of Technical Art Director at Volition on a unannounced title.

My job responsibilities as a Technical Art Director are:

- Managing a team of Technical Artists
- Scheduling
- Identify and evaluate project risks
- Design game systems, architecture, pipelines and tools
- Develop tools in C, C#, Python and MaxScript
- Develop content creation methods and guidelines to maximize rendering performance and console memory effeciency
- Develop run-time shaders

Jason Hayes’s Specialties:

Project planning, problem solving, system and tool design, risk assessment and management, pipeline and workflow design, traditional figure drawing, lighting and shading, C, C#, Python, MaxScript, HLSL
