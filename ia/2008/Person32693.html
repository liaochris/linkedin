C. Erik Lundh’s Summary

Mr. Lundh is an experienced foreign affairs and economics analyst based in Taipei, Taiwan. Having recently relocated to East Asia he is currently studying Mandarin Chinese and researching MBA programs and employment opportunities in the region. Between 2003 and 2007 he worked in Washington, DC as a China / Taiwan Analyst for the United States Congress and as a Corporate Affairs Associate for an organization promoting business and trade relations between the United States and Taiwan.

C. Erik Lundh’s Specialties:

Research, Analysis, Writing, Editing, Event Planning, Government Liaison, Industry Liaison, Taiwan, China, East Asia

  • Policy Analyst, Economics and Trade

    (Government Agency; 11-50 employees; Government Administration industry)

    May 2006 — December 2007 (1 year 8 months)

    Professional responsibilities included monitoring, investigating, and submitting to Congress qualified findings and ad hoc research on the U.S.-China economic and security relationship. Together with fellow analysts and congressionally appointed Commission leadership I coordinated relevant public hearings, drafted formal findings, supplied senior American policy makers and their staff with timely information, and made significant contributions to the development and drafting of the Commission's 2006 and 2007 annual reports to the United States Congress. My portfolio included general Sino-American economic and trade relations, U.S.-Taiwan relations, cross-Strait relations, China's automotive and information technology industries, and Chinese media and internet regulations..
