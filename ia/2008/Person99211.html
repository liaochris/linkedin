Joel Sobelson’s Summary

After a successful career in general advertising I brought this experience to the world of Direct Marketing by becoming the Chief Creative Officer of Wunderman/ New York. In four short years I developed and successfully managed a department of over 100 creatives, producing some of the freshest, most creative direct response work in the industry. I was responsible for the agency’s creative output on brands such as AT&T, IBM, Microsoft, Citibank, Kraft, Pfizer, Club Med, Gannett Newspapers, Toys R Us, and the NY Philharmonic. Under my leadership, Wunderman/NY blossomed, winning more creative awards than any time in its history culminating in winning a Cannes Lion in 2004.

Presently I work at G2 Direct and DIgital, after working at Javelin Direct,a small pharmaceutical CRM agency pitching and winning accounts such as Spear Pharmaceuticals, Merck Pharmaceuticals, Roche and Pogo.com.

Prior to that I worked for a small agency in Connecticut designing websites for Church and Dwight on brands such as Trojan Condoms, a 2007 Webbie award winner and Arm and Hammer baking soda (armandhammer.com).


I am very active in the advertising and scholastic communities. I have been a sitting judge at the Cannes Direct Lions Competition. I was one of three worldwide Creative Directors chosen to host a seminar for the “Young Lions” in Cannes. I have spoken at the Direct Marketing Association conferences in Washington, D.C., New York, and New Orleans and was a Finals Judge for the prestigious Caples and RX Awards.

I’ve lectured on marketing at the University of North Carolina, the Advertising Club of North Carolina, Texas Tech University, Lehigh University, the University of Akron, Fairfield University, and Quinnipiac University and recently begun to work with the Advertising Education Foundation’s Inside Advertising Speakers Program.

I recently was published by Praeger Press with my writing on creative in the 21st Century in the book Marketing in the 21st Century.
