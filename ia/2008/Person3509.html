April DuBois’s Summary

Ms. DuBois has more than twelve years of experience in international business and economic development and has authored several books, including Taking on the World, for which she received recognition from the U.S. Department of Commerce Economic Development Administration.

She serves on the board of several bi-lateral organizations in the Washington D.C. area and is co-founder and active board member of Integrity Trade Solutions, a woman-owned, small business that assists global companies in securing contracts with the U.S. government. She was also recently selected to be profiled in the 2007 Who’s Who in America®.

Ms. DuBois has spoken around the world on issues relating to economic development, international business, U.S. government contracting, microenterprise development, global poverty, and anti-trafficking and has been interviewed by the BBC, New York Times, Forbes, Univision, the Washington Post, the Washington Business Journal, Enterprise Ireland, Business & Finance, The Canberra Times, among others.

Her humanitarian interests first began in the early 1990s when she began teaching English to refugees and immigrant families for Catholic Charities. This experience, coupled with her work as a teen mentor, led DuBois to begin focusing on the needs of impoverished women, orphans, and children—both in her local community and around the world. For the past several years, she has been involved in organizing a donor support group for an orphanage in the Dominican Republic that provides a permanent home to more than 120 children who have been severely abused and/or abandoned.

April DuBois’s Specialties:

microenterprise development, microfinance, microinsurance, education, government contracting, business development, public speaking, international trade, exporting, marketing, account management, event management, grant writing, fundraising, project management, and technical training

  • Director, International Business Development

    Greater Washington Initiative (GWI)

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; International Trade and Development industry)

    April 2002 — October 2007 (5 years 7 months)

    In her position with GWI, Ms. DuBois developed international strategy for attracting global companies to the Greater Washington region. A main focus of her work was to help international firms understand the business potential of selling to the U.S. federal government. She oversaw a team of international consultants who work side-by-side with global companies to connect them with potential partners and service providers in Greater Washington region.

    During her tenure at GWI, she developed its global series of “Doing Business with the U.S. Government” conferences aimed at attracting new international businesses to the region; she launched the organization’s first overseas offices; and, as a direct result of her work, brought an economic impact estimated at $350 million per year to the Washington region.

  • Export Specialist

    Susquehanna Economic Development Association-Council of Governments (SEDA-COG)

    (Non-Profit; 51-200 employees; International Trade and Development industry)

    Ms. DuBois developed extensive trade and export-related expertise while serving as Export Specialist at SEDA-COG, a regional, public-private economic development organization in Pennsylvania. During her tenure at SEDA-COG, she assisted over 400 companies in exporting their products and services overseas; managed numerous overseas trade missions; served as liaison between local companies and the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania’s 15 overseas offices; and published two books on international business and trade, Taking on the World and The Building & Construction Marketing Guide. She received national recognition from the U.S. Department of Commerce Economic Development Administration and several regional organizations for her work on Taking on the World.
