Dave Whittle’s Summary

I've been engaged in the business of blessing lives by sharing technology for almost 30 years now. My firm, Webworking Services, helps people find quality products and technologies, while also helping companies with quality products and exciting new technologies reach user groups, influential individuals, early adopters, and opinion-makers. We bridge the gap between people and any technology that has the power to increase individual enjoyment, strengthen families, and enhance personal fulfillment.

I've been passionately involved in the PC industry since 1978, and have personally worked with or served many major companies in the industry, including Intel, Microsoft, IBM, and a variety of smaller companies with great products and technologies. I started one of the very first stores in the country selling personal computers from a mall, and programmed a database management system for BYU's MBA program that they used for over 10 years. I joined IBM in Boca Raton (birthplace of the PC) in 1984. In the early '90s, as IBM's first and probably only official "OS/2 Evangelist," I was an online marketing pioneer, igniting a grass roots marketing revolution at IBM named Team OS/2 (see Wikipedia).

Over the years, I've presented to hundreds of audiences and to well over 100,000 individuals in six countries and 42 states, at trade shows, respected universities, government agencies, user groups, clubs, and associations.

In the late 80's, I created the "PS/2 Reference Guide," which had saved IBM a documented $22 million by 1990. In the mid-90's, I authored "Cyberspace: The Human Dimension," co-authored "Dvorak's Guide to OS/2," and contributed a feature article on technology to "Beyond the New Millennium." Currently, I write for various publications, including Smart Computing and user group newsletters.

I'm currently building marketing systems for two emerging superstar companies: muvee Technologies of Singapore, and INVISUS Direct of American Fork, Utah.

Dave Whittle’s Specialties:

Strategic marketing, business development, online communications and grass roots marketing - specifically understanding and reaching high-tech early adopters. Named by Working Woman Magazine as "one of America's most original technological thinkers."
