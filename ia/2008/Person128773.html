Michael Fortson’s Summary

Currently changing the world at Qik.


Previous highlights:

• Simplified web-based Unified Communications, including VOIP, voice and text messaging with one goal: connect anyone, anywhere, instantly and with a single click. Presence and sublime access control, too? Yes please.

• Bootstrapped a company with no funding: Co-founded the Internet agency Medium One in Toronto 1997, helping to grow it to nearly 50 employees by 2000.

• Pioneered the use of hybrid point-of-sale/Internet kiosks, allowing shoppers to easily find items in-stock or not by combining the store systems with an Internet fulfillment system.

• Developed and secured software intellectual property for a wireless and Enterprise security project

  • CTO, co-founder

    Medium One

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry)

    June 1997 — January 2000 (2 years 8 months)

    Pioneering Toronto-based Internet agency. Founded by 5 partners in 1997, grown to ~50 employees with no outside funding in a tough market (Toronto was very unlike Silicon Valley during the boom... every project had to be fought for, and everything required a sound business case). Some of the projects we produced in Toronto are still unmatched by the best that I have seen come out of California since I moved here. Walking into Borders and seeing what they are offering their customers as a kiosk almost makes me want to cry. M1 developers were producing the equivalent of AJAX years before anyone had thought to name it, and the ideas behind the development were unparalleled.

    Combine all that with many of the best people in each category of the business that I have ever worked with (before or since), and it's easy to see why I still miss it. Truly great people don't come around often. I'm fortunate to finally be working with more, but it took a lot longer than I had expected.
