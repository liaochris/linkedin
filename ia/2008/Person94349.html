John Allred, CISSP’s Summary

John Allred earned his BS degree in Electrical Engineering from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. After MIT, he participated in a number of technical firsts – the first internal hard drive for the Macintosh, the first large scale networked simulation of military vehicles (SIMNET), and the first large scale networking engine for video games and distributed systems (RTIME, acquired by Sony as the enabling technology for all networked PlayStation 2 games). He has also done extensive work in layer 4-7 load balancing and SSL termination (F5 Networks); embedded applications and systems (Stratos, Apple); large scale web services, systems, and evangelism (Microsoft); and cryptography, security, hardware interfaces, and malware analysis at a startup. He has held the roles of developer, developer lead, and Chief Technology Officer.

He excels at system architecture definition, technical presentations, and closing the technical sale. He is an inventor on one issued patent and several more in work at the US patent office; holds the Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP) credential; and is a computer forensics examiner.
.

John Allred, CISSP’s Specialties:

Highly scalable server and network architectures, distributed systems, cryptography, network security, evolutionary computing, embedded and real-time applications. Hardware and software interfaces. Computer forensics. Closing the technical sale. Presentations.
