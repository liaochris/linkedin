Greg Stevens’s Summary

My background in experimental psychology, my experience with business analysis and my technical programming skills mean that I can quickly understand the business needs of an organization, work with employees and managers at all levels, and plan and create the technological foundations for enterprise-wide solutions to organizational challenges. I would like to use these skills to contribute to a team that helps clients to transform their businesses through the development of custom analytical tools, business processes and technical applications.

Greg Stevens’s Specialties:

project management, business analysis, network and systems models, customer behavior models, cognitive models, agent-based simulation, comparative financial models, work-load projections, historical and predictive productivity models, trend analyses, technical (database and application) design, technology strategy and planning, technology solution development, business intelligence, application architecture planning, research (experimental design and testing), training and presentation

Greg Stevens’s Experience

  • Managing Partner

    Green Plastics

    June 2007 — Present (1 year 4 months)

    Owner, developer and manager of a small set of internet resources that provide scientific information, professional networking, product-finding resources, and a sense of community for students, scientists and entrepeneurs, that is focused on the new technology of biodegradable plastics made from renewable resources.

  • Senior Staff Consultant

    Idearc Media

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; IAR; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    January 2007 — Present (1 year 9 months)

    As a senior staff consultant, I manage several projects across different areas of the company. For the publishing organization, I built and helped to manage a team that developed a new nationwide productivity tracking tool. I worked with over 70 managers to determine the scope of items to be tracked and monitored, and worked with development resources across the country to evaluate ad hoc tracking and reporting systems and develop an integrated web-enabled platform. For the finance organization, I created and distribute an operating margin analysis report that integrates sales revenue, printing and distribution costs to examine the operating margin for every product published by the company. For the marketing organization, I develop models of customer behavior and sales strategy effectiveness. We then use these models to prepare sales reps in the field, identify the best sales leads and develop strategies for dealing with different customer groups.

This included developing work-load projections, comparative financial models, detailed trend analyses, and historical and predictive productivity and cost analysis tools.

  • Data Analyst & Developer

    Verizon Information Services

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; VZ; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    January 2004 — April 2005 (1 year 4 months)

    As data analyst for the Director of Publishing for the West Region, I produced a large number of automation tools and analytical reports, and integrated regional ad hoc reports into a single unified reporting methodology. I created presentations and training documentation for newly developed automation tools, as well as providing one-on-one training to 9 regional managers. I also worked with regional managers to identify business needs and develop appropriate enhancements when required.

  • Research Associate and Lecturer

    University of California, Los Angeles

    (Educational Institution; 10,001 or more employees; Research industry)

    October 2000 — October 2003 (3 years 1 month)

I also developed and coded simulation models, ran parameter sweeps and tests, and fit results to empirical data.

    As a Lecturer for Introduction to Cognitive Science and Introduction to Neural Networks, I planned course outlines, selected readings, gave lectures, held office hours, created and graded projects and exams.
