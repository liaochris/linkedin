Topaz Bott’s Experience

  • Senior Network Administrator / Engineer

    SeriousISP

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    August 2003 — July 2006 (3 years)

    SeriousISP’s primary business focus was dialup internet aggregator; supporting 200k dialup users though radius proxy to over 60 different companies, and over 300 VISPs. My responsibilities included maintaining the three server networks, in three different co-locations facilities. Each co-located network was kept synchronized and maintained in real-time; with one LDAP tree. Traffic was distributed thought a Round Robin failover. I was responsible for maintaining the Radius, Mail, Web, and MySQL Servers. I developed a content filter for the web browser; as well as an email filter that over the last 2 years has had a 0% false positive rate; with a 1% false negative rate. I was responsible for maintaining the VISP Portal, and maintain real-time data synchronization across the different networks. I was charged with maintaining Cisco 7300 Routers, Cisco 2900 Routers, Cisco Catalyst Switches, and Cisco PIX’s.

  • Senior Network Administrator / Engineer

    DPConnections

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    August 2002 — August 2003 (1 year 1 month)

    DPConnections was originally a buyer agent company that became a VISP wholesaler by merging with DestinyPC. As part of the merger the ISP started reselling Qwest, and UU.net. We consolidated our network down to three different locations; supplementing Qwest for areas where it was cost effective. At the same time that DestinyPC merged with DPConnections CTG Systems merged with DPConnections. Thus the main network was broken into the ISP, DPConnections (buyer agent channel), and CTG (Wholesale Computer/Component Distributor). I was charged with designing and implementing a network that could support and consolidate the different requirements. This merger required migrating three different accounting systems into one that was able to adequately address the needs of all. Consolidating to one LDAP database. I was responsible to maintaining the Radius, Mail, Web Servers, and MySQL / MS-SQL / Oracle Servers.

  • Senior Network Administrator / Engineer

    DestinyPC

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    October 2001 — August 2002 (11 months)

    DestinyPC was a VISP retailer; supporting 10k dialup users though radius proxy to over 30 different VISPs. I was responsible for maintaining their sixteen different server networks, in sixteen different locations. Each Servers Site kept synchronized in real-time. Traffic was distributed thought a Round Robin failover. I was responsible for maintaining the Radius, Mail, Web, content filtering, and MySQL Servers. I was responsible for maintaining the VISP portal, and the real-time data synchronization across the different networks. The modem pools we ran were Cisco 5800/5300s and Lucent PM3s. DestinyPC’s network covered three states, with sixteen different locations, and provided web hosting for 20,000 web sites and email for over 80,000 accounts. I was charged with maintaining Cisco 7300 Routers, Cisco 5300 Modem Pools, Cisco 5300 Modem Pools, Cisco 2900 Routers, Cisco Catalyst Switches, and Cisco PIX’s.

  • Lead Network Administrator

    High Country Internet Service Provider

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    April 1999 — June 2001 (2 years 3 months)

    High Country Internet Service Provider (HCISP) provided internet services. I designed and setup the server network. High Country Internet Service Provider was an ISP I built from the ground up. In addition to being responsible for the Mail, Web, News, DNS servers; I maintained the Versanet, and Ariel modem pool. The Ariel PRI was modem banks that sat in PCI slots, using a Linux kernel and a POSIX OS to customize the PPP negotiation, allowing the push of a firewall that allowed packet translations at the port. I wrote the accounting system. I was charged with maintaining Cisco BRI Network Modems (ISDN Dialup Modems).

  • Lead System Administrator
