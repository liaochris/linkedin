Seth Rosen’s Summary

I have spent the last decade working to further the missions of international development and human rights nonprofit organizations as an attorney, fundraiser, and executive. During this time I have continuously evaluated and restructured major gifts, foundations, and planned giving operations while creating strategic management plans to increase organizational efficiency and mission effectiveness. My experience with leading multi-national nonprofit organizations has allowed me to develop a deep understanding of the social justice, reproductive rights, international development, and human rights landscape in the United States and abroad and form strong connections within the legal and nonprofit sectors.

I received my Masters of Public Administration with a concentration in nonprofit management from New York University and law degree from New York Law School. I also author the nonprofit technology blog, Technovist.com, which explores how nonprofits can better use Web 2.0, social networking, and new technology to further their missions.

Seth Rosen’s Specialties:

Nonprofit management, major gifts fundraising, planned giving fundraising, constitutional law, international development, human rights and reproductive rights advocacy, new business and partnership development, Web 2.0 and social networking by nonprofits.

Seth Rosen’s Experience

  • Partnership Development and Online Organizing

    Malaria No More

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Fund-Raising industry)

    August 2007 — Present (8 months)

    Establish strategic partnerships and business development opportunities with corporations, civic groups, and other non-governmental organizations to increase awareness of malaria as a global health crisis and increase international funding of malaria control. Also promote Malaria No More across social networking and other web platforms to engage and educate our supporters.

    Malaria No More's mission is simple: to end deaths due to malaria. The world has known how to beat this disease for more than a century, yet it remains the number one killer of children under five in sub-Saharan Africa, claiming more than 1 million lives a year. Malaria No More engages individuals, organizations, and corporations in the private sector to provide life-saving bed nets and other critical interventions to families in need. Together these investments will significantly reduce malaria infections and make malaria-related deaths a thing of the past.
