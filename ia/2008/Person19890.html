Barry Krembs’s Summary

With a long history of service as: a Medic and Nurse with the U.S. Army, specializing in Weapons of Mass Destruction (WMD) Medical Response, and the EMS system as a Critical Care EMT and Mass Casualty Incident Medical Coordinator. with extensive work in the hospital setting that includes working from the bottom up to the first "Force Health Protection Coordinator" for Ft.   • Force Health Protection Coordinator

    US Army

    (Government Agency; USA; Military industry)

    2000 — 2001 (1 year)

    I spent my last year in the Army as The "First" Force Health Protection Coordinator while at Ft. Drum N.Y. I left the service and continued on as a civilian in this same role. This allowed the start of a "Force Health Protection Coordinator at other military bases. While each base had various persons doing these duties, they were secondary in nature. We realized that this position was so important that it required a set person with this as their sole priority. We coordinated the deployability and safety of our troops and their families in peace time as well as conflict. My duties were to coordinate the necessary immunizations needed for deployments. I became associated with the Vaccine Healthcare Network, a combined CDC and DOD agency overlooking the safe and proper immunization of our troops and their families. I was regarded as the field expert, due to a long history of immunizing troops for deployment and also insuring that they had their proper documentation attesting to this.
