Travis Millward’s Summary

Here is my story...

All through high school my plan was to attend the University of Idaho to study Wildlife Management so I could become a Game Warden? I grew up with a passion and respect for the outdoors. I was a Boy Scout, my Dad took me hunting, loved camping & backpacking...you name it - I loved doing it outdoors. Fast forward...I got married in 2003. That summer my wife gave me this crazy book, "Rich Dad, Poor Dad" by Robert Kiyosaki, for my birthday and that literally changed everything.

I began to read business books, I changed my major and started studying business and real estate. I ended up getting my real estate license and mortgage license. I soon realized my desire and passion was to be a full-time real estate investor. So I began my hiatus of attending real estate seminars.

My first deal was a REO in Pocatello, Idaho. I thought I was so cool...it was a terrible deal, but luckily I didn't lose money on that deal. I bought my first house in Detroit 2006. And I began doing deals in my local marketplace - Saint George, Utah in 2007. To date I have done 12 deals and have raised a total of $1,143,000 in private funds.

I'm at a point in my career where I am now going to really ramp up the volume because I have the ground work laid but my focus is not the money - my purpose is to now revitalize communities and neighborhoods in Metro Detroit. Vacant houses are a plague to society and neighborhoods...the breeding ground for drugs, theft, squatting & vandalism. Metro Detroit's housing is rotting from the inside-out. Something needs to be done!

So my wife, two boys and I packed up our things in Southern Utah, left our comfortable home and moved 2,000 miles across the country to Detroit, Michigan. My mantra is "Turn houses into homes."
