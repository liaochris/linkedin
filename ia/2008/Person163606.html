Roger Carlson’s Specialties:

Roger Carlson’s Experience

  • James Webb Space Telescope Integration & Test

    Northrop Grumman Space Technology

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; NOC; Defense & Space industry)

    March 2003 — Present (5 years 8 months)

    Integration and Test Lead for the JWST telescope and combined telescope & science instrument module. Scope includes assembling and testing the composite telescope structure (deployments, vibration, acoustics), integration of large lightweight optics to the structure, integration and alignment of the telescope and science instrument module, and optical testing of the payload in a large vacuum chamber under cryogenic conditions at 20 Kelvin. Responsibilities also include negotiation of requirements for government furnished facilities (clean rooms, environmental test facilities, vacuum chamber) and transportation of large payloads (10m by 5m). Developing ground support equipment concepts for handling and assembly of large, precision, deployable structure. Leading numerous trades to optimize plans and meet budgetary challenges.

  • James Webb Space Telescope Aq Manager

    Northrop Grumman Space Technology

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; NOC; Defense & Space industry)

    March 2003 — September 2007 (4 years 7 months)

    Acquisition Manager / Contracting Officer's Technical Representative for large subcontract supporting planning and execution of telescope optical integration and cryovac testing, as well as providing associated optomechanical GSE.

  • Adaptive Optics Algorithm Testbed Lead

    TRW Space and Electronics

    (Defense & Space industry)

    January 1998 — December 2002 (5 years)

    Technical lead for adaptive optics algorithm test bench hardware and software, an optical bench with deformable mirror supporting rapid evaluation of wavefront reconstruction algorithms. Purchased optics, built working optical system from scratch, authored software control system.

  • Salmon Passage Monitoring System Lead Engineer

    Ecologic

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    August 2000 — December 2002 (2 years 5 months)

    Hardware lead, unix administrator, and co-creator of a system to remotely monitor salmon passage at a dam on the Columbia River with digital video systems. Automated system captured video at the dam, eliminated empty water scenes, transferred video to university computer systems where workers counted and identified species with custom software client. Hands-on jack-of-all-trades work: designed, built, and installed system of lights, cameras, and housings; designed, built, and fielded server and data handling architecture; wrote the scripts that moved data from cameras to servers to counters to archives; wrote the software used by counters to count fish and process totals.

  • Space Interferometry Mission Optical I&T Lead and Technical Subcontract Manager

    TRW Space and Electronics

    (Defense & Space industry)

    January 1999 — July 2000 (1 year 7 months)

    Integration and test lead for a satellite optical science payload in cross-organizational team. Keeper of optical performance budget system engineering simulation. Managed subcontract for development of a set of interferometry simulation web pages to support the Space Interferometry Mission

  • Chandra X-ray Space Telescope I&T

    TRW Space and Electronics

    (Defense & Space industry)

    January 1995 — December 1997 (3 years)

    On-site responsible engineer during calibration of flight optics and science instruments of Chandra/AXAF x-ray telescope satellite payload, under time-critical year-long series of cryogenic vacuum tests with cross-organizational team. Supported planning of tests, controlling requirements and interfaces across organizations. Developed requirements for cleanrooms, vacuum chambers, transportation. Developed tools for efficient generation of procedures and configuration commands from test databases. Managed subcontract for a man-rated support structure, including design, RFP, vendor selection, subcontract management, and installation.
