Paul Siaens’s Summary

Having spent 3 years working on conservation projects and travelling through Africa as an Expedition Leader with Exodus travels, I returned to the UK to find myself involved in Graduate Recruitment with two oraganisations who were at the start of building their graduate brands. I have now joined a third.

I joined Teach First with the aim of helping them to help others make a difference in education. Over 3 years I was closely involved in developing and delivering the key mission to address educational disadvantage. As a result of my work I helped create a legacy of 1000 years of teaching and helped propell Teach First to number 19 in the Times Top 100 Graduate Employers of Choice list.

Off the back of my work at Teach First, I was invited to join as the Graduate Resourcing Manager. My remit was to develop a marketing and attraction strategy that would allow TfL to increase its graduate intake from 30 to 130 within 2 years. I accomplished this, developing the TfL graduate brand into one that is recognised accross UK universities and awarded the Times Graduate Employer of Choice in the Transport and Logistics industries and achieved a New Entry of 89th Spot in THe Times Top 100. I was responsible for a team of two people.

I have now joined Infosys, an Indian based technology company, to deliver their Global Talent Programme to the UK and EU Markets. Having spent the past 5 years working on Graduate Marketing and Selection strategies that have resulted in both companies winning accolades I feel I am best equiped to take Infosys' graduate strategies up to the next level. This is always the challenge I relish and one I have thus far delivered.
