Scott Miller (MojoBaby Inc)’s Experience

  • Founder / COO

    MojoBaby Inc.

    (Internet industry)

    June 2006 — Present (2 years 5 months)

    MojoBaby, Inc. was formed in 2007 to develop new ways of connecting Mobile Phones, Social Networks, Video Games and Web Technology.

    Our first project MojoBaby.com helps users instantly share pictures/video/text from mobile phones to social networks, blogs and forums. More than a blog or social networking site, MojoBaby helps users instantly share their experiences with family and friends.

    Our second project MojoGamer helps connect MMOGs, Social Networks, Mobile Phones and the web. The MojoGamer Platform helps users interact with your game and its community from anywhere, using a mobile phone or web browser.

    We also develop custom functionality and graphic design for each game, ensuring that each MojoGamer site is unique. MojoGamer is easy to setup and is typically hosted on a subdomain of your website. The best part is it can be setup with practically zero impact on your development schedule.

  • Founder

    Dedicated Gamer Consulting (Sole Proprietorship)

    (Sole Proprietorship; Computer Games industry)

    March 2002 — December 2005 (3 years 10 months)

    Dedicated Gamer Consulting (DGC) is an marketing and public relations agency specializing in Internet, Website and brand promotion. Founded in 2002 by Scott Miller, DGC's goal is to bring a new level of creativity to our clients marketing and PR tactics.

  • Founder and Editor in Chief

    UnknownPlayer.com (Sole Proprietorship)

    (Sole Proprietorship; 11-50 employees; Online Media industry)

    June 2000 — January 2002 (1 year 8 months)

    UnknownPlayer.com was a online news site providing coverage for the gaming industry with a focus on investigative journalism. We broke several mid to high profile stories in the two years I ran it, including "Black Snow vs. Mythic" and several of the Ubisoft/Shadowbane marketing stories. I also ran a radio show called Scott Miller Live. Each week we would bring a game developer on the air and have people call in to ask questions.

    The site still exists, but the archives were removed due to some hacker problems and I hope to get them back up this summer.

    Here is the official blurb from my last resume.

    Oversaw all aspects of a new start-up business, including recruiting, writing/editing of articles, webpage/backend design, supervision of 20+ volunteer writers/programmers and day-to-day business operations. Implemented marketing plan, including development of weekly events and frequent interviews.
    Managed a growing readerbase from 100 to 500,000 in under a year.
