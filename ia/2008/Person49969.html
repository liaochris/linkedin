Dennis Widmyer’s Summary

Dennis Widmyer has gained an impressive level of experience from working on big budget Hollywood films to non-paying indie shorts. Learning the many assets of filmmaking, he spent his days on movie sets and his nights pounding out spec screenplays. In 2002, he formed Kinky Mule Films with his writing partner, Kevin Kolsch, a company to showcase their writing and directing abilities.

Kinky Mule Film's first project was a feature length documentary on best-selling author Chuck Palahniuk (Fight Club) titled 'Postcards From The Future'. The film documents Chuck's landmark year of 2003 with the release of two new books and the unveiling of two world tours. Dennis premiered the film in Portland, Oregon to a sold-out crowd and the DVD later went on to sell thousands of copies online.

In the summer of 2005, Dennis turned his attention back to narrative film with 'Absence'¸ a low-budget thriller about a kidnapping in an idyllic suburb and how it affects three very different people, each with their own motives. The film was recently completed and had a successful premiere at The Long Island Film Festival. It also took two nominations at the Erie Horror Film Festival. 'Absence' now seeks distribution.

Dennis is now developing his next production, 'Our Lady Of Sorrow', a script about a young couple who move to a backwoods town the same day the body of a missing girl floats up from the local lake -- and 'Shed', a gritty horror film about a gruesome ordeal that takes place in a shed outside a cabin in the woods of the Pacific Northwest.

'Our Lady Of Sorrow' (www.ourladyofsorrow.com) is now seeking investors for a 2008 production and 'Absence' (www.absencemovie.com) seeks distribution.

See Dennis' reel at www.denniswidmyer.com.
