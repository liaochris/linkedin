Jay Ganaden’s Summary

I wish I had a romanticized story about how I mowed lawns, bought my first camera, and the "rest is history," but my path was a little different.

My path to Advertising Photography started in Business School.

I first chose a career in Management Consulting. I was fascinated by the idea of never working in the same 4 walls, nor with the same people, nor in the same day-to-day work. In my twenties, the business travel was fun and exciting too.

The Consulting/Professional Services world taught me how to be a likable and hard-working guy for our clients, how to manage expectations, how to run a project on time and within budget so we get hired again, and how to navigate a sensitive, political environment.

I would later connect the dots, and see that these experiences and inclinations would give me an advantage in Advertising Photography too.

I spent 7 years in Consulting, and ultimately learned some things about myself: 1) I was too impatient to become a "Partner"; I wanted to run my own business immediately, and 2) That in spite of being pre-destined by my parents to be a physician, that I did have a creative bone in my body after all.

So in 2002, I took a deep breath and jumped in!

These days I mostly photograph products for Retailers and Graphic Designers, including Gap, Old Navy, World Market and Sephora. My personal work is health and fitness lifestyle, and fashion photography.

Somewhere along the way I wanted to use everything I've learned to add the most possible value to others too, in a very genuine and visceral way. So I also have non-profit, education, and Design advocacy pursuits that I enjoy as well.

Maybe that's a romanticized story after all...
