John Greene, Ph.D. (john_greene@sra.com)’s Summary

Dr. Greene is a senior bioinformatics scientist with comprehensive expertise in bioinformatics and genomics. A Phi Beta Kappa graduate of MIT who received his Ph.D. from Harvard, he was trained as a molecular biologist and has 16 years of genomics experience at leading biotechnology companies including Gene Logic and Human Genome Sciences, where he was one of the first scientists hired and discovered TIMP-4 (VasoSten™) for therapy of restenosis.

Dr. Greene has 12 years of industrial bioinformatics experience, and develops Web systems to analyze and store biological data from relational databases as well as in using sequence analysis and microarray analysis programs for gene discovery and characterization. As Director of Bioinformatics for SRA, Dr. Greene assists SRA senior management in determining strategy and partnering for bioinformatics/genomics business development, in proposing architecture and functionality of new bioinformatics systems, and in recruitment of additional staff for SRA's ~75 person bioinformatics business. He is currently the Principal Investigator for the Enteropathogen Resource Integration Center, a $14.25M contract from NIAID, and the Project Manager for support for NCICB's Pathway Interaction Database and EAGLE Translational Research Analysis. He now holds a DSS Top Secret clearance.

Dr. Greene is also Adjunct Faculty in Bioinformatics at The Johns Hopkins University, where he has taught 410.634 Practical Computer Concepts for Bioinformatics for nine years, and just developed a follow-on course, 410.712 Advanced Computer Concepts for Bioinformatics.

He has also co-founded a sideline business for small business bioinformatics consulting, Complexigen, LLC, where he is President and Chief Scientific Officer.
