Anindya Saha’s Experience

  • Director of VLSI Group

    Saankhya Labs

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Consumer Electronics industry)

    April 2007 — Present (1 year)

    Leading the VLSI Group, contributing towards product definition.

  • Senior Staff Design Engineer

    Broadcom India Research Ltd @ Bangalore, India

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; BRCM; Semiconductors industry)

    2005 — 2007 (2 years)

    Prior to me joining this particular networking group in Broadcom, embedded CPU's were never integrated as part of the XGS switches. Got an opportunity to define, architect and develop a CPU Subsystem for XGS Ethernet Switch family. The first device which i worked on used a MIPS-32 CPU with several home grown peripheral interfaces, an efficient cross-bar interconnect and a high performance SDR/DDR interface controller.

    This won the best product award by Analog Zone in 2006.

  • Senior Technologist and Design Manager (dual capacity)

    Texas Instruments India @ Bangalore, India

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; TXN; Semiconductors industry)

    1996 — 2005 (9 years)

    Did several things here...during my initial years where i focussed most of my time learning new stuff. Starting from - RIP printer based products, Custom designed memories, Pixel interface asics, DSP peripherals for c55x, Pipelined memory interfaces.

    Definition and design management of several devices including Broadband controllers for DSL and Cable modems (AR5 family), Single-chip DSL modems belonging to AR7 family (based on c6x). The AR7 family of products also went on to win several international awards like EDN product of the year in 2004.

    A low cost version of AR7 DSL modem got nominated as the Innovative design for the Quarter in 2005 internally within TI.

  • Design Engineer

    Semiconductor Complex Ltd, Chandigarh, India

    (Public Company; 11-50 employees; Semiconductors industry)

    1993 — 1994 (1 year)

    These were my early days right out of IT-BHU when i was involved in the development and design of SRAMs, Standard cell layouts - from spice simulation, characterization till layout. SCL had a wonderful atmosphere where I got to learn a lot of things on my own given the constraints of available tools.
