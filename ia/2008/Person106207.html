Kurt Hayden’s Summary

I am a recent Doctoral graduate from the College of Engineering and Applied Science of Western Michigan University in Kalamazoo, Michigan. I am currently employed in Houston, Texas for an oil field support company.

While I have established over seven years experience in the manufacturing industry, during my doctoral studies I worked exclusively in research and academia. I had greatly enjoyed my industrial career and missed working in the manufacturing environment. Specifically I had missed working in production environments developing new products and processes as well as optimizing existing ones.

Goals:
- Develop, optimize and produce novel designs, products, and processes.
- Work with teams to solve ‘mysteries’ and ‘puzzles’ through designed experimentation and statistical analysis that is neither inappropriately rushed nor “off-the-cuff.”
- Develop other’s potential by increasing their understanding of plastics materials, processes and tooling.
- Establish a diverse personal history and wisdom.
- Remain flexible enough to pursue my many and varied subjects of interest.

Electronics skills:
PLC programming - Relay logic design - Circuit Analysis & Design

Computer Skills:
Moldflow - Cadkey - AutoCad - 3D Studio Max - Solidworks - Statistica - Minitab

Industrial Skills:
Injection molding process and tool design - Plastics processing knowledge and experience - PPAP Procedures - FMEA and Control Plan development - Experience with application of lean manufacturing practices - Industrial application of DOE methods - Fixture and gage design and construction - Robotics and Automation - Design of Assembly systems - Project evaluation and justification - Group management skills.

Kurt Hayden’s Specialties:

My areas of expertise are within the field of plastics processing and specifically the injection molding process.
