Austin S. Lin’s Summary

After a near-miss with law school, I started my career shopping and cooking – first for a couple with triplets, then for the Masters' receptions at Dunster House. In 1994, I landed at VideoGuide (think pre-historic TiVo) which led to a career of perl, database, and interweb hacking at Firefly Network, Internet Securities, D. E. Shaw, The MathWorks, Mambo.com, Yahoo!, Millennium Pharmaceuticals, and Harvard University. I've recently organized Everyday Ventures LLC to develop products to simplify and improve everyday life. Our first product is the Lampbuddy™, a small table that attaches to a torchiere lamp.

My latest bio for HLS Coalition Against Gender Violence's symposium on GPS Tracking of Domestic Batterers:

"Born in Michigan to Taiwanese parents, Austin grew up in Louisiana attending public and parochial schools. After graduating from Harvard College, he worked as a live-in cook, photographer, copywriter, college admissions interviewer, caterer, and temp for five years before settling into his first career in technology at such companies as VideoGuide, Firefly Network, MathWorks, D.E. Shaw, Yahoo!, Millennium Pharmaceuticals, and Harvard. In 2005, Austin decided to change careers and pursue a MSW at Simmons College School of Social Work so that he could integrate his interests in mental health, social justice, and feminist activism. He has served on the boards of Green Street Studios, Rebecca Stenn Company, and the Massachusetts NOW Foundation and is currently serving his second term on the national board of The National Organization for Women (NOW). Austin resides in Cambridge, MA, and spends his free time practicing yoga, spinning, cooking, reading, writing, and inventing."
