Dan Wallace’s Summary

Marketing leaders from mid-sized firms work with me to figure out what to do and how to do it. Most clients are so engrossed in day-to-day operations that they don't have time to keep up with marketing trends. Some feel stuck in ruts and worry about the future. I expand the range of practical options. Most often the goal is to increase sales.

Clients make all the decisions. My role is to ask powerful questions, contribute fresh ideas, and develop internal knowledge and skills. I also assist with plans, budgets and talent searches. In a pinch, I co-create marketing programs with clients. All work is interactive and collaborative.

Some clients enjoy working at Idea Food Labs, a space that is custom designed for innovation. The studio has an extensive business library and files, with plenty of stimuli to help generate new ideas. When it comes time to execute plans, clients tap into the SpiralNet database, which tracks relationships with over 500 talented marketing specialists (e.g., web developers, PR firms, designers, videographers, agencies, etc.). Clients have access to the SpiralNet database.

I have been involved in hundreds of engagements since starting this business in 1986; first as a copywriter, then as a marketing agency owner, and now as a Marketing Coach. I have a B.S in Marketing from ASU and an Executive MBA from the University of St. Thomas. Previously I worked at Fallon Worldwide on the Wall Street Journal account. I regularly read new business books and frequently attend marketing workshops through he AMA. Idea Food Corporation also has three products in development.

Representative Clients Include: BioMedix; City of Minneapolis; e-fortunecookie.com; Hamline University; IBM; Leonard, Street & Deinard; PDI; Research International; Mindware; Saint Mary's PowerTrak MBA; Summit Brewing; The Hill Library; The Prouty Project; Wize.com.

To talk about your needs, call me at 612-339-7848. There is no charge for an initial meeting.
