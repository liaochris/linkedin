  • Hand Held and Vehicle Mount Terminal Program Director

    Telxon

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Computer Hardware industry)

    September 1995 — July 1997 (1 year 11 months)

    One of my favorite places to work. I rose quickly starting out in Product Management for Hand Held and Vehicle Mount terminals. I managed product lines worth an aggregate of over $110 Million in revenue, with a client list of WalMart, Home Depot, Ford, Avis, Kroger, Neimen-Marcus, Ahold, Amway, Emery Air Freight, Hertz, R & L Trucking and more. I streamlined a product line that was in chaos, in to something very manageable, and delivered not only sales, but margin back into the line.

    In my last year there, I was promoted by Bob Meyerson to head up and advanced team of product development staff. Under my leadership we produced 7 new handhelds, and took the industry by surprise with the new 870IM, all in less than one year.

    My experience in JIT/virtual and outsourced development took development lead times through the floor, put new products out the door. I received 5 patents pending as a result. The firm moved to Houston, I was not willing to follow, but I loved working there.

  • National Sales Manager Optical Media

    Maxtor

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; MXO; Computer Hardware industry)

    February 1994 — September 1995 (1 year 8 months)

    Actually worked for their optical drive group: Maxoptix. Was brought over from Mistui Petrochemical when their optical media sales efforts were turned down due to poor yields, and the high cost of ramping their production lines. Handled sales nationally for Maxoptix of optical media through OEM, Distribution, and Private Label channels. Had own base of accounts and interfaced with Drive Sales Team as SME when needed. Grew business by 20% in my tenure. Left due to re-organization of Sales Teams when a new President was hired who did not value the contribution my product line was having on the company's bottom line. Media is a profit center because it is a constant 'repurchase'. He preferred to give it away, and thus my compensation was shattered.

  • Independent Marketing Consultant

    Mitsui Chemicals

    (Privately Held; 10,001 or more employees; Chemicals industry)

    March 1989 — March 1994 (5 years 1 month)

    Worked closely with Board Member level Senior Team to develop all Marketing and Sales effort for their 'Lightstore' optical media line entry into the US market.

    The program was a great success. We closed all major optical drive companies, and major optical drive integrators in the first year. Production capacity was full within 12 months. Managed key OEM's such as Maxoptix who hired me when MPC decided they must exit the business due to lack of capital to fund move to full volume production.

    Reported through to Mr. A. Mori of MPC. Yasuo Kofune, and At Sushi Sakai were my Managers.

  • International Marketing Manager

    Tandon

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Computer Hardware industry)

    1985 — 1989 (4 years)

    Began my career there in OEM and National Accounts. Made steady promotions handling accounts such as IBM, Tandy, Tandem, and was promoted into the company's highest growth area, Tandon Computer Company which developed and produced its own brand of PC's.

    Worked closely with International Sales Teams and was eventually put in charge to turnaround our lagging Asia Pacific group. With the help of Roger Yuen, we completely restructured our distributor channel, and become Tandon's most profitable business unit.
