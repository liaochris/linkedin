Barbara Houtz’s Summary

I am currently working as the new Director of Outreach for the Eberly College of Science at the Pennsylvania State University. In this capacity, I write grants for educational outreach projects, collaborate with faculty and administrative members across the university, and advise faculty on how best to incorporate broader impacts and wide dissemination of research findings into grant proposals. I develop, implement, and manage programs relating to science education and science instruction to a wide variety of audiences.

I also teach a course on "Scientists Teaching Science" for NIH bench researchers, PhD Fellows, and graduate teaching assistants at various campuses of the National Institutes of Health.

I have had over 15 years of experience in combining education and technology. I have developed curriculum materials for grades K through post-graduate, and have worked with educators from across the nation and the globe. I have expert knowledge of national and state education standards and how to use these to guide instruction.

I have finished writing my first book on instructional practices and incorporating technology.

I plan to earn a Ph.D. or D.Ed. in an on-line university within the next 7 years.

Barbara Houtz’s Specialties:

Outreach, higher education, curriculum development, technology applications for working adults and educators, technology applications for use in a classroom, writing and editing, training adults in uses of technology.
