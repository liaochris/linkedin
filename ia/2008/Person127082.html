Michael Crites’s Summary

I've always been curious. Maybe too much so.

What I see today, regardless of industry, is a winnowing of stand-pat business functions. Titles and elevator pitches can't possibly capture what it means to fundamentally understand your market, your business, or how to measure success (or failure for that matter.)

Today, more than ever, I think professionals need to be curious about business. We need to take a real interest in the P&L. Express curiosity as the why duplicate content is bad for our websites. Understand the difference between data & information.

If you know where to look, and how to listen, there's a masters class on any subject somewhere in the world. You just need to be curious enough to find, listen, and learn.

That, I think, is my greatest asset - I'm curious enough to find & apply things that can really impact business. I've had wonderful success building businesses with information that is freely available to us all.

Michael Crites’s Experience

  • Director of Marketing

    Associated Crane & Truck

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Machinery industry)

    September 2007 — Present (10 months)

    With Associated Crane & Truck I lead the development of multi-channel marketing strategies which align our messaging and products with our customer's needs in an effort to maximize both existing customer value & new acquisition effectiveness.

    Basically, I make our products and services relevant & easy to find for the people who need to find them.

  • Marketing Director

    A-CTi

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Internet industry)

    January 2006 — August 2007 (1 year 8 months)

    In retrospect, I think A-CTI hired me because they needed someone to do the heavy lifting.

    I lead the development of new online identities and located opportunity areas within brand and marketing strategies of legacy A-CTI websites. You could say it involved "cross-functional team interviews" and "internal web analytics", but it was mostly just me.

    My goal was always to drive decisions through data, and derive actionable insight from our online brands, which was really fun. Working with the CEO was a strange delight, as his passion for business was really infectious. I learned more just geeking out on Google Finance with him than any other single professional experience.

    I also lead the planning and implementation of website-specific PPC and SEO campaigns, managing more than $500,000 annual spend across multiple search engine platforms.

    A-CTI set the stage for what I believe will be my life-long career - search marketing.

  • Principal

    Subversive Designs

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Graphic Design industry)

    2002 — 2006 (4 years)

    Subversive Designs was established to provide design services to an existing group of long-standing industry contacts developed over the years.

    It grew from the initial set of clients to a broader group on referral-based business with no money, I mean not a dime, put into marketing the company.

    We were contracted to build corporate identities, provide web design services, trade show signage, direct mail pieces, etc.

    If it was MarCom, there's a fair chance we did it.

  • Production Assistant

    Clear Channel Portland

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; CCU; Broadcast Media industry)

    2003 — 2005 (2 years)

    Clear Channel is an odd duck. You've got an amazing group people, loaded to the gills with talent, who are doing what they love - but struggle with the organization they are doing it for.

    I had the honor of processing (I wouldn't call it producing) the John Tesh program for nightly air, which was about as fun as it sounds.

    On the bright side, I voiced a number of commercial spots (to my wife's delight), edited the voice tracks of some really good DJs, worked with some absolutely incredible people, and had a chance to really see what makes radio tick.
