Doug Dawson’s Summary

I got my degree in Biology, looking to go into grad school and immunological research. But soon after graduation in 1991 my brother-in-law Phil, who had recently started a multimedia company, was having issues with a text-based database. Completely self-taught, I managed to whip up a few macros to streamline his process, and he offered me a job.

In the sixteen years since, I have run a digital slide and imaging service bureau, established technology procedures for word processing and graphics departments for a major business outsource services provider, and trained folks in Photoshop and QuarkXpress production.

I worked in a very cool video production house's multimedia department in St. Louis. It was with that company that I scored my highest award - a St. Louis Addy for "Interactive Disk - Based Sales Package" for Artex International.

I then worked with a Flash-only firm called Chameleon Creative. That crew, brought together by Jeff Stein, was incredible. He and his partner sold at the top, and I moved on to join my long-time friends at Digital Solutions Laboratories.

I also have tried to extend my knowledge and skills by stepping away from digital design and development. On September 12th, 2001, I told Curt and Shawn I was leaving DSLAB.

I took a job as a mobile geochemist, driving a modified RV around Missouri. When that job was changed to be office-based, I went back to music and theater and began working with IATSE in St. Louis. That led to a gig with The Shakespeare Festival of St. Louis.

Soon after that time I had begun to get an itch to get back to the field I truly knew best: digital design and development. Revitalized, refreshed, and with a new appreciation and viewpoint on the field, I am currently having a blast working with folks both young and old to make really cool stuff.

Doug Dawson’s Specialties:

Web strategy, client relations, project scope and requirements analysis, proposal writing, development team management, production management, presentations and public speaking, and general enthusiasm.

  • Mobile Geochemist

    Mid-America Environmental

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Environmental Services industry)

    October 2001 — May 2004 (2 years 8 months)

    Hermon and Susan Atkinson and Wes Robb ran this mobile lab facility for some time before I talked my way on board. Convincing Hermon that I could indeed be a good field chemist, I quickly learned their process for running soil and water samples through EPA (modified) standards 8010 and 8020. I was soon on my own driving one of our two modified RV's to various locations around Missouri, testing soil and water for petroleum contamination.

    Missouri was enlightened enough to maintain a petroleum tank insurance fund, knowing that any tank buried within the ground would inevitably fail. The Fund would pay for our RV labs to be onsite and test soil and water as it was being removed to ensure that it was clean or dirty. I dug it. Unfortunately the DNR changed the regs in favor of fixed-base labs, and I decided on a change.

    I earned my Federal HAZWOPER Certification for this job and renewed for two more years; it has since lapsed.
