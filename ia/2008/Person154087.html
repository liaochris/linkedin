Paul Setlak’s Summary

Currently, I am a Medical Services Specialist II at Abbott, specifically assigned to the therapeutic areas of neuroscience, pain, anesthesia and pulmonary (NAPP). Additionally, I am an adjunct assistant professor at Midwestern University Chicago College of Pharmacy, focusing on drug information and the therapeutic areas I work with at Abbott, in addition to infectious diseases.

Secondly, my focus is in management as it relates to product lifecycles and subsequent strategies towards achieving both company and customer postive outcomes. Additionally, I am involved in competitive intelligence efforts and how that information can then be translated into tangible material that can be incorporated into marketing, product and lifecycle strategy and development.

My third area of involvement focuses on emergency preparedness and response to natural and manmade disasters (i.e. influenza epidemics, emerging infectious diseases, bioterrorism, chemical/nuclear terrorism).

Most recently, I have successfully authored a handbook, published by the American Pharmacists Association (APhA) for Pharm.D. students doing their pharmacy practice experiences (PPEs), or clinical rotations, which is meant to guide them in the areas of presentation skills, direct patient care and rotational expectations/successful outcomes. Additionally, the handbook features an extensive appendix section focusing on various equations and algorithms useful to those students on rotations.

On a part-time basis, I am also a practicing pharmacist working for Walgreen’s Pharmacy and have a deep interest in financial markets and investment analysis/management.

Paul Setlak’s Specialties:

Pharmacotherapeutics, drug information, pharmacovigilance, toxicology, drug development, product lifecycle management, consumer markets & buying behaviors, supply chain management, product communications, marketing management, product branding, environmental health, competitive intelligence, critical care, weapons of mass destruction (bio/chem/nuclear), infectious diseases, crisis management, public health, emergency preparedness & response, international strategic studies, intelligence analysis
