Donald L. ("Skip") Conover’s Summary

Goal: Work with a major U.S. or International corporation to develop business in India and the Middle East, particularly Saudi Arabia, Qatar, UAE, and the other GCC member countries. India's real estate boom is unprecedented and the Middle East and India combined represent the largest economic opportunity of the next quarter century.

I have been developing international businesses as an executive and entrepreneur since 1979.

In the period 1998-2006, I founded and led CBay Systems, Ltd. to become the 3rd largest in a 1,500 company healthcare services industry. Starting from zero in 1998, my colleagues and I built a company, which now grosses over $50 million annually, and went public on AIM in June 2007. CBay has over 650 clients in the United States and the Middle East, and over 5,200 employees throughout the World. I have retired as Vice Chairman of the Board of Directors, but continue to handle the company's marketing efforts in the Middle East. This work has involved supervision of leading-edge computer hardware and software sales and services.

In the period 1991-1998, I pioneered several of the first business process outsourcing industries in the Republic of India, building a 500 person operation in Madras (now Chennai).

In the period 1979-1985, I created a new Japanese subsidiary of an American multinational corporation serving the following industries: automotive, civil engineering, building products and office equipment. During this period we introduced over 200 product lines, and I created two different manufacturing operations, in different industries: automotive and building products.
