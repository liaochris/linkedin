Barry Ehrmann’s Summary

Barry Ehrmann is founder & producer for Enliven Entertainment, a company specializing in event, concert and video production for DVD & Television. Partners include HBO, Showtime, VH1, VH1 Classic, CMT, InHD, HDnet, PBS, APT, Bravo, Comedy Central, DirecTV, Sony BMG, Universal, Warner, Eagle, EMI, Image, Rhino, Pioneer, SPV, Salient and others.

Concert Video credits include: Cheap Trick/Sgt. Pepper; ZZ Top; Black Sabbath/Heaven and Hell; Barenaked Ladies; Katt Williams; Rush; Harry Connick, Jr.; Queensryche; Mo’Nique; Collective Soul; Aimee Mann; Cheap Trick; Stray Cats; The Who; Slipknot; Branford Marsalis; Warped Tour; Alanis Morissette; Brian Setzer; Counting Crows; Judy Collins; Sammy Hagar; Ozzfest; Farm Aid and others. Barry's first film is being released – Katt Williams, American Hustle

Barry was Supervising Producer for Decades Rock Live!, a concert series for VH1 featuring: Elvis Costello with Billie Joe Armstrong, Fiona Apple & Death Cab for Cutie; Heart with Alice in Chains, Dave Navarro & Gretchen Wilson; Cyndi Lauper with Scott Weiland, Shaggy & Pat Monahan; Pretenders with Incubus, Iggy Pop and Shirley Manson; Lynyrd Skynyrd with 3 Doors Down, Hank Williams, Jr., Bo Bice.

Barry served at MCY Music World Inc. from 1999 to 2001, where he produced the first series of on-line concerts, pay-per-view specials and DVD releases featuring the Backstreet Boys, *N’Sync, Ozzfest and Sammy Hagar. From 1996 to 1998, Barry Ehrmann served as SVP for Paradigm Music Entertainment, an alternative and catalog based record label.

Between 1992 and 1996, Mr. Ehrmann served at King Biscuit Flower Hour Records, and was responsible for the acquisition of the classic King Biscuit Flower Hour radio archives, consisting of 20 years of live concert recordings from over 450 artists and the subsequent release of these concerts on CD.
