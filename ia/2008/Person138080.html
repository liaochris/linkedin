Nancy Delain’s Summary

I am a registered patent attorney practicing in upstate New York. I concentrate my practice in intellectual property law and business law.

I work extensively with:

* prosecuting patents, trademarks and copyrights through to issuance or final rejection;

* licensing and franchising intellectual property rights (i.e., commercialization);

* new businesses, regarding the most advantageous form for that particular business;

* keeping clients on track with necessary filings with NY State and the feds (franchise taxes and the like);

* drafting business documents (especially contracts);

* counseling non-profit boards; and

* assisting with winding up businesses with a careful view of the fate of the business's intellectual property.

I also train both experienced and inexperienced inventors in the patenting process. I market this service to human resources professionals and training departments of technical businesses (IT, engineering, architectural, manufacturing, biotechnology, nanotechnology,...).

I spent over 20 years as a technical writer and trainer in the software industry, doing mostly freelance writing for companies as diverse as GE, 3M, RCA/David Sarnoff Research Center, CSC, New York State, and Rational Software. Technical documentation, however, is not my life's goal, so I started law school in the (phenomenal) Special Part Time Program at New England School of Law in Boston, MA. My job circumstances dictated a change, so I transferred to Franklin Pierce Law Center, then one of the top five law schools in Intellectual Property Law, from which I graduated in 2003.

Nancy Delain’s Specialties:

Intellectual property law and practice, including patent, trademark, copyright work, trade secrets, unfair practices, licensing and franchising; training interested laypersons in any and all of the above

Business law and practice, including counseling start-ups, managing negotiations, drafting legal documents, protecting corporate assets, overseeing business closing and winding-up of affairs.

Training inexperienced inventors to the patenting process.
