Tor Ulstein’s Summary

I came to game testing after graduating UW with a BA in Communication. I focused my studies along an interest in media technology, media history and media theory. These disciplines prepare a foundation of systems thinking through their study and discussion of the how media is adopted and ultimately used by the masses.

Working from track tester to test lead has been a great experience. The caliber of people in the industry keep me passionate about working in test, but like most contract testers I dream of transitioning to something more cerebral.

The following statement is true and it can't help but come out strange; I pitched free roaming city and flashing high beams and a few more brain busters at a racing game focus group. A production cycle later Need For Speed Underground was released.

Fieldworks Seattle called me back for football games, I don't play them. But a production cycle later EA was touting a college game so real that crowd intensity impacted game play and more wins means better fans. Just saying, media should be empowering. Don't be limited by what can't be done, be inspired by what could.
