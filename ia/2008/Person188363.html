Thomas Stirling’s Summary

I have been fascinated with the subject of web design since around 2000. I initially took classes that taught me the fundamentals of HTML through hand coding websites from scratch without the assistance of WYSIWYG editors. I feel that learning this way gave me a solid understanding of the basics.

I built on this knowledge over the years with many other classes as well as learning a lot of things in my spare time. I have been using Photoshop for over 10 years and together with this knowledge and an artistic eye, I feel I have combined my artistic skills with the technical elements of website design to become a strong designer.

Since moving to the United States in the summer of 2006 from Glasgow Scotland, I have found success in the area of web design and implementation with a number of commercial projects under my belt. I feel I have a good well rounded skill base, and am capable of taking direction from clients or running with my own ideas for projects.

Thomas Stirling’s Specialties:

HTML, CSS, PHP, PHP MyAdmin (MySQL Database Administration Tool), Adobe Flash (animation, video/media streaming and also interactivity) Dreamweaver, Adobe Photoshop (used mostly for graphic creation on websites, also design for print) Adobe Image Ready (slicing web graphics) Adobe Fireworks, Sony Soundforge, Search Engine Optimization, Traditional Artwork (Original Drawings, Paintings & Photography)

Thomas Stirling’s Experience

  • Web Designer

    DataOne Software

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    March 2007 — Present (1 year 8 months)

    In this role I am responsible for creating unique ways of presenting websites for the automotive industry. I listen to client’s ideas and create a custom design using Adobe Photoshop that’s tailored to their individual needs.

    Once the design meets approval from the client I then slice the design and code it using Adobe Dreamweaver and upload it to one of our servers. I then use PHP to integrate database functionality and incorporate the existing online software.

    I am responsible for creating Adobe Flash components, which range from animation, interactivity and streaming video.

    I have also created a number of design for print projects such as in house marketing documents and corporate identities and logos. I also created the company's homepage using Adobe Flash, Photoshop, HTML, CSS and XML, which can be viewed at http://www.dataonesoftware.com

  • Web Designer

    Stirling Creative (Self-employed)

    (Self-Employed; Myself Only; Internet industry)

    July 2006 — March 2007 (9 months)

    Upon arriving in the United States I decided to build a portfolio of live client websites to further my experience as a web designer. I worked with a broad range of clients.

    The topics I built sites for included Churches, Journalism, Estate Sales, Non-Profit Organizations, Artists and even Blacksmithing. I feel that during this time I built a very strong portfolio as well as good people skills.

    This body of work was key in my success to gaining a full time position as a Web Designer at DataOne Software in early 2007.

  • Senior Graphic Designer

    Shannon Games Ltd

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Publishing industry)

    November 2004 — June 2006 (1 year 8 months)

    In this role I was responsible for creation of graphics for bespoke board games to be used in the Scottish tourist industry. I mainly used Photoshop and Illustrator to create design for print. I took the initial raw ideas of the creative director and transformed those ideas into tangible eye catching products

    A highlight of this role was having several games of my design chosen by the Scottish Tourism Board to be included in gift baskets at the 2005 G8 Summit in Gleneagles Scotland. Dignitaries that received these games included former British Prime Minister Tony Blair and US President George W Bush.

    I also designed and coded the company website which can be viewed at http://www.shannongames.com which was key in boosting the online sales of the products as well as attracting a large volume of enquiries regarding bespoke design.

  • Director / Web Designer

    Net46 Design Ltd

    (Internet industry)

    December 2002 — November 2004 (2 years)

    This was a business formed by myself and several other young designers and developers from The Glasgow area of central Scotland. We targeted small to mid sized businesses, focusing on giving clients a one on one type of service that many of the larger web design companies simply didn’t offer.

    I was responsible for meeting with clients and listening to there ideas and vision for what they required of their website. Over the 2 years I was involved with the business I worked on a large number of diverse projects that ranged from industries such as Real Estate, Musical Acts and the Hospitality Industry.

This course inspired me to take the graphical and artistic skills i had aquired and take them to the field of website design and development.
