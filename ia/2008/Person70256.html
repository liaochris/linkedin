Giorgio Dominese’s Summary

Giorgio Dominese graduated in Economics from Trieste University and in Political Sciences from Padua University. He attended a specialization course in International Relations at the Johns Hopkins University, Bologna Center.
He has a long experience of university teaching and research. He is founder and Coordinator of CEEUN-Central Eastern European University Network, involving a large group of foreign researchers in Central and Eastern Europe, Black Sea, Eurasia and the Mediterranean. He is the Editor of Transition Studies Review (JEL, EconLit indexing), a quarterly journal of international Economy, Finance, Innovation, Regional, Strategic and Juridical Studies, published in English by Springer Wien New York (http://www.springerlink.com/content/1614-4015). He is Professor Economy and Policy of Transition at the International Relations Postgarduate at LUISS University Rome, At the same time he is professor of Economics and Finance of Transition at Udine University.
Member of the Kuratorium of Wifo ( Austrian Institute of Economic Research), IDM in Vienna and some other Italian Institutions, he is also consultant for international activities of some important Italian industrial and banking groups. Since 1996 he has participated in the executive body of SECI-Southeast European Co-operative Initiative. He is Adviser of the Special Coordinator of the Stability Pact for South Eastern Europe, Erhard Busek, where he is also member of the BAC-Business Advisory Council. Other International Institutes and Association memberships: AEA-American Economy Association; IISS-International Institute Strategic Studies, London; EEA-European Economic Association; PfP Consortium; ERSA-European Regional Science Association; EACES-European Association for Comparative Economic Studies; IPSA-International Political Science Association.

Giorgio Dominese’s Specialties:

International capital flow, FDI, international finance, economy of growth, international relations, aerospace industry, technology transfer, currency and rate of exchange issues, country risk, strategic studies, transition economies and finance, development policy, public choice, institutional building, university Bologna process
