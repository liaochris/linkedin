Geoff Ongley’s Summary

My background is UNIX, that is, FreeBSD and Solaris, and a large stack of server based software that runs on these platforms.

I typically work in a hosting kind of environment, that is, we either host everything from the server right up through the application and support layer, or we provide a platform and advice for others to run applications.

I currently look after a large and very diverse infrastructure, both hardware and sotware wise. Core platforms are fairly consistent (Solaris 10, Windows Server 2003, LDAP, Active Directory and so on) but we look after a large amount of different applications that live on top of that core infrastructure.

I have a large range of experience, starting working on computers at the age of 8, basic programming at 12, computer construction at 14, TCPI/IP networking at 16, and then later running my own business while completing my B. Computer Science degree.

After that I was offered a Job at The University of Newcastle, surprisingly, because I wasn't looking for work. I took on the role because it was actually what I pictured as the dream job, that is working on and managing a large Solaris Infrastructure, and on a wide range of applications and cool new technologies. This job in its nature is very diverse and allowed me to work on a lot of cool stuff with some of the brightest people I've met This is my happy place.

Lately I have been moved into a management position, but I still spend 80% of my time on purely technical work, as a Systems Administrator. This is mostly because my real interest is in servers and various cool technologies, like Solaris Zones, ZFS, Logical Domains, Clustering and lots of other Sun stuff. I think it also works well with my team as I am more like a team member, than a manager telling them what to do.

From time to time I am seconded to do purely technical work still, and get to hang up the manager gloves for a week or two, basically, to make problems go away.
