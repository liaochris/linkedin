Nicolas Oriol’s Summary

With an electronics engineering background I began my professional career in the field of quantitative finance at Banco Santander. In 1998 I went to MIT where I earned an MSc degree in 2000 and, more important, an unforgettable personal experience. In 2000, I joined Bankinter, the 5th largest bank in Spain known for having an advanced technology, an ability to introduce innovations in financial services and for being one of the best places to work in Spain. I spent 7 years in different areas of Bankinter (innovation, mobile banking, IT, corporate development). I left the bank in april-07 to co-found Unience (www.unience.com) with a firm specialized in private banking and financial advisory services. Unience is a start-up that will bring mass collaboration and open innovation to financial services.
My actual professional goal is to work with great people to build a great company that will change the rules of financial services.
I’m happily married and a proud parent of 4.
