Jen McCabe Gorman’s Summary

A hybrid firestarter/connector sparking healthcare change from home-base & abroad, Jen covers healthcare/hospital innovation @ Health Management Rx.

In her spare time, Jen is neck-deep in consumer-centric health. Current research includes whiteboarding the evolution of Health 2.0, 3.0, and 4.0 and harnessing the semantic web to spark healthcare change.

A first-mover Health 2.0 NL organizer, Jen secures entrepreneurs/sponsors. As a Health 2.0 USA analyst/contributor to The Health Care Blog, she's identifying star players in consumer-centric wellness.

Accepted to Georgetown's Master of Health Services Administration program, Jen deferred to study the Dutch system & poignant points of international healthcare intersection.

Jen formerly served as Associate Administrator at Christ House, performing ops, HR, safety, & administrative/management functions for the DC-area 33-bed nonprofit medical facility.

Jen cut her teeth as Analyst & Director during start-up phase at The Hanover Research Council (www.hanoverresearch.com), closing the first multiyear membership agreement and cultivating HRCs early development in the hospital/healthcare vertical.

Prior to joining Hanover, Jen developed PM skills via total responsibility (budget & content) for production of a bimonthly magazine, Cast Polymer Connection, at the American Composite Manufacturers' Association.

Ms. Gorman serves as Vice Chair, Board of Directors, for Baltimore-based MEDBANK of Maryland, Inc. (www.medbankmd.org), a nonprofit providing free prescription medications to 35,000 residents via pharmaceutical firms' patient assistance programs (PAPs).

She is a current Washington Metro Society of Healthcare Marketing & PR member and a member of the American College of Healthcare Executives (ACHE).

A Paul H. Nitze Scholar, she received her B.A. in Literature, with a minor in Women, Gender, & Sexuality studies, magna cum laude, from St. Mary's College of Maryland (www.smcm.edu).
