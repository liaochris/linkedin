Sean Fenlon’s Summary

DoublePositive Marketing Group was founded in 2004 after I recognized the tremendous opportunity that was represented at the intersection of data-driven online marketing and live human-based call centers. The company is currently considered the industry leader in both selling and delivering “LIVE Hot Transfer Leads" for sales professionals.

As CEO of DoublePositive, I am responsible for the company's performance, strategic vision, and the continued development of DoublePositive’s patent-pending business model and related optimization technologies.

I began my career as a mortgage broker, growing my organization into one of the largest in the region during the mid- to late-90’s. In 1999, I was the founder and CEO of ListenSmart.com, a business that helped to shift the music industry paradigm and leverage new opportunities for independent musicians.

Most recently, I was the co-owner of TheLoanPage.com, an Internet mortgage lead company with a business model very similar to that of Lending Tree, which grew to over $10 Million in sales and over $3 Million EBIDTA before the company was sold to Battery Ventures at the end of 2003. The transaction was unique as it represented the first-ever full acquisition of a company by Battery. The company has since be re-sold to HouseValues.com (NASDAQ: SOLD).

The goal of DoublePositive is to extend the evolution of Internet advertising and online marketing to "CPT" (cost-per-transfer) -- a natural extension of existing pricing models:

CPM --> CPC --> CPA --> CPT (Cost-per-TRANSFER)

With the right team in place, DoublePositive is a genuine home run opportunity.

  • Founder & CEO

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry)

    September 1999 — January 2001 (1 year 5 months)

    In the Spring of 1999, I founded an Internet startup in an attempt to build a radically new business that would help shift the music industry paradigm and leverage new opportunities for independent musicians. I personally developed a completely unique web-enabled business model with multiple online and offline profit centers - including conventional e-commerce, advertising, affiliate relationships, and a revolutionary new approach to generating music publishing royalties.

    The fundamental business concept was to share 50 percent of all advertising and e-commerce revenues with the participating artists based on the relative popularity of their music on the site. In exchange for this revenue-sharing status, artists would establish ListenSmart as their contractual music publisher - thus creating potentially explosive revenue growth through music publishing royalties.
