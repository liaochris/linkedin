David Meadows’s Summary

I've been a serial entrepreneur since graduating college in 1991. My businesses have been in publishing, software and printing. I've also worked for non-profits and I now run the United States Center for Entrepreneurship at www.uscfe.com

David Meadows’s Specialties:

I'm the creative networker--always bringing people together with new ideas and the means to bring those ideas to life.

David Meadows’s Experience

  • Executive Director

    United States Center for Entrepreneurship

    (Educational Institution; 1-10 employees; E-Learning industry)

    July 2007 — Present (1 year)

    The United States Center for Entrepreneurship assists entrepreneurs, business owners and idea generators through membership, education, services and opportunities that provide access, learning and business development with seasoned entrepreneurs, industry experts, best-selling authors, advisors, consultants and peer entrepreneurs. (www.uscfe.com)

  • Co-Founder

    Mimeo.com

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Printing industry)

    March 1998 — December 1999 (1 year 10 months)

    In March of 1998, I co-founded Mimeo.com—which was the first copy center accessible via the Internet. Similar to Kinko’s but with a 140,000 sq. foot “centralized” print factory, Mimeo enables customers to print, bind and deliver documents from their desktop. Mimeo will produce over $80 Million in 2007 revenue and has truly revolutionized the print market. Mimeo can be found at www.mimeo.com.

  • President

    ScreenShop Software

    (Privately Held; Computer Software industry)

    March 1996 — December 1999 (3 years 10 months)

    ScreenShop Software produced many creative computer screen savers and multimedia CD’s for corporate marketing. Clients included American Express, Citibank, Compaq, Lucent, Lycos, Sony, NBC and many others. Projects for sports teams such as the Tampa Bay Devil Rays, New York Knicks, San Diego Chargers, the Super Bowl and Final Four were very successful and exciting to produce. Additionally, ScreenShop produced the first “personalized” screen saver that included 250,000 Spider Man screen savers distributed individually through 2,000 Wal-Mart stores. This was a huge hit and generated great press!

  • President

    Worldbits Corporation

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Publishing industry)

    June 1993 — March 1996 (2 years 10 months)

    I left ACE to create the Worldbits! Country-A-Day calendar that became Rand McNally’s # 3 best selling product in 1995. The printed calendar explored countries of the world on a daily basis. Computer screen savers were popular at that time, so I transitioned the calendar to a screen saver and created ScreenShop Software in March of 1996.

  • U.S. Campus Development Director

    Association of Collegiate Entrepreneurs (ACE)

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    July 1992 — June 1993 (1 year)

    After graduating from college and traveling for a while, I was hired by the Association of Collegiate Entrepreneurs as their full-time U.S. Campus Development Director. I will never forget the international convention in 1993 that I hosted in New York City with Michael Dell as our keynote speaker and 500 other entrepreneurs from around the world. Some of my best entrepreneur friends still exist nearly 15 years later from that event!
