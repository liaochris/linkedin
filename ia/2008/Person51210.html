Experienced publishing professional with significant people and project management skills. Dianna S. Leach’s Experience

  • Principal and Consultant

    D.S. Leach Consulting, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Publishing industry)

    November 2005 — Present (2 years 7 months)

    Our firm provides client-specific editorial, publishing technology and project management solutions to a diverse and global clientele.

  • Senior Attorney Editor

    Thomson West

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; TOC; Publishing industry)

    August 2001 — October 2005 (4 years 3 months)

    As a senior attorney editor, I served as publication editor of New York Pattern Jury Instructions—Civil, a premier Thomson West publication, successfully managing all aspects of product development and author relations, enhancing publication quality and increasing revenue annually. I also served as publication editor of several other notable Thomson West publications, including West’s McKinney’s Forms for New York and Guide to Employment Law and Regulation. In this role, I collaborated with team members to develop and implement best practices in utilizing editorial resources and coordinating work-flows, which resulted in increased efficiencies for the team and allowed us to increase our workload with each deliverable.

  • Assistant Director of PBI/Publications Attorney; Program Attorney

    Pennsylvania Bar Institute

    (Non-Profit; 51-200 employees; Law Practice industry)

    June 1999 — August 2001 (2 years 3 months)

    As an assistant director, I advised PBI’s board of directors, executive director, and other staff on best practices for publication and continuing legal education development, production and delivery.
    As a publications attorney, I contributed to the successful publication of all PBI Press titles, including new books, new editions of existing books and supplements; edited manuscripts by PBI Press authors; composed advertising copy and attended conferences to promote PBI Press; and helped the PBI Press Director launch the PBI Press online bookstore and manage the PBI Press budget, staff and other departmental resources.
    As a program attorney, I helped launch PBI’s online continuing legal education programs; managed the seminar planning process to produce high quality courses; edited seminar manual manuscripts; and collaborated with the marketing director and PBI's authors to develop copy for letters, flyers and catalogs promoting PBI seminars and conferences.
