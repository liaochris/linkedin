    The Royal Veterinary College

    (Educational Institution; Veterinary industry)

    January 2005 — October 2007 (2 years 10 months)

    The Royal Veterinary College is the largest veterinary school in the UK. The College has about 1000 students, 450 staff and a turnover of approx £29m a year.

    I drive the team in the design, development and architecture of a hospital electronic patient record (EPR) system. The College has three specialist hospitals which are managed from paper records. The EPR project’s remit is to replace all hospital and patient management paper processes with fully integrated SQL 2005 database and C# 2.0 Winform applications.

    The EPR project design adopts an Object Oriented approach modeled using UML notations such as Data Flows and Class diagrams.

    As the architect of this new EPR system I am intrinsically involved with the full development lifecycle (Business requirements, Development, Testing, Implementation and training) and the full project lifecycle (Risk assessment, budgets and key stakeholder liaisons).

    Quality is ensured through an XP development methodology.
