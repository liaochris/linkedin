Gregory Moulinet’s Summary

French national with residence in Japan, Greg travels regularly between Tokyo, New York, Miami and Paris.

He is an achieved brand designer with more than 20 years of experiences in art direction, visual identity, advertising, digital animation, digital architectural simulation, storyboarding and prototyping.

At 16 years old, Greg became a freelance graphist and illustrator, earning as much as an adult, experienced designer. While successfully demonstrating a complete ineptitude to study in public schools, Greg joined the Grenoble Art University from which he graduated "Magna Cum Laude" in Contemporary Arts studies, 3 years later.

At age 20, winning a private grant from Wheelabrator, Greg discovered Japan. During this trip he proposed to Nintendo a digital animation for the Game Boy console, that was produced and exhibited worldwide. After a year in France at Villa Arson International art university (Nice), Greg returned to Japan to work with Art Knight Inc. and Vantan Design Institute. Until 1999, Greg worked on accounts such as Recruit, SAP Japan, J League, Advantest, Just System, PriceWaterhouse, JRC, Fuji TV, NTT, Tokyo Disneyland, the Japan Tourism Ministry and collaborated on major exhibitions/events with Dentsu, Hakuhodo & Atamatote International.

Greg worked with Imagence on medical/scientific accounts such as Bayer, Wyeth Lederle, Biocodex and Astra Zeneca. He then moved to New York city in 2000, creating the Times Square TDK logo animation, storyboarding sequences for movies such as Technolust, designed for Webster Hall (historic NYC nightclub) and the United Nation.

After 9/11 while in NYC, Greg created an online corporate identity service morphing into NOMADESIGN.jp 'the Mark Maker'. Since then Greg has worked on more than 600 identities for more than 25 different countries. In 2006 Greg and his wife Yoko founded NOMADESIGN Inc. in Tokyo, merging their nomadic lifestyle and creative talents.
