Peter B. Lee’s Summary

I have been working within the sales profession for almost 10 years, starting with my first position immediately after my graduation from the University of Waterloo. I have had the privilege of representing very large and complex organizations such as Oracle but also smaller, growth oriented startups such PlateSpin Ltd.
As I look back over my relatively young career, I am amazed at much I have managed to learn and look forward to another 10 years of similar growth and experience. I will continually learn, for I am always a student and I will continually teach, for I am forever a teacher.

My professional sales goal is to continually grow as a sales representative in all aspects of sales, including but not limited to prospecting, building relationships, and closing large complex deals. Many industry professionals, and recruiters suggest that all sales people motivated by money and only money. But I disagree strongly with this particular statement. In my experience, truly great and inspirational salespeople feel passionate about sharing their services and value proposition with customer who will in turn do great things with those same services. Money is simply a reward for work well done, not the all the end goal.

My passion lies within technology, and using technology to create new avenues for human kind to share and express themselves. My end goal is to lead a team of creative and talented individuals to create such avenues.
