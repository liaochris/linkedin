Karen Frizzell, MA, MLS’s Summary

I am a professional librarian with experience providing reference services to students and faculty in an academic library. I also have experience managing a museum library and creating exhibits utilizing a collection of rare books, documents, valuable coins, photographs, and other historic artifacts. I currently oversee the daily operations of a photo archive of over 250,000 historic photographs. Recently, I managed the Curatorial Department for three months while the Museum searched for a new curator, during which time I initiated two digital projects to enhance the visibility of two major photo collections. Working with digital collections in a photo archive serves my professional interests and skills perfectly.

Karen Frizzell, MA, MLS’s Specialties:

In addition to my experience with museums and libraries, I have experience with project management, professional writing, and photography. I am also a certified grip through the Iowa Motion Picture Association and have worked as a Production Assistant in 2008.

I spent 4 weeks studying literature at Trinity College in Dublin and focused my thesis studies on contemporary Irish women's poetry, particulary the poetry of Paula Meehan. My thesis was divided into three sections, and I crafted each section into three separate conference papers, which I delivered in 2004 at: the University of Missouri in Columbia; The American Conference for Irish Studies at Emory University; and the International Association for the Study of Irish Literatures at the National University of Ireland, Galway, Ireland.
