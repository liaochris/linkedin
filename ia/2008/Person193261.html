Taka Torimoto’s Summary

The challenge of limited memory and processing power, as well as its ability to "become anything" because of its integrated and compact architecture attracted Taka to the world of embedded systems.
He is always interested in opportunities where his passion for embedded development can be utilized towards the architecting and development of high-quality embedded products, and help accelerate its time to market.
A self-starter with a love for the latest gadgets and technology, Taka also does freelance work doing video production, computer graphics/animation, and web development (in PHP & mySQL).
Besides programming and video production, his hobbies and interests include soccer, tennis, ping-pong, golf, skiing, and snowboarding.

Taka Torimoto’s Education

  • Georgia Institute of Technology

    Bachelor's, Electrical Engineering, 1991 — 1996

    Taka became interested in Engineering in elementary school, when he became fascinated with toy robot kits in Japan (OWI MOVIT kits and Tomy Omnibots) and Heathkit HEROs. His older brother started teaching him BASIC programming on a Panasonic JR-200 (6802) when Taka was in 5th grade. During high school, his interests veered towards designing computer motherboards and decided to go into Electrical Engineering. In his Junior year at Georgia Tech, he took an Embedded Systems course and fell in love with it because of its software development challenges from limited processing power and memory as well as its ability to control low-level hardware via register/bit twiddling.

    Tech Television Network (video production club) and Georgia Tech Cable Network (created animated graphics and assisted with on-campus cable shows, eventually editing/producing my own TV show, tTV)
