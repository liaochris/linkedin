Kennedy Grey’s Summary

The first time I ever made money writing was during college in the heart of Vermont. This prince from Kuwait was wildly wealthy, and pretty much there to party and meet girls. So being from a poor family and completely self supported, I took it upon myself to become a subcontractor of this generous young prince's school papers--and he paid me to write pretty much everything he turned in. This was some of my finest advertising work to date, ironically, because i was tasked with making Kuwaiti-flavored mistakes and religious/politically-believable positioning. He got better grades than I did.
The first official paychecks were as a photo-journalist in New England at Cape Cod Newspaper Group, where i wrote every piece from front to back.
As a masterstroke of shameless self-promotion, I somehow convinced editors at national magazines to buy articles from me using only the my weekly newspaper writing and crafty cover letters.
The advancing digital sector created a need for writers who knew technology, and i began getting assignments from magazines including WIRED, DV, MovieMaker, Red Herring, MacWorld, Film & Video, dozens of others.
This attraction to the digital world helped me switch tracks to advertising, beginning in 1999 at the Boston headquarters of Digitas, now a part of Publicis/Leo Burnett. Two years there was followed by 8 more years of ad agency work for Modem Media, Wunderman/Y&R, Organic, Arc Worldwide/Publicis and others leading up to working as the copy/content lead at Digital Pond in San Francisco on Nike direct work as well as freelance broadcast creative for Ubisoft for X-Box games including Far Cry II, Brothers in Arms: Hells Highway, and others.
