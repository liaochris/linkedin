Timothy Langley’s Summary

Formerly General Counsel to Apple Computer (Japan), General Motors (Japan); Far East Trade & Investment Director for the US Commonwealth of Kentucky; first non-Japanese inside the Japanese Parliament as aide to a Member of the Upper House and Foreign Minister; published author of Japanese politics / business (in Japanese). Georgetown University Foreign Service & JD, Tohoku University Law, University of Denver LLM.

Timothy Langley’s Specialties:

Guide and fulfill provision of legal and legal-like services for satisfying requirements for the Japanese corporate/regulatory environment as well as those expected from US headquarters. Active in the realm of government relations (oil, gas & energy, environmental, pharmaceuticals, high-tech, space, trade & investment). Active in certifying NGO and NPO organizations to account for new rules governing non-profit organizations in Japan.

  • General Counsel & Director of Public Policy

    General Motors Asia Pacific (Japan)

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; GM; Logistics and Supply Chain industry)

    Assist GM emerge in Japan as a contender to Toyota; facilitate membership in several organizations including joining JAMA as the first foreign auto manufacturer; establish GM as serious player in Japan's tough auto-market by developing political clout; aid the company in integrating relationships with Fuji Heavy, Suzuki Motors, Isuzu. As General Counsel, manage the various legal issues ordinarly confronting large foreign companies in Japan as well as the one-off larger projects; manage foreign and local lawyers and law firms for the provision of legal services to the corporation. Assist senior management in Japan, report regularly to regional as well as world headquarters.

  • General Counsel & Director of Public Policy

    Apple Computer (Japan)

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Apple; Computer Hardware industry)

    September 1992 — August 1995 (3 years)

    Establish the legal department; form and launch public policy strategy to address Apple's emerging position in the Japanese market. First Apple attorney in Asia, very active with the Government Relations division working with CEO Sculley. As one component, established the first Diet DoorKnock initiative, formed under the auspicies of the American Chamber of Commerce in Japan (ACCJ) to allow US business-people to directly lobby Japanese politicans. Managed legal issues confronted by Apple as it began to gain market-share from .7% to roughly 8% during tenure.
