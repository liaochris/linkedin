Evan Beigel’s Summary

In 1997, Evan began building his commercial recording facility from the ground up on a 3000 square foot, dilapidated, industrial building site. After its completion, Evan began recording an eclectic variety of independent artists. Evan’s reputation grew more popular as he had developed a pure and detailed approach to production and engineering while acquiring a keen sense of intuition with his mixing skills. By the year 2000, Evan’s facility featured two fully operational recording studios and attracted a talented group of Singer/Songwriters and Bands. In 2001, Evan formed Jojo Ocean Music-a cutting-edge, production company dedicated to producing records for developing artists, creating scores for feature films, and custom music libraries for film and television. During this time, Evan would work with a variety of projects with artists including award-winning inventor, Ray Kurzweil, Badi Assad, Mr. Big, and Eight Stops 7 while composing for several commercials (Ford, Budweiser, Nicorette), feature films (Death’s Door, Camp Stories), television projects (X-Men, Playboy), and Music Libraries (De Wolfe Music UK, Killer Tracks). In 2005, Evan was contracted as Producer, Music Director, and Director of A&R by Blue Fish Entertainment-an independent record label and Production Company. After producing the acclaimed country band, Merchants of Moonshine in 2006, Evan completed the production of ‘Who Is Mandy Moon?’ by Ambrose released in the fall of 2007. Last month, Evan completed his third feature film score for the upcoming romantic comedy, ‘Dear Me’. Currently, Evan is producing the second album for British Singer/Songwriter, Alex Troup and composing a new CD for DeWolfe Music, UK.
