Jeffrey Ullman’s Summary

First half of my adult work life: Struggle against the stigma of ISO (In Search Of...one's other romantic half).

In the first decade of the 21st Century, it's conventional and normal to see one's mate (of any kind, for any reason) online. But.....back in 1976? Using the "Way Back Machine"...then, it seemed to some that people who joined Great Expectations video dating, were thought by some others as socially inept or romantically-challenged (In other words: Do not touch them with a 10-foot pole.) We were the pioneers of new courtship rituals; the ones who placed a greater priority on actually living life with the one we loved. We were okay with letting others complain about their frustration with spending too many personal resources looking for the right person. The silly logic of the frustrated was: "No one of quality who sincerely desires a quality relationship would meet THAT way," Oh yeah? Tell that to the 25,000 couples who met & married through through my several creations: Founder/President, Great Expectations; (even the ahead-of-its-time Greater Relations Worldwide); Co-creator: Love Connections (12 seasons) and Taildaters (4 seasons); Author (The Singles Almanac; 12 Secrets for Helping Find Love & Commitment; The Workaholic's Lost Love Life).

I was honored to work with fine co-workers, franchisees, and vendors who helped make so many peoples' lives so romantically enriched.
