Fred Flores, CFA’s Summary

April 2007 to Present: Research, develop, and monitor global equity investment strategies within a fundamental, systematic framework.

Feb 2002 to Feb 2007 (5 years): Investment research, asset allocation and manager selection/oversight for a private foundation with $8 billion in assets. Experience with derivative strategies to port alpha, manage asset allocation, and manage risk from large single stock positions.

Jun 1995 to Feb 2002 (7 years): Senior product and project manager for Barra, Inc., a research and consulting firm specializing in investment decision-making tools for portfolio managers, securities analysts, pensions officers and other institutional investors. Developed institutional quality investment tools for measuring investment risk and performance, optimizing portfolios, and developing stock return expectations.

Jun 1991 to Jun 1995 (4 years): Client relationship officer for State Street Bank & Trust. Managed global custody, accounting, cash management, securities lending, and performance evaluation services for large public pension funds.

Oct 1988 to Sep 1990 (<2 years): Operations supervisor for Crum & Forster, a property/casualty insurer.

Fred Flores, CFA’s Specialties:

Investment analysis, strategic and tactical asset allocation, external manager selection and oversight (traditional, long-only and alternative strategies), quantitative modeling, capital market research, portable alpha, currency overlay, and covered call writing.
