Gilles Ciment’s Summary

Gilles Ciment is born March 7, 1962. After creating (in 1983) and heading the Paris Cinema Library, in 1991 he was appointed Project Leader at the Audiovisual Department of the French National Library, and entered in 1994 the Forum des Images. He joined the MK2 group in 1995 as Director of the Communication, before becoming the group’s Deputy Managing Director in charge of artistic direction, communication, marketing and human resources. In 1999, he moved to the Ocean Films group to produce Asian films (Wong Kar Wai's In the Mood for Love and 2046, Hou Hsiao Hsien's Millennium Mambo and Three Times). He also created the group's video edition company, which he directed (releasing 45 films on DVD and the first HD DVD in Europe), and managed the whole internet activity (official website, films sites, newsletter, professional extranet and online trade). He became independant in 2006 in order to develop personal projects through his own production company, Vous alez voir, starting with the production of I Travelled 9000 km To Give It To You, Wong Kar Wai's short film as a tribute to cinema.
Since December 2007, he is the General Manager of the Cité Internationale de la Bande Dessinée et de l'Image in Angoulême.

For more than 20 years, he has been thinking over cinema (mostly animated films), comics and cartoons, resulting in his collaboration on a number of publications (including "Positif" and "Neuivième Art") and books, and teaching at the Sorbonne University in Paris. He has also directed several books and contributed to numerous publications and exhibitions.
He is an expert at the Centre National du Cinéma, and also sits in various commissions.
He is also President of La Maison des Auteurs in Angoulême and founder member of OuBaPo.
For a feature script, he has been prize winner of Beaumarchais contest, and finalist of the Grand Prix du Meilleur Scénariste in 2004.
Knight of Arts and Letters, Gilles Ciment is married and father of two sons.

    Since the creation of the International Comics Festival in 1974, Angoulême has become the unrivalled capital of the 9th art.

    LA MAISON DES AUTEURS opened its doors in 2002 in order to provide positive support to image creators would like to spend time there as resident creators, with the following purposes in mind :
    - offering a working environment that encourages creativity, hosting authors with a view to completing a professional project,
    - acting as a showcase for creative achievements in the fields of comics, animated films and multimedia, through exhibitions and events,
    - providing a centre of technical and documentary resources,
    - providing a forum for meetings and exchanges,
    - helping to protect the author's status and to defend intellectual property rights.
