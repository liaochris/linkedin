Don Kirkland’s Summary

Professional ideas herder.

Started my games industry as a producer, but realised after a few years that my real passion is for herding gameplay ideas into a tight set and turning them into a game.

If I was given millions of dollars and carte blanche to make any game I wanted to, I'd subcontract the entire thing out to a different studio so I could enjoy the finished product.

I make games for other people.

And some random advice I've found:
* QA are the people who play your game time and time again. This is a valuable resource for more than just knowing about bugs.

* People join the game industry because they want to make games. That means they usually have lots of ideas about games. Beg and borrow what you can, but don't steal!

* Heavy design documents make great doorstops when nicely bound.

Don Kirkland’s Specialties:

I do: Technical/scripting, level design and implementation, game mechanics design, production pipeline design, game balancing and way too many hours spent bug-hunting.

I don't do: Creative Writing, Coming up with all the ideas on my own, Being quiet.
