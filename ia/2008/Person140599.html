Nick Lansley’s Summary

Story so far:

A Tesco since 1987 being given steeper and steeper challenges until I was asked in 1995 to "see what I could do with this thing called the Internet"!

A "founding father" of tesco.com, building web and downloadable applications to enable wine and grocery home shopping on narrowband (9600bps) telephone connections in 1996.

First web application built 1996 for grocery (www.tesco.co.uk) and was opened by IT Marketing Director Tim Mason and Bill Gates from Microsoft.

Managed grocery web development through to 2000, then took on research role to help tune out irregularities in the changing business and technical models in order to deliver a service better for customers, simpler for staff and frankly, cheaper for Tesco.com - work that continues apace to this day!

My Goals:

1. Excite Tesco.com board with innovate solutions to business issues.
2. Put great new technology to work in a down-to-earth context - never technology for technology's sake.
3. Have Tesco.com board's trust in my consultative advice. Never veer from the total and honest truth.
4. Keep abreast of technology so that Tesco.com can come to me for advice on new technologies in the business and consumer space.
5. Keep the whole company motivated and excited about themselves and what they do by involving them in celebrating success (usually through video films I direct and conferencies & business parties I organise with my co-conspirator and company secretary Jenny Deans!).

Some words about me:

3. Motivated

6. Mountain biker

Nick Lansley’s Specialties:

  • Web Developer

    Tesco.com

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; TSCL; Supermarkets industry)

    September 1995 — May 1999 (3 years 9 months)

    Recruited as an internet/web developer to 'see what we could do with this this called the Internet'!

    Due to challenge of customers choosing 50+ grocery items from 20,000 using a 14.4kbps or 28.8kbps dialup-modem, I developed a CD-ROM based application called 'Tesco Home Shopper' that was sent to customers to install on their computers. Every week a compressed database of products was downloaded to keep prices/availability up to date. Customers could search and select products offline on their computer, only connecting to upload their order.

    In November 1996, launched web-based equivalent service using Microsoft software. Microsoft were so delighted that there was at last a company in the FTSE 100 using their software and operating systems that Bill Gates flew over to London and paid for the launch at the QE2 conference center in Westminster!

    From 1996 to 1999, time spend time improving and developing the service for customers and rolling it out to 200+ stores.
