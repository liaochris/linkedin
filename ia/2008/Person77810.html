Howard Liebeskind’s Summary

Dr Liebeskind has been team podiatrist to the U.S. Men’s and Women’s National soccer teams since 1991, serving the teams through seven World Cups. He currently sits on the Medical Advisory Board of the United States Soccer Federation and is a member of the American Academy of Podiatric Sports Medicine.

Liebeskind also serves as team podiatrist to CD Chivas USA and the Los Angeles Galaxy of Major League Soccer, Los Angeles Kings of the National Hockey League, Los Angeles Avengers of the Arena Football League, Los Angeles Riptide of Major League Lacrosse, and the Pepperdine University Athletic Department.

He has previously provided services to the UCLA Athletic Department and Los Angeles Clippers of the National Basketball Association.

Dr. Liebeskind is a graduate of Syracuse University where he received his Bachelors of Arts degree in 1973. He received his D.P.M. degree from the Illinois College of Podiatric Medicine in Chicago, Illinois in 1978. Dr. Liebeskind later completed his Residency in Podiatric Surgery at the Van Nuys Community Hospital.

Dr. Liebeskind has contributed to numerous sport related articles, journals and texts including “Soccer America” and “Biomechanics Magazine” He has also chaptered a segment in the textbook “International Football & Soccer Medicine: Caring for the Soccer Athlete Worldwide” published by the American Orthopedic Society for Sports Medicine.

Liebeskind has served the medical and technical advisory boards of Runners World Magazine and Tennis Magazines. His advice is regularly sought by the worlds largest athletic shoe fabricators for his extensive knowledge of biomechanical design concepts in athletic footgear. He was also recently featured in the cover article of the "American Podiatric Association News" a magazine distributed to members of the podiatric profession for his contributions to Sports Medicine in the United States.
