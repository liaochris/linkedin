Davide Cappelli’s Summary

I'm the result of 3 different assets: in family I've been "quietly" trained to management issues, at school I've been taught about philosophy and then (at university) about people mind, by myself I've learned to develop software. The sum, the synergy, of these 3 assets is me: I'm not the "classical" developer because I go for the user experience and needs (rather than the "machine"'s ones); I'm not the "classical" (proto-)psychologist because I go for the organizational point of view (rather than the individual's one); I'm not the "classical" manager because I'm "stuck" on the systemic/olistic perspective.
Therefore I can't be (even if it was less stressful for me) a "classical" human resource; my perfect assignment could be to lead a team or a group from a situation to another and then leave.
