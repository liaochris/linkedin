Vinicius Garcia’s Summary

Born in Rio de Janeiro - Brazil and live for 20 years in Salvador (Bahia), Vinicius is a member of the Brazilian Computer Society, and a senior member of the RiSE (Reuse in Software Engineering) Group, at the Federal University of Pernambuco. He received his B.Sc. in computer science from the Salvador University in 2001, and the M.Sc. in computer science from Federal University of São Carlos in 2005. He is currently a Ph.D. candidate at the Federal University of Pernambuco since 2005, and also a Systems Engineer at the Recife Center for Advanced Studies and Systems (C.E.S.A.R.) since 2005. Vinicius Garcia is (co-)author of over 30 referenced publications presented at conferences such as ECOOP, CBSE, ICSR and EUROMICRO, amongst others. He is currently involved in four research projects focused on the various reuse aspects, such as methods, processes and tools, more specifically in Software Reuse Maturity Models and Software Reuse Adoption in Software Development Process. In the reuse efforts, Vinicius Garcia has worked as researcher involved since the conception solutions until their deployment. At the Porto Digital, Vinicius Garcia in conjunction with the RiSE group has been the main vehicle to introduce reuse ideas in industrial and academic environment been responsible also to aid in the definition of the M.Sc. and Ph.D. courses focused on software reuse at the Federal University of Pernambuco and C.E.S.A.R, in conjunction with Dr. Almeida, Dr. Meira and M.Sc. Alvaro. In the last year, Vinicius Garcia was Visiting Researcher in a important group working with software reuse in the University of Mannheim (Colin Atkinson's Group). He has gained one award of the Brazilian Symposium on Software Engineering - Tools Session (2004). In addition he was one of the authors of the first open source book on software reuse around the world and has worked as independent consultant involved in training, mentoring, consultant and solution development focused on software reuse.
