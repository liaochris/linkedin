Marcel Sorger’s Summary

There're a lot of topics I'm interested in ranging from hardcore coding to psychology and management. I’ve studied these topics extensively in university, on the job and in other aspects of my life. This makes me an excellent analyst and developer.
My extensive knowledge of computer systems combined with my focus on long term technical as well as business effects make me ideally suited as an architect.
Effective communication skills and political insight help me keep my projects moving in the right direction. Seeing all sides of a problem is critical for creating the optimum solution.
Possessing creativity and down to earth logic enables me to come up with unconventional but practical solutions.

Professionally I am always trying to sharpen my skills and to learn new technologies.
I’m currently specialised in .NET development.
I believe the new .NET 3.5 features will revolutionise the development process.

For instance WCF makes it possible to configure your applications communication protocol after its release. Eliminating the need for an architectural choice or lessening the need for a flexible network design. It's a no brainer for a connected application.
An even bigger gain for dev process could be WPF and Silverlight. The speed and ease of WinForms development combined with the beauty and the ease of deployment of a website. Designers can work seamlessly together with programmers. Imagine a line of business application finally looking good and having great usability
Another interesting indication of its revolutionary nature is that it took 5 years to develop, witch is twice the planned development cycle (just like .NET originally), by both the Windows AND Explorer teams. It’s really meant to be a replacement for HTML and the Windows API.
Anybody using .NET without the new features is not serious about software quality. I’m not interested in projects that do not involve .NET 3.5. If your project uses .NET 3.5 feel free to send me an email.
