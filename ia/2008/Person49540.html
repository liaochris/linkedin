Demetrius Thompson’s Summary

Could the Global Mobile Alert hold the key to driving with distractions?

Demetrius Thompson of Global Mobile Alert Corporation of Los Angeles, developed and was issued a Patent on December 11, 2007, Patent No. 7,308,247 for the first mobile alerting system in the United States which uses a Global Positioning System (GPS) chip embedded in a cell phone. He came up with the idea when he was Seattle resident after being struck twice by drivers talking on cell phones.

Whenever a driver is talking on a mobile phone and approaches within 100 yards of a stoplight, the system emits a distinct intermittent chirp during the driver's conversation, alerting him or her to pay attention. The system is designed to help restore full concentration 7 to 10 seconds before reaching an intersection says inventor Demetrius Thompson.

Marketing consultant and legislative liaison for Thompson’s company, Randy Anderson of Marketing Enterprises in Bethesda, Md., said, “ I have been with the project for a decade. The reckless use of cell phones while driving is proving deadlier by the day; one young tenacious inventor is doing something about that. As a victim of a distracted driver and nearly losing his life, Demetrius Thompson is a driven entrepreneur whose motivation is to save lives. Every parent who has a love one with a driver’s license should be thinking of MyMobile Alert.”

Anderson further points out that, “Demetrius is determined to have his alert software in every cell phone that is installed in automobiles. He has it, it works, and it will save lives.”
