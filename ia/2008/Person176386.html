Simon Graham’s Experience

  • Technical Architect

    Morrisons Supermarkets

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Semiconductors industry)

    October 2007 — Present (1 year 1 month)

    Working within “Technical Services” on a large transformation programme I was heavily involved in designing the infrastructure as Morrisons seeks to migrate from a mainframe environment to Enterprise Unix with several Oracle COTS applications for finance, retail and human resources. During the planning and scoping phase I was responsible for the production of sizing and cost models, HA & DR strategies with input from systems integrators and service providers including HP, IBM, Oracle and WiPro.
    In addition I was deployed as a technical authority to several projects to ensure that service offerings were compliant with Morrison’s evolving standards to ensure that the services being offered are fit for current and future purposes whilst maintaining compliance with a non-existent enterprise architecture and supporting models.

  • Technical Design Authority

    Capita

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    July 2007 — September 2007 (3 months)

    I joined an established bid team part way through a due diligence phase of a very large managed service proposal becoming responsible for the data centres, their servers, the distributed server estate and the services that they supported.
    Analysing the available data led to me changing the approach for my responsibilities in consultation with the bid management team. The purpose of the change in approach was to quantify technical risk and minimise commercial risk by aligning the information towards service lines via the applications, their databases and the infrastructure they in turn relied on making any cost models more representative of the true costs to the business and exposing any shortcomings between SLAs and the reality of the systems underpinning these.
    In parallel I produced high level technical designs, an approach for migration from which I helped to produce detailed and justifiable costs for expected lifespan of the managed services proposal.

  • Programme Architect

    The Environment Agency

    (Government Agency; 10,001 or more employees; Government Administration industry)

    August 2006 — June 2007 (11 months)

    I joined as a Project Architect where I assessed technical options, reviewed existing and proposed solutions. I provided internal consultancy, undertook architecture governance and managed variances from standards. Detailed advice was also provided when required on diverse IT subjects for multiple, concurrent projects.
    The type and amount of contribution required varied according to the project’s maturity, the ability to deliver and the commercial aspects surrounding each project.
    As a Programme Architect attached to the Infrastructure Programme the functions and outputs are similar with the added need to coordinate the activities within multiple concurrent projects within the programme to ensure that maximum value is derived from the programme.
    Projects assigned to include strategic remote access, secure Internet access, web based sales of fishing licenses, wireless LAN, hosting, virtualisation, server rationalisation and data-centre capacity management.

  • Technical Architect

    EDS UK

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    July 2000 — July 2006 (6 years 1 month)

    I worked on multiple client accounts in government, defence & banking sectors; the largest with 160,000 desktops, 5000 servers & 2000 sites. For 6 years as a contractor I performed many roles; often concurrently, including Technical Design Authority, Consultant, Technical Specialist, Bid Support, Solution Designer, Process Design and Improvement, Project Manager and 3rd Level Technical Support.
    I was involved in many key client relationships including the client’s Desktop User Group, its Technology Office and the joint Infrastructure Management Board.
    I was requested by the EMEA Architects Office on several occasions to deliver some of their obligations due to my overarching knowledge. I was a member of the “Computer Council”; a board that acted as the ultimate technical authority.
    I was also involved in the creation of several teams for which I created the terms of reference, interviewed the candidates and helped establish key processes and relationships at their inception.
