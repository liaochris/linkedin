Shawn Walsh’s Summary

I am a well-rounded digital artist working steadily for more than 10 years in visual effects and 3D animation. I have most regularly been a "shots guy" working primarily in colour and lighting technical direction and compositing at the feature film level. I have used a great variety of software and techniques to achieve results and I happily shift gears all the time. The only constant in my work has been an organic trial and error accumulation of cinematic knowledge and an earnest desire to figure out "how to make something look cool." Imagining the non-existant has always driven me and continues to challenge me every day on the job. As a result of the broad range of work I have undertaken in my career, I have worked extensively in Character Animation, Editing, Lighting, Rendering, Compositing, Texture Painting and Surface Shading. I have recently begun a position as a Visual Effects Executive Producer at Image Engine in Vancouver. This has lead to a lot more focus on the management side of production, pipeline design, bidding, client relations, etc. however, I seem to never be far from the trenches and I am always ready to pick up a digital shovel!

Shawn Walsh’s Specialties:

I have been a Maya user since 2001 rendering with the Maya Renderer, Mental Ray, RenderMan & 3Delight. I have used primarily Shake and occasionally After Effects & NUKE as compositing software. I have five antiquated years experience using Softimage V2.8 - V.3.9, including Eddie. I have experience in UNIX/LINUX/NT o.s. environments. I maintain a reasonable understanding of shell scripting, MEL and (recently) python.

  • Lighting Technical Director

    Rhythm and Hues Studios

    (Privately Held; 501-1000 employees; Motion Pictures and Film industry)

    March 2005 — October 2005 (8 months)

    My position at Rhythm and Hues Studios was "Scene Lighter." In selected shots as required by feature film production, I was responsible for final 3D scene assembly incorporating the illumination and rendering of all 3D elements required to complete shots as well as pre-compositing to ensure quality and coherency of the elements generated. This position requires such traditional skills as a strong artistic eye for cinematography, composition, photography and high-resolution detail, as well as extensive understanding of the principles of digital film compositing such as multi-pass rendering for compositor based control. I was required to be comfortable working within a complex rendering pipeline, using an entirely proprietary tool set, and in a script driven environment.
