Barbara Cobuzzi’s Summary

As an Industrial Engineer and MBA by education and an experienced coder and biller, I provide a great deal of knowledge, not only about billing and compliantly optimizing income, but how to put processes in place to create the desired results over and over again. I bring my education and knowledge of this field together to my clients and provide what most in this field are devoid. We need systems, processes and procedures so that we can get the same results over and over again, not only for compliance, but for efficiency and optimized reimbursement. My understanding of the coding rules along with this background makes me a unique individual in this field.

Barbara Cobuzzi’s Specialties:

Coding, Otolaryngology Coding in particular although I can code in just about any specialty. I am a polished speaker and educator. I also have experience writing articles. Finally, I have experience and have been very successful for my clients as an expert witness including on the stand testimony.

  • Director of Outreach Programs

    American Academy of Professional Coders

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Hospital & Health Care industry)

    March 2006 — January 2008 (1 year 11 months)

    The Amereican Academy of Professional Coders (AAPC) is a membership organization representing professional coders, those who translate the services physicians provide for their patients into codes as well as descriptors defining what is wrong with the patients into another set of codes. This profession is quite complex and fraught with many rules and compliance dictates from the federal and state government. The AAPC provides a certification exam which certifies those who work in this industry in basic coding as well as provide specialty certification to demonstrate expertise in various medical specialty coding. My position with the AAPC is with the eLearning division, assisting in development of courses, sales of programs to colleges for resale to their students, writing specialty exams and study guides (Otolaryngology) and providing Outreach for the membership (over 60,000 of which about 2/3 are certified).

We offered coding, appeals, consulting, compliance, and consulting in addition to our full service billing services. While president of the company, I was sought out on a regular basis as a speaker for professsional organizations, state medical societies and specialties, as well as private practices. I also consulted for CMS as well as wrote for the AMA Physician Advocacy Department.
