Ed Quazi’s Summary

A Successful Biotech Industry professional with greater than 10 years experience in various management roles within Pharma R&D, Biotech Product Development, and Sales and Marketing in a multinational environment.

A BSc (Hons), PhD education at Kings College, University of London was followed by R&D for the drug/vaccine pipeline at the blue chip pharma company, Chiron (now part of Novartis) at their major site in Siena, Italy.

On moving back to the UK, Business Development & Sales and Product Development roles were performed in a number of companies, namely, Biogene Ltd, Eurogentec Ltd, MWG and Transgenomic Inc.

Currently at Sidec.

  • EU Marie Curie Research Fellow

    The Research Centre, Chiron Vaccines, Siena, Italy

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; Pharmaceuticals industry)

    1996 — 1999 (3 years)

    Employed by Chiron directly and then aided by an EU Fellowship, a Post-Doctoral Research period was spent in Siena, Italy. Research involved designing a transgenic mouse for two variants of the green fluorescent protein (GFP) gene and binding sites specific for T helper cell 1 (Th1) or T helper cell 2 types (Th2). This mouse would be then instrumental in deciding whether a specific infection is a Th1 or a Th2 type infection. Vaccines can then be made specifically for that type of an infection by making them interact with the respective cell type. In addition to this I made contributions in understanding the development of a disease caused by a bacteria Helicobacter Pylori and this work was published with me as a joint author with my collaborators at the Immunology Department of the University of Florence.
