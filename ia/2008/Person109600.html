Kurt Carlsen’s Summary

Co-founded Redmap Networks in 1996.

Redmap was one of Australia's first entrants to the information management industry. 13 years on we are still committed to producing quality products and are continuing to build our business.

Starting out as a scanning bureau in the early 90s, the opportunities that lay in developing simple applications for the SMB market quickly became evident. After selling the scanning bureau to Brambles Recall in 1996, the directors decided to embrace the challenge and pursue the opportunities they saw in offering a quality product to the emerging information management industry.

As both the business and software have matured, the company’s strengths and the product’s high standards have been recognised in many arenas. Most notably, Redmap was awarded the Queensland State Premiers ICT Exporter of the Year award for three consecutive years (’02 through to ’05) and the Australian National ICT Exporter of the Year award in 2004. These successes have seen a number of the Redmap directors on advisory boards for the Federal Department of Trade, Austrade, and the Australian Information Industry Bureau.

The focus on simple yet powerful software has not only underpinned the success of the business, but has also made it attractive as an OEM provider. Over the last few years Redmap has delivered a number of OEMs, most recently the Re-Rite product produced for Toshiba, which is shipped worldwide with their multi-function printers.

In the last three years Redmap has enjoyed rapid expansion, opening offices in the USA, Hong Kong and the Philippines, as the demand for our software applications continues to grow.
