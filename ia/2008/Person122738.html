Mark Torrance’s Summary

Currently running Vinq, a software development consulting company which leverages open source software to accelerate time-to-market and agile development for our customers. We employ information architects, graphic designers, software and database engineers, and project and product managers who work together to achieve great things for our clients.

Mark Torrance’s Specialties:

Software Development leveraging open source, Ruby on Rails, Perl, PHP, Java, HTML, Apache, MySQL, web applications, email, server architecture, graphic design, user experience, contextual design, Nielsen-Norman interaction design

Mark Torrance’s Experience

  • CEO

    Vinq

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry)

    April 2003 — Present (5 years 6 months)

    Vinq is an internet software development consultancy specializing in high traffic customer-facing web sites. Our principal client to date is Fannie Mae Foundation, based in Washington, D.C., for whom we have developed knowledgeplex.org, dataplace.org, fanniemaefoundation.org, homebuyingguide.org, and helpthehomelessdc.org covering all of their public online initiatives. We also do work for Robert Wood Johnson Foundation, SRI International, and other clients.

  • Information Architecture Consultant

    Fannie Mae Foundation

    (Non-Profit; 51-200 employees; Internet industry)

    January 2003 — April 2003 (4 months)

    Prior to founding Vinq, Mark worked as an independent consultant for Fannie Mae Foundation to develop a working prototype for a new version of their KnowledgePlex web portal in support of the affordable housing and community development industry. This prototype application proved to be more scalable, had faster page-load times, and delivered a more compelling user experience than the existing Plumtree portal which it replaced. The core of this application is still in use today as part of the KnowledgePlex content management framework, now offered by KnowledgePlex, Inc., a nonprofit spinoff of Fannie Mae Foundation.

  • Chief Technology Officer

    Knowmadic

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    January 2001 — January 2003 (2 years 1 month)

    At Knowmadic, Mark provided technical leadership and guidance, contributed to the creation of several new products, and assisted the company to secure venture financing from Hummer Winblad and 3i. Mark conceived and prototyped in Java a new business process workflow application that incorporated Knowmadic's core web scraping technology as a component. He developed new multistrategy approaches to web scraping that increased reliability of the product. And he designed and implemented Knowmadic OnTap, which included client-server software to provide real-time data feeds that dynamically update cells within a Microsoft Excel workbook.

  • Chief Scientist

    Red Herring Magazine

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Internet industry)

    June 2000 — January 2001 (8 months)

  • CEO and Founder

    StockMaster.com

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Internet industry)

    August 1995 — June 2000 (4 years 11 months)

    Founded StockMaster.com in August, 1995, after creating it at MIT as the first financial service on the Web in 1993. Grew the company, with revenues from a blend of online advertising sold by Doubleclick and investor relations web services sold by a direct telephone sales force. At the peak StockMaster had over 1 million unique monthly visitors, and 50 million monthly page-views. Sold to Red Herring Magazine in March, 2000.

  • Research Staff Intern

    Sun Microsystems

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; SUNW; Computer Hardware industry)

    June 1994 — April 1996 (1 year 11 months)

    Worked with Bill Woods in the Conceptual Indexing group. Developed Active Notebook, a very early proxy-based bookmark system that leveraged Bill's LUNAR ontology to group your favorites and bookmarks into collections based on how you manually categorized them with "tags". This work was published at an AAAI Spring Symposium.

  • Research Staff Intern

    Mitsubishi Electric Research Labs

    (Public Company; 501-1000 employees; Computer Software industry)

    June 1992 — September 1992 (4 months)

    Worked with Charles Rich on simulation of autonomous agents, writing software to tie together a dynamic robot simulation developed by Marc Raibert of MIT, and an emotion simulation system developed by Joseph Bates of CMU. The system I built this summer was capable of learning sequences of instructions delivered in spoken English, and additionally exhibited manic/depressive behavior depending on the frequency and enthusiasm of your interaction with the virtual robot. The goal of this project at MERL was to develop remote-learning systems and agents that could populate virtual worlds to help students with distance learning.
