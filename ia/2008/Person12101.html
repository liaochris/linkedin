Andrianna Jobin’s Summary

Instructional technologist and trainer experienced in designing media to communicate across a broad variety of cultures. Substantial experience targetting media and training non-native English speakers. Direct experience training colleagues, faculty, and teachers. Interested in instructional technology and training opportunities.

Also a certified English language instructor specializing in adult and young adult learning styles. Emphasis on time-shifted communication, peformance based training (PBT), and project/problem based learning (PBL) models. Offer dual-mode (distance and on-site) courses in American idioms and culture, intercultural communication, and conversation management.

Andrianna Jobin’s Specialties:

I. Instructional systems design: ISD models (ADDIE, rapid prototyping, Dick & Carey, ARCS) and ID approaches (behaviorism, cognitivism, constructivism)
II. Web tools: CMS/courseware (Blackboard), communication (blogs, podcasts, forums), and collaboration (wikis, groups, web 2.0 communities)
III. Knowledge acquisition and memory: adult language acquisition, vocabulary acquisition, memory encoding and retrieval strategies
IV. Training: Adult and adolescent learning styles, PBT, PBL models
