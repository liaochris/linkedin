Dave Butler’s Summary

For over 20 years, my career has centered on solving various application problems through the utilization of engineering materials. The focus has been not only in the industrial sector but also for commercial and military and light industries as well. I have represented several Fortune 500 companies developing business and interest for several applied materials including carbon graphite, solid ceramics, powder metal. carbides, polyimides and a host of ferrous and non-ferrous oxide coatings. (Including thermal spray, PVD, CVD, EPVD and PEO processes) I have several years of domestic and international experience and am seeking employment that will afford me the opportunity to utilize skills and broaden my experience.

Some highlights of my career include the development of a long-term government contract for the Mark 66 missile program which resulted in annual contracts in excess of $4 million USD/year for it’s duration. I have also been heavily involved in several gas turbine engine programs, oil & gas development projects, wire, textile, motor sports, laser, semiconductor, power generation and most other heavy industries.

Presently, I am doing consulting work and am working with some materials and coatings and thermal spray principals. My focus for them is business development.

Most recently, I held a part ownership share in CCTI (Ceramic Coatings Technologies Inc.) where I acted as Director of Business Development. Our charter was to create a production facility and develop applications for an emerging coating technology. (microarc oxidation) The project involved raising venture capital, developing the process techniques and creating a customer base and sales group to grow the business.

I plan to continue my path in the materials arena. It affords me the challenge to develop new relationships and assist in solving tribological problems with technology.
