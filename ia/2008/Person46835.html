  • Mechanical Engineer, Founder & Host

    Nashua After-Hours Networking (Sole Proprietorship)

    (Sole Proprietorship; 51-200 employees; Civic & Social Organization industry)

    June 2006 — Present (2 years 1 month)

    Nashua After-Hours Networking (NAHN) is a networking group I founded based in Nashua, New Hampshire. We meet in the evenings, twice monthly. It's an informal group, devoted to building the connections that help people find work. Our foundation principles are:

    1. The best time to develop your network is when you are employed and not desperate
    2. You never know whose “coworker’s neighbor’s poker buddy” is that one person you need to meet; don’t ignore a contact simply because they’re not in the “right” industry or profession
    3. If you want people to help you, you need to help them; in networking, truly, what goes around, comes around
    4. Lastly, having a network requires active care; stay in occasional touch with everyone, share information or articles to keep you in the back of peoples’ minds – someone may know of a perfect opening and be willing to help you get that inside referral
