Kay Bell’s Summary

S. Kay Bell is the principal of Austin, Texas-based SKB Editorial Services LLC, where she provides writing, editing and consulting services to a wide-ranging clientèle.

Kay is a proven professional in both journalism and corporate communications. Her creative career includes almost two decades in Washington, D.C., as Congressional staff as well as in the private sector, and several years before and since her D.C. adventure writing for mass-circulation publications and Web sites.

Kay's forte: Translating difficult subject matter into lively, readable copy.

Kay's specialty: Personal finance, with a keen eye on taxes.

Kay's passion: Helping people attain a better understanding of how to make, save and wisely spend their money.

Her clients range from respected mutual fund companies to national trade associations to local private publishers. In addition, during the federal tax-filing season, Kay writes copy for, edits and manages the tax component of Bankrate.com, a pre-eminent personal finance Web site. The Bankrate job is a natural for Kay who, for five-plus years, was in-house tax writer/reporter/editor for the Web site's tax channel, which she helped launch in October 1999.

Kay also is experienced and comfortable writing TV/radio copy. While at Bankrate, she provided regular reports on tax and other personal finance topics for AP Radio and appeared on-air for expert interviews, live and taped, on various broadcast outlets. Most recently, she was interviewed by the NPR program Marketplace on tax responsibilities of prize winners.

A collection of Kay's Bankrate stories on the taxation of gambling winnings also is included in the book, "The Gambler's Guide to Taxes: How to Keep More of What You Win" (Kensington Publishing, 2003).

In her free time, Kay maintains her blog, Don't Mess With Taxes, which has been named one of the best tax sites in the blogosphere. She also writes the Bankrate.com tax blog, Eye on the IRS.

Kay Bell’s Specialties:

In November 2006, Kay was named to the Taxpayer Advocacy Panel (TAP), a group of volunteers dedicated to helping the IRS identify ways to improve customer service and satisfaction. TAP is a Federal Advisory Committee established under the authority of the Department of the Treasury.

Kay also has conducted workshops on improving business writing skills for the annual conference (2006 and 2007) of the Executive Women in Texas Government.
