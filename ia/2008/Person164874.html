Ross Rayners (ross@saintmoonriver.com)’s Summary

There is only one goal for me and that is to produce movies. I knew this upon the first movie ‘Lethal Weapon’ I laid my eyes upon. In the years between then and now, even though I haven’t been involved in the industry, every job, every experience was geared towards movies. I would consciously con myself to belief that no matter what job I performed, I was gaining experience enabling me to make my aspiration of producing come true. Every opportunity in the industry I have had was due to hard work, dedication and a definiteness of purpose. True success never comes quickly, it takes hard work, a price need to be paid and a sacrifice need to be made.

For the better of 2006 I was an unpaid knowledge worker. At the height of desperation in November I went for an interview in Parklands, Table View for some call centre yearn. Sitting in a taxi, after the interview, driving past Table Mountain Motion Picture Studios, a red and blue neon light thumped me on my head. In that second my dream, my goal, my life flashed through my head. I had an epiphany ‘I have to work there’ raced through my head. In no time at all, two days actually, I managed to secure an interview with the CEO the following Monday. I started to work the Tuesday. The arrangement was that I work for free on a trail basis. On Thursday of the same week I needed to work late but traveling was going to be a problem. Before I could finish my sentence the CEO said ‘Take the company car’. As fate would have the Studio Liaison fell ill thus I started doing her work. Impressed with my work ethic and ability, the CEO, recommended that I speak to Emelia Weavind, Set Decorator on 10 000BC. On the spot I was hired. And as they say in the movies the rest is history.

"When the voice and the vision on the inside is more loud and clear than the opinions on the outside, you've begun to master your Life." Dr John F. Demartini
