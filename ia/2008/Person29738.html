Matt Caemmerer’s Summary

I'm an information systems guy. Sometimes this has leaned more toward education. Sometimes t has leaned more toward communication. But information has been the constant focus and expertise.

Through my experience, one job / life skill that most seem to struggle with and I seem to excel at is the process of dynamic definition. Providing working definitions for processes that are constantly in flux - providing structure to ill-defined situations - adding clarity to new strategies / projects / entities.

--------------------------------------

DYNAMIC DEFINITION: Enabling adaptive definitions.

I believe that the process of Dynamic Definition is a skill that will become increasingly necessary and one that I have worked hard to understand and hone over the years.

The need to define things is prevalent in the work environment. Business plans, budgets, systems analysis, school curricula, among many others are all deliverables resulting from a process of definition. The complexity comes in the fact that the Internet and other IT systems accelerate information sharing and information discovery which can cloud the process of definition. Most definition is static. Most teams are dynamic.

My career has focused on expanding my experience with Dynamic Definition

-Information: Masters and PhD-ABD in Information Systems. Been a CIO. I've led the development of software, web services, and business channels.

-Communications: Marketing professional. Registered communications coach. I'm fluent in technology, sales/marketing, and finance.

-Education: Taught every grade from 1st through PhD

-Enterprise: Start-up project executive. Fortune 500 project executive. Information, entertainment, education, manufacturing, telecommunications, professional service, and utility industry experience.

Do you have a story of how definitions have helped you, frustrated you, or compromised you. Send me an email - tell me your story.
