Philip Fierlinger’s Summary

These are the main things that I obsess about on a regular basis:

* How to create designs and interactive experiences where people keep saying to themselves "damn this is good"

* Making sure that whatever I deliver works effortlessly, solidly and dependably

Throughout my career I have worked hard to push the boundaries of interactive design while remaining obsessed with providing the most gratifying user experience.

I work across the entire lifespan of a project: from requirements gathering, to strategy and concept development, through prototyping and user testing, into design and coding, all the way through to user acceptance testing and documentation.

  • Owner / Director

    I co-founded Turntable with my brother in 1994.

  • User Experience Strategist, Senior Designer, Flash Designer/Developer

    Shift (www.shift.co.nz)

    (Online Media industry)

    January 2002 — June 2006 (4 years 6 months)

    At Shift, I helped define the online strategies and produced major deliverables for key clients such as Tourism New Zealand (www.newzealand.com), New Zealand Now, TeAra.com, National Library, IRD, TEC, Housing New Zealand, NZ Cricket, among numerous others.

    My strategy, design, and coding work on newzealand.com helped Shift win a Webby in 2005, 2006, plus a nomination for best homepage in 2005, and another nomination this year.

  • Design Intern

    General Magic

    (Telecommunications industry)

    January 1992 — June 1992 (6 months)

    I was the first intern at General Magic, an early PDA developer founded by the legends of Apple: Andy Hertzfeld, Jim Atkinson, and Susan Kare. I worked on product development concepts for 3rd generation devices - smart phones, which we projected would take 5-10 years to hit the market. I was pretty surprised that it took so long.

    I got to work with some remarkable people while I was there: Megan Smith, Tony Fadell, Steve Perlman, Scott Knaster, Scott Collins, Kevin Lynch, and heaps of other incredibly talented people that have gone on to do some amazing work.
