Jenny G. Bowen’s Summary

Born into a theatrical home, I have extensive knowledge of arts and culture and my undergraduate thesis was incorporating multi-media & digital arts into the theatrical experience. My dual major was in US History with emphasis in Public Policy. While in college I was engaged in extensive public policy roles through interning in the US Congress and being elected as the Student Body Vice President and President of my University. I also studied documentary film and photography extensively in my liberal arts background.

Post graduation I enrolled in the AmeriCorps VISTA volunteer program. I worked in Reading, PA tutoring students in English, Math, and creating an after school arts community. My time in the program was cut short due to a poor decision of purchasing alcohol for a 20 year old friend at the time. My position was then terminated and I was prosecuted to the fullest extent in the state of Pennsylvania. Due to losing my position I also lost my housing, but I was confined to the city of bail. I consider my time being homeless in one of the most dangerous, crime-ridden and impoverished cities in America to be one of the most beneficial experiences in my life. Going through the confusing court system with an uncaring public defender and securing friendship with 'delinquents' and criminals taught me a great deal about humanity, myself, and some of the divided forces in America.

I returned to my hometown of Asheville where I have dedicated myself to the cause of community, culture and harmony. I have been working in arts management for three years and engage in many active roles throughout the city.
Right now I currently focused on a my community enrichment project and photo documentary: Faces of Asheville, which is in itself an inspiring story.
