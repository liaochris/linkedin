Richard Brubaker’s Summary

For the last 6 years, I have been living in China (Beijing and Shanghai). It has definitely been the most dynamic time of my life, and it is a place where I have found you really can do and be anything. I currently work on a number of projects that are for profit and non-profit ...

In the for profit sector, my specialty is macroeconomic growth within China's 2nd tier cities and logistics. I am working with real estate, manufacturing, and consumer brands to help establish their 2nd and 3rd investments in China and/ or assisting in the design of supply chains.

Through this, I am also assisting medium sized firms manage their China based production. My partner and I have a turn key process that encompasses the entire chain.. raw material sourcing, assembly, packaging, containerization, and final delivery

you can read all about this on my blog All Roads Lead to China.

On the Non-Profit side, I am the founder and Executive Volunteer of Hands On Shanghai (www.handsonshanghai.com) which volunteerism in Shanghai. Currently, we have between 10-15 projects a week at children's hospitals, elderly care centers, and local schools.

Branching off of that I am also involved with the general CSR community through my service at the American Chamber of Commerce (Vice chair of CSR committee) and through another organization I established called Crossroads (www.china-crossroads.com).
