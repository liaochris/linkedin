Eric Edelson’s Summary

I am passionate about helping others and finding unique solutions to really big problems in healthcare.

I recently graduated from the Stanford Graduate School of Business ('07) and am committed to entrepreneurship in the medical device / health and wellness / consumer healthcare industry. My current focus is on physiologic footwear products for people with or who are predisposed to getting knee osteoarthritis, a severely debilitating disease that affects over 10 million in the U.S. I've been working with technology out of Stanford's biomotion lab where we have seen some fantastic clinical results with our footwear products. While at school I checked out a number of different industries - outdoor retail, physical medicine and rehabilitation, more venture capital - and I must say I have been blown away at how exciting this new area of footwear seems to be.

Before school I played around in the investing world while working for Lehman Brothers. I specialized in biopharmaceutical and medtech investing in both the private and public sectors. While investing is a fantastic profession, and one I one day hope to return to, in the midst of the job I realized that my true passion was working on entreprenurial ideas in small companies helping bring new, innovative technologies into the lives of people who need them most.

I almost forgot, I love to run and am always up for training and competing in crazy races.
