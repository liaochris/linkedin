Jan van Bon’s Summary

- Chief Editor of a wide range of professional publications in the field of IT (service) management, including the entire ITSM Library.
- Program Chair of several large conferences on IT management.
- Chief Editor of a network of web platforms (http://www.itsmportal.com).
- Co-initiator of institutes like ITSMF's NL chapter, the IT Service Fund, and the IT Governance Association.
- Vice Chair, Secretary and Endorsement Officer of ITSMF International's Publication Executive Sub Committee
- Chief Editor of ITSMF-NL
- Consultant & trainer in organizational improvement projects of service organizations

Goal: stimulate the development of communities of practice in IT Management.

  • Program Chair, Manager

    NITE - Northern IT E-community and Event

    (Partnership; 1-10 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    October 2007 — Present (9 months)

    The Northern IT Event is held once a year in October, starting in 2008. It offers an information exchange facility for everyone involved in IT and connected to the North of the Netherlands, Germany and countries around the Baltic Sea.
    The event is projected against the IT e-community of experts that is living an/or working in this area. It is the face-to-face meeting of the involved community.   • Office manager & co-initiator

    IT Service Fund

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    October 2001 — Present (6 years 9 months)

    Customer satisfaction is a better indicator of success than the perfection of products or services. This may explain the increasing attention in the IT industry for Service Management. However, the pace with which this idea gets accepted is still too slow. This has led a group of six IT companies to cooperate in founding the "IT Service Fund".

    The objective of the fund is to stimulate professional service management throughout the IT industry and to further the positive image of the IT industry. To this objective, the fund 1) regularly expresses visions on service management, and 2) financially supports initiatives that help realising the fund's objectives. This may be on widely different projects, for instance concerning methods and techniques, benchmarking, higher education, research, regulations, standardisation, community building, knowledge management, certification and globalisation.
