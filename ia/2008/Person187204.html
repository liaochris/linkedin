Margaret Nelson’s Summary

Resourceful all-round creative with strong communication skills. Experienced in education and public speaking, and used to taking a leadership role in statutory and voluntary bodies. Having worked with hundreds of bereaved people, preparing funerals, have a strong interest in recent social history and in social and familial relationships. Interested in helping individuals and organisations to achieve better understanding by expressing themselves clearly and unambiguously, or by speaking or writing on their behalf. Also interested in photographing people in natural situations and poses.

Margaret Nelson’s Specialties:

Some html, Word, Publisher, Digital SLR, Photoshop, illustration and design.

  • Administrator & Humanist Celebrant

    Suffolk Humanists

    (Non-Profit; Myself Only; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    1991 — Present (17 years)

    I'm administrator and spokesperson for a county Humanist group, which involves writing and editing a newsletter, maintaining a website, public speaking, broadcasting on local radio, and educational activities including being a tutor with Suffolk Inter-Faith Resource.

    I'm also a Humanist Celebrant (though after 17 years and over 1000 ceremonies, I only do a few by request), and co-ordinate the Ceremonies Team, dealing with enquiries from the public and from funeral directors. I mainly conduct funeral ceremonies, which involves interviewing bereaved people, preparing funeral scripts, officiating at funerals and other ceremonies, and providing help and advice for people who want to do a ceremony themselves.

    I design and publish all the group's leaflets and other publicity material.

    Have written advertising copy, newspaper, magazine and website articles, and many tributes (eulogies) for humanist funeral ceremonies. I've edited articles for magazines and newsletters. I also write short pieces for local radio.

  • Teacher

    Suffolk County Council

    (Government Agency; 10,001 or more employees; Government Administration industry)

    September 1982 — July 1986 (3 years 11 months)

    After a term in a failing Ipswich school, helping to put the art department back on track, I worked as a supply teacher around the county's middle and upper schools. Although I was trained as an art teacher I did a lot of work in a special needs department, covering for frequent staff absences, at the request of the head of department who was fussy about who worked with his vulnerable charges.

  • Freelance videographic designer

    BT

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; BT; Telecommunications industry)

    1986 — 1986 (less than a year)

    I designed videographics for a speculative project using German computer design software. The designs were meant for a phone with a small screen, like the French Minitel system. Since no one had translated the manual and I don't know much German, I had to work it out for myself. I was expected to produce some finished work within a few weeks, but managed to do so within a week. I was being paid an hourly rate - I should have worked slower! The project was eventually shelved - by then, the Internet was gaining popularity so the project was redundant.
