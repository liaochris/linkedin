  • Staff Research Assistant II

    Helen Wills Neuroscience Institute (UC Berkeley)

    (Educational Institution; 11-50 employees; Research industry)

    August 2007 — February 2008 (7 months)

    Aside from continuing previous work studying information flow and causal structure in neocortex, I'm also a core member of a team interested in understanding the brain mechanisms underlying state changes in conscious awareness, such as those occurring during meditation. Our current work focuses on EEG-based real-time tracking of degree of focused attention (a core component of most meditative practices) while isolating components of brain activity which can be 'fed back' to the user (biofeedback) to enhance user ability for sustained attention. I'm involved in developing paradigm for our EEG study, spectral analysis of data, multivariate statistics, single-trial classification, and writing C++ code for real-time data extraction from the BioSemi acquisition hardware and interfacing this with the MATLAB engine.

    Publications:
    Bastos, A.*, Mullen, T.*, Canolty, R., Pasley, B., Knight, R., Freeman, R. SSVEP-based Single-Trial Classification of Attention. Poster. Submitted, CNS '08

  • Undergraduate Research Assistant

    UCB Helen Wills Neuroscience Institute

    (Educational Institution; 11-50 employees; Research industry)

    March 2005 — August 2007 (2 years 6 months)

    Research Assistant in Dr. Robert Knight's Cognitive Neuroscience Lab.

    My early work involved assisting in statistical analysis and graphics for a project exploring the role of prefrontal-extrastriate preparatory attention networks in response errors.

    Co-authored poster and symposium talk presented at Society For Neuroscience Conference, Washington, DC, Nov 2005.

    More recent projects have involved exploring novel uses of information theory, graph theory, clustering analysis, and bivariate statistics to determine functional connectivity and information flow in the human cortex during language processing. Aside from developing and implementing the algorithms used to determine information flow networks, I wrote a visualization software package to aid in the analysis of complex information flow networks.

    Co-authored poster. Cognitive Neuroscience Society Conference, New York, May, 2007.
