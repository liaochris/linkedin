John Finn’s Summary

While being groomed at KPMG, I realized that fratlife of public accounting was no longer in my future. I parted ways amicably in May of 1995 and pursued a career in the film industry as a freelance accountant. after a few years, i formed JFA, Inc. to provide accounting services to the independent film and television producers. While toiling away at accounting, i saw a further niche of providing payroll services and founded Indiepay, Inc. in 2004.

In order to offer the payroll services, I first had to develop an industry standard accounting software which could then be offered to the producers to use. once this was developed and tested, Indiepay was open for business and we are currently in a growth period, as new producers learn about this new company.

I have worked on various projects from "Lost in Translation" to "Be Kind Rewind" to "Supersize Me".

Presently, both companies have a combined 23 employees.
