Herb Morreale’s Summary

I started my first company in my early twenties and achieved significant success. But equally important to my development as a leader are lessons I've learned through failure. Along the way, I've developed a deep belief that...

A company’s performance is directly tied to the organization's ability to improve capabilities in the areas of strategy, leadership, and execution. In each of these there is always room for improvement - Ineffectiveness in any area will hold back overall performance. Therefore, the role of senior management (regardless of position or title) is to continuously look at the interrelationship of strategy, leadership, and execution, then inspire and motivate others to make clear and decisive decisions that improve key strategic measures, ultimately leading to very high levels of sustainable growth.

Whether a founder, CEO, CTO, investor, consultant, or board member, my goal is to help organizations achieve significant growth. I'm drawn to working with teams and organizations with a strong "social glue" - where the culture offers every employee an opportunity to learn & grow, take pride in their work, live a balanced life, have fun, and be compensated fairly.

At this point in my career I'm particularly interested in "social businesses" - those that blend the best of for-profit and not-for-profit models.
