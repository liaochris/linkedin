whurley *’s Summary

I am currently Chief Architect for BMC Software’s Open Source Strategy and Chairman of The Open Management Consortium.

Prior to this I was the Chief Technology Officer at Qlusters, Inc. Prior to Qlusters, I was among the founders of Symbiot, the pioneering firm in the application of risk metrics to adaptive network security. I am also a founder of the OpenSIMS project, the world's first framework for collaborative "Community Centric Security." I am also a director of the Linux Top Gun and WNDC (World Network Defense Competition) hacking competitions. I have fifteen years' experience in the research and development of groundbreaking technology solutions, which have earned me a number of honors and awards, including IBM's Master Inventor title; an IBM Pervasive Computing Award; two 'Going for the Gold' Awards for innovation, from Apple Computer; and a 2004 Apple Design Award. I have authored numerous patents while working in the research and development labs of Fortune 500 companies including IBM, Tivoli Systems, and Apple Computer.
