Medical Writer Caldwell, Susan, PhD’s Summary

Work experience. Scientist in HIV-AIDS research, then a change in career focus to medical, scientific, and technical writing. Clinical areas of writing expertise include oncology, cardiology, neurology, infectious diseases, drug-device combinations, and other areas. Examples include GBM, brain metastases, squamous cell carcinoma, NSCLC, atherosclerosis, CGD, Crohn’s disease, age-related macular degeneration, neutropenia, influenza, hepatitis B infection, HIV and AIDS, and GVHD.
• Wrote, edited, and reviewed regulatory submissions, clinical protocols, amendments, study reports, investigator brochures (IBs), process development reports, manufacturing (CMC) documents, GXP audit reports, manuscripts, and other document types.
• At the Director and Senior Director levels, managed medical writing groups at four biotechnology companies and now at Biotech Ink, LLC; supervised many medical writers and writing consultants.
• Performed quality checks (QC) on research, clinical drug development, process development, and CMC documents.
• Acquired deep experience in developing infrastructure (eg, templates, SOPs, and style guides) for medical writing tasks in drug and device development environments.

Academic background. PhD in medical microbiology and immunology (emphasis in medical virology, immunology, and protein biochemistry). Performed postdoctoral research for 4 years in infectious diseases.
Publications and presentations. Strong publication record in the peer-reviewed biomedical literature, and experimental data presented at numerous national medical conferences.

    Postdoctoral Fellowship, Internal Medicine, Section on Infectious Diseases, 1983 — 1987

    Postdoctoral research in the Dept of Medicine, Section on Infectious Diseases. Studied the characteristics of human neutrophil (PMN) NADPH oxidase activity during in vitro bacterial or viral infection. Cells were purified from humans who had infections (influenza virus or bacterial), or were derived from patients who had leukemia, chronic granulomatous disease (CGD), or other pathologies affecting cell functions.
