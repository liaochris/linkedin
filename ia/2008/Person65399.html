Frank Caron’s Summary

I have been writing for Ars Technica for over a year now. I previously worked for a smaller news and op-ed site and then moved up to Ars. My writing experience extends quite a ways back to the beginnings of my degree in Rhetoric and Professional Writing, and I have always taken pride in writing to the best of my abilities.

My goal is to continue to contribute to the tech industry with thoughtful writing that gets people talking and keeps people reading. That said, any time I can utilize my expertise in web design, application development, and graphic design to assist in the process, I will gladly do so.

Frank Caron’s Specialties:

Rhetoric, Critical theory, background in Literary Criticism, background in Computer Science

Frank Caron’s Experience

  • Staff Writer

    Ars Technica

    (Partnership; 11-50 employees; Internet industry)

    January 2007 — Present (1 year 5 months)

    At Ars Technica—the name is Latin-derived for the "art of technology"—we specialize in original news and reviews, analysis of technology trends, and expert advice on topics ranging from the most fundamental aspects of technology to the many ways technology is helping us enjoy our world. We work for the reader who not only needs to keep up on technology, but is passionate about it.

    As a staff writer for the gaming section, I strive to deliver late-breaking news and critical reviews of products for the prosumer in a timely fashion, while providing analysis that makes our coverage worth reading in the stead of link-heavy enthusiast blogs. We like to say that you may go to bed with another site, but you'll wake up with Ars. Whether behind a desk or out in the field, our analysis, attention to detail, and care for our work separates us from the competition in the gaming journalism field.
