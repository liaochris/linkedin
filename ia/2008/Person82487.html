Jake Owsley’s Summary

Jake is a dual-degreed engineer from the University of Colorado. BS in Electrical and Mechanical Engineering. He has a broad electrical engineering background with 19+ years experience in product development and manufacturing.

Recently he has work nine years as senior embedded systems developer (C & C++) with combined experience in medical device development and data storage. Embedded system development has been a recent interest, which allows him to stay close to the electronic and PCB designs and maintain flexibility with embedded firmware.

Applications of his work have included embedded architecture, PCBA design, medical devices and data storage devices. Specifically I have been responsible for environmental control, power monitoring, battery charging & conditioning, and low-level applications as USB/serial port communication and analog signal conditioning.

He is fluent in several flavors of Linux and Windows platforms, comfortable developing on either platform. His firmware and GUI applications have included numerous state machine and event-driven designs (C & C++ ) on PowerPC, 8051, TMS320, MSP430 and ARM9 (DSP and GP processors), several RTOSs (Salvo, ThreadX and VxWorks), including several IDEs (Green Hills MULTI, CodeWright) and source control (PVCS, SourceSafe, SVN) packages. Over 11 ten years of C, and 3 years of C++ experience, GUI applications using C++ MFC, Visual Basic and some COM, Python.

In addition working the data storage industry, he developed an in-depth knowledge of ATA, USB, RS232, I2C protocols.

Jake Owsley’s Specialties:

A self-starter with strong communication and inter-discipline skills, Jake has 'worn many hats', from electronic assembler to R&D engineer, Test engineer, OEM engineer and sustaining engineer.
