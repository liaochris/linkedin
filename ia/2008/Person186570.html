Tenia Baker’s Summary

Having received my LLB (Hons) degree at Kingston University, and now nearly finished the BVC at the College of Law, Bloomsbury, I am committed to becoming a personal injury / medical law barrister.

I've made sure to not only concentrate on the academic side of law, involving myself in many practical activities such as mooting and other activities designed to improve my legal skill-set. Having taken part in over twenty moots and taken course in negotiation and client interviewing, I feel I've made sure to progress my skills as rapidly as possible.

While at Kingston University I represented the law school in national negotiation and client interviewing competitions. In May last year I reached the national final of The Negotiation Competition 2007. The year before I won Kingston University client interviewing competition (sponsored by Surrey Law Society) and now regularly take part in pro bono work.

I'm currently applying for pupillages and am looking forward to the opportunity to use the skills I've developed in practice.
