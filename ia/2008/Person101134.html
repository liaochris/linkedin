Jean Vieille’s Summary

Since 30 years I gather experience in various industrial and IT positions, sustaining continuous self-education and looking at the fast World change.
I progressively acquired an inherent cybernetic and systemic mind, but I was quite slow to realize how IT actually intervenes in our day life, and more specifically in manufacturing operations. The difficulties to fund manufacturing IT projects and then to deliver positive value reveal a deep chasm between the usual technology focus and the actual importance of information.
I felt that the many approaches to management such as 6 Sigma, Lean management, and Theory of constraints have commonalities and are intrinsically information matters. The light came from lengthy discussions with Nicos Leon and José Gramdi: Information is much more than computers and all enabling communication technologies. It is:
- ultimate science meta-theory,
- ultimate material particle,
- essence of organization, energy : information drives and supports organization, generates negative entropy to compensate or overcome complex system natural deterioration.
This is exciting and I dedicated myself to the Control Chain Management energetic approach to Manufacturing Information Systems that leverage the power of information to boost the creation of value of industrial companies, for their shareholders of course, but primarily for all the community, achieving its social responsibility by balancing its interaction with the environment. Indeed, efficient information system:
- empowers employee intelligence translating itself into Enterprise intelligence, improving the organization, compensating the entropy
- ensures positive energy balance that guaranties company’s lasting and its sustained performance.
