brock dubbels’s Summary

I have worked for ten years in education and designing instructional environments. My work is built upon the premise that work should be fun, efficient, and employ deep learning. When I design a learning environment, I do so with research in mind, so that I can look at what I created, and what the learners experienced, and build theory. What is unique about what I do is the systems approach and the way I reverse engineer play as a deep and effective learning tool into transformative learning.

brock dubbels’s Interests:

I am interested in creating opportunities that enhance instructional systems, promote questioning, inform and scaffold data representation and analysis, as well as increase engagement and innovation in education and training systems. I have been looking at games, virtual environments, and shared simulations to explore ways to utilize and integrate some of the affordances of these new tools to enhance instructional systems. Along with exploring tool use and communication, games are instructional systems that can also be used for hypothesis testing, delivering instruction, assessment and evaluation, data representation, data mining, and analysis, as well as the production of new artifacts and and tool usage. In addition, good games and instructional systems increasing engagement and time on task, and when well-designed, can embed traditional learning and academic skill sets in cycles of inquiry, reflection, and continuous improvement.
