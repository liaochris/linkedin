Howard Pakosh’s Experience

  • Director, NA Sales

    SONICS, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Semiconductors industry)

    December 2007 — Present (1 year 1 month)

    Sonics Inc.® is the premier supplier in the interconnect segment of the Semiconductor Intellectual Property (SIP) market. Sonics offers SMART InterconnectTM solutions and Memory Management products that facilitate the rapid development of Systems on A Chip (SoC) for embedded systems markets. SMART Interconnect solutions combine advanced fabrics and optionally data flow services that replace the need for SoC developers to design, verify and maintain similar complex functions on their own.

    By using SMART Interconnect solutions, SoC developers can also shift interconnect and intellectual property design and verification from the physical design to the architectural exploration phase of the chip development process.

    The utilization of Sonics’ products has demonstrated repeatedly that SoCs can now be developed in a fraction of the time and at a fraction of the cost when compared to architecting Interconnect solutions in house.

  • President & CEO

    ChipStart

    (Semiconductors industry)

    April 1998 — Present (10 years 9 months)

    About ChipStart

    Headquartered in Palo Alto, CA, ChipStart LLC (www.chip-start.com) was founded in 1998 with the mission to be a leading value-added reseller (VAR) of best-in-class engineering design automation (EDA) and semiconconductor intellectual property (IP) tools and solutions. The team has over 40 years expertise in all aspects of the semiconductor design cycle (concept to design implementation to GDSII mask generation).

    Over the last year, there has been a significant rise to the cost of sales and support of EDA and IP ptoducts and solutions. As a result, small- to medium-sized developers of best-in-class solutions require a sales and support team closer to the customer. The intrinsic value ChipStart provides is better customer outreach and support, intimate territory knowledge, targeted on-demand marketing (TOM) capability and flexible pricing models.

  • Director

    Elliptic Semiconductor

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Semiconductors industry)

    April 2005 — July 2008 (3 years 4 months)

    Elliptic is dedicated to the needs of the security IP market. To serve this need well, Elliptic has expertise in semiconductor IP through its veteran SoC development team, and software IP that includes core interface software and stack development. Elliptic also invests heavily in standards activities including the IEEE, the IETF and industry forums as required to be sure our cores and software are fully compliant to the latest security standard in each of the markets Elliptic serves.

    Many of Elliptic's competitors derive their IP from SoC designs making the design rigid and difficult to integrate. Elliptic designs its IP from the ground up making it fully adaptable to customers' needs. As the team knows SoC design, customers often leverage this expertise to help architect their designs to reach an optimum trade-off in performance, cost and power dissipation that is required to be successful in their target markets.

    May 2001 — October 2007 (6 years 6 months)

These products include embedded memory cores, which are critical components of communications, consumer and computer products including switches, routers, modems, cellular phones, set-top boxes, HDTVs, DVD players and PCs. In addition, the company offers software tools and custom memory design services.
