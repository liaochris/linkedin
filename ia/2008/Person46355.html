David Davis’s Summary

I started my career in Telecommunications in the Army in 1978 as a Technical Controller. Tech Controllers do telecom circuit activations, circuit conditioning, patch and test and lots of long haul troubleshooting and faut isolation. And back in the day we did all this with tri-pacs, datascopes and breaking out signals and making sense out them with HEX cause and diag codes.

Among my DoD assignments were NSA, DIA, DISA, and INSCOM. Suffice to say I've a foundational history in Secure Communications that tracks the initial development of the Arpanet, MILNET and the DSNETs. Another benefit of DoD circuit troubleshooting is a pretty sound foundation in communications electronics.

I left government service in 1990 and have worked for the following companies either as an operations engineer or a pre-post sales engineer; BBNC, ACC, CISCO, BayNetworks, AOL, UUNET, WorldCom, MCI, and now SAVVIS where I'm a Sr. Solutions Design Engineer.

Certifications I've held have been CCIE, BBN OS Unix/Network Administrator, Cisco ATM, Kalpana EtherSwitch, CNE, and CISSP.

IOS's I'm familiar with are JUNOS, ACC OS, Cisco IOS, Site Manager, and BCC.

VPN technologies I'm familiar with are IPSEC RAS, IPSEC Dedicated, MPLS RFC 2547/2547bis, ATM CoS and Context VPN's.

IP Routing technologies I've worked with are OSPF, RIP1&2, ISIS, IGRP, EIGRP, and BGP-4.

My current role is to create and propose standard and custom network/hosting/utility design architectures for SAVVIS customers.

My goal is to mentor rational progress of Internet technology at whatever company I'm currently working for.
