Mayande Gowon’s Summary

Mayande Gowon, an African-American and native of Manhattan, began his entrepreneurial career in the late 80's with the founding of I.E.N. (International Exchange Network), a company that provided Olympic-level training for U.S. athletes in advanced training camps throughout the former Soviet Union. In the 90's he became a self-taught Network and Systems engineer working as an independent consultant for major financial institutions.

In 1999, he founded TGC Global which began as a consortium of highly skilled financial technologists available for hire. Under Mayande's leadership TGC Global grew to a multi-million-dollar professional services firm that continues to expand by leaps and bounds. TGC Global has a variety of technology practices that focus on advising Global 2000 Organizations on how they can leverage technology to monetize existing assets, and be more efficient and competitive in today’s market. In addition to serving as TGC Global's President, Mayande also holds Board positions for several New York-based Internet and Technology companies.

Consultative Background: Project Manager/Senior Network Architect with strong technical, business, and management skills focused on financial markets. Proven success innovating state-of-the-art solutions on time and under budget. Strong understanding of the relationship between technology and strategic business interests with a P&L mindset to drive significant financial savings and to provide for the practical application of cutting-edge technologies. A variety of IT industry experiences that provide for the ability to implement technology as well as to design, manage and document unique solutions. Respected leader of diverse, cross-functional teams, who has managed multiple concurrent projects in the US, Europe, Africa, and Asia involving a wide range of system and infrastructure technologies. Personable, innovative and results oriented with a unique mix of technical and professionally oriented soft skills.

Mayande Gowon’s Specialties:

Information Technology, Telecommunications – Synchronous Digital Hierarchy (SDH), Synchronous Optical Networking (SONET), Dense Wave Division Multiplexing (DWDM), Coarse Wave Division Multiplexing (CWDM), Free Space Optics, the Internet, eCommerce, eContent, Nanotechnology, Market Research, Competitive Intelligence, Business Information, Strategic Consulting, Strategic Planning, Product and Industry Trends, Management & Leadership, Organizational Development, Communications, and Motivation.
