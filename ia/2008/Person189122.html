Tim Long’s Experience

  • Owner

    TiGra Networks (Sole Proprietorship)

    (Sole Proprietorship; Myself Only; Information Technology and Services industry)

    November 2005 — Present (2 years 3 months)

    TiGra Networks specialises in providing and managing Information Technology for small and medium businesses. We'll get involved in any computer-related task or problem and our Managed IT Plans are designed to provide worry-free, secure, reliable computer networks for businesses of any size. Our particular speciality is Microsoft Small Business Server for businesses with 2 to 75 computers.

    I perform all of the technical functions at TiGra, from building computers to installing, configuring, monitoring and troubleshooting software and networks. I also act as a business IT advisor. I am a Microsoft Certified Professional and an accredited Microsoft Small Business Specialist; TiGra Networks is a registered Microsoft Partner.

  • Software Architect

    Software-y-Ddraig (Sole Proprietorship)

    (Sole Proprietorship; Myself Only; Computer Software industry)

    2001 — Present (7 years)

    As the owner and software architect at Software-y-Ddraig (translation: "Dragon Software") I design and produce software and device drivers for astronomers and astronomy equipment. I take on custom development work and produce software sold as ShareWare. I also help amateur and professional astronomers with observatory and telescope commissioning and was involved in commissioning the largest optical telescope in the British Isles.

  • Founder and Group Leader

    South Wales Small Business Specialist Group

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Information Services industry)

    November 2005 — 2007 (2 years)

    SWSBSG was fromed to bring together IT professionals in and around South Wales to form a forum for collaborative ventures. Working on the principle that a rising tide lifts all ships, the group aims to improve our skills and businesses through face-to-face interaction, presentations, workshops and other events. The group was launched with funding from Microsoft though we are independent from any particular vendor. Membership of the group is open to anyone who provides IT and related services to small businesses. Membership is not open to end users or those who are employed as IT personnel within their own organisation. We encourage (but do not require) members to gain the Microsoft Small Business Specialist accreditation.
