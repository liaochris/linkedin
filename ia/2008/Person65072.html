Frances Fast’s Summary

Frances Fast is widely considered as an expert in angel investment and leads the Q1 Private Investor Network which focuses exclusively on privately-held, Canadian technology opportunities.

Since 2001, Frances has focused her efforts on the management of angel investment groups that collectively have invested in 25+ regional early-stage ventures in the high-technology, advanced manufacturing and life science sectors. She served a central role in the development and management of the Toronto Angel Group (TAG), which established the first collaborative community of disparate angel investors in Ontario. In 2005, Frances co-founded the Toronto Network of Angels, a network of sector-focused angel investment groups with a primary mandate to "Get Deals Done!", and launched both the Toronto Life Science Angels and Industrial Angels groups.

Frances is a founding member of the Angel Capital Association (ACA), a professional alliance of angel groups across North America. She is an active contributor to the research efforts of the Angel Capital Education Foundation, and the Center for Venture Research, and consults to the National Angel Organization (NAO), the Toronto-based advocacy organization for angel investors in Canada. A frequent speaker and panelist on entrepreneurship and equity investment, Frances is a contributing author to the “Age of the Angel: Best Practices for Angel Groups” which is in private release through the NAO.
