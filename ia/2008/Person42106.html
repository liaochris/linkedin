Joseph Cook’s Summary

In short:
The Internet holds incredible power. I especially appreciate how online communities and news and information resources can expand our world with social networking, multimedia information packages, and multiple-media coverage. I am happiest when using editorial *and* technical skills in creative ways to meet the needs and interests of a large and curious audience.

In-depth:
An experienced web editor with extensive technical skills and broad topical interests, I first entered the world of web communities and online journalism in 2000 after dabbling in design and site maintenance as an undergrad at Caltech, one of the world's top engineering schools. After 5 years of "trial by fire" in online journalism and community management at "tween"-oriented website Whyville.net, I went to USC Annenberg for my Master's degree, honing my news judgment and HTML/CSS skills at half a dozen internships and part-time jobs. Following a brief stay at ECT News, I found my place (for now) at Paul Hastings, an international law firm where I manage content and design site architecture for the company's intranet Portal.

I have written and edited for business executives and scientists, teenagers and pre-teens. I have covered news about journalism and the law, written how-to's and FAQs and new feature announcements without end, and helped children discover the joys in history and literature and calculus. I have designed UIs for intranet Portals and web applications, "tween"-oriented online newspapers and collegiate science clubs. I have written code and markups in javascript, PHP, HTML, CSS, and reverse-engineered a migraine of an XML/XSLT transformation within a leading company's search engine application. I have managed online meetings between opposing parties within a corporation and within a global web community. And I have directed "real" fights between English and French armies during the Battle of Agincourt, not to mention a little sword-slinging myself.
