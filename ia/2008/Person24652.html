Bob Andelman’s Summary

Bob Andelman is the author or co-author of 10 biographical, business, management and sports books. These include "Fans Not Customers" (with Vernon W. Hill II; Portfolio/Penguin, September 2007); "Will Eisner: A Spirited Life" (Dark Horse/M Press; October 2005); "Built From Scratch" (with Bernie Marcus & Arthur Blank, co-founders of The Home Depot; Crown Business, 1999); "The Profit Zone" (with Adrian Slywotzky; Crown Business); "Mean Business" (with Albert J. Dunlap; Crown Business, 1996); "The Corporate Athlete" (with Jack Groppel; John Wiley, 2000); Stadium For Rent (McFarland and Company, 1993).

Bob Andelman’s Specialties:

Collaborating with senior business executives who have expertise in their field but need assistance telling their story or sharing their wisdom in book form.

  • Author

    Will Eisner: A Spirited Life (M Press/Dark Horse) (Self-employed)

    (Self-Employed; Myself Only; Writing and Editing industry)

    November 2005 — December 2006 (1 year 2 months)

    Will Eisner: A Spirited Life (Dark Horse/M Press) is the biography of the legendary artist and writer who is often credited with taking comic books out of the ghetto in the 1940s and establishing the market for adult, long-form comics – graphic novels – in the 1970s
    Starlog magazine wrote: “The late Will Eisner was a comics legend – and he has been honored with a biography to match.”
    The Miami Herald review said, “Eisner was an innovative and influential artist, but Bob Andelman's new biography also correctly focuses on Will as a groundbreaking businessman.”
    “Andelman's most significant achievement is his seamless tying together of the multiple threads in Eisner's long and ever-changing career,” according to a review in the Roanoke Times.
    Publishers Weekly called Will Eisner: A Spirited Life “an intimate albeit sprawling biography of the legendary comics author and comics innovator.”

  • Co-Author

    The Profit Zone (Self-employed)

    (Self-Employed; Myself Only; Writing and Editing industry)

    September 1997 — September 1998 (1 year 1 month)

    The Profit Zone: Lessons of Strategic Genius from the People Who Created the World’s Most Valued Companies (Times Books/Random House), with Adrian Slywotzky and David Morrison, partners in Boston-based Mercer Management.
    The Profit Zone is Andelman’s best selling book overall. More than 100,000 hardcover copies in print after 10 printings. After five years in hardcover, Three Rivers Press published the paperback edition on February 26, 2002.
    The New York Times ranked The Profit Zone as No. 10 on its best-selling business books list on April 5, 1998.
    Business Week’s John Byrne wrote that The Profit Zone “provides insights and lessons aplenty... It makes practical and usable some compelling theories for how to win in today’s marketplace.”
    The Boston Globe’s David Warsh wrote “The Profit Zone is better than most strategy books... more coherent than a business magazine, more helpful - and more fun.”
