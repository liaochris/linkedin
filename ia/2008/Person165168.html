Robert Padich’s Summary

I have over 30 years of experience in clinical service delivery and research in industrial, academic, and governmental settings. My particular areas of clinical expertise are schizophrenia, depression, anxiety, and cognitive function throughout the life span. My research interests have centered on developmental neurotoxicology, attentional processing/sensorimotor integration, psychopharmacology, experimental psychopathology and personality research. I have broad experience with both US FDA GLPs and GCPs and the EU requirements for drug development, registration, and the developing requirements for postmarketing surveillance. I have over 15 years of experience of conducting and reporting pharmaceutical research in a highly regulated environment, spanning drug development from early toxicology though preclinical efficacy and clinical research studies from first human dose to completion of the marketing application process.

Robert Padich’s Specialties:

Study design, conduct, analysis, interpretation, and reporting of pharmaceutical research under GLP, GCP, and ICH guidelines. Preclinical pharmacology and toxicology of chemical entities affecting the CNS and endocrine systems. Treatment outcomes and quality of life assessment. Preparation of Clinical Protocols and Clinical Study reports, Briefing Documents, INDs, IMPD/CTAs, CTDs and related documents.
