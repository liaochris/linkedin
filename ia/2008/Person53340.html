Donna Sirbasku’s Summary

I have many years’ experience as a hospital pathologist; the Houston VAMC is the largest hospital in the VA system. At the Houston VAMC I did a wide range of surgical pathology specimens, autopsy pathology and Clinical Pathology. We did many frozen sections there and I often cut my own sections.

My experience with gynecological specimens was limited at the VAMC, since the women’s clinics were started only during the last few years I was there. However, at Quest, gyn pathology made up more than 70% of my cases; Quest also had hospital contracts for breast, uterus and ovary specimens. I regularly signed out Pap smears at both Quest and AmeriPath, and did all of the various CLIA Pap/tissue correlations, as well as endometrial biopsies and non-gyn cytology. I am certified in ThinPrep, SurePath and conventional Pap smears and recently passed the CAP certification exam with a score of 100%.

At the Houston VAMC I saw a lot of GI pathology and was trained by Dr. Robert Genta, a well-known GI pathologist. During my time at Austin Pathology Associates I was trained in GI Pathology by the pathologist founders of Pathology Partners (now Caris). At AmeriPath I signed out GI surgical pathology specimens primarily, with pathologists trained by Dr. Petras in the AmeriPath GI Pathology group.

In addition, I am very good at autopsies – and like to do them. The Baylor residents routinely worked with me to learn proper autopsy procedures. I also did all of my own grossing of surgical specimens at the VA, so if needed I can cover for pathology assistants as well.

My continuing medical education is up to date and my scores on the CAP “PIP” quarterly CME tests are always over 90%. I also have CME credits in Non-gyn and Pap cytology.

On days when Quest was short-handed, I signed out as many as 200 specimens (containers) and I routinely work from 7:30 to 5:30.

I now have experience in both hospital and outpatient pathology areas and am comfortable in both.
