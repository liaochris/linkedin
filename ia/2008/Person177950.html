Anne-Marie Nichols’s Summary

THE EVOLUTION OF A BLOGGER AND SOCIAL MEDIA CONSULTANT
WORK 1.0
After graduation, I worked in marketing communications as a copywriter and web content writer in the financial, insurance, and software industries

WORK 2.0 – FREELANCING
In 2004, I went back to my roots – journalism – and started writing for my local newspaper and freelancing by producing a mom’s club monthly newsletter and writing an e-newsletter for MOTHERS.

I also started blogging at my mommy blog, A Mama’s Rant and at group blogs like DotMoms. Then I started a cooking blog, This Mama Cooks!

MY OTHER CAREER - CHARTER SCHOOL FOUNDER
From 2004-2006, I helped start and govern a charter school, Carbon Valley Academy in Frederick, Colorado. Initially I had to cut back on my freelancing since I was writing and working on school’s website. I resigned from the board in December 2006 and went back to freelancing.

GETTING BACK TO THE WRITE SPOT
Starting in June 2006, I was professionally blogging. I was paid for my A Readable Feast blog at ClubMom. Then an editor found me and hired me to write stories of my daily life with kids for an online magazine blog called Citizen Mom’s Family Journal. I won two awards for it - a 2006 webaward and 2006 Silver Davey Award. I also continued to work with clients in creating traditional marketing communication pieces.

2007 AND BEYOND
For now, I plan to expand on my professional blogging efforts. I gave two blogging workshops at the 2008 Northern Colorado Writers Conference and spoke at BlogHer 2008. I'm also teaching professional writers how to blog, and recently joined Mom Central Consulting as their Social Media Manager.

I continue to build a revenue stream by consulting on social media, and on my personal blogs via affiliate programs, sponsorships, advertising, and paid posting.

I blog therefore I am.
