Jamie Moeller’s Summary

I'm currently teaching a criminology class at Carthage College and am an Associate Lecturer/Community Development Program Coordinator for Kenosha County UW-Extension in a "backfill" position that will be ending soon. During my college tenure, I worked on neighborhood revitalization efforts, prison research, political campaigns, civil rights work, and domestic violence research. Since I graduated, I have worked on employment counseling and case management for welfare recipients and on criminal justice policy reform. I moved back to Wisconsin from Washington, D.C. in 2007 for family/personal reasons.

After my appointment with UW-Extension expires, I hope to find a job that deals more directly with the criminal justice system, particularly in sentencing reform, reducing disproportionate minority contact, finding alternatives to incarceration, and promoting the use of restorative justice. However, I am open to working on other social justice issues (i.e. education reform, civil rights, health care policy, progressive politics).
