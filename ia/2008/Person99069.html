Jessica Scheibach’s Summary

I'm a product development leader who loves helping teams build great experiences on the web. I have over 10 years of internet/new media experience, for the past two years at Jobster and prior to that 8+ at Amazon.com. I value companies that are customer focused, that foster innovation, that follow agile (or at least flexible) development processes, that launch things early and often, and most of all, the people who make it all happen. I'm not a fan of bureaucracy, waterfall style program management, and rampant use of the term web 2.0.

My background is in program management; I'm not a designer and I don't write code (though I work with and manage people who do these things really well and they usually don't mind). If you read this far, you might also like to know that I'm a Virgo.

    (Internet industry)

    May 2005 — January 2006 (9 months)

  • Senior Program Manager, Buying & On-site Support Systems

    Amazon.com

    (Internet industry)

    August 2000 — February 2002 (1 year 7 months)

    I was responsible for program management of projects that focused on customer self-service tools such as Your Account and Online Help.

    For about the last year of this period, I had the opportunity to lead a significant strategic effort for the company, called "C4' (the 4 C's all related to customers). It was a cross-company initiative with a goal to significantly reduce operational and customer service costs by dramatically increasing customer satisfaction and providing tons of customer self-service options on the site. We divided into 3 main areas of focus: root cause reduction (figuring out the root cause of customer pain and fixing it), contact automation and customer service efficiency tools, and customer self-service. We accomplished a TON of work in a year and saved millions of dollars. This was a huge proving ground for me at Amazon and paved the way for my career there, and it was also one of the experiences I am most proud of.
