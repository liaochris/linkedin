Rob Jackson’s Summary

Rob Jackson is a multi-skilled professional with an extensive background in advertising, marketing, communications, and graphic design. He has worked on a wide selection of projects ranging from: corporate identity through advertising, product packaging to GUI, web design to 3D modeling, interactive media through audio. He has been involved in interactive media since the mid-1980s and he uses and moves between either platform [PC or MAC] with ease.

Rob uses the latest major software programs and applications for MAC and PC: PhotoShop, Illustrator, InDesign, Flash, DreamWeaver, FireWorks, SharePoint Designer, SharePoint, Acrobat, Distilller, Quark, PowerPoint, Bryce, Poser, ScreenWeaver, FrameMaker, CoolEdit 2000, Icon WorkShop, Premiere, SoundEdit 16, ABC FlowCharter, Bbedit, Cameraman, Fetch, Leech, GifBuilder, Kias PowerTools, Debabblizer, Software Audio WorkShop, and the Microsoft Office Suite.

Rob Jackson’s Specialties:

Creative direction, art direction, corporate identity, print design, web design, graphics, video, illustration, UI/GUI design, animation, flash, information architecture, interaction design, photography, project management, software graphics design, audio selection/coding, multimedia and web storyboarding, research, copywriting, editing and proofing.

  • EDP Co-ordinator/Jr. Designer

    Ted Bates Advertising

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    June 1984 — September 1986 (2 years 4 months)

    What began as my first 'real' job in advertising, grew into something quite different! I joined Ted Bates as a media planning assistant which quickly grew into much more. I performed all research runs (Harris/Donovan) using code from a manual, tracking radio buying through Harris/Donovan New York, and working with a colleage to track tv buying, and finally putting together GRP outputs.

    I continued to build my media planning skills and eventually I was put in charge of making sure all computer terminals (185 or so) were up and running (I guess I was the beginning of an IT Department!) During this phase, I also started developing my creative side through freelance work and even a little artwork for Ted Bates in the evenings.

    I wanted to move into the creative wing of Ted Bates, but due to their needs I was not given the opportunity to move, so I decided to move entirely! And off to Ottawa and the beginnings of my creative career at Proulx Brothers Inc.
