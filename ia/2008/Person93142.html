David Jobling’s Summary

Arts Industry
During my 30 year career path as an actor, major learning experiences have often arisen from working with gifted specialists and exciting companies such as Trans Express (2005), Vertigo Productions (2006) and Nosotros (2006/7) who recently received a community excellence award in theatre for their original production The Crack’d Pot.

My professional experience includes writing/directing the critically acclaimed one-person plays Puppy Love (1985; Australian Nouveau Theatre) and The Grip (1993; Adelaide Festival Centre Trust) plus working on many other productions; most notably Kids Stuff directed by Jean-Pierre Mignon (1985; Australian Nouveau Theatre), Faces in the Street directed by Wayne Harrison (1988; Festival of Sydney), Pastiche (2006; Lampshade Shop) and my own solo performance works such as Filling In (1998; The Performance Space). I have tutored for a range of arts organisations including the Australian Theatre for Young People, Darlinghurst Theatre, NSW Writers’ Centre, SA Writer’s Centre and Australian Drama Teachers Association among others.

In 1999 I graduated from the NIDA Playwrights Studio. I have been a registered songwriter with the Australian Performing Rights Association since 1986.

David Jobling’s Specialties:

• 1998 received an Australia Council Professional Development grant to deliver a workshop Creating the Solo Body and a paper, An Encore of Hope, for the Australasian Drama Studies Association Conference at New Zealand’s Waikato University. A collection of my short plays which includes The Grip was published by all men are equal productions.
