Kim Cary’s Summary

SANS offers excellent courses that cover what you need to know, with great instructors. I heartily recommend them. My .edu & non-profit colleagues should search for 'volunteer' on the sans.org website to receive a huge discount on these wonderful, practical and, I dare say, delightful classes.

I am proud to be joining the Junior ranks of SANS instructors as a mentor. My first mentor course is SANS CISSP+S class, which is prep for the 'mile-wide' CISSP exam. This is one of the few SANS classes that prepares you for an outside certification, but their material is spot on. If you're interested in management in the Information Security field and you want to prepare for CISSP, please feel free to contact me. Everyone has a different learning style. I can talk to you about the different SANS class formats (@Home, Mentor, Community, Classroom) and let you know what they are like. I'm also happy to discuss my view of the philosophy of how the ISC2 exam approaches the security domains, as well as what I did to prep for my successful first time pass at the exam.
