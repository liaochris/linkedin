Stewart Marshall’s Summary

Director of The University of the West Indies Distance Education Centre, UNESCO Chair in Educational Technologies. Held senior management positions in higher education in England, Papua New Guinea, Australia, Southern Africa and the Caribbean. Research and consultancy interests in the role of communication and information technologies in distance education, especially in developing countries. Published several books and over 90 other publications, including book chapters, refereed articles and conference papers. Chief Editor of the International Journal of Education and Development using Information and Communication Technology (IJEDICT) – http://ijedict.dec.uwi.edu/.

Stewart Marshall’s Specialties:

- linking ICT enhanced economic, social and cultural development efforts with emerging opportunities in such areas as e-learning, e-commerce, community and civic networks, telecentres, e-democracy, e-governance, on-line participation, self-help and virtual health communities, advocacy, cultural enhancement and others;
- Web development using HTML, JavaScript, CSS, Drupal, Joomla, Dreamweaver, Flash.
