David Sirbasku’s Summary

For the nearly 200,000 women in the United States who will be diagnosed with breast cancer each year, the most important consideration is eradication. Signe BioPharma Inc plans a four-part program to eradicate breast cancer. For some women, eradication means eliminating localized disease. We are developing a new brachytherapy that kills early/localized forms of breast cancer. For other women, eradication means destroying cancer that has moved from the breast to other locations. This is disseminated or metastatic breast cancer. Today, even with the best treatments, metastatic breast cancer has a poor prognosis. Signe plans new personalized medicine targeted therapies for disseminated cancer. This includes even the difficult "triple negative" types. Although this discussion focuses on breast cancer, similar personalized medicine technology can be applied to prostate and other mucosal cancers. Indeed, mucosal cancers are 80% of the total malignancies in humans.

However, the surest means of eradication is prevention. Signe proposes to exploit the natural secretory immune system by using “oral immunization” to reduce the risk of breast cancer. Immunity is transferred from the gut to the breast via the secretory immune system. This strategy is based on the fact that breast cancer incidence is lowest in areas of the world where oral immune challenges are common and highest in the western countries where similar challenges are restricted. The concept that we are “too clean” will be applied to reduce the current western risk of breast cancer (1 in 8 women) to the 1 in 50 or 1 in 100 rates of the non-western world. In another view, prevention can mean full eradication worldwide. To achieve this, the suspected infectious agents responsible for the development of breast cancer will be sought and “oral” immunizations developed similar to the Sabin oral vaccine for the poliomyelitis virus.

David Sirbasku’s Specialties:

Signe BioPharma Inc is dedicated to the eradication of breast cancer. New treatments will use the concepts of targeted therapies and personalized medicine. Beyond breast cancer, the same personalized medicine technology will be used to treat cancers arising from prostate, colon, ovary, uterus, cervix, bladder, kidney and lung. This requires large supplies of therapeutic proteins. Signe will produce these proteins using transgenic plant-based platforms that deliver low cost large scale supplies.
