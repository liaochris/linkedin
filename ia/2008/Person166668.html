    People-Not-Politics.com

    (Apparel & Fashion industry)

    June 2006 — Present (2 years 5 months)

    Issues divide us. That’s good. In a democracy, they should. But no one political party should be allowed to have a monopoly or inject politics to distort the meaning of a humanitarian cause. Supporting the troops is traditionally a conservative cause. Showing concern for the victims of an American war, a liberal one. This is silly. It’s time this country stop letting people at the political fringes define what these causes mean for the rest of us. There shouldn’t be a stigma for anyone to show that they care about good people going through an unimaginably difficult experience. TakePride and Iraqi Hope are projects to combine art with the inspiring real-life stories of US soldiers and Iraqi children to help people connect to the truth about these causes. To help Americans see people not politics.

    20% of the profit from the sale of TakePride shirts is donated to Wounded Warrior Project. And 20% of the profit from the sale of Iraqi Hope shirts is donated to Generation Iraq

  • Development & Design

    TakePride, LLC

    (Apparel & Fashion industry)

    June 2006 — Present (2 years 5 months)

    We believe that while young Americans are deployed to war, that regardless of what other causes people support, that there’s a unique obligation for the country to let soldiers and their families know that they’re not forgotten. But somehow along the way the cause of showing you care about people became more about politics. We set out to change this.

    Art is at the heart of our effort. TakePride t-shirts tell the real-life stories of young Americans in Iraq and Afghanistan through the designs of artists of the same generation. Through these shirts, distance shrinks and quiet moments emerge where everything fades except for the inspiring humanity of individual Americans doing their best in an extremely difficult situation. Besides meaningful stories, and raising money for an important cause, we also created cool tees that we’d like to wear. We hope you like them too.

    20 percent of the profit is donated to Wounded Warrior Project.
