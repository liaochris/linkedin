Felix Chow’s Summary

For 13 years, Felix Chow has been working in the State of Michigan serving at executive levels as the Director/Principal of two high schools (vocational-technical and alternative: 900 students, $6 million) in Battle Creek, as the Chief Financial Officer of Mott Community College ($55 million) in Flint, and as the Chief Financial and Operations Officer and the Superintendent of Flint Community Schools (25,000 students, $230 million). From December 2005 to December 2007, Dr. Chow was the first Asian American to serve in Hamtramck Public School District (3,200 students, 22 languages, $33 million) as its Superintendent. Currently, he is on sabbatical leave from the School District.

In addition to his executive experiences in public education, Chow worked in the business world for 20 years of which 17 years were in the computer industry (Sperry UNIVAC [now: Unisys] and Digital Equipment Corporation [now: Hewlett Packard]) covering a wide range of responsibilities from teaching customers and employees to managing $4–15 million training businesses (full profit and loss – P&L). In 1987-88, Chow developed and implemented a strategic planning model for a $65 million for-profit training business. Later the model was adopted by a $1 billion computer service business in the same company. In 1989 and 1991, he was instrumental in planning for two separate corporate mergers of the sizes of $250 million and $400 million. His ability in working with and through people truly epitomizes the accomplishments as results of teamwork.

For enjoyment, Chow plays an antique French violin made in 1886 and chess against the computer.
