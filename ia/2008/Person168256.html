Sam Webb’s Summary

During my time at Creative Assembly Australia, I have been involved with testing the Kingdoms expansion for Medieval II: Total War. Whilst in this position I have been responsible for a variety of tasks. Firstly, I am required to identify problems related to usability and functionality, then descriptively log these issues into the TestTrack Pro database. Once these problems have been assessed, I may be required to investigate them further and attempt to recreate the issue by adhering to and following a reproduction method. If the problem has been fixed, I will regress the bug, which confirms that it has been corrected and that it is no longer an area of concern within the game.

It is also necessary for me to identify flaws in the games design or compatibility and then confer with the design team about possible solutions. Issues are also raised in balancing meetings, where members of the QA team address and discuss any issues they have identified with the games playability. During my time at Creative Assembly, I have also gained a solid understanding of the build process and build installation procedures.

My position as a quality assurance tester has allowed me to work to the highest of standards within a competent and cohesive QA team. This has ensured that I understand the role of a QA tester and it has also given me a wealth of knowledge regarding games development policy and procedure. I now have a solid understanding of the roles of various departments and positions within the company, and feel confident and comfortable liasing with other departments.

My ultimate goal in the games development industry is to work as a lead designer on a major game title.
