Giovanni Coriasco’s Summary

I have many years experience in VoIP software development and in dealing with VoIP architectures. I'm looking for a job that can make the most of my competences and let them grow.
Here follow my current job experiences.

In year 2001, I have been employed as software developer for VoIP applications using OpenH323 API. Thanks to this practical activity, helped by studying the standard documents, I could increase my knowledge of H.323 and SIP protocols. At the same time I could grow in experience in managing Cisco Voice Gateway and Cisco CallManager. Thanks to the skills I acquired in VoIP, I could help empowering the VoIP network in my company.

In year 2002, I began dealing with projects envolving VoIP consulting towards external companies, initially only as technician and then as project manager and VoIP expert too.

From year 2003 to year 2005, I worked in building the VoIP network of "AEM Torino", the public company responsible for the electric power of Turin city, counting about 2000 employees and different branches around Piedmont: the activity was replacing the company former non-IP PBX with a Cisco CallManager based IP Telephony network, able to connect the different branches using IP.

From year 2004 to year 2006, I also worked on a project in collaboration with "CSI Piemonte", a public agency responsible for managing the informatic services of the public authority in Piedomnt. The project was abount interconnecting different public agencies in Piedomnt using VoIP (H.323 or SIP protocols). The challenge was that every agency had its specific vendor IP-PABX, with different features and different "standard" implementation of VoIP protocols. My task was to analyse the protocols (H.323 or SIP) "spoken" by every IP-PABX and report any incorrectness to the vendor.

From year 2007, I am the first coordinator of project management of all VoIP projects my company deals with.
