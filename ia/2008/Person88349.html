Jennifer Anderson Lewis’s Summary

International development specialist and trade attorney specializing in democracy and governance/rule of law, market access and regulatory issues, poverty alleviation/business development, environmental management and post-conflict constitutional and legal development. Current legal practice focuses on international trade policy, compliance and regulation, as well as litigation before the World Trade Organization (WTO). Significant USAID experience, both supervising and managing large-scale projects for Chemonics International’s Middle East, Latin America & the Caribbean (LAC), and Europe & Eurasia regions and serving as in-country Chief of Party in Bosnia and El Salvador. Project and in-country experience in Bosnia, Peru, Bolivia, El Salvador, Dominican Republic, Egypt, Morocco, Turkey and Jordan. Serves as Advisory Council Member to the Public International Law & Policy Group (PILPG) and provides legal and policy support to clients in the areas of constitution drafting, peace negotiations, conflict resolution and peace-building, sovereignty, natural resource policies and human rights. PILPG-related experience in Iraq, Kosovo, Darfur and Sri Lanka.
