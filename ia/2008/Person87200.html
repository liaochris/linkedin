Jeff Corbett’s Summary

I served seven years in the military, were I was a communications specialist. Four years of my tour of duty were spent in the USMC. There I was assigned as the VHF radio supervisor. During my four years in the Corps I received numerous awards of excellence for my performance including a meritorious promotion. In the Army I earned the position of team chief as an E4, a position normally reserved for E6 and a few E5's. I also receive a meritorious promotion to Corporal because of my leadership role with my unit. A promotion from specialist to corporal is a rare event for the Army Signal Corps. My team, usually the team assigned to the more remote locations, received complete autonomy because of our consistent performance and dedication to excellence.

I joined a company called American Ref-fuel in 1989. While with American Ref-fuel I achieved the position of Master craftsman, the company's highest position available for a technician which required the cross training and certification in several disciplines, including the National Society of Mechanical engineer QRO certification. I was instrumental in setting up the companies confined space and high angle rescue team. I was elected the team’s captain, and was responsible for training new inductees as well as maintaining a constant state of readiness.

I signed on with Turner Broadcasting System, in July of 2000 where I am currently holding a position as a Network Operations Technician II. I am called upon frequently to handle the more difficult problems and projects because of my attention to detail. Since joining the Network Operations group, I have been instrumental in assisting several of my fellow employees prepare for their CCNA exam, and I have been instrumental in some of their decisions to further pursue higher education, within this field. Since the creation of the A/V Group I have functioned as backup for that team on several occasions, and have help fill in during periods of high demand.
