Stacey Anderson’s Summary

After earning her MBA in Finance from Emory University, Stacey joined Equifax Inc., where she was responsible for mergers and acquisitions, strategic planning, competitive intelligence, data acquisition and vendor management. During her eight-year tenure there, Stacey led a number of key strategic initiatives including: the $250 million acquisition of Polk’s Direct Marketing business, the divestiture of Equifax’s Spanish commercial credit business, the building of the industry’s first small business credit database, and the formulation and execution of new industry strategies into brokerage, insurance, predictive analytics and database management.

In 2004, Stacey was asked by former Equifax CEO, Tom Chapman, to help create the strategy and eventually lead the operations for a $2 million public/private partnership known as Hemisphere Inc. (Chapman had been appointed Vice Chairman of the effort by Governor Sonny Perdue.) This entity was responsible for driving Atlanta’s effort to become the headquarters of the Free Trade Area of the Americas (FTAA). The Board of Directors included Georgia Governor Sonny Purdue, Mayor Shirley Franklin, and the Who’s Who of Atlanta CEO’s.

In 2006, Stacey left Equifax to join Steven Fanning and form Monolith Capital Partners, a venture capital and strategic advisory firm in Atlanta. Monolith Capital helps early stage companies solidify their business models, determine a solid path to revenue and earnings growth, and execute on those plans through a combination of strategic advisory and capital funding alternatives.

Stacey received her BA in Economics from University of Virginia where she was also a full-scholarship volleyball player with All Region and All-Conference distinctions.
