Andy Bater’s Experience

  • Owner

  • Farmer

    Fifth Estate Growers LLC

    (Farming industry)

    June 2007 — Present (1 year 8 months)

    Farm in Centre County Pennsylvania co-owned with my wife.

  • Director RF Systems Engineering

    Tribune Broadcasting

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; TRB; Broadcast Media industry)

    January 1999 — June 2008 (9 years 6 months)

    Responsibility for planning and construction of digital television transmitters, encoding, and multiplexing facilities for 23 television stations, roughly $70 mllion in capital cost.

    Managed FCC engineering regulatory affairs for the group.

    In the latter years moved into MPEG audio, video, and transport protocols, often working down to the packet, hexadecimal, and bit level.

    Chaired the Ad Hoc 2 GHz Reallocation Committee that developed the initial plan and cost estimates for national transition of that band. This led to broadcasters receiving over $500 million in new digital equipment and services.

    Coordinated the transmission restoration efforts of the New York television broadcasters in the months immediately after 9/11.

    Spent much of fall 2005 in Katrina crippled New Orleans during the reconstruction of Tribune’s two TV stations there.

  • Interim Manager

    Tribune Broadcasting

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    July 1997 — December 1998 (1 year 6 months)

    An unusual time in my broadcasting career...

    Represented Tribune's interest in WQCD while it was under marketing agreement to Emmis, i.e. prior to their outright purchase.

    Constructed electronic news gathering facilities for Central Florida Newschannel in Orlando. (Interesting trivia: Wild pigs run around the press area near the NASA countdown clock. Scared the heck out of me during a shuttle launch!)

    Built a television translator for Tribune's DC station, at the Pentagon's mountain top communications site no less.

    Managed construction of a "green room" complex for Geraldo Rivera. (Team of NYC plumbers and carpenters, Thanksgiving day, priceless....)

  • Chief Engineer

    WQCD (Tribune Broadcasting)

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    January 1984 — June 1997 (13 years 6 months)

    Chief Engineer CD101.9 (earlier WPIX-FM) until the radio station's sale to Emmis Broadcasting.

    Coordinated the move of WQCD’s transmitter back to the Empire State Building from the World Trade Center, along with construction of a $6.5 million studio and corporate office complex.

    A leader in live radio broadcasting fielding a remote production truck with an experimentally licensed digital microwave system.

    Mixed and aired concerts from the likes of George Benson and Miles Davis, and also produced events for others including a few memorable broadcasts for Howard Stern and his cohorts
