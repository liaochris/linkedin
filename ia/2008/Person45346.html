Dave Bell’s Summary

Without a free economy and a healthy environment I believe that no society can be truly vibrant and alive, no matter how healthy or well-educated it might be. As a result, I have focused my career on "clean" or "green" businesses, including alternative energy, electric vehicles, environmental testing, drilling and currently water treatment.

Free market capitalism and environmentalism have an important characteristic in common: both are primiarily concerned with the efficient use of scare resources. Some people see a conflict between economic freedom and environmentalism and unfortunatley there are some examples of environmental regulation that support this view. I see well-designed regulations as opportunities to improve both our free economy and our environment.

Our most critical environmental resources, such as water, air, land, biodiversity, fish stocks and oil, are also economic resources, and are often used wastefully. I believe that the biggest single root cause is that we systematically under value resources that are community or government property. I believe that capitalism, rather than government action, can and should be the primary solution to this problem. Environmental regulation should be limited to letting capitalism work by placing and enforcing reasonable values on community resources and then letting market forces allocate those resources efficiently.

I enjoy running small and middle-market companies and helping them grow. My goal is to develop and run a portfolio of businesses that are leaders in untapping the value of our environmental capital, and also valuable contributors to our economy.

On a personal level, I am a passionate birdwatcher and traveler. My wife and I love good food and wine. Nothing restores my soul like a trip to untouched wilderness, especially tropical rainforest.
