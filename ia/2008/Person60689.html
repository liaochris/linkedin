Bjorn Wiklund’s Summary

“What is essential and worthwhile to mention?”

I really hope my description gives a fair view of what I have achieved and what I in the future can achieve.

I have a good knowledge within modern electronics as I have been active in the electronics area all this time and been always active at the frontier of the technology.
I have been a teacher within the area, a researcher within the area and also a senior designer in several modern semiconductor processes:
- High voltage bipolar technology (90V and 400V)
- High voltage CMOS technology
- Low voltage CMOS technology (0.13um at present)

I was active as a teacher at the Royal Institute of Technology between 1974 and 1989 a period during which I developed not only within the areas of education but also related areas on the syllabus of the EE departments at the Royal Institute of Technology.

I have been responsible for lectures and other education activities including generation of student material within:
- Digital electronics
- Analog electronics
- Low noise amplifiers and systems
- Solid state electronics

During my years at the Royal Institute of Technology I was also heavily involved in Electron Beam Lithography research which was also my area of Licentiate thesis.

In 1988 I started with Ericsson Microelectronics as an analog designer.
My most recent assignment is power management units and analog front end concept engineering in our recent DECT circuits.

I was 1994 appointed manager responsible for hiring and training junior engineers to our company.
I was responsible for this hiring and training for almost 10 years during which I hired and trained almost 100 Electrical Engineers.

I have in different time frames been involved in quality related tasks both product quality and internal process quality as well as qualification of circuits.
I have also been responsible for the environmental assessment regarding our products including material declarations.
