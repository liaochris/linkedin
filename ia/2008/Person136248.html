Mike Romine’s Summary

Have very broad background in development of a wide variety of products.

Mike Romine’s Specialties:

I have extensive experience in managing outsourced development efforts following an ODM model, most of which were with Asian based partners. My interdisciplinary background has proven to be very suited to program management of teams developing electro-mechanical devices of various types from consumer electronics to high-tech capital equipment.

Technical skills:
I have an interdisciplinary background, which spans the gap between mechanics, electronics and firmware.

Mike Romine’s Experience

  • Vice President, Printer Systems

    Memjet Photo Retail

    (Privately Held; Consumer Electronics industry)

    November 2007 — Present (9 months)

    Charged with leading development of a revolutionary inkjet printer product by pulling together all technical, business, and manufacturing elements involved in bringing such a product to market.

  • Program Manager

    Eastman Kodak

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; EK; Consumer Electronics industry)

    July 2003 — October 2007 (4 years 4 months)

    As a founding member of the Kodak inkjet products group in San Diego, I was involved in virtually every aspect of the architecture selection through commercialization of Kodak’s inkjet product line. Given the ODM (outsourced design and manufacturing) development model, this entailed management of both internal and external development resources spread across three companies (Kodak, and two Asian ODM partners) operating in 4 countries. I also served as Manufacturing manager at Kodak for the NPI phase for a new line of inkjet printer products. In this capacity, I headed both the NPI (new product introduction) teams, and sustaining engineering teams for Kodak’s first line of inkjet printers. I served this post throughout the NPI phase of Kodak’s new inkjet printer programs, successfully overcoming the significant challenges involved in the delivery of a new printer product line with a new manufacturing partner.

  • Team Lead Mechanical/Systems Engineer

    Phogenix Imaging

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Consumer Electronics industry)

    May 2001 — May 2003 (2 years 1 month)

    • Lead mechanical team which developed a two-axis image cutting system for an inkjet photo minilab (entailed a mix of approximately 500 sheet metal, plastic, machined and elastomeric parts).
    • Lead other mechanical development efforts for various paper feed systems.
    • Served as Systems Engineer, owning all the interfaces between mechanics, electronics and firmware for second-generation inkjet minilab.
    • Wrote electro-mechanical specifications, which governed the interfaces between the disciplines.
    • Directed the actions of Asian contract manufacturers tasked with manufacturing the design.
    • Architected motor control strategy (mechanics, electronics and firmware) for numerous stepper and DC servomotors systems.
    • Designed the control systems for several closed loop DC motors, and implemented them in firmware.
    • Developed active de-skewing system, which optically registered images entering cutter system.

  • Senior Mechanical Engineer

    Overland Storage

    (Public Company; ovrl; Computer Hardware industry)

    May 2000 — May 2001 (1 year 1 month)

    • Developed robotic tape handling systems (tape libraries) for network backup products.
    • Owned the mechanical design of most of the moving mechanisms for the projects worked on.
    • Created electronics and firmware for control of prototype tape libraries.
    • Designed novel passive tape gripping system, which replaced more complex active systems.
    • Worked closely with electrical and firmware teams on interface issues.

  • Senior Member of Technical Staff

    Asymtek

    (Public Company; ndsn; Semiconductors industry)

    May 1998 — May 2000 (2 years 1 month)

    • Designed high-speed fluid dispensers for semiconductor packaging and PCB manufacturing.
    • Lead and managed product development process from inception through full production for new fluid dispenser product.
    • Worked closely with vendors on manufacturing issues related to product robustness.
    • Managed material flow during prototype and early production phases.
    • Designed inspection methods and tooling for miniature high precision parts.
    • Conducted extensive DOE’s for optimizing designs both in house and at customer sites.

  • Mechanical Design Engineer

    HP

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; HPQ; Consumer Electronics industry)

    December 1995 — April 1998 (2 years 5 months)

    • Worked with team to develop photographic quality inkjet printers.
    • Designed high volume electro-mechanical components from composite plastic, rubber and sheet metal.
    • Designed both ASF components and service stations during my tenure.
    • Developed and received patent for novel paper picking system.
    • Developed PID servo systems for motor control and implemented in printer firmware.
    • Designed and implemented electronics and firmware for real time data acquisition systems.
