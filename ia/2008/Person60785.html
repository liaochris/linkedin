Erin Kennedy, CPRW’s Summary

I am a certified resume writer and President of Professional Resume Services. www.proreswriters.com
I write for resumes and cover letters for every level. Entry level/fresh out of college, mid-level professionals, executives, and c-level executives leading billion dollar companies. I've written for just about every industry.

My resumes and cover letters are published in 7 books.

I recently was nominated for two "T.O.R.I" awards (Toast Of the Resume Industry), chosen out of hundreds of resumes and certified writers.

I've never met a client "situation" I didn't like. From too many jobs, to career changes, to a disgruntled former employer, you name it and I'm up to the challenge. Typically though, what a client thinks is a "challenge" really isn't as bad as they think.

My writing is somewhat aggressive-- direct and to-the-point-- but it really depends on the industry and the client's experience. I look at each project as a new "creation". Each project is unique. I don't use templates, or prepared formats. I take pride in my work. There have been many times when I look at a finished product and say, "WOW!".

I try to establish an easy and collaborative relationship with clients. I poke, prod and ask, ask, ask to uncover more tidbits of information. I remain very conscious of what the client wants to get across. To me, that is the key element in a client's satisfaction. Satisfaction is what keeps my clients coming back for updates, and what encourages them to tell their friends and family about me.

I strive to stay very current on what is new and what the latest trends are. Fresh and current are important to me (and to your resume!).

Erin Kennedy, CPRW’s Specialties:

Taking a so-so resume and turning it into a power-packed and exciting document. I like to create a "story" for readers, so it piques their interest and makes them want to read on. My resumes give them a visual of the candidate's steady progression.

Aesthetically, resumes need to look great. Space, font, and format are essential to a well-written resume. Too much context, too little? Everything matters when it comes to format. It has to look perfect.
