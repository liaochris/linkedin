Yen Sullivan’s Summary

Expertise in operations management, process reengineering and business analysis. History of taking ideas from concept stage and leading them through all phases of data development to deliver to the final product. Develops, implements, and disseminates operations strategies to ensure the achievement of company's goals and objectives. Directs the operations function in conjunction with business growth, financial objectives, and timely delivery and quality of products or services. Ensures attainment of efficient and economical performance in relation to profit and year-end goals.

Currently a Director of Data Operations for CARFAX, the leader in on-line vehicle history reports. Carfax is the 4th most visited automotive website with over 25 million consumer visits annually. Oversees the operations of the Data Department. Develops many business logics pending patent for product.


A member of International Freelance Writers Organization, She is currently working on her second manuscript which is a compilation of short stories called “Vertical Slices of Life.” Taking Barbara Kingsolver’s adage to heart, “if you want a slice of life, look out the window. An artist has to look out that window, isolate one or two suggestive things, and embroider them together with poetry and fabrication, to create a revelation,” she hopes to capture the ironies and lessons of life in these vignettes that provide a glimpse into selected moments in people’s lives.

She has also recently completed her first manuscript "Price of Freedom", an unforgettable semi-autobiographical story depicting a chain of events that eventually ends in the aftermath of discovery of underlying secrets, betrayal, and loss of innocence when a Vietnamese family seek political asylum in the United States after the fall of South Vietnam. This book is about their journey dealing their own personal cost for freedom while struggling to be accepted in a country still coming to terms with its first lost War.
