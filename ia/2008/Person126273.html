Maximilien Roche’s Summary

When I graduated from my buisiness school, I chose finance over law for my first professional experience. I had the choice between two carreers and I was kind of confused. But I was just coming back from Shanghai and I was sure about one thing : I didn't want to spend the coming ten years in Paris. I wanted to travel and settle down for a few years abroad. If it wasn't for my girlfriend (and future spouse), I would have stayed in Shanghai and looked for an opportunity there or anywhere else in Asia. But I chose to prioritize my conjugal situation over my need for exoticism.
So I started my carreer in Paris, as a financial auditor, because I was convinced it would make me more mobile professionnally than I would be as a lawyer.
I do not regret this choice today and not only for conjugal considerations.
I am still looking to find an opportunity to settle down for a few years abroad either within my present company or within another one. I am basically open for any destination, but I have to consider my future wife's career opportunities before I make my decision. From where we stand right now, Canada seems like the best bet, preferably British Columbia or Alberta.
If no opportunity presents itself from Paris, I will most probably try to migrate to Canada and look for a job directly on site.
