Neil Gorman’s Summary

I started my professional life as a teacher who worked with "at risk" populations that included BD, ED, and LD students that were having trouble succeeding in traditional educational setting. After working in that field for one year I moved into the substance abuse field which is where I worked as a counselor for at Cornell Interventions in Woodridge Illinois.

During the time that I worked as a substance abuse counselor I continued to work with an adolescent population, and became familiar with several therapeutic approaches including cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT), motivational interviewing, and the psychodynamic approach.

I entered a Masters in Social Work program in the fall of 2006 which I am still in today. While I have been in this MSW program I have been doing a great deal of research on how neuroscience can be used with in the social work filed, and other human services / helping professions.

I returned to the classroom as a teacher in July of 2007 when Cornell created a alternative education program with the Abraxas Education Group to better serve the adolescent substance abusers that make up a great deal of their client base.

I have also maintained a high level of interest in technology, and the ways that technology can be used as a tool in both educational and therapeutic settings.

Neil Gorman’s Specialties:

substance abuse, working with an adolescent population, psychodynamic approach, CBT, "at risk" students
