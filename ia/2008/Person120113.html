Marco Pirrone’s Summary

Marco Pirrone obtained his Master Thesis in Computer Engineering in July 1999 with sum cum laude with the thesis:“Pianificazione condizionale basata sulla percezione e su sensori parzialmente affidabili”.From Oct.99 he has been studying as PhD Student in University of Rome "La Sapienza".He is working on artificial intelligence and robot vision:in this period, inside the "Dipartimento di Informatica e Sistemistica",he has studied the advanced concepts of robot vision and artificial intelligence, in particular he has concentrated his effort on "visual attention".He obtained his Ph.D. in Computer Engineering in Nov.03 with the thesis: “Active Vision and Visual Attention for Indoor Environment Classification”.He is employee in Retis as Senior Program Manager, consultant for Eustema and Teacher at the University of Rome “La Sapienza” with a course of computer science for the Aerospace Engineering.He has participated at different projects in the area of the Document Management, Protocol, BPM and WCM.He has designed and developed the application connectors for the integration of the tool Ultimus BPM Suite with the Hummingbird DM, and with the systems of protocol DocsPA and eProcs.PA.He was involved in problems linked to the project of Content Management tool, named CPS, realized on Bea WebLogic and IBM WebSphere, Web Server IIS and Oracle and SQL, using technology JSP and EJB.During his university activities,he has participated to different projects as Marviss (Anthropomorphic Hand and Virtual Reality for Robotic System on the International Space Station) and PEGASO (PErcept Golog for Autonomous agents in Space station Operations).Furthermore he has collaborated to the design and development of the several autonomous robots software architecture.As consequence of this activities have been produced several scientific articles.Today ,his activities are mainly concentrated on the framework and new innovative development platforms analysis.
