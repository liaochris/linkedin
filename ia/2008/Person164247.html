Ronald Wopereis’s Summary

While i still try to figure out how to design my life, i am living it to the fullest.

This is what i love to do :

- interview people about their passion
- be a representant in family constellations, organizational constellations, brand constellations, money constellations, etc.
- design, facilitate, and/or attend creative sessions
- scout online for people who are like me : highly sensitive, aware of the impossibility to disconnect from humanity, no longer seeking to help or be helped

Couple of years ago i decided i will be 91.

Feel free to ask me questions, to invite me to come to your place, to give interactive lectures and workshops on the subject of attention (my study since 2004) networking (also since 2004) and creative thinking (since 2000). I love to travel so covering expenses would be perfect !

  • Co-Author of the Manifesto [The Paradox of the Technological Age]

    Sophistica

    (Non-Profit; 501-1000 employees; Think Tanks industry)

    September 2005 — December 2006 (1 year 4 months)

    The Message has to get out that The Individual Is Our Most Valuable Resource and That The Individual Counts - Michael Pokocky (editor)

    ABSTRACT:
    I consider attention to be the currency of the 21st century. Like money, attention is the carrier of social energy. But attention has properties that are not found in money. One of the most apparent differences is that money is suited for batch-oriented transactions only - something found exclusively in zero sum games (if i have it, you don't have it). Attention is much a more fluid currency, suitable for playing non-zero games (one plus one makes three). Further evolution as i see it is towards the infinite game, where the aim of the game is to play with the rules (as opposed to playing by the rules). Which properties are within attention is something that science has yet to discover. One scientist recently said in WIE Magazine : we have plenty of evidence - what we need now is a new theory. This article proposes a theory of attention.
