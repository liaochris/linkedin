Most recently built and launched ValueAverager.com, a site that helps individual investors implement the Value Averaging investment strategy. This was my first attempt at building a large-scale AJAX enabled site. In the process, I've learned all of the hard lessons which will help me in the future. I'm ready to begin my next big project!

Previous position was at Fishbowl Marketing where I contributed significantly to Fishbowl's growth and success as the sole developer of two online applications:

- Local Store Marketer - A service for large chain restaurants to allow their individual store managers to send location-specific marketing emails while giving the restaurants' corporate offices complete control over branding and content. Used over 1000 times per month (as of May 2007) by restaurant general managers and franchisees. Consistently praised for ease of use and for meeting client needs.

- FishbowlLocal - An ASP designed to bring the Fishbowl program to independent restaurants and small chains (which make up half of the industry). Created an online service to replicate all aspects of the Fishbowl service for chains. Automated most of necessary operations to serve clients. The result was a product at a price point that small restaurant companies could afford. Functionalities included:
- The setup of new clients
- The setup and management of ongoing triggered emails
- Management of the client's email list
- Management of the client's restaurant locations
- Turn-key web join page for the client's homepage
- Management of pricing and generation of client invoices
- Processing of client payments by credit card and ACH.
- Reporting on revenue and churn
- Numerous helpers and wizards to help the "little guy" develop a marketing program

FishbowlLocal is growing fast and is expected to be a major driver of the company's growth.
