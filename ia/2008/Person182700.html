Stuart Greenfield’s Experience

  • Chairman and Creative Director

    LeepeckGreenfield Ltd

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    July 1999 — April 2008 (8 years 10 months)

    Oversaw all aspects of business operations – including strategic planning, sales, marketing, business development and financial management – to establish the company as a leading regional advertising agency. Focused on the expansion into new markets, most notably the digital sector. Liaised with potential and existing clients to identify and align their needs and requirements with value-added marketing solutions. Authored and presented business cases specifying all project requirements and expected client benefits. Recruited a managing director, digital director and operations director in charge of supervising a staff of 32.

  • Managing Director

    Computer Trade Shows Ltd

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    January 1996 — April 1999 (3 years 4 months)

    Coordinated all aspects of advertising, direct marketing and public relations to effectively promote the computer trade show within the IT industry. Fostered strategic partnerships with computer manufacturers and distributors, providing them with a venue through which to target computer dealers and resellers.

    Operational Highlights:
    • Created the computer trade show after identifying a gap in the IT industry’s events calendar.
    • Responded to the increasing demand for email and the Internet by introducing the IT showcase.
    • Successfully launched the 1998 IT trade show at the National Exhibition Centre (NEC) in Birmingham; exceeded expectations with 100 exhibitors and 12K visitors in attendance.
    • Doubled the size of the 1999 trade show thanks to Government sponsorship, which generated widespread publicity for the handling of the Y2K bug; generated £1.1M in revenue and £400K in profit.
    • Sold the trade show brand to Imark International inc. in 1999.

  • Managing Director

    Greenfield Computer Marketing

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    August 1994 — January 1999 (4 years 6 months)

    Served as Marketing Consultant to diverse IT products sales and distribution businesses. Directed all aspects of clients’ branding, public relations, direct marketing and advertising initiatives as well as vendor relationships and cooperative marketing budgets. Collaborated closely with key business directors to provide the strategic and creative direction needed to effectively position clients in competitive markets. Supervised a team of 8 members.

    Client Highlights:
    • Introduced Fujifilm DX7 – the 1st digital camera for consumers in the UK – after devising a comprehensive launch strategy and securing a distribution partnership with Dixons Stores Group.
    • Created Fujifilm’s 1st consumer website incorporating a groundbreaking content management system.
    • Conceived and developed Fujinet, an online digital photo print service; sold the concept to Boots, who implemented the system using in-store minilabs.

  • Managing Director

    AAshima Distribution Ltd

    (Computer Hardware industry)

    January 1992 — March 1994 (2 years 3 months)

    Appointed to establish a UK subsidiary for the Dutch distribution company. Exercised full P&L management authority over inventories, sales and launched the TRUST brand. Built and led a team of 20 sales, 10 support and 15 administrative representatives tasked with supplying the computer dealer network with IT products for next day delivery.

    Operational Highlights:
    • Increased sales from £4M to £12M and profitability from under £200K to £600K+ in 3 years despite operating during difficult economic conditions. (Introduced credit card payment system for businesses).
    • Built a PC manufacturing plant in Witham, which produced up to 500 ready-to-run PCs per month for the IT reseller market.
