Ricardo Aranda Aupart’s Summary

Ricardo Aranda, better known as "Pekas," began his career as a copywriter at Wunderman, Mexico City, in 1994. He rose to be the Creative Director responsible for brands such as Ford, American Express, Ericsson, United Distillers, IBM and AT&T among others.

In 1999, Pekas shifted to MRM (McCann Relationship Marketing) as Creative Director on Sanofi, Lucent Technologies, and Profuturo GNP.

He then moved to the direct marketing focused, Publicis Dialog, which eventually became an independent Agency called RelationWired Group.

In May of 2003, Pekas accepted a position at JWT Connect (currently named Glue) as Chief Creative Director. During his two and a half years at JWT Connect, he and his team worked on projects that reflected a wide range of media. His team's accounts: Estée Lauder and it's brands, Wal-Mart, DHL, Nokia, and Nestlé among the more than thirty for which he was responsible.

In 2006, he moved to Los Angeles as Associate Creative Director at the Hispanic market agency, LatinSphere Advertising. Among the accounts he handled were Disney, Verizon, and XM Satellite Radio.

Nowadays, Pekas is Chief Creative Director and Co-founder of Plastilina LLC (www.plastilinaideas.com) in Los Angeles, CA, and has accounts like Mohawk Flooring, Southern Wine & Spirits and New American Dimensions.

This bilingual and bi-cultural professional has been the recipient of a range of industry awards including: London International Advertising Awards, Creative Circle, and the ALMADI's Amauta Awards. He was also short listed in the Clio, FIAP, and DMA Awards, and has served as part of the jury for the London International Advertising Awards and the Painani Awards in Mexico. He has written for the InformaBTL magazine, of Merca2.0, and has been a speaker at the AMMD conventions in Mexico and the Santiago de Cali and Medellin Universities, in Colombia.
