Ray Everett-Church’s Summary

In 1999, I was appointed to be the world's first corporate Chief Privacy Officer, and since then I have continued to be one of the foremost recognized authorities on the intersection between Internet-based products/services and consumer privacy. I am a co-author of "Internet Privacy for Dummies" and "Fighting Spam for Dummies" (both by Wiley Publishing), a contributor to several scholarly works on privacy and security, and a columnist for eSecurityPlanet.com. I have substantial expertise in all things related to spam, spyware/adware, and a wide range of technologies that have privacy impacts (e.g., RFIDs, location-based marketing, network monitoring and anti-spam technologies). I have performed expert witness work on various privacy matters, including landmark litigation on the issue of "spyware" and policy matters affecting online marketing and consumer privacy.

I am most interested in working with established or start-up companies offering products/services that have privacy-related impacts. I'm also interested in companies that have products/services that address privacy and security compliance issues.

When you're dealing with sensitive personal information, there's great value and great danger. I can help you plot a course to maximize value, strengthen brand, and avoid disaster.

Ray Everett-Church’s Specialties:

Privacy law & regulation; anti-spam policy, technology and strategy; privacy-related risk management and threat mitigation; privacy issues in Homeland Security market; privacy issues in M&A; HIPAA & Gramm-Leach-Bliley compliance; ECPA, Patriot Act issues for telecoms and ISPs; strategic planning and product development for privacy-impacting technologies. Extensive experience in lobbying and government relations.
