Borys Czerniejewski’s Summary

Since May 2007 Borys Czerniejewski is Technology Director at Institute of Innovation and Information Society (INSI), the very first private R&D centre in IT in Poland, specialising in e-government, e-health, IT security and telematics.

Previously he worked for Infovide-Matrix S.A. (formerly Infovide S.A.) where he was the Director of the International Liaison Office since November 2000 and later on - the Board Advisor, responsible for international business development, including R&D projects, looking for liaisons with partners, building consortia and managing international projects (including M-CAST - Multimedia Content Aggregation System based on TRUST Search Engine - http://www.m-cast.infovide.pl).

Familiar with EU funded programmes. Was a national team leader in the TRUST project – Multilingual Semantic and Cognitive Search Engine for Text Retrieval Using Semantic Technologies (IST-1999-56416) at TiP sp. z o.o.

Graduated in Information Technology from University of Mining and Metallurgy in Kraków. Specialist in information society issues, technology transfer, natural language processing and designing local area networks.

Previously Director of IT Systems Department at the State Committee for Scientific Research (currently Ministry of Science and IT), responsible for financing country-wide IT infrastructure for Polish science.

Member of the Polish Information Processing Society.

Co-author of several strategic documents about ICT development in Poland. Co-organised the European Ministerial Conference ‘Information Society: Accelerating European Integration’ in Warsaw, in May 2000. Participated in the screening of the Polish law for its alignment with the acquis communautaire.

Used to co-operate with the European Commission as a national delegate to the ISTC – one of the Programme Committees in the 5th Framework Programme for RTD and in the EU/CEEC Joint High Level Committee for Information Society (JHLC).

Borys Czerniejewski’s Specialties:
