Frank J. Feher’s Summary

Enthusiastic, creative, and highly self-motivated innovator with the ability to identify, evaluate, and accelerate new technologies into product and technology development pipelines. Distinguished record of accomplishments as both an independent scientist and a research director. Proven ability to work internally with R&D, Marketing and Business Development teams and externally with suppliers, large and small companies, universities, and other centers of excellence. Track record for delivering technology solutions with truly compelling value propositions.

ENTP Myers-Briggs personality type with strong entrepreneurial spirit and ability to drive innovative projects and innovation processes. Practical approaches to problem-solving and extensive experience with “open innovation”.

Excellent written and oral communication and influencing skills. Dynamic speaker with ability to explain complex technical issues to any audience.

Frank J. Feher’s Specialties:

Broad knowledge of technology, especially chemistry, surface science, catalysis, polymers/materials, nanotechnology, and industrial biotechnology. Excellent working knowledge of patent law and intellectual property, especially issues involving industry/university alliances.

Well-versed in project and portfolio management processes, including financial analysis (DCF), risk management (Monte Carlo, Real Options), Stage-Gate, strategic planning and technology forecasting/roadmapping.

Frank J. Feher’s Experience

  • Senior R&D Associate

    The Goodyear Tire & Rubber Company

    (Research industry)

    March 2006 — Present (2 years)

    Returned to role as Senior R&D Associate in 3/06 to spearhead "Open Innovation" within Goodyear's Global Materials Organization.

  • Manager, Polymer Technology

    The Goodyear Tire & Rubber Company

    (Chemicals industry)

    September 2004 — March 2006 (1 year 7 months)

    Promoted to Manager for Polymer Technology when Goodyear transformed its semi-independent Chemical Business into a low-cost producer of polymers for internal use. Executed restructuring plan, established core programs to support manufacturing, and managed 2005 budget of $4.8 MM for >30 associates in 4 facilities.

  • Senior R&D Associate and Manager

    Goodyear Chemical

    (Chemicals industry)

    September 2002 — July 2004 (1 year 11 months)

    Recruited to Goodyear Chemical as Senior R&D Associate to serve as internal consultant for technology development and innovation. Promoted to Manager for Advanced Materials in 7/03 with responsibility for developing new products/technology for non-tire markets. Assumed responsibility for Applications Development in 10/03.

  • Chemistry Professor

    University of California, Irvine

    (Educational Institution; Research industry)

    July 1985 — August 2002 (17 years 2 months)

    Joined the University of California as Assistant Professor in 1985. Promoted to Associate Professor with tenure in 1990 and Full Professor in 1992. On leave at Goodyear Chemical from 2002-4.

    Established internationally recognized, interdisciplinary research program in chemistry, material science, polymers and surfaces. Revolutionized field of silsesquioxane chemistry by developing practical methods for producing useful materials. Published >100 articles and 7 patents. Presented >200 research-related lectures at scientific meetings. Recipient of 2001 Collaboration Success Award (Council for Chemical Research), 2000 R&D 100 Award (R&D Magazine); 1990 Alfred P. Sloan Foundation Fellowship and 1987-92 Presidential Young Investigator Award (National Science Foundation).

    Vice-Chair of Chemistry (1993-2000): Managed space allocations, research facilities, shared instrumentation, safety training, hazardous waste management and annual safety inspections by Cal-EPA.

Frank J. Feher’s Education

  • University of Bristol

    Postdoctoral Researcher, Organometallic Chemistry, 1984 — 1985

    Postdoctoral research with Dr. Michael Green to develop expertise in the synthesis and characterization of molecular clusters.

  • University of Rochester

    Ph.D., Inorganic Chemistry, 1980 — 1984

    Worked in the laboratory of Professor Marshall D. Gates (synthesizing methylated derivatives of morphine) while serving as an organic chemistry teaching assistant and considering which research group to join. Briefly considered becoming a synthetic organic chemist, but opted to pursue a Ph.D. in inorganic/organometallic chemistry under the supervision of Professor William D. Jones. Thesis research focused on exploring the molecular-level details of "C-H activation" by transition-metal complexes and established most of the fundamental knowledge related to C-H activation by cyclopentadienyl complexes of rhodium.

  • Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute

    B.S., Chemistry, 1976 — 1980

    Broad training in physical sciences and chemistry. Early specialization in organic chemistry. Undergraduate research in Physical Organic Chemistry (freshman and sophomore years with Professors Stanley Bunce and Harry Herbrandson), Polymer Chemistry (junior year with Professor James A. Moore) and Synthetic Organic Chemistry (senior year with Professor Arthur G. Schultz). Senior thesis focused on the total synthesis of Aspidosperma alkaloids.
