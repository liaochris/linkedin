Maria Teresa Fernandez’s Summary

• Mariposa, a.k.a. María Teresa Fernández, has performed at colleges and universities throughout the United States and abroad; the Nuyorican Poets Café & the Joseph Papp Public Theater; El Instituto de Cultura Puertorriqueña & the Nuyorican Café, Old San Juan, PR; The United Nations World Conference Against Racism, Durban, South Africa; the 2001 Essence Music Fest in New Orleans where she, in the words of Essence Magazine’s Susan Taylor, “mesmerized an audience of 5,000" and V-Day Harlem, Eve Ensler’s Vagina Monologues at The Apollo Theater, co-produced by Rosie Perez and Lisa Gaye Hamilton.

• Featured on the critically acclaimed HBO documentary “Americanos: Latino Life in the US," produced by Edward James Olmos/Time Warner. Other film credits include the Lifetime TV Documentary “V Day: Until the Violence Stops,” produced by Eve Ensler and HBO Latino's show, Habla, scheduled to air September 2008.

• Author of Born Bronxeña: Poems on Identity, Love & Survival and has published articles and poetry in the Centro Journal, The Hostos Review, Urban Latino Magazine, New York Newsday, El Nuevo Dia, Resistance in Paradise: 100 Years of U.S. Colonialism & Def Poetry Jam’s Bumrush the Page.


• Co-produced, with (twin sister) poet & artist melleSOL, several spoken word/open mic/poetry series in Manhattan and The Bronx such as “Sol in the Heights" at Cafe Largo, "Sol Sistahs from the Underground" at the Nuyorican Poets Cafe, “Sol in El Barrio” and “BX Bohemia.”

Maria Teresa Fernandez’s Specialties:

Non-profits; youth development; conflict resolution; special education - behavior disorders; education - teaching poetry and creative writing; writing and research on issues of identity, gender, race, civil rights; editing; publishing; ghostwriting, speech writing; community organizing.
