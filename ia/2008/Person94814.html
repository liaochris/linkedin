  • Founder / President / Chief Engineer

    EnterZone Network Services

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Computer & Network Security industry)

    1997 — 2006 (9 years)

    As the founder and more specifically the Chief Engineer of EnterZone, John designed and operated the world class high-security datacenter facilities and ATM/IPv4/IPv6 networks. EnterZone operated both IPv4 and IPv6 production networks, participated in the 6bone world-wide IPv6 deployment testbed and was the 16th network to be assigned production IPv6 address space by ARIN. John conceived and operated the EZ-IP dynamic DNS service including all DNS architecture providing service to more than 30,000 clients world-wide. John also founded, designed and operated CMH-IX (The Columbus Internet Exchange Point) - an open, multi-lateral peering point allowing NSPs and ISPs to peer directly through a multi-gigabit fabric. John wrote custom code for the Zebra BGP routing suite to provide the Route-Server functionality for the redundant peering route-servers operated by CMH-IX. These are but a few highlights of the nearly ten years in which John was at the helm of EnterZone.

  • Senior Engineer / Imagineer

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Defense & Space industry)

    Integral in the design and testing of the Next Generation encrypted space communications platform known as the LinkIP/LinkXX - currently used by the US DEA and other US Govt. entities. Designed hardware (physical) and network (OTA) authentication schemes required to eliminate man-in-the-middle and signals intercept compromise vectors. Integral in the design, engineering and tuning of RF segment sharing implementation known as DAMA (Demand Assigned Multiple Access).
