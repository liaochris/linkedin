Morris Olmsted’s Summary

I started out as an environmental artist at Idol Minds, a small independent studio in Boulder, CO in 1999. During my time there, we managed to ship 4 titles in the four year period I was there. This is where I first started to dabble in the dark arts called Technical Art. Because of my involvement with the Alias|wavefront and gamming community, I was named one of 43 top Maya Mel Developers by PixelCreation in 2002. After four years, I found myself at Rockstar San Diego as a full time Technical Artist. I really learned a lot about how far Maya could be pushed and significantly improved my knowledge and development of pipeline and tools for production. While at Rockstar, I had the privilege to be a guest speaker at GDC 2004 where Wade Schin and I gave a talk called "Maya for Games - Tools & Pipelines in Maya." After an intense year at Rockstar I left with ten other people to help form a new THQ studio called Concrete Games. Again this was a great learning experience as I was able to be part of the entire development path of all game production from scratch. Here I also had the opportunity to become a lead and learn about its ups and downs; however with the Technical Art team that I was responsible for, I couldn’t have been happier. After a little over three and a half years, the studio closed and I moved over to BottleRocket Entertainment, literally just down the road.

4 shipped titles:
- PS2 - My Street Environment Artist/Technical Artist
- PS2 - Coolboarders 2001 Environment Artist/Technical Artist
- PS1 - Coolboarders 2001 Environment Artist
- PS1 - Coolboarders 4 Environment Artist
