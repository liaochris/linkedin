Masaru IKEDA’s Summary

Masaru started contributing to IT magazines at 12 years old.

After entering the university, which was just at the launching time of Internet, based on the request by Kazuhiko Nishi, ASCII Corp.’s founder and president at that time, Masaru started research on IT industries in Asian nations and Silicon Valley, and went on trips for covering new technologies/services and interviewing the people there frequently.

Masaru has also many experiences as a technical architect and engineer, and he’s well versed in designing the computer system as well. Masaru achieved network platform for a giant nationwide ISP, and also served as a technical advisor for a radio program of Nippon Broadcasting System.

Masaru has lots of knowledges and intelligence on cutting-edge technologies and innovative services using Internet, he also provided the due-diligence evaluation on IT start-ups at a venture capital firm in Tokyo.

In 2002, Masaru co-founded Skywill Inc., a company sending technical consultants, programmers and engineers to the giant system integrators such as Fujitsu, NEC, NTT Data etc.

As well as the managing director’s role in the company, Masaru has been contributing columns to the nationwide newspapers, teaching open source based system integration to the overseas students in the Japan’s national development aid program, and involved in the project developing a cellphone-based wallet system by Japan’s largest cellphone operator.
