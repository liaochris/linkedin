Koen Kam’s Summary

If internet teleporting was invented today, I would have my own internet teleporting company setup tomorrow.

I like to found new companies based on innovative internet technology. High tech stuff that is cool and new, but hasn't been tipped to the big masses yet.

I like internet projects that have scale. Big internet projects. I don't agree with Donald Trump: "Big Is The Same as Small". Big definitely is not the same as small when you're talking internet, innovation and fun.

Koen Kam’s Specialties:

Programming, innovation, entrepeneurship, management by beer :-)

At the TU Delft I programmed Turbo Pascal, xbase, assembler, C++, Lisp.
I did about 10 years of VB/ASP/C++/MSSQL and all that stuff, and about 5 years of PHP programming. At first I was a little sceptic about PHP as it appears to be a very dirty language. Luckily, a lot of great frameworks and patterns have been ported from the Java world to PHP which makes it excellent for creating large scale websites.

  • Cofounder and IT/programming

    HubHop

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry)

    March 2002 — March 2004 (2 years 1 month)

    It was a lot of fun to do HubHop. At HubHop we build our own community of public wifi hotspots. In 2003, we sold HubHop to KPN.

    Other cofounders: Boris Veldhuijzen van Zanten en Caspar Wenckebach.

    I have fond memories of the development of the HubHop Access Point, running the community and doing a lot of fun things with the other founders.

    I liked working at KPN for a couple of months, to see what it is like to be part of a bigger organisation instead of a startup. I never had a job at a big company before. The dynamics at a big company are quite different from the dynamics of a startup.

  • Cofounder

    Websilon

    (Public Company; 11-50 employees; Internet industry)

    April 1999 — December 2001 (2 years 9 months)
