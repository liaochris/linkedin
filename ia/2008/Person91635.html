Jim Poulette’s Summary

Sr. Mechanical Engineer, Sr. Design Engineer

Project and program management, outsourcing coordination, and design qualification which normally accompanies any senior level mechanical engineering design effort.

Current projects include "from scratch" design and performance validation of multiple element, linear actuated, air cooled, zoom collimators used for fiber laser beam manipulation.

Jim Poulette’s Specialties:

Bringing "concepts to reality", Design Validation, Thermal Analysis, Structural Analysis, Deformation analysis from Vacuum and Thermal Stress Forces, Mechanism Design, Passive Athermalization for Hostile Environments, X-ray system design and containment, Optics (refractive, reflective, and catadioptric), and Ruggedization using Solidworks, COSMOS, FloWorks, MATLAB, and Pro-Engineer. Casual user of ZEMAX & Adobe CS3.

Jim Poulette’s Experience

  • General Partner

    Cheshire Analytical LLP

    (Mechanical or Industrial Engineering industry)

    June 1996 — Present (12 years 5 months)

    Ongoing since 1996, RPI based Cheshire Analytical, located in western New Hampshire, provides thermal analysis, structural design validation, and novel passive athermalization techniques for applications in precision mechanisms, astronomical devices, and opto-mechanical / optronic apparatus. Applications include land based, airborne, shipboard, submersible, ground mobile, and human wearable.

  • Sr. Mechanical Engineer

    Janos Technology Inc.

    (Mechanical or Industrial Engineering industry)

    October 2005 — February 2008 (2 years 5 months)

    Opto-mechanical design of refractive and catadiotric lens assemblies, manual and motorized focus and FOV mechanisms, and mirror mounting structures. Analysis of the deformation of large optical elements (germanium and silicon) under vacuum forces during diamond turning operations. Project management tasks related to the refractive lens manufacturing and QA, mirror outsourcing, mechanical fabrication outsourcing, drive components selection and purchasing, and the design and outsourcing of metrology fixtures for internal final design validation.

  • Sr. Mechanical Engineer

    Kollmorgen Electro-Optical

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; Defense & Space industry)

    October 2002 — October 2005 (3 years 1 month)

    Sr. mechanical engineer assigned to the optronics cell - mechanical design and analysis tasks related to the upgrade and support of the optronics mast used in the USN Virginia class submarine. Extensive use of Pro-Engineer and Eigner. Specific tasks included analysis, life testing, and thermal profiling of miniature DC motors used to move optical elements (filters and lenses) within the optical path in the mast head. Project management tasks related to managing and documenting drive component (motors) test data for submission the the USN. Contributed to a major presentation to the USN on the redesign, and dynamic thermal analysis (including sea-water cooling and solar load), of the KEO optronic mast head.

  • Sr. Mechanical Engineer

    Kaiser Systems, Inc.

    (Electrical/Electronic Manufacturing industry)

    June 2000 — October 2002 (2 years 5 months)

    Design of an x-ray generating device mounted on a rotating gantry used in luggage scanning applications. Specific task included thermal management of the x-ray tube, collimation of the fan beam, stray x-ray containment, and precise focal point positioning and validation during assembly. Program management tasks included intimate customer interaction during the design activity, compliance with X-ray regulatory criteria for human radiological exposure, and test/documentation of X-ray containment validation for final submission to the customer.

  • Sr. Mechanical Engineer

    Raytheon Electronic Systems

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; RTN; Defense & Space industry)

    June 1997 — June 2000 (3 years 1 month)

    Sr. mechanical engineer tasks related to the THAAD and XBR programs.

  • Sr. Mechanical Engineer

    Lockheed Martin LMMFC

    (Public Company; 11-50 employees; LMT; Defense & Space industry)

    January 1992 — June 1997 (5 years 6 months)

    Thermal management and mechanical packaging of miniature RF transponder modules for use on the THAAD program, including substrate layouts, die on board metholodogies, planar interconnect schemes, and custom cavity/channel shielded enclosures.

  • Mechanical Engineer

    Espey Mfg. & Electronics Corp.

    (Electrical/Electronic Manufacturing industry)

    May 1981 — January 1992 (10 years 9 months)

    Mechanical design, MIL-grade level III documentation, and assembly / test support of military grade high voltage (voltages to -45kV), liquid cooled, power supplies and power converters used on railroad locomotives and for shipboard and ground mobile applications. Mechanical design of high voltage magnetic components including transformers, inductors, and divider assemblies.
