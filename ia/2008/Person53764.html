Douglas Keeney’s Summary

The magic of marketing is to induce consumers to do what you want them to do when you want them to do it -- to get a person to take an action when you want them to take it. David Ogilvy said "its not creative unless it sells," and that's where I started my marketing career. I worked for 15 years in ad agencies in Los Angeles and New York and then two years on the client side before starting the first of more than six companies, three of which went public. What's a great idea? I've sold millions of collar stays and thousands of shoe shine kits and created products for cereal boxes. My first customer for my first product was Neiman-Marcus. I've launched a hugely successful super premium ice cream, wi-fi, and an online digital photo processor. So -- what's a good idea and what's a bad one?
