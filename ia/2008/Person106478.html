Pavel Khokhryakov’s Summary

Pavel Khokhryakov graduated with distinction from Moscow Economic and Statistical Institute in 1994 majoring in Economics and Finance.

He joined Inkombank, the second largest Russian bank at the time, during his studies at the Institute in 1993. By 1997 he was promoted to a position of Executive Director in Investment Banking Division of the bank. During his work for Inkombank Pavel also taught courses in Banking, Accounting and Audit at Moscow Economic and Statistical Institute and wrote a textbook on accounting in Russian banks.

In 1999 he joined Promsvyazbank, one of top-20 Russian banks, to lead its international operations. By 2002 he was promoted to the position of the Board Member, Director of International Business and Vice President for his achievements in making the bank one of the leaders in international business on the Russian market.

Pavel is quoted in numerous articles of business newspapers and magazines, such as The Banker, The Economist, Euromoney, Vedomosti, Kommersant, Kariera, SmartMoney, Profil, etc.

Pavel received professional certificates from the Central Bank of Russia (banking audit) and Russian Ministry of Finance (securities), as well as certificates of business training from various institutions, such as Bankers Trust, Oxford University, KPMG, etc.

Pavel studied at Stanford Graduate School of Business in 2002-2004 and in June 2004 graduated with an MBA degree. During the summer of 2003 he worked for Citigroup in Corporate Finance.

After study at Stanford Pavel returned to PromSvyazCapital Group, which is a private equity fund with investments in various industries, including financial services, machinery, retail, real estate, mass media, food processing, telecommunications, IT, agriculture, etc. Total amount of assets under management of PromSvyazCapital exceeded 6 bln USD in 2007.

In December 2004 he was promoted to the position of President and CEO of PromSvyazCapital group.
