Nick Texidor’s Summary

Where do I start? Is there enough space? :^) Well, they said to Summarise, so here I go:

- Been in IT for 20+ years starting as Trainee Computer Operator on Wang Computers
- Sidestepped over to COBOL programming about 3 years later, mainly in Insurance
- Worked in Oxford, London, Brighton (in UK) before moving to Australia.
- Directed a three-tier client-server project for major book publisher
- Sidestepped into Web Development in 1996, starting with ASP coding
- In '97 worked for major web dev company in Sydney, highlight was being one of the driving forces behind a large web-based publishing system for a national TV station.
- Became partner in my own web dev company, TV station (voluntarily) came with me!!
- Developed many database-driven, dynamic web sites in ColdFusion. Became an Allaire-recommended developer.
- I'm a self-confessed Mac nut, but have a very solid background in Windows, Linux and Mac. Am comfortable supporting all three if required, but my heart is all Mac now. :^)

After a huge project and the birth of my daughter, I started to feel a little burnt out and jaded with the web industry. It turned into the new 'desktop publishing' industry, i.e. anyone from home could throw a page together and build a 'web site'. I wound down my Web Dev company, preferring to do work on a freelance basis, and then fell into my 'dream' job... I have been running a DVD/CD/Game store for the last three years, in parallel to maintaining some web sites for clients.

Over the last 12 months, I have rekindled my relationship with the web, and have started to pick up the pace again. I have been interested in the whole Joomla thing, and have recently released a couple of modules/components which are listed on the Joomla extensions directory.

Now I'm looking for my next gig. I can code, I can run a business (including employing & managing staff), it's just finding something that fits!

Nick Texidor’s Specialties:

IT: Trained in 'art' of structured programming. COBOL, VB, ASP, Coldfusion, PHP. Good hardware knowledge. Comfortable using Mac, Windows, Linux.

Retail: Employing & managing staff. Buying stock. Sales. Day to day Accounts. Day-to-day running of shop.

General: Strong points would be loyal; honest; trustworthy; hard working; fast learner; good analysis & problem solving skills; ability to work well in a team, or alone; good people skills at all levels (so I'm told).
