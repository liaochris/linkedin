Elizabeth Hammen’s Summary

Career goal: to serve as a liaison across scales between human use of the environment and habitat conservation through ecological planning and sustainable land use practices.


Recent projects:

STEWARDSHIP MASTER PLAN, Katonah, NY
•Developed a master plan for a 171-acre sanctuary in Westchester County, NY that addressed water quality, erosion control, invasive plant species management, ecological restoration, and design of a multi-use trail system. The final plan included a phased action plan, cost estimates, and stormwater and erosion control construction details.

OPEN SPACE AND RECREATION PLAN, Ashburnham, MA
•Researched and wrote an Open Space and Recreation Plan for the 25,000-acre town of Ashburnham, MA. The report included GIS maps and a five-year action plan based on current conditions, citizen participation, and preservation of the town’s natural, cultural, and historic resources.

PRIVATE RESIDENCE, Buckland, MA
•Designed a site plan for a 1700's farm house on five-acres in western Massachusetts that incorporated native plants, onsite water conservation, permaculture principles, and organic food production.
