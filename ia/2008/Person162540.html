Robert Stinnett’s Summary

My past is filled with a wide variety of experience and accomplishments. I've been lucky enough to experience everything from mainframe computers, to servers so small you'd think they were a paperweight. However, no matter what platform I have used I've always been looking towards the future. Today, I believe that future lies in the "clouds" -- or cloud computing. It's one of my main interests and passions. I can see a time when IT data centers no longer exist in the way we know them today -- instead, they are massively huge complexes that interconnect to form computing clouds that Enterprises large and small connect into.

I also come from the school of thought that business plays a crucial role in IT. Today, IT departments are run as if they are the business, with so many people forgetting that any change they make -- no matter how small -- can have serious implications on the business they support. That is why I believe in Business Service Management (BSM), and have used extensively the products from BMC Software to manage BSM. This includes Performance Manager, Patrol, Impact Manager, Service Impact Manager and the CMDB.

There is also a part of me that sees a new future for what we used to call batch scheduling. Today, I prefer to call it business process scheduling. Except, the batch world of today doesn't exist -- instead products like CONTROL-M which I have used for years are helping drive data across the company and across the world. They react to events and triggers both internal and external to process and manage work flows across different systems and geographic boundaries. In the future the ability to manage work flows will become even more important as we move into the cloud.

It's going to be a great time for technology -- and I'm lucky enough to be along for the ride.
