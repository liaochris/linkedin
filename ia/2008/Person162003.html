Robert Cain (rob@sparkLLC.com)’s Summary

• Conceived and co-founded media ventures which have raised nearly $200 million in VC funding; Made strategic recommendations which led to MGM’s $825 million investment in Rainbow Holdings.

• Finance, development and distribution executive on numerous theatrical films over an 18 year period, including “The Usual Suspects,” “Breakdown,” “Bound,” “Blade 2,” and “Cabin Fever.”

• Advised numerous corporate clients including such global leaders as Universal Studios, Sony Pictures, the BBC, NBC, DirecTV, MGM, Kodak, Mattel, New Line Cinema, the Screen Actors Guild and many others.

• Special areas of expertise include: valuation and financial modeling; mergers & acquisitions; new ventures; domestic and international business development; detailed business and financial plans.

Have worked for or consulted to more than 40 entertainment companies on 4 continents, in roles including CEO, CFO, COO and Consultant. As President of Venture Spark, LLC, I've advised production companies in executing strategies to leverage their strengths and expand their operations.

My film experience extends from finance and distribution management to creative duties on more than a dozen successful films, including such critical and commercial successes as “The Usual Suspects,” “Breakdown,” “Bound” and “Cabin Fever.” I Executive Produced the adult comedy "The Amateurs," the drama “Laura Smiles,” the action epic “Mongol,” (nominated for a 2008 Academy Award), animated film "Foodfight," Co-Produced "The Grand Design" starring Eric Stoltz, and was Consulting Producer of "Shanghai Kiss," starring Kelly Hu and Hayden Panetierre.

I am also recognized as an emerging markets specialist, having led international film and TV projects in Russia, China, India, Mexico, and Korea. I have also lived and worked in Hong Kong, Taiwan, Italy, Canada and the U.K. I hold a bachelor’s degree with honors from Harvard University, and an MBA with honors from Wharton Business School.
