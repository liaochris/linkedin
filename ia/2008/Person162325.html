Roberto Abramowitz’s Experience

  • Editor in Chief

    Fútbol Mundial

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Publishing industry)

    June 2002 — Present (5 years 10 months)

    I'm the founding Editor-In-Chief of what is now the largest Spanish language men's magazine in the US with an audited circulation of over 1 million copies and readership of close to 3 million.
    I'm also the Managing Editor of our other titles, Béisbol Mundial which is also written in Spanish and has a similar reach as Fútbol Mundial, and Fútbol Mundial USA which is our first English language publication. 'FM-USA' is distributed inside USA Today's Sports Weekly six times a year and has a circulation of over 250,000 and readership of over 2.8 million.

  • Sportscaster

    ESPN Deportes and ESPN International

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; dis; Sports industry)

    May 1994 — Present (13 years 11 months)

    I'm a Spanish Language Sportscaster/Analyst for a variety of sports.
    I presently broadcast the NFL, NBA, Euroleague (basketball) professional bowling and pool (I was also lead play-by-play for the NHL when we owned the rights).
    I'm also a regular member Cronómetro (ESPN's sports issue discussion show) and a host of ESPN Deportes Radio weekend morning show (show ended in May of 2007).

  • Sports Broadcaster

    New York Knicks/Monday Night Football/Army Football and more

    (Privately Held; Security and Investigations industry)

    April 1994 — Present (14 years)

    I've been a freelance sportscaster since 1994 starting with the first ever broadcast in Spanish on radio for the New York Knicks.
    Since then I've done a variety of events which include, New York Rangers Hockey (2006), Monday Night Football, BCS College Football, NHL All Star Game, NBA Basketball on ABC (via SAP in Spanish), NHL Hockey on FOX, and boxing, Real Sports, De la Hoya Mayweather 27/7 and Hard Knocks on HBO (via SAP in Spanish).
    Presently I still call games for the New York Knicks, Army Football and Monday Night Football (Westwood One/Univisión Radio).

  • Commercial Voiceovers

    Self-employed

    (Self-Employed; Myself Only; Broadcast Media industry)

    1994 — Present (14 years)

    I've done voiceovers/commercials in Spanish for, among others: McDonald's, Thera-Flu, Tropicana, and ABC Sports.
    Presently I'm the voice for all Spanish language commercials for the NBA, and I've done extensive work for the NFL.
    I was also the Spanish language voice for News Broadcast Network from 2002 to 2005.
