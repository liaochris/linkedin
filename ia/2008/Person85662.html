Jay Bedecarre’s Experience

  • Director of Communications

    Home Builders Association of Northern California

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Real Estate industry)

    March 2004 — January 2007 (2 years 11 months)

    Managed marketing communications and public relations for trade association representing homebuilders and related industries in 14 counties of greater San Francisco Bay Area. Developed and implemented
    publication of monthly magazine, three Association directories and two weekly email newsletters; design and content management of website and weblog; coordination of media relations for staff, board and member experts; executive producer of weekly program on KNBR Radio securing nationally-prominent guests, and development of Association`s internal and external communications strategy. Redesigned magazine, website and e-letters, more than doubling the circulation of each. Produced acclaimed HBANC video Who Are We? Supervised nearly $1million in annual advertising and sponsorship sales. Managed membership and events departments for 18 months during management transition. Created largest, most successful events in organization history at AT&T Park and Moscone Center.

  • President

    Lewis Benedict Bedecarre Advertising & Public Relations

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    January 1987 — February 2004 (17 years 2 months)

    Supervised creative, production and media buying of television, radio, print, direct mail, websites and collateral for diverse client roster at full service advertising agency. Major industry categories were homebuilding, entertainment, legal, sports, trade show and hospitality. Agency roster also included mortgage companies, automobile dealerships, recreation, travel agencies, radio stations and resorts. Principal agency contact with clients and media. Coordinated research, special events and strategic planning. Marketed sales of over 25,000 new homes throughout California and Nevada for a
    dozen public and private homebuilders.

  • Marketing Director

    Concord Pavilion

    (Government Agency; 201-500 employees; Entertainment industry)

    October 1974 — December 1986 (12 years 3 months)

    Diverse management experience at preeminent outdoor performing arts, sports and entertainment center. Directed marketing, programming, public relations, advertising, box office sales and promotions. Increased annual revenue from $650,000 to $10+ million. Planned $2.6 million annual media budget. Supervised
    nine full-time and 35 part-time employees. Negotiated artist and media contracts, secured corporate sponsorships (Anheuser-Busch, JVC, Fujitsu, Ford, Pepsi, See's Candies, Toyota, Chevron, IBM and others), served as organization spokesman to media and public, edited and published publications, coordinated ticket sales and telemarketing, wrote press releases and prepared grant proposals.
