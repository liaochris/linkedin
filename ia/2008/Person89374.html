Jeremy Nel’s Summary

1988 to 1999 - CEO and shareholder of Intertoll, a South African based international Toll Road oganisation operating in South Africa, Brazil, the USA, Central Europe, India and Bangladesh. Participated in all aspects of over 10 infrastructure development projects, and bid on several more. Did an MBO in 1997 and sold out in 1999.
1999 to 2001 - Venture capital investments in South Africa. Started MegaWan, a wireless WMAN ISP in Johannesburg, in April 1999. Built WMAN in 6 major metropolitan areas in Johannesburg. Did consulting for an American corporation in India and the middle east on water, waste water and desalination projects, successfully negotiating participation in a waste water project in India.
2001 to 2007- did consulting work for NTR plc in Dublin, Ireland on PPP Toll Road projects. Investigated the possibility of wireless broadband in Ireland. Found a suitable regulatgory framework and proceeded to do a feasibility study with NTR plc. In May 2002 met with my partner, Paul Doody and founded Irish Broadband Internet Services Ltd. with NTR plc as the main financier. Currently the largest wireless network owner in Ireland after the mobile phone companies.
Present - Am on the Board of Irish Broadband and still an investor, but after 5 years it is time to move on as the business has matured. Doing general consulting work for a while before deciding what next!

Jeremy Nel’s Specialties:

Excellent skills in starting new businesses across a variety of industries. Involved in various aspects from strategy, funding through to technical deployment and daily management of the business. Broad general understanding of most technical aspects enabling me to have an excellent grasp of the strategic aspects of the industry.
