Chris Marentis’s Summary

With over twenty-five years experience in media, marketing, and technology, Chris has a proven track record building businesses in turnaround, start-up and high growth situations. He has successfully created and implemented strategic plans, forged high level strategic relationships, and bridged marketing and media solutions with cutting edge technology.

Chris was most recently President and COO at FortiusOne, a next-generation intelligent mapping solutions technology company. Brought in by the founder and board to lead the company’s next generation product launch and operationalize/scale the business, FortiusOne launches the next generation GeoCommons 2.0 platform in April '08.

Prior to FortiusOne, Chris was CEO at Clearspring Technologies, a content/widget syndication platform for the distributed web. At Clearspring, Chris led the company from its seed funding to becoming the leading media platform for the distributed web. When recruited by the initial investors, Chris was tasked with setting up the company, establishing and implementing a strategy and recruiting a strong team. Chris is currently an adviser to the company and remains an investor.

At AOL, Chris was SVP responsible for new product development in the Interactive Marketing group. While there, Chris formulated a plan to create a new addressable marketing platform, delivering data/targeting services, message delivery services, and optimization systems that are being implemented today. Prior to that, Chris was SVP of Business Development and co-lead the AOL business development efforts. Chris is recognized for creating the “Anchor Tenant” content strategy, and developing strategic content relationships that generated over $500 million annually.

Earlier in his career, Chris held executive positions at many large premiere media, marketing and Internet companies. As the founder and CEO of Retail Sports Television, he has experience building a business from the ground-up.

Chris Marentis’s Specialties:

Business strategy (particularly around technology, media and marketing) that will maximize enterprise value. Creating high performance teams. Identifying and forging strategic relationships.
