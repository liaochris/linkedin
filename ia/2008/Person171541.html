Ron Aberdeen’s Summary

I started writing screenplays in March 2005 and posting them on the Script Web site, InkTip.

By July 05, I received my first commission to pen a feature script for an established Canadian 'Horror' Director/Producer, in October I received a commission to write a Sci-Fi screenplay for a UK Producer. After both had read my work on InkTip.

Both films go into production this year and will give me my first IMDB credits.

In April 2006, I received by first option, for my horror script, The Box, from Covent Garden Films.

So far I have completed eight original screenplays – ‘Boudicca’ – ‘American Genocide’ – ‘The Box’ – ‘The Script Writer’ – ‘Recompense’ – ‘The Jaguar’ – ‘Missing Presumed Dead’ and ‘The 13th Sign’.

Also five joint script projects, ‘Kane Farm’ written with Ian Noakes, ‘The Gates of Hell’ and 'Out of Nowhere' with Miriam Paschal and 'Stilettos' and ‘Lifeboat’ with novelist John Fiore.

I recently retired from business, where I was known in many industries for my individual creativity, with a business reputation for effective solutions through conceptional management, including taglines, original advertising concepts and magazine articles.

But this is more fun.

Keeping busy is the secret of keeping busy.

Personally, I am a musician, poet, artist and now a scriptwriter with my foot on the first (maybe second) rung of the Screenplay writers ladder.

I am sixty years young and growing old disgracefully. I’ve got the drive and imagination of people half my age because I don’t keep taking the pills. It also helps me to work fast. This is a new career for me and I will succeed.

My first year as a scriptwriter has brought some early success. Now I am hungry to build on that. I am flexible and happy to amend my screenplays to fit your criteria, after all, the customer is always right.
