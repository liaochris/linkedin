Glen Ivey’s Summary

A full-time telecommuter since 1997.

Software architect/developer with experience across all size projects, all levels of development from assembly language and hardware control to desktop and web-based UIs, across a broad range of industries and technologies, and including some hardware design. Most effective working on architecture and high-level design with some time spent coding; as fluent in English and specifications as in Java and C++.

Glen Ivey’s Specialties:

Java, C/C++, Perl. (Debian) Linux. HTML, CSS, XML, DocBook, JavaScript, MPEG, JPEG, Palm OS 3.

  • Staff Software Engineer

    DiviCom, Inc.

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; Telecommunications industry)

    1999 — 2000 (1 year)

    While I was at DiviCom, it was a wholly-owned subsidiary of C-Cube (hence the overlap in dates employed).

    Software for a new rack-mount digital video processing platform based on a compact-PCI backplane using VxWorks. In addition to MPEG-specific coding, worked extensively on design for coordinating multiple independent CPU boards on backplane and for hot-swap of all individual board-level components.

  • Principal Engineer; Software Development Manager

    C-Cube Microsystems, Inc.

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; Computer Hardware industry)

    1992 — 2000 (8 years)

    Three years as first-line manager for internal tools development group. Up to 9 engineers responsible for development of tools used for development and QA testing of C-Cube's ICs and embedded software and for definition of internal development board-level hardware. Simultaneously program manager for the CL480, C-Cube's second-generation MPEG1 A/V decoder IC, primarily used in video games and "VideoCD" players in the China and Asia markets.

    As an individual developer: 1) Wrote and maintained production embedded assembly-language firmware for CL450 MPEG1 video decoder IC; customer visits for both support of existing product and definition of future software features and follow-on ICs. 2) Responsible for WinNT drivers and application software for firmware development/test for C-Cube's first single-chip MPEG video encoder. 3) Developed GUI application for prototyping DVD player front-panel, remote control, UI screens and behavior; automated firmware source generation.
