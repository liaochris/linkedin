Alberto Campagnolo’s Summary

Library and Archive Conservator with speciality skills and knowledge in the fields of modern materials and preservation projects by digital means. Fluent in Italian and English, able to act as a translator and cultural advisor. Versatile experience in project managment and project evaluation. Active interest in the new technologies and their application to the fields of Cultural Heritage.

Language skills: Italian mother tongue; excellent knowledge of written and spoken English; good knowledge (written and spoken) of the International Language Esperanto; basic knowledge of Modern Greek; basic knowledge of Welsh; reading knowledge of French, Spanish, German, Latin, and Ancient Greek.

IT skills: excellent understanding of web based researches and of use of the Internet in general, including Electronic Mail; excellent knowledge of Microsoft® Office programs (Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Publisher, and Access); good understanding of database theory and its applications - design of a relational database with Microsoft® Access; good understanding of digital photography, imaging and software applications; good understanding of (X)HTML and basic understanding of XML.

Alberto Campagnolo’s Specialties:

Library and archive materials conservation and preservation;
Experience in the issues related to the conservation/preservation of modern library materials;
Book repair techniques and projects;
Digitization of documents both for preservation purposes and access;
Digital culture and technologies.

  • Corso Europeo di Formazione Specialistica per Conservatori - Restauratori di Beni Librari

    Conservazione, Conservazione dei Beni Librari, 1998 — 2001

    European Course for Conservators/Restorers of Book Materials - Three-year program focused on the preservation/conservation of books, but also covering other materials (fine art works, archive documents, photographs, parchment, leather, wood, metals). The curriculum was comprehensive, encompassing codicology, preservation, and conservation. Courses were taught by leading specialists, coming from both Italy and abroad; among them Christopher Clarkson, Nicholas Pickwoad, Anthony Cains, Tom and Sylvia Albro, Robert Futernick, John L. Sharpe, Armando Petrucci, Carla Bozzolo, Robert E. Child.
    The program consisted of two years in academic and bench studies, with at least two third-year internships in recognised conservation laboratories both in Italy and abroad. Students were required to have a reading and speaking knowledge of Italian and English. Fifteen students were selected, from a large pool of European applicants, by an examination, every second year. Final examination mark: 98/100

    Activities and Societies:
    History and literature of the Medieval and Renaissance Periods, Latin Palaegraphy, Bibliography and Cataloguing, History of Bookbinding Techniques, History and Analysis of Library Materials, Techniques of Conservation practice I and II, Codicology and Cataloguing of Manuscripts, Study of the Art of the Manuscript and Printed Book, Environmental and Biological Deterioration, Bookbinding Conservation, History and Conservation of Photography, Law in Conservation Fields, Chemistry and Biology.
