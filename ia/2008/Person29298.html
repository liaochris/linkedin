Burl Barer’s Summary

Extensive career launching and utilizing new media.

Synergistic multimedia concert and motion picture advertising and promotion.

Formed the largest independent multi-system cable television advertising interconnect in America. Oversaw a staff of sixteen sales, marketing, and production professionals, while writing and producing hundreds of television commercials for local and national markets.

Developed with Time-Warner and Paramount cross-platform promotional strategies for such major motion pictures as Maverick and The Saint, involving the research and writing of mass-market consumer tie-ins via studio publishing divisions (both domestically and in Israel, Japan, China, France, Germany), along with interactive Web site development.

Created comprehensive electronic media campaigns for such entertainment-based clients as Warner/Elektra/Atlantic Records, Jerry Weintraub Management, and 20th Century Fox, for such artists as Frank Sinatra, Bob Dylan, Eric Clapton, Fleetwood Mac, Billy Joel, the Eagles, and the Moody Blues. These were America’s first nationally coordinated electronic media campaigns for touring performance acts. Created legendary media campaigns for The Sahara and the Aladdin Theater for the Performing Arts in Las Vegas.

Burl Barer’s Specialties:

Content creation and Concept Realization: Edgar Award winning author w/10 books in print, writer/producer of hundreds of electronic ads and promotions, electronic press kits, radio programs, movie trailers, television commercials, print campaigns, and public relations material.
Extensive experiemce (and awards) in Electronic and Print media.

  • radio personality

    KYAC RADIO

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Broadcast Media industry)

    1966 — 1968 (2 years)

    Soul Radio KYAC -- Burl took over afternoon drive after the regular jock lost his temper with a cart deck and screamed obscenities on the air. An instant hit, Barer's show gained "cult" status at the University of Washington, adding an entirely new fan base to KYAC. Barer moved to morning drive where he and Allen Goldblatt created and produced The Adventures of Thomas Jefferson and Alexander Hamilton -- a twisted history characterized by elaborate production (for the time) and horrific puns.

    Barer was made an "Honorary Soul Brother" in an elaborate on stage ceremony during the Motown Review, starring Smokey Robinson, the 4 Tops, the Temptations, Martha Reeves and many more .

    Burl Barer is acknowledged as the first American DJ to play The Jimi Hendrix Experience on the radio.
