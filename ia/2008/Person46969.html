David Moldoff’s Summary

Mr. Moldoff is a life long entrepreneur with over 35 years of experience launching and running technology companies building long term value, ecstatic clients and remarkable solutions. He loves leading the charge, tackling market opportunities - and coming up with novel solutions. He assembles collaborative teams who excel without fear of failure. Over his career, Mr. Moldoff has led the development and launch of over 45 applications including two Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) product lines and continues to work on more.

Mr. Moldoff - beginning in the 70's, was a pioneer in object oriented development, n-tier computing architectures, enterprise application integration (EAI) and application development frameworks. A well-known expert in software architecture, he is a board member on the Postsecondary Education Standards Council and Open Enterprise Application Integration Foundation.

Mr. Moldoff loves to write and has authored transformational papers, including "Higher Education Capacity and Utilization Issues for the 21st Century", “The Economics of Integration and Interoperability;” “Enterprise Architecture Matters;” and “The Information Access Capability Maturity Model.”

Mr. Moldoff is an active Rotarian, current WEGO Foundation President, past president of the WEGO Club, Paul Harris Fellow, Guy Gundaker Fellow and donor to various charities in the Philadelphia region.
