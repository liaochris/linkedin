Jodi Rudick’s Summary

I'm considered one of the nation’s premiere parks and recreation marketing experts and am passionately dedicated to promoting participation, advocacy and support for parks, recreation, camps, libraries and other community services. I've spoken at more than 1200 conferences, workshops, trainings and seminars for recreation professionals and leaders. Also an author, I've just completed my seventh book related to promoting everything from fitness and camps to libraries and recreation.

Since 1959, my company (which was started by my Dad, Art) has been creating innovative promotions and marketing campaigns for clients ranging from Fortune 500 giants to non-profits, government agencies and budding entrepreneurs. My company which incorporated in 1986 has three United States offices in Scottsdale, Arizona; Minneapolis, Minnesota and Southern California. I'm the creative force behind the firm and is responsible for new project development, creative management, training and consultation. In 1996 I coined the phrase "Parks and Recreation: The Benefits Are Endless…" which continues to be one of the most visible branding campaigns in the profession today.

I have an Advertising/Journalism degree from the University of Minnesota and hold the Master Advertising Specialist designation, the highest educational honor in the promotional marketing industry.

My private sector client list includes Motorola, Mazda, Monsanto, the Irvine Companies, Smith Barney and hundreds of others. My goal is to bring entrepreneurial insight trhough training and education into the public sector and non profit organizations.

I was twice was named National Speaker of the Year by the Promotional Products Association. I've also worked for Cal State University San Marcos coordinating their Marketing Certificate Program.

Jodi Rudick’s Specialties:

thinking (and helping other think) way, way way outside the boring brochure or box, speaking, training, writing, customer service

I've written seven books specific to parks and recreation marketing and communications. I also publish The blog "Little Red's Big Ideas (www.littleredsbigideas.typepad.com) and regularly contributor to trade, business and consumer publications.
