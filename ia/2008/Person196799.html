Vincent van Wylick’s Summary

My interests and goals can be split into three segments: long-terms/constant, medium-term, and short-term. In the long-term, some of my interests and goals include: business strategy, starting (a) business(es), marketing, some logistics and finance, telling stories (aka packaging info in an entertaining and comprehensible fashion), and learning.

In the medium-term, i.e. the next 5-15 years, I'm particularly interested in the area of setting up retail- / restaurant- / music-venues, as I plan to dedicate my career to that. I blog on my experiences there on http://foodandretail.blogspot.com.

My other side venture/interest is in the funding-process for high-tech/innovative start-ups and I am writing my thesis on that, though it is much more complex than I expected. I also have a lot of experience blogging and am an occasional writer on exciting technology and business on the blog TechITEasy.org. It's an exciting medium and community and I'm learning a lot there too.

Vincent van Wylick’s Specialties:

strategic planning, business development, entrepreneurial finance, portfolio-management, event-management, information-management, communication, story-telling, virtual teams, publishing, blogging, entertainment.

  • Co-founder

    C-Walls

    (Partnership; 1-10 employees; Paper & Forest Products industry)

    January 2005 — April 2005 (4 months)

    C-walls was a student-project, which we took slightly too seriously. The idea was to develop a business-plan on the basis of a patent from the European Patent Office (Espacenet). Instead we (3 partners) decided to search for business-ideas through our network, found an interesting idea in the shape of cardboard-based office-walls, and tried to convince the paper-industry to go into this new market. In the end it failed because of the low-margins related conservatist attitude in the industry and because it was an ill-fated idea.

    The project involved setting up a strategic plan, marketing study and plan, financial prognosis, and winning a business-plan competition. We came third.
