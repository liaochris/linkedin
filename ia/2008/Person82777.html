James T. Chiles’s Summary

Since 1985, Mr. Chiles has maintained his executive offices in the Bank of America Plaza building, Suite 2600 901 Main Street Dallas, Texas 75202. With financial stability, combined with a long term track record in telecommunications, Chiles has allowed his companies to develop unique fiber and wireless solutions for business and government. Many of his companies were sold. Sprint (1999), AT&T Cellular (1986), Global Signal,Inc.(2001) and most recently Alpheus Communications a joint venture between Genesis Park, LP and the El Paso Corporation (2007).

Mr. Chiles is a "telecommunication executive" with broad experience in fiber optic transmission and wireless spectrum development. His direct experience with broadcast towers, data centers, metro fiber optic networks and wireless spectrum have been the key drivers of his business development activities.

James T. Chiles’s Experience

  • Founder, President and CEO

    Telecommunication Properties, Inc. "TPI"

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Broadcast Media industry)

    December 1985 — Present (22 years 5 months)

    Telecommunication Properties, Inc. ("TPI)" 901 Main Street Suite 2600 Bank of America Plaza Dallas, Texas 75202.

    Mr. Chiles founded TPI in 1985 to operate large scale broadcast tower antenna facilities to serve the television broadcast industry. Our customers included WMAQ-TV, WFAA-TV, KXAS-TV, KTVT-TV and KDFW-TV just to name a few. Through our broadcast transmission facilities, we provide the primary transmitter or ENG points for nearly every television station in Chicago, Dallas and Philadelphia. In 2008, TPI will be opening a 1,367 foot television and FM tower in Philadelphia.

  • President and CEO/Founder

    Aspen Communications, LLC Aquired by Alpheus Communications / El Paso Corporation

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Telecommunications industry)

    January 2001 — March 2007 (6 years 3 months)

    Aspen built its own fiber optic network in and around Dallas/Ft. Worth. Launched company with two data centers. The data centers provided IT and data center operations with 24/7 on site managed services.

    Using public right aways, Aspen built a fiber optic backbone using all Internet Protocol technology. Leveraging the simplicity of this technology Aspen deployed networks using both fiber and wireless to gain access to customers quickly.

  • Founder, President and CEO

    BROADCAST TOWERS, INC. Aquired by Global Signal,Inc. in 2001

    (Public Company; 11-50 employees; BIGT; Wireless industry)

    January 1993 — November 2000 (7 years 11 months)

    Broadcast Towers, Inc .owned and managed over 600 wireless and broadcast towers in 42 states. Mr. Chiles started the company in 1993 and became a NYSE public company in 1996. After the telecom industry fall out, BIGT emerged through bankrupcy. Today, Broadcast Towers, Inc. continues as a privately held company, based in Sarasota, FL. operating broadcast antenna sites nation wide.

  • Founder

    Netlink Communications, Inc. Aquired by Sprint/Nextel in 1999

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Telecommunications industry)

    March 1991 — November 1999 (8 years 9 months)

    In 1991 James Chiles founded Netlink Communications, Inc. Netlink owned and operated 800 Mhz cellular telephone networks in California and Texas. In 1999 James Chiles sold the business and the 800 Mhz spectrum affiliated with the operations to Sprint/Nextel.

  • Founder/Owner

    Liberty Communications, Inc. Aquired by Nextel in 1989

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Telecommunications industry)

    December 1987 — December 1989 (2 years 1 month)

    Chiles founded Liberty Communications, Inc. in 1987. Liberty was originally located at 901 Main Street Suite 3612 Dallas, Texas. Liberty was an operator of 800 Mhz Cellular Telephone Sytems in seven counties serving the FCC's Dallas/Ft. Worth MSA. In December, 1989 Sprint/Nextel Communications purchased 100% of the stock in Liberty Communications and merged it into Sprint/Nextel of Texas.

  • Founder

    CellPage, Inc. Aquired by AT&T Cellular in 1986

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Telecommunications industry)

    January 1984 — September 1985 (1 year 9 months)

    Chiles co-founded CellPage, Inc. along with two Motorola executives in 1984. CellPage, Inc. was an early operator of 800 MHz mobile telephone systems. By 1985, CellPage gained a 35% share of the Dallas/Ft. Worth cellular telephone market. Aquired by Southwestern Bell Mobile Systems in late 1985, Chiles left CellPage to continue wireless spectrum developement as an independent company.

  • National Cellular Manager Infratructure Systems

    Motorola, Inc.

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; MOT; Telecommunications industry)

    July 1977 — December 1983 (6 years 6 months)

    Mr. Chiles joined Motorola, Inc. in July 1977 in the Communication and Electronics division. As a radio communications sales engineer Chiles began selling two-way mobile and portable communications equipment to business and government.
    In 1983, Chiles was promoted to National Cellular Infrastructure Manager and directed the implementation of some of the first cellular telephone systems in the United States. In 1984, Chiles left Motorola with two fellow Motorola executives, James E. Treatch and Bud Macausland to form a cellular telephone company CellPage, Inc.
