Stuart Urban’s Summary

I had my first film shown at the Cannes Film Festival in 1972 at 14. After graduating from Oxford, I worked as writer, director and producer. Among popular TV drama I have done is The Bill and Bergerac. My BBC drama An Ungentlemanly Act, dramatising the first 36 hours of the Falklands War, starring Ian Richardson and Bob Peck, won a BAFTA as Best Single Drama and many international awards.

In 1995, I directed Our Friends in the North, the most successful drama for 15 years on BBC2, taking several BAFTAs including Best Drama. I cast the then hardly known Daniel Craig in his first major role.

In 1995, I wrote the award-winning HBO/BBC movie Deadly Voyage. In 1997 I wrote, produced and directed cult film Preaching to the Perverted, theatrically released worldwide (www.preachingtotheperv.com). In 1999, Harold Pinter co-wrote with me, and I produced and directed Against the War, a hard-hitting documentary attacking the NATO bombing of Serbia for the BBC and Cyclops Vision.

In 2001 I co-produced, wrote and directed Revelation, a Cyclops Vision Production for Romulus Films. This mystical/supernatural thriller stars Terence Stamp and Udo Kier and concerns the quest to locate and understand a relic that heralds the fusion of science and religion. Budget is $10.5 million and theatrically released in UK and overseas, sold to 24 countries (www.revelation-movie.com).

I have directed extensively in factual television, most recently for BBC’s Panorama where I did the much-publicised 2005 edition Blair v Blair which covered civil liberties and terrorism.

Tovarisch I Am Not Dead (www.tovarisch.net) is a theatrical feature documentary about my father, Garri, who survived and escaped from both the Holocaust and the Gulag. The film was 14 years in the making and is touring festivals in 2007.

In the corporate sector I have directed many award winning videos for Glaxo, ICI etc.

Stuart Urban’s Specialties:

An all-round film-maker or "hyphenate", I am primarily a director but happy in most roles and, as technology progresses, found myself operating camera again on BBC Panorama and my own feature documentary. I speak French, Italian, Spanish and Serbocroat with basic Hebrew and some Russian.
