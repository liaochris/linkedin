Jeffrey Toy’s Summary

I am a student awaiting to graduate from UC Davis by the end of March 2008 and am currently seeking a Financial Analyst career opportunity. As an International Relations and Economics double major student at University of California - Davis, I have studied intermediate micro- and macroeconomics with an emphasis on interest rates, theories, and concepts of international micro- and macroeconomics with an emphasis on exchange rates, and the principles of public finance, corporate finance, tax analysis, the bond market, and financial institutions. Recently, I have also taken Financial and Managerial Accounting courses. During the last school year, I served as an assistant to the Collections Manager of the Accounting and Financial Services Department of UC Davis, by handling the auditing and processing of confidential federal student loans.

Over the past summer, I interned at Enterprise Rent-A-Car as a salaried Regional Accountant. My responsibilities included performing work on the Accounts Receivable and Accounts Payable for all the branches in the San Francisco Region as well as creating expense fluctuation analysis. From June to December of 2006, I was an intern for Morgan Stanley and worked with a Senior Vice-President Financial Advisor. From this internship, I learned the importance of keeping up with business news, the principles and concepts of the stock, bond, and mutual funds markets, and put in practice fundamental, quantitative, and technical analysis towards the research of publicly traded companies. For the past four years, I had an internship at a bookkeeping firm, Logan & Associates. This salaried internship had enlightened me with accounting concepts and trained me to generate and analyze financial statements. My administrative experience includes writing correspondence, generating data, performing data analysis, and handling many other office tasks. I am experienced with Microsoft Office Suite and QuickBooks software.
