Don Nasca’s Summary

Delta designs and manufacturers many many gadgets for the worlds largest electronics companies. Our web site is www.delta.ww.com; however it's not really a marketing site as much as it is a capabilities showcase. I specialize in medium to high volume custom designed power supplies for data centers, computers, printers, consumer products, and medical devices. We are also now introducing Solar Power Inverters.

My prior experience in the power supply industry includes handling aerospace, military, and space applications. I worked in several power supply factories in the states:

Faratron Products, NJ (long gone) as a Technician

ERA Transpac, NJ (long gone) as a Technician

Modular Equipment Corp (long gone) as a Test Department Supervisor.

Transistor Devices in NJ, first at the Test Department Supervisor, then as a Project Design Engineer

Abbott Transistor Labs, NJ (now Martek) as an Applications Engineer

Avionic Instruments, NJ as a Regional Sales Manager

If you are one of the few of us still in the hardware business and you have a product that needs power (10 watts to 10KW), I can probably help you.

Don Nasca’s Specialties:

I help our customers get the best custom power solutions available for the lowest possible price. I manage the language, cultural, and time zone differences of Asia an Europe and have a good understanding of the hardware development process, mass production process, and the key technical issues (electrical, mechanical, and environmental) faced by both the application and the designer. Then, I have to close the sale or all this knowledge is not worth talking about.

  • Corporate Sales Manager

I was heavily immersed in Lean Thinking, Toyota Production System, Kanban, Mistake Proofing, 5S, Six-sigma, Black Belt, Visual Factory, Total Productive Maintenance, and Team Building.

    I was part of the executive team that directed the development of new products and the company business strategy. My role was involved in implementing the integrated sales, marketing, and customer service strategy, tactics, and operational activities to achieve our corporate goals.

  • General Manager

    Abaris Systems

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Wireless industry)

    1984 — 1989 (5 years)

    After I and 3,000 like me were laid off from TRW Electronics Components Group, I started my own business at the age of 27 with my 401K account. What is retirement at 26?

    We did all kinds of interesting electronics repair service contracts for companies large and small. Bob Carver once said if he needed his amp fixed he would have Don Nasca do it. We even had Les Paul bring in his gadgets.

    We expanded and opened a second location to sell Amateur Radio, CB, and commercial two-way radio equipment. I always loved ham radio as a boy and there were literally no radio stores in NJ at that time.

    I sold the service business to my most loyal employee and he is still going strong under a new name. We moved out west to be near my wifes family and although they have all since passed away or moved away, we never went back.

  • Account Manager

    TRW

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; TRW; Electrical/Electronic Manufacturing industry)

    1982 — 1986 (4 years)

    TRW once owned a dozen or more electronics manufacturing companies all located her in the United States of America. I sold VLSI chips from CA, Optocouplers from Texas, IRC Resistors from NC; Capacitors from Ogallala, Nebraska; Cinch Jones Connectors form IL, Power, RF and Microwave semiconductors; we even made power transformers in the heart of New York City!

    I covered the NY and NJ Metropolitan area and I must say it was interesting to work with all the Military Contractors such as ITT Defense, ITT Avionics, Singer Kearfott, Bendix .

Don Nasca’s Education
