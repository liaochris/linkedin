Jeffrey Nappi’s Summary

I started my IT career when I was 13 years old. My first paid job was configuring virtual domains under NCSA (what became Apache) on a BSDi server for a local BBS operator.

Once he saw the skill that I had, he introduced me to Kim Stimpson and Bill Basham - two pioneers in the ISP business. At the age of 14 I went to work for Bill and Kim at CyberHighway Internet Services Network Operations Center where I was mentored by former HP storage division engineers in the fundamentals of unix systems engineering and programming.

When I graduated from High School, I came to Arizona and began my college career as a Computer Systems Engineering major where I started i4vegas with my partners Michael Zaletel and Edward Zaletel. I later switched programs to Computer Information Systems where I spent more time focusing on the business aspects of IT.
