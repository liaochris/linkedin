Paul Armstrong-Taylor’s Summary

My interests are very broad. I have experience in investment banking and management consulting as well as a PhD in economics from Harvard University. Currently I am planning to continue my economics research in Shanghai, China while founding an educational organization.

I am interested in research and love to teach, but also plan to pursue some "real world" projects outside of academia. In particular, I have become very interested in education and I am currently exploring non-profit and for-profit education initiatives in China and more broadly. I think there are many complementarities between these initiatives and research experience within economics and my work experience in management consulting.

I have a strong academic background in economics, but also have a wide range of business experience. Since graduating from high school with the best A-level results in the UK, I received my Bachelors degree and Masters degree from Cambridge University in the UK and my PhD from Harvard. During this time, I won several awards including the prize for the best student in my Master's class at Cambridge.

In addition to my academic background, I also have a wide range of business experience in both the UK and the US. In the UK, I worked on the fixed income trading floor for Morgan Stanley and for London Economics (an economics consultancy) on the electricity sector.

Currently I am working for the Monitor Group which is a leading management / strategy consulting firm. I have been involved with a project to redesign the commercial model for a major pharmaceutical firm and to develop a new business opportunity for a large chemical firm. I believe these experiences give me the opportunity to apply my academic economic knowledge to the real business world.

I am very excited by the development of China and I believe that I can use my education and experience, as well as my Chinese language skills, to help this process.
