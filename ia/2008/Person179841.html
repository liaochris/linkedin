  • Space Science Division

    NASA Ames Research Center

    (Government Agency; 10,001 or more employees; Defense & Space industry)

    July 2004 — March 2006 (1 year 9 months)

    Mars Weather Analyst - Look at high-resolution satellite photos of Mars in order to understand current weather patterns. Determine prevailing wind directions on Mars by analyzing lee waves, cloud formations that occur downwind of craters. Take latitude and longitude measurements of lee waves using ISIS software developed by the USGS. Enter data into Excel spreadsheets to plot coordinates. The prevailing wind directions are then compared to the predictions of the MGCM, the Mars Global Circulation Model, a computer simulation of the atmosphere. Look at yardang formations on Mars to compare modern wind patterns with ancient wind patterns. Use JMARS software developed by ASU to measure the topology of water-carved features in order to reconstruct the climate of ancient Mars. Create PowerPoint presentations. Update the Space Science Division website.

    Arabidopsis Plant Care - Care and maintenance of transgenically altered Arabidopsis research plants within a clean growth room environment. My job as a plant care lab assistant was to care for the hundreds of research plants while scientists monitor the plants and collect data. Daily duties include watering, fertilizing, and staking young plants, autoclaving soil, preparing flats, planting seeds, and thinning out seedlings. Because the collection of data requires standardization, pots are weighed, soil is measured, and an attempt is made to water and fertilize all plants uniformly. Once plants are mature, the plants are left to dry, and the seed heads are bagged. The seeds are then harvested, sorted, and cleaned.
