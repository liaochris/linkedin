Andrew Cherry’s Summary

Seasoned professional with proven record of success gained across seven world class businesses spanning the Public and Private Equity domains - including the last 10 years as CFO in three varied and challenging circumstances. Expertise spans telecoms, media, technology, service and consumer sectors with experience working and living in over a dozen countries.

Andrew Cherry’s Specialties:

Strategy development and planning; organic and acquisitive growth; multi-site international commerce; operational and commercial execution; new business development; B2C and B2B sales and customer insight; turnarounds and performance improvement; optimising cost and investment levels; building high-performing teams; improved management information and decision making; problem solving; debt and equity fundraising; stakeholder management and communication; creating shareholder wealth.

Andrew Cherry’s Experience

  • Chief Financial Officer

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Telecommunications industry)

    Hi-tech company providing speech-enabled messaging and unified communications products to telecommunications service providers, enterprises and Web 2.0 players. The company is backed by more than a dozen HNWI's plus institutional investors including Goldman Sachs, GLG, Blue Mountain, Tosca Fund, ABN Amro, Allen & Co., Gartmore, Tisbury Capital and others.

  • Chief Financial Controller

    Vodafone UK

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; VOD; Telecommunications industry)

    2006 — 2006 (less than a year)

    Vodafone is the world's largest telcoms group. With a market cap of £99Bn it is the 3rd largest company in the UK and the 16th in the world. The UK operation provides mobile, broadband and fixed line services in the group's home market and is the single largest business unit by revenue with £5.5bn turnover and 16,000 staff. On a standalone basis Vodafone UK would have a market cap of ~£16Bn and be a FTSE 25 company.

  • Chief Financial Officer

    Cable & Wireless Jamaica

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; CW.L; Telecommunications industry)

    2003 — 2005 (2 years)

    CW Jamaica offers mobile, broadband, domestic and international fixed line services to residential and business users. With US$500m turnover and 3,000 staff it was the largest profit center of the C&W group but required a turnaround due to a rapid deterioration in performance following de-regulation. CW Jamaica was a bellwether for C&W's International division and as a publicly listed company (with a market cap of ~US$500m and a US$100m free float) was scrutinised intensely by both Caribbean and UK analysts.

  • Founding Chief Financial Officer

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; Internet industry)

    1999 — 2003 (4 years)

    GoIndustry was created to revolutionise the business of valuing and selling used industrial equipment through the application of leading edge Internet technologies and professional management practices plus the consolidation of a previously fragmented cottage industry. It is now the global leader in its sphere and is listed on AIM with a market cap of ~ £100m.
