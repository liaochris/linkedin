Jerry Stone’s Summary

Space exploration has been my life-long passion. I have promoted spaceflight since 1969 by giving presentations around the UK and abroad, and in presenting public exhibitions.
In 2004 I presented an 8-part evening course on Space Exploration at the University of Hertfordshire.
I gave around 30 presentations a year when I was doing it in my spare time. Venues have included the British Festival of Space, Oxford University and the Mars Society conference in Colorado.
I have been interviewed on TV and radio in the UK, the USA and in Romania, where they broadcast my footage of the 1999 eclipse. I have covered missions to the ISS for the BBC and Sky News.
My first public exhibition on space exploration was in 1975, and I staged a major event for the centenary of powered flight. Entitled “From the Wright Brothers to the International Space Station”, this was the only commemorative event outside the United States to be officially recognised by NASA, and attracted around 5,000 visitors.
In June 2004 I set up “UK For Aurora”, a campaign to increase public awareness and support for UK involvement in this long-term ESA programme. Lord Sainsbury announced a £5million interim payment for the project, citing public support as being a contributing factor towards the decision.
In 2005 I established the Sir Arthur Clarke Awards to recognise and reward UK space achievement. I present the awards at the annual UK Space Conference.
I was co-organiser of the Mars Society’s 2005 European Conference, which had delegates from the UK, the Netherlands, France, Germany, Canada, the USA and Australia.

I am now a freelance presenter on astronomy and space exploration, travelling all over the UK. As well as my public talks, I present a range of space workshops for schools - see my website for details. My aim is to use space to inspire students to follow courses in science, technology, engineering and mathematics.
