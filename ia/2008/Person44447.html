Dan Pacheco’s Summary

I've spent over a decade in the trenches of the online operations at several major media companies.

I currently serve as Senior Manager of Digital Products for the family-owned Bakersfield Californian. My team helps this forward-thinking independently owned newspaper grow hyper-local audiences and revenue through social networking products that target different demographic and psychographic groups. These include the award-winning Bakotopia.com -- the first U.S. newspaper-owned Web site to provide free, community-based Classifieds and social networking in a local market -- as well as 8 other sites in Bakersfield. Our "Bakomatic" technology is also licensed by a growing number of newspapers, including the Arizona Republic and The Victoria Advocate.

I stumbled across the Internet in the mid 1990s. I was writing a series of articles about online communities as a reporter for The Denver Post. This quickly developed into an all-encompassing interest in the online realm. After helping The Denver Post launch Denverpost.com, I moved on to Digital Ink, The Washington Post's first online venture, which a year later developed into Washingtonpost.com.

Since my days at The Washington Post, I have been a Multimedia Producer at Knight Ridder-Tribune Interactive, and a Programming Director and then Principal Product Manager at America Online. My work at America Online focused almost exclusively on community products, including AOL chat, Message Boards, Groups, Home pages, Journals (blogs), and the "You've Got Pictures" digital photo sharing platform.
