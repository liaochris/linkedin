Jeevan Kalanithi’s Summary

After 5 years of working in both research and the arts in New York City, I began graduate school at the Media Lab at MIT in 2005. I completed my degree in August 2007 with the support of a National Science Foundation Graduate Fellowship. I am now working as an independent design and technology consultant, helping companies create products centered on physical and social computing -- I design both the high level interactions as well as design and engineer working hardware prototypes and associated software.

My research interests lie at the intersection of social and physical computing; I am interest in systems that take advantage of the way in which people use physical objects to accomplish both social and cognitive tasks (giving gifts, sorting and stacking photos), and how to design systems that take advantage of these behaviors.

I look forward to developing these concepts in the research, art and commercial domains.

As my most recent education experience, the Media Lab deserves a brief explanation: The Media Lab specializes in the development and application of new technologies that address how we can live better and more humanely in a digital world. It consists of a wide variety of people, who all share skills in design and engineering, but practice a wide variety of professions: scientists, engineers (mechanical, electrical, software), designers (graphic, industrial), and artists. We are particularly geared toward realizing our visions in terms of start up companies, products and services. Check out the Media Lab website for a better overview (www.media.mit.edu).
