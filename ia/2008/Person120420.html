Marcus Rivas’s Experience

  • Director

    Green Works in Kansas City

    (Primary/Secondary Education industry)

    May 2008 — Present (6 months)

    Our Mission
    The mission of Green Works in Kansas City is to build and foster career and life management skills through environmental stewardship and experiential learning.

    History
    Green Works in Kansas City was incorporated in the state of Missouri in February of 2007 and recognized as a not-for-profit corporation under Section 501(c)3 of the Internal Revenue Code in September 2007. We are currently managing the second year of the ECOS program at DeLaSalle Education Center and conducting a fund-raising campaign to support expansion of the ECOS program into additional schools.

  • Moderator

    Kansas City Forums

    (Government Administration industry)

    2000 — Present (8 years)

    Kansas City Forums brings community members together to deliberate on both local and global issues. As a moderator with Kansas City Forums, Marcus has worked with two national organizations, the Kettering Foundation and MacNeil/Lehrer Productions. Locally, his Fall 2008 sessions will include Johnson County Library; Kansas City, Missouri, Public Library; and the Harry S. Truman Presidential Library.

    Deliberation is the “tough love” method of public involvement. Marcus' job is to ask people to consider what they’re willing to give up in order to get what they want. It requires that they take others’ views into account. It is designed to move community members past wishful thinking and denial and move towards informed public judgment. It works. A survey conducted after one deliberation showed that people who participated knew more and had more moderate opinions than those who didn’t, who knew less and had more extreme views.

  • Environmental Engineer

    U.S. Environmental Protection Agency

    (Government Administration industry)

    1984 — Present (24 years)

    Marcus Rivas is a public servant. He pioneers voluntary and community-based environmental management by helping businesses, institutions and residents obtain the level of environmental protection that is appropriate for their community. His work in St. Louisans helped that community determine what toxic air pollutants are harming their health and what actions will improve their air quality. The past nine years he coached Indian Tribes to develop environmental policies. For more than twenty years, Marcus has been bringing polluted land back to a better and higher land use using both enforcement and voluntary tools.

    Marcus is a very active mediator, facilitator, tutor and mentor.

    Marcus Rivas believes that the voluntary efforts for stewardship excellence are the new paradigm for environmental governance.

  • Director & Treasurer

    Center for Understanding the Built Environment

    (Architecture & Planning industry)

    2000 — 2002 (2 years)

    CUBE brings together educators with community partners to effect change that will lead to a quality built and natural environment, one and interdependent.

    The Fourth "R" in the CUBE educational model is Responsible Action. Children will learn to value the built environment while improving their problem-solving and social skills. The ultimate goal is knowledgeable community participation. This means cities that work for adults and children; buildings and spaces that are healthy and aesthetically pleasing; streetscapes and landscapes that reach to the future while celebrating the past.

    Increasing diversity in America creates challenges and opportunities. Harmony is a resource for dealing effectively with both.

    Harmony is poised to address the new and complex tiers in human relations emerging in the 21st century. With award-winning programs and services, Harmony is building inclusive communities in a more inclusive America.
