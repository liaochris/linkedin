    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    May 2006 — Present (2 years 1 month)

    From our regional office located on the campus of Congregation Ohav Shalom in Albany, New York, the Empire Region serves member congregations throughout NY State (except the immediate New York City metropolitan area), western MA, and southern VT.

    Empire Region provides extensive programming, publications, consultation and other services to its congregations to support synagogue initiatives in education, youth programming, Israel awareness, administration, leadership development, policy dissemination and interpretation, and much more.

    Please contact our Executive Director for more information about Empire Region and its programs and services.

    Vision Statement: The Empire Region of USCJ promotes a valued and meaningful Conservative Jewish life for our region's congregations, which includes promoting the role of synagogue in Jewish life and motivating Conservative Jews to perform mitzvot encompassing ethical behavior, spirituality, Judaic learning, and ritual observance.

  • Founder and Director

    Project: Continuum (Poughkeepsie & New City, NY)

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Religious Institutions industry)

    September 1993 — July 2004 (10 years 11 months)

    P:C was an improvisational theatre course for Jewish high school students that trained them to become peer educators. P:C explored themes of Jewish identity using topical social issues in the Jewish & secular world. These issues were the basis for studying Jewish text & philosophies. Improvisational scenes were developed based on these issues & were performed in an interactive format with an audience. P:C was the graduation course curriculum at the Beth-El High School in Poughkeepsie, NY through the spring of 1998 & was a High School course at the New City Jewish Center in New City, NY through the spring of 2000. Its students performed for a USY New Members Institute, a USY International Convention advisor's training session, the graduation ceremonies at Beth-El from 1994 until 1998, & for the Religious High School College Program at New City Jewish Center. The program was featured at the 1995 CAJE conference & has won awards from USY for achievements in original programming.

  • Director of Youth Activities

    New City Jewish Center (New City, NY)

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Religious Institutions industry)

    July 1995 — June 2004 (9 years)

    As the full-time Director of Youth Activities at the largest Conservative synagogue in Rockland County, NY, it was my responsibility to develop a comprehensive youth activities program that fully integrated the formal and informal educational initiatives for the youth of our synagogue, comprising approximately 700 kindergarteners through twelfth graders. To that end, the following programmatic initiatives were developed under my leadership:

    1. New pre/k-5 youth group programs that were fully integrated into religious school program;

    2. Enhancement of existing youth group programs that were fully integrated into religious school;

    3. Development of a college outreach program;

    4. Lay leadership development - reorganization of lay leadership structures, vision, and political status in the synagogue;

    5. Innovative Programming Based on Personal and Organizational Vision;

    6. Full Administrative and Programmatic Responsibility for the Youth Community.
