Dan Lyne’s Summary

Dan specializes in targeting and facilitating corporate relocation and real estate expansion projects for global technology firms. He markets the region's technology landscape, advises on municipal program development for business technologies, and serves as liaison for corporate technology leadership to the City of Chicago, WBC Board and Chairman.

Dan has held positions on the Mayor's Council of Technology Advisors, the former Chicago Software Association Public Policy Board, the Illinois CIO & IT Executive Exchange, the Advisory Board for DePaul University’s College of Computer Science, Telecommunications and Information Systems, and Chicago's Broadband Development Task Force,. He also serves on the Midwest Executive Committee for the national FIRST Robotics competition.

In addition, Dan has assumed various leadership roles through the National Skills & Standards Board and the Chicago Public Schools eBrigade program.

At his current post, Dan speaks to local, national & international audiences representing public/private technology & digital communications initiatives with respect to the Chicago region.

He holds a Juris Doctor from Chicago's John Marshall School of Law, with a concentration on International Business Transactions and EU Policy through a joint program at Trinity College of Law in Dublin, Ireland. He holds a Bachelor of Arts degree in English Literature & Creative Writing from Kenyon College in Gambier, Ohio.
