Devaraj de Condappa’s Summary

I am a hydraulic engineer with 4 years of experience in research. I am specialised in soil hydrology (hydrogeology). I have field work experience in India, Benin and Burkina Faso. I did also a lot of digital mappings and hydrological modelling (simulation of groundwater recharge, vegetation transpiration, soil water storage etc).

That’s for the technique. From my field work experience, I want to have a holistic vision on water issues. I understood in-situ that an approach solely technical is not enough to solve in a realistic manner the water crisis. In India, for example, the underground water is overexploited, mainly to irrigate water demanding crops (such as rice or sugarcane). In this case, it is not enough to plan only a technical solution (favouring the groundwater recharge for example). It is also required to work with the local farmers to identify water saving crops / cultural practices which are suited to the local socio-economic context.

This example refers to a general idea: I think that the solution to an environmental issue is not really to “believe” in technology, but rather to question our habits.

So here I am heading toward the path of interdisciplinarity. I am completing my technical speciality with knowledge in water social and economical dimensions. For instance, I followed recently a course in water governance at the UNESCO – IHE, where I especially focussed on participatory methods for water management. And I am currently working on an interdisciplinary project in the Volta basin, West Africa.

To sum up, my interest is to contribute to a better management of water resource, having an interdisciplinary approach, to say technical, social, regulatory and economic. And, importantly, in direct connexion with the field: to be genuine and realistic.
