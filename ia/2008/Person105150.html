Ken Stram’s Summary

• Executive with 20 years of experience in communications, government affairs, public relations, marketing, brand management, and new business development.
• Founded and led the world’s first economic development program for the LGBT community, which has served nearly 5,000 people.
• Helped hundreds of small business clients develop brand messages, business plans, marketing plans, and growth strategies.
• Oversaw an $87 million budget and directed the day-to-day operations of the U.S. Small Business Administration’s 79 district and regional field offices as a presidentially appointed Schedule C employee.
• Accomplished leader, mentor, trainer, team-builder, writer, and public speaker.

Ken Stram’s Specialties:

• Experience spans a variety of sectors including technology, financial services, professional services, human resources, life sciences, small business, government, and nonprofit.

Ken Stram’s Experience

  • Director

    San Francisco LGBT Community Center

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    April 2004 — Present (4 years 10 months)

    In April '04, I founded the nation’s first Economic Development Program for lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender (LGBT) community members. The program includes: a Small Business Business Development Program; a Workforce Development Program; a Transgender Economic Empowerment Initiative (TEEI), which represents the first coordinated, public/private effort to transform the economic health of transgender people by linking employees with employers and by improving the safety of San Francisco Workplaces; and a Financial Growth Program that helps folks reduce debt, accumulate savings, and invest in the future.

  • President

    2Bridge Communications

    (Public Relations and Communications industry)

    September 2001 — April 2004 (2 years 8 months)

    In September '01, I started my own full-service public relations firm. We represented clients from a variety of industry segments including financial services, mortgage lending, real estate, Internet, telecommunications, life sciences, professional services, consumer products, and fine arts.

  • Manager

    Burson-Marsteller

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; WPP; Public Relations and Communications industry)

    January 1999 — August 2001 (2 years 8 months)

    I worked in the technology practice at Burson-Marsteller leading public relations campaigns for a wide variety of dot-com and technology clients. The work included writing strategic brand positioning messages, press releases, and technical white papers, and leading multiple PR teams simultaneously to achieve outstanding results.

  • Director

    San Francisco Chamber of Commerce

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    September 1997 — January 1998 (5 months)

    We conducted national and international marketing campaigns to attract businesses to San Francisco, and we coordinated lobbying efforts and grassroots marketing campaigns to retain businesses in the city.

  • Associate Administrator for Field Operations

    US Small Business Administration

    (Government Agency; 1001-5000 employees; Government Administration industry)

    May 1995 — August 1997 (2 years 4 months)

    I was a political appointee (Schedule C) in charge of managing an $87 million budget and directing the day-to-day operations of the SBA's 79 district and regional offices representing 2,000 employees. During that time, we oversaw a massive reorganization of the national field office structure; developed marketing efforts to double the number of loans to women and minorities; launched twelve "One Stop Capital Shops" to serve entrepreneurs in urban and rural Enterprise and Empowerment zones; and worked with senior managers to negotiate annual performance measures and census-based lending goals.

  • Deputy Associate Administrator for Congressional Affairs

    US Small Business Administration

    (Government Agency; 1001-5000 employees; Government Administration industry)

    February 1993 — April 1995 (2 years 3 months)

    I was a political appointee (Schedule C) and second in command of the SBA's office of congressional affairs, which involved steering the SBA through the annual congressional budget process; managing congressional relations strategies; and writing testimony for congressional budget, oversight, and field hearings.
