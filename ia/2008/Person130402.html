Mike Moore’s Summary

A profit minded executive who has built a strong rolodex of senior level contacts at big box and mass merchant retailers as well as distributors, premium incentive and VAR channels. A skilled negotiator who can also help develop cost effective strategies for market expansion for multiple channels of distribution focused on long term relationships. A leader who stays abreast of current trends so he can help companies align their logistical, accounting, and marketing departments to meet the needs of their customers. A straight forward implementation style that guarantees world class execution.

  • Executive Vice President Sales & Marketing

    GPX, INC

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Consumer Electronics industry)

    February 2003 — April 2004 (1 year 3 months)

    I was recruited to GPX to help the company regain market share and profitability. I was re-united with a former supervisor from Sprint who had become CEO of the company. GPX had seen its overall business drop by greater than 40% in the two years prior to my coming on board. Working directly with the CEO; we were able to re-organize the entire company, improve efficiencies by reducing GS&A by more than 15% and cull the number of factories we did business with in Asia and improving our terms with the remaining factories. We also were successful in developing and launching a new line up of products (118 SKU's) which helped us regain market share and profitability by increasing sales to existing customers and adding new customers like Wal-Mart, Circuit City and Target. In addition we positioned the company for sale and successfully sold it in April of 2004. As part of the consolidation of the new company I agreed to a buy out after a short transition period.

  • Executive Director National Retail Sales & Operations

    T-Mobile

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; DT; Wireless industry)

    2002 — 2003 (1 year)

    was recruited to T-Mobile to head up their National Retail Sales channel based on my success within that channel at Sprint PCS. In a very short time I was able to re-organize the sales staff terminating under achievers and bringing on experienced closers. The new team was able to expand or presence at Best Buy into all their doors and sign on new partners like Comp USA, Fry's, 7-11 and Circle K. I also negotiated a semi exclusive relationship with Circuit City resulting in a greater than 50% increase in our overall business. In addition to the account management, I also had responsibility for our 250+ sales force that supported the national retail channel. This included the part time sales people we used to staff Best Buy and Circuit City on the weekends. By implementing a new stack rank procedure and coaching guidelines we were able to increase the efficiencies of these staffers by greater than 50%. I left T-Mobile when I was recruited to GPX by a former Supervisor at Sprint.
