Alecia Janczak’s Summary

After 17 years in the corporate world, I have decided to give it up, go back to school, and become a nurse. I certainly won't make the same kind of money, but I've come to realize its not about that anymore. I want to wake up and say, "I'm going to make a difference for someone today!"

I'm grateful to to the company I worked for during the first 15 years. Without much college, they took a chance on me and I returned each promotion/position with dedication, creativity, and enthusiasm to make a difference to the operational side of the company. In the end, 2 things happened. After working in many different departments for this distributor, I decided to try my hand at sales. My background provided an excellent platform for the job, but because we had over 900,000 products in our portfolio, almost all relating to science, I never felt confident in the entire sales process. It was my belief that if I am to be successful, I really need to know what the hell I am selling. Sure for 4 years, I faked it, learned a little, and did well here and there. But I didn't love my job like all the other ones I had previously. The 2nd reason I left was because an investment company bought us. Word was they were going to dump loads of money and resources into us to make us more successful, and then sell us. As a result, jobs were cut and the stakes were raised to an unbelievable high. Morale went down the tubes; expenses and tools necessary to do our jobs were cut; mgmt became ruthless, expecting more and more from us; and sales reps were dropping like flies. I hung on for another year. It took me that long to work up the courage to quit and go after a dream to get paid for doing something you love. And I love helping people.

These next few years will be a huge sacrifice. I am not married, so I don't have a 2nd income, but I don't have kids, either. I won't be able to live my ample life style anymore, that's for sure! I just know this will be worth it in the end!
