Miles Lewitt’s Summary

Miles Lewitt is a vice president at Intuit. He has managed Product Development groups for Intuit’s two largest businesses (Tax and Small Business) and the Shared Development and Services organization for Intuit's Corporate Technology group.

In the tax business he led the delivery of consumer and accountant tax products for two years with a focus on reversing years of software entropy and improving the user experience of the tax products.

In Small Business he leads the effort to create the next generation platform and offerings for small to medium sized businesses.

In Corporate Technology he led the team that develops the shared common software infrastructure and shared tools that support Intuit offerings and software development groups companywide.

Before joining Intuit in August 2001, Miles worked as vice president of research and development at ADP. In that role he was responsible for developing systems software, data communications software and applications software for the company's dealer services division. He previously held positions at Cadre Technologies, Intel and Honeywell. At Intel, he launched and managed the company's first overseas software development group and managed the development of many hardware and software products, including the Product of the Year as selected by Electronic Products magazine.

Miles earned a master's degree in mathematics from Arizona State University and his bachelor's degree in computer science from the City College of New York. He is a senior member of the IEEE and a member of the Association for Computing Machinery, the American Society for Quality and the IEEE Computer Society. Lewitt has taught college-level computer science courses, presented at computer science conferences, and was on the executive committee of the American Electronics Association Oregon Council.

Miles Lewitt’s Specialties:

Building a world class software engineering organization, software architecture, software design, software engineering process, innovative solutions to customer problems, developing reusable components, software product lines, Six Sigma as a management tool, designing computer systems (processor architecture, operating system design, etc.) and real time systems.

A topic I am exploring in my personal time is mitigating the complexity of a large number of multi-cores on developing software.
