    (Non-Profit; 1001-5000 employees; International Trade and Development industry)

    The Indo-Canada Chamber of Commerce (ICCC) is the premier, privately funded, not-for-profit, business organization representing over 1000 members from the Canadian business and professional communities. It is a coast to coast organization with Business Councils in Vancouver, Calgary, Ottawa, Montreal and Halifax in addition to the head office in Toronto.

    I was instrumental in bringing the Indo Canada Chamber of Commerce to Halifax and launching the Halifax Business Council. In this role, I am responsible for creating the strategic plan for the Halifax Business Council and implementing it. The plan also involves identifying strong partners and aligning our goals with theirs and achieving the set targets. The objective for council is to tap into the enormous economic opportunities that exist in India and leverage existing connections to create opportunities in Atlantic Canada

    August 2007 — Present (11 months)

    CMC Atlantic is the representative organization for CMC-Canada in atlantic canada. CMC Canada is the single voice of management consultants across Canada and it promotes management consulting at its highest level.

    The Association promotes a professional community of management consultants where members can exchange ideas and share experiences. Our members have unique opportunities to learn from each other and gain a competitive edge. They know more about the changes taking place in their discipline, and in the consulting industry. As a result, our members are well rounded and well informed.

    As Director – Membership Development, I am responsible for growing the membership of the organization and identifying opportunities for growth in new markets. I work closely with the President and Chair to ensure our strategic objectives related to membership are met. I am also the first point of contact for potential members and serve as a public relations officer in these circumstances.
