Qiyu Liu’s Summary

My background is tailor-made for a career in capital markets. I have an M.S. in electrical and computer engineering to go along with my B.S. in electrical engineering which provide me with a quantitative and analytical background that extends beyond the offerings of those with just an economics, finance, or management background. My first and uncompleted Ph.D. was to develop and model cost-effective solar technology. My current Ph.D. is in finance and focuses on asset pricing and modeling short-term trading trends. I’ve worked for multiple S&P 500 companies as an electrical engineer, manufacturing engineer, and software engineer.

Still, my education and work background belies the entirety of my skill set. I find markets to be the most exciting arena. Capital markets are especially captivating because of the opportunities for anyone to participate. The idea of trillions of dollars and global economies at stake in the ultimate game of wits is alluring beyond compare. Compared with coursework for my engineering degrees, I spent at least an order of magnitude more time following securities. I am so engaged that, with the exception of my car, I have committed my entire net worth to them. And it’s not just that I like the subject – I’m also very good; my obsessive research yielded a return in 2007 that non-trivially outperformed any major Western index.

Though I have the stock of an engineer, I am a far cry from my peers. In order to continually expand my knowledge, I have taken courses ranging from particle physics to sociological theory (always with disregard and sometimes to the detriment of my GPA). As I move forward toward the professional phase of my life, I could not be any more excited about the opportunity to pursue something that I love.
