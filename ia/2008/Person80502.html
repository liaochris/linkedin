Iovanca Stratan’s Summary

My experience in massage therapy started in 2002, with Yumeiho Therapy, in Timisoara, Romania.
Yumeiho Kotsuban Therapy was founded by Dr Saionji Masayuki in Japan.
My teacher, Mr. Sorin Iga, is the president of Romanian Yumeiho Society, and Level 5 Yumeiho Therapy Instructor. Mr. Sorin Iga, a famous Romanian Osteopath, had the great privilege to spend much time observing and learning from Dr. Saionji Masayuki in person.
With 2 years experience as Prophylactic Yumeiho Therapist, Level I and II, I decided to achieve further knowledge enrolling in Registered Massage Therapy Education in 2005. My Canadian education started with Sutherland-Chan School and Teaching Clinic, where I have attended three parts of the curriculum. I continued with the fourth and last part, at ICT Kikkawa College. This way I had the amazing chance to be taught by some of the greatest Massage Therapy Instructors and Teachers of Ontario. I had the privilege to learn professionalism within therapeutic relationship, positive attitude, assertiveness, and critical thinking, under the strong tradition of the famous Torontonian “mother” and “daughter” Kikkawa and Sutherland-Chan.
A great bonus offered by the curriculum, was the opportunity to learn, Hydrotherapy, Peripheral Joint Mobilization, Rhythmic Mobilization, Sports Massage, Thai Massage and Deep Myofascial Release.
My passion grows stronger and my interest to experience and learn more keeps the excitement alive. This last autumn, I had the chance to meet with Mr. Sorin Iga and upgrade my education in Yumeiho Therapy. I returned to Toronto with a Diploma in Yumeiho Therapy Treatment, Level III. It is almost common sense that I should not stop here, and work further to obtain the permission to teach Yumeiho Therapy as well.
