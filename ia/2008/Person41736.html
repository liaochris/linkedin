Curt Baker’s Experience

  • Deputy Superintendent

    Roanoke City Public Schools

    (Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Education Management industry)

    August 2007 — Present (11 months)

  • Chief Financial Officer

    School District of Lancaster

    (Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Education Management industry)

    November 2004 — August 2007 (2 years 10 months)

    When I arrived, the District was in financial distress. The former Superintendent had been convicted of financial crimes and the District was in the red for the fifth time in six years.

    Personal successes include:

    • Achieving financial stability. By implementing tightened financial controls and through the use of effective cash management techniques, the District finished the 2006-07 fiscal year with an (unaudited) $1.9 million General Fund fund balance – a three year improvement of $5.3 million.

    • Funding curricular improvements. Significant increases were made in the funds available for class size reduction, instructional materials, and teacher support -- $4 million for alternative education, $3.5 million for reading and science, $2 million for educational technology.

    • Assuring fiscal and operational transparency. Successfully navigated a challenging political environment, restoring public confidence in the District’s stewardship of its financial resources.

  • President and Chief Operating Officer

    The Sports Institute, LLC

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Sports industry)

    August 2003 — November 2004 (1 year 4 months)

    Key personal and organizational successes include:

    • Financial & Operational Performance. Year-over-year revenue increased by over 400% in 2004. Gross profit increased to 62% of revenue, up from 46% in 2003. Customer feedback surveys indicated that the quality of delivered events was outstanding.

    • Launch of New Business Units. In 2003, The Sports Institute was comprised of two business units – The Lacrosse Institute and The Football Institute. Under my leadership, three new business units were added – The Rugby Institute, The Athlete Institute (speed, strength, and agility training), and The Field Hockey Institute. These business units contributed about 40% of 2004 revenue.

    • Acquisitions and Strategic Alliances. Negotiated numerous strategic alliance, partnership, and acquisition deals that provided a nationwide footprint for the company. Examples include significant deals with Major League Lacrosse and STX.

  • Chief Financial Officer & Principal

    Skipping Stone, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Management Consulting industry)

    February 2000 — August 2003 (3 years 7 months)

    Launched the Mergers, Acquisitions & Strategic Finance practice for this management consulting company serving the energy industry. Client work included securing new wholesale supply credit facilities for a $100 million retail energy marketer whose prior credit facilities were prematurely terminated on short notice, then negotiating a separation agreement with the prior credit provider, thus avoiding protracted litigation.

    Oversaw all aspects of Skipping Stone’s finances, directed business planning, and managed corporate services (accounting, human resources, information systems). Under my leadership, every standard measure financial performance showed step-change improvement.

  • President

    Baker Consulting (Sole Proprietorship)

    (Sole Proprietorship; Myself Only; Management Consulting industry)

    January 1997 — January 2000 (3 years 1 month)

    Provided strategic, operational, and financial control services, primarily for workout clients. Examples:

    • OBOS, INC. (Wilmington, DE). Doubled top-line revenues. Rebuilt the supply chain, reducing per unit manufacturing costs and improving the quality of a proprietary scanner produced by the firm. Reduced the burn rate by more than 40%.

    • MEDIA SOLUTIONS INTERNATIONAL, INC. (King of Prussia, PA). Successfully implemented a “stabilize to sell” strategy for this niche provider of software products to the direct response advertising industry by enhancing revenue and improving software performance.

  • Principal

    Gemini Consulting

    (Privately Held; 1001-5000 employees; Management Consulting industry)

    August 1993 — December 1996 (3 years 5 months)

    Led the firm’s largest and most complex engagements – “transformational” projects involving the integration of multiple strategic, reengineering, information technology, change management, and organization design workstreams.

    • Relocated to Denmark to serve as the engagement leader for a project at a $3 billion European chemicals client. Delivered more than $125 million in first year financial benefits. Reengineered the firm’s supply chain, implemented a targeted new sales and marketing approach, introduced activity based management and balanced scorecard metrics, and effected a disciplined new product development approach.

    • Led a 1.2 billion regulated, cost-of-service Canadian pipeline utility through a massive change program that delivered more than $100 million in first year financial benefits. Created a long-term growth strategy for the firm, reengineered support services and operations, improved customer service, and created a new information systems architecture.

  • Senior Acquisitions Representative – Worldwide Land & Acquisitions

    Conoco Inc.

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; Oil & Energy industry)
