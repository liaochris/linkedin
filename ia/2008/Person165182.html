Ryan Parman’s Summary

Founder of Foleeo/Warpshare, a startup in the Social Media/Networking space. Creator and co-developer of SimplePie, an RSS and Atom syndication management API written in PHP that is quickly gaining raving fans all over the world. Leading front-end development of the Spring '08 revision of the Yahoo! Messenger website.

Front-end web developer with substantial experience in Web Standards based development and design, working with XML/JSON-based web services (Amazon Web Services, Flickr, Twitter, RSS, etc.), and content syndication and aggregation. Experienced with organic SEO methods, front-end performance tuning, and usability and user-centered design principles. I'm also interested and involved in the future of (X)HTML, RSS, and Atom, and have actively taken steps to be involved in those communities.

Looking to grow fantastic new products, services, and businesses by listening to people (users, customers, human beings) and adjusting accordingly. Believes that passion, smarts, and people-centered attitudes are what make good product teams.

Ryan Parman’s Specialties:

Web Standards, XHTML, XML, CSS 2, unobtrusive JavaScript/JSON, AJAX, PHP, SEO, RSS, Atom, syndication, Subversion/CVS, usability, user-centered design, web accessibility, cross-browser development, project management, startup environments, business planning, working with remote teams, managing a successful open-source project, marketing, copy writing, etc.

Ryan Parman’s Experience

  • Frontend Web Developer, Messenger

    Yahoo!

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; YHOO; Internet industry)

    Ryan is currently leading the front-end development of the Spring 2008 re-launch of the Yahoo! Messenger website. He has collaborated with design, marketing, back-end, and server teams to provide increased usability, accessibility, organic search engine optimization (SEO), and exceptionally tuned performance for 30 locales across multiple platforms and devices.

  • Founder and Chief Information Officer

    Foleeo, Inc. (aka WarpShare)

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Online Media industry)

    September 2006 — Present (1 year 5 months)

    Ryan's role at Foleeo (and specifically on our primary WarpShare service) revolves around users: user experience, user interaction, and usability. He is passionate about how people interact with the web in fun, cool, and useful new ways.

    Ryan's extensive experience in web standards and content syndication (precursors and fundamental components of the current "Web 2.0" renaissance) along with a keen understanding and desire for the latest bleeding edge technologies gives him a relatively unique view into the current web, where it's going, and where it has the potential to go.

    Being technically-oriented but people-minded, Ryan is currently involved in planning and developing a marriage between brilliant back-end technology and a user experience and user features that people are clamoring for, but no one is giving them. Ryan is also leading the development of REST-based web services to allow third-party access to Foleeo's services.

  • Creator and Co-Developer

    SimplePie.org

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Internet industry)

    July 2004 — Present (3 years 7 months)

    Ryan is the creator, evangelist, and co-developer of the SimplePie project -- an API library, written in PHP, that enables web developers to simply and easily integrate RSS and Atom syndication feeds (from blogs, news sites, etc.) into their websites and web applications.

    After recruiting a new co-developer (Geoffrey Sneddon) in June 2005, Ryan began to shift from a development-focused role to a people-focused role, where he currently works to ensure that people are aware of, and can easily use SimplePie through support, documentation, tutorials, plugins, and evangelism. Ryan spends a lot of time looking for pain points involved in working with feeds, and works to simplify those irritants from the developer's workflow, enabling a more efficient experience.

    Under his vision, guidance, and people-oriented approach, SimplePie has seen explosive growth in the developer community, and has been hailed as one of the best software libraries available for managing syndication feeds.

  • Blogger

    Skyzyx.com/RyanParman.com (Self-employed)

    (Self-Employed; Myself Only; Internet industry)

    March 2003 — Present (4 years 11 months)

    It is here where Ryan maintains his "Lifestream" (powered by SimplePie, of course), and blogs about a range of topics on his personal weblog.

    Ryan's goal when he began blogging back in 2003 was to share all of the cool new methods and techniques he'd picked up during his conversion to the religion of "web standards". He became an outspoken evangelist to that effect, with a heartfelt desire to lead web designers away from the darkness of 90's "tag soup" into the awesomeness and glory of the web standards revolution!

    During this time, Ryan became most well-known for putting together and hosting the Internet Explorer standalones that so many web designers still use to this very day. (Joe Maddalone came up with the method, Ryan packaged them).
