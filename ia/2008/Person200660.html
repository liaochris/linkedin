Bill Sieglein’s Summary

Mr. Sieglein has over 25 years in the IT industry specializing in information security, risk and compliance management. His experience is broad and includes assessments, architecture development, infrastructure planning, disaster recovery planning, product evaluation and implementation, program and policy development, project management and training & awareness development and delivery.

His career includes 11 years with the National Security Agency, 6 years with Booz, Allen & Hamilton consulting the US and foreign intelligence communities, 3 years with T. Rowe Price Investment Technologies, 3 years as the Security Solutions Architect for Fortrex Technologies, 3 years as VP of Corporate Strategy with True North Solutions. He is now Senior Director of the Information Assurance, Security and Compliance practice at Apptis Technology Solutions. Mr. Sieglein is also the Founder and Executive Director of the CSO Breakfast Club.

Mr. Sieglein has held key positions with many of his major clients. As a consultant, he has been the Director of IT Security for an international, touch-less bank; the Director of Information Security for a large, non-profit, membership organization and most recently he held the position of Chief Security Officer for the Public Company Accounting Oversight Board (PCAOB) – the organization established by the Sarbanes-Oxley Act of 2002.

Mr. Sieglein is a Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP) and is a frequent speaker at conferences. He recently published the book “Security Planning & Disaster Recovery” with former Fortrex CTO Eric Maiwald. He has authored numerous articles for paper and on-line magazines and was the security expert for one of CMP publications on-line magazines.

Mr. Sieglein holds a Bachelors Degree in Computer Science from the University of Maryland and a Masters Degree in Technical Management from Johns Hopkins University. He lives in Baltimore, Maryland with his wife and three children.
