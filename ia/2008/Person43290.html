Daniel B. Kaplan’s Summary

Dementia Care Solutions helps families and businesses assure the highest quality of care for individuals with Alzheimer’s disease and related illnesses—offering a full range of services.

Founder and sole-practitioner Daniel B. Kaplan has extensive expertise in dementia care, and is currently enrolled in the Ph.D. program at Columbia University School of Social Work. A licensed social worker in New York and Massachusetts, Daniel has worked most recently as the director of social services for the Alzheimer’s Foundation of America, helping to build a vibrant clinical support division, professional training division, and dementia care setting assessment program. He has provided numerous expert presentations on Alzheimer’s disease and dementia care, offered expertise and guidance to organizations throughout the care spectrum, and overseen the development of countless nationwide initiatives.

Prior to this, Daniel worked in an Elder Protective Services and Elder at Risk program—conducting forensic investigations and extensive interventions on behalf of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts. The alleviation of physical abuse, emotional abuse, financial exploitation and other risks to community-dwelling seniors required the utmost comprehension of family dynamics and dysfunction, the implementation of community resources, counseling for issues of abuse and trauma, legal and financial protections, and navigation through the intricacies of mental competence and cognitive impairment.

Daniel has continued to challenge himself academically and to strive for professional excellence by accruing more than 140 post-graduate continuing education hours, consuming countless volumes of clinical and medical journals, reading numerous professional- and family-focused books on Alzheimer's disease and dementia, and reviewing an exhaustive library of newsletters, Web pages, message boards and teleconferences.
