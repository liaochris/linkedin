    (Non-Profit; 51-200 employees; Publishing industry)

    October 2006 — Present (1 year 6 months)

    The pick of the kidlitters--a contest to reward the best kids' and YA books started with a snarky comment I made on another blog. Don't like existing kidlit awards? Let's start our own! Hundreds of books and months later, we crowned the winners ... and made Internet history. For this year's reprise, I'll continue edit the website and act as referee, ethicist, ombudsman and fundraiser.

    (Self-Employed; 1-10 employees; Writing and Editing industry)

    October 2004 — Present (3 years 6 months)

    Dreamed up a children's literature site before the blogging stampede began. I post reviews of picture books Monday-Friday at www.bookbuds.net, with roughly 500 unique visitors each week. I've "hired" (and fired) volunteer reviewers--we're now up to two--to do reviews, and I edit their work as well. I had to beg free copies off publishers until books began arriving daily, and somehow kept the site going after having a baby and then during a cross-country move. I've built up a loyal following among Mommy bloggers, librarians, booksellers and the kidlit obsessed.
