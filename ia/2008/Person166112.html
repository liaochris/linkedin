Rutger van Zuidam’s Summary

As a Business ICT student, Rutger held board positions at various organisations like the Hanze Univeristy joint decision-making board and Groningen Student Golf Club, where Rutger was founding chairman. Rutger moved to Amsterdam in March 2005, where he did his graduation thesis at Ogilvy: “How to strengthen Ogilvy’s overall position by embedding Digital Marketing Communication in the marketing resources of its customers.”

Rutger is co-founder of Accepté BV, which started off developing community software and concepts in 2005, but also online (e-)ticketing softwareplatform “Paylogic”. Under his supervision, the Amsterdam office was established, Accepté got first round funding by VC Crossmedia Fund / Endeit owned by Joop van den Ende and Hubert Deitmers, and Paylogic became a significant Dutch primary ticketing brand with over 500.000 e-tickets sold online in 2007. When Accepté changed focus from online ticketing back to offline ticketing Rutger left Accepté in November 2007 and sold his stake.

In April 2008, Rutger became partner and strategic advisor at BID BOYS. Makelaarsbod.nl is the first concept of BID BOYS, where house buyers/sellers submit their request free of charge and house brokers reply with customised bids.

Rutger founded his new company Cherrytwist BV in September 2008. Rutger, supported by his strategic partners, advises, develops and exploits concepts or applications, which act on opportunities that rise from heavily changing markets and shifting paradigms. Cherrytwist e.g. offers a smooth transition from traditional interruptive marketing and (brand) strategy to an engaging, transparent and participating conversation with and between individuals and communities.

Rutger as a person is an inspirational and directive motivator. He’s highly entrepreneurial, self-educating, connective, innovative, disruptive, creative, and visionary and combines (long term) strategy with direct (sales) results.
