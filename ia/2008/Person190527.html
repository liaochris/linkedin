Robert Tolmach’s Summary

I have worked as an architect, investment banker and entrepreneur, and now as a social entrepreneur.

I am President of WellGood LLC ( www.WellGoodLLC.com), which uses businesslike methods to do good. Our main current project is www.ChangingThePresent.org.

Previously, I started a new nonprofit, Glasses For Humanity (please see www.GlassesForHumanity.org).

Before that, I was President of New York City Expo – Ltd., developer of a New York City Expo aboard a reconstructed Staten Island Ferry in Hong Kong.

I was also a Managing Director of Zebra Fund LLC, which manages a $45 million fund to invest in properties with environmental issues.

Previously, I was senior real estate manager for James D. Wolfensohn, Incorporated, where I was involved in large real estate development projects, including Queens West, an 8 million square foot mixed-use development across the East River from the United Nations in New York City. While there, I also provided real estate advisory services to the clients of the firm and of Fuji Wolfensohn International, a joint venture with the Fuji Bank.

Prior to 1986, I was a principal at Arquitectonica International Corporation, a nationally prominent architectural firm, and ran their Texas office. During that time, I also developed real estate in Texas.

I received my BA in Architecture and Fine Arts, and my Bachelor of Architecture, both from Rice University.

Robert Tolmach’s Specialties:

I have experience in design, finance, deal structuring, joint ventures, real estate development, cause related marketing, social franchising, microfinance. I've now shifted my attention to the social sector, where I use these business skills to make a social impact.
