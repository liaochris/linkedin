Mike Rundle’s Summary

I've been designing for the web since 1996, and in that time I've worked with internet startups, Fortune 100-5,000,000 companies, large and small consulting firms, non-profit organizations, and government contractors.

Mike Rundle’s Specialties:

Visual design direction and leadership, user experience and interface design, human factors research, information architecture, usability testing, XHTML/CSS, frontend user experience development with JS/Ajax, backend development with PHP/MySQL, web accessibility, building a successful company from scratch.

  • Co-Founder, Creative Director

    Business Logs

    (Partnership; 1-10 employees; Online Media industry)

    February 2004 — March 2007 (3 years 2 months)

    Business Logs was one of the very first blog-focused design firms in the world, and is the professional consulting arm of 9rules, Inc.

    As the Creative Director, I lead all design initiatives for our clients, including design prototyping, standards compliant XHTML/CSS code generation, weblog implementation, and all deliverables. We've won a bunch of awards, check them out at the bottom of this profile.

    Business Logs was sold in Spring 2007.

    Skills used: business and communication strategy, grass-roots marketing, "weblogs for business", negotiation, creative.

  • User Interface Designer

    Northrop Grumman

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; NOC; Defense & Space industry)

    May 2004 — August 2004 (4 months)

    Led the standards-compliant redesign of a 500+ page computer-based training website that trains military personnel to use TBMD (Theater Ballistic Missile Defense) software as part of a U.S. Department of Defense contract. Site deployed around the world to U.S. and coalition troops, as well as NATO countries with whom we are partnered. Surpassed Section 508 Accessibility Guidelines, fixed numerous usability problems. Trained design team to use XHTML, CSS, XML, and XSLT. Developed style guide to accompany deployment which outlined all code, styles, and colors.

    Skills used: team training, heuristic evaluation, information architecture, making things easier to use.

  • Interaction Designer

    Dunn Solutions Group

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Online Media industry)

    May 2003 — September 2003 (5 months)

    Worked with graphic designers to implement usability and human factors best practices into their work. Developed and analyzed user personas and task scenarios, developed user interaction and interface guidelines for Ibbotson Associates' iPlan financial planning software.

    Skills used: user scenarios, human factors research, requirements gathering, information architecture.

  • Usability Consultant

    Xerox Corporation

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; XRX; Electrical/Electronic Manufacturing industry)

    February 2003 — May 2003 (4 months)

    Led a team effort to make Xerox's Support & Drivers section of their website more usable as part of a graduate class at RIT. Designed and implemented all aspects of a formal usability test, provided interface and interaction design advice based on test findings. Xerox's web team continues to redesign the driver search interface and architecture as per our recommendations.

    Skills used: formal usability testing, low-fidelity prototyping, data analysis, human factors research, interdisciplinary team collaboration, user and task scenarios.
