Rahul Bhimjiani’s Summary

He has worked for almost 9 years each as an executive (always general management), teacher and independent professional, mostly in academia, finance, HR, media, NGO and other services sector organisations.

As CEO/COO/Project Manager of start-ups/turnarounds, he has been his own Marketing, Finance and HR manager, responsible for operations, as also organisational and strategic aspects of both functions.

As a consultant, he focuses on change management, straddling strategic, organisational and functional issues, often going beyond making a report, to also get involved in implementation issues.

To this end, he has, apart from older generation corporate interventions, also handled some ICT (Information and Communications Technology) enablement work in the last few years.

A lot of it is with start-ups, organisations in transition, family businesses and other SMEs, often hands-on at site with clients all over the country

As a teacher and trainer, he focuses on teaching OB, Communications and Soft Skills on one hand, as also Marketing, General Management and Perspectives on the other – having taught at over 60 institutions all over the country, and about an equal number of clients for in-house programmes.

A parallel activity is as a coach, counsellor and mentor to both younger and older people, on both personal and professional fronts – this is done both face-to-face, telephonically and online through mail/chat/sms.

His research and writing activities on a range of topics has led to an output of over 60 pieces of various sorts.

With roots in Bombay, Ahmedabad, Guwahati and Nagpur, he is currently based in Hyderabad, with short stints in various parts of the country.

He has operated throughout India as an independent management consultant and trainer, apart from online work for offshore clients.

Rahul Bhimjiani’s Specialties:

Management of non-profits, Social Marketing, SME Growth, ICT enablement, Change Management, Building Networks, Strategy Formulation, Financial Restructuring, BPR.
