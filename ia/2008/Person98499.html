Joy Hopkins’s Summary

A resident of the District of Columbia for nearly 10 years, Joylyn Hopkins is very active in professional and social groups in the region. She has been an event planner for local social organizations, as well as a board member of the Young Nonprofit Professionals Network of DC. She is also involved in local alumni chapters of both James Madison University and Sigma Alpha Iota.

Most recently serving as the Development Officer for The Posse Foundation, Joylyn is excited to take on new challenges with the American Architectural Foundation.

Joylyn possesses 9 years of non-profit experience with organizations including the KaBOOM!, Washington Chamber Symphony, Washington Review, American Diabetes Association, Wolf Trap, the National Symphony Orchestra, the Iraqi Cultural Society and the Washington Shakespeare Company. Her duties have included publications management, public relations, sales, event management, office management and project management.

As part of the staff of the Washington Scholarship Fund Joylyn supported all development functions including proposals, solicitations and events and also served as WSF’s Office Manager, overseeing all day-to-day office operations. Prior to that, Joylyn was as a Project Assistant with The Language Doctors, Inc., where she coordinated several translation and interpretation contracts and proposals.

In 2000, Joylyn founded a non-profit performing arts production company, Omaemoda Productions, which uses the performing arts to raise funds and awareness for other charitable organizations in the DC Metro area. Past productions have included concerts, theatrical productions, play readings and cabaret shows that have raised funds for organizations such as So Others Might Eat, Calvary Women’s Shelter and DC Central Kitchen.

Joylyn holds a M.A. from American University and a Bachelor of Music from James Madison University.

Joy Hopkins’s Specialties:

Fundraising and development, event planning, database management, publications management, writing and editing, prospect research.
