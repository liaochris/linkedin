Stephen Bove’s Summary

20 years experience managing cross-disciplinary teams (some cross-dressing) of highly motivated ("let's change the world...err, get rich!") smart (lucky counts) people (a few were sociopaths) building complex (what's this thing supposed to do anyway?) products (beta?) in very competitive (razor thin margins) new industries (mirages).

Over the past 13 years, these teams have generally been part of fast-paced (sometimes misdirected) start-ups (likely to explode on launch) backed by top-tier (and a few cut-rate) early-stage ("do you already have 10 million users?") private equity firms (that's finance talk for "going to the races with other people's money").

Track record: participant in drilling 3 wells that yielded over $1billion in returns for investors (HOMS, CMDX, The Lion King), several low-to-negative-multiple acquisitions, a few boring-but-profitable slow-growth companies, several that resulted in shareholder litigation (not funny, not of my doing), and at least 10 very expensive dry-holes -- never a dull moment.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Entertainment industry)

    February 2008 — Present (4 months)

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; KAB-TO; Media Production industry)

  • VP Product Management

    BitTorrent, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    February 2006 — June 2006 (5 months)

    Refocused product delivery schedule on BitTorrent main-line client (delivered in mid 2006). Supervised overhaul of Torrent seeding/hosting infrastructure and Torrent search engine. Strongly advocated acquiring uTorrent (utorrent.com) as a means to i) expand client footprint, ii) increase advertising revenue from Bittorrent's search engine, iii) use uTorrent as possible replacement for mainline torrent client and iv) enter strategically critical set-top-box market with a small, lightweight, fast (compiled C) hardware embeddable client. uTorrent was subsequently acquired. Hired Design Director, Product Manager, Director of Customer Support (all still at BitTorrent, Inc.). Participated in closing and support of deals with major motion picture studios for selling feature films, television shows and music via BitTorrent's online media store. Planned implementation of DRM environment, secure transaction processing system, and storefront, all subsequently launched in early 2007.

  • CEO & Founder

    NewEdu, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; E-Learning industry)

    February 2003 — August 2005 (2 years 7 months)

    I funded and led this experiment in e-learning -- a teaching marketplace inspired by the research of Howard Gardner (Multiple Intelligences) and Fred Keller (Personal System of Instruction). The alpha/beta site was built by a dedicated team including James Home (now at MetaWeb), William Pietri (back to Scissor consulting), Brian Slesinsky (now at Google), Jim Kingdon (now doing non-profit work) and Justin Sampson (at some hot start-up I forget the name of). We used team-programming and the Agile development process to achieve outstanding efficiency (see our team room here: http://www.scissor.com/resources/teamroom/). The project was loads of fun (see photos: http://www.flickr.com/photos/stephenbove/sets/693742/ ) but has sadly fizzled due to lack of funding. The site is still live (http://www.newedu.com) while I decide if there's anyway to relaunch it.

  • VP, Product Strategy and Production

    Homestore, Inc.

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; MOVE; Venture Capital & Private Equity industry)

    February 1999 — February 2002 (3 years 1 month)

    Move, Inc. (NASDQ: MOVE), formerly Homestore.com (NASDQ: HOMS), operates the largest real estate information network on the Internet (super-sites Realtor.com, HomeBuilder.com & RentNet.com). My tenure there included 10 quarters as a public company during which revenue was grown from less than $30M to over $325M in 2001. I built and managed a product development organization of more than 130 people in four locations serving nearly 300,000 real estate professionals and millions of consumers in several vertical markets (existing homes, newly built homes, high and low density rentals, mortgage origination, remodeling, decorating and home maintenance). By 2000, the Homestore.com network was the exclusive provider of home and real-estate information to AOL and visited by over 5 million unique visitors per month. I had responsibility for annual budgets greater than $12M, was an organizer of the company's intellectual property development processes, and a lead inventor on two patents pending.

  • Director of Product Development

    Chemdex

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; NXPS; Internet industry)

    February 1996 — January 1998 (2 years)

    I was part of the initial team (employee #3 after the two founders) and an investor in Kleiner Perkins/Warburg Pincus backed Chemdex (NASDQ: CMDX) renamed Ventro (NASDQ: VNTR) and now NexPrise (NASDQ: NXPS), the bellwether B2B player in the life-science and health care markets. At Chemdex, I led all product development efforts during the company’s first ~two years of operations, supervising the design and launch of the world's first online market place for biochemicals and reagents, supervising initial integrations with buy-side companies Genetech and SKB, and helping pave the way for the company's first revenue, successful IPO and ultimate market capitalization of over $10 billion.
