Rich Gray’s Summary

As Vice President & Group General Counsel of Spirent Communications plc, publicly traded on the London Stock Exchange, Rich is based in Silicon Valley and is responsible for overseeing worldwide legal matters for Spirent and its subsidiaries in the Americas, Europe and Asia. Prior to joining Spirent, Rich was at Claria Corp. for over three years and served as its SVP, General Counsel and Chief Privacy Officer overseeing all legal, regulatory and privacy matters for the company. Prior to that, Rich was for almost four years Vice President and General Counsel of the Dublin, Ireland based Trintech Group plc, publicly traded on NASDAQ and, at that time, in Germany. At Trintech, he was the company's first in-house lawyer and oversaw all legal matters for the company's European, South American and U.S. subsidiaries. Prior to Trintech, Rich was in private practice for almost 19 years, including nine years as a partner at Silicon Valley's Bergeson, Eliopoulos, Grady and Gray. Rich has taught numerous seminars on law and technology, has been quoted extensively in the media on IP and antitrust questions, and has taught an antitrust course as an adjunct faculty member in the High Tech Law program at Santa Clara University Law School. He also wrote over 60 columns as part of the San Jose Mercury News’ award winning web coverage of the Microsoft antitrust trial. Rich graduated summa cum laude, Phi Beta Kappa with a B.A. from Boston College and received his J.D. at Stanford Law School where he won awards for best oral advocate and best brief in the Kirkwood Moot Court competition. Rich is licensed to practice law in California, Massachusetts, and Texas.
