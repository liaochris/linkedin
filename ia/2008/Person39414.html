Collin Udell’s Summary

My practice is focused on appellate matters and bankruptcy litigation. With a background in federal appellate practice, financial services litigation, federal securities litigation, environmental litigation, complex commercial litigation and patent infringement litigation, I have represented clients in a wide variety of commercial appeals before the U.S. Supreme Court and the U.S. Courts of Appeals. I have also authored and argued dispositive motions in federal district court and bankruptcy court.

Prior to joining Bingham, I was an attorney at Goodwin Procter in Boston, where I gained extensive complex litigation experience. Also informing my practice is my experience as a law clerk to the Hon. Bruce M. Selya at the U.S. Court of Appeals, First Circuit, the Hon. José A. Cabranes at the U.S. Court of Appeals, Second Circuit, and the Hon. Robert N. Chatigny at the U.S. District Court, District of Connecticut.

I have also taught criminal law as a visiting lecturer at the University of Connecticut School of Law.
