Amy Johns’s Summary

Amy Johns is a business and marketing strategist. She is passionate about launching and building high-performing startups as well as helping established businesses harness change to innovate or accelerate growth. In her 15 years leading strategy, she has been responsible for or directly involved with several media startups and has consulted to a range of clients, from small enterprises to Fortune 500 corporations.

Amy is a creative problem-solver and ideas packager with expertise in developing innovative solutions that meet business goals and customer needs and creating compelling stories that speak meaningfully to their audiences. She has experience in business and marketing planning, ideation processes, change management, brand identity, and communications. A leader, she has strong interpersonal skills and enjoys inspiring individuals and teams to achieve results.

Amy is a lead strategist with Vibrant Planet, a strategy and communications firm. She currently serves as acting CMO for her client Climate Clean, a carbon offset startup, developing their marketing plan, positioning, and value proposition. Prior to this Amy worked with Stone Yamashita Partners, a management consulting firm in San Francisco. Her accomplishments include creating new vendor partnership vision, goals, and communications for Gap Inc., and co-producing HP's new brand-identity system to streamline the company's image after its merger with Compaq.

Amy comes to consulting from a 10-year career in the media technology industry, where she helped build several companies. In 2000, as assistant managing editor of Time Inc.'s Business 2.0, she provided leadership in the launch of the magazine and website, which exceeded growth goals. Prior to Business 2.0, Amy was a senior editor and manager at Wired, which was acquired by Condé Nast for $80 million. Amy has a BA from Scripps College and an MBA from UCLA Anderson School of Management.
