Jukka Ala-Mutka, Dr. Tech.’s Summary

My core competences are growth company process, management of change program for large corporations, development of strategic capabilities, visualization of the business model and education of strategic thinking to the management team.

Dr. Ala-Mutka is founder and CEO of the Board of CONE ADVISOR and Senior Research Fellow and lecturer at Helsinki School of Economics.

He holds a Dr. of Technology from Tampere University of Technology and his key research interests are entrepreneurial process, strategic management of high-technology, knowledge-intensive and high-growth ventures (e.g., Born Globals) and strategy theory, formulation and implementation for rapid growth.

Dr. Ala-Mutka held a Postdoctoral Research Fellow position at the Desautels Faculty of Management, McGill University in Canada. He is the founding member of the Finnish Co-Entrepreneurs SKY Association. He worked also as a researcher in e-Business Research at Tampere University of Technology (TUT) in Finland. He was a senior consultant in management consultant firms specializing in customer relationship management, strategic management and change management. He worked as a development and a sales manager in Siemens in Finland and in the Baltic region.

Jukka Ala-Mutka, Dr. Tech.’s Specialties:

Dr. Ala-Mutka is a management advisor and he have rich experience in several industries, including brewing and beverages, retail (oil products, car sales, and daily consumer goods), catering services, industrial services, business services, metal manufacturing and products, furniture, construction, information and communications technology, and road administration (governmental organization), among others.

    JONOKA Research Program 2006-2008
    Management of Rapid Growth and Continuous Change

    Project will examine the sources of competitiveness and the formation and process of competitive strategies in the fastest growing young firms. These firms grow fast internationally to become early Born Globals and they should be viewed as Global Gazelles in early stages of their lives. This project starts with a review of the theoretical traditions lending support to increased growth rate and competitiveness to form a theoretical foundation first. It will then develop 12 to 15 in-depth case studies that will cover some 7 to 15 years of growth and internationalization to form and solidify the empirical foundation of this project. Tekes funding is 235 000 €.

  • McGill University

    Post-Doctoral Research Fellow, Management, 2005 — 2006

    McGill University, Post-Doctoral Research Fellow, Desautels Faculty of Management, Montreal, Canada

    The postdoctoral research project focuses on the fastest growing start-ups in Canada, their strategy and internationalization patterns and the holistic view of the entrepreneurial process from inception to rapid growth. The empirical evidence suggests that most of the rapidly-growing smaller firms, which are relatively resource-poor, combines multiple advantages and deploys a dynamic patterns and strategies for the rapid growth and internationalization.
