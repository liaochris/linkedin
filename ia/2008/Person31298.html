Carol Webb’s Summary

With an Ancient History & Social Anthropology BA (UCL) and a PhD in Qualitative Management Research (Cranfield), I work towards making practical application and take a ‘soft’ approach to research: looking for meaning at the local level, the influence of history and time at the broader level, taking into consideration diversity between individuals. A common denominator between my work is in the domain of learning; specifically, research and development of educational and training provision in Higher Education.

Previous UK industrial partners include: Stockholm Environmental Institute - Oxford Office Limited; CEH Wallingford; Bhs; British Airways; British Standards Institution; CAD for CAD; e centre; C&A; Freemans; Gieves & Hawkes; GUS Home Shopping; Hamamatsu Photonics UK; Hobson and Sons (London); John Lewis Partnership; Littlewoods; N Brown; NCR Financial Solutions Group; New Look; Oasis; Rohan Designs; RNIB; Shoemaster, CSM3D Int; Video Networks Ltd; Wicks & Wilson Ltd; DTI.

Previous international industrial partners include: Geological Service of Denmark & Greenland; Taskent Agricultural Mechanisation and Irrigation Institute (Uzbekistan); Cemagref (France); International Institute of Applied Systems Analysis (austria); Ecologic Institute for International & European Environmental Policy (Germany); Institute of Natural Resources (South Africa); Water Research Institute of National Research Council (Italy); Environmental Technologies Centre (Portugal); International Union for the Conservation of Nature; Institute for Inland Water Management & Waste Water Treatment (Holland); SEECON (Germany); Innovation Ecology (Israel); Grupo Antolin (Spain); Cezanne Software (Italy); The Node (formerly CDN, Spain); CCSO, Syllogos, Hotela, and Seyonic (Switzerland); Hoffmann (Germany); INNA (Austria).

Carol Webb’s Specialties:

Complexity science; organisational theory & research methods; change management; adaptive management; strategy; business development; leadership; project management; performance measurement; enterprise systems implementation; water sector management; group dynamics; training and educational development; knowledge management.

Foreign languages: German (advanced), Spanish (intermediate), Russian (beginners), French (beginners), and Italian (beginners).

Carol Webb’s Education

  • Cranfield University

    PhD, Management Research, 2002 — 2005

    In my research I explored the ways in which individuals made sense of their working lives by means of complexity science, as evidenced in work-focussed diaries - qualitative management research. Thirteen individuals volunteered to write weekly, work-focussed diaries, with the intention of continuing for one year: a goal which some met, but some did not. As a result of this study, the novelty presented by this research includes: the development of an interactive and open-ended, personal, journal-like diary method, facilitating longer term research in conversations with research participants, producing thickly descriptive and narrative data; a complexity science learning model representing the development of individual interest, learning and potential areas of application; and, a replicable approach incorporating specific methods of interaction with individual learners and the knowledge around such an intervention.
