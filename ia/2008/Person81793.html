Jackie Sanders’s Summary

Jackie Sanders Hayes is an award-winning broadcaster, actress and musician. She began as a DJ and newscaster on local radio in her native Georgia and moved into theatre, television and film upon moving to New York City. She toured extensively in shows such as “A Chorus Line” and “42nd Street” before beginning to work more in the city with celebrated playwright, Charles Busch among others. While working in New York’s theatre community, Jackie continued her broadcasting work as the newscaster and announcer on the Lifetime Network’s daily talk/variety show, Live from Queens, featuring Joy Behar. Jackie is also an avid jazz saxophonist. Recent accomplishments include writing, producing and directing a feature documentary film about the life of the late broadcaster and actress, Arlene Francis, which received critical acclaim from Liz Smith in her NY Post column. Summer of 2008, Jackie was selected by Sam Shepard to create the percussion underscoring for his new play, "Kicking a Dead Horse" starring Stephen Rea at NYC's Public Theatre.

Jackie Sanders’s Specialties:

Excellent voiceover skills; Musical Instruments - Alto Saxophone, Clarinet, Piano, 5-string Banjo, Guitar; Composer; Extensive research and writing skills; Music Composition for Film and commercials. Continually studying toward fluency in French!
