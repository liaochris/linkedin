Amit Narayan’s Experience

  • Vice President, Product Development

    Magma Design Automation

    (Public Company; 501-1000 employees; lava; Computer Software industry)

    September 2006 — Present (1 year 7 months)

    As Vice President of Product Development at Magma (NASDAQ: LAVA), I lead a global R&D team responsible for software development and product management activities for Magma's products in the area of design implementation. Combined, these products have over $150 Million in annual revenues and constitute over 80% of Magma's revenues.

  • Founder and Vice President of Engineering

    Berkeley Design Automation

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    July 2003 — September 2006 (3 years 3 months)

    As the founding CEO and subsequently the Vice President of Engineering, I was responsible for BDA's product vision and execution including all research and development, product management and customer deployment activities.

    I led the development of all BDA products from inception to deployment with over 15 on-time releases of increasing functionality and performance, "out innovating" much larger competitors in the process.

    BDA's products have been adopted by a growing list of world's leading semiconductor companies including Qualcomm, Broadcom, Fujitsu, Toshiba, Panasonic, Spansion, LG Electronics, SigmaTel (Freescale), Silicon Labs, Atheros, and several others, and are generating multi-million dollars in revenue.

    BDA's flagship products were named to EDN's top 100 products in electronics in 2006, and were recognized with EDN's exclusive "Innovation for the Year" award in 2007 for delivering significant advances in technology and product development to Electronics Industry.

  • Founder and CEO

    Berkeley Design Automation

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    October 2001 — July 2003 (1 year 10 months)

    Founded BDA in October 2001 and served as its President and CEO until July 2003. As the founder, identified the market opportunity for next generation of Analog and RF IC design tools and authored the company business plan to raise $17 Million in funding from top-tier venture capital firms in Silicon Valley.

    Forged partnerships with some of the largest semiconductor companies in the world and closed BDA's first ever revenue generating deal (multi-year, multi-license deal) prior to series A funding. Built an exceptionally talented development, applications engineering and marketing team with over 200 years of domain experience.

    In 2006, BDA was selected as a winner of the annual “Red Herring 100 North America” award by Red Herring magazine - the first EDA company in 5 years to earn this exclusive distinction - for playing a leading role in innovating the technology business.

  • Member Technical Staff

    Monterey Design Systems

    (Computer Software industry)

    1998 — 2001 (3 years)

    Monterey Design Systems was a high profile startup in the EDA Physical Design space that raised over $150 Million in venture financing since its inception until it was acquired by Synopsys (SNPS), Inc in 2004. I played a key role in architecting Monterey’s flagship product - DolphinTM.
