Molly Steenson’s Summary

I've worked with the Internet since 1994 and with mobile technology since 2003. In my professional practice, I am a design strategist and researcher, bringing together ethnographic approaches in order to probe how people, technology, social interaction and space intersect.

Academically speaking, I was formerly an associate professor at the Interaction Design Institute Ivrea in Ivrea, Italy (where I taught service design and design for social networks). Now, I am a PhD student at Princeton University School of Architecture, where I am tying together my interests in architecture, design and technology.

Ethnography, user research, mobile strategy, web interaction design, mobile interaction design, service design, content strategy, information architecture, design strategy, architectural history, design history, curation, design education

Molly Steenson’s Education

  • Princeton University

    PhD, Architecture, 2007 — 2012 (expected)

    Working on a PhD in Architecture at Princeton University. I research interactivity and responsiveness and their connections to architecture and urbanism.

  • Yale University

    Masters of Environmental Design, Architectural theory, history and criticism, 2005 — 2007

    The Master of Environmental Design program is Yale's architectural history and theory degree.

    My thesis, titled "The Architect, The Sketch and The Response: Construing and Constructing Cedric Price's Generator," took on Price, a British architect and iconoclast who had a prescient approach to interaction and responsiveness in architecture. Specifically, I wrote about Price's Generator (1976-9), an unbuilt project involving mobile 12' x 12' cubes on a field, outfitted with sensors, that would generate new layouts when bored.
