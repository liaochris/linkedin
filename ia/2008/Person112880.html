Leon Wynter’s Summary

Veteran (25 years) journalist, author and speaker with broad interest in business, marketing, race, popular culture and high technology.

I'm the author of two well reviewed books, "And I Haven't Had a Bad Day Since, (the memoir of Ways & Means Committee chairman Charles B. Rangel) (St. Martins Press, 2007) and "American Skin" (Crown, 2002)

I've addressed corporate clients including Time-Warner, Pepsico, Smithkline-Glaxo and Cox Cable on issues of race, diversity and marketing.

I was Manager, Public Relations, for the Guardian Life Insurance Co. (2000-2002)

I was a staff writer and columnist for The Wall Street Journal (1984-2000)

I've been a professor of journalism at Baruch (CUNY) and Emerson (Boston) colleges.

I've got a broadcast quality voice, and have broadcast dozens of essays and hosted programs on National Public Radio.
