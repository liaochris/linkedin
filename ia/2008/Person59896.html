Eric Inouye’s Summary

With over 13 years of Market Research experience I bring a wide spectrum of upper funnel research knowledge ranging from Advanced Product Planning, Product Planning, Vehicle Positioning, Vehicle Pricing, Brand Advertising, and Sales and Marketing. In addition, I've worked extensively with a distinct list of Automobile manufacturers in the design and development of research programs that were implemented throughout manufacturing facilities to improve overall vehicle quality.

Prior to joining J.D. Power and Associates, I served as a Research Manager at Nissan North America. Primary responsibilities included working directly with Brand Managers, Product Planning, Model Line, and Advertising in the design, development and implementation of research programs ranging from Focus Groups, Marketing clinics, Pricing clinics and Ad Copy Testing for all new product launch vehicles. I worked as an internal consultant to Nissan’s Executive Management Team by providing them with key market insights and strategies.

Prior to joining Nissan North America, I consulted for the State of California on the design and development of customized outcomes system. As a consultant I analyzed quantitative and qualitative outcomes data to better understand the overall effectiveness of State ran facilities. I also consulted on the development of customized web-based reporting software, and analysis tools.

Prior to consulting for the State of California I worked for American Suzuki Motor Corporation as a District Sales Manager in the South East region. While at American Suzuki, I was also instrumental in developing specialized stair step incentive programs for the American Suzuki dealer body. In addition, I was looked upon to assist in the development and implementation of the American Suzuki Fleet Sales Program.

Eric Inouye’s Specialties:

I have an extensive understanding of pipe line research within the automobile industry from ranging from Product Planning, Positioning, Marketing, Pricing, Brand Advertising, Sales and Marketing, with special emphasis on Conjoint Analysis used to effectively price new product launch vehicles.
