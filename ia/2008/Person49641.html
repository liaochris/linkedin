Denise Castellucci’s Summary

My strengths are in developing and organizing content online in a way that fills a niche or explores new territory and provides a new service.

I was a published contributor in "Opposing Viewpoints Series - Adoption" by Roman Espejo; Greenhaven Press; 1 edition (October 9, 2001) ISBN: 0737707895 "Adoption Records Should Be Opened" http://www.worldcatlibraries.org/oclc/47101016?tab=details

Denise Castellucci’s Experience

  • Producer & Designer

    Voices of Adoption

    (Non-Profit; Myself Only; Information Services industry)

    September 1994 — March 2003 (8 years 7 months)

    Produced and designed an award-winning and pioneering website about adoption. I wrote articles and opinion pieces, organized volumes of information, created graphics, collected stories, poetry, and art from the public, dealt with press inquiries, and marketed my website to interested parties through online promotion and attending various adoption conferences. My site received national and local press attention.

    I was named one of the Top 25 Most Influential Women on the Web and was a Webby Award Judge based on what I accomplished with Voices of Adoption. Forbes Magazine called Voices of Adoption "the NPR of Adoption Sites."

  • Co-Founder

    T.I.E.S. (Terminal Illness Emergency Search)

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    December 1997 — January 2000 (2 years 2 months)

    Co-founded and was on the board of T.I.E.S. (Terminal Illness Emergency Search), a 501 (c3) organization that provided free search and support services to birth parents and adopted persons that have been diagnosed with a terminal illness and do not have the time to wait for laws to change that would give them access to their birth records.
    As one of the founders and board members I oversaw a major reorganization of the organization to organize the documentation, verification of diagnoses, and increase our volunteer force to help clients. T.I.E.S got national and local press coverage during this time and helped families reunite.
