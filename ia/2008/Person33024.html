Catherine Schenck-Yglesias’s Summary

Currently available for consulting projects: demographics, global health, informatics, international development, measuring and communicating strategic results.
_______________________________________


From 2005 - 2008, Ms. Catherine Schenck-Yglesias co-chaired the Health Management Information Systems (HMIS) Technical Working Group for the President’s Emergency Plan for AIDS Relief (PEPFAR). She has over 10 years of experience in public health program strategic information (SI), designing and implementing global health program SI frameworks and monitoring achievements, while strengthening routine health information systems. She has applied World Health Organization (WHO) human resources for health (HRH) models to project health sector staffing needs in Africa and led the implementation of information systems to track health sector human capacity development activities in over 15 developing countries. She has led teams in human resource management assessment and reproductive health program evaluation. Ms. Schenck-Yglesias has developed training curricula for HIV/AIDS program monitoring & evaluation (M&E) and trained 300 participants in Africa and the Caribbean. She developed and taught HMIS/GIS workshops in Africa, Asia, Latin America & the Caribbean, the Middle East and U.S. In addition to having reviewed abstracts, moderated panels, and presented at global health meetings, she has authored numerous technical reports and articles. While at USAID, Ms. Schenck-Yglesias worked actively with several PEPFAR teams on strategies for information system design and implementation for Orphans and Vulnerable Children, Human Capacity Development and Supply Chain Management. Ms. Schenck-Yglesias represented USAID on the International Health Facility Assessment Network (IHFAN) and as a liaison with the Partnership for Quality Medical Donations.

Catherine Schenck-Yglesias’s Specialties:

International development, public health informatics, geographic information systems, program monitoring and evaluation, research; SPSS, ArcGIS
