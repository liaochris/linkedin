Domenico Ruggiero’s Summary

Domenico Ruggiero joined Booz Allen Hamilton in November of 2007 and has professed a desire to be a lifetime employee of the firm. He is currently providing results that endure to the NASA Constellation Program for the Ground Systems Project at the Kennedy Space Center. His roles and responsibilities are that of a Systems Engineer for NASA's Systems Engineering & Integration division, Deputy Task Manager, and becoming an expert in his specialties. His professional interests include becoming known as a promoter of the firm's Core Values as well as a proficient team-builder and people-builder.

Prior to joining Booz Allen, Domenico spent many years serving United Space Alliance in various capacities. In addition to participating in the Orbiter Major Modification (OMM) of OV-103 Discovery, his experience in the group was utilized during the STS-107 Columbia Reconstruction Effort following the unfortunate loss of Columbia's crew on February 1, 2003.

He also played a part in the resolution of a an in-flight anomaly for OV-103 during STS-121. The sequence of events leading up to the in-flight anomaly was professionally filmed by a production team and can be found at the following link -- http://www.countdownonline.tv/cdc01_link06_full.htm.

Domenico is a high-performing, ambitious and self-motivating individual. He is also a responsible family-man with a strong work ethic and high standards of integrity. His proven ability to gain the confidence of customers, fellow employees, and senior management has propelled his career in ways unimaginable to him. He credits his ability to provide effective and professional communication as a key contributer to his success. Domenico believes that the opportunities available to him within Booz Allen Hamilton, matched with the firms dedication to Core Values, shall grant him countless ways to provide many long years of selfless service to the firm and their clients.
