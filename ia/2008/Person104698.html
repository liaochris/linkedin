Ken Barber’s Summary

Jobs at Standard & Poor's, Digitas, and Pure along with my Babson MBA have provided me with considerable experience in the way businesses work at the Macro level, how consumers act at the Micro level, and how to identify and take advantage of entrepreneurial opportunities in both environments. My current expertise lies in helping forward-thinking companies identify and activate their digital marketing strategy and then prove to senior executives the effectiveness and value of that strategy.

But work to live, right? If money was no object I would spend my time checking off boxes on my Things to Do Before I Die list (which I actually have, somewhere) and traveling and hiking as much as possible.

Ken Barber’s Experience

  • Digital Strategist

    Pure Brand Communications

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    July 2007 — Present (11 months)

    I joined Pure to lead their digital marketing efforts with both clients and prospects. In this role I advise companies on how they can use online channels to speak to consumers in an efficient, cost-effective, and completely accountable way.

    This includes a discussion of which types of digital media make the most sense for their brand, budget, and strategy, how these channels can be implemented seamlessly with their traditional channels, and how to measure performance to prove that business goals were indeed met.

    During the first year I have designed and executed strategies involving websites, online product demos, e-newsletters, paid search, and social media.

    My role as Digital Strategist also allows me to write a regular column on Interactive Marketing in the Denver Business Journal where I discuss how businesses should be using digital marketing tactics. Recent articles discuss best practices of search & email marketing, corporate blogging, and building a quality website.

  • Manager, Strategy & Analysis Group

    Digitas

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    August 2003 — July 2007 (4 years)

    Digitas was a 4-year masters-level course in marketing. I worked on digital campaigns for world-class brands--GM, Intercontinental Hotels Group--with the sharpest and most creative minds in advertising. In the evenings I attended business school and learned marketing theory that I applied to my clients during the day. Fun times!

    • CRM solutions: Launched personalized email campaigns to generate increased sales from GMC owners and Holiday Inn loyalty club members

    • Integrated campaigns: Used right mix of impressions & engagement across websites, email, banner ads, and partner channels to achieve client objectives

    • Strategic partnerships: Executed strategic marketing alliances to gain access to coveted audiences and marketing channels (GMC/HGTV, Pontiac/Survivor, Holiday Inn/MLB)

    • Account Management: Served as Strategy lead for Holiday Inn’s $14 million account, coordinating work flow among junior staff, internal departments, and vendors to exceed client expectations

  • Latin America Economist

    Standard & Poor's DRI

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; Market Research industry)

    August 1997 — July 2003 (6 years)

    This job taught me the importance of understanding a client's complete macroeconomic situation when providing them with strategic business advice.

    I was responsible for producing economic forecasts and investment risk assessments for 12 countries in the Americas. The position involved advising Fortune 500 clients through market research briefs and phone conversations about the growth opportunities and potential business risks of conducting business with the countries that I covered.

    This was a great first job out of college with tremendous responsibility for someone in their early 20's and I quickly fine-tuned my analytic, forecasting, and writing skills along with my comfort level with senior-level clients.
