nick boris’s Summary

with fifteen years of broadcast, digital, and interactive development experience i have consistently strived to see that distinctions between mediums blur into a continuous landscape of user experience.

starting "when the web was grey" i found success working with the nfl, nbc, fox, mlb, and others extending their media presences on-line to capture a greater audience and broaden demographic appeal.

frustrated by the technological limitations of the digital landscape at the time, i moved to traditional broadcast at (abc)news and learned the techniques and philosophies necessary for developing a rich user experience in a more passive medium while waiting for the digital world to catch up to my dreams.

at (abc)news i worked in the advertising & promotions department overseeing and developing the (increasingly cross-media) advertising, marketing, &c. campaigns for (abc)news. i was also involved in 'special projects' from the development and marketing of new programming such as 'downtown', up-front presentations, breaking news, and the transition from analog to fully digital production.

as the "new media" landscape matured, i worked to blur the lines between on and off line broadcast with (abc)news and to extend traditional deliverables into the digital realm to provide richer content and greater interactivity.
this culminated with the launch of (abc)news NOW a 24-hour all-digital delivery news channel. (abc)news NOW, a radical departure from the traditional news channel, was developed to be configured by end users for their specific interests and viewing habits; based around short news 'bites' allowing users to quickly gain information and news updates or to string together their own 'news magazine' - all with rich interactive on-line content to complete the experience.

i left (abc) in 2006 because of a family health crisis but continued my involvement in the convergence between traditional and digital mediums, most notably with npr and pbs affiliates.
