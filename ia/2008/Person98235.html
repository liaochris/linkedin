Joshua Smith’s Experience

  • Graduate Research Assistant

    North Carolina State University Surface Science Lab

    (Educational Institution; 11-50 employees; Research industry)

    2002 — 2007 (5 years)

    The title of my dissertation is, "Thermionic Energy Conversion and Particle Detection Using Diamond and Diamond-Like Carbon Surfaces."

    I developed and implemented a theoretical model of space charge limited electron transport across a vacuum thermionic energy conversion device with an emitter electrode featuring negative electron affinity.

    Additionally, I worked on the IBEX-Lo neutral particle detector. I developed a hydrogen termination procedure using an electron cyclotron resonance chemical vapor deposition technique for the conversion surface facets of the detector that did not significantly roughen the surface. I also processed all of the flight facets for the IBEX-Lo detector using this technique.
