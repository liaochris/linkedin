Vishal Talwar ’s Summary

Lecturer and Marketing Manager with experience in developing and implementing marketing and communication strategies.

My principal research interests aim to understand how managers manage organisational markets as well as business customers/clients where relationships are complex and there is a prevalence of multiple networks, interdependencies and complexities, in the context of both developed and emerging markets. This highlights the differences in external environment, its effects on overall strategy, intensity of cooperation/competition, standardisation/flexibility and thus variations in how markets and customers are managed.

My current research is located within the Customer Portfolio Management stream of marketing literature in order to contribute towards expanding and developing further understanding of managing different portfolios of customers. This understanding goes beyond the matrix orientated approach which currently prevails within the academic literature (but is not necessarily adopted by practitioners). Two of my publications, currently in the latter stages of review will highlight and contribute towards an alternative approach to managing resource intensive organisation-customer relationships.

Expanding further on the resource-based view of buyer-seller relationships, the viability of a portfolio approach to customer management is being questioned through a research project being conducted in collaboration with marketing researchers from other business schools in the UK.

In addition to my main research interests, I am also interested in understanding performance measurement systems in the context of higher education with the aim of recommending an approach to managing performance at universities and business schools worldwide.

Teaching Strategic Marketing, Managing Customer Value, International Brand Strategy and Market-Led Management at the Postgraduate (including MBAs) and Undergraduate levels
