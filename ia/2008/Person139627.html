Neil Wickman’s Summary

"A self-motivated and confident 3D Animator, interested in tackling new challenges. Dependable and possess experience working independently or as part of a team. Personally committed to excellence and a lifelong love for the field of animation. Greatly desires to apply passion and extensive personal communication skills to the fast-paced modern workplace."

I'm also a fun-loving high-energy guy who considers working in animation a real dream come true. I love long hours and blistering schedules and being bunkered down with a team, and there's no industry that has as many challenges and rewards as the arts. A sassy attitude never hurt either, as well as good listening skills and an ability to take something constructive out of even the most personal criticisms.

My longterm goals include moving to a management position, where I can put my technical aspects to good use keeping things organized and on schedule, and I'd love to get to a Creative Director's position as well. I love seeing the process of animating work out, not just the drawing and clicking that makes it happen.

I also have a near-obsessive love of Game Design that I put to work in my free time, and I would love to make it a profession: I would literally never stop working. Using their own toolset, I created a total replacement for the Sword of the Stars graphic pack which was so influential and well recieved that many aspects appeared in their own expansion pack, and I was asked to contribute to the creation of an entirely new faction to the game, which is an ongoing process.

  • Freelance Editor

    Self-employed

    (Self-Employed; Myself Only; Broadcast Media industry)

    July 2006 — Present (2 years)

    Editors are responsible for taking a piece of film and cutting it together into a logical format. Often the raw footage is taken from several tries, of varying quality, and if is your goal as an editor to add feeling and energy to these ingredients by mashing them together. Animators use these same programs for creating Motion Graphics and 2D animation, so using them to edit film is a short leap of imagination. I am often asked to edit the work I do for a Freelance animation project, but I also take purely editing jobs when the opportunity is right.

    As an animator who edits his own work, I'd describe my style as logical and energetic. I like smart cuts that follow the movement, and I like to utilize a wide range of tools to keep the picture interesting.
