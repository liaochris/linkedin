Nick Labosky’s Summary

BIOGRAPHY

Chairman, Xyntropy Inc. (www.Xyntropy.com)
In late 2008, Mr. Labosky accepted the role of Chairman for San Jose-based Xyntropy Inc. Xyntropy (pronounced: ZEN-tru-pee) is a social networking company for the mobile space. Xyntropy is early-stage and is currently in ‘stealth-mode’. More on Xyntropy as things develop!

CEO, Digital Delivery Networks, Inc. (www.DDNi.com):
In early 2006, Mr. Labosky joined Digital Delivery Networks as CEO. He has accomplished the responsibilities of leading the company through the early Angel & VC fund raising, staffing the company's tech development, markt'ing, bizdev areas, setting up the initial distribution and rev-generating software/services partnerships and casting the marketing and platform dev-direction. DDNi now has over 100 content/services partnerships, solid Venture Capital investors, a strong Board that included the late Alan Shugart (founder, Seagate) as a valued member.
DDNi launched it's service/platform with a major PC-OEM and 'whitebox' manufacturers late in 2007.

General Manager, Hewlett-Packard Company:
In 2000, Nick Labosky joined Hewlett-Packard. As GM of the Ww group focused on the sales and marketing of aftermarket PC Internet services & soft/hard products from the Consumer/SMB PC desktop. Mr. Labosky led his team to engineer dramatic profit growth. Labosky drove his team to land key rev-generating partnerships with many touchstone Internet organizations including AOL, Microsoft, MSN, Earthlink, Symantec, Real Networks, Yahoo, Google, and others. Labosky left HP to join DDNi as CEO.

Executive, Stamps.com, Inc.:
Mr. Labosky was a 'charter' (employee #29), pre-IPO member of the Stamps.com, Inc. team, joining them in 1998 to secure nearly all initial OEM distribution partnerships & helping drive the market cap from $253 million to more than $2.5 billion by mid-2000. Mr. Labosky left Stamps.com Inc. in late 2000 to join Hewlett-Packard as General Manager.
