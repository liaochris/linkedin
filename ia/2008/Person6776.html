Alex Hunt’s Summary

Alex Hunt is a marine ecologist with a Masters degree in tropical coastal management. Before joining ITOPF as a marine pollution adviser he worked as a project scientist and research diver on three separate natural resource assessment projects in South East Asia and one in Puerto Rico. He also co-founded a multi-disciplinary environmental project in Nicaragua that provided the government with a coastal management plan for two of their Caribbean islands.

As part of the technical team at ITOPF, his main responsibility is to attend on site at oil and chemical spills world-wide to advise on clean-up strategies to minimise environmental and economic impacts, to promote effective response measures and to facilitate payment of compensation for clean up and damages caused by spills.

Since joining in February 2004 he has attended ship-sourced spills in Europe (Norway and Greece), North Africa (Tunisia), Asia (India, China, Japan, Korea, Indonesia and The Philippines), South America (Colombia, Brazil and Chile) and Antarctica. These include the SOLAR 1 and HEBEI SPIRIT oil spills. He has also attended an incident at an offshore installation in Newfoundland and has carried out consultancy work for the offshore industry in the Caspian Sea (Azerbaijan). Following advice given on site, he has co-ordinated claims advice to shipowners, P&I insurers and the International Oil Pollution Compensation Funds.

In addition to his spill response work Alex has covered several training commitments as a speaker during workshops and courses for international governments, leading oil companies, shipping insurers, offshore operators and NGOs. In 2005 he presented to delegates at the International Oil Spill Conference (IOSC) in Miami and in 2007 he assisted the Namibian government with the development of their National Oil Spill Contingency Plan.

Alex Hunt’s Specialties:

Ecological & economic resource damage assessment, aerial surveillance, project management, strategy negotiation, maritime law and marine pollution conventions, technical assesment of claims for compensation.

Alex Hunt’s Education

  • University of Newcastle-upon-Tyne

    MSc with Distinction, Tropical Coastal Management, 2002 — 2003

    The taught course provided comprehensive training in management of tropical coastal ecosystems, assessment and mapping of coastal resources, fisheries assessment and aquaculture, marine pollution impacts and assessment, wastewater management, environmental appraisal and impact assessment, principles and practice of ICM, policy and governance, environmental economics, data analysis, communication and information technology.
