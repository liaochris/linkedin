Michael Chaney’s Summary

My experience in New Hampshire began at the New Hampshire Humanities Council in 1986 where I served as Associate Director until 1996. The state affiliate of the National Endowment for the Humanities, I directed the grant making program, special projects and general administration. From 1996 to 1999 I served as Chief Operating Officer of the New Hampshire Historical Society and editor of Historical New Hampshire from 1995-1997. Before joining the New Hampshire Political Library in 2001 I served as Executive Director of Celebrate New Hampshire Culture, a private non-profit supporting the State of New Hampshire's presentation at the Smithsonian Festival of American Folklife in Washington, DC and its presentation in Hopkinton, New Hampshire the following year at a five-day festival.
I have always participated in projects that tell the story of life and work in northern New England (author of White Pine on the Saco River, co-author of Deeply Rooted: New Hampshire Traditions in Wood, and guest editor of a special issue of Historical New Hampshire on the New Hampshire Primary. Promoting New Hampshire's historic role in national politics since the state's founding is central to our work at the New Hampshire Political Library.

Michael Chaney’s Specialties:

Non profit management and the affiliation of cultural organizations that have similar mission and goals. I have spoken and written extensively about the culture of northern New England--from woods work to presidential primary politics.
