Francesco Longo’s Summary

Francesco Longo took his degree in Mechanical Engineering, summa cum Laude, in October 2002 from the University of Calabria. He received his Ph.D. from the Department of Mechanical Engineering at University of Calabria in January 2006. He is currently researcher in the same Department.

During the Ph.D. courses he was visiting scholar at the Production Engineering Department (Laboratory of Logistics, Simulation Team) of University of Genoa (February—May 2004). He was visiting scholar at the Department of Industrial and Systems Engineering of Rutgers University, the State University of New Jersey, USA (April—September 2005). In the same Department he was visiting researcher during 2006. Currently he is also an active member of the Simulation Team (a research group operating at University of Genoa, Italy).

He has published several scientific papers participating as speaker and chairman to international and national conferences. He is Program Co-Chair of the 19th European Modeling & Simulation Symposium, Track Chair of the Emergency Simulation track at the Summer Computer Simulation Conference. He is part of SCSC, EMSS and ECMS international program committees. He participated as advisor to ICAMES 2004 and ICAMES 2006 (International Cultural and Academic Meeting of Engineering Students, in Istanbul).

Starting from 2003 he worked on private research projects involving production and logistics systems operating in Italy. He has acquired experiences in consulting for Business Process Re-engineering and Logistics in different areas. He is currently involved in research projects with Italian and foreign universities.

He is Responsible of the MS&Net center at Mechanical Department of University of Calabria(Modeling & Simulation Network, an institution of the Society for Computer Simulation International with over than 12 worldwide centers: Usa, Korea, France, Spain, Canada etc.) . He is also member of the Liophant Simulation Club (personal page).

Francesco Longo’s Specialties:

He has acquired experience with Office package, simulation packages (eM-Plant, AnyLogic, Arena, eM-Workplace) graphic modelers (Pro-engineer, Autocad), software for realtime 3D modeling and general purpose programming languages.
