Brett Scarlett’s Summary

Brett's work experience has developed from an information technology foundation to now also include an outstanding depth and breadth of understanding in relation to conventional and innovative business strategies and their implementation.

The practical foundation of this broader business perspective has come from his involvement working on projects with many different organisations (such as Australia Post, Telstra, Oracle, Benchmark International, and the Treasury Corporation of Victoria) and by working on such things as business cases for major IT projects; the assessment of financing proposals for operating businesses and business acquisitions; and proposals for business initiatives requiring strategic, process and delivery-mode changes.

Roles including: business consultant and IT project director, vendor / contract manager, strategist and adviser with about 20 years experience in IT and management consulting. Client engagement experience in a range of industries: Internet and Application Service Providers, Telecommunications, Utilities, Transportation, Financial Services, Government and Media.

Voluntary community activities including: team mentor supporting the winning team in the 2006 RMIT Business Plan Competition: Kegs on Legs (KOL). The KOL team, winners of the first prize, were awarded $25,000 by RMIT on the basis of their business plan and presentation. This was the fourth consecutive year Brett has been a team mentor for the RMIT Business Plan Competition.

Brett Scarlett’s Specialties:

Project experience including: contract management (outsourced services), strategic consulting, architecting technology and application solutions, process redesign, systems integration, software and production systems support and project management.
