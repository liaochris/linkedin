Chris Smoot’s Summary

As of September 1, 2007, Chris Smoot will hold the title of Programme Director - World Vision Somalia. Prior to assuming this position, he was the Programme Development Manager for World Vision Somalia. Before joining World Vision Somalia, he worked for World Vision in Mauritania as the DAP Project Manager, which was their USAID food monetization development programming. Previously, he has worked as Country Director of the Adventist Development & Relief Agency – Bangladesh for 3 ½ years and Southern Sudan for 2 ½ years. He served as the Director of Corporate and Foundation Relations at Andrews University. Additionally, he served as a Governor’s Fellow with the State of New Jersey.

He has worked with a variety of funding partners in his career - USAID, DFID, AusAid, Global Fund, DANIDA, Carter Center, Canadian Foodgrains Bank, CIDA, and SIDA, and he has managed programs in food security, health, water and sanitation, micro-credit, education, food monetization, and disaster relief.

In his major leadership positions he has gained recognition for a number of achievements. Included in these are administrative team-building, strategic organizational planning, human resource development, international outreach, sponsored project development with funds coming from Europe, Japan, Korea, Australia and North America and higher education institutional fundraising. He has conducted leadership and management seminars for various organizations in the United States, Asia, and Africa.

He has received a Bachelor of Arts in History from Andrews University in 1984. In 1986, he received a Master of Public Administration with emphasis in Urban Administration degree from the University of Missouri – Kansas City. In 2001, he received a Master of Science in Administration with emphasis in International Development from Andrews University. Currently, he is a degree candidate in a Ph.D, program in Organizational Leadership from Eastern University.

International Development, Community Development, Disaster Relief, Leadership in INGOs, Higher Education Fund Raising, Government Relations, Strategic Planning
