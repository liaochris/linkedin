Kayce Reed’s Summary

My research interests lie primarily within political computing, massively multiplayer games, user-generated new media, data visualization, and human-computer interaction design.

My graduate capstone project is devoted to designing and developing a new form of political discourse online, a type of blogging environment where participants compete to bring their political conversations into a common ground of understanding or consensus (The Sweet Spot). Further, I am working on a project which attempts to gauge public perception of congress based on the design of their websites.

I have an extensive background in music performance and education (primarily piano and percussion). I have taught private percussion lessons to a number of students since 1998. I was also a member of the Indiana University Symphonic Band and the Marching Hundred throughout my undergraduate career at IU (2002-2006).
