Daniel Corbe’s Summary

E-Mail: dcorbe AT gmail DOT com

I am a well rounded network engineer with both voice and data exerience and I am also a life long student of communications technology.

My telecommunications experience includes CLEC, IXC, Mobile, Broadand, Termination and Origination. I have worked extensively with GSM, CDMA, TDM (including SS7 and ISDN) and IP-based voice (VOIP) (including SIP, H.323, MGCP and SIGTRAN).

My service provider experience includes large-scale IP networks and data centers. I've worked on and designed implementations of IP VPN, L2VPN, Peering Fabrics and Transit Services as well as access solutions including Cable, DSL, Dial-Up and Metro Ethernet. I have extensive experience working with BGP, MPLS, OSPF and IS-IS.

I am an experienced programmer with strong analytical and software design skills. I have expert level knowledge of C, C++, Perl, PHP and TCL. I have done extensive network programming in both Unix and Embedded environments.

I am an experienced system administrator with exposure to: BSD, Linux, Mac OS X, Sun Microsystems (Solaris), HP/Compaq (HP-UX), and more.

I have worked extensively with over a dozen equipment vendors of communications technology including (but not limited to)

IP: Cisco, Juniper, Foundry, Riverstone, Redback
Soft Switches and MSCs: UTStarCom, Telos, Porta-One, Sylantro, FreeSWITCH
BSC/BTS systems: UTStarCom and UltraWave
Other Mobile Products: Telenity (CANVAS products)
VOIP SBCs: Acme Packet, Nextone, Covergence, Netrake
TDM Gear: Sonus, Telus, Cisco, Quintum, Alcatel and Nortel
PBX Systems: Asterisk, Cisco, Avaya
Other: SER (SIP Express Router), OpenSER

I am also an Open Source advocate and have worked extensively with various open source software solutions to create robust core service offerings and back office solutions. I have and continue to contribute to the open source community by providing documentation and code contributions to numerous projects.

Daniel Corbe’s Specialties:

Engineering white-label IP and Voice solutions. Building robust and scalable IP and Voice products. Implementation of protocol stacks in C and C++.
