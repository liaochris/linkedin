Bryan Campen’s Summary

Exerienced new media professional and connector who works in networked communities for creative purposes, produces compelling online and virtual world content, directs short documentaries and builds online communities. More than 5 years online production experience, leading and providing vision for strategic multimedia initiatives. Strategic thinker who is also able to execute effectively and efficiently.

Speaking Engagements:

Wheaton College (2007)

Bryan Campen’s Specialties:

Bryan Campen’s Experience

  • Online Communications Coordinator

    Interfaith Youth Core (ifyc.org)

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    2007 — Present (1 year)

    "Interfaith Youth Core builds mutual respect and pluralism among young people from different religious traditions by empowering them to work together to serve others."

    I design and implement our use of online and participatory media partnerships, programs and tools. My background is deeply rooted in social media and online community development.

    Before my arrival at IFYC I worked as founder of blueair.tv, a company dedicated to non-profit community-building both on the web and in virtual worlds. Since 2003 I have been deeply involved with online communities ranging from blogs to Flickr, Second Life and Facebook.

    This summer I produced the remix of 77 Million Paintings by Brian Eno in Second Life. The project raised awareness for The Long Now Foundation and was covered in major media outlets from Wired to Current.tv.

    I have also contracted on a number of online and virtual campaigns for clients such as Toyota and Governor Mark Warner.

  • Founder

    blueair.tv

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Online Media industry)

    2006 — Present (2 years)

    Blueair.tv focuses on gallery and artist experiences, from a mixed-reality opening for client Jen Bekman Gallery in New York City, to currently directing development of blueair.tv client Long Now Foundation.

    Our sponsors and collaborators have included Joi Ito Labs, Leeds College of Art and Design, Art Center in New York, and CADRE in San Jose.

    Most recently work revolved around production of 77 Million Paintings by Brian Eno in Second Life. This spanned coordinating over 12 international contractors for the virtual installation, live camera feeds from the San Francisco installation, PR, marketing and procuring designers for avatars and other builds.

    Our work has been featured in Reuters, Business Week, Time Out New York, Photo District News, and MAKE Magazine.

    Reuters: http://tinyurl.com/2crf6e

    Time Out New York: http://tinyurl.com/yxdu84

    MAKE: http://tinyurl.com/2we5h6

    Also at work on several projects with artists in New York to open in 2008.

  • Co-Founder

    Visitor 17

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Fund-Raising industry)

    June 1996 — Present (11 years 8 months)

    Co-founder of Visitor 17, a 501(c)3 created to benefit the families of mental health patients enduring financial hardship due to mental illness.

    --Produced the photo documentary project "Commitment", which covered the hospitalization of my father, Cyrus Campen, in May of 2005. This project received coverage in Slate and was the impetus for the largest mental health community on flickr, "Mad in America"

    LINK: http://flickr.com/photos/campen/sets/473858/

    --Conducts all PR for Visitor 17, including press releases to local and regional media outlets, letters to editors at local papers, and interviews with press.

    --Produced and acted in Twelfth Night by William Shakespeare to raise funds for families in need due to mental health issues.

  • Owner

    Blue Air Pictures (blueairpictures.com)

    (Motion Pictures and Film industry)

    January 2003 — December 2006 (4 years)

    --Shot and edited New Hope, an 18-minute documentary short on the first African American community in DeKalb, Illinois. Screened film to audience of 300 in Wheaton, Illinois.

    LINK: http://blueairpictures.com/newhope.mov

    --Created two flash sites, one to supplement our documentary work with New Hope, one to extend my "Commitment" series.
