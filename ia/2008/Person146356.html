Kim Lindholm’s Summary

Kim Lindholm (33), Director & Co-Founder of Motion Avenue International Ltd, has been involved in the convergence of television and wireless communication since 1999. As an early project, he developed "Window playing", games played on shop window TV screens using text messaging. It did not become big business, until someone came up with the idea of exposing similar concepts to television – something we today refer to as Participation TV. Since, Kim has been an innovator in more than thirty multiplayer, interactive and cross-media formats for television. Some were world firsts: first interactive multiplayer action game on TV, text-to-speech based interactive parody, quiz show formats with 3D virtual audiences. Kim has also been a frequent speaker at industry events such as 3GSM, MipTV/Milia, Discop, TeleTV Summit and SMS meets TV. He has also participated as a juror in the International Interactive Emmy® Awards in 2006 and 2007.
Prior to founding Motion Avenue, Kim worked as an e-commerce consultant at Siemens, and as Business Development Manager at Frantic Media (one of Finland’s most reputable New Media agencies). Before that, he worked as an entrepreneur and managed projects in Europe and South America. Kim has a Master’s degree in Business Administration and Entrepreneurship from the Helsinki Swedish School of Economics and Business Administration, and he speaks fluent Swedish, Finnish, English and Spanish.

The main focus of Kim Lindholm's company, Motion Avenue, is to help broadcasters, production companies and cable and satellite operators to define and implement their mobile service offerings. Motion Avenue offers a complete range of services for adding interactivity to TV shows, and a wide selection of Participation TV formats, including multiplayer action, sports and mind games, quiz shows and cross-media and community concepts. Motion Avenue’s most visible project include BOXi, an innovative 3D avatar quiz show.
