Fred Barrett’s Summary

Product development exec with strong marketing and sales background, specializing in startup, early-stage and turn-around situations. Most recent experiences in supply chain, RFID, asset tracking and telemetry, satellite and GSM communications, and back-end data center integrations and management, but still very current in internet services, content management, and user application design.

Fred Barrett’s Experience

  • Founder

    AscentIQ, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Logistics and Supply Chain industry)

    May 2007 — Present (1 year 4 months)

    AscentIQ allows you to visually create M2M applications and application mashups through a unique drag-and-drop user interface, reducing your RFID application development time from days and weeks to minutes and hours!

    Prior to AscentIQ, Barrett co-founded RFTrax, Inc., a technology company focused on Supply Chain visibility and high-value asset management in 2001. Mr. Barrett co-developed and received a patent for “inter-modal telemetry and monitoring solution” (U.S. Patent #7135976). RFTrax was acquired in 2005 by Fairfield Industries, where Barrett continued to lead product development through the completion of his contract in April, 2007.
    Mr. Barrett lives in Allyn, WA and is an active Rotarian, past Trustee of the North Mason Chamber of Commerce, Board President of the Pacific Northwest Salmon Center, and Board President of the North Mason Education Foundation.

  • Founder, VP Product Development

    RFTrax, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Logistics and Supply Chain industry)

    January 2001 — April 2007 (6 years 4 months)

    RFTrax, Inc., is an Asset Tracking, Telemetry and Management company. As Founder and CTO, I led the design and development of the company's container and vehicle tracking hardware and software systems. I was also responsible for creating the back-end data center to catch all data telemetry from the remote field devices, and providing client integrations for the telemetry data. The company was acquired by Fairfield Industries, Sugar Land, TX in April, 2005, and my "Golden Handcuffs" were removed in April, 2007.
