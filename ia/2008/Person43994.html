Daniel Taghioff’s Summary

I am a researcher, writer and trainer with a background in communications, environment and development.

My research and writing has been published in a wide variety of formats: Via academic journals and conferences, educational publishers, websites (I design CMS based sites, and write copy for them), evaluation reports to central government and commercial market research reports, as well as in the media, the Prospect magazine site being one outlet.

I have also delivered communications training in a variety of settings, from Swedish Central Government (MInistry of Environment), the Swedish Stock Exchange, the Swedish White Collar workers union, as well as in English Universities (SOAS, Leicester).

I am curently undertaking a Phd and a book project, and hope to continue my career in the direction of research and publication (by whichever chanel is appropriate), especially in the areas of communications, environment and development.

Daniel Taghioff’s Specialties:

Qualitative research and ethnography, group facilitation, expertise in climate change, low input agriculture in the tropics, rural development, development communications, building Joomla-based websites, usability testing of sites, and evaluation of public communication and consultation processes, as well as of development projects and organisational learning.

  • Project manager and lead research contractor

    Direct Excellence

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Research industry)

    June 2006 — July 2007 (1 year 2 months)

    I was the lead contract researcher on the Web-monitor project, a usability study of websites across the bulk of the UK insurance industry. The top 20 sites in the motor, home and travel insurance sectors were reviewed via focus group and structured ranking exercises. I reported directly to the Executive Director, as we developed and launched this product. I was responsible for overseeing every stage of the project, from the research design, to setting up focus groups and moderating them, to developing the scoring of the sites, as well as devising web-visibility rankings. I was the lead author on the final reports, which were sold to clients on an industry-wide basis. The product was written up in Post, the main insurance industry magazine.
