Robert Newby’s Education

  • University of Bath

    BSc, Physics, 1995 — 1999

    4 year course with a year out in industry where I studied alongside the Medical Physics team at Bath's Royal United Hospital. My studies concentrated on medical imaging using hypersensitive photodiodes to detect minute (single photon) amounts of gamma radiation emitted from Technecium-99 tracers injected into patients. The aim was to research the building of much smaller imaging cameras than are currently available, those which are used today weighing up to a ton and standing over 2 metres high.
    Experimental evidence showed that the diodes were able to detect the radiation in these amounts, but budget constraints hindered the project from making progress. Much of the rest of the project time was spent developing imaging software.

    Activities and Societies:
    Dabbling with C and C++ and extensive use of early email programs such as PINE and Eudora in the University at this time started me on my career in IT. I was advised whilst at University that it was much more fun playing with network devices, so concentrated on engineering in that field.
