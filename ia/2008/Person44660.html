  • Vice President Global Sales & Operations

    Vormetric, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Computer & Network Security industry)

    January 2005 — October 2006 (1 year 10 months)

    Vormetric markets a hardware and software solution that offers customers a better way to protect sensitive data at rest. In this position I am responsible for North American Sales and Professional Services. 2005 sales increased over 400%, and 2006 Q3 was a record quarter for the company.

    Accomplishments:
    • Under my direction we closed sales at major accounts and established Vormetric as a corporate security standard at Ann Taylor, The GAP, BMW, Boeing, CNN, Disney, EDS, CIA, DOE, ABN AMRO, Schwan Food Services, The NBA., TVA, Wells Fargo and many others.

    • Created an Inside Sales Team focused on the SMB marketplace that contributed to the overall sales growth at the company.

    • Worked with Marketing to create a campaign focused on the Payment Card Industry Data Security Standards market. Increased revenues from this segment to over 40% of the company’s revenue.

  • Senior Vice President

    Callidus Software, Inc.

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; CALD; Computer Software industry)

    August 1998 — October 2004 (6 years 3 months)

    In Q1 of 2004 Callidus acquired the source code rights, and the Italian development team, for the product now called TruePerformance. I was selected to take over the new business unit and in addition all EMEA operations. My assignment was to launch a new product out of the Bari operation by the end of 2004, and to grow the sales and service unit to 15% of company revenues.

    Prior to this, as founder of the Client Services line of business for this start-up company I created the consulting, education, and technical support operations. At of the end of 2003 the combined business unit was operating at a $50m run rate, and producing at least 27% margins. I had global responsibility for client services including several major EMEA, and Asia Pac projects. Callidus offers follow the sun technical support with support centers in London, Sydney, and Austin.

  • Vice President, Sales & Services

    Brightware, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Computer Software industry)

    May 1993 — July 1998 (5 years 3 months)

    Brightware was the market leader in Intelligent Interactivity for E-Commerce on the internet. Brightware offered customer-direct application products that used AI technology to establish the two-way communication required to make the internet a viable marketing and sales channel. In FY97 every representative in my organization made club.

Inference was the leading provider of self-service and knowledge management solutions that help people in business solve problems on-line. I received the “Sales Area of the Year” award in ’95 and an award for “most improved business unit” in ‘93.
