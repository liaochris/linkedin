David Janik-Jones’s Summary

Following the success of Widget Monkeys, with more than 750,000 downloads, our goal is to create simple, elegant, joyful, fun “little games” for Apple's touch-enabled platforms. We are unique in that we have had extensive experience creating our widget “games” and showcasing them to users around the world. We us a small, experienced team of programmers/artists who focus on making unique games that are beautiful, simple and technologically elegant. We are NOT eager to create run-of-the-mill games like many of our competitors. We will redefine causal gaming by creating “little games” that focus on simplicity, joy and pure fun. Games that people worldwide love to play for a few minutes to relax or challenge themselves. That they simply love to look at. It is this core philosophy that makes us different than gaming firms like Aspyr or EA. We are creating toys, not games, that make people happy in the simplest sense of the word. That allows them to be childlike in play.

Our focus at Cocoa Touch Games is more on creativity and inventing simple, fun games and less on custom technology. Beyond Apple's own development toolset(s) we are using widely available and supported open-source technologies to create our games to allow us to bring our ideas to market quickly, ensure they work well, and permit us to focus on the joy and the ideas of playability and fun. These include 2D physics libraries like Chipmunk and Box 2D, and 3D libraries where needed like ODE. Things like on-line high score lists delivered via WiFi will use industry-standards like MySQL, php, etc. We have been extensively involved with the Unity game engine and hope that we will be able to incorporate that into our production as it develops to support the iPhone SDK. Trading off intensive custom technologies in our market space that provide little modular value from title to title will enable us to create games that focus on simplicity, joy, art and fun.
