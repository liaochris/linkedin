Eric Heggestad’s Summary

Although my graduate training was in personality research, I am an industrial and organizational (I/O) psychologist. Although I/O psychology scholars and practitioners are all interested in understanding people at work, there are, as the name of the disciple clearly implies, two rather distinct focuses within the field. One the one hand, organizational psychology focuses on relationships within a work context; relationships between employees, between the employee and a supervisor, between the employee and the organization, and even between the employee and his or her life outside of work. On the other hand, industrial psychology focuses on understanding, predicting, and improving the performance of employees at work. My professional interests and expertise fall well to the industrial side of the field. Specifically, my research interests are associated with the assessment of personality and use of those assessments for understanding and predicting employee behavior. Likewise, with respect to teaching, I have taught courses on personnel psychology, psychological measurement, and personality psychology, all of which relate to key topics and issues from the field of industrial psychology.
