Dustin Hodge’s Summary

Dustin Hodge has been an undergraduate student at the University of Washington since 2002. In June of 2008 he will graduate with a Bachelor of Science Informatics, the study of information systems and information technology from a user-centered perspective, and a Bachelor of Science in Applied and Computational Mathematical Sciences (ACMS), a cross-discipline degree consisting of courses from Applied Math, Computer Science, Mathematics, and Statistics. His concentration in ACMS is in Discrete Mathematics and Algorithms.

Dustin also spent February through December of 2005 studying abroad as a foreign exchange student at La Trobe University in Melbourne, Australia.

Upon graduation, Dustin looks forward to a rewarding career that allows him to combine his mathematics training and user-centered design skills to solve challenging, novel, and interesting problems. The perfect job would also allow him to gain out-of-class development experience. Currently, he is most interested in Web Analylitics, User Experience Design, Project Management, Information Architecture, Web Development, and Requirements Engineering.

Within the next 5 years, Dustin hopes to have lived and worked abroad (or be living and working abroad) and to be pursuing a Masters degree in a field related to his work experience.
