Ashby Rice’s Summary

* Plastics & Rubber Industry senior executive with more than 32 years of experience in the development and manufacture of all types of polethylenes and high performance elastomers. In June 2005, I retired from DuPont Dow Elastomers LLC, where I served as Vice President Technology & I.T for nine years (1996-2005). DuPont Dow was a 50/50 joint venture between Dupont (DD) and The Dow Chemical Company (DOW), with annual revenues of approx. one billiion dollars. DuPont Dow manufactured a broad line of high performance elastomers including polychloroprene, chlorinated polyethylene, chlorosulfonated polyethylene, ethlene-HAO elastomers and EPDM rubber (both produced using Dow's proprietary INSITE technology), and fluoro and perfluoro elastomers. Primary industries served were automotive, electronics, construction, aerospace, seals & gaskets, and footwear.

* Prior to joining DuPont Dow at it's formation in 1996, I spent 23 years with The Dow Chemical Company in various R&D positions. The majority of this time (1973-1991) was spent in overseeing process and product development for Dow's global polyolefin business, with a principle focus on polyethylene.

*Two of the remaining five years of my Dow career (1991-1993) were spent as Vice President of R&D and EH&S for Dow Chemical Canada Inc. The final three years were divided evenly between two Global R&D Director roles: Emulsion Polymers (1993-1994) and Liquid Separations (1994-1996)..

*During the last five years of my DuPont Dow career, I was also responsible for corporate Information Techology. In addition to managing day-to-day I.T. operations, which included a stand-along SAP application and desktop computing and network infrastructure, I was heavily involved in the creation of DuPont Dow's intranet, e-Business, and document and knowledge management capabilities.
