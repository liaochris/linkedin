  • Founder

    Green Challenge

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Venture Capital & Private Equity industry)

    July 2007 — Present (8 months)

    The Green Challenge calls on creative, innovative minds to come up with concrete solutions to fight climate change. Anyone with a good idea for a product or service that reduces greenhouse gas emissions in a consumer-friendly way and contributes to a sustainable lifestyle is welcome to take part. The Dutch Postcode Lottery will provide €500,000 for the execution of the winning plan.

    The Jury includes Sir Richard Branson (Founder of Virgin), Avery Baker (EVP Global Marketing and Communications Tommy Hilfiger), Steve Howard (CEO The Climate Group), Peter ter Kulve (Senior Vice President Ice Cream Category Unilever), John Thackara (Director Doors of Perception), Liesbeth van Tongeren (Executive Director Greenpeace Netherlands) and Eckart Wintzen (Founder Ex’tent Green Venture Capital). All members are closely involved with green projects and/or accomplished players in the business world.

  • Member

    Innovation Platform

    (Government Agency; 11-50 employees; Government Administration industry)

    May 2007 — Present (10 months)

    It all started with the European summit in Lisbon in 2000 where the European leaders defined a new mission for Europe: to become the most competitive and dynamic knowledge economy in the world. With regards to the Lisbon Treaty, the Netherlands stated the ambition of becoming one of Europe's leaders in the knowledge economy by 2010.

    The Innovation Platform aims to strengthen the innovation potential of the Netherlands in order to secure a leading role for this country in the European knowledge economy of 2010. To achieve this the Netherlands should re-establish/regain values such as excellence, ambition and
    entrepreneurship.

    The Innovation Platform consists of 17 members, originating from the business community (large and SME), knowledge institutions and the government (members of the cabinet) and is chaired by the Prime Minister Dr. Jan Peter Balkenende.
