David Willson’s Summary

I have had a widely varied career with many lead creative roles in advertising and publishing.

I began as an advertising art director, graphic designer and illustrator, ultimately founding and operating my own studio in the Palm Beach area. As such, I frequently took a hands-on approach to designing, writing, editing, illustrating and photographing our projects.

When Mac-based computer production became a reality (combining all of those disciplines at one station) I joined American Media Inc. as part of a team formed to launch new publications. In the process, we also helped develop the beta version of the fully digital production path that was later implemented across all AMI titles.

I went on to primarily specialize in launching and redesigning magazines for a variety of clients. Also for the last 15 years, I have been the freelance editorial cartoonist for the Palm Beach Daily News. I frequently write and illustrate for various publications, and design and produce advertising and brand marketing materials as a freelancer or under contract.

I am not currently employed full time. I am always ready to take a new magazine or design project under my wing. I would also be very interested in the editorial cartoonist position with a metropolitan newspaper or for syndication.

Please feel free to e-mail me at DWllsn@gmail.com if you need creative services.

David Willson’s Experience

  • Editor in Chief

    ValueRich Inc.

    (Public Company; 1-10 employees; IVA; Publishing industry)

    September 2003 — January 2008 (4 years 5 months)

    I launched ValueRich magazine under contract, producing the first two issues out of my studio and then eventually became the Editor in Chief of the quarterly magazine. I also helped the new company with all of its brand identity and marketing materials as well as providing corporate communications when needed.

  • Senior Art Director

    American Media

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Publishing industry)

    February 2000 — July 2000 (6 months)

    I was rehired by AMI to be the lead art director for several "true crime" titles acquired from the purchase of Globe Communications. The object was to redesign and reposition sleazy crime magazines for distribution through mass market channels to crime enthusiast readers. The distributors were very excited about what we developed, but the projects were eventually killed by the publisher for internal strategic reasons.

  • Creative Director

    The International Association of Corporate Real Estate Executives (NACORE)

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Real Estate industry)

    September 1993 — February 2000 (6 years 6 months)

    I was Art Director of the Corporate Real Estate Executive, a monthly magazine about corporate strategic management. When I joined NACORE, I significantly redesigned the magazine and their other publications, bringing their computer graphics up to date in the process.

    I was also Creative Director of all of the association's marketing outreach, such as their Web site; collateral and marketing materials, catalogs, brochures, advertisements and signage for their continuing education institute and national symposium events.

    Duties included creative staff management, art direction, computer graphics, photo effects and illustration; planning, purchasing, pre-press and print management.

    NACORE was merged into a competitor association in 2001.

  • Desk Editor

    American Media

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Publishing industry)

    August 1991 — September 1993 (2 years 2 months)

    I was a desk editor for a team that was assembled to launch new publications. We began by launching the weekly fan magazines Soap Opera Magazine and Country Weekly magazine.

    Our team was also the beta testers of the prototype all-digital production concept, later implemented throughout all AMI owned operations.

    Duties included Macintosh networked computing, design, illustration, layout, headline/caption writing, copy editing. Additionally, created the initial design concepts for prospective new publications on a project basis.

  • President, Creative Director

    David Willson's Artmasters Inc.

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Graphic Design industry)

    June 1985 — August 1991 (6 years 3 months)

    I was the founder, president and creative director of a full-service graphic design studio. Our clients included: Allied Signal Aerospace Bendix/King, First National Bank in Palm Beach, Servico Corporation, Med-X Corporation of Miami, The Burt Reynolds Jupiter Theatre and various advertising agencies and public relations firms from Miami to Orlando, Florida.

    Also at this time I served as an artist in residence at the Palm Beach County School of the Arts, teaching drawing and painting to students in grades 7 - 9 and adjudicating entry portfolios.
