Mary Ann Cicala’s Summary

MaryAnn Cicala brings nearly 10 years of event production, city government, non-profit, arts administration, and academic administration experience to her work.

As the Associate Director of Alumni Relations at Emerson College, MaryAnn works to foster and maintain the spirit and devotion of the alumni in an effort to promote the reputation and growth of the College.

Prior to returning to Emerson, MaryAnn worked for the Cambridge Arts Council (CAC), a public non-profit and city agency in Cambridge, MA that provides arts services and programming designed to stimulate public awareness of and support for the arts. In this capacity, she helped broker strong relationships between the City and local artists and organizations by managing the state and city funds in the Local Cultural Council (LCC) grant program, overseeing the lively Street Performer Program, and producing arts and music festivals. MaryAnn has a strong history in building community participation and civic engagement. This expertise extends to event planning, volunteer management, production, and stage management for major audiences as the Co-producer of the Cambridge River Festival, a one day celebration of the arts on the Charles River that attracts over 100,000 people and Producer of the Summer in the City performance series that takes place in the many green spaces in the City of Cambridge.

MaryAnn holds a BFA in Theatre Arts Management from Emerson College where she is also a Masters candidate in Communication Management. She has been on the teaching staff at the Harvard Extension School for Oral Communication: Business and Organizational. MaryAnn's affiliations include membership on the Board of Directors with the Passim Folk Music and Cultural Center, Federal Club membership of the Human Rights Campaign, Zeta Phi Eta (A National Professional Fraternity for Communication Arts and Sciences), and a past contributing member of the Hong Kong Dragon Boat Festival Boston.
