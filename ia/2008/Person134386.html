Matthew Malchow’s Summary

I have moved back and forth between the private industry and the public agencies related to freight transportation. Significant focus has been in the maritime world. I use the analytical skills I developed with my engineering background and the passion and understanding I have of financial markets and economic forces to evaluate future opportunities.

These opportunities can be the best investments for a private company to make regarding future activity or the best regulatory actions for a government agency to take if it is to meet its objectives.

Matthew Malchow’s Specialties:

Economic modeling, Inventory development, Process change analysis, Market Analysis, Financing, Transportation systems analysis, Spreadsheet and database management

Matthew Malchow’s Experience

  • Air Resources Engineer

    California Air Resources Board

    (Government Administration industry)

    January 2008 — Present (1 year 1 month)

    I analyze the emissions that result from movement of freight to/from/within California and all factors that affect those emissions. I use my engineering and analytical background to apply economic principles to and evaluate data needs for our analysis of the goods movement system.

    We work with trucks primarily but also marine vessels, trains, and cargo handling equipment. We work with multiple fuel types to determine which are cleanest and most effective toward moving goods in a cost-efficient, environmentally friendly manner.

    My group works with others to set the regulations for the industry in which my previous employer resides - I am helping them to understand how the industry works and at the same time learning how the regulatory agency works in the event that I ever work with industry again.

    These regulations include that for greenhouse gases and climate change as well as those for criteria pollutants and the resulting local health impacts.

  • Business Process Analyst

    Marine Terminals Corporation

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Maritime industry)

    March 2005 — January 2008 (2 years 11 months)

    The former MTC operated marine container terminals at the Ports of Long Beach, Los Angeles, Oakland, Portland, Seattle, and Tacoma, among others. I was part of the Operations group within the managements side.

    My essential function at MTC was to evaluate process changes, be they technology deployment or assignment of labor responsibilities, to determine the impact on MTC's operations. The key benchmarks related to productivity of operations (got to get the ship's turntimes down) and cost of operations (make each move as simple and efficient as possible).

    While there I worked with the people at the port, with Customs, with union labor, and others as well as the management at MTC - who was acquired by AIG and combined with the former P&O Ports on the East Coast to make PortsAmerica.

  • Planner

    United States Coast Guard

    (Government Agency; 10,001 or more employees; Military industry)

    June 2004 — December 2004 (7 months)

    I evaluated possible resource allocations for the US Coast Guard. The resources were primarily personnel, facilities, and equipment (cutters, small boats, helicopters, aircraft). The objectives ranged from search and rescue to homeland security to protection of Alaska's fishing resources.

    Alaska is awesome. One project related to the future needs for Coast Guard resources in the Arctic Ocean as global warming makes travel there more accessible and greenness makes travel there more in vogue. Another project related to the development of facilities in Valdez ro protect the pipeline from the North Slope. A third project related to the protection of cruise ships and their projected travel patterns in Southeast Alaska.

  • Visiting Postdoctoral Researcher

    (Educational Institution; 10,001 or more employees; Higher Education industry)

    October 2002 — September 2003 (1 year)

    We worked with the Calfiornia Department of Transportation to build a website that would be used to enhance the benefit-cost analysis associated with transportation investments.

    The website is accessible at:
    http://www.dot.ca.gov/hq/tpp/offices/ote/benefit_cost/index.html

  • Transportation Planner

    JWD Group

    (Logistics and Supply Chain industry)

    June 2001 — June 2002 (1 year 1 month)

    I worked with others to evaluate the capacity of and need for investment in transportation facilities, primarily marine terminals. The facilities worked with ranged from those in Southern California to those in Hong Kong, Holland, and all other corners of the world.

    We used spreadsheet analysis and simulation software developed in-house. I worked with the application of and programming behind the C++-based simulation software.

    This consulting firm was later acquired by and integrated into DMJM Harris.
