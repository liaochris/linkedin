Thomas Hruska’s Summary

Been developing little software projects (mostly for myself) for 18 years. Started programming on the TI-99/4A. Moved to the Tandy 1000 (MS-DOS 2.11.22). That should date me pretty well. In other words, I've been around for a really long time.

Sometime in the past couple years, a light-bulb finally clicked on. I realized that most software sucks in some aspect or other and also realized the reason why it sucks. I wrote a book called Safe C++ Design Principles that contains the embodiment of knowledge on how to become a software developer. The book is somewhat of a work-in-progress as I refine what it means to be a software developer and it isn't exactly C++-specific. Many of the principles are applicable to other languages but there is a heavy slant toward C++. There is a huge difference between a programmer and a software developer. VerifyMyPC and MyUpdate Toolkit are shining examples of the principles in practice.

I am a person of many hats. I take pride in my artistic/graphics design skills - every last pixel on my website and in my products is my handiwork. I'm the author of a book - despite how it sounds, book writing isn't easy. I like to help people do their jobs better through the software I write.

My goal is to drastically alter the software industry. Particularly, how software is developed and for whom it is developed. There are a lot of disparate technologies and a lot of poorly designed wannabe duplication. Standardization efforts create bloated, poorly-designed, overhyped ideas like XML.
