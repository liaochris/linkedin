Jesse Hsu’s Summary

Enterprise rises and falls on leadership.
As professionals carry increasing loads and meet rising expectation, we use gas pedal much more than rear mirror. We speed through bumps and thrust beyond engine red line without noticing. We lose sight of what's going on behind us, and ignore the overheat under the engine hood. And we spin ourselves out of alignment with our basic support system. We lost balance.
We find work as routine but lacks meaning, ourselves hollow, and career bored. Life seems to be distant and deprived from us. I observed this pattern from many respected high achievers, and tasted some myself.
I started a process call "half time planning" in 2000. And fast forward, I took steps to re-engineer my path and proficiency.
Now I am a leadership coach, with holistic work-life balance in mind, serving corporate clients seeking to retain and develop their individual key talents.


My Path:
Tech start-up experience brought me in touch with rising trends and international exposure. Kellogg MBA broadened my career path and set my eyes on service industry.
Procter & Gamble and McKinsey & Co. jump-started my pursuit for best practices and for private equity investment.
Focus On the Family inspired me to give back, and be vigilant about corporate politics beyond profit seeking.
Various board and consulting experience refined my approaches in advisory and put me in closer friendship with prevailing leaders.

Now I am contently contributing from an Executive Coach role. Columbia University Advanced Coaching Program provided excellent peers and vigorous training for my career transition. The rise of Chinese economy and demand for top talent give me plenty of opportunities to develop motivated managers. I take joy in listening to real leaders, and generate tailored solutions to orbit these rising executives to higher track and fuller lives.
