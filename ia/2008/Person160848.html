Christiaan Erik Rijnders’s Summary

Competition is now harder than ever and progress faster so as engineers we have no choice. We must take calculated risks with new technologies and challenge the way things are done or else many companies risk missing the boat.

Until April 2005 in a key role within the dominant Ferrari Formula 1 team. Left Formula 1 because felt the new rules (no engine development, less electronics, no tire development, etc) go against the fundamental spirit and attraction of Formula 1.

As a racecar performance development engineer now prefer working in series such as Formula Renault Eurocup, A1GP and the new Superleague Formula.

As a consultant driven on introducing new "intelligent" sensor and simulation technologies within diverse engineering fields such as racing cars, battlespace integration, UAV's, road car active safety, energy and emissions and the private space industry.

My focus with "intelligent" sensors is on the parallel necessary evolution of systems engineering and quality management tools and processes.
