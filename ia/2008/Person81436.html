Ivy Clark’s Summary

Goal - I'm currently exploring the possibility of freelancing in Perth, Australia; where I am now based.

With over 10 years of varied work experience all relating to digital or interactive design, backed by a mix of commerce, computing and art qualifications, I have been a valuable asset to all my employers and clients.

I have been a project manager, web designer/developer, information architect, instructional designer and trainer.

Project management is part and parcel to most of my past employments. There is always user/business requirements gathering, matching of requirements with available technologies/resources, evaluating vendor proposals, development of project plan, ensuring the project is on track, ensuring everyone (colleagues, vendors, business users, etc) is on the same page, producing project/system documentation and providing the necessary post-project support.

As a web designer/developer and information architect, my responsibility was to gather business requirements, perform the necessary environmental analysis to design and develop user-centric sites/systems, such as:
- corporate web sites,
- online shopping carts
- GUI design for voip dialers
- GUI design for online streaming control panels

Before I started working on web projects, I was an instructional designer tapping on my prior experience as a trainer. I put together the storyboards of computer based training for military/air force use by the Singapore government -- Training objectives were identified, existing materials (e.g. books, manuals, tools) were provided, and through close consultation with the Subject Matter Expert, I worked out ways to convert static/paper materials into more engaging and interactive tutors complete with quizes.

Ivy Clark’s Specialties:

Strong communication skills, business requirements gathering; competitive web site analysis, deriving user centric information architecture, storyboarding for computer based training (CBT), web design and development, HTML, XML, CSS, JavaScript, usability, universal accessibility, Photoshop, Dreamweaver, PHP, Coldfusion, Java, ASP.
