Nicholas Brooke’s Summary

You could say that Internet is my passion. When I finished my Economics Bachelor degree @ ULB, I needed new spaces to explore and rapidly turned to the Internet. The Web is fascinating in the way it transforms all conventional systems, business and social networks, opening doors to all kinds of innovations. Every day is a discovery journey full of new ideas and opportunities.

Soon, the Internet and its applications growth will be powered by the 3G mobile phone, so you should get on board quickly if you want to keep understanding where we are going.

Consultant is not very concrete. So I describe myself as a Web Architect or an eMarketing Architect. The Web Architect builds a bridge between worlds. It is a key position in the Web organization today, the visionary who bridges the Web content, e-Marketing and technical domains, acting as a pivot point between the technical and the nontechnical members of the Web team.So, I am not a developer as an Architect is not Mason or a Plumber. I design the project and then make it happen by dealing with the different persons/functions involved.

It is not only a bridge between IT and the the rest of the company, it is also the education of the management and marketing department to understand the new environment, integrate new skills and the fundamental changes the Web brings into their world (dialog, marketing automation, SEO, consumer generated content, third party sellers like eBay, Web Analytics, Pay-per-performance, click through rate…). Most of the time companies limit their Web plan to a website, nothing very developed and certainly not integrated to their business model.

So it is my job to help building awareness about this, bringing some structure into it and make it happen.
